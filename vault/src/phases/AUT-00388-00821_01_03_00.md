## AUT-00388-00821

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Varta Facility in Graz, Austria

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2024**: Varta is establishing a large research facility for battery technology in Graz, focusing on optimizing existing battery technologies and developing "post-lithium technologies," particularly for the automotive sector. The facility will cover an area of approximately 3,500 square meters. The aim is to operate one of the most modern research facilities for battery technology in Europe, with a focus on sustainability, reducing energy consumption, and using recycled materials.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2023**: Varta concluded an acceptance agreement indicating a continuation of work on series production of battery cells for electric cars, following a pause in 2022 due to cost reasons. The facility's development is part of Varta's strategy to consolidate its position as a technology and innovation leader in battery technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **End of 2021**: Pilot production of the V4Drive cylindrical cell in the 21700 format began.
- **2023**: Initial plans for mass production of the V4Drive cells with a capacity of at least 2 GWh were paused.
- **January 2024**: Varta aims for full operation of the new research facility by the beginning of the second quarter of 2024, with expectations to increase the number of international researchers from 30 to 50 by the end of the fourth quarter.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2024**: The facility is expected to create additional jobs, increasing the number of researchers from around 30 to 50 as the facility becomes operational.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is operated by Varta Innovation GmbH, a research spin-off of Varta Microbattery GmbH and Graz University of Technology. The ownership structure indicates that Varta has a significant stake in the facility, although it is a collaborative effort with Graz University of Technology.
