### AVL Facility in Graz, Austria - Evolutionary Timeline

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2020**: The Graz University of Technology, in collaboration with AVL, opened the Christian Doppler Laboratory focused on the development of solid-state batteries. This facility aims to address the reduction of interfacial resistances within solid-state batteries, which are expected to offer significantly higher energy and power densities compared to traditional batteries with liquid electrolytes. The laboratory is equipped to explore various approaches to improve battery performance, including the introduction of intermediate layers for lithium transport and alternative charging methods.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2020**: The budget for the Christian Doppler Laboratory is approximately two million euros, funded through a combination of public and private sources. The Federal Ministry for Digitalisation and Business Location (BMDW) is identified as the primary public funding agency for this initiative.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2020**: The Christian Doppler Laboratory was officially opened, marking the establishment of the twelfth CD laboratory at Graz University of Technology. The joint research project is scheduled to last for seven years, indicating a long-term commitment to advancing solid-state battery technology.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2020**: The facility employs seven staff members dedicated to research and development in solid-state battery technology. The establishment of this laboratory is expected to contribute to job creation in the field of battery research and development.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2020**: The facility operates as a collaborative effort between Graz University of Technology and AVL, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by either party. AVL's involvement is crucial as they are a developer of innovative drive batteries, and the research findings from this laboratory are expected to significantly benefit their future battery module developments.
