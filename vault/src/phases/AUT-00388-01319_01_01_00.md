## AUT-00388-01319

### Evolutionary Timeline of the TU Graz Facility in Graz, Austria

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12-10-2020**: TU Graz researchers developed a sustainable redox flow battery using vanillin instead of liquid electrolyte, aiming for a production capacity of up to 800 MWh. The technology is scalable and suitable for constant production.
- **12-11-2020**: TU Graz opened the Christian Doppler Laboratory for solid-state batteries in collaboration with AVL, focusing on reducing interfacial resistances to enhance performance. The laboratory is part of a seven-year joint research initiative with a budget of around 2 million euros.
- **02-11-2021**: TU Graz and Rouge H2 Engineering announced the production of high-purity hydrogen from biogas using a new chemical looping process. The initial setup is a 10 kW plant, with potential scaling to a 3 MW hydrogen production facility.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12-11-2020**: The Christian Doppler Laboratory for solid-state batteries is funded by public and private sources, with a total budget of 2 million euros, primarily from the Federal Ministry for Digitalisation and Business Location (BMDW).
- **10-07-2024**: TU Graz received funding for the 'Battery4Life' project, with a total of approximately 6.5 million euros from the Austrian Research Promotion Agency (FFG), alongside contributions from the province of Styria and corporate partners totaling around 9 million euros.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12-10-2020**: Announcement of the development of a sustainable redox flow battery.
- **12-11-2020**: Opening of the Christian Doppler Laboratory for solid-state batteries.
- **02-11-2021**: Announcement of the world's first high-purity hydrogen production from biogas.
- **10-07-2024**: Approval of funding for the 'Battery4Life' project, marking the establishment of a new battery research center.

#### 4. Labour
- **12-11-2020**: The Christian Doppler Laboratory for solid-state batteries employs seven researchers, contributing to job creation in the field of battery technology.
- **10-07-2024**: The 'Battery4Life' project involves collaboration with various international partners, indicating potential job creation through expanded research activities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The TU Graz facility operates under a collaborative model, with joint ventures such as the Christian Doppler Laboratory with AVL and partnerships with Rouge H2 Engineering for hydrogen production. The ownership is not exclusive, as it involves multiple stakeholders including public funding agencies and corporate partners. 

This timeline encapsulates the significant developments and milestones of the TU Graz facility, highlighting its contributions to clean technology and energy storage solutions.

### Technology and Operations
- **Redox Flow Battery Development**: "Researchers at TU Graz University claimed that they had developed a redox flow battery that utilizes conventional vanillin instead of liquid electrolyte, which could make the battery more environment friendly." (12-10-2020)
- **Solid-State Battery Laboratory**: "In Austria, the Graz University of Technology has opened a new facility for the development of solid-state batteries with their industrial partner AVL." (12-11-2020)
- **Hydrogen Production from Biogas**: "Austrian researchers have demonstrated a chemical looping system can be retrofitted into biogas plants... High-purity hydrogen for fuel cells is produced from real biogas; not only in the laboratory but actually on an industrial scale." (02-11-2021)

### Investment and Development
- **Battery Research Funding**: "The FFG is funding the project with a total of around 6.5 million euros, the province of Styria is contributing 2.6 million euros and the state of Upper Austria will provide another 600,000 euros." (10-07-2024)
- **Corporate Investment in Battery Research**: "Corporate partners from the automotive and electronics industries are investing around nine million euros over the planned duration of four years." (10-07-2024)

### Project Status and Milestones
- **Redox Flow Battery Plans**: "The researchers have plans to commercialize the redox flow technology, which is scalable and suitable for constant production." (12-10-2020)
- **Solid-State Battery Laboratory Opening**: "The new CD Laboratory just opened to further work on solid-state batteries." (12-11-2020)
- **Hydrogen Production Plant Potential**: "Instead of the 1%, the researchers would also be able to channel the entire biogas flow of the biogas plant (about 480 cubic metres per hour) through a correspondingly up-scaled chemical looping plant. This would lead to a 3 MW hydrogen production plant." (02-11-2021)

### Labour
- **Job Creation in Battery Research**: "The budget of the CD Laboratory with its seven employees totals around two million euros." (12-11-2020)
- **Battery Research Team Expansion**: "The new facility will see a team led by Christian Ellersdorfer from the Institute for Vehicle Safety conduct research into improving the safety, service life and sustainability of batteries." (10-07-2024)

### Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture for Solid-State Battery Lab**: "Drive and testing specialist AVL is on board the CD Laboratory for solid-state batteries." (12-11-2020)
- **Collaborative Research**: "The COMET (Competence Centres for Excellent Technologies) ‘Battery4Life’ builds on the work from the COMET project ‘SafeLIB’ and can also draw on testing capacities from the Battery Safety Center Graz located at campus Inffeld." (10-07-2024)
