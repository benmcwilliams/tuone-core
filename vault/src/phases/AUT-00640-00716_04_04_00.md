## AUT-00640-00716

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Siemens Facility in Linz, Austria

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2019**: The H2Future plant, a 6MW green hydrogen production facility, begins operation at Voestalpine’s site in Linz. It utilizes a Siemens Silyzer 300 PEM electrolyser to produce hydrogen from water using renewable electricity from Verbund. The facility is noted as the most effective and state-of-the-art of its type, aimed at testing the technology for industrial-scale applications.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2019**: The H2Future project receives €18 million (approximately $20 million) in EU funding to support its development and to explore the potential for providing network services and compensating for power grid fluctuations.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2019**: The H2Future plant officially begins operations, marking a significant milestone in the industrial application of electrolysis for green hydrogen production.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2019**: The establishment of the H2Future plant is expected to contribute to job creation in the region, particularly in the steel production sector, as the clean hydrogen produced will be utilized in steel manufacturing processes.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2019**: The facility is a collaborative project involving Siemens, Voestalpine, Verbund, Austrian Power Grid, and the Dutch research organization TNO. This indicates a joint venture ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by Siemens.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones related to the Siemens facility in Linz, Austria, focusing on its technology, investments, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
