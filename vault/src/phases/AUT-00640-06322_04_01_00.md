### Evolutionary Timeline of the Voestalpine Facility in Linz, Austria

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **November 2019:** The H2Future plant at Voestalpine’s site in Linz begins operation. It features a 6MW Siemens Silyzer 300 PEM electrolyser that produces green hydrogen by splitting water molecules using renewable electricity from Verbund. The facility is noted as the most effective and state-of-the-art of its type, aimed at testing the industrial scalability of green hydrogen production. The hydrogen produced will be utilized in steel production.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **November 2019:** The H2Future project receives €18 million (approximately $20 million) in EU funding to support its development and investigate the potential for providing network services and compensating for power grid fluctuations.
