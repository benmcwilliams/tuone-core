### Evolutionary Timeline of the Johannes Kepler Universität Linz Facility in Linz, Austria

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04/2024**: Research conducted at the Johannes Kepler Universität Linz focused on high-temperature heat pumps (HTHPs) powered by renewable energy sources (wind and solar). The study highlighted that these heat pumps are the most cost-effective and environmentally friendly option for generating industrial steam. The specific HTHP examined has a nominal heat supply capacity of **250 kW** and is designed for use in industrial processes requiring low-pressure steam of up to **5 bar**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04/2024**: The research team recommended that policymakers provide subsidies for industrial projects utilizing heat pumps to generate steam, suggesting a tariff of **€3 ($3.2) per gigajoule (GJ)**. The analysis indicated that a total subsidy of **€129,600** could be justified based on the **43.20 Terajoules (TJ)** produced over the plant's lifetime.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04/2024**: The research findings were published in the paper titled “Life cycle assessment and shadow cost of steam produced by an industrial-sized high-temperature heat pump” in the journal *Sustainable Production and Consumption*. The study compared various steam-generating techniques, establishing the viability of HTHPs in industrial applications.
