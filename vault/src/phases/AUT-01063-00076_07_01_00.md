### Evolutionary Timeline of the BMW Facility in Steyr, Austria

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2020**: The BMW plant in Steyr begins producing housings for the electric drive of the BMW iX3. The facility aims to increase its annual production capacity to **460,000 units by 2025**.
- **June 2022**: BMW announces plans to develop and produce the next generation of electric drives at the Steyr plant starting in **2025**, with a target production capacity of **600,000 e-drives per year**. This includes manufacturing the rotor, stator, gearbox, inverter, and housing for the sixth generation of e-drives.
- **December 2022**: Preparations for the production of the next generation of electric drive systems begin, including the conversion of existing production areas and the creation of new areas.
- **February 2024**: Completion of new production halls for the New Class electric drive systems is announced, with pre-series production scheduled to start in **July 2024**. The facility will produce over **600,000 drive systems per year**, alongside diesel and petrol engines.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2022**: BMW commits to investing around **one billion euros** in the Steyr facility by **2030** to support electric drive production and achieve CO2 neutrality.
- **February 2024**: The investment includes over **500 million euros** specifically for plant and equipment to support the production of the New Class electric drives.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2020**: Start of production for electric drive housings for the iX3.
- **June 2022**: Announcement of the development and production of the sixth generation of electric drives to begin in **2025**.
- **December 2022**: Initiation of preparations for the new electric drive production.
- **February 2024**: Completion of new production halls; pre-series production set to start in **July 2024** and series production scheduled for **autumn 2025**.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2022**: BMW states that by **2030**, around **half of the 4,400 employees** at the Steyr plant will work in electromobility, indicating job creation in the electric drive sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The BMW facility in Steyr is owned exclusively by BMW, as indicated by the company's direct involvement in the development and production processes at the site. 

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic decisions regarding the BMW facility in Steyr, Austria, focusing on its transition towards electric drive production and the associated investments and operational changes.
