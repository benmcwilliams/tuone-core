## AUT-01350-01890

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Renalfa IPP Facility in Vienna, Austria

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Renalfa IPP facility is involved in the development of solar energy projects. It is a partner in Tenevo Solar Technologies EAD, which is constructing a 237.6 MW solar plant in southeastern Bulgaria. This facility is expected to generate over 300 GWh of electricity annually and will be the first renewable energy plant over 100 MW in Bulgaria to operate without a support scheme or corporate power purchase agreement.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - On November 23, 2024, it was reported that the solar plant secured a total of €103 million ($107.2 million) in financing. This includes €50 million from the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development (EBRD) and €53 million from Raiffeisen Bank International. The investment is part of a broader initiative to foster sustainable energy projects in Bulgaria.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - The announcement regarding the funding and development of the 237.6 MW solar plant was made on November 23, 2024. The project is currently in the development phase, with plans to add 250 MW of behind-the-meter energy storage on site in the long term.

4. **Labour:**
   - The article does not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the Renalfa IPP facility or the Tenevo Solar Technologies project.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is part of a joint venture, Tenevo Solar Technologies EAD, which is equally owned by Renalfa IPP (Vienna-based developer) and Danish independent power producer Eurowind Energy.
