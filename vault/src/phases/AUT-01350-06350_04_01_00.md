## AUT-01350-06350

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Wien Energie Facility in Vienna, Austria

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2023**: Wien Energie, in collaboration with OMV, is set to build the first deep geothermal plant in Aspern by 2026. This plant aims to generate up to 20 MW of climate-neutral district heating, sufficient to supply approximately 20,000 households.
- **07/2023**: Wien Energie is conducting a joint operational test at its Donaustadt power plant, mixing hydrogen with natural gas. This test is noted as the first of its kind globally for a commercially used gas and steam turbine plant.
- **04/2024**: Wien Energie launches a new green hydrogen plant with a production capacity of 1,300 kilograms of green hydrogen daily, enough to fuel up to 60 buses or trucks. This plant is part of a broader initiative to establish a green hydrogen economy in eastern Austria.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **11/2023**: The joint venture "deeep" between Wien Energie and OMV involves a joint investment of around EUR 20 million for the planning and implementation of deep geothermal projects in Vienna.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2023**: Announcement of the joint venture for deep geothermal energy development in Vienna, with plans for the first plant in Aspern.
- **11/2023**: Confirmation of the joint venture "deeep" and the initiation of planning for multiple deep geothermal plants in the region.
- **04/2024**: Opening of the first green hydrogen plant in Vienna, marking a significant milestone in the city's transition to hydrogen fuel.

#### 4. Labour
- **04/2024**: The establishment of the green hydrogen plant is expected to create jobs related to the operation and maintenance of the facility, although specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **09/2023**: Wien Energie operates the facility in a joint venture with OMV for the geothermal projects, indicating a partnership rather than exclusive ownership.
- **11/2023**: The joint venture "deeep" confirms that Wien Energie and OMV are collaborating on the geothermal energy projects, further emphasizing the shared ownership structure.
