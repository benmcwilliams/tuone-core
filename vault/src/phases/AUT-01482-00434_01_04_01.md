### Evolutionary Timeline of Kreisel Electric Facility in Rainbach, Austria

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2017**: Kreisel Electric's facility in Rainbach, covering 7000 square meters, began operations with a fully-automated production line for their home storage system, Mavero. The initial production aimed to manufacture the first 500 units of several thousand reserved systems.
- **September 2022**: Following John Deere's acquisition of a majority stake in Kreisel Electric, the facility's production capacity was set to expand by more than 2 GWh to support electric and hybrid drive systems.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2022**: John Deere announced a considerable capital commitment to expand Kreisel's production capacities. This investment included plans for a new plant at John Deere's factory in Orléans-Saran, France, and additional battery assembly investments in the US.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2017**: The Kreisel Electric facility officially opened and began production.
- **December 2021**: John Deere announced its intention to acquire a majority stake in Kreisel Electric.
- **Early 2022**: The acquisition deal was completed, allowing John Deere to access Kreisel's battery and charging technology and initiate capacity expansions.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2022**: The expansion of production capacities was expected to create additional jobs, although specific job creation figures were not disclosed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Early 2022**: John Deere acquired a majority stake in Kreisel Electric, indicating a shift in ownership structure. Despite this, Kreisel Electric continued to operate under its own brand, maintaining a level of operational independence while benefiting from John Deere's resources and expertise.
