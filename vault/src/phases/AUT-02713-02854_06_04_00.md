### Energetica Facility Timeline - Liebenfels, Austria

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2019**: Energetica began producing solar modules at its facility in Liebenfels, Austria, with an initial production capacity of 550 MW per year. The company aimed to ramp up production to 1 GW annually.
- **December 2022**: After being acquired by IRMA Power, Energetica resumed production, achieving an annual production capacity of 350 MW. The facility produces two types of monocrystalline modules, “e.Classic” and “e.Prime,” with outputs of up to 390 W. Plans were announced to introduce facade modules and transition from M6 to M10 wafers.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2022**: Following the acquisition by IRMA Power, investments were made to ramp up module production and restore operations after the insolvency of Energetica in November 2021. Specific investment amounts were not disclosed, but the focus was on scaling production and improving product quality.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2021**: Energetica filed for insolvency due to financial difficulties.
- **December 2022**: Energetica resumed production under the ownership of IRMA Power, with plans for further scaling and the introduction of new products.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2022**: The facility employed around 100 people, including many specialists who had previously worked for Energetica before its insolvency. The transition to a four-shift operation was planned to increase production capacity.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2022**: Energetica was acquired by IRMA Power, marking a change in ownership from being independently operated to being part of a larger entity focused on continuing module production and improving product quality. The facility is now under the full ownership of IRMA Power.
