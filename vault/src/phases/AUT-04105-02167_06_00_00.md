## AUT-04105-02167

### Evolutionary Timeline of the BayWa Facility in Grafenwörth, Austria

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2023**: BayWa r.e. completed the construction of a **24.5 MW floating solar PV plant** in Grafenwörth, Austria. The facility is built on the water surface of two lakes at a former sand and gravel pit, covering approximately **14 hectares**. It is noted as the **largest floating solar installation in Central Europe**. The system consists of **45,304 solar modules** and is expected to produce **26,700 MWh** of green electricity annually, sufficient to power around **7,500 households**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2023**: The project was developed by BayWa r.e.'s subsidiary **ECOwind** in collaboration with Austrian utility **EVN**. Specific investment amounts for the Grafenwörth facility were not disclosed in the articles, but it is part of BayWa r.e.'s broader strategy to expand its floating PV portfolio, which includes a total of **15 FPV projects globally** with a combined capacity of **230 MW**.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2022**: Construction of the floating solar PV plant began.
- **February 2023**: The facility was completed and operational, marking it as the **fourth-largest floating PV plant in Europe**.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify the exact number of jobs created or lost as a result of the construction and operation of the Grafenwörth facility. However, the rapid construction timeline and the involvement of local utility EVN suggest potential job creation during the construction phase.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is developed through a **joint venture** between BayWa r.e.'s subsidiary **ECOwind** and **EVN**. Therefore, it is not exclusively owned by BayWa r.e. but rather operates under a collaborative partnership.
