### Evolutionary Timeline of the Zillertaler Verkehrsbetriebe Facility in Jenbach, Austria

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **03/2023**: The Tirol provincial government endorsed the conversion of the Zillertalbahn (760 mm gauge) to hydrogen traction. The facility aims to implement a climate-friendly, sustainable, and hydrogen-powered operation for the 31.7 km line linking Jenbach with Mayrhofen im Zillertal. The facility plans to source green hydrogen through electrolysis using hydropower generation, with early estimates suggesting a potential saving of up to 900,000 liters of diesel fuel per year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **03/2023**: The estimated cost for the hydrogen project is around €100 million, with hopes that the federal government will contribute at least half of this amount. The switch to hydrogen is expected to increase operating costs by approximately €2.7 million per annum, which is anticipated to be covered by contributions from Zillertal tourism associations.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **03/2023**: The Tirol provincial government has commissioned further work on the development of the hydrogen project, including a project structure, implementation strategy, and preparation of a tender for hydrogen trains. However, procurement of new rolling stock cannot begin until a revised operating concession is tendered, raising doubts about the operational timeline for the fuel cell trains, which were initially expected to be operational by 2027.
