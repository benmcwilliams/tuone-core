### Evolutionary Timeline of the Infineon Facility in Villach, Austria

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2021**: Infineon Technologies announced a partnership with Linde plc for the on-site production and storage of high-purity green hydrogen at the Villach facility. Linde will build a two-megawatt electrolyzer plant using Proton Exchange Membrane (PEM) technology, marking the first use of green hydrogen in Infineon’s semiconductor manufacturing process. This initiative is part of Infineon’s strategy to reduce greenhouse gas emissions at the site.
- **August 2023**: Infineon announced plans to convert the existing plant in Villach to produce 200-millimetre silicon carbide (SiC) semiconductors, aligning with its broader strategy to enhance production capabilities in response to market demand.

- **August 2021**: The establishment of the green hydrogen plant and air separation unit by Linde at the Villach site is part of Infineon’s investment in sustainable manufacturing practices, although specific financial figures for this investment were not disclosed.
