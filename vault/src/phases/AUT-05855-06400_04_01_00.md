#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **03-07-2023**: The Tirol provincial government endorsed the conversion of the Zillertalbahn to hydrogen traction. The facility will produce hydrogen-powered trains for the 31.7 km line linking Jenbach with Mayrhofen im Zillertal. The operator, Zillertaler Verkehrsbetriebe, plans to source green hydrogen through electrolysis using hydropower generation. This transition is expected to save approximately 900,000 liters of diesel fuel annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development

The switch to hydrogen is projected to increase operating costs by approximately €2.7 million per year, which is expected to be offset by contributions from Zillertal tourism associations.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **03-07-2023**: The Tirol provincial government has commissioned further work on the project structure and implementation strategy, including the preparation of a tender for the hydrogen trains. However, the procurement of new rolling stock cannot commence until a revised operating concession is tendered, raising doubts about the operational timeline for the fuel cell trains, which were initially expected to be operational by 2027.
