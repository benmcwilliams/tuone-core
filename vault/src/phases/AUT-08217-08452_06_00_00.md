### Evolutionary Timeline of Burgenland Energie Facility in Tadten-Wallern, Austria

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2024**: Burgenland Energie is constructing a 164 MW tracker-based agrivoltaic facility near Tadten-Wallern on Lake Neusiedl. The facility spans 180 hectares, with part of the site dedicated to organic farming, initially growing potatoes and chickpeas. The photovoltaic (PV) plant will feature 260,000 modules installed on trackers, aiming for a 10% higher electricity yield compared to fixed structures.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2024**: The facility is positioned to be Austria's largest agrivoltaic plant, with no specific investment figures mentioned in the articles. However, it is noted that the project is significant for the region, aiming to supply clean electricity to the people of Burgenland.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2024**: Construction of the facility began, marked by the first pile driving.
- **November 2024**: The facility is expected to be completed by the end of the year, with production anticipated to start at the beginning of the following year.

#### 4. Labour

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2024**: Burgenland Energie is 51% owned by the state of Burgenland through a holding company, with additional shares owned by the state of Lower Austria. This indicates a partial public ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by Burgenland Energie.
