### Evolutionary Timeline of the OMV Facility in Weinviertel, Austria

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** Q4 2019
   - **Details:** The OMV facility in Weinviertel is set to produce solar energy through a utility-scale photovoltaic (PV) installation with a total capacity of 16MW. The installation will consist of 60,000 solar panels spread over 200,000 square meters of land owned by OMV. The facility will be divided into two sites: 9MW in Schönkirchen and 7MW in Neusiedl an der Zaya. The expected annual production is 18GWh, which is designed to meet the energy needs of approximately 5,500 households and reduce CO2 emissions by 12,000 tonnes each year.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 02-07-2019
   - **Details:** The project is a collaboration between OMV and Verbund, with OMV being a €23-billion oil group. The partnership aims to explore mostly-renewable generation and storage schemes to power OMV's operations, indicating significant investment in renewable energy infrastructure.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 02-07-2019
   - **Details:** The announcement of the project was made, detailing the partnership between OMV and Verbund to deploy the 16MW PV installation in Weinviertel. The facility is expected to become operational in Q4 2019.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
