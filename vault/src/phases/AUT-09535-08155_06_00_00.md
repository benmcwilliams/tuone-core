### Evolutionary Timeline of the Verbund Facility in Weinviertel, Austria

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** Q4 2019
   - **Details:** The facility produces solar energy through a utility-scale photovoltaic (PV) installation with a total capacity of 16MW. The installation consists of 60,000 solar panels spread over 200,000 square meters of land. It is divided into two sites: 9MW in Schönkirchen and 7MW in Neusiedl an der Zaya. The facility is designed to generate approximately 18GWh of electricity annually, sufficient to power 5,500 households and reduce CO2 emissions by 12,000 tonnes each year.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 02-07-2019
   - **Details:** The project is a collaboration between OMV, a €23-billion oil and gas conglomerate, and Verbund, Austria's top utility. The partnership aims to explore renewable generation and storage schemes, indicating significant investment in clean technology.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 02-07-2019
   - **Details:** The announcement of the project was made, detailing the partnership and the planned operational start in Q4 2019. The project is part of a broader initiative to assess renewable energy projects across various regions, including Austria, Germany, Romania, and beyond.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
