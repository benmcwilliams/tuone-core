### Evolutionary Timeline of the Wien Energie Facility in Aspern, Austria

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **December 2024:** Wien Energie, in partnership with OMV Group, announced the start of drilling for the first deep geothermal heating project in Vienna, specifically at the Aspern site. The facility will produce geothermal heating with a capacity of 20 MWt. The project includes drilling three wells to depths exceeding 3000 meters, targeting the "Aderklaaer Konglomerat" formation, which contains natural hot water at approximately 100 °C. The geothermal heating plant is scheduled to be commissioned in 2028, with plans for additional plants to follow.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Late 2023:** OMV and Wien Energie established the "deeep" joint venture, aimed at developing 200 MW of geothermal district heating. The Aspern site was selected as the first project site for this initiative. Specific investment amounts were not detailed in the article.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **December 2024:** Drilling commenced at the Aspern site for the geothermal project. The project is expected to undergo production tests to verify the availability, temperature, and chemical composition of the formation water before the commissioning of the plant in 2028.
