### Evolutionary Timeline of the OMV Group Facility in Aspern, Austria

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **December 2024:** OMV Group, in partnership with Wien Energie, announced the start of drilling for the first deep geothermal heating project in Vienna, Austria, specifically at the Aspern site. The facility will produce geothermal heating with a capacity of 20 MWt. The project includes drilling three wells to depths exceeding 3000 meters, targeting the "Aderklaaer Konglomerat" formation, which contains natural hot water at approximately 100 °C. The geothermal heating plant is scheduled to be commissioned in 2028, with plans for additional plants to follow.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Late 2023:** OMV and Wien Energie established the "deeep" joint venture, aimed at developing 200 MW of geothermal district heating. The Aspern site was selected as the first project site for this joint venture, indicating a significant investment in geothermal technology.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **December 2024:** Official announcement of the start of drilling activities at the Aspern site for the geothermal project. Preparation work for the drilling site began in the latter half of 2024. The project is set to undergo production tests to verify the geothermal resource before commissioning the plant in 2028.
