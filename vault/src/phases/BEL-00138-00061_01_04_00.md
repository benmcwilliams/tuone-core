### Evolutionary Timeline of the Audi Facility in Brussels, Belgium

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2016**: Audi announces plans to produce an electric SUV at the Brussels facility, with a battery assembly line to be established. The electric SUV is expected to have a range of up to 500 km and production is set to begin by 2018.
- **March 2018**: Audi begins mass production of the e-tron, its first fully electric model, at the Brussels facility. The facility underwent significant upgrades to support this production, achieving CO2-neutral production status.
- **January 2019**: Audi clarifies that deliveries of the e-tron will begin in Europe in March 2019, following initial deliveries to select importers.
- **December 2022**: Audi starts production of the Q8 e-tron, a revised version of the e-tron, at the Brussels plant. The Q8 e-tron features improved aerodynamics and battery technology, increasing its range to up to 582 km.
- **October 2023**: Audi announces plans to produce the Q4 e-tron at the Brussels facility starting in late 2023, in addition to its production in Zwickau.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2017**: Audi invests in upgrading the Brussels facility to accommodate electric vehicle production, with costs running into the double-digit million area.
- **March 2018**: The facility is modernized to support the production of the e-tron, including the establishment of a new battery production assembly.
- **November 2021**: Audi confirms plans to build the Q8 e-tron at the Brussels plant, indicating ongoing investment in the facility's capabilities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2018**: Audi begins mass production of the e-tron in Brussels.
- **March 2019**: Audi starts delivering the e-tron to customers in Europe.
- **December 2022**: Production of the Q8 e-tron begins at the Brussels facility.
- **November 2023**: Audi plans to start overflow production of the Q4 e-tron at the Brussels plant.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2019**: Audi's Brussels facility employs additional staff to support the ramp-up of e-tron production.
- **January 2020**: Audi announces temporary layoffs for 145 workers due to supply chain issues affecting battery production.
- **February 2024**: Audi considers restructuring the Brussels facility, which could lead to job losses due to declining demand for the Q8 e-tron.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: Audi has full ownership of the Brussels facility, which has been dedicated to electric vehicle production since the transition from the A1 model to the e-tron in 2018. The facility operates under Audi's management, with no joint ventures reported.

This timeline captures the key developments and changes at the Audi facility in Brussels, focusing on its evolution in technology, investment, project milestones, labor dynamics, and ownership structure.
