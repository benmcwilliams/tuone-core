## BEL-00138-00832

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Volkswagen Facility in Brussels, Belgium

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2021**: Volkswagen announces plans to electrify its European locations, including the Brussels facility, as part of a broader strategy to invest in electromobility and digitalization. The facility is set to produce electric vehicles, including the Audi Q8 e-tron, which will be built as a successor to the e-tron quattro.
- **09/2022**: Volkswagen's subsidiary PowerCo and Umicore establish a joint venture for cathode and prematerial production in Europe, with the Brussels facility as its headquarters. The joint venture aims to produce materials sufficient for 2.2 million fully electric vehicles annually by the end of the decade.
- **03/2023**: Regulatory approvals for the PowerCo and Umicore joint venture are received, allowing preparations for large-scale industrial production of cathode materials to begin. The production site is still under consideration.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2022**: The joint venture between PowerCo and Umicore is set to invest €3 billion in developing and producing cathodes and other primary materials in Europe, with the Brussels facility being a key location for this investment.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2022**: Announcement of the joint venture between PowerCo and Umicore, with plans to produce cathode materials starting in 2025.
- **03/2023**: Confirmation of regulatory approval for the joint venture, allowing for the swift commencement of preparations for production.
- **09/2023**: Ongoing discussions about the production site for the joint venture, with no specific location confirmed yet.

#### 4. Labour
- **09/2022**: The establishment of the joint venture is expected to create jobs related to the production of cathode materials, although specific job creation numbers have not been disclosed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **09/2022**: The Brussels facility will serve as the headquarters for the joint venture between PowerCo and Umicore, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by Volkswagen. 

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic decisions regarding the Volkswagen facility in Brussels, focusing on its transition towards electric vehicle production and the establishment of a significant joint venture for battery materials.
