## BEL-00138-01131

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ionway Facility in Brussels, BEL

1. **Technology and Operations**
   - **Date**: 06-10-2023
   - **Details**: The Ionway facility, a joint venture between Volkswagen's battery subsidiary PowerCo and Belgian materials technology group Umicore, is established to produce precursor (pCAM) and cathode active materials (CAM) for sustainable battery materials. The facility aims to expand its annual production capacity to 160 GWh by the end of the decade, which is sufficient to meet the demand of approximately 2.2 million battery electric vehicles.

2. **Investment and Development**
   - **Date**: 06-10-2023
   - **Details**: The joint venture is expected to involve an investment of three billion euros for the development and production of cathodes and other primary materials, as mentioned during the initial announcement of the joint venture a year prior.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**
   - **Date**: 06-10-2023
   - **Details**: The Ionway joint venture officially opens for business, marking the start of large-scale industrial production for battery materials in Europe. This is noted as the first partnership of its kind between a European carmaker and a leading global CAM manufacturer.

4. **Labour**
   - **Date**: 06-10-2023
   - **Details**: The establishment of Ionway is expected to create hundreds of highly skilled jobs, contributing to the local economy and supporting the energy transition.

5. **Ownership Structure**
   - **Date**: 06-10-2023
   - **Details**: Ionway operates as a joint venture between PowerCo and Umicore, indicating that the facility is not owned exclusively by either company but rather shared between the two entities. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic intentions surrounding the Ionway facility in Brussels, highlighting its role in the evolving landscape of sustainable battery production in Europe.
