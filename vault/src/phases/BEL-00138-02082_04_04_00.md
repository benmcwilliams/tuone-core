### Evolutionary Timeline of the European Clean Hydrogen Alliance Facility in Brussels, BEL

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2020**: The European Clean Hydrogen Alliance was launched, focusing on the development of a clean hydrogen value chain. The strategy envisions the deployment of around **6 GW** of new electrolyzer capacity to produce **1 million tons** of green hydrogen in the first four years, ramping up to **40 GW** and **10 million tons** from 2025-2030.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2020**: The European Commission announced a long-term investment plan that could unlock up to **€340 billion** for new solar and wind projects and **€470 billion** for electrolyzer capacity over the next decade. The strategy anticipates that **€24-42 billion** will be needed for electrolyzer capacity up to 2030, with total production costs projected to be between **€180-470 billion** by mid-century.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2020**: The European Clean Hydrogen Alliance was officially launched, tasked with building a hydrogen investment pipeline and demand for clean hydrogen. The alliance aims to support decarbonization efforts in energy-intensive industries such as steel and chemicals.

#### 4. Labour
- **09/2020**: The European Clean Hydrogen Alliance is expected to advise on necessary labor market adjustments and skills required for hydrogen adoption, indicating potential job creation in the clean hydrogen sector as projects develop.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **09/2020**: The facility is part of the European Clean Hydrogen Alliance, which includes industry leaders, civil society, policymakers, and the European Investment Bank. The ownership structure is not defined as exclusive; it appears to be a collaborative effort among various stakeholders rather than full ownership by a single entity.
