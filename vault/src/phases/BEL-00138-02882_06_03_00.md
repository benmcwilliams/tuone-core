### Evolutionary Timeline of the European Solar Manufacturing Council Facility in Brussels, BEL

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2018**: The European Solar Manufacturing Council (ESMC) was founded during the 35th EU Photovoltaic Solar Energy Conference (EU PVSEC) in Brussels. The council aims to rebuild a major European photovoltaic (PV) production line along the entire value chain, including wafers, solar cells, and modules. The focus is on constructing PV production on a gigawatt scale.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2024**: The European Commission announced the European Solar Charter (ESC) to support the EU photovoltaic sector. The ESC includes proposals for expedited permitting for solar manufacturing projects and access to EU funding for solar manufacturing projects under various EU funding mechanisms, including the Recovery and Resilience Facility and Horizon Europe.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2018**: The ESMC was officially launched, marking a significant milestone in the effort to revitalize the European solar manufacturing industry.
- **April 2024**: The signing of the European Solar Charter by representatives of 23 EU member states and industry bodies, including the ESMC, aimed at supporting the competitiveness of the European PV manufacturing industry.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2024**: The ESC emphasizes the need for job creation in the European solar manufacturing sector, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles. The commitment to local production aims to bolster employment in the industry.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify the ownership structure of the ESMC facility in Brussels, but it is implied that the council is a collaborative effort involving multiple stakeholders, including research institutes, mechanical engineers, and manufacturers, rather than being solely owned by a single company.
