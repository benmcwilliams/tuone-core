## BEL-00138-03023

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hydrogenics Facility in Brussels, BEL

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date**: 11-10-2019
  - **Details**: The Hydrogenics facility in Brussels is part of the "Silver Frog" project, which aims to produce green hydrogen using renewable energy sources. The project proposes the construction of a 2 GW solar module factory to support the production of 100% green hydrogen. The facility is expected to produce approximately 800,000 tons of green hydrogen over eight years.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date**: 11-10-2019
  - **Details**: The project seeks backing from the European Union as one of the Important Projects of Common European Interest, which would provide significant funding opportunities. The emphasis on developing new European production capacities for solar cells and modules indicates a substantial investment in renewable energy infrastructure.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date**: 11-10-2019
  - **Details**: The "Silver Frog" project was pitched at the Hydrogen for Climate Conference in Brussels, with the intention of securing designated status from the EU. The project aims to integrate solar module manufacturing with green hydrogen production, marking a significant milestone in the facility's development.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date**: 11-10-2019
  - **Details**: The project is expected to create at least 6,000 jobs, contributing to employment in the renewable energy sector and supporting the decarbonization of heavy industries.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date**: 11-10-2019
  - **Details**: The Hydrogenics facility is part of a joint venture involving multiple partners: Hydrogenics (Belgium), Meyer Burger (Switzerland), Ecosolifer (Hungary), and European Energy (Denmark). This indicates that Hydrogenics does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a collaborative effort with other companies.
