## BEL-00138-03196

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Meyer Burger Facility in Brussels, BEL

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **11-10-2019**: Meyer Burger, as part of the "Silver Frog" project, proposed the construction of a 2 GW solar module factory in Brussels. This facility aims to produce solar modules that will support the generation of 10 GW of renewable energy capacity, including wind power, to produce 100% green hydrogen. The project anticipates producing around 800,000 tons of green hydrogen over eight years.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **11-10-2019**: The "Silver Frog" project seeks backing from the EU as an Important Project of Common European Interest, which would provide significant funding opportunities. The project emphasizes the need for new European production capacities for solar cells and modules, aligning with the EU's Green Deal.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **11-10-2019**: The proposal for the "Silver Frog" project was pitched at the Hydrogen for Climate Conference in Brussels, with the intention of securing designated status from the European Commission in the following year.

#### 4. Labour
- **11-10-2019**: The project is expected to create at least 6,000 jobs over its duration, contributing significantly to employment in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **11-10-2019**: Meyer Burger is involved in a collaborative effort with other companies (Hydrogenics, Ecosolifer, and European Energy) for the "Silver Frog" project, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership of the facility.

### Summary
The Meyer Burger facility in Brussels is part of an ambitious project aimed at establishing a significant solar module production capacity to support green hydrogen production. The project is still in the proposal stage as of 2019, with expectations of job creation and substantial investment opportunities tied to EU support. The facility is not solely owned by Meyer Burger but is part of a joint venture with other companies in the renewable energy sector.
