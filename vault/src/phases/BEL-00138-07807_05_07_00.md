## BEL-00138-07807

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fusion for Energy Facility in Brussels, BEL

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility is involved in the management of the European Union's contribution to the JT-60SA fusion device project, which is a tokamak designed to operate with hydrogen heated to 200 million degrees Celsius to create plasma. 
   - The JT-60SA is designed to perform modeling to assist in the preparation for the ITER fusion project and to help design future fusion power plants. 
   - The facility has a significant role in training a new generation of fusion experts and has been involved in the fabrication of components for the project, collaborating with various countries including Belgium, France, Germany, Spain, and Italy.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The total cost of the JT-60SA project is estimated at EUR 560 million (USD 608 million). 
   - The project is a result of the Broader Approach Agreement between the European Union and Japan, aimed at promoting fusion technology.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **October 2023:** The first plasma operation of the JT-60SA fusion device was achieved, marking a significant milestone in the project.
   - Construction of the JT-60SA began in 2007 and was completed in 2020.

4. **Labour:**
   - The project has involved a total of 500 researchers from Europe and Japan, indicating job creation in the research and development sector related to fusion technology.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - Fusion for Energy manages the EU's contribution to the JT-60SA project, indicating that it is not exclusively owned by a single entity but rather operates in collaboration with Japan under the Broader Approach Agreement. 

This timeline captures the key developments and contributions of the Fusion for Energy facility in Brussels, focusing on its role in the JT-60SA project and its implications for future fusion energy advancements.
