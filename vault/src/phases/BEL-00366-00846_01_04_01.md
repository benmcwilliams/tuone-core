## BEL-00366-00846

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Volvo Group Facility in Ghent, Belgium

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05/2019**: Volvo Group signs contracts with CATL and LG Chem for battery supply, establishing a foundation for the electrification of their models, including the XC40 and Polestar 2. A battery assembly line is being installed at the Ghent plant.
- **03/2020**: Inauguration of the battery assembly line at the Ghent facility, which will produce battery packs for the XC40 Recharge P8 and the first electric Volvo model, scheduled for production in the autumn.
- **10/2020**: Series production of the fully electric XC40 Recharge begins at the Ghent plant, alongside combustion and plug-in hybrid versions.
- **01/2021**: Volvo announces plans to triple electric vehicle production capacity at Ghent, targeting 60% of total production to be electric by 2022.
- **10/2021**: Production of the C40 Recharge begins at the Ghent facility, marking it as the first Volvo model offered exclusively with electric drive.
- **05/2022**: Volvo Trucks opens a battery assembly plant in Ghent for heavy-duty electric trucks, integrating battery assembly into their production flow.
- **10/2022**: Announcement of plans to build a battery module production facility at the Ghent plant, scheduled to start operations in 2025.
- **05/2023**: Volvo announces that the EX30 will be produced in Ghent starting in 2025, increasing production capacity for expected demand in Europe and global export.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **03/2020**: No specific production capacity figures are disclosed for the battery assembly plant, but it is indicated that capacity will change significantly in the coming months.
- **01/2021**: Volvo plans to increase electric vehicle production capacity to 135,000 units per year in Ghent.
- **10/2021**: Investment in a new battery module production facility at Ghent is announced, costing around 75 million euros.
- **05/2023**: Plans to increase production capacity for the EX30 in Ghent are confirmed, reflecting the company's strategy to meet growing demand.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **03/2020**: Battery assembly line inaugurated; production of XC40 Recharge P8 begins.
- **10/2020**: Start of series production for the XC40 Recharge.
- **10/2021**: Production of the C40 Recharge begins.
- **05/2022**: Opening of the battery assembly plant for heavy-duty electric trucks.
- **05/2023**: Announcement of EX30 production in Ghent starting in 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **03/2020**: 200 new employees hired for the electrification effort, bringing the total workforce at the Ghent plant to 6,500, making it the largest industrial employer in Flanders.
- **01/2021**: As production capacity for electric vehicles increases, further hiring is anticipated to support the ramp-up.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: Volvo Cars is owned by the Chinese conglomerate Zhejiang Geely Holding since 2010, but operates independently, maintaining its focus on safety, quality, and value for money. The Ghent facility is part of Volvo's global production network, with no indication of joint ventures or shared ownership for this specific facility.
