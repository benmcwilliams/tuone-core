## BEL-00480-00799

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Umicore Facility in Hoboken, Belgium

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2022**: Umicore's facility in Hoboken has an annual recycling capacity of **7,000 tonnes** for lithium-ion (Li-ion) batteries and production waste from battery manufacturing, equivalent to about **35,000 electric vehicle batteries**. The facility is set to introduce the latest generation of proprietary recycling technology in 2022, which is expected to achieve a yield of over **95%** for a wide range of battery chemistries. This technology is noted for its ability to recover most lithium, addressing a key constraint in current recycling processes.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2023**: Umicore signed two long-term power purchase agreements (PPAs) with Engie and Axpo to source renewable electricity for its operations, including the Hoboken facility. The agreement with Engie secures **28MW** from offshore wind turbines, providing around **100GWh** of renewable electricity per year until 2030. The Axpo agreement ensures **35GWh** per year from an onshore wind farm until 2035.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2022**: Umicore signs an agreement with Automotive Cells Company (ACC) for battery recycling services, specifically utilizing the Hoboken facility for recycling production waste from ACC's pilot plant in Nersac, France.
- **January 2023**: The power purchase agreements with Engie and Axpo commenced, aimed at supporting the Hoboken facility's electricity needs.
- **September 2024**: Stellantis and Orano announced the cancellation of a joint venture for recycling electric car batteries, but Umicore continues its role in recycling production waste from ACC at the Hoboken facility.
