### Eiffage Métal Facility in Hoboken, Belgium - Evolutionary Timeline

1. **Technology and Operations**:
   - **Production**: Eiffage Métal is involved in the engineering and construction of offshore substations, specifically for the Saint-Brieuc offshore wind farm.
   - **Production Capacity**: The facility will manufacture the upper part of the substation, which includes transformers and electrical equipment, as well as the foundation for the substation.
   - **Updates**: The construction work for the topside systems is being carried out at the ENGIE Solutions yard in Hoboken, Belgium, with engineering and construction already started.

2. **Investment and Development**:
   - **Investments**: The project is part of a larger investment by Ailes Marines, which is 100% owned by Iberdrola, for the construction of the Saint-Brieuc offshore wind farm. Specific investment amounts are not detailed in the article.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**:
   - **Announcement**: On 07-09-2020, it was announced that Eiffage Métal and Engie Solutions would build the offshore substation for the 496 MW wind farm.
   - **Construction Start**: Engineering and construction works for the topside and jacket have already begun as of September 2020.
   - **Sail Away Schedule**: The sail away of both the topside and the jacket is scheduled for spring/summer 2022.
   - **Commissioning**: The wind farm is expected to be fully commissioned in 2023.

4. **Labour**:
   - **Job Creation**: The project is expected to mobilize 250 jobs over two years, contributing to local employment through the construction of the offshore wind farm.

5. **Ownership Structure**:
   - **Ownership**: The facility is part of a collaboration between Eiffage Métal and Engie Solutions, with no indication of exclusive ownership by Eiffage Métal. The project is under the management of Ailes Marines, which is fully owned by Iberdrola.
