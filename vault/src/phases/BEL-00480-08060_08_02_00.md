### Evolutionary Timeline of the Sif Facility in Hoboken, Belgium

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Sif facility in Hoboken produces transition pieces (TP) and monopiles for offshore wind farms. 
   - As of November 2024, the facility is involved in the production of the last transition piece for the 3.6 GW Dogger Bank Wind Farm, the largest offshore wind farm under construction globally.
   - The facility has delivered 276 out of 277 transition pieces required for the Dogger Bank project, with manufacturing for the third phase starting at the beginning of 2024.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The consortium comprising Sif and Smulders was awarded contracts for the manufacture of all monopiles and transition pieces for the Dogger Bank Wind Farm in November 2020, with subsequent contracts for the third phase awarded in late 2021.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **November 2020:** The consortium secured contracts for the first two phases (Dogger Bank A & B) of the Dogger Bank Wind Farm.
   - **Late 2021:** The consortium was awarded the contract for the third phase (Dogger Bank C).
   - **2021:** Manufacturing of the first transition piece began.
   - **Autumn 2023:** All monopiles for Dogger Bank A were in place, with ongoing installation of transition pieces and wind turbines.
   - **May 2024:** Installation of monopiles and transition pieces for Dogger Bank B commenced.
   - **Early 2027:** Expected completion date for the entire Dogger Bank Wind Farm.
