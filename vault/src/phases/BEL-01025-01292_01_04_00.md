## BEL-01025-01292

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Solithor Facility in Sint-Truiden, Belgium

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2022**: Solithor announces the establishment of its European competence centre for solid-state batteries in Sint-Truiden, Belgium. The facility will initially focus on pre-production of solid-state technology, with plans to expand production to include entire battery modules and systems within three years. The facility will also produce prototype batteries for electric vehicles and the aviation sector.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2022**: Solithor commissions specialists Munters to build a 400-square-metre drying room at the facility, with the order valued at “several million euros.” This investment is part of the setup for the production of solid-state batteries.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2023**: Solithor plans to occupy the 3,000m² factory site in Sint-Truiden, which it has secured through a ten-year contract with Punch Powertrain.
- **February 2023**: Solithor announces a collaboration with Sonaca to develop solid-state lithium cells for regional aviation and urban air mobility (UAM). The production of these cells will take place at the Sint-Truiden facility, with expectations for advancements in battery technology for aircraft by around 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2023**: Solithor employs approximately 25 staff members at the facility. The collaboration with Sonaca, which has a workforce of 3,500, may lead to further job creation as the project progresses.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2022**: Solithor has taken over the facility from Punch Powertrain, indicating that it now has exclusive ownership of the site for its operations in solid-state battery production. 

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Solithor facility in Sint-Truiden, Belgium, highlighting its focus on solid-state battery technology and collaborations aimed at advancing the aviation sector.
