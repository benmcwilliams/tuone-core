### Evolutionary Timeline of the SolarDuck Facility in North Sea, Belgium

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2022**: Announcement of the Merganser project, a pilot offshore floating solar plant with a capacity of **500 kW**. The facility is designed to operate in one of the most challenging offshore environments, specifically near an offshore wind farm off the coast of Ostend, Belgium. The technology involves triangular-shaped platforms that float above the water, keeping critical components dry and ensuring structural integrity.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2022**: RWE has committed to investing in the Merganser pilot project to accelerate the commercialization of SolarDuck’s floating solar PV technology. This investment is aimed at gaining insights into the technology's performance in harsh North Sea conditions, which is expected to facilitate faster commercialization from **2023** onwards.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2022**: The Merganser project is officially announced, with expectations to go online in **2023**. The project is part of a broader strategy to explore the potential of offshore floating solar technology, with plans for a larger demonstration project at the Hollandse Kust West (HKW) offshore wind farm in the Netherlands.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is a **joint venture** between RWE and SolarDuck, with both companies collaborating on the Merganser project. RWE is the primary investor, while SolarDuck provides the technology and expertise in offshore floating solar systems.
