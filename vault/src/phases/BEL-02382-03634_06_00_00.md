## BEL-02382-03634

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Tractebel Facility in North Sea, Belgium

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2019**: Tractebel, as part of a Belgian consortium, announced the development of a floating solar array in the North Sea. The pilot project is designed to be cost-competitive and will integrate ecosystem considerations. The project is part of a €2 million initiative, with the aim of producing renewable energy offshore.
- **December 2021**: Tractebel unveiled a new offshore hydrogen storage concept, which involves the storage of hydrogen in underground salt caverns. The design allows for the compression and storage of up to 1.2 million cubic meters of hydrogen, with a capacity equivalent to converting 2 GW of green offshore wind power into hydrogen.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2019**: The floating solar project has a total investment of €2 million, supported by the Agency for Innovation and Entrepreneurship (VLAIO) and the Blue Cluster, a Flemish organization promoting marine energy projects.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2019**: Announcement of the floating solar array project in the North Sea, marking the launch of the first offshore photovoltaic project in the region.
- **December 2021**: Completion of the design study for the offshore hydrogen storage concept, indicating a significant milestone in the development of renewable energy infrastructure.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
