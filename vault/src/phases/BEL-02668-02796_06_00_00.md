## BEL-02668-02796

### Evolutionary Timeline of the DEME Facility in Ostend, Belgium

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2023**: The SeaVolt consortium, which includes DEME, announced the installation of its first offshore floating solar energy test platform in the Belgian North Sea, specifically off the port of Ostend. This platform will consist of a few solar panels for testing purposes and aims to gather data on the impact of environmental factors such as waves, rain, and salt spray on various solar panel configurations. The project will monitor the energy output affected by varying inclinations due to waves and wind.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2023**: The SeaVolt consortium is preparing for a large-scale demonstration project to explore the integration of offshore floating solar within offshore wind power plants. The project is part of a broader initiative to advance offshore solar energy technology in Belgium, supported by the government’s recovery plan to invest in technology and know-how among local players.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2023**: The SeaVolt consortium announced the launch of an offshore floating PV demonstrator off the Belgian coast. The project is currently in the final assembly phase, with the main contractor being Equans. The floating array is expected to collect data for at least one year starting in August 2023 to scale up the technology.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2023**: While specific job creation or loss figures were not mentioned in the articles, the project involves multiple companies, including DEME, Tractebel, Equans, and Jan De Nul, indicating potential job opportunities related to the construction and operation of the floating solar platform.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2023**: The facility is part of a consortium (SeaVolt) that includes DEME, Tractebel, Equans, and Jan De Nul. This indicates a joint venture ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by DEME.

This timeline captures the key developments related to the DEME facility in Ostend, Belgium, focusing on its technological advancements, project milestones, and collaborative ownership structure.
