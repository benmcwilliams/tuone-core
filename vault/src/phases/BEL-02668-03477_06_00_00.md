## BEL-02668-03477

### Evolutionary Timeline of the SolarDuck Facility in Ostend, Belgium

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2022**: SolarDuck, in collaboration with RWE, announced the development of the Merganser pilot project, which will feature a floating solar installation with a rated output of **500 kW (0.5 MWp)**. This will be SolarDuck's first offshore pilot project, following a successful inland pilot in the Netherlands. The technology utilizes a unique triangular platform design that floats above the water, allowing it to follow waves and keep critical components dry and stable.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2022**: RWE confirmed its investment in the Merganser pilot project, which is aimed at gaining experience with SolarDuck's innovative floating solar technology. This investment is part of a broader strategy to explore the integration of floating solar systems with offshore wind projects, particularly in the context of the Dutch Government's Hollandse Kust West (HKW) tender.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2022**: The Merganser pilot project was officially announced, with plans for installation off the coast of Ostend in the Belgian North Sea. The project is set to go live in **2023**. This pilot is a crucial step in the collaboration between RWE and SolarDuck, laying the groundwork for future larger-scale projects.

#### 5. Ownership Structure

### Summary
The SolarDuck facility in Ostend, Belgium, is centered around the Merganser pilot project, which aims to deploy innovative floating solar technology with a capacity of 500 kW. RWE is investing in this project as part of a strategic partnership with SolarDuck, marking a significant step in the development of offshore renewable energy solutions. The project is expected to commence operations in 2023, contributing to the integration of solar and wind energy in marine environments.
