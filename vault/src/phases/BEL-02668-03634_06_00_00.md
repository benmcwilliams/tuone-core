## BEL-02668-03634

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Tractebel Facility in Ostend, Belgium

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2023**: Tractebel, in collaboration with DEME, Equans, and Jan De Nul, announced the installation of an offshore floating solar energy test platform in the Belgian North Sea, specifically off the port of Ostend. This platform will serve as a demonstrator for offshore floating photovoltaic (PV) technology, collecting data on various solar panel configurations and their performance under marine conditions. The project aims to gather data for at least one year starting in August 2023.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2023**: The SeaVolt consortium, which includes Tractebel, is preparing for a large-scale demonstration project to integrate offshore floating solar with offshore wind power plants. The project is part of Belgium's recovery plan to invest in technology and know-how among local players, although specific investment amounts tied to this project were not disclosed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2023**: The SeaVolt consortium announced the launch of the offshore floating PV demonstrator, with the main contractor Equans completing the final assembly. The project is designed to assess the structural integrity of the floating array and its performance in real-life conditions. The project is expected to collect data for a year, with the aim of scaling up offshore solar technology.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2023**: While specific job creation or loss figures were not mentioned in the articles, the project is expected to contribute to local employment through the involvement of multiple companies in the consortium, including Tractebel and Equans.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2023**: The facility is part of a consortium (SeaVolt) involving multiple companies: Tractebel, DEME, Equans, and Jan De Nul. This indicates a joint venture ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by Tractebel.
