## BEL-02668-05114

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ghent University Facility in Ostend, Belgium

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2023**: The SeaVolt facility, in collaboration with Ghent University (UGENT), is set to launch a first-of-its-kind solar energy test platform specifically designed for offshore conditions. This facility will test floating solar panels in the POM-West Vlaanderen owned ‘Blue Accelerator offshore test zone’. The technology aims to gather data on the performance of solar panels in rough sea conditions, including their structural integrity and ecological impact.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2023**: The project is supported by the Blue Cluster funded research project MPVAQUA and additional funding from the federal government via BELSPO. This investment is aimed at advancing the development of offshore solar energy technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2023**: The SeaVolt marine floating solar test installation is under construction by Equans in Ostend. This installation is not a full-scale prototype but serves as a laboratory for testing various configurations and materials for future developments in offshore solar energy.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2023**: The project is expected to contribute to job creation in the region, as indicated by statements from government officials highlighting the potential for new jobs resulting from the development of offshore solar technology.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2023**: The facility is a collaborative effort involving multiple partners, including Ghent University, Equans, and other companies like Soltech, Tree Composites, Versteden, and Multitech. It is not exclusively owned by Ghent University but is part of a consortium working together on the SeaVolt project. 

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Ghent University facility in Ostend, focusing on the SeaVolt project and its implications for offshore solar energy technology.
