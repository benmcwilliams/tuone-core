### Evolutionary Timeline of the Multitech Facility in Ostend, Belgium

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2023**: The SeaVolt project, a first-of-its-kind floating solar energy test platform, is under construction in the port of Ostend. This facility aims to test solar panels specifically designed for rough sea conditions, with a modular design suitable for integration with offshore wind farms. The installation will gather data on the performance of various solar panel configurations under real-life marine conditions.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2023**: The SeaVolt project is supported by the Blue Cluster funded research project MPVAQUA and additional funding from the federal government via BELSPO. This investment is aimed at advancing the technology necessary for large-scale offshore solar energy development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2023**: The SeaVolt marine floating solar test installation is officially under construction, marking a significant milestone as the first installation in the Belgian North Sea aimed at developing offshore solar energy. This project is described as a laboratory for technological development rather than a full-scale prototype.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2023**: The project is expected to contribute to job creation in the region, as highlighted by various stakeholders, including government officials who emphasize the potential for new jobs resulting from the development of offshore solar technology.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
