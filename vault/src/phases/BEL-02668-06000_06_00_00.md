## BEL-02668-06000

### Evolutionary Timeline of the SeaVolt Facility in Ostend, Belgium

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2023**: SeaVolt announced the launch of its first floating solar energy test platform in the Belgian North Sea, specifically designed for rough sea conditions. The platform will gather data on the performance of various solar panels under real-life conditions, including the impact of waves, rain, and salt sprays. The test aims to assess the structural integrity of the floating technology and the ecological impact of the materials used.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2023**: The SeaVolt project is supported by the Blue Cluster funded research project MPVAQUA and additional funding from the federal government via BELSPO. This investment is aimed at advancing the technology necessary for large-scale offshore solar energy development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2023**: The construction of the SeaVolt test installation is underway in the port of Ostend, with operations expected to begin in August 2023. The project is described as a proof of concept rather than a full-scale prototype, serving as a laboratory for technological development.
