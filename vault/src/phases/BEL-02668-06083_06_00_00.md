### Evolutionary Timeline of the Soltech Facility in Ostend, Belgium

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2023**: The SeaVolt facility, in collaboration with Soltech, is under construction in the port of Ostend. This facility is designed to test a first-of-its-kind floating solar energy platform tailored for rough sea conditions. The technology aims to complement offshore wind farms and is part of a broader initiative to develop offshore solar energy in the Belgian North Sea.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2023**: The SeaVolt project is supported by the Blue Cluster funded research project MPVAQUA and additional funding from the federal government via BELSPO. This investment is aimed at developing and testing the floating solar technology, which is expected to play a significant role in the future of offshore renewable energy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2023**: The SeaVolt marine floating solar test installation is officially under construction. This project is described as a laboratory for testing various solar panel configurations and materials, including novel lightweight carbon fiber, to assess their performance in marine conditions. The project is positioned as a crucial step towards large-scale offshore solar energy development.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2023**: The project is expected to contribute to job creation in the region, as highlighted by various stakeholders, including government officials and company executives. The development of this facility is seen as a means to foster green economic growth and create new jobs in the renewable energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2023**: The facility is a collaborative effort involving multiple partners, including Soltech, Equans, Tree Composites, Versteden, and Multitech. This indicates a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by Soltech.
