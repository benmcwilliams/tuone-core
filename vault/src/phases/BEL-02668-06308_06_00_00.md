## BEL-02668-06308

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Versteden Facility in Ostend, Belgium

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2023**: The Versteden facility is involved in the construction of the SeaVolt marine floating solar test installation in the port of Ostend. This facility is designed to test a new floating solar energy technology specifically tailored for rough sea conditions. The installation will gather data on the performance of various solar panel configurations under real-life marine conditions, including the impact of waves, rain, and salt spray.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2023**: The SeaVolt project is supported by the Blue Cluster funded research project MPVAQUA and additional funding from the federal government via BELSPO. This investment is aimed at advancing the development of offshore solar energy technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2023**: The SeaVolt test installation is under construction, marking a significant milestone as it is the first of its kind in the Belgian North Sea aimed at large-scale offshore solar energy development. This project is described as a laboratory for testing and advancing the technology rather than a full-scale prototype.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2023**: The project is expected to contribute to job creation in the region, as highlighted by various stakeholders, including government officials who emphasize the potential for new jobs resulting from the development of offshore solar energy technology.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2023**: The facility is part of a collaborative effort involving multiple partners, including Equans, Soltech, Tree Composites, Versteden, and Multitech Floating Laboratory. The ownership structure appears to be a joint venture among these companies, rather than exclusive ownership by Versteden.
