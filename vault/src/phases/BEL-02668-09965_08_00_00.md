### Evolutionary Timeline of the SeaMade Facility in Ostend, Belgium

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The SeaMade facility is an offshore wind farm located up to 50 kilometers off the coast from Ostend, integrating two wind sites known as Mermaid and Seastar.
   - The facility has a production capacity of 487 MW, making it the single largest wind farm being funded and built in Belgium.
   - The wind farm will utilize 58 Siemens 8.4 MW turbines, with inter-array cables designed to carry the electricity generated to an offshore substation for transmission to the offshore grid.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The project is developed by SeaMade, a consortium consisting of Otary RS (70% stake), Electrabel (17.5%), and Eneco Wind Belgium (12.5%).
   - Specific investment amounts are not detailed in the article, but the project is noted as the largest offshore wind farm in Belgium, indicating significant financial backing.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **05-06-2019:** JDR Cables signed a contract with DEME Offshore to provide inter-array cables and accessories for the SeaMade offshore wind farm. The cables will be assembled in JDR’s Hartlepool facility, with delivery expected by the end of 2019.
   - The facility is expected to be operational by 2020.
