### Evolutionary Timeline of the KU Leuven Facility in Leuven, Belgium

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2022**: KU Leuven researchers developed hydrogen-producing rooftop solar panels that capture solar power and water from the air. Each panel produces 250 liters of hydrogen per day at an efficiency of 15%. The technology is part of the Solhyd project, transitioning from research to commercialization. The panels are designed to be small-scale, modular, and compatible with existing PV systems.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2022**: The establishment of pilot production lines in a new 350 square-meter production space near Leuven was supported by Flemish government funding. This funding is crucial for the transition from research to industrial production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2022**: The Solhyd project moved from university labs to a dedicated production space, marking a significant milestone in the transition to commercial production. The team plans to produce a few dozen panels for small-scale pilot projects initially, with an expectation to scale up to 5,000 panels per year by 2026. The product is anticipated to be commercially available by 2026.
