### Evolutionary Timeline of the Perpetum Energy Facility in Brugelette, Belgium

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2019**: Perpetum Energy announced plans to build the world’s largest solar carport in Brugelette, Belgium, with a production capacity of 20 MW, featuring 62,750 solar panels covering 104,000 square meters. The facility is designed to generate approximately 20 GWh of electricity annually, sufficient to meet the needs of the nearby Pairi Daiza zoo.
- **July 2024**: The facility was expanded to a total capacity of 40 MW, making it the largest wooden solar carport globally. The new installation included 93,786 solar panels and spanned 200,000 square meters.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2024**: The total investment for the expanded solar carport reached €40 million (approximately $43 million). This investment was part of the development of the second phase of the project, which included significant upgrades to the electrical infrastructure to support increased capacity.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2019**: Construction of the initial solar carport began.
- **Spring 2020**: The original 20 MW solar carport was scheduled for commissioning.
- **July 2024**: The expansion to 40 MW was completed, with the facility becoming operational as the largest wooden solar carport.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2019**: The project was developed by a special purpose vehicle, Green 4 Power, which is 30% owned by Perpetum Energy and 70% by Integrale, an investment company.
- **July 2024**: The ownership structure included Perpetum Energy and Socofe, indicating a shift in ownership dynamics with the involvement of additional partners.
