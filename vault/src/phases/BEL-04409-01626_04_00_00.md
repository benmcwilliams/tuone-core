### Evolutionary Timeline of the Engie Facility in Doel, Belgium

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2021**: Engie, in collaboration with INEOS, launched a hydrogen pilot project at the INEOS Phenol site in Doel. The project aims to replace 10% of the natural gas feed with hydrogen in a commercial-scale cogeneration plant, with plans to increase this to 20% if successful. This facility is notable for being one of the first to conduct such tests on an industrial scale in Belgium. The project is part of a broader strategy to transition towards climate-neutral energy.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: The hydrogen pilot project represents a significant investment in clean technology, although specific financial figures are not disclosed. The project is part of INEOS's roadmap to achieve climate neutrality by 2050 and reduce emissions by 55% by 2030.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2021**: Announcement of the hydrogen pilot project at the INEOS Phenol site in Doel, marking the start of industrial-scale tests for hydrogen use in cogeneration.
- **January 2024**: The Belgian government announced plans to extend the operation of Doel 4 and Tihange 3 nuclear reactors by 10 years, allowing for the retention of 2 GWe of nuclear generation capacity. This decision was made in light of the ongoing energy transition and the need for stable energy sources.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2021**: The hydrogen project is expected to create jobs related to the design, installation, and operation of the new technology, although specific job creation numbers are not provided.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2021**: Engie is involved in a partnership with INEOS for the hydrogen pilot project, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership of the facility. Engie is responsible for the design, installation, and operation of the technology at the INEOS site.
