### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ineos Facility in Lillo, Belgium

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **02-03-2021**: INEOS launched a hydrogen pilot project at its facility in Lillo, Belgium. The project aims to use hydrogen in a commercial-scale cogeneration plant to generate electricity and heat from natural gas. Initially, 10% of the gas feed will be replaced by hydrogen, with plans to increase this to 20% if successful. The facility has experience in handling hydrogen as a raw material and has the necessary permits for this project. INEOS currently produces 300,000 tons of hydrogen annually as a co-product of its chemical processes.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- The pilot project is part of INEOS's broader roadmap to achieve climate neutrality at its Antwerp sites by 2050 and to reduce emissions by 55% by 2030 compared to 1990 levels. This roadmap includes investments in electrification, the switch to recycled or bio-based raw materials, and the use of renewable energy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **02-03-2021**: Announcement of the hydrogen pilot project, marking the first industrial-scale tests of hydrogen use in Belgium. The project is a collaboration between INEOS and ENGIE, with ENGIE responsible for the design, installation, and operation of the technology.
