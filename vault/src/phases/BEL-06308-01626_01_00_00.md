### Evolutionary Timeline of the Engie Facility in Vilvoorde, Belgium

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: Engie Belgium announced plans for a battery energy storage system (BESS) project in Vilvoorde with a capacity of 200 MW and 800 MWh. This facility aims to support the power grid by managing peak demand and integrating renewable energy sources.
- **July 2024**: Construction began on the Vilvoorde BESS project, which will consist of 320 battery modules and occupy a 3.5-hectare site. The project is designed to operate under a 15-year contract with Elia, Belgium’s electricity grid operator. The commissioning of the facility will occur in two phases: 100 MW in September 2025 and another 100 MW in January 2026.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: Engie submitted permit applications for the 380 MW of battery storage projects in Belgium, including the 200 MW project in Vilvoorde. The investment is part of a broader strategy to enhance energy storage capabilities in response to increasing renewable energy production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: Engie announced the submission of permit applications for the Vilvoorde BESS project.
- **July 2023**: Engie received a construction permit for the Vilvoorde BESS project.
- **October 2023**: The Vilvoorde BESS project was selected for capacity remuneration, ensuring its financial viability.
- **July 2024**: Construction officially commenced on the Vilvoorde BESS project.
