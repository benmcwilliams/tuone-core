## BEL-08143-08390

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Belgoprocess Facility in Dessel, Belgium

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2023**: Ondraf/Niras has been granted a permit to construct and operate a surface disposal facility for low- and intermediate-level, short-lived radioactive waste at the Dessel site. This facility will condition waste in concrete containers, which will be placed in concrete modules and covered with soil.
- **April 2022**: Belgoprocess submitted an application to expand its facility with a new installation for the reception and storage of non-conditioned radioactive waste, referred to as the ROC or 165X building. This expansion aims to create sufficient storage capacity for existing and future non-conditioned waste.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2022**: The application for the ROC building was submitted to FANC, indicating a significant investment in expanding the facility's capabilities to manage non-conditioned waste.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2006**: The federal government approved the development of the surface storage project in Dessel.
- **January 2013**: Ondraf/Niras submitted the initial version of the license application to FANC.
- **End of 2017**: Ondraf/Niras responded to the last of the 300 queries from FANC regarding the application.
- **February 2019**: A completed version of the application was submitted to FANC, including a comprehensive safety case.
- **16 May 2023**: A royal decree was published granting the nuclear license for the waste facility.
- **23 April 2023**: The construction and operating license for the ROC building was granted by royal decree.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
