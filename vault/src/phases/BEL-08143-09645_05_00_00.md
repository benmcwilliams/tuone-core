## BEL-08143-09645

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ondraf/Niras Facility in Dessel, Belgium

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2023**: Ondraf/Niras has been granted a permit to construct and operate a surface disposal facility for low- and intermediate-level, short-lived radioactive waste at the Dessel site. This facility will include several modules designed to safely contain and isolate radioactive waste for hundreds of years. The facility will be constructed in two phases, with the first phase consisting of 20 modules and the second phase consisting of 14 modules. Each module will contain approximately 900 monoliths and will be designed to prevent water infiltration and degradation.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2022**: Belgoprocess, Ondraf/Niras's industrial subsidiary, submitted an application to expand storage capacity for non-conditioned radioactive waste at the Dessel site. The project aims to create sufficient storage capacity for waste that is already present or will be produced in the future. The construction and operating license for this expansion were granted on 23 April 2023.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2006**: The federal government approved the development of the surface storage project in Dessel.
- **January 2013**: Ondraf/Niras submitted the initial version of the license application to the Federal Agency for Nuclear Control (FANC).
- **End of 2017**: Ondraf/Niras answered the last of the 300 queries from FANC regarding the application.
- **February 2019**: The completed version of the application, which included over 20,000 pages detailing the safety of the facility, was submitted to FANC.
- **16 May 2023**: A royal decree was published granting the nuclear license for the waste facility, marking a significant milestone in the long-term management of radioactive waste in Belgium.
- **2024**: Construction work is expected to begin, with the first radioactive waste anticipated to be placed in the facility within a few years.
