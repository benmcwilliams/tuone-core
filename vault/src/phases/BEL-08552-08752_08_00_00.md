### Evolutionary Timeline of the Elia Facility in Princess Elisabeth Island, Belgium

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **03-10-2022:** Elia presented draft plans for the Princess Elisabeth Island, which will serve as the world's first artificial energy island. It will connect offshore wind farms in the Princess Elisabeth zone (maximum capacity of 3.5 GW) to the onshore high-voltage grid. The island will utilize both high-voltage direct current (HVDC) and alternating current (HVAC) technologies. The construction will involve concrete caissons filled with sand, covering an area of approximately five hectares.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **03-10-2022:** The project is set to receive a subsidy of around EUR 100 million from Belgium’s post-COVID-19 recovery plan, pending approval from the European Commission. This funding is part of a broader initiative to enhance offshore wind capacity and energy independence.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **03-10-2022:** The tender process for the construction of the energy island is being prepared, along with the environmental impact assessment and permitting procedures. Construction is scheduled to start in 2024, with completion expected by mid-2026. Full connection capacity to the Elia grid is targeted for 2030.
