### Evolutionary Timeline of the Nyrstar Facility in Lommel, Belgium

1. **Technology and Operations**
   - **June 2019**: The Crystal Solar Park in Lommel, with a capacity of 99.5 MW, was completed. This facility is the largest solar project in Belgium to date. Nyrstar, a local zinc and lead manufacturer, signed a 15-year Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) in July 2019 to purchase some of the power generated at the solar park. The annual generation from the project is expected to be around 85 GWh, which will be used to power Nyrstar's zinc smelter, making its production process greener by utilizing renewable energy.

2. **Investment and Development**
   - **June 2019**: The solar park was granted a premium of €0.0479 per kWh on top of the market power price by the Flemish government for the power it injects into the grid. This premium was reduced from an earlier estimate of €0.078 per kWh after Engie's tender process, indicating a more efficient project requiring 34% less support than initially calculated.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**
   - **October 2018**: Construction on the Lommel solar project began.
   - **June 2019**: The Crystal Solar Park was officially completed and operational.

4. **Labour**
   - The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the solar park or Nyrstar's operations.

5. **Ownership Structure**
   - The Crystal Solar Park is owned by Limburgse investeringsmaatschappij, a Flemish investment company. Nyrstar does not own the facility exclusively but has a long-term agreement to purchase power generated from it.
