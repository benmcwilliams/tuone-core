### Evolutionary Timeline of ZF Wind Power Facility in Lommel, Belgium

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: ZF Wind Power ordered a 15 MW end-of-line test rig from R&D Test Systems, which will enable the testing of two powertrains per day. The first serial-produced 15 MW powertrain for the offshore wind market is expected to leave the facility in the first quarter of 2024. The facility is capable of producing innovative powertrain models for offshore turbines that can generate up to 80 GWh/year, sufficient for the annual electricity consumption of 20,000 households.
- **September 2023**: ZF Wind Power shipped the first 15 MW powertrain for the Vestas V236-15MW turbine. Serial production of the 15 MW powertrain is set to commence in the first quarter of 2024. The logistics for transporting powertrains will shift to barge transport from Lommel to Antwerp, optimizing costs and sustainability.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: R&D Test Systems received a follow-up order from ZF Wind Power for the development of the end-of-line test bench, which is part of the facility's investment in enhancing testing capabilities for high-power offshore wind turbines. The test rig is expected to be the most powerful in the industry.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones

- **September 2023**: The first 15 MW powertrain was shipped, marking a significant milestone in the facility's production capabilities.
