### Evolutionary Timeline of the IRE Facility in Fleurus, Belgium

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2023**: The IRE facility in Fleurus is involved in the production of medical radioisotopes, specifically molybdenum-99 (Mo-99) and iodine-131 (I-131). The facility has transitioned to using low-enriched uranium (LEU) targets for Mo-99 production, completing the conversion from highly-enriched uranium (HEU) to LEU, which is a significant milestone in non-proliferation efforts. The facility is also set to process radioactive residues from isotope production through the RECUMO project, which will recover usable materials, including low-enriched uranium, from these residues.

#### 2. Investment and Development

This conversion was part of a broader commitment to enhance nuclear security and reduce proliferation risks.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2023**: A ground-breaking ceremony was held for the RECUMO facility, which is designed to recycle radioactive residues from the production of medical radioisotopes. The facility is expected to be operational by 2025 and will process current and future residues until 2038.
- **April 2020**: IRE received approval from the Federal Agency for Nuclear Control (FANC) to start Mo-99 production using LEU targets, marking a significant shift in their production process.
- **May 2020**: IRE produced its first batch of Mo-99 using LEU targets.
