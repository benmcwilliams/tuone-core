### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ineos Inovyn Facility in Jemeppe-sur-Sambre, Belgium

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **February 2024:** Ineos Inovyn confirmed the development of a 60MW solar farm at the Jemeppe production site, which is set to come online in July 2024. This solar farm will supply power exclusively to the Jemeppe facility, which employs over 540 people. The initiative is part of Ineos's strategic decarbonisation roadmap aimed at reducing CO2 emissions across its sites by more than 30% by 2030 and achieving Net Zero by 2050.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **February 2024:** The solar farm project is being developed in collaboration with PerPetum Energy and Green4Power. Green4Power finances and owns the solar farm, while PerPetum Energy is responsible for its build, commissioning, and maintenance. Ineos Inovyn will acquire all green electricity produced from the solar farm under a power purchase agreement for the next 15 years.

   - **February 2024:** Announcement of the solar farm project, with a planned operational date of July 2024.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
