## BEL-09987-08068

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Smulders Facility in Port of Albany, BEL

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Production Capacity:** The facility will produce offshore wind towers and transition pieces, with the capability to fabricate wind turbine sections up to 33 feet in diameter and 164 feet long.
   - **Facility Size:** The total manufacturing, assembly, and office space will amount to 590,000 square feet, including a 67,000 square foot building for assembly and a 292,000 square foot building for welding and metal plate work.
   - **Operational Start:** Manufacturing is expected to begin by the end of 2023.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Federal Grant:** The Port of Albany received a USD 29.5 million Federal grant for the design and construction of the necessary infrastructure for the offshore wind tower manufacturing facility.
   - **Contract Award:** A USD 42.7 million contract was awarded to CDMG Metal Buildings to supply four metal buildings for the facility.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Announcement Date:** The project was announced on June 5, 2022.
   - **Construction Start:** Construction is set to begin following the signing of the contract with CDMG, with the first pre-engineered building expected to be shipped by late September or early October 2022.
   - **Production Start:** Manufacturing operations are anticipated to commence by the end of 2023.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Job Creation:** The facility is expected to create approximately 550 jobs, including positions for electricians, welders, painters, assembly workers, and support staff.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Joint Venture:** The facility is being developed by Marmen Welcon, a joint venture between two manufacturers of wind towers, in collaboration with Smulders, a Belgian company, for the production of transition pieces.
