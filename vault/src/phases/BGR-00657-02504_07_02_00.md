## BGR-00657-02504

### Evolutionary Timeline of the e.Go Facility in Lowetsch, Bulgaria

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05/2021**: Next.e.GO Mobile resumes production of the e.GO Life small electric car in Aachen, Germany, with plans to produce 500 cars by the end of the year and 2,500 units in 2022. By 2023, production could exceed 10,000 cars annually.
- **07/2021**: Next.e.GO Mobile announces plans for a micro-factory in Lowetsch, Bulgaria, which will utilize "revolutionary production technology" to produce up to 30,000 vehicles per year starting in the first quarter of 2024. The facility will be a "cyber-physical twin" of the Aachen factory and will produce the next generation of the e.GO Life.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07/2021**: Next.e.GO Mobile confirms an investment of 140 million euros for the construction of the micro-factory in Lowetsch, Bulgaria.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **07/2021**: Next.e.GO Mobile officially signs an agreement to build the micro-factory in Lowetsch, confirming the location and the creation of 1,000 jobs. The Start of Production (SOP) is planned for the first quarter of 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **07/2021**: The establishment of the micro-factory in Lowetsch is expected to create 1,000 jobs in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **07/2021**: Next.e.GO Mobile is the owner of the facility, having been re-established under Dutch investor ND Industrial after filing for protective shield proceedings in 2020. The company is pursuing a decentralized growth strategy with full ownership of the Lowetsch facility.
