### Evolutionary Timeline of the GE Facility in Sofia, Bulgaria

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility is involved in the development of the Sofia offshore wind farm's HVDC (High Voltage Direct Current) platform, which will convert electricity generated by 100 turbines from 66kV AC to 320kV DC for transmission to shore. The total capacity of the Sofia offshore wind farm is 1400MW.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The project is being developed by RWE, with GE Renewable Energy and Sembcorp Marine selected as preferred bidders for the transmission system. The full contract for the project is contingent upon the final investment decision expected in the first quarter of 2021.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **July 2020:** Iv-Offshore & Energy signed an early works contract with Sembcorp Marine Offshore Platforms to provide engineering services for the Sofia HVDC platform. This includes detailed design for the topside and jacket foundation, as well as procurement of topside equipment.
   - **2018:** A Front End Engineering Design (FEED) study was completed by UK Grid Solutions and Iv-Offshore & Energy for the proposed Sofia HVDC platform.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
