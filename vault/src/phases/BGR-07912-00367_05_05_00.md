### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hyundai Facility in Kozloduy, Bulgaria

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2024**: Hyundai, in collaboration with Westinghouse, is shortlisted for the engineering, construction, and commissioning of units 7 and 8 at the Kozloduy nuclear plant, which will utilize Westinghouse’s AP 1000 technology. The planned capacity for these two reactors is a combined 2,300 megawatts (MW). Unit 7 is expected to be completed by 2033.
- **November 2024**: A contract is signed between the Kozloduy Nuclear Power Plant and a consortium of Westinghouse and Hyundai for the construction of the new nuclear reactors using the AR-1000 technology. The first reactor is expected to generate electricity by 2034.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2024**: The estimated cost for the construction of the two reactors is projected to be around €17.6 billion, with only 30% of the costs financed by the state budget. The Bulgarian government anticipates receiving $8 billion in financing from the US Exim Bank and similar loans from South Korean institutions.
- **November 2024**: The Bulgarian government has invested nearly $1 billion in the Kozloduy-New Capacities project company, with expectations of additional funding from the sale of two unused Russian reactors to Ukraine.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2024**: The Bulgarian parliament votes in favor of Hyundai for the nuclear reactor project, marking a significant milestone in the project’s approval process.
- **March 2024**: Concerns are raised regarding the economic viability of the new nuclear project, but the government continues to pursue the construction of the reactors.
- **November 2024**: The project is declared "irreversible" following the signing of the contract for construction, with the government set to finalize the project's cost and economic feasibility by the end of the year.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2024**: While specific job creation figures are not mentioned, the construction of the new reactors is expected to create jobs in the region, particularly in relation to the involvement of Bulgarian subcontractors and the anticipated financing from US and South Korean institutions.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
