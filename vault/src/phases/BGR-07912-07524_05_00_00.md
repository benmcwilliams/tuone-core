### Evolutionary Timeline of the Westinghouse Electric Company Facility in Kozloduy, Bulgaria

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2024**: Westinghouse Electric Company, in collaboration with South Korea’s Hyundai Engineering & Construction, signed an engineering services contract for the construction of two AP1000 nuclear reactors at the Kozloduy nuclear power plant. The first AP1000 reactor is expected to begin commercial operations in **2035**. The AP1000 is a Generation III+ reactor known for its passive safety systems and modular design.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2023**: Westinghouse extended its Front-End Engineering and Design (FEED) contract with Kozloduy NPP-Newbuild to support the construction of the two AP1000 reactors. This extension followed an initial FEED contract awarded in **June 2023**. The project is expected to enhance Bulgaria's energy security and has already seen Westinghouse sign memoranda of understanding with 22 Bulgarian suppliers.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2023**: Initial FEED contract awarded to Westinghouse for the construction of two AP1000 reactors.
- **October 2023**: Extension of the FEED contract to further support the project.
- **November 2024**: Signing of the engineering services contract with Hyundai Engineering & Construction, marking a significant step in the project’s development.

#### 4. Labour
- The project is anticipated to create job opportunities for Bulgarian companies involved in the construction of the reactors, although specific job creation numbers have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is not exclusively owned by Westinghouse Electric Company; it is a collaborative project involving a consortium with Hyundai Engineering & Construction and the local entity Kozloduy NPP-Newbuild.
