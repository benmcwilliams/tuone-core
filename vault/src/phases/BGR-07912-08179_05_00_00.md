## BGR-07912-08179

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Westinghouse Facility in Kozloduy, Bulgaria

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: Westinghouse signed a 10-year contract to supply VVER-1000 nuclear fuel to Kozloduy unit 5, marking a shift from Russian-supplied fuel. The facility operates two VVER-1000 units, which have undergone refurbishment to extend their operational life from 30 to 60 years.
- **October 2023**: The Bulgarian government began construction of unit 7 at the Kozloduy nuclear power plant using Westinghouse AP1000 technology, which had not been operational in Europe before. The first new unit is expected to be operational by 2035, with the second unit following in 2037.
- **February 2024**: The Bulgarian Parliament approved talks with Hyundai Engineering & Construction for the construction of the new Westinghouse AP1000 units.
- **March 2024**: The first loading of Westinghouse fuel at Kozloduy was planned for May 2024, with a phased transition to the new fuel type expected to take four years.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2023**: The Accelerated Program for Implementation of Secure VVER Fuel Supply (APIS) was co-funded by the EU with EUR 10 million to support diversification of fuel supplies, including for Kozloduy.
- **February 2024**: The financial framework for constructing units 7 and 8 at Kozloduy was estimated to be around $14 billion, with plans for 25-30% self-financing and the remainder to be loan-financed.
- **November 2024**: A contract was signed between Kozloduy NPP and a consortium of Westinghouse and Hyundai for the construction of the new reactors, with expected costs exceeding $14 billion.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2022**: Westinghouse signed a 10-year supply contract for VVER-1000 fuel for Kozloduy unit 5.
- **October 2023**: Construction of unit 7 commenced.
- **February 2024**: Five companies submitted applications to build the new units, with the target completion for the first unit set for 2033.
- **March 2024**: The first loading of Westinghouse fuel was scheduled for May 2024.
- **November 2024**: The project was declared "irreversible" with the signing of a contract for the construction of the new reactors.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2024**: The construction of the new units is expected to create jobs, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2023**: Westinghouse is involved in a long-term partnership with Bulgaria for the supply of nuclear fuel and the construction of new reactors, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership. The facility operates under the ownership of the Bulgarian government, with Westinghouse providing technology and fuel supply.
