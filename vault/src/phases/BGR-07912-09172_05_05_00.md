### Evolutionary Timeline of Hyundai Engineering & Construction Facility in Kozloduy, Bulgaria

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2024**: Hyundai Engineering & Construction (E&C) was shortlisted to construct and commission two new Westinghouse AP1000 nuclear units at the Kozloduy nuclear power plant. The total capacity of these new units is projected to be 2300 MWe, which will exceed the 1760 MWe capacity of the previously closed units 1-4.
- **November 2024**: An engineering services contract was signed between Westinghouse Electric Company and Hyundai E&C for the construction of the two AP1000 reactors. The first reactor is expected to begin commercial operations in 2035.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2024**: The Bulgarian government aims to achieve a construction cost of approximately EUR6 billion (USD6.5 billion) for each of the new units. The project is part of Bulgaria's strategy to enhance its nuclear capabilities and energy security.
- **April 2024**: The Bulgarian Parliament set a deadline for negotiations on the contract clauses with Hyundai E&C by April 15, 2024, with a final investment decision expected by mid-2025.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2023**: The Bulgarian government approved the decision to move forward with the construction of the new AP1000 units at Kozloduy.
- **February 2024**: Hyundai E&C was selected as the sole constructor for the new units after a competitive application process involving five companies.
- **April 2024**: The Bulgarian Parliament approved the initiation of contract negotiations with Hyundai E&C, with the aim for the first unit to be operational by 2035 and the second by 2037.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2024**: The project is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers have not been disclosed. The Bulgarian government has indicated that 30% of the project work should be allocated to Bulgarian contractors, which may further enhance local job creation.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2024**: Hyundai E&C is involved in a partnership with Westinghouse for the construction of the new units, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership. The project is managed by Kozloduy NPP - New Builds Plc, which oversees the overall development of the new nuclear units.
