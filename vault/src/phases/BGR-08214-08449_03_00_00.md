### Evolutionary Timeline of the Danube Hydropower Facility, Bulgaria

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2023**: The proposed hydropower plants on the Danube River, specifically the Turnu Magurele-Nikopol project, are expected to have a combined installed power capacity of over 800 MW, with Bulgaria sharing half of this capacity. The Bulgarian Energy Ministry emphasizes the importance of utilizing this natural resource for energy sovereignty.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2023**: The projected cost for the Turnu Magurele-Nikopol hydropower plant is over €2 billion, with potential funding sources including the EU's Modernisation Fund and transborder cooperation funds. However, the exact funding sources remain unclear, and the costs are expected to rise due to inflation.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2023**: The project is in the planning stages, with political support from both Bulgaria and Romania. However, environmental concerns have been raised regarding the impact on local ecosystems and existing projects, such as the Fast Danube navigation improvement initiative.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2023**: Local residents express optimism about the job creation potential of the hydropower plants, indicating that the project could provide salaries and taxes that would significantly benefit local budgets. The construction phase is expected to require numerous specialists and create a substantial number of jobs.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2023**: The ownership structure of the facility has not been explicitly detailed in the articles, but it is implied that the project is a joint initiative between Bulgaria and Romania, with shared capacity and investment responsibilities.
