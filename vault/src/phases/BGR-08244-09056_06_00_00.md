### Evolutionary Timeline of the Green Solar Energy Facility in Silistra Municipality, Bulgaria

**1. Technology and Operations:**
- **February 2024:** The St George solar park, which Green Solar Energy is involved in, is set to produce 229 MW of solar energy. The facility will be constructed on a 165-hectare brownfield site, the former Silistra airport, and will consist of nearly 400,000 solar panels. The power generated will be sold to commercial and industrial users through long-term power purchase agreements (PPAs).

**2. Investment and Development:**
- **February 2024:** The project will be financed through loan facilities from a consortium of international lending partners and regional commercial lenders. The first PPA for the St George project is expected to be announced soon, marking it as one of the first PPAs signed in Bulgaria.

**3. Project Status and Milestones:**
- **February 2024:** Construction work for the St George solar park is scheduled to start shortly, with the plant expected to come online in the following year. The project is a collaboration between Rezolv Energy, CMC Europe, Solarpro, and Green Solar Energy, with the latter responsible for high voltage work.

**4. Labour:**
- **February 2024:** The construction phase of the St George project is anticipated to create up to 200 jobs, with a focus on hiring local residents. Additionally, training and skills development programs will be implemented to support the workforce.

**5. Ownership Structure:**
- **February 2024:** The facility is part of a collaborative project involving multiple partners, including Rezolv Energy, CMC Europe, Solarpro, and Green Solar Energy. The specific ownership structure regarding exclusivity or joint venture arrangements has not been detailed in the available information.
