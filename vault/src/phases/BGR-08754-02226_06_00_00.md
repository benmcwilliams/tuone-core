## BGR-08754-02226

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Eurowind Energy Facility in Tenevo, Bulgaria

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2024**: The Tenevo Solar Technologies EAD facility is set to produce 238MW of solar energy. It is expected to generate over 300GWh of electricity annually and save 250,000 tonnes of carbon emissions each year. There are plans for a long-term addition of 250MW of behind-the-meter energy storage capacity.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2024**: The European Bank for Reconstruction and Development (EBRD) is providing €50 million in funding for the Tenevo solar farm. Additionally, Raiffeisen Bank International is contributing a parallel financing facility of €53 million, bringing the total investment to €103 million. This financing is part of the EBRD’s InvestEU Framework for Sustainable Transition, marking the first use of the InvestEU guarantee in Bulgaria.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2024**: The project is announced as the first renewable energy plant in Bulgaria over 100MW to operate fully merchant without a support scheme or corporate power purchase agreement. The construction and operation of the facility are being managed by Tenevo Solar Technologies EAD, a joint stock company.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2024**: While specific job creation figures are not mentioned, the project is expected to strengthen the private sector presence in Bulgaria's energy sector, which may imply job creation opportunities as the facility becomes operational.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2024**: Tenevo Solar Technologies EAD is a joint stock company equally owned by Renalfa IPP (an Austrian joint venture between Renalfa Solarpro Group and RGreen Invest) and Eurowind Energy. This indicates a joint ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by Eurowind Energy.
