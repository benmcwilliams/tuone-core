### Evolutionary Timeline of the Rezolv Energy Facility in Silistra, Bulgaria

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2023**: Rezolv Energy acquired the rights to build and operate a 229 MW solar plant named the St George solar project on the site of the former Silistra airport, covering 165 hectares and consisting of nearly 400,000 solar panels. The plant will connect to the main 110 kV transmission grid via two independent connection lines totaling about 6 km in length. The project is expected to be operational by 2025.
- **September 2024**: Rezolv Energy signed engineering, procurement, and construction (EPC) contracts with CMC Europe, Solarpro, and Green Solar Energy for the construction of the 229 MW solar PV project. The project is anticipated to be one of the largest solar projects in Bulgaria upon completion.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2023**: The acquisition of the St George solar project from YGY Industries JSC was completed for an undisclosed sum. Rezolv Energy was launched in 2022 with backing from €500 million from sustainable infrastructure investor Actis, focusing on renewable energy developments in Central and Eastern Europe.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2023**: Rezolv Energy announced plans to start construction of the St George solar project by the end of 2023, with completion scheduled for early 2025.
- **September 2024**: The signing of EPC contracts marked a significant milestone in the project's development, indicating progress towards the construction phase.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2023**: The St George solar project is expected to create approximately 500 full-time equivalent (FTE) jobs, including roles for light assembly and highly-skilled engineering positions. The project will provide local employment for skilled and semi-skilled labor throughout its 30-plus years of operation.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2023**: Rezolv Energy acquired the St George solar project from YGY Industries JSC, indicating that Rezolv Energy owns the facility exclusively.
