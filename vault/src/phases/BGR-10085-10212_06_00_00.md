## BGR-10085-10212

### Evolutionary Timeline of Tenevo Solar Technologies EAD Facility in Southeastern Bulgaria

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2024**: Tenevo Solar Technologies EAD announced the development of a 237.6 MW solar plant in southeastern Bulgaria. This facility will be the first renewable energy plant in Bulgaria over 100 MW to sell all its output in the market without a support scheme or a corporate power purchase agreement. Once operational, it is expected to generate over 300 GWh of electricity annually. The project also plans to incorporate 250 MW of behind-the-meter energy storage in the long term.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2024**: The facility secured a total investment of €103 million ($107.2 million) for its development. This funding includes €50 million from the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development (EBRD) and €53 million from Raiffeisen Bank International. The EBRD's contribution is supported by first loss risk cover under its InvestEU Framework for Sustainable Transition.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2024**: The announcement of the funding and project details marks a significant milestone for Tenevo Solar Technologies EAD, indicating the project's readiness to move forward with construction and operations.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the facility's development.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Tenevo Solar Technologies EAD is a Bulgarian joint stock company that is equally owned by two partners: Vienna-based developer Renalfa IPP and Danish independent power producer Eurowind Energy. This indicates a joint venture ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by a single entity.
