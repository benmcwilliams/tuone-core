### Evolutionary Timeline of the Westinghouse Facility at Kozloduy Nuclear Power Plant, Bulgaria

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **October 2023:** The Bulgarian Government commenced construction of Unit 7 at the Kozloduy Nuclear Power Plant, utilizing Westinghouse's AP1000 technology. This technology has not been operational in Europe before. The combined capacity of the two new units (Unit 7 and the planned Unit 8) is projected to be 2,300 MW, which will exceed the 1,760 MW capacity of the previously closed first four units.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **October 2023:** The Bulgarian government allocated €250 million (Lv488.96 million) for the construction of Unit 7. This investment is part of a broader strategy to enhance nuclear power capabilities in Bulgaria and to access the European market through the Westinghouse project.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **October 2023:** The construction of Unit 7 officially began. The government is in the process of selecting a contractor to design, construct, and commission the new power unit, with a completion deadline set for 2033. Preparations for Unit 8 have also been approved, with an expected completion timeline of two to three years after Unit 7.

4. **Labour:**
   - The articles do not specify exact job creation or job loss figures related to the construction of Unit 7 or the preparations for Unit 8. However, the construction of new nuclear units typically involves significant job creation during both the construction and operational phases.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is being developed under a partnership with Westinghouse, which is supplying the AP1000 technology. The Bulgarian government is leading the project, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership by Westinghouse. The government has also signed a ten-year deal with Westinghouse for fuel supply for Kozloduy Unit 5, further solidifying the partnership.
