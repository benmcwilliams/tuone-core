## CHE-00050-00100

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Blackstone Resources Facility in Baar, CHE

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2021**: Blackstone Technology, a subsidiary of Blackstone Resources, announced the readiness for mass production of a new lithium-ion battery cell based on LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate) technology. The production is set to begin in summer 2021 at their facility in Döbeln, Germany. The company targets an energy density of over 220 Wh/kg for these cells, utilizing a proprietary 3D printing technology that allows for various shapes suitable for electric vehicles, buses, and motorcycles. The facility aims to produce 100 million battery cells annually in the first phase.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2021**: Blackstone Resources announced a significant investment in state-of-the-art battery production facilities in Döbeln, Germany, supported by a convertible loan facility of up to 20 million Swiss francs (approximately 18.5 million euros). This investment is aimed at laying the groundwork for both the LFP battery cell technology and future solid-state technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2020**: Blackstone Resources announced the establishment of its production facility in Döbeln, Germany.
- **February 2021**: The company confirmed that the LFP battery cell technology would enter volume production by summer 2021.
- **December 2021**: Blackstone Technology unveiled its manufacturing process for lithium-ion batteries using 3D printing at its plant in Döbeln, marking the start of series production.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2022**: Following the spin-off of Blackstone Technology into a new holding company, Blackstone Technology Holding AG, the restructuring aimed to provide the battery division with more autonomy and investment opportunities. However, specific job creation or loss figures were not mentioned in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2022**: Blackstone Resources AG spun off its German battery division into Blackstone Technology Holding AG, which is a 100% subsidiary of Blackstone Resources AG. This indicates that Blackstone Resources maintains full ownership of the facility through its subsidiary.
