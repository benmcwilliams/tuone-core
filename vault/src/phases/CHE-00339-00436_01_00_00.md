## CHE-00339-00436

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Kyburz Facility in Freienstein, CHE

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **07-09-2020**: Kyburz opens a battery recycling facility in Freienstein, Zurich. The facility is designed to recycle lithium iron phosphate (LiFePO4) batteries used in Kyburz's electric vehicles, with an initial processing capacity of 4,000 cells per year. The final capacity is projected to reach up to 24,000 cells per year, which corresponds to the annual production of 3,000 vehicles. The recycling process recovers up to 91% of the metals contained in the batteries and is developed with the support of EMPA and ZHAW, focusing on sustainable practices without the use of chemicals.

- **No specific investment or subsidy details** were mentioned in the articles regarding the Freienstein facility. However, the development of the recycling process was supported by academic institutions, indicating a collaborative investment in research and development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **07-09-2020**: The facility officially begins operations as a battery recycling plant.
- **22-05-2024**: Kyburz announces a cooperation agreement with Vitesco Technologies to industrialize a new recycling process for lithium-ion batteries at a new facility in Limbach-Oberfrohna, Germany, based on the technology developed at the Freienstein facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **22-05-2024**: The partnership with Vitesco aims to secure jobs at the Limbach-Oberfrohna site, which will transition from diesel injector production to sustainable battery recycling. While specific job creation numbers for the Freienstein facility were not provided, the collaboration indicates a focus on long-term job security in the recycling sector.
