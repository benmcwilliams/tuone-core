## CHE-00421-00029

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Alpiq Facility in Gösgen, Switzerland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2019**: Alpiq, in collaboration with H2 Energy, announced plans to construct Switzerland's first commercial hydrogen production plant at the Gösgen hydroelectric power station. The facility will produce hydrogen using 100% hydropower, with an initial production capacity of 2 megawatts (MW). The hydrogen produced will be used to power the first 50 of a total of 1,600 fuel cell trucks.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2019**: The joint venture Hydrospider, equally owned by Alpiq and H2 Energy, submitted a planning application for the hydrogen production plant. The project is positioned as a significant step towards the decarbonization of goods transport in Switzerland, emphasizing the use of renewable energy sources for hydrogen production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **End of 2019**: The commissioning of the commercial hydrogen production plant is scheduled for completion. The facility aims to be operational by the end of the year, marking a significant milestone in Switzerland's hydrogen production capabilities.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify exact job creation or job loss figures related to the establishment of the hydrogen production facility at Gösgen.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is a joint venture between Alpiq and H2 Energy, with both companies holding equal shares (50% each) in Hydrospider, the entity responsible for the hydrogen production plant.
