### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hyundai Facility in Gösgen, CHE

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Production:** The facility is focused on the production of hydrogen using hydroelectric power.
   - **Production Capacity:** A 2 MW hydrogen production plant is planned to be built at the Gösgen hydroelectric power station.
   - **Updates:** The hydrogen produced will be used for the first 50 of a total of 1,600 fuel cell trucks, with production starting at the end of 2019.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Investments:** The facility is part of a joint venture between Alpiq and H2 Energy (H2E), with Hyundai Hydrogen Mobility committing to supply 1,600 fuel cell-powered heavy trucks from 2019 to 2025. This commitment includes an increase of 600 trucks compared to the original agreement made in 2018.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Announcement:** The planning application for the hydrogen production plant was submitted in July 2019.
   - **Construction Start:** The construction of the hydrogen production plant is expected to begin following the planning approval, with production slated to start at the end of 2019.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Job Creation:** Specific job creation details related to the facility have not been mentioned in the article.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Ownership:** The facility is part of a joint venture called Hydrospider, in which Alpiq and H2E each hold a 50% stake. Hyundai Hydrogen Mobility is involved through its partnership with H2E but does not exclusively own the facility.
