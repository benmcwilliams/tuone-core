### Evolutionary Timeline of the Framatome Facility in Gösgen, Switzerland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 1979**: The Gösgen nuclear power plant, a 1010 MWe pressurized water reactor, began commercial operation.
- **2002**: Kernkraftwerk Gösgen-Däniken AG (KKG) began modernizing its instrumentation and control (I&C) systems in cooperation with Framatome, transitioning from analog to digital technologies.
- **April 2019**: Framatome was awarded a contract to modernize the reactor protection system (RPS) at Gösgen, which included the entire RPS modernization using Framatome's TELEPERM XS digital I&C platform.
- **2022**: Installation and commissioning of the new RPS were carried out during the plant's planned outage.
- **2024**: Framatome's Enhanced Accident Tolerant Fuel (E-ATF) technology, utilizing chromium-coated cladding, completed four 12-month operational cycles at Gösgen, demonstrating enhanced safety and performance.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2019**: The contract awarded to Framatome for the RPS modernization indicates a significant investment in upgrading the facility's safety systems, although specific financial figures are not disclosed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2019**: Contract awarded to modernize the RPS.
- **2022**: Completion of the installation and commissioning of the new RPS during the planned outage.
- **2024**: Successful completion of four operational cycles for the E-ATF technology, with the lead fuel rods reinserted for a fifth cycle.
