### Evolutionary Timeline of the Leclanché Facility in Yverdon-les-Bains, Switzerland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2021**: Leclanché announced the development of a new generation of lithium-ion modules, named M3, designed for energy-intensive electrified transport applications. The new modules feature higher energy and power density, with a long lifetime of up to 20,000 cycles (LTO) or 8,000 cycles (G/NMC). The facility inaugurated a new production line for large-scale module manufacturing with a capacity of up to 500 MWh per year, increasing the current capacity by a factor of six. The line is expected to produce over 60,000 modules annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2021**: The new production line was developed in cooperation with Comau Spa, an industrial automation provider. The investment in this highly automated production line is part of Leclanché's long-term planning and development strategy, although specific financial figures for the investment were not disclosed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2021**: Leclanché announced the commissioning of the new module series production line at its facility in Yverdon-les-Bains. The company plans to move its headquarters to the same building in the fourth quarter of 2021.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2021**: While specific job creation figures were not mentioned, the expansion of the production line and the move of the headquarters suggest potential job growth as a result of increased production capacity and operational scale.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2021**: Leclanché operates the facility in Yverdon-les-Bains, and there is no indication of joint ownership or partnerships regarding the facility itself. The company is described as one of the few in Europe producing both cells and modules on a large scale, indicating full ownership of the facility's operations.
