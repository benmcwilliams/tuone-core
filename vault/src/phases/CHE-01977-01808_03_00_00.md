## CHE-01977-01808

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Nant de Drance SA Facility in Valais Canton, Switzerland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Capacity**: The Nant de Drance facility has a total capacity of 900 MW, consisting of six pump turbines, each rated at 150 MW.
- **Function**: The facility is designed to stabilize the electricity grid in Switzerland and across Europe by generating or storing large quantities of power quickly, which is crucial due to the increasing volatility from renewable energy sources.
- **Operational Status**: The plant is expected to be fully operational by the third quarter of 2021. Initial tests with water on the turbines are scheduled to begin in early 2020.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Amount**: The total investment for the Nant de Drance project is €1.5 billion.
- **Ownership Structure**: The facility is owned by Nant de Drance SA, which is a consortium comprising Alpiq (39%), SBB (36%), IWB (15%), and FMV (10%).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Milestone Achievements**: 
  - As of November 2019, the facility reached a significant milestone with the filling of the two penstocks with water for the first time.
  - The filling of the penstocks will occur in stages, starting at the end of November 2019, with initial tests to ensure the integrity of the pipes and valves.
  - The first tests with water on the six pump turbines are set to begin in early 2020, continuing for several months.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: Specific job creation figures related to the facility's construction or operation have not been detailed in the articles provided.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The Nant de Drance facility is not owned exclusively by a single entity but is a joint venture among four companies: Alpiq (39%), SBB (36%), IWB (15%), and FMV (10%).
