### Evolutionary Timeline of the Axpo Facility in Brugg, CHE

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2021**: Axpo, along with Voegtlin-Meyer, IBB Energie, and the town of Brugg, signed a letter of intent to build a green hydrogen facility near the power plant in Brugg. The facility is designed for an installed capacity of up to **15 MW** and aims to produce approximately **2,000 tonnes of green hydrogen annually** through electrolysis. The electricity required for this production will be sourced from the nearby Wildegg-Brugg hydropower plant owned by Axpo.

- **November 2021**: The announcement of the facility's development indicates a significant investment in green hydrogen technology, although specific financial details regarding the investment or subsidies were not disclosed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2021**: The letter of intent was signed, marking the initial step towards the establishment of the facility. The municipality of Brugg was set to decide on granting building rights in December 2021. If approved, the commissioning of the plant is planned for the **end of 2023**.
