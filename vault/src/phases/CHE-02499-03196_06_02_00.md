## CHE-02499-03196

### Evolutionary Timeline of Meyer Burger Facility in Thun, Switzerland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2010**: Meyer Burger reported strong sales and highlighted high manufacturing volumes at its Thun facility, which was involved in wafering and module assembly operations. The facility was noted for its diamond wire technology aimed at reducing solar wafer production costs.
- **09/2012**: The company announced the establishment of a new solar technology center at its headquarters in Thun, consolidating operations from other locations, including the closure of its Lyss facility.
- **11/2017**: Meyer Burger announced plans to close its Thun facility, which was the main site for assembling diamond wire wafer cutting tools, and shift assembly to China. The Thun facility also produced module assembly tools and BIPV manufacturing.
- **04/2018**: Meyer Burger decided to outsource the manufacturing of its SmartWire Connection Technology (SWCT) to Mondragon Assembly, ceasing production at the Thun facility by the end of 2018.
- **02/2019**: The company sold its photovoltaic, semiconductor, and sapphire wafering business to Precision Surfacing Solutions (PSS), with the transaction expected to be completed by the end of Q1 2019. This included transferring around 70 employees to PSS.
- **10/2019**: Meyer Burger announced the sale of its headquarters building in Thun to raise cash, while retaining a lease for part of the building.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2010**: Meyer Burger reported a significant increase in sales and order bookings, indicating strong financial health and potential for further investments in technology development.
- **11/2017**: The closure of the Thun facility was expected to incur a one-off non-cash charge of about CHF 40 million, alongside CHF 10 million in restructuring costs.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2012**: Establishment of a new solar technology center at the Thun headquarters was announced, consolidating operations from other facilities.
- **11/2017**: Announcement of the closure of the Thun facility, with all manufacturing activities expected to be discontinued by the end of 2018.
- **04/2018**: The decision to outsource SWCT production to Mondragon Assembly was finalized, marking the end of production at the Thun facility.
- **02/2019**: Completion of the sale of the wafering business to PSS, with the transaction expected to finalize by the end of March 2019.

#### 4. Labour
- **11/2017**: The closure of the Thun facility was expected to lead to the loss of around 180 jobs over the next 15 months.
- **04/2018**: The outsourcing of SWCT production was expected to result in the loss of 100 jobs at the Thun facility.
- **02/2019**: The sale of the wafering business to PSS involved transferring around 70 employees to the new owner, safeguarding jobs in Thun.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **09/2010**: Meyer Burger operated the Thun facility as part of its wholly-owned operations.
- **04/2018**: Following the outsourcing agreement with Mondragon Assembly, Meyer Burger retained ownership of the SWCT patents and sales, while production was transferred to Mondragon.
- **02/2019**: The sale of the wafering business to PSS involved a long-term rental agreement for the facility, indicating a shift in operational focus while retaining ownership of the building.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and changes at the Meyer Burger facility in Thun, Switzerland, highlighting its evolution in technology, operations, investments, project milestones, labor impacts, and ownership structure.
