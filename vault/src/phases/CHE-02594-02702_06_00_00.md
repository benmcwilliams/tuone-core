## CHE-02594-02702

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Bioschmid GmbH Facility in Lucerne, CHE

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2023**: Bioschmid GmbH has partnered with Swiss startup Insolight to complete an agrivoltaic pilot project at their raspberry farm. The facility spans 2,600 m² and features a 160 kW installation comprising 800 PERC bifacial modules and an inverter from Huawei. The system includes an adjustable reflective screen designed to optimize light for raspberry cultivation and is equipped with a control system and tracking algorithms.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2023**: The agrivoltaic plant is supported by the Swiss Federal Office of Energy through the P+D program (pilot and demonstration). Additional support comes from the Canton of Lucerne (Swisslos), the Fondation sur la Croix, The Foundation Valery, and various system suppliers. However, specific details regarding capital expenditure or the levelized cost of electricity have not been disclosed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2023**: The agrivoltaic pilot project is completed, and monitoring will take place over the next three years to assess photovoltaic performance and its impact on agricultural productivity. The Bern University of Applied Sciences will be involved in measuring the PV-related performance.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the agrivoltaic project at the Bioschmid facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Bioschmid GmbH is identified as the owner of the raspberry farm where the agrivoltaic facility is installed, indicating full ownership of the facility.
