## CHE-02594-03054

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Insolight Facility in Lucerne, Switzerland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2023**: Insolight completed a new agrivoltaic pilot project at a raspberry farm in Lucerne, Switzerland. The facility spans 2,600 m² and features a production capacity of 160 kW, utilizing 800 PERC bifacial modules and an inverter from Huawei. The installation includes an adjustable reflective screen designed by Insolight to optimize light for agricultural use.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2023**: The agrivoltaic plant is supported by the Swiss Federal Office of Energy through the P+D program (pilot and demonstration). Additional support comes from the Canton of Lucerne (Swisslos), the Fondation sur la Croix, and The Foundation Valery, although specific investment amounts or capital expenditures have not been disclosed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2023**: The completion of the agrivoltaic pilot project was announced, with plans to monitor the facility for three years to assess photovoltaic performance and its impact on agriculture. The Bern University of Applied Sciences will measure the PV-related performance during this monitoring period.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2023**: The articles do not specify job creation or job loss directly associated with the agrivoltaic project at the Lucerne facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2023**: The facility is owned by Insolight, which is a Swiss startup. The project is located on a raspberry farm operated by Bioschmid GmbH, indicating a partnership but not a joint ownership structure.
