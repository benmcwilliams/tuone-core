## CHE-02692-02825

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EPFL Facility in Lausanne, CHE

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **06-01-2023**: EPFL researchers developed a device that harvests water from the air to produce hydrogen fuel, powered entirely by solar energy. This system utilizes porous and transparent electrodes to maximize contact with water and sunlight exposure, demonstrating the potential for solar-driven hydrogen production.
- **18-04-2023**: A pilot-scale solar reactor was built at EPFL, capable of producing hydrogen, oxygen, and usable heat with unprecedented efficiency. This system integrates all necessary components for solar hydrogen generation, achieving an output power of over 2 kilowatts and marking a significant step towards commercial viability.
- **25-04-2023**: An international research team at EPFL created a quasi-2D perovskite solar cell with a power conversion efficiency of 23.08%, showcasing advancements in solar technology that could enhance energy production capabilities.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **18-04-2023**: The pilot-scale solar reactor project is part of ongoing research and development efforts at EPFL, with potential commercial applications being explored through a spinoff company, SoHHytec SA, which is working on scaling up the technology for industrial use.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **06-01-2023**: The development of the solar-powered hydrogen production device was published, marking a significant milestone in renewable energy research.
- **18-04-2023**: The successful demonstration of the solar reactor system represents the first system-level demonstration of solar hydrogen generation, indicating progress from laboratory-scale to real-world applications.
- **25-04-2023**: The introduction of the 3D/2D perovskite solar cell technology highlights ongoing advancements in solar energy efficiency and stability.

#### 4. Labour
- **18-04-2023**: The scaling up of the solar reactor technology through SoHHytec SA is expected to create job opportunities in the renewable energy sector, particularly in the development and deployment of hydrogen production systems.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The EPFL facility operates under the auspices of the École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne, indicating full ownership by the institution. The spinoff company, SoHHytec SA, is a separate entity focused on commercializing the developed technologies.
