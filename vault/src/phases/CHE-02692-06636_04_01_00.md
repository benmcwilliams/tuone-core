### Evolutionary Timeline of the SoHHytec Facility in Lausanne, CHE

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: The SoHHytec facility, a spinoff from the École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL), is involved in the production of hydrogen, oxygen, and heat through a solar reactor system. The pilot-scale solar reactor utilizes a parabolic dish to concentrate solar radiation nearly 1,000 times, achieving an output power of over 2 kilowatts and a hydrogen production rate of about half a kilogram per day. This system is designed for industrial, commercial, and residential applications, including hydrogen for metal annealing processes, oxygen for hospitals, and heat for hot-water needs.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: SoHHytec is working on building a demonstration plant at a multi-100-kilowatt scale in collaboration with a Swiss-based metal production facility. This project is part of the scaling up of their solar hydrogen generation technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: The facility achieved a major milestone by demonstrating unprecedented efficiency at high output power densities with their pilot demonstration at EPFL. The results of their scaled-up process were published in the journal Nature Energy, marking the first system-level demonstration of solar hydrogen generation.
- **May 2023**: EPFL researchers unveiled a new solar dish plant design, further solidifying the facility's commitment to advancing solar-to-hydrogen technology.
