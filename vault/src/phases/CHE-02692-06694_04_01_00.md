### Evolutionary Timeline of the École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne Facility in Lausanne, CHE

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2023**: École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL) researchers unveiled a new solar dish plant design. The facility produces hydrogen through a thermally integrated photoelectrochemical device that utilizes concentrated solar irradiation. The solar dish has a diameter of 7 meters and concentrates sunlight into a 144 cm² reactor, achieving a solar-to-hydrogen device-level efficiency of greater than 20% with a hydrogen production rate exceeding 2.0 kW. This marks the first system-level demonstration of solar hydrogen generation.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2023**: An EPFL start-up, SoHHytec, is commercializing the solar dish technology to scale up its application, indicating ongoing investment in the development of this clean technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2023**: The findings from the solar dish project were published in the journal *Nature Energy*, highlighting the successful demonstration of the technology.
