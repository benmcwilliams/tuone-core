## CHE-02881-00029

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Alpiq Facility in Valais, Switzerland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 01-07-2022
   - **Details:** The Nant de Drance pumped hydro energy storage (PHES) plant in Valais began operations with a capacity of 20GWh. It features six turbines, each with a nameplate capacity of 150MW, allowing for a maximum power output of 900MW. The facility is designed to stabilize the electricity grid as renewable energy sources increase.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 30-06-2022
   - **Details:** The total investment in the Nant de Drance project amounted to CHF2.2 billion (approximately US$2.3 billion). The project involved 60 companies and significant construction efforts, including raising the Vieux Emosson dam by 21.5m to double the reservoir's capacity.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 01-07-2022
   - **Details:** The Nant de Drance plant officially started operations after 14 years of construction. An official inauguration is planned for September 2022.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 30-06-2022
   - **Details:** At the peak of construction, the project employed 650 workers on-site, indicating significant job creation during the development phase.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 30-06-2022
   - **Details:** Alpiq holds a 39% stake in the Nant de Drance facility, making it the main shareholder. Other shareholders include Swiss national railway company SFR (36%), Industrielle Werke Basel (IWB) (15%), and Canton-owned FMV (10%).
