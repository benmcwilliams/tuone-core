### Evolutionary Timeline of the FMV Facility in Valais, Switzerland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 01-07-2022
   - **Details:** The FMV facility, part of the Nant de Drance pumped hydro energy storage (PHES) plant, began operations with a total energy storage capacity of 20GWh. The facility features six turbines, each with a nameplate capacity of 150MW, allowing for a maximum power output of 900MW. The plant is located 600m underground between the Emosson and Vieux Emosson reservoirs and plays a crucial role in stabilizing the electricity grid as renewable energy sources increase.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 30-06-2022
   - **Details:** The total investment in the Nant de Drance project amounted to CHF2.2 billion (approximately US$2.3 billion). The project involved 60 companies and significant construction efforts, including the excavation of 400,000 cubic meters of rock and the drilling of 17km of tunnels. Additionally, CHF22 million was allocated to 14 environmental offset projects to mitigate the ecological impact of the facility.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 01-07-2022
   - **Details:** The Nant de Drance plant officially began operations on this date, marking the conclusion of 14 years of construction. The facility is set to be inaugurated in September 2022.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** During construction (exact date not specified)
   - **Details:** At the peak of construction, the project employed 650 workers on-site, indicating significant job creation during the development phase.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 30-06-2022
   - **Details:** The ownership of the Nant de Drance facility is shared among several stakeholders: Alpiq (39%), Swiss national railway company SFR (36%), Industrielle Werke Basel (IWB) (15%), and Canton-owned FMV (10%). This indicates that FMV does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a joint venture with other companies.
