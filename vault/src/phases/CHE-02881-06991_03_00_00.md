## CHE-02881-06991

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Industrielle Werke Basel (IWB) Facility in Valais, Switzerland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **June 2022:** The Nant de Drance pumped hydro energy storage (PHES) plant in Valais, Switzerland, officially began operations on July 1, 2022. The facility has a total energy storage capacity of 20GWh, with six turbines each having a nameplate capacity of 150MW, resulting in a maximum power output of 900MW. The plant is located 600m underground between the Emosson and Vieux Emosson reservoirs.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **June 2022:** The total investment in the Nant de Drance project amounted to CHF2.2 billion (approximately US$2.3 billion). The project involved raising the Vieux Emosson dam by 21.5m, effectively doubling the reservoir's capacity. Additionally, CHF22 million was allocated to 14 environmental offset projects aimed at mitigating the ecological impact of the facility.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **June 2022:** The construction of the Nant de Drance plant concluded after 14 years, with the official inauguration scheduled for September 2022. The facility was developed with the collaboration of 60 companies and involved extensive excavation and tunneling work.

4. **Labour:**
   - **June 2022:** At the peak of construction, the project employed 650 workers on-site, contributing to local job creation during the construction phase.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **June 2022:** The ownership of the Nant de Drance facility is shared among several stakeholders: Alpiq (39%), Swiss national railway company SFR (36%), Industrielle Werke Basel (IWB) (15%), and Canton-owned FMV (10%). This indicates that IWB does not have exclusive ownership of the facility but is part of a joint venture.
