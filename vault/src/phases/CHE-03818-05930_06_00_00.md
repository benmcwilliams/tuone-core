## CHE-03818-05930

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Romande Energie Facility in Lac des Toules, Switzerland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2019**: Romande Energie is deploying a **448 kW floating solar array** on Lac des Toules, located at an altitude of **1,810 meters**. The facility is designed to operate under harsh weather conditions and is expected to produce **50% more power** than similar projects on flat land due to the albedo effect from snow and the lake's reflective properties. The installation consists of **1,400 bifacial solar modules** mounted on **36 floating structures**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2019**: The project has a total investment of **CHF 2.35 million** (approximately **$2.37 million**), which was approved by the Canton of Valais in **September 2017**. The facility is entitled to a **feed-in tariff** under Switzerland’s renewable energy incentive scheme.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2019**: The floating solar array is reported to be nearing completion, with all floating structures installed on the water. Final elements are pending before commissioning.
- **December 2019**: The facility is predicted to produce over **800,000 kWh** of electricity annually, sufficient to supply up to **220 homes** in the region.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the facility's development.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned exclusively by **Romande Energie**, developed in partnership with the local municipality of Bourg-St-Pierre.
