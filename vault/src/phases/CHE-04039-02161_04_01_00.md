### Evolutionary Timeline of the Axpo Facility in Bürglen, Switzerland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2024**: Axpo, in collaboration with EWAenergieUri and AVIA Schätzle, announced plans to produce green hydrogen at the H2Uri facility in Bürglen. The facility will have a production capacity of **up to 260 tonnes of green hydrogen per year** and will utilize **2 MW** of hydropower.
- **December 2024**: Construction of the green hydrogen plant officially began. The facility is designed to fuel the first hydrogen passenger vessel on Lake Lucerne, named **MS Saphir**, which is undergoing conversion to a hydrogen fuel cell system.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2024**: The project is supported by the partners involved, including Axpo, EWAenergieUri, and AVIA Schätzle, indicating a collaborative investment approach. Specific financial details regarding investments or subsidies were not disclosed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2024**: Announcement of the H2Uri project and plans for hydrogen production in Bürglen, with operations expected to commence by **fall 2025**.
- **December 2024**: Groundbreaking for the hydrogen production plant took place, with expectations for the facility to be operational as early as **2026**.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2024**: While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned, the construction and operation of the hydrogen facility are expected to contribute to local employment opportunities, particularly in the context of the conversion of the MS Saphir vessel and the establishment of a hydrogen refueling station.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2024**: The facility is a joint venture under the company **H2Uri**, which includes Axpo, EWAenergieUri, and AVIA Schätzle as shareholders. This indicates that Axpo does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a collaborative ownership structure.

### Summary
The Axpo facility in Bürglen is set to become a significant player in the green hydrogen sector, with a focus on producing hydrogen to power a passenger vessel on Lake Lucerne. The project is a joint venture involving multiple partners, highlighting a collaborative approach to advancing clean technology in Switzerland. The facility is expected to be operational by 2026, contributing to local job creation and the broader hydrogen economy.
