### Evolutionary Timeline of the Axpo Facility at Muttsee Dam, Switzerland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **02/2020**: Axpo announced plans to build a 2 MW vertical photovoltaic (PV) plant on the Muttsee dam, located at an altitude of nearly 2,500 meters. The facility is expected to generate approximately 2.7 GWh of solar power annually, with a total of around 6,000 solar modules to be installed over an area of 10,000 square meters.
- **01/2021**: The capacity of the plant was updated to 2.2 MW, and it was confirmed that the facility would supply around 3.3 million kWh of solar power per year, with about half of the production occurring during the winter months.
- **06/2021**: Construction of the 2.2 MW vertical PV system commenced, with the expectation of completion within four months.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **01/2021**: The project was reported to be built at a cost of CHF 8 million (approximately $9 million). Axpo is also collaborating with IWB, which holds a 49% stake in the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **11/2019**: Axpo presented plans for the PV plant, which received approval from the authorities in the canton of Glarus in early 2020.
- **01/2021**: Axpo secured a power purchase agreement (PPA) with supermarket chain Denner, marking it as the first bilateral deal for an unsubsidized solar project in Switzerland.
- **06/2021**: Construction officially began on the vertical solar project.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the Muttsee facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Axpo is the primary developer of the facility, with IWB holding a 49% stake, indicating a joint venture structure for the project.
