## CHE-04042-05328

### Evolutionary Timeline of the IWB Facility at Muttsee Dam, Switzerland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2021**: Axpo announced the development of a 2.2 MW solar plant at the Muttsee dam, which is located at an altitude of almost 2,500 meters above sea level. The facility is designed to produce approximately 3.3 million kWh of solar power annually, with a significant portion generated during the winter months due to optimal sunlight conditions at high altitudes. The project involves the installation of around 6,000 solar modules over an area of 10,000 square meters.
- **June 2021**: Construction of the vertical solar plant commenced, with the first equipment, including a crane, transported to the site via helicopter due to the inaccessibility of the dam wall for road transport. The construction was expected to be completed within four months.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2021**: The total cost of the solar project was reported to be CHF 8 million (approximately $9 million). This investment was made to promote renewable energy and contribute to the energy transition in Switzerland, especially in light of the planned decommissioning of nuclear power plants.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2021**: Axpo secured a power purchase agreement (PPA) with the supermarket chain Denner for the solar project, marking it as the first bilateral deal for an unsubsidized solar project in Switzerland.
- **June 2021**: Construction officially began on the solar project after securing building approval. The project was expected to be operational within four months.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the construction or operation of the facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is a joint venture, with Axpo as the primary developer and IWB (Interkantonale Wasserversorgung Basel) holding a 49% stake in the project through its subsidiary, Paneco. Axpo is the lead company managing the project.
