### Evolutionary Timeline of the Axpo Facility in Beznau, CHE

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Beznau facility operates two 365 MWe Westinghouse pressurized water reactors, making it the oldest power reactors in Switzerland. 
   - Unit 1 began operations in 1969, and Unit 2 started in 1972. 
   - The facility produces approximately 6 TWh of electricity annually, sufficient to meet the needs of 1.3 million four-person households. 
   - Both units also provide district heating.
   - Axpo has committed to operating Unit 2 until 2032 and Unit 1 until 2033, after which both units will be decommissioned and dismantled.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - Since commissioning, Axpo has invested over CHF 2.5 billion in retrofitting and modernizing the Beznau units.
   - In April 2024, Axpo announced an additional investment of CHF 350 million to ensure the operation of the facility until 2033.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - In April 2024, Axpo announced plans to operate the Beznau plant for 60 years and began assessing the feasibility of extending operations beyond that timeframe.
   - The decision to operate Unit 2 until 2032 and Unit 1 until 2033 was made public in the article published on September 12, 2024.
