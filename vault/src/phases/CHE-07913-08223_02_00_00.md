### Evolutionary Timeline of the AGEPP Facility in Lavey-les-Bains, CHE

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2022**: The AGEPP (Alpine GEothermal Power Production) facility in Lavey-les-Bains commenced drilling operations on January 17, 2022. The facility aims to harness geothermal energy by drilling down to a maximum depth of 3,000 meters. It is designed to produce heating and electricity for approximately 900 households, generating an estimated 4.2 gigawatt hours (GWh) of power annually. Additionally, it will supply 15.5 thermal GWh to the local spa center and provide heat resources to the municipalities of Lavey-Morcles and Saint-Maurice, with future plans for heating greenhouses and fish farms.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2022**: The project has a budget set at slightly above CHF 40 million. It is part-funded by the Swiss Federal Office of Energy and has received financial backing from Vaud Canton as a pilot project for generating power.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2022**: Drilling began on January 17, 2022, with the first stage involving a vertical drill down to 1,000 meters. Subsequent stages will continue drilling down to 2,500 meters, with the possibility of reaching 3,000 meters if initial flow rates and temperatures are insufficient. The project is expected to be completed in the second half of 2023.
