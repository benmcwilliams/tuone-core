### Evolutionary Timeline of the Alpiq Facility in Gondo-Zwischbergen, CHE

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 10-02-2022
   - **Details:** Alpiq announced the construction of an 18MW vertical bifacial solar plant named Gondosolar in Gondo-Zwischbergen, Switzerland. The facility will utilize 4,500 bifacial modules over a 10-hectare area, producing approximately 23.3 million kWh of electricity annually. The design allows for the generation of about twice as much electricity per square meter compared to similar facilities on the Swiss plateau, with 55% of its electricity produced in winter.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 10-02-2022
   - **Details:** The project is expected to require an investment of CHF 42 million (approximately $45.5 million). Alpiq indicated that the project’s economic viability is contingent upon the federal parliament enacting a subsidy model, with a maximum subsidy of 60% being sought.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 10-02-2022
   - **Details:** Alpiq is in the process of securing approval and funding for the Gondosolar project. Construction is anticipated to be completed within three years from the start of construction, although specific dates for construction start and completion were not provided.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 10-02-2022
   - **Details:** The article does not specify job creation or job loss associated with the project.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 10-02-2022
   - **Details:** The Gondosolar plant will be owned and operated by the local utility Electrique du Simplon (EES), with Alpiq being the largest shareholder of EES, indicating a joint ownership structure.
