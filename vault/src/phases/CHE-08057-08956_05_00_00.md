## CHE-08057-08956

### Evolutionary Timeline of GNS Gesellschaft für Nuklear-Service mbH Facility in Mühleberg, CHE

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **1972**: The Mühleberg facility, comprising a single 373 MWe boiling water reactor, began operations.
- **20 December 2019**: The facility was shut down.
- **6 January 2020**: Dismantling operations commenced.
- **15 September 2020**: The facility was considered permanently out of service when its operating licence was replaced by a decommissioning order.
- **March 2023**: Uniper Nuclear Services was contracted to dismantle, disassemble, and package the two moisture separator reheaters at Mühleberg.
- **End of 2025**: Work on the dismantling and packaging of the reactor pressure vessel (RPV) is scheduled to start, as part of the UfG consortium's responsibilities.
- **By the end of 2030**: Mühleberg is expected to be free of radioactive material, with conventional dismantling set to begin in 2031.
- **By 2027**: BKW plans to submit an application for conventional dismantling to the authorities.
- **2034**: The site is expected to be available for other uses.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- The total value of the contract for the dismantling and packaging of components has not been disclosed. However, it involves significant operations related to the decommissioning of the facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **20 March 2024**: Announcement of the contract for the removal of the Mühleberg reactor vessel by the UfG consortium.
- **Late June 2022**: BKW submitted its application to the Swiss Federal Nuclear Safety Inspectorate for the second decommissioning phase of Mühleberg.
- **September 2021**: Completion of the first decommissioning phase, which involved the removal of all 418 fuel elements, 16 months ahead of schedule.

#### 4. Labour
- Specific job creation or job loss figures related to the dismantling operations have not been disclosed in the articles. However, the dismantling and decommissioning process typically involves a workforce, which may lead to job creation in the short term.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned by BKW, which is responsible for the decommissioning process. GNS Gesellschaft für Nuklear-Service mbH is part of the UfG consortium, which includes Uniper Nuclear Services GmbH and Framatome GmbH, indicating a collaborative approach to the dismantling operations rather than exclusive ownership by GNS.
