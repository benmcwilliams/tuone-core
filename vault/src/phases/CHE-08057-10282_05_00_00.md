## CHE-08057-10282

### Evolutionary Timeline of Uniper Nuclear Services GmbH Facility in Mühleberg, CHE

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **1972**: The Mühleberg facility, comprising a single 373 MWe boiling water reactor, began operations.
- **20 December 2019**: The facility was shut down.
- **6 January 2020**: Dismantling operations commenced.
- **15 September 2020**: The facility was considered permanently out of service when its operating licence was replaced by a decommissioning order.
- **March 2023**: Uniper Nuclear Services was contracted to dismantle, disassemble, and package the two moisture separator reheaters at Mühleberg.
- **End of 2025**: Work on the dismantling and packaging of the reactor pressure vessel (RPV) is scheduled to start.
- **By the end of 2030**: Mühleberg is expected to be free of radioactive material, with conventional dismantling set to begin in 2031.
- **By 2027**: BKW plans to submit an application for conventional dismantling to the authorities.
- **2034**: The site is expected to be available for other uses.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- The value of the contract for the dismantling and packaging of components has not been disclosed. However, it is part of a broader decommissioning effort that includes multiple phases and significant operational changes.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **20 March 2024**: The contract for the removal of the Mühleberg reactor vessel was announced, involving the UfG consortium (Uniper Nuclear Services GmbH, Framatome GmbH, and GNS Gesellschaft für Nuklear-Service mbH).
- **June 2022**: BKW submitted an application to the Swiss Federal Nuclear Safety Inspectorate for the second decommissioning phase of Mühleberg.
- **September 2021**: Completion of the first decommissioning phase, which involved the removal of all 418 fuel elements, 16 months ahead of schedule.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify job creation or job loss directly related to the dismantling operations or the decommissioning phases.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned by BKW, with Uniper Nuclear Services GmbH operating as a contractor for the dismantling and decommissioning processes. There is no indication of exclusive ownership by Uniper; rather, it is part of a consortium.
