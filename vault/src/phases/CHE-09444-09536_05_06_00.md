### Evolutionary Timeline of the Nagra Facility in Würenlingen, CHE

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2022**: Nagra proposed the construction of a used fuel encapsulation plant at the existing Zwilag interim storage facility in Würenlingen, as part of a broader plan for deep geological disposal of radioactive waste. The facility will support the encapsulation of used nuclear fuel before its final disposal.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 19, 2024**: Nagra submitted applications for general authorization, which include a safety report, security report, environmental impact study, and justification for the site choice. These documents are essential for the development of the deep geological repository and the encapsulation plant.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 19, 2024**: Applications for the deep geological repository and the used fuel encapsulation plant were lodged. The Swiss Federal Nuclear Safety Inspectorate (ENSI) and the Federal Nuclear Safety Commission (CNS) will begin examining the applications, with a detailed assessment expected to be completed by 2027. The Federal Council is anticipated to decide on the application in 2029, with a national referendum planned for 2031.
