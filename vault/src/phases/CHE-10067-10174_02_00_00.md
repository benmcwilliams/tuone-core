### Evolutionary Timeline of the Swiss Heating Coop Facility in Riehen, CHE

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **1994**: The original geothermal plant began operations, supplying geothermal energy to households in Riehen and Lörrach for 26 years.
- **2020**: The facility operates with hot water at 67 degrees Celsius derived from two 1,500 meter deep wells, feeding into a 38 km district heating network that serves approximately 8,500 people. The energy output has increased from 10 GWh to 23.3 GWh over the past decade.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2020**: Swiss Heating Coop announced plans to invest up to CHF 49 million (around $51 million) for the construction of a second geothermal heating plant, "geo2riehen," to meet increasing demand for climate-neutral energy. The investment for "geo2riehen" is estimated at around CHF 14 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2018**: A grant was requested from the Swiss Federal Office of Energy (SFOE) for the project, which was examined.
- **January 2019**: A subsidy contract of CHF 1.2 million was signed for a measurement campaign following the completion of trial drilling.
- **May 2020**: The Riehen residents’ council is expected to approve the submission for the new plant in June 2020.

#### 4. Labour
- The announcement of the new geothermal plant indicates potential job creation, although specific numbers related to job creation or loss have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The ownership structure is set to change with the expansion. Currently, the municipality of Riehen holds 72.9% of the shares, while the Industrielle Werke Basel (IWB) holds 27.1%. Post-expansion, the shares will be adjusted to 50% for Riehen and 50% for IWB, indicating a shift towards a joint venture model for the facility.
