### Evolutionary Timeline of the VGTG Facility in Thurgau, Switzerland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The VGTG facility is focused on geothermal energy development in Thurgau. The TEnU 2030 project aims to enhance the understanding of geothermal potential in the region, which will eventually support the production of geothermal energy for heat or electricity. The project is structured in three phases: 
     - **Prospecting (2022-2024):** Identifying suitable areas for geothermal exploration.
     - **Exploration (2025-2028):** Determining 1 to 3 promising sites for exploratory drilling through 3D seismic campaigns.
     - **Development (2029-2031):** Collecting data for future drilling projects by drilling an exploration well and analyzing subsurface data.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The total estimated cost for the TEnU 2030 project up to the deep drilling phase is CHF 74 million. The federal government is expected to cover around 60% of this cost, with the VGTG requesting approximately CHF 30 million from the cantonal fund.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **August 2023:** The TEnU 2030 project received a favorable vote from the cantonal authorities, allowing it to proceed with its planned phases. The project has been in discussion since at least 2021, when it was initially seeking federal funding.
