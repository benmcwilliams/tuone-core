### Volkswagen Facility in Eastern Europe, CZE - Evolutionary Timeline

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2021**: Volkswagen announced plans for a new standardized battery cell to be produced at six "Gigafactories" across Europe, including one in Eastern Europe. The facility aims to produce a uniform prismatic battery cell that will be used in up to 80% of Volkswagen's electric vehicles by 2030. The production capacity for each Gigafactory is set at 40 GWh annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: Volkswagen's investment strategy includes a significant increase in battery production capacity, with a total requirement of 240 GWh of cells by 2030. The company is in discussions with the Czech government and the EU Commission regarding the establishment of the Eastern European facility, with a planned production start in 2027.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2021**: Volkswagen confirmed that the Eastern European factory is part of its broader strategy to establish six Gigafactories in Europe. The planned start of production at this facility is set for 2027, although specific location details have not yet been disclosed.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2021**: While specific job creation numbers for the Eastern European facility have not been detailed, the establishment of the Gigafactory is expected to create numerous jobs in the region, contributing to local employment and economic growth.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2021**: Volkswagen is expected to operate the Eastern European facility independently, as part of its strategy to increase its own battery production capabilities in Europe. The company has not indicated any joint venture arrangements for this specific facility.
