### Evolutionary Timeline of the Škoda Facility in Kvasiny, Czech Republic

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2017**: Škoda announced plans to produce its first all-electric model, the Citigo e iV, and the plug-in hybrid model, the Superb iV, at its facilities. The Kvasiny plant was designated for the production of the Superb iV.
- **May 2019**: Production of the Superb iV was set to begin in the autumn of 2019, with the vehicle featuring a 1.4 TSI petrol engine and an electric motor producing a combined output of 160 kW (218 PS). The electric range was estimated at up to 55 kilometers (34 miles).
- **January 2020**: Production of the Superb iV commenced at the Kvasiny plant, with the vehicle available for order in the UK starting at £31,970. The electric range was confirmed to be up to 56 kilometers (35 miles).

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2019**: As part of its electrification strategy, Škoda announced intentions to convert production in its Czech factories to green energy by the second half of the next decade. This included plans for recycling or secondary use of traction batteries.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2017**: Announcement of the production of the first all-electric model and the plug-in hybrid model.
- **Autumn 2019**: Production of the Superb iV began at the Kvasiny plant.
- **January 2020**: The Superb iV became available for order in the UK, marking a successful market launch.
