### Evolutionary Timeline of the Skoda Facility in Mlada Boleslav, Czech Republic

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Focus**: The Skoda facility in Mlada Boleslav primarily produces the Skoda Enyaq iV, an electric SUV based on the MEB platform.
- **Production Capacity**: At full capacity, the facility can produce slightly more than 300 Enyaq and Enyaq Coupé vehicles per day.
- **Updates**: In March 2022, production was halted due to supply chain issues stemming from the war in Ukraine, affecting the availability of parts, particularly wiring harnesses. Production resumed in late March but was impacted by ongoing supply chain challenges throughout 2022. By January 2023, the facility was expected to increase capacity to around 80,000 units annually with the addition of the Enyaq Coupé.

- **Investments**: Specific investment figures related to the Mlada Boleslav facility were not detailed in the articles, but the overall strategy of Skoda and Volkswagen indicates a significant push towards electrification and capacity expansion in response to high demand for electric vehicles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Production Start**: The Skoda Enyaq iV began production at the Mlada Boleslav facility prior to 2021.
- **Production Halt**: In March 2022, production was halted due to a critical shortage of parts from Ukraine, with a planned restart initially set for early April.
- **Resumption of Production**: Production resumed in late March 2022 but continued to face challenges due to supply chain disruptions.
- **Model Expansion**: In October 2022, Skoda added the RS model to the Enyaq lineup, indicating ongoing development and expansion of the product range.

#### 4. Labour

### Summary
The Skoda facility in Mlada Boleslav has been a key player in the production of electric vehicles, particularly the Skoda Enyaq iV.
