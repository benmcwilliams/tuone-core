## CZE-00736-00722

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Škoda Facility in Mladá Boleslav, Czech Republic

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2017**: Škoda announces plans to produce its first all-electric model at the Mladá Boleslav facility, with production set to begin in 2020. The facility will also supply electric components for plug-in hybrid electric vehicles (PHEVs) starting in 2019.
- **February 2019**: The facility is preparing for the launch of electric vehicles, with significant investments in electric infrastructure, including over 300 charging points.
- **September 2020**: The Enyaq iV, Škoda's first all-electric SUV based on the MEB platform, is presented. Production is set to start at the Mladá Boleslav plant.
- **November 2020**: Series production of the Enyaq iV begins, with a capacity of up to 350 units per day.
- **May 2022**: Škoda starts manufacturing MEB battery systems at the Mladá Boleslav facility, aiming for an annual production of over 250,000 battery systems.
- **March 2023**: Plans to increase battery system production capacity to 1,500 units per day are announced.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2019**: Škoda invests approximately €2 billion in electric vehicle development, with €3.4 million allocated for expanding electric infrastructure at the Mladá Boleslav facility in 2018.
- **November 2020**: The conversion of the Mladá Boleslav plant for Enyaq production costs €32 million.
- **March 2023**: Announcement of an additional assembly line for battery systems to increase production capacity.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2017**: Announcement of the first all-electric model to be produced in Mladá Boleslav.
- **February 2019**: Preparations for electric vehicle launch begin.
- **September 2020**: Presentation of the Enyaq iV.
- **November 2020**: Start of series production of the Enyaq iV.
- **May 2022**: Start of battery system production.
- **March 2023**: Announcement of increased battery production capacity.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2019**: The expansion of electric infrastructure and production capabilities is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers are not provided.
- **May 2022**: The new battery system production line is expected to employ around 250 workers.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Škoda facility in Mladá Boleslav is owned exclusively by Škoda Auto, a subsidiary of the Volkswagen Group. There is no indication of joint ventures or shared ownership in the provided articles.
