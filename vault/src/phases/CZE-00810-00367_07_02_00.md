### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hyundai Facility in Nosovice, Czech Republic

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2019**: Hyundai announces plans to produce the fully-electric Kona Electric at its Nosovice plant starting in the first half of 2020, with a production capacity of 46,000 units for the Kona Electric in the first year. The facility is set to become Hyundai's largest overseas production base for electric cars, also producing hybrid versions of the Tucson.
- **October 2020**: Production of the Kona Electric begins at the Nosovice facility, with the plant manufacturing the 150 kW version for the European market.
- **October 2023**: Hyundai places its first battery cell order with Samsung SDI for future electric vehicles, indicating a shift in its battery supply strategy while continuing to use LG Energy Solution batteries for the Kona Electric.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2019**: Hyundai announces an investment of 105.7 billion won (approximately 80 million euros) in the Nosovice facility to support the production of the Kona Electric and hybrid Tucson models.
