### Evolutionary Timeline of the ORLEN Group Facility in Prague, CZE

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2020**: The ORLEN Group announced plans to construct hydrogen refueling stations at two Czech Benzina stations, including one in Prague. These stations are part of a broader initiative to develop environmentally friendly hydrogen technology, with the first stations expected to be operational in 2021. The hydrogen will be produced from crude oil processing at UNIPETROL refineries in Litvínov and Kralupy.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2020**: ORLEN Group plans to invest over PLN 25 billion in projects aimed at reducing environmental impact and developing alternative fuels and low carbon technologies. This investment is part of their strategy to lead in hydrogen technology and new mobility solutions in Central Europe.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2020**: Announcement of the construction of hydrogen refueling stations in Prague and Litvínov, with plans for additional stations in Brno, Plzeň, and along the D10 motorway. The first hydrogen refueling points are expected to be operational in 2021.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2020**: The construction of hydrogen refueling stations is expected to create jobs, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the article.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2020**: The facility is part of the ORLEN Group, with UNIPETROL, a Czech company within the group, overseeing the construction of the refueling stations. The ownership structure indicates that the ORLEN Group has full ownership of the facility.
