### Evolutionary Timeline of the CEZ Facility in Dukovany, Czech Republic

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2022**: The Czech government announced a tender for a new nuclear unit at the Dukovany nuclear power plant to replace an existing unit, with plans for construction to start by 2036.
- **February 2023**: ČEZ announced an investment of over CZK 2.3 billion (USD 104 million) for modernizing the instrumentation and control systems at the Dukovany plant, ensuring safe operation until at least 2047. All four VVER-440 units are transitioning to 16-month fuel cycles.
- **March 2023**: ČEZ signed an agreement with Westinghouse for the supply of VVER-440 fuel assemblies, marking a shift from Russian-supplied fuel due to security concerns.
- **June 2023**: A zoning permit was issued for the new nuclear unit, marking a significant milestone in the project.
- **October 2023**: The third unit at Dukovany achieved a thermal output of 1475 MWt, equivalent to 511 MWe, reflecting a 2.3% increase in capacity without additional fuel consumption.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2022**: The tender for the new unit at Dukovany is part of a project estimated to cost €6 billion (USD 6.4 billion), representing the largest investment in modern Czech history.
- **February 2023**: The investment of CZK 2.3 billion is aimed at modernizing the plant's safety systems.
- **October 2023**: The project to increase output is expected to result in an additional 300,000 MWh of annual production starting in 2025.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2022**: The tender for a new unit was launched, with bids from Westinghouse, EDF, and KHNP.
- **June 2023**: A zoning permit was issued, allowing the project to advance to the next stage.
- **October 2023**: The third unit achieved a capacity increase, with plans to increase outputs of the first and second units later in the year.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2023**: ČEZ indicated that with the planned new build, it expects to almost double its nuclear workforce in the coming years.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: The Dukovany nuclear power plant is operated by ČEZ Group, which is a majority state-owned utility. The facility is not a joint venture; it is fully owned by ČEZ.
