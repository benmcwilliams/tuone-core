## CZE-06236-05465

### Evolutionary Timeline of Korea Hydro & Nuclear Power Facility in Dukovany, Czech Republic

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **02/2024**: Korea Hydro & Nuclear Power (KHNP) proposed its APR1000 reactor as part of a tender for a new fifth unit at the Dukovany nuclear power plant, with plans for additional units. The construction of two new units at Dukovany was confirmed later in July 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07/2024**: The estimated cost for one new unit at Dukovany was projected at 200 billion crowns (approximately $8.65 billion). The Czech government indicated that the price of electricity generated would be less than 90 euros per megawatt hour (MWh).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **02/2024**: The Czech government announced a change in the tender process to allow binding offers for multiple reactors instead of just one, with a timeline for construction start in 2029 and trial operation in 2036.
- **07/2024**: KHNP was selected as the preferred bidder to build two new units at Dukovany, with discussions for two additional units at the Temelin power station.
- **08/2024**: The contract for the two Dukovany units is expected to be finalized by March 2025, with construction anticipated to begin in 2029 and completion by 2036.

#### 4. Labour
- **08/2024**: The project is expected to create significant employment opportunities, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles. Westinghouse claimed that the project would export jobs from the US to Korea, indicating potential job losses in the US.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **07/2024**: KHNP was selected as the preferred bidder for the construction of the new units, indicating that they will have a significant role in the ownership and operation of the new reactors at Dukovany. The facility is operated by ČEZ, which is 70% state-owned, suggesting a collaborative ownership structure between KHNP and the Czech government through ČEZ.

### Summary
The Korea Hydro & Nuclear Power facility in Dukovany is undergoing significant developments with plans to construct two new nuclear reactors, with potential for further expansion. The project is backed by substantial investment estimates and is expected to create jobs, although there are ongoing legal disputes regarding technology rights that could impact the timeline and execution of the project.
