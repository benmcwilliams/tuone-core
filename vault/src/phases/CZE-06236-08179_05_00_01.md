### Evolutionary Timeline of the Westinghouse Facility in Dukovany, Czech Republic

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2022**: The Czech government announced a tender to build a new unit at the Dukovany nuclear power plant to replace an existing unit, with plans for the new unit to be operational by 2036. The current facility operates four VVER-400 reactors, each with a capacity of 510 MW, totaling 2,040 MW.
- **August 2023**: CEZ, the operator, confirmed that the new reactor would be a 1,200-MW unit, representing a significant upgrade to the facility's capacity. The project is expected to be the largest investment in the modern history of the Czech Republic, estimated at €6 billion ($6.4 billion).

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2022**: The investment for the new unit at Dukovany is part of a broader strategy to modernize the Czech nuclear power sector, with the government planning to finalize the supplier by 2024 and obtain construction permits by 2029.
- **August 2023**: The investment for the new reactor at Dukovany is confirmed to be €6 billion ($6.4 billion), marking a significant financial commitment to expanding nuclear capacity in the Czech Republic.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2022**: The Czech government launched a tender for a new unit at Dukovany, with the expectation of finalizing the supplier by 2024 and starting construction by 2029.
- **July 2023**: Westinghouse signed a contract to supply nuclear fuel for the Dukovany plant, with deliveries expected to begin in 2024.
- **February 2024**: Bids for the new fifth unit at Dukovany were submitted, with Westinghouse proposing its AP1000 reactor. The government plans to evaluate these bids and finalize contracts within the year.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2022**: The expansion project is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers were not detailed in the articles. The involvement of local Czech companies in the construction and supply chain is anticipated to further enhance job creation.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
