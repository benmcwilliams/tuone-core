### Evolutionary Timeline of the Westinghouse Electric Co. Facility in Dukovany, CZE

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - Westinghouse Electric Co. proposed to deliver the advanced AP1000 reactor for the Dukovany project, which features a modular construction design and a small footprint per megawatt electrical.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The estimated value of the project for building two nuclear reactors in Dukovany is approximately 24 trillion won ($17.3 billion).

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **August 2024:** Westinghouse filed an appeal with the Czech's antimonopoly watchdog, challenging Korea Hydro & Nuclear Power's (KHNP) selection as the preferred bidder for the Dukovany project.
   - The contract for the two Dukovany units is expected to be finalized by **March 2025**.
   - Construction is anticipated to begin in **2029**, with a completion goal set for **2036**.

4. **Labour:**
   - Westinghouse claimed that the deployment of the APR1000 reactor over the AP1000 would result in the loss of tens of thousands of clean energy jobs in both the Czech Republic and the US, including 15,000 jobs in Pennsylvania.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - Westinghouse does not own the facility exclusively; it is involved in a competitive bidding process where KHNP has been selected as the preferred bidder, despite Westinghouse's legal challenges and claims regarding technology rights.
