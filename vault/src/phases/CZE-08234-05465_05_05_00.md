### Evolutionary Timeline of the Korea Hydro & Nuclear Power Facility in Temelin, CZE

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2024**: Korea Hydro & Nuclear Power (KHNP) was selected to build two new nuclear power units at CEZ's Dukovany nuclear plant, with discussions for two additional units at the Temelin power station. The estimated cost for one new unit is approximately 200 billion crowns ($8.65 billion). The reactor model proposed is the APR1000.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2024**: The Czech government indicated that the price of electricity generated from the new units would be less than 90 euros per megawatt hour (MWh). The financing model for the first new unit at Dukovany includes low-interest loans and a contract for difference scheme to guarantee CEZ a return.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2024**: KHNP was awarded the contract over French bidder EDF, marking a significant step in the Czech Republic's nuclear expansion plans. The government aims to finalize contract details with KHNP by the first quarter of 2025.
- **August 2024**: Appeals were filed by EDF and Westinghouse regarding the tender decision, which could impact the timeline for the project. The Czech anti-monopoly office is reviewing these appeals.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2024**: The project is expected to create jobs as part of the construction and operation of the new nuclear units, although specific job creation numbers have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
