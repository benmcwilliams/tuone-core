### Evolutionary Timeline of the Rolls-Royce SMR Facility in Temelin, Czech Republic

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2024**: Rolls-Royce SMR is selected for a small modular reactor (SMR) project in the Czech Republic, specifically at a site near the existing Temelin nuclear power plant. The Rolls-Royce SMR design has a capacity of 470 MWe, which is larger than many of its SMR competitors, and is based on a small pressurized water reactor. The facility aims to provide consistent baseload generation for at least 60 years, with 90% of the SMR built in factory conditions to reduce on-site activity and project risk.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2024**: The Czech government, through ČEZ, is establishing a strategic partnership with Rolls-Royce SMR, which is expected to facilitate local companies' participation in the development and implementation of the new SMR technology. The partnership is seen as an opportunity for Czech industry to engage in the global supply chain for SMRs.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2024**: The Czech Ministry of Trade announces the selection of Rolls-Royce SMR for the SMR project, with plans for the first reactor to be operational in the 2030s, ahead of a new large nuclear unit expected before 2040. The project is still in the planning and assessment phase, with safety evaluations being conducted.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2024**: The establishment of the strategic partnership is expected to create jobs in the Czech Republic, as local companies will be involved in the development and supply chain for the SMR technology.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2024**: The facility will be a result of a strategic partnership between ČEZ and Rolls-Royce SMR, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by either party. Specific terms of the cooperation are still under negotiation.
