### Evolutionary Timeline of the Westinghouse Facility in Temelin, Czech Republic

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Production**: The Temelin Nuclear Power Station is a 2,056-MW facility with two VVER-1000 units, operational since 2000 and 2002.
- **Future Plans**: CEZ plans to add two new generators to the facility, with construction expected between 2028 and 2030. This expansion aims to modernize the plant and extend its operational life for at least another 60 years.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Amount**: The investment for the new generators at Temelin is approximately $160 million.
- **Fuel Supply Agreement**: In March 2023, CEZ signed a deal with Westinghouse to supply nuclear fuel for the Dukovany plant, with deliveries expected to begin in 2024. CEZ also selected Westinghouse and Framatome to supply fuel for the Temelin plant in 2022.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Tender Launch**: In May 2023, CEZ launched a tender for the addition of two generators at the Temelin Nuclear Power Station.
- **Contract Finalization**: CEZ aims to finalize contracts for the two-generator project by the first quarter of 2024.
- **Construction Timeline**: The construction of the new generators is scheduled to take place between 2028 and 2030.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: While specific job creation numbers related to the new generators have not been detailed, the expansion is expected to create opportunities for local suppliers and contractors, as indicated by a Westinghouse-Bechtel event aimed at engaging Czech companies.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The facility is owned by CEZ, which is the primary operator. There is no indication of joint ownership or partnerships in the context of the facility's operations, although Westinghouse is involved as a contractor for fuel supply and potential construction projects.
