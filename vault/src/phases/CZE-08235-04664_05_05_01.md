### Evolutionary Timeline of the ČEZ Facility in Temelín, Czech Republic

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2000-2002**: Two VVER-1000 units began operation at the Temelín nuclear power plant.
- **February 2023**: ČEZ announced an investment of CZK3.6 billion for modernization projects at Temelín, including extending the fuel campaign from one year to approximately 18 months, modernizing control systems, and preparing for a generator replacement.
- **May 2023**: ČEZ launched a tender for two new steam generators expected to increase output from 1125 MWe to 1150 MWe, with installation planned between 2028 and 2030.
- **September 2023**: During a shutdown, ČEZ began placing 163 fuel assemblies in the reactor, including 48 fresh assemblies, as part of the transition to an 18-month fuel cycle, aimed at increasing efficiency and reducing operational downtime.
