## CZE-08236-05929

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Rolls-Royce SMR Facility in Tušimice, Czech Republic

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2024**: Rolls-Royce SMR is selected by the Czech Republic for a small modular reactor (SMR) project. The Rolls-Royce SMR design has a capacity of 470 MWe, which is larger than many of its SMR competitors, and is based on a small pressurized water reactor. The facility aims to provide consistent baseload generation for at least 60 years, with 90% of the SMR built in factory conditions to reduce on-site activity and project risk.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2024**: The Czech government emphasizes the strategic partnership with Rolls-Royce SMR, highlighting the opportunity for Czech companies to participate in the development and implementation of the new SMR technology. This partnership is expected to support local industry and contribute to the global supply chain.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2024**: The Czech Ministry of Trade announces that ČEZ (the Czech nuclear power company) will establish a strategic partnership with Rolls-Royce SMR. The first SMR is planned to be located near the existing Temelin nuclear power plant in the 2030s, with the aim to complement large nuclear units expected to be operational by 2040. The government is conducting safety assessments of Rolls-Royce SMR as part of the selection process.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2024**: The strategic partnership is expected to create jobs in the Czech Republic, as local companies will be involved in the development and supply chain of the SMR technology.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2024**: The facility is part of a strategic partnership between ČEZ and Rolls-Royce SMR, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by either party. Specific terms of the cooperation are still under negotiation.

This timeline captures the key developments related to the Rolls-Royce SMR facility in Tušimice, focusing on technology, investment, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure as of September 2024.

### Technology and Operations
- "The Rolls-Royce SMR is a 470 MWe design based on a small pressurised water reactor." 
- "Its capacity is larger than many of its SMR rivals - the general definition of an SMR is of a reactor unit with an output of up to 300 MWe."
- "90% of the SMR - measuring about 16 metres by 4 metres - will be built in factory conditions, limiting on-site activity primarily to assembly of pre-fabricated, pre-tested, modules which significantly reduces project risk and has the potential to drastically shorten build schedules."

### Investment and Development
- "The Czech government says it would be an 'advantage ... that Rolls-Royce SMR is just forming its supply chain, and Czech companies thus have a unique opportunity to stand at its birth and participate to the maximum extent possible.'"
- "This important strategic partnership further strengthens Rolls-Royce SMR’s position as Europe’s leading SMR technology."

### Project Status and Milestones
- "The first small modular reactor (SMR) is planned by ČEZ at a site near the existing Temelin nuclear power plant in the 2030s."
- "The target date to complete that final stage is August 2026."
- "If Rolls-Royce is successful in the UK’s SMR selection contest, the aim is for a final investment decision to be taken in the UK in 2029."

### Labour
- "Rolls-Royce SMRs will be a source of clean, affordable, reliable electricity for Czechia - creating jobs, enabling decarbonisation, reducing the reliance on imported energy and supporting the global effort to reach net zero."

### Ownership Structure
- "ČEZ (the Czech nuclear power company) wants to establish a strategic partnership" with Rolls-Royce SMR, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership.
