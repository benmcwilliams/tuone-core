### Evolutionary Timeline of the Westinghouse Facility at Dukovany Power Plant II, Czech Republic

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **01-11-2023:** Westinghouse Electric, along with EDF and KHNP, submitted bids for a new nuclear unit (Dukovany Unit 5) at the Dukovany Power Plant II facility. The new unit is scheduled for trial operation in 2036. Westinghouse, in partnership with Bechtel, is proposing to supply four AP1000 reactors to the Czech Republic. Additionally, starting in 2024, Westinghouse plans to supply nuclear fuel to both the Dukovany and Temelin nuclear sites.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **01-11-2023:** The US Government's approval is being sought by Westinghouse and Bechtel for their proposal to supply the reactors. The strategic energy security perspective highlighted by the US Ambassador indicates that selecting US technology would not only provide a reliable source of clean energy but also create thousands of green jobs across the nuclear supply chain.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **01-11-2023:** The bids for the new nuclear unit were submitted, and CEZ subsidiary Elektrárna Dukovany II is set to evaluate these bids and award final contracts before November 2024. The tender process for the new nuclear unit began in March 2022.

4. **Labour:**
   - **01-11-2023:** The US Ambassador mentioned that selecting US technology would create thousands of green jobs across the nuclear supply chain, indicating a positive impact on job creation as a result of the project.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is under the ownership of CEZ, with Westinghouse participating as a bidder for the construction of the new nuclear unit. The ownership structure regarding the new unit is not explicitly stated, but it is implied that CEZ will retain ownership while Westinghouse would be a contractor if selected.
