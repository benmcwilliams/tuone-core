### Evolutionary Timeline of the S.A.G. Solarstrom Facility in Stříbro, Czech Republic

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **May 2009:** S.A.G. Solarstrom announced the construction of a 13-MWp photovoltaic power plant at a former military site in Stříbro. This facility is noted as the company's first in the Czech Republic and its largest project to date. The installation will utilize Yingli Solar multicrystalline modules and Kaco XP350-HV inverters. The expected annual power generation is approximately 13.65 million kWh, sufficient to power at least 3,100 households.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **May 2009:** The project is supported by the Czech Republic's feed-in tariff, which guarantees a rate of CZK12.79 (approximately €0.48) for each kilowatt-hour of electricity fed into the grid for a duration of 20 years. This financial structure provides a stable investment environment for the facility.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **May 2009:** The construction of the facility is scheduled to begin in July 2009, with completion expected in the fall of the same year. This timeline indicates a proactive approach to project execution.
