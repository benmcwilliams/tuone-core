### Evolutionary Timeline of the Cylib Facility in Aachen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2022**: Cylib GmbH, a start-up focused on holistic battery recycling, announces a proprietary, sustainable recycling method capable of recovering all materials from lithium-ion batteries with over 90% efficiency. The facility is planned to be set up in Aachen.
- **September 2023**: Cylib opens its pilot plant in Aachen, capable of recycling 500 kilograms of battery materials per day. This facility is the first phase of a larger implementation aimed at establishing an industrial line with a capacity of several thousand tons per year.
- **May 2024**: Cylib announces plans for a larger brownfield facility in Germany to scale up production, following the successful operation of the pilot line.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2022**: Cylib secures €3.6 million in seed funding to develop its recycling processes.
- **February 2023**: The company raises an additional €8 million, bringing total seed funding to €11.6 million, to support the establishment of the pilot factory in Aachen.
- **May 2024**: Cylib completes a Series A financing round totaling €55 million, which will be used to expand production and enhance its team. This round is noted as the largest financing round in European battery recycling to date.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2022**: Cylib is spun off from RWTH Aachen University and announces plans for a pilot factory in Aachen.
- **September 2023**: The pilot plant officially opens and becomes operational, achieving its initial recycling capacity.
- **May 2024**: Cylib announces the acquisition of a brownfield industrial plant in Germany for further expansion.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2024**: Cylib employs around 60 people, with plans for further recruitment as the company expands its operations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2022**: Cylib is established as a spin-off from RWTH Aachen University, indicating that it is not exclusively owned by any single entity but rather operates as an independent start-up with various investors involved.
