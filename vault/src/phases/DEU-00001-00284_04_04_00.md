## DEU-00001-00284

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fraunhofer Facility in Aachen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2019**: The Fraunhofer Institute for Energy Infrastructures and Geothermal Energy (IEG) is founded, integrating the International Geothermal Centre in Bochum and establishing research locations including Aachen. The facility focuses on geothermal energy, energy infrastructures, and technologies for heat, electricity, and transport sector coupling.
- **April 2021**: Fraunhofer IPT in Aachen begins developing a continuous production line for fuel cell stack production aimed at hydrogen-powered vehicles. The goal is to achieve a throughput of at least one bipolar plate per second, addressing the need for mass production facilities in Europe.
- **August 2021**: Researchers from multiple Fraunhofer institutes, including IPT in Aachen, work on reducing manufacturing costs for electrolysers by over 25%. This involves creating a digital library of manufacturing processes to optimize production costs and efficiency.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2021**: The federal government funds the electrolyser manufacturing project with €22 million through the H2Giga hydrogen project, aimed at enhancing the production processes and reducing costs.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2020**: The International Geothermal Center Bochum is integrated into the Fraunhofer-Gesellschaft as a core component of the new IEG.
- **April 2021**: The pilot line for fuel cell production is presented during the Hannover Messe Digital Edition, showcasing advancements in automation and production efficiency.
- **August 2021**: The project to reduce electrolyser manufacturing costs is officially launched, with contributions from multiple Fraunhofer institutes.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2021**: The development of the continuous production line for fuel cells is expected to create jobs related to the automation and mass production of fuel cells, although specific job creation numbers are not mentioned.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Fraunhofer facility in Aachen is part of the Fraunhofer-Gesellschaft, which is a large application-oriented research organization in Germany. The facility operates under the umbrella of this organization, indicating that it is not independently owned but rather part of a larger network of institutes.
