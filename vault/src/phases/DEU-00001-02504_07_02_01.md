## DEU-00001-02504

### Evolutionary Timeline of the e.Go Facility in Aachen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2017**: e.Go Mobile announces three variants of the e.Go Life electric vehicle with power options of 20, 40, and 60 kW, and respective battery capacities of 14.9 kWh, 17.9 kWh, and 23.9 kWh.
- **07/2018**: e.Go Mobile opens its first factory in Aachen, targeting a production capacity of 10,000 vehicles per year. The facility is designed to implement Industry 4.0 technology.
- **10/2018**: Production of the e.Go Life is delayed due to supplier issues, with initial deliveries expected to start in April 2019.
- **02/2019**: Plans for a German battery cell factory are discussed, with e.Go Mobile involved in a consortium for potential battery production.
- **12/2020**: The company rebrands as Next.e.GO Mobile and plans to resume production in early 2021 after being acquired by ND Industrial B.V.
- **06/2021**: Production of the e.Go Life resumes, with plans to produce 500 cars by the end of the year and increase to 2,500 in 2022.
- **08/2022**: Next.e.GO Mobile announces plans for a micro-factory in Northern Macedonia with a capacity of up to 30,000 vehicles per year, starting production in late 2024.
- **10/2023**: Plans for a microfactory in the USA are announced, aiming to leverage subsidies from the US Inflation Reduction Act.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07/2018**: Total investment for the Aachen facility is reported at €25.7 million, with €2.6 million from the state government of NRW.
- **01/2020**: e.Go Mobile reveals it was on the verge of insolvency but secured additional funds from investors to avoid bankruptcy.
- **09/2020**: ND Industrial acquires e.Go Mobile, providing the necessary capital to resume operations.
- **02/2021**: A Series B financing round raises over €30 million to support production start-up and growth plans.
- **08/2021**: A Series C financing round raises €48 million to expand production and develop new models.
- **07/2023**: Next.e.GO Mobile raises $75 million in fixed-rate debt financing to accelerate corporate strategy and production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **07/2018**: Factory in Aachen officially opens.
- **10/2018**: Initial production start delayed to April 2019.
- **12/2020**: Transition to Next.e.GO Mobile and plans to restart production in early 2021.
- **06/2021**: Production of the e.Go Life resumes with the launch of the special edition e.Go Life Next.
- **10/2023**: Announcement of plans for a microfactory in the USA.

#### 4. Labour
- **07/2018**: The factory is set to employ 142 workers initially, with plans to add a second shift by mid-2019 to increase production capacity.
- **01/2020**: The company faces financial difficulties, leading to potential job losses.
- **06/2021**: Employees are gradually brought out of short-time work as production resumes.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **09/2020**: e.GO Mobile is acquired by Dutch investment group ND Industrial B.V., which takes over all operating assets and employees.
- **01/2021**: The company operates under the new name Next.e.GO Mobile, indicating a shift in ownership and management structure.

This timeline captures the key developments and changes at the e.Go facility in Aachen, Germany, highlighting its technological advancements, financial investments, project milestones, labor dynamics, and ownership transitions.
