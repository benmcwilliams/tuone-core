## DEU-00037-00110

### Evolutionary Timeline of Bosch Facility in Arnstadt, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2010**: Bosch Solar Energy begins installation of a manufacturing execution system (VPC-MES) at the Arnstadt facility, aimed at increasing production capacity by approximately 400MWp. Production is scheduled to start in early summer 2010.
- **August 2010**: The Arnstadt crystalline solar cell production plant is officially opened. The facility has a production capacity of 10,000 cells per hour, equating to 90 million solar cells annually. The nominal capacity of Bosch's operations is expected to nearly triple to around 630MWp with the completion of the facility.
- **March 2014**: Bosch completes the sale of its solar cell and module production in Arnstadt to SolarWorld, which will migrate its PERC cell technology to the facility and start producing new high-efficiency PV modules.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2010**: Bosch announces an investment of around €530 million in the Arnstadt location by 2012, which includes the construction of the R&D center and a new administrative building.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2010**: Installation of the VPC-MES system begins, with production set to start in early summer 2010.
- **August 2010**: Official opening of the Arnstadt facility.
- **March 2014**: Completion of the sale of the Arnstadt facility to SolarWorld, with all regulatory approvals obtained.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2010**: The establishment of the Arnstadt facility creates a total of 1,100 jobs.
- **March 2014**: The Arnstadt complex originally employed 1,400 people. Following the sale to SolarWorld, 800 positions are transferred to the new owner, securing 1,000 jobs in total.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2014**: Bosch sells the Arnstadt facility to SolarWorld, indicating that Bosch no longer holds ownership of the facility. Prior to the sale, Bosch was the exclusive owner of the facility.
