### Evolutionary Timeline of the SolarWorld Facility in Arnstadt, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/2014**: SolarWorld officially completed the acquisition of the Arnstadt facility from Bosch, which had a production capacity of 700MW for solar cells. The facility was noted to employ 1,400 people, with 800 positions transferred to SolarWorld.
- **03/2015**: SolarWorld announced plans to add 500MW of monocrystalline ingot production and upgrade all 700MW of solar cell production to next-generation PERC technology at the Arnstadt facility. The facility was running at full capacity.
- **05/2015**: Production upgrades to PERC technology began at the Arnstadt facility, with the introduction of monocrystalline wafer production restarting.
- **03/2016**: SolarWorld reported that it had upgraded between 800MW to 1GW of conventional production lines to PERC technology.
- **02/2017**: SolarWorld planned to concentrate all German cell production at Arnstadt, while wafer production would be scaled up at Freiberg.
- **02/2017**: SolarWorld announced a shift to focus exclusively on monocrystalline PERC products, with plans to shut down multicrystalline production.
- **05/2017**: SolarWorld reported shipment growth, with a focus on monocrystalline PERC products being implemented as planned.
- **05/2017**: The facility was noted to be part of the insolvency proceedings, with production still ongoing.
- **07/2017**: The provisional insolvency administrator announced that production would continue with a reduced staff.
- **02/2019**: The insolvency administrator declared the search for an investor over, with plans to auction off the production equipment at the Arnstadt facility.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12/2014**: Bosch provided SolarWorld with a €130 million payment to facilitate the acquisition of the Arnstadt facility.
- **03/2015**: SolarWorld received funding from German agencies for R&D programs aimed at reducing costs in monocrystalline ingot production.
- **10/2016**: SolarWorld announced a mid-double-digit million investment in high-efficiency technologies and expansion plans.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2014**: Acquisition of the Arnstadt facility from Bosch completed.
- **03/2015**: Announcement of plans for 500MW ingot production and upgrades to PERC technology.
- **05/2015**: Restart of monocrystalline wafer production at Arnstadt.
- **02/2017**: Announcement of the shift to focus on monocrystalline PERC production.
- **05/2017**: SolarWorld filed for insolvency, affecting operations at the Arnstadt facility.
- **07/2017**: Production continued under provisional administration.
- **02/2019**: Announcement of plans to auction off the Arnstadt facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **12/2014**: 1,400 jobs were initially associated with the facility, with 800 transferred to SolarWorld.
- **03/2015**: The addition of 60 jobs was expected with the new ingot production.
- **09/2016**: SolarWorld announced layoffs of 200 temporary workers at the Arnstadt facility due to market conditions.
- **02/2017**: Potential job losses were indicated due to the planned relocation of production capabilities.
- **02/2019**: The facility had around 600 employees at the time of the second bankruptcy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **12/2014**: SolarWorld acquired the Arnstadt facility from Bosch, marking full ownership of the site. The facility was previously owned by Bosch, which exited the solar industry.
