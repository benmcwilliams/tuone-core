## DEU-00037-03508

### Evolutionary Timeline of Solarworld Industries GmbH Facility in Arnstadt, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **08/2017**: Solarworld Industries GmbH aims to continue cell and module production at its German facilities, including Arnstadt. The facility is involved in the production of photovoltaic cells and modules.
- **05/2018**: Cell production in Arnstadt is set to halt due to low raw material availability. The facility is idled while negotiations for potential investors are ongoing.
- **09/2018**: Production in Arnstadt has been discontinued since June 2018, with equipment being auctioned off. The facility is no longer operational for cell production.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **08/2017**: An agreement is reached with an investor group, including the Qatar Foundation, to take over Solarworld Industries GmbH, which includes the Arnstadt facility. The purchase involves the transfer of property, plant, equipment, and certain receivables.
- **05/2018**: The insolvency administrator is open to the idea of establishing a "research PV factory" in Arnstadt, indicating potential future investment in R&D rather than production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **08/2017**: An agreement is signed to continue production at the Arnstadt facility, pending creditor approval.
- **05/2018**: Cell production in Arnstadt is officially halted as part of the insolvency proceedings.
- **09/2018**: Module production has also been suspended, and the facility is in the process of being sold, with no binding offers yet received.

#### 4. Labour
- **08/2017**: The agreement to continue operations includes the maintenance of around 180 jobs at the Arnstadt site.
- **05/2018**: Approximately 200 employees are still on payroll until the end of September, but the facility's closure leads to job uncertainty.
- **09/2018**: The workforce is significantly reduced, with only a small team remaining to manage the shutdown process and hazardous materials.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **08/2017**: Solarworld Industries GmbH is taken over by a newly registered company, with Frank Asbeck as CEO, in collaboration with the Qatar Foundation. This indicates a joint ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by Solarworld.
