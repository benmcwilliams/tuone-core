### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fortum Facility in Artern, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2022**: Fortum Battery Recycling announces plans to build a new recycling hub in Artern, Thuringia, focusing on the mechanical processing of used batteries and production waste to produce 'black mass'. This process is similar to that used in Fortum’s first German recycling plant in Kirchardt, Baden-Württemberg, which began commercial operations in April 2023.
- **September 2023**: Fortum is developing a pilot plant for new methods to produce cathode and anode materials, including lithium titanium oxide (LTO). However, details about the location and operational timeline of this pilot plant are not disclosed.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2022**: Fortum plans investments in the mid double-digit million range for the Artern facility, although specific figures are not provided.
- **September 2023**: Fortum receives a €4.5 million grant from Business Finland for expanding mechanical processing capacity at its facility in Ikaalinen, Finland, which indirectly supports the Artern facility by ensuring a steady supply of 'black mass'.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2022**: Fortum announces its plans for the Artern facility, initiating discussions with local authorities about a development plan.
- **September 2022**: A public hearing regarding the facility is postponed due to nearly 1,500 objections from local residents, which delays the authorization procedure.
- **April 2023**: Commercial operations begin at Fortum's first German recycling plant in Kirchardt, which is relevant to the operations planned for Artern.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2022**: Fortum anticipates creating around 70 jobs at the Artern facility, which is supported by local political figures, including the Mayor of Artern.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2022**: The articles do not specify the ownership structure of the Artern facility, but it is implied that Fortum Battery Recycling is the primary entity behind the project, suggesting full ownership unless stated otherwise in future developments.
