### Evolutionary Timeline of the Quantron AG Facility in Augsburg, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **08/2021**: Quantron AG announces the launch of a twelve-metre-long low-floor bus as a Battery Electric Vehicle (BEV) and plans to introduce a Fuel Cell Electric Vehicle (FCEV) version in spring 2022. The BEV version will have a maximum power of 250 kW, a torque of 3,400 Nm, and a maximum battery capacity of 422 kWh, with a range of 160 to 280 kilometers depending on battery configuration. The bus can carry up to 95 passengers.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **08/2021**: Quantron AG enters a strategic partnership with Ev Dynamics, which involves a share swap. This partnership aims to jointly bring BEV and FCEV vehicles to the market, with Quantron responsible for product development and Ev Dynamics for production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **08/2021**: Announcement of the cooperation with Ballard Power Systems to develop fuel-cell powered trucks, with the first delivery of Quantron fuel cell trucks in Germany scheduled for the second half of 2022. The partnership focuses on integrating Ballard’s FCMove fuel cell modules into Quantron’s electric vehicles.

#### 4. Labour
- **08/2021**: The partnership with Ev Dynamics and the development of new vehicles are expected to create job opportunities, although specific job creation numbers are not mentioned in the article.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **08/2021**: Quantron AG is involved in a joint venture with Ev Dynamics, indicating that it does not exclusively own the facility but collaborates with another entity for production and development of vehicles.
