## DEU-00057-00284

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fraunhofer Facility in Baden-Württemberg, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2023**: The Fraunhofer Institute for Solar Energy Systems (Fraunhofer ISE) reports the operation of an agrivoltaic system at the Heuchlingen experimental fruit farm in Bad Friedrichshall, with a production capacity of **113 kW**. The facility integrates solar power generation with the cultivation of various soft fruits (raspberries, blueberries, currants, strawberries, gooseberries, and blackberries) over an area of nearly **2,000 square meters**. The system employs fully closed circuits for water and nutrients, utilizing collected rainwater and excess liquid from the substrate for irrigation.
  
- **April 2024**: A new pilot project is initiated at a quartz sand excavation site, featuring a **135 kW** grid-connected overhead solar PV system designed to provide shading for young fir trees. This system is part of a land rehabilitation initiative and is designed to be removable and reusable.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2024**: The project for the overhead solar PV system at the quartz sand excavation site is financed by Emil Steidle GmbH, with additional support from the Ministry of Food, Rural Affairs, and Consumer Affairs of the Baden-Württemberg state.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2023**: The third LVWO pilot plant under the Model Region Agri-photovoltaics for Baden-Württemberg research project is officially operational.
  
- **April 2024**: The installation of the overhead solar PV system for young fir trees is reported, marking the beginning of a new phase in agrivoltaic research.
