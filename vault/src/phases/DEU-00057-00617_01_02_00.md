### Evolutionary Timeline of the Porsche Facility in Baden-Württemberg, Germany

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** July 2021
   - **Details:** Porsche, through its joint venture Cellforce Group with Custom Cells, is developing high-performance battery cells for electric vehicles (EVs) at a production plant in Tübingen, Germany. The facility is expected to begin operations in 2024 with an initial production capacity of 100 MWh/year, sufficient to power 1,000 motorsport and high-performance EVs. The facility will focus on high-energy density nickel-cobalt-manganese (NCM) cathode materials.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** July 2021
   - **Details:** BASF is providing high-energy density NCM cathode materials for the battery production plant. The partnership emphasizes sustainable access to battery materials and aims to reduce the CO2 footprint of the cathode materials by up to 60% through recycling processes.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** July 2021
   - **Details:** The announcement of the partnership between BASF and Porsche regarding the development of the battery production plant was made. The plant is set to begin operations in 2024.

4. **Labour:**

   - **Details:** While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned in the article, the establishment of the battery production plant is expected to create jobs related to the development and production of high-performance battery cells.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** July 2021
   - **Details:** The facility is part of a joint venture between Porsche and Custom Cells, indicating that Porsche does not own the facility exclusively but shares ownership with its partner.
