### Evolutionary Timeline of the ZSW Facility in Baden-Württemberg, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/2009**: The Centre for Solar Energy and Hydrogen Research (ZSW) achieved a European record with a 19.6% efficiency for copper-indium-gallium-diselenide (CIS) thin film solar cells under pilot production conditions. This was accomplished using an inline multistage process in an automated coating plant, marking a significant step towards cost reduction and efficiency improvement in solar cell production.

- **11/2022**: ZSW developed a tandem solar module combining perovskite and CIS technology, achieving an efficiency of 21.1%. This module, measuring 9 square centimeters, is characterized by an industrially suitable and scalable architecture, indicating advancements in the technology and potential for larger applications.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07/2022**: TubeSolar AG, in collaboration with ZSW, aimed to commercialize a flexible photovoltaic film based on perovskite, achieving a power conversion efficiency of 14%. The partnership focused on optimizing the production process and expanding TubeSolar's production capacity to 250 MW, indicating significant investment in the development of new solar technologies.
