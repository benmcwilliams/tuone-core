### Evolutionary Timeline of the Statkraft Facility in Bavaria, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2020**: The Schornhof solar park, located in Bavaria, is under construction with a total planned capacity of 110 MW. This includes a 50 MW power purchase agreement (PPA) with Statkraft and an additional 30 MW awarded a feed-in premium tariff. The facility is noted to be the largest unsubsidized solar power project in Germany.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2020**: Umweltbank provided a €55 million loan for the Schornhof solar park, which is part of a €60 million project. This investment is significant as it represents the bank's largest investment to date in PPA-backed renewable energy projects.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2020**: Statkraft signed a PPA for 50 MW of the solar park's capacity with developer Anumar. Additionally, a short-term deal for a 30 MW solar power plant was secured in German PV tenders.
- **August 2020**: The Schornhof solar park is confirmed to be under construction and is expected to be completed before April 2021.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the Schornhof solar park.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Statkraft is involved in the Schornhof solar park through a power purchase agreement but does not appear to have exclusive ownership of the facility. The project is developed by Anumar, indicating a collaborative approach rather than full ownership by Statkraft.
