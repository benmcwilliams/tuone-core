### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mercedes-Benz Facility in Berlin, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2017**: Mercedes-Benz announces plans to accelerate its electric vehicle (EV) rollout, targeting 10 EV models by 2022. This includes expanding its electric lineup under the EQ label.
- **July 2018**: The eCitaro electric bus is unveiled, with plans for improved battery technology. The facility in Berlin is set to assemble electric drive modules for future EQ brand vehicles starting from the beginning of the next decade.
- **January 2022**: Mercedes-Benz announces plans to produce electric drives in-house starting in 2024, with the Berlin facility designated to build high-performance electric motors for the new AMG.EA platform.
- **June 2022**: The Berlin plant is confirmed to expand its production portfolio to include the manufacture and assembly of high-performance electric motors from Yasa, a British specialist.
- **March 2023**: The Berlin facility is highlighted as a key site for producing electric motors for the future AMG.EA platform, as part of a broader strategy to increase vertical integration in EV production.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2017**: Daimler announces a €10 billion investment to expand its electric vehicle lineup.
- **June 2022**: Mercedes-Benz announces a €2 billion investment in its European assembly plants, including the Berlin facility, to prepare for the shift to electric vehicles by the end of the decade.
- **December 2022**: Mercedes-Benz commits to a mid-single-digit billion euro investment in its European powertrain production sites, including the Berlin plant, to support the production of electric drive systems.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2017**: Announcement of the electric vehicle strategy and investment.
- **July 2018**: Unveiling of the eCitaro electric bus, with the Berlin facility set to play a role in its production.
- **January 2022**: Announcement of in-house production of electric drives starting in 2024.
- **June 2022**: Confirmation of the Berlin plant's role in producing high-performance electric motors.
- **March 2023**: Further confirmation of the Berlin facility's involvement in the production of electric motors for the AMG.EA platform.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2022**: The shift to in-house production of electric drives is expected to impact job roles, with potential job cuts in the company’s own plants due to previous outsourcing of electric drive systems. However, the exact number of jobs created or lost has not been specified.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility in Berlin is owned exclusively by Mercedes-Benz (Daimler AG), which manages its operations as part of its broader strategy for electric vehicle production and development.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic decisions related to the Mercedes-Benz facility in Berlin, focusing on its evolution towards electric vehicle production and the associated investments and operational changes.
