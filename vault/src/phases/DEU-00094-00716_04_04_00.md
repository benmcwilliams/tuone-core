## DEU-00094-00716

### Evolutionary Timeline of Siemens Facility in Berlin, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **01/2022**: Siemens Energy announced plans to start producing Proton Exchange Membrane (PEM) hydrogen electrolyzers at its facility in Huttenstrasse, Berlin, with production scheduled to begin in 2023. The facility will utilize an existing production hall currently manufacturing gas turbines, with new production lines set up on 2,000 square meters at a cost of around €30 million.
- **06/2022**: Siemens Energy and Air Liquide formed a joint venture to mass-produce hydrogen electrolysers in Berlin, aiming for an output of 3 GW by 2025. The facility will produce electrolysis modules based on PEM technology, which is suitable for harvesting renewable energy.
- **11/2023**: The joint venture between Siemens Energy and Air Liquide inaugurated their gigawatt electrolyzer factory in Berlin, with plans to ramp up production from 1 GW to 3 GW annually by 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **01/2022**: Siemens Energy announced a €30 million investment to set up production lines for PEM electrolysers in Berlin.
- **06/2022**: The joint venture with Air Liquide aims to secure funding under the EU’s Innovation Fund and other initiatives to support large-scale hydrogen projects.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **01/2022**: Announcement of the start of production for PEM hydrogen electrolyzers in Berlin, with a target for 2023.
- **06/2022**: Formation of a joint venture with Air Liquide to mass-produce hydrogen electrolysers, with production expected to begin in the second half of 2023.
- **11/2023**: The factory officially inaugurated, with industrial series production of stacks set to begin in November 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **06/2022**: The establishment of the joint venture and the factory is expected to create jobs in the Berlin area, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **06/2022**: Siemens Energy holds a 74.9% stake in the joint venture with Air Liquide, which holds the remaining 25.1%. This indicates a joint ownership structure for the facility focused on hydrogen electrolyzer production.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Siemens facility in Berlin, focusing on its transition towards hydrogen production and the establishment of a joint venture with Air Liquide.
