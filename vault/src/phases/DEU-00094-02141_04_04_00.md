## DEU-00094-02141

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Air Liquide Facility in Berlin, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2022**: Air Liquide and Siemens Energy announced a joint venture to mass-produce hydrogen electrolysers at a facility in Berlin. The factory is set to produce electrolysis modules using proton exchange membrane (PEM) technology, which is designed to efficiently harness renewable energy. The production capacity is expected to reach **3GW by 2025**.
- **November 2023**: The gigawatt electrolyzer factory in Berlin was inaugurated, with plans to increase production from **1GW to 3GW annually by 2025**. The facility spans over **2,000 square meters** and employs automation and robotics for the production of PEM electrolyzer modules.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2022**: The joint venture aims to apply for funding under the EU’s Innovation Fund, Green Deal, and Important Project of Common European Interest (IPCEI) scheme for hydrogen, which indicates a significant investment strategy to support the facility's development and operations.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2022**: Announcement of the joint venture between Siemens Energy (74.9% ownership) and Air Liquide (25.1% ownership) to establish the Berlin facility.
- **July 2022**: Confirmation of the facility's production start in the **second half of 2023** with a ramp-up to 3GW capacity by 2025.
- **November 2023**: Inauguration of the gigawatt electrolyzer factory in Berlin, marking a significant milestone in the project timeline.

#### 4. Labour

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is a **joint venture** between Siemens Energy (74.9% ownership) and Air Liquide (25.1% ownership), indicating that Air Liquide does not own the facility exclusively but shares ownership with Siemens Energy.
