### Evolutionary Timeline of the Oxford Photovoltaics Facility in Berlin, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2023**: Oxford PV announced a global record for efficiency of commercially available solar cells, achieving 28.6% energy conversion from sunlight. This technology utilizes perovskite-on-silicon solar cells, which are designed to be more efficient than traditional silicon cells (22-24% efficiency).
- **June 2023**: The company confirmed the development of a new commercial-scale solar cell with a record-breaking efficiency of 28.6%, surpassing their previous record of 26.8%. The technology combines a thin layer of perovskite with conventional silicon, allowing for at least 20% more electricity generation compared to silicon-only cells. The facility near Berlin focuses on scaling up production and improving manufacturing processes for these commercial-sized devices.

#### 2. Investment and Development

The company is looking for favorable conditions to establish a full-scale factory, which suggests potential future investments in the Berlin facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2023**: Oxford PV announced its intention to ramp up production and improve commercial-sized solar cells at its pilot facility near Berlin. The company is also exploring options for establishing a full-scale factory in either Germany or the US.
- **June 2023**: The facility's pilot operations are confirmed to be focused on scaling up production of the newly developed solar cells, with plans for future expansions.

#### 4. Labour
