### Evolutionary Timeline of the Sulfurcell Facility in Berlin, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **11/2008**: Sulfurcell announces plans to expand its manufacturing capabilities with the construction of a new plant in Berlin for the production of CIS/CIGS thin-film solar modules. The plant is set to have an annual production capacity of 75MW, with an intermediate expansion to 35MW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **11/2008**: The expansion is supported by a successful equity funding round of €85 million, led by Intel Capital, which contributed €24 million. Other investors include Climate Change Capital Private Equity, AIG Investments, Zouk Ventures, BankInvest Group, and Conetwork.
- **01/2011**: Sulfurcell secures an additional €18.8 million in equity funding aimed at achieving 14 percent efficiency for its thin-film photovoltaics modules. This funding will be used for acquiring additional CIGSe equipment and ongoing R&D.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **11/2008**: Announcement of the new plant construction in Berlin.
- **01/2011**: Sulfurcell announces that it has achieved 12.6 percent thin-film efficiency and aims to reach 14 percent within the next 12 to 18 months.

#### 4. Labour
- **01/2011**: The funding and technological advancements are expected to contribute to job creation as the company ramps up its production capabilities and fully automated manufacturing facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify the ownership structure of the facility, but it is implied that Sulfurcell is the primary operator, with significant backing from investors, including Intel Capital and long-term investors like Vattenfall Europe and GdF Suez.
