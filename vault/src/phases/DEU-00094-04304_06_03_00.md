### Evolutionary Timeline of the Technische Universität Berlin Facility in Berlin, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date: 02-05-2024**  
  The facility is involved in the development of water-saving technologies for PERC (passivated emitter and rear) silicon solar cell manufacturing. Researchers from the Technische Universität Berlin, in collaboration with Fraunhofer ISE and RENA Technologies GmbH, developed models that illustrate the potential for significant water savings (up to 79%) and wastewater discharge reductions (up to 84%) in a 5 GW solar cell factory. The facility's production characteristics include a throughput of 2819 m² of cells per hour, with products having a thickness of 160 µm, an area of 244.32 cm², a power output of 5.37 W, and a cell power conversion efficiency of 22%.

### Technology and Operations
- "Researchers from Germany's Fraunhofer Institute for Solar Energy Systems (Fraunhofer ISE) developed models to assess and compare two circular water strategies in a 5 GW passivated emitter and rear (PERC) solar cell factory."
- "The team compared two freshwater and wastewater flow scenarios with a water strategy at a conventional PERC cell manufacturing plant."
- "The first scenario, known as low contaminated wastewater (LCR), reclaims rinsing waters so that they can be indirectly discharged in line with regulations."
- "The second scenario, known as minimal liquid discharge (MLD), reduces the wastewater amount leaving the factory as much as possible, while identifying and retrieving possible valuables from the wastewater."

### Investment and Development
- "The researchers provided a capital expenditure (CAPEX) and operating expenditures (OPEX) for each of the two circular water strategies."
- "They noted that MLD enables freshwater reduction of 79%, and wastewater reduction of 84%, resulting in annual savings in freshwater and wastewater costs, as well as the opportunity to recover 'valuable by-products' for use in other industries."

### Project Status and Milestones
- "The novel water circulation concept was presented in the study 'Circular water strategies in solar cells manufacturing,' published in Solar Energy."
- "Looking ahead, the researchers see adoption in tunnel oxide passivated contact (TOPCON) and heterojunction (HJT) factories as 'many of the wastewater streams analyzed are similar.'"
