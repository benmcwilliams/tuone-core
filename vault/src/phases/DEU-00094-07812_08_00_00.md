### Evolutionary Timeline of the GE Grid Solutions Facility in Berlin, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2023**: GE Grid Solutions' facility in Berlin is designated to lead overall project management for multiple high voltage direct current (HVDC) projects awarded by TenneT, a Dutch-German grid operator. The facility will not produce equipment but will oversee the management of projects that include the manufacturing of primary HVDC sub-systems and equipment at GE's Stafford facility in the UK.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: GE, as part of a consortium with McDermott, is involved in contracts totaling approximately €10bn for HVDC projects in Germany. TenneT plans to award two additional HVDC contracts to this consortium in April 2023, valued at around €4bn.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2023**: TenneT awards a total of 22GW of offshore grid contracts, with GE being part of the consortium responsible for several projects. Preparatory work for the projects is set to begin immediately, with expected delivery between 2029 and 2031.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2023**: The contracts associated with the projects are expected to create over 200 new positions across the UK, France, and Germany, including roles in the supply chain.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2023**: The facility in Berlin is part of a consortium with McDermott, indicating a joint venture structure for the projects being managed, rather than exclusive ownership by GE.
