## DEU-00094-08827

### Evolutionary Timeline of Enovos Renewables O & M GmbH Facility in Berlin, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2011**: The facility, a 5 MW ground-mounted solar plant, was commissioned. It features modules oriented south at a 30-degree angle, a central inverter, and string-level monitoring. The system is designed to generate approximately 5 million kilowatt-hours per year, feeding into the public grid.
- **2017**: Monitoring systems reported a 4% reduction in yield due to potential failures in bypass diodes across multiple modules. The performance ratio was lower than the same month in the previous year, indicating operational challenges.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2017**: The facility receives a feed-in tariff (FIT) of €0.2111/kWh. The estimated annual revenue from optimal operation is around €1,055,500, with a revenue shortfall of over €42,000 due to the yield reduction.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **08-11-2017**: Articles published discussing the troubleshooting of bypass diode failures, highlighting the operational challenges faced by the facility. The investigation into the yield reduction led to the decision to conduct thermographic examinations using drones to identify faulty modules.

#### 4. Labour
- **2017**: The operational challenges and troubleshooting efforts may have implications for job roles within the facility, particularly for technicians involved in the maintenance and monitoring of the solar plant.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Enovos Renewables O & M GmbH (Erom) operates as a manufacturer-independent service provider for technical operations management. It is the O & M department of the former Q-Cells and has taken over O & M contracts from Solon. The facility is managed by its own staff in Berlin and the metropolitan area of Brandenburg, indicating a full ownership structure.

### Summary
The Enovos Renewables O & M GmbH facility in Berlin has evolved since its commissioning in 2011, facing operational challenges related to bypass diode failures that have impacted its yield and revenue. The facility operates under a full ownership structure, with a focus on maintaining and troubleshooting its solar technology to ensure optimal performance.
