## DEU-00095-00521

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mercedes-Benz Facility in Berlin-Marienfelde, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04/2021**: Mercedes-Benz announces the transformation of the Berlin-Marienfelde plant into a center of excellence for digitalization and the production of electric vehicle (EV) components. The facility will focus on the final assembly of electric components for future compact EQ models and the EE compartment for integrating power electronics for battery systems, with production expected to start before the end of the year. The plant will also expand its electric product portfolio to include the assembly of high-performance electric motors from Yasa, a British specialist acquired by Daimler.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04/2021**: Mercedes-Benz is investing a double-digit million sum in the transformation of the Berlin plant. This investment is part of a broader strategy to shift from conventional engine production to electric mobility and digitalization.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04/2021**: Announcement of the transformation of the Berlin facility to focus on EV components and digitalization.
- **03/2021**: Start of battery production for the EQS model at the Untertürkheim site, with the EE compartment being assembled at the Berlin-Marienfelde plant.

#### 4. Labour
- **04/2021**: The transformation will lead to job cuts as conventional engine production is phased out. The company is preparing the workforce with qualification measures, but specific numbers of job losses have not been disclosed. The plant currently employs around 2,500 people.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **04/2021**: The facility is owned exclusively by Mercedes-Benz AG, as there is no mention of joint ventures or partnerships in the articles.
