## DEU-00107-00010

### Evolutionary Timeline of the AMG Lithium Facility in Bitterfeld, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2021**: AMG Lithium GmbH announced plans to build a lithium hydroxide plant in Bitterfeld-Wolfen, with an initial production capacity of 20,000 tonnes per year, expandable to 100,000 tonnes in the future. The plant is intended to supply the European battery industry with battery-grade lithium hydroxide.
- **September 2024**: The facility officially opened, producing up to 20,000 tonnes of lithium hydroxide annually, sufficient for approximately 500,000 electric vehicle batteries. The first module of the plant is sold out, and plans are in place to expand production to 100,000 tonnes by 2030.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2021**: AMG Lithium announced an investment of approximately 120 million euros for the construction of the facility.
- **September 2024**: The investment for the factory was updated to about 140 million euros, with an additional 5.5 million euros from regional economic development programs.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: Announcement of the construction of the lithium hydroxide plant, with operations expected to start in 2023.
- **May 2023**: AMG Lithium confirmed the ongoing construction of the plant, scheduled to begin operations in autumn 2023.
- **September 2024**: The facility officially opened, marking the launch of Europe’s first lithium refinery.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2021**: The construction of the facility is expected to create 80 jobs.
- **September 2024**: The opening of the plant confirmed the creation of 80 jobs, as initially projected.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2021**: AMG Lithium GmbH is a subsidiary of the Dutch Advanced Metallurgical Group (AMG), indicating that the facility is not owned exclusively but is part of a larger corporate structure. 

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the AMG Lithium facility in Bitterfeld, Germany, focusing on its technology, investments, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
