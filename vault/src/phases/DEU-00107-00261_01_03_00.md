## DEU-00107-00261

### Evolutionary Timeline of Farasis Energy Facility in Bitterfeld, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2019**: Farasis Energy announces plans to build an electric car battery production plant in Bitterfeld-Wolfen, Saxony-Anhalt, with an initial production capacity of 6 GWh per year, expandable to 10 GWh by the end of 2022. The facility will produce battery cells, modules, and packs.
- **03/2021**: Reports indicate that the start of battery cell production has been delayed from April 2022 to October 2024 due to prioritization of existing factories in China.
- **11/2021**: The planned battery cell factory is reported to be uncertain, with potential cancellation of the project as Farasis evaluates its European localization strategy.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2019**: Farasis Energy announces an investment of over 600 million euros for the new plant in Bitterfeld-Wolfen.
- **03/2020**: Mercedes-Benz acquires a 3% stake in Farasis Energy, providing financial support and planning security for the Bitterfeld plant's capacity expansion.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2019**: Announcement of the facility's establishment and investment.
- **03/2020**: Strategic partnership with Mercedes-Benz solidified, with the plant designated to supply battery cells primarily for Mercedes plants.
- **02/2021**: Reports of potential issues with the partnership and delays in construction due to lack of building permits and quality concerns regarding cell samples.
- **08/2021**: Confirmation of further delays, with production now expected to start in October 2024.
- **01/2022**: Reports indicate that the construction of the facility may not proceed as planned, with the city of Bitterfeld-Wolfen terminating the urban development contract with Farasis.

#### 4. Labour
- **09/2019**: The facility is expected to create at least 600 new jobs upon completion.
- **03/2020**: Job creation estimates increase to up to 2,000 new jobs due to the partnership with Mercedes-Benz.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **09/2019**: Farasis Energy Europe is established as a subsidiary of Farasis Energy, indicating that the facility is under the ownership of Farasis Energy, with a strategic partnership involving Mercedes-Benz, which holds a minority stake (3%).

### Summary
The Farasis Energy facility in Bitterfeld, DEU, was announced in September 2019 with significant investment and job creation potential. However, the project has faced multiple delays and uncertainties regarding its construction and operational timeline, with the latest reports indicating a possible cancellation of the project. The facility was intended to be a key supplier for Mercedes-Benz, which has a financial stake in Farasis, but recent developments have raised questions about the future of the facility.
