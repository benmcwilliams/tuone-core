## DEU-00107-03196

### Evolutionary Timeline of Meyer Burger Facility in Bitterfeld, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10/2020**: Meyer Burger announces plans to use former SolarWorld and Sovello facilities for heterojunction solar cell and module production. The Bitterfeld facility will focus on heterojunction solar cells with an initial capacity of 400 MW, while the Freiberg facility will produce modules with a similar capacity.
- **03/2021**: Production at the Bitterfeld facility is scheduled to start in May 2021, with plans to ramp up to 1.4 GW by the end of 2022.
- **06/2021**: Meyer Burger aims to expand production capacity in Bitterfeld to 1.4 GW and Freiberg to 1 GW by the end of 2022.
- **09/2021**: The Bitterfeld facility is reported to produce 321 MW of solar cells, with plans to increase capacity to 1.4 GW by the end of 2023.
- **10/2023**: The facility is producing 1 million solar cells daily, with a claim of yielding 20% more energy than competitors.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10/2020**: Meyer Burger announces a capital increase of CHF 165 million to support the establishment of its production facilities.
- **06/2021**: The company secures €185 million for expanding its production capacity, including €125 million from a consortium of banks.
- **03/2023**: Meyer Burger applies for EU Innovation Fund support to aid its expansion into gigawatt-scale production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **10/2020**: Meyer Burger confirms the acquisition and rental of facilities in Bitterfeld and Freiberg, with production expected to start in the first half of 2021.
- **05/2021**: Production begins at the Bitterfeld facility.
- **12/2021**: Meyer Burger announces plans to establish a new 400 MW module manufacturing facility in the U.S., while continuing to ramp up production in Germany.
- **03/2023**: The facility meets its production volume target of 321 MW for the year.

#### 4. Labour
- **03/2021**: Meyer Burger is looking to hire over 300 new employees for its Bitterfeld and Freiberg facilities, while offering transfers to some employees from its Hohenstein-Ernstthal site.
- **10/2023**: The Bitterfeld facility employs around 50 workers overseeing automated production.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **10/2020**: Meyer Burger is establishing itself as a dedicated manufacturer of solar cells and modules, indicating full ownership of the Bitterfeld facility as part of its strategic shift from equipment supplier to manufacturer.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones of the Meyer Burger facility in Bitterfeld, Germany, focusing on its technology, investments, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
