## DEU-00107-03238

### Evolutionary Timeline of the NexWafe Facility in Bitterfeld, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2018**: NexWafe announces plans for a 250 MW kerfless wafer factory in Bitterfeld, Germany. The facility will produce epitaxially grown, kerfless silicon wafers, which are expected to significantly reduce the costs associated with solar module production.
- **October 2020**: Plans are revealed to expand the facility to a 400 MW production capacity, with future aspirations to scale up to 3 GW.
- **April 2022**: NexWafe is set to begin construction on a pilot facility in Bitterfeld, aiming for operations to start in 2023 and ramping up to around 500 MW of production by 2024.
- **May 2023**: NexWafe secures €30 million to accelerate the construction of its first commercial-scale wafer plant in Bitterfeld, with plans to utilize its EpiNex process, which simplifies polysilicon production and reduces energy consumption.
- **December 2024**: NexWafe announces plans to commission its epitaxial facility in Bitterfeld by June 2025, with a focus on achieving high efficiency and low carbon emissions in wafer production.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2018**: NexWafe secures €9.5 million in financing, including €1.5 million from Chemiepark Bitterfeld-Wolfen GmbH, to support the establishment of the facility.
- **October 2021**: NexWafe completes a €10 million capital raise, followed by a €39 million Series C funding round in 2022, with significant investments from Reliance Industries and other investors.
- **May 2023**: The company secures an additional €30 million to further accelerate the construction of the wafer plant.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2018**: Announcement of the facility's establishment.
- **2019**: Production begins at the facility, although initial production was delayed due to supplier issues and the COVID-19 pandemic.
- **2023**: Construction of the pilot facility is scheduled to begin, with operations expected to start in 2024.
- **June 2025**: Planned commissioning of the epitaxial facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2018**: The establishment of the facility is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers have not been disclosed in the articles.
- **April 2022**: The pilot facility is anticipated to create additional employment opportunities as production ramps up.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2018**: NexWafe is identified as a spin-off from Fraunhofer ISE, indicating that it operates independently but may have ties to the research institute.
- **April 2022**: NexWafe is planning to license its technology to strategic partners globally, suggesting a model that may involve collaborations but retains NexWafe's ownership of the facility.
