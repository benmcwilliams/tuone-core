## DEU-00107-09692

### Evolutionary Timeline of the PV Crystalox Solar Facility in Bitterfeld, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2011**: PV Crystalox Solar reported a nameplate capacity of 535MW for wafer production and announced plans to expand capacity to 750MW by early 2012. The polysilicon production plant was operating at a nameplate capacity of 1,800MT.
- **October 2011**: The company temporarily suspended polysilicon production at the Bitterfeld facility due to significant reductions in wafer shipments and ongoing price declines.
- **March 2012**: The company reported a total shipment volume of 384MW for 2011, slightly up from 378MW in 2010. However, it faced significant financial challenges, including a €27.9 million impairment charge for the polysilicon facility.
- **July 2013**: PV Crystalox sold its polysilicon production operations in Bitterfeld to a management buyout team, which renamed the facility Silicon Products Research Engineering Production.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2013**: PV Crystalox contributed €12.3 million to the management buyout of the polysilicon production operations, which was deemed a cheaper solution than dismantling the plant.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2011**: PV Crystalox announced an expansion of wafer production capacity to 750MW by early 2012.
- **October 2011**: The company suspended polysilicon production at the Bitterfeld facility.
- **March 2012**: The company reported a significant impairment charge related to the polysilicon facility.
- **July 2013**: The management buyout of the polysilicon production operations was completed.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2011**: The suspension of polysilicon production led to short-time working for employees at the Bitterfeld facility, although specific job loss numbers were not disclosed.
- **July 2013**: The management buyout allowed for some jobs to be maintained at the Bitterfeld facility under the new management.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2013**: PV Crystalox Solar sold its polysilicon production operations in Bitterfeld to a management team, indicating a shift from full ownership to a new ownership structure under Silicon Products Research Engineering Production.
