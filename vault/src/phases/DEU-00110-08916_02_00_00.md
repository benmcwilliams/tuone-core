## DEU-00110-08916

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fraunhofer Institute for Energy Infrastructures and Geothermal Energy Facility in Bochum, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2023**: The facility is involved in a geothermal heating project that aims to utilize warm water from an abandoned coal mine. The project includes two wells: one drilled to 340 meters and another to 820 meters. The geothermal plant is designed to supply 75% of the heating requirements for a new commercial area and can also provide cooling during summer.
- **January 2024**: The facility continues to develop the geothermal project, with the deeper well extracting water at temperatures around 27-28 °C, which will be heated to 48 °C for the heating network. The shallower well taps into a reservoir at 16 °C for summer cooling.
- **December 2024**: A pilot project named "MissElly" is implemented to provide industrial cooling using seasonal subsurface storage. This project aims to reduce reliance on conventional refrigeration technology by utilizing geothermal probes for cooling in an industrial setting.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2024**: The "MissElly" project is funded by the state of North Rhine-Westphalia through the European Regional Development Fund (ERDF). This funding supports the feasibility phase and the implementation of the geothermal probe cold storage system.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2022**: The first well is drilled to a depth of 340 meters, followed by a second well drilled to 820 meters.
- **March 2023**: Pumping tests are conducted to assess the geothermal resource's temperature and flow rate.
- **January 2024**: Positive results from drilling and well testing are reported, allowing the project to proceed with plans for heating and cooling supply.
- **December 2024**: The MissElly project is launched, marking the implementation of a geothermal probe cold storage system for industrial cooling.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is a collaboration between the Fraunhofer Institute for Energy Infrastructures and Geothermal Energy (Fraunhofer IEG) and local utility Stadtwerke Bochum, indicating a joint venture structure for the geothermal project.
