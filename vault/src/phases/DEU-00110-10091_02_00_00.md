## DEU-00110-10091

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Stadtwerke Bochum Facility in Bochum, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2022**: The first geothermal well was drilled to a depth of 340 meters, and a second well was drilled to 820 meters to tap into warm water from an abandoned coal mine.
- **Early 2023**: Pumping tests were conducted on the wells, extracting water at temperatures of 27 to 28 °C from the deeper well and 16 °C from the shallower well. The project aims to supply 70% to 75% of the heating and cooling needs for the connected buildings at the MARK 51°7 site.
- **June 2023**: The project was reported to be the first major industrial zone with an integrated heating and cooling system using geothermal energy, district networks, underground storage, and large heat pumps.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2023**: Fraunhofer IEG received over EUR 27 million in funding from federal and state governments for five years, with EUR 18 million allocated for investments in real estate and initial equipment for the research laboratory.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2023**: Announcement of pumping tests for the geothermal heating project.
- **June 2023**: Plans for the expansion of the research facility were announced due to the doubling of the workforce at Fraunhofer IEG from 40 to 80 employees.
- **January 2024**: Positive results from drilling and well testing were confirmed, with plans to feed the extracted water into a heat pump to increase its temperature for the heating network.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2023**: The workforce of Fraunhofer IEG doubled from 40 to 80 employees, indicating job creation as a result of the project’s success and expansion plans.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is a collaboration between Stadtwerke Bochum and Fraunhofer IEG, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by Stadtwerke Bochum.
