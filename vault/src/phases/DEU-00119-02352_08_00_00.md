### Evolutionary Timeline of the MHI Vestas Facility in Brandenburg, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2016**: Vestas is contracted to supply nine V112 3.45MW turbines for the Odervorland wind farm, with a total capacity of 31MW. The deal includes installation, commissioning, and a 20-year service agreement. Turbine delivery and commissioning are scheduled to start in Q4 2016.
- **September 2018**: Vestas announces the delivery of V150 4.2MW turbines to the Lubbenow 2 & 3 projects in Brandenburg, marking the first delivery of this model to the German market.
- **December 2019**: Vestas introduces the next-generation EnVentus wind turbine nacelles, with plans to deploy the V150-5.6MW at the Beesenberg II project in Brandenburg.
- **June 2020**: Vestas secures a 43MW turbine order for the Gunthersdorf wind project, supplying eleven V136-3.45MW turbines and one V126-3.45MW turbine, all optimized for 3.6MW power mode.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2016**: The partnership with MLK Group for the Odervorland wind farm indicates a significant investment in repowering efforts, although specific financial figures are not disclosed.
- **June 2020**: The Gunthersdorf wind project reflects ongoing investment in local wind energy infrastructure, with a focus on optimizing turbine technology for local conditions.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2016**: Announcement of the contract for the Odervorland wind farm.
- **Q4 2016**: Planned start for turbine delivery and commissioning for the Odervorland project.
- **September 2018**: Vestas begins delivery of V150 4.2MW turbines for the Lubbenow 2 & 3 projects.
- **December 2019**: Vestas announces the deployment of the V150-5.6MW at the Beesenberg II project.
- **June 2020**: Vestas receives a 43MW order for the Gunthersdorf wind project, with deliveries scheduled to start in Q2 2021.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2020**: The Gunthersdorf project is expected to contribute to local job creation through the delivery of turbines from the nearby Lauchhammer factory, enhancing local economic benefits.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2016**: The Odervorland wind farm is primarily owned by MLK Group, with Vestas acting as the turbine supplier and service partner.
- **June 2020**: The Gunthersdorf project is developed by Notus Energy Plan, indicating a collaborative ownership structure with Vestas as the turbine supplier.
