### Evolutionary Timeline of the Meyer Burger Facility in Brandenburg, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2019**: Meyer Burger signs a collaboration agreement with Oxford PV to expedite the mass production of perovskite-on-silicon heterojunction (HJT) tandem solar cells. Meyer Burger will provide a 200 MW HJT manufacturing line for Oxford PV's facility in Brandenburg, expected to be operational by the end of 2020.
- **July 2021**: Oxford PV completes the construction of its new manufacturing facility in Brandenburg, which is set to begin full production in 2022. This facility will house the first volume manufacturing line of perovskite-on-silicon tandem solar cells.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2019**: Oxford PV is awarded a €2.8 million grant from the German Ministry of Economic Affairs and Energy to prepare perovskite-silicon solar cells for high-volume manufacturing.
- **March 2019**: As part of the collaboration, Meyer Burger acquires a stake of up to 18.8% in Oxford PV, with an option to increase this stake to 31.6% by the end of 2020.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2019**: The collaboration agreement is signed, aiming for a 27% efficiency target for the initial phase of the project.
- **July 2021**: Oxford PV announces the completion of its manufacturing facility in Brandenburg and the termination of its exclusive partnership with Meyer Burger.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2021**: The completion of the facility is expected to lead to job creation as Oxford PV prepares to bring its perovskite technology to market, although specific job numbers are not mentioned.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2019**: Meyer Burger becomes the largest shareholder of Oxford PV with an initial stake of 18.8%, which later increases to 19.76% following the termination of the partnership in July 2021. Despite the end of the exclusive cooperation, Meyer Burger retains a significant ownership interest in Oxford PV.
