## DEU-00119-03269

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Oxford Photovoltaics Facility in Brandenburg, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **11/2016**: Oxford Photovoltaics (Oxford PV) acquires the former Bosch Solar CIS thin-film production site in Brandenburg an der Havel, Germany. The facility is intended to scale up perovskite solar technology to industry-standard wafer size and optimize manufacturing processes for commercial deployment.
- **12/2016**: Oxford PV begins developing a pilot line for perovskite-silicon tandem solar cells at the Brandenburg facility, aiming to bring the technology closer to commercialization.
- **07/2017**: The facility is operational, focusing on the production of perovskite tandem solar cells, which feature a perovskite layer on top of a standard crystalline silicon cell.
- **03/2019**: Oxford PV partners with Meyer Burger to establish a 200 MW heterojunction line for the production of tandem solar cells at the Brandenburg facility.
- **12/2020**: Oxford PV achieves a new world record efficiency of 29.52% for its perovskite-silicon tandem cells produced at the Brandenburg facility.
- **07/2021**: The facility completes the fit-out of its 100 MW tandem cell line, with plans to begin commercial production in early 2022.
- **05/2023**: Oxford PV sets a new efficiency record of 28.6% for a full-size tandem cell produced at the Brandenburg facility.
- **09/2024**: Oxford PV announces the commercial launch of its perovskite tandem modules, achieving an efficiency of 24.5% and delivering the first commercial modules to US customers.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **11/2016**: Oxford PV raises £8.7 million (US$11 million) in Series C funding to support the development of its pilot line in Brandenburg.
- **12/2017**: The European Investment Bank (EIB) provides €15 million in financing to Oxford PV to support the commercialization of its tandem solar cell production.
- **03/2019**: Oxford PV raises £31 million (US$41 million) in a Series D funding round, which includes investment from Meyer Burger.
- **07/2019**: Oxford PV secures an additional £34 million in funding for the 200 MW heterojunction solar cell line from Meyer Burger.
- **07/2021**: Oxford PV completes the build-out of its 100 MW facility, with plans for further expansion to gigawatt-scale production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **11/2016**: Acquisition of the Brandenburg facility is announced.
- **12/2016**: Development of the pilot line begins.
- **03/2019**: Collaboration with Meyer Burger is established to enhance production capabilities.
- **12/2020**: Record efficiency of 29.52% achieved.
- **07/2021**: Completion of the 100 MW facility fit-out.
- **05/2023**: New efficiency record of 28.6% set for full-size tandem cells.
- **09/2024**: First commercial distribution of perovskite tandem modules begins.

#### 4. Labour
- **11/2016**: Recruitment of staff begins to support operations at the Brandenburg facility.
- **07/2021**: Oxford PV announces plans to hire shift workers for the production line as it prepares for commercial operations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **03/2019**: Meyer Burger acquires an 18.8% stake in Oxford PV as part of their collaboration agreement. This partnership is significant as it provides both investment and technological support for the development of perovskite tandem solar cells.
- **07/2021**: Oxford PV ends its exclusive partnership with Meyer Burger, although Meyer Burger remains the largest single shareholder with a 19.76% stake.
