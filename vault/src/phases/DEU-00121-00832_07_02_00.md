## DEU-00121-00832

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Volkswagen Facility in Braunschweig, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04/2018**: Volkswagen announces plans to construct a second production building to manufacture EV battery systems, aiming to increase output fivefold by 2020. The facility will primarily supply battery systems for VW's I.D. family based on the MEB platform.
- **12/2018**: The Braunschweig plant is designated as the competency center for battery systems in Europe, with a target production of 500,000 battery systems per year.
- **01/2019**: The facility begins producing the first chemical storage units for the ID. family, with deliveries expected by the end of 2019.
- **11/2019**: The plant ramps up production to support the ID.3 and other MEB models, with a focus on battery system assembly.
- **04/2021**: Volkswagen expands battery system production, increasing capacity to 600,000 battery systems per year, including 300,000 for plug-in hybrid vehicles by 2023.
- **07/2022**: Plans are announced to further expand production to 800,000 battery systems annually by 2026, with an investment of around €1 billion.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05/2018**: Volkswagen announces a total investment of €1.3 billion across three sites, including Braunschweig, for e-mobility initiatives.
- **11/2020**: Over €300 million is invested in expanding the production facility for battery systems.
- **07/2022**: An additional investment of around €1 billion is planned for the transformation of the Braunschweig site to support future electric vehicle production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04/2018**: Construction of a second production building begins.
- **12/2018**: The facility is officially recognized as the competency center for battery systems in Europe.
- **01/2019**: Production of chemical storage units for the ID. family starts.
- **11/2019**: The facility begins ramping up production for the ID.3.
- **04/2021**: The second production line for battery systems is launched, increasing capacity to 600,000 units per year.
- **07/2022**: Plans for further expansion to 800,000 battery systems per year are announced.

#### 4. Labour
- **01/2019**: Approximately 300 jobs are created at the Braunschweig facility as production ramps up for battery systems.
- **07/2022**: The expansion plans are expected to secure jobs and enhance the workforce at the Braunschweig site.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Volkswagen facility in Braunschweig is fully owned by Volkswagen Group Components, which has been restructured to focus on battery production and e-mobility initiatives. The facility operates under the umbrella of Volkswagen, with no joint ventures reported.

This timeline captures the significant developments and strategic decisions made regarding the Volkswagen facility in Braunschweig, highlighting its evolution as a key player in the company's electric vehicle strategy.
