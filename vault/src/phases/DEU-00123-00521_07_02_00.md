### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mercedes-Benz Facility in Bremen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2016**: Mercedes-Benz announces plans to produce the EQ electric vehicle in Bremen by the end of the decade, alongside the fuel cell GLC.
- **January 2018**: The EQC, an all-electric model, is set to roll off the production lines in Bremen starting in 2019.
- **February 2018**: Preparations for the EQC production intensify at the Bremen plant.
- **May 2019**: Production of the EQC begins at the Bremen facility, with plans to produce 100 units per day, ramping up to 200 units per day in the following year.
- **February 2020**: The Bremen plant is confirmed to continue producing the EQC and will also manufacture a new electric model, details of which are yet to be disclosed.
- **December 2020**: The Bremen plant is designated to produce the EQE, an electric adaptation of the E-Class, starting in the second half of 2021.
- **March 2022**: Production of the EQE is confirmed to begin in Bremen later in the year.
- **October 2022**: The facility is expected to produce the electric GLC, which will replace the EQC, with production anticipated to start in 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2018**: Daimler earmarks €10 billion for its electrification strategy, with a portion allocated to the Bremen facility for the EQC production.
- **February 2019**: Mercedes-Benz announces investments to enhance battery production capabilities at the Bremen plant.
- **June 2022**: Mercedes announces a €2 billion investment in European assembly plants, including Bremen, to prepare for the shift to electric vehicles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2018**: The EQC is officially presented, with production slated to begin in Bremen.
- **May 2019**: Production and sales of the EQC commence at the Bremen plant.
- **February 2020**: Confirmation that the Bremen plant will continue producing the EQC and a new electric model.
- **March 2022**: Production of the EQE is set to begin in Bremen.
- **October 2022**: Plans for the Bremen plant to produce the electric GLC are announced, with production expected to start in 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2018**: The announcement of the EQC production at Bremen is expected to create additional jobs, although specific numbers are not disclosed.
- **May 2019**: The ramp-up of EQC production to 200 units per day is anticipated to lead to increased employment at the Bremen facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: The facility in Bremen is fully owned by Daimler AG, with no joint ventures reported for this specific location.
