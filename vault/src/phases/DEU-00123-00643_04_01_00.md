### Evolutionary Timeline of the RWE Facility in Bremen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2022**: RWE and ArcelorMittal signed a memorandum of understanding to develop, build, and operate offshore wind farms and hydrogen facilities. The initial focus is on producing green hydrogen to support low-emissions steel production at ArcelorMittal's sites, including Bremen. A 70 MW pilot plant is planned for construction by 2026, with intentions to scale up to gigawatt-scale projects in the long term, contingent on public funding.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2022**: The partnership between RWE and ArcelorMittal aims to secure public funding for the development of the hydrogen facilities and offshore wind farms. The success of these projects is tied to the regulatory framework, particularly the amendment of the “Wind Energy at Sea Act” (WindSeeG), which is crucial for establishing a competitive cost structure in the offshore wind sector.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2022**: Announcement of the partnership between RWE and ArcelorMittal to develop offshore wind and hydrogen facilities. The project includes plans for a 70 MW pilot plant in Bremen and Eisenhüttenstadt by 2026, with future expansions to gigawatt-scale projects depending on funding approvals.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2022**: The partnership is expected to create jobs related to the construction and operation of the offshore wind farms and hydrogen facilities, although specific job creation numbers have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2022**: The facility is part of a joint venture between RWE and ArcelorMittal, indicating that RWE does not own the facility exclusively but is collaborating with ArcelorMittal for its development and operation.
