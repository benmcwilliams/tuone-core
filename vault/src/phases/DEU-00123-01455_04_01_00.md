### Evolutionary Timeline of the ArcelorMittal Facility in Bremen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2021**: ArcelorMittal Bremen is involved in the Clean Hydrogen Coastline project, which aims to integrate hydrogen into the energy network. The facility plans to build an iron ore reduction plant with an electric arc furnace by 2026, targeting a production capacity of 1.5 million tons of crude steel with significantly lower CO2 emissions.
- **June 2022**: ArcelorMittal announces a partnership with RWE to develop offshore wind farms and hydrogen facilities to supply renewable energy and green hydrogen for low-emissions steel production. They plan to start with a 70 MW pilot plant by 2026, with intentions to scale up to gigawatt-scale projects.
- **January 2024**: ArcelorMittal Bremen and EWE begin working on a 10 MW hydrogen production plant, expected to be operational within the year, to use hydrogen instead of coal as a reducing agent in steel production.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: The Clean Hydrogen Coastline project involves a total investment of up to €1.3 billion, with a goal of integrating 400 MW of electrolysis capacity by 2024.
- **June 2022**: The partnership with RWE focuses on developing renewable energy sources and green hydrogen, essential for the decarbonization of ArcelorMittal's production sites in Germany.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2021**: Announcement of the Clean Hydrogen Coastline project, with plans for hydrogen integration by 2026.
- **June 2022**: Signing of a memorandum of understanding with RWE to develop offshore wind and hydrogen facilities.
- **April 2023**: Initiation of a 10 MW hydrogen production plant project in collaboration with EWE and SWB.
- **January 2024**: The 10 MW hydrogen production plant is expected to go into operation within the year.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2021**: The transformation process at ArcelorMittal Bremen is expected to create jobs related to the new hydrogen production and steelmaking technologies, although specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure

The facility is not mentioned as a joint venture; thus, it is assumed to be under full ownership by ArcelorMittal, although it collaborates with other companies like EWE and RWE for specific projects.
