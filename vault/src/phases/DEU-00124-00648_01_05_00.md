### Evolutionary Timeline of Redwood Materials Facility in Bremerhaven, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2018**: Redux Recycling GmbH, the facility in Bremerhaven, opened with a processing capacity of **10,000 tons per year** for lithium-ion batteries. The facility is equipped to recycle various battery types, including electric vehicle (EV) batteries, e-bike batteries, stationary storage systems, and consumer electronics.
- **September 2023**: Redwood Materials acquired Redux Recycling, enhancing its operational capabilities in Europe. The facility utilizes a multi-stage process for recycling, achieving recovery rates of over **95%** for metals and critical raw materials like nickel, cobalt, and lithium.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2018**: The Redux Recycling facility was established with an investment of approximately **3 million euros**.
- **September 2023**: No specific financial details were disclosed regarding the acquisition by Redwood Materials, but the acquisition is part of Redwood's broader strategy to expand its operations in Europe and increase battery recycling capacity.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2022**: Redwood Materials announced its plans for expansion into Europe, indicating intentions to establish at least two large-scale factories for battery recycling and sustainable production of battery materials.
- **September 2023**: Redwood Materials completed the acquisition of Redux Recycling GmbH, marking a significant milestone in its European expansion efforts.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2023**: The acquisition of Redux Recycling brought approximately **70 employees** into Redwood's European team, including chemical engineers, metallurgists, and material scientists.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2023**: Redwood Materials acquired Redux Recycling GmbH, indicating **full ownership** of the facility in Bremerhaven, which was previously owned by the Austrian Saubermacher Group. This acquisition allows Redwood to establish a strong presence in the European battery recycling market.
