## DEU-00124-01259

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Redux Recycling Facility in Bremerhaven, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2018**: Redux Recycling, in collaboration with Saubermacher, installed a battery recycling plant in Bremerhaven. The facility is capable of processing all types of lithium-ion batteries with a production capacity of **10,000 tonnes per year**. The recycling process includes discharge, disassembly, thermal treatment, and mechanical processing, achieving a recycling rate of **60% to 70%**, with a long-term goal of **100%**. The facility recovers secondary raw materials such as stainless steel, aluminum, copper, plastic, and active materials.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2018**: The total investment in the plant was **3 million euros** by Redux Recycling and Saubermacher. This investment was aimed at developing a multi-stage recycling process to achieve higher recycling rates.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2018**: The facility officially opened after six years of development and two years of construction.
- **September 2023**: Redwood Materials announced the complete acquisition of Redux Recycling, which includes the Bremerhaven facility. This acquisition is part of Redwood's strategy to expand its operations in Europe.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2023**: Following the acquisition by Redwood Materials, the facility employs **70 workers**, including chemical engineers, metallurgists, and materials scientists, who are now part of Redwood’s European team.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2018**: Initially, the facility was a joint venture between Redux Recycling and Saubermacher Group.
- **September 2023**: The facility is now fully owned by **Redwood Materials**, following its acquisition of Redux Recycling from Saubermacher Group.
