### Evolutionary Timeline of Siemens Gamesa Facility in Bremerhaven, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2021**: Siemens Gamesa commissioned the hydrogen-ready service operation vessel (SOV) named REM Energy. The vessel is designed to operate emission-free using fuel cells and battery technology. It is capable of running on green hydrogen or batteries with offshore charging, although it will initially be powered by efficient diesel generators until a green hydrogen economy is established in Germany. The REM Energy is almost 90 meters long and 20 meters wide, serving as a workplace and living quarters for up to 75 offshore service technicians and 24 crew members.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2021**: The commissioning of the REM Energy SOV represents a significant investment in sustainable maritime operations for servicing offshore wind farms, specifically Global Tech 1 and Trianel Windpark Borkum 1. The vessel is equipped for future upgrades, including a 12MW battery to reduce energy consumption.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 3, 2021**: The REM Energy was delivered from the Norwegian shipyard Green Yard Kleven.
- **December 16, 2021**: Siemens Gamesa officially commissioned the REM Energy, marking a milestone in their commitment to enhancing service operations for offshore wind farms.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2021**: The REM Energy will accommodate up to 75 offshore service technicians and 24 crew members, indicating job creation associated with the operation of the vessel.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
