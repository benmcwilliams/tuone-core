### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fraunhofer Institute for Wind Energy Systems Facility in Bremerhaven, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **08/2018**: Construction began on a new testing facility at the Fraunhofer Institute for Wind Energy Systems (IWES) in Bremerhaven. The facility will feature a high-speed generator and converter system designed to certify wind turbine systems of up to 6-7 MW with two to three-stage gearboxes. The facility aims to provide a quicker and lower-cost method for turbine certification compared to traditional nacelle testing. The facility is set to open in **spring 2019**.
  
- **09/2020**: The IWES announced an expansion of the turbine test facility, which will enable grid compatibility verification of generators and converters under laboratory conditions. This expansion includes a new test bench and a future hall for testing multi-megawatt wind turbine systems. The new testing method, part of the Hil-GridCoP project, allows for electrical certification in a laboratory setting, independent of wind conditions.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2020**: The IWES is investing €30 million in the expansion of the testing facility. The Hil-GridCoP project is funded by the Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs and Energy (BMWi) with €8.9 million. Additional funding for construction and infrastructure connections comes from the IWES's own funds and the Fraunhofer Society. A further project, Mobil-Grid-CoP, is expected to receive €12.7 million from BMWi for developing the world’s largest grid simulator.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **08/2018**: Announcement of the construction start for the new testing facility.
- **Spring 2019**: Expected opening of the initial testing facility.
- **09/2020**: Announcement of the expansion of the turbine test facility and the initiation of the Hil-GridCoP project.
