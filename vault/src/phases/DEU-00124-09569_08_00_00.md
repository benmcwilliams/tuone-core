### Evolutionary Timeline of the Nordex Energy GmbH Facility in Bremerhaven, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2020**: The Fraunhofer Institute for Wind Energy Systems (IWES) announced the expansion of a testing facility for wind turbine generators and converters in Bremerhaven. This expansion includes a new test bench aimed at verifying grid compatibility for multi-megawatt wind turbines. The facility will enable more comprehensive and future-proof testing options, reducing the time and costs associated with verification tests.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2020**: The Hil-GridCoP project, which is part of the facility's expansion, is funded by the Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs and Energy (BMWi) with a total of €8.9 million. Additional funding comes from the Fraunhofer IWES’s own resources and the Fraunhofer society. Future projects at the site, including the development of the world’s largest grid simulator, are also supported by BMWi funding of €12.7 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2020**: The announcement of the new test bench and the Hil-GridCoP project marks a significant milestone in the facility's evolution, focusing on the electrical certification of wind turbines in a laboratory setting rather than in the field.
