#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2020**: The Fraunhofer Institute for Wind Energy Systems (IWES) announced the expansion of a testing facility for wind turbine generators and converters in Bremerhaven. This expansion includes a new test bench as part of the Hil-GridCoP project, which focuses on verifying grid compatibility in laboratory conditions. The facility aims to enhance testing capabilities for multi-megawatt wind turbines, allowing for more comprehensive and future-proof testing options.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2020**: The total investment for the Hil-GridCoP project is €30 million, with €8.9 million funded by the Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs and Energy (BMWi). The funding supports the construction of new infrastructure and connections to the existing Dynamic Nacelle Testing Laboratory (DyNaLab). Additionally, a separate project, Mobil-Grid-CoP, is set to receive €12.7 million from BMWi for developing the world’s largest grid simulator.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2020**: The announcement of the new test bench and the expansion of the testing facility marks a significant milestone in the project. The new infrastructure is expected to facilitate shorter testing periods and innovation cycles for wind energy technology.
