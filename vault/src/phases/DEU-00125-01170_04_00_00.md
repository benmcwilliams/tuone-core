### Evolutionary Timeline of the Linde Facility in Bremervörde, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2022**: Linde opened one of the world’s largest hydrogen refueling stations for trains in Bremervörde. This facility is designed to support 14 Alstom hydrogen-powered trains operating in Lower Saxony. The station has a storage capacity of 1,800 kilograms of hydrogen, distributed over 64 high-pressure storage tanks, and is equipped with six hydrogen compressors supplying two dispensers for refueling.
- **August 2022**: The hydrogen filling station became operational as part of the world’s first network with hydrogen fuel cell trains in passenger service. The trains have a range of 1,000 kilometers on a single tank of hydrogen, allowing them to operate without refueling during the day.
