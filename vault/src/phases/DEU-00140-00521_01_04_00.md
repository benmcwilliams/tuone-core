## DEU-00140-00521

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mercedes-Benz Facility in Brühl, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **06/2019**: Construction began on a new battery sub-plant at the Brühl site, part of Mercedes-Benz's global battery production network. The facility will assemble batteries for future electric vehicles of the EQ family on a 12,000 square meter area.
- **12/2020**: Mercedes announced that battery systems for the EQS and EQE will be assembled in Brühl starting in 2022. The facility will focus on assembling battery systems from purchased cells, not manufacturing cells.
- **03/2021**: The Brühl facility is confirmed to be part of the broader Mercedes-Benz Drive Systems Campus, which will focus on electric drive and battery technology development.
- **12/2022**: Mercedes confirmed that the Brühl facility will produce battery systems for new models of the MMA and MB.EA platforms starting in 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **06/2019**: Daimler announced an investment of over one billion euros in its battery production network, which includes the Brühl facility.
- **03/2021**: Daimler indicated a mid-single-digit billion euro investment in its European powertrain production sites, including Brühl, as part of its strategy to focus on electric vehicles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **06/2019**: Groundbreaking for the battery sub-plant in Brühl.
- **12/2020**: Announcement of the start of battery system production in Brühl for the EQS and EQE in 2022.
- **03/2021**: Confirmation of Brühl's role in the Mercedes-Benz Drive Systems Campus.
- **12/2022**: Announcement of the production of battery systems for the MMA and MB.EA platforms starting in 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **06/2019**: The establishment of the battery factory is part of a job security agreement that aims to retrain employees from gasoline and diesel engine production for new electric powertrains, ensuring job security through 2029.
- **03/2021**: The transition to electric drive systems and battery assembly may lead to a gradual phasing out of conventional engine production, impacting employment profiles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Brühl facility is owned exclusively by Mercedes-Benz, as part of the larger Untertürkheim plant complex. The facility is integrated into the company's broader strategy for electric vehicle production and battery assembly.
