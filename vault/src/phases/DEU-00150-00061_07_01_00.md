## DEU-00150-00061

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Audi Facility in Böllinger Höfe, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/2019**: Audi announces plans for a fully electric successor to the R8 sports car, named RS e-Tron, to be assembled at the Böllinger Höfe factory starting in 2023 or 2024. The facility is currently converting production for the e-tron GT, which is set to begin assembly at the end of 2020. The RS e-Tron will feature four electric motors with a total output of around 700 kW and a 95-kWh solid-state battery.
- **04/2024**: Audi plans to establish a production facility for battery modules at Böllinger Höfe, aiming to manufacture up to 1,000 battery modules per day for its future Scalable Systems Platform (SSP). This move is part of Audi's strategy to increase vertical integration in battery production.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10/2022**: Audi announces a shift in production strategy, reducing the volume of the combustion engine A8 and increasing production of the e-tron GT. This involves reallocating personnel from A8 production to the e-tron GT assembly at Böllinger Höfe, indicating a strategic investment in electric vehicle production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2019**: Announcement of the RS e-Tron project, with production slated to begin in 2023 or 2024.
- **10/2022**: Confirmation of increased production capacity for the e-tron GT starting in January 2023, with over 15,000 units produced since its launch.
- **04/2024**: Confirmation of plans to set up battery module production at Böllinger Höfe, enhancing the facility's role in Audi's electrification strategy.

#### 4. Labour
- **10/2022**: Job reallocations occur as Audi cuts production of the A8 and shifts personnel to the e-tron GT production line at Böllinger Höfe. This indicates a focus on maintaining employment levels despite the transition from combustion engine vehicles to electric vehicles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify any joint ventures or shared ownership; it can be inferred that Audi operates the Böllinger Höfe facility under its full ownership as part of its broader manufacturing strategy.
