### Evolutionary Timeline of Heckert Solar Facility in Chemnitz, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Production Capacity**: As of May 2020, Heckert Solar has an annual production capacity of around **400 MW** at its facility in Chemnitz, Germany.
- **New Product Development**: In November 2023, Heckert Solar, in collaboration with Fraunhofer ISE, developed a new **400 W rooftop solar panel** featuring M12 half cells and a **20.5% efficiency** rating. A new production line was established at the Chemnitz facility specifically for this module series.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Announcement**: In August 2022, Heckert Solar announced a significant investment of **€2 billion ($2.2 billion)** in PV production in Germany, in collaboration with Interfloat and Wattkraft.
- **Funding for New Product Development**: The development of the new 400 W module was funded by Germany's Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs and Climate Protection under the “Komoger” research project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Production Start**: Production at the Chemnitz facility has been ongoing, with the new 400 W module entering series production at the beginning of 2023.
- **Expansion**: In May 2020, it was announced that Heckert Solar would begin production at a new factory in Langenwetzendorf, Germany, with production set to start in early 2021.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The establishment of the new production line for the 400 W module and the expansion in Langenwetzendorf are expected to create new jobs, although specific numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: Heckert Solar is owned by the Trinkerl family, indicating that the facility is under **full ownership** by the company.
