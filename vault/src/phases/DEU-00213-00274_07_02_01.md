### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ford Facility in Cologne, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04/2019**: Initial discussions about establishing a battery cell production facility at Ford's Cologne site, with plans for a production capacity of 1 gigawatt initially, scaling up to 8 gigawatts.
- **10/2020**: Ford announces plans to build its first purely electric small car at the Fiesta plant in Cologne, transitioning to electric vehicle (EV) production.
- **02/2021**: Ford confirms that it will manufacture its first electric model in Cologne based on Volkswagen’s Modular Electric Drive (MEB) platform, with production expected to start in 2023.
- **06/2023**: Ford opens the Electric Vehicle Center in Cologne, with an investment of $2 billion, enabling an annual production capacity of 250,000 electric vehicles, starting with the all-electric Ford Explorer.
- **06/2024**: Series production of the Ford Explorer begins in Cologne, with plans for a second electric model to follow.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **06/2020**: Ford announces a $1 billion investment to prepare the Cologne plant for electric vehicle production.
- **02/2021**: Ford plans to invest around $2 billion in the Cologne facility for the production of electric vehicles.
- **06/2023**: The Electric Vehicle Center is officially opened after a $2 billion investment, marking a significant upgrade to the facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **06/2023**: The Electric Vehicle Center is inaugurated, with the first electric model, the Ford Explorer, set to roll off the production line.
- **06/2024**: The first production model of the Ford Explorer is completed, marking the start of electric vehicle production at the facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **10/2020**: Ford announces plans to create around 3,300 jobs at the Cologne facility as it transitions to electric vehicle production.
- **06/2023**: The opening of the Electric Vehicle Center is expected to secure long-term employment for workers as the facility shifts focus to electric vehicles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **04/2021**: Ford operates the Cologne facility as a wholly-owned subsidiary, with no indication of joint ventures or partnerships affecting ownership at this site.
