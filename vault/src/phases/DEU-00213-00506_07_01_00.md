## DEU-00213-00506

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Marelli Facility in Cologne, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2020**: Marelli announces plans to establish a production facility for electric vehicle drives in Cologne, Germany, with a production line set to occupy 18,000 square meters. The facility aims to support the growing market for electric mobility.
- **March 2021**: Marelli collaborates with Fraunhofer IZM to develop a power module for electrified traction applications in motorsport, indicating a focus on high-performance electric drives. The technology developed may also be applicable to road vehicles in the future.
- **May 2022**: Marelli introduces a Wireless Distributed Battery Management System (wBMS) aimed at enhancing battery management technologies for electric vehicles, further diversifying its product offerings.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2021**: Marelli Electric Powertrain Strasbourg, a joint venture between Marelli and Punch, is established, indicating ongoing investment in electric powertrain technologies. The joint venture received its first series order for electric axles in December 2021.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2020**: Announcement of the new production facility for electric drives in Cologne, with operations expected to start in the first half of 2021.
- **March 2021**: Confirmation that the production line for electric drives is being installed in the Cologne facility.
- **May 2021**: The facility is operational, with production of electric drives commencing as planned.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2020**: At the start of production, Marelli plans to employ around 160 people, with an additional 60 workers to be hired within the year, totaling 220 jobs created as a result of the facility's establishment.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Marelli operates the facility in Cologne as a wholly-owned subsidiary, following its formation from the merger of Magneti-Marelli and Calsonic Kansei in 2019. There is no indication of joint ownership or partnerships regarding the facility itself, aside from the joint venture established for electric powertrain technologies.
