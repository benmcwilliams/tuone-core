### Evolutionary Timeline of the Shell Facility in Cologne, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2019**: Construction began on the world's largest hydrogen plant at Shell's Rheinland Refinery, utilizing advanced polymer electrolyte membrane (PEM) technology. The plant is designed to produce up to 1,300 tons of hydrogen per year at peak rates. The completion of the plant was expected in the second half of 2020.
- **July 2021**: The facility was activated as Europe’s largest PEM green hydrogen electrolyser, capable of producing up to 1,300 tonnes of green hydrogen annually. This hydrogen is intended for use in producing lower carbon intensity fuels and for decarbonizing other industries.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2019**: The project is part of the REFHYNE European consortium, which received funding from the European Commission through the Fuel Cells and Hydrogen Joint Undertaking (FCH JU). This funding supports the development of the electrolyser technology and its application in the refining industry.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2019**: Announcement of the construction start for the hydrogen plant.
- **Second Half of 2020**: Expected completion date for the construction of the hydrogen plant.
- **July 2021**: Official activation of the hydrogen electrolyser, marking it as fully operational.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify exact job creation or job loss figures related to the construction or operation of the facility. However, the project is expected to contribute to job creation in the context of the new hydrogen model region in Cologne.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned by Shell, which operates it as part of its Energy and Chemicals Park near Cologne. The project involves collaboration with partners such as ITM Power and other organizations, but Shell retains ownership and operational control of the facility.
