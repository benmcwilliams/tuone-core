## DEU-00244-00024

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Akasol Facility in Darmstadt, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2017**: Akasol operates a facility in Darmstadt focused on small-scale production of lithium-ion battery systems for utility vehicles, rail, and marine applications. The facility targets an annual output of 300 MWh, sufficient for approximately 1,500 electric buses, with potential expansion to 600 MWh.
- **February 2019**: Akasol announces plans to supply second-generation lithium-ion battery systems to a Swedish commercial vehicle manufacturer, likely Volvo, starting in 2020 until the end of 2023.
- **March 2020**: Akasol opens a second production line at its Langen site, increasing its capacity to 800 MWh.
- **October 2020**: Akasol moves into its new headquarters in Darmstadt, referred to as "Gigafactory 1," with an initial production capacity of 2.5 GWh, expandable to 5 GWh.
- **August 2021**: Akasol officially commissions its Gigafactory 1, which can produce enough battery systems for over 10,000 commercial vehicles annually.
- **November 2021**: Akasol presents its third generation of battery systems, the AKASystem 9 AKM 150 CYC, with series production scheduled to start in 2023.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2018**: Akasol prepares for an IPO to raise €100-125 million for expanding production and R&D capacities in Europe and the US.
- **February 2020**: Akasol commissions Manz for production equipment for its Darmstadt facility, with an investment potentially reaching €20 million.
- **April 2020**: Akasol orders production systems from Woll Maschinenbau for its Gigafactory 1, further enhancing its production capabilities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2019**: Akasol extends a framework agreement to supply battery systems to a Swedish manufacturer.
- **September 2019**: Akasol announces a follow-up order from a major commercial vehicle manufacturer, likely Volvo, for high-energy battery systems.
- **October 2020**: Akasol moves into its new headquarters and begins production at Gigafactory 1.
- **August 2021**: Akasol starts production at Gigafactory 1, with plans for further expansion.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2020**: Akasol announces that the establishment of Gigafactory 1 in Darmstadt will create up to 500 jobs.
- **October 2020**: The new headquarters and production facility in Darmstadt are expected to employ up to 350 people in various roles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2021**: BorgWarner announces plans to acquire Akasol, securing a majority of shares. Akasol will continue to operate independently under its brand, with CEO Sven Schulz remaining in leadership.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones of the Akasol facility in Darmstadt, Germany, focusing on its technology, operations, investments, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
