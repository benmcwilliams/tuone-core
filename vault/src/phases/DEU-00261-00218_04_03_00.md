## DEU-00261-00218

### Evolutionary Timeline of ElringKlinger Facility in Dettingen, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2020**: ElringKlinger and Plastic Omnium announced the establishment of the EKPO Fuel Cell Technologies joint venture, focusing on the development, large-scale production, and marketing of fuel cell stacks. The facility in Dettingen will produce fuel cells for various applications, including cars, commercial vehicles, and special vehicles. The initial production capacity is set at **10,000 fuel cell stacks per year**.
- **March 2021**: The EKPO Fuel Cell Technologies joint venture officially began operations, with plans to expand production capacity and innovate in fuel cell technology.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2020**: The joint venture with Plastic Omnium involves significant investments, including the acquisition of ElringKlinger Fuelcell Systems Austria GmbH (EKAT) for **15 million euros**. The joint venture aims to achieve sales of **700 million to 1 billion euros** by 2030.
- **March 2021**: ElringKlinger announced a major order for cell contact systems, valued in the mid-three-digit million euro range, to be supplied to a global battery manufacturer, indicating ongoing investment in battery technology alongside fuel cells.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2020**: Announcement of the joint venture EKPO Fuel Cell Technologies.
- **March 2021**: Official start of operations for EKPO Fuel Cell Technologies, with a focus on commercial vehicles and buses, and plans for future expansion into other mobility applications.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2020**: The establishment of the joint venture involves the contribution of **more than 150 employees** from ElringKlinger, indicating job retention and potential job creation as the facility expands its operations in fuel cell technology.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2020**: ElringKlinger holds **60% ownership** of the EKPO Fuel Cell Technologies joint venture, while Plastic Omnium holds **40%**. This indicates a joint venture ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by ElringKlinger.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones of the ElringKlinger facility in Dettingen, DEU, focusing on its advancements in clean technology, particularly in fuel cells and battery systems.
