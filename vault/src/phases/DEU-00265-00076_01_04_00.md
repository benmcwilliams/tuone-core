### Evolutionary Timeline of BMW Facility in Dingolfing, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2015**: BMW began producing electric motors at the Dingolfing plant, initially for the i3 model.
- **2018**: The facility was designated as the "BMW Group competence center for electric drive production," focusing on high-voltage batteries and electric drive systems.
- **2020**: The Competence Centre for E-Drive Production was officially opened, expanding to cover 125,000 square meters.
- **2021**: Production of the all-electric BMW iX began, marking the facility's first fully battery-powered vehicle.
- **2022**: The plant was set to produce electric drives for over 500,000 vehicles annually, including the iX, i4, and i7 models.
- **2023**: The all-electric BMW i5 began rolling off the production line, with plans for 300,000 electric cars to be built in the coming year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2018**: BMW announced a mid-double-digit million euro investment for battery production for the all-electric Mini.
- **2019**: BMW invested around €400 million in the Dingolfing plant for the production of the iNEXT.
- **2020**: An additional investment of over €500 million was planned for the Competence Centre for E-Drive Production.
- **2021**: BMW announced a total investment of around €790 million across various sites, including Dingolfing, for expanding electric drive component production.
- **2023**: Over €1 billion was invested in the production of the i5, i7, and iX at Dingolfing.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2018**: Announcement of the iNEXT project, with production slated to begin in 2021.
- **2020**: The Competence Centre for E-Drive Production was opened.
- **2021**: Series production of the BMW iX commenced.
- **2022**: Production of the i4 and i7 began, with the i7 being the first fully electric luxury sedan produced at Dingolfing.
- **2023**: The i5 began production, marking the third electric vehicle produced at the facility within two years.

#### 4. Labour
- **2018**: The Dingolfing plant employed around 18,000 people, with plans to increase the workforce in electric drive production from 600 to 2,000 employees.
- **2020**: By the end of the year, the number of employees in electric mobility was expected to reach 1,400.
- **2022**: The workforce in electric drive production grew to over 2,300 employees, reflecting the ramp-up in production capacity.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- BMW owns the Dingolfing facility exclusively, as it is part of the BMW Group's manufacturing network. The facility is integral to BMW's strategy for electrification and production of electric vehicles.

This timeline encapsulates the significant developments at the BMW facility in Dingolfing, highlighting its evolution towards becoming a key player in electric vehicle production within the automotive industry.
