### Evolutionary Timeline of the Bosch Facility in Dresden, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2017**: Bosch announces plans to invest approximately €1 billion in a new semiconductor factory in Dresden, focusing on producing chips for autonomous driving, e-mobility, and IoT applications. The factory is expected to be completed by 2019, with production starting in late 2021.
- **April 2018**: Construction of the chip factory begins, which will primarily produce processors for e-mobility. The facility is noted as the largest investment in Bosch's history.
- **March 2021**: Bosch successfully completes a fully automated production run of silicon wafers, marking a significant step towards the start of production, which is planned for the end of 2021.
- **June 2021**: The factory is officially opened, with production of chips on 300-millimeter wafers intended for the automotive industry. Series production is set to start in July 2021, with the first chips expected to be sold in mid-2022.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2017**: Bosch announces a €1 billion investment for the Dresden facility.
- **June 2021**: The German federal government provides approximately €140 million in funding for the construction of the factory, which is described as the first fully digitalized and highly networked semiconductor factory.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2017**: Announcement of the new factory.
- **April 2018**: Groundbreaking for the factory.
- **March 2021**: Completion of the first fully automated production run of silicon wafers.
- **June 2021**: Official opening of the factory and commencement of production.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2017**: The facility is expected to create 700 jobs upon completion.
- **June 2021**: The factory employs around 250 people at the time of opening, with plans to increase to 700 as production ramps up.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2021**: Bosch owns the facility exclusively, as there are no mentions of joint ventures or partnerships regarding the ownership of the Dresden semiconductor factory.
