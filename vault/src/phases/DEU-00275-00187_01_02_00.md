#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/2019**: The Dresden facility, as part of the ExcellBattMat Center at the Fraunhofer IWS, is involved in the development of new battery cell electrodes made of silicon or lithium layers. The goal is to create batteries that can store at least 70% more energy than conventional lithium-ion batteries, enabling electric vehicles to drive up to 700 kilometers on a single charge. The facility is focused on developing new materials, design principles, and processing technologies for battery cell electrodes, aiming for an energy density of over 1,000 watt-hours per liter.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12/2019**: The research is part of the BMBF-funded ExcellBattMat competence cluster for battery materials, indicating governmental support and investment in the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2019**: The Dresden facility announces its three-year research contribution to the KaSiLi project (Structural Mechanical Cathode Adaptation for Silicon and Lithium Materials), with functional demonstrators expected to be ready by 2022. The project is integrated into the larger "Battery Research Factory" initiative, which aims to enhance battery production capabilities in Germany.
