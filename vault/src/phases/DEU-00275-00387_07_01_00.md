## DEU-00275-00387

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Infineon Facility in Dresden, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/2018**: Infineon acquires Siltectra, a Dresden-based startup, for €124 million. This acquisition focuses on the "cold split" technology, which allows for the efficient processing of silicon carbide (SiC) wafers, effectively doubling chip output from a single wafer. The development of this technology will occur at the Siltectra facility in Dresden and Infineon’s facility in Villach, Austria.
- **02/2023**: Infineon announces plans for a new chip manufacturing plant in Dresden, referred to as the "Dresden Fab," which will produce semiconductors and analogue/mixed-signal components for various applications, including electric vehicles and IoT. The facility will utilize 300mm wafer technology.
- **08/2023**: Infineon confirms that the Dresden Fab will be part of a larger semiconductor ecosystem in Dresden, which includes a joint venture with TSMC, Bosch, and NXP.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **02/2023**: Infineon plans to invest €5 billion in the new Dresden plant, marking the largest single investment in the company's history. The company is also seeking €1 billion in public funding to support this expansion.
- **12/2024**: The German government announces it will provide up to €5 billion in subsidies for the TSMC joint venture in Dresden, which includes Infineon. This funding is part of a total investment of at least €10 billion for the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **02/2023**: Infineon confirms the construction of the new chip plant in Dresden, with production expected to start in 2026.
- **08/2023**: The joint venture with TSMC, Bosch, and NXP is announced, with construction expected to begin in the second half of 2024 and production starting by the end of 2027.
- **12/2024**: The groundbreaking ceremony for the TSMC joint venture factory takes place, with production slated to begin in 2027.

#### 4. Labour
- **02/2023**: Infineon anticipates the creation of 1,000 jobs as a result of the new Dresden facility.
- **12/2024**: The TSMC joint venture is expected to create around 2,000 direct jobs at the factory.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **12/2024**: The TSMC joint venture, named ESMC (European Semiconductor Manufacturing Company), will be 70% owned by TSMC, with Infineon, Bosch, and NXP each holding a 10% stake. This indicates that Infineon does not have exclusive ownership of the facility but is a significant partner in the joint venture.
