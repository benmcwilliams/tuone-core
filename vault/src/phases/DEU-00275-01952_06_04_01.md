### Evolutionary Timeline of the Solarwatt Facility in Dresden, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2010**: Solarwatt officially opened a new advanced module manufacturing facility in Dresden, doubling its nameplate capacity to 400 MW.
- **March 2020**: Solarwatt announced a 250 MW expansion of its module production lines in Dresden.
- **September 2021**: Solarwatt inaugurated a new 300 MW module production facility, bringing its total PV module production capacity to 550 MW. The facility also included new battery pack assembly lines.
- **May 2021**: Solarwatt launched two bifacial glass-glass panels with half-cell design, produced at its Dresden facility.
- **March 2022**: Solarwatt introduced CO2 compensation for its solar modules, emphasizing sustainability in production.
- **May 2024**: Solarwatt announced the closure of its 300 MW solar panel manufacturing facility in Dresden, citing economic burdens and a shift in production to Asia.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2021**: Solarwatt invested €15 million in the deployment of its new F8 production line in Dresden.
- **September 2021**: The company invested around €35 million in the new module and battery production lines at its Dresden facility, with plans to invest a total of €100 million in further development over the next few years.
- **May 2024**: Solarwatt indicated that it would move all battery and solar panel production to China for economic reasons.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2010**: Opening of the advanced module manufacturing facility in Dresden.
- **March 2020**: Announcement of a 250 MW expansion of production lines.
- **September 2021**: Inauguration of a new 300 MW module production facility and battery assembly lines.
- **August 2024**: Closure of the 300 MW solar panel manufacturing facility in Dresden.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2014**: Solarwatt employed around 150 people at its Dresden facility.
- **August 2024**: The closure of the facility will affect approximately 190 of the 750 employees across Europe, with efforts to reassign many to other positions within the company.
- **May 2024**: The company plans to reduce its workforce from 850 employees in 2023 to 350 in 2025, with a significant number of layoffs due to the closure of the Dresden facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Solarwatt operates the Dresden facility as a wholly-owned entity, with no indication of joint ventures or external ownership.
