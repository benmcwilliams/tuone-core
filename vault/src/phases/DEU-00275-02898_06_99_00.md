### Evolutionary Timeline of the Flaxres Facility in Dresden, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10-06-2021**: Flaxres, in collaboration with Veolia, is involved in a €4.8 million EU-funded research project aimed at developing a process to recycle all components of photovoltaic (PV) modules. The facility is expected to process around 5,000 tons of solar modules annually by 2023. The project focuses on recovering pure silicon, silver, and glass using innovative delamination technology.
- **22-07-2022**: Flaxres has developed an industrial process for recycling PV modules and has begun operating a pilot facility at its site in Dresden. The pilot facility has a capacity to recycle 10 tons of solar modules daily, with a potential annual capacity exceeding 1,000 tons. The FlaxThor recycling system achieves a cycle time of 10 seconds per module, and the pilot plant has successfully recycled 7.5 tons of solar modules, recovering over 200 kg of silicon, 4 kg of silver, and 4.9 tons of high-quality glass.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10-06-2021**: The facility is part of a larger EU-funded initiative (ReProSolar) with a budget of €4.8 million aimed at developing efficient recycling processes for solar modules.
- **22-07-2022**: Flaxres plans to develop mobile recycling equipment based on the pilot facility, which will be leased to international customers. The company has already signed contracts for the total recycling capacity of five new plants planned for construction in 2024.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **10-06-2021**: Announcement of the EU-funded research project to develop a recycling process for solar modules, with feasibility testing to be conducted by the end of the year.
- **22-07-2022**: The pilot facility begins operations, with plans for a full-scale stationary plant to be constructed in 2024 and available to customers by 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **22-07-2022**: The development of the pilot facility and future plants is expected to create jobs, although specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **10-06-2021**: Flaxres is involved in a consortium with Veolia and other partners, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership.
- **22-07-2022**: Flaxres operates the facility and plans to lease the developed recycling equipment to customers, suggesting a business model focused on service rather than ownership transfer.
