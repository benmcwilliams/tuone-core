### Evolutionary Timeline of Heliatek Facility in Dresden, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2009**: Heliatek moves to the production phase of organic solar cells, having achieved a power conversion efficiency of 6.07% for a tandem solar cell. The facility is set to produce organic solar cells on lightweight foil substrates using a continuous vacuum coating process.
- **March 2012**: Heliatek officially opens its first roll-to-roll flexible organic photovoltaic (OPV) thin-film plant in Dresden, with an initial production capacity of 2-3 MW for the year, targeting building-integrated photovoltaic (BIPV) applications.
- **April 2012**: Heliatek achieves a world record efficiency of 10.7% for a 1.1 cm² OPV cell and plans to begin roll-to-roll production of OPV modules in the third quarter of 2012.
- **September 2016**: Heliatek announces plans to expand its manufacturing capacity by one million m² per annum, establishing a new roll-to-roll facility at the existing site in Dresden.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2009**: Heliatek receives US$27 million in venture capital funding to build its first production facility in Dresden.
- **March 2012**: The opening of the €14 million roll-to-roll OPV production plant is announced.
- **March 2012**: Heliatek plans to raise an additional €50 million for a second production line expected to be completed in 2014.
- **September 2016**: Heliatek raises €80 million to expand its HeliaFilm manufacturing capacity, which includes €42 million in equity, €20 million in debt, and €18 million in grants and subsidies.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2012**: Heliatek opens its first flexible organic solar panel production facility in Dresden.
- **March 2012**: The company announces plans to begin module production within the year.
- **September 2016**: The company announces the establishment of a new manufacturing facility at its existing site in Dresden, expected to be completed within 18 months.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2016**: The expansion of the manufacturing facility is expected to create more than 50 new high-tech jobs in Saxony, Germany.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
