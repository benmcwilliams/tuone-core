## DEU-00275-10148

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Sunfilm AG Facility in Dresden, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Production Capacity:** The facility, after a merger in May 2009 with Sontor, had a combined production capacity of 145 MW, utilizing a-Si thin film technology and tandem junction cells with claimed efficiencies of 8%.
   - **Updates:** The production line in Grossroehrsdorf, Germany, employs SunFab thin-film technology from Applied Materials. However, the technology's prospects have been revised downwards, leading to a downscaling of activities by Applied Materials.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Initial Backing:** Sunfilm AG was initially supported by Q-Cells, Good Energies, and Norsun. However, Q-Cells has since undergone a major reorganization, focusing on a smaller core of subsidiaries.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Insolvency Filing:** On March 26, 2010, Sunfilm AG filed for insolvency in the district court in Dresden, Germany. This decision was influenced by competitive pressures from crystalline solar technologies and the dominance of CdTe thin film producers like First Solar.
   - **Strategic Realignment:** The insolvency is viewed as a potential opportunity for a new investor to take control and restructure the company, aiming to reduce debts and re-enter the market.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Job Impact:** Approximately 300 employees at the two manufacturing plants had been on shorter working hours since late 2009. The merged company had around 400 workers at the time of the merger announcement.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Ownership Status:** Sunfilm AG was initially backed by multiple investors, including Q-Cells, but the current ownership structure post-insolvency is uncertain as the company seeks a new investor for restructuring.

This timeline captures the critical developments and challenges faced by the Sunfilm AG facility in Dresden, DEU, highlighting its production capabilities, financial struggles, and workforce implications.
