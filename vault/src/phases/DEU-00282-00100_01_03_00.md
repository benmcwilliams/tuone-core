## DEU-00282-00100

### Evolutionary Timeline of Blackstone Resources Facility in Döbeln, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2019**: Blackstone Resources announces plans to invest €200 million in battery cell production in Germany, aiming for an initial production of 100 million battery cells annually, enough for 25,000 to 100,000 electric car batteries.
- **October 2020**: Blackstone Technology GmbH begins operations in the Am Fuchsbau industrial park in Döbeln, with a production capacity of 0.5 GWh per year, utilizing 3D printing technology.
- **February 2021**: Blackstone unveils a new lithium-ion battery cell based on LFP technology, ready for mass production by summer 2021, targeting an energy density of over 220 Wh/kg.
- **July 2021**: Blackstone announces plans to increase production capacity from 50 MWh to 500 MWh in 2022, focusing on LFP batteries and solid-state battery development.
- **December 2021**: Blackstone begins series production of 3D-printed battery cells, specifically LFP cells, using a new 3D printing process.
- **March 2022**: Blackstone announces plans to develop 3D-printed sodium-ion batteries, with a pilot plant investment of €32 million.
- **June 2023**: Blackstone Technology files for insolvency, indicating that series production of battery cells never took place as planned.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2021**: Blackstone signs a convertible loan agreement for up to 20 million Swiss francs (approximately €18.5 million) to finance mass production of 3D-printed solid-state batteries.
- **July 2021**: Blackstone secures a further investment of €40 million from its parent company to expand production capacity to 500 MWh.
- **March 2022**: Blackstone announces a €32 million investment for the development of sodium-ion batteries, supported by funding from the German Federal Ministry of Economics and Climate Protection.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2020**: Announcement of the Döbeln site as the production facility.
- **February 2021**: Pre-production sample cell ready for mass production.
- **July 2021**: Official operating permit received for small-scale production.
- **September 2021**: Production is set to start after machinery installation.
- **December 2021**: Series production of 3D-printed battery cells begins.
- **June 2023**: Blackstone Technology files for insolvency, indicating failure to achieve production targets.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2021**: 14 employees are reported at the Döbeln facility, with plans to expand to 38 employees in the following year.
- **June 2023**: Reports indicate that staff and suppliers have been waiting for payments, leading to lawsuits against Blackstone.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2022**: Blackstone Resources spins off its German battery division into Blackstone Technology Holding AG, a 100% subsidiary of Blackstone Resources AG, granting more autonomy to the battery division.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and challenges faced by the Blackstone Resources facility in Döbeln, Germany, highlighting its ambitious plans for battery production and the subsequent difficulties leading to its insolvency.
