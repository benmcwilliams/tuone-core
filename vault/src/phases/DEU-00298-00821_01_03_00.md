### Evolutionary Timeline of the Varta Facility in Ellwangen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10/2019**: Varta aims to mass-produce silicon-anodes as part of its lithium-ion technology development. The company is involved in the European Battery Alliance, focusing on increasing energy density in battery cells.
- **06/2020**: Varta receives €300 million in government funding to develop large format lithium-ion cells, marking a strategic shift towards larger battery applications for energy storage, robotics, and mobility. The company plans to create a pilot production line for these cells.
- **07/2024**: Varta announces plans for restructuring due to financial difficulties. The company had previously intended to start mass production of its V4Drive cells (suitable for electric vehicles) by the end of 2023, with a production capacity of at least 2 GWh. However, plans were put on hold in late 2022 pending customer commitments.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10/2019**: Varta is part of a consortium receiving EU subsidies as part of a broader initiative to support battery production in Europe, although the specific amount allocated to Varta is not disclosed.
- **06/2020**: The German government allocates €300 million to Varta for the development of large format lithium-ion cells, with funding expected to continue until the end of 2024.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **10/2019**: Varta is involved in the European Battery Alliance, focusing on the development of battery technology.
- **06/2020**: Varta's project for large format lithium-ion cells is declared an Important Project of Common European Interest (IPCEI).
- **07/2024**: Varta announces a restructuring plan to avoid insolvency, indicating a significant shift in its operational strategy and future production plans.

#### 4. Labour
- **06/2020**: Varta announces the creation of approximately 1,000 new jobs by the end of 2021 in Ellwangen and Nordlingen as part of its expansion into large format lithium-ion battery production.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **07/2024**: Discussions are ongoing regarding a potential investment by Porsche in Varta, which could lead to Porsche acquiring a majority stake in Varta's subsidiary V4Drive and possibly in Varta AG itself. This indicates a shift in ownership dynamics, as Varta is currently majority-owned by Montana Tech Components, an Austrian company.
