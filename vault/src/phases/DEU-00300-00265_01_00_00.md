### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fenecon Facility in Elverlingsen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2020**: The facility, located in a former coal-fired power plant, began operations with a new stationary battery storage system utilizing electric car batteries. The initial storage capacity is 3 MWh, composed of 72 batteries from Renault Zoes. The system is designed to serve as energy storage for the power grid, with a unique configuration that separates the batteries and inverters for efficiency and reliability. The project is part of Renault's Advanced Battery Storage initiative, with plans for future expansion to a total capacity of 20 MWh.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2020**: The project is developed in collaboration with Renault and The Mobility House, with a financing model that does not require additional costs from customers beyond providing space and a suitable grid connection. The project aims to save over 200,000 euros in electricity costs annually and generate additional revenue through energy provision on the primary control market.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2020**: The facility is officially commissioned and operational, marking it as a significant industrial project rather than a pilot. The partners announced plans for an additional 17 MWh of capacity, bringing the total to 20 MWh.
