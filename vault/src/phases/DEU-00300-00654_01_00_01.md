### Evolutionary Timeline of the Renault Group Facility in Elverlingsen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2020**: The facility, located in a former coal-fired power plant, began operations of a new stationary battery storage system utilizing electric car batteries. The system has an initial storage capacity of **3 MWh**, made up of **72 batteries** previously used in Renault Zoes. The batteries are repurposed for energy storage in the power grid as part of Renault’s **Advanced Battery Storage project**. Plans for an additional **17 MWh** of capacity were announced, bringing the total to **20 MWh**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2020**: The project is a collaboration between Renault, The Mobility House, and Fenecon, with no specific investment figures disclosed. The financing model is designed to require no upfront costs from customers, who only need to provide space for the battery and transformer containers and a suitable grid connection.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2020**: The stationary battery storage system was officially put into operation. The project is described as a "first industrial project, but not a pilot project," indicating its significance and potential for scalability. The partners expressed intentions to develop further projects utilizing batteries still installed in vehicles.
