## DEU-00300-01331

### Evolutionary Timeline of The Mobility House Facility in Elverlingsen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2020**: The facility in Elverlingsen began operations with a stationary battery storage system utilizing 72 electric car batteries from Renault Zoes, providing a storage capacity of 3 MWh. The system is designed for energy storage for the power grid and is part of Renault’s Advanced Battery Storage project. Plans for an additional 17 MWh were announced, bringing the total capacity to 20 MWh.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2024**: The Mobility House announced a joint venture with Green Energy Storage Initiative SE (GESI) to develop large-scale battery storage systems, aiming for a total storage capacity of up to 8 GW in Europe by 2035. This initiative is part of a broader strategy to enhance energy transition efforts and reduce grid bottlenecks.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2020**: The facility was officially commissioned and began operations as a stationary battery storage system.
- **June 2024**: The joint venture with GESI was established, with plans for a large storage facility of 250 MW in the planning stage, followed by an additional 500 MW in a second expansion phase.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify exact job creation or job loss figures related to the operations or expansions of the facility in Elverlingsen.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility in Elverlingsen is part of a joint venture established in 2018 between The Mobility House and other partners, indicating that it is not exclusively owned by The Mobility House but rather operates in collaboration with other entities, including Renault and GESI.
