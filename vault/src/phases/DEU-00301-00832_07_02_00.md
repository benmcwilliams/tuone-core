### Evolutionary Timeline of the Volkswagen Facility in Emden, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04/2018**: The Emden facility produces the Passat, with a production capacity of approximately 250,000 vehicles annually. The plant is noted for its full capacity due to high demand for plug-in models.
- **11/2018**: Volkswagen confirms plans to convert the Emden plant to produce electric vehicles (EVs) starting in 2022, aiming for a maximum production capacity of 300,000 vehicles per year.
- **05/2022**: Production of the ID.4 begins at the Emden plant, marking its transition to an EV production facility.
- **08/2023**: Production of the ID.7 starts at Emden, with the plant now focusing on producing both the ID.4 and ID.7 models.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **11/2018**: Volkswagen announces a total investment of €44 billion in future technologies, with a significant portion allocated to electrification, including the Emden facility.
- **07/2020**: Volkswagen begins the conversion of the Emden plant for EV production, with an investment of around €1 billion.
- **05/2022**: The Emden plant is officially recognized as a high-tech location for electric mobility, following a two-year conversion phase.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **11/2018**: Volkswagen confirms the Emden plant will be converted for EV production starting in 2022.
- **05/2022**: The ID.4 begins production at Emden.
- **08/2023**: Official production of the ID.7 commences at the Emden plant.
- **11/2023**: Production of ID.4 and ID.7 is temporarily halted due to a lack of electric motors.

#### 4. Labour
- **11/2018**: Volkswagen guarantees employment for workers at the Emden plant until 2028 during the transition to EV production.
- **11/2023**: Due to reduced production, approximately 300 temporary workers at the Emden plant are not retained as part of cost-cutting measures.
- **12/2023**: A hiring freeze is implemented at the Emden plant as part of broader efficiency programs.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Volkswagen owns the Emden facility exclusively, with no joint ventures reported in the context of its operations.
