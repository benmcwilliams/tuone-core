### Evolutionary Timeline of the Statkraft Facility in Emden, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2023**: Statkraft announced plans for a **10 MW pilot project** at the existing power plant site in Emden, expected to be operational by **2025**, pending necessary permits and funding. The facility aims to operate electrolysers to produce green hydrogen with a total capacity of up to **200 MW** connected to the future hydrogen pipeline network by **2030**.
- **By 2030**: Statkraft plans to have at least **250 MW** of installed electrolysis capacity operational in Germany, with the Emden facility being a key component of this strategy.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2024**: Statkraft secured financial support from the **European Union** to set up a hydrogen production site in Emden. The company was selected to negotiate a grant decision for up to **€107 million** (approximately **$116 million**) for its project, which includes the **200 MW electrolysis plant** and a **50 MW heat pump system**.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: Statkraft announced plans for the **10 MW pilot project** at the Emden site.
- **October 2024**: Statkraft plans to make a final investment decision for the **10 MW green hydrogen pilot project** by the end of the year.
