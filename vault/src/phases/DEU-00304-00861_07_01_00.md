## DEU-00304-00861

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Wolfspeed Facility in Ensdorf, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2023**: Wolfspeed announced plans to build the world's largest silicon carbide (SiC) semiconductor plant in Ensdorf, Saarland, primarily for electric vehicle applications. The facility will be a highly automated 200 mm wafer plant, marking Wolfspeed's first fab facility in Europe.
- **February 2023**: The facility is confirmed to be part of the Important Project of Common European Interest (IPCEI) for Microelectronics and Communication Technologies. The factory is expected to be the most modern of its kind and will revive the site of a former coal-fired power plant.
- **May 2023**: Plans for a joint research and development center for silicon carbide power electronics in the Nuremberg metropolitan region were announced, complementing the Ensdorf factory.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2023**: The project is contingent on government subsidies, with ZF Friedrichshafen making a sizable financial investment as part of a strategic partnership, although specific investment amounts were not disclosed.
- **February 2023**: The investment from ZF is described as being in the hundreds of millions of dollars in exchange for common shares, indicating a significant financial commitment to the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2023**: Initial announcement of the facility's construction.
- **February 2023**: Official confirmation of the project and its location, with political support from German government officials.
- **Expected Production Start**: Series production is anticipated to begin in four years from the announcement, which would be around early 2027.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2023**: The new factory is expected to create approximately 600 jobs, contributing to local employment and economic growth in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2023**: Wolfspeed will own the facility, but ZF will hold a minority stake in the factory and a majority stake in the associated research center, indicating a joint venture structure for the R&D efforts while maintaining Wolfspeed's primary ownership of the semiconductor plant. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic decisions surrounding the Wolfspeed facility in Ensdorf, highlighting its significance in the semiconductor industry and the collaboration with ZF.
