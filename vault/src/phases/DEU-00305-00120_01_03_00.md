### Evolutionary Timeline of the CATL Facility in Erfurt, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **07-06-2018**: CATL announces plans to establish a battery cell manufacturing facility in Erfurt, Germany, targeting an initial production capacity of 14 GWh, with potential expansions to 24 GWh later.
- **09-07-2018**: CATL confirms the facility will cost approximately 240 million euros and create around 600 jobs.
- **12-11-2018**: CATL reveals plans to accelerate the construction schedule, aiming for production to start as early as 2020 instead of 2022.
- **15-02-2019**: CATL updates its plans, indicating a potential capacity increase to 100 GWh by 2025 due to rising demand.
- **26-10-2019**: Groundbreaking ceremony for the Erfurt facility takes place, with production lines expected to be operational by early 2022.
- **31-12-2022**: CATL begins manufacturing battery cells at the Erfurt plant.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07-06-2018**: CATL plans to raise 722 million euros through a public offering to finance its expansion, including the Erfurt facility.
- **29-06-2018**: BMW announces a contract worth over 1 billion euros with CATL, directly linked to the establishment of the Erfurt facility.
- **18-06-2019**: CATL increases its investment in the Erfurt facility from 240 million euros to 1.8 billion euros, reflecting the growing demand for battery cells.
- **25-10-2021**: CATL announces plans to raise approximately 9 billion US dollars through a share issue to finance various projects, including the Erfurt facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **11-06-2018**: CATL's plans for the Erfurt facility are confirmed by multiple sources, including BMW and Volkswagen.
- **19-10-2019**: Groundbreaking ceremony for the Erfurt facility is held.
- **26-10-2019**: Official ground-breaking ceremony takes place, marking the start of construction.
- **10-07-2020**: CATL announces the completion of the first construction phase is on track for early 2022.
- **12-12-2021**: CATL begins personnel recruitment for the Erfurt facility, with plans to increase the workforce significantly.

#### 4. Labour
- **09-07-2018**: The facility is expected to create around 600 jobs in the region.
- **12-11-2018**: CATL plans to hire additional employees, with a total of 2,000 jobs expected to be created by the facility's completion.
- **31-12-2022**: CATL reports that the workforce at the Erfurt facility has grown to around 225 employees, with additional staff from China assisting in construction.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **11-06-2018**: CATL is identified as the sole owner of the facility, with plans to establish a project company in Thuringia as an independent business entity.
- **29-07-2018**: CATL's ownership structure remains unchanged, with no joint ventures reported for the Erfurt facility.
