## DEU-00305-00284

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fraunhofer Facility in Erfurt, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2020**: The Fraunhofer IKTS opened the Battery Innovation and Technology Center (BITC) at Erfurter Kreuz. The facility focuses on developing solutions for integrated, digitally supported production and quality assurance of battery cells and modules. The BITC aims to test ceramic materials and scale production to quantities exceeding millions of units while maintaining consistent quality.
- **October 2020**: The BattLife research project was launched at BITC, establishing a digitized test center for batteries and battery components. The project aims to develop more accurate lifetime predictions for batteries and enhance their longevity.
- **October 2023**: A new joint project, BattForce, was announced to continue improving battery life predictions, focusing on battery swelling as a critical factor in battery lifecycle. The BITC has created the first battery lifetime development model of its kind in Germany.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2020**: The state of Thuringia is supporting the development of the BITC over the next five years, although specific investment amounts for this phase were not detailed.
- **October 2020**: The state of Thuringia provided five million euros to support the BattLife research project.
- **October 2023**: The ongoing collaboration between CATL and Fraunhofer IKTS is supported by the state of Thuringia, with plans for further expansion of the research center.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2020**: Opening of the BITC at Erfurter Kreuz.
- **October 2020**: Launch of the BattLife project at BITC.
- **October 2023**: Announcement of the BattForce project, extending the collaboration between CATL and Fraunhofer IKTS until 2027.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2020**: The BITC is expected to contribute to job creation through training skilled workers and teaching industry 4.0 concepts in battery production, although specific job numbers were not provided.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The BITC is operated by Fraunhofer IKTS, and the building complex is owned by the State Development Corporation of Thuringia (LEG). There is no indication of joint ownership with CATL; rather, the facility appears to be fully owned by Fraunhofer IKTS while collaborating closely with CATL.
