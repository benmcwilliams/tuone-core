## DEU-00315-00503

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mahle Facility in Fellbach, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2020**: Mahle opened a new test bench for electric drives in Fellbach. The facility is designed to test individual drive units and complete electric axle drives for electric cars and plug-in hybrids. The test stand has a nominal load capacity of 350 kW and a peak torque of 8,400 Nm, with a continuous torque of 7,000 Nm. It can simulate a voltage range from 48 to 1,000 volts and temperature conditions from -30 to 130 degrees Celsius.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2020**: The test bench was inaugurated with an investment of three million euros. This investment is part of Mahle's strategy to expand its global services for e-mobility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2020**: The facility was officially opened, and the first test for a customer was completed shortly after the inauguration.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the opening of the facility in Fellbach.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Mahle operates the facility as a wholly-owned entity, as it is a direct investment by the Stuttgart-based supplier Mahle.

### Summary
The Mahle facility in Fellbach, Germany, opened in November 2020, focusing on testing electric drives for electric vehicles and hybrids. It was established with a three million euro investment and features advanced testing capabilities. The facility is fully owned by Mahle, although there is no specific information on job impacts from this development.
