### Evolutionary Timeline of the Astronergy Facility in Frankfurt, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/2013**: Astronergy acquires the Conergy Frankfurt factory, which will operate under the name Astronergy Solarmodule. The facility is set to produce thin film solar modules.
- **01/2017**: The facility is upgraded to produce five busbar modules, enhancing its production capabilities.
- **01/2019**: Manufacturing operations at the Frankfurt facility are ceased due to increased competition from Chinese products. The site is repurposed into a sales and service hub for the European market, focusing on marketing, customer care, logistics, and customs clearance.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12/2013**: The acquisition of the Frankfurt factory is part of Astronergy's strategy to integrate the facility into its existing structures, aiming for profitable production and future development. Specific financial details of the acquisition are not disclosed.
- **01/2017**: The upgrade to produce five busbar modules indicates investment in enhancing production technology, although specific investment amounts are not mentioned.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2013**: Astronergy signs contracts to acquire the Frankfurt factory from Conergy.
- **01/2017**: Announcement of the upgrade to produce five busbar modules.
- **01/2019**: Official announcement of the closure of manufacturing operations at the Frankfurt facility, with a transition to a sales and service hub.

#### 4. Labour
- **12/2013**: The acquisition secures 210 jobs long-term, but there will be a reduction of 70 jobs, with affected workers receiving interim employment and training.
- **01/2019**: The closure of the manufacturing facility results in the loss of 200 jobs, leaving approximately 30 staff members to remain for the new sales and service operations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **12/2013**: Astronergy acquires the Frankfurt factory from Conergy, indicating full ownership of the facility. There is no mention of joint ventures or shared ownership in the articles.
