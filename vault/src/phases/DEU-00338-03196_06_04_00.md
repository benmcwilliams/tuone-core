## DEU-00338-03196

### Evolutionary Timeline of Meyer Burger Facility in Freiberg, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10/2020**: Meyer Burger announces plans to produce highly efficient solar cells and modules at two sites in Germany, including Freiberg. The facility will initially have a production capacity of 400 MW for solar modules, with plans to expand to 5 GW in the future.
- **05/2021**: The Freiberg module factory officially opens with an initial capacity of 400 MW, utilizing heterojunction/SmartWire technology.
- **06/2021**: Meyer Burger announces plans to expand the Freiberg facility's capacity to 1 GW, up from the initial 400 MW.
- **01/2024**: Meyer Burger announces the closure of the Freiberg module production facility due to unfavorable market conditions in Europe, although it will continue to produce solar cells at its Thalheim site to support operations in the US.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10/2020**: Meyer Burger secures CHF 165 million (USD 175.3 million) in funding to support the establishment of its production facilities.
- **01/2021**: The company receives €22.5 million in public subsidies from the regional government of Saxony-Anhalt and the German central government for its heterojunction cell factory.
- **06/2021**: Meyer Burger secures €185 million in financing to expand its production capacity, including the Freiberg facility.
- **10/2022**: Meyer Burger announces plans to raise CHF 250 million (USD 255.2 million) to support the expansion of production capacity to 3 GW.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05/2021**: Production begins at the Freiberg module factory.
- **05/2021**: The Freiberg facility is officially opened, with production ramping up to 400 MW.
- **01/2024**: Meyer Burger announces plans to cease module production in Freiberg due to market conditions, with production expected to halt by April 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **12/2020**: Meyer Burger announces plans to create over 300 new jobs at its new factories in Freiberg and Bitterfeld-Wolfen.
- **01/2024**: The company plans to lay off approximately 500 employees at the Freiberg facility due to its closure.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **10/2020**: Meyer Burger is establishing its facilities in Freiberg and Bitterfeld-Wolfen, indicating full ownership of the facilities as it transitions from a production equipment manufacturer to an integrated PV manufacturer.
