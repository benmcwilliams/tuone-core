## DEU-00338-03483

### Evolutionary Timeline of the SolarWorld Facility in Freiberg, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **08/2008**: SolarWorld AG and its subsidiary Deutsche Solar AG announced the construction of a new plant in Freiberg, Germany, aiming to increase wafer production from 270 MW (2007) to 1 GW by 2010.
- **05/2009**: A €350 million investment was celebrated for a new crystalline solar cell manufacturing plant in Freiberg, expected to add 500 MW of capacity by the end of 2010, reaching a total of 1 GW across multiple facilities.
- **05/2010**: The inauguration of a new wafer production facility in Freiberg increased internal solar wafer capacity to 750 MW, with plans for further expansion to 1 GW.
- **01/2017**: SolarWorld announced the introduction of diamond wire saws at the Freiberg facility to enhance wafer production efficiency and quality.
- **02/2017**: Plans were made to concentrate all German cell production at Arnstadt while increasing wafer output in Freiberg.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **08/2008**: SolarWorld announced a capital expenditure of approximately $520 million for 2008, including the Freiberg facility.
- **05/2009**: The €350 million investment for the new solar cell manufacturing plant was confirmed.
- **01/2010**: SolarWorld raised €400 million to fund continued production capacity expansion, primarily allocated to the Freiberg facilities.
- **01/2017**: SolarWorld invested in upgrading its Freiberg wafer production facility with diamond wire saws.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **08/2008**: Announcement of the new plant construction in Freiberg.
- **05/2009**: Topping-out ceremony for the new solar cell manufacturing plant.
- **05/2010**: Inauguration of the new wafer production facility.
- **05/2011**: Commissioning of the new module production facility, increasing module capacity in Freiberg to 600 MW.
- **05/2017**: SolarWorld filed for preliminary insolvency, impacting operations at the Freiberg facility.
- **07/2017**: Announcement of potential layoffs and restructuring due to insolvency proceedings.
- **02/2019**: The insolvency administrator announced the auction of the Freiberg module factory due to failed investor search.

#### 4. Labour
- **05/2009**: Initial creation of 450 jobs at the new facility, bringing total employment in Freiberg to over 1,500.
- **01/2010**: Expansion plans projected to create approximately 500 new jobs, increasing total employment to over 2,000.
- **05/2017**: The company employed around 1,850 people in Germany, with layoffs anticipated due to insolvency.
- **07/2017**: Approximately 1,200 employees were to be transferred to a transitional company as part of the insolvency process.
- **02/2019**: Following the failed investor search, the module factory was set to be auctioned, impacting remaining jobs.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **08/2008**: SolarWorld AG, through its subsidiary Deutsche Solar AG, owned the Freiberg facility exclusively.
- **05/2017**: The company filed for insolvency, leading to discussions about potential acquisition by investors, indicating a shift in ownership structure.
- **02/2019**: The insolvency administrator announced plans to auction the Freiberg facility, indicating a potential change in ownership.
