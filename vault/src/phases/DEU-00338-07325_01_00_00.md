### Evolutionary Timeline of TU Bergakademie Freiberg Facility in Freiberg, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2024**: TU Bergakademie Freiberg initiated the use of a new shredder plant designed to enhance the efficiency of battery recycling. This facility can shred used battery cells weighing up to 2.5 kilograms in a single step, producing fine particles known as "black mass," which contains valuable raw materials such as nickel, cobalt, and lithium. The facility aims to optimize the quality of the black mass to meet future European Battery Regulation requirements.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2024**: The shredder facility was acquired as part of the ‘InfraDatRec’ project, which received funding of approximately 7 million euros from the Federal Ministry of Education and Research through the special ‘Energy and Climate Fund’. This funding is aimed at supporting the development of advanced recycling technologies.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2024**: The new shredder system became operational, with initial tests for shredding batteries commencing in the summer of the same year. The facility is designed to conduct extensive experimental studies to improve the recycling process.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2024**: The establishment of the new shredder plant is expected to contribute to job creation in the field of battery recycling and electromobility, although specific job numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2024**: The facility is owned by TU Bergakademie Freiberg, with the shredder system being part of a project funded by the Federal Ministry of Education and Research, indicating full ownership by the university.
