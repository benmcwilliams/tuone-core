### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mercedes-Benz Facility in Gaggenau, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2021**: The Gaggenau facility, which has historically specialized in heavy-duty commercial vehicle transmissions, is set to transform into a competence center for electric drive components and the assembly of hydrogen-based fuel cell drive components. This shift is part of a broader strategy to focus on electrified drives across multiple Daimler Truck locations.
- **November 2024**: The Gaggenau plant begins production of central components for the new Mercedes-Benz eActros 600, including mechanical parts for a 4-speed axle drive and bodyshell components for the cab. The facility has implemented new automated processing centers to enhance production capabilities.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2021**: Major investments are announced to support the realignment of the Gaggenau facility towards electric drive components and hydrogen fuel cell technology. Specific investment amounts are not detailed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2021**: Daimler Truck announces the strategic realignment of the Gaggenau facility as part of a network for electric drive components and battery systems.
- **November 2024**: Production starts for components of the eActros 600, marking a significant milestone in the facility's transition to electric vehicle component manufacturing.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2024**: The Gaggenau facility employs approximately 4,600 people, making it the largest employer in the city and the region's largest training company. The transition to electric drive components is expected to maintain or potentially increase job opportunities, although specific job creation or loss figures related to the recent changes are not provided.
