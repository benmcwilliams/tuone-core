### Evolutionary Timeline of the Max Planck Institute for Plasma Physics Facility in Garching, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: Researchers at the Max Planck Institute for Plasma Physics (IPP) in Garching discovered a method to significantly reduce the distance between hot plasmas in nuclear fusion devices and the vessel wall. This advancement was achieved in the ASDEX Upgrade tokamak, which serves as a blueprint for future fusion power plants, including ITER. The distance was reduced from at least 25 centimeters to less than 5 centimeters, potentially allowing for nearly double the plasma volume within the same vessel dimensions, thereby increasing achievable fusion power.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2024**: The German Federal Ministry of Education and Research (BMBF) announced a new funding program named "Fusion 2040," aimed at advancing technologies, components, and materials necessary for a fusion power plant. The program is set to provide over EUR1 billion for fusion research by 2028, with a focus on collaborative research between institutions, universities, and industry.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2023**: IPP signed a cooperation agreement with Proxima Fusion, a company spun out of IPP, to further develop the stellarator concept for fusion power. This collaboration aims to advance technological approaches while leveraging IPP's expertise in stellarator physics.

#### 4. Labour

#### 5. Ownership Structure
