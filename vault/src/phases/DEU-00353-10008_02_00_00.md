## DEU-00353-10008

### Evolutionary Timeline of Silenos Energy GmbH Facility in Garching, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2019**: Silenos Energy GmbH presented plans for a co-generation plant in Garching, Bavaria, which includes:
  - Three gas-powered cogeneration units, each with a capacity of 900 kW.
  - A power plant with a total capacity of 4.3 MW, utilizing Isobutan as the working fluid.
  - The facility will have black start capability, allowing it to operate independently of the external electric power network.
  - The plant is designed to utilize heat for a district heating network with a thermal capacity of up to 6.9 MW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2019**: The Bavarian state government announced funding initiatives to support the expansion of geothermal energy, which will benefit the Garching facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2017**: Silenos Energy GmbH took over the Garching geothermal project.
- **January 2019**: Successfully completed drilling work on a doublet, with two wells drilled into the same fault zone, reaching a temperature of 125 degrees Celsius.
- **August/September 2020**: Expected commissioning of the power plant and the start of electricity production.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2019**: The project is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers were not mentioned in the article.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2019**: Silenos Energy GmbH is part of a joint venture, with managing directors from Strabag and RAG involved in the project. The specific ownership structure details were not provided, but it indicates that Silenos Energy GmbH does not own the facility exclusively.
