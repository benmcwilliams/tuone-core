## DEU-00399-00768

### Evolutionary Timeline of Tesla's Gigafactory in Grünheide, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/11/2019**: Tesla announces plans to build Gigafactory 4 in Grünheide, near Berlin, focusing on the production of batteries, powertrains, and vehicles, starting with the Model Y. Initial production capacity is set at 150,000 vehicles per year, with potential expansion to 500,000 vehicles.
- **01/2020**: Official approval process for the factory begins, with plans to produce Model Y and Model 3.
- **03/2022**: Production of Model Y begins, with a target of 5,000 vehicles per week by early 2023.
- **03/2023**: Production capacity reaches 375,000 vehicles per year, with plans to expand to 1 million vehicles.
- **01/2024**: Tesla starts producing the Model Y base variant using BYD batteries, with a structural battery pack design.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12/11/2019**: Tesla plans to invest approximately €4 billion in Gigafactory 4.
- **03/2021**: Tesla announces plans to apply for state funding of €1.135 billion for battery cell production.
- **09/2021**: Tesla declines the state funding for battery production due to unfavorable conditions.
- **11/2022**: Tesla's expansion plans include increasing battery storage production capacity from 50 to 100 GWh.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2019**: Announcement of the factory location in Grünheide.
- **01/2020**: Official approval process begins.
- **03/2022**: Tesla officially opens Gigafactory Berlin and begins deliveries of Model Y Performance.
- **03/2023**: Production capacity reaches 5,000 vehicles per week.
- **03/2024**: Tesla submits an application for the expansion of the plant to increase production capacity to 1 million vehicles per year.

#### 4. Labour
- **12/11/2019**: Initial plans indicate the creation of 3,000 jobs, with a total of up to 12,000 jobs expected.
- **03/2022**: Approximately 5,000 employees are working at the factory, with plans to increase to 12,000.
- **01/2024**: Reports indicate that Tesla is facing staffing challenges, impacting production rates.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **12/2019**: Tesla establishes a European public limited company, Tesla Manufacturing Brandenburg SE, for operations at the Grünheide site. The facility is fully owned by Tesla.

### Summary
Tesla's Gigafactory in Grünheide has undergone significant development since its announcement in late 2019. The facility is designed to produce electric vehicles, primarily the Model Y, with an initial capacity of 150,000 vehicles per year, which has since been targeted for expansion to 1 million vehicles. The project has faced various challenges, including environmental objections and staffing issues, but has also received substantial investment commitments and plans for further expansion, particularly in battery production. The factory is fully owned by Tesla and has created thousands of jobs in the region.
