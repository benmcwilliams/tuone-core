## DEU-00422-08998

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Georg-August University of Göttingen Facility in Göttingen, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility is involved in the mesoTherm project, which focuses on researching geothermal energy in northern Germany. The project aims to gain knowledge about geothermal reservoirs and their potential for energy use. The geothermal plant is expected to provide a purely geothermal heat output of around 6 MW, sufficient to supply approximately 4,700 households, with potential expansion to over 6,000 households through a multi-stage heating process.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The project is supported by the Federal Ministry of Economics and Climate Protection (BMWK), which is funding it with a total of EUR 22.5 million. This funding is aimed at supporting research and development in future-oriented energy technologies, particularly in the context of the Integrated Heat Transition Wilhelmsburg (IW3) project.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - As of August 2023, the construction of the boiler house for the geothermal project is expected to start in spring 2024, with heat supply planned to commence by spring 2025. The project has successfully completed injection tests, indicating progress in the development of the geothermal facility.

4. **Labour:**
   - The articles do not specify job creation or job loss directly associated with the facility's operations or expansions.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is part of a collaborative project led by the Georg-August University of Göttingen, in partnership with Geothermie Neubrandenburg GmbH (GTN) and the Leibniz Institute for Applied Geophysics (LIAG). The ownership structure is not described as exclusive; rather, it indicates a joint effort among multiple organizations.
