### Evolutionary Timeline of the RWE Facility in Hambach, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2023**: RWE began construction of the RWE Neuland Solar Park at the Hambach opencast lignite mine site. The facility is designed to have a solar capacity of **8.4 MW** and an **8 MWh** battery storage system, intended to buffer power generation and the grid. This project is in close proximity to the existing RWE Neuland 1 Solar Park, which has a planned capacity of **17.6 MW**.
- **September 2023**: The RWE Neuland Solar Farm is confirmed to be under construction, with a capacity of **12.2 MW** and a battery storage system of **4.1 MW / 8.1 MWh**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2023**: RWE is part of a broader initiative to achieve **500 MW** of renewable energy projects in the Rhenish mining area by **2030**. The specific investment amount for the Hambach facility was not disclosed, but it is part of RWE's larger investment strategy to expand its renewable portfolio significantly.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2023**: Construction of the RWE Neuland Solar Park commenced, with completion expected by the end of **2023**.
- **September 2023**: The facility is confirmed to be under construction, with ongoing developments in the solar and battery storage systems.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify job creation or job loss directly associated with the RWE Neuland Solar Park at the Hambach site. However, the overall expansion of renewable projects in the region is expected to contribute positively to local employment.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- RWE is the primary developer and operator of the facility, indicating **full ownership** of the RWE Neuland Solar Park at the Hambach opencast mine.

### Summary
The RWE facility in Hambach, Germany, is evolving as a significant solar and battery storage project, with construction beginning in August 2023 and expected completion by the end of the year. The facility will contribute to RWE's broader goal of expanding renewable energy capacity in the Rhenish mining area, with a focus on integrating solar power with battery storage solutions. RWE maintains full ownership of the facility, aligning with its strategic investments in renewable energy.
