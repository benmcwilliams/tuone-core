### Evolutionary Timeline of the Airbus Facility in Hamburg, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2021**: Airbus is part of the Hamburg Hydrogen Hub, which plans to produce hydrogen from wind and solar power at the Hamburg-Moorburg power plant site. The hub includes a scalable electrolyzer with an initial output of **100 MW**. This facility aims to contribute to a green hydrogen network in Hamburg, linking production, distribution, and use across various applications.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2021**: The Hamburg Hydrogen Network, which includes Airbus, submitted an application for funding under the EU-wide "Important Projects of Common European Interest" (IPCEI) program. This funding is crucial for the implementation of the hydrogen production projects and to close the price gap between green hydrogen and fossil solutions.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: Announcement of the Hamburg Hydrogen Hub and the associated projects, including the 100 MW electrolyzer. The network aims to reduce carbon dioxide emissions in Hamburg by **170,000 tonnes** annually by 2026.
- **October 2024**: Airbus is expected to showcase its latest innovations at the Hydrogen Technology Expo in Hamburg, indicating ongoing involvement in hydrogen technology and its applications.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2021**: The establishment of the Hamburg Hydrogen Hub and the associated projects is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers are not detailed in the articles. The overall initiative is anticipated to have positive spillover effects on job creation in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2021**: Airbus is part of a collaborative network (Hamburg Hydrogen Network) that includes multiple companies such as Shell, Vattenfall, and ArcelorMittal. This indicates that Airbus does not own the facility exclusively but is involved in a joint initiative with other stakeholders.

This timeline captures the key developments related to the Airbus facility in Hamburg, focusing on its role in the emerging hydrogen economy and collaborative efforts in clean technology.
