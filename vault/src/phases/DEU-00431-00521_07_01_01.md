## DEU-00431-00521

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mercedes-Benz Facility in Hamburg, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2016**: Mercedes-Benz announces plans to transform its Hamburg plant into a high-tech site for producing drive components for electric transport. The revamp aims to enhance production capabilities and future-proof 2,500 jobs.
- **November 2018**: The Hamburg facility is set to produce electric drive modules, rear axles, lightweight components, and integrated starter generators (iSG) for the Mercedes EQC, marking its integration into Daimler's network for electric mobility.
- **December 2022**: The facility is designated as one of the core plants for assembling electric axles and components, with plans to produce drive units for the MB.EA platform starting in 2024. The Hamburg plant has been supplying electric axles for the EQC since 2019 and for the EQE since 2022.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2016**: A revamp of the Hamburg plant is announced with an investment of 500 million euros aimed at enhancing its capabilities for electric vehicle components.
- **December 2022**: Mercedes-Benz commits to a mid-single-digit billion euro investment in its European powertrain production sites, including the Hamburg facility, to support the assembly of electric drive systems and battery systems.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2016**: Announcement of the transformation of the Hamburg plant into a high-tech site for electric transport components.
- **November 2018**: Confirmation that the Hamburg facility will produce key components for the Mercedes EQC, with production starting in 2019.
- **December 2022**: Official announcement that the Hamburg plant will produce drive units for the MB.EA platform starting in 2024, solidifying its role in the electric vehicle production network.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2016**: The revamp of the Hamburg plant is expected to make 2,500 jobs more future-safe, indicating job security rather than direct job creation or loss.
- **December 2022**: The new production arrangements and investments are described as resulting from discussions with employee representatives, suggesting a focus on job security and future employment prospects.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2018**: It is noted that the factories in Hamburg and Berlin are managed as a joint entity since 2017, indicating a collaborative operational structure rather than exclusive ownership by Mercedes-Benz.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic decisions regarding the Mercedes-Benz facility in Hamburg, highlighting its evolution towards electric vehicle production and the associated investments and operational changes.
