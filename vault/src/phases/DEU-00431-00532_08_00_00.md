## DEU-00431-00532

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mitsubishi Facility in Hamburg, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2021**: Mitsubishi Heavy Industries (MHI), along with Shell, Vattenfall, and Wärme Hamburg, signed a letter of intent to produce hydrogen from wind and solar power at the Moorburg power plant site. The facility plans to construct a scalable electrolyser with an initial output of **100 MW**. The project aims to develop the site into a ‘Green Energy Hub’ for large-scale hydrogen production, with production anticipated to start in **2025**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2021**: The partners intend to apply for funding under the EU programme ‘Important Projects of Common European Interest’ (IPCEI) to support the project. This funding application is crucial for the financial viability of the hydrogen production initiative.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2021**: The letter of intent was signed, marking the formal start of the project planning.
- **April 2021**: The Hamburg Hydrogen Initiative was unveiled, which includes the 100 MW electrolyser plan as part of a broader network involving multiple companies. The application for IPCEI funding was submitted, encompassing nine complementary projects aimed at establishing a hydrogen economy in Hamburg.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2021**: The establishment of the hydrogen network and the planned projects are expected to create job opportunities, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2021**: The facility is not exclusively owned by Mitsubishi; it is a joint venture involving multiple partners: Shell, Vattenfall, Wärme Hamburg, and Mitsubishi Heavy Industries. This collaborative approach indicates a shared ownership structure for the hydrogen production facility.

### Summary
The Mitsubishi facility in Hamburg is part of a collaborative effort to produce green hydrogen at the Moorburg power plant site, with an initial capacity of 100 MW planned for 2025. The project is supported by a consortium of companies and aims to secure EU funding to facilitate its development. The joint venture structure involves multiple stakeholders, indicating a shared commitment to advancing the hydrogen economy in the region.
