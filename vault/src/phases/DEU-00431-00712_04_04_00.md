## DEU-00431-00712

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Shell Facility in Hamburg, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2021**: Shell, along with Mitsubishi Heavy Industries (MHI), Vattenfall, and Wärme Hamburg, signed a letter of intent to jointly produce hydrogen from wind and solar power at the Hamburg-Moorburg power plant site. The facility plans to construct a scalable electrolyser with an initial output of **100 MW**. The project aims to develop the site into a ‘Green Energy Hub’ for large-scale hydrogen production, with production anticipated to start in **2025**.
- **April 2021**: The Hamburg Green Hydrogen Hub, which includes Shell, Vattenfall, MHI, and Wärme Hamburg, announced plans for a **100 MW electrolyser** as part of a broader hydrogen network. This network aims to link hydrogen production, distribution, and use across various applications in the Port of Hamburg.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2021**: The partners intend to apply for funding under the EU programme ‘Important Projects of Common European Interest’ (IPCEI) to support the development of the hydrogen project. The application was planned for submission in the **first quarter of 2021**.
- **November 2021**: Shell announced plans to build hydrogen refuelling sites in Germany, including the Hamburg facility, as part of a broader strategy to increase hydrogen production capacity across Europe.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2021**: The signing of the letter of intent marked the official start of the project planning phase for the hydrogen production facility.
- **April 2021**: The Hamburg Green Hydrogen Hub was unveiled, detailing the collaborative efforts of multiple companies, including Shell, to establish a hydrogen network in Hamburg.
- **2025**: Anticipated start of green hydrogen production at the Hamburg facility.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific details on job creation or job loss related to the Shell facility in Hamburg. However, the development of the hydrogen hub and associated projects is expected to create jobs in the region as the hydrogen economy expands.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is not exclusively owned by Shell; it is a collaborative project involving **joint ownership** with Mitsubishi Heavy Industries, Vattenfall, and Wärme Hamburg. The consortium aims to leverage the existing infrastructure at the Moorburg site for the hydrogen production initiative.
