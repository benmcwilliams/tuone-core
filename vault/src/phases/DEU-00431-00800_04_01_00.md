### Evolutionary Timeline of the Uniper Facility in Hamburg, Germany

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **2015:** Uniper constructed a power-to-gas facility in Hamburg, which is part of its efforts to produce green hydrogen. This facility is designed to utilize renewable electrical energy for hydrogen production.
   - **2020:** Uniper announced plans to transform its gas power plants and storage facilities to accommodate the production and use of green hydrogen, as part of a broader initiative to decarbonize its operations.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **2020:** Uniper and Siemens signed a cooperation agreement to advance the decarbonization of power generation, which includes investments in the transformation of existing gas-fired hydrogen production plants to support green hydrogen initiatives.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **2013:** Uniper built its first power-to-gas plant in Falkenhagen.
   - **2015:** The Hamburg facility was established as a power-to-gas plant.
   - **2020:** Uniper and Siemens announced a joint initiative to assess and implement the use of green hydrogen in Uniper's existing gas assets, with a commitment to shutter or repurpose coal-fired power plants within five years.

4. **Labour:**
   - **2020:** The cooperation agreement between Uniper and Siemens is expected to create jobs related to the development and implementation of green hydrogen technologies, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - Uniper operates the Hamburg facility as part of its portfolio, having been spun out from E.ON in 2016. The articles do not indicate any joint ownership or partnerships beyond the cooperation with Siemens for specific projects.
