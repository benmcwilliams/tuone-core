## DEU-00431-00822

### Evolutionary Timeline of Vattenfall Facility in Hamburg, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2021**: Vattenfall, in collaboration with Mitsubishi Heavy Industries, Shell, and Wärme Hamburg, signed a letter of intent to produce hydrogen from wind and solar power at the Moorburg power plant site. The project includes the construction of a scalable electrolyser with an initial output of **100 MW**. The facility aims to develop into a ‘Green Energy Hub’ and is expected to start producing green hydrogen by **2025**.
- **April 2021**: The Hamburg Green Hydrogen Hub was announced, which includes the 100 MW electrolyser and aims to link hydrogen production, distribution, and use across various applications in the Port of Hamburg.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2021**: The partners intend to apply for funding under the EU programme ‘Important Projects of Common European Interest’ (IPCEI) to support the project. This application was planned for the **first quarter of 2021**.
- **April 2021**: The Hamburg Hydrogen Network, comprising Vattenfall, Shell, and other partners, submitted an application under the IPCEI funding program, which includes nine complementary projects aimed at reducing carbon dioxide emissions in Hamburg by **170,000 tonnes annually**.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2021**: The letter of intent was signed, marking the official start of the project planning.
- **April 2021**: The Hamburg Hydrogen Network was formed, and the application for IPCEI funding was submitted, indicating progress towards the establishment of the hydrogen production facility.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify exact job creation or job loss figures related to the investments or expansions at the Vattenfall facility in Hamburg. However, the development of the hydrogen hub is expected to create jobs associated with the construction and operation of the electrolyser and related infrastructure.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is not exclusively owned by Vattenfall; it is a joint venture involving **Mitsubishi Heavy Industries, Shell, Wärme Hamburg**, and Vattenfall. This consortium is working together to develop the hydrogen production capabilities at the Moorburg site.

### Summary
The Vattenfall facility in Hamburg is evolving into a significant player in the green hydrogen sector, with plans for a 100 MW electrolyser and the establishment of a Green Energy Hub. The project is backed by a consortium of major companies and aims to leverage EU funding to support its development, with an anticipated operational start in 2025.
