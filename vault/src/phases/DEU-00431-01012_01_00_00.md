### Evolutionary Timeline of the EMR Facility in Hamburg, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2023**: The EMR facility in Hamburg, operated in partnership with Northvolt, officially opened. The facility is designed to process **10,000 tonnes of battery packs per year**. It focuses on discharging and dismantling complex traction batteries from electric vehicles, recovering high-grade copper and aluminum for reuse in battery housing. The remaining battery modules are sent to Northvolt’s facilities for further processing, including the recovery of lithium, nickel, manganese, and cobalt.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2023**: The opening of the Hamburg facility is part of Northvolt's broader strategy to establish a circular battery industry in Europe. Northvolt has raised **$1.2 billion** in capital to finance its expansion, which includes the Hamburg facility as a key component of its recycling strategy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2023**: The facility was inaugurated, marking the beginning of operations focused on battery recycling. The facility is part of Northvolt's ongoing efforts to scale up recycling capabilities, with plans for further expansions in Sweden.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2023**: The opening of the Hamburg facility is expected to contribute to job creation, although specific numbers regarding job creation or loss were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2023**: The EMR facility in Hamburg is operated as a **joint venture** between EMR and Northvolt. EMR is responsible for the operation and equipment of the facility, while Northvolt handles planning and operational integration.

This timeline captures the key developments and operational aspects of the EMR facility in Hamburg, highlighting its role in the evolving landscape of battery recycling in Europe.

### EMR Facility in Hamburg, DEU - Contrasting Summary

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: The Hamburg facility is expected to process **10,000 tonnes of battery packs per year** (Article 1, 24-08-2023). In contrast, the Birmingham facility, which is EMR's second of this kind, has a capacity of **more than 2,000 tons of electric vehicle batteries per year** (Article 2, 26-09-2024).
- **Operations**: The Hamburg facility focuses on **discharging and dismantling battery packs**, specifically complex traction batteries from electric cars (Article 1, 24-08-2023). The Birmingham facility, however, includes a process to **test batteries to determine if they should be repaired, reused, or recycled** (Article 2, 26-09-2024).

#### 2. Investment and Development

There is no mention of similar investment details for the Birmingham facility.

- **Future Plans**: Northvolt plans to produce cells with **50% recycled material by 2030** across all operations, indicating a long-term goal for the Hamburg facility (Article 1, 24-08-2023). The Birmingham facility's development is tied to a **four-year research initiative** and **two years of development** at the site (Article 2, 26-09-2024).
