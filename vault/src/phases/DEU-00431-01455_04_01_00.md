## DEU-00431-01455

### Evolutionary Timeline of the ArcelorMittal Facility in Hamburg, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2021**: ArcelorMittal's Hamburg plant is set to undergo a transformation to achieve climate-neutral steel production by 2030 as part of the H2H project. This includes the construction of a hydrogen-powered demonstration plant for direct reduction of iron ore (H2First) and upgrading the existing direct reduction plant (H2Ready) to utilize green hydrogen instead of natural gas.
  
#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2021**: The Hamburg Hydrogen Network, which includes ArcelorMittal, submitted an application for funding under the EU's "Important Projects of Common European Interest" (IPCEI) program. This funding is crucial for the development of the hydrogen production and distribution network, which aims to reduce carbon emissions in Hamburg by 170,000 tonnes annually by 2026.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: Announcement of the Hamburg Hydrogen Initiative, which includes plans for a 100MW electrolyser and the conversion of ArcelorMittal's Hamburg plant to climate-neutral steel production by 2030.
- **June 2022**: ArcelorMittal and RWE sign a memorandum of understanding to collaborate on developing offshore wind farms and hydrogen facilities to supply renewable energy and green hydrogen for low-emissions steel production in Germany.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2022**: The partnership with RWE is expected to create jobs related to the development and operation of offshore wind farms and hydrogen facilities, although specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2022**: ArcelorMittal is involved in a partnership with RWE for the development of renewable energy sources, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership of the facility. The facility is not mentioned as a joint venture but rather as a partnership for specific projects related to decarbonization.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic initiatives related to the ArcelorMittal facility in Hamburg, focusing on its transition towards sustainable steel production through the integration of green hydrogen and renewable energy sources.
