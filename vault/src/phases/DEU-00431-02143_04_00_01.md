### Evolutionary Timeline of the Air Products Facility in Hamburg, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2022**: Air Products, in collaboration with Mabanaft, announced plans to build Germany's first large green ammonia import terminal at the Port of Hamburg. The facility aims to import green ammonia from Air Products' global facilities and convert it back into hydrogen for local distribution. Operations are expected to begin in **2026**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2022**: Air Products has committed significant investments in low-carbon hydrogen, with CEO Samir Serhan stating that the company has already invested **$15 billion** in this sector. The Hamburg terminal is part of this broader investment strategy, although specific funding for the Hamburg facility was not detailed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2022**: Air Products signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with Hamburg regarding the project.
- **November 2022**: The German government confirmed its collaboration with Air Products for the construction of the green ammonia import terminal.
- **2026**: The terminal is expected to begin operations, marking a significant milestone in Germany's hydrogen economy.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify exact job creation or job loss figures related to the Hamburg facility. However, the establishment of the terminal is expected to contribute positively to local employment through the development and operational phases.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is a joint venture between Air Products and Mabanaft, indicating that Air Products does not own the facility exclusively but is collaborating with another entity for its development and operation.
