### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hamburger Hafen und Logistik Facility in Hamburg, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2021**: The Hamburg Hydrogen Network, which includes Hamburger Hafen und Logistik, is formed to develop a green hydrogen network. The network plans to produce hydrogen from wind and solar power at the Hamburg-Moorburg power plant site, deploying a scalable electrolyzer with an initial output of **100 MW**. This project is part of a broader initiative that aims to reduce carbon dioxide emissions in Hamburg by **170,000 tonnes annually** by 2026.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2021**: The Hamburg Hydrogen Network submitted an application under the EU-wide ‘Important Projects of Common European Interest’ (IPCEI) funding program. This application includes nine projects aimed at creating a hydrogen economy in the Hamburg area, which will require **financial support from the federal German government**.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: The Hamburg Hydrogen Network is officially announced, detailing plans for the **100 MW electrolyzer** and other complementary projects in the vicinity of the Port of Hamburg. The network aims to link hydrogen production, distribution, and use across various applications.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2021**: The projects under the Hamburg Hydrogen Network are expected to create **positive spillover effects** throughout Germany and Europe, which may include job creation, although specific job numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2021**: Hamburger Hafen und Logistik is part of a collaborative network with **11 other companies**, including Shell, Vattenfall, and Mitsubishi Heavy Industries, indicating a **joint venture** structure for the hydrogen initiatives rather than exclusive ownership of the facility.
