### Evolutionary Timeline of the Wärme Hamburg Facility in Hamburg, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2021**: Wärme Hamburg, in collaboration with Mitsubishi Heavy Industries (MHI), Shell, and Vattenfall, signed a letter of intent to produce hydrogen from wind and solar power at the Moorburg power plant site. The facility plans to construct a scalable electrolyser with an initial output of **100 MW**. The project aims to develop the site into a ‘Green Energy Hub’ for large-scale hydrogen production, with production anticipated to start in **2025**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2021**: The partners intend to apply for funding under the EU programme ‘Important Projects of Common European Interest’ (IPCEI) to support the project. This funding application is crucial for the financial viability of the hydrogen production initiative.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2021**: The letter of intent was signed, marking the formal start of the project planning phase.
- **2025**: Subject to future investment decisions, the production of green hydrogen is expected to commence.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2021**: While specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles, the project is expected to create jobs related to the construction and operation of the electrolyser and the development of the hydrogen network in the port area.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2021**: The facility is a collaborative project involving four partners: Wärme Hamburg, Mitsubishi Heavy Industries, Shell, and Vattenfall. This indicates a **joint venture** ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by any single company.
