### Evolutionary Timeline of Hamburg Energie Geothermie GmbH Facility in Hamburg, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2023**: The facility is expected to produce a purely geothermal heat output of around 6 MW, sufficient to supply approximately 4,700 households. With a planned multi-stage heating process, this capacity could increase to over 6,000 households. The construction of the boiler house is anticipated to start in spring 2024, with heat supply planned to commence by spring 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2023**: The project is supported by the Federal Ministry of Economics and Climate Protection (BMWK) with a total funding of EUR 22.5 million. This funding is aimed at supporting research and development in future-oriented energy technologies, particularly in the context of the IW3 project, which focuses on the heat transition in Wilhelmsburg.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2023**: Successful completion of the injection test, where pumped thermal water was fed back into a 130-meter thick sandstone layer via a second borehole. The project is part of the IW3 – Integrated Heat Transition Wilhelmsburg initiative, which aims to provide almost CO2-free heat to residential areas.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2023**: While specific job creation or loss figures are not mentioned, the expansion of the district heating network and the geothermal heating plant is expected to contribute positively to local employment opportunities in the context of renewable energy development.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2023**: Hamburg Energie Geothermie GmbH is a wholly owned subsidiary of Hamburger Energiewerke GmbH, indicating full ownership of the facility by Hamburger Energiewerke GmbH.
