### Evolutionary Timeline of Hamburger Energiewerke GmbH Facility in Hamburg, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2023**: The Hamburg geothermal project is set to produce a purely geothermal heat output of around 6 MW, sufficient to supply approximately 4,700 households. With a planned multi-stage heating process, this capacity could increase to over 6,000 households. The construction of the boiler house is expected to commence in spring 2024, with heat supply planned to start by spring 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2023**: The project is supported by a total funding of EUR 22.5 million from the Federal Ministry of Economics and Climate Protection (BMWK) to facilitate research and development in geothermal energy technologies.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2023**: Successful completion of injection tests for the geothermal project, confirming the feasibility of the thermal water injection into a 130-meter thick sandstone layer. The project is part of the IW3 - Integrated Heat Transition Wilhelmsburg initiative, which aims to provide almost CO2-free heat to residential areas.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2023**: The expansion of the Wilhelmsburg heating network, which includes the new geothermal heating plant, is expected to create job opportunities related to the construction and operation of the facility, although specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the article.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2023**: The geothermal project is being developed by Hamburg Energie Geothermie GmbH, which is a wholly owned subsidiary of Hamburger Energiewerke GmbH, indicating full ownership of the facility by Hamburger Energiewerke GmbH.
