### Evolutionary Timeline of the Volkswagen Facility in Hanover, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2018**: Volkswagen confirmed that the Hanover facility would be converted to produce electric vehicles (EVs), specifically the I.D. Buzz family, alongside conventional vehicles starting in 2022.
- **June 2022**: Series production of the ID. Buzz began, with an initial target of producing up to 15,000 units in the first year, ramping up to a potential capacity of 130,000 vehicles annually.
- **July 2022**: Production was briefly halted due to quality issues with the battery from a new supplier but resumed shortly after.
- **July 2023**: The facility was prepared for additional variants of the ID. Buzz, including a long-wheelbase version and a GTX model with all-wheel drive.
- **June 2024**: The Hanover plant was set to introduce a new entry-level variant of the ID. Buzz with a smaller battery.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2018**: Volkswagen announced a total investment of €44 billion in future technologies, with a significant portion allocated to electric mobility, including the Hanover facility.
- **May 2022**: Approximately €1 billion was invested in the conversion of the Emden plant for electric vehicle production, which also indirectly supported the Hanover facility's transition.
- **March 2023**: VWCV planned to build 44,000 ID. Buzz vehicles in 2023 to meet growing demand and reduce delivery times.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2018**: Confirmation of the Hanover facility's conversion for EV production.
- **June 2022**: Start of series production for the ID. Buzz.
- **July 2022**: Production resumed after a brief halt due to battery quality issues.
- **July 2023**: Production of additional ID. Buzz variants began.
- **June 2024**: Planned introduction of a new entry-level variant of the ID. Buzz.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2018**: Employment assurance was provided for workers at the Hanover facility until 2028 during the transition to electric vehicle production.
- **June 2022**: Approximately 4,000 employees were trained for the new production processes related to the ID. Buzz.
- **March 2023**: VWCV aimed to ramp up production to meet demand, indicating potential job stability and growth.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Volkswagen facility in Hanover is fully owned by Volkswagen Commercial Vehicles (VWCV), which is a subsidiary of the Volkswagen Group. There are no indications of joint ventures or external ownership in the provided articles.
