### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mercedes-Benz Facility in Hedelfingen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **06/2019**: Construction begins on a new battery sub-plant at the Brühl site, part of the Untertürkheim plant, which includes the Hedelfingen section. The facility will assemble batteries for the EQ family of electric vehicles, covering an area of 12,000 square meters.
- **03/2021**: Daimler announces the start of battery production for the EQS at the Hedelfingen plant, which has a production area of approximately 16,500 square meters. The assembly integrates battery systems from purchased cells into existing transmission production lines.
- **12/2022**: The Hedelfingen plant continues to assemble battery systems for the EQS and EQE models, with a focus on the assembly of ready-to-install battery systems from purchased cells.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **06/2019**: Daimler announces an investment of over one billion euros in its global battery production network, which includes the Hedelfingen facility.
- **03/2021**: Daimler commits to a multi-million euro investment in the Mercedes-Benz Drive Systems Campus, which will include the Hedelfingen plant as a key site for electric drive and battery technology development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **06/2019**: Groundbreaking for the battery sub-plant at the Brühl site, which includes the Hedelfingen section.
- **03/2021**: Start of battery production for the EQS at the Hedelfingen plant.
- **07/2021**: The Hedelfingen plant is confirmed as a battery assembly site for the EQS and EQE models, with production processes integrated into existing operations.

#### 4. Labour
- **06/2019**: The establishment of the battery factory is part of a broader strategy to transition employees from traditional combustion engine production to electric vehicle technology, with a job security agreement in place until 2029.
- **03/2021**: The transition to electric vehicle production may lead to changes in employment profiles, as conventional engine production is gradually phased out.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Hedelfingen facility is part of the larger Mercedes-Benz Untertürkheim plant, which is wholly owned by Daimler AG. There is no indication of joint ventures or external ownership in the provided articles.
