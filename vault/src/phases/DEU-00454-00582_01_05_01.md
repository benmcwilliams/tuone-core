## DEU-00454-00582

### Evolutionary Timeline of Northvolt Facility in Heide, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2022**: Northvolt announces plans to build a battery factory named "Northvolt Drei" in Heide, Schleswig-Holstein, with an annual production capacity of 60 GWh, sufficient for approximately one million electric vehicles (EVs). The facility will also include a battery recycling plant.
- **May 2022**: Northvolt confirms the planned production start in 2025, with the facility expected to create around 3,000 jobs.
- **October 2023**: The EU Commission approves funding for the factory, confirming the production capacity of 60 GWh, with production expected to start in 2026 and reach full capacity by 2029.
- **March 2024**: Groundbreaking ceremony for the factory takes place, marking the official start of construction.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2022**: Northvolt receives IPCEI funding notification from the German government amounting to over €155 million, with 70% from the federal budget and 30% from the state of Schleswig-Holstein.
- **December 2023**: The German government allocates an additional €600 million for the factory through a convertible bond.
- **January 2024**: The EU Commission approves a total of €902 million in state aid for the factory, which includes a €700 million direct grant and a €202 million guarantee.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2022**: Announcement of the Northvolt Drei project.
- **May 2022**: Confirmation of funding and job creation estimates.
- **October 2023**: EU funding approval.
- **March 2024**: Groundbreaking ceremony for the factory.
- **January 2024**: Finalization of funding agreements and commitment to proceed with construction.
- **2026**: Expected start of production, with full capacity anticipated by 2029.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2022**: Northvolt announces the creation of approximately 3,000 jobs at the facility.
- **March 2024**: The construction phase begins, with ongoing commitments to job creation in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Northvolt is the sole owner of the facility, operating as an independent GmbH in Germany, separate from its Swedish parent company. The company has emphasized its financial independence and commitment to the project despite challenges faced in its broader operations.

### Summary
The Northvolt facility in Heide, Germany, is a significant clean technology project focused on battery production and recycling, with a planned capacity of 60 GWh. The project has received substantial financial backing from both the German government and the EU, with construction officially commencing in March 2024. The facility is expected to create around 3,000 jobs and play a crucial role in the European transition to sustainable energy and electric mobility.
