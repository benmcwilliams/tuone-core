## DEU-00454-00582

### Evolutionary Timeline of Northvolt Facility in Heide, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2022**: Northvolt announces plans to build a battery factory named "Northvolt Drei" in Heide, Schleswig-Holstein, with an annual production capacity of 60 GWh, sufficient for approximately one million electric vehicles (EVs).
- **May 2022**: Northvolt plans to include a battery recycling plant at the site to process by-products and old batteries, aiming to cover 50% of its raw material needs from recycling by 2030.
- **March 2024**: Groundbreaking ceremony for the factory takes place, with production expected to start in 2026 and full capacity reached by 2029.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2022**: Northvolt receives IPCEI funding notification from the German government amounting to over €155 million, with 70% from the federal budget and 30% from Schleswig-Holstein.
- **December 2023**: The German government allocates an additional €600 million in funding for the factory, including a convertible bond guarantee.
- **January 2024**: The EU approves a total of €902 million in state aid for the factory, which includes a €700 million direct grant and a €202 million guarantee.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2022**: Announcement of the Northvolt Drei factory.
- **May 2022**: Confirmation of funding from the German government.
- **March 2024**: Groundbreaking ceremony for the factory.
- **January 2024**: EU approval of funding for the factory.
- **2026**: Expected start of production.
- **2029**: Full production capacity anticipated.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2022**: Northvolt announces that the factory will create approximately 3,000 jobs in the region.
- **January 2024**: The project is expected to create thousands of additional jobs in the industry and service sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Northvolt is the sole owner of the facility, operating as an independent GmbH in Germany, separate from its Swedish parent company. 

### Summary
The Northvolt facility in Heide, Germany, is a significant project aimed at producing lithium-ion batteries with a capacity of 60 GWh, sufficient for one million EVs annually. The project has received substantial funding from both the German government and the EU, with a total investment projected at €4.5 billion. The factory is expected to create around 3,000 jobs and includes plans for a battery recycling plant. Despite challenges, including high energy prices and production delays, Northvolt remains committed to the project, with construction officially starting in March 2024 and production slated for 2026.
