## DEU-00454-01010

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Heide, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04/2020**: The facility is set to build a 30 megawatt (MW) hydrogen electrolysis plant as part of the WESTKUESTE 100 project. This plant will utilize electricity from wind turbines to produce carbon-free hydrogen, which will replace fossil fuel-based hydrogen at the Heide refinery. The hydrogen produced will also serve various industrial needs, including heat, kerosene, and plastics.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04/2020**: The total investment for the project is €89 million, with €30 million approved in funding from Germany's Federal Ministry of Economic Affairs and Energy. The remaining €59 million is being provided by the project partners.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04/2020**: The project receives funding approval, allowing work to commence on the first phase, which is expected to last five years. The initial phase involves the construction of the 30 MW electrolyser. Future plans include scaling the electrolysis capacity to 700 MW, powered by an offshore wind farm.

#### 4. Labour
- **04/2020**: Specific job creation or loss figures are not mentioned in the articles, but the project is expected to contribute to the decarbonization of industry and create a new green business model, which may imply job creation in the renewable energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **04/2020**: The facility is part of a newly formed joint venture named H2 Westkuste, which includes EDF Deutschland, Orsted, and Raffinerie Heide. This indicates that the facility is not owned exclusively by EDF but is a collaborative effort among multiple partners.
