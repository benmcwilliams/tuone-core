### Evolutionary Timeline of the Orsted Facility in Heide, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04/2020**: The facility is part of the Westkuste 100 initiative, which aims to produce green hydrogen using a 30 MW electrolyser powered by offshore wind energy. This project is designed to decarbonize industrial processes, mobility, and the heating market. The long-term vision includes scaling the electrolysis plant to 700 MW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04/2020**: The total investment for the Westkuste 100 project is €89 million, with €30 million approved in funding from Germany's Federal Ministry of Economic Affairs and Energy. The remaining €59 million is being provided by the project partners.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04/2020**: The project received go-ahead and funding approval, marking the start of the first phase, which is set to run for five years. The construction of the 30 MW electrolyser is part of this initial phase, with plans for future expansion to 700 MW.

#### 4. Labour
- **04/2020**: While specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the articles, the project is expected to create jobs related to the construction and operation of the electrolyser and the broader hydrogen production initiative.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **04/2020**: The facility is part of a newly formed joint venture called H2 Westkuste, which includes Orsted, EDF Deutschland, and Raffinerie Heide. This indicates that Orsted does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a collaborative partnership.
