### Evolutionary Timeline of the Thuega Facility in Heide, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **04-08-2020:** The Thuega facility, part of the Heide oil refinery project known as WESTKUESTE 100, announced the construction of a 30 megawatt (MW) hydrogen electrolysis plant. This facility aims to produce carbon-free hydrogen by using electricity from wind turbines to extract hydrogen from water. The hydrogen produced will replace fossil fuel-based hydrogen at the refinery and will also serve various raw material needs, including heat, kerosene, and plastics.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **04-08-2020:** The total investment for the hydrogen electrolysis plant is 89 million euros, which includes 30 million euros in funding from the German economy ministry and 59 million euros from the project partners. This investment is part of Germany's national hydrogen strategy, which aims to develop 5,000 MW of electrolysis capacity by 2030.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **04-08-2020:** The announcement of the construction of the 30 MW electrolysis plant marks a significant milestone in the development of hydrogen as a fuel in Germany. The project received approval for funding and is considered a game changer in the hydrogen sector.

4. **Labour:**
   - The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the Thuega facility at this time.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is being constructed through a newly founded joint-venture partnership involving multiple partners, including the privately-owned Heide refinery, EDF, Orsted, and Thuega, among others. This indicates that the facility is not exclusively owned by a single company but is a collaborative effort among several stakeholders.

### Technology and Operations
- "Companies involved in a hydrogen project at the Heide oil refinery near Hamburg said on Monday they would build a 30 megawatts electrolysis plant as a first step towards developing commercial-scale carbon-free energy."
- "The plant will pass electricity from wind turbines through water to extract carbon-free hydrogen that will be used by the Heide refinery instead of fossil fuel-based hydrogen."
- "The plant will also produce alternative hydrogen fuels for the raw material needs of the partners in the project ranging from heat to kerosene and plastics."

### Investment and Development
