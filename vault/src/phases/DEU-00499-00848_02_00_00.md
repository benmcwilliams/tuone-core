### Evolutionary Timeline of the Vulcan Energy Facility in Höchst Chemical Park, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2021**: Vulcan Energy announces the production of lithium hydroxide monohydrate (LHM) using geothermal energy for the first time. The facility will process lithium chloride from geothermal and sorption plants into lithium hydroxide monohydrate. The initial production capacity is set at **15,000 tonnes per year**, with plans for a second stage expansion to increase capacity by an additional **25,000 tonnes**, totaling **40,000 tonnes per year** by 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2023**: Vulcan Energy reports investments in a joint research well in collaboration with various stakeholders, including the City of Frankfurt and the Hessian Ministry of Economics, to test heat and lithium concentrations in the region. This investment is part of their strategy to enhance their dual lithium and geothermal energy production capabilities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2021**: Vulcan Energy announces the relocation of its planned central processing facility to Höchst Chemical Park, marking a significant milestone in the development of the Zero Carbon Lithium project. The first pilot plant is expected to go into operation by the end of 2021.
- **September 2023**: Vulcan Energy is granted a new exploration license in the Frankfurt region, which includes the Höchst Chemical Park, indicating a strong commitment to the area and its renewable energy goals.
