### Audi Facility in Ingolstadt, Germany: Evolutionary Timeline

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **03/2017**: Workers at Audi's Ingolstadt plant call for the production of electric vehicles (EVs) to be established at the main plant, emphasizing the need for electrification.
- **12/2018**: Audi management agrees to install a battery assembly line in Ingolstadt, enhancing the facility's capabilities for future electric vehicle production. The timeline for producing two new electric SUVs is set for 2021 and 2022.
- **03/2019**: Audi faces battery supply issues affecting the production of the e-tron SUV, which is manufactured in Brussels, indicating the reliance on external suppliers for battery components.
- **11/2019**: Audi announces plans to invest approximately €12 billion in electric mobility by 2024, with a focus on expanding its electric vehicle offerings.
- **02/2020**: Audi plans to assemble battery packs at its Ingolstadt facility, with cells supplied by LG Chem.
- **04/2020**: Audi confirms that the main plant in Ingolstadt will be converted to produce electric SUVs based on the new Premium Platform Electric (PPE), with the first model, the Q6 e-tron, expected to start production in 2023.
- **12/2021**: Audi announces that the Ingolstadt plant will only produce electric cars from 2028, marking a significant shift in its production strategy.
- **03/2024**: The Q6 e-tron, the first model based on the PPE platform, is unveiled, showcasing Audi's commitment to electric mobility.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12/2018**: Audi confirms an investment of over €14 billion for e-mobility advancements, including the establishment of a battery assembly line in Ingolstadt.
- **11/2019**: Audi announces a total investment of approximately €37 billion over five years for research, development, and infrastructure, with a significant portion allocated to electric mobility.
- **12/2021**: Audi plans to invest €500 million in employee training for the transition to electric vehicle production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2018**: Agreement to install a battery assembly line in Ingolstadt.
- **03/2020**: Audi begins preparations for the assembly of battery packs at the Ingolstadt facility.
- **12/2021**: Announcement that the Ingolstadt plant will transition to exclusively producing electric vehicles by 2028.
- **03/2024**: World premiere of the Q6 e-tron, marking the start of electric vehicle production at the Ingolstadt plant.

#### 4. Labour
- **12/2018**: The agreement to establish a battery assembly line guarantees at least 60,000 jobs at the Audi HQ until 2025.
- **11/2019**: Audi announces plans to cut 9,500 jobs by 2025, but also states that 2,000 new jobs will be created in the field of electric mobility.
- **06/2023**: Audi advertises 500 new jobs for the production of the Q6 e-tron, indicating a focus on job creation in the electric vehicle sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Audi operates the Ingolstadt facility as a wholly-owned subsidiary, with no indications of joint ventures or external ownership in the context of the facility's operations.

This timeline encapsulates the evolution of the Audi facility in Ingolstadt, highlighting its transition towards electric vehicle production, significant investments, and the impact on employment within the facility.
