## DEU-00516-00848

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Vulcan Energy Facility in Insheim, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2021**: Vulcan Energy announces plans to extract lithium from thermal water in the Upper Rhine Graben, with a pilot plant to be added to the Insheim geothermal power plant. The facility aims to produce 2,000 metric tons of lithium hydroxide annually, with commercial operations expected to start in 2024.
- **December 2021**: Vulcan Energy acquires the Insheim geothermal power plant, which has a capacity of 4.8 megawatts. The company plans to upgrade the plant to establish a test facility for low-carbon lithium production. The extraction process involves filtering lithium hydroxide from thermal water.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2021**: Vulcan Energy announces a capital raise of A$200 million (approximately $142.9 million) to finance the acquisition of the geothermal plant and the development of lithium extraction technology. The financial requirement for moving from the pilot plant to the first stage of commercial production (15,000 tonnes of lithium hydroxide per year) is estimated at €700 million, with an additional €400 million needed for the second stage expansion.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2021**: Vulcan Energy signs a long-term supply contract with LG Energy Solution (LGES) for lithium hydroxide, with plans to gradually increase the purchase volume from 5,000 to 10,000 metric tons per year.
- **August 2021**: Vulcan Energy plans a dual listing on the stock exchanges in Australia and Germany to enhance its international exposure.
- **December 2021**: Vulcan Energy completes the acquisition of the Insheim geothermal plant for €31.5 million ($35.7 million) and aims to start commercial lithium extraction in 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2021**: The acquisition and planned upgrades of the Insheim facility are expected to create jobs, although specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2021**: Vulcan Energy acquires full ownership of the Insheim geothermal power plant, previously owned by Pfalzwerke AG. The facility is now operated by Vulcan Energy through its German subsidiary, Vulkan Energie Ressourcen GmbH.
