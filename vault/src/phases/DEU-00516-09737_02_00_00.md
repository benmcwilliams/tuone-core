### Evolutionary Timeline of Pfalzwerke geofuture GmbH Facility in Insheim, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Operational Details**: The Insheim geothermal power plant has been operational for seven years as of November 2019. It operates at a thermal water temperature of 165°C, producing a maximum of 4.8 MWel (megawatt electrical) power and 10 MW thermal energy. The facility can supply electricity to approximately 8,000 households and heat to 600-800 households.
- **Current Production**: The facility is currently pumping lithium-rich brine to the surface for energy generation but is not processing or extracting the lithium, which is instead reinjected into the reservoir.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Partnership and Future Plans**: On November 27, 2019, Pfalzwerke geofuture GmbH signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with Vulcan Energy Resources to explore the feasibility of lithium extraction from geothermal brine. This partnership may lead to the construction of a demonstration plant at Insheim as part of a definitive feasibility study (DFS) for lithium extraction.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **MoU Signing**: On November 27, 2019, Pfalzwerke geofuture GmbH and Vulcan Energy Resources signed an MoU to collaborate on lithium extraction, marking a significant step towards potential future developments at the facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation Potential**: While specific job creation figures were not mentioned in the article, the establishment of a demonstration plant and potential commercial-scale lithium production could lead to job creation in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: Pfalzwerke geofuture GmbH is 100% owned by Pfalzwerke Gruppe, making it the exclusive owner of the Insheim geothermal facility.
