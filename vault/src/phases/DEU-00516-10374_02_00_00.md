## DEU-00516-10374

### Evolutionary Timeline of Vulcan Energy Resources Ltd. Facility in Insheim, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** November 2019
   - **Details:** Vulcan Energy Resources Ltd. signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with Pfalzwerke geofuture GmbH to explore the extraction of lithium from geothermal brine at the Insheim geothermal power plant. The facility currently pumps lithium-rich brine for energy generation but does not process or extract lithium, reinjecting the brine into the reservoir. The Insheim plant operates at a thermal water temperature of 165°C, producing a maximum of 4.8 MWel power and 10 MW thermal energy, supplying around 8,000 households with electricity and 600-800 households with heat.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** November 2019
   - **Details:** The MoU marks the beginning of a collaboration that will lead to a definitive feasibility study (DFS) for lithium extraction. Following the DFS, Pfalzwerke geofuture may co-contribute to the construction of a commercial-scale lithium plant or opt for a royalty on lithium production.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** November 2019
   - **Details:** The signing of the MoU initiates a feasibility study phase, with plans for Vulcan to construct and implement a demonstration plant at Insheim as part of the DFS.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** November 2019
   - **Details:** The announcement does not specify job creation or loss directly but indicates that the partnership aims to accelerate lithium production, which may imply future job opportunities related to the construction and operation of the planned lithium extraction facility.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** November 2019
   - **Details:** Pfalzwerke geofuture GmbH, which operates the Insheim geothermal plant, is 100% owned by Pfalzwerke Gruppe. Vulcan Energy Resources Ltd. will earn up to 80% of the lithium rights at Insheim through the completion of the DFS, indicating a joint venture structure for the lithium extraction project. 

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic partnerships related to the Vulcan Energy Resources Ltd. facility in Insheim, Germany, as of November 2019.
