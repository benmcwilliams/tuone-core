### Evolutionary Timeline of the Forschungszentrum Jülich GmbH Facility in Jülich, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2022**: Researchers at Forschungszentrum Jülich GmbH developed a self-referencing algorithm to detect and quantify underperformance in photovoltaic (PV) installations. This algorithm can be applied to inspect various components of PV systems, including modules, strings, arrays, inverters, and transformers. The facility operates a 9 MW PV power station that has been functional since 2011, featuring three-phase inverters across 1,719 strings.

- **February 2023**: The Institute of Energy and Climate Research IEK-5 Photovoltaik, part of Forschungszentrum Jülich GmbH, proposed a direct PV-battery coupling method as an alternative to maximum power point tracking (MPPT) for optimizing PV system performance. This method is applicable at the scale of a single PV module and aims to enhance efficiency and reduce costs associated with traditional MPPT systems.
