## DEU-00546-00596

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Opel Facility in Kaiserslautern, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **01/2019**: Announcement of a Franco-German initiative to establish a battery cell production facility involving PSA, Opel, and Saft. The Opel component plant in Kaiserslautern is identified as a potential production site for battery cells.
- **12/2019**: The EU Commission approves the establishment of a battery cell production facility at the Opel factory in Kaiserslautern, with a planned production capacity of 32 GWh, contributing to a total of 64 GWh when combined with a sister plant in France.
- **01/2020**: The German Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs announces a planned production capacity of 24 GWh at the Opel site, with the intention to build three blocks of 8 GWh each starting in 2023.
- **02/2020**: Opel Managing Director Michael Lohscheller confirms that the facility will produce battery cells capable of powering around 500,000 vehicles annually, with production expected to start in 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05/2019**: Initial investment for the battery cell production project is estimated at 5-6 billion euros, with the EU Commission awarding a maximum of 1.2 billion euros in funding.
- **12/2019**: The EU Commission approves subsidies totaling 3.2 billion euros for the battery cell production project, with Germany contributing 1.25 billion euros and France around 960 million euros.
- **02/2020**: A total investment of around 2 billion euros is confirmed for the Kaiserslautern facility, part of a larger investment of nearly 5 billion euros for the entire project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05/2019**: Announcement of the consortium and the need for EU funding to advance the project.
- **12/2019**: EU Commission gives the green light for the battery alliance, confirming the establishment of the facility in Kaiserslautern.
- **2023**: Construction of the new battery cell production plant at the Opel factory is scheduled to begin.
- **2024**: Production at the Kaiserslautern facility is expected to commence.

#### 4. Labour
- **12/2019**: The project is expected to create around 2,000 jobs at the Kaiserslautern facility, which is significant for the local economy, especially as the plant has faced uncertainty due to declining demand for combustion engine components.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **01/2020**: The facility is part of a joint venture called Automotive Cell Company (ACC) between PSA and Saft, with an initial 50:50 ownership structure. Saft's share is expected to decrease to 33% during the commercial production phase.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones related to the Opel facility in Kaiserslautern, Germany, focusing on its evolution in technology, investment, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
