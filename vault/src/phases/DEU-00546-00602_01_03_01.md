## DEU-00546-00602

### Evolutionary Timeline of the PSA Group Facility in Kaiserslautern, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **01-05-2019**: The Franco-German initiative for setting up a battery cell production facility is announced, with the Opel component plant in Kaiserslautern identified as a potential location for future battery cell production.
- **10-12-2019**: The EU Commission approves subsidies for 32 GWh battery cell production at the Opel factory in Kaiserslautern, contributing to a total production capacity of 64 GWh when combined with a sister plant in France.
- **08-02-2020**: The planned battery cell production capacity at the Opel site is confirmed to be 24 GWh, with production expected to start in 2024. The facility will produce battery cells for various Opel models and potentially for other manufacturers.
  
#### 2. Investment and Development
- **01-05-2019**: Initial investment for the battery cell production project is reported to be between five to six billion euros, with the EU Commission awarding a maximum of 1.2 billion euros for the consortium.
- **10-12-2019**: The German government promises 1.25 billion euros and France contributes around 960 million euros towards the project.
- **08-02-2020**: A total investment of around two billion euros is confirmed for the Kaiserslautern facility, with the overall project investment nearing five billion euros.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **01-05-2019**: Announcement of the Franco-German battery consortium involving PSA, Opel, and Saft.
- **10-12-2019**: EU Commission gives the green light for the battery alliance, approving subsidies for the Kaiserslautern facility.
- **08-02-2020**: The German Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs confirms the planned production capacity and timeline, with construction expected to start in 2023 and production in 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **10-12-2019**: The new plant is expected to create around 2,000 jobs in Kaiserslautern, providing a significant boost to local employment.
- **08-02-2020**: The announcement of job creation is reiterated, emphasizing the importance of the project for future-proof jobs in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **08-02-2020**: The joint venture called ACC (Automotive Cell Company) is established between PSA, Opel, and Saft, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by PSA. The joint venture aims to pool resources and expertise for battery cell production in Europe.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the PSA Group facility in Kaiserslautern, Germany, focusing on its evolution in technology, investment, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
