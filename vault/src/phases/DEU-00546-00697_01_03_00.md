## DEU-00546-00697

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Saft Facility in Kaiserslautern, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2019**: The German government, through the BMBF, announced plans for a research factory for lithium-ion cell production, with Kaiserslautern as a potential location.
- **December 2019**: The EU Commission approved subsidies for a battery cell production facility at the Opel factory in Kaiserslautern, with a production capacity of 32 GWh. This facility is part of a consortium with PSA and Saft, aiming for a total capacity of 64 GWh across two plants (one in Germany and one in France).
- **2023**: Construction of the new plant at the Opel factory in Kaiserslautern is set to begin.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2019**: The project received significant financial backing, with the EU Commission approving a total of 3.2 billion euros for the battery cell production initiative. Germany committed 1.25 billion euros, while France contributed around 960 million euros.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2019**: The decision regarding the location for the research factory was anticipated, with Kaiserslautern being a strong candidate.
- **December 2019**: The EU Commission officially approved the project, allowing for the construction of the battery cell production facility in Kaiserslautern to commence in 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2019**: The establishment of the new plant is expected to create around 2,000 jobs, providing a significant boost to local employment in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2019**: The facility is part of a consortium involving Saft, PSA, and Opel, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by any single entity. Saft, as a French battery cell manufacturer, plays a key role in providing technology for the facility.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Saft facility in Kaiserslautern, highlighting its technological focus, financial backing, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
