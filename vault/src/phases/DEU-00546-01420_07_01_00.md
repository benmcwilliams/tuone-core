## DEU-00546-01420

### Evolutionary Timeline of the TotalEnergies Facility in Kaiserslautern, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Production Capacity:** The facility is set to produce battery cells with a planned capacity of 24 GWh, which will enable the powering of around 500,000 vehicles annually. The average battery size is expected to be 48 kWh per vehicle.
   - **Updates:** The facility is part of a larger consortium that includes a parallel factory in France, which will also have a capacity of 24 GWh, leading to a total production capacity of 48 GWh when combined.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Investment Amount:** Approximately two billion euros will be invested in the Kaiserslautern facility as part of the battery production project.
   - **Total Investment Volume:** The total investment for the entire project, including the French facility, is nearly five billion euros.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Announcement Date:** The project was announced on 08-02-2020.
   - **Construction Start:** Production at the Kaiserslautern facility is scheduled to start in 2024, following pilot production that began in January 2020 at the Saft site in France.
   - **Joint Venture Formation:** The joint venture called ACC (Automotive Cell Company) was established to develop activities for the production of electric vehicle batteries in Europe, receiving EU Commission approval shortly after the announcement.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Job Creation:** The project is expected to create around 2,000 jobs at the Kaiserslautern site.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Joint Venture:** The facility is part of a joint venture involving TotalEnergies (through its subsidiary Saft), PSA, and Opel, indicating that TotalEnergies does not own the facility exclusively but is a key partner in the joint venture.
