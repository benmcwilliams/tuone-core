## DEU-00546-01424

### Evolutionary Timeline of the ACC Facility in Kaiserslautern, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Initial Plans**: The ACC facility in Kaiserslautern is part of a joint venture aimed at producing lithium-ion battery cells for electric vehicles. The initial production capacity was set at **8 GWh**, with plans to expand to **24 GWh** by 2030 (Article 2, September 2020).
- **Expansion Plans**: In March 2022, it was announced that the capacity would be upgraded to **40 GWh** as part of a broader strategy to reach a total of **120 GWh** across all ACC facilities by 2030 (Article 5, March 2022).
- **Current Status**: As of November 2023, construction work on the facility has been paused to switch to more cost-effective battery technologies due to declining demand for electric vehicles (Article 11, June 2024).

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Initial Investment**: The project received a total investment commitment of **€5 billion**, with **€1.3 billion** in financial support from French and German authorities (Article 2, September 2020).
- **Funding Notifications**: In September 2021, ACC received funding notifications totaling **€436.8 million** from the German government to support the construction of the facility (Article 3, September 2021).
- **Debt Financing**: In February 2024, ACC secured a loan of **€4.4 billion** to finance the construction of its gigafactories, including the one in Kaiserslautern (Article 10, February 2024).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Joint Venture Formation**: The ACC joint venture was officially established in September 2020 (Article 2, September 2020).
- **Construction Timeline**: The construction of the facility was initially scheduled to start in 2023, with production expected to begin in **2025** (Article 3, September 2021).
- **Current Pause**: As of June 2024, construction has been put on hold to reassess the technology and market needs (Article 11, June 2024).

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The facility is expected to create around **2,000 jobs** upon completion, with production capacity aimed at supporting battery cells for approximately **500,000 electric vehicles** annually (Article 3, September 2021).
- **Impact of Pause**: The current pause in construction may affect job creation timelines, but specific job losses have not been reported as of now.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Ownership**: The ACC facility is a joint venture owned by Stellantis, Mercedes-Benz, and TotalEnergies/Saft. As of March 2024, the ownership structure is **45% Stellantis**, **30% Mercedes-Benz**, and **25% Saft** (Article 10, February 2024).
