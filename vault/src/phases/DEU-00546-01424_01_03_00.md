## DEU-00546-01424

### Evolutionary Timeline of the ACC Facility in Kaiserslautern, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **01-05-2019**: The facility is identified as a potential site for battery cell production as part of a Franco-German initiative involving PSA, Opel, and Saft. The production aims to focus on large-format and sustainable battery cells.
- **10-12-2019**: The EU Commission approves subsidies for a total production capacity of 64 GWh, with 32 GWh allocated to the Opel factory in Kaiserslautern. Construction is set to begin in 2023.
- **08-02-2020**: The planned production capacity at the Opel site is confirmed to be 24 GWh, with the facility expected to produce battery cells for approximately 500,000 vehicles annually.
- **12-09-2021**: The joint venture ACC aims for a total capacity of 48 GWh by 2030, with initial production blocks of 8 GWh planned.
- **10-01-2023**: The facility is expected to start production in 2025, with a total capacity of 40 GWh planned.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **01-05-2019**: Initial investments of 5-6 billion euros are proposed for the battery cell production initiative.
- **10-12-2019**: The German government commits 1.25 billion euros, while France contributes around 960 million euros to support the project.
- **12-09-2021**: ACC secures funding notifications totaling 436.8 million euros from the German government for the construction of the battery cell factory.
- **22-02-2024**: ACC secures a loan of 4.4 billion euros to finance the construction of three gigafactories, including the one in Kaiserslautern.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **01-05-2019**: Announcement of the Franco-German battery cell production initiative.
- **10-12-2019**: EU Commission approval for the battery alliance, including the Kaiserslautern facility.
- **12-09-2021**: Official establishment of the Automotive Cells Company (ACC) for battery cell production.
- **10-01-2023**: Construction of the facility is set to begin in 2023.
- **23-06-2024**: Construction work on the Kaiserslautern plant is paused to switch to more cost-effective battery technologies.
- **27-09-2024**: The Italian government withdraws funding commitment for the ACC plant in Termoli, affecting the overall project timeline.

#### 4. Labour
- **10-12-2019**: The project is expected to create around 2,000 jobs at the Kaiserslautern facility.
- **12-09-2021**: The construction and operation of the facility are projected to secure future jobs in the region.
- **23-06-2024**: The pause in construction raises concerns about job security and future employment opportunities at the site.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **12-09-2021**: The ACC joint venture is established with equal shares among Stellantis, Total (Saft), and Mercedes-Benz, each holding 33% of the company.
- **22-02-2024**: Following a capital increase, Stellantis owns 45% of ACC, while Mercedes-Benz holds 30% and Saft retains 25%.

This timeline captures the key developments and changes related to the ACC facility in Kaiserslautern, Germany, focusing on its technology, investments, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure.
