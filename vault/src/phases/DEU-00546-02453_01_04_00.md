## DEU-00546-02453

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Stellantis Group Facility in Kaiserslautern, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2021**: Stellantis announced plans for a battery cell factory in Kaiserslautern, with production originally scheduled to start in 2024. However, this was later pushed back to the end of 2025. The factory is part of a joint venture called Automotive Cells Company (ACC) with Total and Mercedes-Benz, aimed at producing battery cells and modules.
- **July 2021**: Stellantis confirmed that the Kaiserslautern facility is part of a broader strategy to electrify its vehicle lineup, with a target of 98% of models in Europe being electrified by 2025.
- **November 2021**: Stellantis entered a binding lithium offtake agreement with Vulcan Energy, which is part of its electrification strategy, indicating the facility's role in the supply chain for battery production.
- **September 2022**: Construction work at the Kaiserslautern facility was paused to switch from nickel-based cell chemistry to more cost-effective battery technologies.
- **July 2024**: The facility's construction was still on hold as Stellantis aimed to improve plans for the production facility, focusing on new technologies for battery cells and modules.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2021**: The project received financial support of €1.3 billion from French and German authorities as part of an Important Project of Common European Interest (IPCEI).
- **July 2021**: Stellantis announced an investment of over €30 billion in electrification and software, which includes the development of the Kaiserslautern facility.
- **July 2024**: The Italian government pressured Stellantis to commit to the ACC factory in Kaiserslautern, indicating the importance of public funding and investment in the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: Initial announcement of the battery cell factory in Kaiserslautern with a production start originally planned for 2024.
- **April 2021**: Production start date was pushed back to the end of 2025.
- **September 2022**: Construction paused to switch to more cost-effective battery technologies.
- **July 2024**: Ongoing pressure from the Italian government for Stellantis to commit to the factory's plans.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2021**: The establishment of the facility is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers were not detailed in the articles.
- **July 2024**: The ongoing uncertainty regarding the facility's construction and operation may impact job creation, as the Italian government expressed concerns over public funding and commitments.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2021**: The facility is part of the Automotive Cells Company (ACC), a joint venture between Stellantis, Total, and Mercedes-Benz, indicating that Stellantis does not own the facility exclusively but is a major stakeholder in the joint venture.

This timeline captures the key developments and changes related to the Stellantis Group facility in Kaiserslautern, Germany, focusing on its technology, investments, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.

### Technology and Operations
- **Production Start Date**: "The battery factory in Douvrin, northern France, will start production at the end of 2023 as planned. The second factory at the Opel site in Kaiserslautern, on the other hand, will not start production until the end of 2025." (Article 1, 18-04-2021)
- **Initial Production Plans**: "The start of production in Kaiserslautern was originally planned for 2024 but has now been pushed back by at least one year." (Article 1, 18-04-2021)
