### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mercedes-Benz Facility in Kamenz, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2012**: The first battery assembly facility (Plant 1) in Kamenz begins production of drive batteries for hybrid and electric vehicles.
- **2018**: A second battery production facility (Plant 2) is inaugurated, quadrupling the production and logistics area to a total of 80,000 square meters. This facility is designed to produce battery packs for the upcoming EQ series and other electric models.
- **2020**: Production capacity is increased to exceed 500,000 battery systems per year, preparing for future electric EQ models in the compact segment.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2016**: Daimler announces a total investment of €14.5 billion in research and development, with a significant portion allocated for the expansion of the Kamenz battery facility.
- **2016**: Daimler invests €500 million in the second battery production facility at Kamenz, aiming to make it one of the largest and most modern battery factories in Europe.
- **2018**: An additional investment of €1 billion is earmarked for the global battery production network, with a substantial portion directed to the Kamenz facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2016**: Announcement of the expansion plans for the Kamenz facility as part of Daimler's electrification strategy.
- **October 2016**: Groundbreaking for the second battery production facility at Kamenz.
- **April 2017**: Official groundbreaking ceremony attended by German Chancellor Angela Merkel.
- **January 2018**: Completion of the second battery factory is anticipated, with production ramping up for the EQC model.
- **November 2018**: The workforce at the Kamenz plant is set to double to 1,000 employees ahead of schedule.
- **2020**: The facility is prepared to produce over 500,000 battery systems annually.

#### 4. Labour
- **2016**: Initial job creation is anticipated with the expansion of the Kamenz facility.
- **2018**: The workforce is doubled to 1,000 employees, reflecting the ramp-up in production capacity and the need for skilled labor in battery assembly.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Kamenz facility is owned exclusively by Daimler AG through its subsidiary, Accumotive. There are no indications of joint ventures or shared ownership for this specific facility.
