### Evolutionary Timeline of Hynamics Facility in Karlsruhe, Germany

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** November 2020
   - **Details:** Hynamics, a subsidiary of EDF, established Hynamics Deutschland to provide green hydrogen solutions in Germany. The facility focuses on the design, investment, construction, and operation of electrolysis units. Hynamics has a track record in electrolysis and fuel cell technologies, leveraging 20 years of experience from its hydrogen competence center in Karlsruhe.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** November 2020
   - **Details:** The establishment of Hynamics Deutschland is part of EDF's strategy to expand its hydrogen generation business across Europe. The Westküste 100 project, which Hynamics is involved in, is partly funded by the German Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs and Energy, indicating governmental support for hydrogen initiatives.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** November 2020
   - **Details:** Hynamics is actively participating in the Westküste 100 project, which includes plans to build a 30 MW electrolyzer at the Heide refinery, with a vision to expand to at least 700 MW in subsequent phases. This project is a significant milestone in Hynamics' commitment to hydrogen production.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** November 2020
   - **Details:** The establishment of Hynamics Deutschland and its involvement in projects like Westküste 100 is expected to contribute to job creation in the hydrogen sector, although specific job numbers were not mentioned in the article.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** November 2020
   - **Details:** Hynamics Deutschland is a subsidiary of Hynamics, which is part of the EDF group. This indicates that the facility is not owned exclusively by Hynamics but is part of a larger corporate structure under EDF.
