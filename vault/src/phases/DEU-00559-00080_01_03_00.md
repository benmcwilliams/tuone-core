### Evolutionary Timeline of the BMZ Facility in Karlstein, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2016**: BMZ inaugurated two new buildings in Karlstein-Großwelzheim, aiming to become Europe’s largest battery maker. The facility is set to produce up to 800 million Li-ion batteries with a total storage volume of around 40 GWh per year by 2020. The focus includes pedelec batteries and the development of a super cell prototype.
  
- **September 2018**: BMZ announced the construction of an additional production hall measuring 3,000 sqm and a new logistics center, aimed at producing high-tech battery products. The expansion is part of a response to increasing demand for their battery systems across various electric vehicles.

- **November 2018**: BMZ plans to install volume production of battery cells in Germany by 2020, taking over the TerraE consortium to enter the automotive battery market. The facility aims for a production capacity of 34 GWh by 2028.

- **July 2019**: BMZ and Kion established a joint venture, Kion Battery Systems, to produce lithium-ion batteries specifically for Kion products. The first batteries are expected to come off the production line in the second quarter of 2020.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2018**: BMZ earmarked €120 million for the expansion of its battery factory in Karlstein, which includes the construction of a new production hall and logistics center. This investment is part of a broader strategy to meet the growing demand for battery systems.

- **November 2018**: BMZ committed to invest €300 million in the first stage of the TerraE expansion, targeting a 4 GWh output for battery cell production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2016**: Inauguration of new production and logistics facilities.
  
- **September 2018**: Announcement of the construction of a new production hall and logistics center, with completion expected in September 2019.

- **October 2019**: Completion of the logistics hall, which has space for 1,200 pallets.

- **July 2019**: Formation of the joint venture Kion Battery Systems, with production expected to start in the second quarter of 2020.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2018**: The new production hall is expected to create 300 jobs in battery manufacturing in Europe.

- **July 2019**: The joint venture with Kion is projected to employ around 80 people, primarily in research, development, and production.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2019**: BMZ and Kion each hold 50% ownership in the newly formed joint venture, Kion Battery Systems. Prior to this, BMZ operated the facility independently.
