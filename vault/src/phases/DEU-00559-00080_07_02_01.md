### Evolutionary Timeline of the BMZ Facility in Karlstein, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2018**: BMZ announces a €120 million investment to expand its battery factory for various electric vehicles in Karlstein. This includes the construction of an additional production hall of 3,000 sqm and a new logistics center. The expansion aims to meet increasing demand for battery systems used in electric vehicles, including cranes, excavators, buses, electric bikes, and robot lawn mowers.
- **July 2019**: BMZ and Kion establish a joint venture named Kion Battery Systems to produce lithium-ion batteries. The first batteries are expected to come off the production line in the second quarter of 2020. The facility will primarily serve Kion's products, with cells sourced from Asia.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2018**: BMZ allocates €120 million for the expansion of its Karlstein facility, which includes the new production hall and logistics center.
- **November 2018**: BMZ commits to an additional €300 million for the first stage of expansion of the TerraE consortium, aimed at establishing a battery cell production facility with an initial output of 4 GWh.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2019**: Completion of the expansion of the new production hall and logistics center is expected.
- **Second Quarter 2020**: The first batteries from the joint venture with Kion are anticipated to be produced.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2018**: The expansion of the facility is projected to create 300 jobs in battery manufacturing in Europe.
- **July 2019**: The joint venture with Kion is expected to employ around 80 people, primarily in research, development, and production.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2019**: The joint venture Kion Battery Systems is established, with both BMZ and Kion holding 50% ownership each. This indicates a shared ownership structure for the new battery production initiative.
