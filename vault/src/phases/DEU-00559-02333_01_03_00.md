### Kion Group Facility in Karlstein, DEU - Evolutionary Timeline

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **11/2019**: Kion Group announces the establishment of a joint venture with BMZ Group to produce lithium-ion batteries in Germany, specifically for Kion products. The facility aims to replace lead-acid batteries with lithium-ion technology, which offers better charging times, service life, and energy efficiency.
- **12/2020**: Kion Battery Systems (KBS) officially starts production of lithium-ion batteries for industrial trucks in a newly built production hall of approximately 4,000 square meters. The facility has the capacity to manufacture over 12,000 batteries annually, specifically for heavy-duty forklifts and industrial trucks.
- **2021**: KBS expands its production capacity by commissioning a second production line, increasing total annual production capacity to 30,000 batteries. The facility begins producing 24-volt batteries for mobile warehouse equipment in addition to existing 48-volt and 80-volt batteries for counterbalance trucks.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **11/2019**: The joint venture between Kion and BMZ is formed to facilitate the establishment of the battery production facility, indicating a significant investment in clean technology.
- **12/2020**: The production start at KBS marks a substantial investment in the facility, with plans to expand capacity to meet growing demand in the intralogistics market.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **11/2019**: Announcement of the joint venture and plans for the battery production facility.
- **12/2020**: Official start of production at KBS in Karlstein.
- **2021**: Expansion of production capacity with the commissioning of a second production line.

#### 4. Labour
- **11/2019**: It is projected that the joint venture will employ around 80 people by 2023.
- **12/2020**: The employment forecast is revised to 150 people by 2023, reflecting the growth in production and operations at the facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **11/2019**: Kion Group and BMZ Group each hold 50% ownership of the joint venture, Kion Battery Systems, indicating a joint venture ownership structure for the facility.
