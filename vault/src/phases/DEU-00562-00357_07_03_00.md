### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hexagon Purus Facility in Kassel, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **30-09-2021**: Hexagon Purus, a Norwegian company, announces its intention to take over Wystrach, a German supplier specializing in high-pressure systems and hydrogen technology. The Kassel facility is identified as the European production site for tanks, where Hexagon Purus produces high-pressure tanks, fuel storage and distribution systems, and battery packs for commercial vehicles. The takeover aims to enhance capabilities in hydrogen technology, with Wystrach's facility in Weeze becoming the main assembly site for hydrogen systems, while container production remains in Kassel.
  
- **24-11-2021**: Hexagon Purus signs a long-term agreement with a European bus manufacturer (unnamed) to supply hydrogen tanks for 12- and 18-meter fuel cell buses. The production of these tanks will occur at the Kassel facility, with deliveries scheduled from 2021 to 2024. The order is valued at approximately €30 million.

- **02-11-2022**: Hexagon Purus signs a four-year supply agreement with CaetanoBus to provide hydrogen fuel systems for fuel-cell transit buses. The estimated potential value of this agreement is €35 million, with production taking place at the Kassel facility.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **30-09-2021**: The acquisition of Wystrach is valued at up to €43.3 million, indicating a significant investment aimed at enhancing the capabilities of the Kassel facility in hydrogen technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **30-09-2021**: Announcement of the takeover of Wystrach, expected to close in Q4 2021, subject to approvals.
- **24-11-2021**: Signing of a long-term supply agreement for hydrogen tanks, marking a significant milestone in the facility's operations.
- **02-11-2022**: Signing of a four-year supply agreement with CaetanoBus, further solidifying the facility's role in hydrogen fuel system production.
