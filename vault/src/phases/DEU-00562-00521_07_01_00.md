### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mercedes-Benz Facility in Kassel, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **31-07-2021**: The Mercedes-Benz plant in Kassel is designated to expand its focus on commercial vehicle axles and become a competence center for electric drive systems. This aligns with Daimler Truck's strategy to specialize in different components of electrified drives across its plants.
- **13-11-2024**: The facility begins production of the new generation of the e-axle for the eActros 600. The plant assembles the complete electric drive systems, including the newly designed electrically driven rear axle, which features innovations for higher performance and efficiency. A new assembly line is established for this purpose, including test and inspection stations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **31-07-2021**: Major investments are announced to accompany the realignment of production strategies across the Daimler Truck facilities, including Kassel, to support the development of electric drive components and battery systems.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **31-07-2021**: Announcement of the facility's future orientation and specialization in electric drive systems as part of a broader strategy involving multiple plants.
- **13-11-2024**: Official production start for central components of the new Mercedes-Benz eActros 600 at the Kassel plant, marking a significant milestone in the facility's evolution towards electric vehicle components.

#### 4. Labour
- **31-07-2021**: The transition towards electric drive systems and the establishment of competence centers is expected to create optimum conditions for job security and potentially increase employment opportunities, although specific job creation numbers are not mentioned.
- **13-11-2024**: The facility employs approximately 2,700 workers, contributing to its status as one of the largest commercial vehicle axle plants in Europe and the largest industrial employer in Kassel.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned exclusively by Daimler Truck, as indicated by the references to the company's strategic decisions and investments in the plant's future.
