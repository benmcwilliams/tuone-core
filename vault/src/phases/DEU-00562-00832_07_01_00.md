### Evolutionary Timeline of the Volkswagen Facility in Kassel, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2016**: Volkswagen announces that electric motors will be built in Kassel as part of its expansion into electric mobility.
- **September 2018**: The Kassel facility is confirmed to be involved in the production of components for the ID. family of electric vehicles, including the ID.3.
- **July 2019**: The facility begins producing electric drives for MEB vehicles, with a capacity of up to 500,000 units per year.
- **April 2022**: Volkswagen announces plans to expand the Kassel site to become a key supplier for e-mobility, doubling the number of production lines for e-motors and other components.
- **March 2023**: A new competence center for power electronics is opened at the Kassel site, focusing on the development of pulse inverters for e-drives.
- **November 2023**: The facility faces production issues due to ramp-up problems with the new APP550 electric motor, affecting output.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2018**: Volkswagen announces a total investment of €6 billion for e-mobility, with €1.3 billion allocated to the Kassel facility and other sites for the production of electric vehicle components.
- **April 2022**: Volkswagen commits to investing around €1.2 billion in the Kassel site by 2026 to expand its capabilities for electric components.
- **March 2023**: The Kassel facility is set to become the group-wide competence center for electric drive systems, indicating a strategic investment in future technologies.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2016**: Announcement of electric motor production in Kassel.
- **September 2018**: Confirmation of Kassel's role in the production of components for the ID. family.
- **July 2019**: Start of pre-series production of electric drives.
- **April 2022**: Announcement of plans to expand the facility into a key supplier for e-mobility.
- **March 2023**: Opening of a new competence center for power electronics.
- **November 2023**: Production issues reported due to ramp-up problems with the APP550 motor.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2016**: Volkswagen announces a reduction of 30,000 jobs but commits to creating 9,000 new jobs in electric mobility, indicating a shift in workforce needs.
- **April 2022**: The Kassel facility employs around 16,000 people, with a quarter of the workforce engaged in e-mobility.
- **November 2023**: A temporary hiring freeze is implemented at the Kassel site due to ongoing efficiency programs.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Volkswagen facility in Kassel is owned exclusively by Volkswagen Group Components, which operates as an independent business unit within the Volkswagen Group. The facility is being transformed to focus solely on electric vehicle components, indicating a strategic shift in ownership focus towards e-mobility.

This timeline captures the key developments and changes at the Volkswagen facility in Kassel, Germany, highlighting its evolution towards becoming a central hub for electric vehicle component production.
