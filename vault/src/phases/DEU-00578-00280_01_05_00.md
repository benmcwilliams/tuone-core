## DEU-00578-00280

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fortum Facility in Kirchardt, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2023**: Fortum Battery Recycling commenced operations at its facility in Kirchardt, Baden-Württemberg, focusing on the recycling of traction batteries from electric vehicles. The facility is capable of processing over 3,000 tons of batteries annually, utilizing a combination of mechanical and hydrometallurgical technologies to recover 95% of valuable metals from the battery's black mass.
- **April 2023**: The facility began commercial operations, marking it as Fortum's first German recycling plant. The operations include pre-treatment services and the production of black mass, which is then sent to Fortum's hydrometallurgical plant in Harjavalta, Finland, for further processing.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: Fortum announced investments in the mid double-digit million range for the establishment of the Kirchardt facility, although specific figures were not disclosed. The facility is part of a broader strategy to expand battery recycling capabilities in Europe.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2023**: Fortum Battery Recycling officially started its first commercial operations in Kirchardt.
- **April 2023**: The facility was confirmed to be operational and processing batteries, with environmental approval under the Federal Immission Control Act.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2023**: Fortum announced plans to create approximately 70 jobs at the Kirchardt facility, contributing to local employment and economic development.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Fortum Battery Recycling operates as a subsidiary of the Finnish energy company Fortum, indicating that Fortum has full ownership of the Kirchardt facility.
