## DEU-00607-00521

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mercedes-Benz Facility in Kuppenheim, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2021**: Daimler considers building a research facility for battery recycling at the Kuppenheim site, focusing on dismantling batteries to recover raw materials.
- **November 2021**: Daimler confirms plans to build a battery recycling factory in Kuppenheim, with production start planned for 2023.
- **March 2022**: The facility will utilize a hydrometallurgical process with a recycling rate of 96%. The pilot plant is expected to have an annual capacity of 2,500 tons.
- **March 2023**: Foundation stone laid for the battery recycling plant, with the first stage (mechanical dismantling) expected to start commissioning by the end of 2023.
- **October 2024**: The facility officially opens, marking it as the first integrated mechanical-hydrometallurgical battery recycling plant in Europe, capable of processing 2,500 tons annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2022**: Mercedes plans to invest a double-digit million amount in the construction of the pilot factory, with funding from the German Federal Ministry of Economics and Climate Protection.
- **April 2022**: The German Federal Ministry announces funding of 16.66 million euros for the LiBinfinity project, which includes the Kuppenheim facility.
- **March 2023**: Mercedes announces an unspecified investment in the double-digit millions for the construction of the recycling plant.
- **January 2024**: Primobius awarded an 18.8 million euro contract to build the hydrometallurgical refinery at Kuppenheim.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2021**: Initial consideration for a battery recycling facility.
- **November 2021**: Confirmation of the recycling plant's location and production start date.
- **March 2022**: Detailed plans for the recycling process and capacity announced.
- **March 2023**: Foundation stone laid for the plant; mechanical dismantling to start by the end of 2023.
- **October 2024**: Official opening of the battery recycling factory.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2021**: Local mayor estimates around 1,000 new jobs could be created if the project is successful, though Daimler anticipates 50 jobs for the pilot plant and 150 for regular operations.
- **March 2023**: Job creation details remain unclear, but the facility is expected to contribute to local employment.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2021**: Licular GmbH, a joint venture between Mercedes-Benz AG and Daimler Truck Holding AG, is established to manage the battery recycling operations at Kuppenheim, with each holding a 50% share.
