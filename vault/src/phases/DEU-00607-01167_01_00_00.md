## DEU-00607-01167

### Evolutionary Timeline of Licular GmbH Facility in Kuppenheim, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2021**: Daimler announced plans to build a battery recycling factory in Kuppenheim, Germany, with production start planned for 2023, depending on discussions with public authorities. The facility aims to create and secure recycling capacities for electric vehicle batteries, with a focus on second-life applications before recycling.
- **July 2022**: The pilot recycling plant at the Mercedes-Benz site in Kuppenheim is confirmed to have an annual capacity of 2,500 tonnes. The facility will utilize a mechanical-hydrometallurgical process to enhance recycling efficiency, avoiding energy-intensive steps.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2022**: The German Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs and Climate Protection (BMWK) announced funding of 16.66 million euros for the LiBinfinity project, which Licular GmbH leads. This funding supports the development of a holistic recycling approach for lithium-ion batteries.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2021**: Daimler confirmed the establishment of Licular GmbH, a joint venture between Mercedes-Benz Group AG and Daimler Truck Holding AG, to manage battery recycling efforts.
- **July 2022**: The construction of the pilot recycling plant in Kuppenheim is officially underway, with the project partners aiming to develop innovative recycling processes.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2021**: Initial estimates suggested that around 1,000 new jobs could be created in the district if the project is successful. However, Daimler anticipated approximately 50 jobs for the pilot plant and 150 jobs for regular operations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2021**: Licular GmbH is a joint venture, with Mercedes-Benz Group AG and Daimler Truck Holding AG each holding a 50% share in the company. 

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Licular GmbH facility in Kuppenheim, Germany, focusing on its technology, investments, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
