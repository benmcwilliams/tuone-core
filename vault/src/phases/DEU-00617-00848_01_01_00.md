## DEU-00617-00848

### Evolutionary Timeline of Vulcan Energy Facility in Landau, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2023**: Vulcan Energy announces the opening of the Lithium Extraction Optimization Plant (LEOP) in Landau, which will utilize geothermal resources to extract CO2-neutral lithium. The facility is designed to produce lithium chloride, which will be processed into lithium hydroxide at a separate facility in Frankfurt-Höchst.
- **April 2024**: Vulcan Energy begins production of lithium chloride at the LEOP, marking the first lithium produced entirely in Europe. The facility operates at a scale of 1:50 compared to the planned commercial lithium extraction plant.
- **November 2024**: Vulcan produces lithium hydroxide for the first time at the Central Lithium Electrolysis Optimization Plant (CLEOP) in Frankfurt-Höchst, using lithium chloride sourced from the LEOP in Landau.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2023**: The Landau City Council approves the construction of the geothermal lithium plant, which is part of Vulcan's Zero Carbon Lithium project. This approval is crucial for the first phase of the project, which aims for a production capacity of 24,000 tonnes of lithium hydroxide per year.
- **April 2024**: Vulcan announces a partnership with ABB to optimize production processes for the lithium extraction project, indicating a strategic investment in technology and efficiency.
- **November 2024**: Vulcan secures federal funding for the HEAT4LANDAU Project, which includes infrastructure for geothermal heat generation, part of the broader Phase One Lionheart project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2023**: Opening of the LEOP in Landau.
- **December 2023**: Approval from the Landau City Council for the construction of the geothermal lithium plant.
- **April 2024**: Start of lithium chloride production at the LEOP.
- **November 2024**: Production of lithium hydroxide begins at CLEOP in Frankfurt-Höchst.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2024**: The LEOP facility is utilized for training the operating team, indicating job creation related to the operational phase of the facility. Specific job numbers are not mentioned, but the expansion and operational phases typically involve hiring.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Vulcan Energy appears to have full ownership of the LEOP facility in Landau, as there are no mentions of joint ventures or partnerships regarding ownership. The company is actively involved in all aspects of the project, from extraction to processing.
