### Evolutionary Timeline of the Akasol Facility in Langen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05/2017**: Akasol opens a battery plant in Langen with an initial production capacity of 300 MWh annually, capable of manufacturing storage systems for up to 1,500 electric buses. The facility is designed for serial assembly of battery systems for European bus manufacturers, with potential to expand to 600 MWh.
- **04/2018**: Akasol announces it will deliver all battery systems for Daimler’s Citaro electric bus, with production starting at the Langen facility. The batteries can deliver a maximum of 243 kWh and are designed for fast-charging.
- **07/2018**: Akasol begins work on next-generation power packs for the eCitaro, aiming for a capacity of 330 kWh while maintaining the same size.
- **03/2020**: Akasol opens a second production line at the Langen facility, doubling its capacity to 800 MWh, enabling the production of second-generation battery systems.
- **10/2020**: Akasol continues production at Langen while also moving to a new headquarters in Darmstadt, where it plans to expand production capacity further.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05/2017**: Akasol invests €10 million into the implementation of a semi-automatic production line at the Langen facility.
- **06/2018**: Akasol prepares for an IPO to raise €100-125 million for expanding production and R&D capacities in Europe and the US.
- **03/2020**: The second production line at Langen is implemented six months ahead of schedule, reflecting Akasol's proactive investment strategy in response to increasing demand.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05/2017**: Opening of the Langen battery plant.
- **04/2018**: Announcement of battery supply for Daimler’s Citaro electric bus.
- **03/2020**: Commissioning of the second production line at Langen, increasing capacity to 800 MWh.
- **10/2020**: Move to new headquarters in Darmstadt, marking the start of series production at the new facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **03/2020**: Akasol plans to transition from a two-shift to a three-shift operation at the Langen site, indicating potential job creation as production capacity increases.
- **04/2020**: Despite the pandemic, Akasol maintains production to secure jobs, indicating a commitment to workforce stability.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **06/2020**: Akasol remains an independent entity, although it is noted that BorgWarner completed a takeover of Akasol in June 2021, while the management team continues to run the company. The ownership structure prior to this acquisition was not specified as a joint venture or partnership, indicating full ownership by Akasol prior to the acquisition.
