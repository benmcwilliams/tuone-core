### Evolutionary Timeline of the Akasol Facility in Langen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05/2017**: Akasol announces the opening of a battery plant in Langen with an initial production capacity of 300 MWh, capable of producing battery systems for up to 1,500 electric buses annually. Plans for expansion to 600 MWh are mentioned.
- **04/2018**: Akasol begins delivering battery systems for Daimler’s Citaro electric bus, with the facility in Langen being responsible for assembling power packs using Samsung cells.
- **06/2018**: The facility is noted as Europe’s largest production plant for commercial vehicle battery systems, with a capacity of 300 MWh, and plans to expand to 600 MWh by 2020.
- **03/2020**: Akasol commissions a second production line at the Langen facility, doubling its capacity to 800 MWh, operational six months ahead of schedule.
- **10/2020**: The facility continues to operate while Akasol moves its headquarters to Darmstadt, maintaining production at Langen.
- **11/2021**: Akasol presents the third generation of battery systems, with production scheduled to start in 2023 at the Darmstadt facility, while Langen remains operational.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05/2017**: Akasol invests €10 million into the implementation of a semi-automatic production line at the Langen facility.
- **06/2018**: Akasol prepares for an IPO to raise €100-125 million for expanding production capacity, including at Langen.
- **03/2020**: The investment in the second production line at Langen is completed, increasing capacity to 800 MWh.
- **04/2020**: Akasol announces a total investment of €84 million in expanding its facilities, including Langen.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05/2017**: Opening of the Langen battery plant.
- **04/2018**: Start of battery deliveries for Daimler’s Citaro electric bus.
- **03/2020**: Commissioning of the second production line at Langen, increasing capacity to 800 MWh.
- **10/2020**: Akasol moves headquarters to Darmstadt, while production continues at Langen.
- **11/2021**: Presentation of the third generation of battery systems, with production planned for 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **06/2018**: The IPO and expansion plans are expected to create up to 500 jobs at the new headquarters in Darmstadt, indirectly impacting employment at the Langen facility.
- **03/2020**: Transition to a three-shift operation is possible at Langen due to increased capacity, although the company is cautious due to the pandemic's impact on demand.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **02/2021**: Akasol enters a strategic partnership with BorgWarner, which aims to take over Akasol. The company will continue to operate independently under the Akasol brand, with CEO Sven Schulz remaining in charge.
- **06/2021**: BorgWarner completes the acquisition of Akasol, securing around 89% of the shares.
