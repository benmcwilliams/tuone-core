### Evolutionary Timeline of BMW Facility in Leipzig, Germany

- **February 2016**: Production of the limited Protonic Red Edition of the BMW i8 begins at the Leipzig facility.

- **May 2018**: BMW announces an expansion to increase production capacity from 250,000 to 350,000 vehicles per year by 2020.
- **July 2018**: Production capacity for i-models is increased to 200 vehicles per day.
- **March 2019**: BMW announces plans to start battery module production at the Leipzig plant in 2021.
- **May 2021**: Series production of battery modules begins at the Leipzig plant.
- **November 2023**: Production of the new Mini Countryman, including electric variants, begins at the Leipzig plant.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2017**: BMW announces a €200 million investment to secure the future of the Leipzig plant.
- **June 2018**: BMW invests over €300 million in the expansion of the Leipzig factory.
- **September 2020**: BMW announces an investment of over €100 million in Leipzig for battery module production.
- **2021-2024**: BMW plans to invest up to €800 million in expanding e-component production at the Leipzig plant.
- **August 2023**: BMW announces an additional investment of €100 million for a logistics center for high-voltage batteries.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2017**: Announcement of a model offensive focusing on electric mobility.
- **July 2018**: Production capacity for i-models increased to 200 vehicles per day.
- **May 2021**: Start of battery module production.
- **November 2023**: Start of production for the new Mini Countryman, including electric variants.
- **March 2024**: Start of production for the Mini Countryman Electric.

#### 4. Labour

- **2021-2024**: The workforce in e-component production is expected to increase from over 800 to more than 1,000 employees.
- **November 2023**: The production of the Mini Countryman is expected to create 900 new jobs.

- The BMW Group owns the Leipzig facility exclusively, with no mention of joint ventures or partnerships regarding ownership.
