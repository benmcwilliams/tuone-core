### Evolutionary Timeline of the BMW Facility in Lower Bavaria, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2023**: BMW officially confirmed the establishment of a new battery assembly plant in Lower Bavaria. The facility will produce high-voltage battery packs for electric vehicles, which will be supplied to BMW plants in Munich, Dingolfing, and Regensburg. The plant will utilize cylindrical battery cells from Eve Energy, specifically the 4695 format, which offers higher energy density compared to Tesla's cells.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: BMW announced its investment in the new battery assembly plant, although specific financial details were not disclosed. The plant is part of a broader strategy to enhance battery production capabilities and support the transition to electric vehicles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2024**: BMW received planning permission for the battery assembly plant, marking a significant milestone in the project. The district office approved early soil removal and construction site preparations.
- **April 2024**: Construction began with the removal of topsoil and the erection of the first concrete pillar, indicating the start of the physical construction phase.
- **June 2024**: The construction of the production building is on track, with plans to complete the shell construction by the end of the year. The facility will be part of a global network of five battery production sites.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2024**: The first construction phase is expected to create approximately 1,600 jobs at the site. The battery supply from this facility is anticipated to secure existing jobs at BMW's Bavarian automotive plants and create new positions in the e-mobility sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify any joint ventures or partnerships; it is implied that BMW owns the facility exclusively as part of its global production network.
