## DEU-00659-00529

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Microvast Facility in Ludwigsfelde, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2019**: Microvast announces plans to establish a facility in Ludwigsfelde, Germany, to manufacture battery systems for various vehicles, with an initial production capacity of 300,000 to 500,000 battery modules for 10,000 to 15,000 electric vehicles annually. The final expansion phase aims for production for 50,000 to 75,000 vehicles per year.
- **July 2020**: Topping-out ceremony for the new facility is celebrated, with plans to assemble cells produced by Microvast Power Systems in China. Initial production capacity is set at 1.5 GWh per year, with future expansion to 6 GWh.
- **March 2021**: Production of battery modules and packs begins at the facility.
- **October 2020**: Microvast unveils a new fast-charging pouch cell, the MpCO 17.5Ah, suitable for various vehicles, which will be integrated into standard battery modules at the Ludwigsfelde plant.
- **December 2020**: Introduction of another fast-charging pouch cell, the HnCO-52Ah, designed for light and medium-duty commercial vehicles, to be produced at the facility.
- **January 2022**: Microvast, in collaboration with FPT Industrial, announces plans to supply battery systems for the new electric city and intercity bus from Iveco Bus.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2019**: Microvast announces an initial investment of 43 million euros for the first expansion phase of the facility, with total investments expected to reach a three-digit million sum.
- **July 2020**: The investment in the first expansion stage is reported to be around 50 million euros.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2019**: Announcement of the facility's establishment in Ludwigsfelde.
- **July 2020**: Topping-out ceremony for the facility.
- **March 2021**: Official start of production at the facility.
- **February 2021**: Microvast opens its European headquarters in Ludwigsfelde.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2019**: Initial employment of around 100 people is expected, with future growth to 200-250 jobs.
- **July 2020**: The facility is projected to create up to 250 jobs as production ramps up.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Microvast operates the facility through its German subsidiary, Microvast GmbH, indicating full ownership of the Ludwigsfelde facility.
