### Evolutionary Timeline of BASF Facility in Ludwigshafen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2021**: BASF and RWE announced plans for a **2GW offshore wind farm** to supply the Ludwigshafen facility with green electricity and support a green hydrogen plant by **2030**. The project aims to electrify production processes for basic chemicals, potentially avoiding **3.8 million tonnes of CO2 emissions** annually, with **2.8 million tonnes** directly at the Ludwigshafen facility.
- **November 2023**: BASF received funding approval for a **54MW water electrolysis plant** capable of producing up to **8,000 metric tons of hydrogen per year**. This plant will significantly reduce greenhouse gas emissions at the site by up to **72,000 metric tons annually** and is expected to be operational by **2025**.
- **October 2024**: BASF announced plans for the construction of the **world’s largest industrial heat pump** at the Ludwigshafen site, with a capacity of **500,000 metric tons of steam per year**, scheduled for commissioning in **2027**.
- **November 2024**: BASF entered a partnership with Vulcan Energy to explore geothermal energy utilization at the Ludwigshafen site, potentially producing **4 million tonnes of geothermal energy** annually, which could prevent approximately **800,000 metric tons of CO2 emissions**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2021**: The offshore wind project with RWE is expected to proceed without public sector financial support, although a regulatory framework is necessary.
- **November 2023**: The German Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs and Climate Protection approved funding of up to **€124.3 million** for the water electrolysis project, with **€37.3 million** from the Rhineland-Palatinate government.
- **October 2024**: The German government awarded **€310 million** for the industrial heat pump project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2021**: Announcement of the **2GW offshore wind farm** project.
- **November 2023**: Funding approval for the **54MW water electrolysis plant**.
- **October 2024**: Announcement of the **industrial heat pump** project.
- **November 2024**: Agreement with Vulcan Energy to explore geothermal energy.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2021**: The offshore wind project is expected to create jobs related to the construction and operation of the wind farm and the green hydrogen plant, although specific job numbers were not mentioned.
- **November 2023**: The water electrolysis plant and industrial heat pump projects are anticipated to create jobs in construction and operation, contributing to local employment.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2021**: BASF and RWE signed a letter of intent for the offshore wind project, indicating a **joint venture** structure for the wind farm.
- **November 2024**: The geothermal energy project with Vulcan Energy suggests a **collaborative partnership**, with BASF acting as the heat offtaker for the geothermal heat produced.

This timeline captures the significant developments and strategic initiatives at the BASF facility in Ludwigshafen, highlighting its commitment to transitioning towards sustainable and low-emission technologies.
