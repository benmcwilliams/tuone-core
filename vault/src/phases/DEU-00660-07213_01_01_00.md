## DEU-00660-07213

### Evolutionary Timeline of Vulcan Energy Resources Limited Facility in Ludwigshafen, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2024**: Vulcan Energy Resources Limited (Vulcan) announced a staged agreement with BASF SE to develop a geothermal heat project at BASF’s Verbund site in Ludwigshafen. The project aims to produce approximately 2000–2500 GWh/a of geothermal heat, which will significantly contribute to BASF's heat demand. Additionally, the feasibility of constructing a lithium extraction plant on BASF premises to produce lithium from geothermal brine is being evaluated.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2024**: Vulcan received EUR 100 million in federal funding from the Ministry of Economics and Climate Protection of Germany, which supports the development of the geothermal heat project and the potential lithium extraction facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2024**: The project is in the initial stages, with plans for 2D seismic campaigns during the winter season of 2024/2025, followed by a 3D seismic survey in the winter of 2025/2026. The success of these surveys will determine the next steps, including drilling exploratory wells.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2024**: The announcement does not specify job creation or loss directly related to the geothermal project; however, the collaboration with BASF, which employs around 34,000 people, indicates potential indirect job support through the project’s development.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2024**: The facility is a joint development between Vulcan Energy Resources Limited and BASF SE. BASF will act as the heat offtaker for 100% of the geothermal heat produced, and both companies will share the costs of the seismic surveys and other initial project phases.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic plans for the Vulcan Energy Resources Limited facility in Ludwigshafen, DEU, as of November 2024.

### Technology and Operations
- "Vulcan Energy Resources Limited (Vulcan) has entered into a staged agreement with chemical producer BASF SE (BASF) to joint develop a geothermal heat project to supply BASF’s Verbund site in the city of Ludwigshafen."

- "This announcement follows up on two similarly big announcements that Vulcan has made within the last two weeks – the start of lithium hydroxide production at the company’s CLEOP site in Frankfurt, and the awarding of EUR 100 million federal funding from the Ministry of Economics and Climate Protection of Germany."
