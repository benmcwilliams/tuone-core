### Evolutionary Timeline of The Mobility House Facility in Lünen, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2024**: The Mobility House (TMH) and Green Energy Storage Initiative SE (GESI) announced a joint venture to construct and market large-scale battery storage systems (BESS). The goal is to achieve a storage capacity of up to 8 GW in Europe by 2035. The facility in Lünen is part of this initiative, which focuses on utilizing new or discarded electric car batteries for energy storage. The facility has been prequalified for the provision of primary control power, demonstrating the effectiveness of the 2nd Life approach for battery usage.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2024**: The joint venture aims to develop a large storage facility with an initial capacity of 250 MW, with plans for an additional 500 MW in a second expansion stage. GESI will handle financing and project coordination, while TMH will manage the intelligent marketing of the batteries on European energy markets.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2024**: The announcement of the joint venture marks a significant milestone in the facility's evolution, with preparations for the first large-scale storage project already well advanced. The facility in Lünen is part of a broader strategy to build up to 8 GW of storage capacity by 2035.

### Technology and Operations
- "The Mobility House and Green Energy Storage Initiative SE (GESI) are establishing a joint venture focusing on the construction and marketing of battery storage systems (BESS)."
- "The duo aims to ensure a storage capacity of up to 8 GW in Europe by 2035."
- "Preparations for the first joint project are already well advanced with a large storage facility with a storage capacity of 250 MW in the planning stage."
- "A further 500 MW are to be added in the second expansion stage."
