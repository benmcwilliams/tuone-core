## DEU-00669-00455

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Li-Cycle Facility in Magdeburg, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2023**: Li-Cycle announces plans for a new facility in Magdeburg, Germany, which will utilize its patented submerged shredding technology to produce 'black mass' from lithium-ion batteries. The facility is designed to process up to 30,000 tons of battery materials annually, making it the largest 'Spoke' in Li-Cycle's portfolio.
- **August 2023**: The facility begins trial operations with the first main processing line capable of handling 10,000 tons of material per year. A second line is planned to be operational later in the year, with a total capacity of 30,000 tons expected.
- **September 2023**: The facility is officially inaugurated, confirming its operational status. It employs Li-Cycle’s Generation 3 Spoke technology, which allows for the processing of all types of lithium-ion battery waste without the need for disassembly or thermal processing.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: Li-Cycle enters into a partnership with Kion Group, which will supply batteries for recycling at the Magdeburg facility. This partnership is expected to support Kion's compliance with EU battery regulations.
- **September 2023**: The facility is part of Li-Cycle's broader strategy to expand its European operations, with plans for additional Spokes in France and Norway.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2023**: Announcement of the Magdeburg facility and its expected operational timeline.
- **August 2023**: Start of trial operations at the facility.
- **September 2023**: Official opening of the facility, marking it as Li-Cycle's first European plant and the largest Spoke in its network.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2023**: The Magdeburg facility creates approximately 50 new jobs as it begins operations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2023**: Li-Cycle is the sole owner of the Magdeburg facility, operating it independently as part of its global network of battery recycling facilities. The partnership with Kion Group does not indicate shared ownership but rather a strategic collaboration for battery supply.
