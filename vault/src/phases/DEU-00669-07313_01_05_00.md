## DEU-00669-07313

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Battery Lifecycle Company Facility in Magdeburg, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2024**: The Battery Lifecycle Company (BLC) opened a new facility in Magdeburg, which features the world's first fully automated battery deep discharge system. This facility is designed to prepare batteries for safe and raw material-optimized recycling by dismantling and discharging them. The facility can process up to 15,000 tonnes of batteries annually, which is approximately six times the capacity of their previous site in Rheda-Wiedenbrück. The technology for the deep discharge system was supplied by Bosch Rexroth, allowing for the automatic discharge of eight lithium-ion batteries in less than 15 minutes, compared to the manual process that takes up to 24 hours.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2024**: The opening of the Magdeburg facility marks a significant investment in capacity expansion for BLC, establishing one of the largest battery recycling facilities in Europe. The strategic location near major cities like Wolfsburg, Berlin, Dresden, and Leipzig is intended to enhance logistics efficiency for customer access.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2024**: The facility officially commenced operations with the launch of the automated battery discharge system. This was a planned development since the company's founding in 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2024**: The automation of the battery discharge and dismantling processes is aimed at reducing the manual workload on staff, thereby improving health, motivation, and process efficiency. The facility's design allows employees to focus on quality checks and data analyses rather than manual disassembly, which is labor-intensive and potentially harmful.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2024**: The Battery Lifecycle Company operates as a joint venture between the Rethmann sister companies Rhenus Automotive and TSR Automotive GmbH.
