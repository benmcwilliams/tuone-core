### Evolutionary Timeline of the Horiba FuelCon Facility in Magdeburg-Barleben, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Focus**: The facility specializes in manufacturing test, production, and diagnostic systems for fuel cells and batteries, catering to the automotive and supplier industries.
- **Production Capacity**: The expansion includes a new building with 7,000 square meters of production area and 3,000 square meters of office space.
- **Updates**: The facility was officially named eHUB and serves as the new headquarters for Horiba FuelCon.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Amount**: A total investment of approximately 36 million euros was made for the expansion of the Magdeburg-Barleben site and another site in Leichlingen.
- **Investment Timeline**: The investment in the new building was announced in November 2019, with completion expected by the end of 2021.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Announcement Date**: November 27, 2019 - Announcement of the expansion and investment plans.
- **Construction Start**: The construction of the new building began following the announcement.
- **Production Start**: The facility was officially commissioned and opened at the end of April 2022, experiencing a delay of about four months due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The expansion is expected to create 250 new jobs, increasing the total number of employees from 110 to 360.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: Horiba FuelCon is a subsidiary of the Japanese Horiba Group, indicating that the facility is part of a larger corporate structure but operates under the Horiba brand.
