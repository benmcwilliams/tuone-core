### Evolutionary Timeline of Epsilon Advanced Materials Facility in Moosburg, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **05-02-2024:** Epsilon Advanced Materials (EAM) takes over a technology center in Moosburg, Germany, previously built by Johnson Matthey. The facility is designed for the production of LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate) and LMFP (Lithium Manganese Iron Phosphate) cathodes on a laboratory and pilot scale. EAM plans to produce 100,000 tonnes of LFP cathode material by 2030. The facility will also enable the production of both cathode and anode materials for lithium-ion batteries.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **05-02-2024:** EAM is planning to invest approximately 100 million euros in building an anode material plant in Bellari, India, alongside other facilities in the USA and Finland. This investment is part of their broader strategy to enhance their production capabilities in the EV battery market.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **05-02-2024:** The acquisition of the Moosburg facility marks a significant milestone for EAM, allowing them to accelerate their plans for LFP cathode production, particularly in India, where a cathode material plant is scheduled to commence operations within the same year.
