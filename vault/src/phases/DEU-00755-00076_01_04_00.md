### Evolutionary Timeline of the BMW Facility in Munich, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **11/2018**: BMW announces a €200 million investment in the Munich plant to prepare for the series production of the all-electric BMW i4, aiming to start production in 2021. The facility will produce the i4 alongside combustion engine models, integrating electric vehicle production into existing lines.
- **03/2019**: Production of the BMW 3 Series plug-in hybrid is set to start in Munich, with expectations of high demand.
- **07/2020**: The Munich plant will undergo a six-week closure for extensive upgrades to accommodate the production of the i4, which will roll off the same assembly line as combustion engine models.
- **10/2021**: Production of the all-electric BMW i4 begins at the Munich plant, marking the first fully electric vehicle produced there.
- **11/2023**: BMW ends combustion engine production at the Munich plant to focus entirely on electric vehicles, with the i4 already being produced there since 2021.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **11/2018**: BMW invests €200 million in the Munich plant for the i4 production.
- **03/2020**: BMW announces plans for a €400 million investment to adapt the Munich plant for electric vehicle production.
- **11/2020**: BMW confirms a €400 million investment to convert the Munich plant for electric vehicle assembly, phasing out combustion engine production.
- **01/2024**: BMW announces an additional €650 million investment to prepare the Munich plant for exclusive electric vehicle production by the end of 2027.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **11/2018**: Announcement of the i4 production plans.
- **03/2019**: Start of preparations for the 3 Series plug-in hybrid.
- **07/2020**: Closure of the Munich plant for upgrades.
- **10/2021**: Start of i4 production.
- **11/2023**: End of combustion engine production at the Munich plant.
- **01/2024**: Announcement of exclusive electric vehicle production by the end of 2027.

#### 4. Labour
- **11/2018**: The Munich plant employs around 8,000 people.
- **07/2020**: Approximately 6,000 employees are on forced vacation during the plant's closure for upgrades.
- **11/2023**: The 1,200 employees previously working in engine production will be retrained for electric vehicle production.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- BMW operates the Munich facility as a wholly-owned site, with no joint ventures or external partnerships mentioned in the context of the facility's operations.
