### Evolutionary Timeline of the Sono Motors Facility in Munich, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **02/2016**: Sono Motors presents its electric minivan, the Sion, which features a 30 kWh battery pack and a solar roof that adds an extra 30 km of range. Production is planned to start in 2018.
- **12/2016**: Announcement of the solar-powered Sion EV to be presented in Munich in mid-2017, with test drives planned for crowdfunding supporters.
- **07/2017**: Official launch of the Sion in Munich, with production set to begin once 5,000 orders are received. The vehicle features 330 integrated solar cells, providing an additional 30 km of range per day.
- **08/2019**: The interior of the Sion is revealed, emphasizing a design for car-sharing. Series production is scheduled for the second half of 2020, with a total production target of 260,000 vehicles over eight years.
- **03/2020**: Sono Motors announces plans to build two prototypes of the Sion and further develop the "goSono" app for mobility services.
- **04/2022**: Sono Motors integrates its solar technology into the municipal bus fleet in Munich, deploying solar-powered trailer units to enhance energy efficiency.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12/2019**: Following failed negotiations with a financial investor, Sono Motors launches a crowdfunding campaign to raise €50 million for continued operations, which is successfully achieved by mid-January 2020.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **07/2017**: Official launch of the Sion with production contingent on receiving 5,000 orders.
- **08/2019**: Over 10,000 prepaid reservations for the Sion are reported, with production scheduled to start in the second half of 2020.
- **04/2022**: The first solar bus trailer for the municipal fleet is set to hit the streets of Munich.

#### 4. Labour
- **03/2020**: The crowdfunding campaign and subsequent production plans are expected to create jobs, although specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **08/2019**: The Sion is manufactured by National Electric Vehicle Sweden (NEVS), a company that has undergone ownership changes and is now owned by Chinese property giant Evergrande. Sono Motors does not own the manufacturing facility exclusively but relies on NEVS for production.
