### Evolutionary Timeline of the Technical University of Munich Facility in Munich, Germany

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** March 2024
   - **Details:** The Technical University of Munich is leading a project to explore geothermal potential in the greater Munich area. The project aims to harness untapped geothermal resources, with an existing capacity of 400 MW from 42 boreholes tapping into a deep thermal water reservoir. There is an estimated remaining potential of around 1 GW. The project will involve a large-scale 3D seismic campaign covering approximately 1000 square kilometers to create a detailed subsurface model for sustainable management of thermal water resources.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** March 2024
   - **Details:** The project has received funding of around EUR 11 million from the Ministry, contributing to a total project cost of nearly EUR 20 million. This investment is aimed at further exploring and developing geothermal energy in the region.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** March 2024
   - **Details:** The project, referred to as GIGA-M, was officially announced with initial plans reported back in November 2023. The project is focused on coordinating measures for the development and use of geothermal energy in the greater Munich area.

4. **Labour:**

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** March 2024
   - **Details:** The facility is not owned exclusively by the Technical University of Munich. It is a collaborative project involving multiple partners, including the Munich district, Stadtwerke München, the Ebersberg-Munich energy agency, and Energie-Wende Garching.
