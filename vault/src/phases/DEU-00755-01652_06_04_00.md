### Evolutionary Timeline of the First Solar Facility in Munich, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2016**: First Solar introduced the Series 5 thin-film PV module at Intersolar Europe in Munich. The module has a starting capacity of 365W and aims to reduce balance of system costs and installation times. The production process at the facility allows for a fully manufactured module in approximately 3 to 3.5 hours.
- **June 2023**: First Solar showcased the pre-commercial Series 6 Plus Bifacial module, which utilizes cadmium telluride (CdTe) semiconductor technology. This module is undergoing field and laboratory testing and is expected to combine bifaciality with high efficiency and reliability.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2023**: First Solar announced a limited production run of the bifacial module, indicating ongoing investment in R&D and innovation in solar technology. The company is also investing $370 million in a new R&D innovation center in Perrysburg, Ohio, set to begin operations in 2024.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2016**: The Series 5 module was launched at Intersolar Europe, marking a significant milestone in First Solar's product offerings.
- **June 2023**: The debut of the Series 6 Plus Bifacial module at Intersolar Europe represents a key milestone in the company's product development, showcasing advancements in thin-film technology.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2023**: The announcement of the bifacial module and the associated investments are expected to contribute to job creation, although specific numbers related to the Munich facility were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2016**: The thin-film technology used in the Series 5 module is fully owned by First Solar, indicating exclusive ownership of the technology and the facility's operations.
