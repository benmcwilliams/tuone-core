## DEU-00755-02675

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Avancis Facility in Munich, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2008**: Avancis began production of CIS (copper indium selenide) thin film modules at its facility. Initial production efficiency was 9%.
- **January 2010**: Avancis achieved a conversion efficiency of 11% for its standard production modules, with a capacity of 20 MWp per annum at its plant in Torgau, Saxony.
- **April 2014**: Avancis announced a new record conversion efficiency of 16.6% for its CIS cells, verified by the U.S. National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL).

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2014**: The Chinese building materials group CNBM acquired Avancis, planning to restart manufacturing at the mothballed 100 MW Fab 2 in Torgau and expand solar module development and production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2008**: Start of production at the facility.
- **January 2010**: Confirmation of a 15.1% conversion efficiency record for CIS thin film modules by NREL.
- **April 2014**: Announcement of the acquisition by CNBM and plans to restart operations at Fab 2.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2014**: Following the acquisition by CNBM, all 240 employees of Avancis were given the opportunity to be retained by the new company.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2010**: Avancis was a subsidiary of Saint-Gobain.
- **April 2014**: Avancis was sold to CNBM, indicating a change in ownership structure from full ownership by Saint-Gobain to ownership by CNBM.
