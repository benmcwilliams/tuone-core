## DEU-00755-10099

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Stadtwerke München Facility in Munich, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Production Capacity**: As of October 2023, the geothermal systems in Bavaria, including those operated by Stadtwerke München (SWM), have an installed capacity of 300 MW, with the potential to generate over 1.5 TWh of district heating and 145,506 MWh of climate-friendly electricity annually.
- **Future Plans**: SWM plans to build the largest inner-city geothermal energy plant in continental Europe at the Michaelibad site, with drilling expected to begin in early 2025. Additionally, ten geothermal energy projects with over 50 new deep drillings are planned, alongside the expansion of existing geothermal sites.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Funding for Geothermal Survey**: In March 2024, it was announced that the Ministry would provide around EUR 11 million for a large-scale geothermal survey project in Munich, which has a total cost of approximately EUR 20 million. This project aims to explore the geothermal potential in the greater Munich area.
- **Investment Volume for Heating Transition**: SWM has committed to an investment volume of around EUR 9.5 billion for the heating transition in Munich, focusing on expanding geothermal energy and other renewable sources.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Geothermal Survey Project**: Initial plans for the GIGA-M project were reported in November 2023, with the project officially announced in March 2024.
- **Geothermal Plant Construction**: Concrete work on the drilling site and heating station at Michaelibad is expected to begin in 2024, with drilling anticipated to start in early 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: While specific job creation figures were not mentioned in the articles, the expansion of geothermal projects and the heating network is expected to create jobs in the region as new facilities and infrastructure are developed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: Stadtwerke München operates the facility and is involved in joint projects with local partners, such as the cooperation with Erdwärme Grünwald (EWG) for geothermal development. However, the articles do not specify whether SWM has exclusive ownership of all facilities or if there are joint ventures involved.
