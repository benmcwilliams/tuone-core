### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fraunhofer Facility in Münster, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2021**: The Fraunhofer Research Fabrication Battery Cell (FFB) began its research activities, focusing on accelerating innovation and commercialization processes for battery cell production technologies.
- **February 2024**: The FFB is advancing its fourth sub-project, focusing on solid-state batteries, which aims to address fundamental production questions and create necessary infrastructure for manufacturing these batteries.
- **April 2024**: The first construction phase of the FFB, known as PreFab, was opened, providing 6,800 square meters of research space. The larger FFB Fab is planned to provide an additional 20,000 square meters for large-scale production research and development.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2024**: The German Federal Ministry of Education and Research is funding the development of battery cell research production with up to €500 million. The state of North Rhine-Westphalia is contributing up to €320 million for land and new buildings, increasing its previous commitment from €180 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2019**: A consortium led by the Fraunhofer IPT was awarded the contract for battery cell research production, leading to the establishment of the FFB in Münster.
- **February 2021**: Initial criticism arose regarding delays in the establishment of the FFB due to increasing influence from the Fraunhofer Research Institute.
- **April 2024**: The PreFab facility was officially opened, marking a significant milestone in the establishment of the FFB.
