### Evolutionary Timeline of the Audi Facility in Neckarsulm, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **03/2016**: Audi is designated to lead the Volkswagen Group's fuel cell efforts at a center in Neckarsulm, Germany.
- **12/2016**: Audi announces plans to start building electric vehicles (EVs) at the Neckarsulm location starting in 2020.
- **03/2018**: Audi announces the e-tron GT, a fully electric model to be produced at the Böllinger Höfe near Neckarsulm starting in 2020.
- **07/2019**: Audi begins preparations for the production of the e-tron GT alongside the R8 at the Neckarsulm facility.
- **12/2020**: Audi starts series production of the e-tron GT at the Neckarsulm site, marking it as the first electric Audi produced in Germany.
- **01/2021**: Audi plans to increase production of the e-tron GT, with a target of 10,000 units per year.
- **10/2022**: Audi decides to produce its flagship electric model at Neckarsulm instead of Hanover, with production expected to start in 2026.
- **04/2024**: Audi plans to set up a production facility for battery modules at the Böllinger Höfe site to support the Neckarsulm vehicle plant.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12/2018**: Audi announces an investment of over 14 billion euros for e-mobility advancements, which includes the development of electric vehicles at Neckarsulm.
- **10/2021**: Audi confirms a significant investment in a battery competence center at Neckarsulm, with an estimated total investment of 500 to 600 million euros.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **03/2016**: Announcement of Audi's leadership in fuel cell technology at Neckarsulm.
- **12/2016**: Confirmation of EV production plans starting in 2020.
- **03/2018**: Announcement of the e-tron GT production plans.
- **12/2020**: Start of series production of the e-tron GT.
- **10/2022**: Decision to produce a flagship electric model at Neckarsulm, moving from Hanover.
- **04/2024**: Plans to establish battery module production at Böllinger Höfe.

#### 4. Labour
- **12/2018**: Audi guarantees 60,000 jobs at its Ingolstadt headquarters until at least 2025, with some job creation expected in electric mobility.
- **10/2022**: Audi plans to shift personnel from the A8 production to increase e-tron GT production, indicating a reallocation of jobs rather than a net increase.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Audi operates the Neckarsulm facility as a wholly-owned site, with no indications of joint ventures or shared ownership in the provided articles. The Böllinger Höfe, while part of the Neckarsulm plant, is noted as a separate facility dedicated to small-series production, including the e-tron GT.
