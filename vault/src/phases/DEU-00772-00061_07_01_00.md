### Evolutionary Timeline of the Audi Facility in Neuburg an der Donau, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2020**: Audi introduced the MGU05 powertrain at its Motorsport Center in Neuburg an der Donau for the Formula E racing car, the e-tron FE07. This powertrain is notable for its efficiency, achieving over 95% overall efficiency and 97% for the inverter unit.
- **August 2022**: Audi announced its entry into Formula 1 starting in 2026, developing a hybrid drive unit at the Neuburg facility. The new engine regulations will see a significant increase in the electric power component of the drive unit.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2022**: Audi is undertaking expansions in personnel, buildings, and technical infrastructure at the Neuburg facility to support the development of the Formula 1 hybrid power unit. The completion of these expansions is expected by the end of 2023.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2020**: Audi presented its Formula E racing car, marking a significant milestone in its motorsport activities.
- **November 2020**: Audi announced its withdrawal from Formula E at the end of the 2021 season, transitioning to focus on the Dakar Rally with a hybrid race car.
- **August 2022**: Audi confirmed its partnership with the Sauber team for its Formula 1 project, with plans to use the developed power unit starting in 2026. The first test drives are scheduled for 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2022**: The expansion of the Neuburg facility for the Formula 1 project is expected to create new jobs, although specific job creation numbers have not been disclosed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2022**: Audi is developing the Formula 1 hybrid power unit through a newly established subsidiary, Audi Formula Racing GmbH, indicating a structured approach to ownership and management of the project. Audi is also planning to acquire a stake in the Sauber Group, which will operate as an Audi works team in Formula 1.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic shifts at the Audi facility in Neuburg an der Donau, highlighting its focus on advanced electric and hybrid technologies in motorsport.
