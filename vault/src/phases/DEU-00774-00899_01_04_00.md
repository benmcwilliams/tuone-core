### Evolutionary Timeline of the ATW Facility in Neuwied, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Production Focus:** ATW (Assembly & Test Europe GmbH) specializes in producing battery assembly systems for automotive clients, including major manufacturers like BMW, Daimler/Accumotive, and Volkswagen.
   - **Operational Status:** The facility was facing closure due to financial difficulties exacerbated by a decline in orders related to the COVID-19 pandemic.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Acquisition Announcement:** On October 1, 2020, Tesla announced its intention to acquire ATW, which was approved by the German Federal Cartel Office on October 21, 2020. This acquisition was seen as a means to preserve the facility and its operations.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Merger Approval:** The German Federal Cartel Office approved Tesla's takeover of ATW on October 21, 2020. This approval was crucial for the continuation of operations at the Neuwied site, which was previously slated for closure.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Job Preservation:** The acquisition by Tesla was expected to preserve over 200 jobs at the Neuwied facility, which were threatened due to the company's financial struggles and impending closure.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Ownership Transition:** ATW was owned by the Canadian company ATS Automation Tooling Systems prior to the acquisition. Following the merger, it is anticipated that Tesla will take over the facility, although it remains unclear whether Tesla or its subsidiary Grohmann Automation will manage the operations post-merger.
