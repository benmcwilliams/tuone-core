### Evolutionary Timeline of the e.Go Facility in North Rhine-Westphalia, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2019**: e.Go-Mobile was part of a consortium aiming to establish battery cell production in North Rhine-Westphalia. The initial plan involved a production volume of 1 gigawatt, with a long-term goal to increase capacity to 8 gigawatts over several years.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2019**: The total investment required for the battery cell production facility was projected to be 1.2 billion euros, with an immediate need for 250 million euros for the first phase. e.Go-Mobile, along with other partners, intended to contribute hundreds of millions of euros towards this investment.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2019**: e.Go-Mobile CEO Günther Schuh announced his departure from the consortium pursuing the battery cell factory, citing a lack of binding commitments from partners. Despite this, the BMZ Group, along with StreetScooter and TerraE, continued to pursue the project and planned to submit a funding application.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2019**: While specific job creation figures were not mentioned, the establishment of the battery cell production facility was expected to create jobs in the region, as indicated by the scale of investment and production planned.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2019**: e.Go-Mobile was part of a consortium and did not have exclusive ownership of the facility. The project involved multiple partners, including BMZ Group and StreetScooter, indicating a collaborative approach rather than full ownership by e.Go-Mobile.
