### Evolutionary Timeline of the Meyer Burger Facility in North Rhine-Westphalia, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04/2020**: Meyer Burger is considering establishing a manufacturing facility in North Rhine-Westphalia for high-efficiency photovoltaic (PV) cells and modules. The facility aims to supply panels for a 10 GW floating solar project at the Hambach open-cast coal mine. This project is intended to replace some of the jobs lost in the local coal industry and ensure the area remains a significant energy generation center post-coal phase-out.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04/2020**: The facility's establishment is part of Meyer Burger's strategic shift from low-margin solar equipment sales in Asia to high-efficiency PV production in Europe. The company has formed partnerships with REC Group and Oxford PV to bring its heterojunction-based solar technology to market, indicating potential investment in the facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04/2020**: CEO Gunter Erfurt announces plans for the facility during a radio interview, indicating that the location is yet to be finalized but is likely to be in North Rhine-Westphalia. The facility is intended to support a significant floating solar project at the Hambach site.
- **12/2020**: The project is still in the planning stages, with discussions ongoing about the establishment of a gigawatt-scale factory in the region.

#### 4. Labour
- **04/2020**: The floating solar project associated with the facility could potentially replace some of the 10,000 jobs expected to be lost in the local coal industry, highlighting the facility's role in job creation amidst the energy transition.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **04/2020**: There is no explicit mention of the ownership structure in the articles, but Meyer Burger is indicated to be the primary company behind the facility, suggesting full ownership unless otherwise stated in future developments.
