## DEU-00821-00821

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Varta Facility in Nördlingen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **06/2020**: Varta receives €300 million funding to develop large format lithium-ion cells, marking a shift towards larger battery production for applications in energy storage, robotics, and mobility. The facility is involved in pilot production for mass production of lithium-ion batteries.
- **04/2021**: Varta presents the V4Drive round cell in 21700 format as a booster battery.
- **End of 2021**: Pilot production of V4Drive cells begins, with mass production initially scheduled for 2023, targeting a production capacity of at least 2 GWh.
- **11/2022**: Construction of a new factory for electric car battery cells is paused due to cost reasons, with production continuing at the pilot plant to fulfill contracts, notably with Porsche.
- **10/2024**: Porsche acquires a majority stake in Varta's subsidiary V4Drive Battery, which focuses on large-format lithium-ion round cells.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **06/2020**: The facility receives €300 million in government funding as part of an Important Project of Common European Interest (IPCEI) to support the development of large format lithium-ion cells.
- **10/2024**: Porsche and major shareholder Michael Tojner each invest €30 million in Varta as part of a reorganization plan to secure financing until the end of 2027.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **06/2020**: Announcement of funding for the development of large format lithium-ion cells.
- **04/2021**: Presentation of the V4Drive round cell.
- **End of 2021**: Start of pilot production for V4Drive cells.
- **11/2022**: Announcement of the pause in construction for the new electric vehicle battery factory.
- **10/2024**: Porsche's acquisition of a majority stake in V4Drive Battery and continuation of the planned production plant for booster cells in Nördlingen.

#### 4. Labour
- **06/2020**: Varta announces the creation of around 1,000 new jobs by the end of 2021 in Nördlingen and Ellwangen due to the mass production of lithium-ion batteries.
- **11/2022**: Implementation of short-time work at the Nördlingen production site due to cost-cutting measures and temporary capacity adjustments.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **10/2024**: Porsche acquires a majority stake in Varta's subsidiary V4Drive Battery, indicating a shift from exclusive ownership to a joint venture model for this specific business unit. Prior to this, Varta operated the facility independently. 

This timeline captures the key developments and changes at the Varta facility in Nördlingen, Germany, highlighting its evolution in technology, investment, project milestones, labor impacts, and ownership structure.
