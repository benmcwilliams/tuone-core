### Evolutionary Timeline of the University of Oldenburg Facility in Oldenburg, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2024**: The University of Oldenburg is involved in the BATTwin research project, which focuses on developing efficient and sustainable production methods for lithium-ion batteries. The facility is specifically working on reducing the reject rate of battery cell production, which currently stands at around ten percent. The research team, led by computer scientist Andreas Rauh, is developing models to optimize the production process and improve quality control.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2024**: The BATTwin project is funded by the EU’s Horizon Europe programme with a total budget of approximately 6.4 million euros. The University of Oldenburg will receive around 630,000 euros from this funding to support its research activities within the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2024**: The BATTwin project is officially launched, with a goal to establish a European “zero-defect gigafactory” by May 2027. The project aims to develop digital twins of battery factories to enhance production efficiency and reduce waste, energy requirements, and emissions.
