## DEU-00829-05110

### Evolutionary Timeline of the German Aerospace Center (DLR) Facility in Oldenburg, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2024**: The DLR facility in Oldenburg has established a new laboratory specifically for the HyCAVmobil project, which focuses on hydrogen storage. This facility is utilized to determine the quality and purity of hydrogen, with samples from the cavern in Rüdersdorf being examined. The project has demonstrated that hydrogen can be safely stored in underground caverns, with high purity levels suitable for fuel cell applications.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2024**: The total investment for the HyCAVmobil hydrogen storage project amounts to over 14 million euros, with approximately 6.5 million euros received as a grant from the Federal Ministry for Digital and Transport (BMDV) under the National Innovation Programme for Hydrogen and Fuel Cell Technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2019**: The HyCAVmobil research project commenced, focusing on the feasibility of hydrogen storage in caverns.
- **Spring 2021**: Installation and cementing of 160 steel pipes to a depth of 1,000 meters in an existing borehole in the salt dome below Rüdersdorf.
- **Autumn 2022**: Proof of leakage of the cavern borehole to a depth of 1,000 meters was established, confirming the borehole's leak-proof status.
- **Autumn 2022 to Spring 2023**: Brine removal and creation of the cavity in the salt dome for hydrogen storage.
- **Spring to Autumn 2023**: Construction of the above-ground facility for the operation of the hydrogen storage facility.
- **Autumn 2023**: First hydrogen storage occurred.
- **Winter 2023 to Autumn 2024**: Operational tests of the hydrogen cavern and storage technology are planned to assess various operational parameters.
