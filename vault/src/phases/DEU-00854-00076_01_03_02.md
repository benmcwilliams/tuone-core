## DEU-00854-00076

### Evolutionary Timeline of BMW Facility in Parsdorf, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **01-07-2020**: BMW announces plans for a pilot plant in Parsdorf for the production of battery cells for electric cars. The facility will initially dedicate one-third of a 40,000 square meter hall for pilot production, with future expansion capabilities.
- **30-07-2020**: BMW confirms the establishment of pilot battery cell production in Parsdorf, with operations scheduled to start by the end of 2022. The facility aims to optimize production processes for lithium-ion battery cells.
- **04-11-2021**: Manz is contracted to set up a battery production line at the Parsdorf site, focusing on electrode coating and cell assembly.
- **22-03-2022**: BMW plans to build five gigafactories for electric car batteries, indicating a strategic shift towards more localized production.
- **23-05-2022**: The Cell Manufacturing Competence Centre (CMCC) in Parsdorf is set to open in autumn 2022, focusing on near-series production of lithium-ion battery cells.
- **23-01-2023**: BMW begins testing solid-state battery production at the CMCC, collaborating with Solid Power to develop prototype lines.
- **26-10-2023**: The CMCC starts producing the first battery cell samples for the upcoming New Class models, utilizing round cells of the 4695 format.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **30-07-2020**: BMW receives a €60 million grant from the German federal government and the state of Bavaria for battery research, which will support the pilot production in Parsdorf.
- **30-07-2020**: BMW announces an investment of €110 million for the pilot plant in Parsdorf.
- **23-05-2022**: The investment for the first expansion stage of the CMCC amounts to approximately €170 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **01-07-2020**: Initial announcement of the pilot plant project.
- **30-07-2020**: Confirmation of the pilot plant's establishment and operational timeline.
- **04-11-2021**: Contract awarded to Manz for setting up the production line.
- **23-05-2022**: CMCC is set to open in autumn 2022.
- **26-10-2023**: Production of battery cell samples begins at the CMCC.

#### 4. Labour
- **01-07-2020**: Initial plans indicate that 50 engineers and technicians will work in production, with an increase to about 150 employees as operations expand.
- **23-05-2022**: Approximately 80 employees are expected to work at the CMCC.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- BMW is establishing the facility as part of its strategy to deepen its competence in battery cell production. The facility is not a joint venture; it is owned and operated by BMW, although it collaborates with external partners like Manz and Solid Power for specific technological developments.
