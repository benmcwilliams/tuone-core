### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mercedes-Benz Facility in Rastatt, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **02/2017**: The Rastatt facility is integrated into Daimler's electric vehicle (EV) production strategy, focusing on compact EQ models.
- **05/2017**: Rastatt is confirmed as the third plant in Germany to produce plug-in hybrid vehicles (PHEVs) for Daimler.
- **01/2018**: Rastatt is designated as a production hub for electric vehicles as part of Daimler's global electrification strategy.
- **02/2018**: The facility is set to produce the EQ compact model, with plans for future electric models.
- **12/2020**: Production of the EQA, a compact electric vehicle, begins at Rastatt ahead of its world premiere.
- **03/2023**: The facility prepares for the production of vehicles based on the new Mercedes-Benz Modular Architecture (MMA), with conversion work starting in summer 2023.
- **09/2023**: The Concept CLA Class is unveiled, indicating that the electric CLA will be produced at Rastatt starting in 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **01/2018**: Daimler announces a €10 billion investment for its global electrification strategy, which includes Rastatt.
- **06/2022**: Mercedes announces over €2 billion in investments for European assembly plants, including Rastatt, to prepare for electric vehicle production.
- **03/2023**: A significant investment is confirmed for the conversion of Rastatt to support the MMA platform.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **02/2017**: Announcement of Rastatt's involvement in EV production.
- **05/2017**: Confirmation of Rastatt's role in producing PHEVs.
- **12/2020**: Start of EQA production at Rastatt.
- **03/2023**: Announcement of the start of conversion work for MMA production.
- **09/2023**: Official confirmation of the electric CLA's production at Rastatt, with production set to start in 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **02/2018**: The integration of battery production and electric vehicle assembly is expected to create new jobs, although specific numbers are not detailed.
- **09/2023**: The transition to electric vehicle production may lead to job shifts as the A-Class and B-Class models are phased out, but management expresses confidence in maintaining core employment levels.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Rastatt facility is fully owned by Daimler AG, with no indications of joint ventures or external ownership.
