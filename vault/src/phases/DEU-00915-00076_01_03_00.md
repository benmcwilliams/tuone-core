### Evolutionary Timeline of the BMW Facility in Regensburg, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2020**: BMW announced plans to expand production capacities for electric drives at the Regensburg plant. From 2021, battery cells for high-voltage batteries will be coated, and complete high-voltage batteries will be produced starting in 2022. The plant already produces plug-in hybrids of the X1 and X2 compact SUVs alongside combustion engine variants.
- **April 2021**: The Regensburg plant began painting battery cells for high-voltage batteries, with plans to expand to four production lines by the end of 2021. From 2022, the plant will manufacture ready-to-install high-voltage batteries from modules.
- **November 2022**: Series production of the iX1 electric SUV commenced, with the plant capable of producing combustion engines, plug-in hybrids, and fully electric models on a single production line.
- **October 2023**: Series production of the iX2 electric SUV began, with the plant operating on a three-shift schedule to accommodate increased production volumes.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2020**: BMW announced an investment of over 150 million euros in the Regensburg site to support battery production for electrified vehicles.
- **April 2021**: Total investment in expanding production capacities for electrified vehicles across various sites, including Regensburg, was reported to be around 790 million euros, with Regensburg and Leipzig accounting for approximately 250 million euros.
- **April 2023**: BMW planned to invest over 350 million euros in vehicle production at the Regensburg plant to prepare for an increasing number of battery-electric cars.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: The Regensburg plant began painting battery cells.
- **October 2022**: Assembly of high-voltage batteries commenced at the Regensburg plant.
- **November 2022**: Production of the iX1 electric SUV started.
- **October 2023**: Production of the iX2 electric SUV began, marking the addition of a second fully electric model at the facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2020**: The investment in new production areas was expected to create at least 300 sustainable jobs by 2024.
- **April 2023**: BMW planned to hire around 500 new employees at the Regensburg site.
- **October 2023**: With the start of production for the iX2, the plant added a third shift and planned to hire an additional 600 employees in 2024.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The BMW facility in Regensburg is fully owned by BMW Group, with no mention of joint ventures or shared ownership in the provided articles.
