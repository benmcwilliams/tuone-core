### Evolutionary Timeline of the Bosch Facility in Reutlingen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **08/2019**: Bosch announces plans to produce silicon carbide (SiC) semiconductors at the Reutlingen plant. The new SiC chips are expected to enhance electric vehicle range by 6% and reduce energy loss in power electronics by 50%. The facility has been producing semiconductors since 1971 and is currently using 150- and 200-millimeter wafer technology.
- **12/2021**: Bosch begins large-scale production of SiC semiconductors at the Reutlingen facility. The production is driven by the growing demand for electromobility, with plans to expand production capacity to a three-digit million range.
- **04/2023**: Bosch announces plans to expand the cleanroom area in Reutlingen from approximately 35,000 square meters to over 44,000 square meters by the end of 2025 to accommodate increased production of SiC chips.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **08/2019**: Bosch announces an initial investment in the "three-digit million range" for a pilot line for SiC semiconductors, with a total investment of 500 million euros planned for the Reutlingen facility.
- **12/2021**: Bosch indicates ongoing investments for further development and expansion of SiC semiconductor production, with a focus on increasing production capacity.
- **04/2023**: Bosch has invested over 2.5 billion euros in semiconductor manufacturing facilities in Reutlingen and Dresden since 2010, with additional plans for a three billion euro investment in Europe by 2026.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2021**: Large-scale production of SiC semiconductors officially begins at the Reutlingen facility.
- **04/2023**: Bosch announces the expansion of the cleanroom area in Reutlingen, with plans to complete the expansion by the end of 2025.
