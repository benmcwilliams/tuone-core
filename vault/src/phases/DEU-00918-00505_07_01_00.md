## DEU-00918-00505

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Manz Facility in Reutlingen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **08/2009**: Manz Automation AG announced a reduction in employee hours at the Reutlingen plant due to decreased demand. The facility was involved in producing solar-cell production lines for crystalline silicon solar cells in partnership with Roth & Rau AG.
- **12/2018**: The Reutlingen facility generated €105 million in sales from its solar business, although solar EBIT fell from €31 million in 2017 to €14.4 million in 2018.
- **07/2022**: Manz announced its exit from the solar business, discontinuing all activities in the CIGS thin-film area after writing off €23.2 million due to a failed fab project in China. The company decided to focus on automotive, electromobility, battery manufacturing, electronics, energy, and medical technology sectors.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **11/2021**: Manz was awarded a contract to set up a battery production line for BMW, with the total investment expected to be in the double-digit million euro range. This project is part of a broader strategy to penetrate the entire value chain for battery cells.
- **12/2021**: Manz received an order from Britishvolt for machines for a battery production facility in Northumberland, with the order volume exceeding €70 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04/2020**: Manz announced plans to partially resume operations at its Reutlingen facility after a temporary shutdown due to the COVID-19 pandemic.
- **07/2022**: Manz officially exited the solar business, marking a significant shift in its operational focus.

#### 4. Labour
- **08/2009**: The company cut employee hours by up to 20% at the Reutlingen plant, affecting 75% of the 413 workers impacted across both Reutlingen and Tübingen facilities. The German government provided support for workers with reduced hours.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Throughout the timeline, there is no indication of joint ventures or shared ownership for the Reutlingen facility; it appears that Manz AG has maintained full ownership of the facility.
