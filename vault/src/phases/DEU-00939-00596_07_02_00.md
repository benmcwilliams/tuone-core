### Evolutionary Timeline of the Opel Facility in Rüsselsheim, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2016**: Opel opened the Global Propulsion Center in Rüsselsheim, a €210 million facility focused on engine testing and electric drivetrains.
- **May 2019**: Opel announced plans to electrify its entire product range by 2024, with the new generation of the Opel Corsa set to debut as a fully electric version.
- **June 2021**: Opel revealed details about the new Astra, which will be electrified and produced at the Rüsselsheim plant later that year.
- **September 2021**: Opel confirmed that the Astra would also be available as a battery-electric vehicle (BEV) starting in 2023.
- **June 2022**: Opel introduced the Vivaro-e Hydrogen, a plug-in fuel cell electric vehicle (FCEV) with a range of over 400 kilometers.
- **June 2024**: Opel announced the Astra Sports Tourer Electric, emphasizing its practicality and efficiency.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2016**: The establishment of the Global Propulsion Center involved a significant investment of €210 million.
- **May 2018**: Following PSA's acquisition of Opel, investments in R&D were planned to secure the future of the Rüsselsheim facility and its workforce.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2016**: Opening of the Global Propulsion Center.
- **May 2019**: Announcement of the fully electric Corsa and plans for electrification of the product range by 2024.
- **June 2021**: Production of the new electrified Astra was set to begin.
- **September 2021**: Confirmation of the Astra's availability as a BEV from 2023.
- **June 2022**: Launch of the Vivaro-e Hydrogen.
- **June 2024**: Introduction of the Astra Sports Tourer Electric.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2018**: An agreement was reached to secure jobs at Opel facilities in Germany, including Rüsselsheim, until at least 2023, amidst PSA's takeover.
- **June 2021**: The electrification strategy and new model production were expected to maintain job security at the Rüsselsheim plant.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2018**: Following the acquisition by PSA Group, Opel became a subsidiary of Stellantis, indicating a change in ownership structure from General Motors to a joint venture under PSA.
