## DEU-00939-02453

### Evolutionary Timeline of Stellantis Group Facility in Rüsselsheim, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2021**: Stellantis begins extensive work at the Opel headquarters in Rüsselsheim to implement the electrified Efficient Modular Platform 2 (EMP2). This platform allows for the production of both combustion and electrified vehicles on the same line, enhancing flexibility and model diversity. The facility will introduce a second production shift in autumn 2021, enabling the production of models like the electric version of the new Opel Astra.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2021**: Stellantis announces a significant investment in the Rüsselsheim facility to implement the EMP2 platform, although the exact figure is not disclosed. The investment includes updates to the body shop, paint shop, and final assembly areas, as well as the introduction of driverless transport systems and a new test track.
- **January 2023**: Stellantis and Vulcan Energy explore the use of geothermal energy to decarbonize the Rüsselsheim plant. Stellantis intends to finance half of the geothermal project, which aims to meet a significant portion of the facility's energy needs starting in 2025.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Autumn 2021**: The second production shift at the Rüsselsheim facility is scheduled to start, coinciding with the implementation of the EMP2 platform.
- **January 2023**: Stellantis and Vulcan Energy sign a term sheet to advance the geothermal energy project, marking the first phase of a multi-phase project aimed at decarbonizing the facility's energy mix.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2021**: The introduction of the second production shift is expected to create approximately 300 additional jobs in the final assembly line. Employees will be moved from other underutilized areas within the company to fill these positions.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: Stellantis operates the Rüsselsheim facility as part of its operations following the merger of PSA and FCA in January 2021. The facility is not mentioned as a joint venture, indicating that Stellantis has full ownership of the plant. Additionally, Stellantis is a significant stakeholder in Vulcan Energy, which supports its decarbonization efforts. 

This timeline captures the key developments and changes at the Stellantis facility in Rüsselsheim, Germany, focusing on technology, investment, project milestones, labor impacts, and ownership structure.
