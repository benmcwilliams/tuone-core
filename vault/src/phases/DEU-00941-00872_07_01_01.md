## DEU-00941-00872

### Evolutionary Timeline of the ZF Facility in Saarbrücken, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **07-11-2022**: ZF announces the conversion of its transmission plant in Saarbrücken into a lead location for pure electric drives. New production lines for electric drive systems are set to be installed in 2023, with series production starting in 2024. The specific products to be produced are not detailed, but they will involve purely electric drive systems.
- **09-08-2024**: ZF plans to manufacture two additional purely electric passenger car axle drives for European manufacturers at the Saarbrücken plant, with series production set to start in 2025. The first electric axle drive production commenced in 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07-11-2022**: ZF announces a significant investment in the range of three-digit millions for the conversion of the Saarbrücken plant to support electric drive production. This investment is part of a broader strategy to transition towards e-mobility.
- **09-08-2024**: The investment in a new, ultra-modern, and flexible production line for the additional electric axle drives is expected to secure 300 jobs at the Saarbrücken facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **07-11-2022**: Announcement of the conversion of the transmission plant into a leading location for electric drives, with production lines to be installed in 2023 and series production starting in 2024.
- **09-08-2024**: Confirmation of the start of production for the first electric axle drive in 2024 and the announcement of plans for two additional electric axle drives to begin production in 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **09-08-2024**: The investment in the new production line is expected to secure 300 jobs in production at the Saarbrücken facility. However, ZF acknowledges that the site will face job cuts due to lower value creation in electric mobility, indicating a need for structural adjustments.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify any joint ventures or partnerships regarding ownership of the Saarbrücken facility, implying that ZF owns the facility exclusively. However, ZF is mentioned as a minority stakeholder in a separate project with Wolfspeed for a SiC semiconductor plant, indicating collaboration but not shared ownership of the Saarbrücken facility itself. 

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic shifts at the ZF facility in Saarbrücken, focusing on its transition towards electric mobility and the associated investments and impacts on employment.
