### Evolutionary Timeline of the SVOLT Facility in Saarland, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2020**: SVOLT announced plans to build a battery cell factory in Saarland with a production capacity of **24 GWh**. This facility will also include a research center and will focus on producing cobalt-free battery cells.
- **January 2021**: SVOLT introduced its first cobalt-free battery cells, known as **NMX cells**, which will be produced at the Saarland facility by the end of **2023**.
- **July 2021**: SVOLT confirmed that the NMX cells, which consist of **75% nickel and 25% manganese**, will also be produced in Saarland for the European market.
- **April 2023**: SVOLT announced plans to build five battery cell factories in Europe, with the Saarland facility being one of the confirmed sites.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2020**: SVOLT committed to investing up to **€2 billion** in the Saarland facility, which is expected to create up to **2,000 jobs** upon full expansion.
- **August 2021**: SVOLT raised **€1.33 billion** in a financing round, which will be used to support the establishment and expansion of production capacities, including the Saarland facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2020**: Announcement of the construction of the Saarland facility.
- **End of 2023**: Scheduled start of cell production at the Saarland facility.
- **April 2023**: Construction of the Saarland facility was postponed to at least **2027** due to local protests, although SVOLT signed a lease for a site for module and pack assembly in Heusweiler, Saarland.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2020**: The planned investment in the Saarland facility is expected to create up to **2,000 jobs** in the final expansion stage.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- SVOLT is a subsidiary of the Chinese car manufacturer **Great Wall Motors**. The facility in Saarland is owned exclusively by SVOLT, with no indication of joint ventures or partnerships regarding ownership.
