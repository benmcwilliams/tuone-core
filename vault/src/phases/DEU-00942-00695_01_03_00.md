### Evolutionary Timeline of the SVOLT Facility in Saarland, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2020**: SVOLT announces plans to build a battery cell factory in Saarland with a production capacity of **24 GWh/year**. The facility will include a module pack factory and a cell module factory, with production expected to start by the end of **2023**.
- **April 2021**: SVOLT begins mass production of cobalt-free cathode materials in China, which will also be produced at the Saarland facility by the end of **2023**.
- **July 2021**: SVOLT starts series production of cobalt-free nickel-manganese battery cells (NMX) in China, with plans to produce these cells in Saarland for the European market by the end of **2023**.
- **October 2021**: SVOLT announces plans to increase its production capacity to **600 GWh** by **2025**, including the Saarland facility.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2020**: SVOLT commits to a total investment of **€2 billion** in the Saarland facility.
- **August 2021**: SVOLT completes a financing round of **€1.33 billion**, which will be used to expand production capacities, including the Saarland facility.
- **October 2021**: SVOLT raises an additional **€833 million** in financing, further supporting the development of the Saarland facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2020**: Announcement of the construction of the Saarland facility.
- **End of 2023**: Expected start of production at the Saarland facility.
- **April 2023**: Construction of the Saarland facility is postponed to at least **2027** due to local protests.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2020**: SVOLT anticipates creating up to **2,000 jobs** at the Saarland facility upon full expansion.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- SVOLT is a subsidiary of Great Wall Motors and operates the Saarland facility as a wholly-owned entity. There are no indications of joint ventures or shared ownership for this facility.
