### Evolutionary Timeline of the Creos Deutschland Facility in Saarland, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2020**: Creos Deutschland, in collaboration with French natural gas transmission operator GRTgaz, announced the creation of a new cross-border pure hydrogen transmission network called mosaHYc. This project aims to convert existing gas infrastructure to transport hydrogen, utilizing 70 kilometers of pipelines capable of transporting up to 20,000 m³/h (60 MW) of pure hydrogen. The facility is positioned to leverage existing gas pipelines for hydrogen transport, indicating a focus on retrofitting rather than new construction.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2020**: The European Commission is set to support clean hydrogen gas as part of its €750 billion recovery fund, with Germany planning to invest €7 billion in new businesses and research to ramp up hydrogen production capacity to 5 GW by 2030 and 10 GW by 2040. This investment aligns with the development of the hydrogen infrastructure in the Saarland region.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2020**: The announcement of the mosaHYc project marks a significant milestone for the Creos Deutschland facility, as it establishes a framework for hydrogen transport in the Saarland region. The project is part of a broader initiative to create a hydrogen valley, which aims to integrate hydrogen production and consumption in a self-sufficient manner.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2020**: While specific job creation figures were not mentioned in the articles, the development of the hydrogen infrastructure and the associated projects are expected to create jobs in the region, particularly in the construction and operation of the retrofitted pipelines and hydrogen production facilities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2020**: The facility operates as a joint venture between Creos Deutschland and GRTgaz, indicating that it is not under exclusive ownership by Creos Deutschland but rather a collaborative effort to develop hydrogen infrastructure across borders.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic initiatives related to the Creos Deutschland facility in Saarland, DEU, focusing on its role in the emerging hydrogen economy in Europe.
