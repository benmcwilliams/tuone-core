### Evolutionary Timeline of the Salzgitter Flachstahl Facility in Salzgitter, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2021**: Salzgitter Flachstahl is involved in the GET H2 initiative, which aims to develop a cross-border pipeline for green hydrogen. This project is expected to connect various production, transport, storage, and industrial applications of green hydrogen between 2024 and 2030. The initiative aims to significantly reduce CO2 emissions in steel production and other industrial uses.

- **May 2024**: Salzgitter Flachstahl GmbH enters into a long-term power purchase agreement (PPA) with Iberdrola Deutschland for the supply of over 900 GWh of green electricity starting in 2025. This electricity will support the facility's low-CO2 steel production and decarbonization efforts. Additionally, a new photovoltaic (PV) project in Saxony-Anhalt will supply around 60 GWh per year, further enhancing the facility's renewable energy supply.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: The GET H2 initiative partners, including Salzgitter Flachstahl, submit an expression of interest for funding under the IPCEI program to support the development of hydrogen infrastructure. This funding is crucial for the project's implementation, which aims to create a sustainable hydrogen economy.

- **May 2024**: The PPA with Iberdrola is part of Salzgitter's broader energy strategy to secure renewable energy sources for its operations, particularly for the SALCOS® transformation program aimed at climate-neutral production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2021**: The GET H2 initiative is announced, with plans for a hydrogen pipeline and infrastructure development between 2024 and 2030.

- **May 2024**: The PPA with Iberdrola is finalized, marking a significant milestone in securing renewable energy for the facility's operations.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2024**: The expansion of renewable energy supply through the PPA with Iberdrola is expected to support job creation in the context of the facility's transition to low-CO2 steel production, although specific job numbers are not mentioned.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2024**: Salzgitter Flachstahl GmbH is identified as a subsidiary of Salzgitter AG, indicating that the facility is not owned exclusively but is part of a larger corporate structure.
