### Evolutionary Timeline of PowerCo SE Facility in Salzgitter, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2023**: PowerCo SE, a subsidiary of Volkswagen Group, is developing a new dry coating procedure for battery electrode manufacturing in Salzgitter. This technology aims to reduce energy consumption by approximately 30% and eliminate two of the four traditional process steps in electrode production. The dry coating allows for a more efficient application of materials directly onto foils, enhancing energy density and fast-charging capabilities of batteries. The technology is currently being tested and optimized in a pilot line in Northern Germany, with an estimated production start date projected for 2026/2027.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2023**: PowerCo SE has entered into a Joint Development Agreement with Koenig & Bauer AG to further develop the dry coating technology. The new process is expected to save hundreds of millions of Euros annually by reducing energy consumption and floor space requirements. The facility is designed for quick retrofits to accommodate future innovations, with about 30 foreseeable innovations anticipated.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2023**: Announcement of the dry coating technology development and partnership with Koenig & Bauer AG. The technology is expected to exit the development stage by the end of 2024, with production anticipated to start in 2026/2027.

#### 4. Labour

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2023**: PowerCo SE is a subsidiary of Volkswagen Group, indicating that it does not have exclusive ownership of the facility but operates under the larger corporate structure of Volkswagen.
