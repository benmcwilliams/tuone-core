### Evolutionary Timeline of the Salzgitter Group Facility in Salzgitter, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Autumn 2023**: The Boitzenburger Land solar farm, a 180MW facility, begins operations. This solar farm is one of the largest in Germany and will supply Salzgitter Group with up to 64 GWh of electricity per year starting in 2027.
- **2026 Onwards**: Salzgitter Group plans to gradually convert its steel production processes to electricity- and hydrogen-based methods, aiming for an almost CO2-free production process.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **06-08-2024**: Salzgitter Group signs a power purchase agreement (PPA) with RWE Supply & Trading for electricity from the Boitzenburger Land solar farm. The contract is for seven years, starting in 2027, supporting Salzgitter's sustainability strategy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Autumn 2023**: The Boitzenburger Land solar farm becomes operational.
- **2027**: The PPA with RWE Supply & Trading will commence, providing Salzgitter with renewable energy.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify any direct job creation or loss related to the solar farm or the conversion of steel production processes.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Boitzenburger Land solar farm is jointly owned by Solarenergie Boitzenburger Land, GP Joule Group, and Mainova. Salzgitter Group does not own the solar facility exclusively but will benefit from the energy produced through the PPA.

### Summary
The Salzgitter Group facility in Salzgitter, Germany, is evolving towards a more sustainable operation by integrating renewable energy sources into its steel production processes. The facility will leverage electricity from the Boitzenburger Land solar farm, which began operations in autumn 2023, and will transition to electricity- and hydrogen-based steel production starting in 2026. The partnership with RWE Supply & Trading through a PPA marks a significant step in Salzgitter's commitment to reducing its carbon footprint.
