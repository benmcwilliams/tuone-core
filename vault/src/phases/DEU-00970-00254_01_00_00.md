## DEU-00970-00254

### Evolutionary Timeline of the FEV Facility in Sandersdorf-Brehna, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10/2020**: FEV announced the opening of the eDLP (development and test centre for high-voltage batteries) at its Sandersdorf-Brehna site. The facility spans 12,000 square meters and includes 54 climate chambers with an electrical output of 30,000 kW. It is designed for testing modules and complete high-voltage batteries for both passenger cars and commercial vehicles. The facility features a unique 350 kN shaker for combined mechanical and electrical tests under varying ambient conditions (-40 to 100 degrees Celsius). 

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10/2020**: The state of Saxony-Anhalt supported the eDLP project with over 6 million euros. This investment is aimed at enhancing FEV's capabilities in providing development services for electric vehicles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2020**: FEV officially opened the eDLP facility in Sandersdorf-Brehna, claiming it to be the world’s largest development and test centre for high-voltage batteries for passenger and commercial vehicles. The facility is equipped to conduct various tests necessary for achieving series-production readiness.

#### 4. Labour
- **09/2020**: The opening of the eDLP facility is expected to create almost 100 new jobs in the region, contributing to local employment and expertise in electric vehicle technology.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify any joint ventures or partnerships regarding the ownership of the eDLP facility, indicating that FEV operates the facility independently.
