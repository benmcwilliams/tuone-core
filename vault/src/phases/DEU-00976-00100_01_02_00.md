### Evolutionary Timeline of Blackstone Resources Facility in Saxony, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2022**: Blackstone Resources announced plans to produce 3D-printed sodium-ion batteries, with a target market introduction in 2025. The facility in Saxony is involved in the development and production of these batteries, building on previously validated laboratory results. The facility had already started series production of 3D-printed lithium-ion battery cells in December 2021. The project aims to upscale sodium-based solid-state electrolytes for production by 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2022**: A total investment of €32 million is allocated for the pilot plant at the Saxony production site and for extensive developments related to the sodium-ion battery project. Additionally, the project receives funding from the German Federal Ministry of Economics and Climate Protection, as part of the Battery Cell Manufacturing Ecosystem, amounting to up to €24.1 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2021**: Series production of 3D-printed lithium-ion battery cells commenced at the facility in Döbeln, Saxony.
- **March 2022**: Announcement of plans to bring 3D-printed sodium-ion batteries to market by 2025, with the establishment of a German industry and research consortium to demonstrate the technology in a real-world environment.
