### Evolutionary Timeline of the Sovello Facility in Saxony-Anhalt, Germany

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Sovello facility produces photovoltaic (PV) panels using the 'String Ribbon' technology. 

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The facility was initially set to receive an €11.6 million EU grant for its construction. However, this grant was withdrawn due to an investigation by the European Commission, which determined that the influence of shareholder Q-Cells exceeded its 24.9% shareholding, disqualifying Sovello from the 'SME Bonus' intended for small and medium-sized enterprises.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **February 2010:** The European Commission announced the withdrawal of the €11.6 million EU grant for the construction of the Sovello facility.

4. **Labour:**
   - Sovello reported the creation of 1,200 jobs associated with the facility's operations, despite the loss of the EU grant.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - Sovello, formerly known as EverQ, has a significant shareholder, Q-Cells, which holds a 24.9% stake. However, the facility is not exclusively owned by Sovello due to this joint ownership structure.
