### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mercedes-Benz Facility in Sindelfingen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **02/2017**: Daimler announces that the Sindelfingen facility will produce electric vehicles (EVs) as part of a broader strategy to integrate electric car production across existing facilities. The goal is to manufacture ten EV models by 2025.
- **02/2018**: Groundbreaking for Factory 56 at Sindelfingen, which is designed to be the most modern facility for producing electric vehicles, including the next-generation S-Class and EQ models. The factory aims to reduce CO2 emissions by 75% compared to current production methods.
- **09/2020**: Factory 56 officially opens, allowing for the production of various models, including the EQS and S-Class, on a single assembly line, enhancing flexibility and efficiency.
- **05/2021**: Production of the EQS electric sedan begins at Factory 56, marking it as the first fully electric model produced at this facility.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **03/2017**: Daimler announces a €10 billion investment to expand its electric vehicle offerings globally, with a portion allocated to the Sindelfingen facility.
- **02/2018**: Daimler invests €730 million in Factory 56, with total investments in the Sindelfingen plant reaching €2.1 billion to enhance production capabilities for electric vehicles.
- **06/2022**: Mercedes-Benz announces an additional investment of over €2 billion in its European assembly plants, including Sindelfingen, to prepare for the shift to electric vehicles by 2025.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **02/2018**: Groundbreaking for Factory 56.
- **09/2020**: Factory 56 opens, enabling the production of multiple vehicle types on a single line.
- **05/2021**: Series production of the EQS begins.
- **03/2023**: Mercedes-Benz announces plans to produce the AMG.EA platform at Sindelfingen starting in late 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **02/2018**: The construction of Factory 56 is expected to create numerous jobs, although specific numbers are not detailed.
- **04/2022**: The opening of the Electric Software Hub in Sindelfingen is expected to create around 1,000 new jobs for software developers, with a total of up to 2,000 jobs globally.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: The Sindelfingen facility is wholly owned by Daimler AG, with no indications of joint ventures or external ownership.
