### Evolutionary Timeline of the MEMC Facility in Straßkirchen, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** July 2009
   - **Details:** The facility in Straßkirchen, Bavaria, is a joint venture between Q-Cells SE and MEMC Electronic Materials, with a total production capacity of approximately 50 MWp. It is designed to be the largest ground-mounted photovoltaic (PV) system in Germany utilizing crystalline solar cell technology. The project will utilize around 225,000 solar modules installed over a surface area of approximately 135 hectares. The expected output will power about 15,000 households and reduce CO2 emissions by around 35,000 tons annually.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** July 2009
   - **Details:** MEMC is set to invest up to $100 million in the joint venture during the third and fourth quarters of 2009. Q-Cells is expected to match this investment with an additional $100 million to cover bridging finance during the construction phase.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** July 2009
   - **Details:** The construction of the facility has been announced, with expectations for completion and connection to the public electricity network by the end of 2009. The facility is intended to be sold to a third party upon completion.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** July 2009
   - **Details:** The article does not specify job creation or job loss directly associated with the investment or construction of the facility.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** July 2009
   - **Details:** The facility is a joint venture between Q-Cells SE and MEMC Electronic Materials, with each company holding a 50% share in the project.
