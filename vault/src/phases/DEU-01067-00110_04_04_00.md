### Evolutionary Timeline of the Bosch Facility in Stuttgart, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2021**: Bosch, in collaboration with Qingling Motors, established the Bosch Hydrogen Powertrain Systems joint venture in China to develop, assemble, and market fuel cell systems. Small-scale production was scheduled to start in 2021, with a test fleet of 70 trucks equipped with Bosch's Fuel Cell Power Module planned for deployment. The market launch of the fuel cell system was anticipated for 2022/2023.
- **May 2022**: Bosch announced plans to invest up to €500 million to develop components for hydrogen electrolyzers, focusing on the production of stacks for electrolyzers. The first pilot plant for these components was expected to go into operation in 2023, with mass production starting in 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2022**: Bosch committed to investing up to €500 million in the development of hydrogen electrolyzers by 2030, as part of a broader strategy to invest approximately €3 billion in climate-neutral technologies over the next three years.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: Bosch announced the establishment of the Bosch Hydrogen Powertrain Systems joint venture, with small-scale production set to begin in the same year.
- **May 2022**: Bosch's plans for a pilot plant for electrolyzer components were revealed, with operations expected to commence in 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2021**: The establishment of the Bosch Hydrogen Powertrain Systems joint venture and the associated production activities are expected to create jobs, although specific numbers were not disclosed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2021**: Bosch operates the Bosch Hydrogen Powertrain Systems facility as a joint venture with Qingling Motors, indicating a shared ownership structure for this specific facility in China. However, the Stuttgart facility's ownership details regarding the electrolyzer development were not specified, but Bosch is expected to have full ownership of its operations in Germany.
