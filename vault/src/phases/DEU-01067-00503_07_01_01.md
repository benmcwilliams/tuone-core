### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mahle Facility in Stuttgart, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2021**: Mahle Powertrain (MPT), a subsidiary of Mahle, operates a test bench for electric drive systems in Stuttgart. The facility focuses on the development and validation of electric drive systems, fuel cells, and battery technologies.
- **February 2023**: Mahle integrates a DC fast charging system into its climatic wind tunnel in Stuttgart, allowing for charging electric vehicles with up to 350 kilowatts under various climatic conditions. This upgrade enhances the facility's capability to simulate extreme charging scenarios for battery testing.
- **November 2023**: Mahle opens a new test and development center for batteries in Stuttgart, spanning over 1,300 square meters. This facility accommodates all aspects of battery development, from prototyping to small-scale production, and includes climate chambers and a dual-robot laser welding facility for high-voltage battery modules.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: Mahle Powertrain announces a total investment of 12 million euros for the construction of five new test and development facilities, including enhancements at the Stuttgart site.
- **November 2023**: The new battery test and development center in Stuttgart is designed to meet the needs of European customers, indicating ongoing investment in expanding capabilities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
