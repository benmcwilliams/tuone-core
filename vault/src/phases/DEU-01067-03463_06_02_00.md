## DEU-01067-03463

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Solar Cluster Baden-Württemberg Facility in Stuttgart, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Solar Cluster Baden-Württemberg is involved in supporting the development of the Green Silicon project, which aims to establish a vertically integrated photovoltaic (PV) production facility in Brazil and Paraguay. The facility is expected to have an annual production capacity of 1.7 GW, producing approximately 6,800,000 solar modules with an output of 250 W each.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The project is supported by the German industry association, Solar Cluster Baden-Württemberg, which has been involved since 2013. The feasibility study for the project was conducted by several German research institutes, including Fraunhofer IPA, Fraunhofer ISE, and ZSW, indicating a significant investment in research and development for the project.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **January 2018:** The feasibility study for the Green Silicon project was highlighted, with plans for the first phase involving ingot and wafer production in Paraguay, followed by cell and module production in Brazil. The project is still in the planning stages as of this date.

4. **Labour:**
   - The Green Silicon project is expected to create 4,310 direct jobs during its full operational phase, generating an income of €54.8 million and adding €113.4 million in value annually to the local economy.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is not owned exclusively by the Solar Cluster Baden-Württemberg; it is a collaborative effort involving the Brazilian-Paraguayan power company ITAIPU and the industry trade association of the Brazilian state of Paraná (FIEP), indicating a joint venture structure for the project.
