## DEU-01111-00120

### Evolutionary Timeline of the CATL Facility in Thuringia, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2019**: CATL is in the process of building its first European EV battery factory in Thuringia, Germany. The facility is expected to have a total production capacity of 14 GWh.
- **April 2022**: The facility receives approval to mass-produce 8 GWh cells per year. It consists of two plants: G1 for assembling cells into modules and G2 for making battery cells. The factory is designed with strict requirements for clean rooms and sustainability features, including solar panels on the roof.
- **December 2022**: Production officially starts at the Thuringia plant.
- **September 2023**: CATL announces plans to produce its fast-charging Shenxing battery at the Thuringia facility, which promises significant charging capabilities.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2019**: CATL announces an increase in its investment in the Thuringia facility from €240 million to a maximum of €1.8 billion.
- **April 2022**: The total investment for the facility is confirmed at €1.8 billion (approximately $1.96 billion).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2019**: Groundbreaking for the Thuringia facility occurs.
- **April 2022**: The facility is licensed to mass-produce EV batteries.
- **December 2022**: Production begins at the Thuringia plant.
- **September 2023**: CATL announces the upcoming production of the Shenxing battery in Europe, including at the Thuringia facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2022**: The facility is expected to create 1,500 local jobs upon its initial operation, with plans to increase this number to 2,000 as production ramps up.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned exclusively by CATL, as it is the first CATL plant outside of China, indicating full ownership without joint ventures.
