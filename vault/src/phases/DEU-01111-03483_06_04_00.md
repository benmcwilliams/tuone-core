### Evolutionary Timeline of the SolarWorld Facility in Thuringia, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10/2017**: SolarWorld announced a shift in production focus to monocrystalline PERC (Passivated Emitter Rear Contact) cells and modules, moving away from multicrystalline products. This transition aims to build economies of scale with high-efficiency products. The facility in Thuringia is part of this realignment, with an overall module production capacity expected to increase to around 2GW over the next two years, up from approximately 1.3GW in 2016.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10/2017**: SolarWorld indicated plans to invest a mid-double-digit million amount in the expansion and improvement of its high-efficiency technologies, which includes the facility in Thuringia as part of its broader strategy to enhance production capabilities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **10/2017**: The company announced the relocation of smaller production entities in Arnstadt and Freiberg to focus on the Thuringia facility for module production. The restructuring included the closure of smaller module production in Arnstadt, emphasizing the Thuringia facility's role in the company's future operations.

#### 4. Labour
- **10/2017**: The shift in production focus and restructuring led to the announcement of around 400 job losses by the end of 2019. This was part of a broader strategy to streamline operations and enhance efficiency at the Thuringia facility and others.
