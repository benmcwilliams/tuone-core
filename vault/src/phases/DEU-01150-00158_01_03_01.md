## DEU-01150-00158

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Customcells Facility in Tübingen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/2020**: Customcells, in collaboration with Porsche, plans to build a battery cell factory in Tübingen. The facility aims to produce high-performance storage units with high energy density and low internal resistance, targeting a production capacity in the gigawatt range in the medium term.
- **07/2021**: The joint venture Cellforce Group, which includes Customcells and Porsche, aims to operate a production plant with a capacity of at least 100 MWh per year, sufficient for 1,000 vehicles. Operations are expected to start in 2024.
- **11/2021**: Customcells announces that one of the most modern cell production lines in Europe has gone into operation at the Tübingen site.
- **05/2022**: Customcells begins a project to digitize battery production at the Tübingen site, aiming to enhance quality and traceability through a digital twin technology.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07/2021**: Customcells completes a financing round, bringing in three new shareholders, including Porsche, to support the expansion of its Tübingen site and further development of technologies.
- **11/2022**: Customcells raises €60 million in a Series A financing round to expand activities in electrification, research and development, and internationalization.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2020**: Announcement of the planned battery cell factory in Tübingen, pending EU approval for state funding.
- **07/2021**: The German antitrust authority reviews the Cellforce Group, confirming the Tübingen site as the likely location for the battery factory.
- **09/2021**: Porsche's Board of Directors is set to decide on the final location for the factory, with Tübingen being a strong contender.
- **10/2021**: The foundation stone for the Cellforce Group's battery production plant is laid in Reutlingen-Nord/Kirchentellinsfurt, with plans to increase production capacity to over 1 GWh.
- **04/2023**: Customcells tests a digital twin for battery cell production at the Tübingen site as part of the TwinTRACE project.

#### 4. Labour
- **07/2021**: The workforce at the Cellforce Group is expected to grow from 13 to 80 employees by 2025.
- **11/2021**: Customcells employs around 110 people at the Itzehoe site, with plans to increase to 160 by mid-2023, indicating growth in associated operations that may impact Tübingen indirectly.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **07/2021**: Customcells operates as a joint venture with Porsche, with Porsche holding 83.75% ownership of the Cellforce Group, which is responsible for the Tübingen facility. Customcells remains a significant player in the joint venture, retaining majority ownership of its operations.
