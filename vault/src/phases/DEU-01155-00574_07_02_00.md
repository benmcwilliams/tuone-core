### Evolutionary Timeline of the Nikola Facility in Ulm, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2020**: Nikola announced plans to produce the Nikola Tre trucks at the Ulm facility, with a production capacity of 35,000 trucks per year. The facility will manufacture both battery-electric and hydrogen fuel cell versions of the Nikola Tre, with production starting in 2021 for the battery version and in 2023 for the fuel cell version.
- **September 2021**: The manufacturing facility was officially inaugurated, confirming the production of the Nikola Tre trucks. The first battery-electric models are set to be delivered in 2022, while the fuel cell electric vehicle (FCEV) model is expected to enter production by the end of 2023.
- **November 2021**: Nikola announced a long-term battery supply contract with LG Energy Solution for the Ulm facility, which will support the production of the Nikola Tre trucks.
- **January 2022**: Nikola signed a multi-year contract with Proterra for battery systems to be used in the Nikola Tre BEV and FCEV trucks, with the first prototypes expected in the fourth quarter of 2022.
- **September 2022**: Nikola announced the unveiling of the European Nikola Tre FCEV beta version, with serial production expected to start in late 2023 in North America and shortly thereafter in Ulm.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2021**: The inauguration of the facility marked the culmination of investments and development efforts between Nikola and Iveco, aimed at establishing a manufacturing platform for the Nikola Tre trucks.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2020**: Nikola announced plans for the Ulm facility and the production of the Nikola Tre trucks.
- **September 2021**: The facility was inaugurated, marking the official start of operations for the production of the Nikola Tre trucks.
- **November 2021**: Nikola secured a battery supply contract with LG Energy Solution, enhancing the operational capabilities of the Ulm facility.
- **January 2022**: Nikola entered into a contract with Proterra for battery systems, further solidifying the supply chain for the Ulm facility.
- **September 2022**: Nikola announced the upcoming unveiling of the Nikola Tre FCEV beta version, with production timelines established for both the North American and European markets.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2021**: The inauguration of the facility is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers were not disclosed in the articles. The partnership with Iveco suggests a collaborative workforce effort to ensure the success of the operation.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2020**: Nikola is engaged in a joint venture with Iveco for the production of the Nikola Tre trucks at the Ulm facility, indicating that the facility is not solely owned by Nikola but operates under a partnership model with shared responsibilities and resources.
