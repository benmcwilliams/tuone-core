## DEU-01155-00876

### Evolutionary Timeline of the ZSW Facility in Ulm, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **08/2018**: ZSW opens a new pilot manufacturing line for Li-ion cells, utilizing a novel stacking method instead of winding for electrode strips. This aims to enhance cell properties and establish stacking as a new technology in Germany. The facility already assembles prismatic cells with wound electrodes in a fully automated workflow.
- **02/2024**: ZSW inaugurates the "Powder-Up!" pilot plant, enabling the manufacturer-independent production of battery materials and their precursors, with a capacity of up to 100 kilograms. This facility covers all production steps for high-energy battery materials and aims to shorten development cycles for new materials.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **08/2018**: The STACK project, aimed at developing an ultra-fast manufacturing process for Li-ion cells, is funded with €2.7 million from the Federal Ministry of Education and Research (BMBF).
- **10/2021**: The HyFaB research factory project receives funding of €18.5 million from the state, with contributions from the Baden-Württemberg Ministry of Economics and the Ministry of the Environment. This facility focuses on fuel cell stack manufacturing processes.
- **02/2024**: The construction of the "Powder-Up!" pilot plant is funded with €24 million from the BMBF and €10 million from the Baden-Württemberg State Ministry of Economic Affairs, Labour and Tourism.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **01/2018**: The STACK project begins, focusing on the development of a new manufacturing method for Li-ion cells.
- **10/2021**: Groundbreaking for the HyFaB factory occurs, with commissioning planned for early 2022.
- **12/2022**: Construction of the "Powder-Up!" pilot plant begins.
- **02/2024**: The "Powder-Up!" pilot plant is inaugurated, marking a significant milestone in battery material production in Germany.

#### 4. Labour
- **02/2024**: The ZSW facility employs around 300 experts and approximately 100 scientific and student assistants across its sites in Stuttgart and Ulm, indicating a stable workforce supporting its research and development activities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The ZSW facility in Ulm is part of the Centre for Solar Energy and Hydrogen Research Baden-Württemberg (ZSW), which operates as a research institution. The ownership structure is not explicitly stated as exclusive or joint venture in the articles, but it is implied that ZSW operates the facility independently while collaborating with various industrial partners for specific projects.
