### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iveco Facility in Ulm, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2021**: The Iveco facility in Ulm was inaugurated, marking the start of production for the Nikola Tre trucks, which are based on the Iveco S-WAY truck platform. The facility is designed to produce both battery-electric and fuel cell electric vehicles (FCEVs). The first battery-electric models are expected to be delivered to select customers in the US in 2022, with FCEV production slated to begin by the end of 2023.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2023**: Iveco Group and Nikola Corp. agreed to split their operations, with Iveco Group assuming full ownership of the Ulm facility. This transition allows Iveco to freely access and develop vehicle control software for jointly developed battery electric vehicles (BEVs) and FCEVs.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2021**: Inauguration of the manufacturing facility for the Nikola Tre trucks.
- **End of 2023**: Expected start of production for the fuel cell electric vehicle (FCEV) model.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2023**: Iveco Group took full ownership of the joint venture in Ulm, Germany, which was previously a collaboration with Nikola Corp. This change allows Iveco to independently manage the facility and its operations related to BEVs and FCEVs.
