## DEU-01164-00521

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mercedes-Benz Facility in Untertürkheim, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2017**: Mercedes-Benz announces plans to prepare the Untertürkheim facility for EQ production, focusing on electric motors and prototypes in a new e-technology center (E-Technikum).
- **January 2018**: Untertürkheim is designated as a hub for battery assembly and electric mobility know-how, part of a global strategy to launch 10 all-electric models by 2022.
- **February 2018**: Daimler confirms that Untertürkheim will host a competence center for batteries, integrating battery research with its subsidiary Accumotive.
- **March 2018**: The facility is set to produce parts of the electric powertrain (eATS) for the EQ family, marking a shift towards in-house production of electric drives.
- **July 2018**: Mercedes announces an expansion of battery production capacity at Untertürkheim, planning to quadruple local battery production capacity.
- **January 2019**: Production of electric drives (eATS) is confirmed to begin in Untertürkheim from 2024, securing 350 jobs.
- **June 2022**: Mercedes plans to double electric drive production capacity to up to one million units per year, with production starting at the end of 2024.
- **December 2022**: The facility will produce battery systems for new electric models, with a focus on in-house production of electric drives and components.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2018**: Daimler earmarks €10 billion for its global electrification strategy, with a portion allocated to the Untertürkheim facility for battery assembly.
- **July 2018**: An additional investment of over €1 billion is announced for global battery production, including expansions at Untertürkheim.
- **June 2022**: A mid-single-digit billion euro investment is planned for European powertrain production sites, including Untertürkheim, to support electric vehicle production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2018**: Daimler announces the establishment of a competence center for batteries at Untertürkheim.
- **March 2018**: The facility is confirmed to produce parts of the electric powertrain for the EQ family.
- **July 2018**: Expansion plans for battery production at Untertürkheim are announced.
- **January 2019**: Confirmation of electric drive production starting in 2024.
- **June 2022**: Plans to double production capacity for electric drives are confirmed.
- **December 2022**: Official announcement of the production of battery systems for new electric models.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2018**: The integration of Accumotive’s 300 employees into Daimler’s workforce is confirmed, with job security ensured through negotiations with the works council.
- **March 2018**: The establishment of a project house for electric drive systems is expected to create over 250 jobs.
- **January 2019**: The production of eATS is expected to secure 350 jobs at the facility.
- **June 2022**: The doubling of production capacity is anticipated to create additional job opportunities as the facility ramps up electric drive production.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility in Untertürkheim is fully owned by Daimler AG, with no joint ventures or external ownership reported in the articles. The integration of Accumotive and the establishment of the competence center for batteries further solidify Daimler's control over operations at this site. 

This timeline captures the significant developments at the Mercedes-Benz facility in Untertürkheim, highlighting its transition towards electric vehicle production and the strategic investments made to support this shift.
