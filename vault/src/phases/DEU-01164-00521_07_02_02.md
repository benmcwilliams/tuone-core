### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mercedes-Benz Facility in Untertürkheim, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2017**: Mercedes-Benz announces plans to prepare the Untertürkheim facility for EQ production, focusing on electric motors and establishing an E-Technikum (e-technology center) for prototypes.
- **January 2018**: Untertürkheim is designated as a hub for battery assembly and electric mobility know-how, part of a global strategy to launch 10 all-electric models by 2022.
- **February 2018**: Daimler confirms that Untertürkheim will host a competence center for batteries, integrating battery research with its subsidiary Accumotive.
- **March 2018**: The facility is set to assemble components for the GLC F-Cell, including the fuel cell stack and hydrogen storage system.
- **July 2018**: Mercedes announces an expansion of battery production capacity at Untertürkheim, planning to quadruple local battery production capacity.
- **April 2019**: Construction begins on a battery sub-plant at the Brühl site within Untertürkheim, aimed at assembling batteries for future EQ vehicles.
- **December 2019**: Daimler decides to produce parts of the electric powertrain (eATS) at Untertürkheim, with production expected to start in 2024.
- **January 2022**: Mercedes plans to produce the entire drive system for new electric architectures in-house from 2024, including at Untertürkheim.
- **June 2022**: Production capacities for electric drives at Untertürkheim are set to double, aiming for up to one million units per year by the end of 2024.
- **December 2022**: Mercedes confirms the assembly of battery systems for new electric models at Untertürkheim, with a focus on increasing vertical integration.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2018**: Daimler earmarks €10 billion for its electrification strategy, with a portion allocated to battery assembly at Untertürkheim.
- **July 2018**: Over €1 billion is invested in expanding battery production capacities at Untertürkheim.
- **May 2023**: Mercedes announces a significant investment to boost electric drive production capacities at Untertürkheim, part of a broader strategy to electrify its vehicle lineup.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2018**: Announcement of the establishment of a competence center for batteries at Untertürkheim.
- **April 2019**: Groundbreaking for the battery sub-plant at the Brühl site.
- **December 2019**: Agreement to produce eATS components at Untertürkheim starting in 2024.
- **June 2022**: Confirmation of plans to double electric drive production capacity, with production set to start by the end of 2024.
- **December 2022**: Official confirmation of the assembly of battery systems for new electric models at Untertürkheim.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2018**: The integration of Accumotive’s 300 employees into Daimler’s workforce at Untertürkheim is confirmed, with job security agreements in place.
- **July 2018**: The expansion of battery production is expected to create over 250 new jobs at Untertürkheim.
- **December 2019**: The production of eATS is expected to secure 350 jobs at Untertürkheim.
- **June 2022**: The doubling of production capacity is anticipated to further secure jobs at the facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Throughout the timeline**: The facility is owned exclusively by Daimler AG, with no joint ventures or external ownership reported.
