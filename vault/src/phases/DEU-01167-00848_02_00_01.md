### Evolutionary Timeline of the Vulcan Energy Facility in Upper Rhine Graben, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **02/2021**: Vulcan Energy announces plans to extract lithium from geothermal brine in the Upper Rhine Graben. The facility aims to produce 2,000 metric tons of lithium hydroxide annually from a pilot plant at the Insheim geothermal power plant, with commercial operations expected to start as early as 2022. By 2024, two plants are projected to extract 15,000 tons annually, and a second phase starting in 2025 aims for three additional plants to reach a total capacity of 40,000 tons.
- **01/2023**: A definitive feasibility study reveals an increase in production capacity to 24,000 tonnes of lithium hydroxide monohydrate (LHM) per year for Phase 1, with the identification of new production sites. The project will also generate over 300 GWh of renewable electricity and more than 250 GWh of renewable heat annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **02/2023**: The investment for the first phase of the lithium project is estimated at €1.496 billion. This investment is aimed at developing the sites and starting production by the end of 2025.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **02/2021**: Vulcan Energy begins works on a pilot plant at the Insheim geothermal power plant.
- **01/2023**: The definitive feasibility study is published, confirming the increased production capacity and outlining the investment needed for the first phase. The company targets the start of production by the end of 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **02/2023**: While specific job creation figures are not mentioned, the expansion of production capacity and the development of new sites are expected to create jobs in the region as the facility scales up operations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **01/2023**: Vulcan Energy is noted as one of Stellantis' largest suppliers of lithium, and Stellantis is mentioned as a major shareholder in Vulcan Energy. This indicates a joint venture or partnership structure rather than exclusive ownership.
