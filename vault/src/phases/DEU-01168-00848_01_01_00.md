### Evolutionary Timeline of the Vulcan Energy Facility in Upper Rhine Valley, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2021**: Vulcan Energy Resources successfully produced its first battery-quality lithium hydroxide monohydrate (LHM) from its sorption pilot plant at a geothermal renewable energy plant in the Upper Rhine Valley. The facility aims to produce battery-quality lithium hydroxide from geothermal brine, targeting a production capacity of 24,000 tons of LHM per year in its first phase.
- **April 2024**: Vulcan Energy announced a partnership with ABB to optimize production processes for lithium extraction from geothermal brine, further enhancing operational efficiency.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2021**: Stellantis invested €50 million in Vulcan Energy, becoming the second-largest shareholder with an 8% stake. This investment is aimed at expanding Vulcan's production capacity at the Upper Rhine Valley facility.
- **June 2022**: Stellantis extended its binding off-take agreement for lithium hydroxide with Vulcan by five to ten years, indicating a long-term commitment to sourcing lithium from the facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2021**: Announcement of the successful production of battery-quality lithium hydroxide from pilot operations.
- **February 2023**: Completion of a definitive feasibility study for the first phase of the lithium project, confirming the planned production capacity of 24,000 tons of LHM per year.
- **April 2024**: The first geothermal cluster began producing lithium chloride, which is being stored until the completion of the electrolysis plant for further processing.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2022**: The investment from Stellantis is expected to support job creation related to the expansion of production capacity at the Upper Rhine Valley facility, although specific job numbers were not disclosed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2021**: Following Stellantis' investment, Vulcan Energy is partially owned by Stellantis, indicating a joint venture structure where Vulcan retains majority ownership but has significant investment and influence from Stellantis.
