### Evolutionary Timeline of the Valmet Facility in Weihenbronn, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2020**: Valmet Automotive launched a new Battery Test Center (BTC) in Weihenbronn, expanding testing capacities in the region. The facility spans 1,500 square meters and includes a workshop, prototype construction, and an electrical laboratory equipped with advanced test benches for battery technology. It focuses on abuse testing at the cell and module level.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2021**: Valmet Automotive announced plans to expand its engineering activities in Germany, including the Weihenbronn facility, to strengthen development areas related to battery systems and testing. Specific investment amounts were not disclosed, but the expansion is part of a broader strategy to enhance e-mobility capabilities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2020**: The Weihenbronn Battery Test Center was officially commissioned.
- **February 2021**: Valmet Automotive confirmed ongoing expansions in engineering and testing capacities at the Weihenbronn site, alongside other locations in Germany.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2021**: Valmet Automotive announced the creation of new jobs in Germany, including positions related to testing battery systems at the Weihenbronn facility. The specific number of jobs created at this location was not detailed, but the overall hiring initiative was part of a larger recruitment effort across multiple sites.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Valmet Automotive operates the Weihenbronn facility as a wholly-owned site, focusing on its role as a contract manufacturer and system supplier for battery systems. There is no indication of joint ventures or shared ownership in the provided articles.
