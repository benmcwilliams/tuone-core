## DEU-01237-01759

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Leclanché Facility in Willstätt, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2011**: Leclanché has been operational at the Willstätt location, producing Lithium Titanate and Lithium Graphite/NMC cells.
- **June 2021**: Leclanché announces the development of a new generation of lithium-ion modules (M3) designed for energy-intensive electrified transport applications. These modules are manufactured at the Willstätt facility.
- **June 2022**: Leclanché achieves a breakthrough in battery safety by adding a special additive to the electrolyte formula, significantly reducing the risk of thermal events. The new cells are expected to be available for production from Q1 2023.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2018**: Leclanché's largest shareholder, FEFAM, approves an investment of up to CHF 20 million for the joint venture with Exide Industries in India, which will source cells from the Willstätt facility until local production begins.
- **December 2021**: Leclanché announces a spin-off of its electric transport business into a new wholly-owned company, Leclanché E-Mobility S.A., as part of a strategic move to enhance its operations.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2018**: Announcement of a joint venture (JV) between Leclanché and Exide Industries to manufacture lithium-ion batteries in India, with cells sourced from Willstätt until local production starts.
- **June 2021**: Launch of a new lithium battery module for electrified transport applications.
- **December 2021**: Establishment of Leclanché E-Mobility S.A. as a separate entity for transport batteries.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2018**: The joint venture with Exide is expected to leverage Leclanché's existing production capabilities in Willstätt, potentially impacting job creation or retention at the facility, although specific job numbers are not mentioned.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2018**: Leclanché enters a joint venture with Exide Industries, where Exide holds a 75% stake and Leclanché retains a 25% stake, making it a strategic minority shareholder in the JV. The Willstätt facility remains fully owned by Leclanché.
