### Evolutionary Timeline of the Volkswagen Facility in Wolfsburg, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2016**: Volkswagen announced plans to extend electric vehicle (EV) production from Wolfsburg to other locations, including Dresden and Zwickau. The facility in Wolfsburg was already producing the Golf and Tiguan.
- **April 2018**: Volkswagen confirmed plans to produce EVs globally at 16 locations by 2022, emphasizing the importance of electrification and digitization.
- **June 2018**: Production capacity for the e-Golf was planned to increase from 120 to 160 vehicles daily at the Wolfsburg facility.
- **January 2019**: Volkswagen announced a significant investment of approximately 9 billion euros for electrification initiatives, including upgrades to the Wolfsburg plant.
- **March 2020**: The Wolfsburg plant was set to become a model factory for the highly automated production of electric vehicles.
- **December 2021**: Plans were confirmed for the ID.3 to be produced in Wolfsburg starting in 2023, with full production ramping up in 2024.
- **November 2022**: Volkswagen confirmed that the ID.3 would be produced in Wolfsburg, alongside an all-electric SUV model planned for 2026.
- **March 2023**: Volkswagen announced that the ID.3 would start production in Wolfsburg in autumn 2023, with plans for an additional all-electric SUV model.
- **September 2023**: Volkswagen confirmed that the ID.3 production would be expanded to Wolfsburg, with initial production in small numbers.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2016**: Volkswagen announced plans for a pilot battery factory in Salzgitter, Germany, as part of its EV strategy.
- **April 2018**: Volkswagen committed to investing heavily in battery development and electrification strategies.
- **January 2019**: A total of 9 billion euros was earmarked for electrification initiatives, including upgrades to the Wolfsburg facility.
- **December 2021**: Volkswagen announced a 460 million euro investment in the Wolfsburg plant to prepare for the production of the ID.3 and a new electric SUV.
- **November 2022**: Volkswagen confirmed a 1 billion euro investment in the conversion of the Emden plant for electric vehicle production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2020**: Volkswagen announced that the main plant in Wolfsburg would become a model factory for electric vehicle production.
- **December 2021**: The planning round confirmed that the ID.3 would be produced in Wolfsburg starting in 2023.
- **March 2023**: Production of the ID.3 was set to begin in Wolfsburg in autumn 2023.
- **September 2023**: Volkswagen confirmed that the ID.3 would be produced in Wolfsburg, with initial production in small numbers.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2016**: Volkswagen announced a reduction of 30,000 jobs but agreed to create 9,000 new jobs in electric mobility.
- **July 2021**: Reports indicated that Volkswagen was considering job cuts due to low demand for electric vehicles.
- **November 2021**: Volkswagen and IG Metall reached a compromise that included cutting 35,000 jobs by 2030.
- **September 2023**: Volkswagen announced a temporary hiring freeze at several locations, including Wolfsburg, due to ongoing efficiency programs.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: Volkswagen maintains full ownership of the Wolfsburg facility, with no joint ventures reported for this specific location.
