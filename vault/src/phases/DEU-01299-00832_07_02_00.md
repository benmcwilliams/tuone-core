### Evolutionary Timeline of the Volkswagen Facility in Zwickau, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2016**: Volkswagen announced plans to extend EV production to Zwickau, alongside other facilities.
- **May 2017**: VW specified that serial production of the I.D. concept would begin in Zwickau in late 2019, with a production capacity of 300 vehicles daily.
- **November 2017**: VW confirmed Zwickau would become its first pure electric mobility plant, focusing on the MEB platform.
- **January 2018**: VW announced plans to produce 1,500 EVs daily in Zwickau by 2020, with modifications to production lines starting in summer 2019.
- **March 2018**: Production of the I.D. family was confirmed to start in Zwickau by the end of 2019.
- **November 2019**: Production of the ID.3 began, marking the transition to a fully electric vehicle plant.
- **August 2020**: Production of the ID.4 commenced, expanding the facility's output.
- **January 2022**: Production of the ID.5 began, completing the transformation of Zwickau into a dedicated electric vehicle plant.
- **November 2022**: Zwickau set a production record of 7,100 electric cars in one week.
- **March 2023**: Production of the ID.3 was planned to start in Wolfsburg, but was later canceled, consolidating production in Zwickau.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2017**: VW announced a production investment of €300 million for the Zwickau facility.
- **November 2018**: VW confirmed an investment of €1.2 billion to upgrade Zwickau for electric vehicle production.
- **2021**: VW planned to invest €21 billion in its German plants, including Zwickau, to support electric vehicle production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2019**: ID.3 production began, marking the start of Zwickau's transformation.
- **August 2020**: ID.4 production started.
- **January 2022**: ID.5 production commenced.
- **March 2023**: Production of the ID.3 was planned to start in Wolfsburg but was canceled, keeping production in Zwickau.
- **October 2023**: Production was paused due to low demand and supply chain issues.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2016**: VW announced a reduction of 30,000 jobs but planned to create 9,000 new jobs in electric mobility.
- **2023**: VW announced the termination of temporary contracts for around 1,000 employees due to low demand.
- **October 2023**: Reports indicated that 2,200 jobs could be cut in Zwickau if demand did not improve.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: Volkswagen maintains full ownership of the Zwickau facility, which is dedicated exclusively to electric vehicle production.

This timeline captures the significant developments in technology, investment, project milestones, labor changes, and ownership structure of the Volkswagen facility in Zwickau, Germany, focusing on its transition to electric vehicle production.
