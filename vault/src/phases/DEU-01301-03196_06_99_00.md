### Evolutionary Timeline of the Meyer Burger Facility in Zülpich, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **11/2012**: Meyer Burger consolidates its manufacturing and technology operations, stating that the Zülpich facility will remain responsible for production and equipment assembly. The facility was involved in manufacturing optical measuring and testing technologies for photovoltaic wafer and cell production.

- **12/2019**: Meyer Burger announces the closure of the Zülpich manufacturing facility, which was used for Hennecke-branded optical measuring and testing technologies. Operations will be shifted to the Hohenstein-Ernstthal facility.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12/2019**: The closure of the Zülpich facility is part of a broader cost-cutting strategy due to falling demand and unattractive margins in the photovoltaic (PV) market, particularly in the Chinese market. The company expects restructuring costs of around CHF 7 million (€6.4 million) as a result of this closure.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2019**: Meyer Burger confirms the decision to close the Zülpich facility, with production expected to cease by mid-2020. The company plans to concentrate its German production in Hohenstein-Ernstthal.

#### 4. Labour
- **12/2019**: The closure of the Zülpich facility will affect approximately 60 employees. Meyer Burger states it will offer "socially compatible solutions" to those affected, indicating support for job transitions.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not provide specific details regarding the ownership structure of the Zülpich facility, but it is implied that Meyer Burger owned the facility exclusively as it was part of their operations before the closure announcement.
