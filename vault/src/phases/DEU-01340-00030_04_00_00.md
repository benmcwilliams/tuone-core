### Evolutionary Timeline of the Alstom Facility in Lower Saxony, Germany

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **August 2022:** The facility is involved in the operation of the world’s first network with hydrogen fuel cell trains in passenger service. The facility produces and operates 14 hydrogen-powered Coradia iLint regional trains, which have replaced 15 diesel trains. The hydrogen filling station built in Bremervörde has a storage capacity of 1,800 kilograms of hydrogen, with plans for future on-site hydrogen production through electrolysis.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **August 2022:** The total project volume for the hydrogen train network is over 93 million euros. This investment supports the transition from diesel to hydrogen-powered trains, showcasing a commitment to renewable energy and climate neutrality in the transport sector.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **September 2018:** The first two pre-series Coradia iLint trains were put into service on the route between Cuxhaven and Buxtehude.
   - **August 2022:** The complete fleet of 14 Coradia iLint trains in series trim officially goes into passenger service.
