### Evolutionary Timeline of the Byton Facility in Ismaning, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility in Ismaning was associated with Byton GmbH, a subsidiary of the Chinese electric car start-up Byton, which was focused on producing electric vehicles, specifically the M-Byte model. However, by early 2021, reports indicated that the facility had been largely inactive, with the building reportedly empty for months.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - In January 2021, it was announced that Byton had reached an agreement with Foxconn for an investment of $200 million, aimed at joint production of the M-Byte starting in 2022. However, the effectiveness of this investment was called into question due to the company's financial difficulties.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - On April 20, 2021, the Munich District Court ordered provisional insolvency administration for Byton GmbH, indicating a significant downturn in the facility's operational status. The insolvency proceedings were a result of ongoing financial struggles, including unpaid salaries for employees at the Ismaning facility.

4. **Labour:**
   - Reports indicated that employees at the Ismaning facility had not been paid for months leading up to the insolvency proceedings. Additionally, Byton had furloughed half of its US employees in April 2021, reflecting broader job losses within the company.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - Byton GmbH was a subsidiary of the Chinese parent company Byton. The articles do not indicate any joint venture or shared ownership structure for the Ismaning facility, suggesting that Byton GmbH operated as a wholly-owned subsidiary of the parent company.
