## DEU-01476-01140

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Jungheinrich Facility in Glauchau, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2019**: Jungheinrich, in partnership with Triathlon Holding, founded a joint venture named JT Energy Systems aimed at producing and reconditioning lithium-ion battery systems. The facility in Glauchau is intended to support this operation by providing modules for battery systems and chargers.
- **August 2019**: JT Energy Systems announced plans to establish a highly automated module production facility in Glauchau, which will supply battery systems and chargers for electric forklifts and lift trucks.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2019**: The joint venture JT Energy Systems acquired the former Solarworld plant in Freiberg, Germany, to create what is described as the largest European production center for batteries and charging systems in intralogistics. This acquisition is part of a broader strategy to expand lithium-ion battery production capabilities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2019**: JT Energy Systems confirmed that the operational start for the Freiberg plant is planned for the first quarter of 2020. This facility will be crucial for the assembly of battery systems and chargers, with modules sourced from the Glauchau facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2019**: The establishment of JT Energy Systems and the acquisition of the Freiberg plant is expected to create around 200 jobs in the short term, contributing to local employment in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2019**: The facility in Glauchau is part of a joint venture, JT Energy Systems, which is equally owned by Jungheinrich and Triathlon Holding, with each company holding a 50% stake in the venture.
