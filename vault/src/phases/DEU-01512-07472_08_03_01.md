### Evolutionary Timeline of the Nordex Facility in Rostock, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2019**: Nordex began serial production of the Delta4000 range of wind turbines at the Rostock facility. The first units of the N149/4.0-4.5 model were produced, with over 700MW in orders already received.
- **February 2023**: The facility commenced serial production of nacelles for the 6MW class turbines, specifically the N163/6.X model. The plant is noted as the primary facility for nacelle production in Nordex's global network, with plans for an annual output of 1GW for these turbines.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2023**: Nordex invested approximately €2 million to convert the Rostock production plant to accommodate the new 6MW turbine production lines.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2019**: Start of serial production for the Delta4000 turbine series.
- **February 2023**: Official start of serial production for the 6MW turbine nacelles, marked by a ceremony attended by company executives and local government officials.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2022**: Nordex announced plans to cease blade production at the Rostock factory, impacting about 600 employees due to market challenges and competition.
- **February 2023**: The workforce at the Rostock plant was around 770 employees, with plans to hire and train an additional 75 specialists to support the new 6MW turbine production.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify any joint ventures or shared ownership; thus, it can be inferred that Nordex operates the Rostock facility under full ownership.
