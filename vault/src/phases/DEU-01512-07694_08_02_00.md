### Evolutionary Timeline of EEW Special Pipe Constructions Facility in Rostock, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2021**: EEW Special Pipe Constructions (SPC) received an order from Iberdrola to manufacture 50 monopile foundations for the Baltic Eagle offshore wind farm. Production was scheduled to start in January 2022 and last until January 2023. The monopiles will have a diameter between 8.75-9 meters and a maximum length of 90 meters, with the heaviest monopiles weighing 1,402 tonnes.
- **August 2022**: EEW SPC manufactured its heaviest monopile to date, weighing 1,421 tonnes and measuring 89.36 meters in length. The production of the monopiles was expected to be completed by the end of November 2022.
- **January 2023**: EEW SPC completed the production of all 50 monopiles for the Baltic Eagle project, with the last monopile leaving the production line in December 2022.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2021**: The contract with Iberdrola for the monopile foundations was a significant investment in the facility's production capabilities, involving the processing of approximately 64,600 tonnes of steel.
- **April 2023**: EEW SPC entered a conditional agreement with OX2 for the delivery of up to 230 monopile foundations for two offshore wind projects in Sweden, indicating ongoing investment in expanding production capacity.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2022**: Production of monopiles for the Baltic Eagle project commenced.
- **November 2022**: Production of monopiles was expected to be completed.
- **December 2022**: The last monopile for the Baltic Eagle project was produced.
- **Spring 2023**: The monopiles were scheduled to be floated to the installation site for the Baltic Eagle offshore wind farm.
- **April 2023**: EEW SPC secured a conditional agreement for the supply of monopile foundations for two offshore wind projects in Sweden.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2021**: The contract with Iberdrola was expected to create 800 jobs and 1.3 million man-hours during the production of the monopiles and transition pieces.
