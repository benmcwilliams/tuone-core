### Evolutionary Timeline of the EEW Facility in Rostock, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2019**: EEW SPC completes the first pipe for the 72 monopile foundations for Vattenfall's 604.8MW Kriegers Flak offshore wind farm. Production is expected to be completed by September 2019.
- **November 2019**: EEW completes the production of the last monopiles for the Kriegers Flak project, with coating scheduled for February 2020.
- **April 2020**: Foundations for two 6-megawatt Siemens Gamesa turbines are loaded onto a cargo ship in Rostock, marking EEW's involvement in the U.S. offshore wind market.
- **November 2021**: EEW wins a contract to manufacture 176 monopiles for the 2.6GW Coastal Virginia Offshore Wind (CVOW) project, processing over 200,000 tonnes of steel starting in spring 2023.
- **January 2023**: EEW signs a contract to manufacture 100 monopiles for RWE’s 1.4GW Sofia offshore wind project in the UK, with production scheduled to begin in June 2023.
- **September 2023**: The first eight monopiles for the CVOW project are shipped from Rostock to the U.S. The monopiles are 83 meters long and weigh 1,538 tonnes.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2021**: The contract for the CVOW project is part of a larger investment strategy by Dominion Energy, which includes a €1.6bn contract awarded to a consortium for the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2019**: Announcement of the start of monopile production for the Kriegers Flak project.
- **November 2019**: Completion of monopile production for Kriegers Flak.
- **April 2020**: Loading of monopiles for the U.S. offshore wind project.
- **November 2021**: Contract awarded for the CVOW project.
- **September 2023**: First shipment of monopiles for the CVOW project to the U.S.
- **October 2023**: Arrival of the first monopiles at the Portsmouth Marine Terminal for the CVOW project.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2022**: The Baltic Eagle project, which involves EEW, is expected to create around 800 jobs during its construction phase.
- **May 2024**: The installation of monopiles for the CVOW project will involve skilled American union pile drivers, contributing to local job creation.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: EEW SPC operates as a fabricator and supplier for various offshore wind projects, including joint ventures and contracts with companies like Dominion Energy and RWE, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership of the facility.
