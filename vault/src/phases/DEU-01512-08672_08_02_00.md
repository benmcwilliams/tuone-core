### Evolutionary Timeline of the EEW Group Facility in Rostock, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Focus**: The facility in Rostock, known as EEW SPC, specializes in the production of monopiles for offshore wind energy.
- **Operational Updates**: As of August 2024, the facility is part of the newly established EEW Offshore Wind Holding, which organizes the monopile business and associated mechanical engineering activities.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Partnership**: On 19 August 2024, it was announced that Japan-based Sumitomo Corporation acquired an equity stake in EEW Offshore Wind Holding, effective from 1 July 2024. This partnership aims to enhance EEW's capabilities in handling large-scale offshore wind projects and aligns with Sumitomo's commitment to renewable energy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Establishment of Holding Companies**: The formation of EEW Offshore Wind Holding, along with two other holding companies (EEW Pipe Solutions Holding and EEW AOS Holding), was announced on 19 August 2024. This restructuring is intended to improve the organization of EEW Group's business segments.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: While specific job creation figures were not mentioned in the articles, the partnership with Sumitomo and the restructuring of the company suggest potential for growth in employment opportunities as the facility aims to expand its operations in the offshore wind market.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership Details**: The EEW Offshore Wind Holding, which includes the Rostock facility, is primarily owned by the founding families of Schorge and Dietze, with Sumitomo Corporation acquiring a minority stake. The majority shares remain with the owner families, indicating a joint venture structure for this facility.
