## DEU-01524-01242

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Prime Lithium Facility in Stade Chemical Park, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Focus**: Prime Lithium aims to produce high-quality lithium hydroxide monohydrate (LHM) for electric vehicles (EVs).
- **Production Capacity**: The facility is designed to produce lithium for 500,000 EVs per year.
- **Future Plans**: The company plans to operate its first production plant, referred to as ‘Train 1’, by 2030, with potential for additional factories in the future.
- **Research and Development**: A dedicated R&D center has been established in Stade since July 2023, focusing on optimizing the soda leach process to enhance production efficiency and sustainability.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Ownership and Investment**: Prime Lithium is a subsidiary of Deutsche Rohstoff AG, which owns 85% of the company. The remaining 15% is held by CEO Axel Heitmann. The facility is located on 22 hectares of land contractually reserved from Dow Chemical.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Founding**: Prime Lithium was founded in 2021.
- **Pilot Plant Plans**: The company aims to realize a pilot plant on-site by the end of 2025.
- **Production Start**: The target for the first production plant operation is set for 2030.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The establishment of the R&D center and future production facilities is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers have not been disclosed in the article.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: Prime Lithium is primarily owned by Deutsche Rohstoff AG (85%), with CEO Axel Heitmann holding the remaining 15%. The facility operates under a contractual agreement with Dow Chemical for the land use.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic plans for the Prime Lithium facility in Stade Chemical Park, highlighting its role in the European battery value chain and the company's commitment to sustainable lithium production.
