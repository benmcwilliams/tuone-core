## DEU-01538-00695

### Evolutionary Timeline of the SVOLT Facility in Lauchhammer, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2022**: SVOLT confirmed plans to build a battery cell factory in Lauchhammer, Brandenburg, with an initial production capacity of **16 GWh**. The facility is a brownfield project, repurposing a former wind turbine plant owned by Vestas. The company aims to modernize the existing infrastructure and start production by **early 2025**. 
- **April 2023**: SVOLT announced plans to expand its production capacity in Europe to at least **50 GWh** by the end of the decade, with the Lauchhammer site being part of this strategy. The facility is expected to produce batteries for approximately **300,000 to 500,000 electric vehicles per year**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2022**: SVOLT applied for an IPO in China, intending to raise **15 billion yuan (around 2 billion euros)** to invest in production capacity expansion and R&D, which includes the Lauchhammer facility.
- **October 2023**: SVOLT's plans for the Lauchhammer facility were confirmed to involve significant investments, although specific amounts were not disclosed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2022**: SVOLT announced the acquisition of the Lauchhammer site and plans for a battery cell production facility.
- **April 2023**: Construction was reported to be underway for the conversion of the existing plant into a **12 GWh** facility.
- **May 2024**: SVOLT announced it would not proceed with the Lauchhammer facility due to reassessment of its location strategy and challenges in the automotive market. This included the cancellation of a significant customer project and ongoing planning difficulties.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2024**: The announcement of the cancellation of the Lauchhammer facility did not specify job creation or loss, but it implied that the planned investments and operations would not materialize, potentially affecting local employment opportunities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2022**: SVOLT took over the Lauchhammer site from Vestas, indicating **full ownership** of the facility. However, the subsequent cancellation of the project in May 2024 raises questions about the future of this ownership in terms of operational capacity.

### Summary
The SVOLT facility in Lauchhammer, DEU, was initially planned as a significant investment in battery cell production with a capacity of 16 GWh, aimed at supporting the growing electric vehicle market. However, due to market challenges and planning difficulties, SVOLT ultimately decided not to proceed with the facility, marking a significant shift in its European expansion strategy.
