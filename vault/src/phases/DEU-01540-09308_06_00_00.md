### KACO Facility in San Antonio, DEU - Evolutionary Timeline

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 30-05-2014
   - KACO, a German-based renewable energy company, is involved in the construction of the 400MW Alamo solar project in San Antonio. The facility is part of a consortium of manufacturers that includes OCI Solar Power and Ercam Trackers. The project will utilize solar panels and PV equipment sourced from KACO and other partners. The fourth phase of the Alamo project is expected to be operational by the end of 2014, producing power for approximately 6,000 homes with 150,000 solar panels installed across 600 acres.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 30-05-2014
   - OCI Solar Power has an employment and economic growth agreement that aims to provide permanent employment for at least 800 jobs and stimulate business and economic development of at least US$700 million annually in Greater San Antonio. The reorganization of Ercam Trackers, which is part of the new joint venture with Paru Co, is expected to increase OCI Solar Power’s capital investment in San Antonio through the relocation to a larger manufacturing facility.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 30-05-2014
   - The fourth phase of the Alamo solar project is scheduled to be online by the end of 2014. This phase is part of a larger initiative that includes the construction of a new PV facility in San Antonio called 'Mission Solar Energy', which is also expected to be completed in 2014.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 30-05-2014
   - Ercam Trackers laid off over 50 workers as a result of the reorganization following the joint venture with Paru Co. Despite the layoffs, OCI Solar Power maintains its commitment to creating 800 permanent local jobs as part of the Alamo project.
