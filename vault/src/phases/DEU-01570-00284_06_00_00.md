### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fraunhofer Facility in Rhineland-Palatinate, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2021**: The Fraunhofer Institute for Solar Energy Systems ISE, in collaboration with BayWa r.e., established an agrivoltaic facility in Rhineland-Palatinate. The facility features a 258 kWp solar array designed to explore how solar systems can protect agricultural crops, specifically apple production, from extreme weather conditions. The project involves testing various PV module configurations to assess their impact on plant growth and crop yields.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2022**: The German government announced an initiative to support solar projects on agricultural land, which could potentially benefit the agrivoltaic facility by providing funding under the Renewable Energy Act (EEG) and the European Union’s Common Agricultural Policy (CAP). This initiative aims to promote the dual use of land for both agriculture and solar energy production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2021**: The agrivoltaic facility was officially constructed and began its research project, which is publicly funded and set to last for five years. The project aims to compare apple production under different crop protection systems, including rain-blocking foil, hail protection nets, and various types of PV modules.
