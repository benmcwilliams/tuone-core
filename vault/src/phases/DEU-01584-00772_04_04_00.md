### Evolutionary Timeline of ThyssenKrupp Facility in Duisburg, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **01/2020**: ThyssenKrupp, in partnership with E.On, successfully tested a hydrogen electrolysis plant with a capacity of 2 MW as part of the Carbon2Chem pilot scheme in Duisburg. This plant is designed to produce green hydrogen efficiently and flexibly, allowing it to participate in balancing energy markets.
- **02/2021**: ThyssenKrupp announced plans to establish a 500 MW hydrogen electrolysis plant at its Duisburg steel production site, known as the HydrOxy Hub Walsum project. This facility aims to support climate-neutral steel production across Europe.
- **01/2024**: ThyssenKrupp Steel Europe launched a public tender for the procurement of up to 151,000 tons of low-carbon hydrogen to supply its direct-iron reduction facilities in Duisburg, indicating a significant ramp-up in hydrogen production capabilities.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **02/2021**: ThyssenKrupp and Steag are seeking recognition for the HydrOxy Hub Walsum project as an Important Project of Common European Interest (IPCEI) to secure investment funding from federal and state sources. The project is expected to be powered by renewable energy sources, including wind and solar.
- **02/2024**: The German Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs and Climate Protection, along with the state of North Rhine-Westphalia, is funding the hydrogen ramp-up for the direct reduction plant project with approximately €2 billion.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **01/2020**: The partnership with E.On to integrate hydrogen electrolysis plants into the electricity market was announced, marking a significant step towards making these plants market-ready.
- **02/2021**: Plans for the 500 MW hydrogen electrolysis plant were announced, with an investment decision expected in spring 2023 and a target connection to the grid by 2025.
- **02/2024**: ThyssenKrupp issued a call for tenders for hydrogen supply to its direct reduction plant, aiming for full hydrogen operation by 2029.

#### 4. Labour
- **02/2024**: The development of the hydrogen economy and the establishment of the direct reduction plant are expected to create jobs, although specific job creation figures have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- ThyssenKrupp appears to have full ownership of the facility, particularly in the context of the direct reduction plant and the hydrogen production initiatives, although it collaborates with partners like E.On and Steag for specific projects.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic initiatives of the ThyssenKrupp facility in Duisburg, highlighting its role in advancing hydrogen technology and sustainable steel production.
