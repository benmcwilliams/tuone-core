### Evolutionary Timeline of the ArcelorMittal Facility in Duisburg, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2022**: ArcelorMittal, in partnership with RWE, announced plans to transition to low-emissions steel production. The facility aims to replace coal with renewable energy sources, specifically offshore wind power and green hydrogen, to decarbonize its operations. The facility will move towards using electric arc furnaces (EAFs) and direct reduction (DRI) plants, with a goal to gradually replace natural gas with green hydrogen as it becomes cost-competitive.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2022**: The partnership with RWE includes plans for a 70 MW pilot green hydrogen electrolysis plant by 2026, with intentions to scale up to gigawatt-scale projects in the long term, contingent on public funding approval. This indicates significant investment in renewable energy infrastructure to support the facility's transition to low-emissions steel production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2022**: The memorandum of understanding between ArcelorMittal and RWE marks a significant milestone in the facility's evolution towards decarbonization. The companies are assessing options for joint participation in offshore wind farm tenders in the North Sea, which is crucial for securing renewable energy for the facility.
