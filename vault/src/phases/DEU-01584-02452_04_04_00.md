### Evolutionary Timeline of the Steag Facility in Duisburg, Germany

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **February 2021:** Steag, in collaboration with Thyssenkrupp, announced plans to establish a 500 MW green hydrogen electrolysis plant at the Thyssenkrupp steel production site in Duisburg-Walsum. The facility aims to generate green hydrogen powered by a mix of wind and solar energy. The project, named HydrOxy Hub Walsum, is designed to support climate-neutral steel production across Europe.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **February 2021:** The companies are seeking recognition for the HydrOxy Hub Walsum project as an Important Project of Common European Interest (IPCEI) to secure investment funding from federal and state sources. However, the realization of the project is not solely dependent on this funding.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **February 2021:** The project is in the planning phase, with an investment decision expected in spring 2023. If approved, the electrolyzer is projected to be connected to the grid by 2025.
