### Evolutionary Timeline of the Air Liquide Facility in Oberhausen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2021**: Air Liquide announced plans to build a 30 MW electrolyzer for green hydrogen production in Oberhausen, with an initial capacity of 20 MW, expected to be operational by early 2023. The facility will be integrated into existing hydrogen and oxygen pipeline infrastructure to supply key industries and mobility sectors in the region.
- **June 2023**: Air Liquide inaugurated the 20 MW electrolysis plant, named "Trailblazer," claiming it to be the largest climate-neutral hydrogen production facility in Germany, with an annual capacity of up to 2,900 tons.
- **August 2024**: The facility was confirmed to be operational, supplying renewable hydrogen for key industries and zero-emission mobility applications in the Rhine and Ruhr regions.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2021**: Air Liquide announced an investment of €8 billion ($9.4 billion) in the low-carbon hydrogen value chain by 2035, which includes a total of 3 GW electrolysis capacity by 2030. The project in Oberhausen received funding from the German Federal Ministry of Economic Affairs and Energy.
- **June 2023**: Air Liquide signed a power purchase agreement (PPA) with Statkraft for 45 MW of wind power to supply the electrolyzer plant, ensuring renewable energy for hydrogen production starting in 2024.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2021**: Announcement of the project and partnership with Siemens for the electrolyzer.
- **Early 2023**: Expected operational date for the initial 20 MW capacity.
- **June 2023**: Inauguration of the 20 MW electrolysis plant.
- **August 2024**: Confirmation of the facility's operational status and its capacity to produce renewable hydrogen.
