### Evolutionary Timeline of the Bosch Facility in Feuerbach, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **06/2020**: Bosch enters into a 16-year power purchase agreement with RWE for solar electricity generated by a 50 MW solar plant in southern Germany. This agreement is part of Bosch's climate neutrality goals.
- **07/2023**: Bosch begins production of Fuel Cell Power Modules (FCPM) at the Feuerbach facility. The FCPM has a total output exceeding 200 kW and weighs more than 500 kilograms. The facility will also produce components for hydrogen technologies, including a water treatment system scheduled for launch in 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **06/2022**: Bosch announces an investment of up to €500 million to develop hydrogen electrolyzers, focusing on the production of stacks for electrolyzers. This investment is part of a broader commitment to invest about €3 billion in climate-neutral technologies over the next three years.
- **07/2023**: Bosch plans to invest nearly €2.5 billion in the development and manufacturing of hydrogen technologies between 2021 and 2026, which is an increase from previous investment plans.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **06/2020**: Bosch signs multiple solar power purchase agreements, including one with RWE, to support its climate neutrality goals.
- **07/2023**: Production of Fuel Cell Power Modules begins at the Feuerbach facility, marking a significant milestone in Bosch's hydrogen technology development.

#### 4. Labour
- **07/2023**: The production of fuel-cell power modules and the development of hydrogen technologies at the Feuerbach facility are expected to create jobs, although specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Bosch owns the facility in Feuerbach exclusively, as indicated by the company's direct involvement in the production and development of technologies at this site.
