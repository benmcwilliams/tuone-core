## DEU-01805-00219

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EnBW Facility in Forbach, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Production**: The existing Rudolf-Fettweis-Werk (RFW) has a total output of approximately 71 MW, consisting of four individual power plants.
- **New Developments**: EnBW plans to convert and expand the RFW into a pumped storage power plant. The new facility will feature:
  - A pump turbine with approximately 54 MW in turbine operation and 57 MW in pump mode.
  - Three additional Francis turbines with a total capacity of around 23 MW.
- **Construction Details**: The project includes the expansion of the existing Forbach equalization reservoir to incorporate a cavern water reservoir, enhancing its capacity for the new pumped storage power plant.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Amount**: EnBW has committed approximately €280 million ($304.9 million) for the conversion and expansion of the facility.
- **Contract Awards**: In July 2023, construction company Porr was awarded a contract valued at €98.3 million for civil works related to the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Planning Approval**: The planning approval notice for the project was issued on March 1, 2023, after over five years of intensive work by experts and authorities.
- **Construction Start**: Construction is set to commence in autumn 2023.
- **Completion Timeline**: The project is expected to be completed by the end of 2027.
- **Tunnel Construction**: As of July 27, 2024, tunnel construction has started, involving significant excavation work to accommodate the new facilities.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The project is expected to create jobs during the construction phase, although specific numbers have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: EnBW Energie Baden-Württemberg AG exclusively owns the facility, as indicated by their direct investment and management of the project.
