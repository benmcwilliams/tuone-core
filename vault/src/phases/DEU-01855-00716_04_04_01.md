## DEU-01855-00716

### Evolutionary Timeline of Siemens Facility in Wunsiedel, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2020**: Siemens announced plans to build a hydrogen production plant in Wunsiedel, which will convert renewable energy from solar PV and wind power into storable hydrogen. The initial capacity is set to produce over 900 tonnes of hydrogen per year, with potential expansion to 2000 tonnes.
- **July 2021**: Construction began on the 8.75 MW green hydrogen plant, which aims to produce up to 1,350 tons of hydrogen annually. The facility will utilize a PEM electrolyzer and is designed to meet hydrogen demand in regions of Germany and Czechia.
- **September 2022**: Siemens commissioned the 8.75 MW PEM electrolyzer, marking it as Germany's second-largest electrolyzer. The facility is designed to operate solely on renewable energy sources.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2020**: The project is a collaboration between Siemens Smart Infrastructure and Siemens Energy, indicating significant investment in the development of green hydrogen technology.
- **July 2021**: Siemens signed a letter of intent for a 100 MW battery energy storage system in Wunsiedel, which will complement the hydrogen production facility by managing renewable energy integration.
- **September 2022**: The project is supported by Umweltbank, indicating financial backing for the commissioning of the electrolyzer.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2020**: Announcement of the hydrogen production plant with groundbreaking scheduled for the end of the year.
- **July 2021**: Construction of the green hydrogen plant commenced.
- **Summer 2022**: The facility is scheduled to begin operations, producing green hydrogen.
- **September 2022**: The commissioning of the 8.75 MW electrolyzer was completed.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2021**: The construction and operation of the facility are expected to create jobs in the region, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2021**: The facility is jointly owned by Siemens and German gas company Rießner Gase, each holding a 45% stake, while the remaining 10% is owned by Stadtwerke Wunsiedel, a local public utility. This structure indicates a collaborative approach to ownership and operation.
