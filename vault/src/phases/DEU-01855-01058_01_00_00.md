## DEU-01855-01058

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fluence Facility in Wunsiedel, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2021**: Siemens and Fluence announced plans to construct a large-scale battery energy storage system (BESS) with a capacity of 100 MW / 200 MWh in Wunsiedel. The facility will utilize lithium-ion battery technology to store surplus energy generated from renewable sources, which will be injected into the grid during peak demand periods.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2021**: The project is part of a collaboration with Zukunftsenergie Nordostbayern, a consortium of regional stakeholders, which aims to develop a financing concept for the construction of the facility. Specific investment amounts have not been disclosed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2021**: Siemens and Zukunftsenergie Nordostbayern signed a letter of intent to construct the 100 MW battery storage facility. The project is intended to enhance the integration of renewable energy resources and manage peak load constraints on the local power grid.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the construction or operation of the facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is a joint venture between Siemens and AES Corporation, under the brand Fluence. Siemens is responsible for project management, while Fluence provides the battery storage system. The ownership structure indicates a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership by a single entity.
