### Evolutionary Timeline of the RWE Facility in Inden, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2022**: RWE began construction of the RWE Indeland Solarpark at the Inden opencast lignite mine. The facility has a production capacity of **14.4 MW** and includes a battery storage system designed for a **two-hour power consumption** to buffer between power generation and the grid. The solar modules are arranged in two strips, each 1.4 km long and 100 meters wide.
- **September 2023**: The RWE Indeland Solarpark has been operational since 2022, generating solar electricity from over **26,500 solar modules**. The electricity produced is sufficient to supply approximately **4,000 German households**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2022**: RWE announced an investment of **€11 million** for the construction of the RWE Indeland Solarpark, which is part of a broader strategy to develop renewable energy capacity in the Rhenish lignite mining area, aiming for at least **500 MW** by 2030.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2022**: Construction of the RWE Indeland Solarpark commenced.
- **September 2022**: The facility began operations, marking it as the first of its kind in Europe to be built on an opencast lignite mine site.

### Summary
The RWE facility in Inden, Germany, represents a significant step in transitioning from fossil fuels to renewable energy, with a focus on solar power generation and battery storage. The facility's development is part of RWE's broader strategy to enhance renewable energy capacity in the region, with substantial investments and operational milestones achieved since its inception.
