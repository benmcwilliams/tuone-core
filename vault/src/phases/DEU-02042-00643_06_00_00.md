### Evolutionary Timeline of the RWE Facility in Jackerath, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date: 09/2023**: The Jackerath facility has a solar photovoltaic (PV) capacity of **12.1 MWp** and a battery energy storage system (BESS) with a capacity of **4.1 MW/8.1 MWh**. It is located on the western edge of the Garzweiler opencast mine. The facility utilizes more than **58,000 bifacial solar PV modules**, which can generate solar electricity for over **7,250 households** annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date: 11/2022**: RWE announced the construction of the Jackerath project as part of a broader initiative to develop solar and battery storage facilities in opencast mines. The company aims to achieve a total renewable energy capacity of **500 MW** in the Rhenish lignite mining area by **2030**.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date: 09/2023**: The Jackerath facility officially began operations alongside the Garzweiler solar PV project, which has a capacity of **19.4 MWp** and a BESS of **6.5 MW/13 MWh**. The combined area of both projects is approximately the size of **38 football fields**.
