## DEU-02043-00643

### Evolutionary Timeline of RWE Facility in Lingen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2021**: RWE announced the installation of a battery system with a total capacity of 117 MW at its power plants in Lingen and Werne. The Lingen facility will have a capacity of 45 MW, contributing to balancing energy and grid stability.
- **December 2021**: RWE selected SMA Solar Technology to provide system technology for the 117 MW battery storage project, which will enhance grid stability and support renewable energy fluctuations.
- **January 2023**: RWE completed the installation of Germany's first mega battery system with a total capacity of 117 MW, including 49 MWh at Lingen. The system is designed to work in conjunction with RWE's hydropower plants to stabilize the electricity grid.
- **May 2022**: RWE secured funding for a 14 MW electrolyzer test facility in Lingen, which will test two electrolyzer technologies under industrial conditions.
- **August 2024**: RWE commissioned a 14 MW pilot electrolyzer plant in Lingen, capable of generating up to 270 kg of green hydrogen per hour using renewable electricity.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2021**: RWE invested approximately €50 million in the battery storage project at Lingen.
- **May 2022**: RWE announced an investment of €30 million for the 14 MW electrolyzer test facility, with an additional €8 million from the state of Lower Saxony.
- **July 2024**: RWE secured €619 million in funding from the German government for the construction of a 300 MW electrolyzer in Lingen as part of the GET H2 Nukleus project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2021**: Announcement of the battery storage project.
- **December 2021**: Technology selection for the battery storage project.
- **January 2023**: Completion of the mega battery installation.
- **May 2022**: Approval and funding for the 14 MW electrolyzer test facility.
- **August 2024**: Commissioning of the 14 MW pilot electrolyzer plant.
- **September 2024**: Final investment decision (FID) on the 300 MW GET H2 Nukleus project.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2022**: The construction of the 14 MW electrolyzer facility is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers were not disclosed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- RWE owns the facility exclusively, taking full control of project planning, modeling, system integration, and commissioning for the battery and electrolyzer projects.
