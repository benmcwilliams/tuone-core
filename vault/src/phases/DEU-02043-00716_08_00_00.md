### Evolutionary Timeline of the Siemens Facility in Lingen, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Production Capacity:** Siemens Energy is set to supply converter platforms for the DolWin4 and BorWin4 offshore grid connection systems, which will transport up to 1.8 GW of power from wind farms in the German North Sea to land.
   - **Updates:** The facility will include two converter platforms at sea and two associated stations on land, with the technology designed to convert alternating current from wind turbines into direct current for efficient long-distance transmission.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Investment Value:** The order value for the grid connection systems is in the high three-digit million-euro range, marking it as Siemens Energy's largest offshore grid connection order to date.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Announcement Date:** 22-07-2022
   - **Construction Start:** The construction of the land converter stations is planned to take place near Amprion’s Hanekenfähr substation in Lingen.
   - **Production Start:** The connections are scheduled to begin operating in 2028, with BorWin4 expected to be connected to the grid one year earlier than originally planned.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Job Creation:** While specific job creation numbers are not mentioned, the construction and operation of the converter stations and associated technology are expected to create jobs in the region, particularly as the facility will replace the generation capacity of the Emsland nuclear power plant, which is scheduled to shut down at the end of 2022.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Ownership:** Siemens Energy is responsible for the technology supply and maintenance of the converter solutions, indicating full ownership of the operational aspects of the facility, while the design and construction of the offshore platforms are handled by the Spanish consortium partner Dragados Offshore S.A., a subsidiary of the VINCI Group.
