## DEU-02043-01042

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Evonik Facility in Lingen, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Evonik facility is involved in the production and utilization of green hydrogen as part of the GET H2 initiative. The project aims to connect production, transport, storage, and industrial acceptance of green hydrogen from Lingen to various locations, including Gelsenkirchen and Salzgitter, between 2024 and 2030. The initiative is designed to facilitate the use of green hydrogen in refineries and steel production, contributing to a significant reduction in CO2 emissions.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - In March 2021, Evonik, along with other partners in the GET H2 initiative, submitted an expression of interest for funding under the IPCEI (Important Project of Common European Interest) program to the Federal Ministry of Economics and Technology. This funding is crucial for the development of the hydrogen infrastructure and network construction.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - The GET H2 initiative, which includes Evonik, is set to develop a hydrogen pipeline and infrastructure from Lingen to various locations by 2030. The project is currently in the planning phase, with expressions of interest for funding submitted in March 2021.

4. **Labour:**
   - The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the Evonik facility in Lingen as a result of the hydrogen project.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The Evonik facility is part of a collaborative effort within the GET H2 initiative, which includes multiple partners (bp, Nowega, OGE, RWE, Salzgitter Flachstahl, and Thyssengas). Therefore, it is not exclusively owned by Evonik but is part of a joint initiative with shared ownership among the partners involved. 

This timeline captures the key developments related to the Evonik facility in Lingen, focusing on its role in the green hydrogen initiative and the collaborative nature of its ownership and operations.
