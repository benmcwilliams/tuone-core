### Evolutionary Timeline of the Linde Facility in Lingen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2021**: RWE announced plans to create a 200 MW electrolyser plant in Lingen, Germany, as part of its GET H2 project, with the ambition to scale up to 2 GW by 2030. The initial stage will consist of two 100 MW proton exchange membrane (PEM) electrolysers powered by offshore wind.
- **August 2024**: RWE commissioned a 14 MW pilot electrolyzer plant at the Lingen site, generating up to 270 kilograms of green hydrogen per hour. This pilot plant includes a 10 MW alkaline electrolyzer and a 4 MW PEM electrolyzer designed by Linde. RWE plans to commission a 100 MW electrolyzer by 2025, with an expansion to 300 MW expected by 2027.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2021**: The project is subject to the award of state aid from the Federal Republic of Germany, which is crucial for the detailed engineering, procurement, and construction phases. The investment decision is contingent on securing public funding under the IPCEI mechanism.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2021**: RWE preselected Linde as the technical provider for the electrolyser plants and signed an agreement for permit planning.
- **August 2024**: The pilot electrolyzer plant was commissioned, marking a significant milestone in RWE's hydrogen production efforts at the Lingen facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2024**: The commissioning of the pilot plant and the planned expansions are expected to create jobs, although specific job creation figures were not mentioned in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2021**: The articles do not specify the ownership structure of the facility, but RWE is the primary company involved in the project, with Linde serving as the technical provider. There is no indication of joint ownership or partnerships mentioned in the provided articles.
