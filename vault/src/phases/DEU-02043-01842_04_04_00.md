### Evolutionary Timeline of the Orsted Facility in Lingen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10/2020**: Orsted and BP announced plans to develop a 50 megawatt (MW) electrolyser at the Lingen facility, aimed at producing green hydrogen through the electrolysis of water using renewable energy. The electrolyser is expected to generate up to one tonne of renewable hydrogen per hour, totaling almost 9,000 tonnes annually, which would replace approximately 20% of the refinery's current grey hydrogen consumption. The project aims for operational status by 2024 and has long-term ambitions to expand to over 500 MW of renewable hydrogen capacity.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10/2020**: Orsted and BP applied for funding from the EU Innovation Fund to support the Lingen Green Hydrogen project, which is part of a broader initiative to develop innovative low-carbon technologies.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **10/2020**: Announcement of the collaboration between Orsted and BP for the green hydrogen project at the Lingen refinery, with a target for the electrolyser to be operational by 2024.
- **11/2020**: Confirmation of the joint development of the 50 MW renewable hydrogen project, with a focus on maximizing the efficiency of the electrolyser system and integrating it into the refinery operations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
