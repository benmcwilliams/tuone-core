## DEU-02043-02164

### Evolutionary Timeline of the BP Facility in Lingen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10-11-2020**: BP and Orsted announced plans to develop a 50 MW electrolyser at the Lingen refinery, aiming to produce nearly 9,000 tonnes of green hydrogen annually, which would replace about 20% of the refinery's grey hydrogen consumption. The project is expected to be operational by 2024.
- **16-07-2024**: BP revealed plans to develop a 100 MW green hydrogen installation at the Lingen refinery, capable of producing 10-11,000 tonnes of green hydrogen per year. This facility will be the largest industrial-scale green hydrogen production plant BP has developed to date.
- **19-12-2024**: BP confirmed the final investment decision (FID) for the 100 MW Lingen Green Hydrogen project, with commissioning set for 2027. The plant will be connected to Germany’s hydrogen core network.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10-11-2020**: BP and Orsted applied for funding from the EU Innovation Fund for the initial 50 MW electrolyser project.
- **16-07-2024**: The German government granted €4.6 billion for 23 green hydrogen projects, including funding for BP's 100 MW project at Lingen, supported by the Important Projects of Common European Interest (IPCEI) program.
- **19-12-2024**: BP secured funding from both the German federal government and the State of Lower Saxony for the 100 MW project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **10-11-2020**: Announcement of the collaboration between BP and Orsted for the green hydrogen project.
- **15-03-2021**: BP, as part of the GET H2 initiative, submitted an expression of interest for funding to develop a cross-border hydrogen pipeline, which includes the Lingen facility.
- **19-12-2024**: BP made the final investment decision for the 100 MW Lingen Green Hydrogen project, with construction expected to start in 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **16-07-2024**: While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned, the development of the 100 MW green hydrogen project is expected to create jobs in the region, contributing to the local economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **20-12-2024**: The 100 MW Lingen Green Hydrogen project will be the first hydrogen facility that BP will fully own and operate, indicating a shift towards exclusive ownership of this facility.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the BP facility in Lingen, Germany, focusing on its evolution in technology, investment, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.

### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: "The electrolyser at Lingen will powered by renewable energy generated by an Orsted wind farm in the North Sea. It should produce almost 9,000 tonnes of green hydrogen a year" (Article 1, 10-11-2020).

### 2. Investment and Development
- **Initial Funding**: "BP and Orsted have together applied for funding from the EU Innovation Fund" (Article 1, 10-11-2020).

- **IPCEI Support**: "The plant could produce up to 11,000 tonnes of green hydrogen per year, for use in bp’s own refineries and other industrial customers in the region" (Article 9, 19-12-2024).

### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Initial Announcement**: "They intend to build a 50 megawatt electrolyser at the Lingen facility and are working towards a final investment decision in early 2022" (Article 1, 10-11-2020).
- **Final Investment Decision**: "BP has made a final investment decision for its 100 MW Lingen Green Hydrogen project in Germany" (Article 10, 20-12-2024).
- **Construction Timeline**: "With construction scheduled to start in 2025, bp expects to commission the plant in 2027" (Article 9, 19-12-2024).
