### Evolutionary Timeline of the Sunfire Facility in Lingen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2023**: Sunfire delivered eight pressurized alkaline electrolyzer modules with a total capacity of 10 MW to RWE's construction site at the Emsland gas-fired power station in Lingen. These modules are intended for a pilot plant that will generate up to 290 kg of green hydrogen per hour using renewable energy.
- **September 2023**: A high-temperature solid oxide electrolyser (SOEC) developed by Sunfire, with an output of 250 kW, was installed at the facility. This electrolyser can produce approximately 170 kg of hydrogen per day, primarily for research purposes as part of the GET H2 TransHyDE project.
- **August 2024**: RWE commissioned a 14 MW pilot electrolyzer plant at the Lingen site, capable of generating up to 270 kg of green hydrogen per hour. This plant features both the 10 MW alkaline electrolyzer from Sunfire and a 4 MW PEM electrolyzer from ITM Power.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2023**: The GET H2 TransHyDE project, which includes the installation of the 250 kW SOEC, received funding of 11.63 million euros from the Federal Ministry of Education and Research to support its development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2023**: Announcement of the delivery of 10 MW alkaline electrolyzers to RWE for the pilot project.
- **September 2023**: The first hydrogen was produced using the 250 kW SOEC, marking the start of hydrogen production in Lingen.
- **August 2024**: RWE officially commissioned the 14 MW pilot electrolyzer plant, with plans for further expansion under the GET H2 Nukleus project, aiming for a 100 MW electrolyzer by 2025 and a potential expansion to 300 MW by 2027.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
