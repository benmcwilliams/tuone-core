### Evolutionary Timeline of the OGE Facility in Lingen, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Focus**: The OGE facility is involved in the production and transport of green hydrogen as part of the GET H2 initiative.
- **Capacity and Infrastructure**: The facility is part of a larger project aimed at creating a cross-border pipeline for green hydrogen, connecting various locations from Lingen to Gelsenkirchen and beyond. The project aims to integrate production, transport, storage, and industrial acceptance of green hydrogen.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Funding Initiatives**: In March 2021, OGE, along with other partners in the GET H2 initiative, submitted an expression of interest for funding under the IPCEI (Important Project of Common European Interest) program to the Federal Ministry of Economics and Technology. This funding is crucial for the development of the hydrogen infrastructure.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Project Timeline**: The overall project is set to develop between 2024 and 2030, with the goal of avoiding CO2 emissions of up to 16 million tonnes by 2030 through the use of green hydrogen in various industrial applications.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: While specific job creation figures are not mentioned in the article, the development of the hydrogen economy and infrastructure is expected to create jobs in the region as the project progresses.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture**: OGE is part of a consortium of seven companies (including bp, Evonik, Nowega, RWE, Salzgitter Flachstahl, and Thyssengas) involved in the GET H2 initiative, indicating that the facility is not owned exclusively by OGE but is part of a collaborative effort.
