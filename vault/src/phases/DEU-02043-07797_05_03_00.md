### Evolutionary Timeline of the Framatome Facility in Lingen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date: 11/2024**: Framatome's fabrication facility in Lingen produced fuel assemblies utilizing the PROtect technology, which includes chromium-coated cladding. This technology enhances oxidation resistance, improves mechanical performance at high temperatures, and reduces hydrogen generation in case of cooling loss. The facility's fuel rods completed four 12-month operational cycles at the Gösgen nuclear power plant in Switzerland, demonstrating the effectiveness of the E-ATF technology.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date: 01/2024**: Framatome planned to extend the manufacturing plant in Lingen with a dedicated VVER-fuel production line. However, the project faced opposition from the Lower Saxony Government, which does not have veto rights under Atomic Law, leaving the final decision to the federal government.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date: 01/2024**: The decision regarding the extension of the manufacturing plant for VVER-fuel production was still pending from the federal government.
- **Date: 11/2024**: The successful operational milestone of the PROtect technology was announced, confirming the facility's capability to produce advanced fuel assemblies.

#### 4. Labour

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date: 01/2024**: Framatome operates the facility as a subsidiary of Électricité de France (EDF). There was an initial plan for a joint venture with Rosatom's subsidiary TVEL for VVER fuel production, but this was redirected to a setup in France due to anticipated opposition from the German government.
