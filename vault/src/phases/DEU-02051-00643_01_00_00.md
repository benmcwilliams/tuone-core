### Evolutionary Timeline of RWE Facility in Werne, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2021**: RWE announced plans to install a battery system with a total capacity of 117MW at its power plants in Lingen and Werne. The system at Werne is designed to have a capacity of 72MW, contributing to balancing energy and enhancing flexibility in the power grid.
- **November 2021**: RWE confirmed the selection of SMA Solar Technology to provide the system technology for the battery storage facilities, which will include 420 lithium-ion battery racks across both locations.
- **January 2023**: RWE completed the installation of Germany's first megabattery, with a total capacity of 117MW (128MWh). The Werne facility specifically has a battery capacity of 79MWh. The system is virtually connected to RWE’s run-of-river power plants along the Moselle River, allowing for enhanced grid stability.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2021**: RWE announced an investment of approximately €50 million for the battery storage project at Werne and Lingen.
- **January 2023**: The completion of the megabattery project at Werne and Lingen was achieved with the same investment of around €50 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2021**: Announcement of the battery storage project.
- **November 2021**: Confirmation of technology provider and project specifications.
- **January 2023**: Completion of the megabattery installation, with the system already feeding electricity into the grid and undergoing trial runs. Commercial operations were scheduled to start shortly after.
