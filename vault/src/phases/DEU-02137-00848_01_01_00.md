### Evolutionary Timeline of the Vulcan Energy Facility in Höchst Chemical Park, Germany

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** September 2021
   - **Details:** Vulcan Energy Resources announced plans for its Central Lithium Plant (CLP) as part of its Zero Carbon Lithium Project at Höchst Chemical Park. The facility is intended to process lithium chloride from combined geothermal and lithium sorption plants into battery-quality lithium hydroxide monohydrate (LHM). The CLP will serve as a processing hub, significantly reducing the transport footprint of lithium supply for the battery and electric vehicle industry.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** September 2021
   - **Details:** Vulcan secured a site for the CLP by signing an agreement with Infraserv, the chemical park management company. The site offers advantages such as proximity to project areas, low carbon transport options, renewable power availability, and space for future expansions.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** September 2021
   - **Details:** Vulcan successfully produced its first battery-quality lithium hydroxide monohydrate from pilot operations prior to the announcement. The company is in the process of obtaining necessary permits for the construction and operation of the CLP.
