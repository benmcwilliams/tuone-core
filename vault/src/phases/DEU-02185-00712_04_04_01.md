## DEU-02185-00712

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Shell Facility in Wesseling, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2021**: Shell officially opened a 10 MW polymer electrolyte membrane (PEM) hydrogen electrolysis plant at the Wesseling site of its Rheinland refinery. This facility is noted as the largest of its kind in Europe. The plant is part of the Refhyne European consortium and is the first to use this technology at such a large scale in a refinery. Shell plans to expand the electrolyzer's capacity to 100 MW, with a potential 'Refhyne II' facility expected to be ready by 2024. Additionally, Shell aims to produce sustainable aviation fuel (SAF) using renewable energy and biomass at the site.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2021**: The construction of the 10 MW electrolyzer involved significant investment, although specific financial figures were not disclosed. The project received European Commission funding through the Fuel Cells and Hydrogen Joint Undertaking (FCH JU).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2021**: The 10 MW PEM electrolyzer was officially opened on July 2, 2021, marking a significant milestone in the facility's development. Plans for future expansion to 100 MW were also announced.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2021**: While specific job creation figures were not mentioned, the opening of the electrolyzer and the planned expansion are expected to contribute positively to local employment opportunities in the clean technology sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: Shell operates the facility as part of its Rheinland refinery, indicating full ownership of the site. There is collaboration with other partners in the Refhyne consortium, but Shell retains exclusive ownership of the facility.

### Summary
The Shell facility in Wesseling, Germany, has evolved significantly with the opening of a 10 MW PEM electrolyzer in July 2021, which is the largest in Europe. The facility is part of a broader strategy to transition to green hydrogen production, with plans for future expansions to increase capacity. The project has received European funding and is expected to create job opportunities, while Shell maintains full ownership of the facility.
