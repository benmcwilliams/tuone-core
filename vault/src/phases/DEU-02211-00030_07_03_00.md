### Evolutionary Timeline of the Alstom Facility in Niedersachsen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2022**: Alstom's Coradia iLint trains are launched as part of the world's first operational hydrogen train route in Niedersachsen, Germany. The facility produces hydrogen-powered trains that can operate all day on a single hydrogen tank, which is refueled overnight at a Linde hydrogen filling station. The trains are equipped with Cummins fuel cell systems.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2022**: Cummins announces it will provide an additional 54 fuel cell systems to Alstom for an order of 27 Coradia iLint trains, which are set to be delivered to the Frankfurt metropolitan area. This indicates ongoing investment in the hydrogen train technology and infrastructure.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2022**: The Niedersachsen facility officially begins operations with 14 hydrogen-powered trains as part of a plan to gradually replace diesel locomotives on a 100 km stretch of track in northwestern Germany. This marks a significant milestone in the transition to hydrogen-powered public transport.
