### Evolutionary Timeline of the EWE Facility in Huntorf, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **11/2021**: The EWE facility in Huntorf is part of a hydrogen hub project planned in collaboration with Uniper. The facility aims to utilize green hydrogen as a key component for the energy transition, with research being conducted on the potential use of green hydrogen in thermal processes at the site.

- **09/2024**: EWE announced plans to convert a natural gas cavern in Huntorf to store hydrogen. This project is part of the larger "Clean Hydrogen Coastline" initiative, which focuses on the production, storage, transport, and use of green hydrogen, particularly in industrial applications.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **11/2021**: The German government confirmed funding of €6 million for five innovative research projects across Lower Saxony, including the hydrogen hub in Huntorf. This funding is aimed at supporting the development of green hydrogen technologies.

- **09/2024**: EWE received funding notices from the Federal Minister of Economics for the "Clean Hydrogen Coastline" project, which includes the Huntorf facility. The project is currently in the detailed planning phase, with hydrogen storage expected to commence in the next three to four years.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **11/2021**: The announcement of the hydrogen hub in Huntorf marks a significant milestone in the development of green hydrogen infrastructure in Germany.

- **09/2024**: EWE is in the detailed planning phase for the conversion of the natural gas cavern in Huntorf to hydrogen storage. The project is part of the European IPCEI program, indicating its importance in the broader European hydrogen strategy.

#### 4. Labour

#### 5. Ownership Structure
