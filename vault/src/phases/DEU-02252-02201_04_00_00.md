### Evolutionary Timeline of the EWE Facility in Rüdersdorf, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2021**: EWE began construction of a hydrogen storage cavern in Rüdersdorf with a capacity of **500 cubic meters** at a depth of **1,000 meters**. This cavern is designed to test hydrogen storage and is expected to operate by **spring 2022**. The facility aims to gather data on hydrogen purity during storage and extraction, which is crucial for its use in the mobility sector.
- **May 2024**: EWE completed the first storage cycle of the test cavern, reporting satisfactory results over a three-month operation. The technical systems for injection and withdrawal functioned well, and the quality of the hydrogen changed minimally after storage.
- **December 2024**: EWE announced the completion of the **HyCAVmobil** research project, demonstrating the feasibility of safely storing hydrogen in underground caverns. The project confirmed that the purity of hydrogen remains high after storage, which is essential for fuel cell applications.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2024**: The total investment for the HyCAVmobil project reached **14 million euros**, with EWE contributing nearly **8 million euros** and receiving approximately **6.5 million euros** in grants from the Federal Ministry for Digital and Transport as part of the National Innovation Programme for Hydrogen and Fuel Cell Technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Spring 2021**: Installation and cementing of **160 steel pipes** to a depth of **1,000 meters** began, laying the groundwork for the test cavern.
- **Autumn 2022**: EWE successfully proved the leak-proof integrity of the cavern borehole down to **1,000 meters**, a critical milestone for safe hydrogen storage.
- **Autumn 2022 to Spring 2023**: Brine removal and cavity creation in the salt dome were completed, followed by operational tests.
- **Autumn 2023**: The first hydrogen storage occurred, marking a significant operational milestone.
- **Winter 2023 to Autumn 2024**: Ongoing operational tests of the hydrogen cavern and storage technology were conducted, focusing on various flow velocities and pressures.
