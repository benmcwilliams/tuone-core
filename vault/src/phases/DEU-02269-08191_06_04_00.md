## DEU-02269-08191

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Aleo Solar Facility in Prenzlau, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2010**: Aleo solar announced an increase in the combined annual production capacity of its three factories from 250MW to 390MW by the end of 2011. The main factory in Prenzlau was set to modernize its oldest production line, “aleo I,” to enhance production efficiency and utilize solar cells with a higher output rating. The future capacity was projected to be around two million solar modules per year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2010**: An investment of around €20 million was allocated for the expansion of production capacity at the Prenzlau facility and two other sites.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2010**: Announcement of the capacity increase and modernization plans for the Prenzlau facility, with a target completion date by the end of 2011.

- **February 2014**: Announcement of the acquisition of aleo solar by a consortium led by Taiwanese and Japanese firms, which included the sale of significant parts of the company, primarily its module manufacturing and sales operations, to SCP Solar GmbH.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2010**: The expansion was expected to create a total of 100 new jobs at the Prenzlau facility.

- **February 2014**: The acquisition deal was said to save around 200 jobs, although it was noted that more jobs could not be saved during the acquisition process.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2014**: Aleo solar was acquired by a consortium consisting of Sunrise Global Solar Energy and CHOSHU Industry, with the operations being transferred to SCP Solar GmbH. The acquisition involved a token purchase price of €1.00, with additional financial arrangements in place to support aleo solar during the transition. The facility in Prenzlau remained a significant part of the company under the new ownership structure.
