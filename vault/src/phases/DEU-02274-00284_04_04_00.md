## DEU-02274-00284

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fraunhofer Facility in Offshore, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: The Fraunhofer Institute for Solar Energy Systems (ISE) developed a technical concept and design for an offshore hydrogen generation plant as part of the “OffsH2ore” project. The facility utilizes proton exchange membrane (PEM) electrolysis and is designed to produce up to **50,000 tons of green hydrogen per year**. The electrolysis platform is connected to an offshore wind farm with a capacity of **500 MW**. Fresh water for the electrolysis process is sourced by desalinating seawater using residual heat from the electrolysis.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: The project received funding from the **German Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs and Climate Protection**. This funding supports the development of the integrated offshore hydrogen production plant and its associated technologies.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: The project was officially announced, detailing the technical and economic feasibility of producing hydrogen directly at sea using PEM electrolysis. The consortium involved in the project includes Fraunhofer ISE, PNE AG (project coordinator), SILICA Verfahrenstechnik GmbH, KONGSTEIN GmbH, and Wystrach GmbH.
