### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fraunhofer Institute for Solar Energy Systems (ISE) Facility in Freiburg, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **03/2009**: Fraunhofer ISE collaborates with Entech Solar to test high-efficiency back-contact silicon solar cells for concentrating solar systems.
- **06/2010**: Research on using purified steam in solar cell fabrication is presented, enhancing the quality of thermal oxides for silicon solar cells.
- **08/2010**: Confirmation of a new world record for CIGS solar cell efficiency at 20.3% by researchers at ZSW, with validation from Fraunhofer ISE.
- **05/2011**: Launch of a new Photovoltaic Module Technology Center (MTC) aimed at reducing efficiency losses from cell to module to only 2.5%.
- **09/2014**: Research indicates that German PV systems are generating 5% more power than expected due to increased solar radiation levels.
- **02/2017**: Announcement of a record conversion efficiency of 21.9% for n-type multicrystalline cells using TOPCon technology.
- **04/2018**: Collaboration with SCHMID Group to develop lower-cost BIPV modules, achieving a 35% reduction in production costs.
- **01/2019**: Achievement of a new efficiency record of 22.3% for a multi-junction solar cell made of silicon and III-V semiconductor materials.
- **07/2019**: Research on bifacial solar panel performance shows potential energy gains of up to 20% over traditional designs.
- **04/2020**: Launch of the “Lade PV” project to develop vehicle-integrated solar modules for electric vehicles and trucks.
- **01/2021**: Development of a technique to produce colored solar modules with minimal efficiency loss, based on the structure of morpho butterfly wings.
- **11/2021**: Introduction of a solder-free aluminum interconnection technology for shingled PV modules, improving efficiency by 0.7%.
- **10/2023**: First successful application of shingling technology to perovskite-silicon tandem solar cells, achieving a module efficiency of 22.8%.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **06/2018**: The BIPV-Fab project, partially funded by the German Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs and Energy, aims to reduce production costs for BIPV modules.
- **02/2020**: The Lade PV project receives funding for the development of vehicle-integrated solar modules.
- **04/2020**: The establishment of a new battery research center in Freiburg, funded by the Federal Ministry of Education and Research and the Baden-Württemberg Ministry of Economic Affairs.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **03/2009**: Collaboration with Entech Solar begins.
- **05/2011**: Opening of the Photovoltaic Module Technology Center (MTC).
- **04/2018**: Completion of the BIPV-Fab project.
- **01/2021**: Introduction of the MorphoColor technology for colored solar modules.
- **11/2021**: Launch of the FoilMet interconnection technology for shingled modules.
- **10/2023**: Successful demonstration of shingled perovskite-silicon tandem solar cells.

#### 4. Labour
- **05/2011**: The establishment of the MTC is expected to create jobs related to the development and testing of new photovoltaic technologies.
- **04/2020**: The Lade PV project aims to create jobs in the development of vehicle-integrated solar technologies.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Fraunhofer Institute for Solar Energy Systems (ISE) operates as a research institute under the Fraunhofer Society, indicating that it is not privately owned but part of a larger network of research facilities in Germany. The facility in Freiburg is fully owned by the Fraunhofer Society.
