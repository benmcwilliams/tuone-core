### Evolutionary Timeline of the NexWafe Facility in Freiburg, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **11/2017**: NexWafe GmbH announces plans for kerfless wafer manufacturing, targeting a production capacity of 5 MW with a future goal of scaling up to 250 MW. The technology involves a unique EpiWafer process that eliminates kerf loss, enhancing resource efficiency.
- **06/2018**: NexWafe announces plans for a 250 MW kerfless wafer factory in Bitterfeld, Germany, while continuing operations at the pilot line in Freiburg.
- **02/2019**: NexWafe secures funding for the commissioning of its pilot line in Freiburg to produce EpiWafer substrates for solar manufacturing customers.
- **10/2020**: NexWafe plans to build a 400 MW wafer production facility in Germany, with future expansion plans to reach 3 GW. The pilot line in Freiburg is operational, producing 1 million wafers annually for qualification purposes.
- **12/2024**: NexWafe claims a power conversion efficiency of 24.4% for heterojunction solar cells built with its ultrathin wafers, demonstrating the effectiveness of its direct gas-to-wafer process.

- **06/2018**: An additional €1.5 million is secured from Chemiepark Bitterfeld-Wolfen GmbH as part of a broader funding round totaling €9.5 million for commercialization efforts.
- **02/2019**: New funding is secured from Wermuth Asset Management for the pilot line, although specific financial details are not disclosed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones

- **02/2019**: Funding secured for the pilot line in Freiburg, indicating progress towards operational status.
- **10/2020**: Production at the pilot line begins, with plans for a new factory to start construction in the following year.
