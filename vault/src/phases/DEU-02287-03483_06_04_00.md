### Evolutionary Timeline of the SolarWorld Facility in Freiburg, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2008**: SolarWorld announces a €350 million investment to double the capacity of its main solar manufacturing factory in Freiburg, Germany, aiming to increase production capacity to 1 GW.
- **May 2011**: SolarWorld opens the SolarFactory III in Freiburg, expanding its annual production capacity from 170 MW to 600 MW. The facility is expected to produce 6,000 solar modules daily, bringing total nominal capacities to 1,400 MW.
- **May 2015**: Upgrades to PERC (Passivated Emitter Rear Cell) technology begin at the Freiburg plant, aiming to harmonize cell production across all plants and increase overall production capacity to above 1 GW.
- **November 2015**: Delays in implementing planned production capacity expansions and new technologies at the Freiburg plant are reported, impacting earnings and pushing back timelines by three to six months.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2008**: SolarWorld invests $500 million in advanced photovoltaics technology in the U.S., with a significant portion allocated to the Freiburg facility for capacity expansion.
- **May 2011**: The opening of the SolarFactory III represents a significant investment in expanding production capabilities in Freiburg.
- **May 2015**: Capital expenditures related to production expansion and upgrades at the Freiburg plant total €1.3 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2011**: The SolarFactory III is officially opened, marking a significant milestone in the facility's expansion.
- **May 2015**: Upgrades to PERC technology begin at the Freiburg plant.
- **November 2015**: The company acknowledges delays in production capacity expansions and technology upgrades.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2011**: The expansion of the SolarFactory III is expected to create over 250 additional jobs in Freiburg.
- **September 2016**: SolarWorld announces the layoff of around 300 temporary workers at the Freiburg facility due to significant declines in PV module prices, although permanent staff remain unaffected.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify any joint ventures or shared ownership for the Freiburg facility, indicating that SolarWorld operates the facility with full ownership.
