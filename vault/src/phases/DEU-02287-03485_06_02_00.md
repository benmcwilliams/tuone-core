### Evolutionary Timeline of SolarWorld Industries GmbH Facility in Freiburg, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations**:
   - **Date**: August 2017
   - **Details**: SolarWorld Industries GmbH officially started production, taking over the production plants of the former SolarWorld AG. The Freiburg facility focuses on wafer and module assembly. The company has approximately 600MW of mono ingot/wafer capacity and 1,000MW of nameplate cell and module capacity in Germany. The facility is transitioning to higher efficiency P-type monocrystalline PERC (Passivated Emitter Rear Cell) technology, including bifacial cells and modules.

2. **Investment and Development**:
   - **Date**: August 2017
   - **Details**: The formation of SolarWorld Industries GmbH involved investments from key stakeholders, including Dr. Ing. Eh Frank Asbeck and Qatar Solar Technologies (QST), which is rumored to have taken a major shareholding in the new company. Specific financial details regarding investments tied to expansions or capacity were not disclosed in the article.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**:
   - **Date**: August 2017
   - **Details**: The facility officially commenced production under the new company structure. The transition from SolarWorld AG to SolarWorld Industries GmbH marked a significant milestone in the continuation of operations at the Freiburg site.

4. **Labour**:
   - **Date**: August 2017
   - **Details**: A total of 227 employees from the Freiburg facility were transferred to SolarWorld Industries GmbH as part of the transition from SolarWorld AG. Additionally, 67 employees from the R&D facilities in Freiberg, Saxony, were also transferred. The overall workforce of SolarWorld AG was approximately 3,034 at the end of 2016, with 3,378 employees allocated to production operations in Germany.

5. **Ownership Structure**:
   - **Date**: August 2017
   - **Details**: SolarWorld Industries GmbH is majority owned by Dr. Ing. Eh Frank Asbeck and Qatar Solar Technologies (QST). This indicates that the facility is not under exclusive ownership, as it involves a significant shareholding from QST.
