## DEU-02287-08548

### Evolutionary Timeline of Concentrix Solar Facility in Freiburg, Germany

1. **Technology and Operations**
   - **12/2008**: Concentrix Solar inaugurated its production line for concentrator photovoltaic (CPV) modules with a capacity of 25 MW. The facility is designed for quick expansion to 100 MW. The production line utilizes advanced technology developed over a decade at the Fraunhofer Institute for Solar Energy Systems ISE, featuring Fresnel lenses to concentrate solar energy on III-V triple-junction solar cells.

2. **Investment and Development**
   - **11/2009**: Soitec, a semiconductor wafer producer, announced its acquisition of a major holding in Concentrix Solar, valuing the company at €55 million. This acquisition is part of Soitec's strategic expansion into the solar market, leveraging its technology for the development of high-performance photovoltaic solutions.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**
   - **10/2008**: Commercial production of Flatcon CPV modules began after two years of pilot-line work. The facility was officially inaugurated in December 2008, marking a significant milestone in the industrial production of CPV technology.

4. **Labour**
   - **12/2008**: The facility initially employed 60 people, with plans to increase the workforce to 100 by the following year, including recruitment for qualified personnel in electronics, electrical engineering, and power plant engineering.

5. **Ownership Structure**
   - **11/2009**: Following the acquisition by Soitec, the ownership structure changed, with Soitec holding a major stake in Concentrix Solar while Fraunhofer ISE retained a 20% stake. This indicates a shift from full ownership by Concentrix to a joint venture model involving Soitec and Fraunhofer ISE. 

This timeline captures the key developments and changes at the Concentrix Solar facility in Freiburg, Germany, highlighting its technological advancements, investment activities, project milestones, labor dynamics, and ownership structure.
