## DEU-02296-03483

### Evolutionary Timeline of the SolarWorld Facility in Bonn, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10/2017**: SolarWorld AG, an integrated PV module manufacturer, announced it was entering insolvency proceedings due to chronic overcapacity and declining module prices. The company had been transitioning from multicrystalline to mono-PERC production to focus on high-efficiency modules.
- **05/2017**: The facility was involved in the production of solar modules but faced significant operational challenges leading to job cuts and a shift in production strategy.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07/2018**: The creditors of SolarWorld decided to discontinue the loss-making business by the end of September 2018 if no investor was found. This decision was made in light of the company's ongoing financial struggles and lack of prospects for recovery.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05/2017**: SolarWorld AG filed for insolvency, and its key German subsidiaries, including SolarWorld Industries GmbH, also entered insolvency proceedings. The insolvency court appointed a law firm as the preliminary insolvency administrator.
- **07/2018**: A creditors' meeting concluded that the business would be stopped by the end of September 2018 unless an investor was found.

#### 4. Labour
- **05/2017**: The company shed around 400 jobs as part of its transition to a more focused production strategy.
- **07/2018**: More than 500 employees were reported to be working across three German locations (Bonn, Arnstadt, and Freiberg). Plans were initiated for a social plan and transfer companies to support affected employees in case of closure.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **05/2017**: SolarWorld AG was the parent company, and its subsidiaries were also under its ownership. However, the insolvency proceedings indicated a potential shift in ownership structure if an investor was not found.

This timeline captures the critical developments and challenges faced by the SolarWorld facility in Bonn, Germany, highlighting its operational struggles, financial difficulties, and the impact on its workforce.
