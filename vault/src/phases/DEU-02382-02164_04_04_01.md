## DEU-02382-02164

### Evolutionary Timeline of the BP Facility in North Sea, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2023**: BP, along with other industry players, called for the establishment of 10 GW of offshore electrolysis capacity as part of Germany's National Hydrogen Strategy. This initiative highlights the potential for offshore hydrogen production in the North Sea, specifically referencing the SEN-1 area, which is designated for testing electrolysis capacity of up to 1 GW.

- **July 2023**: BP secured development rights for two North Sea zones (N-12.2 and N-11.1), each with a capacity of 2 GW, totaling 4 GW. This acquisition is part of BP's strategy to produce renewable power, which will support the demand for green hydrogen and other low-carbon energy solutions.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2023**: BP's total investment for the two North Sea zones amounted to €6.8 billion, with an initial payment of €678 million due by July 2024. The remaining 90% of the investment will be paid over a 20-year period as the projects become operational.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2023**: The German offshore wind and hydrogen industry associations called for strategic planning for the first offshore green hydrogen tender, following the designation of the SEN-1 area as a significant milestone for offshore hydrogen development.

- **July 2023**: BP won development rights in a competitive auction for four sites in the German dynamic tender round, marking a significant step in BP's decarbonization plans in Germany.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2023**: While specific job creation figures were not mentioned, BP's executive vice president indicated that the renewable power projects would anchor significant demand for green electrons, which could imply job creation in the renewable energy sector as these projects develop.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2023**: BP exclusively owns the two North Sea zones (N-12.2 and N-11.1) it secured in the auction, indicating full ownership of these facilities as part of its integrated energy strategy in Germany. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic moves of BP in the North Sea, DEU, focusing on their commitment to renewable energy and hydrogen production.
