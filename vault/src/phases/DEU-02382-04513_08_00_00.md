### Evolutionary Timeline of the AquaVentus Facility in North Sea, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2023**: The AquaVentus initiative is part of a broader appeal for the establishment of 10 GW of offshore electrolysis capacity in Germany's National Hydrogen Strategy. The facility is associated with the SEN-1 area in the North Sea, which spans over 100 square kilometers and is designated to test and connect an electrolysis capacity of up to 1 GW with a hydrogen pipeline.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2023**: The appeal emphasizes the need for a stable legal framework and incentives to support the development of the offshore hydrogen economy, which is crucial for attracting investments. The industry calls for the German government to strategically plan the first offshore green hydrogen tender to facilitate further projects.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2023**: The Federal Maritime and Hydrographic Agency (BSH) outlines the first offshore hydrogen area in the North Sea, designated as SEN-1, allowing for the testing of up to 1 GW of electrolysis capacity.
- **May 2023**: The designation of the SEN-1 area is recognized as an important milestone for the development of an offshore hydrogen economy in Germany and Europe.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2023**: The appeal indicates that the development of the offshore hydrogen economy is expected to create jobs along the entire value chain, although specific job creation numbers are not provided.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2023**: The AquaVentus initiative involves multiple stakeholders, including BP, Siemens Gamesa, RWE, Gasunie, Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (CIP), EnBW, Equinor, and Lhyfe, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership by a single company.
