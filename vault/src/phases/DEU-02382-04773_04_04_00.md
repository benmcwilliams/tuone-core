### Evolutionary Timeline of the Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (CIP) Facility in North Sea, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2023**: The facility is part of the German offshore wind-to-hydrogen initiative, with a focus on developing offshore electrolysis capacity. The designated area, SEN-1, spans over 100 square kilometers in the North Sea and is set to allow for an electrolysis capacity of up to **1 GW** to be tested and connected with a hydrogen pipeline.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2023**: The appeal signed by CIP and other industry players calls for the German government to strategically plan the first offshore green hydrogen tender, which is seen as a stepping stone for further projects. The industry aims for a total of **10 GW** of offshore green hydrogen generation capacity by **2035** as part of the National Hydrogen Strategy (NWS).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2023**: The designation of the SEN-1 area is highlighted as an important milestone for the development of an offshore hydrogen economy in Germany and Europe. The appeal emphasizes the need for a binding and stable legal framework for the production and transport of offshore green hydrogen.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2023**: The appeal indicates that companies along the entire value chain are ready to promote the development of the offshore hydrogen economy, suggesting potential job creation, although specific job numbers are not mentioned.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2023**: The articles do not specify the ownership structure of the facility, but CIP is mentioned as a key player in the initiative alongside other companies, indicating potential collaboration rather than exclusive ownership.
