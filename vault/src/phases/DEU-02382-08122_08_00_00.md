### Evolutionary Timeline of the TenneT Facility in North Sea, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2022**: TenneT launched a 2GW cable tender for high voltage direct current (HVDC) circuits aimed at at least 10 offshore grid connections in the North Sea. This initiative is part of TenneT's strategy to bundle electricity transmission capacities and reduce environmental impact. The facility's current connection capacity is approximately 7.2GW in the German North Sea and 2.8GW in the Dutch North Sea. The 2GW initiative is critical for achieving the offshore expansion targets set for 2030.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2022**: The 2GW cable tender is part of TenneT's broader EUR 30 billion investment plan to connect 40GW of new offshore wind capacity in Germany and the Netherlands by 2030. This investment is aligned with the collective goal of installing at least 65GW of offshore wind energy by 2030, as agreed by Germany, the Netherlands, Denmark, and Belgium.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2022**: TenneT announced the launch of a large-scale HVDC cable tender, marking a significant step in the offshore grid expansion in the North Sea. This tender follows an earlier tender focused on offshore converter platforms and onshore converter stations, indicating a structured approach to the development of offshore grid connections.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2022**: TenneT's initiatives, including the 2GW cable tender, are expected to boost employment in the offshore market by providing investment security and facilitating the development of resources and supply chains.
