### Evolutionary Timeline of the RWE Facility in Brunsbüttel, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2021**: RWE announced plans to utilize the LNG terminal in Brunsbüttel for future hydrogen imports, indicating its potential as a key location for green hydrogen integration into Germany's energy system.
- **March 2022**: RWE confirmed its involvement in a regasification plan at Brunsbüttel, initially for LNG imports, with future plans to convert the terminal for "green molecules" (hydrogen).
- **December 2022**: RWE signed a memorandum of understanding with Hyphen Hydrogen Energy for the supply of green ammonia from Namibia, with plans to build an import terminal at Brunsbüttel, aiming for commissioning in 2026.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2021**: The German government allocated €52 million for hydrogen research, which supports RWE's initiatives in the hydrogen sector, including projects at Brunsbüttel.
- **December 2022**: The Hyphen project, which RWE is involved with, is estimated to require around $10 billion for its two phases, indicating significant investment in green hydrogen infrastructure.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: RWE expressed intentions to book capacity at the LNG terminal in Brunsbüttel for future hydrogen imports.
- **March 2022**: RWE's CEO announced plans to restart work on LNG terminals, including Brunsbüttel, to enhance energy security amid geopolitical tensions.
- **December 2022**: RWE's import terminal at Brunsbüttel is set to be commissioned in 2026, marking a significant milestone in the facility's development.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2021**: While specific job creation figures were not mentioned, the expansion into hydrogen and the development of the terminal are expected to create jobs in the region as part of the broader green energy transition.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2021 - December 2022**: RWE is the primary operator of the facility, with plans to collaborate with Hyphen Hydrogen Energy for ammonia supply, indicating a partnership but maintaining RWE's ownership of the terminal.
