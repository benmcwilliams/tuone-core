## DEU-02426-00800

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Uniper Facility in Wilhelmshaven, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **03/2021**: Uniper announced the conversion of its coal-fired power plant in Wilhelmshaven to a hydrogen-run facility, ceasing coal operations by 12/2021. The site will develop a comprehensive hydrogen infrastructure.
- **04/2021**: Plans for the Green Wilhelmshaven project were unveiled, aiming to establish a national hydrogen hub capable of meeting 10% of Germany's hydrogen demand by 2030. This includes a 410 MW electrolysis plant and an ammonia cracker for green hydrogen production.
- **09/2021**: A strategic partnership with Ørsted was formed to integrate offshore wind power with hydrogen production at Wilhelmshaven, with plans for a 70 MW electrolysis plant by 2025, expanding to 410 MW by 2030.
- **02/2022**: Uniper announced plans for a large-scale electrolysis plant with a capacity of up to 1 GW, alongside an import terminal for converting green ammonia back into hydrogen.
- **04/2023**: Uniper plans to build two solar power plants with a combined capacity of 317 MW to power the hydrogen production facility.
- **11/2024**: EH2 was selected as the exclusive electrolysis partner for a 200 MW electrolyser at the site, part of a planned 1 GW hydrogen production facility.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04/2021**: The Green Wilhelmshaven project was submitted as an 'Important Project of Common European Interest' (IPCEI) to the German Federal Ministry of Economics.
- **09/2021**: The German government allocated €52 million for hydrogen research, supporting projects like Wilhelmshaven.
- **02/2022**: The project was recognized as a significant step towards decarbonizing steel production in Lower Saxony, indicating substantial investment in infrastructure.
- **11/2024**: The electrolyser and ammonia terminal were designated as Projects of Common Interest (PCI) by the EU, highlighting their importance in the hydrogen economy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2021**: The coal-fired power plant ceased operations.
- **04/2021**: Announcement of the Green Wilhelmshaven project.
- **09/2021**: Strategic partnership with Ørsted announced.
- **02/2022**: Plans for a 1 GW electrolysis plant and ammonia import terminal were confirmed.
- **08/2023**: Development decision received for two solar plants to support hydrogen production.
- **11/2024**: Pre-FEED work commenced for the 200 MW electrolyser.

#### 4. Labour
- **03/2021**: COO David Bryson mentioned discussions with employees regarding alternative job placements as the coal plant closed, indicating potential job losses but also opportunities in the new hydrogen sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Uniper is the sole owner of the Wilhelmshaven facility, with partnerships formed for specific projects (e.g., Ørsted for offshore wind integration and EH2 for electrolysis technology).

This timeline encapsulates the significant developments and strategic shifts at the Uniper facility in Wilhelmshaven, reflecting its transition from coal to a hydrogen production hub, supported by various partnerships and investments aimed at meeting Germany's future energy needs.
