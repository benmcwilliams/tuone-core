### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ørsted Facility in Wilhelmshaven, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2021**: Ørsted and Uniper announced a strategic partnership to develop offshore wind-powered green hydrogen production at the Wilhelmshaven site. The initial electrolysis plant is planned to have a capacity of **70 MW**, with an expansion to **410 MW** targeted by **2030**. The renewable electricity for this electrolysis will come from Ørsted's offshore wind turbines.
- **February 2022**: Uniper revealed plans for a larger electrolysis plant with a potential capacity of up to **1,000 MW** to produce green hydrogen, alongside an import terminal for converting green ammonia back into hydrogen. This facility aims to support the SALCOS project for climate-compatible steel production.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2021**: The partnership between Ørsted and Uniper is part of a broader initiative to integrate hydrogen production with offshore wind energy, although specific investment amounts were not disclosed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2021**: Announcement of the strategic partnership and plans for the electrolysis plant to be operational by **2025**.
- **February 2022**: Confirmation of plans for a large electrolysis plant and an import terminal, indicating ongoing development and planning stages for the hydrogen production facility.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify exact job creation or job loss figures related to the investments or expansions at the Wilhelmshaven facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Ørsted does not own the facility exclusively; it is a **joint venture** with Uniper, indicating a collaborative approach to developing the hydrogen production capabilities at the site.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic plans for the Ørsted facility in Wilhelmshaven, highlighting its role in advancing green hydrogen production in Germany.
