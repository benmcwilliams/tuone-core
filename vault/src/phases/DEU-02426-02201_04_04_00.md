### Evolutionary Timeline of the EWE Facility in Wilhelmshaven, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2022**: EWE, in collaboration with Tree Energy Solutions (TES), announced plans to build a **500 MW electrolyzer** at the TES Green Energy Hub in Wilhelmshaven. This facility is part of a larger initiative to achieve **1 GW of green hydrogen capacity** at the site, with potential for up to **2 GW of electrolyzers** in total. The electrolyzer is expected to be operational by **2028**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2022**: The joint project between EWE and TES is part of the German energy strategy aimed at harnessing clean energy from the North Sea to enhance hydrogen production capabilities. Specific investment amounts have not been disclosed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2022**: Announcement of the **declaration of intent** to build the electrolyzer, marking a significant milestone in the development of green hydrogen infrastructure in Germany.
- **March 2024**: EWE is actively involved in multiple hydrogen projects, including the **500 MW electrolyzer** in Wilhelmshaven, indicating ongoing progress in the project.
