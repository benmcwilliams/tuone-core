### Evolutionary Timeline of the RWE Facility in Düsseldorf, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Focus**: The RWE facility is involved in the production of green hydrogen. 
- **Production Capacity**: Plans include a 410-megawatt electrolysis plant capable of supplying around 295,000 metric tons of hydrogen annually, which is projected to meet approximately 10% of Germany's hydrogen demand by 2030.
- **Future Plans**: RWE is part of a broader initiative involving around 30 hydrogen projects, primarily located in Germany, the Netherlands, and the UK.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Funding**: The German government allocated €52 million for hydrogen research as part of a larger €300 million project called Norddeutsches Reallabor (North German Physical Laboratory), which aims to explore the transformation of the energy system with a focus on green hydrogen.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Announcement Date**: On April 16, 2021, RWE and other companies announced their commitment to advancing green hydrogen projects in Germany, including the establishment of a national hydrogen hub.
- **Future Developments**: The facility is part of the European Hydrogen Backbone initiative, which aims to create a 39,700 km hydrogen network by 2040.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: While specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles, the expansion into hydrogen production and the establishment of a national hub are expected to create jobs in the green energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: RWE operates the facility as a subsidiary of the larger RWE Group, indicating full ownership of the facility in Düsseldorf.
