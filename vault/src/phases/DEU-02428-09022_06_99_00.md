### Evolutionary Timeline of the Glaston Facility in Düsseldorf, Germany

1. **Technology and Operations**
   - **Date**: September 2010
   - **Details**: Glaston, in partnership with Beneq Oy, launched the ‘TFC2000’ line for TCO (transparent conductive oxide) coated glass, specifically designed for thin-film photovoltaic (PV) modules. The TFC2000 line boasts a production capacity of 250 MW per annum and features a compact design with a footprint of only 50 meters. This technology utilizes atmospheric sub-micron-sized precursor deposition, which allows for lower material usage and improved coating uniformity. The system is capable of producing TCO glass with variable thicknesses and haze levels, and it is fully temperable, with options for laser-scribing or wet-etching.

2. **Investment and Development**
   - **Date**: September 2010
   - **Details**: The launch of the TFC2000 line represents a significant investment in technology aimed at expanding the market for independent glass producers. The partnership with Beneq is expected to facilitate the scaling of thin-film production, thereby increasing the demand for TCO coated glass in key markets, including the U.S., Germany, and Asia.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**
   - **Date**: September 2010
   - **Details**: The TFC2000 line was announced at the glasstec/solarpeq trade show in Düsseldorf, marking a significant milestone in the facility's operations. The system is positioned to meet the anticipated increase in demand for TCO coated glass due to the growth of thin-film PV module production.
