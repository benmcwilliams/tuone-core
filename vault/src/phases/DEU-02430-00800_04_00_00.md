### Evolutionary Timeline of the Uniper Facility in Krummhörn, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2022**: Uniper announced plans to convert the former salt cavern storage facility in Krummhörn for large-scale hydrogen storage. The facility is expected to have a storage volume of up to 250,000 m³ of hydrogen and aims to be operational by 2024.
- **August 2024**: Uniper officially launched its Hydrogen Pilot Cavern (HPC) Krummhörn plant, featuring nearly 500,000 norm cubic meters of green hydrogen storage. The facility is designed to gather essential experience for large-scale and commercial hydrogen storage.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2022**: Uniper committed to investing €10 million in the conversion of the Krummhörn facility for hydrogen storage.
- **September 2024**: The Lower Saxony Ministry for the Environment, Energy, and Climate Protection provided close to €2.4 million ($2.6 million) in funding for the project as part of the hydrogen economy pilot and demonstration initiative.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2022**: Announcement of the conversion project for the Krummhörn facility.
- **August 2024**: Official opening of the Hydrogen Pilot Cavern (HPC) Krummhörn demonstration plant.
- **September 24, 2024**: Scheduled gas tightness test to commence trial operations at the facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2024**: While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned, the project is expected to contribute to local employment through the development and operation of the hydrogen storage facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Uniper owns the Krummhörn facility exclusively, as there is no mention of joint ventures or partnerships in the provided articles.
