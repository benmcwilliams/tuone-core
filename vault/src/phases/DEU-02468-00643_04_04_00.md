### Evolutionary Timeline of the RWE Facility in Eisenhüttenstadt, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2022**: RWE and ArcelorMittal announced a partnership to develop offshore wind farms and hydrogen facilities to supply renewable energy and green hydrogen for low-emissions steel production in Germany. The facility in Eisenhüttenstadt is part of this initiative, which aims to replace coal with wind power and green hydrogen in steel production. A pilot electrolysis plant with a capacity of 70 MW is planned to be operational by 2026, with intentions to scale up to gigawatt-scale projects in the long term.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2022**: The partnership between RWE and ArcelorMittal is contingent on the approval of public funding for the development of the electrolysis plants and offshore wind farms. The companies are assessing options for joint participation in tenders for offshore wind farm sites, which will require significant investment.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2022**: The memorandum of understanding was signed, marking the official start of the collaboration between RWE and ArcelorMittal to decarbonize steel production in Eisenhüttenstadt and other locations in Germany. The pilot plant is targeted for completion by 2026.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2022**: While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned, the transition to green hydrogen and renewable energy sources is expected to create jobs in the development and operation of the new technologies and facilities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2022**: The facility in Eisenhüttenstadt is part of a partnership between RWE and ArcelorMittal, indicating a joint venture structure for the development of the offshore wind and hydrogen facilities. RWE does not own the facility exclusively; it is a collaborative effort with ArcelorMittal. 

This timeline captures the key developments related to the RWE facility in Eisenhüttenstadt, focusing on the partnership with ArcelorMittal and the planned advancements in clean technology for steel production.
