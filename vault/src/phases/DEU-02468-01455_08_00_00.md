### Evolutionary Timeline of the ArcelorMittal Facility in Eisenhüttenstadt, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2022**: ArcelorMittal, in partnership with RWE, announced plans to develop offshore wind farms and hydrogen facilities to supply renewable energy and green hydrogen for low-emissions steel production. The facility in Eisenhüttenstadt is set to transition from coal-intensive blast furnace technology to electric arc furnaces (EAFs) and direct reduction (DRI) plants, with a pilot green hydrogen plant of 70 MW planned by 2026, aiming for gigawatt-scale projects in the long term.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2022**: The partnership with RWE is contingent on public funding for the development of the hydrogen facilities and offshore wind farms. The companies are assessing options for joint participation in tenders for offshore wind farm sites, which will be crucial for securing the necessary renewable energy for the facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2022**: A memorandum of understanding was signed between RWE and ArcelorMittal to collaborate on the development of offshore wind and hydrogen facilities. The initial step includes the construction of a 70 MW pilot plant in Eisenhüttenstadt by 2026, with plans for future expansions to gigawatt-scale projects.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2022**: The transition to renewable energy sources and the establishment of hydrogen production facilities are expected to create jobs in the region, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2022**: The facility in Eisenhüttenstadt is part of a partnership between ArcelorMittal and RWE, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership by ArcelorMittal. The companies intend to establish long-term purchase agreements for both wind power and green hydrogen, integrating energy supply into the steel production process.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic initiatives related to the ArcelorMittal facility in Eisenhüttenstadt, focusing on its transition towards sustainable steel production through renewable energy and hydrogen technology.
