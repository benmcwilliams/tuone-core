### Evolutionary Timeline of the HH2E Facility in Lubmin, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2022**: HH2E, in collaboration with the Swiss MET Group, announced plans to develop a green hydrogen production facility in Lubmin, aiming for an initial production capacity of approximately 6,000 tonnes (over 200,000 MWh) of green hydrogen per year. The project includes a second-stage expansion to increase capacity to over 1 GW.
- **July 2023**: The facility's initial phase is confirmed to be 100 MW, with plans for scalability to more than 1 GW by 2030.
- **January 2024**: The facility is expected to start production by the end of 2025, with a grid-connection agreement signed with Gascade Gastransport.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2023**: JLEN Environmental announced a EUR 9.2 million investment in the green hydrogen project, which will be used for detailed engineering designs and procurement of long lead items.
- **September 2024**: CIBC and BNP Paribas signed on as lead investors for the HH2E project, aiming to secure over €200 million in loans to bring the project closer to its financial goals.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2022**: Announcement of the project and its initial production capacity.
- **July 2023**: Funding completion for the green hydrogen production site, with construction expected to finish by mid-2025.
- **January 2024**: Confirmation of the expected production start by the end of 2025.
- **September 2024**: Investment agreements with CIBC and BNP Paribas to finalize funding for the project.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2023**: The project is expected to create jobs in the construction and operational phases, although specific job creation numbers have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2022**: HH2E is noted as the developer and operator of the facility, indicating that it has a significant ownership stake in the project, although specific ownership percentages or joint venture details with the MET Group are not provided in the articles.
