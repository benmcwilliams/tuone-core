### Evolutionary Timeline of the First Solar Facility in Frankfurt (Oder), Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **08/2010**: First Solar announces plans to double the production capacity at its Frankfurt (Oder) facility from 223 MW to approximately 446 MW by Q4 2011 due to strong demand from European customers.
- **02/2011**: The new manufacturing plant is expected to be operational by June 2011, with a collective annual module capacity of over 238 MW, bringing the total capacity to 477 MW upon completion.
- **04/2012**: First Solar announces the permanent closure of its Frankfurt (Oder) facility, which was part of a broader restructuring plan due to deteriorating market conditions in Europe.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **08/2010**: The proposed expansion to 446 MW is part of First Solar's strategy to increase its overall production capacity to over 2.1 GW across its facilities in Germany, the U.S., and Malaysia.
- **02/2011**: The new plant in Frankfurt (Oder) is part of a $173 million investment aimed at enhancing production capabilities and sustainability through energy generated from a PV installation on the roof.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **08/2010**: Announcement of the expansion plan to double capacity.
- **02/2011**: Completion of the roof of the new manufacturing plant, with expectations to begin operations by June 2011.
- **04/2012**: Announcement of the closure of the Frankfurt (Oder) facility, with operations expected to cease in Q4 2012.

#### 4. Labour
- **02/2011**: The expansion is expected to create up to 500 new jobs at the Frankfurt (Oder) facility.
- **04/2012**: The closure of the facility will result in the loss of approximately 2,000 jobs, as part of a broader restructuring effort that affects 30% of First Solar's workforce.
