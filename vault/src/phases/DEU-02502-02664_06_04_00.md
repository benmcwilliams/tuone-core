## DEU-02502-02664

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Astronergy Facility in Frankfurt (Oder), Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2017**: Astronergy announced a transition from 4-busbar (4BB) to 5-busbar (5BB) technology to enhance conversion efficiencies at its Frankfurt (Oder) facility. Upgrades to five production lines were set to begin in June 2017, with completion expected by the end of July 2017. New modules under the ‘PENTA’ label were to be available from summer 2017.
- **March 2018**: Astronergy reported a series of upgrades that increased production efficiency by 40% at one of its production lines, allowing an increase in output from 700 to 1,000 modules per day. The upgrades were completed in December 2017 and were implemented by the company’s engineers.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2018**: The upgrades to the K-line production line were completed under budget, indicating a strategic investment in improving production capabilities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2019**: Reports indicated that the Frankfurt (Oder) facility was facing challenges, with short-time work halted due to wage issues and insufficient orders for production.
- **February 2019**: Astronergy announced that production at the Frankfurt (Oder) facility would be halted, with over 200 of the 230 jobs at risk.
- **April 2019**: Astronergy officially closed its Frankfurt (Oder) facility, transitioning to a sales and service center. Approximately 180 employees were laid off, while 40 were transferred to a different company.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2019**: The facility had 235 employees, but reports indicated that short-time work was implemented due to insufficient orders.
- **February 2019**: The closure announcement indicated that more than 200 jobs would be lost as production ceased.
- **April 2019**: Following the closure, about 180 employees were laid off, with 40 employees transitioning to a transfer company.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **2014**: The facility was acquired by Chint Group from the insolvent solar manufacturer Conergy. Astronergy operates as a subsidiary of Chint Group, indicating a full ownership structure prior to the closure.

This timeline captures the key developments and changes at the Astronergy facility in Frankfurt (Oder), Germany, highlighting its technological advancements, operational challenges, and eventual closure.
