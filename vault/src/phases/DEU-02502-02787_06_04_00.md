### Evolutionary Timeline of the Conergy Facility in Frankfurt (Oder), DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2009**: Conergy's Frankfurt (Oder) facility is primarily focused on producing solar modules. The company indicates that it has sufficient access to materials for production needs despite legal disputes with MEMC regarding supply contracts.
- **September 2010**: Conergy announces an investment of €5 million to enhance production installations for solar cells at the Frankfurt (Oder) factory. This investment aims to integrate "OneStep Selective Emitter" technology, which is expected to increase solar module efficiency by up to 0.5 percentage points. The facility is described as one of the most modern solar factories globally.
- **September 2011**: Conergy halts wafer and cell manufacturing at the Frankfurt site due to over-capacity and price pressures but continues PV module production. The company plans to source cells from external suppliers.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2010**: Conergy invests €5 million in new production installations to improve efficiency and expand manufacturing capacities at the Frankfurt (Oder) facility.
