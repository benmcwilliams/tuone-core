### Evolutionary Timeline of the Suntech Facility in Bittfeld-Wolfen, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **01-07-2011**: Suntech announced the termination of its solar wafer supply agreement with MEMC and the discontinuation of investment in its thin film subsidiary, CSG Solar AG. This decision was influenced by rapid market changes for silicon wafers. The facility was involved in the production of photovoltaic modules but ceased operations related to thin film technology due to low efficiency and high costs.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **01-07-2011**: Suntech incurred expenses of approximately USD $236 million due to the termination of the wafer agreement and the discontinuation of the thin film venture. The company projected cost savings of over $400 million over the next five years by optimizing its silicon sourcing strategy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **01-07-2011**: Suntech officially announced the end of its thin film operations at the CSG facility in Bittfeld-Wolfen, Germany, which had previously ceased commercial scale production two and a half years prior. The facility's operations were ultimately deemed uncompetitive in the current market.

#### 4. Labour
- **01-07-2011**: The termination of operations at CSG Solar AG resulted in job transfers within Suntech, with some positions moving to other Suntech locations in Germany and Australia. However, the exact number of jobs affected was not specified.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **01-07-2011**: Suntech was the owner of the facility, but the discontinuation of the thin film operations indicated a shift in focus away from joint ventures like CSG Solar AG, which had previously been a collaborative effort. The facility was fully owned by Suntech, but the abandonment of the thin film project marked a significant change in its operational strategy.
