### Evolutionary Timeline of the SolarWorld Facility in Arnhem, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2017**: SolarWorld announced a strategic shift in production focus to monocrystalline PERC (Passivated Emitter Rear Contact) cells and modules, moving away from multicrystalline products. This transition aims to build economies of scale with high-efficiency products. The facility in Arnhem is emphasized as a key site for this production realignment.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2017**: SolarWorld indicated plans to invest a mid-double-digit million amount in the expansion and improvement of its high-efficiency technologies, specifically targeting the enhancement of PERC processes through its R&D subsidiary, SolarWorld Innovations.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2017**: The company announced that overall module production capacity would be raised to approximately 2GW over the next two years, increasing from around 1.3GW in 2016. This expansion is part of the broader restructuring and focus on high-efficiency products.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2017**: The restructuring plan included the loss of around 400 jobs by the end of 2019, as production was realigned to focus on monocrystalline PERC products. This job reduction was part of a larger strategy that had previously announced around 500 job cuts in September 2016.

- The articles do not provide specific details regarding the ownership structure of the Arnhem facility, but it is implied that SolarWorld operates the facility as part of its integrated production strategy.
