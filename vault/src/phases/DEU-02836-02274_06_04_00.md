### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hanergy Group Facility in Thalheim, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05-06-2012**: Hanergy Group officially acquires Solibro, a CIGS thin-film technology manufacturer, from Q-Cells. Following the acquisition, the facility in Thalheim is set to ramp up production to 100MW (135MW nameplate capacity) to support Hanergy’s European customer base. The existing production capacity and technology of Solibro are expected to be fully realized under Hanergy's management.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05-06-2012**: The acquisition of Solibro by Hanergy is a strategic investment aimed at enhancing the production capacity and performance of the Thalheim facility. Specific financial terms of the acquisition were not disclosed, but it is indicated that Hanergy will provide extensive network support and long-term R&D investment.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05-06-2012**: Announcement of the acquisition of Solibro by Hanergy, with plans to ramp up production capacity to 100MW at the Thalheim facility. The acquisition is confirmed to retain the current workforce and management.

#### 4. Labour
- **05-06-2012**: Hanergy confirms that the existing workforce at the Thalheim facility will be retained post-acquisition, ensuring job security for current employees.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **05-06-2012**: Following the acquisition, Hanergy Group becomes the exclusive owner of the Solibro facility in Thalheim, Germany, as it transitions from being a subsidiary of Q-Cells to a wholly-owned subsidiary of Hanergy.
