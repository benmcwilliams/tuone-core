## DEU-02836-02275

### Evolutionary Timeline of Hanwha Group Facility in Thalheim, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2008**: Q-Cells' facility in Thalheim had an initial production capacity of 20MW, which was set to expand to 80MW later in 2008, focusing on using metallurgical silicon for solar cell production.
- **August 2008**: The production capacity at the Thalheim facility was further expanded from 603MWp to 800MWp, with expectations to reach this target by mid-2009.
- **July 2011**: A new production line for the Q.Peak high-performance module was launched, with a capacity of 130MWp, producing 1,400 solar modules per day.
- **January 2012**: Q-Cells began pilot production of its Q.ANTUM solar cells and modules at the Thalheim facility, achieving new efficiency records for multicrystalline and quasi-mono solar modules.
- **May 2021**: Hanwha Q Cells announced plans to produce n-type solar panels with passivating contacts at the Thalheim facility, investing €15.5 million in R&D activities related to this technology.
- **November 2022**: A pilot manufacturing line for silicon-perovskite tandem cells was established at Thalheim as part of the PEPPERONI project, aiming for industrial production of tandem cells.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2011**: An investment of €17 million was made to expand production expertise at the Thalheim facility.
- **January 2012**: Q-Cells committed to investing €125 million in R&D in Germany over three years, with at least €45 million allocated to the Thalheim site.
- **May 2021**: An additional investment of €15.5 million was announced for R&D activities related to n-type solar module technology at Thalheim.
- **November 2022**: The PEPPERONI project, co-funded by the EU, allocated €14.5 million for the establishment of a pilot line for tandem cell production at Thalheim.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2008**: Announcement of the expansion of the Thalheim facility's capacity from 20MW to 80MW.
- **August 2008**: Expansion of production capacity from 603MWp to 800MWp was confirmed.
- **July 2011**: Launch of the new 130MW production line for Q.Peak modules.
- **January 2012**: Pilot production of Q.ANTUM solar cells began at Thalheim.
- **November 2022**: Establishment of a pilot manufacturing line for silicon-perovskite tandem cells as part of the PEPPERONI project.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2015**: Hanwha Q CELLS announced the closure of all solar cell and module manufacturing in Germany, resulting in the loss of 550 jobs, although 350 jobs were retained for R&D activities at the Thalheim site.
- **April 2018**: Qcells indicated that its R&D facility in Thalheim would continue to grow, with ongoing recruitment efforts in Germany and Europe.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **2015 Onwards**: The facility is owned by Hanwha Q CELLS, following the acquisition of Q-Cells AG by Hanwha Group. The facility operates as part of Hanwha Q CELLS' global network, focusing on R&D and advanced solar technologies.
