### Evolutionary Timeline of Meyer Burger Facility in Thalheim, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05/2021**: Meyer Burger begins construction of its heterojunction solar cell production facility in Thalheim, with an initial production capacity of 400 MW. Plans for future expansion to 1.4 GW are mentioned, contingent on successful debt financing.
- **05/2021**: Production lines at the Thalheim facility commence operations, producing up to 200,000 solar cells daily.
- **04/2022**: Meyer Burger announces plans to expand solar cell production capacity in Thalheim from 400 MW to 1.4 GW, responding to ongoing global logistics and supply chain issues.
- **01/2024**: The facility continues to operate, supplying solar cells to the newly established module assembly plant in Goodyear, Arizona, despite the closure of the module assembly plant in Freiberg, Germany.
- **06/2024**: The Thalheim facility is confirmed to maintain its production capacity of 1.4 GW, supplying cells to the Goodyear plant.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05/2021**: Meyer Burger secures €22.5 million in public subsidies for the Thalheim facility, with €15 million from the regional government of Saxony-Anhalt and €7.5 million from a fund aimed at improving regional economic structure.
- **04/2022**: The company announces a strategic shift to focus on expanding production capacity in Germany, particularly in Thalheim, due to ongoing global supply chain issues.
- **10/2022**: Meyer Burger plans to raise CHF 250 million ($255.2 million) to support the expansion of production capacity in Thalheim and the development of module production in the US.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05/2021**: Production starts at the Thalheim facility.
- **04/2022**: Meyer Burger announces the expansion of its Thalheim facility to 1.4 GW capacity.
- **01/2024**: The Thalheim facility continues to operate and supply solar cells to the US module assembly plant.
- **04/2024**: The company announces the closure of its module assembly plant in Freiberg, but the Thalheim facility remains operational.

#### 4. Labour
- **01/2024**: The closure of the Freiberg module assembly plant is expected to affect nearly 500 employees, but the Thalheim facility's operations will not be impacted, ensuring job retention there.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Throughout the timeline, Meyer Burger maintains full ownership of the Thalheim facility, with no indications of joint ventures or partnerships affecting its ownership status.
