### Evolutionary Timeline of Heckert Solar Facility in Langenwetzendorf, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2021**: Heckert Solar inaugurated a 400 MW solar panel factory in Langenwetzendorf, Thuringia. The facility is dedicated to producing the new module series NeMo 3.0 120 M. The factory spans 24,000 m², with the first production line covering 8,000 m². The facility is more automated than the existing 400 MW factory in Chemnitz, and it is planned to operate in four shifts. After ramp-up, the facility will have an annual production capacity of 800 MW.
  
- **August 2023**: A consortium including Heckert Solar announced plans to increase the production capacity of the Langenwetzendorf facility from 400 MW to 2.8 GW as part of a €2 billion investment in vertically integrated solar module production across Germany.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2021**: Heckert Solar invested €21 million in the construction of the 400 MW factory in Langenwetzendorf.
  
- **August 2023**: The consortium (Heckert Solar, Wattkraft, and Interfloat) plans to invest around €2 billion in manufacturing facilities, which includes the capacity expansion of the Langenwetzendorf facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2021**: Inauguration of the 400 MW solar panel factory in Langenwetzendorf.
  
- **August 2023**: Announcement of plans to expand the facility's capacity to 2.8 GW as part of a larger investment initiative.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2021**: The new facility is expected to employ 275 people after the ramp-up of production.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility in Langenwetzendorf is owned exclusively by Heckert Solar, as indicated by the context of the articles. There is no mention of joint ventures or shared ownership regarding this specific facility.
