### Evolutionary Timeline of Innotech Solar ASA Facility in Halle/Saale, Germany

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** September 2011
   - **Details:** Innotech Solar ASA opened a solar cell testing and optimization factory in Halle/Saale, Germany. The facility has a production capacity to test and optimize up to 20,000 solar cells per hour. The factory spans a production area of 7,000 square meters. The facility operates under ITS Halle Cell GmbH, a subsidiary of Innotech Solar. The process developed by Innotech involves analyzing solar cells from various manufacturers and isolating impurities using special lasers, allowing the cells to return to their full capacity. The optimized cells are then used in Innotech Solar modules, which are noted for being eco-friendly due to lower energy and raw material usage in their production.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** September 2011
   - **Details:** An initial investment of €20 million was made to establish the factory.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** September 2011
   - **Details:** The factory was officially opened, marking the commencement of operations in Halle/Saale. The construction period lasted for eight months prior to the opening.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** September 2011
   - **Details:** The opening of the facility created 50 new jobs, primarily filled by residents from the Halle/Saale region. An additional 80 jobs and apprenticeships are anticipated in a second expansion phase, although specific details regarding this phase have not been disclosed.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Details:** The facility is operated by ITS Halle Cell GmbH, which is a subsidiary of Innotech Solar ASA, indicating that Innotech Solar has full ownership of the facility.
