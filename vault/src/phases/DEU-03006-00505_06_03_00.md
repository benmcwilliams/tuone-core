## DEU-03006-00505

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Manz Facility in Schwäbisch Hall, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2011**: Manz acquires Würth Solar's CIGS thin-film production line, which had a capacity of 30MW. Post-acquisition, the capacity is reduced to 6MW for R&D purposes.
- **September 2012**: Manz achieves a module efficiency of 14.6% using its CIGSfab mass production line in Schwäbisch Hall.
- **April 2015**: Manz sets a new efficiency record for a CIGS thin-film module at 16%, verified by TÜV Rheinland. The module incorporates a CIGS cell developed in collaboration with the Centre for Solar Energy and Hydrogen Research (ZSW).

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2018**: Manz announces a strategic cooperation with Chinese companies, unlocking CIGS orders worth €263 million, which includes a €50 million purchase price for Manz CIGS Technology GmbH.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2011**: Manz's acquisition of Würth Solar's CIGS operations is expected to conclude by early 2012.
- **September 2012**: Manz begins small-scale production of CIGS modules.
- **April 2015**: Manz unveils a record-breaking CIGS module at the SNEC show in China.
- **January 2018**: Ground breaks on a new CIGSfab line in China, with the existing innovation line in Schwäbisch Hall continuing operations.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2011**: The acquisition of Würth Solar results in the integration of 116 employees into Manz, but approximately 32 jobs are lost during the transition.
- **January 2018**: The announcement of the CIGSlab research line indicates the creation of around 170 jobs in Schwäbisch Hall.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2011**: Manz acquires Würth Solar's CIGS thin-film production line, indicating full ownership of the facility post-acquisition. 

This timeline captures the key developments and changes at the Manz facility in Schwäbisch Hall, Germany, focusing on technology, investment, project milestones, labor impacts, and ownership structure.
