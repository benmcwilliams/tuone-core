### Evolutionary Timeline of the Meyer Burger Facility in Saxon Hohenstein-Ernstthal, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Focus**: The facility is involved in the pilot production of heterojunction (HJT) and tandem solar cells.
- **Capacity**: There are discussions about establishing a mass production facility with an annual capacity of 5 GW to 10 GW, although this has not yet been realized.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Proposals**: Sentis Capital, the largest single shareholder of Meyer Burger, has urged the company to raise capital for the establishment of a GW-scale production facility for HJT technology. They suggested a capital increase or a strategic partnership to finance this initiative.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Current Status**: As of December 2018, Meyer Burger is evaluating the proposal to establish HJT and tandem solar cell production, supported by a Swiss Investment Bank. The company has not yet made a definitive decision on this expansion.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Impact**: In mid-October 2018, Meyer Burger announced plans to cut 100 full-time jobs in Switzerland as part of a transformation program aimed at improving long-term profitability. However, specific job creation or loss related to the Saxon facility's potential expansion has not been detailed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: Meyer Burger Technology AG is the owner of the facility, but there are indications that they may consider strategic partnerships or capital increases to support future expansions.

### Summary
The Meyer Burger facility in Saxon Hohenstein-Ernstthal is currently focused on pilot production of advanced solar cell technologies, with discussions ongoing about expanding to a larger production capacity. The facility's future developments are contingent on securing investments, as urged by its largest shareholder, Sentis Capital. The company is also undergoing restructuring, which includes job cuts in Switzerland, but the impact on the Saxon facility remains to be clarified.
