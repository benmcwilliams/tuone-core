## DEU-03135-02675

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Avancis Facility in Torgau, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2008**: Avancis began production of copper-indium-selenide (CIS) thin-film solar modules at its 20MW plant in Torgau, Germany.
- **January 2010**: The facility achieved an efficiency level of 11% for its standard production modules, which had increased from an initial 9% since the start of production.
- **June 2010**: Avancis broke ground on a new 100MW capacity plant in Torgau, expected to utilize third-generation cell process technology.
- **April 2014**: Manufacturing at the mothballed 100MW Fab 2 in Torgau was planned to be restarted following the acquisition by CNBM.
- **September 2014**: Work to restart an improved production process at the 100MW plant in Torgau began after CNBM completed its acquisition.
- **May 2016**: Avancis reported a record conversion efficiency of 17.9% for a champion mini-module produced at the Torgau facility.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2010**: The construction of the new 100MW facility was announced, marking a significant investment in expanding Avancis' production capabilities.
- **April 2014**: CNBM's acquisition of Avancis was aimed at expanding solar module development and production, indicating further investment intentions.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2008**: The 20MW plant in Torgau came online.
- **June 2010**: Groundbreaking for the 100MW facility occurred.
- **Q1 2012**: The new 100MW facility was expected to commence operations.
- **April 2014**: CNBM announced plans to restart operations at the mothballed 100MW Fab 2.
- **September 2014**: CNBM began work to restart production at the 100MW plant.
- **May 2016**: Avancis achieved a new efficiency record for its modules.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2010**: The existing 20MW facility employed 150 people, with an additional 50 researchers based in Munich.
- **April 2014**: Following the acquisition by CNBM, all 240 employees were offered the opportunity to be retained by the new company.
- **September 2014**: The majority of the workforce agreed to transfer to the new entity under CNBM.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2009**: Avancis was a joint venture between Shell and Saint-Gobain, with Shell holding a 50% share.
- **April 2014**: Avancis was sold to the China National Building Materials Group Corporation (CNBM), marking a shift to full ownership by CNBM.
