### Evolutionary Timeline of Singulus Technologies AG Facility in Kahl am Main, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2017**: Singulus Technologies AG secured an order for the supply of its heterojunction (HJT) solar cell production equipment, specifically the Silex II production line and the new sputtering system Generis PVD. This system is designed for very thin substrates, such as wafers for HJT solar cells, and ensures high uniformity in layer thickness with low operating costs.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2017**: Singulus announced a capital increase of up to 10% through the issuance of new shares to strengthen its equity base and finance current and expected order intake. This investment is tied to the development of the Silex process equipment, which aims to allow photovoltaic manufacturers to produce more cost-effectively.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2018**: Singulus reported that it was working on major orders from China, including a significant project initiated in 2016 with China National Building Materials (CNBM) for equipment for two CIGS module plants in China, valued at approximately €110 million. The company expected further contracts and advance payments related to these projects.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2018**: As a result of increased capacity utilization in the solar segment, Singulus reported a significant improvement in its financial performance, which may imply job stability or creation, although specific job numbers were not mentioned.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2017**: Singulus formed a joint venture with two Chinese photovoltaic companies, Golden Concord Holdings Limited (GCL) and China Intellectual Electric Power Technology Co., Ltd. (CIE), to develop, optimize, build, and distribute complete production lines for high-efficiency HJT solar cells. This indicates that Singulus does not exclusively own the facility but is involved in a collaborative venture for specific projects.
