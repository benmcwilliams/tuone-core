### Evolutionary Timeline of the SolarWorld Facility in Bitterfeld-Wolfen Chemical Park, Germany

1. **Technology and Operations**:
   - **09/2011**: A new joint venture, SolarCycle GmbH, is established to recycle photovoltaic (PV) modules. The facility will produce metal products and other valuable materials from old PV modules. It will be located in the Bitterfeld/Wolfen Chemical Park and will feature a largely automated, second-generation recycling plant capable of processing crystalline solar modules and other types of solar modules and compound materials. The facility is designed to process up to 30,000 tons of recycling material per year.

2. **Investment and Development**:
   - **09/2011**: An investment of €12.7 million is announced for the construction of the recycling plant, which will be carried out in two phases. The first phase is expected to be completed by the end of 2012.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**:
   - **09/2011**: Construction on the recycling plant is scheduled to begin in autumn 2011, with the first phase completion targeted for the end of 2012.

4. **Labour**:
   - **09/2011**: The establishment of the facility is expected to create new jobs, although the specific number of jobs created has not been disclosed.

5. **Ownership Structure**:
   - **09/2011**: SolarWorld holds a 24 percent stake in the joint venture SolarCycle GmbH, indicating that the facility is not fully owned by SolarWorld but is part of a joint venture with Preiss-Daimler Chemical Park and other unnamed companies.
