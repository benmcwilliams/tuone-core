## DEU-03192-03485

### Evolutionary Timeline of SolarWorld Industries GmbH Facility in Sachsen, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **08/2017:** SolarWorld Industries GmbH, founded by Dr. Ing. Eh Frank Asbeck, acquired key manufacturing and R&D assets from the bankrupt SolarWorld AG, including the facility in Sachsen. The facility is involved in the production of photovoltaic (PV) modules. Asbeck announced plans to restart production in Germany, which would save over 500 jobs.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **08/2017:** The acquisition of the assets was supported by the Qatar Foundation, which is a shareholder in SolarWorld Industries GmbH alongside Asbeck. Financial details of the acquisition were not disclosed, but it was noted that the purchase price mainly consisted of the repayment of liabilities secured by creditor protection rights.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **08/01/2017:** SolarWorld AG entered insolvency proceedings.
   - **08/08/2017:** Asbeck's new company, SolarWorld Industries GmbH, signed a purchase and transfer agreement for the assets of SolarWorld AG, including the Sachsen facility.
   - **08/11/2017:** Asbeck confirmed that production in Germany, including the Sachsen facility, would restart the following week.

4. **Labour:**
   - **08/11/2017:** Asbeck announced that the restart of production in Germany would save over 500 jobs at the Sachsen facility. However, the closure of Ulbrich Solar Technologies' plant in Oregon indicated potential job losses in the U.S. related to the insolvency of SolarWorld AG.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **08/2017:** SolarWorld Industries GmbH is co-owned by Dr. Ing. Eh Frank Asbeck and Qatar Solar Technologies, a subsidiary of the Qatar Foundation, indicating a joint venture structure for the facility in Sachsen. 

This timeline captures the key developments and changes at the SolarWorld Industries GmbH facility in Sachsen, DEU, following the acquisition and restructuring efforts led by its founder.
