### Evolutionary Timeline of the Vattenfall Facility in Moorburg, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2015**: The Moorburg coal power station was opened by Vattenfall, with a generation capacity of nearly 1.6 gigawatts.
- **2021**: Vattenfall announced plans to transition the facility to one of the first 100-megawatt scale green hydrogen sites in Europe, with an initial plan to establish a 100 MW electrolyzer powered by wind and solar, aiming for green hydrogen production to start around 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2021**: The facility is applying for funds from the Important Projects of Common European Interest initiative to support the transition to green hydrogen production. The project is also part of Germany’s €45 billion coal phaseout plan, which includes compensation for the closure of coal capacity.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2021**: Vattenfall acknowledged the economic unviability of the coal plant, leading to its planned closure within the same year. The transition to green hydrogen is set to begin with production expected around 2025.

#### 4. Labour

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **2021**: Vattenfall is identified as the owner of the facility, with plans to collaborate with partners such as Shell, Mitsubishi Heavy Industries, and Wärme Hamburg for the green hydrogen project. There is no indication of joint ownership; Vattenfall appears to maintain exclusive ownership of the facility while engaging in partnerships for the new project.
