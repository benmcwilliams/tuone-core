### Evolutionary Timeline of the ArcelorMittal Facility in Moorburg, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2015**: The Moorburg coal power station was opened by Vattenfall, with a generation capacity of nearly 1.6 gigawatts.
- **2021**: Plans were revealed to transform the facility into one of the first 100-megawatt scale green hydrogen sites in Europe, with an initial plan to establish a 100 MW electrolyzer powered by wind and solar, aiming for green hydrogen production to start around 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2021**: The project is applying for funds from the Important Projects of Common European Interest initiative to support the transition to green hydrogen production. The facility is also part of Germany’s €45 billion coal phaseout plan, which includes compensation for the closure of coal capacity.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2021**: Vattenfall announced the closure of the coal plant, which was deemed not economically viable. The transition to green hydrogen is set to begin with production expected around 2025.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
