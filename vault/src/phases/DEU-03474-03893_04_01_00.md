### Evolutionary Timeline of the Dillinger Facility in Dillingen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2020**: The Dillinger facility commenced operation as Germany’s first hydrogen-based steel production plant. This facility utilizes hydrogen as a reducing agent in the blast furnace, significantly reducing carbon emissions. The technology involves injecting hydrogen-rich coke gas, which is a crucial step towards producing green steel. The facility aims to transition to fully hydrogen-based steel production as green hydrogen becomes available in sufficient quantities.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2024**: Dillinger entered into a large-scale supply agreement with Ørsted, which is expected to accelerate investments in lower-emission steel production. This agreement is part of a broader strategy to decarbonize steel production, with specific investments tied to the production of lower-emission steel starting from 2027-2028.

- **May 2024**: Dillinger signed an addendum to its existing framework agreement with Sif, which includes plans for a monopile recycling factory. This partnership is aimed at enhancing the circular economy in steel production and is part of a larger investment strategy to produce green steel.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2020**: The facility officially began operations focused on hydrogen-based steel production.
  
- **March 2024**: Ørsted secured exclusive access to lower-emission steel from Dillinger, with production expected to start from 2027-2028.

- **May 2024**: Dillinger finalized a memorandum of understanding with Sif for developing a decommissioning process for offshore wind foundations, further solidifying its role in the green steel supply chain.
