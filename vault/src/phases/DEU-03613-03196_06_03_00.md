## DEU-03613-03196

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Meyer Burger Facility in Talheim, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Production**: The Talheim facility produces solar cells, which are intended to support module production operations in the United States.
- **Production Capacity**: As of July 2023, Meyer Burger achieved a production volume of 300 MW in the first half of 2023. The facility is part of a broader strategy to support a 2 GW module production facility under construction in the US.
- **Future Plans**: Meyer Burger had initially targeted around 3 GW of new annual production capacity in Germany by the end of 2024, but this plan has been affected by market conditions.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Funding**: In July 2023, Meyer Burger secured €200 million (approximately US$222 million) from the European Commission’s Innovation Fund to support its operations, including the Talheim facility.
- **Redirected Investments**: Equipment initially intended for the Talheim plant's expansion was redirected to a new solar cell plant in Colorado, USA, indicating a shift in investment focus.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Expansion Plans**: The expansion of the Talheim plant was planned for a later date after securing funding, but specific timelines were not detailed.
- **Production Changes**: On January 17, 2024, Meyer Burger announced it would cease PV module production in Germany, including the Talheim facility, while continuing solar cell production to support US operations.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Impact**: The decision to cease PV module production in Germany, including the closure of the Freiberg facility, is expected to affect approximately 500 jobs. However, the specific impact on the Talheim facility's workforce was not detailed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: Meyer Burger Technology AG, headquartered in Switzerland, owns the Talheim facility. There is no indication of joint ventures or shared ownership in the provided articles.

### Summary
The Meyer Burger facility in Talheim, Germany, is primarily focused on solar cell production to support module manufacturing in the US. While the facility had plans for expansion and increased production capacity, recent market conditions have led to a shift in focus towards US operations, with significant investments redirected away from Talheim. The facility's future remains uncertain, particularly with the announcement of ceasing module production in Germany.
