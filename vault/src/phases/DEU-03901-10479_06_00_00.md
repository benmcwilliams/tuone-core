### Evolutionary Timeline of the ib vogt GmbH Facility in Benban, Egypt

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2017**: The Infinity 50 solar park in Benban was officially approved, with a production capacity of 64.1 MW. The facility aims to produce approximately 110,000 MWh of electricity annually, which will be sold to the Egyptian Electricity Transmission Company (EETC) under a 25-year Power Purchase Agreement. Construction involves around 200,000 solar arrays over a 98.6-hectare site.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2017**: The project financing was secured with 85% of the debt from Bayerische Landesbank and 15% from Arab African International Bank, supported by the German government. This financing is crucial for the development of the solar park as part of the larger 1.6 GW Benban solar complex.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2017**: Official approval of the Infinity 50 solar park by the Egyptian Electricity Transmission Company.
- **January 2019**: The 30 MW plant, developed by a consortium including ib vogt, was connected to the grid as part of the Benban solar complex. This marked the first of 16 solar plants financed by the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development (EBRD) to come online.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific details regarding job creation or job loss associated with the investments or expansions at the ib vogt facility in Benban.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is developed as part of a consortium that includes ib vogt GmbH, Infinity Solar Energy SAE, investment vehicle MMID, and private equity firm BPE Partners. This indicates that ib vogt does not have exclusive ownership of the facility but is part of a joint venture.
