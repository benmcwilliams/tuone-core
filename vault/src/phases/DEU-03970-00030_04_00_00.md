## DEU-03970-00030

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Alstom Facility in Taunus, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Project Overview**: The facility is involved in the production and deployment of hydrogen trains as part of a large-scale pilot project aimed at reducing CO2 emissions.
- **Current Production Capacity**: Out of a planned fleet of 27 hydrogen trains, only 12 are currently operational as of June 2023. The goal is to have the entire fleet operational by September 2023.
- **Operational Challenges**: The project has faced delivery problems, technical issues, and a shortage of staff, impacting the reliability and availability of the trains.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Details**: While specific investment amounts have not been disclosed, Alstom is responsible for covering the costs associated with a replacement bus service due to the operational disruptions caused by the delays in train delivery.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Key Dates**:
  - **June 2023**: The RMV expects to receive four more hydrogen trains, aiming for full operational capacity by September 2023.
  - **December 2022**: The timetable was changed, originally intending for the Taunus route (RB 15) to host the world’s largest fleet of hydrogen trains.
  - **Current Status**: Construction work is underway on the RB 15 line, leading to temporary closures.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation/Loss**: The project is currently facing a staff shortage, with around 20 out of approximately 60 engine drivers having resigned, and nearly 20% of the remaining staff on sick leave. This has created challenges in shift planning and operational stability.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: Alstom is the manufacturer of the hydrogen trains but the operational aspect is managed by Start, a subsidiary of Deutsche Bahn. The ownership structure indicates that Alstom does not exclusively own the facility, as it is part of a broader operational framework involving other entities.

### Summary
The Alstom facility in Taunus, DEU, is focused on the development and deployment of hydrogen trains aimed at reducing CO2 emissions. As of June 2023, the project is facing significant challenges, including operational delays and staff shortages, but aims to have a full fleet operational by September 2023. Alstom is responsible for certain costs due to these delays, and the ownership structure involves collaboration with Deutsche Bahn's subsidiary, Start.
