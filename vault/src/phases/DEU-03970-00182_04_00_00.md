## DEU-03970-00182

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Deutsche Bahn Facility in Taunus, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **07/2023**: The Taunus Hydrogen Train Project is a large-scale pilot initiative aimed at reducing CO2 emissions. The facility is intended to operate a fleet of 27 hydrogen trains, but only 12 are currently in operation due to various setbacks. The project aims to deploy hydrogen trains on three additional routes once fully operational. The original plan was for the Taunus route (RB 15) to host the world’s largest fleet of hydrogen trains.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07/2023**: The project has faced delivery problems and technical issues, which have hindered its progress. The manufacturer, Alstom, has not disclosed additional costs incurred due to these setbacks, but they are responsible for covering the expenses of a replacement bus service currently in use.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **07/2023**: The RMV (Rhein-Main-Verkehrsverbund) expects to receive four more hydrogen trains in June, with the goal of having the entire fleet operational by September. However, delivery issues have prevented the full launch of the fleet. Construction work is underway on the RB 15 line, resulting in a temporary closure, with free transportation offered in August and September to compensate passengers.

#### 4. Labour
- **07/2023**: The operator Start, a subsidiary of Deutsche Bahn, is experiencing a staff shortage, with around 20 out of approximately 60 engine drivers having resigned and nearly 20% of the remaining staff on sick leave. This has made shift planning difficult and has contributed to operational disruptions.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **07/2023**: The facility is operated by Start, a subsidiary of Deutsche Bahn, indicating that Deutsche Bahn has a controlling interest in the facility, although the specific ownership structure (full ownership or joint venture) is not explicitly stated in the article. 

### Summary
The Deutsche Bahn facility in Taunus is focused on implementing a hydrogen train project aimed at reducing CO2 emissions. Despite facing significant challenges, including staff shortages and technical issues, the project is progressing with plans for expansion and operational improvements. The facility is primarily owned and operated by Deutsche Bahn through its subsidiary, Start.
