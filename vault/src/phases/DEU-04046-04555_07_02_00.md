## DEU-04046-04555

### Evolutionary Timeline of the B-ON Facility in Düren, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2022**: B-ON, then known as Odin Automotive, acquired the Deutsche Post subsidiary StreetScooter along with the Düren facility, which has an annual production capacity of 30,000 electric vehicles.
- **October 2023**: B-ON introduced a new electric delivery van called the Pelkan, which has a maximum output of 85 kW and utilizes the Mobility in Harmony (MIH) open vehicle platform from contract manufacturer Foxconn.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2022**: B-ON entered into a cooperation with Hitachi for charging infrastructure and energy management, with Hitachi Venture becoming a shareholder.
- **September 2023**: B-ON filed for insolvency, citing delivery bottlenecks and quality issues with components, which led to a decline in production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2024**: Following B-ON's insolvency in September 2023, the initial founder of StreetScooter, Günther Schuh, took over operations and assets of Nob Manufacturing GmbH, which includes the Düren facility. A strategic partnership was formed with Neapco for assembly, ensuring the continuation of operations and job security.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2024**: The transition to e.Volution, led by Schuh, aims to secure jobs at Nob, Neapco, and other suppliers, following the acquisition of operations from B-ON.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2022**: B-ON acquired the Düren facility as part of its takeover of StreetScooter, indicating full ownership at that time.
- **January 2024**: After B-ON's insolvency, the operations and assets were taken over by e.Volution, indicating a change in ownership structure from B-ON to e.Volution.
