### Evolutionary Timeline of the Streetscooter GmbH Facility in Düren, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2010**: Streetscooter GmbH was founded by Günther Schuh.
- **2014**: The company was sold to DHL, which became its pilot customer.
- **2022**: DHL sold Streetscooter to the Luxembourg-based company B-ON (formerly known as Odin Automotive). The facility had a production capacity of 30,000 vehicles per year.
- **September 2023**: B-ON filed for insolvency, citing delivery bottlenecks and quality issues, leading to a decline in production of the Sherpa model.
- **October 2023**: B-ON introduced a new electric delivery van, the Pelkan, with a maximum output of 85 kW, utilizing the Mobility in Harmony (MIH) open vehicle platform from contract manufacturer Foxconn.
- **January 1, 2024**: Günther Schuh's new company, e.Volution, took over operations and assets of Nob Manufacturing GmbH, continuing the production of electric delivery vans in Düren.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2024**: e.Volution entered a strategic partnership with Neapco, which assembles the EVs in Düren. Neapco acquired assembly equipment from DHL, facilitating the continuation of operations.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2023**: B-ON filed for insolvency.
- **January 1, 2024**: e.Volution took over operations and assets of Nob Manufacturing GmbH, ensuring the continuation of production and securing jobs.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2024**: The transition to e.Volution and the partnership with Neapco aimed to secure jobs at Nob, Neapco, and other suppliers involved in the production process.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2024**: e.Volution, founded by Günther Schuh, took over the operations of the facility, indicating a shift in ownership from B-ON to e.Volution, with a strategic partnership with Neapco for assembly operations.
