### Evolutionary Timeline of the e.Volution Facility in Düren, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2024**: e.Volution takes over operations and assets of Nob Manufacturing GmbH. The facility continues to produce electric delivery vans, specifically the Streetscooter, with an initial production capacity of 30,000 vehicles per year. The new electric delivery van, Pelkan, is introduced, featuring a maximum output of 85 kW and utilizing the Mobility in Harmony (MIH) open vehicle platform from Foxconn. e.Volution aims to enhance sustainability through a durable chassis and a digital vehicle file for regular upgrades.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2024**: The transition of operations involves a strategic partnership with Neapco, which assembles the electric vehicles. Neapco acquired assembly equipment from DHL Group, facilitating the continuation of operations at the facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 1, 2024**: e.Volution officially takes over the operations and assets of Nob Manufacturing GmbH. This transition is crucial for maintaining production and securing jobs at Nob, Neapco, and other suppliers.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2024**: The takeover by e.Volution and the partnership with Neapco are expected to secure jobs at Nob Manufacturing and Neapco, following the insolvency of B-ON, the previous owner.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2024**: e.Volution, founded by Günther Schuh, takes full ownership of the operations and assets of Nob Manufacturing GmbH, following the insolvency of B-ON. The facility operates under a strategic partnership with Neapco for assembly but is primarily owned by e.Volution.
