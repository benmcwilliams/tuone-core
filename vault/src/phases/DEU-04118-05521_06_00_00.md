### Evolutionary Timeline of the Luxcara Facility in Lewitzrand, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2020**: Luxcara acquired a 172MW photovoltaic project in Germany from Belectric. The facility is located in the municipalities of Tramm and Lewitzrand in Mecklenburg-Western Pomerania. Belectric is responsible for building and operating the solar farm. The project features 420,000 PV modules and is noted as Luxcara's largest photovoltaic project in Germany not owned by a utility.
- **January 2022**: The Tramm-Göthen solar farm, which includes the Lewitzrand facility, has a planned capacity of more than 171MWp, making it Germany’s largest independent solar photovoltaic project. The facility is currently under construction and is expected to become operational in the first quarter of 2022.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2022**: Luxcara reached financial close for the Tramm-Göthen solar project with UmweltBank, which provided financing in the mid-double-digit million region. Renewable energy funds advised by Luxcara provided the equity for the project, supporting ongoing construction works.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2020**: Announcement of the acquisition of the 172MW solar farm from Belectric, with construction expected to start before the end of the year and commercial operation anticipated by the end of 2021.
- **January 2022**: The facility is under construction, with expectations for operational status in the first quarter of 2022. The clean energy generated will be sold under a ten-year power purchase agreement (PPA).

#### 5. Ownership Structure
