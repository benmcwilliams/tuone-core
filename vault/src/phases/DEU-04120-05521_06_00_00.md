### Evolutionary Timeline of the Luxcara Facility in Tramm, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2020**: Luxcara acquired a 172MW photovoltaic project in Tramm and Lewitzrand, Mecklenburg-Western Pomerania, from Belectric. The facility will utilize 420,000 PV modules and is expected to generate enough energy to power nearly 50,000 homes annually.
- **January 2022**: The facility, now referred to as the Tramm-Göthen solar farm, has a planned capacity of more than 171MWp, making it Germany’s largest independent solar photovoltaic project. 

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2022**: Luxcara reached financial close for the Tramm-Göthen solar project with UmweltBank, which provided financing in the mid-double-digit million region. Renewable energy funds advised by Luxcara provided the equity for the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2020**: Construction was announced to start by the end of the year, with commercial operations expected to begin at the end of 2021.
- **January 2022**: The project was under construction, with expectations for it to become operational in the first quarter of 2022.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify exact job creation or job loss figures related to the facility's construction or operation.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Luxcara owns the facility through renewable energy funds for institutional investors that it advises. It is noted that this facility is the largest photovoltaic project in Germany not owned by a utility, indicating that Luxcara has exclusive ownership through its investment structure.
