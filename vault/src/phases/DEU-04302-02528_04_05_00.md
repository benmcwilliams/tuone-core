## DEU-04302-02528

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hydrogenious LOHC Technologies Facility in Dormagen, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2021**: Construction begins on the world's largest plant for storing green hydrogen in liquid organic hydrogen carriers (LOHC) at CHEMPARK Dormagen. The facility will utilize proprietary LOHC technology, specifically using Benzyltoluene as the carrier medium. The planned capacity is approximately 1,800 tons of hydrogen per year. The facility aims to integrate heat energy from the hydrogenation process into the site's steam network to enhance overall efficiency. Commissioning is scheduled for 2023.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: The project receives funding of €9 million from the progres.nrw program of the state of North Rhine-Westphalia. Additionally, €2 million of this funding is allocated for scientific studies at the Jülich Research Centre, which is a scientific partner in the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2021**: Official announcement of the construction start for the facility, marking it as the largest industrial-scale LOHC storage project globally. The project is referred to as "Storage of green hydrogen in LOHC on a tonne scale at Chempark Dormagen site" (HECTOR). The commissioning of the plant is anticipated in 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2021**: The project is expected to create new, future-oriented jobs in the region, contributing to the development of the Rhenish Revier as a leading hydrogen location.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2021**: The facility is managed by LOHC Industrial Solutions NRW GmbH, a subsidiary of Hydrogenious LOHC Technologies. Covestro Deutschland AG, a shareholder in Hydrogenious since mid-2019, provides the site area and plans to supply green hydrogen as an industrial by-product. Royal Vopak is also a co-investor in the project, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by Hydrogenious LOHC Technologies.
