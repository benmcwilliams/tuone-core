## DEU-04302-04777

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Covestro Facility in Dormagen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2021**: Construction begins on the world's largest plant for storing green hydrogen in liquid organic hydrogen carriers (LOHC) at CHEMPARK Dormagen. The facility will utilize proprietary LOHC technology to store approximately 1,800 tons of hydrogen per year. The plant is designed to integrate heat energy from the hydrogenation process into the site's steam network, enhancing overall efficiency. Commissioning is scheduled for 2023.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: The project receives €9 million in funding from the state of North Rhine-Westphalia's progres.nrw program. Additionally, €2 million of this funding is allocated for scientific studies at the Jülich Research Centre, which is a partner in the project. Covestro Deutschland AG will supply the site area and future green hydrogen as an industrial by-product.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2021**: Official announcement of the construction start for the LOHC storage facility, marking a significant milestone in the development of hydrogen supply chains in the region.
- **2023**: Scheduled commissioning of the LOHC storage plant.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2021**: The project is expected to create new, future-oriented jobs in the Rhenish Revier region as part of the implementation of the Hydrogen Roadmap North Rhine-Westphalia.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2021**: Covestro Deutschland AG is a key investor in the facility and has been a shareholder in Hydrogenious LOHC Technologies since mid-2019. The facility is not exclusively owned by Covestro, as it involves collaboration with Hydrogenious LOHC Technologies and Royal Vopak, indicating a joint venture structure for the project.

#### Additional Developments
- **December 2021**: Covestro signs a long-term corporate power purchase agreement (PPA) with EnBW for solar-generated electricity, securing 63 MW of capacity from a solar farm. This renewable energy will be utilized at the Dormagen site for producing more sustainable plastics, further aligning Covestro's operations with its climate-neutral goals. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic initiatives related to the Covestro facility in Dormagen, highlighting its role in advancing clean technology and sustainable practices in the region.
