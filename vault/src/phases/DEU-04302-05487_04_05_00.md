## DEU-04302-05487

### Evolutionary Timeline of LOHC Industrial Solutions NRW GmbH Facility in Dormagen, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2021**: Announcement of the construction of a plant for storing green hydrogen in liquid organic hydrogen carriers (LOHC) at CHEMPARK Dormagen. The facility will utilize proprietary LOHC technology based on the carrier medium Benzyltoluene. The planned production capacity is approximately **1800 tons of hydrogen per year**. This facility is set to be the **world's largest pilot plant** for LOHC technology.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: The project receives **€9 million** in funding from the state of North Rhine-Westphalia through the progres.nrw programme. Additionally, **€2 million** of this funding is allocated for scientific studies at the Jülich Research Centre.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2021**: Construction officially starts on the LOHC facility. The commissioning of the plant is scheduled for **2023**. The project is part of the initiative to create a significant hydrogen supply chain between Dormagen and Rotterdam, Netherlands.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2021**: The project is expected to create new, future-oriented jobs in the Rhenish Revier region, contributing to the local economy and supporting the development of a sustainable hydrogen economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2021**: The facility is managed by **LOHC Industrial Solutions NRW GmbH**, a subsidiary of **Hydrogenious LOHC Technologies**. Covestro Deutschland AG provides the site area and will supply green hydrogen as an industrial by-product. The facility is not exclusively owned by one entity but involves partnerships with Covestro and Royal Vopak, indicating a collaborative ownership structure.

### Technology and Operations
- "A plant for storing green hydrogen in liquid organic hydrogen carriers (LOHC) on an industrial scale is being built at CHEMPARK Dormagen, Germany."
- "With the planned plant capacity, about 1800 t of hydrogen per year can be stored in LOHC."
- "The planned plant in Dormagen represents a significant upscaling of hydrogen storage in the form of the LOHC carrier Benzyltoluene and has about twenty times the single-storage capacity of previous plant sizes."
