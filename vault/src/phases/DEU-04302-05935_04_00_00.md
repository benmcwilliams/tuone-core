## DEU-04302-05935

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Royal Vopak Facility in Dormagen, Germany

1. **Technology and Operations**:
   - **March 2021**: Construction begins on a plant for storing green hydrogen in liquid organic hydrogen carriers (LOHC) at CHEMPARK Dormagen. The facility aims to store approximately 1,800 tons of hydrogen per year using LOHC technology based on Benzyltoluene. This facility is set to become the world's largest pilot plant for LOHC hydrogen storage.

2. **Investment and Development**:
   - **March 2021**: The project receives funding of €9 million from the state of North Rhine-Westphalia through the progres.nrw programme. An additional €2 million is allocated for scientific studies at the Jülich Research Centre.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**:
   - **March 2021**: Announcement of the project and construction start. The commissioning of the plant is scheduled for 2023.

4. **Labour**:
   - **March 2021**: The project is expected to create new, future-oriented jobs in the Rhenish Revier region, contributing to its development as a leading hydrogen location.

5. **Ownership Structure**:
   - **March 2021**: Royal Vopak is identified as a co-investor in the project alongside Hydrogenious LOHC Technologies and Covestro Deutschland AG. The facility is not exclusively owned by Royal Vopak but is part of a collaborative investment with other stakeholders. 

This timeline captures the key developments related to the Royal Vopak facility in Dormagen, focusing on its technological advancements, investments, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure.

### Technology and Operations
- "A plant for storing green hydrogen in liquid organic hydrogen carriers (LOHC) on an industrial scale is being built at CHEMPARK Dormagen, Germany."
- "With the planned plant capacity, about 1800 t of hydrogen per year can be stored in LOHC."
- "The planned plant in Dormagen represents a significant upscaling of hydrogen storage in the form of the LOHC carrier Benzyltoluene and has about twenty times the single-storage capacity of previous plant sizes."

### Investment and Development
- "The state of North Rhine-Westphalia is supporting the project with funding of €9 million from the progres.nrw programme."
- "€2 million of the funding for accompanying scientific studies will go there."
- "Covestro Deutschland AG will provide the site area and, in the future, the green hydrogen."

### Project Status and Milestones
- "Commissioning of the plant is scheduled for 2023."
- "The project ‘Storage of green hydrogen in LOHC on a tonne scale at Chempark Dormagen site’ (HECTOR) is funded by the Ministry of Economic Affairs, Innovation, Digitalization and Energy of the State of North Rhine-Westphalia, Germany."

### Labour
- "The project in Dormagen is another important step in the implementation of our Hydrogen Roadmap North Rhine-Westphalia... It also forms the nucleus for the creation of new, future-oriented jobs."

### Ownership Structure
- "Royal Vopak, one of the world’s leading companies in the provision of infrastructure for the chemical and energy industries and an investor in Hydrogenious LOHC Technologies, has also announced its support."
- "From the beginning of our investment in Hydrogenious, we see ourselves as a strategic partner with the common goal of developing cross-regional and global hydrogen logistics."
