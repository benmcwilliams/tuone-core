### Evolutionary Timeline of the Johnson Matthey Facility in Böhlen-Lippendorf, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **October 2023:** Johnson Matthey (JM) announced that their co-developed Fischer Tropsch (FT) CANS technology was selected for EDL’s HyKero plant in Böhlen-Lippendorf. The facility is planned to produce 50,000 metric tons (MT) of sustainable aviation fuel (SAF) per year, including eSAF from a power-to-liquids (PtL) route. Additionally, it is expected to produce 14,000 MT of sustainable naphtha and approximately 1,000 MT of electrolytic (green) hydrogen annually. The plant will be strategically located to serve Leipzig-Halle international airport and is expected to be operational by 2027.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **October 2023:** The HyKero plant is part of a broader initiative that includes government funding negotiations for the East Coast Cluster as part of Phase 2 of the cluster sequencing process for Carbon Capture, Utilization, and Storage (CCUS).    - **October 2023:** Announcement of the selection of JM’s technology for the HyKero plant. The first phase of the plant is planned to be operational by 2027. The project is under the responsibility of EDL’s subsidiary XFuels HyKero GmbH.
