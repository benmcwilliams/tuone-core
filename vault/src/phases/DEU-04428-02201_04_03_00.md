## DEU-04428-02201

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EWE Facility in Northwest German Coastline, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2021**: Announcement of the Clean Hydrogen Coastline project, which aims to integrate hydrogen into the northwest German coastline. The project plans to establish a capacity of up to 400MW for electrolysis and corresponding hydrogen storage by 2026. The focus is on producing green hydrogen for export and industrial applications, particularly in the steel industry.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: The project is backed by a significant investment of $1.5 billion from several major industrial companies in North Germany, indicating strong financial support for the development of hydrogen technology in the region.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2021**: The Clean Hydrogen Coastline project is officially unveiled, with a target completion date for the hydrogen integration by 2026. This includes plans for an iron ore reduction plant and electric arc furnace by ArcelorMittal Bremen, which will utilize green hydrogen in steel production.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2021**: The project is expected to create jobs related to the production and integration of hydrogen technology, particularly in the transport sector, where FAUN Group plans to expand its production to support the introduction of fuel cell-powered vehicles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2021**: The facility is part of a collaborative project involving multiple companies, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by EWE. The project includes various partners from the German hydrogen network chain, each responsible for different aspects of the hydrogen integration process. 

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic plans for the EWE facility in the context of the Clean Hydrogen Coastline project as of March 2021.
