## DEU-04474-00219

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EnBW Facility in Weesow-Willmersdorf, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **06-02-2019**: EnBW announces plans for a 175 MW solar park in Weesow-Willmersdorf, which, if built, would become Germany’s largest solar plant. The project is part of a pipeline of unsubsidized projects totaling 800 MW.
- **17-03-2020**: EnBW starts construction on the 187 MW solar park, which will be the largest solar park in Germany built without public subsidies. The project is located on 164 hectares of land and is expected to be fully operational within the year.
- **29-06-2020**: Trina Solar begins module delivery for the Weesow-Willmersdorf solar farm, confirming the deployment of 465,000 solar modules, with completion targeted by the end of 2020.
- **10-03-2021**: The solar park is reported to feature around 465,000 high-performance modules and is confirmed to have been fully connected to the grid since March 2021.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10-03-2021**: EnBW invests approximately €100 million (US$119 million) in the development of the Weesow-Willmersdorf solar park, emphasizing the project's significance in demonstrating the viability of subsidy-free solar energy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **06-02-2019**: Announcement of the project and acquisition of the solar park from Procon Solar.
- **17-03-2020**: Start of construction on the solar park.
- **10-03-2021**: Confirmation of the project being fully operational and connected to the grid.
- **30-12-2021**: EnBW signs a long-term corporate power purchase agreement (PPA) with Covestro for 63 MW of capacity from the solar farm, marking the first PPA for the project.

#### 4. Labour
- **17-03-2020**: The construction phase of the solar park is expected to provide up to 150 jobs, with ongoing maintenance jobs anticipated after commissioning.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- EnBW exclusively owns the Weesow-Willmersdorf solar facility, as indicated by the company's direct involvement in the development, construction, and operation of the project without any joint ventures or external ownership stakes mentioned in the articles.
