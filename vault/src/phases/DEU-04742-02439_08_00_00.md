### Evolutionary Timeline of the Siemens Gamesa Facility in Butendiek Offshore Wind Farm, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Butendiek offshore wind farm, operational since **2015**, has an installed capacity of **288 MW** and produces approximately **1,200 GWh** of electricity annually. The facility features **80 Siemens Gamesa wind turbines**, each with a capacity of **3.6 MW**.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - As of **October 2023**, the facility entered into a new Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) with a large multinational company for **62.5%** of its total output, which is about **700 GWh per year** for a duration of **six and a half years**. This PPA is significant as it is one of the first green PPAs for hydrogen production in Germany, aligning with the EU's goal of producing **10 million tonnes of green hydrogen by 2030**.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **2015**: Butendiek Offshore Wind Farm becomes operational.
   - **October 2023**: Announcement of a new PPA for 62.5% of the total output, facilitating electricity supply to a green hydrogen production facility.

4. **Labour:**
   - The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the recent developments at the Butendiek Offshore Wind Farm.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The Butendiek Offshore Wind Farm is not exclusively owned by Siemens Gamesa. It is owned by a consortium that includes **Wpd, Schroders Greencoat, Ewz, Industriens Pension, and Itochu/CITIC Pacific**.
