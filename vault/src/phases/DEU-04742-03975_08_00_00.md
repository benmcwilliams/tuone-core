### Evolutionary Timeline of the Greencoat Facility in Butendiek Offshore Wind Farm, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Operational Since**: 2015
- **Installed Capacity**: 288 MW
- **Annual Electricity Production**: Approximately 1,200 GWh
- **Wind Turbines**: 80 Siemens Gamesa turbines, each with a capacity of 3.6 MW.
- **Recent Development**: As of October 2023, the facility signed a Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) to supply 62.5% of its total output (around 700 GWh per year) to a green hydrogen production facility operated by an unnamed global leader in the energy industry.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Recent Investment**: The new PPA is part of a strategic move to support green hydrogen production, aligning with the EU's goal of producing 10 million tonnes of green hydrogen by 2030. The specifics of the financial investment related to this PPA have not been disclosed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **PPA Announcement**: On October 2, 2023, Greencoat Renewables announced the signing of a new PPA.
- **PPA Details**: The PPA is for a duration of six and a half years and is one of the first green PPAs for hydrogen production in Germany, meeting EU standards set in July 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The articles do not specify the exact number of jobs created or lost as a result of the new PPA or the green hydrogen production facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The Butendiek Offshore Wind Farm is owned by a consortium that includes Wpd, Schroders Greencoat, Ewz, Industriens Pension, and Itochu/CITIC Pacific. Greencoat Renewables is part of this consortium but does not have exclusive ownership of the facility.
