## DEU-04742-09214

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Industriens Pension Facility in Butendiek Offshore Wind Farm, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Operational Since**: 2015
- **Installed Capacity**: 288 MW
- **Annual Electricity Production**: Approximately 1,200 GWh
- **Recent Development**: As of October 2023, the Butendiek offshore wind farm signed a Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) to supply 62.5% of its total output (around 700 GWh per year) to a green hydrogen production facility operated by an unnamed global leader in the energy industry. This PPA is noted as one of the first green PPAs for hydrogen production in Germany.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Recent Investment**: The new PPA is part of a strategic move to support green hydrogen production, aligning with the EU's goal of producing 10 million tonnes of green hydrogen by 2030. Specific investment amounts related to this PPA have not been disclosed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2023**: Greencoat Renewables announced the signing of a new PPA for the Butendiek offshore wind farm, which will provide electricity for green hydrogen production.
- **July 2023**: The PPA is expected to meet the standards set under the EU Delegated Acts on green hydrogen.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The articles do not specify the exact number of jobs created or lost as a result of the new PPA or the green hydrogen production facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The Butendiek offshore wind farm is owned by a consortium that includes Wpd, Schroders Greencoat, Ewz, Industriens Pension, and Itochu/CITIC Pacific. Therefore, Industriens Pension does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a joint venture. 

This timeline captures the key developments and operational details of the Butendiek Offshore Wind Farm as related to the involvement of Industriens Pension.
