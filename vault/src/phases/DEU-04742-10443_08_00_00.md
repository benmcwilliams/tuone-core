### Evolutionary Timeline of the Wpd Facility in Butendiek Offshore Wind Farm, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Operational Since**: 2015
- **Installed Capacity**: 288 MW
- **Annual Electricity Production**: Approximately 1,200 GWh
- **Turbine Details**: The facility features 80 Siemens Gamesa wind turbines, each with a capacity of 3.6 MW.
- **Recent Development**: As of October 2023, the Butendiek offshore wind farm has signed a Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) to supply electricity for green hydrogen production.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Recent Investment**: In October 2023, Greencoat Renewables announced a new PPA for 62.5% of the total output (around 700 GWh per year) for a period of six and a half years. This PPA is significant as it is one of the first green PPAs for hydrogen production in Germany and aligns with EU goals for green hydrogen production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **PPA Announcement**: On October 2, 2023, Greencoat Renewables announced the signing of a new PPA.
- **Press Release**: On October 31, 2023, Pexapark confirmed the agreement and its implications for green hydrogen production.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The articles do not specify job creation or loss directly related to the recent PPA or the facility's operations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The Butendiek offshore wind farm is owned by a consortium that includes Wpd, Schroders Greencoat, Ewz, Industriens Pension, and Itochu/CITIC Pacific. Therefore, Wpd does not own the facility exclusively; it is part of a joint venture.
