### Evolutionary Timeline of the Stadtwerke Kiel Facility in Kiel, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10-03-2023**: Stadtwerke Kiel, in collaboration with INNIO, announced plans to convert their coastal power plant to operate fully on green hydrogen by 2035. The facility is currently a 190MW large-scale engine-based combined heat and power plant, supplying eco-friendly district heating to over 73,500 homes and generating electricity for the region. The conversion will involve the installation of 20 hydrogen-ready Jenbacher engines provided by INNIO, transitioning from natural gas to green hydrogen.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10-03-2023**: The announcement of the MoU between Stadtwerke Kiel and INNIO indicates a commitment to invest in the conversion of the power plant to hydrogen, although specific investment amounts were not disclosed. The project aims to set global standards for climate-neutral energy supply.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **10-03-2023**: The signing of the MoU marks a significant milestone in the project, with a target completion date for the conversion set for 2035. The facility is positioned to become entirely climate neutral by this date, contingent on the availability of hydrogen in Europe.

#### 4. Labour
- **10-03-2023**: While specific job creation or loss figures were not mentioned in the articles, the transition to a hydrogen-based operation may imply potential job creation related to the installation and maintenance of new technology, as well as ongoing operations of the converted facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **10-03-2023**: The articles do not specify the ownership structure of the facility, but it is implied that Stadtwerke Kiel operates the facility in collaboration with INNIO, suggesting a partnership rather than exclusive ownership.
