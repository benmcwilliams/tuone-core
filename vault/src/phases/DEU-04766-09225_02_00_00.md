## DEU-04766-09225

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Innargi Facility in Kiel, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2023**: Innargi signed a letter of intent with Stadtwerke Kiel to investigate the subsurface for the potential development of geothermal district heating in Kiel. The facility aims to extract geothermal water at temperatures up to 76 degrees from depths of two to three kilometers, which will be heated to 90 degrees for the district heating network using high-temperature heat pumps. If the investigation yields positive results, production could start in approximately five years.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: The investigation into geothermal potential is part of a broader initiative to transition Kiel's district heating to carbon neutrality. Specific investment amounts or subsidies related to this project have not been disclosed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2023**: The agreement to explore geothermal district heating was made, with the investigation expected to be completed by the end of the year. If successful, further agreements for building and operating geothermal heating plants in Kiel will follow.
- **November 2023**: Innargi confirmed that the results from the evaluation in Flensburg do not negatively impact the project in Kiel, which remains promising from a geothermal perspective.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2023**: The project is expected to contribute to job creation in Kiel, although specific numbers have not been provided. The focus on geothermal energy is anticipated to support local employment in the energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2023**: The facility is being developed in collaboration with Stadtwerke Kiel, indicating a partnership rather than exclusive ownership by Innargi. The exact ownership structure (e.g., joint venture details) has not been specified in the articles.
