## DEU-05055-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Baltic Sea, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2021**: Iberdrola operates the Wikinger offshore wind farm in the Baltic Sea and is developing the Baltic Eagle wind farm, which will have a capacity of 476 MW. The Baltic Eagle is part of a larger 1.1 GW offshore wind complex.
- **April 2022**: Baltic Eagle is confirmed to be under construction, with operations expected to begin by the end of 2024. The wind farm will generate enough energy to power 475,000 homes and displace over 800,000 tons of CO2 annually.
- **July 2023**: The Baltic Eagle wind farm is valued at €1.6 billion and will consist of 50 wind turbines, each with a capacity of 9.53 MW, with an anticipated annual production of 1.9 TWh.
- **December 2024**: The Baltic Eagle wind farm is on track to be operational by the end of 2024, contributing to Iberdrola's goal of reaching 4,800 MW of offshore wind capacity by 2026.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2022**: The Baltic Eagle project is part of a €3.5 billion investment in the offshore wind complex.
- **July 2023**: Iberdrola and Masdar sign a strategic agreement to co-invest in the Baltic Eagle wind farm, valued at €1.6 billion.
- **December 2024**: Iberdrola's total investments in offshore wind exceed €15 billion, with the Baltic Eagle contributing to this total.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2022**: Iberdrola receives approval from the German Federal Maritime and Hydrographic Agency to develop the Baltic Eagle wind farm.
- **July 2023**: The Baltic Eagle wind farm is confirmed to be under construction, with a timeline for initial operations by the end of 2024.
- **December 2024**: The Baltic Eagle wind farm is expected to be operational by the end of 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2022**: The construction of the Baltic Eagle wind farm is expected to create around 800 jobs.
- **July 2023**: The project is anticipated to support thousands of highly skilled jobs as it progresses.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2023**: Iberdrola retains a majority 51% stake in the Baltic Eagle wind farm, while Masdar co-invests, strengthening their partnership in renewable energy projects.
- **December 2024**: Iberdrola continues to control and manage the Baltic Eagle wind farm, providing operation and maintenance services.
