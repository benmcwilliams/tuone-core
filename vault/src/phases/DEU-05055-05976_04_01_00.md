### Evolutionary Timeline of the Salzgitter Flachstahl Facility in Baltic Sea, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: Salzgitter Flachstahl announced plans to produce hydrogen using green electricity sourced from the "Baltic Eagle" offshore wind farm, which is under construction in the Baltic Sea. The wind farm is expected to have a total capacity of 476 MW upon commissioning at the end of 2024. The green hydrogen produced will be a core component for Salzgitter AG to manufacture virtually carbon-neutral steel.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: The German Minister of Economics, Robert Habeck, announced funding for the SALCOS (Salzgitter Low CO2 Steelmaking transformation program). The first development stage of SALCOS will receive approximately €700 million in federal funding and €300 million from the state government. Salzgitter AG has also approved its own funds exceeding €1 billion for this project, ensuring financing for the first development stage, which is set to be implemented by the end of 2025.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: Salzgitter Flachstahl signed a long-term agreement with Iberdrola Deutschland to source electricity from the Baltic Eagle offshore wind farm. The wind farm is scheduled to go online at the end of 2024, marking a significant milestone in the facility's transition to green hydrogen production.
