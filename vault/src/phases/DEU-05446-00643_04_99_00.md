### Evolutionary Timeline of RWE Facility in Weisweiler, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2023**: RWE announced plans to construct a hydrogen-ready combined-cycle gas turbine (CCGT) power plant at its Weisweiler site. The plant is designed to have an output capacity of **800 megawatts** and will be capable of using at least **50% hydrogen** at the time of starting operations, with the potential for full conversion to **100% hydrogen** by **2035**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2023**: The project is contingent upon a final investment decision, which will be made once the regulatory framework for hydrogen-ready gas-fired power plants is established in Germany, expected by **2025**. The approval planning for the plant has commenced, and the ordering of components will follow once all necessary permits are obtained.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2023**: RWE awarded a contract to a consortium of **Ansaldo Energia** (Italy) and **Técnicas Reunidas** (Spain) for the construction of the hydrogen-ready power plant. The approval planning is the first step, with further actions dependent on the regulatory framework and economic viability of the project. The aim is to begin electricity production by the **end of the decade**.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the construction of the new facility in Weisweiler.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- RWE is pursuing the construction of the facility at its existing site in Weisweiler, indicating **full ownership** of the facility, although it is collaborating with external partners (Ansaldo Energia and Técnicas Reunidas) for the construction process.
