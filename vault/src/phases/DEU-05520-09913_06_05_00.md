### Evolutionary Timeline of the SMA Solar Technology Facility in Niestetal, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2013**: SMA Solar Technology faced a significant downturn in the photovoltaic (PV) market, leading to a reduction in sales and production capacity. The company sold 1.2GW of PV inverters in Q1 2013, down 38.1% from the previous year.
- **April 2023**: SMA Solar announced the construction of a new inverter factory in Niestetal, which will increase its production capacity from 20GW to 40GW. The factory is expected to produce system solutions for large-scale PV power plants and is set to begin operations in 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: The investment for the new factory in Niestetal is €80 million (approximately US$87.8 million). This investment is part of SMA's strategy to leverage the growing demand for renewable energy solutions.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: Groundbreaking for the new 20GW inverter factory in Niestetal was officially announced, with operations expected to start in 2025.
- **December 2023**: SMA Solar confirmed that the expansion in Niestetal is part of a broader strategy that includes a new inverter production site in the US, both expected to be operational in 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2013**: SMA Solar announced further job cuts due to a decline in the PV market, following a previous reduction of 500 jobs in autumn 2012.
- **April 2023**: The expansion in Niestetal is expected to create additional jobs, although specific numbers related to job creation from this expansion were not detailed.
