### Evolutionary Timeline of the Solvay Facility in Ahaus, Germany

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **January 2024:** The Ahaus facility is set to host a Compressed Air Energy Storage (CAES) project with a power-generating capacity of 320MW. The project will utilize two of the site’s four existing salt caverns for the CAES technology, with the first cavern scheduled for handover in early 2027. The facility will support the development of a regional, industrial-scale, renewable green hydrogen value chain.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **June 2023:** Corre Energy secured the Ahaus site through an agreement with Solvay, which is currently extracting salt from the caverns. The project will involve co-development and co-investment with Eneco, which will acquire a 50% stake in the project.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **June 2023:** Agreement between Corre Energy and Solvay for the Ahaus site.
   - **January 2024:** Announcement of the partnership between Corre Energy and Eneco for the CAES project, marking it as the first CAES facility to be developed in Germany since 1978.
