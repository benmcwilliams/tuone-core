## DEU-05908-01557

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Corre Energy Facility in Ahaus, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2024**: Corre Energy, in partnership with Eneco, announced the development of a Compressed Air Energy Storage (CAES) project in Ahaus, Germany, with a power-generating capacity of **320 MW**. The facility will utilize **two of the four existing salt caverns** for the initial phase, which will provide **220 MW of compression capacity**. The project is designed to support long-duration energy storage, with the ability to store surplus sustainable electricity by compressing air in underground caverns.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2024**: The partnership between Corre Energy and Eneco includes co-development and co-investment, with Eneco acquiring a **50% stake** in the project. Financial terms related to development capital and construction equity are yet to be finalized. The project is expected to capture around **€1 billion** in net revenue over the duration of a **15-year offtake agreement**.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2023**: Corre Energy secured the Ahaus site through an agreement with Solvay, a global chemical company, which is currently extracting salt from the caverns.
- **January 2024**: Construction of the caverns is already underway, with the handover of the first two caverns expected in **early 2027**. The project represents the first CAES facility to be developed in Germany since the Huntorf plant in 1978.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify exact job creation or job loss figures related to the Ahaus facility. However, the partnership and construction activities imply potential job creation in the region, particularly through the involvement of Eneco's German subsidiary, LichtBlick.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2024**: Corre Energy and Eneco have formed a partnership where Eneco will hold a **50% stake** in the project, indicating a **joint venture** ownership structure for the Ahaus facility. Corre Energy retains the other **50%** ownership.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic partnerships that define the Corre Energy facility in Ahaus, Germany, as of January 2024.
