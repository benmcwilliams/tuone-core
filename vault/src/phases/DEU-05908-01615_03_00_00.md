## DEU-05908-01615

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Eneco Facility in Ahaus, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2024**: Eneco and Corre Energy announced a partnership to develop a Compressed Air Energy Storage (CAES) project in Ahaus, Germany, with a power-generating capacity of 320 MW. The project will utilize two of the site’s four existing salt caverns, with the first cavern scheduled for handover in early 2027. The CAES technology will allow for the storage of surplus sustainable electricity by compressing air, which can later be released to generate electricity.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2024**: The partnership agreement between Eneco and Corre Energy includes co-development and co-investment, with Eneco acquiring a 50% stake in the project. Financial terms for the project, including development capital and construction equity, are yet to be finalized. The project is expected to capture around €1 billion of net revenue over the duration of a 15-year offtake agreement.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2023**: Corre Energy secured the Ahaus site through an agreement with Solvay, a global chemical company, which is currently extracting salt from the caverns.
- **January 2024**: Construction of the caverns is already underway, with the first two expected to be handed over in early 2027. The project represents the first CAES facility to be developed, constructed, and operated in Germany since the Huntorf plant in 1978.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2024**: The project is expected to create jobs through its development and operation phases, although specific job creation figures have not been disclosed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2024**: Eneco will hold a 50% stake in the Ahaus CAES project, indicating a joint venture with Corre Energy. The project will be co-developed and co-financed through Eneco’s German subsidiary, LichtBlick.
