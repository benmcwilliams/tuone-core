## DEU-05908-07281

### Evolutionary Timeline of the LichtBlick Facility in Ahaus, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2024**: The LichtBlick facility in Ahaus is set to co-develop a Compressed Air Energy Storage (CAES) project with a power-generating capacity of 320MW. The facility will utilize two of the four existing salt caverns for the CAES technology, with the first cavern scheduled for handover in early 2027. The project marks the first CAES facility to be developed in Germany since the Huntorf plant in 1978.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2024**: Financial terms for the CAES project, including development capital and construction equity, are yet to be finalized. The project is part of a long-term offtake agreement that secures Eneco the entire storage capacity for the initial phase.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2023**: Corre Energy secures the Ahaus site through an agreement with Solvay, a global chemical company, for the development of the CAES facility.
- **January 2024**: The partnership between Corre Energy and Eneco is announced, with Eneco acquiring a 50% stake in the project. Construction of all four caverns is already in progress.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2024**: The project is expected to create jobs, although specific job creation numbers have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2024**: The facility is co-owned by Corre Energy and Eneco, with Eneco acquiring a 50% stake in the project. LichtBlick, as Eneco’s German subsidiary, will co-develop, finance, and operate the Ahaus project alongside Corre Energy Germany.
