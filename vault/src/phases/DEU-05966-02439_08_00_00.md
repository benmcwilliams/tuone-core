## DEU-05966-02439

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Siemens Gamesa Facility in Butendiek, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Operational Since**: 2015
- **Installed Capacity**: 288 MW
- **Annual Electricity Production**: Approximately 1,200 GWh
- **Turbine Specifications**: The facility comprises 80 Siemens Gamesa wind turbines, each with a capacity of 3.6 MW.
- **Recent Developments**: As of January 1, 2024, Semco Maritime has been awarded a 10-year service contract for operations and maintenance, which includes scheduled and unscheduled maintenance and inspections of the offshore substation and turbine foundations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Service Contracts**: In November 2023, Semco Maritime secured a 10-year balance-of-plant service contract with Siemens Gamesa for the Butendiek offshore wind farm, indicating ongoing investment in operational support and maintenance.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Construction Start**: Early 2014
- **Completion**: 2015
- **Recent Milestone**: In November 2023, Siemens Gamesa announced a partnership with Semco Maritime for a 10-year service contract starting January 1, 2024. Additionally, Acteon was contracted for maintenance tasks, indicating ongoing operational activities.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The awarding of the 10-year service contract to Semco Maritime is expected to create jobs related to operations and maintenance activities, although specific numbers were not disclosed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The Butendiek offshore wind farm is owned by a consortium that includes CITIC Pacific Limited, Industriens Pensionsforsikring, ITOCHU Corporation, Greencoat Renewables, Octopus Energy Generation, and wpd. Siemens Gamesa does not own the facility exclusively but is responsible for turbine maintenance and logistics.
