### Evolutionary Timeline of Siemens Gamesa Facility in Cuxhaven, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2018**: Siemens Gamesa completed assembly of the first nacelle at its new factory in Cuxhaven, which has a production capacity of around 250 nacelles per year. The facility produces nacelles for Siemens Gamesa's 8MW turbines for Ørsted’s Borssele 1&2 wind complex.
- **June 2020**: The facility celebrated the production of its 500th nacelle, which was part of the 8.4MW turbines for Ørsted's Hornsea 2 offshore wind farm.
- **January 2022**: The first serial production nacelle for the SG 11.0-200 DD turbine was completed, marking the start of production for the Hollandse Kust Zuid offshore wind farm.
- **February 2024**: The facility produced the first nacelles for the 14MW turbines for the Moray West offshore wind project in Scotland.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2016**: Construction of the Cuxhaven nacelle factory began with a total investment of around EUR 200 million.
- **2021**: The facility underwent upgrades to accommodate the production of the new generation of turbines, specifically the SG 11.0-200 DD.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2018**: The first nacelle was manufactured at the Cuxhaven plant.
- **August 2021**: Serial production of the SG 11.0-200 DD wind turbines for the Hollandse Kust Zuid offshore wind farm commenced.
- **April 2024**: The first nacelles for the Moray West offshore wind farm were loaded for shipment to Scotland.

#### 4. Labour
- **2020**: The Cuxhaven facility employed nearly 600 workers, contributing to local job creation in the renewable energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Siemens Gamesa owns the Cuxhaven facility exclusively, as indicated by the consistent references to the facility as part of Siemens Gamesa's operations without mention of joint ventures or partnerships in ownership.
