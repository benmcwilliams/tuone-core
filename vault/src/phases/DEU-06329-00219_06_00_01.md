### Evolutionary Timeline of the EnBW Facility in Werneuchen, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **06-02-2019**: EnBW announces the development of the Weesow-Willmersdorf solar park, which will have a capacity of 175 MW and cover 164 hectares, generating approximately 175 GWh of electricity annually.
- **16-10-2019**: EnBW takes a final investment decision to build the Weesow-Willmersdorf solar farm, now confirmed to have a capacity of 180 MW, comprising around 465,000 solar modules. The project is expected to be fully commissioned by the end of 2020 and will generate around 180 million kWh of electricity annually.
- **17-03-2020**: The solar park is confirmed to have a capacity of 187 MW, with plans to operate for 40 years. The installation will include high-performance solar modules mounted at a 20-degree angle.
- **25-11-2020**: The solar park begins feeding power into the grid, although construction is still ongoing. The project is noted as Germany's largest solar park without public subsidy.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **16-10-2019**: EnBW confirms the investment in the Weesow-Willmersdorf solar park, which is a €100 million subsidy-free project. This marks a significant step in EnBW's strategy to expand its renewable energy portfolio.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **06-02-2019**: Initial announcement of the Weesow-Willmersdorf project.
- **16-10-2019**: Final investment decision taken; initial cable laying work is scheduled to begin in early 2020.
- **17-03-2020**: Confirmation of the project details and commencement of construction.
- **25-11-2020**: The solar park begins delivering power to the grid, with construction still in progress.

#### 4. Labour
- **17-03-2020**: More than 40 companies are commissioned to work on the construction of the solar park, indicating job creation associated with the project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **16-10-2019**: EnBW acquired the Weesow-Willmersdorf project from Procon Solar at a pre-developed stage in mid-2018. EnBW is the sole owner of the facility, as it is developing the project independently without any subsidies.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones related to the EnBW facility in Werneuchen, Germany, focusing on its technology, investment, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
