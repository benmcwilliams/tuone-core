### Evolutionary Timeline of RWE Facility in Neurath, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2022**: RWE flagged the Neurath facility's 300 MW lignite power plant, which was decommissioned in 2021, and an 800 MW hard coal plant, decommissioned in 2020. The facility was under review for potential temporary use to ensure energy security amid rising energy market concerns due to the Ukraine conflict.
- **December 2023**: RWE announced the installation of the first battery storage system at the Neurath power plant, comprising 690 battery blocks with a total storage capacity of 235 MWh and an output of 220 MW. The first 250 battery blocks, with a capacity of 80 MW/84 MWh, were installed, with plans for additional blocks in the second half of 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2022**: RWE committed to significant investments in the energy transition, including the expansion of renewable energy sources. The company agreed to acquire Con Edison Clean Energy Businesses for $6.8 billion, effectively doubling its renewable project portfolio in the US.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2022**: RWE reached an agreement with the German government to phase out lignite-based electricity generation by 2030, eight years ahead of the original plan. The Neurath D and E power plant units, with a combined capacity of 1.2 GW, were allowed to continue operations until March 31, 2024, due to the existing gas supply situation.
- **December 2023**: The installation of the first battery storage system at Neurath was announced, marking a significant milestone in the facility's transition towards more sustainable energy solutions.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2022**: RWE's CEO emphasized that the acceleration of the coal phase-out must not come at the expense of employees, indicating a commitment to job security amid the transition to renewable energy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify any joint ventures or partnerships regarding the ownership of the Neurath facility, implying that RWE owns the facility exclusively.
