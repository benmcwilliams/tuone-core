## DEU-06713-07317

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Encory GmbH Facility in Kirchroth, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** November 2024
   - **Details:** Encory GmbH, operating in a facility in Kirchroth, will implement a direct recycling process for battery cells. This facility will mechanically dismantle battery cells and production residuals into valuable components, focusing on extracting lithium, cobalt, graphite, manganese, nickel, and copper. The facility spans an area of 2,200 m² and is integrated into the expansion of an existing building in the Kirchroth-Nord industrial park.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** November 2024
   - **Details:** BMW Group is investing approximately 10 million euros for the construction of the battery cell recycling centre.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** November 2024
   - **Details:** Announcement of the construction of the battery cell recycling centre in Kirchroth. Installation work is scheduled to begin in the second half of 2025. The facility will not be operated by BMW but by Encory GmbH, a joint venture.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** November 2024
   - **Details:** Once the facility is completed, it is expected to create around 20 jobs.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** November 2024
   - **Details:** The facility is operated by Encory GmbH, which is a joint venture between BMW Group and Interzero Group, with both partners holding a 50% stake in the company.

**1. Technology and Operations:**
- "The company will employ a process called ‘direct recycling’, which enables residual materials from battery cell production, as well as whole battery cells, to be mechanically dismantled into their valuable components."
- "Unlike conventional methods, the main characteristic of direct recycling is that raw materials from battery cells are not reverted to their original state, but are instead fed back 'directly' into the cell production cycle."

**2. Investment and Development:**
- "For the construction of the new recycling facility, BMW is investing around 10 million euros."

**3. Project Status and Milestones:**
- "Installation work at the building is scheduled to begin in the second half of 2025."
- "Once construction is completed, BMW writes that validation of the recycling method in near-series processes will get underway."

**4. Labour:**
- "Once completed, about 20 people will be employed in the new Competence Center."

**5. Ownership structure:**
- "The centre will not actually be operated by BMW, however, despite owning the relevant intellectual properties, but by Encory GmbH."
- "It is a joint venture of the BMW Group and Interzero Group, which develops and implements logistics and consulting solutions in areas such as collection, recycling, and remanufacturing of vehicle components."
- "Both partners hold a 50 per cent stake in the company."
