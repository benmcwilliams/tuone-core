### Evolutionary Timeline of the RWE Facility in Borssele, Netherlands

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Borssele nuclear power station, operational since 1973, is the only nuclear power facility in the Netherlands. It has a production capacity of 485 MW and utilizes a pressurized water reactor. As of 2022, it provided approximately 3.3% of the country's electricity.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The Dutch government has earmarked €14 billion ($15.2 billion) for a nuclear power construction program, which includes funding for the Borssele facility. Currently, about €4.5 billion ($4.9 billion) is allocated for this program, which aims to study the feasibility and safety of extending Borssele's operational life beyond its scheduled license expiration in 2033.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - As of June 2024, the Dutch government is considering extending the operation of the Borssele nuclear power plant beyond 2033. A tender is planned to determine the technology for at least the first two new nuclear plants, indicating ongoing developments in the nuclear sector.

4. **Labour:**

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The Borssele nuclear power plant is 30% owned by the German utility RWE, while the remaining 70% is owned by the Zeeuwse Energie Houdstermaatschappij, which is a public limited company comprising groups including the province of Zeeland and various municipalities in the province.
