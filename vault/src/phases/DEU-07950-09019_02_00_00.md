### Evolutionary Timeline of the German Research Centre for Geosciences (GFZ) Facility in Potsdam, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2022**: The GFZ facility is involved in the development and application of deep geothermal energy technologies. The facility aims to contribute significantly to Germany's heating sector by utilizing hydrothermal reservoirs, which can provide stable and weather-independent energy supply. The roadmap indicates that geothermal energy could potentially cover over a quarter of Germany's annual heat consumption, equating to more than 300 TWh, with an installed capacity of approximately 70 gigawatts.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2022**: The roadmap developed by GFZ, in collaboration with the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT) and the Helmholtz Centre for Environmental Research (UFZ), emphasizes the need for investments in key technologies such as drilling methods, reservoir management, and large-scale heat storage systems to expand the use of geothermal energy in Germany.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2022**: The announcement of the roadmap for the expanded use of geothermal energy in Germany highlights the establishment of the underground research laboratory GeoLaB, a joint initiative involving GFZ, KIT, and UFZ. This facility is intended to enhance the understanding of geothermal systems and support the safe and sustainable use of geothermal energy.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2022**: The roadmap indicates that the growing geothermal energy industry could create approximately five to ten full-time equivalent jobs per megawatt of installed power. This suggests a significant potential for job creation in technology development, planning, production, and plant construction and operation as the geothermal sector expands.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2022**: The GFZ facility is part of a collaborative initiative with KIT and UFZ, indicating a joint venture structure for the GeoLaB project. However, specific details regarding exclusive ownership of the GFZ facility itself are not provided in the article.
