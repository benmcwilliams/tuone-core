## DEU-08050-01842

### Evolutionary Timeline of Ørsted's Borkum Riffgrund 3 Facility, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04/2020**: Ørsted announces Siemens Gamesa as the preferred turbine supplier for Borkum Riffgrund 3, which will utilize the new SG 11.0-200 DD offshore wind turbine with a capacity of 11 MW and a rotor diameter of 200 meters. The project is expected to have a total capacity of 900 MW.
- **06/2021**: Ørsted awards contracts to CRP Subsea for cable protection systems for Borkum Riffgrund 3, indicating the project's progress towards installation.
- **06/2022**: Siemens Gamesa confirms a firm order for 83 SG 11.0-200 DD turbines for Borkum Riffgrund 3, totaling 913 MW. Installation is scheduled for 2024, with commissioning expected in 2025.
- **12/2023**: Jan De Nul Group begins the installation of monopile foundations for Borkum Riffgrund 3, marking the start of physical construction activities.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12/2023**: Ørsted secures a €400 million loan from the European Investment Bank (EIB) to finance the Borkum Riffgrund 3 project as part of a larger €1.2 billion facility aimed at supporting renewable energy projects in the North Sea.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04/2020**: Ørsted announces plans for Borkum Riffgrund 3, subject to final investment decisions and regulatory approvals.
- **06/2021**: Project engineering work for cable protection systems begins, with installation activities scheduled for 2024.
- **06/2022**: Siemens Gamesa confirms the turbine order, solidifying the project's timeline for installation in 2024 and commissioning in 2025.
- **12/2023**: The installation of monopile foundations begins, indicating active construction on the project.

#### 4. Labour
- **06/2022**: The project is expected to create jobs related to the installation and maintenance of the wind turbines, although specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **06/2022**: Borkum Riffgrund 3 is co-owned by Ørsted (50%) and Glennmont Partners (50%). Ørsted will manage the construction and operations under a full-scope EPC contract and will provide maintenance services for 20 years.
