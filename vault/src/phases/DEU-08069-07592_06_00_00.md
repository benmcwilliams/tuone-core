### Evolutionary Timeline of the BP Solar Facility in Köthen, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **August 2009:** Construction began on a 45MWp solar power plant at a former military airfield in Köthen, Saxony-Anhalt. The facility will utilize 205,000 crystalline-silicon-based solar modules supplied by BP Solar, rated at 220W each. The project is designed to be the largest solar power plant in Saxony-Anhalt and the world's largest outfitted with string inverters. The expected production capacity is approximately 42.5 MW-hours of electricity, sufficient to supply around 9,400 households.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **August 2009:** A total investment of €133 million was announced for the project. This investment is tied to the construction and operation of the solar power plant.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **August 19, 2009:** Ground-breaking ceremony held, marking the official start of construction. The project is scheduled for completion and grid-activation by the end of 2009.

4. **Labour:**
   - The articles do not provide specific details regarding job creation or job loss associated with the construction or operation of the facility.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is developed by RGE Energy, which is responsible for planning, financing, construction, management, and maintenance. BP Solar is a supplier of the solar modules but does not appear to have exclusive ownership of the facility, indicating a collaborative relationship rather than full ownership.
