### Evolutionary Timeline of the Juwi Group Facility in Cottbus, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2019**: Juwi Group announced plans to build a 10MW solar farm near Cottbus, Germany. The facility will generate approximately 10 million kilowatt-hours of electricity annually and will be connected to the grid at the Bahnsdorf substation. The solar farm will consist of 27,000 solar modules supplied by Astronergy and two central inverters from SMA.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2019**: The project is being developed for Lausitz Energie Bergbau and Lausitz Energie Kraftwerke (LEAG), indicating a partnership in the investment and development of renewable energy in the lignite mining region.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2019**: Construction of the solar farm is set to begin in about seven weeks, with commissioning scheduled for the end of October 2019. This marks the initiation of the project as LEAG's first solar project in the region.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the construction or operation of the facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is developed for Lausitz Energie Bergbau and Lausitz Energie Kraftwerke, suggesting a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by Juwi Group. Juwi will handle the technical management of the plant through Juwi Operations & Maintenance.
