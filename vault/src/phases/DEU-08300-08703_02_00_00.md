## DEU-08300-08703

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Eavor Facility in Geretsried, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **08/2022**: Eavor announces the development of its first commercial Eavor-Loop project in Geretsried, which will utilize a closed-loop geothermal system to harness heat for electricity generation. The facility is designed to produce 8.2 MWe, capable of powering approximately 4,900 homes. The project aims to avoid 44,000 tonnes of CO2 emissions annually. Full operational capacity is expected by 2026.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **08/2022**: Eavor partners with Turboden to implement the Organic Rankine Cycle (ORC) system for the Geretsried project, indicating a significant investment in advanced geothermal technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **07/2023**: Drilling officially begins for the Geretsried project, with plans to reach a vertical depth of 4,500 meters before deflecting horizontally. The project is progressing with the construction of air-cooled condenser (ACC) units for the future ORC power plant.
- **12/2024**: Eavor reports drilling has reached a measured depth of 7,000 meters, with two drilling rigs operating in parallel. The project continues to advance towards its operational goals.

#### 4. Labour
- **12/2024**: The involvement of Odfjell Technology for tubular running services indicates job creation in the region, as they successfully ran 20,000 meters of casing without incidents, contributing to the project's progress.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **08/2022**: Eavor is identified as the Canadian developer behind the Geretsried project, with no indication of joint ownership or partnerships affecting the ownership structure. The facility appears to be under Eavor's exclusive control, with Turboden as a technology partner.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones of the Eavor facility in Geretsried, Germany, highlighting its technological advancements, project status, and operational goals.
