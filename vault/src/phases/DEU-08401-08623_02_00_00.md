### Evolutionary Timeline of the Deutsche Erdwärme Facility in Graben-Neudorf, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2023**: The facility is focused on geothermal energy production. The first test borehole was drilled to a depth of approximately 3900 meters, targeting a reservoir at the Buntsandstein formation, which has high natural permeability and temperatures above 160 degrees Celsius. The project aims for a target thermal capacity of 40 MW.
- **August 2023**: Thermal water was pumped for the first time during the "discovery test," marking a significant milestone. The test aims to assess the temperature and flow rate of the geothermal well to support the target capacity. The water vapor column indicated successful testing.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2024**: The project has encountered unexpectedly high temperatures (over 200 °C) at Graben-Neudorf, which has delayed the target commissioning date as the company seeks high-temperature solutions. Funding for further development is being applied for, indicating ongoing investment efforts.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Mid-2022**: Drilling operations commenced at the Graben-Neudorf site.
- **June 2023**: The discovery test phase began, with technical inspections of the first test borehole.
- **August 2023**: The successful pumping of thermal water marked a major milestone in the project.
- **May 2024**: The project remains under development, with plans for further drilling in Dettenheim, indicating ongoing progress.
