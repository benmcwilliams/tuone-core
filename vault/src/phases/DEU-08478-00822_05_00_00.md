### Evolutionary Timeline of the Vattenfall Facility in Krümmel, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **1983**: The Krümmel Nuclear Power Plant (NPP), featuring a single 1260 MWe boiling water reactor, was connected to the grid.
- **2007**: The facility suffered a transformer fire, leading to a period of downtime.
- **June 2009**: After repairs, the plant was restarted but was taken offline again the following month due to another transformer fault.
- **2011**: Following the Fukushima accident, the German government withdrew operating licenses from Krümmel and seven other older reactors as part of the nuclear phase-out policy.
- **2015**: Vattenfall submitted an application to decommission and dismantle the plant.
- **2017**: The last fuel element was removed from the facility.
- **2019**: The last individual fuel rods were removed, and approximately 99% of the radioactive inventory was cleared from the plant.
- **2024**: The decommissioning and dismantling process officially began, with a focus on the reactor pressure vessel internals, which constitute the majority of the remaining radioactive inventory.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2024**: The total estimated cost for the dismantling of the Krümmel facility is around €1 billion (USD 1.1 billion). 

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **22-08-2024**: The nuclear supervisory authority issued a permit for decommissioning and dismantling, marking a significant milestone towards transforming the site into a 'greenfield'.
- **24-08-2024**: Official announcement of the commencement of decommissioning work, with expectations for the dismantling process to take approximately 15 years, targeting completion of the reactor pressure vessel internals by 2027.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify job creation or loss directly related to the decommissioning process. However, it is implied that the dismantling work will involve a consortium of specialist companies, which may lead to job opportunities in that sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Krümmel NPP is jointly owned by Vattenfall and E.ON, with Vattenfall operating the facility.
