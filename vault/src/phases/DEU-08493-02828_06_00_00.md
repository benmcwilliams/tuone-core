### Evolutionary Timeline of the ET Solar Facility in Paehl, Germany

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 08-01-2010
   - **Details:** ET Solar completed a 1.48MW ground-mounted photovoltaic (PV) power plant in Paehl, approximately 50km south of Munich. The facility is expected to generate approximately 32 million kWh of electricity over a 20-year subsidized period. The plant's designed efficiency is reported to be almost 82%. The modules for the project were manufactured at ET Solar's facility in China, and the project was fully designed by ET Solar’s German engineering team.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 08-01-2010
   - **Details:** The project was financed by Wattner, a Cologne-based investment trust specializing in renewable energy investments, which acted as the sole equity investor through its publicly raised SunAsset fund. Additionally, a syndicate of two regional German savings banks provided project finance for the facility.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** November 2009
   - **Details:** Main construction work on the plant began in November 2009.
   - **Date:** 08-01-2010
   - **Details:** The project was completed and connected to the grid at E.ON, the largest grid operator in Germany.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 08-01-2010
   - **Details:** The article does not specify job creation or job loss directly related to the project, but it mentions the collaboration between ET Solar and Wattner, indicating potential future job opportunities stemming from their partnership.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 08-01-2010
   - **Details:** The facility is not exclusively owned by ET Solar; it is a joint venture with Wattner, which is the sole equity investor in the project.
