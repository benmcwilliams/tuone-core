### Evolutionary Timeline of the EVSE Facility in Schwerin, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **January 2020:** The EVSE facility is developing a geothermal district heating system designed to cover approximately 10% of Schwerin’s heating needs. The geothermal unit will utilize two wells: one for extracting geothermal brine from a depth of about 1,250 meters and another for reinjecting the cooled brine back into the ground. The system will enhance the existing district heating infrastructure by integrating renewable geothermal energy, which will significantly increase the share of renewables in the energy mix.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **January 2020:** The total investment for the geothermal project is EUR 15.9 million, with EUR 9.2 million funded by the EU’s European Regional Development Fund as part of the “Mecklenburg-Vorpommern” Operational Programme for the 2014-2020 period. This investment aims to support the reduction of CO2 emissions in the region.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **2018:** Drilling for the geothermal project began, with expectations for the facility to become operational in 2020. 
   - **June 2018:** Updates indicated that construction of the production well was anticipated to start in the third quarter of 2018, with the injection well drilling scheduled for 2019.
