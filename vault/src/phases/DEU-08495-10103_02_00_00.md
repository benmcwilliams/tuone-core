### Evolutionary Timeline of the Stadtwerke Schwerin Facility in Schwerin, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: The Stadtwerke Schwerin geothermal heating plant was reported to be completed, with hot water being pumped from a depth of almost 1300 meters at a temperature of 56 degrees Celsius. The facility utilizes four large heat pumps to increase the water temperature for supply to the district heating network. The plant is designed to provide heating for approximately 2,000 households, which accounts for about 15% of the district heating customers in Schwerin.
- **March 2024**: The facility's operational start was delayed to June 2024 due to high sediment content in the thermal fluids, which required the design and integration of a filter system to protect the heat exchangers. The installation of the filters was completed, and the next step involves gradually putting the heat pumps into operation.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: Stadtwerke Schwerin invested EUR 20.5 million in the geothermal project, with EUR 6.8 million subsidized by the government. This investment supports the development of geothermal systems and the application for a research and development project at the Federal Ministry of Economics.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: An inauguration ceremony was held, attended by Chancellor Olaf Scholz and Minister-President Manuela Schwesig, marking the completion of the geothermal heating plant.
- **June 2024**: The facility is targeted to begin operations, following delays due to technical challenges related to sediment in the thermal fluids.
