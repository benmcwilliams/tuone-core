### Evolutionary Timeline of the EWE Facility in Jemgum, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2024**: EWE has successfully completed the HyCAVmobil research project at its gas storage site in Rüdersdorf, demonstrating the safe storage of hydrogen in underground cavern facilities. The project involved a 500 cubic meter test cavern, with plans to scale up to larger caverns (1,000 times the size). EWE operates 37 salt caverns, holding over 15% of Germany's hydrogen-suitable cavern storage facilities.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2024**: The total investment for the HyCAVmobil project exceeded 14 million euros, with EWE contributing nearly 8 million euros. The remaining 6.5 million euros were received as a grant from the Federal Ministry for Digital and Transport under the National Innovation Programme for Hydrogen and Fuel Cell Technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Spring 2021**: Installation and cementing of 160 steel pipes to a depth of 1,000 meters in the salt dome at Rüdersdorf.
- **Autumn 2022**: Proof of leakage integrity of the cavern borehole down to 1,000 meters.
- **Autumn 2022 to Spring 2023**: Brine removal and creation of the cavity for hydrogen storage.
- **Spring to Autumn 2023**: Construction of the above-ground facility for hydrogen storage operation.
- **Autumn 2023**: First hydrogen storage achieved.
- **Winter 2023 to Autumn 2024**: Operational tests of the hydrogen cavern and storage technology.
