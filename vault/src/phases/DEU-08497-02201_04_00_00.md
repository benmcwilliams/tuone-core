## DEU-08497-02201

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EWE Facility in Nüttermoor, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2024**: EWE completed the HyCAVmobil research project at its gas storage site in Rüdersdorf, demonstrating the safe storage of hydrogen in underground cavern facilities. The project involved a 500 cubic meter test cavern, with plans to scale up to larger caverns (1,000 times the size). The facility aims to store green hydrogen produced from renewable energies, which is crucial for achieving climate targets.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2024**: The total investment for the HyCAVmobil project exceeded 14 million euros, with EWE contributing nearly 8 million euros. The remaining 6.5 million euros were received as a grant from the Federal Ministry for Digital and Transport under the National Innovation Programme for Hydrogen and Fuel Cell Technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2019**: The HyCAVmobil research project commenced.
- **Spring 2021**: Installation and cementing of 160 steel pipes to a depth of 1,000 meters in the salt dome below Rüdersdorf.
- **Autumn 2022**: Proof of leakage of the cavern borehole to a depth of 1,000 meters was achieved.
- **Autumn 2022 to Spring 2023**: Brine removal and creation of the cavity for hydrogen storage.
- **Spring to Autumn 2023**: Construction of the above-ground facility for the hydrogen storage operation.
- **Autumn 2023**: First hydrogen storage occurred.
- **Winter 2023 to Autumn 2024**: Operational tests of the hydrogen cavern and storage technology are planned.
