### Ecop Technologies Facility in Meldorf, DEU - Evolutionary Timeline

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2024**: Ecop Technologies launched a 700 kW industrial heat pump designed for the recovery and reuse of waste heat. The heat pump can achieve output temperatures of up to 200°C and is suitable for various applications, including process steam, washing, cooking, distilling, and pasteurizing. The system features an advanced rotor system utilizing diffusion bonding technology, which allows for higher temperature lifts and improved manufacturability. The heat pump has a coefficient of performance (COP) ranging from 4.0 to 7.0, depending on integration, and supports operational flexibility by adapting output temperature through varying rotational speeds.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2024**: Ecop Technologies was awarded €8.5 million ($9.2 million) in funding from the European Innovation Council (EIC) Accelerator to support the development of its heat pump technology. This funding is part of the company's efforts to innovate in high-temperature industrial processes and district heating.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2024**: Ecop Technologies announced the delivery of two heat pumps to Germany's first underground storage facility being constructed in Meldorf. The facility will utilize waste heat from a nearby heating factory to supply hot water and heating to the community, integrating the heat pumps into the district heating grid of Meldorf. Future plans include the addition of a solar thermal installation to further heat the storage.
