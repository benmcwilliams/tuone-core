### Evolutionary Timeline of the EnBW Facility in Neckarwestheim, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: The Neckarwestheim nuclear power plant, specifically Unit 2, was officially shut down on April 15, 2023. This unit was a 1400 MWe pressurized water reactor that began operations in 1989 and generated over 11 TWh of electricity in 2022. The dismantling program for this unit has been approved, marking the transition from operational status to decommissioning.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2016**: EnBW applied for a permit to decommission and dismantle Unit 2 of the Neckarwestheim plant, indicating a long-term investment in the decommissioning process.
- **April 2023**: The Baden-Württemberg Ministry of the Environment granted the necessary decommissioning permit, allowing EnBW to proceed with dismantling operations.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 15, 2023**: Unit 2 of the Neckarwestheim plant was disconnected from the electricity grid, marking the end of its operational phase. Preparatory work for dismantling was set to begin shortly thereafter.
- **April 2023**: EnBW announced plans to start dismantling Unit 2 following the issuance of the decommissioning license.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2023**: The shutdown of Unit 2 and the transition to dismantling operations will likely impact the approximately 650 employees remaining at the facility, although specific job losses or gains were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- EnBW operates the Neckarwestheim facility exclusively, as indicated by the articles discussing the company's plans and actions regarding the decommissioning and dismantling of the nuclear power plant.
