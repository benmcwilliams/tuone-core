### Evolutionary Timeline of EnBW Kernkraft GmbH Facility in Neckarwestheim, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: EnBW Kernkraft GmbH received approval to decommission and dismantle unit 2 of its Neckarwestheim Nuclear Power Plant (NPP). The facility, which operated a 1400 MWe pressurized water reactor, began operations in 1989 and generated over 11 TWh of electricity in 2022. The decommissioning process is part of Germany's nuclear phase-out following the Fukushima disaster in 2011.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2016**: EnBW submitted the application for the decommissioning permit for Neckarwestheim unit 2, marking the beginning of the formal process for dismantling the facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: The Neckarwestheim unit 2 was officially approved for decommissioning, with the shutdown scheduled for later in the month. This approval signifies the completion of the necessary permits for all of EnBW's nuclear power plants in Germany, making it the first operator to achieve this status.
