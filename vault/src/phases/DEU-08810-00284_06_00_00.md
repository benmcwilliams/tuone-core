### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fraunhofer Facility in Ahrweiler, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **03-08-2020:** The Fraunhofer Institute for Solar Energy Systems ISE (Fraunhofer ISE) is involved in a research initiative focused on agrophotovoltaics (APV) at a German apple orchard in Ahrweiler. The project aims to explore the dual use of land for solar energy production and agriculture, specifically studying the growth of apple trees under photovoltaic (PV) systems. The research will assess how PV modules can protect fruit trees from extreme weather conditions, such as hail and excessive sunlight, which are exacerbated by climate change.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **03-08-2020:** The project received €740,000 (approximately US$866,429) in funding from the Rhineland-Palatinate Ministry of the Environment. This funding is part of a four-year initiative aimed at addressing land use conflicts between solar farms and agricultural land.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **03-08-2020:** The project is officially announced, with a focus on researching the viability of dual land use for solar energy and agriculture. The initiative builds on previous successful trials conducted by Fraunhofer ISE, including a pilot project in 2017 that utilized bifacial solar modules to optimize sunlight for crops.
