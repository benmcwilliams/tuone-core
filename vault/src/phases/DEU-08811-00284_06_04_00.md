### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fraunhofer Facility in Heggelbach, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2016**: The Fraunhofer ISE facility in Heggelbach established a unique 194 kWp agrophotovoltaics (APV) pilot plant. The facility aims to explore the dual use of land for electricity production using photovoltaic (PV) modules while growing crops (wheat, trefoil, potatoes, and celery) on the same land. The pilot project occupies two and a half hectares, with the APV system covering about one third of a hectare to facilitate direct crop yield comparisons.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2016**: The APV-Resola project is sponsored by the German Federal Ministry for Education and Research (BMBF) and FONA (Research for Sustainable Development). This funding supports the research into the commercial viability of agrophotovoltaics.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2016**: The project was announced as the largest research initiative in Germany focused on agrophotovoltaics. The pilot plant was built and operational, with monitoring planned over the harvesting seasons of 2017 and 2018. A final report analyzing the crop yields will be published after the two-year experiment.
