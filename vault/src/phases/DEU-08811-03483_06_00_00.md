### Evolutionary Timeline of the SolarWorld Facility in Heggelbach, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** September 2016
   - **Details:** A unique 194 kWp agrophotovoltaics (APV) pilot plant was established in Heggelbach, Germany. The facility utilizes SolarWorld’s bifacial modules, which are designed to produce a higher electricity yield per unit area and provide a more homogeneous light distribution for crops grown underneath the APV system. The pilot project occupies two and a half hectares, with the APV system covering about one third of a hectare. The project aims to monitor the yield of four different crops (wheat, trefoil, potatoes, and celery) over the harvesting seasons of 2017 and 2018.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** September 2016
   - **Details:** The APV-Resola project is sponsored by the German Federal Ministry for Education and Research (BMBF) and FONA (Research for Sustainable Development). This funding supports the research and development of the dual-use agricultural and photovoltaic system.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** September 2016
   - **Details:** The APV pilot plant was officially built and operationalized as part of the APV-Resola project. The project is a collaborative effort involving various partners from agricultural, scientific, and technical sectors, as well as local inhabitants. The results from the crop yields will be analyzed and published in a final report after the two-year experiment.
