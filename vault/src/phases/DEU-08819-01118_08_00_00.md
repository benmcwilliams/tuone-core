### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility at Baltic Eagle Offshore Wind Farm, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **06/2021**: The Baltic Eagle offshore wind farm is announced, with a planned capacity of 476 MW, comprising 50 Vestas wind turbines, each with a capacity of 9.525 MW. The project is located northeast of Rügen island in the Baltic Sea and is expected to be commissioned by 2024.
- **02/2023**: The offshore substation topside (OSS) is successfully installed, marking a significant milestone. The OSS will collect electricity from the wind turbines and transform the supply voltage for the onshore grid. The substation is designed to export approximately 1.9 TWh of electricity annually, enough to power over half a million homes and reduce CO2 emissions by 800,000 tonnes per year.
- **05/2024**: The first wind turbines are installed, marking the beginning of the turbine installation phase, which is expected to continue until September 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05/2021**: Iberdrola and 50Hertz sign an agreement to cooperate on the construction, installation, and commissioning of the OSS for the Baltic Eagle offshore wind farm, indicating a collaborative investment in the project.
- **02/2023**: The OSS installation involves significant engineering efforts, with various contractors involved, including Heerema Marine Contractors and Siemens Gas and Power, showcasing a substantial investment in infrastructure.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **06/2021**: Announcement of the Baltic Eagle offshore wind farm project.
- **10/2022**: The jacket foundation for the OSS is placed on site.
- **02/2023**: Successful installation of the OSS, a key milestone in the project.
- **05/2024**: Installation of the first wind turbines begins.

#### 4. Labour
- **02/2023**: The project is expected to create high-quality jobs across the renewable energy value chain, contributing to the local economy and supporting the reindustrialization of shipyard facilities in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **05/2024**: The Baltic Eagle offshore wind farm is a joint venture between Iberdrola and Abu Dhabi-based Masdar, with Iberdrola leading the project construction and operation.
