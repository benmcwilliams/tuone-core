### Evolutionary Timeline of HeliService Facility at Baltic Eagle Offshore Wind Farm, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2024**: HeliService, in collaboration with Vestas, initiated a pilot project to transport technicians and crew to the Baltic Eagle offshore wind farm using Leonardo helicopters powered by approximately 40% Sustainable Aviation Fuel (SAF). This marks the first use of SAF-fuelled helicopters at such a high blend rate during the construction phase of an offshore wind farm.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2024**: The pilot project is part of Vestas' sustainability strategy, which aims to reduce CO2 emissions in offshore wind operations. The SAF for the project is provided by DCC & Shell Aviation Denmark, indicating a commitment to sustainable fuel sources.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2024**: The Baltic Eagle offshore wind farm, which is currently under construction, saw its first wind turbines installed at the beginning of May. The project is scheduled to be commissioned by the end of 2024 and will consist of 50 Vestas V174-9.5 MW turbines.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2024**: The use of SAF for crew transport is expected to contribute to CO2 savings of approximately 32% per flight compared to conventional jet fuel, reflecting a commitment to sustainable operations, although specific job creation or loss figures were not mentioned in the article.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2024**: The Baltic Eagle offshore wind farm is a joint venture between Abu Dhabi-based Masdar and Iberdrola, with Iberdrola leading the project construction and operation. 

This timeline captures the key developments and operational aspects of the HeliService facility at the Baltic Eagle offshore wind farm as of May 2024.
