### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hanwha Group Facility in Amsdorf, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** April 20, 2012
     - The GERO Solarpark, a 28MW solar power plant, was inaugurated in Amsdorf, Germany. The facility is designed to cover the annual energy requirements of approximately 8,000 households and is capable of saving around 15,000 tonnes of carbon emissions annually. The total electrical generating capacity at Amsdorf is reported to be around 120MW, with 75MW sourced from renewable energy.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 2012
     - The project involved an investment of nearly €50 million, primarily financed by GETEC green energy AG. This investment was crucial for the successful commissioning of the solar power plant.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 10-05-2012
     - The GERO Solarpark was officially inaugurated, marking the completion of the project which was realized in a record time of seven weeks from the start of construction. The project was developed as a joint venture between GETEC green energy AG and the ROMONTA group, with Q-Cells acting as the system integrator.
