### Evolutionary Timeline of the GETEC Green Energy AG Facility in Amsdorf, Germany

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** April 20, 2012
     - The GERO Solarpark, a 28MW solar power plant, was inaugurated in Amsdorf, Germany. The facility is capable of covering the annual energy requirements of approximately 8,000 households and is designed to save around 15,000 tonnes of carbon emissions annually. The total electrical generating capacity at Amsdorf is reported to be around 120MW, with 75MW originating from renewable sources.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 2012
     - The project involved a total investment of nearly €50 million, which was primarily secured by GETEC green energy AG. This investment was crucial for the successful commissioning of the GERO Solarpark.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 10-05-2012
     - The GERO Solarpark was officially inaugurated, marking the completion of the project which took less than seven weeks from the start of construction. The project was a joint venture between GETEC green energy AG and the ROMONTA group, with Q-Cells acting as the system integrator.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The GERO Solarpark is a joint venture between GETEC green energy AG and the ROMONTA group. Therefore, GETEC does not own the facility exclusively but shares ownership with ROMONTA.
