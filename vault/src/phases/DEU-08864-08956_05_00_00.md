### Evolutionary Timeline of GNS Gesellschaft für Nuklear-Service mbH Facility in Grohnde, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2023**: The facility, which previously operated a 1360 MWe pressurized water reactor, is now in the decommissioning phase. Preparatory work for dismantling has begun, including decontamination of the primary cooling circuit and removal of non-required systems. The dismantling process is expected to involve the recycling of approximately 15,000 tonnes of material and the treatment of low- and intermediate-level radioactive waste.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2023**: GNS Gesellschaft für Nuklear-Service mbH announced that it will deliver the last of its CASTOR used fuel storage casks to Grohnde in 2025. This order is part of a larger plan where over 1400 CASTOR casks have already been loaded with used fuel and vitrified reprocessing waste from German nuclear power plants.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2017**: PreussenElektra applied for approval to decommission and dismantle the Grohnde facility.
- **December 31, 2021**: The Grohnde reactor was officially shut down.
- **December 2023**: The Ministry of the Environment approved the procedural steps for decommissioning, allowing the facility to begin dismantling operations. The completion of the nuclear dismantling is scheduled for 2037.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2023**: The article does not specify job creation or loss directly related to the decommissioning process, but it implies adjustments in personnel organization as part of the preparatory work for dismantling.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2023**: The facility is owned by PreussenElektra, a subsidiary of EOn Group, which is responsible for the decommissioning of Grohnde and other nuclear power plants in Germany. GNS Gesellschaft für Nuklear-Service mbH is involved in providing CASTOR casks for the storage of used fuel but does not own the facility.
