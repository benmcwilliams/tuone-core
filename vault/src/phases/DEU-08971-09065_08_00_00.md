## DEU-08971-09065

### Evolutionary Timeline of Greenpeace Energy Facility in Rheinische Revier, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** November 2018
   - **Details:** Greenpeace Energy plans to replace RWE’s coal power plants in the Rheinische Revier with 8.2 GW of renewable energy systems, specifically 3.8 GW of wind power and 4.4 GW of solar power. The new facilities are expected to be built on former open-cast mining areas and are projected to deliver over 15 TWh of renewable electricity annually by 2030.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** November 2018
   - **Details:** The total investment for the renewable energy project is estimated at approximately €7 billion. This investment will be made without any public support and is designed to be financially fair for all stakeholders involved.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** November 2018
   - **Details:** Greenpeace Energy intends to gradually take over RWE’s lignite mines and coal power plants starting in 2020, with plans to shut them down by 2025. The project includes the closure of the Hambach mine and six oldest power plant blocks in 2020, the Inden mine and six further blocks by 2022, and the Garzweiler mine and the last three blocks by 2025.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** November 2018
   - **Details:** The transition plan includes the transferral of RWE’s jobs in the coal business to the new renewable energy operations. An employment company will gradually take over all employees from RWE, providing opportunities for them to work in land restoration, power plant decommissioning, or receive further qualifications for jobs in renewable energy and other industries.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** November 2018
   - **Details:** Greenpeace Energy is pursuing a model that includes citizen participation and has set up several new companies to implement the project. The ownership structure appears to involve collaboration with public and private entities, but specific details on exclusive ownership or joint ventures are not explicitly stated in the article.

### Technology and Operations
- "Greenpeace Energy plans to gradually take over the lignite mines and coal power plants of German utility RWE in the Rheinische Revier region of Western Germany from 2020, and shut them down by 2025."
- "New PV and wind power plants with a total capacity of around 8.2 GW... are to be built on all suitable former open-cast mining areas."
- "Wind power and PV systems with a capacity of 3.8 GW and 4.4 GW, respectively, are to be built."
- "In 2030, they are expected to deliver more than 15 TWh of renewable electricity – around a quarter of what Rhenish lignite currently supplies."

### Investment and Development
- "Approximately €7 billion would be invested in the new facilities, which would be built without any public support."
- "The construction of the entire renewable power plant park would cost around €7 billion."

### Project Status and Milestones
- "The plan is to be implemented through citizen participation, and would include investments from public and private companies."
- "The operation is valued at €384 million... The price is based on assumptions of the profit that could be made with these power plants selling power on the electricity market until they become unprofitable due to rising CO2 prices."
- "The closing of the Hambach mine and the six oldest and least efficient power plant blocks in 2020, the Inden mine and six further power plant blocks by 2022, and the Garzweiler mine and the last three blocks by 2025."
