### Evolutionary Timeline of the Technical University of Munich Facility in Loisach River, Germany

1. **Technology and Operations**
   - **Date**: Spring 2020
     - The facility, known as the world’s first ‘shaft hydropower plant’, began operations. It features a turbine concealed in a shaft in the river bed, allowing fish to pass freely during their migration. The plant produces enough electricity to supply approximately 800 households. The exact generating capacity has not been disclosed. The design aims to minimize environmental impact compared to conventional hydropower plants.

2. **Investment and Development**
   - **Date**: 2020
     - The development of the facility was part-financed by the German Federal Ministry for Economics and Energy and the Bavarian Environment Ministry. This funding supports the innovative design that balances hydropower generation with environmental preservation.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**
   - **Date**: 22-07-2020
     - The facility was officially reported as operational in a newspaper article, highlighting its unique design and environmental benefits. The project was developed by a team at the Technical University of Munich (TUM) and involved the construction of two shafts at an existing ramp on the Loisach River.
