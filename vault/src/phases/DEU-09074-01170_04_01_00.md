### Evolutionary Timeline of the Linde Facility in Leuna Chemical Complex, Germany

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** January 2021
   - **Details:** Linde announced the installation of a 24 megawatt (MW) electrolyser at the Leuna Chemical Complex. This facility will produce green hydrogen using a proton exchange membrane (PEM) electrolyser, which is set to be the world’s largest of its kind. The hydrogen produced will supply Linde’s industrial customers and will also be distributed as liquefied green hydrogen to refuelling stations and other industrial clients in the region. The facility is designed to enhance the capacity and efficiency of green hydrogen production.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** January 2021
   - **Details:** The electrolyser will be constructed by ITM Linde Electrolysis GmbH, a joint venture between Linde and ITM Power. This project represents a significant investment in clean hydrogen technology, although specific financial figures related to the investment were not disclosed in the article.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** January 2021
   - **Details:** The project is set to start production in the second half of 2022. This marks a significant milestone as it will be the first sale through the joint venture with ITM Power, highlighting the commitment to scaling up electrolyser capacity.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** January 2021
   - **Details:** The article does not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with this project.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** January 2021
   - **Details:** The facility will be built, owned, and operated by Linde. However, it is important to note that the electrolyser is part of a joint venture with ITM Power, indicating a collaborative ownership structure for the technology involved.
