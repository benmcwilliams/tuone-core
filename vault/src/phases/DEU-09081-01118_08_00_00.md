### Evolutionary Timeline of Iberdrola's Baltic Eagle Facility, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2022**: Iberdrola received approval from the German Federal Maritime and Hydrographic Agency (BSH) to develop the Baltic Eagle offshore wind farm, which will have a capacity of 476 MW. The facility is part of a larger 1.1 GW offshore wind complex located 30 km northeast of the island of Rügen. Once operational, it is expected to power 475,000 homes and displace over 800,000 tons of CO2 emissions annually.
- **September 2023**: Iberdrola announced that the installation of 50 monopiles for the Baltic Eagle project was completed, marking a significant milestone in the construction process. The project is progressing with the installation of transition pieces and the laying of approximately 70 km of inter-array cables.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2022**: Iberdrola announced a €3.5 billion investment for the Baltic Eagle offshore wind farm as part of its broader strategy to expand its offshore wind capacity, which is projected to reach 4,800 MW by 2026.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2022**: Approval received for the Baltic Eagle offshore wind farm.
- **September 2023**: Completion of monopile installation and commencement of cable installation for the Baltic Eagle project. The project is on track for commissioning.
