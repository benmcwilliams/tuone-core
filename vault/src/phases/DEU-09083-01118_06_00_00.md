### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Boldekow, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05/2023**: Iberdrola announced the construction of its first photovoltaic system in Germany, located in Boldekow, Mecklenburg-Western Pomerania. The facility will have a total output of approximately **56MWp** and will consist of **80,000 bifacial solar modules**. It is expected to generate **50GWh** of green electricity annually and will avoid emitting **20,000 tonnes of CO2** per year once operational, which is projected for **late 2024**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07/2023**: Iberdrola signed a **€1 billion** financing deal with the European Investment Bank (EIB) to support the construction of solar PV projects, including the Boldekow facility. This investment is part of a broader strategy to enhance renewable energy capacity across Spain, Portugal, and Germany.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05/2023**: Announcement of the Boldekow project as Iberdrola's first solar facility in Germany.
- **07/2023**: Confirmation that the Boldekow plant will be fully operational by **2024**. The facility will supply **100% of its energy** to Vodafone Germany under a power purchase agreement (PPA).

#### 4. Labour
- **05/2023**: During the construction and operational phases, the Boldekow facility is expected to create local jobs and contribute over **€5 million** in property taxes over a 30-year period.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **05/2023**: The Boldekow facility is owned exclusively by Iberdrola, as it is described as Iberdrola's first solar project in Germany, indicating full ownership without joint ventures or partnerships in the ownership structure.
