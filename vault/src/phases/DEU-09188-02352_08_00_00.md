### Evolutionary Timeline of the MHI Vestas Facility in Deutsche Bucht, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Mid-2018**: Construction of the Deutsche Bucht offshore wind farm began. The facility is designed to produce 269 MW using 33 V164-8.4 MW turbines from MHI Vestas.
- **08-01-2019**: Installation of 31 monopile foundations was completed, marking the first phase of construction. The next phase involved inter-array medium-voltage cabling.
- **05-06-2019**: The first turbine was successfully installed at the facility, with the project expected to come online by the end of 2019. The turbines are installed on 31 monopiles and two mono bucket foundations, the latter being part of a pilot project.
- **27-11-2019**: Installation of the first mono bucket foundation was paused due to a technical issue, but this did not affect the installation of the 31 monopile foundations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2018**: The Deutsche Bucht project was included in a tender by the German Federal Network Agency (BNetzA), which allocated an additional 16.8 MW for two pilot wind turbines mounted on mono-bucket foundations. Approval for the pilot was granted by the Federal Maritime and Hydrographic Agency of Germany (BSH) on 08-05-2018.

- **27-11-2019**: Work on the mono bucket foundations was paused due to a technical issue, but the main project was unaffected.
