## DEU-09188-08151

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Van Oord Facility in Deutsche Bucht, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2018**: Van Oord contracted Universal Foundation to support the installation of two mono bucket foundations at the Deutsche Bucht offshore wind farm. The facility will feature a total of 33 MHI Vestas Offshore Wind V164-8.4MW turbines, with 31 on monopiles and two on mono buckets. The project is notable for being the first offshore wind farm worldwide to test mono bucket foundations under commercial operating conditions.
- **January 2019**: The installation of 31 monopile foundations was completed, marking the first phase of construction. The next phase involves inter-array medium-voltage cabling, with commissioning expected in the second half of 2019.
- **May 2019**: Van Oord entered into a contract with Jack Up Barge for accommodation and supply of balance of plant operations, further solidifying its role in the design, engineering, procurement, construction, and installation of the wind farm's infrastructure.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2018**: The Deutsche Bucht project has a total investment of EUR 1.4 billion. The project received the necessary permits from the Federal Maritime and Hydrographic Agency of Germany (BSH) to proceed with the installation of the mono bucket foundations.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2018**: Financial close on the Deutsche Bucht Mono Bucket pilot demonstrator project was reached.
- **September 2018**: Monopile installation commenced at the Deutsche Bucht site.
- **Q2 2019**: Installation of the two mono bucket foundations is scheduled to begin.
- **Second Half of 2019**: The entire wind farm is expected to be commissioned.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2019**: Van Oord's operations at Deutsche Bucht involve a crew of around 100 people, indicating job creation associated with the construction and operational phases of the wind farm.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2018**: The Deutsche Bucht offshore wind farm is owned by Northland Power Inc., a Canadian power producer. Van Oord operates as the general contractor for the Balance of Plant, indicating that it does not have exclusive ownership of the facility but is a key operational partner.

This timeline encapsulates the significant developments and milestones related to the Van Oord facility in Deutsche Bucht, Germany, highlighting its technological advancements, investment details, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
