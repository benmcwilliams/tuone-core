### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mercedes-Benz Facility in Nabern, Germany

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Current Production Focus:** The facility specializes in fuel-cell technology, particularly for stationary fuel-cell generators intended for CO2-neutral emergency power generation.
   - **Expertise Development:** Over the past two decades, Daimler has developed significant expertise in fuel-cell technology at the Nabern site, which is currently the headquarters of Mercedes-Benz Fuel Cell GmbH.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Joint Venture Formation:** In May 2020, Daimler Truck AG announced plans to establish a joint venture with the Volvo Group for the large-scale development, production, and commercialization of fuel-cell systems. This joint venture will consolidate all of Daimler's fuel-cell activities, including those at the Nabern facility.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Cooperation Agreement:** On May 28, 2020, Daimler Truck AG and Rolls-Royce plc announced their intention to cooperate on stationary fuel-cell generators, with a comprehensive cooperation agreement expected to be signed by the end of 2020.
   - **Production Plans:** The joint venture with Volvo Group aims to start large-scale production of heavy-duty fuel-cell commercial vehicles in the second half of the decade, with earlier production capabilities for stationary applications.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Job Creation:** The establishment of the joint venture and the consolidation of fuel-cell activities are expected to create job opportunities, although specific numbers were not detailed in the article.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Ownership Transition:** The facility is currently part of Daimler Truck AG, which will bring together all fuel-cell activities into a new entity for the joint venture with Volvo Group. This indicates a shift in ownership structure as operations of Mercedes-Benz Fuel Cell GmbH will be allocated to Daimler Truck AG.
