## DEU-09378-00521

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mercedes-Benz Facility in Wörth, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **End of 2021**: Series production of the battery-electric Mercedes-Benz eActros for heavy-duty distribution haulage commenced at the Wörth plant. The eActros 300 and 400 models feature battery packs with an installed capacity of 112 kWh, enabling ranges of up to 220 km and 400 km, respectively.
- **June 2023**: The eActros 300 Tractor underwent road endurance testing, covering 3,000 km from Wörth to Turkey, demonstrating its readiness for series production scheduled for autumn 2023. The vehicle is compatible with common European semitrailers and features advanced electric drive technology.
- **November 2024**: Daimler Truck announced plans for liquid hydrogen-powered trucks, with final assembly to occur at the Wörth facility. These trucks are expected to have a range of 1,000 km and are part of a broader initiative to enhance hydrogen use in heavy-duty transportation.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2023**: A joint venture was established to explore geothermal energy in Wörth, with an exploration permit granted for the "Bertha" area. The project aims to determine optimal sites for geothermal energy production, with potential operational status by 2028. This initiative aligns with Daimler Truck's commitment to sustainable transport.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2023**: The joint venture for geothermal exploration was publicly announced, with initial community engagement planned for July 18, 2023.
- **Autumn 2023**: Series production of the eActros 300 Tractor is set to begin, following successful testing.

#### 4. Labour
- The Wörth facility employs around 10,000 people, making it the largest local employer. The ongoing green transformation and production of new electric models are expected to influence job dynamics positively, although specific job creation or loss figures related to the recent expansions or projects have not been detailed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned exclusively by Daimler Truck, which is committed to achieving CO2-neutral transport by 2050 and is actively involved in the green transformation of its operations.
