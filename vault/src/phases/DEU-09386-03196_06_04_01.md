### Evolutionary Timeline of the Meyer Burger Facility in Thalheim, Bitterfeld-Wolfen, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04/2021**: Meyer Burger is in the process of ramping up its heterojunction (HJ) solar cell and module assembly capacity. The initial production volume is set at **400MW** for 2021, with plans to expand to **1.4GW** by 2023.
- **05/19/2021**: The facility officially starts commercial operations with a capacity of **400MW**. The company aims to reach a total manufacturing capacity of **5GW** by **2026**, producing approximately **200,000 solar cells daily**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04/2021**: Meyer Burger is set to receive a total of **€22.5 million** in regional German government grants to support the establishment of the HJ solar cell production plant. This includes **€15 million** in environmental protection aid and **€7.5 million** for regional economic benefits associated with the new facility.
  
#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04/2021**: Meyer Burger announces plans to start production in the **second quarter of 2021**.
- **05/19/2021**: The facility in Thalheim, Bitterfeld-Wolfen, officially opens and begins commercial operations.
- **05/26/2021**: A second facility in Freiberg is also opened, both facilities having an initial capacity of **400MW** each.
