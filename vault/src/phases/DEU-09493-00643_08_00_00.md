### Evolutionary Timeline of RWE Facility in German North Sea, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: RWE and Northland Power announced the Nordseecluster project, which consists of four offshore wind farm sites with a total capacity of up to 1.6 gigawatts (GW). The first phase, Nordseecluster A, will have a capacity of 660 megawatts (MW) and is expected to be operational by early 2027. The second phase, Nordseecluster B, will add another 900 MW, with operations starting in early 2029.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2024**: RWE made the final investment decision to proceed with the Nordseecluster project. The project involves significant investments in the construction of foundations and wind turbines, with Dajin Heavy Industry selected to manufacture 104 foundations for the entire cluster.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: Announcement of Hellenic Cables as the preferred supplier for the Nordseecluster, with cable installation expected to begin towards the end of 2025 for Nordseecluster A.
- **May 2024**: The first steel cut for the foundations of Nordseecluster A was conducted, marking the start of the production stage for the project.

#### 4. Labour
- Specific job creation or loss details related to the Nordseecluster project have not been mentioned in the articles. However, the scale of the project suggests potential job creation during the construction and operational phases.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- RWE holds a 51% stake in the Nordseecluster, while Northland Power owns the remaining 49%. This indicates a joint venture ownership structure for the facility.
