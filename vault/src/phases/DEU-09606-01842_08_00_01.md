### Evolutionary Timeline of Ørsted's Gode Wind 3 Facility, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04/2020**: Ørsted announces Siemens Gamesa as the preferred turbine supplier for Gode Wind 3, which will utilize the new SG 11.0-200 DD offshore wind turbine, featuring a rotor diameter of 200 meters and a nominal output of 11 MW. The project has a capacity of 242 MW.
- **06/2021**: Ørsted confirms the installation of cable protection systems for Gode Wind 3, with project engineering work commencing immediately and installation scheduled for 2024.
- **05/2024**: The first of 23 Siemens Gamesa turbines is installed at Gode Wind 3, marking the beginning of electricity generation. The facility produces its first power on 07/05/2024, feeding electricity into the national transmission grid.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04/2020**: The final investment decision for Gode Wind 3 is still pending, contingent on receiving final grid dates and consents from German authorities.
- **06/2021**: Planning approval for Gode Wind 3 is granted by Germany’s Federal Maritime and Hydrographic Agency (BSH), allowing construction to proceed in parallel with the Borkum Riffgrund 3 project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04/2020**: Ørsted expects installation of Gode Wind 3 to begin in 2023, with commissioning completed in 2024.
- **06/2021**: Project engineering work for cable protection systems begins, with installation activities scheduled for 2024.
- **05/2024**: Gode Wind 3 successfully delivers its first power, with the project expected to be fully commissioned in 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **05/2024**: The installation of the turbines and associated infrastructure is expected to create jobs, although specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **05/2024**: Gode Wind 3 is owned by Ørsted and Nuveen Infrastructure, indicating a joint ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by Ørsted.
