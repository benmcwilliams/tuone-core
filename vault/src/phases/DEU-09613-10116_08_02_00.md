## DEU-09613-10116

### Evolutionary Timeline of Steelwind Nordenham Facility in Nordenham, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2019**: Steelwind Nordenham began fabricating monopiles for the WPD's 640MW Yunlin offshore wind farm in Taiwan, with production expected to start in July 2019.
- **September 2021**: Steelwind was contracted to supply 66 monopiles for the Gode Wind 3 (242MW) and Borkum Riffgrund 3 (900MW) offshore wind farms, with monopiles measuring up to 100 meters long and weighing over 1200 tonnes.
- **March 2022**: Steelwind dispatched the first three XXL monopiles for the 257MW Arcadis Ost 1 offshore wind project, marking the start of their production in August 2021.
- **October 2023**: Steelwind secured a contract to supply 34 monopile foundations for the Baltica 2 offshore wind farm, with production expected to start in the second half of 2025.
- **October 2024**: Steelwind shipped the first monopiles for the 1.2 GW Baltic Power offshore wind project, with manufacturing starting in 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2021**: The contracts with Ørsted for Gode Wind 3 and Borkum Riffgrund 3 were part of a strategic volume contract aimed at ensuring key components' availability and encouraging investment in manufacturing facilities.
- **October 2024**: RWE signed a capacity reservation agreement with Steelwind for the production of up to 300 monopile foundations, reserving capacity for 320,000 tonnes of steel over a 24-month period starting in 2027.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2020**: Foundation installation for the Yunlin offshore wind farm was expected to start.
- **2021**: Fabrication of monopiles for Gode Wind 3 and Borkum Riffgrund 3 began.
- **May 2022**: Installation of the first monopiles for Arcadis Ost 1 was scheduled to commence.
- **October 2023**: Contract signed for Baltica 2, marking a significant milestone in securing key components for the offshore project.
- **October 2024**: First monopiles for Baltic Power were shipped, with installation expected to begin in early 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2021**: The contracts for Gode Wind 3 and Borkum Riffgrund 3 were expected to create many new jobs in the Odense area, although specific job numbers were not detailed.
- **October 2024**: The agreement with RWE indicated a significant step towards securing production capacity, which may lead to job creation, although specific figures were not provided.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Steelwind Nordenham operates as a subsidiary of Dillinger, which supplies the steel for the monopiles. The facility is not mentioned as a joint venture, indicating that Steelwind Nordenham has full ownership of its operations. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones of the Steelwind Nordenham facility, highlighting its role in the offshore wind industry and its contributions to renewable energy projects.

### Technology and Operations
- **Production Details**: "Steelwind Nordenham will be supplying 66 monopiles" for the Gode Wind 3 and Borkum Riffgrund 3 offshore wind farms (published 06-09-2021). Contrastingly, "Steelwind Nordenham is responsible for the delivery of monopiles" for the Baltic Power offshore wind project, which consists of 78 monopiles in total (published 15-10-2024).
- **Production Capacity**: "The monopiles will be up to 100 metres long, have a diameter of almost 10 metres, and weigh 1200 tonnes or more" for Gode Wind 3 and Borkum Riffgrund 3 (published 06-09-2021). In contrast, the Baltic Power monopiles are noted to be "76 for wind turbines and two for offshore substations" (published 15-10-2024).
- **Expansion Updates**: "Fabrication of the monopiles started in August 2021 at the Steelwind Nordenham factory" for the Arcadis Ost 1 project (published 01-03-2022). However, for the Baltica 2 project, "production expected to start in the second half of 2025" (published 31-10-2023).

### Investment and Development

In contrast, "the bidding process for all key components for Baltica 2 took place in 2022 and early 2023" amid "unprecedented macroeconomic challenges" (published 31-10-2023).

### Project Status and Milestones

In contrast, "the first monopiles have been shipped from Steelwind’s facility in Nordenham" for the Baltic Power project, with installation expected to commence in early 2025 (published 15-10-2024).
- **Production Start Dates**: "Fabrication of the monopiles started in August 2021" for Arcadis Ost 1 (published 01-03-2022). However, for Baltica 2, "production expected to start in the second half of 2025" (published 31-10-2023).
