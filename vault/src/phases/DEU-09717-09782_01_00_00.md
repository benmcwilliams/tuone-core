### Evolutionary Timeline of PreussenElektra Facility in Brokdorf, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2017**: PreussenElektra applied for approval to decommission and dismantle the Brokdorf nuclear power plant, which is a pressurized water reactor (PWR) with a gross installed capacity of 1410 MWe.
- **December 31, 2021**: The Brokdorf plant was officially shut down.
- **October 23, 2024**: The Schleswig-Holstein Ministry for Energy Transition issued the first decommissioning and dismantling permit for the Brokdorf plant.
- **December 13, 2024**: PreussenElektra notified authorities that it was using the decommissioning permit, marking the transition from the post-operation phase to the dismantling phase. The first dismantling action involved shutting down components of the nuclear intermediate cooling system.
- **Future Plans**: A second dismantling permit is required for the reactor pressure vessel and biological shield, with plans to transport fuel elements to an interim storage facility in 2025. Additionally, plans were announced for the construction of the largest battery storage facility in the EU at the Brokdorf site, with a capacity of up to 800 MW and 1600 MWh, expected to begin commissioning in 2026.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2023**: PreussenElektra, along with EOn group companies, announced plans for the construction of a large battery storage facility at the Brokdorf site, indicating significant investment in renewable energy infrastructure.
