### Evolutionary Timeline of PreussenElektra Facility in Grafenrheinfeld, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2015**: The Grafenrheinfeld nuclear power plant, a pressurized water reactor (PWR) with a net capacity of 1275 MWe, was officially shut down following the 13th amendment to Germany's Nuclear Power Act.
- **April 2018**: Dismantling operations began after PreussenElektra received the decommissioning and dismantling permit.
- **December 2022**: A second dismantling permit was granted, allowing for the dismantling of the reactor pressure vessel and the biological shield surrounding it.
- **August 2023**: The facility reached the "free of water" stage of decommissioning, with plans to begin dismantling the 400-tonne reactor pressure vessel in November 2023, expected to take around eight months.
- **August 2024**: The cooling towers of the Grafenrheinfeld plant were successfully demolished, resulting in approximately 55,000 tonnes of rubble, which will be processed for future use on-site.

#### 2. Investment and Development

### Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: The Grafenrheinfeld plant had a net capacity of 1275 MWe (net) as a pressurized water reactor, which achieved first criticality in December 1981 and entered commercial operation in June 1982. 
- **Decommissioning Milestone**: As of August 2023, the facility reached the "free of water" stage of decommissioning, with plans to dismantle the 400-tonne reactor pressure vessel starting in November 2023, expected to take around eight months to complete.
- **Dismantling Progress**: Since dismantling began in April 2018, over 12,100 components have been removed, and around 3100 tonnes of material have been dismantled, cleaned, and measured.

### Investment and Development
