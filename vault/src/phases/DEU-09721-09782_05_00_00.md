### Evolutionary Timeline of PreussenElektra Facility in Isar 2, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Shutdown Date**: The Isar 2 reactor, a 1400 MWe pressurized water reactor, was officially shut down on **April 15, 2023**. Prior to its shutdown, it contributed approximately **18%** of Bavaria's electricity production and was noted as the most powerful nuclear power plant in Germany.
- **Dismantling Permit**: On **March 21, 2024**, the Bavarian State Ministry for the Environment and Consumer Protection issued a permit for the dismantling of Isar 2, allowing PreussenElektra to begin the decommissioning process. The first dismantling activities will involve the main coolant pumps and the reactor pressure vessel internals.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Decommissioning Application**: PreussenElektra submitted an application for the decommissioning and dismantling of Isar 2 in **July 2019**. This application was part of a broader strategy as the company is responsible for the decommissioning of eight nuclear power plants in Germany.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Shutdown Announcement**: The shutdown of Isar 2 was part of Germany's planned exit from nuclear power, which was confirmed in the article published on **April 17, 2023**.
- **Dismantling Approval**: The approval for dismantling was granted on **March 21, 2024**, marking a significant milestone in the facility's transition from operational to decommissioned status.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Impact**: The articles do not provide specific details on job creation or loss directly associated with the shutdown or dismantling of Isar 2. However, it is implied that the transition from operational to decommissioning may affect employment levels at the facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: PreussenElektra is the sole operator of the Isar 2 facility, as indicated by its responsibility for the decommissioning of the plant and its other nuclear facilities in Germany.
