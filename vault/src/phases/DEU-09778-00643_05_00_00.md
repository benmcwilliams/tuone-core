## DEU-09778-00643

### Evolutionary Timeline of the RWE Facility in Gundremmingen, Germany

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Gundremmingen facility consisted of two boiling water reactors (BWR): Gundremmingen B (1284 MWe) and Gundremmingen C (1288 MWe).
   - Gundremmingen B was disconnected from the grid on December 31, 2017, after 33 years of operation.
   - Gundremmingen C permanently shut down on December 31, 2021.
   - As of May 2024, RWE has been granted permits for the dismantling of both reactors, with ongoing dismantling activities expected to be completed by 2030.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - In December 2021, RWE awarded a contract to Westinghouse for the dismantling of the two reactors, indicating a significant investment in the decommissioning process.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - March 2019: License for dismantling unit B granted by the Bavarian State Ministry of the Environment and Consumer Protection.
   - May 2021: License for dismantling unit C granted.
   - December 2021: Contract awarded to Westinghouse for dismantling activities.
   - May 28, 2024: Final license for dismantling all remaining plant components granted, marking a significant milestone in the dismantling project.

4. **Labour:**
   - The dismantling project is supported by a team of RWE staff and partner companies, although specific job creation or loss figures are not provided in the article.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - RWE is the owner of the Gundremmingen facility, as indicated by their management of the dismantling process and contracts related to the facility.
