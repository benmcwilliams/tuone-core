### Evolutionary Timeline of the Westinghouse Facility in Gundremmingen, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Gundremmingen facility originally consisted of two boiling water reactors (BWR): Gundremmingen B (1284 MWe) and Gundremmingen C (1288 MWe). 
   - Gundremmingen B was disconnected from the grid on December 31, 2017, after 33 years of operation, and Gundremmingen C permanently shut down on December 31, 2021.
   - As of June 2024, RWE has been actively dismantling the plant, with several thousand tonnes of material already removed and processed for recycling. The dismantling of all plant components is now permitted, with a target completion date set for the mid/late 2030s.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - In December 2021, RWE awarded a contract to Westinghouse for the dismantling of the two reactors, which includes dismantling and packing the reactor pressure vessels and associated structures. The completion of this work is expected by 2030.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - March 2019: Licence granted for dismantling unit B.
   - May 2021: Licence granted for dismantling unit C.
   - December 2021: Contract awarded to Westinghouse for dismantling work.
   - May 28, 2024: Final licence issued for dismantling all remaining plant components.
   - Expected completion of dismantling work by 2030.

4. **Labour:**
   - The dismantling project is being carried out by a team composed of RWE's own staff and partner companies, indicating job creation in the context of dismantling operations. Specific job numbers were not mentioned in the article.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is owned by RWE, with Westinghouse contracted for the dismantling operations. There is no indication of joint ownership; RWE appears to have full ownership of the facility.
