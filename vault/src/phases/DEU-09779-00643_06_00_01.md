### Evolutionary Timeline of the RWE Facility at Hambach Lignite Mine, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2023**: RWE began construction of the Neuland Solar Farm at the Hambach lignite mine, which will have a capacity of **8.4 MWac**. This solar project will be complemented by a **battery energy storage system (BESS)** with a power capacity of **8 MWh**. The solar project aims to meet some of the power needs of the mine, which has been operational since 1978 and has an annual coal output of **23 million tonnes**. RWE also announced plans for an additional solar project, the Neuland 1 Solar Farm, expected to have a capacity of **17.6 MW**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2023**: RWE is investing around **€4 billion gross** in renewable energy projects across Germany, with a significant portion allocated to the Rhenish mining district, where the Hambach mine is located. This investment is part of a broader strategy to increase the capacity of renewable energy projects to **1 GW by 2030**, with at least **500 MW** in the Rhenish mining district.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2023**: RWE announced the start of construction for the Neuland Solar Farm, with expectations to complete construction and begin commercial operations by the end of the year. The company also indicated plans to start construction on the Neuland 1 Solar Farm in the winter of 2023.

### Summary
The RWE facility at the Hambach lignite mine is undergoing a significant transition towards renewable energy, with the construction of solar projects aimed at meeting the mine's power needs while preparing for the eventual closure of coal operations by 2029. The company is heavily investing in solar technology and energy storage systems, marking a strategic shift in its operational focus.
