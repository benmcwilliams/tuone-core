### Evolutionary Timeline of RWE Offshore Wind Farm, Germany

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** May 2021
   - **Details:** RWE and BASF announced plans for a 2GW offshore wind farm aimed at supplying electricity to BASF's Ludwigshafen chemicals facility and powering a green hydrogen plant by 2030. The project is designed to electrify production processes for basic chemicals that were previously reliant on fossil fuels. The wind farm is expected to produce approximately 30 million kilograms of green hydrogen annually, with around 300MW of the wind farm's capacity dedicated to this production.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** May 2021
   - **Details:** The project is expected to avoid around 3.8 million tonnes of CO2 emissions annually, with up to 2.8 million tonnes directly at the Ludwigshafen facility. RWE and BASF indicated that financial support from the public sector for the construction of the wind farm would not be required, although a suitable regulatory framework is necessary for implementation.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** May 2021
   - **Details:** RWE and BASF signed a letter of intent to cooperate on the Lighthouse Offshore-to-X development. The project is positioned as a significant step towards the electrification of the chemical industry in Germany, with plans to initiate a tender for the 2GW wind farm, which would be in addition to Germany's existing plans for 20GW of offshore wind capacity by 2030.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** May 2021
   - **Details:** The announcement did not specify job creation or job loss figures directly associated with the project, but the development of the offshore wind farm and the associated green hydrogen production is expected to contribute positively to job creation in the renewable energy and chemical sectors.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** May 2021
   - **Details:** RWE will develop, build, and operate the offshore wind farm, while BASF will own a stake in the project, indicating a joint venture structure between the two companies.
