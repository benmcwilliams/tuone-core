### Evolutionary Timeline of the Siemens Facility in Potsdamer Platz, Berlin, Germany

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **March 2021:** Siemens Energy and Vattenfall signed an agreement to build an 8MW high-temperature heat pump at Potsdamer Platz. The facility is designed to provide green district heating by utilizing waste heat and renewable electricity from the local grid. The heat pump will deliver thermal capacity of 8MW, with forwarding temperatures ranging from 85°C to 120°C. The facility aims to improve energy efficiency in the district heating network and is expected to support approximately 12,000 offices, 1,000 housing units, and various cultural facilities.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **March 2021:** The project, known as the Qwark3 project (German acronym for “coupling of district heating, power, and cooling”), was initiated with a focus on integrating renewable energy sources into the district heating system. Specific financial details regarding the investment were not disclosed, but the spokesperson noted that the cost of the heat pump is project-specific and influenced by economies of scale and power prices.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **March 2021:** Agreement signed between Siemens Energy and Vattenfall to construct the facility.
   - **March 2022:** The facility was reported to be under construction, with expectations to begin commercial operations by the end of 2022.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
