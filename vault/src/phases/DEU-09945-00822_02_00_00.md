### Evolutionary Timeline of the Vattenfall Facility in Potsdamer Platz, Berlin, Germany

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **March 2021:** Vattenfall and Siemens Energy signed an agreement to build an 8MW high-temperature heat pump at Potsdamer Platz. The facility is designed to provide green district heating using waste heat and renewable electricity, integrating with the local district heating network. The heat pump will deliver thermal capacity of 8MW, with forwarding temperatures between 85°C and 120°C. The facility is expected to serve approximately 12,000 offices, 1,000 housing units, and various cultural facilities.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **March 2021:** The project, known as the Qwark3 project (German acronym for “coupling of district heating, power, and cooling”), was initiated. Specific financial details regarding the investment were not disclosed, but the cost of the heat pump is noted to be project-specific and influenced by economies of scale and power prices.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **March 2021:** Agreement signed between Vattenfall and Siemens Energy to commence the construction of the heat pump facility.
   - **March 2022:** The facility was reported to be under construction, with expectations to begin commercial operations by the end of 2022.

4. **Labour:**
   - The articles do not specify job creation or job loss directly associated with the construction or operation of the facility.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is a joint project between Vattenfall Wärme Berlin AG (a unit of Vattenfall) and Siemens Energy AG, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by Vattenfall.
