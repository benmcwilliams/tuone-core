### Evolutionary Timeline of the Solarion Facility in Saxon, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **May 2011:** Solarion announced the groundbreaking of its CIGS (Copper Indium Gallium Selenide) thin-film solar cell and module factory in Saxon, Germany. The facility is designed for mass manufacturing of thin-film PV technology, with an initial production capacity of 20MW. The production facility will cover 129,167 square feet on a 7.4-acre site at the Zwenkau-South industrial park. The rollout of frameless glass-glass modules is scheduled for 2012, followed by flexible foil modules in 2013.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **May 2011:** The project has a total investment of €40 million, which includes funding from the Free State of Saxony and a signed investment from Taiwanese company Walsin Lihwa.
