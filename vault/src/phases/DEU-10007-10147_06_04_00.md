### Evolutionary Timeline of the Sunfilm Facility in Grossroehrsdorf, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** April 2009
   - **Details:** Sunfilm, utilizing Applied Materials' 'SunFab' turnkey thin-film production technology, merged with Q-Cells’ a-Si thin-film subsidiary Sontor. The combined production capacity reached 85MW, with an additional 60MW capacity under construction at the Grossroehrsdorf facility. The facility focused on tandem-junction thin-film technology, achieving module efficiencies of up to 8%.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** April 2009
   - **Details:** The merger was aimed at capitalizing on the potential of tandem-junction thin-film technology, with investments from Q-Cells (holding a 50% stake), Good Energies (approximately 35% stake), and NorSun (approximately 15% stake). The focus was on enhancing R&D for improving cell efficiencies.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** April 2009
   - **Details:** The merger announcement indicated that the legal effectiveness of the merger would occur by the end of May 2009. The combined entity aimed to establish itself as a significant player in the photovoltaic market.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** April 2009
   - **Details:** The new Sunfilm company was expected to employ approximately 400 employees post-merger.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** April 2009
   - **Details:** The ownership structure post-merger included Q-Cells with a 50% stake, Good Energies with approximately 35%, and NorSun with approximately 15%. This indicates that Sunfilm was not exclusively owned by a single entity but was a joint venture among these companies.
