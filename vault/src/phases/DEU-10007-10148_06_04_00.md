## DEU-10007-10148

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Sunfilm AG Facility in Grossroehrsdorf, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2009**: Sunfilm AG announces that it has secured IEC certification for five module sizes, including larger models. The facility has an approximate annual production capacity of 85MWp across its two German sites, including Grossroehrsdorf. An additional 60MWp plant is under construction at the Grossroehrsdorf facility, positioning Sunfilm as one of the largest manufacturers of tandem junction silicon-based thin-film modules.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2009**: The construction of the additional 60MWp plant at the Grossroehrsdorf facility indicates significant investment in expanding production capacity. 

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2010**: Sunfilm AG files for insolvency in the district court in Dresden, Germany, due to financial difficulties exacerbated by market competition and operational challenges. This filing indicates a significant shift in the project status, as the company seeks a new investor for restructuring.

#### 4. Labour
- **Late 2009**: Approximately 300 employees at the two manufacturing plants, including Grossroehrsdorf, had been on shorter working hours due to the company's financial struggles. The merged company had around 400 workers at the time of the merger in May 2009.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2010**: Sunfilm AG was initially backed by Q-Cells, Good Energies, and Norsun. However, following the insolvency filing, the ownership structure is uncertain as the company seeks a new investor to take control and restructure.

This timeline captures the key developments and challenges faced by the Sunfilm AG facility in Grossroehrsdorf, DEU, highlighting its technological advancements, investment efforts, project milestones, labor impacts, and ownership dynamics.
