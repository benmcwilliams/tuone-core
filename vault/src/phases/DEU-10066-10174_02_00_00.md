### Evolutionary Timeline of the Swiss Heating Coop Facility in Lörrach-Stetten, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **1994**: The original geothermal plant began operations, supplying heat to households in Riehen and Lörrach.
- **2020**: The facility operates with hot water at 67 degrees Celsius derived from two 1,500-meter deep wells. The system feeds into a 38 km district heating network, covering the heating requirements of approximately 8,500 people. The energy output has increased from 10 GWh to 23.3 GWh over the past decade.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2020**: Swiss Heating Coop announced plans to invest up to CHF 49 million (around $51 million) for the expansion of geothermal district heating to meet increasing demand for climate-neutral energy. The new plant, referred to as "geo2riehen," is expected to supply heat to an additional 4,000 people. The investment requirement for the project is estimated between CHF 42 to 49 million, with "geo2riehen" accounting for around CHF 14 million.
- **December 2018**: A grant was requested from the Swiss Federal Office of Energy (SFOE) for the project, which was later approved with a subsidy contract of CHF 1.2 million signed in January 2019 for a measurement campaign.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2018**: A feasibility study was conducted, indicating good prospects for the project.
- **May 2020**: The Riehen residents’ council was expected to approve the submission for the expansion project in June 2020.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The ownership structure is set to change, with the municipality of Riehen and the Industrielle Werke Basel (IWB) reviewing their partnership. The new share distribution will be 50% for Riehen and 50% for IWB, compared to the previous distribution of 72.9% (Riehen) and 27.1% (IWB).
