### Evolutionary Timeline of Tesla Facility in Gruenheide, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2020**: Tesla announced plans to build its first European factory in Gruenheide, Brandenburg, with a production capacity aimed at 500,000 Model 3 sedans and Model Y SUVs per year. 
- **November 2020**: Elon Musk revealed plans for the world's largest battery-cell factory at the Gruenheide site, starting with a capacity of 100 GWh per year, with plans to ramp up to 250 GWh.
- **March 2022**: The approval process for the electric vehicle and battery factory was nearing completion, with production expected to start soon after overcoming local opposition and regulatory delays.
- **March 2024**: The factory was temporarily closed due to a fire at a nearby power pylon, impacting production capabilities and halting the output of approximately 6,000 vehicles per week.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2020**: Tesla agreed to purchase a 300-hectare property in Gruenheide for a preliminary price of 40.91 million euros ($45.36 million) to establish the factory.
- **November 2020**: The announcement of the battery plant indicated significant investment in expanding production capabilities, although specific investment figures were not disclosed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2020**: Tesla's board approved the purchase agreement for the property, marking a significant step towards establishing the factory.
- **November 2020**: Construction of the Gigafactory began, with expectations for it to open in the following year.
- **March 2022**: The facility was awaiting final approval to begin production, which had been delayed due to regulatory challenges.
- **March 2024**: The factory was closed due to an arson attack on a power pylon, with plans to restart operations the following week.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2020**: The establishment of the factory was expected to create up to 7,000 jobs in Brandenburg, generating significant employment opportunities in the region.
- **March 2024**: The temporary closure of the factory due to the fire left 12,500 employees in limbo, with production halted and potential financial losses for the company.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Tesla owns the facility exclusively, as indicated by the purchase agreement for the property and the plans for the factory's operations. There is no mention of joint ventures or partnerships regarding the ownership of the Gruenheide facility.
