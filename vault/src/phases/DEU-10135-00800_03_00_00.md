### Evolutionary Timeline of the Uniper Facility in Happurg, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2024**: Uniper announced the recommissioning of the Happurg pumped storage plant, which has a production capacity of 160 MW and can store approximately 850 MWh of electricity. The plant was originally shut down in 2011 for safety reasons due to damage to the upper reservoir. The refurbishment will include repairing the upper reservoir and overhauling the equipment in the powerhouse. The recommissioned plant is expected to be back online by 2028.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2024**: Uniper committed to an investment of around €250 million for the refurbishment and recommissioning of the Happurg pumped storage plant. This investment is part of Uniper's strategy to transition towards a greener business, aiming for 80% of its generation capacity to be carbon neutral by 2030.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2024**: Uniper announced the start of construction work on the Happurg plant, with operations scheduled to resume in 2028. The project received approval from the District Office of Nuremberg following a thorough feasibility study and planning approval procedure.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2024**: While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned, the refurbishment of the plant is expected to require a high level of skilled personnel, indicating potential job creation in the region as Uniper expands its hydropower portfolio.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
