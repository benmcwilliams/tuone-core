### Evolutionary Timeline of the Uniper Facility in Petershagen, DEU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2022**: Uniper announced the restart of the Heyden IV hard coal-fired power plant, which has a production capacity of 875MW. The plant was set to produce electricity from 29 August 2022 until 30 April 2023, with the possibility of extending operations for another year. However, operations were limited due to rail transportation capacity for hard coal.
- **September 2023**: Uniper confirmed the permanent closure of the Heyden IV power plant, scheduled for 30 September 2024, as part of Germany's emissions reduction plan. The plant had been deemed "systemically relevant" by the German regulator but was ultimately set for decommissioning.
- **October 2024**: Uniper announced plans to construct a 50MW/100 megawatt-hours battery energy storage system at the Heyden power plant site, with operations expected to begin in 2025.
