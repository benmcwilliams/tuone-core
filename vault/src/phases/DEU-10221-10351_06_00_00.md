### Evolutionary Timeline of the Viessmann Facility in Allendorf, Germany

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **March 2018:** Viessmann commissioned a 2 MW solar PV park at its headquarters in Allendorf, Hessen. The facility consists of 7,400 solar modules, which are expected to deliver approximately 1.8 GWh of solar power annually. This installation increases Viessmann's self-produced electricity by 50%, with about one-fifth of its electricity being self-produced prior to this project. The additional 7% generated by the PV system is primarily for direct consumption at the site.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The solar park was developed completely outside of the German renewable energy law (EEG) subsidy scheme, indicating a significant investment by Viessmann without reliance on government subsidies. The amortization of the PV system is expected to occur within a few years, depending on electricity price developments.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **March 2018:** The solar PV park was officially commissioned after almost five months of construction. The project was noted for its advanced planning and implementation, including the use of a detailed 3D model for construction.

4. **Labour:**
   - The article does not specify any job creation or job loss directly associated with the solar park project.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - Viessmann owns the facility exclusively, as it is developed and operated under Viessmann Werke Allendorf GmbH, a subsidiary of the Viessmann Group.
