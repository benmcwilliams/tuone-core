### Evolutionary Timeline of Wacker Chemie Facility in Nünchritz, Germany

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2010**: Wacker Chemie received approval for a US$126.8 million investment aid to produce solar-grade polysilicon in Nünchritz, expanding its existing silicon/silane plant.
- **03/2011**: Wacker announced plans to expand polysilicon production by adding 10,000MT capacity through debottlenecking measures, increasing Nünchritz's capacity from 10,000MT to 15,000MT.
- **10/2011**: Wacker began ramping up the new polysilicon plant in Nünchritz, with a total investment of €900 million aimed at achieving a capacity of 15,000MT per annum.
- **04/2012**: The 15,000MT polysilicon plant officially started volume production, reaching full capacity shortly thereafter.
- **05/2015**: Wacker targeted an increase in polysilicon capacity to around 80,000MT by 2017 through optimization of existing processes at the Nünchritz site.
- **02/2016**: Despite falling average selling prices (ASPs), Wacker maintained full capacity at its Nünchritz plant while ramping up production at its new US facility.

#### 2. Investment and Development

- **04/2012**: The facility reached its full nominal capacity of 15,000MT after the €900 million investment.
