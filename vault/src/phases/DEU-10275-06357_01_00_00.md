### Evolutionary Timeline of the Wirsol Energy Facility in Wagga Wagga, DEU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Bomen Solar Farm, developed by Wirsol Energy, is located in Wagga Wagga and is part of a larger renewable energy initiative. The facility includes a 40 MWh battery storage component and features bifacial PV panels supplied by Jinko Solar, inverters from SMA, and trackers from NEXTracker. The project is expected to have a total output of 69 MW under a Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) with Flow Power.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The total costs for the Bomen Solar Farm are projected to be approximately A$188 million. The project is part of a broader investment strategy in renewable energy, with significant financial backing from various stakeholders, including Spark Infrastructure, which acquired the project from Renew Estate, partly owned by Wirsol Energy.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - The Bomen Solar Farm was announced in June 2019, with commercial operations slated to commence in the second quarter of 2020. The project is currently under construction, and it is expected to employ around 250 workers throughout this phase.

4. **Labour:**
   - The construction of the Bomen Solar Farm is anticipated to create approximately 250 jobs during the construction phase.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is not exclusively owned by Wirsol Energy; it is part of a joint venture structure where Spark Infrastructure holds a stake in the project, having acquired it from Renew Estate, which is partly owned by Wirsol Energy.
