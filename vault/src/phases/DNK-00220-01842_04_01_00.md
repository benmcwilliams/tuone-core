## DNK-00220-01842

### Evolutionary Timeline of Ørsted Facility in Copenhagen, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2020**: Ørsted, in partnership with a consortium of Danish companies, announced plans to develop an industrial-scale transport fuel production facility in Copenhagen, powered by offshore wind. The facility aims to produce renewable hydrogen, methanol, and e-kerosene, with a vision to scale up to a total electrolyser capacity of 1.3 GW by 2030, potentially making it one of the largest facilities of its kind globally.
- **May 2021**: Ørsted broke ground on its first renewable hydrogen project, H2RES, at Avedøre Holme, with an initial capacity of 2 MW, expected to produce around 1,000 kg of renewable hydrogen per day for road transport in the Greater Copenhagen area. The first hydrogen production was anticipated by late 2021.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2021**: The Danish government awarded DKK 34.6 million (€4.65 million) to the H2RES project, which involves multiple partners including Ørsted, Everfuel Europe, and DSV Panalpina. This funding supports the development of the facility and its integration with offshore wind power.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2020**: Announcement of the Green Fuels for Denmark project, which includes three phases of development: 10 MW (operational by 2023), 250 MW (by 2027), and 1.3 GW (by 2030).
- **May 2021**: Groundbreaking for the H2RES project, marking the start of construction for the 2 MW hydrogen production facility.
- **June 2021**: Ørsted and HOFOR entered an agreement to link the offshore wind farm with the Green Fuels for Denmark project, enhancing the project's power supply.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2021**: The H2RES project is expected to create jobs in the renewable hydrogen sector, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Ørsted is the primary developer of the facility, collaborating with various partners including Copenhagen Airports, A.P. Moller - Maersk, DSV Panalpina, DFDS, SAS, and others. The facility is not exclusively owned by Ørsted but is part of a consortium effort to develop sustainable fuel production in Denmark.
