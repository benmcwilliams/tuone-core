### A.P. Moller-Maersk Facility in Copenhagen, Denmark: Evolutionary Timeline

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2020**: A.P. Moller-Maersk partners with Ørsted on a major green hydrogen project aimed at decarbonizing transport. The project will initially utilize a 10-megawatt electrolyzer to produce green hydrogen, with plans to scale up to 250 megawatts by 2027 and 1.3 gigawatts by 2030. The hydrogen will be used to create methanol for shipping and e-kerosene for aviation.
- **August 2021**: A.P. Moller-Maersk collaborates with REintegrate to produce green e-methanol for its first carbon-neutral methanol-operated vessel, with operations expected to start in 2023. The facility will utilize renewable energy and biogenic CO2.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2020**: The green hydrogen project is in the initial regulatory phase and is seeking public funding for a feasibility study. A final investment decision is anticipated in the following year.
- **August 2021**: The partnership with REintegrate is part of A.P. Moller-Maersk's strategy to scale production of green fuels, indicating a significant investment in renewable energy sources.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2020**: Announcement of the green hydrogen project in partnership with Ørsted, with a phased approach to increase electrolyzer capacity over the years.
- **August 2021**: Confirmation of the collaboration with REintegrate to establish a methanol facility, with operations expected to commence in 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2021**: The establishment of the e-methanol facility is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers are not detailed in the articles. The scaling of production and the development of the green hydrogen project may also contribute to job creation in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2020**: A.P. Moller-Maersk is involved in a partnership with Ørsted for the green hydrogen project, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership.
- **August 2021**: The collaboration with REintegrate suggests a joint venture model for the e-methanol facility, rather than full ownership by A.P. Moller-Maersk.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic moves of the A.P. Moller-Maersk facility in Copenhagen, focusing on its initiatives in clean technology and sustainable fuel production.
