## DNK-00220-03873

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Copenhagen Airport Facility in Copenhagen, Denmark

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **05-11-2020:** The facility is involved in a green hydrogen project led by Haldor Topsoe, aiming to develop a wind-powered green hydrogen electrolysis plant. The project plans to scale production in three phases:
     - **Phase 1:** Targeting 10MW-equivalent of hydrogen production by 2023.
     - **Phase 2:** Increasing production to 250MW-equivalent by 2027, utilizing carbon capture from waste and biomass to produce renewable methanol for ships and planes.
     - **Phase 3:** Aiming for a full capacity of 1.3GW by 2030, with enhanced CO₂ capture.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **05-11-2020:** Haldor Topsoe and its partners have jointly invested at least $118 million (€100 million) into the project. The companies are also seeking substantial funding from Innovation Fund Denmark, a government investment scheme.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **05-11-2020:** The project is in the planning stages, with the first phase expected to produce hydrogen by 2023. The development of the latter phases is contingent upon the completion of an offshore wind farm near Bornholm island.

4. **Labour:**
   - The articles do not specify job creation or job loss directly related to the facility's operations or investments.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is not exclusively owned by Haldor Topsoe; it is a collaborative effort involving multiple stakeholders, including transport companies such as Scandinavian airline SAS, ferry and shipping operator DFDS, and the operators of Copenhagen Airport. 

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic plans for the Copenhagen Airport facility as part of the green hydrogen project initiated by Haldor Topsoe and its partners.
