## DNK-00220-03881

### Evolutionary Timeline of the DFDS Facility in Copenhagen, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2020**: Ørsted announced the first major green hydrogen project targeting the transport sector, which includes DFDS as a participant. The project will utilize a 10-megawatt electrolyzer initially, with plans to scale up to 250 megawatts by 2027 and eventually to 1.3 gigawatts by 2030. The hydrogen produced will be used for fleet buses, trucks, and potentially for DFDS's hydrogen-powered ferry service between Copenhagen and Oslo.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2020**: The project is in the early stages, seeking public funding for a feasibility study. A final investment decision for the first phase is expected in 2021. The project aims to leverage power from a proposed 5-gigawatt offshore wind cluster around Bornholm, Denmark.
- **June 2021**: Ørsted and the Greater Copenhagen Utility (HOFOR) are collaborating on the Green Fuels for Denmark project, which aims to create synergies between offshore and onshore green power infrastructure. This project is expected to support the hydrogen production for the shipping industry, including DFDS.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2020**: Announcement of the green hydrogen project, with initial regulatory processes underway.
- **June 2021**: Continued development of the Green Fuels for Denmark initiative, with plans for a 250 MW offshore wind farm to support the hydrogen production.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2021**: The Green Fuels for Denmark project is expected to contribute significantly to reducing greenhouse gas emissions and may create a new industrial stronghold in Denmark, although specific job creation figures related to DFDS are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2020 - June 2021**: The facility is part of a collaborative project involving multiple stakeholders, including Ørsted, DFDS, A.P. Moller-Maersk, SAS, and DSV Panalpina. The ownership structure appears to be a partnership rather than exclusive ownership by DFDS.

This timeline captures the key developments related to the DFDS facility in Copenhagen, focusing on its role in the green hydrogen project and the collaborative efforts with other companies and utilities in Denmark.
