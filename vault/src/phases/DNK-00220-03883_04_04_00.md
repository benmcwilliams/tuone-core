## DNK-00220-03883

### Evolutionary Timeline of the DSV Panalpina Facility in Copenhagen, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2020**: DSV Panalpina is part of a consortium led by Ørsted to develop an industrial-scale transport fuel production facility in Copenhagen, powered by offshore wind. The facility aims to produce renewable hydrogen, methanol, and e-kerosene, with a vision to become one of the world's largest electrolyser and sustainable fuel production facilities.
- **May 2021**: Ørsted breaks ground on the H2RES project, a renewable hydrogen facility with a capacity of 2MW, which will produce approximately 1,000 kg of renewable hydrogen daily for zero-emission transport in Greater Copenhagen. DSV Panalpina is involved in this project.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2020**: The consortium, including DSV Panalpina, is seeking public co-funding to conduct a full feasibility study for the project. A final investment decision for the first stage could be made as soon as 2021.
- **June 2021**: Ørsted and HOFOR plan to create synergies between their offshore and onshore green power projects to support the Green Fuels for Denmark initiative, which includes DSV Panalpina as a key partner.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2020**: Announcement of the partnership to develop the transport fuel production facility, with plans to scale up production in three stages: 10MW by 2023, 250MW by 2027, and 1.3GW by 2030.
- **May 2021**: Groundbreaking ceremony for the H2RES project, marking the start of construction for the 2MW hydrogen production facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2020**: The project is expected to create jobs in the renewable energy sector, particularly in the development and operation of the hydrogen production facility, although specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2020**: The facility is part of a consortium involving multiple companies, including DSV Panalpina, Ørsted, A.P. Moller - Maersk, DFDS, SAS, and Copenhagen Airports. This indicates a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by DSV Panalpina.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the DSV Panalpina facility in Copenhagen, focusing on its role in the broader initiative for sustainable fuel production and the transition to renewable energy.
