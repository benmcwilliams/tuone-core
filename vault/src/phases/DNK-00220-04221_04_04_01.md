## DNK-00220-04221

### Evolutionary Timeline of the SAS Facility in Copenhagen, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2020**: A consortium led by Ørsted, including SAS, announced plans to develop an industrial-scale transport fuel production facility in Copenhagen, powered by offshore wind. The facility aims to produce renewable hydrogen, renewable methanol, and renewable jet fuel (e-kerosene) for various transport sectors. The project is envisioned to have a total electrolyser capacity of 1.3GW by 2030, producing over 250,000 tonnes of sustainable fuel annually, which could reduce carbon emissions by 850,000 tonnes per year.

- **Stages of Development**:
  - **Stage 1 (by 2023)**: A 10MW electrolyser to produce renewable hydrogen for buses and trucks.
  - **Stage 2 (by 2027)**: Expansion to a 250MW electrolyser, integrating carbon capture to produce renewable methanol and e-kerosene.
  - **Stage 3 (by 2030)**: Full capacity of 1.3GW, enhancing production and CO2 capture.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2020**: The consortium, including SAS, sought substantial funding from Innovation Fund Denmark, a government investment scheme, to support the development of the facility. The total investment from the involved companies, including Haldor Topsoe, was reported to be at least $118 million (€100 million).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2020**: Announcement of the project and partnership formation.
- **2021**: A final investment decision for the first stage of the project was anticipated, contingent on the feasibility study's outcomes.
- **2023**: Expected operational start for the first stage (10MW electrolyser).
- **2027**: Target for the second stage (250MW electrolyser) to be operational.
- **2030**: Full operational capacity of 1.3GW expected.

#### 4. Labour
- The project is expected to create jobs in the renewable energy sector, particularly in the construction and operation of the facility, although specific job creation numbers have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is a collaborative project involving multiple stakeholders, including SAS, Ørsted, A.P. Moller - Maersk, DSV Panalpina, DFDS, and Copenhagen Airports. It is not exclusively owned by SAS but is part of a joint venture among these companies.
