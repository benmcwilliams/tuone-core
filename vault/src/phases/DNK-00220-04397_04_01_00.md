## DNK-00220-04397

### A.P. Moller - Maersk Facility in Copenhagen, Denmark: Evolutionary Timeline

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2020**: A.P. Moller - Maersk is part of a consortium that plans to develop an industrial-scale transport fuel production facility in the Copenhagen area, powered by offshore wind. The facility aims to produce renewable hydrogen, renewable methanol, and renewable jet fuel (e-kerosene). 
- **Production Capacity**: The project envisions a total electrolyser capacity of 1.3GW, which could produce over 250,000 tonnes of sustainable fuel annually when fully scaled by 2030. 
- **Stages of Development**:
  - **Stage 1**: A 10MW electrolyser operational by 2023 for renewable hydrogen production.
  - **Stage 2**: A 250MW electrolyser operational by 2027, combining hydrogen production with sustainable carbon capture to produce renewable methanol and e-kerosene.
  - **Stage 3**: Upgrading to 1.3GW electrolyser capacity by 2030.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2020**: The consortium, including A.P. Moller - Maersk, is set to seek public co-funding for a full feasibility study of the project. A final investment decision for the first stage could be made as soon as 2021.
- **June 2021**: The partnership with Orsted and HOFOR aims to create synergies between offshore and onshore green power infrastructure to support the Green Fuels for Denmark project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2020**: Announcement of the partnership and project vision.
- **2021**: Anticipation of a final investment decision for the first stage of the project.
- **2023**: Expected operational date for the first stage (10MW electrolyser).
- **2027**: Expected operational date for the second stage (250MW electrolyser).
- **2030**: Expected operational date for the third stage (1.3GW electrolyser).

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify exact job creation or loss figures related to the facility's development. However, the project is expected to contribute significantly to the decarbonization of transport and could create jobs in the sustainable fuel sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- A.P. Moller - Maersk is part of a consortium with other companies, including Copenhagen Airports, DSV Panalpina, DFDS, SAS, and Orsted. The facility is not exclusively owned by A.P. Moller - Maersk but is a collaborative effort among multiple stakeholders. 

This timeline captures the key developments and plans for the A.P. Moller - Maersk facility in Copenhagen, focusing on its role in the Green Fuels for Denmark initiative and the broader goals of sustainable fuel production.
