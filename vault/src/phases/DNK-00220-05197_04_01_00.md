## DNK-00220-05197

### Evolutionary Timeline of the HOFOR Facility in Copenhagen, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **01-06-2021**: HOFOR, in collaboration with Ørsted, announced plans for the Green Fuels for Denmark project, which aims to produce sustainable fuels, including hydrogen for the shipping industry. The project is structured in three phases with total electrolysis capacities of approximately 10 MW, 250 MW, and 1,300 MW. The facility is expected to significantly reduce carbon emissions by up to 850,000 tons annually. HOFOR's offshore wind farm project in the Øresund Strait is integral to this initiative, with the first power expected to be delivered in 2024/2025.

- **17-04-2023**: HOFOR confirmed the installation of 26 Siemens Gamesa SG 11.0-200 DD wind turbines at the Aflandshage offshore wind farm, located about ten kilometers off the south coast of Copenhagen. The wind farm is projected to provide electricity equivalent to the consumption of 300,000 households.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **01-06-2021**: The partnership with Ørsted for the Green Fuels for Denmark project indicates significant investment in renewable energy infrastructure, although specific financial figures were not disclosed.

- **17-04-2023**: HOFOR is awaiting final approval of the project economy from its owner, The Municipality of Copenhagen, which suggests ongoing financial assessments and potential investments related to the Aflandshage wind project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **01-06-2021**: Announcement of the Green Fuels for Denmark project and the collaboration with Ørsted to utilize power from HOFOR’s offshore wind farm.

- **17-04-2023**: Confirmation of a contract with Cadeler for the transportation and installation of wind turbines at the Aflandshage wind farm, with installation scheduled to begin in 2026. The project has been in planning for eleven years, indicating a long development timeline.

#### 4. Labour
- **01-06-2021**: The Green Fuels for Denmark project is expected to create jobs in the renewable energy sector, particularly in the production of sustainable fuels and hydrogen, although specific job creation numbers were not mentioned.

- **17-04-2023**: The development of the Aflandshage wind farm and its associated projects is anticipated to contribute to job creation in the local area, particularly in construction and operations, but exact figures were not provided.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **01-06-2021**: HOFOR is identified as the developer of the offshore wind farm and is in partnership with Ørsted, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership.

- **17-04-2023**: HOFOR continues to operate under the ownership of The Municipality of Copenhagen, which suggests that HOFOR does not have exclusive ownership of the facility but operates as a municipal utility.
