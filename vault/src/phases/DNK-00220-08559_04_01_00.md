## DNK-00220-08559

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Copenhagen Airports Facility in Copenhagen, DNK

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2020**: A consortium led by Ørsted, including Copenhagen Airports, announced plans to develop an industrial-scale transport fuel production facility powered by offshore wind in the Baltic Sea. The facility aims to produce renewable hydrogen, renewable methanol, and renewable jet fuel (e-kerosene). 
- **Projected Capacity**: When fully scaled-up by 2030, the facility could deliver over 250,000 tonnes of sustainable fuel annually, based on an electrolyser capacity of 1.3GW, making it one of the largest facilities of its kind globally.
- **Stages of Development**:
  - **Stage 1 (Operational by 2023)**: A 10MW electrolyser producing renewable hydrogen for buses and trucks.
  - **Stage 2 (Operational by 2027)**: A 250MW electrolyser facility producing renewable hydrogen and sustainable carbon capture for methanol and e-kerosene.
  - **Stage 3 (Operational by 2030)**: Upgrading to 1.3GW electrolyser capacity, capturing more sustainable CO2 to meet the production goals.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2020**: The consortium plans to seek public co-funding to conduct a full feasibility study for the project. A final investment decision for the first stage could be made as soon as 2021, contingent on the feasibility study's confirmation of the project's viability.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2020**: Announcement of the partnership and project vision.
- **2021**: Anticipated final investment decision for the first stage of the project, pending feasibility study results.
- **2023**: Expected operational start for the first stage (10MW electrolyser).
- **2027**: Expected operational start for the second stage (250MW electrolyser).
- **2030**: Expected operational start for the third stage (1.3GW electrolyser).

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify exact job creation or loss figures related to the facility's development. However, the project is expected to contribute significantly to the decarbonization of transport and could create jobs associated with the production of sustainable fuels.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is part of a consortium that includes Copenhagen Airports, A.P. Moller - Maersk, DSV Panalpina, DFDS, SAS, and Ørsted. Therefore, it is not exclusively owned by Copenhagen Airports but is a joint venture among multiple stakeholders.
