### Evolutionary Timeline of the Blaest Facility in Aalborg, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2023**: Blaest inaugurated a new test rig on 26 September, designed to accommodate wind turbine blades over 120 metres in length. The first blade tested is the 115-metre-long B115 blade for Siemens Gamesa's SG 14-236 Direct Drive turbine. The test rig weighs over 1,000 tonnes and features advanced design for operational efficiency.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2023**: The new test rig was financed through a bank loan with a guarantee from the Export and Investment Fund of Denmark (EIFO). Blaest operates on a purely commercial basis and does not receive public grants.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2023**: The new test rig was inaugurated and began testing the B115 blade. Blaest also previously started testing the 115.5-metre blade for Vestas’ V236-15.0 MW wind turbine as part of the certification process.

#### 4. Labour
- No specific information regarding job creation or job loss was mentioned in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Blaest is jointly owned by Danish Technical University (DTU), FORCE Technology, and Det Norske Veritas (DNV).
