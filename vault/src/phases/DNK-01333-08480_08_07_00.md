### Evolutionary Timeline of the CS Wind Offshore Facility in Aalborg, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2024**: The first of three offshore substation jacket foundations, weighing approximately 2,445 tonnes and standing 60 metres tall, arrived at the production site in Aalborg, Denmark. CS Wind Offshore is responsible for the design, procurement, and manufacturing of the steel structures and jacket foundations for the offshore wind project developed by Dominion Energy.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2021**: A consortium comprising Semco Maritime and CS Wind Offshore (previously Bladt Industries) was awarded a contract for the supply of three 880 MW substations for the Coastal Virginia Offshore Wind (CVOW) project. This contract signifies a significant investment in the facility's capabilities to produce large-scale offshore wind infrastructure.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2024**: The first offshore substation jacket foundation departed from the Danish Port of Esbjerg en route to the US for installation at the CVOW project. This marks a key milestone in the project timeline, showcasing the operational capabilities of the Aalborg facility.
