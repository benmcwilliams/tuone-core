## DNK-01333-09985

### Evolutionary Timeline of Semco Maritime Facility in Aalborg, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2024**: The first of three 880 MW offshore substations for Dominion Energy’s Coastal Virginia Offshore Wind (CVOW) project arrived at the Semco Maritime production site in Aalborg for outfitting. Each substation will weigh 3,800 tonnes, excluding jacket foundations and pin piles. Semco Maritime is responsible for the design, procurement, and installation of electrical equipment, energy systems, and inter-array cables.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2021**: A consortium comprising Semco Maritime and Bladt Industries (now CS Wind Offshore) was awarded a contract for the supply of three offshore substations for the 2.6 GW CVOW project. Specific investment amounts are not detailed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2024**: The first offshore substation arrived at the production site in Aalborg.
- **December 2024**: The first offshore substation jacket foundation departed from the Danish Port of Esbjerg en route to the US for installation at the CVOW project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is part of a consortium with CS Wind Offshore (previously Bladt Industries). The ownership structure is not explicitly stated, but it indicates a joint venture arrangement for the project with shared responsibilities between Semco Maritime and CS Wind Offshore.
