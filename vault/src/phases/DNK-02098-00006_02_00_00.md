## DNK-02098-00006

### Evolutionary Timeline of the ABB Facility in Esbjerg, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09-11-2021**: ABB announced its involvement in the construction of a **50 MW seawater heat pump** in Esbjerg, which will be the world’s largest using CO2 as a refrigerant. The facility will utilize electro-thermal energy storage (ETES) technology, allowing for the simultaneous storage, use, and distribution of electricity, heat, and cold. The heat pump will supply approximately **235,000 MWh of heat annually** to around **100,000 local inhabitants**.
- **11-10-2023**: ABB was contracted to provide electrical engineering for a **1 GW hydrogen production facility** in Esbjerg, which is expected to produce up to **90,000 tons of hydrogen per year**. This facility will convert renewable electricity from offshore wind into green hydrogen, supporting decarbonization efforts in heavy industry and road transportation.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **11-10-2023**: The hydrogen project is backed by **H2 Energy Esbjerg**, a joint venture of Trafigura and H2 Energy, with an estimated investment of around **1 billion euros**. The specific value of ABB's contract for electrical engineering was not disclosed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09-11-2021**: Announcement of the construction of the **50 MW seawater heat pump**.
- **11-10-2023**: ABB's contract for electrical work at the **1 GW hydrogen project** was announced, with operations scheduled to start in **2027**.

#### 4. Labour
- **11-10-2023**: The hydrogen project is expected to create up to **60 permanent jobs**.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility for the hydrogen project is a **joint venture** between Trafigura and H2 Energy, indicating that ABB does not own the facility exclusively but is a contractor providing essential services. The ownership structure of the seawater heat pump facility is not explicitly stated, but ABB is the supplier of integrated electrical infrastructure, suggesting a collaborative role rather than full ownership.
