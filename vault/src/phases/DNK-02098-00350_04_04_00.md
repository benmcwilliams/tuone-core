## DNK-02098-00350

### Evolutionary Timeline of H2 Energy Facility in Esbjerg, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2021**: H2 Energy Europe announces plans to build a Power-to-X (PtX) facility in Esbjerg to convert green electricity into hydrogen, aiming for operation by 2024. The facility is expected to be Europe’s largest, utilizing surplus electricity from North Sea offshore wind farms.
- **September 2021**: The facility is confirmed to have a production capacity of 1 GW, with hydrogen intended for heavy land transport.
- **May 2022**: H2 Energy Europe places an order for the world’s largest electrolyser from Plug Power, which will enable the production of up to 100,000 tonnes of green hydrogen annually, sufficient for approximately 15,000 heavy-duty vehicles per day.
- **January 2024**: H2 Energy Europe secures environmental approval for the facility, which will produce CO2-neutral surplus heat for district heating in Esbjerg.
- **June 2024**: H2 Energy Europe obtains a building permit for the 1-GW green hydrogen project, confirming its operational plans.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2021**: The Danish Ministry of Foreign Affairs highlights the significant investment in the PtX facility, which aligns with Denmark's strategy to reduce carbon emissions by 70% by 2030.
- **May 2022**: The electrolyser order from Plug Power represents a major investment in the facility's technology, enhancing its production capabilities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2021**: H2 Energy Europe announces the acquisition of an 11-hectare site near Esbjerg for the PtX facility.
- **September 2021**: The project is confirmed to be operational by 2024.
- **May 2022**: The electrolyser order is noted as a significant milestone for the project.
- **January 2024**: Environmental approval is granted, moving the project closer to a Final Investment Decision (FID).
- **June 2024**: Building permit is obtained, allowing construction to commence.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2024**: The facility is expected to create approximately 60 permanent jobs and up to 700 jobs during the construction phase.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2022**: H2 Energy Europe is identified as a joint venture between Trafigura Pte Ltd and H2 Energy Holding AG, indicating that the facility is not solely owned by one entity but rather a collaborative effort between these two companies.
