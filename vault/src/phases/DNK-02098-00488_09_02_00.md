### Evolutionary Timeline of the MAN Facility in Esbjerg, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09-11-2021**: Announcement of the construction of a 50 MW seawater heat pump in Esbjerg, utilizing CO2 as a refrigerant. The facility is designed to generate low-carbon energy and will be powered by nearby wind farms. It is part of an electro-thermal energy storage (ETES) system that integrates electricity, heat, and cooling.
- **29-11-2024**: The facility officially starts operation with a total heating capacity of 70 MW, providing climate-neutral heat to Esbjerg and the neighboring town of Varde. It is now capable of supplying approximately 280 GWh of heat annually, covering the heating needs of 25,000 households. The facility operates alongside a 60 MW wood chip boiler and a 40 MW electric boiler for peak and backup loads.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09-11-2021**: The project is supported by ABB, which is supplying integrated electrical infrastructure. Specific investment amounts are not detailed in the articles, but the collaboration indicates significant financial backing for the facility's development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09-11-2021**: Announcement of the project and its specifications.
- **06-2023**: Installation of the heat pump project begins.
- **29-11-2024**: The facility is commissioned and begins operation.
