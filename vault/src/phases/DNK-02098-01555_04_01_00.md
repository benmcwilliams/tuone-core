### Evolutionary Timeline of the Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners Facility in Esbjerg, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2021**: Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (CIP) announced plans to build a Power-to-X plant in Esbjerg, which will convert power from offshore wind turbines into green ammonia. The facility will have a production capacity of 1 GW electrolysis, making it Europe’s largest production facility for CO2-free green ammonia. The ammonia produced will serve the agriculture sector as CO2-free green fertilizer and the shipping industry as CO2-free green fuel. Additionally, the excess heat generated will provide heating for approximately one-third of local households in Esbjerg.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2021**: CIP introduced its new Energy Transition Fund aimed at investing in Power-to-X and other next-generation renewable technologies. This fund is expected to attract investors, including PensionDanmark, to support the Esbjerg project and contribute to the decarbonization of agriculture and transportation sectors.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
