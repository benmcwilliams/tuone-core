### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ørsted Facility in Esbjerg, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2020**: The Port of Esbjerg announced plans to develop a multi-purpose fabrication facility aimed at the offshore wind market, with operations expected to begin in 2025. The facility will focus on producing wind turbine components such as blades, nacelles, towers, and foundations. The design includes a fabrication hall measuring 350 x 170 meters, a 700-meter-long load-out quay, and significant storage space.

- **March 2020**: Ørsted is constructing a new central warehouse at the Port of Esbjerg for storing essential components (gearboxes, generators, and transformers) for its offshore wind farms. This warehouse is being built at the former DFDS terminal and is expected to be completed in summer 2020.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2020**: The Port of Esbjerg is seeking investors to help build the new fabrication facility, indicating a significant investment opportunity tied to the offshore wind industry.

- **March 2020**: Ørsted has entered into an agreement to rent the new warehouse, which is part of its investment in expanding operational capacity at the Port of Esbjerg.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2020**: Announcement of plans for the multi-purpose fabrication facility, with operations expected to start in 2025.

- **March 2020**: Announcement of the construction of the new central warehouse, with completion slated for summer 2020.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2020**: The development of the fabrication facility is expected to create job opportunities in the region, although specific job creation numbers were not mentioned.

- **March 2020**: The establishment of the new warehouse is anticipated to support Ørsted's operational needs, potentially leading to job creation related to the storage and management of components.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2020**: The fabrication facility will be owned by the Port of Esbjerg, with clients (including Ørsted) leasing the space to install their own fabrication equipment.

- **March 2020**: Ørsted is renting the new warehouse, indicating that while they are not the owners of the facility, they are securing operational space for their components.
