#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date**: 08-02-2022
  - Seaborg Technologies has developed a molten salt thermal energy storage technology that utilizes sodium hydroxide to store large amounts of intermittent wind and solar power. 
  - The facility's storage capacity ranges from 250 MWh to 5 GWh, with a specific mention of a 1 GWh facility capable of serving around 100,000 households for 10 hours of discharge.
  - The technology is based on two-tank molten salt storage designs, originally developed for concentrated solar power plants, and operates at high temperatures (700°C) for efficient energy extraction.
