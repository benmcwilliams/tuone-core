### Evolutionary Timeline of the Danish Ministry for Climate, Energy and Utilities Facility in North Sea, DNK

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 04-02-2021
   - **Details:** The facility will be an artificial island located 80 km off the coast of Jutland, serving as an offshore energy hub. It will function as a power plant that gathers and distributes green electricity from hundreds of surrounding wind turbines. The initial capacity will be 5GW, with plans to expand to 12GW. The island will cover at least 120,000 square meters and is expected to provide green energy to three million European households.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 04-02-2021
   - **Details:** The project will be developed through a public-private partnership model, with the Danish state owning the majority of the island. Specific details regarding private partnerships will be outlined in a forthcoming tender.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 04-02-2021
   - **Details:** The Danish government has reached a landmark agreement to construct the energy hub, marking the start of the project. This initiative is described as the largest construction project in Danish history and is part of Denmark's broader commitment to sustainable energy production.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 04-02-2021
   - **Details:** The climate minister emphasized that the project will contribute to growth and green jobs, although specific job creation numbers were not provided in the article.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 04-02-2021
   - **Details:** The facility will be owned primarily by the Danish state, with plans to engage private partners through a tender process for the development of the energy hub.
