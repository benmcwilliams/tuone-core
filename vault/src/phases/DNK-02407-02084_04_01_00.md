### Everfuel Facility in Brande, Denmark - Evolutionary Timeline

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production**: The facility produces green hydrogen using a setup that combines an onshore Siemens Gamesa SWT 3.0-113 DD wind turbine with an electrolyzer stack from Green Hydrogen Systems.
- **Capacity and Innovations**: The facility is designed to enable industrial-scale volumes of green hydrogen production. The integration of battery technology aims to enhance grid stability and increase the operational time of electrolyzers, allowing for greater hydrogen output.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Regulatory Support**: In the summer of 2021, Danish authorities granted the Brande Hydrogen test site official regulatory test zone status, facilitating operations outside existing electricity regulations. This regulatory support is crucial for the development and testing of the hydrogen production system.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **First Production**: On November 11, 2021, it was announced that the Brande facility produced its first green hydrogen as part of the testing and commissioning phase. This marked a significant milestone in the project’s development.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned in the article, the establishment of the hydrogen production facility and its operations are expected to contribute positively to job creation in the region, particularly in the clean technology and renewable energy sectors.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Partnership**: The facility is a collaborative project involving Siemens Gamesa and Everfuel. Everfuel is responsible for distributing the produced green hydrogen to hydrogen stations in Denmark, indicating a partnership rather than exclusive ownership by either company.
