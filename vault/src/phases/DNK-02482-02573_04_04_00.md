### Evolutionary Timeline of the Topsoe Facility in Herning, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2022**: Topsoe announced the construction of the “world’s largest electrolyzer production facility” in Herning, Denmark, with an initial production capacity of 500 MW, scalable up to 5 GW. The facility is focused on Solid Oxide Electrolyzer Cells (SOEC) technology.
- **December 2023**: The facility is expected to be operational by 2024, with a confirmed capacity of 500 MW and the option to upscale.
- **June 2024**: Topsoe, in collaboration with ABB and Fluor, is working on a standardized concept for building future SOEC factories, leveraging the experience gained from the Herning facility.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2022**: Topsoe revealed a total investment of over DKK 2 billion (approximately $268.8 million) for the construction of the Herning facility, marking it as the single largest investment in the company's history.
- **December 2023**: Topsoe secured a €94 million grant from the EU’s Innovation Fund to support the development of the SOEC factory in Herning.
- **April 2024**: Topsoe announced plans for a $400 million investment in a new electrolyzer factory in the US, which, combined with the Herning facility, would position Topsoe as the largest SOEC manufacturer globally.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2022**: Announcement of the construction of the facility.
- **September 2022**: Final Investment Decision (FID) was taken for the facility, with construction expected to start in the second half of 2022.
- **December 2023**: Confirmation that the facility is expected to be operational by 2024.
- **June 2024**: The facility is still on track for completion, with ongoing collaboration for future projects.
