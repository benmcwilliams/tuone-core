### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ørsted Facility in Greater Copenhagen, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2020**: The Greater Copenhagen facility is planned to have a capacity of 1.3GW, primarily powered by offshore wind. It aims to produce hydrogen for buses and trucks, as well as e-fuels derived from green hydrogen and captured CO2 for shipping and aviation. The facility is expected to produce 250,000 tonnes of sustainable fuel per year. A 10MW pilot project is scheduled to begin as soon as 2023, with a larger 250MW expansion planned for 2027.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2020**: The facility is in the feasibility study stage, with a view to making a final investment decision in 2021. Specific investment amounts have not been disclosed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2020**: The project is currently in the feasibility study phase, with plans for a pilot project (10MW) to commence in 2023 and a larger capacity (250MW) targeted for 2027. The completion date for the entire facility is set for 2030.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2020**: There are no specific details provided in the articles regarding job creation or job loss associated with the facility's development.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2020**: The facility is a collaborative project involving Ørsted, Maersk, DSV Panalpina, DFDS, and SAS, indicating that it is not solely owned by Ørsted but is part of a joint venture with multiple stakeholders.
