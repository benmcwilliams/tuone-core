## DNK-03469-04221

### Evolutionary Timeline of the SAS Facility in Greater Copenhagen, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2020**: The Greater Copenhagen facility is identified as a gigawatt-scale project with a planned capacity of 1.3GW. It aims to produce hydrogen for buses and trucks, as well as e-fuels derived from green hydrogen and captured CO2 for shipping and aviation. The facility is expected to produce 250,000 tonnes of sustainable fuel per year. A 10MW pilot is planned to commence as soon as 2023, with a larger 250MW expansion targeted for 2027.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2020**: The facility is in the feasibility study stage, with a view to making a final investment decision in 2021. Specific investment amounts have not been disclosed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2020**: The project is currently in the feasibility study phase, with plans for a pilot project to begin in 2023 and a larger capacity expansion to follow by 2027. The completion date for the entire facility is set for 2030.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2020**: There are no specific details provided in the articles regarding job creation or job loss associated with the facility at this stage.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2020**: The facility is a collaborative project involving multiple developers, including Orsted, Maersk, DSV Panalpina, DFDS, and SAS. The ownership structure is not specified as exclusive; it appears to be a joint venture among these companies.
