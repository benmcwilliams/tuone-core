### Evolutionary Timeline of the Green Fuels for Denmark Facility in Greater Copenhagen, DNK

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2021**: The Green Fuels for Denmark project is designed to produce sustainable fuels with a total planned electrolysis capacity of 1.3 GW. The project will be developed in three phases: approximately 10 MW, 250 MW, and 1,300 MW. The facility aims to have a decarbonisation potential of 850,000 tonnes.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2021**: Ørsted and HOFOR entered into an agreement to secure green power for the Green Fuels for Denmark project, which is part of the potential 1.3 GW capacity. The agreement includes the offtake of power from HOFOR’s 250 MW Aflandshage offshore wind farm, which is expected to deliver first power in 2024/2025, subject to regulatory approvals.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2021**: The partnership behind Green Fuels for Denmark includes A.P. Moller – Maersk, DSV Panalpina, DFDS, SAS, Copenhagen Airports, and Ørsted. The project is backed by Molslinjen, the City of Copenhagen, and the Capital Region of Denmark. The first phase of the project is being developed in collaboration with Nel, Haldor Topsøe, and Everfuel.
