### Evolutionary Timeline of RWE Facility in Lemvig, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04/2023**: RWE began construction of the onshore substation for the Thor offshore wind farm, which has a production capacity of 1 GW. The facility will feature 72 SG 14-236 DD wind turbines, with half utilizing CO2-reduced steel towers and 40 equipped with recyclable rotor blades. The project is expected to generate enough clean energy to power over one million households in Denmark.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **11/2024**: RWE secured the final permit from the Danish Energy Agency, marking the completion of regulatory requirements for the offshore construction of the Thor project. The project is part of RWE's broader investment in sustainable technology, including the use of recyclable materials in turbine construction.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04/2023**: Ground-breaking ceremony for the onshore substation held in Lemvig, marking the start of the Thor project.
- **Spring 2025**: RWE plans to commence offshore construction.
- **Early 2025**: Preparation of the seabed for foundation installations is scheduled.
- **2026**: Turbine installation phase is set to begin.
- **End of 2027**: The Thor offshore wind farm is projected to be fully operational.

#### 4. Labour
- **04/2023**: RWE committed to employing at least 30 vocational trainees for the project and expects to recruit at least 60 local people for the operation and maintenance of the Thor offshore wind farm.
- **11/2024**: The operations hub at the port of Thorsminde is expected to create 50-60 jobs in the local community.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- RWE is the sole owner of the Thor offshore wind farm facility, as indicated by the absence of any mention of joint ventures or partnerships in the articles.
