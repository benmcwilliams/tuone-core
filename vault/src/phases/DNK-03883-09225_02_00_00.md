### Evolutionary Timeline of the Innargi Facility in Aarhus, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2023**: Innargi is specialized in the utilization of geothermal energy for district heating. The facility in Aarhus is set to be the largest geothermal district heating system in the EU, with a total capacity of **110 MW** across **7 sites**.
- **November 2023**: Drilling for the geothermal project commenced, with the first two wells completed at the Port of Aarhus and Skejby. The drilling of a third well began in the same location in Skejby.
- **June 2024**: Construction began on the first heating plant in Skejby, which will transfer geothermal heat to Kredsløb’s district heating network using heat exchangers and heat pumps. The system aims to cover **20%** of the heating needs of Aarhus.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2024**: AGR was contracted to provide technical consultancy for the Aarhus geothermal project, leveraging their expertise in well and reservoir engineering for geothermal projects.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2023**: Drilling for the geothermal project started.
- **February 2024**: Drilling moved to the Skejby site after initial work at the Aarhus Harbour.
