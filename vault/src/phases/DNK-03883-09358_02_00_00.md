### Evolutionary Timeline of the Kredsløb Facility in Aarhus, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **03/2023**: The facility is set to produce geothermal heating, aiming to provide green heat to 35,000 homes in Aarhus. The project is a collaboration between Innargi A/S and Kredsløb, with plans for the first large-scale geothermal heating plant in Denmark.
- **11/2023**: Drilling operations commence at Aarhus Harbour, marking the transition from planning to action. The drilling rig is set to reach a depth of 2.5 kilometers, with the first facility in Skejby expected to be operational by 2025, and another facility on Sumatravej anticipated to be completed by early 2027.
- **06/2024**: Construction begins on the first heating plant in Skejby, which will transfer geothermal heat to Kredsløb’s district heating network. The geothermal system aims to cover 20% of Aarhus's heating needs and is part of a larger plan involving a total of seven sites.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **03/2023**: The new law adopted by the Danish Parliament is expected to encourage investments in geothermal heating projects, providing a stable framework for development and reducing risks for consumers and district heating companies.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **03/2023**: Announcement of the geothermal heating project in Aarhus, with drilling locations identified.
- **11/2023**: Drilling operations officially start at Aarhus Harbour.
- **02/2024**: Drilling moves to the Skejby site after initial operations at the harbour.
- **06/2024**: Construction of the first heating plant begins, following the completion of test wells in Skejby.

#### 4. Labour
- **11/2023**: The project is expected to create jobs related to the drilling and construction phases, although specific job creation numbers are not mentioned in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is a joint venture between **Innargi A/S** and **Kredsløb**, with both companies collaborating on the development and operation of the geothermal heating system.
