### Encavis Facility in Ringkøbing, Denmark - Evolutionary Timeline

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2022**: Encavis AG, in partnership with BELECTRIC and GreenGo Energy Group, began construction of a 12.6 MWp solar park near Ringkøbing. This facility is notable for deploying tracker systems with bifacial modules, which can absorb solar radiation from both sides, enhancing efficiency and reducing electricity production costs.
- **September 2023**: Encavis reached the ready-to-build stage for a new solar project in Ringkøbing with a capacity of 132 MW. This facility will also utilize bifacial solar modules and single-axis trackers, similar to the earlier project.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2022**: The 12.6 MWp solar park project was part of Encavis' strategic development pipeline of over 500 MW of solar power projects in Denmark, indicating significant investment in the region.
- **September 2023**: Encavis announced plans for the 132 MW solar project, which is part of a broader strategy to develop 600 MW of solar capacity across three projects in collaboration with GreenGo Energy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2022**: Start of construction for the 12.6 MWp solar park, expected to begin delivering green energy in the second half of 2022.
- **September 2023**: Encavis announced that the 132 MW solar project has reached the ready-to-build stage, with expectations to begin commercial operations in the first quarter of 2026.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2022**: The construction of the 12.6 MWp facility is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers were not detailed in the articles.
- **September 2023**: The announcement of the 132 MW project suggests further job creation, but again, specific figures were not provided.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2022**: Encavis AG is involved in the solar park project in collaboration with BELECTRIC and GreenGo Energy, indicating a partnership rather than exclusive ownership.
- **September 2023**: The 132 MW project is also developed in partnership with GreenGo Energy, suggesting that Encavis does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a joint development effort.
