### Evolutionary Timeline of Ørsted Facility in Fredericia, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2019**: Ørsted announced plans for a 2 MW electrolysis plant with hydrogen storage in Fredericia, aimed at producing green hydrogen for the transport sector. The facility is expected to generate approximately 600 kg of hydrogen daily, sufficient to power 20-30 buses and test its use in trucks and taxis.

- **November 2020**: The facility's project, named H2RES, confirmed the construction of the 2 MW electrolysis plant, utilizing energy from Ørsted’s Siemens Gamesa 3.6 MW offshore wind turbines located at Avedøre Power Station.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2019**: The Danish government allocated DKK 128 million ($19 million) for two large-scale hydrogen projects, including the HySynergy project in Fredericia. Ørsted received DKK 34.6 million ($5.2 million) from the Energy Technology Development and Demonstration Programme (EUDP) to support the H2RES project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2019**: Announcement of the HySynergy project in Fredericia as part of the Danish government's initiative to develop green hydrogen projects.
  
- **November 2020**: Confirmation of funding and project development for the H2RES project, which includes the construction of the electrolysis plant.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify exact job creation or job loss figures related to the Ørsted facility in Fredericia. However, the development of the hydrogen production facility is expected to contribute positively to job creation in the clean energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Ørsted is developing the facility in partnership with several companies, including Everfuel Europe A/S, NEL Hydrogen A/S, GreenHydrogen A/S, DSV Panalpina A/S, Hydrogen Denmark, and Energinet Elsystemansvar A/S. This indicates that the facility is not under exclusive ownership by Ørsted but is a collaborative project with multiple partners involved.
