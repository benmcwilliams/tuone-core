### Evolutionary Timeline of the GreenHydrogen Facility in Fredericia, Denmark

1. **Technology and Operations**
   - **05-11-2020**: The GreenHydrogen facility, part of the H2RES project, is set to produce renewable hydrogen (H2) using offshore wind power. The facility will feature a 2-MW electrolysis plant with hydrogen storage, aiming to generate approximately 600 kg of hydrogen daily. This production capacity is intended to support road transport, including buses, trucks, and potentially taxis.

2. **Investment and Development**
   - **05-11-2020**: The project received funding of Dkr34.6 million (US$5.2 million) from the Energy Technology Development and Demonstration Programme (EUDP) to support the development of the facility and its operations.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**
   - **05-11-2020**: Announcement of the H2RES project, detailing the construction of the electrolysis plant and the use of energy from Ørsted’s offshore wind turbines for hydrogen production.

4. **Labour**
   - No specific information regarding job creation or job loss was mentioned in the article.

5. **Ownership Structure**
   - The facility is part of a collaborative project involving multiple partners, including Ørsted, Nel Hydrogen, Everfuel Europe, GreenHydrogen, Hydrogen Denmark, Energinet Elsystemansvar, and DSV Panalpina. Therefore, it is not exclusively owned by any single company but is a joint venture among these partners.
