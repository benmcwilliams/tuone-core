### Evolutionary Timeline of the European Energy Facility in Aabenraa, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2020**: European Energy announced plans to construct a 300 MW solar farm in Aabenraa, which will be the largest facility of its kind in Northern Europe. The facility is designed to supply enough green power for over 75,000 Danish households and is expected to contribute to annual CO2 emissions reductions of approximately 110,000 tons.
- **July 2023**: The facility's capacity was updated to 304 MW following the completion of construction. Additionally, an e-methanol facility with an annual production capacity of 42,000 tonnes is planned to be operational in 2024, powered by the connected solar park.
- **September 2023**: The solar project was confirmed to be online since the first half of 2023, and the e-methanol facility is under construction.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2020**: The total investment for the solar farm project was reported to exceed 1 billion Danish kroner.
- **July 2023**: European Energy announced plans to sell a 49% stake in the 304 MW solar plant to Mitsui, which is expected to accelerate progress in the e-methanol industry.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2020**: The local council in Aabenraa voted in favor of the solar farm project, providing the green light for construction to begin.
- **Early 2021**: Construction of the solar farm was expected to commence.
- **End of 2021**: Grid connection for the solar farm was anticipated to be completed.
- **July 2023**: The sale of a 49% stake in the solar plant to Mitsui was announced, subject to approval from Danish and EU authorities.
- **September 2023**: The sale of the minority stake was completed, and the solar project was confirmed to be operational.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2023**: European Energy announced the sale of a 49% stake in the facility to Mitsui, indicating that European Energy retains a 51% ownership stake, thus making it a joint venture for the solar park and the associated e-methanol facility.
