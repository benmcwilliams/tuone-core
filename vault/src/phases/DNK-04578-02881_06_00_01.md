### Evolutionary Timeline of the European Energy Facility in Kassø, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **02-11-2020**: European Energy received approval to build a **300MW solar farm** near Kassø, which is the largest solar project in Northern Europe. The facility is designed to supply enough green power for over **75,000 Danish households** and is expected to reduce annual CO2 emissions by approximately **110,000 tons**. Construction was anticipated to start in early **2021**, with grid connection expected by the end of **2021**.
- **25-09-2023**: The facility has expanded to include a **combined solar and e-methanol plant** with a total capacity of **304MW**. The solar farm has been operational since the first half of **2023**, now generating enough clean energy to power over **80,000 European households**. The e-methanol facility, currently under construction, will produce **42,000 tonnes of e-methanol annually** using renewable energy to generate hydrogen and captured CO₂.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **25-09-2023**: Mitsui acquired a **49% stake** in Kassø Midco, the owner of the solar and e-methanol facility, from European Energy. This acquisition was completed after receiving necessary clearances from Danish and European authorities. The partnership aims to develop sustainable e-methanol projects based on renewable energy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **02-11-2020**: Approval for the solar farm project was granted, with construction expected to begin in early **2021** and grid connection by the end of **2021**.
- **25-09-2023**: The solar farm became operational in the first half of **2023**. The e-methanol facility is under construction, with the electrolyser installation planned for later in **2023** and expected to be operational in the first half of **2024**.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **25-09-2023**: European Energy initially owned the facility exclusively but has since entered into a joint venture with Mitsui, which now holds a **49% stake** in Kassø Midco, the owner of the facility.
