## DNK-05270-05699

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Norwegian Hydrogen Facility in Hjørring, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Production**: Norwegian Hydrogen is constructing hydrogen plants in Hellesylt, Norway, and Hjørring, Denmark. The facility in Hjørring is part of a broader initiative to produce green hydrogen and green ammonia.
- **Future Capacity**: Norwegian Hydrogen is also developing the FjordH2 project in Ålesund, Norway, which is expected to have an annual production capacity of 40,000 tonnes of green hydrogen by 2030.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment by Fortescue**: On **30-10-2023**, Fortescue Metals Group announced an investment of A$13.5 million (approximately NOK 100 million or $8.96 million) to acquire a 12.5% stake in Norwegian Hydrogen, making it the third largest shareholder. This investment is aimed at scaling up the green hydrogen value chain.
- **Additional Funding**: Norwegian Hydrogen raised approximately NOK 50 million ($4.4 million) from existing and new investors, valuing the company at around NOK 750 million ($66.7 million).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Construction Announcement**: As of **30-10-2023**, Norwegian Hydrogen is actively building its first hydrogen plants in Hellesylt and Hjørring.
- **Future Projects**: The FjordH2 project, one of the largest in the Nordic region, is also in the pipeline, with plans to establish a facility in Ålesund, Norway.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: While specific job creation numbers for the Hjørring facility have not been detailed in the articles, the overall expansion of Norwegian Hydrogen and its projects is expected to contribute to job creation in the green hydrogen sector across Norway and Denmark.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Ownership**: Following Fortescue's investment, Norwegian Hydrogen is no longer exclusively owned by its original stakeholders. Fortescue now holds a 12.5% stake, indicating a joint ownership structure with other investors, including Mitsui, which had previously invested in the company.
