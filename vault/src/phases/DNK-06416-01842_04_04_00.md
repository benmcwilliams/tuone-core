## DNK-06416-01842

### Evolutionary Timeline of Ørsted Facility at Avedøre Power Station, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2021**: Ørsted, in collaboration with HOFOR, announced the potential development of the "Green Fuels for Denmark" project, which aims to produce sustainable fuels in the Greater Copenhagen area. The project is envisioned to have a total electrolysis capacity of 1.3 GW, with phases of 10 MW, 250 MW, and 1,300 MW. The facility will utilize power from HOFOR’s 250 MW Aflandshage offshore wind farm, which is located approximately ten kilometers from Amager.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2021**: The partnership between Ørsted and HOFOR is aimed at securing green power for the Green Fuels for Denmark project, which is expected to require significant investment. The project is backed by various stakeholders, including A.P. Moller – Maersk, DSV Panalpina, DFDS, SAS, and Copenhagen Airports, indicating a strong collaborative investment approach.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2021**: The agreement between Ørsted and HOFOR was announced, detailing the cooperation to enable Ørsted to offtake power from the Aflandshage offshore wind farm. The project is subject to regulatory approvals, with HOFOR expecting to deliver first power from the wind farm in 2024/2025. The Green Fuels for Denmark project is positioned to meet the power demand for its first phase and potentially supply hydrogen for a proposed hydrogen-powered ferry.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2021**: The Green Fuels for Denmark project is anticipated to create significant job opportunities as it aims to establish a new industrial stronghold for Denmark in sustainable fuel production. The project has a decarbonisation potential of 850,000 tonnes, which suggests a substantial impact on the local economy and job market.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2021**: Ørsted owns the Avedøre Power Station, where the Green Fuels for Denmark project could be located. The facility operates in partnership with HOFOR, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic initiatives surrounding the Ørsted facility at Avedøre Power Station, highlighting its role in advancing sustainable fuel production in Denmark.

### Technology and Operations
- "Ørsted and HOFOR will work towards enabling Ørsted to offtake the power produced at HOFOR’s 250 MW Aflandshage offshore wind farm project."
- "Green Fuels for Denmark is Denmark’s most ambitious vision for the large-scale production of sustainable fuels and has a decarbonisation potential of 850,000 tonnes."
- "The vision for the partnership is that Green Fuels for Denmark will be built in three phases with approximately 10 MW, 250 MW, and 1,300 MW in total electrolysis capacity, respectively."

### Investment and Development
- "To reduce the price, the production of sustainable fuels must be matured and build-out at industrial scale, and the costs must be reduced."
- "For this to be feasible, the industry must join forces with governments to create a framework that provides incentives for private investments in large-scale sustainable fuel production."

### Project Status and Milestones
- "HOFOR expects the project to deliver first power in 2024/2025."
- "Ørsted will continue to pursue opportunities to secure green power from additional sources for Green Fuels for Denmark towards the commissioning of the planned energy hub at the Danish island Bornholm."

### Labour

### Ownership Structure
