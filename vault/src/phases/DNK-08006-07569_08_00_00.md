### Evolutionary Timeline of the Aquila European Renewables Income Fund Facility in Svindbaek, Denmark

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **March 2020:** The facility, known as the Svindbaek wind farm, has an installed capacity of 22.4 MW. The acquisition of three additional wind turbines, referred to as Svindbaek 2, adds 9.6 MW of capacity. The facility has been operational since 2018 and is designed to save approximately 18,337 tonnes of carbon dioxide emissions annually, totaling 458,433 tonnes over its projected lifetime.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **March 2020:** Aquila European Renewables Income Fund (AERIF) completed the acquisition of Svindbaek 2 for approximately €13.2 million. This investment is part of a larger commitment where AERIF has invested about 63.1% and committed another 18.9% of its total proceeds (€194.3 million) towards the Svindbaek Vindkraft HoldCo.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **March 2020:** The acquisition of Svindbaek 2 is announced, completing the acquisition of all turbines associated with the Svindbaek wind farm. The project benefits from a Danish premium tariff structured as a contract for difference, expected to last for approximately nine years.
