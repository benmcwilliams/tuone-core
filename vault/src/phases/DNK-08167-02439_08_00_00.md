### Evolutionary Timeline of the Siemens Gamesa Facility in Kriegers Flak, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2019**: The facility is part of Vattenfall's Kriegers Flak offshore wind farm, which will feature 72 Siemens Gamesa 8.4-167 turbines. The total production capacity of the wind farm is 604 MW, which is expected to cover the annual electricity consumption of approximately 600,000 Danish households.
- **June 2021**: All 72 wind turbines have been successfully installed, marking Kriegers Flak as Denmark's largest offshore wind farm. The installation was completed ahead of schedule despite challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2019**: The project involves significant investment in the construction of 72 monopile foundations, with the first pipe structure completed by EEW SPC. The transportation of these foundations to the project site is scheduled to start in May 2020.
- **June 2021**: The completion of turbine installation indicates the successful execution of the project, which is expected to increase Danish wind production by approximately 16%.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2019**: Announcement of the completion of the first pipe for the monopile foundations.
- **February 2021**: The first turbine installation begins.
- **June 5, 2021**: The last of the 72 turbines is installed, with final testing and certification to follow for the inauguration of the wind farm after summer 2021.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2021**: The successful installation of the turbines and the operational capacity of the wind farm are expected to contribute positively to job creation in the region, particularly in the service and maintenance sectors associated with the wind farm.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is part of a project led by Vattenfall, with Siemens Gamesa providing the wind turbines. The ownership structure indicates a partnership rather than exclusive ownership by Siemens Gamesa, as Vattenfall is the primary developer of the Kriegers Flak offshore wind farm.
