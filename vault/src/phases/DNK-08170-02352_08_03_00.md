### Evolutionary Timeline of the MHI Vestas Facility in Lindø, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **06/2022**: Vestas announced the development of the V236-15.0 MW offshore wind turbine, which will be produced at the Lindø facility. The turbine is designed to produce 80 GWh/year and will be the tallest and most powerful wind turbine in the world upon installation.
- **11/2022**: The nacelle for the V236-15.0 MW prototype, assembled at the Lindø factory, was delivered to the Østerild National Test Centre for installation. This marked a significant step towards the turbine's serial production, expected to begin in 2024.
- **08/2023**: The prototype nacelle was completed at the Lindø factory, and the turbine reached its nominal power rating of 15 MW after installation at the test center.
- **06/2024**: Vestas contracted Maersk for the transport of the powertrains for the V236 turbine, indicating ongoing operations and logistics related to the nacelle assembly at Lindø.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **06/2022**: Vestas secured a EUR 475 million green loan from the European Investment Bank (EIB) to support research, development, and innovation activities, including the V236-15.0 MW turbine development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2022**: The V236-15.0 MW prototype was installed at the Østerild National Test Centre, marking a key milestone in the project.
- **08/2023**: The prototype set a world record for the most power output by a single wind turbine in 24 hours, producing 363 MWh.
- **12/2023**: Vestas received a type certificate for the 15 MW wind turbine, a crucial step for its commercial production.

#### 4. Labour
- **11/2023**: Vestas announced plans to hire 300 new employees in its offshore wind turbine production business, primarily at the Lindø facility, while also making 200 job cuts in its onshore division. This reflects a strategic shift towards increasing offshore production capabilities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not provide specific details regarding the ownership structure of the Lindø facility, but it is implied that Vestas operates the facility as part of its manufacturing network, likely under full ownership.
