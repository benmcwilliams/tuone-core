## DNK-08262-02439

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Siemens Gamesa Facility in Øresund, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: Siemens Gamesa has contracted Cadeler for the transportation and installation of 26 SG 11.0-200 DD wind turbines at the Aflandshage offshore wind farm, located in Øresund, approximately ten kilometers off the south coast of Copenhagen. The installation is set to begin in **2026**. The project will utilize a new O-class vessel equipped with a larger main crane capable of lifting 1,600 metric tons.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: The Aflandshage wind project has been in planning for eleven years. The project is expected to provide electricity equivalent to the consumption of **300,000 households**. HOFOR (Greater Copenhagen Utility) is the developer, and they are awaiting final approval of the project economy from the Municipality of Copenhagen. The project is part of a broader initiative to support the **Green Fuels for Denmark** project, which aims to produce sustainable fuels in the Greater Copenhagen area.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: The Danish Energy Agency resumed processing the Aflandshage wind project after a temporary suspension. The project involves the installation of gravity-based foundations surrounded by large stones to create an artificial reef, promoting local biodiversity and marine life. The project is a significant step towards the green transition in the Øresund region.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2023**: While specific job creation figures are not mentioned, the project is expected to contribute to local employment opportunities through the installation and operation of the wind farm, which will support the green transition in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2023**: Siemens Gamesa is involved in the Aflandshage wind project through a contract with HOFOR, indicating a collaborative relationship rather than exclusive ownership. HOFOR is the primary developer of the wind farm, suggesting a joint venture or partnership model rather than full ownership by Siemens Gamesa.
