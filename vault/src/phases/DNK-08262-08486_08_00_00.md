## DNK-08262-08486

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Cadeler Facility in Øresund, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: Cadeler A/S signed a contract with Siemens Gamesa for the transportation and installation of 26 SG 11.0-200 DD wind turbines at the Aflandshage offshore wind farm, located about ten kilometers off the south coast of Copenhagen. The installation is set to begin in **2026**. Cadeler will utilize one of its O-class vessels, which will be upgraded with a new main crane featuring a lifting capacity of **1,600 metric tons** at a radius of **40 meters**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: The planning of the Aflandshage wind project has taken **eleven years**. The project is being developed by HOFOR (Greater Copenhagen Utility), which is awaiting final approval of the project economy from the Municipality of Copenhagen. The project is expected to provide electricity equivalent to the consumption of **300,000 households**.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: The Danish Energy Agency suspended the open-door scheme for wind farm applications in February, citing potential breaches of EU law. However, in March, the agency resumed processing the Aflandshage wind project. The project involves the placement of gravity-based foundations on the seabed, surrounded by large stones to create an artificial stone reef to foster local biodiversity.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2023**: While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned, the project is expected to contribute to the green transition and may lead to job creation in the region as the wind farm becomes operational.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2023**: The facility is developed by HOFOR, with Cadeler contracted for installation services. There is no indication of joint ownership; Cadeler is involved as a contractor rather than an owner of the facility.
