## DNK-08637-08788

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Energinet Facility in North Sea Energy Island, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2023**: The North Sea energy island is set to have a production capacity of 3 GW of zero carbon electricity by 2033, which is enough to power approximately 3 million households. By 2040, this capacity is expected to increase to 10 GW, sufficient to power 10 million homes in Northern Europe. The facility will act as a hub to gather power from nearby offshore wind farms and distribute it to Denmark and neighboring countries.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2023**: The project has a total value of DKK 225 million (approximately EUR 30 million) for the establishment of the electricity infrastructure on the artificial island. This investment is part of a broader initiative to develop offshore electrical infrastructure and contribute to the green transition.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2023**: Ramboll is awarded a contract to assist Energinet in establishing the electricity infrastructure for the North Sea energy island. This project is noted as one of the world’s first and largest offshore electrical infrastructure projects of its kind. The project is currently in the advisory phase, with Ramboll's involvement marking their fifth contract related to energy islands.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2023**: The project is expected to employ 35 full-time experts from Ramboll over the next 10 years, contributing to job creation in the renewable energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2023**: Energinet is the Danish transmission system operator responsible for the electrical facilities and cabling connecting the energy island with surrounding wind farms and the mainland. The ownership structure is not explicitly stated, but Energinet is the primary operator of the facility, indicating a significant level of control over the project. 

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Energinet facility in the North Sea energy island, highlighting its role in advancing renewable energy infrastructure in Denmark and Europe.
