### Evolutionary Timeline of the Sønderborg Varme Facility in Sønderborg, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2013**: Sønderborg Varme, in collaboration with DONG Energy, established a geothermal heating plant. However, this plant was taken out of commission due to issues with the reinjection of fluids back into the subsurface.
- **April 2023**: Sønderborg Varme is investigating the potential for a new geothermal heating project, with expectations to deliver heat to the district heating network by the end of 2026 or beginning of 2027. The facility anticipates an addition of 50,000 MWh per year due to expansions from recent mergers with district heating companies in Augustenborg and Gråsten.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: The investigation into geothermal heating is part of a broader vision for carbon-neutral district heating. Innargi, the company collaborating with Sønderborg Varme, is expected to utilize a different technology compared to the previous plant, indicating potential investment in new infrastructure.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: The parties involved expect to take about one year to determine commercial circumstances and analyze local conditions before actual drilling and construction can begin. The project is positioned as a significant opportunity for the expansion of the district heating network in Sønderborg.
