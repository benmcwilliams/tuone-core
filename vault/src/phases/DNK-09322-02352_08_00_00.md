## DNK-09322-02352

### Evolutionary Timeline of MHI Vestas Facility at Lotus Creek Wind Farm, Queensland, Australia

1. **Technology and Operations**:
   - The Lotus Creek Wind Farm will produce renewable energy using 46 V162-6.2MW wind turbines supplied by Vestas. 
   - The total production capacity of the wind farm is 285MW.
   - Vestas will also provide a long-term service agreement to ensure optimal energy production.

2. **Investment and Development**:
   - The project has a total investment of $1.3 billion (A$1.96 billion).
   - Financial backing includes $624.3 million from the Queensland Government’s Renewable Energy and Hydrogen Jobs Fund and an additional $300 million sourced from coal royalties, as announced in the 2023–24 Budget.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**:
   - **August 2024**: CS Energy announced the acquisition of the Lotus Creek Wind Farm, marking the project’s entry into the construction phase.
   - The delivery of wind turbines is scheduled to begin in Q1 2026, with commissioning expected in Q4 2026.

4. **Labour**:
   - At the peak of construction, the project will employ up to 400 people on-site.
   - Once operational, it is expected to create 10–15 long-term regional service roles.

5. **Ownership Structure**:
   - CS Energy has acquired the Lotus Creek Wind Farm, making it the first 100% publicly owned wind farm in Queensland. 
   - Vestas is involved in the project as a supplier and service provider but does not own the facility.
