### Evolutionary Timeline of the MHI Vestas Facility in Osterild, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2018**: Vestas installed a prototype of its V150 turbine model at the Osterild test site. The turbine has a capacity of 4.2MW and is over 200 meters tall. It is designed with an upgraded gearbox, generator system, converter, and new blade design, which includes 73.7-meter blades that increase energy production by 21% compared to the previous model (V136-3.45MW).
  
- **October 2021**: Vestas announced plans to install the V236-15MW offshore prototype wind turbine at the Osterild national test center. This turbine will be 280 meters tall and is expected to produce 80 gigawatt-hours annually. The installation is scheduled for the second half of 2022, with the first power output planned for Q4 2022. The prototype will undergo testing to obtain a Type Certificate, crucial for serial production expected in 2024.

- **November 2021**: Vestas commissioned a fault ride through (FRT) test system rated at 66 kV at the Osterild facility. This system is designed to ensure that offshore wind turbines meet international grid connection standards, allowing for testing at the operating voltage of 66 kV.
