### Evolutionary Timeline of Ørsted Facility in Avedøre, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2019**: Ørsted announced plans to develop a 2 MW electrolysis plant at the Avedøre facility, which will produce green hydrogen for the transport sector. The facility will utilize electricity generated from Ørsted's two Siemens Gamesa 3.6 MW offshore wind turbines located in Avedøre. The expected daily hydrogen production is around 600 kg, sufficient to power 20-30 buses, and will also facilitate testing for trucks and taxis.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2019**: The Danish Energy Agency allocated DKK 34.6 million (approximately $5 million) to Ørsted for the construction of the 2 MW electrolysis plant. This funding is part of a broader initiative to support large-scale hydrogen projects in Denmark.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2019**: Ørsted's electrolysis plant project was officially announced, with plans for development in partnership with several companies, including Everfuel Europe A/S and NEL Hydrogen A/S. The project is part of Denmark's strategy to produce green hydrogen from renewable sources, with implementation expected between 2020 and 2025.
