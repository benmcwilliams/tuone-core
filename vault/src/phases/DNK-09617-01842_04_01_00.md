### Evolutionary Timeline of Ørsted Facility in Skrive, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2019**: Ørsted announced plans to develop a 2 MW electrolysis plant in Skrive, which will produce green hydrogen for the transport sector. The facility will utilize electricity from Ørsted's offshore wind turbines and is expected to produce approximately 600 kg of hydrogen daily, sufficient to power 20-30 buses.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2019**: The Danish government allocated DKK 128 million ($19 million) to support two large-scale hydrogen projects, including the one in Skrive. Ørsted received DKK 34.6 million from the Danish Energy Agency specifically for the development of the 2 MW electrolysis plant.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2019**: Announcement of the project in Skrive as part of the broader initiative to develop renewable-powered energy storage projects, with implementation planned between 2020 and 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2019**: While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned, the development of the electrolysis plant is expected to contribute to job creation in the region, particularly in the renewable energy and transport sectors.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2019**: Ørsted is developing the facility in partnership with several companies, including Everfuel Europe A/S, NEL Hydrogen A/S, GreenHydrogen A/S, DSV Panalpina A/S, Hydrogen Denmark, and Energinet Elsystemansvar A/S.
