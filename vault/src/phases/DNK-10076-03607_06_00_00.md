### Evolutionary Timeline of the Technical University of Denmark Facility in Ørum, Denmark

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2020-2022**: The facility operates a solar-assisted district heating system that includes a ground-mounted solar thermal plant, a 2.5 MW air-to-water heat pump, a gas boiler, and a vertical cylindrical hot water tank with a capacity of 1,000 m³. The solar thermal plant covers heat demand during summer, while gas boilers are utilized in winter to meet higher demand. The system is designed to improve flexibility in response to fluctuating energy prices.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2021-2022**: A techno-economic analysis indicates that the integration of the air-to-water heat pump and an economizer enhances the system's efficiency, leading to a reduction in the levelized cost of heat by up to 19% in 2021 and 37% in 2022. The heat pump's coefficient of performance (COP) is improved from 3.33 to 3.53 with the use of an economizer.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2020**: The system operates without the heat pump in its first year.
- **2021-2022**: The heat pump is integrated into the system, enhancing its operational capabilities and efficiency.
