### Evolutionary Timeline of the Chery Facility in Barcelona, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Start**: In April 2024, Chery officially began production at the former Nissan plant in Barcelona, marking it as their first manufacturing site in Europe. The facility aims to produce a mix of combustion engine, hybrid, and electric vehicles, with an initial focus on the Omoda brand.
- **Production Capacity**: The joint venture between Chery and Ebro-EV Motors targets an annual production capacity of 50,000 vehicles by 2027, with plans to increase this to 150,000 vehicles per year by 2029.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Amount**: The joint venture plans to invest €400 million in revitalizing the former Nissan plant. This investment is part of a broader strategy to establish a center for electromobility in the Zona Franca area.
- **Public Aid**: While it is not confirmed if Chery received specific public aid, Spain's PERTE program is offering a total of €1.7 billion in loans and grants for electric vehicle production, which could potentially benefit Chery.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Agreement Finalization**: On April 12, 2024, it was reported that Chery was close to finalizing an agreement to start production in Barcelona.
- **Production Kickoff**: Chery officially kicked off production in Barcelona on April 22, 2024.
- **Future Plans**: The facility is set to produce the Omoda 5 EV, with additional models planned for future production.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The joint venture is expected to create approximately 1,250 jobs, helping to revitalize the area after the closure of the Nissan plant, which resulted in the loss of 1,600 jobs.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture**: The facility operates as a joint venture between Chery and Ebro-EV Motors, with Ebro-EV Motors holding the majority stake in the partnership. This collaboration signifies a strategic move for Chery to strengthen its presence in the European market.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic moves related to the Chery facility in Barcelona, highlighting its significance in the context of the European automotive landscape.
