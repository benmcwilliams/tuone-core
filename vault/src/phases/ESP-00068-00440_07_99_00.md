## ESP-00068-00440

### Evolutionary Timeline of the LG Facility in Barcelona, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2021**: Discussions emerged regarding the transformation of Nissan's car plant in Barcelona into a battery production facility for electric vehicles. LG Energy Solutions (LGES) expressed interest in producing batteries for Volkswagen's Seat brand, which plans to manufacture electric cars at its Martorell plant starting in 2025. The facility is proposed to be part of a larger project called Battery Hub, which aims to establish a battery production and recycling plant in the region.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2021**: The Catalan regional government is backing the Battery Hub project, which could potentially receive €6.8 billion in funding from Spain’s share of the EU’s Covid-19 pandemic aid. However, the allocation of these funds had not yet been decided.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2021**: The project to convert the Nissan plant into a battery production facility is still in the proposal stage, with various interested parties, including LGES, being considered. A task force was formed to evaluate proposals for the facility until March 31, 2021.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2021**: The transformation of the facility into a battery production plant is aimed at preserving jobs, as Nissan's closure of the plant would result in significant job losses. The plant previously employed between 2,800 and 3,000 workers.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2021**: The ownership structure of the facility is not explicitly stated, but LGES is positioned as a potential partner in the public-private battery factory project near Barcelona, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership.

### Summary
The LG facility in Barcelona is in the early stages of development, with plans to convert a former Nissan car plant into a battery production facility for electric vehicles. The project is supported by significant potential funding from the EU, and LGES is one of the interested parties aiming to produce batteries for major automotive brands. The initiative is also focused on job preservation in the region.
