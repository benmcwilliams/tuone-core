## ESP-00068-00577

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Nissan Facility in Barcelona, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2016**: Nissan engineers in Barcelona developed a modified 48 kWh Leaf for participation in the Spanish ECOseries motorsport event, showcasing the facility's capability in electric vehicle innovation.
- **March 2020**: Production of the e-NV200 was affected by the COVID-19 pandemic, leading to temporary shutdowns.
- **April 2020**: The Barcelona plant resumed production of the e-NV200 after a temporary closure due to the pandemic.
- **May 2020**: Nissan announced the closure of the Barcelona plant as part of a restructuring strategy, indicating a shift away from European operations.
- **December 2021**: The last Nissan e-NV200 was produced, marking the end of vehicle production at the Barcelona facility.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2020**: Nissan's restructuring plan included a significant reduction in global production capacity by 20%, indicating a strategic shift away from European markets, particularly affecting the Barcelona facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2020**: Official announcement of the closure of the Barcelona plant, with the intention to focus on the Sunderland plant in the UK.
- **December 2021**: The facility ceased all production activities, concluding its operational status.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2020**: The closure of the Barcelona plant was expected to impact approximately 2,800 to 3,000 jobs, highlighting significant job losses due to the restructuring.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2020**: The Barcelona facility was owned exclusively by Nissan, but the restructuring indicated a shift in focus towards the Renault-Nissan-Mitsubishi alliance, with Nissan planning to derive its vehicles from Renault models in Europe.

### Summary
The Nissan facility in Barcelona, Spain, was primarily involved in the production of electric vehicles, including the e-NV200 and Leaf models. The facility underwent significant changes due to the COVID-19 pandemic, leading to temporary shutdowns and ultimately the decision to close the plant as part of a broader restructuring strategy. This closure resulted in substantial job losses and marked a strategic pivot for Nissan away from European markets, focusing instead on operations in North America, China, and Japan. The facility ceased production in December 2021, concluding its operational history.
