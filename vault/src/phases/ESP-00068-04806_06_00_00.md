## ESP-00068-04806

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Danone Facility in Barcelona, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: Danone's facilities in Spain, including Barcelona, will receive 100% renewable electricity from the Francisco Pizarro photovoltaic plant, which has a capacity of 590 MW. This plant is set to be operational in 2022 and will supply Danone's factories and logistics centers across Spain.
- **Renewable Energy Supply**: The agreement with Iberdrola ensures an annual output of 73 GWh/year, with an additional green energy contract bringing the total to 104 GWh/year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Amount**: The project associated with the renewable energy supply is valued at €300 million.
- **Employment Contribution**: The project is expected to create employment for more than 1,200 workers during its development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Power Purchase Agreement (PPA)**: On January 21, 2021, Danone and Iberdrola signed a 10-year PPA, which will commence in April 2022. This agreement is pivotal for Danone's sustainability strategy and ensures a long-term supply of renewable energy.
- **Operational Timeline**: The Francisco Pizarro solar plant is expected to be operational in 2022.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The €300 million project is anticipated to contribute to the employment of over 1,200 workers, enhancing local industry and supporting economic recovery.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture**: The facility does not operate under exclusive ownership by Danone; it is part of a collaborative effort with Iberdrola, which is responsible for the development of the solar plant supplying renewable energy to Danone's facilities.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic initiatives surrounding the Danone facility in Barcelona, emphasizing its commitment to sustainability and renewable energy.
