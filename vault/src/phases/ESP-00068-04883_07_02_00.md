### Evolutionary Timeline of the EV Motors Facility in Barcelona, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Focus**: The facility is set to produce a mix of internal combustion engine (ICE) vehicles, hybrid vehicles, and electric vehicles (EVs). Chery plans to manufacture its vehicles at the former Nissan plant, which will include the production of EV trucks and vans under the Ebro brand by EV Motors.
- **Production Capacity**: Specific production capacity details have not been disclosed in the articles, but the facility aims to transform into a hub for electric vehicles.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Public Aid**: Spain is planning to launch two tenders this year as part of the PERTE program, offering a total of €1.7 billion in loans and grants for electric vehicle production. However, it is unclear if Chery will receive any public aid for its operations in Barcelona.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2024**: EV Motors acquired full corporate control of the hub at the former Nissan plant.
- **April 2024**: Chery is in the final stages of negotiations to start production in Barcelona, with formalization expected soon. The facility is set to be Chery's first manufacturing site in Europe.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The facility aims to bring back some of the jobs lost when Nissan ceased production in 2021, which affected approximately 1,600 workers. The exact number of jobs to be created by Chery and EV Motors has not been specified.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: EV Motors has acquired full corporate control of the facility, indicating exclusive ownership of the hub. Chery is negotiating to utilize the facility for its production, but the ownership remains with EV Motors.
