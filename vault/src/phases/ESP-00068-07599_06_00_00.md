## ESP-00068-07599

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Barcelona City Council Facility in Barcelona, ESP

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2024**: The Barcelona City Council announces the testing of two solar system prototypes designed to generate photovoltaic energy while providing shade and filtering air. The prototypes are:
  - **Adaptable Modular Solar Shade (OSMA)**: A modular system that prioritizes electricity generation and shade during hot months. It features retractable capabilities for winter sunlight and uses glass-crystal monocrystalline PV panels that filter 90% of light. Built with recyclable materials, it is designed to withstand wind and weather.
  - **Sombra Viva**: A multifunctional solar shelter that offers shade, energy generation, and air filtering. It has adaptable modules that adjust shade based on climate conditions to enhance community well-being.
- **Summer 2025**: Both systems are scheduled to be installed at UPC’s Campus Nord and Campus Besòs, where they will undergo a year of testing to validate their functionality.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2024**: Each team involved in the project receives €50,000 (approximately $52,771) in funding to cover 80% of project costs. This funding is part of a competition for innovative photovoltaic solutions promoted by BIT Habitat, the urban innovation agency of the council, with support from the local Energy Agency.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2024**: Announcement of the testing phase for the two solar prototypes.
- **Summer 2025**: Planned installation of the prototypes at UPC’s Campus Nord and Campus Besòs, followed by a year of testing.
