## ESP-00668-00782

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Toyota Facility in Madrid, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **02/2021**: The facility inaugurated Spain’s first hydrogen refuelling station for long-range fuel cell vehicles. This station is part of a consortium involving Toyota and other partners, demonstrating the viability of hydrogen as a transport fuel. It operates at a pressure of 700 bar and has a supply capacity of 10 kg of green hydrogen per day, with a purity greater than 99.98%. The station is designed to refuel a fleet of 12 Toyota Mirais, which have a range of approximately 550 km after refuelling.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **06/2021**: A €100 million investment initiative was announced to generate hydrogen via a 10 MW electrolyzer powered by a 20 MW solar plant. This project aims to replace at least 1,000 traditional taxis with hydrogen-powered vehicles by 2026, with Toyota Spain participating as a vehicle supplier.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **02/2021**: Inauguration of the hydrogen refuelling station in Madrid.
- **06/2021**: Announcement of the green hydrogen project for Madrid taxis, with the first hydrogen vehicles expected to be operational in 2022.

#### 4. Labour
- **06/2021**: The hydrogen project is expected to create jobs related to the development of hydrogen production infrastructure and vehicle recharging sites, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility operates as a collaborative project involving multiple partners, including Toyota, Enagás, Urbaser, Carburos Metálicos, Sumitomo Corporation, and the Spanish Confederation of Service Station Employers (CEEES). Therefore, Toyota does not own the facility exclusively but is a key partner in the consortium.
