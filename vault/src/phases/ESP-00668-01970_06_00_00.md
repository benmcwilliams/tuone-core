## ESP-00668-01970

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Statkraft Facility in Madrid, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2019**: Statkraft, historically focused on hydro, announced its entry into solar asset development in Iberia, specifically targeting the Spanish and Portuguese markets. The company aims to have **1GW of ready-to-build solar capacity** in Iberia by **2025**. The initial focus will be on acquiring third-party solar projects and developing its own pipeline.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2019**: Statkraft has engaged in Power Purchase Agreements (PPAs) with BayWa r.e. for the Don Rodrigo solar projects (175MWp and 50MWp). The company is looking to expand its PPA deal flow as part of its investment strategy in the Iberian solar market.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2019**: Statkraft's new Iberian office in Madrid marks its transition from an offtaker to a developer in the solar sector. The company plans a two-staged approach to reach its 1GW goal, with the first phase focusing on mergers and acquisitions (M&A) to build a project pipeline.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2019**: As part of its expansion into solar development, Statkraft is in the process of building a team to support its operations in Iberia, although specific job creation numbers were not mentioned.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2019**: Statkraft is establishing its presence in the Iberian solar market, indicating a move towards full ownership of solar projects as it transitions from an offtaker to a developer. The company is open to various project structures, including potential joint ventures, but the focus appears to be on developing its own projects.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic moves of Statkraft in Madrid, Spain, as it aims to establish a significant presence in the solar energy sector.
