### Evolutionary Timeline of the Enagás Facility in Madrid, Spain

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** February 2021
   - **Details:** The facility inaugurated Spain’s first hydrogen refuelling station for long-range fuel cell vehicles. It operates at a pressure of 700 bar and has a supply capacity of 10 kg of green hydrogen per day, with a purity greater than 99.98%. The refuelling time for vehicles is less than 5 minutes, similar to traditional fuel vehicles. The station supports a fleet of 12 Toyota Mirais.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** February 2021
   - **Details:** The hydrogen refuelling station is part of a pilot project initiated two years prior, involving a consortium of companies committed to the energy transition. The project reflects a collaborative investment approach among Enagás, Toyota, and other partners, emphasizing innovation and industry promotion in Spain.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** February 2021
   - **Details:** The hydrogen refuelling station was officially inaugurated, marking a significant milestone in the development of hydrogen infrastructure in Spain.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** February 2021
   - **Details:** The project is noted for its commitment to promoting industry and employment in Spain, although specific job creation or loss figures were not provided in the article.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** February 2021
   - **Details:** The facility is a collaborative initiative involving multiple partners, including Enagás, Toyota, Urbaser, Carburos Metálicos, Sumitomo Corporation, and the Spanish Confederation of Service Station Employers (CEEES). This indicates a joint venture rather than exclusive ownership by Enagás.
