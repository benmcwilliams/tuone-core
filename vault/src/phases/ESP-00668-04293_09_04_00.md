### Evolutionary Timeline of the Synhelion Facility in Madrid, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **07-08-2023**: Synhelion, in collaboration with Cemex, begins construction of a high-concentration solar tower designed to produce synthetic fuels for a cement production facility near Madrid. The facility utilizes solar tower technology to generate high-temperature process heat for clinker production, aiming to eliminate fossil fuel use in the rotary kiln process. The facility will also produce syngas, which can be converted into fuels like jet fuel, gasoline, or diesel.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07-08-2023**: Synhelion and Cemex secure $3.2 million in funding from the US Department of Energy to study conditions for maximizing heat transfer to the raw cement mix. This funding is tied to the development of the solar tower technology and its application in cement production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **07-08-2023**: Announcement of the start of construction for the solar tower facility. The project follows a small-scale pilot installation introduced in early 2022, with plans to complete a small-scale industrial plant in Móstoles by 2026 and a large-scale industrial plant with a capacity of 150 MWth by the end of the decade.
