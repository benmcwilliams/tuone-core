## ESP-00668-04806

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Danone Facility in Madrid, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: Danone's facilities in Spain, including the one in Madrid, will receive 100% renewable electricity from the Francisco Pizarro solar photovoltaic plant, which has a capacity of 590 MW. This plant is set to be operational in 2022 and will supply Danone's factories across Spain.
- **Renewable Energy Supply**: The facility will benefit from an annual output of 73 GWh/year from the solar project, supplemented by an additional green energy contract with Iberdrola, bringing the total to 104 GWh/year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Amount**: The Francisco Pizarro project represents a €300 million investment. This investment is part of a broader initiative by Iberdrola to enhance renewable energy capacity in Spain, with a total investment program of €75 billion planned between 2020-2025, of which €14.3 billion is earmarked for Spain.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Power Purchase Agreement (PPA)**: On January 21, 2021, Danone and Iberdrola signed a long-term PPA to secure renewable electricity for Danone's operations in Spain, effective from April 2022.
- **Operational Timeline**: The Francisco Pizarro solar plant is expected to be operational in 2022.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The construction and operation of the Francisco Pizarro project are expected to contribute to the employment of more than 1,200 workers during key stages of its delivery.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture**: The facility in Madrid is part of a broader collaboration between Danone and Iberdrola, indicating a joint effort in sustainability and renewable energy supply rather than exclusive ownership by Danone.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic initiatives related to the Danone facility in Madrid, highlighting its commitment to renewable energy and sustainability.
