## ESP-00690-00709

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Seat Facility in Martorell, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **03/2018**: Seat announced that the Martorell factory would manufacture the plug-in hybrid version of the Seat Leon, set for market release in 2020. Additionally, a fully electric model based on the VW MEB platform was promised for 2020, with a range of up to 500 km.
- **03/2020**: Production of the new Seat Leon began at the Martorell plant, alongside the Cupra Leon and Cupra Formentor, which would also be available as plug-in hybrids.
- **07/2020**: Seat announced plans to start production of battery electric vehicles (BEVs) at the Martorell plant from 2025, with a total investment of 5 billion euros allocated for R&D projects.
- **10/2020**: Construction of a battery testing lab (Test Center Energy) began at the Martorell plant, with an investment of over 7 million euros, aimed at developing and testing battery systems for electric and hybrid vehicles.
- **03/2021**: Seat aimed to produce 500,000 electric cars annually from 2025, including a new urban electric vehicle.
- **05/2022**: Seat confirmed plans to convert the Martorell plant for the production of small electric cars, with an investment of 10 billion euros from the Volkswagen Group.
- **02/2023**: Seat announced a 3 billion euro investment for the conversion of the Martorell plant to produce small electric cars, including the Cupra UrbanRebel and VW ID.2.
- **07/2023**: Seat announced an additional investment of 300 million euros for a battery assembly plant at the Martorell facility, which will produce battery packs for electric vehicles.
- **04/2024**: Production of the Cupra Raval and VW ID.2 is set to begin at the Martorell plant by the end of 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **03/2020**: Seat announced a 5 billion euro investment for R&D projects, including the development of electric vehicles at the Martorell plant.
- **10/2020**: Investment of over 7 million euros for the construction of the Test Center Energy at Martorell.
- **05/2022**: Volkswagen and Seat increased their investment in e-mobility in Spain from 7 billion to 10 billion euros, aimed at transforming the Martorell plant for electric vehicle production.
- **02/2023**: Seat committed to invest 3 billion euros for the conversion of the Martorell plant.
- **07/2023**: An additional 300 million euros was allocated for the construction of a battery assembly plant at Martorell.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **03/2018**: Announcement of the production of the plug-in hybrid Seat Leon and a fully electric model at Martorell.
- **03/2020**: Production of the new Seat Leon commenced.
- **07/2020**: Announcement of BEV production starting in 2025.
- **10/2020**: Construction of the battery testing lab began.
- **05/2022**: Confirmation of the 10 billion euro investment for the Martorell plant's transformation.
- **02/2023**: Announcement of the 3 billion euro investment for the plant's conversion.
- **07/2023**: Announcement of the battery assembly plant construction.
- **04/2024**: Production of the Cupra Raval and VW ID.2 set to begin by the end of 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **07/2023**: The battery assembly project is expected to create 400 direct and 100 indirect jobs at the Martorell plant.
- **02/2023**: The overall transformation project is expected to positively impact the Spanish economy and create up to 140,000 new jobs across the automotive industry.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Seat facility in Martorell is owned by Seat, a subsidiary of the Volkswagen Group. There is no indication of joint ventures or shared ownership in the provided articles.
