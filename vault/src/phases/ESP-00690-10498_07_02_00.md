### Evolutionary Timeline of the Volkswagen Seat Facility in Martorell, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **03/2018**: Seat announces the production of the plug-in hybrid version of the Seat Leon at the Martorell factory, with plans for a fully electric model based on the VW MEB platform to be released in 2020.
- **03/2020**: Production of the Cupra Formentor SUV as a plug-in hybrid begins, alongside the new Seat Leon.
- **07/2020**: Seat announces plans to start production of battery electric vehicles (BEVs) at the Martorell plant from 2025.
- **10/2020**: Construction of a battery testing lab (Test Center Energy) begins at the Martorell plant, aimed at developing and testing battery systems for electric and hybrid vehicles.
- **03/2021**: Seat aims to produce 500,000 electric cars annually from 2025, including a new urban electric vehicle.
- **05/2022**: Seat announces a strategic plan to electrify the Martorell and Pamplona factories, including the construction of a battery factory in Sagunt.
- **03/2023**: Seat invests €3 billion in the conversion of the Martorell plant for the production of small electric cars, including the Cupra Raval and VW ID.2.
- **07/2023**: Seat announces an additional investment of €300 million for a battery assembly plant at Martorell, set to optimize logistics for electric vehicle production.
- **04/2024**: Production of the Cupra Raval and VW ID.2 is set to begin at the Martorell plant by the end of 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **03/2020**: Seat announces a €5 billion investment in R&D projects for new facilities and models, including electrification.
- **10/2020**: Investment of over €7 million for the construction of the battery testing lab at Martorell.
- **05/2022**: Volkswagen and Seat increase their investment in e-mobility in Spain from €7 billion to €10 billion, focusing on the Martorell and Pamplona plants.
- **03/2023**: Seat announces a €3 billion investment for the conversion of the Martorell plant for electric vehicle production.
- **07/2023**: An additional €300 million is allocated for the battery assembly plant at Martorell.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **03/2018**: Announcement of the Seat Leon PHEV and a fully electric model.
- **03/2020**: Launch of the Cupra Formentor PHEV.
- **07/2020**: Announcement of BEV production starting in 2025.
- **10/2020**: Construction of the Test Center Energy begins.
- **03/2021**: Seat sets a target of producing 500,000 electric cars annually from 2025.
- **05/2022**: Announcement of the Future: Fast Forward initiative for electrification.
- **03/2023**: Investment of €3 billion for plant conversion announced.
- **07/2023**: Announcement of the battery assembly plant construction.
- **04/2024**: Production of the Cupra Raval and VW ID.2 set to begin by the end of 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **03/2023**: The conversion of the Martorell plant is expected to create 400 direct and 100 indirect jobs through the battery assembly project.
- **02/2023**: The overall transformation project is expected to create up to 140,000 new jobs in the Spanish automotive industry.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned exclusively by Seat, a subsidiary of the Volkswagen Group, which is investing heavily in its electrification and development.
