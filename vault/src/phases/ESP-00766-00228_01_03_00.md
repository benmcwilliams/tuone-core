### Evolutionary Timeline of Envision AESC Facility in Navalmoral de la Mata, Spain

1. **Technology and Operations**
   - **Announcement of Battery Factory**: On **July 19, 2022**, Envision AESC announced plans to build a battery cell factory in Navalmoral de la Mata, Spain. The facility is expected to have a production capacity of up to **50 GWh** and is part of a larger strategic agreement with the Spanish government. The factory is set to become operational in **2025**.

2. **Investment and Development**
   - **Funding and Subsidies**: The battery factory will be subsidized through Spain’s **PERTE programme**. While the specific funding amount was not disclosed, the project is part of the broader **VENERGY+** initiative, which aims to localize the supply of vehicle parts and components using advanced technologies.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**
   - **Construction Announcement**: The facility's construction was officially announced on **July 19, 2022**, with an operational target set for **2025**. The project includes not only the battery factory but also a digital net-zero center, a green hydrogen plant, and an assembly plant for smart wind turbines in various regions of Spain.

4. **Labour**
   - **Job Creation**: The establishment of the battery factory is expected to create up to **3,000 jobs** in the region, contributing significantly to local employment.

5. **Ownership Structure**
   - **Ownership**: Envision AESC is a joint venture, originally founded in 2007 with Nissan as the largest shareholder. However, since 2018, the Chinese company Envision has acquired a majority stake in AESC. The specific ownership structure of the Navalmoral de la Mata facility has not been detailed in the articles, but it is implied that Envision AESC will maintain ownership of the facility.
