### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Navarra, Spain

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 08-06-2020
   - **Details:** Iberdrola commenced operations of the CAVAR wind power complex in Navarra, Spain. The facility has an installed capacity of 111 MW, consisting of four wind farms located in the municipalities of Cadreita and Valtierra. The complex is equipped with 32 SG 3.4-132 wind turbines and is noted as Iberdrola's largest completed wind farm in Spain since 2012. The facility is designed to offset 84,000 tonnes of carbon emissions annually and can generate enough clean energy to meet the average annual power consumption of 45,000 homes.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 08-06-2020
   - **Details:** The construction of the CAVAR wind power complex involved an investment of over €100 million, which was green financed by the European Investment Bank (EIB).

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 08-06-2020
   - **Details:** The CAVAR wind power complex officially began operations, marking a significant milestone for Iberdrola in the renewable energy sector.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 08-06-2020
   - **Details:** The construction phase of the CAVAR complex created hundreds of jobs in the region. Additionally, the project generated €80 million in purchases from local suppliers.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 08-06-2020
   - **Details:** Iberdrola owns the CAVAR wind power complex exclusively, as there is no mention of joint ventures or partnerships in the provided information.
