## ESP-00848-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Palencia, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2022**: Iberdrola announced the construction of a new 375 MW photovoltaic plant in Cedillo, Cáceres, which will utilize over 576,000 bifacial photovoltaic modules. This facility is expected to produce more than 582,400 MWh of green energy annually. The plant will also support local suppliers and contribute to job creation during its construction phase.

- **July 2023**: The Velilla solar facility in Palencia, with a capacity of 400 MW, was completed and is part of a power purchase agreement (PPA) with Vodafone to supply 410 GWh of annual solar power.

- **July 2024**: Iberdrola signed a PPA with Burger King to supply power from a 553 MW solar PV plant, which includes 75 MW allocated specifically for Burger King's operations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2022**: The construction of the Cedillo photovoltaic plant is part of Iberdrola's broader investment plan to install 10,000 new MW of renewable energy in Spain by 2030, with a significant focus on the Extremadura region.

- **July 2023**: Iberdrola secured a €1 billion financing deal with the European Investment Bank (EIB) to support the construction of 2.2 GW worth of solar PV projects across Spain, Portugal, and Germany.

- **July 2024**: The Francisco Pizarro solar PV plant, which is part of the agreements with various corporations, represents an investment of over €300 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2022**: Announcement of the new 375 MW photovoltaic plant in Cedillo, with construction expected to boost local job creation and support the energy transition.

- **July 2023**: Completion of the 400 MW Velilla solar facility in Palencia, which is now operational and supplying power under a PPA with Vodafone.

- **July 2024**: The Francisco Pizarro solar PV plant, which includes a significant portion of the capacity dedicated to corporate clients, is operational and part of multiple PPAs.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2022**: The construction of the Cedillo photovoltaic plant is projected to create up to 800 jobs during peak construction periods.

- **July 2023**: The investment plan for renewable energy projects in Extremadura is expected to create approximately 7,400 jobs over the 2020-2025 period.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2022**: Iberdrola is the sole owner of the facilities mentioned, including the new photovoltaic plant in Cedillo and the Velilla solar facility in Palencia, as well as the Francisco Pizarro plant.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Iberdrola facility in Palencia, Spain, focusing on technology, investment, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
