## ESP-00848-02424

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Repsol Facility in Palencia, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **08-02-2023**: Repsol begins generating energy at the PI wind project, which includes seven wind farms with a total installed capacity of 175 MW. The facility is capable of generating 596 GWh of renewable energy annually, sufficient to power approximately 170,900 households. The first wind turbines at the La Serna wind farm have been linked to the grid and are delivering green electricity on a trial basis.

- **09-05-2023**: Repsol signs contracts with Siemens Gamesa for the supply of 40 SG 5.0-145 onshore wind turbines for six wind farms in Spain, including four wind farms in Palencia as part of the PI project. This will add a total capacity of 140 MW to the PI project.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **01-03-2023**: Repsol acquires three wind farms and two solar plants from ABO Wind, adding 250 MW of renewable assets in Spain. The three wind farms contribute 150 MW and the two solar plants add 100 MW. These projects are at an advanced stage of development and are expected to be operational between 2024 and 2025.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **08-02-2023**: Announcement of the start of energy generation at the PI project in Palencia, marking it as Repsol's first renewable project in Castilla y León.

- **01-03-2023**: Completion of the acquisition of additional renewable assets, enhancing Repsol's portfolio in Spain.

- **09-05-2023**: Signing of contracts with Siemens Gamesa for the installation of wind turbines, furthering the development of the PI project.

#### 4. Labour
- **09-05-2023**: The installation of the new wind farms is expected to have a positive effect on the wind industry in Spain, benefiting local suppliers and creating jobs in the manufacturing and installation sectors.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Repsol owns the PI facility exclusively, as it is a standalone project under the company's renewable energy initiatives in Spain. The acquisition of additional wind farms and solar plants from ABO Wind further consolidates Repsol's ownership and control over its renewable energy assets in the region. 

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones of the Repsol facility in Palencia, Spain, focusing on its technology, investments, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
