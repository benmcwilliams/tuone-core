### Evolutionary Timeline of the Volkswagen Facility in Pamplona, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **06/2021**: Volkswagen plans to produce small electric vehicles based on the MEB platform, with the Pamplona plant being a potential production site for the VW ID.2 X crossover and a Skoda model.
- **05/2022**: The Pamplona plant is confirmed to produce the VW ID.2 X and the Skoda Elroq, both expected to be small electric SUVs.
- **03/2023**: VW announces plans to produce over 3 million small electric vehicles at its Spanish plants, including the Pamplona facility, between 2025 and 2030.
- **07/2023**: Kuka is contracted to supply 700 robots for production at the Pamplona plant, which will manufacture the Skoda Epiq and the ID.2 X.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05/2022**: Volkswagen and Seat announce an increase in investment for e-mobility in Spain from €7 billion to €10 billion, which includes the conversion of the Pamplona plant.
- **11/2022**: The Spanish government releases funding under the PERTE eMobility programme, enabling the €10 billion investment to proceed.
- **03/2024**: Volkswagen confirms a significant investment in automation, with Kuka supplying robots to enhance production capabilities at the Pamplona facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05/2022**: VW and Seat confirm the conversion of the Pamplona plant for electric vehicle production.
- **03/2023**: VW announces the target of producing 3 million small electric vehicles at the Pamplona plant by 2030.
- **07/2023**: Kuka begins the delivery of 700 robots to the Pamplona plant to support the production of electric models.

#### 4. Labour
- **07/2023**: The investment in automation and the production of electric vehicles at the Pamplona plant is expected to create approximately 400 direct and 100 indirect jobs.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility in Pamplona is owned by Volkswagen Group, with Seat as a key brand involved in the production of electric vehicles. The ownership structure indicates that Volkswagen operates the facility, likely with full ownership, as it is part of their broader strategy for electrification in Spain. 

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic decisions regarding the Volkswagen facility in Pamplona, Spain, focusing on its evolution towards electric vehicle production.

### Volkswagen Facility in Pamplona, ESP - Contrastive Summary

- **Production Models**: The Pamplona plant is set to produce the VW ID.2 X crossover variant and a Skoda model called Epiq, as well as the offshoot for Skoda, which will be called Elroq. (Article 5, 10-11-2022)
- **Production Capacity**: Volkswagen plans to produce a total of three million fully electric small cars at its Spanish plants in Martorell and Pamplona between 2025 and 2030. (Article 8, 23-03-2023)
- **Updates on Production**: The Pamplona plant will build electric SUVs, but specific brands or models were not detailed in earlier announcements. (Article 2, 09-12-2021)

#### 2. Investment and Development

- **Funding Dependencies**: The investment plans were initially dependent on the Spanish government’s funding under the PERTE eMobility funding programme, which was later confirmed. (Article 5, 10-11-2022)

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
