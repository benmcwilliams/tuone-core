### Evolutionary Timeline of the Volkswagen Facility in Sagunt, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2022**: Volkswagen announces plans for a battery cell factory in Sagunt, with a production capacity of 40 GWh per year.
- **March 2022**: Official confirmation of the factory's location in Sagunt, with plans for next-generation cell production.
- **May 2022**: VW announces an increase in investment for the factory to €10 billion, with construction scheduled to begin in Q1 2023 and production set to start in 2026.
- **March 2023**: Construction of the battery factory begins, with a potential for future expansion to 60 GWh capacity.
- **October 2023**: PowerCo confirms that the factory is on schedule for series production of the unified cell, with the first production block completed.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2022**: Initial investment planned at around €3.5 billion for the Sagunt facility.
- **March 2022**: Total investment in e-mobility in Spain confirmed to exceed €7 billion.
- **May 2022**: Investment increased to €10 billion, aimed at building a supply chain and supporting the battery factory.
- **November 2023**: Spanish government increases funding for the PowerCo factory from €98 million to €152 million under the PERTE program.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2022**: Announcement of the Sagunt location for the battery factory.
- **March 2022**: Official confirmation of the factory plans.
- **May 2022**: Increase in investment and announcement of construction timeline.
- **March 2023**: Groundbreaking for the factory.
- **October 2023**: Confirmation of construction progress and production timeline.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2022**: Project expected to create around 3,500 jobs.
- **March 2022**: Plans to employ more than 3,000 people in Valencia.
- **October 2023**: Potential for up to 30,000 indirect jobs at suppliers and partners in Spain.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2022**: The facility is owned exclusively by Volkswagen Group through its battery subsidiary, PowerCo. There are no indications of joint ventures or partnerships regarding ownership of the Sagunt facility.
