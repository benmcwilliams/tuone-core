### Evolutionary Timeline of the Volkswagen Facility in Sagunt, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2022**: Volkswagen announces plans for a battery cell factory in Sagunt, with a production capacity of 40 GWh per year.
- **March 2022**: Official confirmation of the factory's location in Sagunt, with plans for next-generation cell production.
- **May 2022**: VW increases the planned production capacity to 60 GWh, with construction scheduled to begin in Q1 2023 and production set to start in 2026.
- **March 2023**: Construction of the battery plant officially begins.
- **March 2023**: VW announces that the factory will be powered entirely by renewable energy and will include facilities for recycling and reprocessing.
- **October 2023**: The factory is confirmed to be on schedule for production start in 2026.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2022**: Initial investment planned at around €3.5 billion for the Sagunt facility.
- **March 2022**: Total investment in e-mobility in Spain is confirmed to exceed €7 billion.
- **May 2022**: Investment increases to €10 billion, aimed at the battery factory and local supply chain development.
- **March 2023**: The Spanish government increases funding for the Sagunt factory from €98 million to €152 million under the PERTE program.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2022**: VW announces the decision to build the battery factory in Sagunt.
- **March 2022**: Official confirmation of the factory plans.
- **May 2022**: Announcement of increased investment and construction timeline.
- **March 2023**: Groundbreaking ceremony for the factory.
- **March 2023**: Production is scheduled to start in 2026.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2022**: The factory is expected to create around 3,500 jobs.
- **May 2022**: VW announces plans to employ more than 3,000 people at the facility.
- **March 2023**: VW states that the factory could create up to 30,000 indirect jobs through suppliers and partners.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2022**: The facility is to be owned and operated by Volkswagen's battery subsidiary, PowerCo, indicating full ownership by the Volkswagen Group.
