## ESP-01000-01626

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Engie Facility in Seville, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2023**: Engie España and Heineken España inaugurated a 30 MW concentrated solar power (CSP) plant in Seville, which includes a storage capacity of 68 MWh. This facility is noted as Europe’s largest industrial CSP plant, designed to significantly reduce fossil gas consumption at Heineken's brewery by over 60%. The plant utilizes water as a heat transfer medium, enhancing energy reliability and eliminating the need for synthetic oils.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2023**: The CSP plant required a total investment of €21 million (approximately $21.9 million), which was co-financed with FEDER funds and managed by the Institute for Energy Diversification and Saving (IDAE).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2023**: The CSP plant was officially commissioned and began operations. The construction of the facility took 12 months, which was nearly double the initially projected timeframe.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2023**: The construction of the CSP plant contributed to job creation, although specific numbers related to job creation during this phase were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2023**: The facility operates under a Thermal Purchase Agreement (TPA) framework, where Heineken España is responsible for energy consumption and provides the land, while Engie España is committed to delivering renewable energy at a predetermined rate. Upon the expiration of the 20-year term of the agreement, ownership of the solar thermal plant will transfer to Heineken.

### Additional Developments
- **January 2024**: Engie inaugurated a 72 MW photovoltaic park in Huévar del Aljarafe, near Seville, which consists of two 36 MW plants covering 150 hectares. This project is expected to save nearly 30,000 tons of CO2 annually and created approximately 350 jobs during its construction phase. Engie continues to expand its renewable energy footprint in Andalusia, emphasizing its commitment to the region's energy transition and economic development.
