## ESP-01000-02167

### Evolutionary Timeline of the BayWa Facility in Seville, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10/2018**: BayWa began preliminary work on the **170MW Don Rodrigo solar plant** near Seville, which is expected to be commissioned by the end of the year. This facility is noted as one of the first subsidy-free solar projects of this size in Europe.
- **02/2018**: BayWa achieved financial close on **€100 million** for the **174 MW solar PV project** in Seville, employing modules from Chinese manufacturers and inverters from Huawei. The project is expected to be grid-connected by the end of the year.
- **05/2020**: The **50MW Don Rodrigo 2 solar farm** became operational, supplying power to the grid. This facility is located just 3 km from the original Don Rodrigo plant and is backed by a **12-year PPA** with Statkraft.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **02/2018**: BayWa secured **€100 million** in construction bridge financing from NORD/LB for the Don Rodrigo solar PV park.
- **05/2020**: The Don Rodrigo 2 project was completed and operational, marking it as the second subsidy-free solar project in Spain.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04/2018**: Construction on the Don Rodrigo solar plant began.
- **12/2018**: The **175MW Don Rodrigo solar plant** was sold to MEAG, marking a significant milestone as it was the first utility-scale solar park built without subsidy in Europe.
- **01/2020**: The **50MW Don Rodrigo 2** was sold to insurer Talanx after its completion.
- **05/2020**: Don Rodrigo 2 was fully commissioned and operational.

#### 4. Labour
- **02/2019**: BayWa announced plans to hire **10 professionals** for a new office in Madrid to scale up its Spanish renewables portfolio, indicating job creation linked to the expansion of operations in Spain.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **12/2018**: BayWa sold the **175MW Don Rodrigo** plant to MEAG, indicating that BayWa does not exclusively own this facility.
- **01/2020**: The **50MW Don Rodrigo 2** was sold to Talanx, further confirming that BayWa operates on a build-and-sell model rather than maintaining full ownership of its facilities.
