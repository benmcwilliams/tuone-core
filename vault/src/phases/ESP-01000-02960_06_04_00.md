### Evolutionary Timeline of the Greenland Gigafactory Facility in Seville, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2021**: The Greenland Gigafactory is set to be a vertically integrated solar module factory located near Sevilla, Spain. It is planned to have a production capacity of **5 GW** and will manufacture monocrystalline silicon wafers in the M10 format for Passivated Emitter and Rear (PERC) solar cells. The factory will produce multi-busbar interconnected half or triple cell modules with a minimum power output of **540 W**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2021**: The project is supported by the Fraunhofer Institute for Solar Energy Systems (ISE), which will provide advisory services and oversee factory planning and advanced cell technology development. The engineering company Bosch Rexroth is responsible for the planning and design of the factory, indicating a significant investment in state-of-the-art production technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: Announcements confirm the establishment of the Greenland Gigafactory, with plans for construction and operation in a free zone within the port of Seville. The project is positioned as a response to the growing demand for solar energy in Europe, with a focus on achieving international competitiveness.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2021**: While specific job creation numbers are not mentioned in the articles, the establishment of a large-scale factory like the Greenland Gigafactory is expected to create numerous jobs in the region, contributing to local employment in the renewable energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2021**: The facility will be owned and operated exclusively by the newly created company **Greenland Gigafactory**.
