### Evolutionary Timeline of Heineken España Facility in Seville, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2023**: Heineken España, in collaboration with Engie España, inaugurated a 30 MW concentrated solar power (CSP) plant in Seville. This facility features a storage capacity of 68 MWh and is recognized as Europe’s largest industrial CSP facility. The plant is designed to significantly reduce the brewery's fossil gas consumption by over 60% and doubles the production capacity of superheated water for industrial use. It utilizes water as a heat transfer medium, eliminating the need for synthetic oils.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2023**: The CSP plant required a total investment of €21 million (approximately $21.9 million), which was co-financed with FEDER funds and managed by the Institute for Energy Diversification and Saving (IDAE).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2023**: The CSP plant was officially commissioned and began operations, marking a significant milestone as the largest of its kind in Europe. The construction of the plant took 12 months, which was nearly double the initially projected timeframe.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the CSP plant's construction or operation.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility operates under a Thermal Purchase Agreement (TPA) framework, where Heineken España is responsible for energy consumption and provides the land, while Engie España commits to delivering renewable energy. Upon the expiration of the 20-year agreement, ownership of the solar thermal plant will transfer to Heineken España, indicating that the facility is not exclusively owned by Heineken at the outset but will be fully owned after the agreement period.
