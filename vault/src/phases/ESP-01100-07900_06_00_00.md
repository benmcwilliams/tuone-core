### Evolutionary Timeline of the Intergia Facility in Tauste, Spain

1. **Technology and Operations**:
   - **Date**: November 2022
   - **Details**: Intergia is installing a floating photovoltaic (PV) system for self-consumption in the slurry pond of a pig farm in Tauste, Aragón. The system will consist of a 20.7 kW prototype with 46 photovoltaic modules, covering 142.7 m² (23.8% of the pond's surface). The floating array aims to reduce ammonia emissions by up to 40% and is expected to supply approximately 38% of the farm's annual electricity consumption. The project will also study the long-term degradation of the PV components in the ammonia-rich environment.

2. **Investment and Development**:
   - **Date**: November 2022
   - **Details**: The project is part of a broader initiative to address ammonia emissions in intensive livestock farming. Specific investment details are not provided in the article, but the project involves collaboration with Spanish specialist Isigenere for the floating solution.

   - **Details**: The project is scheduled for completion in October 2023. During this period, ammonia levels, material degradation, and panel operation will be studied. The pilot installation will remain operational for its expected useful life of about 25 years.
