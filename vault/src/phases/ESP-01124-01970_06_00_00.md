## ESP-01124-01970

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Statkraft Facility in Toledo, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2019**: Statkraft signed a five-year Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) to acquire 330 GWh of clean energy from the 50 MW Bargas solar park, located in Toledo province. The project is part of a subsidy-free solar initiative and is being developed by Swiss company aventron, with construction already underway. The Bargas plant is expected to reach commercial operation by July 2020.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2019**: The Bargas solar park is backed by a debt package from Spanish financier Banco Sabadell, with a debt-to-equity ratio of approximately 50:50. This financial structure supports the development of the facility as part of aventron's broader goal to achieve 1 GW of installed renewable capacity, with an interim target of 600 MW by late 2020.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2019**: The Bargas solar park is under construction and is slated to begin commercial operations by July 2020. This project is one of two being developed by aventron in Spain, with the other being a 50 MW facility in the southern province of Murcia.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the Bargas solar park or the Statkraft facility in Toledo.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is not exclusively owned by Statkraft; it is a project developed by aventron in collaboration with co-developer Synergia Energy Solutions and built by Spanish EPC Solarpack Corporación Tecnológica. Statkraft acts as the offtaker for the energy produced by the facility through the PPA.
