### Evolutionary Timeline of the Volkswagen Facility in Valencia, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2023**: Volkswagen officially announced the construction of its second battery cell factory, PowerCo, in Valencia. The facility is designed to produce 'unit cells' with an initial annual production capacity of **40 GWh**, with potential expansion to **60 GWh** in the future. The factory will be located in Sagunto, covering an area of approximately **130 hectares**, with an additional **70 hectares** for a supplier park, totaling **200 hectares**. The facility aims to supply battery cells to vehicle plants in Martorell and Pamplona.
- **2026**: Production is scheduled to start at the Valencia facility.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: The Valencia gigafactory is part of a **€10 billion investment program** called "Future: Fast Forward," which includes contributions from Volkswagen, Seat, and other partners, supported by significant government subsidies. The factory is expected to generate annual sales of over **€20 billion** by 2030.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2023**: Groundbreaking for the Valencia battery plant was officially announced, marking the start of construction.
- **2023**: The facility is part of a broader strategy to electrify Volkswagen's car factories in Spain, with production in Valencia set to begin in **2026**.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2023**: The construction and operation of the Valencia facility are projected to create **more than 3,000 direct jobs** and up to **30,000 indirect jobs** through suppliers and partners in Spain.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned by **Volkswagen's subsidiary PowerCo**, which was established in **July 2022**. PowerCo is responsible for the global battery business of the Volkswagen Group, indicating that Volkswagen has **full ownership** of the facility.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic plans for the Volkswagen battery facility in Valencia, highlighting its significance in the company's broader electrification and sustainability goals.
