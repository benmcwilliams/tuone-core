## ESP-01177-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Valencia, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **03/2021**: Iberdrola announced plans to develop three solar farms in Valencia with a combined capacity of **450MW**. The facilities include:
  - **PSF Ayora 1** and **FV Cofrentes I** (200MW each)
  - **FV Alhorines** (50MW)
- **04/2021**: Iberdrola added a **20MW electrolyser** project powered by renewable energy, including a **40MW solar PV system** at bp’s refinery in Castellón, with potential for future expansion to **115MW**.
- **08/2024**: Iberdrola received authorization for the **154.66MW Cofrentes I photovoltaic plant** in Valencia, which will utilize bifacial monocrystalline silicon modules and various inverters.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **03/2021**: The investment for the three solar farms in Valencia is over **€235 million** (US$284 million).
- **04/2021**: The green hydrogen project at bp’s refinery will require a **US$90 million** investment.
- **08/2024**: The total investment for the five photovoltaic and hybridization projects across various provinces, including Valencia, is **€380 million**. Specifically, the Cofrentes I project will involve an investment of over **€100 million**.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **03/2021**: Announcement of the three solar farms in Valencia.
- **04/2021**: Announcement of the green hydrogen project feasibility study.
- **08/2024**: Authorization received for the construction of the **Cofrentes I photovoltaic plant**.

#### 4. Labour
- **03/2021**: The three solar parks are expected to create **1,450 jobs**.
- **04/2021**: The construction and start-up of the green hydrogen project are anticipated to create additional jobs, although specific numbers are not provided.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify any joint ventures or partnerships regarding ownership of the facilities. It can be inferred that Iberdrola is the primary owner of the facilities mentioned, as they are described as projects being developed by Iberdrola.
