## ESP-01177-04806

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Danone Facility in Valencia, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: Danone's facilities in Spain, including Valencia, will receive 100% renewable electricity from the Francisco Pizarro solar photovoltaic plant, which has a capacity of 590 MW. This plant is set to be operational in 2022 and will supply Danone's factories and logistics centers across Spain.
- **Renewable Energy Supply**: The agreement with Iberdrola ensures an annual output of 73 GWh/year, with an additional green energy contract bringing the total to 104 GWh/year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Amount**: The Francisco Pizarro project represents a €300 million investment.
- **Employment Contribution**: The project is expected to create employment for more than 1,200 workers during its development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Power Purchase Agreement (PPA)**: On January 21, 2021, Danone and Iberdrola signed a 10-year PPA, which will commence in April 2022. This agreement guarantees the supply of renewable electricity to Danone's facilities in Spain.
- **Operational Date**: The Francisco Pizarro solar plant is expected to be operational in 2022.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The €300 million investment in the solar project is projected to contribute to the employment of over 1,200 workers during key stages of its delivery.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture**: The facility does not indicate exclusive ownership by Danone; rather, it is part of a collaborative effort with Iberdrola to secure renewable energy supply through a long-term PPA.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic initiatives related to the Danone facility in Valencia, Spain, focusing on its commitment to renewable energy and sustainability.
