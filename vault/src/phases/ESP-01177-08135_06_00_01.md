### Evolutionary Timeline of the Umbrella Facility in Valencia, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: The Umbrella facility is focused on photovoltaic energy, with a total capacity of 212MWp across a portfolio of 14 solar PV projects located in Extremadura, Andalucía, and Comunidad Valenciana.
- **Construction Start**: Construction activities for the most advanced projects are set to start in 2022, with IM2 Energía Solar, the company’s EPC subsidiary, entrusted with these activities.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2022**: Umbrella signed a portfolio-wide merchant term loan construction financing agreement with Incus Capital for a total amount of €106.5 million to fund the construction of the portfolio.
- **June 2022**: Umbrella closed an investment agreement with RGreen Invest, which committed to invest up to €42 million in the construction of the projects under a preferred equity scheme.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2022**: Umbrella secured funding for its 212MWp photovoltaic portfolio through a debt facility provided by Incus Capital and RGreen Invest's Infragreen IV Fund. The financial close for the first 27MWp project was expected to be achieved shortly after this date.
- **Project Infinity**: This project represents a key milestone in Umbrella's strategy to further integrate across the energy value chain and consolidate as an Independent Power Producer (IPP) with proprietary operating assets.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: While specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the articles, the construction of the solar projects and the operational activities are expected to create jobs, particularly through the involvement of IM2 Energía Solar, which has experience in engineering, procurement, and construction of over 150 projects in Spain and Chile.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: Umbrella is a global vertically integrated company that historically owned and developed the portfolio of solar PV projects. In June 2022, it closed an investment agreement with RGreen Invest to acquire a minority stake in the joint venture vehicle that will own and operate the 14 special purpose vehicles (SPVs) associated with the projects.
