## ESP-01180-00654

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Renault Group Facility in Valladolid, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Production**: The Valladolid facility currently produces the Renault Captur, which is available as a Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicle (PHEV). The facility previously assembled the Twizy until 2019.
- **Future Plans**: There are indications that Renault may expand its operations to include the production of purely electric vehicles at this facility, although this has not been officially confirmed.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Battery Supply Negotiations**: As of November 2023, Renault is in advanced negotiations with AESC (formerly Envision AESC) to supply battery cells for its vehicle plants in Valladolid and Palencia. AESC is planning to establish a battery cell factory in Navalmoral de la Mata, Cáceres, set to begin operations in 2025 with a capacity of up to 50 GWh.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Current Production**: The Valladolid plant is actively producing the Renault Captur as of the latest reports.
- **Future Production Plans**: Reports suggest that Renault may start producing electric vehicles for Polestar at its Spanish plant in Palencia as early as 2025, although this is still under consideration.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The potential expansion into electric vehicle production and the establishment of a battery cell supply chain could lead to job creation, although specific numbers have not been disclosed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The Renault Group owns the Valladolid facility exclusively, with no indications of joint ventures or partnerships affecting ownership.

### Summary
The Renault Group facility in Valladolid, Spain, is currently focused on producing the Renault Captur, including its PHEV variant. Future plans may involve the production of electric vehicles, supported by negotiations for battery supply with AESC. The facility is fully owned by Renault, and while job creation is anticipated with potential expansions, specific details remain unconfirmed.
