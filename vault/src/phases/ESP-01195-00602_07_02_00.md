### Evolutionary Timeline of the PSA Group Facility in Vigo, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2019**: PSA announces plans to assemble battery packs for electric and hybrid cars at its Vigo plant, in addition to its Trnava plant in Slovakia. The facility will support the production of models like the Peugeot e-208 and Opel Corsa-e.
- **November 2019**: PSA confirms that it has already started manufacturing battery packs with cells imported from China at its Vigo plant. The facility will also produce electric cars starting in 2020, including the new Peugeot 2008 and Citroën C4.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2019**: PSA plans to invest €250 million in its Figueruelas plant by 2021, which is part of a broader investment strategy that includes the Vigo facility. Additionally, PSA is set to benefit from public subsidies of €4 million per year.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2019**: Announcement of battery pack assembly plans at the Vigo facility.
- **November 2019**: Confirmation of battery pack production commencement at the Vigo plant and the start of electric car production in 2020.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2019**: The three PSA plants in Spain, including Vigo, employ approximately 14,000 workers, with 86% of the cars produced being exported. The announcement of electric vehicle production is expected to maintain or potentially increase job levels due to the expansion of operations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify any joint ventures or shared ownership for the Vigo facility, indicating that PSA Group likely owns the facility exclusively.
