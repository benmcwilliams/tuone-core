### Evolutionary Timeline of Stellantis Group Facility in Vigo, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2023**: Stellantis announced plans to convert its plants in Vigo and Zaragoza to produce electric small cars based on the STLA Small platform starting in 2025. The STLA Small platform is designed for micro and compact cars, enabling ranges of at least 500 kilometers.
- **November 2023**: Stellantis confirmed that production of small electric cars based on the STLA Small platform would begin in Vigo, with production expected to start in 2027 or 2028. The facility will produce models such as the Peugeot e-2008.
- **February 2024**: Reports indicated that the Vigo plant would start producing the new Peugeot e-2008 from September 2027, with production forecasts estimating 182,000 units for the e-2008 in 2028.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2023**: Stellantis plans to invest over one billion euros in its Spanish plants, including Vigo, to support the transition to electric vehicle production. The company applied for 150 million euros in subsidies under the Perte support program.
- **November 2023**: Stellantis expressed disappointment over receiving only 55.9 million euros in promised funding for a three-billion-euro project, which was significantly lower than the anticipated 200 million euros.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2023**: Initial announcement of plans to produce electric vehicles based on the STLA Small platform.
- **November 2023**: Official go-ahead for production of small electric cars in Vigo, with a timeline set for 2027 or 2028.
- **February 2024**: Confirmation of production schedules, with the Peugeot e-2008 expected to begin production in September 2027.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2023**: The announcement of the production of small electric cars is expected to create jobs, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify whether Stellantis owns the Vigo facility exclusively or if it is part of a joint venture. However, it is implied that Stellantis has significant control over the operations and production decisions at the facility. 

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic decisions regarding the Stellantis facility in Vigo, Spain, as it transitions towards electric vehicle production.
