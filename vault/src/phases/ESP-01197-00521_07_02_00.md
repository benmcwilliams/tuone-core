### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mercedes-Benz Facility in Vitoria, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2020**: Mercedes-Benz began taking orders for the EQV, a premium electric van produced at the Vitoria facility. The EQV features a 150 kW electric motor and a 100 kWh battery, with a range of up to 418 kilometers. The facility also produces combustion engine models, allowing for flexible production based on customer demand.
- **May 2023**: Mercedes-Benz announced the VAN.EA electric architecture for midsize and large vans, which will also be produced at the Vitoria plant. This architecture aims to reduce complexity and increase efficiency in the production of electric vans.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2020**: The production of the EQV at the Vitoria facility is part of Daimler's broader strategy to achieve climate-neutral production by 2022 across all its plants, including the Vitoria facility.
- **May 2023**: The Vitoria plant is set to produce VAN.EA-based midsize vans, indicating ongoing investment in electric vehicle technology and infrastructure.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2020**: The EQV was officially launched, with production starting at the Vitoria facility alongside the combustion engine models.
- **July 2020**: The EQV became available for order in the UK, further solidifying its market presence.
- **May 2023**: Announcement of the VAN.EA architecture indicates a significant milestone in the facility's evolution towards electric vehicle production.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2020**: The introduction of the EQV and its production at the Vitoria facility is expected to create jobs related to the manufacturing of electric vehicles, although specific job creation numbers were not mentioned.
- **May 2023**: The transition to producing VAN.EA-based vans may lead to job creation as the facility expands its electric vehicle production capabilities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility in Vitoria is owned exclusively by Mercedes-Benz, as it is part of the company's global production network for its commercial vehicles.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic shifts at the Mercedes-Benz facility in Vitoria, Spain, focusing on its transition towards electric vehicle production and the associated impacts on technology, investment, project milestones, labor, and ownership.
