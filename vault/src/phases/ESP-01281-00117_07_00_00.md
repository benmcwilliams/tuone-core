## ESP-01281-00117

### Evolutionary Timeline of the CAF Facility in Zaragoza, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **08/2021**: The CAF facility in Zaragoza is involved in the FCH2RAIL project, developing a hydrogen fuel cell hybrid power pack for rail applications. The facility is responsible for equipping a Renfe commuter train with a hybrid propulsion system that combines energy from overhead lines with hydrogen fuel cells and batteries. Static tests were successfully completed at the Zaragoza plant, and dynamic tests began on external tracks.
- **06/2023**: The first hydrogen-powered train prototype, developed by CAF, commenced testing on the Canfranc line. The train operates in electric mode on electrified sections and switches to hybrid mode on non-electrified sections.
- **12/2024**: The FCH2RAIL demonstrator train successfully completed testing on six commercial lines in Spain and Portugal, achieving over 800 km autonomy and demonstrating its capabilities on the demanding Zaragoza-Canfranc line.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **08/2021**: The FCH2RAIL project has a total budget of €14 million, with €10 million funded by the European Clean Hydrogen Partnership. This funding supports the development and testing of the hydrogen drive system.
- **06/2023**: Iberdrola participates as a supplier of green hydrogen, contributing to the project's success.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **08/2021**: The FCH2RAIL project officially starts, with the first reference routes and operating scenarios defined for the prototype.
- **06/2023**: The hydrogen-powered train enters the testing phase on the Railway Network of General Interest, marking a significant milestone for the project.
- **12/2024**: The demonstrator train achieves authorization to run tests on the Spanish National Railway Network, completing its first test run on the Zaragoza-Canfranc line.

#### 4. Labour
- **12/2024**: The project has led to the training of train drivers and managers from Renfe to operate the converted bi-mode hydrogen train, indicating job creation related to the new technology.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is part of a consortium involving multiple partners, including CAF, Renfe, Adif, DLR, Toyota, CNH2, IP, and Stemmann-Technik. This indicates that CAF does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a collaborative effort for the FCH2RAIL project.
