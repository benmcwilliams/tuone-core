## ESP-01281-00120

### Evolutionary Timeline of the CATL Facility in Zaragoza, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2024**: Stellantis and CATL announced the establishment of a joint venture to create an LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate) battery cell production facility in Zaragoza. The facility is targeted to start production by the end of 2026, with an anticipated annual capacity of up to 50 GWh, subject to market conditions and regulatory support.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2024**: The joint venture between Stellantis and CATL involves an investment of up to €4.1 billion for the construction of the battery plant. This investment aims to enhance Stellantis' LFP battery offerings in Europe and support the production of affordable electric vehicles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2023**: Stellantis and CATL signed a non-binding Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) for the local supply of LFP battery cells and modules.
- **June 2025**: Construction work is scheduled to begin on the facility.
- **March 2028**: Expected completion date for the construction of the battery plant.
- **End of 2026**: Targeted start date for battery production at the facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2024**: The new battery cell factory is expected to create approximately 3,000 direct jobs in the region, contributing significantly to the local economy and the automotive sector in Aragon.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2024**: The facility will be operated as a 50:50 joint venture between Stellantis and CATL, indicating shared ownership and collaboration in the production of LFP batteries.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic decisions regarding the CATL facility in Zaragoza, highlighting its significance in the context of the electric vehicle market and the broader clean technology landscape.
