### Evolutionary Timeline of the Opel Facility in Zaragoza, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2018**: Opel announced plans to launch an all-electric version of the Corsa, set to begin production in Zaragoza by 2020. This marked the facility as the first in Europe to produce an all-electric Opel/Vauxhall model. The Corsa will be part of a broader strategy to electrify all European Opel/Vauxhall passenger car models by 2024.
- **August 2019**: The facility was highlighted for its flexibility in production, allowing for various drive variants of the new Opel Corsa to be built on a single production line, thanks to the Common Modular Platform (CMP).
- **July 2023**: Production of the Peugeot e-208 was transferred to Zaragoza, alongside the Opel Corsa Electric, indicating an expansion of the facility's production capabilities for electric vehicles.
- **November 2023**: Opel confirmed that the successors to the Crossland and Grandland models will also be produced at the Zaragoza facility, with the electric version of the Crossland expected to launch in 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2018**: The announcement of the electric Corsa indicated a strategic investment in the Zaragoza facility to support the production of electric vehicles, aligning with PSA's broader electrification strategy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2018**: Announcement of the all-electric Corsa production in Zaragoza, with a target production start in 2020.
- **August 2019**: Opel's strategy to produce various electrified models on the CMP platform was detailed, emphasizing the facility's role in this initiative.
- **July 2023**: Confirmation of the transfer of Peugeot e-208 production to Zaragoza, indicating a milestone in expanding the facility's electric vehicle production.
- **November 2023**: Official confirmation of the electric successors to the Crossland and Grandland models, with production slated for Zaragoza.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2018**: The transition to electric vehicle production was expected to create new job opportunities at the Zaragoza facility, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed.
- **November 2023**: The expansion of production capabilities for electric vehicles, including the upcoming models, is likely to result in job creation, although exact figures were not provided.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility in Zaragoza is owned by Opel, which is a subsidiary of the PSA Group (now part of Stellantis). There is no indication of joint ventures or shared ownership for this specific facility.
