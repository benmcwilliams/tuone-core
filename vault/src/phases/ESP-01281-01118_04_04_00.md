### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Zaragoza, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2021**: The Puylobo Wind Farm in Zaragoza began operations with an installed capacity of 49 MW, utilizing 14 SG 3.4-132 wind turbines. The facility is designed to provide green wind energy to 13,500 supply points of Vodafone’s network in Spain.
- **March 2021**: Iberdrola announced the development of a 10 MW green hydrogen generation project at the PLAZA Logistics Platform in Zaragoza. This facility will produce green hydrogen for heavy road and rail transport, incorporating a photovoltaic power plant for self-consumption.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: The green hydrogen project at PLAZA represents an investment of over €36 million. This initiative is part of Iberdrola's broader investment strategy, which includes a historic investment plan of €75 billion for the period 2020-2025, with approximately €14.3 billion allocated for projects in Spain.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2020**: The Puylobo Wind Farm was completed and began operations after a construction period of eight months, despite the challenges posed by the pandemic.
- **March 2021**: Announcement of the green hydrogen generation project, which is one of 150 initiatives submitted to the EU’s Next Generation programme.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2021**: The green hydrogen project is expected to create 1,800 direct and indirect jobs during its construction and operational phases. This includes contracts for local companies in Aragón, enhancing regional employment and economic activity.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify any joint ventures or partnerships regarding ownership of the facilities. It can be inferred that Iberdrola is leading these projects, indicating a level of exclusive ownership or control over the facilities mentioned.
