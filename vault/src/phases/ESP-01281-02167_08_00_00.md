### Evolutionary Timeline of the BayWa Facility in Zaragoza, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2018**: BayWa completed a 288kW solar PV self-consumption installation for Pastores Grupo Cooperativo at their lamb processing plant in Zaragoza. This installation is designed to save the cooperative €700,000 on energy costs over 25 years. The project utilized Huawei inverters and Longi panels, and was part of the European SCOoPE project aimed at enhancing energy efficiency in agri-businesses.

- **March 2024**: BayWa announced the construction of the Rueda Sur Cluster, which includes two photovoltaic plants with a total capacity of 53 MW, alongside three wind energy plants totaling 135 MW. This project is expected to generate approximately 475 GWh of green electricity annually, enough to power over 121,000 homes.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2024**: The Rueda Sur Cluster project represents a total investment of over €280 million, combining both wind and solar technologies to optimize land use and reduce infrastructure costs.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2018**: Completion of the 288kW solar installation for Pastores Grupo Cooperativo.
- **March 2024**: Announcement of the Rueda Sur Cluster project, with construction expected to begin shortly and completion anticipated by the end of 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2024**: The Rueda Sur Cluster project is expected to create jobs and promote local community benefits, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2018**: BayWa r.e. is the sole developer of the solar installation for Pastores Grupo Cooperativo, indicating full ownership of that facility.
- **March 2024**: BayWa r.e. is also the sole developer of the Rueda Sur Cluster, indicating full ownership of this project as well.
