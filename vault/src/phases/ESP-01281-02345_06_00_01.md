### Evolutionary Timeline of the Lightsource Facility in Zaragoza, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **03-02-2020**: Lightsource BP announces its first solar project in Spain, a 250 MW cluster in Zaragoza, utilizing bifacial panels and trackers for maximum efficiency. The project is backed by a seven-year power purchase agreement with BP European Gas and Power.
- **24-06-2021**: The 247 MW Vendimia solar plant in Zaragoza begins operations, supplying energy to BP’s European power trading team under a long-term PPA. 
- **16-12-2021**: Construction begins on the 50 MW Torrubia solar project in La Muela, Zaragoza, marking it as the eighth photovoltaic plant in the region for Lightsource BP.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **03-02-2020**: Lightsource BP secures a senior debt facility from NatWest to finance the 250 MW solar cluster in Zaragoza, which is part of a larger 300 MW acquisition.
- **16-12-2021**: The construction of the Torrubia project is financed through a credit line of $1.8 billion that Lightsource BP recently closed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **03-02-2020**: Announcement of the 250 MW solar cluster in Zaragoza, with construction expected to start later in the year.
- **23-09-2020**: The 250 MW solar portfolio in Zaragoza is in the final stages of construction, expected to begin operations by the end of 2020.
- **24-06-2021**: The Vendimia solar plant in Zaragoza is powered up and operational.
- **16-12-2021**: Construction of the Torrubia solar project begins.

#### 4. Labour
- **16-12-2021**: The construction of the Torrubia project is expected to contribute to job creation in the region, although specific numbers are not provided in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **03-02-2020**: Lightsource BP is a joint venture, with 50% ownership by British energy company BP and 50% by UK solar developer Lightsource. The facility in Zaragoza is part of this joint venture structure.
