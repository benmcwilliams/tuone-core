### Evolutionary Timeline of Repsol Facility in Zaragoza, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2019**: Construction began on the Delta wind project, a 335MW wind farm located in the provinces of Zaragoza and Teruel, featuring 89 turbines. The project is part of Repsol's broader renewable energy strategy.
- **October 2020**: Repsol completed the grid connection for the first turbines of the Delta wind complex, with full commercial operations expected to begin by the end of 2020.
- **March 2021**: The Delta wind farm became fully operational, producing an estimated 992GWh of clean energy annually, equivalent to the average annual consumption of 300,000 households.
- **May 2023**: Repsol signed contracts with Siemens Gamesa for the supply of 12 onshore turbines totaling 60 MW as part of the Delta II project, further expanding its wind energy capacity in Zaragoza.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2020**: Repsol announced a total investment of €600 million across three renewable projects, including Delta, Kappa, and Valdesolar, with Delta representing a significant portion of this investment.
- **October 2020**: The investment in the Delta project was reported to be €300 million, contributing to Repsol's goal of increasing its renewable energy capacity.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2019**: Construction of the Delta wind project commenced.
- **October 2020**: Delta wind project completed grid connection for initial turbines; commercial operations expected by year-end.
- **March 2021**: Delta wind farm officially became operational.
- **May 2023**: New contracts signed for additional turbines as part of the Delta II project.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2020**: During the construction of the Valdesolar solar project, it was reported that the project would employ between 300 and 500 people, indicating a similar trend for the Delta project during its construction phase.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2021**: Repsol sold a 49% stake in the operational Delta wind farm to Pontegadea for €245 million, indicating a joint venture structure for the facility while Repsol retains majority ownership.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones of the Repsol facility in Zaragoza, Spain, focusing on its operations, investments, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
