## ESP-01281-02453

### Evolutionary Timeline of Stellantis Group Facility in Zaragoza, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2024**: Stellantis and CATL announced a 50:50 joint venture to establish an LFP battery cell production facility in Zaragoza. The facility is targeted to start production by the end of 2026, with a potential capacity of up to 50 GWh, depending on the electric vehicle market's evolution in Europe and support from authorities.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2023**: Stellantis and CATL confirmed an investment of up to €4.1 billion for the battery plant in Zaragoza. This investment aims to enhance Stellantis' battery offerings in Europe, focusing on high-quality, durable, and affordable battery-electric vehicles.
- **June 2023**: Stellantis announced plans to invest over €1 billion in its Spanish plants, including Zaragoza, to produce electric small cars based on the STLA Small platform starting in 2025. The company applied for €150 million in subsidies under the Perte support program.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2023**: Stellantis and CATL signed a non-binding MOU for local supply of LFP battery cells and modules for electric vehicle production in Europe.
- **June 2025**: Construction of the battery plant is scheduled to begin.
- **March 2028**: Completion of the battery plant is expected.
- **End of 2026**: Battery production is set to commence at the existing Zaragoza vehicle plant.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2023**: The new battery cell factory is expected to create approximately 3,000 direct jobs in Zaragoza, contributing significantly to the local economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2023**: The facility will be a 50:50 joint venture between Stellantis and CATL, indicating that Stellantis does not own the facility exclusively but shares ownership with CATL.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic decisions regarding the Stellantis facility in Zaragoza, Spain, focusing on its technological advancements, investments, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure.
