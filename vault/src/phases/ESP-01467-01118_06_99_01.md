### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Puertollano, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05-09-2019**: Iberdrola submitted a proposal for a 250MW solar portfolio, including the unique 100MW Puertollano II project featuring bifacial panels, daisy-chain inverters, and a 5MW/20MWh battery system.
- **17-03-2020**: Announcement of a 100 MW/20 MWh solar-plus-storage plant linked to hydrogen production in Puertollano, marking it as one of the largest installations of its kind in Europe.
- **18-03-2020**: The project will consist of a 100 MW solar plant, a 20 MWh lithium-ion battery system, and a 20 MW electrolyzer for hydrogen production.
- **26-05-2022**: The plant was inaugurated, now recognized as the largest operational green hydrogen plant in the world, capable of producing up to 3,000 tonnes of green hydrogen annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **17-03-2020**: The project requires an investment of up to €150 million.
- **27-04-2023**: Iberdrola announced a €2 billion plan to replace imported gas with green hydrogen for ammonia production, with a potential investment of €1.8 billion for the project in Puertollano.
- **25-04-2022**: The European Investment Bank (EIB) and Instituto de Credito Oficial (ICO) agreed to lend €88 million to finance the green hydrogen project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **17-03-2020**: Announcement of the solar-plus-storage plant linked to hydrogen production.
- **18-03-2020**: Detailed project description released.
- **26-05-2022**: Official opening of the green hydrogen plant in Puertollano.
- **01-06-2022**: Iberdrola confirmed the plant's operational status and its capacity to produce green hydrogen.

#### 4. Labour
- **09-04-2020**: The project is expected to create up to 700 jobs during its implementation phase.
- **25-04-2022**: An estimated 300 jobs will be created during the implementation phase of the project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **17-03-2020**: Iberdrola is the sole owner of the facility, as it is responsible for the production of green hydrogen from 100% renewable sources, in partnership with Fertiberia for the utilization of the produced hydrogen.
