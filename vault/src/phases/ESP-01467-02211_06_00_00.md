### Evolutionary Timeline of the Enagas Facility in Puertollano, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2021**: Enagas, in collaboration with Repsol, is developing a cutting-edge technology for the production of green hydrogen through photoelectrocatalysis, which utilizes solar energy directly. The facility aims to construct a demonstration plant in Puertollano, with a production capacity of 100 kg of renewable hydrogen per day, scheduled to start operations in **2024**. A larger industrial phase is planned for **2028**, targeting a production capacity of up to 10 tons of hydrogen per day.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2021**: Repsol and Enagas have invested €8 million in the development of the photoelectrocatalysis project. This investment is part of their commitment to advancing renewable hydrogen technologies and achieving commercial maturity by **2030**.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2021**: The project has reached a significant milestone with the installation of a 1 m² pilot plant at Repsol Technology Lab, following the proof of concept launched in **November 2020**. The next steps include the construction of a demonstration plant in Puertollano in **2024** and a larger industrial plant by **2028**.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify exact job creation or job loss figures related to the investments or expansions at the Puertollano facility. However, the project is noted for being led by women and emphasizes a commitment to diversity in technological projects.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2021**: The facility is co-owned by Repsol and Enagas, as they have formed a joint venture to develop the photoelectrocatalysis technology. Both companies hold proprietary rights to the technology, which has resulted in 65 patent applications across more than 30 countries.
