## ESP-01467-02232

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fertiberia Facility in Puertollano, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2020**: Iberdrola and Fertiberia announced plans to develop a landmark solar-storage-hydrogen facility in Puertollano, which will include a 100 MW solar PV plant, a 20 MWh lithium-ion battery system, and a 20 MW electrolyser. This facility is expected to be one of the largest industrial green hydrogen facilities in Europe and will supply green hydrogen to Fertiberia’s ammonia plant, allowing a reduction in natural gas demand by over 10%.
- **December 2021**: The facility began producing green hydrogen, marking it as the largest green hydrogen production plant for industrial use in Europe. The plant utilizes a 100 MW solar array and a 20 MW electrolyzer.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2020**: The project was initiated with a total investment of €150 million (approximately $175 million), with Iberdrola and Fertiberia contributing €50 million ($58.5 million) specifically for the construction of the plant.
- **October 2020**: Iberdrola and Fertiberia announced a broader partnership to develop 800 MW of green hydrogen production capacity with an investment of €1.8 billion over seven years, which includes the initial plant in Puertollano.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2020**: Announcement of the project and investment details.
- **2021**: The facility was scheduled to become operational, with the first production of green hydrogen reported in December 2021.
- **April 2023**: The facility was noted as operational, providing 10% of the energy needed for Fertiberia's neighboring factory, with plans for future expansions to meet 100% of the factory's energy needs.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2020**: The project is expected to create up to 700 jobs during its construction and operational phases, contributing to local employment and industrial growth.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2020**: The facility is a joint venture between Iberdrola and Fertiberia, with both companies collaborating on the development and operation of the green hydrogen production plant.
