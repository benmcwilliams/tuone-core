## ESP-01467-02424

### Evolutionary Timeline of Repsol Facility in Puertollano, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2021**: Repsol and Enagás announced the development of a pioneering technology for producing green hydrogen through photoelectrocatalysis, which uses solar energy directly. The facility aims to reach commercial maturity before 2030, with a demonstration plant planned for construction in Puertollano.
- **2024**: A demonstration plant is scheduled to be built in Puertollano, with an initial production capacity of 100 kg of renewable hydrogen per day. A larger plant is planned for 2028, targeting a production capacity of up to 10 tons per day.
- **2021-2023**: Repsol has been working on scaling up the photoelectrocatalysis technology, which integrates the hydrogen production process into a single step, improving efficiency and reducing costs.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2021**: Repsol and Enagás have invested €8 million in the development of the photoelectrocatalysis project, which has resulted in 65 patent applications globally.
- **Future Plans**: Repsol aims to install a total of 557 MW of renewable hydrogen capacity in Spain by 2025 and reach 1.9 GW by 2030, indicating significant future investments in hydrogen production infrastructure.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2021**: Announcement of the construction of a demonstration plant in Puertollano, with the goal of achieving commercial maturity by 2030.
- **2024**: Planned construction of the electrolyzer based on photoelectrocatalysis at the Puertollano industrial complex.
- **2028**: Target for the installation of a larger plant in Puertollano, with a production capacity of up to 10 tons of hydrogen per day.

#### 4. Labour
- **Future Projections**: The broader hydrogen initiatives, including the Basque Hydrogen Corridor, are expected to create over 1300 jobs, although specific job creation related to the Puertollano facility has not been detailed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2021**: Repsol and Enagás are co-owners of the photoelectrocatalysis technology and the associated projects, indicating a joint venture structure for the facility's development.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic plans for the Repsol facility in Puertollano, focusing on its innovative approach to renewable hydrogen production and the collaborative efforts with Enagás.

### Technology and Operations
- **Article 1 (25-08-2021)**: "The next step will be the construction of a demo plant in Repsol’s Puertollano industrial complex, with the aim of reaching commercial maturity before 2030."
- **Article 3 (27-08-2021)**: "The plan to reach the industrial and economic maturity of this technology has two additional scaling stages defined: A demonstrator that will start up in 2024 in Puertollano, with an area close to half a hectare and with a production of 100 kg a day of renewable hydrogen; and a final phase, scheduled for 2028, with the installation, also in Puertollano, of a plant, already in the industrial phase, of about 60 hectares and with planned production of up to 10 t a day."
- **Article 5 (09-10-2023)**: "Repsol inaugurated its 2.5 megawatt (MW) renewable hydrogen electrolyzer at the Petronor refinery in Muskiz, Spain."
