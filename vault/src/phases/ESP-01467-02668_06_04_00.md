### Aurinka Facility Evolution Timeline - Puertollano, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2020**: Aurinka announces plans to start production of upgraded metallurgical grade silicon (UMG-Si) in Puertollano, utilizing a patented low-carbon production process. The facility aims to produce silicon with a purity of 99.999999% and has already manufactured 20 tons of silicon, which was certified by TÜV Rheinland. The facility is expected to contribute to the revival of Spain's photovoltaic manufacturing sector.
- **April 2021**: Aurinka tests the degradation rate of solar cells made from 100% UMG silicon, finding it comparable to conventional polysilicon. The construction of the UMG factory in Puertollano is reported to be nearing completion, albeit slowly.
- **March 2022**: Aurinka plans to open a solar module factory with an annual capacity of 300 MW, producing bifacial modules with a power output of 600 W. This facility is part of a broader industrial project that includes UMG silicon manufacturing in Puertollano.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2020**: The project in Puertollano requires an investment of nearly €60 million. The Reindus program from the Ministry of Industry awarded the project the highest score that year, indicating strong governmental support for the investment.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2020**: Announcement of UMG silicon production plans in Puertollano.
- **April 2021**: Construction of the UMG factory is reported to be finishing, although at a slow pace.
- **March 2022**: Plans for a solar module manufacturing facility are announced, with a capacity of 300 MW, indicating ongoing development and expansion of operations.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2020**: The announcement of the facility's development suggests potential job creation, although specific numbers are not provided in the articles. The revival of the photovoltaic manufacturing sector in Spain is expected to contribute positively to local employment.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2020**: Aurinka operates the facility through a joint venture with Técnicas Reunidas and X-Elio, indicating that it does not have exclusive ownership but is part of a collaborative effort to establish the UMG silicon production in Puertollano.
