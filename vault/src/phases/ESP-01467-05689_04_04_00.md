## ESP-01467-05689

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Nordex Electrolyzers Facility in Puertollano, ESP

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2023**: The first prototype of a 50 kW pressurized alkaline electrolyzer became operational at the Nordex Electrolyzers facility in Puertollano. It is currently in the testing and characterization phase, showing promising results in efficiency and flexibility with renewable energies.
- **June 2024**: Nordex Electrolyzers developed a 500 kW pressurized alkaline electrolyzer prototype, capable of producing over 10 kg/h of hydrogen with an energy consumption of less than 50 kWh/kg. This prototype is designed to handle power changes and frequent stops, differentiating it from competitors.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2024**: The electrolyzer development plan is partially financed with €12 million from the European Commission through the IPCEI (Important Project of Common European Interest) under the Hy2Tech program. This funding supports the strategic development of the electrolyzer technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2022**: Nordex Electrolyzers was created as a joint venture between the Government of Navarra and Nordex Group, focusing on the design and manufacturing of pressurized alkaline electrolyzers.
- **2023**: The 50 kW prototype became operational at the Puertollano facility.
- **June 2024**: The 500 kW prototype was publicly presented and inaugurated at the Nordex facility in Lumbier, with plans for further development leading to a serializable MW-scale product by 2026.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2024**: The facility currently employs 26 people, with plans for growth as technological development milestones are achieved. The joint venture aims to create additional jobs as production capacity expands.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **2022 - Present**: Nordex Electrolyzers operates as a joint venture between the Government of Navarra (holding a 15% stake through SODENA) and Nordex Group. This indicates that the facility is not under exclusive ownership by Nordex Group but is a collaborative effort with public sector involvement.
