### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Álava, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2021**: Iberdrola commissioned the Arañuelo III photovoltaic plant, which has a production capacity of 40 MW. This facility is notable for being the first photovoltaic project in Spain to feature a storage system, specifically a 3 MW battery with 9 MWh of storage capacity. The Arañuelo complex, which includes Arañuelo I, II, and III, has a total capacity of 143 MW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2021**: Iberdrola invested €80 million to develop the Campo Arañuelo complex, which includes the Arañuelo III plant. This investment is part of a broader plan where Iberdrola aims to increase its renewable energy capacity significantly, with a total investment of €14.3 billion in Spain by 2025, of which €7 billion is specifically for renewable energy projects.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2021**: The Arañuelo III plant was officially commissioned, marking a significant milestone as it is the first renewable energy project in Spain to incorporate battery storage technology.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2021**: The construction of the Arañuelo complex involved 500 workers, with most fieldwork and civil engineering contracted out to local companies, thereby stimulating job opportunities in the region.
