## ESP-01745-01549

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Cobra Infrastructuras Hidráulicas Facility in Gran Canaria, ESP

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05/2023**: Cobra Infrastructuras Hidráulicas, in a consortium with GE, is contracted to deliver six pumped storage turbines for the 200MW Chira Soria pumped storage power plant in Gran Canaria. The facility will have a total energy storage capacity of 3.5 GWh and is designed to stabilize the grid by acting as a natural battery. The project includes a seawater desalination plant with a capacity of 5,200 m³ per day and a 24 km pressure pipe.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05/2023**: The Chira Soria project is expected to increase renewable energy production on Gran Canaria by 37% and raise the average annual coverage of demand using renewable generation to 51%. It is projected to save €122 million ($135 million) annually by reducing dependence on expensive energy imports.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05/2023**: The project is announced, with the hydropower plant expected to come online in 2027. The scope of work includes the design, supply, transport, and commissioning of turbines, generators, main transformers, and electrical balance-of-plant equipment.

#### 4. Labour
- **05/2023**: While specific job creation or loss figures are not mentioned in the articles, the project is expected to enhance the security of the electricity system, which may imply potential job creation in the renewable energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **05/2023**: The facility is part of a consortium between Cobra Infrastructuras Hidráulicas and GE, indicating a joint venture structure for the Chira Soria project.
