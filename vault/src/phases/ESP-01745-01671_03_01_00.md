## ESP-01745-01671

### Evolutionary Timeline of the General Electric Facility in Gran Canaria, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05/2023**: General Electric (GE) has secured a contract to supply six pumped storage turbines for the 200MW Chira Soria pumped storage power plant in Gran Canaria. The turbines will have a total energy storage capacity of 3.5 GWh and are expected to become operational in 2027. The project aims to stabilize the grid by acting as giant natural batteries, enhancing the integration of renewable energy sources.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05/2023**: The Chira Soria project is expected to increase renewable energy production on Gran Canaria by 37%, raising the average annual coverage of demand using renewable generation to 51%. The project is anticipated to save €122 million ($135 million) annually by reducing dependence on expensive energy imports.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05/2023**: GE, in consortium with Cobra Infrastructuras Hidráulicas, has been selected by Red Eléctrica to deliver the turbines for the Chira Soria project. The project includes the design, supply, transport, and commissioning of turbines, generators, main transformers, and electrical balance-of-plant equipment. The hydropower project is set to come online in 2027.

#### 4. Labour
- **05/2023**: While specific job creation numbers are not mentioned, the project is expected to enhance the security of the electricity system in the Canary Islands, which may imply potential job creation related to the construction and operation of the facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **05/2023**: The facility is part of a consortium involving GE and Cobra Infrastructuras Hidráulicas, indicating a joint venture structure for the Chira Soria project, rather than exclusive ownership by GE.
