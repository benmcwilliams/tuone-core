### Evolutionary Timeline of the Red Eléctrica de España Facility in Gran Canaria, ESP

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2022**: Construction began on the **Salto de Chira** pumped hydro energy storage (PHES) plant with a capacity of **200 MW** and **3,500 MWh** of storage. The facility will utilize two existing reservoirs formed by the Chira and Soria dams. It is expected to be partially operational by **2026** and will provide **36%** of the island's peak demand.
- **May 2023**: Work on the Salto de Chira project resumed after a temporary halt due to archaeological concerns. The facility will continue to have a storage capacity of **3.5 GWh**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2022**: The total investment for the Salto de Chira facility is over **€400 million** (approximately **$453.75 million**). This investment aims to promote energy independence and reduce fossil fuel imports, potentially saving the electricity grid **€122 million** annually.
- **May 2023**: The investment remains at around **€400 million** ($439.3 million) as the project continues to develop.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2022**: Announcement of the start of construction on the Salto de Chira PHES plant.
- **May 2023**: The project was resumed after a suspension due to archaeological findings, confirming that the cavities and canals discovered were not of significant archaeological value.
