### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fred Olsen Renewables Facility in Gran Canaria, ESP

1. **Technology and Operations**:
   - **Date**: January 2021
   - **Details**: Fred Olsen Renewables, in collaboration with Ocean Sun, is developing a 250kWp floating solar pilot project off the coast of Gran Canaria. This project aims to explore the performance of floating solar technology in challenging sea conditions, specifically with waves up to 10 meters and high winds. The technology utilizes a thin polymer membrane to mount PV modules.

2. **Investment and Development**:
   - **Date**: January 2021
   - **Details**: The pilot project has a budget of €4 million, funded by the European Union’s Horizon 2020 research and innovation programme. This funding is specifically tied to the development of the floating solar pilot project.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**:
   - **Date**: January 2021
   - **Details**: The pilot project is set to last for 30 months. Following installation, the project will analyze all aspects of the system to develop a plan for further commercialization and large-scale deployments. The project serves as a certification effort for Ocean Sun’s floating solar technology for offshore applications in non-sheltered locations.

4. **Labour**:

5. **Ownership Structure**:
   - **Date**: January 2021
