## ESP-01745-07851

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Greenalia Facility in Gran Canaria, ESP

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** September 2020
   - **Details:** Greenalia announced plans to build four floating offshore wind projects in Gran Canaria, each with a capacity of 50MW, totaling an installed capacity of 200MW. The facilities will utilize four 12.5MW offshore wind turbines on floating foundations anchored to the seabed at depths of 61m to 92m. The projects are expected to produce enough clean energy to power over 280,000 homes and offset approximately 560,000 tons of carbon emissions annually.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** September 2020
   - **Details:** Greenalia filed project and environmental planning applications for the development of the four wind facilities, indicating the initiation of investment in the projects. Specific investment amounts were not disclosed in the article.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** September 2020
   - **Details:** The announcement marked the beginning of the development process for the Dunas, Mojo, Cardon, and Guanche wind facilities. The projects are part of the broader goal to achieve 310MW of offshore wind capacity as outlined in the Canary Islands 2015-2025 Strategic Energy Plan.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** September 2020
   - **Details:** During the construction phase of the projects, it is expected that hundreds of direct and indirect jobs will be created, contributing to the development of a green economy in the Gran Canaria area.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** September 2020
   - **Details:** The article does not specify whether Greenalia owns the facility exclusively or if it is a joint venture. However, it implies that Greenalia is the primary developer of the projects.
