## ESP-01745-08139

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Universidad Europea de Canarias Facility in Gran Canaria, ESP

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 12-03-2021
   - **Details:** The facility is involved in a feasibility study for a 1.53 MW floating solar plant designed to power the Piedra Santa desalination plant in Gran Canaria. The floating PV system aims to cover the catchment basin of the desalination plant, potentially meeting around 3% of its total power demand. The study indicates that covering the water surface with solar panels could improve the efficiency of the reverse osmosis process by stabilizing seawater temperatures.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 12-03-2021
   - **Details:** The planned floating solar project is expected to require an investment of approximately €2 million ($2.4 million).

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 12-03-2021
   - **Details:** The project is currently in the feasibility-study phase, with no construction or production start dates announced yet.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 12-03-2021
   - **Details:** There is no specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with this project at this time.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 12-03-2021
   - **Details:** The facility is part of a collaboration involving Emalsa, Instituto Tecnologico de Canarias (ITC), and Universidad Europea de Canarias, indicating a joint effort rather than exclusive ownership by any single entity.
