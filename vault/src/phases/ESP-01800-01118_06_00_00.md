## ESP-01800-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Andalusia, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2020**: Iberdrola is involved in multiple solar projects, including a 50 MW solar project in Andalusia as part of a larger 3 GW renewable energy project pipeline aimed for completion by 2022.
- **May 2021**: Iberdrola announces plans to build Europe’s largest solar-storage-hydrogen project in Andalusia, indicating a focus on integrating solar energy with hydrogen production.
- **August 2022**: Iberdrola is set to commission 1.4 GW of solar PV in Spain, with projects in Andalusia contributing to a total capacity of 271 MW across six projects in the region.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2021**: Iberdrola allocates over €1 billion for a floating offshore wind farm project, which is part of a broader investment strategy that includes solar projects in Andalusia.
- **August 2022**: Iberdrola secures a US$562 million green loan from the European Investment Bank (EIB) to boost solar and wind projects in Spain, including those in Andalusia.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2020**: Iberdrola announces the construction of a 328 MW solar park in Extremadura, which is part of its broader renewable energy strategy but highlights its ongoing commitment to solar energy in Spain.
- **May 2021**: Iberdrola reveals plans for a solar-plus-hydrogen project in Andalusia, marking a significant milestone in integrating renewable energy with hydrogen production.
- **August 2022**: Iberdrola has 12 solar projects under construction, with several located in Andalusia, indicating active development in the region.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2021**: The floating offshore wind farm project is expected to generate between 1,000 and 2,000 jobs during the initial phases (2021-2022) and over 2,800 jobs per year until operational in 2026, contributing to job creation in the renewable sector.
- **May 2021**: The announcement of the solar-storage-hydrogen project in Andalusia is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers are not provided.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2023**: Iberdrola sells a 49% stake in its 1.27 GW renewable energy portfolio to Norges Bank Investment Management (NBIM), retaining 51% ownership and continuing to control and manage the assets, which include solar projects in Andalusia.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic moves of Iberdrola in Andalusia, focusing on solar energy production, investments, project milestones, job creation, and ownership structure.
