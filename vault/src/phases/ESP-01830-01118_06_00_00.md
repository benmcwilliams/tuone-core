### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Cuenca, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2019**: Iberdrola announced plans for two solar parks near Cuenca, with a total generation capacity of **800 MW** (Tarancón I and II, each with **400 MW** capacity). The project is in the preliminary development phase.
- **February 2020**: Iberdrola is developing the **Romeral** (50 MW) and **Olmedilla** (50 MW) photovoltaic plants in Cuenca, which will produce clean energy for approximately **24,500** and **30,000 homes** respectively, preventing emissions of **15,000** and **18,000 tons of CO2** annually.
- **August 2021**: Iberdrola commissioned its first photovoltaic plant in Castile-La Mancha, the **Barcience plant**, with an installed power of **50 MW**.

- **June 2019**: The total investment for the 800 MW solar project near Cuenca is not specified, but it is part of Iberdrola's broader investment strategy to expand its renewable energy portfolio.
