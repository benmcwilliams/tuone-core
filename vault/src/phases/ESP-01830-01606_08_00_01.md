### Evolutionary Timeline of the Elecnor Facility in Cuenca, Spain

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **November 2021:** Elecnor is constructing the Gecama Wind Farm, which will have an installed power capacity of over 300 MW. The facility is designed to generate approximately 1,000 GWh of energy annually, sufficient to power around 280,000 homes for a year. The project includes the construction of 69 turbine foundations, 114 km of 30 kV underground networks, 18 km of 132 kV underground lines, three substations, and a 48 km 400 kV evacuation line.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **November 2021:** The total investment for the Gecama Wind Farm is reported to be 64.3 million euros. This investment is associated with the construction of the facility and its infrastructure.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **November 2021:** Announcement of the Gecama Wind Farm project, marking it as the largest single-phase wind project in Spain.
   - **Mid-2022:** Expected completion date for the initial construction phase of the Gecama Wind Farm. Additionally, Elecnor has been awarded a contract for an extension involving the installation of four additional turbines, which will be constructed concurrently with the first phase.

4. **Labour:**
   - **November 2021:** The construction of the Gecama Wind Farm is projected to create up to 460 jobs simultaneously during the construction phase.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **November 2021:** The Gecama Wind Farm is promoted by Enlight Renewable Energy, with Elecnor responsible for the construction. The ownership structure indicates that Elecnor is not the exclusive owner of the facility, as it is a collaboration with Enlight Renewable Energy.
