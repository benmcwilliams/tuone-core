### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hive Facility in Cuenca, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2020**: Hive Energy received environmental and planning approval for the **169MW Sabinar solar farm** in Cuenca, Spain. The project is planned to cover an area of **333 hectares** and is part of a larger initiative that includes the **83MW Sabinar 2** project, collectively generating **421MW** of renewable energy. This facility is part of Hive Energy's **2.5GW pipeline** of PV projects worldwide.
  
- **March 2021**: Hive Energy sold the **252MW Sabinar project** to a joint venture between Noy Fund and Nofar Energy, named **Andromeda Solutions**. The project will be constructed in two phases: **Sabinar 1 (169MW)** and **Sabinar 2 (83MW)**, with construction expected to start in the **second quarter of 2021**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: The sale of the Sabinar project to Andromeda Solutions marks a significant investment move, allowing Hive Energy to focus on expanding its solar and green hydrogen portfolio in Spain. The financial details of the sale were not disclosed, but it is noted as a "landmark" project for Hive.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2020**: Approval for the Sabinar solar farm was granted, with construction anticipated to begin in **summer 2021**.
  
- **March 2021**: The sale of the Sabinar project was completed, marking a key milestone for Hive Energy as it transitions to focus on other projects within its pipeline.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific details regarding job creation or job loss associated with the Sabinar project or its sale.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2020**: Hive Energy exclusively owned the Sabinar project prior to its sale.
  
- **March 2021**: Following the sale, the Sabinar project is now owned by a joint venture (Andromeda Solutions) between **Noy Fund** and **Nofar Energy**.
