## ESP-01932-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Almaraz, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **11-01-2022**: The Arañuelo III solar plant, developed by Iberdrola, goes online. It has a production capacity of 40 MW and is linked to a 3MW/9MWh energy storage system. This facility is part of a larger photovoltaic complex with a total capacity of 143 MW. The plant is notable for being the first large-scale solar plant in Spain to incorporate a battery storage system in a distributed manner, utilizing a DC-coupling configuration.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **20-09-2022**: Iberdrola announces plans for a new 375 MW photovoltaic plant in Spain, which will utilize over 576,000 bifacial photovoltaic modules. This project is part of Iberdrola's broader strategy to expand its renewable energy portfolio, with a significant investment planned for the development of solar energy in Extremadura.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **13-01-2022**: The Arañuelo III plant is officially inaugurated, marking it as Spain's first large-scale solar PV plant integrated with an energy storage system.
- **20-09-2022**: Iberdrola obtains the Environmental Impact Statement for the new 375 MW photovoltaic plant, indicating progress towards construction.

#### 4. Labour
- **20-09-2022**: The construction of the new 375 MW photovoltaic plant is expected to create up to 800 jobs during peak construction periods. Additionally, Iberdrola's investment plan for the 2020-2025 period in Extremadura is projected to create approximately 7,400 jobs.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify any joint ventures or partnerships for the Almaraz facility; it is implied that Iberdrola owns the facility exclusively as the developer and builder of the solar plant.
