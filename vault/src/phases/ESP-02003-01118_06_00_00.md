## ESP-02003-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of Iberdrola Facility in Extremadura, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2019**: Construction began on the Núñez de Balboa PV plant, with a maximum grid-connected capacity of 391MW, later confirmed to be 500MW. The facility is equipped with over 1.4 million PV panels across a 1,000-hectare site.
- **September 2020**: The Núñez de Balboa plant commenced operations, supplying enough power for 250,000 people.
- **June 2019**: Iberdrola announced plans for the Francisco Pizarro project, a 590MW PV plant, awaiting ministerial approval.
- **August 2022**: The Francisco Pizarro project began operations, becoming the largest solar PV plant in Europe, with a capacity to power over 334,000 homes.
- **April 2023**: Iberdrola announced plans for the Hidro Cedillo project, Spain's first hybrid PV-hydroelectric plant, with a capacity of 86.4 MW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2019**: The Núñez de Balboa project required an investment of around €300 million.
- **July 2019**: The European Investment Bank and Instituto de Crédito Oficial agreed to finance the Núñez de Balboa project with a €285 million loan.
- **June 2022**: The Francisco Pizarro project was backed by a €300 million investment.
- **April 2023**: Iberdrola submitted a proposal for a 1.6GW PV module assembly plant, requiring European funding to ensure competitiveness.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2019**: Construction of the Núñez de Balboa plant commenced.
- **September 2020**: The Núñez de Balboa plant was operational.
- **August 2022**: The Francisco Pizarro project began operations.
- **April 2023**: Environmental approval was obtained for the Hidro Cedillo project.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2019**: The Francisco Pizarro project was expected to create up to 1,000 jobs.
- **July 2019**: The Núñez de Balboa project employed over 1,000 workers during construction.
- **August 2022**: The Francisco Pizarro project created 1,500 jobs, with 60% filled by local workers.
- **April 2023**: The proposed PV module assembly plant is expected to create up to 500 direct jobs.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2019 - Present**: Iberdrola exclusively owns the Núñez de Balboa and Francisco Pizarro facilities, with no joint ventures reported. The company also maintains full ownership of the proposed PV module assembly plant and the Hidro Cedillo project.

This timeline encapsulates the significant developments and milestones of Iberdrola's clean technology facilities in Extremadura, Spain, highlighting their commitment to renewable energy and local economic growth.
