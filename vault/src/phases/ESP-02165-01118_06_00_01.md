### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Cartagena, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04/2020**: Iberdrola announced the development of a 100 MW renewable energy project, which is claimed to be the world’s largest on-site photovoltaic self-consumption system. The facility will consist of 263,000 solar modules and will power SABIC’s Cartagena industrial facility, making it the region’s first large-scale chemical production facility to operate on 100% renewable electricity.
- **06/2023**: Planning consent was obtained for the construction of the 100 MW solar plant for on-site self-consumption at SABIC’s industrial complex in Cartagena. The facility will include more than 260,000 solar modules, with commissioning expected in 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04/2020**: The project is valued at €70 million ($82 million).
- **06/2023**: The investment required for the construction of the solar plant is more than €60 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04/2020**: Iberdrola signed a 25-year power purchase agreement (PPA) with SABIC for the solar project.
- **06/2023**: Final permits were granted to build the Cartagena solar plant, marking a significant milestone towards the project’s realization. Construction is set to begin, with commissioning expected in 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **04/2020**: The construction of the project is expected to generate up to 500 jobs.
- **06/2023**: The construction of the PV plant will require 450 people.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **04/2020**: The solar plant will be located on land owned by SABIC, indicating a partnership rather than exclusive ownership by Iberdrola. The facility operates under a joint venture model, with both companies collaborating on the project.
