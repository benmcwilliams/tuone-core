## ESP-02165-02424

### Evolutionary Timeline of Repsol Facility in Cartagena, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2021**: Repsol produced its first batch of hydrogen using biomethane as the feedstock at its Cartagena Industrial Complex, generating 10 tonnes of renewable hydrogen. This process utilized 500 megawatt hours of biomethane, avoiding approximately 90 tonnes of CO2 emissions.
- **September 2021**: Repsol announced plans to install a 100 MW electrolyzer at its Cartagena industrial complex as part of its Green Hydrogen Platform in the Region of Murcia, aimed at increasing renewable hydrogen production.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2021**: Repsol increased its investment targets for renewable generation and emission reductions, planning to produce 552 MW equivalent of renewable hydrogen by 2025 and 1.9 GW by 2030. This includes the installation of electrolyzers and biogas production plants at its industrial complexes.
- **July 2023**: The European Investment Bank (EIB) granted a loan of €120 million to Repsol for the construction and operation of the first advanced biofuels production plant in Spain, located in Cartagena.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2021**: Repsol announced the installation of two electrolyzers with a capacity of 100 MW in Cartagena and Petronor to supply renewable hydrogen to its complexes.
- **October 2023**: Repsol announced a freeze on hydrogen investment in Northern Spain, including projects in Cartagena, due to regulatory uncertainty affecting potential investments worth around €1.5 billion.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2021**: The Basque Hydrogen Corridor initiative, which includes Repsol, aims to create over 1300 jobs through the mobilization of €1.431 billion until 2026 across various hydrogen projects, although specific job creation numbers for the Cartagena facility were not detailed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Repsol operates the Cartagena facility as part of its broader industrial complex strategy, focusing on renewable hydrogen production. There is no indication of joint ventures or shared ownership for this specific facility; it is implied that Repsol has full ownership.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic initiatives related to the Repsol facility in Cartagena, Spain, focusing on its evolution in technology, investment, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure.
