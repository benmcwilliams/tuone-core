## ESP-02165-07147

### Evolutionary Timeline of the SABIC Facility in Cartagena, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04/2020**: SABIC signed a 25-year power purchase agreement (PPA) with Iberdrola for a 100 MW renewable energy project, which will be the world's largest on-site photovoltaic self-consumption system. This facility will enable SABIC's Cartagena industrial site to operate on 100% renewable electricity, consisting of 263,000 solar modules.
- **06/2023**: Planning consent was obtained for the construction of the 100 MW solar plant, which will be located on land owned by SABIC. The commissioning of this facility is expected in 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04/2020**: The renewable energy project is valued at €70 million ($82 million).
- **06/2023**: The investment required for the solar plant is more than €60 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04/2020**: Announcement of the PPA and project details, marking the beginning of the renewable energy initiative for the Cartagena facility.
- **06/2023**: Final permits granted for the construction of the solar plant, with construction expected to start soon and commissioning anticipated in 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **04/2020**: The construction of the solar project is expected to generate up to 500 jobs.
- **06/2023**: The construction of the PV plant will require 450 workers.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **04/2020**: The solar plant will be located on land owned by SABIC, indicating that SABIC has exclusive ownership of the facility where the solar project will be developed.
