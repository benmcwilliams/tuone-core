## ESP-02171-01205

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Naturgy Facility in La Robla, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2020**: Naturgy, in partnership with Enagás, announced plans to build Spain's largest green hydrogen plant at the site of the shuttered La Robla thermal power plant. The facility aims to produce up to 9,000 tons of renewable hydrogen annually, utilizing a 400 MW photovoltaic plant and a 60 MW electrolyzer.
- **February 2023**: The project was updated to a 280 MW renewable hydrogen production plant, with an investment of €485 million. The capacity was increased from the initial 60 MW due to demand from local industry consumers and proximity to a future hydrogen pipeline. The plant is set to be operational by 2026.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2023**: The updated project for the La Robla hydrogen production plant requires an investment of €485 million. This investment is aimed at enhancing the production capacity and supporting local industry decarbonization.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2020**: Announcement of the green hydrogen plant project alongside the 400 MW solar PV project.
- **February 2023**: Presentation of the updated plans for the 280 MW hydrogen production plant, with a target operational date of 2026.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2023**: The development of the La Robla hydrogen production plant is expected to support the decarbonization of local industries, which may lead to job creation in the region, although specific job numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2020**: The La Robla thermal power plant, where the hydrogen facility will be located, is owned exclusively by Naturgy. The partnership with Enagás for the hydrogen project indicates a collaborative effort but does not change the ownership of the facility itself.

This timeline captures the key developments and changes related to the Naturgy facility in La Robla, Spain, focusing on its technological advancements, investments, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure.
