## ESP-02173-02081

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Enel Facility in Teruel, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2019**: Enel Green Power España began constructing three wind farms in Teruel, with a total capacity of 90MW. The largest, Sierra Costera I, has a capacity of 50MW, while the Allueva and Sierra Pelarda facilities have capacities of over 25MW and 15MW, respectively. This brings the total capacity of Enel's wind farms in Teruel to 218MW.
- **April 2020**: A new 21MW wind project was initiated in Teruel and Zaragoza, featuring six 3.55MW turbines.
- **June 2020**: The Oriche wind farm, with a capacity of 14.4MW, was connected to the grid, featuring four 3.6MW turbines.
- **September 2020**: Enel announced plans for a 1,585MW solar plant in Teruel, which will be linked to 139MW of wind generation capacity and a 159.3MW energy storage system, intended to replace a 1.1GW coal-fired power plant.
- **April 2023**: Enel Green Power announced plans for a 259.2MW hybrid wind-solar plant in Teruel.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2019**: The total investment for the three wind farms in Teruel was reported at €88 million.
- **April 2020**: The 21MW wind project was valued at €20 million.
- **June 2020**: The Oriche wind farm represented an investment of €14.8 million.
- **September 2020**: The planned solar facility in Teruel is estimated to require an investment of €1.4 billion.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2019**: Construction of three wind farms commenced.
- **April 2020**: Construction began on the 21MW wind project.
- **June 2020**: The Oriche wind farm was connected to the grid.
- **September 2020**: Announcement of a new solar project in Teruel.
- **April 2023**: Plans for a hybrid wind-solar project were announced.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2020**: Enel Green Power utilized local labor for the construction of the 21MW wind farm, which also included local catering services and workers' accommodation.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility in Teruel is owned by Enel Green Power España, a subsidiary of the Italian energy company Enel. The ownership structure indicates that Enel has full ownership of the facility, as it operates under its renewable energy subsidiary, Enel Green Power.
