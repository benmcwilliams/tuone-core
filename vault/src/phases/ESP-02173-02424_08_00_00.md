## ESP-02173-02424

### Evolutionary Timeline of Repsol Facility in Teruel, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2019**: Construction began on the Delta wind installation, a 335MW wind farm located in the provinces of Zaragoza and Teruel, featuring 89 turbines.
- **October 2020**: Repsol completed the grid connection for the first turbines of the Delta wind complex. The facility is expected to produce 992GWh of clean energy annually, equivalent to the average consumption of 300,000 households, and will avoid the emission of one million tons of CO2 per year.
- **End of 2020**: The Delta wind farm is projected to begin full commercial operations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2019**: Repsol announced an investment of €300m for the Delta wind project.
- **2020**: Repsol is part of a larger investment strategy, with a total of €600m allocated across three major renewable projects (Delta, Kappa, and Valdesolar) in Spain, which collectively have a capacity of 2GW.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2019**: Construction of the Delta wind project commenced.
- **October 2020**: Integration of the first wind turbines into the Spanish grid was completed.
- **End of 2020**: Delta wind farm expected to start commercial operations.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2020**: The Valdesolar solar project, another of Repsol's renewable projects, is expected to employ between 300 and 500 people during its construction phase. While specific job creation numbers for the Delta project are not mentioned, it is implied that similar employment opportunities may arise.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Repsol owns the Delta wind facility exclusively, as indicated by the absence of any mention of joint ventures or partnerships in the articles.
