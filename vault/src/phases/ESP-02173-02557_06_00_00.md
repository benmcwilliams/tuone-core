## ESP-02173-02557

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Endesa Facility in Teruel, Spain

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Endesa facility in Teruel, specifically the Andorra thermal power plant, was operational until its closure. Following this closure, there are plans to develop renewable energy capacity, specifically 1,300 MW of photovoltaic energy in the regions of Compostilla and Andorra. This transition aims to replace the capacity lost due to the thermal plant's closure.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The Spanish government, through the Ministry for the Ecological Transition, is facilitating an auction to grant grid access capacity of up to 1.3 GW at the Mudejar node, which is directly related to the closure of the Andorra thermal power plant. This initiative is part of a broader effort to promote renewable energy and economic recovery in the region.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **May 2021:** The Spanish Ministry for the Ecological Transition announced the auction for grid access capacity at the Mudejar node, which is affected by the closure of the Andorra thermal power plant. This auction is part of the Just Transition Agreement aimed at supporting areas impacted by the closure of fossil fuel plants.

4. **Labour:**
   - The Just Transition Agreement emphasizes job creation and vocational training for the local population, particularly for those affected by the closure of the Andorra thermal power plant. Endesa has committed to employing workers from the closed plants in the new photovoltaic projects.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - Endesa operates the facility as a unit of the Italian power utility Enel. The ownership structure indicates that Endesa is not exclusively owning the facility but is part of a larger corporate structure under Enel.

This timeline captures the key developments related to the Endesa facility in Teruel, focusing on its transition from thermal power to renewable energy and the associated socio-economic impacts.
