## ESP-02173-06044

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Sivortex Facility in Teruel, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2024**: Sivortex unveiled the Actuel Solar project, which will combine 100 MW of photovoltaic (PV) capacity with a 200 MWh liquid CO2 storage system. This innovative technology replaces conventional batteries with a thermodynamic transformation of CO2, allowing for energy storage and generation almost 24 hours a day. The system boasts an efficiency of 74.4% and is designed to optimize production with advanced tracking technology.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2024**: The company announced plans to invest €120 million in the construction of the facility, which will occupy 274 hectares in the La Paz business park. The investment will be made through a developer, allowing local companies to potentially participate in funding the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2024**: Sivortex is finalizing plans for the Actuel Solar project to submit to the government of Aragon for necessary administrative and environmental authorizations. This process is expected to take between six to eight months. The facility is anticipated to begin operations by the end of 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2024**: While the CEO noted that the project is not primarily focused on job creation, it is expected to serve as a "tractor element" for attracting companies to the industrial park, providing them with stable energy capacity and potentially cheaper energy prices.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2024**: The facility will be developed through a partnership with a developer, indicating that Sivortex may not have exclusive ownership. However, specific details regarding joint ventures or partnerships have not been disclosed.
