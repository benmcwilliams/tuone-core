## ESP-02179-03487

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Solarcentury Facility in Cáceres, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2020**: Solarcentury, in partnership with Encavis AG, began construction on the Talayuela Solar Project, a 300MW photovoltaic (PV) facility in Cáceres, Extremadura. This project is noted to be one of the largest subsidy-free solar developments in Spain.
- **H2 2020**: The Talayuela project is expected to become fully operational, capable of powering approximately 150,000 homes and reducing CO2 emissions by over 170,000 tonnes annually. The installation will consist of around 977,000 solar panels spread over 822 hectares of land.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2020**: Solarcentury secured a €54.8 million finance facility to support the construction of the Talayuela project, which is part of a larger 500MW solar portfolio in Spain. This financing includes a €27.7 million trade loan from Natwest and €27.1 million in guarantees from HSBC, supported by the UK’s export credit agency, UK Export Finance.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2018**: Solarcentury reached financial close for the Talayuela project.
- **January 2020**: Construction officially commenced on the Talayuela Solar Project.
- **H2 2020**: The project is anticipated to be operational by this time.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2020**: During the construction phase of the Talayuela project, it is expected that up to 400 local jobs will be created, with at least 100 positions filled by residents of Talayuela, as part of a local employment agreement.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2018**: Solarcentury sold an 80% stake in the Talayuela project to Encavis AG, retaining a 20% share. This indicates that the facility is not under full ownership by Solarcentury but is a joint venture with Encavis AG.
