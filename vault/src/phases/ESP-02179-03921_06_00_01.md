### Evolutionary Timeline of the Encavis Facility in Cáceres, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2020**: Construction began on the Talayuela Solar Project, a 300MW photovoltaic (PV) facility in Cáceres, Extremadura, Spain. This project is notable for being one of the largest subsidy-free solar developments in Spain. The facility is designed to power approximately 150,000 homes and is expected to reduce CO2 emissions by over 170,000 tonnes annually. The installation will cover 822 hectares of land, with 320 hectares designated for environmental protection.
- **January 2021**: Encavis announced the completion of the 300MW Talayuela project, marking it as the largest in their portfolio, which includes over 2.3GW of solar and wind capacity globally. This project increased Encavis' total capacity in Spain to 500MW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2020**: The Talayuela Solar Project was developed with an investment of US$250.5 million. Additionally, Solarcentury secured a €54.8 million finance facility to support the construction of this project and another 200MW project in Alcalá de Guadaira.
- **January 2021**: Encavis invested an additional €500,000 (approximately US$614,550) to accelerate the development of its solar projects in Spain, including the Talayuela project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2020**: Construction of the Talayuela Solar Project commenced.
- **January 2021**: The Talayuela project was completed and connected to the grid, marking a significant milestone in Encavis' operations in Spain.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2020**: During the construction phase of the Talayuela project, up to 400 local jobs were created, with at least 100 positions filled by residents of Talayuela, as part of a local employment agreement.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2020**: Encavis holds an 80% stake in the Talayuela Solar Project, while Solarcentury retains the remaining 20%. This indicates a joint venture ownership structure for the facility.
