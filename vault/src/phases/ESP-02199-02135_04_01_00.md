## ESP-02199-02135

### Evolutionary Timeline of Acciona Energy Facility in Mallorca, ESP

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **06-01-2021**: Acciona and Enagás announced the Power to Green Hydrogen Mallorca project, which includes an electrolyser supplied by Hydrogenics (Cummins Group). The electrolyser is capable of producing over 330 tonnes of green hydrogen per year. The facility will utilize renewable energy from two photovoltaic plants located in Petra (5.85 MW) and Lloseta (8.5 MW) to power the electrolysis process. The plant is designed to produce renewable hydrogen for direct energy use and to inject into the natural gas grid.
- **24-12-2021**: The commissioning tests for the renewable hydrogen production began at the facility. The plant is set to produce at least 300 tons of renewable hydrogen per year, with part of the output to be distributed via Spain's first hydrogen pipeline.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **06-01-2021**: The project received a €10 million funding commitment from the European Union’s Fuel Cell and Hydrogen Joint Undertaking (FCH JU) to support the deployment of the infrastructure necessary for the Mallorca renewable hydrogen ecosystem.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **06-01-2021**: Announcement of the Power to Green Hydrogen Mallorca project, with plans for operational status by the end of 2021.
- **24-12-2021**: Start of commissioning tests for the hydrogen production facility, with industrial-scale production expected to begin in early 2022.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the investments or expansions at the facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is a joint venture involving Acciona Energía, Enagás, Cemex, and the public body IDAE, indicating that Acciona does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a collaborative partnership.
