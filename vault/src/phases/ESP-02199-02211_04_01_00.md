## ESP-02199-02211

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Enagás Facility in Mallorca, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **06/2021**: The Power to Green Hydrogen Mallorca project progresses with the acquisition of an electrolyser from Hydrogenics (part of Cummins Group). The electrolyser is capable of producing over 330 tonnes of green hydrogen per year. The green hydrogen will be used as a direct energy source or injected into the natural gas grid to reduce carbon emissions. The project includes the construction of an electrolysis plant and two photovoltaic plants in Petra and Lloseta, along with a green hydrogen filling station in Palma de Mallorca.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **06/2021**: The European Union’s Fuel Cell and Hydrogen Joint Undertaking (FCH JU) pledges €10 million in funding to support the deployment of infrastructure for the Mallorca renewable hydrogen ecosystem as part of the Green Hysland project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2020**: Enagás and Acciona announce the advancement of the Power to Green Hydrogen Mallorca project, having acquired the electrolyser. The project aims to become operational in 2021.

#### 4. Labour
- **No specific job creation or loss details** related to the Enagás facility in Mallorca were mentioned in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **06/2021**: The facility is a collaborative project involving Enagás, Acciona, IDAE (Instituto para la Diversificación y Ahorro de la Energía), and Cemex, indicating a joint venture rather than exclusive ownership by Enagás.
