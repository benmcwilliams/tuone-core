### Evolutionary Timeline of the Naturgy Facility at Isla Verde Wastewater Treatment Plant, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2022**: The Zeppelin project is announced, aiming to produce over 135,000 tons of green hydrogen annually from 99 million tons of waste and 50 million tons of municipal wastewater. The facility will utilize innovative hydrogen production technologies installed by Técnicas Reunidas at the Isla Verde Wastewater Treatment Plant.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2022**: The total budget for the Zeppelin project is reported to be over €7 million, with €4.23 million provided by the Spanish government as part of a tender process initiated in July 2021. This tender received applications from over 500 companies, with 228 projects selected for state subsidies totaling more than €122 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2022**: The consortium, which includes Naturgy, Repsol, and other partners, officially announces the Zeppelin project, marking the beginning of the initiative to establish a green hydrogen production facility at the Isla Verde Wastewater Treatment Plant.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2022**: While specific job creation figures are not mentioned in the articles, the establishment of the hydrogen production facility is expected to create jobs related to the construction and operation of the new technologies at the wastewater treatment plant.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2022**: The facility is part of a consortium led by Aqualia, which includes Naturgy, Repsol, and other companies. This indicates a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by Naturgy.
