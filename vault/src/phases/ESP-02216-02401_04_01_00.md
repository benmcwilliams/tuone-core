### Evolutionary Timeline of the Perseo Facility at Isla Verde Wastewater Treatment Plant, ESP

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2022**: The Zeppelin project is announced, aiming to produce over 135,000 tons of green hydrogen annually from 99 million tons of waste and 50 million tons of municipal wastewater. The facility will utilize innovative hydrogen production technologies to be installed by Técnicas Reunidas at the Isla Verde Wastewater Treatment Plant.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2022**: The total budget for the Zeppelin project is reported to be over €7 million, with €4.23 million provided by the Spanish government as part of a tender process initiated in July 2021. This funding is part of a larger initiative where 228 projects received state subsidies totaling more than €122 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2022**: The consortium, led by Aqualia and including Repsol, Naturgy, Redexis, Reganosa, Norvento, Perseo, and Técnicas Reunidas, officially announces the Zeppelin project, marking the start of the initiative to implement green hydrogen production at the facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2022**: While specific job creation figures are not mentioned, the involvement of multiple companies in the consortium suggests potential job creation related to the construction and operation of the hydrogen production facilities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2022**: The facility is part of a consortium led by Aqualia, indicating a joint venture structure. The consortium includes several major companies, suggesting that the facility is not owned exclusively by any single entity but rather operates under a collaborative framework.
