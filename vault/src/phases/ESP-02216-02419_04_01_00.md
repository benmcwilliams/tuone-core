### Evolutionary Timeline of the Redexis Facility at Isla Verde Wastewater Treatment Plant, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2022**: The Zeppelin project is announced, aiming to produce over 135,000 tons of green hydrogen annually from 99 million tons of waste and 50 million tons of municipal wastewater. The facility will utilize innovative hydrogen production technologies installed by Técnicas Reunidas at the Isla Verde Wastewater Treatment Plant.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2022**: The total budget for the Zeppelin project is reported to be over €7 million, with €4.23 million provided by the Spanish government as part of a tender process initiated in July 2021. This tender received applications from over 500 companies, with 228 projects selected for state subsidies totaling more than €122 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2022**: The consortium, led by Aqualia and including Redexis, announces the Zeppelin project, marking the beginning of the initiative to establish a green hydrogen production facility at the Isla Verde Wastewater Treatment Plant.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2022**: While specific job creation figures are not mentioned, the project is expected to contribute to local employment through the establishment of the hydrogen production facility and associated operations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2022**: The facility is part of a consortium that includes Aqualia, Repsol, Naturgy, Redexis, and Reganosa, indicating that Redexis does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a collaborative effort with multiple stakeholders involved in the project.
