## ESP-02237-02424

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Repsol Facility in Aragon, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2020**: Repsol's Delta wind complex in Aragon, Spain, begins operations. The facility has an installed capacity of **335 MW**, consisting of **89 wind turbines** across **eight wind farms** located in the Zaragoza and Teruel provinces. The facility is expected to produce **992 GWh** of clean energy annually, equivalent to the consumption of **300,000 households**, while avoiding the emission of **one million tons of CO2** per year.

- **February 2023**: Repsol announces progress on the Delta II project, which consists of **26 wind farms** with a total capacity of **860 MW** across the provinces of Huesca, Zaragoza, and Teruel. Four of these wind farms are operational, contributing **160 MW** to the total capacity.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2020**: The Delta project was built with an investment of **€300 million** (approximately **$353 million**).

- **March 2023**: Repsol acquires three wind farms and two solar plants from ABO Wind, adding **250 MW** of renewable assets to its portfolio. This acquisition is part of Repsol's strategy to reach **6,000 MW** of renewable installed capacity by **2025**.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2020**: Repsol completes the grid connection for the first turbines of the Delta wind complex and begins trial operations, with full commercial operations expected by the end of the year.

- **February 2023**: Repsol announces the commissioning of two new operational wind farms within the Delta II project, with construction completed in less than eleven months.

- **March 2023**: Repsol indicates that the construction of the remaining **18 wind farms** in the Delta II project will begin shortly, pending administrative approvals.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the Delta facility or its expansions.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2023**: The Delta project is noted to be **49% owned by Pontegadea**, a leading investment group, indicating that Repsol does not have exclusive ownership of the facility. The ownership structure for Delta II is not explicitly mentioned, but it is part of Repsol's broader renewable energy portfolio.
