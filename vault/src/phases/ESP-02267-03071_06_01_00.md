## ESP-02267-03071

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Isofoton Facility in Los Barrios, ESP

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 06-06-2011
   - **Details:** Isofoton, in collaboration with HKSilicon, announced plans to build one of the world's largest polysilicon factories in Los Barrios, Cádiz, Spain. The initial production capacity was set at 2,500 tons but was later ramped up to 10,000 tons. The facility is intended to produce polysilicon, a critical raw material for solar panel manufacturing.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 06-06-2011
   - **Details:** The initial investment for the facility is projected to be €600 million, funded by HKSilicon. The Regional Government of Andalusia's Idea agency is also involved in facilitating this investment.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 06-06-2011
   - **Details:** The project was announced, but specific dates for the start of construction and production were not provided. The announcement indicated that the project would position Isofoton as the fifth-largest polysilicon manufacturer globally.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 06-06-2011
   - **Details:** The construction and operation of the facility are expected to create over 1,000 jobs.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 06-06-2011
   - **Details:** The facility is a joint venture between Isofoton and HKSilicon, with HKSilicon contributing both funding and technology to the project.
