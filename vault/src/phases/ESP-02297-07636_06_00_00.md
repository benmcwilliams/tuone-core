### Evolutionary Timeline of the Capital Energy Facility in Almería, ESP

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2023**: Capital Energy announced the development of the Peregiles wind farm with an installed capacity of **93 megawatts (MW)** and the La Rambla photovoltaic plant with a capacity of **80 MW**. Together, these facilities are expected to produce enough clean electricity to cover the annual consumption of approximately **190,000 Andalusian homes** and avoid the emission of more than **180,000 tons of CO2**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2023**: The construction of the Peregiles wind farm and La Rambla photovoltaic plant is part of Capital Energy's broader commitment to the decarbonization of the economy and the transformation of the energy model. Specific financial investments tied to these projects were not detailed in the article.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2023**: Capital Energy signed collaboration agreements with the municipalities of Lucainena de las Torres and Sorbas, marking the initiation of the construction phase for both the Peregiles wind farm and La Rambla photovoltaic plant. The projects are framed within Capital Energy's Territories Project, aimed at promoting local socioeconomic development.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2023**: The construction of the Peregiles wind farm is expected to create approximately **420 direct jobs** during peak construction periods, with **12 permanent jobs** during operation and maintenance, along with about **10 indirect jobs**. The La Rambla photovoltaic plant will create around **180 direct jobs** during construction, **5 permanent jobs** during operation, and **4 indirect jobs**.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The article does not specify the ownership structure of the facility, but it indicates that Capital Energy is the primary developer and operator of the projects, suggesting full ownership unless otherwise stated.
