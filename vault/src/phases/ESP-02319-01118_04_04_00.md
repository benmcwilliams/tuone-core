## ESP-02319-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Bilbao, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2020**: Iberdrola announced the establishment of the Global Smartgrid Innovation Hub in Bilbao, aimed at supporting smart grid technology projects. The hub will involve over 200 professionals and is set to complete in 2021, focusing on enhancing electricity network resilience and flexibility.
- **November 2020**: Iberdrola, in partnership with Ingeteam, established a joint venture named Iberlyzer to produce electrolyzers, with an initial capacity of over 200 MW expected by 2023. This facility will support a green hydrogen project in collaboration with Fertiberia.
- **March 2023**: Iberdrola signed a contract with Haizea Wind Group for the fabrication of 50 monopiles for the East Anglia Three offshore wind farm in the UK. This contract will lead to the expansion of the Haizea Bilbao manufacturing plant to over 200,000 m².

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2020**: Iberdrola identified over 120 innovation projects worth €110 million for development at the Global Smartgrid Innovation Hub.
- **November 2020**: The joint venture Iberlyzer was initiated with an initial investment of approximately €100 million to produce electrolyzers.
- **March 2023**: The expansion of the Haizea Bilbao plant involves an investment of more than €150 million, aimed at increasing production capacity for offshore wind components.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2020**: Announcement of the Global Smartgrid Innovation Hub, with completion expected in 2021.
- **November 2020**: Formation of the Iberlyzer joint venture, with operations expected to begin in 2021.
- **March 2023**: Foundation stone laid for the expanded Haizea Bilbao manufacturing plant, marking the start of construction for the new facilities.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2020**: The Iberlyzer project is expected to create 150 direct qualified jobs.
- **March 2023**: The contract with Haizea Wind Group is projected to create 350 direct jobs for one year due to the expansion of the manufacturing plant.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2020**: Iberdrola and Ingeteam jointly own the Iberlyzer facility as a joint venture.
- **March 2023**: The Haizea Bilbao manufacturing plant operates under Haizea Wind Group, which has a contract with Iberdrola for monopile production, indicating a collaborative relationship rather than exclusive ownership.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones of the Iberdrola facility in Bilbao, Spain, highlighting its focus on clean technology and renewable energy initiatives.
