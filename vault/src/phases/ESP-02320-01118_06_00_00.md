## ESP-02320-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Huelva, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2020**: Iberdrola, in partnership with Ingeteam, established a joint venture named Iberlyzer to produce electrolyzers in Spain. The facility is expected to produce over 200 MW of electrolyzers by 2023, which will be utilized for a green hydrogen project in collaboration with Fertiberia at the Palos de la Frontera plant in Huelva. The project aims to achieve a total green hydrogen production capacity of 800 MW by 2027.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2020**: The initial investment for the Iberlyzer project is approximately €100 million. Additionally, Iberdrola and Fertiberia plan to invest €1.8 billion over the next seven years to develop the green hydrogen production capacity.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2020**: Announcement of the establishment of the Iberlyzer joint venture and the planned production of electrolyzers.
- **July 2021**: Iberdrola submitted proposals to hybridize its existing wind farms with solar plants in Huelva, including a 33.3 MW solar plant in El Almendro and a 49.5 MW solar park across multiple municipalities.
- **August 2024**: Iberdrola obtained authorization for the construction of 700 MW of photovoltaic and hybridization projects across several provinces, including Huelva. This includes the Andévalo PV Hybrid BESS project, which will hybridize with the existing Andévalo PV solar park.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2020**: The Iberlyzer project is expected to create 150 direct qualified jobs as a result of the investment and operations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2020**: The facility is part of a joint venture (Iberlyzer) between Iberdrola and Ingeteam, indicating that Iberdrola does not own the facility exclusively but shares ownership with Ingeteam.
