## ESP-02391-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Asturias, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2021**: Iberdrola begins the development of an over 800MW green hydrogen scheme in Puertollano, which includes a 100MW solar farm, a 20MWh lithium-ion battery system, and a 20MW electrolytic hydrogen production system. The facility in Asturias fabricates storage tanks for this project, each with a capacity of 133 cubic meters (2,700 kg of H2).
- **April 2023**: Iberdrola announces plans for a 500MW TOPCon solar module assembly plant in Asturias, in collaboration with Exiom. This facility aims to produce solar panels to meet local demand.
- **October 2023**: The first machinery shipment for the 500MW TOPCon module assembly plant is received, marking the start of production preparations. Refurbishment of the facility is underway to adapt it for module production.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: Iberdrola seeks European funding for the new PV module factory, which is part of a broader initiative to enhance competitiveness in solar manufacturing. The project is tied to the European Commission's Innovation Fund.
- **October 2023**: The facility is part of Spain's broader €750 million renewables and storage manufacturing scheme aimed at incentivizing domestic production of solar panels and related technologies.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2021**: Announcement of the green hydrogen project in Puertollano, with tanks fabricated in Asturias.
- **April 2023**: Announcement of the 500MW TOPCon solar module factory in Asturias.
- **October 2023**: Receipt of the first machinery shipment for the module assembly plant.
- **February 2024**: The plant is expected to be operational in Spring 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2023**: The announcement of the 500MW module factory indicates the creation of 500 direct jobs, contributing to local economic development.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2023**: The facility is a joint venture between Iberdrola and Exiom, focusing on the production of solar PV modules. There is no indication of exclusive ownership by Iberdrola.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Iberdrola facility in Asturias, Spain, highlighting its technological advancements, investments, project statuses, labor impacts, and ownership structure.
