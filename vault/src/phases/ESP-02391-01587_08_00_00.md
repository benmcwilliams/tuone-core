### EDP Facility in Asturias, Spain - Evolutionary Timeline

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2021**: EDP announced plans to develop a floating offshore wind farm off the coast of Asturias, targeting a capacity of approximately 50 megawatts (MW). This project is described as a “semi-industrial scale” initiative and aims to create supply chains and foster industrial development in the region. The facility is part of EDP's broader strategy to transition to renewable energy, with a commitment to cease coal production by 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2021**: EDP plans to invest around 2,000 million euros from 2021 to 2025, with a significant portion allocated to renewable energy growth. Specifically, 700 million euros are earmarked for the Networks business, while the remainder will support renewable initiatives, including the floating offshore wind project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2021**: EDP's CEO announced the floating offshore wind project as part of their strategic plan, indicating that it is in the study phase. The project is positioned as a key component of EDP's transition to green energy, with a target to be fully green by 2030.
