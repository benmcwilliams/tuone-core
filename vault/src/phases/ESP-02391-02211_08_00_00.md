### Evolutionary Timeline of the Enagás Facility in Asturias, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **08/2021**: Enagás, in collaboration with Naturgy, is studying the production of green hydrogen from a combination of a 250 MW floating offshore wind farm and a 100 MW onshore wind farm in Asturias. The project aims to develop an electrolysis and renewable energy plant, with initial deployment on a demo scale involving 50 MW offshore and 100 MW onshore wind power, along with a 5 MW offshore electrolyzer and a 100 MW onshore electrolyzer. The project could later expand to 250 MW offshore wind power and an additional 100 MW onshore electrolyzer.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **08/2021**: The project is part of the Green Crane initiative and has been submitted as a candidate for the Important Project of Common European Interest (IPCEI). Specific investment amounts are not detailed, but the project is expected to significantly contribute to the energy transition and decarbonization efforts in the region.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **08/2021**: The project is in the study phase, with plans for construction and operation that could create over 1,500 jobs. The initiative is aimed at establishing large-scale European green hydrogen production and transport chains.

#### 4. Labour
- **08/2021**: The project is projected to create more than 1,500 jobs during its construction and operational phases, contributing to local employment and economic development.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **08/2021**: The facility is a collaborative effort between Enagás and Naturgy, indicating a joint venture structure for this specific project in Asturias.
