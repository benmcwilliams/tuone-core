## ESP-02391-04806

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Danone Facility in Asturias, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: Danone's facilities in Spain, including the one in Asturias, will receive 100% renewable electricity from the Francisco Pizarro solar photovoltaic plant, which has a capacity of 590 MW. This plant is set to be operational in 2022.
- **Renewable Energy Supply**: The facility will benefit from an annual output of 73 GWh of renewable electricity, supplemented by an additional contract with Iberdrola for a total of 104 GWh/year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Amount**: The Francisco Pizarro project represents a €300 million investment.
- **Employment Contribution**: The project is expected to create employment for more than 1,200 workers during its development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Power Purchase Agreement (PPA)**: On January 21, 2021, Danone and Iberdrola signed a 10-year PPA to supply renewable electricity starting from April 2022.
- **Operational Timeline**: The Francisco Pizarro solar plant is scheduled to be operational in 2022.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The €300 million investment in the solar project is projected to contribute to the employment of over 1,200 workers during key stages of its delivery.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture**: The facility does not operate as a joint venture; however, Danone collaborates with Iberdrola for the renewable energy supply through the PPA, indicating a partnership for sustainability rather than shared ownership of the facility itself.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic initiatives surrounding the Danone facility in Asturias, emphasizing its commitment to renewable energy and sustainability.
