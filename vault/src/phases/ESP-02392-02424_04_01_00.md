### Evolutionary Timeline of Repsol Facility in Cartagena Industrial Complex, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2021**: Repsol produced its first batch of renewable hydrogen at the Cartagena Industrial Complex, utilizing biomethane as feedstock. The facility produced 10 tonnes of renewable hydrogen using 500 megawatt hours of biomethane. This hydrogen is intended for manufacturing fuels such as gasoline, diesel, and kerosene for aviation.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2021**: Repsol announced an increase in its investments for the 2021-2025 period to €19.3 billion, allocating an additional €1 billion specifically for renewable electricity generation capacity and production of renewable hydrogen, among other low-carbon initiatives. The company aims to produce 552 MW of renewable hydrogen by 2025 and 1.9 GW by 2030.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2021**: The production of renewable hydrogen at the Cartagena Industrial Complex marks a significant milestone in Repsol's transition towards decarbonized products and energy efficiency. This production is part of Repsol's broader strategy to transform its industrial complexes into multi-energy hubs.
