## ESP-02448-02164

### Evolutionary Timeline of BP's Facility in Castellón, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2021**: BP, in partnership with Iberdrola and Enagás, announced a feasibility study for a large-scale green hydrogen project at BP's refinery in Castellón. The project will feature a 20 MW electrolyzer powered by a 40 MW solar PV system, with potential future expansion to 115 MW.
- **February 2023**: BP launched plans for a 2 GW green hydrogen cluster at the Castellón refinery, named HyVal. The first phase will install at least 200 MW of capacity by 2027, producing up to 31,200 tonnes of green hydrogen annually. The full 2 GW capacity is expected by 2030.
- **September 2024**: A final investment decision was reached for a 25 MW green hydrogen project at the Castellón refinery, set to start in late 2026. This project will feature a 25 MW electrolyzer powered by renewable energy.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2021**: The green hydrogen project announced requires a US$90 million investment for construction.
- **February 2023**: BP plans to invest up to €2 billion ($2.1 billion) in the Castellón facility by 2030, focusing on green hydrogen, biofuels, and renewable energy.
- **September 2024**: The 25 MW project will benefit from €15 million ($16.6 million) in funding from the Spanish government and NextGenerationEU.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: Announcement of the green hydrogen project and feasibility study.
- **February 2023**: Launch of the HyVal initiative for a 2 GW green hydrogen cluster.
- **September 2024**: Final investment decision for the 25 MW green hydrogen project, with construction set to begin in late 2026.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2023**: The HyVal initiative is expected to support thousands of industrial jobs across the Valencia region.
- **September 2024**: The 25 MW project is anticipated to create 1,000 new green jobs.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2024**: The 25 MW green hydrogen project will be developed under a joint venture named Castellón Green Hydrogen S.L., equally owned by BP and Iberdrola.
