### Evolutionary Timeline of the Repsol Facility in Móstoles, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2021**: Repsol, in collaboration with Enagás, has developed a pioneering technology for renewable hydrogen production through photoelectrocatalysis. A pilot plant of 1 m² has been installed at the Repsol Technology Lab in Móstoles. The technology aims to simplify hydrogen production by integrating the process into a single step, improving solar-to-hydrogen efficiency.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2021**: Repsol and Enagás have invested €8 million in the development of the photoelectrocatalysis project. This investment is part of a broader initiative to reach commercial maturity for the technology by 2030.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2021**: The project has a roadmap that includes a demonstrator plant to be constructed in Puertollano, scheduled to start operations in 2024, with a production capacity of 100 kg of renewable hydrogen per day. A final industrial phase plant is planned for 2028, with a capacity of up to 10 tons of hydrogen per day.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2021**: The project is noted for being led by women, highlighting a commitment to female researchers in the field of hydrogen technology. However, specific job creation or loss figures related to this project have not been mentioned in the article.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2021**: The project is a joint venture between Repsol and Enagás, indicating that both companies share ownership and proprietary rights to the technology developed, which has resulted in 65 patent applications across more than 30 countries. 

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic initiatives related to the Repsol facility in Móstoles, focusing on the advancements in hydrogen production technology and the collaborative efforts with Enagás.
