## ESP-02470-00156

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Cummins Facility in Castilla-La Mancha, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2021**: Cummins announced plans to build a €50 million PEM electrolyzer plant in Castilla-La Mancha, with an initial production capacity of approximately 500 MW/year, scalable to over 1 GW/year. The facility will cover 22,000 square meters and is expected to house system assembly and testing operations. The plant is anticipated to come online in 2023.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2021**: The total investment for the facility is approximately €50 million. This investment is supported by both the Castilla-La Mancha and national Spanish governments, aligning with the objectives of the Spanish and European hydrogen strategies.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2021**: Cummins and Iberdrola signed an agreement to promote a green hydrogen value chain in Spain, with the facility expected to open in 2023. The project is positioned as one of the world’s largest hydrogen plants.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2021**: The construction and operation of the facility are expected to create approximately 350 new jobs as production ramps up.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2021**: The facility is a result of a partnership between Cummins and Iberdrola, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership by Cummins. The partnership aims to accelerate the growth of the green hydrogen market in Spain.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments related to the Cummins facility in Castilla-La Mancha, focusing on its technological capabilities, investment details, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure.
