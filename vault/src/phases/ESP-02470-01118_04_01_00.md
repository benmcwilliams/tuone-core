## ESP-02470-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Castilla-La Mancha, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05/2019**: Iberdrola submitted a proposal for four utility-scale solar PV facilities totaling 250MW in Castilla-La Mancha, including a unique 100MW project (Puertollano II) featuring bifacial panels and a 5MW/20MWh battery system.
- **05/2021**: Iberdrola and Cummins announced plans for a €50 million electrolyzer plant in Castilla-La Mancha, designed for green hydrogen production with an initial capacity of 500MW/year, scalable to over 1GW/year. The facility is expected to open in 2023.
- **01/2023**: Iberdrola reported that 137MW of its renewable energy portfolio is operational in Castilla-La Mancha, contributing to its overall renewable capacity.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05/2021**: The electrolyzer plant, costing approximately €50 million, is part of Iberdrola's strategy to lead in green hydrogen production in Spain.
- **04/2023**: Iberdrola launched a historic investment plan of €47 billion for 2023-2025, with €6 billion earmarked for renewable energy and smart grid development in Spain.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05/2019**: Iberdrola awaits project approval for the 250MW solar PV portfolio in Castilla-La Mancha.
- **08/2021**: Iberdrola and Mapfre formed a joint venture to develop 230MW of clean energy projects, including 130MW of solar PV in Castilla-La Mancha, expected to be commissioned in Q3 2022.
- **05/2021**: Announcement of the green hydrogen electrolyzer plant in Castilla-La Mancha, anticipated to open in 2023.
- **01/2023**: Iberdrola confirmed operational status of 137MW of renewable capacity in Castilla-La Mancha.

#### 4. Labour
- **08/2021**: The joint venture with Mapfre is expected to create jobs in the renewable energy sector as part of Iberdrola's commitment to drive re-industrialization and job creation in Spain.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **08/2021**: In the joint venture with Mapfre, Iberdrola holds a 20% stake, while Mapfre is the majority shareholder with 80%. 
- **04/2023**: Iberdrola became the majority shareholder (51%) in the co-investment vehicle Energías Renovables Ibermap, which includes new solar photovoltaic assets in Castilla-La Mancha.
