## ESP-02546-07946

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Navantia Facility in Avilés, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: The facility is involved in the production of offshore wind components, specifically XXL monopiles, which are over 100 meters in length and can weigh up to 2,500 tons.
- **Expansions/Updates**: A new XXL monopile factory is set to be established at the Navantia shipyard in Fene (A Coruña) as part of a framework agreement with Iberdrola. This factory will enhance the capabilities of the shipyard by introducing a new product line in the fixed marine foundations sector.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investments/Subsidies**: The agreement with Iberdrola is valued at up to 400 million euros for future offshore wind farms, with the investment planned for the adaptation of facilities to manufacture monopiles. The construction of the new factory is expected to be completed within 13 months from the agreement date.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Announcement Date**: The framework agreement was signed on June 7, 2021.
- **Construction Start**: The construction of the new XXL monopile factory is expected to commence following the agreement, with completion anticipated within 13 months.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The initiative is expected to generate several thousand jobs in local contractors, in addition to the more than 2,000 professionals already employed in related offshore wind projects executed by the Navantia-Windar consortium.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The facility is part of a joint venture between Navantia and Windar, which collaborates on various offshore wind projects, including contracts with Iberdrola. The specific ownership structure of the Avilés facility is not detailed, but it operates under the broader framework of the Navantia-Windar partnership.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic initiatives related to the Navantia facility in Avilés, focusing on its role in the offshore wind sector and the collaborative efforts with Iberdrola.

### Technology and Operations
- "The consortium formed by Windar and Navantia will start up an XXL monopile factory on land at the Navantia shipyard in Fene (A Coruña)."
- "The new XXL monopile factory, which will manufacture foundations over 100 m long and weighing up to 2,500 t, will be available within 13 months."

### Investment and Development
- "The investment planned by Windar-Navantia includes the adaptation of a building for curving thick sheet metal with a diameter of up to 16 m, new paint booths and storage areas for finished products, as well as the latest technology equipment necessary for manufacturing."
- "Iberdrola could place orders with these manufacturers worth up to 400 million euros between 2023 and 2025 for future offshore wind farms."

### Project Status and Milestones
- "The construction of this factory will improve the capabilities of the Navantia shipyard in Fene, providing it with a new product within the fixed marine foundations sector."
- "A year ago, Iberdrola awarded the Navantia-Windar joint venture a contract for the construction of 62 jackets to support the offshore wind turbines and the piles required for their installation at the Saint Brieuc wind farm in France."

### Labour
- "More than 2,000 professionals are directly employed in all this work."
- "Several thousand jobs will be generated in local contractors, both in Spain and in France."

### Ownership Structure
