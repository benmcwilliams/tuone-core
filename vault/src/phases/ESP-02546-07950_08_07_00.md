### Evolutionary Timeline of the Navantia-Windar Facility in Avilés, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2019**: Navantia-Windar begins construction of floating foundations for the Kincardine wind farm in Scotland. The production starts with plate cutting and forming at Windar's premises in Avilés, continuing at the Navantia Fene yard for assembly until April 2020.
- **June 2020**: Navantia-Windar secures a contract to manufacture and assemble 62 jacket foundations and pin piles for the Saint-Brieuc wind farm in France. Windar will produce the pin piles at the Avilés facility.
- **July 2022**: The Saint-Brieuc project progresses with the installation of offshore wind platforms built by the Navantia-Windar consortium, contributing to the overall construction of the wind farm.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2020**: The contract for the Saint-Brieuc wind farm is valued at €350 million, marking it as the largest offshore wind contract for Navantia-Windar to date. This contract is part of a broader investment strategy by Iberdrola, which plans to invest €2.4 billion in the Saint-Brieuc project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2019**: Construction of floating foundations for the Kincardine wind farm begins at Windar's premises in Avilés.
- **April 2020**: Completion of the assembly and delivery of floating foundations for the Kincardine wind farm.
- **2021**: Construction of the Saint-Brieuc wind farm is scheduled to begin.
- **2023**: The commissioning of the Saint-Brieuc wind farm is planned, which will have a capacity of 496 MW.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2020**: The contract for the Saint-Brieuc wind farm is expected to create around 1,000 direct jobs in Spain and 1,100 jobs in France. The total job creation from this project is projected to be at least 2,000 direct jobs, with additional jobs created by local contractors.
- **July 2022**: The Saint-Brieuc project has created 1,250 jobs in Avilés and Fene to date, with expectations to generate at least 2,750 jobs in total.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2019**: Navantia-Windar is identified as a joint venture partnership, indicating that the facility is not owned exclusively by one company but rather by both Navantia and Windar.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones of the Navantia-Windar facility in Avilés, Spain, focusing on its operations, investments, project statuses, labor impacts, and ownership structure.
