### Evolutionary Timeline of the Solarcentury Facility in Cerro Verde, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **06/2019**: Solarcentury is developing the Talayuela Solar project in Cerro Verde, which has a production capacity of 300 MW. The project is expected to generate approximately 588.26 GWh of electricity annually, which will be sold through private power supply agreements.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2019**: The estimated development cost for the Talayuela Solar project is between €225-280 million. The project has secured a 10-year power purchase agreement (PPA) to sell energy on the free market, ensuring stable income for the first decade of operation.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **06/2019**: Canadian Solar is contracted to supply 500 MW of solar panels for Solarcentury's projects, including the Talayuela Solar project.
- **09/2019**: The Talayuela project has been designated a strategic project by the Extremadura government, which has helped accelerate its development. Construction was expected to start in the fall of 2019, with grid connection planned for the second half of 2020.

#### 4. Labour
- **09/2019**: The project is anticipated to create jobs, although specific numbers regarding job creation or loss have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **09/2019**: Solarcentury retains a 20% stake in the Talayuela project, while 80% of the project was sold to Encavis AG, indicating a joint venture ownership structure.
