### Evolutionary Timeline of the Encavis Facility in Cerro Verde, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09-10-2018**: Encavis AG acquires an 80% stake in the 300 MW Talayuela solar project located in Cerro Verde, Extremadura. The facility is expected to generate approximately 588.26 GWh of electricity annually, with revenues projected at around €25 million per year from the first year of operation. The project includes the construction of a 30/400 kV substation and a 400 kV transmission line for electricity evacuation.
- **17-09-2019**: A 10-year power purchase agreement (PPA) is signed for the Talayuela project, ensuring stable income for approximately 75% of the production volume. The project is being developed without subsidies and is set to sell energy on the free market.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09-10-2018**: The total financing required for the Talayuela project is €225 million, with Encavis covering 80% of the investment and Solarcentury contributing the remaining 20%. The project is designated as a strategic project by the regional government of Extremadura, which aids in expediting its development.
- **17-09-2019**: The estimated cost of the Talayuela project is reported to be between €225 million and €280 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09-10-2018**: Encavis announces the acquisition of the 80% stake and the planned construction of the Talayuela solar plant, with completion and grid connection expected in the first quarter of 2020.
- **17-09-2019**: Construction of the Talayuela solar plant is expected to start in the fall of 2019, with grid connection planned for the second half of 2020.

#### 4. Labour

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **09-10-2018**: Encavis AG owns 80% of the Talayuela solar project, while Solarcentury retains a 20% stake, indicating a joint venture ownership structure.
