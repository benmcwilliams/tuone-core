### Evolutionary Timeline of the SolarCentury Facility in Cerro Verde, Spain

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility, known as the Talayuela solar project, is a 300 MW photovoltaic (PV) plant located in Cerro Verde, Extremadura, Spain. 
   - Upon completion, it is expected to generate approximately 588.26 GWh of electricity annually, which will be sold to the market through private Power Purchase Agreements (PPAs).

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The total financing for the project amounts to €225 million. 
   - SolarCentury, the project developer, is responsible for 20% of the funding, while Encavis AG has acquired an 80% stake in the project.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **September 2018:** Encavis AG acquires an 80% stake in the Talayuela solar project.
   - **First Quarter 2020:** The completion and grid connection of the Talayuela project are planned for this timeframe.
   - The project received final approval from the Ministry of Ecological Transition of Spain in August 2018, and it was designated as a strategic project by the regional government of Extremadura, which has expedited its development.

4. **Labour:**
   - The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the Talayuela solar project.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is not exclusively owned by SolarCentury; it is a joint venture where Encavis AG holds an 80% stake, and SolarCentury retains a 20% stake in the project.
