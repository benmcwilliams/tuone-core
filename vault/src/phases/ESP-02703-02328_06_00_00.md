### Evolutionary Timeline of the Jinko Solar Facility in Talaván, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2019**: JinkoSolar announced the supply of its Cheetah solar panels for the Talasol PV project located in Talaván, Extremadura. The project is noted to be one of the largest utility-scale solar projects in Europe, with a production capacity of 300 MW. The facility is designed to sell power to the spot market.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2019**: The total investment for the Talasol project is reported to be between €240 million and €260 million. Ellomay Capital, the owner of the project, secured €177 million for construction and an additional €18.4 million from Bankinter. The project also involved structuring non-recourse senior debt financing with Deutsche Bank, with potential support from the European Investment Bank.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2019**: Construction work on the Talasol project began.
- **End of 2020**: Completion of the project was scheduled.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific details regarding job creation or job loss associated with the Talasol project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Talasol project is owned by Ellomay Capital, which acquired it through a €10 million share-purchase agreement in May 2017. There is no indication of joint ownership; thus, it can be inferred that Ellomay Capital has full ownership of the facility.
