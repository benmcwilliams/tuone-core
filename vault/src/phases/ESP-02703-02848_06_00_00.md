### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ellomay Facility in Talaván, ESP

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2017**: Ellomay Capital enters into an agreement to acquire Talasol Solar, which is developing a 300 MW photovoltaic facility in Talaván, Cáceres. The facility is expected to produce approximately 580 gigawatt-hours of electricity annually.
- **June 2017**: The Spanish Ministry of Energy approves the construction of the Talasol Solar PV project, including a 400/30 kV substation and a 23.7 km high-voltage line for grid connection.
- **July 2017**: Final authorization for the 300 MW solar power plant is granted by Spain’s General Directorate of Energy Policy and Mines.
- **September 2019**: JinkoSolar announces it will supply Cheetah modules for the Talasol project, with construction work beginning in mid-June 2019 and completion scheduled for the end of 2020.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2017**: Ellomay Capital agrees to acquire Talasol Solar for approximately €10 million.
- **June 2017**: The total project cost is estimated at €331.8 million, with expected capital expenditure (capex) of €225-255 million.
- **September 2019**: Ellomay announces it has secured €177 million for construction and an additional €18.4 million from Bankinter for the project. The total investment for the project is reported to be between €240-€260 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2017**: Agreement to acquire Talasol Solar is made.
- **June 2017**: Project receives approval from the Ministry of Energy.
- **July 2017**: Final authorization for the project is granted.
- **Mid-June 2019**: Construction work begins on the facility.
- **End of 2020**: Completion of the project is scheduled.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2019**: The project is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2017**: Ellomay Capital acquires Talasol Solar through a share purchase agreement, indicating full ownership of the facility.
