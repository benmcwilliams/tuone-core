### Evolutionary Timeline of the Talasol Solar S.L. Facility in Talaván, ESP

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2018**: Talasol Solar S.L. is set to develop a 300 MW solar PV farm in Talaván, Extremadura, Spain. This facility is notable for being one of Spain's largest unsubsidized solar projects. The project includes ancillary facilities for power injection into the grid, such as a 400 kV step-up substation and a high voltage interconnection line.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2018**: The total capital expenditure (CAPEX) for the Talasol Project is estimated at approximately €200 million, which includes development costs of around €20 million and interest of about €7 million. The project is financed through an agreement with Deutsche Bank for non-recourse senior debt financing, with potential additional financing from the European Investment Bank (EIB).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2018**: An EPC contract worth €192.5 million is signed between Metka (a subsidiary of Mytilineos) and Talasol Solar S.L. for the construction of the facility. Construction is expected to start in the fourth quarter of 2018, with a project duration of 16 months.

#### 4. Labour
- **No specific job creation or job loss details** are mentioned in the articles regarding the Talasol facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2018**: Talasol Solar S.L. is owned by Ellomay Capital Ltd, indicating that the facility is not under exclusive ownership by a single entity but is owned by a parent company.
