### Evolutionary Timeline of the Metka Facility in Talaván, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2018**: Metka signed an EPC contract for the development of the 300 MW Talasol solar PV farm in Talaván, Extremadura. The project is notable for being one of Spain's largest unsubsidized solar PV projects. The scope includes ancillary facilities for power injection into the grid, a 400 kV step-up substation, and a high voltage interconnection line. Metka will also provide two years of operation and maintenance services.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2018**: The total capital expenditure (CAPEX) for the Talasol Project is expected to be approximately €200 million, which includes development costs of around €20 million and interest of about €7 million. Financing is primarily handled by the project owner, Talasol Solar S.L., which is owned by Ellomay Capital Ltd. There are indications that Deutsche Bank is involved in structuring non-recourse senior debt financing, with potential support from the European Investment Bank (EIB).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2018**: Metka announced the signing of the EPC contract and expressed hopes for construction to start in the fourth quarter of 2018, with a project duration of 16 months.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the Metka facility in Talaván.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is not exclusively owned by Metka. It is a project developed by Talasol Solar S.L., which is owned by Ellomay Capital Ltd. Metka is responsible for the engineering, procurement, and construction aspects of the project.
