## ESP-02705-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Cantabria, Spain

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** August 2021
   - **Details:** Iberdrola has commissioned the Herrera II wind complex, which consists of three farms (La Huesca, Valdesantos, and Orbanejoa) with a total installed capacity of 63 MW, comprising 14 wind turbines. The facility is designed to generate enough electricity to supply approximately 60,000 households annually and prevent the release of 50,000 tons of CO2 per year. The wind turbines installed are the SG 4.5-145, which are the largest and most powerful onshore wind turbines in Spain, with a unit power of 4.5 MW.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** August 2021
   - **Details:** Iberdrola invested €70 million in the development of the Herrera II wind complex. The company plans to invest over €2 billion in renewable energy projects in the region by 2025, which is expected to stimulate the local industrial sector.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** August 2021
   - **Details:** The Herrera II wind complex has been officially commissioned. This project is part of Iberdrola's broader strategy to enhance its renewable energy capacity in Spain, where it aims to increase its installed capacity from 17,000 MW to 25,000 MW by 2025.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** August 2021
   - **Details:** The development of the Herrera II wind complex created approximately 800 jobs during the construction phase. Additionally, the investment in renewable projects in the region is projected to create jobs for around 18,000 people.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** August 2021
   - **Details:** Iberdrola owns the Herrera II wind complex exclusively, as there is no mention of joint ventures or partnerships in the provided information.
