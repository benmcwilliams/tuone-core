## ESP-02730-02889

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Exiom Facility in Langreo, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2023**: Iberdrola and Exiom announced the construction of a solar module factory in Langreo, Spain, which will produce tunnel oxide passivated contact (TOPCon) modules. The facility is expected to have an initial annual production capacity of **500 MW**.
- **April 2023**: The facility is described as one of the first large photovoltaic construction plants in Spain and Europe, with operations expected to commence in **2023**.
- **February 2024**: The inauguration of the factory is planned for **spring 2024**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2023**: The initial investment for the facility is reported to be **€20 million** (approximately **$21.3 million**).
- **February 2024**: The project received a subsidy of **€1.2 million** from the Institute for the Just Transition (ITJ) to support job-generating business projects in just transition zones.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2023**: Announcement of the partnership between Iberdrola and Exiom to build the facility.
- **April 2023**: Confirmation that the factory will be operational in **2023**.
- **February 2024**: Announcement of the upcoming inauguration of the factory in **spring 2024**.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2023**: The facility is expected to create **115 direct jobs**, contributing to the economic revival of the mining regions in Asturias.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2023**: Exiom is identified as a manufacturer with **100% Spanish capital**, indicating that it is fully owned by Spanish interests. The partnership with Iberdrola suggests a collaborative effort but does not indicate joint ownership of the facility.
