### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mondragon Facility in Mondragon, ESP

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2018**: Mondragon Assembly begins production of Meyer Burger's SmartWire Connection Technology (SWCT) equipment. Production is set to start no later than the end of the fourth quarter of 2018. This marks the facility's entry into manufacturing advanced solar PV equipment, specifically focusing on the SWCT systems.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2018**: The collaboration with Meyer Burger allows Mondragon to leverage its existing production capabilities and supply chain, although specific investment amounts related to this partnership are not disclosed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2018**: Meyer Burger announces the outsourcing of SWCT production to Mondragon Assembly, coinciding with the closure of its own production site in Thun by the end of 2018. This agreement signifies a strategic shift for both companies, with Mondragon set to take over the manufacturing responsibilities.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2018**: The closure of Meyer Burger's production site in Thun results in the loss of approximately 100 jobs. However, the articles do not specify the number of jobs created at the Mondragon facility as a result of this outsourcing agreement.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2018**: Mondragon Assembly operates under a cooperation agreement with Meyer Burger, indicating a partnership rather than full ownership. While Mondragon will manufacture the SWCT systems, the patents and sales rights remain with Meyer Burger, suggesting a joint venture-like relationship rather than exclusive ownership.

This timeline captures the key developments and changes at the Mondragon facility, highlighting its role in the solar PV equipment manufacturing landscape through its partnership with Meyer Burger.
