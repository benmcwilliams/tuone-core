### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Badajoz, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2019**: Iberdrola commenced construction of the Núñez de Balboa project, a 391MW photovoltaic (PV) plant located in Usagre, Badajoz province. This facility is equipped with over 1.4 million PV panels across a 1,000-hectare site. It is designed to supply enough power to meet the needs of approximately 250,000 people. The facility is billed as Europe’s largest PV project to date and is a significant part of Iberdrola's strategy to add 2GW in solar and wind capacity in the Extremadura region by 2022.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2019**: The construction of the Núñez de Balboa facility requires an investment of around €300 million. This investment is part of Iberdrola's broader plan to commission 10GW of Spanish renewables by 2030.
- **February 2020**: Iberdrola announced a new 328MW PV installation in Extremadura, the Ceclavín project, which will require a total investment of €250 million. This project is part of Iberdrola's strategy to expand its renewable energy capacity in the region.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2019**: Construction of the Núñez de Balboa facility began.
- **September 2020**: The Núñez de Balboa facility is slated to enter operations.
- **February 2020**: The Ceclavín project is set to go live within the same year.
