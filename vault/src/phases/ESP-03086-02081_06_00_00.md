### Evolutionary Timeline of the Enel Facility in Badajoz, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **29-11-2018**: Enel Green Power España (EGPE) begins construction of three solar photovoltaic (PV) plants: Navalvillar, Valdecaballero, and Castilblanco, with a combined capacity of approximately 127MW (42.3MW each). The facilities are expected to generate around 250 GWh annually and are part of a larger portfolio of renewable projects in Spain.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **29-11-2018**: The total investment for the three solar projects is approximately €100 million. These projects are part of a larger initiative where EGPE was awarded a total of 879 MW of renewable energy capacity (solar and wind) in government auctions, representing an overall investment of more than €800 million by 2020.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **29-11-2018**: Announcement of the start of construction for the three solar projects, which are slated to come online by the end of 2019. These projects are part of the seven solar projects awarded to EGPE in the Spanish government's third renewable energy auction held in July 2017.

#### 4. Labour
- **29-11-2018**: The construction phase will involve recruiting labor from the local population, promoting local job creation during the building of the solar facilities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **29-11-2018**: Enel Green Power España, a division of the Spanish power firm Endesa, is responsible for the construction and operation of the facility, indicating that Enel has full ownership of the solar projects in Badajoz.
