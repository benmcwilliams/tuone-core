### Evolutionary Timeline of the Plenitude Facility in Badajoz, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09-04-2024**: Plenitude announced the commencement of construction for the Renopool solar park, a 330MW solar project in Badajoz, which will be the largest solar project ever built by the company globally. The facility will utilize bifacial modules, solar tracking structures, and central inverters. It is expected to generate 660 GWh of energy annually, sufficient to power approximately 200,000 homes. The project will consist of seven photovoltaic plants and an electrical substation near Solana de los Barros.
- **11-04-2024**: Further details confirmed that the construction will be executed under a turnkey formula by top-level companies, including OHLA, Sacyr, and Sarpel. The project is set to be completed in 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09-04-2024**: The announcement of the Renopool solar park indicates significant investment in the renewable energy sector, although specific financial figures were not disclosed. The project is part of Plenitude's broader commitment to the Spanish renewable energy market, where they operate around 400MW of existing capacity and have over 2000MW under development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09-04-2024**: Construction officially began for the Renopool solar park, with completion expected in 2025. The project was inaugurated in the presence of local government officials, indicating community engagement and support.
- **11-04-2024**: The project was confirmed to be on track for completion in 2025, with ongoing construction activities.

#### 4. Labour
- **09-04-2024**: Plenitude stated that the construction of the Renopool solar park will generate new local job opportunities, although specific numbers of jobs created were not mentioned.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **09-04-2024**: The facility is owned by Plenitude, a subsidiary of Eni, indicating full ownership of the Renopool solar park by the company.
