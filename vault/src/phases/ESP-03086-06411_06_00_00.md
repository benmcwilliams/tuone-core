### Evolutionary Timeline of the ib vogt Facility in Badajoz, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10/2020**: Ib vogt announced the commencement of construction of a 180MWp solar farm named Bienvenida near Badajoz, Spain. The facility is designed to produce solar energy and is part of a series of major grid-parity projects in Southern Europe. Ib vogt will perform all operational contracts for the project, including delivery and ongoing operation.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10/2020**: The Bienvenida project was sold to Talanx Group, a European insurance giant. Ib vogt established a long-term power purchase agreement with Shell Energy Europe, ensuring that all power generated from the plant will be acquired by Shell.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **10/2020**: Announcement of the sale and construction start of the Bienvenida solar farm. This project was previously acquired by ib vogt in 2017 and advanced to shovel-ready status.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **10/2020**: The Bienvenida project is owned by Talanx Group, while ib vogt retains operational control through a long-term power purchase agreement with Shell Energy Europe. This indicates that ib vogt does not exclusively own the facility but is involved in its operation.

### Summary
The ib vogt facility in Badajoz, Spain, represents a significant solar energy project with a capacity of 180MWp. The project was sold to Talanx Group while ib vogt continues to manage its operations. The facility is part of a broader strategy to develop solar projects in Southern Europe, supported by a long-term agreement with Shell Energy Europe for power purchase.
