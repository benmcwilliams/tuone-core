## ESP-03406-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Salamanca, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2022**: Iberdrola announced plans for a new 375 MW photovoltaic plant in Cedillo, Salamanca, which will utilize over 576,000 bifacial photovoltaic modules. The plant is expected to produce more than 582,400 MWh of green energy annually.
- **September 2023**: Iberdrola completed the construction of the Villarino photovoltaic plant with a capacity of 50 MW in Salamanca, which is part of a broader initiative to enhance solar energy production in the region.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2022**: The construction of the Cedillo photovoltaic plant is backed by local suppliers and is part of Iberdrola's broader investment plan to install 10,000 new MW of renewable energy in Spain by 2030, with a specific investment of over €1.7 billion in Extremadura from 2020 to 2025.
- **September 2023**: Iberdrola is also investing in additional solar projects in Salamanca, contributing to a cumulative capacity of 400 MW in the region.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2022**: Announcement of the Cedillo photovoltaic plant project, with construction expected to boost local employment and the supply chain.
- **September 2023**: Completion of the Villarino photovoltaic plant (50 MW) in Salamanca, marking a significant milestone in Iberdrola's renewable energy initiatives in the area.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2022**: The construction of the Cedillo plant is projected to create up to 800 jobs during peak construction periods.
- **September 2023**: The development of the Villarino plant involved local professionals, contributing to the revitalization of the local economy and job creation.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2022**: Iberdrola is the sole owner of the Cedillo photovoltaic plant, as indicated by their commitment to fully fund the investment for the assembly and management of the solar community initiative.
- **September 2023**: The Villarino plant is also owned exclusively by Iberdrola, reinforcing their position as a leading developer of renewable energy in Spain.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones of the Iberdrola facility in Salamanca, highlighting its commitment to renewable energy and local economic growth.
