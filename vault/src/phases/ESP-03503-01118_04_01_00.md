## ESP-03503-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Palos de la Frontera, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2020**: Iberdrola, in partnership with Fertiberia, announced plans to develop a green hydrogen facility in Palos de la Frontera as part of a broader initiative to install 800MW of green hydrogen production capacity across multiple sites, including Palos de la Frontera and Puertollano. The project aims to make Spain a leader in green hydrogen technology.
- **May 2021**: A 230MW green hydrogen project in Palos de la Frontera was confirmed as part of the collaboration between Iberdrola and Cummins, with Cummins serving as the electrolyzer supplier.
- **December 2021**: Iberdrola's green hydrogen initiative includes plans for over 800MW of electrolysis capacity, with the Palos de la Frontera facility contributing to this total. The project is expected to produce green hydrogen for Fertiberia's ammonia plant.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2020**: The overall investment for the green hydrogen initiative, which includes the Palos de la Frontera facility, is projected at €1.8 billion over seven years.
- **December 2021**: Iberdrola has submitted 53 projects related to green hydrogen to the Next Generation EU programme, which could trigger investments of €2.5 billion, contributing to the green hydrogen production capacity in Spain.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2020**: Announcement of the green hydrogen partnership and plans for the Palos de la Frontera facility.
- **May 2021**: Confirmation of the 230MW green hydrogen project in Palos de la Frontera as part of the collaboration with Cummins.
- **December 2021**: The project is part of a larger initiative that aims to develop multiple phases of green hydrogen production, with completion expected by 2027.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2021**: The collaboration with Cummins is expected to create 350 new jobs as production ramps up at the new electrolyzer plant, which supports the Palos project.
- **December 2021**: The broader Iberdrola hydrogen projects are anticipated to contribute to the creation of approximately 4,000 skilled jobs at 500 local suppliers.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2020**: The facility is part of a joint initiative between Iberdrola and Fertiberia, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by Iberdrola.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Iberdrola facility in Palos de la Frontera, Spain, focusing on its technology, investments, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.

### Technology and Operations
- **Article 1 (29-10-2020)**: "The partnership could see the installation of 800MW of green hydrogen production capacity."
- **Article 4 (14-12-2021)**: "Iberdrola has developed a project for the construction of more than 800 MW of electrolysers to produce green hydrogen and later green ammonia and fertilisers in four phases, in Puertollano (Ciudad Real) and Palos de la Frontera (Huelva), until 2027."
- **Article 5 (14-12-2021)**: "The initiative, which will start with the commissioning of the Puertollano complex, could see the development of three other projects between 2023 and 2027, which would be carried out at the Fertiberia plant in this municipality of Ciudad Real and at the Palos de la Frontera plant in Huelva.
