### Evolutionary Timeline of Cepsa Facility in Palos de la Frontera, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2024**: Cepsa announced a project to develop a 400-MW green hydrogen production facility at La Rabida Energy Park in Palos de la Frontera. The facility will utilize two technologies: 300 MW will be produced using Thyssenkrupp Nucera's alkaline electrolysers, and 100 MW will use Siemens Energy's Proton Exchange Membrane (PEM) technology. The plant aims to produce up to 47,000 tonnes of green hydrogen annually.
- **July 2024**: Cepsa revealed plans to build a biomethane plant at the same location, with an annual capacity of up to 100 GWh, which will support the production of green hydrogen and renewable fuels. This facility is part of the broader Andalusian Green Hydrogen Valley initiative, which aims for a total capacity of 1 GW in Palos de la Frontera.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2024**: The initial phase of the 400-MW green hydrogen project is still pending a final investment decision (FID). Cepsa's broader strategy includes developing 2 GW of green hydrogen capacity in southern Spain by 2030, with 1 GW planned for Huelva and 1 GW for Cádiz.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2024**: Cepsa selected Siemens Energy to supply 100 MW of PEM electrolyzers for the La Rábida Energy Park. The project is part of a larger plan to develop 400 MW of green hydrogen production capacity.
- **July 2024**: Cepsa and Prezero announced a partnership to develop a biomethane plant at the Palos de la Frontera facility, which will also support the production of green hydrogen.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2024**: The development of the biomethane plant and the green hydrogen facility is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers were not disclosed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: Cepsa appears to be the primary owner of the facilities, as indicated by its announcements and partnerships, particularly with Fertiberia for the green hydrogen project and Prezero for the biomethane plant. There is no indication of joint ownership or external stakeholders in the articles provided.
