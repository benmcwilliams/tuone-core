## ESP-03619-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Cadiz, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2022**: Iberdrola announced the development of a new 375 MW photovoltaic plant in Cedillo, Cadiz. The facility will utilize over 576,000 bifacial photovoltaic modules, allowing for higher energy production. It is expected to generate more than 582,400 MWh of green energy annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2022**: Iberdrola will fully fund the installation of small photovoltaic plants with a combined capacity of 340 kW on municipal facilities and land provided by the City Council, as part of a community solar initiative. The total investment for this project is not specified but is part of Iberdrola's broader investment plan to install 10,000 new MW in Spain by 2030.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2022**: The facility received its Environmental Impact Statement, marking a significant milestone in the project timeline. Construction is expected to begin shortly after this announcement, with the community solar initiative set to be operational by early 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2022**: The construction of the photovoltaic plant is projected to create up to 800 jobs during peak construction periods, contributing to local employment and the supply chain related to the energy transition.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2022**: Iberdrola is the sole owner of the facility, as indicated by their commitment to fully fund the community solar initiative and the overall development of the photovoltaic plant.
