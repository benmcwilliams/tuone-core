### Evolutionary Timeline of the Repsol Facility in Port of Bilbao, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2020**: Repsol announced the construction of a €60 million pilot plant at the Port of Bilbao. The facility will utilize electrolysis to produce hydrogen by splitting water into hydrogen and oxygen, powered by a combination of wind and solar energy. The initial production capacity is set at 50 barrels of synthetic fuel per day, with plans to scale up to a commercial facility producing 500,000 tons per year (equivalent to 11,000 barrels per day) if the pilot is successful.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2020**: The pilot plant is backed by a €60 million investment. The project aims to demonstrate the viability of producing synthetic fuels using renewable energy, with a focus on achieving economically viable electricity costs.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2020**: Announcement of the pilot plant project, with expectations to begin full operations in about three years (around mid-2023). The timeline for validating the technology and products is estimated to take around eight years, with a potential full-scale facility launch by the end of the decade.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2020**: While specific job creation figures were not mentioned, the project is expected to involve a significant workforce, as indicated by Repsol's executive director, Jaime Martin, who noted that "we have a lot of people working on it."

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2020**: The facility is owned exclusively by Repsol, as indicated by the company's direct involvement in the project and its commitment to the development of the pilot plant.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic intentions of Repsol regarding its facility in the Port of Bilbao, focusing on the transition towards green hydrogen and synthetic fuel production.
