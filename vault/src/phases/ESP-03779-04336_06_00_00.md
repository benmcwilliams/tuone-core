### Evolutionary Timeline of the University of Cordoba Facility in Cordoba, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2022**: Researchers at the University of Cordoba developed a methodology to integrate tree-based agriculture with solar power plants using north-south-oriented, single-axis trackers. This model allows for the cultivation of various tree crops (e.g., olives, almonds, peaches) alongside solar panels, improving land equivalent ratio (LER) by 28.9% to 47.2%.
- **March 2024**: A new method was introduced to quantify agricultural production potential beneath dual-axis solar trackers. The methodology identifies non-shading zones between the panels, allowing for the cultivation of crops in these areas. The Molino photovoltaic plant in Cordoba was specifically analyzed, revealing up to 74% of arable land available for cultivation.
- **October 2024**: Researchers created a model to estimate oil production and energy generation in agrivoltaic facilities built in hedgerow olive orchards. This model simulates solar radiation and evaluates the interaction between solar modules and crop growth, allowing for optimized oil and energy production.
