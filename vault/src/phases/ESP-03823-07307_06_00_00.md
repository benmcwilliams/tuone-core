## ESP-03823-07307

### Evolutionary Timeline of the ABO Energy Facility in Yebra, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2024**: Construction began on the Valdezorita solar farm, which is a ground-mounted photovoltaic system located in Yebra, Castilla La-Mancha. The facility is designed to have a total nominal output of approximately **50 MW** and an annual production capacity of around **100 GWh**, utilizing **87,000 solar modules**. The installation of mounting systems and panels is scheduled for the summer of 2024, with commissioning expected in **early 2025**. This project marks ABO Wind's first turnkey project in Spain and is noted for using tracker technology to optimize solar panel yield.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2024**: The project is financed with an investment volume in the **double-digit million-euro range**, supported by funds from the German **KfW bank** and in cooperation with **DAL Deutsche Anlagen-Leasing GmbH & Co. KG**. Additionally, a long-term pay-as-produced Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) has been secured with a large international offtaker.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2024**: Construction of the Valdezorita solar farm commenced.
- **Summer 2024**: Installation of mounting systems and solar panels is scheduled.
- **Early 2025**: The commissioning of the solar farm is expected.

#### 4. Labour
- The project is anticipated to economically benefit the rural area by granting large-scale contracts to Spanish subcontractors, although specific job creation or loss figures are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The facility is owned by **ABO Energy España**, a subsidiary of the German company **ABO Wind**. The company is in the process of changing its name and legal form, with the Spanish subsidiary having already adopted the name **ABO Energy** as of **February 2024**.
