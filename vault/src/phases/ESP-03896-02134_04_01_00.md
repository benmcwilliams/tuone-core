### Evolutionary Timeline of the Acciona Energia Facility in Lloseta, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2021**: The Lloseta facility, part of the Power to Green Hydrogen Mallorca project, began commissioning tests to produce renewable hydrogen. It is noted as the first industrial-scale renewable hydrogen project in Spain and Southern Europe. The facility is powered by renewable energy from two solar plants: one in Lloseta (8.5 MW) and another in Petra (5.85 MW). The plant is set to produce at least 300 tonnes of green hydrogen annually, which will be distributed through Spain's first hydrogen pipeline built by Redexis. The hydrogen produced is certified as 100% renewable using blockchain technology via the GreenH2Chain platform.

- **March 2022**: The Lloseta PV plant, which is part of the hydrogen project, was completed and is responsible for supplying electricity to the hydrogen production facility. The construction of the PV plant took six months and involved a team of 12 engineers from Colway Energia.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2021**: The project received integrated environmental authorization from the government of the Balearic Islands, allowing it to proceed with commissioning tests.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2021**: Start of commissioning tests for the hydrogen production facility.
- **March 2022**: Completion of the Lloseta PV plant, which is integral to the hydrogen production process.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2022**: During the construction of the Lloseta PV plant, Colway Energia employed a team of 12 engineers for the execution and monitoring of the project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is part of a consortium project led by Acciona Energia and Enagas, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by Acciona. Other partners include Cemex and IDAE.
