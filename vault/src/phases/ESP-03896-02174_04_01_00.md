### Evolutionary Timeline of the Cemex Facility in Lloseta, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2019**: Announcement of the Power to Green Hydrogen Mallorca project, which will be a pioneering green hydrogen production plant in the Lloseta industrial area. The facility is designed to produce 10 MW of renewable hydrogen, primarily powered by solar energy. It aims to supply sustainable fuel for public transport buses and other vehicles in the region.
- **12/2021**: The facility begins commissioning tests and is recognized as the first industrial-scale renewable hydrogen project in Spain and Southern Europe. It is powered by two solar plants (8.5 MW in Lloseta and 5.85 MW in Petra) and is set to produce at least 300 tonnes of green hydrogen annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2019**: The estimated budget for the Power to Green Hydrogen Mallorca project is €50 million, funded by Cemex, Enagás, Acciona, and Redexis.
- **12/2021**: The project receives integrated environmental authorization, allowing it to proceed with the start-up procedures.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2019**: The project is announced and is in the public consultation phase, with plans to start operations in 2021.
- **12/2021**: The facility begins commissioning tests, marking a significant milestone in its development. It is expected to start producing hydrogen on an industrial scale at the beginning of 2022.

#### 4. Labour
- **09/2019**: The announcement of the green hydrogen facility follows the cessation of production at the Cemex de Mallorca unit, indicating potential job losses in the cement production sector. However, the new hydrogen facility is expected to create jobs related to the production and distribution of renewable hydrogen.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **09/2019**: The facility is a collaborative project involving multiple companies: Cemex, Enagás, Acciona, and Redexis, indicating a joint venture rather than exclusive ownership by Cemex.
