### Evolutionary Timeline of the ArcelorMittal Facility in Gijón, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2021**: ArcelorMittal announced plans to develop a green hydrogen direct reduced iron (DRI) plant at its facility in Gijón. This facility will support the Sestao plant, which manufactures flat steel products for various sectors. The Gijón facility is expected to produce 1 million tonnes of DRI, which will be transported to Sestao for use in two hybrid electric arc furnaces (EAFs). By 2025, the Sestao plant aims to produce 1.6 million tonnes of zero carbon emissions steel.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2021**: The project involves a significant investment of €1 billion ($1.2 billion) as part of a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with the Spanish Government. This investment is crucial for the construction of the green hydrogen DRI plant and the hybrid EAF.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2021**: Announcement of the project to develop the world’s first full-scale zero carbon-emissions steel plant using hydrogen, with a focus on the construction of the green hydrogen DRI unit in Gijón.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2021**: The initiative is expected to have a positive impact on employment due to the construction of multiple large-scale solar farms and the infrastructure required for hydrogen production in the Iberian Peninsula.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify the ownership structure of the facility in Gijón, but it is implied that ArcelorMittal is the primary operator involved in the development of the facility, particularly in collaboration with the Spanish Government and other partners for the hydrogen supply.
