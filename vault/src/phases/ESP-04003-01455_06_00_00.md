### Evolutionary Timeline of the ArcelorMittal Facility in Sestao, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2021**: ArcelorMittal announced that its Sestao plant is set to become one of the world’s first full-scale zero carbon-emissions steel plants by utilizing green hydrogen. The facility produces a range of flat steel products for the automotive, construction sectors, and general industry. By 2025, the plant is expected to produce 1.6 million tonnes of zero carbon emissions steel. The operation will involve the use of a 2.3 million-tonne green hydrogen direct reduced iron (DRI) unit located in Gijón, with approximately 1 million tonnes of DRI transported to Sestao for use in its two electric arc furnaces (EAFs).

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2021**: A memorandum of understanding (MoU) was signed with the Spanish Government, which includes an investment of €1 billion ($1.2 billion) for the construction of the green hydrogen DRI plant in Gijón and a new hybrid electric arc furnace at the Sestao facility. The project will also involve the construction of multiple large-scale solar farms to support hydrogen production through solar-powered electrolysis.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2021**: Announcement of the project to transition the Sestao plant to a zero carbon-emissions facility, with a commitment to achieve net zero emissions as soon as possible. The project is contingent on the supply of affordable, mass-scale hydrogen and a supportive legal framework.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2021**: The initiative is expected to have a significant impact on employment due to the construction of the infrastructure required for hydrogen production and the development of solar farms.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2021**: The facility is owned exclusively by ArcelorMittal, as indicated by the company's direct involvement in the project and the signing of the MoU with the Spanish Government.
