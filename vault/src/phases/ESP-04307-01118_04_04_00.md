## ESP-04307-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Guadalajara, ESP

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2021**: Iberdrola, in collaboration with Cummins, announced plans for a new €50 million PEM electrolyser plant in the Guadalajara area. The facility will initially have a production capacity of approximately 500 MW per year, scalable to over 1 GW/year. The plant will cover 22,000 square meters and is expected to open in 2023.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2021**: The joint venture between Iberdrola and Cummins involves a significant investment of €50 million for the electrolyser plant. This investment is aimed at accelerating the growth of the green hydrogen market in Spain and Portugal.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2021**: Announcement of the site selection search for the new electrolyser plant in Guadalajara.
- **Expected 2023**: Anticipated opening of the electrolyser plant.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2021**: The establishment of the electrolyser plant is projected to create approximately 350 new jobs as production ramps up.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2021**: The facility is a joint venture between Iberdrola and Cummins, indicating that it is not exclusively owned by Iberdrola.

### Additional Developments
- **September 2022**: Iberdrola announced the construction of a new 375 MW photovoltaic plant in Spain, which will contribute to the energy transition and job creation, although it is not directly related to the Guadalajara facility.
- **December 2024**: Various photovoltaic projects were announced, including those in Guadalajara, but specific details about ownership or direct relation to the Iberdrola facility were not provided.
