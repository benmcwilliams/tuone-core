## ESP-04307-04806

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Danone Facility in Guadalajara, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: The facility in Guadalajara, along with other Danone factories in Spain, will receive 100% renewable electricity from the Francisco Pizarro photovoltaic plant, which has a capacity of 590 MW. This plant is set to be operational in 2022.
- **Renewable Energy Supply**: The facility will benefit from a Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) that guarantees an annual supply of 73 GWh of renewable electricity, with an additional contract bringing the total to 104 GWh/year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Amount**: The renewable energy project, which includes the photovoltaic plant, is part of a €300 million investment. This investment is expected to create significant employment opportunities.
- **Long-term Commitment**: The PPA with Iberdrola is for a duration of 10 years, starting from April 2022, ensuring a stable supply of renewable energy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **PPA Announcement**: On January 21, 2021, it was announced that Danone and Iberdrola signed a long-term PPA for renewable electricity.
- **Operational Date**: The Francisco Pizarro photovoltaic plant is expected to be operational in April 2022.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The €300 million project is anticipated to contribute to the employment of more than 1,200 workers during its key stages of delivery, enhancing local industry and job opportunities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture**: The facility does not operate as a standalone entity but is part of a broader collaboration between Danone and Iberdrola, indicating a joint commitment to sustainability and renewable energy supply.
