## ESP-04307-07758

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Enresa Facility in Guadalajara, ESP

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility is focused on the decommissioning of the José Cabrera nuclear power plant, which operated from 1968 until its closure in 2006. 
   - The plant had a production capacity of 142 MWe and supplied over 75% of Guadalajara's power requirements during its operational period.
   - Pre-dismantling activities were conducted between 2006 and 2009, primarily involving the management of used fuel and operational waste.
   - The dismantling of the plant's containment building began in November 2019, with significant progress reported, including the removal of the containment dome and the completion of the turbine building demolition in June 2022.
   - As of September 2023, Enresa has filled the void left by the dismantled structures with 9500 cubic meters of selected soil, ensuring the site is prepared for final restoration.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The ownership of the José Cabrera nuclear power plant was transferred to Enresa for decommissioning in February 2010, following the completion of pre-dismantling activities.
   - Contracts were awarded to Westinghouse for dismantling the reactor vessel internals in 2010 and the reactor pressure vessel in 2013, indicating investment in specialized services for the decommissioning process.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **February 2010:** Ownership transferred to Enresa for decommissioning.
   - **November 2019:** Dismantling of the containment building commenced.
   - **June 2022:** Completion of the demolition of the turbine building.
   - **September 2023:** Enresa reported the completion of soil filling and ongoing preparations for site restoration.

4. **Labour:**
   - The articles do not specify job creation or job loss directly related to the decommissioning activities at the facility.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - Enresa exclusively owns the facility for the purpose of decommissioning the José Cabrera nuclear power plant.
