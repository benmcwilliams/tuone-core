## ESP-04337-04806

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Danone Facility in Granada, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: Danone's facilities in Spain, including Granada, will receive 100% renewable electricity from the Francisco Pizarro solar photovoltaic plant, which has a capacity of 590 MW. This plant is set to be operational in 2022 and will supply Danone's factories and logistics centers across Spain.
- **Renewable Energy Supply**: The agreement with Iberdrola ensures an annual output of 73 GWh/year, with an additional green energy contract bringing the total to 104 GWh/year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Amount**: The Francisco Pizarro project represents a €300 million investment.
- **Employment Contribution**: The project is expected to create employment for more than 1,200 workers during its development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Power Purchase Agreement (PPA)**: On January 21, 2021, Danone and Iberdrola signed a 10-year PPA, which will commence in April 2022. This agreement is pivotal for Danone's sustainability strategy and ensures a long-term supply of renewable energy.
- **Operational Timeline**: The Francisco Pizarro plant is expected to be operational in 2022.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The €300 million investment in the solar project is projected to contribute to the employment of over 1,200 workers, enhancing local industry and job opportunities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture**: The facility does not operate as a standalone entity but is part of a broader collaboration between Danone and Iberdrola, with Danone relying on Iberdrola for its renewable energy supply through the PPA.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic initiatives surrounding the Danone facility in Granada, Spain, highlighting its commitment to sustainability and renewable energy.
