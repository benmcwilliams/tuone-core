### E.ON Facility in Adeje, ESP - Evolutionary Timeline

1. **Technology and Operations**:
   - **Date**: August 2022
   - **Details**: E.ON, in collaboration with the municipality of Adeje, launched the Adeje Verde project, which is Spain’s largest solar community. The facility produces renewable electricity through a solar photovoltaic (PV) production unit that connects up to 200 consumers within a 500m radius. The first PV system was installed on the rooftop of Adeje’s music school, with a production capacity of up to 149,200 kWh per year. The project aims to supply Adeje with up to 100% energy from local renewable sources in the future.

2. **Investment and Development**:
   - **Date**: August 2022
   - **Details**: The project is part of a broader initiative to establish energy communities in line with the new Electricity Market Directive of the 2019 EU Clean Energy Package. While specific investment amounts are not detailed in the article, the project is positioned as a pilot for future energy systems in the Canary Islands, which indicates significant investment in infrastructure and technology.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**:
   - **Date**: August 2022
   - **Details**: The first PV system was installed, and approximately 200 households began receiving energy from the plant. This marks the start of the Adeje Verde energy community, which is expected to serve as a model for similar projects across Europe.
