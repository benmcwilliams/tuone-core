## ESP-04393-00716

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Siemens Facility in Ferrol, Spain

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 10/2022
   - **Details:** The facility is designed to produce hydrogen with a total planned capacity of 100 MW, developed in phases of 20, 30, and 50 MW. It has an annual production capacity of up to 14,400 tons of hydrogen (H2) for distribution by tanker and injection into a hydro pipeline. The operation is expected to prevent the emission of over 12,000 tons of CO2 equivalent monthly.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 10/2022
   - **Details:** The project is part of the Pole for the Transformation of Galicia and aligns with various EU and national strategies, including the EU Green Deal and the Spanish Just Transition Strategy. Siemens Energy has joined as a technology partner, indicating a significant investment in the development of this facility.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 10/2022
   - **Details:** The facility is recognized as the first large renewable hydrogen plant in Galicia. The project has been in development since late 2019, accumulating 20,000 hours of work involving 30 companies and organizations. Key tasks completed include feasibility studies and engineering work.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 10/2022
   - **Details:** The operation and maintenance of the facility are expected to create 50 direct jobs and 55 indirect jobs. During the construction phase, 186 professionals will be involved directly, with an additional 52 jobs created indirectly. The hiring process prioritizes local workers, especially those affected by the closure of the coal-fired power plant.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 10/2022
   - **Details:** Siemens Energy is a technology partner in the project, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership. The facility is part of a strategic alliance with Reganosa and EDPR, integrating the entire value chain for the decarbonization of the economy in Galicia.
