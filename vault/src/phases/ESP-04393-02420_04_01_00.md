### Evolutionary Timeline of the Reganosa Facility in Ferrol, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10/2022**: The Reganosa facility is designed to produce hydrogen with a total planned capacity of 100 MW, developed in phases of 20, 30, and 50 MW. The facility aims for an annual production capacity of up to 14,400 tons of hydrogen (H2) for distribution by tanker and injection into a hydro pipeline. The operation is expected to prevent the emission of over 12,000 tons of CO2 equivalent monthly.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10/2022**: The project is part of the Pole for the Transformation of Galicia, supported by various strategic plans including the EU Green Deal, the National Energy and Climate Plan, and the Spanish Just Transition Strategy. The development has involved 20,000 hours of work and collaboration from 30 companies and organizations, indicating significant investment in feasibility studies, market analysis, and engineering.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **10/2022**: The project is announced as the first large renewable hydrogen plant in Galicia, with Siemens Energy joining as a technology partner. The facility is part of a broader initiative to establish Ferrolterra as a hub for green energy, which includes additional projects like energy storage and wind parks.

#### 4. Labour
- **10/2022**: The operation and maintenance of the facility are projected to create 50 direct jobs and 55 indirect jobs. During the construction phase, 186 professionals will be involved directly, with an additional 52 jobs created indirectly. The project prioritizes hiring local workers, especially those affected by the closure of the coal-fired power plant.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **10/2022**: The facility is a collaborative effort between Reganosa and EDPR, indicating a joint venture structure for the project, with Siemens Energy as a technology partner. The specific ownership percentages are not detailed in the article, but the strategic alliance suggests shared ownership and responsibilities in the development and operation of the facility.
