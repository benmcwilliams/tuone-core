## ESP-04393-02823

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDPR Facility in Ferrol, Spain

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 10-05-2022
   - **Details:** The facility is a hydrogen production terminal designed to operate in cumulative phases of 20, 30, and 50 MW of power, ultimately reaching a total capacity of 100 MW. It has an annual production capacity of up to 14,400 tons of hydrogen (H2) for distribution by tanker and injection into a hydro pipeline. The facility aims to prevent the emission of more than 12,000 tons equivalent of carbon dioxide into the atmosphere each month.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 10-05-2022
   - **Details:** The project is part of the Pole for the Transformation of Galicia and aligns with various EU and national strategies, including the EU Green Deal and the Spanish Just Transition Strategy. The development has involved 20,000 hours of work from 30 companies and organizations, indicating significant investment in feasibility studies, market analysis, and engineering.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 10-05-2022
   - **Details:** The facility is identified as the first large renewable hydrogen plant in Galicia. The project has been in development since late 2019, with Siemens Energy recently joining as a technology partner. The facility is part of a broader initiative to establish Ferrolterra as a hub for green energy, which includes energy storage and a wind complex.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 10-05-2022
   - **Details:** The operation and maintenance of the facility are expected to create 50 direct jobs and 55 indirect jobs. During the construction phase, 186 professionals will be involved directly, with an additional 52 jobs created indirectly. The project prioritizes hiring local workers, especially those affected by the closure of the coal-fired power plant.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 10-05-2022
   - **Details:** The facility is a collaborative project between EDPR and Reganosa, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by EDPR.
