### Evolutionary Timeline of the Belectric Facility in Ciudad Real, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2019**: Innogy, through its subsidiary Belectric, acquired the rights to build the Alarcos solar park, which will have a production capacity of **50 MW**. The facility is located in the municipalities of Ciudad Real and Poblete in the Castilla-La Mancha region. Construction was expected to begin in April 2019, with commercial operation slated for the end of 2019.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2019**: A Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) was signed between Innogy and Audax Renovables for the supply of **100 GWh** of solar power annually from the Alarcos solar project. This agreement supports the facility's operation without any state subsidies, highlighting the competitive nature of solar energy in the current market.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2019**: Construction of the Alarcos solar park was expected to commence.
- **End of 2019**: The facility was anticipated to begin commercial operations, as indicated by the PPA with Audax Renovables, which was set to start in the first half of 2020.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned by **Innogy**, with **Belectric** as its subsidiary responsible for the construction, operation, and maintenance of the solar park. There is no indication of joint ownership or partnerships mentioned in the articles.
