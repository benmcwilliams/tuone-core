### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Ciudad Real, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **11/2020**: Iberdrola announced plans to build a 100 MW solar project in Ciudad Real as part of a €150 million green hydrogen initiative. This facility will include storage capabilities and is part of a broader effort to enhance renewable energy capacity in Spain.

- **12/2023**: The Erasmopower2X project was launched, which includes the installation of a 2 MW electrolyzer powered by a 4 MW photovoltaic plant. This facility aims to produce up to 330 tons of green hydrogen annually, supporting local public transport and industries.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **11/2020**: The total investment for the solar project in Ciudad Real is part of a larger €150 million green hydrogen project.

- **12/2023**: The Erasmopower2X project has a budget of €7 million, funded through a collaboration between public and private sectors, including Iberdrola.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **11/2020**: Announcement of the 100 MW solar project in Ciudad Real as part of Iberdrola's expansion in renewable energy.

- **12/2023**: The Erasmopower2X project is expected to be completed by the end of 2023, marking a significant milestone in the establishment of a green hydrogen ecosystem in Ciudad Real.

#### 4. Labour
- **12/2023**: The Erasmopower2X project is expected to create new job opportunities in the hydrogen value chain, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **12/2023**: The Erasmopower2X project is a joint initiative involving Iberdrola along with other companies and the regional government, indicating that Iberdrola does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a collaborative effort.
