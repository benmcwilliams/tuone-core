### Evolutionary Timeline of Repsol Facility in Ciudad Real, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2020**: Repsol announced the start of construction on the **126 MW Kappa solar park** located in Manzanares, Ciudad Real. The project consists of three plants with capacities of 45 MW, 45 MW, and 36 MW, respectively. 
- **2021**: The Kappa solar park became operational, contributing to Repsol's renewable energy portfolio.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2020**: The Kappa solar park project required a total investment of **€100 million**. This investment is part of Repsol's broader strategy to expand its renewable energy assets, which collectively have a cumulative capacity of **2,045 MW** under development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2020**: Construction of the Kappa solar park began.
- **2021**: The Kappa solar park was completed and began generating electricity.

#### 4. Labour
- **2020**: The construction of the Kappa solar park contributed to job creation, although specific numbers related to job creation for this facility were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Repsol owns the Kappa solar park **exclusively** through its energy unit, **Repsol Electricidad y Gas**.

### Additional Context
- **May 2023**: Repsol was involved in the **Erasmopower2X project**, a joint initiative aimed at establishing a green hydrogen ecosystem in Ciudad Real, which includes collaboration with other companies and institutions. This project is separate from the Kappa solar park but indicates Repsol's ongoing commitment to renewable energy in the region.
- **January 2024**: Repsol reported that the Kappa solar park was operational and part of its broader renewable energy strategy, which aims to reach **6 GW** of installed capacity by 2025.
