### Evolutionary Timeline of the Innogy Facility in Ciudad Real, Spain

1. **Technology and Operations**
   - **January 2019**: Innogy announced the construction of the Alarcos solar park, a 50 MW photovoltaic facility located in the municipalities of Ciudad Real and Poblete. The facility is developed by Innogy's subsidiary, Belectric, which will also handle its operation and maintenance. The project aims to enhance Innogy's solar portfolio in the Iberian Peninsula.

2. **Investment and Development**
   - **January 2019**: The Alarcos solar park is being built without state subsidies, indicating a shift towards competitive solar energy production. The project is part of Innogy's strategy to expand its renewable energy footprint in Spain.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**
   - **January 2019**: Innogy acquired the rights to build the Alarcos solar park from Synergia Energy Solutions. Construction was expected to begin in April 2019, with commercial operations slated to start by the end of 2019.
   - **November 2019**: A power purchase agreement (PPA) was signed with Audax Renovables for the supply of clean electricity from the Alarcos solar plant, which would commence in the first half of 2020 when the plant became operational.

4. **Labour**
