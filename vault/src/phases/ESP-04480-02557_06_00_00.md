## ESP-04480-02557

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Endesa Facility in Constantí, ESP

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2024**: The Endesa facility in Constantí launched its first photovoltaic park, consisting of 1,902 solar panels. This installation will generate approximately 1,610 MWh of clean energy annually, which is equivalent to the average annual consumption of about 340 homes. The total power output of this facility is 4.7 MWp, contributing to the Tarragona Water Consortium's goal of achieving 15% self-generated green energy by 2026.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2024**: Endesa is investing a total of €5.6 million for the installation of two photovoltaic plants, with the first one located in Constantí. This investment is part of a public-private collaboration model, where Endesa assumes the entire financial responsibility without requiring initial investment from the Tarragona Water Consortium (CAT).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2024**: The first phase of the project at the Constantí facility was launched, marking the operational start of the photovoltaic park. The second phase, which involves another park at the L’Ampolla Drinking Water Treatment Station (ETAP), is already underway and is expected to be operational by the last quarter of 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify any direct job creation or job loss associated with the installation of the photovoltaic park in Constantí.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Endesa operates the facility under a public-private partnership model with the Tarragona Water Consortium. Endesa is responsible for the construction, operation, and maintenance of the facility for a duration of 10 years, indicating that it does not have exclusive ownership but rather a collaborative operational role. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and operational aspects of the Endesa facility in Constantí, highlighting its role in advancing renewable energy within the water treatment sector in Spain.

### Technology and Operations
- "The Tarragona Water Consortium (CAT), together with Endesa, will have the most important photovoltaic installation on water tanks in the hydraulic sector in all of Spain."
- "In total, with an approximate area of five football fields, the CAT will have 8,569 solar modules and a power of 4.7 MWp."
- "There, 1,902 panels have already been installed, which will provide 17.7% of the energy consumed in this equipment."
- "The almost 2,000 panels will generate 1,610 MWh per year of clean and renewable energy."
- "If these two facilities will provide approximately 10% of the energy we require, we have set the goal of reaching 15% self-generated green energy by 2026."

### Investment and Development
- "Thanks to the financing model with which Endesa works, the CAT will not have to make any initial investment, so it will be the energy company that will assume the global investment of 5.6 million euros for the installation of the two plants."
