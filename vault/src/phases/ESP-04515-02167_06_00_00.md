## ESP-04515-02167

### Evolutionary Timeline of the BayWa Facility in Alcalá de Guadaira, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2019**: BayWa r.e. announced the development of the Don Rodrigo 2 solar plant, a 50MWp facility located near Alcalá de Guadaira. This facility is designed to generate 100GWh of solar power annually, sufficient to meet the electricity needs of approximately 30,000 households. The plant is built on a 162-hectare site and is a successor to the 175MWp Don Rodrigo plant.
- **January 2020**: The construction of Don Rodrigo 2 was nearing completion, with plans to connect to the grid by the end of January 2020. The facility features GCL's monocrystalline half-cell modules and Huawei's inverters, along with a single-axis tracker developed by BayWa r.e. and Zimmerman PV-Tracker.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2019**: The Don Rodrigo 2 project is developed as a zero-subsidy plant, indicating that it does not rely on government financial support. The project is part of a growing trend in Spain towards subsidy-free solar energy.
- **September 2019**: BayWa r.e. indicated that they were seeking to finance the project through equity rather than bank loans, focusing on European asset managers and renewable financiers.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2019**: BayWa r.e. and Statkraft announced the development of Don Rodrigo 2, with construction expected to be completed by the end of 2019.
- **January 2020**: BayWa r.e. confirmed that the construction of Don Rodrigo 2 was approaching completion, with commissioning scheduled for the end of January 2020.

#### 4. Labour
- **No specific job creation or job loss information** was provided in the articles regarding the Don Rodrigo 2 facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2020**: BayWa r.e. sold the Don Rodrigo 2 solar plant to Talanx, a major European insurance group. Prior to the sale, BayWa r.e. had retained ownership of the facility during its construction phase. The previous facility, Don Rodrigo, was sold to German asset manager MEAG.
