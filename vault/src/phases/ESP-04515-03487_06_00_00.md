### Evolutionary Timeline of the Solarcentury Facility in Alcalá de Guadaira, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **200MWp Cabrera Array**: The facility in Alcalá de Guadaira is part of the Cabrera solar project, which consists of four individual 50MWp plants. The Cabrera array is designed to be connected to the grid by September 2020.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **€54.8 Million Financing Deal**: On January 8, 2020, Solarcentury secured a €54.8 million finance facility to support the development of the Cabrera array, alongside the 300MW Talayuela project. This financing includes a €27.7 million trade loan from Natwest and €27.1 million in guarantees from HSBC, supported by the UK’s export credit agency, UK Export Finance.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Construction Start**: Construction on the Cabrera solar project began in July 2020, as part of the overall development plan for the 500MW portfolio in Spain, which includes both the Cabrera and Talayuela projects.
- **Grid Connection**: The Cabrera array is slated for connection to the grid by September 2020.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The construction phase of the Cabrera project is expected to contribute to local employment, although specific job creation numbers for this facility alone were not detailed in the articles. However, the Talayuela project, which is part of the same financing deal, is expected to create up to 400 local jobs.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture**: Solarcentury does not own the Cabrera facility exclusively. The company has a joint venture with Encavis AG, which holds an 80% stake in the Talayuela project, while Solarcentury retains a 20% share. The ownership structure for the Cabrera array is not explicitly detailed, but it is implied that a similar arrangement may exist.
