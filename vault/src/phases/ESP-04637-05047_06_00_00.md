### Evolutionary Timeline of the Foresight Facility in Puebla de Guzman, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2019**: Foresight Group announced a 10-year Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) with EDF Trading for power generated by its 50MW solar plant in Puebla de Guzman. The facility is expected to generate approximately 85 gigawatt-hours (GWh) of electricity annually. The plant was mechanically completed and scheduled to become fully operational in the first quarter of 2020.
- **August 2020**: The Guzman solar power plant was officially connected to the grid and began exporting clean electricity on August 19, 2020. The facility, consisting of 150,000 solar panels, confirmed its capacity to generate around 85 GWh per year, contributing significantly to Spain's emissions targets.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2019**: Foresight acquired the Guzman facility, marking it as their largest subsidy-free energy infrastructure investment in the region. The plant was built on a turn-key basis by the Spanish EPC contractor SOLAER.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2019**: Announcement of the 10-year PPA with EDF Trading, securing revenues for the Guzman solar plant.
- **August 2020**: The Guzman facility became fully operational and connected to the grid, marking a significant milestone in Foresight's portfolio of unsubsidised solar assets in the Iberian region.
