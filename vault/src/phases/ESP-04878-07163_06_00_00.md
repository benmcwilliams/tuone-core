### Evolutionary Timeline of the Solaria Facility in Burgos, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2020**: Solaria Energia received approval to build the Barcina-Garoña PV Complex in Burgos, with a capacity of 695MW. This complex is part of a larger portfolio that includes a 626MW plant in Trillo and a 782MW project in Villaviciosa, contributing to Solaria's goal of reaching 3,325MW by 2023.
- **January 2024**: Solaria received construction authorization for the 595MW Garoña solar project, which will replace the decommissioned Santa María de Garoña nuclear plant. This project aims to generate enough energy for 300,000 homes and is part of Solaria's objective to reach 3GW of capacity by the end of 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2023**: Solaria secured a €1.7 billion (US$1.8 billion) financing framework agreement with the European Investment Bank (EIB) to support the development of 120 solar PV projects across Spain, Italy, and Portugal. This investment is part of a broader plan to invest €2.6 billion into European projects over the next three years.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2020**: Approval granted for the Barcina-Garoña PV Complex.
- **January 2024**: Administrative construction authorization received for the 595MW Garoña solar project.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2024**: The construction of the Garoña solar park is expected to create 2,400 jobs, focusing on local employment opportunities. The project will also contribute to indirect job creation in the region.
