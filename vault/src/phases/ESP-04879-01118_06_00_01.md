## ESP-04879-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Cedillo, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2019**: Iberdrola announced plans to build three solar plants in Extremadura, including two 50MW projects (Majada Alta and Santa Antonio) and an up to 300MW facility in Cedillo. The total capacity for the Cedillo projects is 400MW, utilizing 300,000 solar panels.
- **March 2021**: Iberdrola revealed plans for a 375MW solar plant in Cedillo, which will use 925,680 panels and 180 inverters. This facility is expected to come online by 2023.
- **August 2022**: The Majada Alta and San Antonio plants were commissioned, with a combined capacity of 100MW, enough to power 45,000 households.
- **September 2022**: Iberdrola received a permit for the 375MW solar project in Cedillo, which will produce over 582,400MWh of clean energy annually.
- **July 2023**: The Cedillo plant was confirmed to be operational, providing 280GWh of solar energy annually to Vodafone.
- **December 2023**: Iberdrola obtained environmental approval for the HIDRO Cedillo project, a hybrid hydroelectric-solar plant with a capacity of 86.4MW, featuring over 160,000 photovoltaic modules.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: The development of the 375MW Cedillo plant is part of a larger investment of approximately €420 million for multiple solar projects in Extremadura.
- **September 2022**: The construction of the 375MW solar facility is expected to create up to 800 jobs during peak construction, backed by local authorities and the Cedillo Agrarian Society.
- **December 2023**: The HIDRO Cedillo project is part of Iberdrola's broader investment strategy to enhance renewable resources in Extremadura, which has seen significant growth in solar energy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2019**: Announcement of the Cedillo solar projects.
- **March 2021**: Plans for the 375MW solar plant were announced.
- **August 2022**: Commissioning of the Majada Alta and San Antonio plants.
- **September 2022**: Permit secured for the 375MW solar project.
- **July 2023**: The Cedillo plant began operations.
- **December 2023**: Environmental approval obtained for the HIDRO Cedillo hybrid project.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2019**: Up to 200 workers were expected to be involved in the construction of the initial Cedillo projects.
- **March 2021**: The construction and start-up of the 375MW project are projected to create over 1,700 jobs.
- **September 2022**: The 375MW project is expected to create up to 800 jobs during peak construction.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: Iberdrola owns the facility exclusively, as indicated by their direct involvement in the development, construction, and operational management of the solar and hybrid projects in Cedillo.
