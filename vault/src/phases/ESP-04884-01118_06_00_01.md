## ESP-04884-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Alcántara, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2020**: Iberdrola announced plans to build a 328 MW solar plant in Alcántara, part of a larger renewable energy project pipeline of 3 GW to be installed by 2022.
- **March 2021**: Iberdrola added 750 MW of solar PV capacity to its pipeline, including the 380 MW Tagus project in Alcántara and the 375 MW Cedillo plant, both expected to come online by 2023.
- **September 2022**: The Cedillo plant was confirmed to have more than 576,000 bifacial photovoltaic modules, capable of producing over 582,400 MWh of green energy annually.
- **September 2024**: Iberdrola received a positive Environmental Impact Statement (EIS) for the 440 MW Alcántara II reversible hydropower plant, utilizing the existing hydroelectric system on the Tagus River.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2020**: The solar project in Alcántara is part of Iberdrola's strategy to invest in clean power generation, with a commitment to install 3,000 MW by 2022.
- **March 2021**: The development of the Tagus and Cedillo plants required an investment of approximately €420 million.
- **September 2024**: The hydropower project received €44.9 million in funding from the Institute for Energy Diversification and Saving (IDAE).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2020**: Iberdrola secured a long-term Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) with Orange for the electricity generated from the 328 MW solar plant.
- **March 2021**: Announcement of the development of the Tagus and Cedillo solar plants, with expected commissioning by 2023.
- **September 2022**: Confirmation of the construction of the Cedillo plant, which is part of the broader solar initiative in the region.
- **September 2024**: Positive EIS for the Alcántara II hydropower plant, indicating progress in the project.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2021**: The construction and start-up of the Tagus and Cedillo projects are expected to create over 1,700 jobs in the area.
- **September 2022**: The construction of the Cedillo plant is projected to generate up to 800 jobs during peak periods.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2020**: Iberdrola is the sole owner of the facility, as indicated by its direct involvement in the construction and operation of the solar plants and hydropower projects.
