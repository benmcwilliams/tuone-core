## ESP-04890-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Castilla y León, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2021**: Iberdrola began commissioning the Herrera II wind complex, which includes three wind farms (La Huesca, Valdesantos, and Orbanejoa) with a total installed capacity of 63 MW, consisting of 14 wind turbines. The facility is designed to supply electricity to approximately 60,000 households annually and prevent the release of 50,000 tons of CO2 per year. The wind turbines installed are the SG 4.5-145, the largest onshore wind turbines in Spain, with a unit power of 4.5 MW.
- **April 2023**: Iberdrola announced the construction of Spain's first hybrid wind and solar plant in the province of Burgos, which will hybridize the existing 69 MW Ballestas and Casetona wind power complex with two new photovoltaic plants of 41 MW and 33 MW. This project will involve over 170,000 photovoltaic modules.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2021**: Iberdrola invested €70 million in the development of the Herrera II wind complex.
- **April 2023**: The hybrid wind and solar project in Burgos is allocated an investment of over €40 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2021**: Commissioning of the Herrera II wind complex began.
- **April 2023**: Construction of the hybrid wind and solar plant commenced, with the project involving 360 professionals.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2021**: The development of the Herrera II wind complex created approximately 800 jobs, with a significant portion of the civil works entrusted to local companies.
- **April 2023**: The hybrid project will involve 360 professionals, contributing to local employment.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2023**: Iberdrola sold a 49% stake in its 1.27 GW renewable energy portfolio to Norges Bank Investment Management (NBIM), retaining a 51% stake and continuing to control and manage the assets. This portfolio includes renewable assets in Castilla y León, among other regions.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones of the Iberdrola facility in Castilla y León, highlighting its commitment to renewable energy and local economic stimulation.
