## ESP-04897-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Francisco Pizarro, ESP

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2019**: Iberdrola announced the development of the Francisco Pizarro solar plant, which will have a capacity of **590 MW**, making it the largest photovoltaic plant in Europe and Iberdrola's largest of this type globally. The facility will consist of **1.5 million photovoltaic modules** and is expected to generate enough clean energy to supply over **334,000 homes**, preventing the emission of **140,000 tonnes of CO2** annually.
- **September 2022**: The facility was confirmed to have **more than 576,000 bifacial photovoltaic modules**, which allow for higher production due to their dual light-sensitive surfaces. The expected annual production is over **582,400 MWh** of green energy.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2024**: The total investment for the Francisco Pizarro solar PV plant was reported to be over **€300 million** (approximately **$326.41 million**). This investment supports the facility's capacity and infrastructure development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2019**: Announcement of the Francisco Pizarro project as part of Iberdrola's broader renewable energy strategy in Spain.
- **September 2022**: The facility received an **Environmental Impact Statement**, allowing construction to proceed.
- **July 2024**: Iberdrola signed a **Power Purchase Agreement (PPA)** with Burger King to supply power from the Francisco Pizarro plant, indicating operational readiness and commercial viability.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2019**: The construction of the facility is expected to involve up to **200 workers**.
- **September 2022**: The construction phase is projected to create up to **800 jobs** during peak periods, contributing significantly to local employment.
- **September 2022**: Iberdrola's investment plan for the 2020-2025 period anticipates the creation of approximately **7,400 jobs** in Extremadura, where the Francisco Pizarro facility is located.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2019 - July 2024**: Iberdrola is the **exclusive owner** of the Francisco Pizarro facility, as indicated by the absence of any mention of joint ventures or partnerships in the articles.
