### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Murcia, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2022**: Iberdrola announced plans for a new 375 MW photovoltaic plant in Cedillo, Murcia, featuring over 576,000 bifacial photovoltaic modules. The plant is expected to produce more than 582,400 MWh of green energy annually.
- **January 2023**: Iberdrola confirmed the inclusion of a 50 MW solar plant in Penarrubia, Murcia, as part of a joint venture with Norges Bank Investment Management, contributing to a total operational capacity of nearly 200 MW in the joint venture.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2022**: The construction of the Cedillo photovoltaic plant is part of Iberdrola's broader investment plan to install 10,000 new MW in Spain by 2030, with a specific focus on Extremadura, where significant investments are planned.
- **January 2023**: Iberdrola sold a 49% stake in its 1.27 GW renewable energy portfolio, which includes assets in Murcia, to Norges Bank Investment Management, valued at approximately €1.2 billion. Iberdrola retains a 51% stake and continues to manage the assets.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2022**: Iberdrola received an environmental impact statement for the new photovoltaic plant in Cedillo, marking a significant milestone in the project's development.
- **January 2023**: The Penarrubia solar plant in Murcia was incorporated into the joint venture with Norges Bank, marking the operational start of this facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2022**: The construction of the Cedillo photovoltaic plant is expected to create up to 800 jobs during peak construction periods, contributing to local employment and the supply chain.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2023**: Iberdrola retains a 51% ownership stake in the renewable energy portfolio, including the facilities in Murcia, while Norges Bank Investment Management holds a 49% stake. This indicates a joint venture structure for the renewable assets in the region.
