## ESP-04906-01420

### Evolutionary Timeline of TotalEnergies Facility in Murcia, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2023**: TotalEnergies received environmental approval for over 350MW of solar PV projects in Murcia as part of a larger 3GW capacity across Spain. The first projects are expected to be operational in early 2024.
- **November 2023**: TotalEnergies and Soltec received construction authorization for the Luminora Solar Dos PV project, a 200MW solar PV project in Murcia, set to begin construction in 2024. This project will utilize over 337,000 bifacial solar PV modules and more than 5,300 solar trackers.
- **January 2024**: TotalEnergies acquired a 65% stake in the 59MW Campos del Río project in Murcia from Soltec, which is expected to generate 118GWh per year, sufficient for 33,700 families.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2023**: The environmental impact assessment approval is a significant step for TotalEnergies, aligning with their goal of achieving 100GW of gross renewable installed capacity by 2030 worldwide.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2023**: TotalEnergies received a positive environmental impact assessment for 48 solar projects across Spain, including more than 350MW in Murcia. The first projects are expected to be operational in early 2024.
- **November 2023**: Construction authorization was granted for the 200MW Luminora Solar Dos PV project in Murcia, with construction set to begin in 2024.
- **January 2024**: TotalEnergies completed the acquisition of a 65% stake in the Campos del Río project, further solidifying its presence in the Murcia region.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2023**: TotalEnergies plans to provide training to residents in surrounding municipalities during the construction and operation phases of the solar projects, contributing to local job creation.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2024**: TotalEnergies holds a 65% stake in the Campos del Río project, with Soltec retaining a 35% stake, indicating a joint venture structure for this specific project. The overall ownership structure for the other projects in Murcia is not explicitly detailed but suggests a collaborative approach with local developers like Soltec. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic moves made by TotalEnergies in the Murcia facility, highlighting its commitment to renewable energy and local community engagement.
