## ESP-04911-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Torrecillas de la Tiesta, ESP

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 12-08-2022
   - **Details:** The Francisco Pizarro project, located in Torrecillas de la Tiesta and Aldeacentenera (Cáceres), began operations. It is the largest photovoltaic (PV) project in Europe and the largest facility of its type operated by Iberdrola globally. The facility has an installed capacity of 590 megawatts (MW) and consists of approximately 1.5 million photovoltaic modules. It is designed to generate enough clean energy to supply over 334,000 homes and prevent the emission of 150,000 tonnes of CO2 annually.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 12-08-2022
   - **Details:** The total investment for the Francisco Pizarro project exceeded €300 million. Iberdrola has signed long-term power purchase agreements (PPAs) with companies such as Danone, Bayer, and PepsiCo to supply 100% renewable electricity from this facility.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 12-08-2022
   - **Details:** The facility officially began operations, marking a significant milestone as it is the largest PV project in Europe.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 12-08-2022
   - **Details:** The construction of the facility generated over 1,500 jobs during peak construction periods, with 60% of these positions filled by local workers.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 12-08-2022
   - **Details:** Iberdrola operates the Francisco Pizarro project, indicating full ownership of the facility.

### Summary:
The Iberdrola facility in Torrecillas de la Tiesta, known as the Francisco Pizarro project, began operations in August 2022 with a capacity of 590 MW. The project involved an investment of over €300 million and created more than 1,500 jobs during construction. Iberdrola has full ownership of the facility and has established long-term agreements to supply renewable energy to major companies.
