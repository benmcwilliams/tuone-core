### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Usagre, Spain (Núñez de Balboa Solar Plant)

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **30-07-2018**: Iberdrola announces the construction of the Núñez de Balboa solar plant in Usagre, with a planned capacity of 391 MW and an expected annual production of around 850 GWh.
- **19-03-2019**: Construction of the Núñez de Balboa solar plant officially begins, with a total capacity of 500 MW (DC) planned, covering approximately 1,000 hectares.
- **12-01-2020**: The Núñez de Balboa solar park is commissioned, becoming the largest PV plant in Europe with a capacity of 500 MW, featuring 1,430,000 solar panels and two substations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **19-03-2019**: The project is estimated to require an investment of around €300 million.
- **07-06-2019**: The European Investment Bank (EIB) and the Instituto de Crédito Oficial (ICO) agree to provide financial backing totaling €285 million for the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **30-07-2018**: Announcement of the project and securing of a Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) with Kutxabank.
- **12-10-2018**: Iberdrola signs a second PPA with Euskaltel for the solar project.
- **16-11-2018**: A third PPA is signed with Uvesco for the supply of renewable energy from the plant.
- **12-01-2020**: The Núñez de Balboa solar plant officially begins commercial operations after construction completion in December 2019.
- **14-02-2020**: Iberdrola announces plans to install 3 GW of solar capacity by 2022, with 2 GW in Extremadura, including the Núñez de Balboa project.

#### 4. Labour
- **19-03-2019**: The construction of the facility is expected to create numerous jobs, although specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **12-01-2020**: The Núñez de Balboa solar plant is developed by Iberdrola in partnership with local power provider Ecoenergías del Guadiana, indicating a joint ownership structure.

#### 6. Legal Challenges
- **17-06-2022**: A Spanish court rules that 60% of the Núñez de Balboa solar plant must be dismantled due to illegal expropriation of land. Iberdrola plans to appeal the decision, asserting that the facility operates under valid permits.
