### Evolutionary Timeline of the Navantia Facility in Brest, ESP

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **September 2019:** Navantia was selected by Ailes Marines to fabricate the turbine foundations for the Saint-Brieuc offshore wind farm. The facility is involved in producing turbine foundations, which are critical components for offshore wind energy generation.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **July 2020:** Ailes Marines, which is 100% owned by Iberdrola, finalized contracts with first-tier suppliers for the components of the Saint-Brieuc wind farm, indicating significant investment in the project. The specific financial details of the investment related to Navantia's role were not disclosed.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **July 2020:** Ailes Marines announced that Navantia would be fabricating the turbine foundations, marking a significant milestone in the project timeline.
   - **First Semester of 2021:** Installation of pin piles and turbine jacket foundations is scheduled to begin.
   - **2023:** The Saint-Brieuc offshore wind farm is expected to be fully commissioned.

4. **Labour:**
   - **September 2019:** The activity at Navantia's facility in Brest is projected to mobilize 250 jobs over two years, contributing to local employment through the construction of the turbine foundations.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - Navantia is involved in the project as a contractor for Ailes Marines, which is fully owned by Iberdrola. There is no indication that Navantia has exclusive ownership of the facility; rather, it operates as a supplier within the framework of the offshore wind farm project.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Navantia facility in Brest, focusing on its role in the Saint-Brieuc offshore wind farm project.

### Technology and Operations
- "In September 2019, Ailes Marines selected Spain’s Navantia to fabricate the turbine foundations for the Saint-Brieuc offshore wind farm."
- "The foundation of the electrical substation is 63 metres tall, with a total weight of 1,630 tonnes."
- "The upper part of the substation is 55 metres long, 31 metres wide, and 23 metres tall, with a total weight of approximately 3,400 tons."

### Investment and Development
- "Since the final investment decision, Ailes Marines has practically finalized the contracts’ signatures with first-tier suppliers for the components of the Saint-Brieuc wind farm."
- "In May 2020, Ailes Marines contracted several suppliers, including Siemens Gamesa, for the supply of wind turbines in Le Havre, where the construction of the turbine manufacturer’s factory has just started."

### Project Status and Milestones
- "The installation of pin piles and turbine jacket foundations will start in the first semester of 2021."
- "The wind farm will be fully commissioned in 2023."
- "The sail away of both the topside and the jacket is scheduled for the spring/summer of 2022."

### Labour
- "This activity will mobilize 250 jobs over two years."

### Ownership Structure
