## ESP-05204-02557

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Endesa Facility in Canary Islands, ESP

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 02-02-2021
   - **Details:** Endesa plans to develop renewable hydrogen projects in the Canary Islands, which will include a total of 25MW of electrolysers paired with 59.5MW of renewable capacity, primarily solar. The overall goal is to contribute to the production of green hydrogen as part of a broader strategy to transition to renewable energy sources.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 02-02-2021
   - **Details:** The total investment for the green hydrogen projects across Spain, including the Canary Islands, is more than €2.9 billion. This investment is part of a larger initiative to develop 23 green hydrogen projects powered by nearly 2GW of solar and wind capacity.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 02-02-2021
   - **Details:** Endesa has presented plans to the Spanish government for the development of electrolysers with a total capacity of 340MW across various projects, including those in the Canary Islands. The projects are part of a national hydrogen roadmap aiming for 4GW of installed electrolysis capacity by 2030.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 02-02-2021
   - **Details:** The overall projects in Spain, including those in the Canary Islands, are expected to create 620 jobs during construction and 320 jobs in operations and maintenance over the next 20 years.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 02-02-2021
   - **Details:** Endesa is owned by the Italian utility Enel, indicating that the facility in the Canary Islands is not under exclusive ownership but is part of a larger corporate structure.

This timeline captures the key developments related to the Endesa facility in the Canary Islands, focusing on its technology, investments, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure as of February 2021.
