## ESP-05206-01205

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Naturgy Facility in Meirama, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2021**: Naturgy announced a project to build 38 hydrogen fuel stations across Spain, including one at the Meirama facility. The stations will utilize on-site hydrogen production through an electrolyser or an external plant. The network aims to meet an annual demand of approximately 75,500 kg of hydrogen, sufficient for refueling 13 buses or 100 light vehicles. The project is part of a broader initiative to enhance sustainable mobility and reduce emissions, with a projected operational timeline for 80% of the stations between 2023 and 2024.

- **February 2023**: Naturgy, in collaboration with Repsol and Reganosa, is developing a renewable hydrogen production plant in Meirama. The facility will have an initial electrolysis capacity of 30MW, scalable to 200MW, with the potential to produce over 4,000 tonnes of renewable hydrogen annually in its first phase, and up to 30,000 tonnes per year in total.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2023**: The renewable hydrogen production plant in Meirama is part of a larger investment strategy, with the La Robla plant requiring an investment of €485 million. The Meirama facility's development is expected to enhance the competitiveness of local industries by reducing hydrogen production costs.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2021**: Announcement of the hydrogen fuel station project, with plans for 38 stations across Spain, including Meirama.
- **February 2023**: Announcement of the renewable hydrogen production plant in Meirama, with an initial capacity of 30MW and plans for scalability.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2023**: The development of the renewable hydrogen production plant is expected to create job opportunities in the region, contributing to the decarbonisation of local industries and supporting the energy transition.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2023**: The Meirama facility is part of a joint venture involving Naturgy, Repsol, and Reganosa for the renewable hydrogen production plant, indicating that Naturgy does not exclusively own the facility. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic initiatives related to the Naturgy facility in Meirama, Spain, highlighting its role in advancing hydrogen technology and sustainable energy solutions.
