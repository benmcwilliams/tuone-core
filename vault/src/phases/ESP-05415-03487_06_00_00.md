### Evolutionary Timeline of the Solarcentury Facility in Talayuela, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2017**: Solarcentury, in collaboration with Genia Global Energy and Canopy, announced plans for a 300 MW solar power plant in Talayuela, Extremadura. The project will require approximately 830 hectares and includes a substation and a 400 kV transmission line.
- **August 2018**: The Spanish Ministry of Ecological Transition approved the 300 MW solar project, which is expected to generate around 588.26 GWh annually. The project will utilize tracker technology and polycrystalline modules.
- **October 2018**: Solarcentury sold an 80% stake in the Talayuela project to Encavis, retaining a 20% share and overseeing management. The plant is expected to connect to the grid in the first half of 2020.
- **February 2020**: Prodiel was selected as the EPC contractor for the Talayuela project, which is designed to generate 570 GWh per year with 830,000 panels and 15,000 single-axis trackers.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2017**: The project was estimated to cost around €300 million.
- **October 2018**: The sale of an 80% stake to Encavis indicates significant investment in the project, with Solarcentury retaining a minority stake.
- **February 2020**: Solarcentury secured GBP 47.6 million (USD 61.9 million) in government-guaranteed funding for the Talayuela project from the UK’s export credit agency.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2017**: Memorandum of understanding signed for the development of the 300 MW solar plant.
- **August 2018**: Final approval granted by the Spanish government for the project.
- **October 2018**: Announcement of the sale of an 80% stake to Encavis.
- **Late 2019**: Groundbreaking for the Talayuela project commenced.
- **February 2020**: Construction was underway, with completion expected later in the year.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
