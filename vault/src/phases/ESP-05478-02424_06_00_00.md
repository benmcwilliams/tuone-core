## ESP-05478-02424

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Repsol Facility in Manzanares, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2020**: Repsol began construction of its first solar farm, the Kappa project, with a total capacity of 126 MW, distributed across three plants: two at 45 MW and one at 36 MW.
- **June 21, 2021**: The Kappa solar farm was officially opened, with a total installed capacity of 126.6 MW. As of this date, two plants (Perseo Fotón 1 and 2) were operational with a combined capacity of 90.5 MW, while the third plant (Perseo Fotón 3) was still under development and expected to add 36.1 MW upon completion.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2020**: The Kappa project required a total investment of €100 million.
- **June 2021**: The project was reported to have a total cost of €100 million, reaffirming the initial investment figure.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2020**: Construction of the Kappa solar farm commenced.
- **June 21, 2021**: The Kappa solar farm was officially opened.
- **June 2021**: The first two sections of the Kappa plant were energized, with the third section expected to come online shortly thereafter.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2021**: The construction of the Kappa solar farm created approximately 400 jobs, with more than half of the workers sourced locally from Manzanares and its surroundings.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 28, 2022**: Repsol sold a 49% stake in the Kappa solar plant to Pontegadea, an investment company associated with Amancio Ortega, valuing the asset at €109 million, including project debt. This indicates that Repsol retains 51% ownership of the facility.

### Summary
The Repsol facility in Manzanares, Spain, represents a significant step in the company's transition to renewable energy. The Kappa solar farm, with a total capacity of 126.6 MW, was constructed with an investment of €100 million and officially opened in June 2021. The project has created local jobs and has a mixed ownership structure, with Repsol retaining a majority stake after selling a portion to Pontegadea.
