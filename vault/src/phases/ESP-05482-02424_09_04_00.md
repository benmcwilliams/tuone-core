### Evolutionary Timeline of the Repsol Facility in Petronor Industrial Complex, Spain

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **August 2021:** Repsol produced Spain’s first aviation biofuel from waste at the Petronor Industrial Complex, resulting in a batch of 5,300 tonnes of biojet fuel. This production is part of Repsol's strategy to transform its industrial complexes into multi-energy hubs capable of manufacturing decarbonized products.
   - **July 2021:** Repsol produced its first batch of hydrogen utilizing biomethane as the feedstock, generating 10 tonnes of renewable hydrogen. This hydrogen is intended for the manufacture of fuels such as gasoline, diesel, and kerosene for aviation. The process utilized 500 megawatt hours of biomethane, avoiding approximately 90 tonnes of CO2 emissions.
   - **Future Plans:** Repsol plans to start up a 2.5-megawatt electrolyser at the Petronor refinery in the second half of 2022 to produce green hydrogen, with a goal of achieving 1.9 gigawatts of installed capacity across its portfolio by 2030.
