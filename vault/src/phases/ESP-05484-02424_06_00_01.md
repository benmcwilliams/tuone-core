### Evolutionary Timeline of the Repsol Facility in Valdecaballeros, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/09/2018**: Repsol acquires Valdesolar Hive, a special purpose company, which owns a 264 MW solar power project in Valdecaballeros, Extremadura. The project will utilize 775,750 solar modules and 13,375 trackers, and will include a 400 KV transmission line.
- **14/07/2020**: Construction of the Valdesolar facility begins. It is expected to generate 514,490 MWh annually, enough to power approximately 153,000 households. The facility will consist of 648,000 solar modules and will cover an area equivalent to nearly a thousand football pitches.
- **31/03/2022**: Valdesolar becomes operational in the third quarter of 2021.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12/09/2018**: Repsol invests approximately €210 million in the acquisition of the Valdesolar project. Construction costs are estimated at €164.7 million.
- **14/07/2020**: Repsol announces a total investment of €200 million for the construction of the Valdesolar facility.
- **31/03/2022**: The Renewables Infrastructure Group (TRIG) invests €117 million to acquire a minority stake in Valdesolar, valuing the project at €239 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/09/2018**: Repsol announces the acquisition of the Valdesolar project.
- **14/07/2020**: Construction of the Valdesolar facility officially begins.
- **31/03/2022**: Valdesolar is reported to have been operational since the third quarter of 2021.

#### 4. Labour
- **14/07/2020**: During the construction phase, the Valdesolar project is expected to employ between 300 and 500 workers.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **31/03/2022**: Repsol retains operational control of the Valdesolar facility, even after TRIG acquires a minority stake. The ownership structure indicates that Repsol owns 51% of the facility, while TRIG holds 49%.
