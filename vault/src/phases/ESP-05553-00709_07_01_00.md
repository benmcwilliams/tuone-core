## ESP-05553-00709

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Seat Facility in El Prat De Llobregat, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2022**: Seat announces plans to produce components for electric vehicles (EVs) at its transmission plant in El Prat De Llobregat. From 2025, the facility will manufacture five components for the Volkswagen Group’s planned battery electric vehicle (BEV) small car family: Differentials, pivot bearings, battery housings, battery cooling modules, and aluminium for electric drive systems. Currently, the facility produces two types of manual gearboxes for combustion engine cars, but this production will gradually reduce as the focus shifts to electric components.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2022**: Seat plans to invest several million euros for the conversion of the El Prat facility to accommodate the production of EV components. This investment is part of a larger commitment by Volkswagen, which has allocated 10 billion euros for large-scale electrification projects in Spain, including the conversion of other Seat plants in Martorell and Pamplona.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2022**: Announcement made regarding the future production of EV components at the El Prat facility, with a target start date for production set for 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2022**: The facility currently employs around 900 people. The announcement regarding the shift to EV component production is aimed at stabilizing employment during the transformation process and securing the future of Seat Components.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The article does not specify the ownership structure of the El Prat facility, but it is implied that Seat, as a subsidiary of Volkswagen, has a significant role in the operations and future developments at the plant. 

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic shifts at the Seat facility in El Prat De Llobregat, focusing on its transition towards electric vehicle component production.
