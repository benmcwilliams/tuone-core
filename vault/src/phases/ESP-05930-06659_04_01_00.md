### Evolutionary Timeline of the ThyssenKrupp Uhde Facility in Albacete, Spain

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **February 2024:** ThyssenKrupp Uhde is contracted by Hive Energy to deliver a Pre-FEED (Front End Engineering Design) study to support the development of its first green hydrogen and ammonia production plant in Albacete, Spain. The facility is part of the Hive H2 Albamed project, which aims to produce renewable hydrogen using up to 9 GW of solar and wind energy and 4 GW of electrolysis capacity by 2030.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **February 2024:** The total investment for the Hive H2 Albamed project is estimated at more than €10 billion ($10.7 billion). This investment is tied to the overall development of the hydrogen hub, which will include the ThyssenKrupp Uhde facility.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **February 2024:** The Hive H2 Albamed project is set to be finalized in four phases. The first phase includes the construction of a facility with 1.1 GW of PV, 284 MW of wind, and 800 MW of electrolysis capacity, expected to come online in the first half of 2028. A final investment decision (FID) for this phase is planned by the end of 2025.

4. **Labour:**
   - **February 2024:** As of April 2024, it was reported that a hundred people were already working on the infrastructure for the project, indicating job creation associated with the development of the facility.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **February 2024:** The facility is part of a partnership involving Hive Energy and Spanish gas supplier Enagás, indicating that ThyssenKrupp Uhde is not the exclusive owner of the facility but is involved in a collaborative development effort.
