## ESP-05957-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Andalucía, Spain

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** August 2021
   - **Details:** Iberdrola has commissioned the Herrera II wind complex, which consists of three wind farms: La Huesca, Valdesantos, and Orbanejoa. The total installed capacity is 63 MW, with 14 wind turbines. The facility is capable of generating enough electricity to supply approximately 60,000 households annually and will prevent the release of 50,000 tons of CO2 per year. The wind turbines installed are the SG 4.5-145, which are the largest and most powerful onshore wind turbines in Spain, with a unit power of 4.5 MW.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** August 2021
   - **Details:** Iberdrola invested €70 million in the development of the Herrera II wind complex. The company plans to invest over €2 billion in developing more than 2,400 MW of renewable energy in the region by 2025.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** August 2021
   - **Details:** The Herrera II wind complex has been commissioned, marking a significant milestone in Iberdrola's renewable energy projects in Spain. The company is also developing additional renewable projects in the region, including various wind and photovoltaic installations.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** August 2021
   - **Details:** The development of the Herrera II wind complex created approximately 800 jobs during the construction phase. Additionally, the investment plan for renewable energy in the region is expected to stimulate industry and create jobs for around 18,000 people.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** August 2021
   - **Details:** Iberdrola owns the Herrera II wind complex exclusively, as there is no mention of joint ventures or partnerships in the articles.
