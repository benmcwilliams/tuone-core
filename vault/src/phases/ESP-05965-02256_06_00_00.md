### Evolutionary Timeline of the Gransolar Facility in Port of Almería, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2021**: Gransolar announced plans to build a large-scale green hydrogen production plant in the Port of Almería. The facility will produce hydrogen from seawater, powered by a 30 MW solar plant and a 20 MWh storage system with a 4-hour autonomy. The main electrolyzer will have an installed capacity of 20 MW, with an estimated production of 1,000 tons of hydrogen per year. The facility will utilize double-reverse osmosis treatment followed by electrolysis through proton exchange membrane (PEM) and secondary electrolysis of concentrated brine.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2021**: The project requires an investment of €80.5 million. Gransolar has confirmed interest and commitment from the Almería City Council and multiple companies from various sectors, although specific details on these partnerships were not disclosed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2021**: The project is scheduled to be built by the end of 2024. This timeline indicates that construction is expected to commence soon after the announcement, with a focus on completing the facility within the specified timeframe.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2021**: While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned, the establishment of the facility is expected to create jobs related to the construction and operation of the hydrogen production plant, contributing to local employment in the Almería region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2021**: The articles do not specify whether Gransolar will have exclusive ownership of the facility or if it will be a joint venture. However, it is indicated that Gransolar is the primary developer of the project.
