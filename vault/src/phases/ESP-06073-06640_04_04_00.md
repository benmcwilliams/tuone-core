### Evolutionary Timeline of the Soto Solar España Facility in Saceruela, ESP

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2021**: Soto Solar España is developing the Erasmo project, which will be the largest photovoltaic park in Spain with an installed power capacity of 1,000 MW. The facility is designed to generate more than 2,000 GWh/year, sufficient to power over 200,000 households. The project will also include an energy storage facility with a capacity of 80 MW, utilizing lithium-nickel-manganese-cobalt (NMC) batteries, and a green hydrogen production facility with an electrolysis power of about 100 MW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2021**: The project has secured the necessary land for construction and is expected to benefit from EU recovery funds aimed at facilitating the energy transition, as it participated in two Expressions of Interest published by the Ministry of the Ecological Transition and the Demographic Challenge (MITECO) in February 2021 and December 2020.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: Soto Solar has submitted the project proposal to MITECO, with production expected to start in 2023 or 2024. The project has been under development since 2019 and has completed technical and environmental studies.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2021**: The project is anticipated to create jobs, although specific numbers related to job creation or loss have not been detailed in the article.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **2019**: Soto Solar, founded by Stecc and Hartenlust Solar, sold a 50% stake in its business for over 100 million euros to a group of international investors. This indicates that Soto Solar does not have exclusive ownership of the facility, as it operates as a joint venture following the investment.
