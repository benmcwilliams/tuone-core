### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Torrecillas de la Tiesa, ESP

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **07-06-2019**: Iberdrola announced plans for a 590 MW solar park named the Francisco Pizarro project, to be built on a 1,300-hectare site in Torrecillas de la Tiesa and Aldeacentenera, Extremadura. The project is set to be commissioned in 2022 and will contribute to Iberdrola's existing 2 GW solar project portfolio in Spain.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07-06-2019**: The Francisco Pizarro project is estimated to require an investment of approximately €300 million. This investment is part of Iberdrola's broader strategy to develop 10 GW of renewable energy capacity by 2030.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **10-06-2019**: Iberdrola's Francisco Pizarro project is described as awaiting ministerial approval for construction. The project is expected to create up to 1,000 jobs and is projected to be completed by 2022.
- **21-06-2019**: The project is highlighted as part of Iberdrola's ongoing efforts to expand its solar capacity, alongside other projects in Extremadura and Castilla-La Mancha.

#### 4. Labour
- **10-06-2019**: The Francisco Pizarro project is anticipated to create up to 1,000 jobs during its construction phase.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify any joint ventures or partnerships for the Francisco Pizarro project, indicating that Iberdrola likely holds full ownership of the facility.

### Summary
The Iberdrola facility in Torrecillas de la Tiesa, Spain, is set to be a significant solar power project with a capacity of 590 MW, requiring an investment of €300 million and expected to create around 1,000 jobs. The project is part of Iberdrola's strategy to enhance its renewable energy portfolio and is anticipated to be operational by 2022, with full ownership by Iberdrola.
