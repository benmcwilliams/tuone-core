### Volkswagen Facility in Sagunto, ESP - Evolutionary Timeline

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **March 2023:** Volkswagen Group’s PowerCo broke ground for the construction of its second cell factory, known as Gigafactory Valencia, located in Sagunto, 30 km north of Valencia. The facility is designed to produce unified battery cells with an initial production capacity of 40 GWh, which has the potential to be expanded to 60 GWh in the future. The factory will be powered entirely by green electricity generated from solar and wind sources.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **March 2023:** The Gigafactory Valencia is part of a €10-billion investment program called “Future: Fast Forward,” which includes the electrification of Volkswagen's car factories in Martorell and Pamplona. This program involves collaboration with PowerCo, SEAT, and 49 partners.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **March 2023:** Groundbreaking for the construction of the Gigafactory Valencia was officially announced. The factory is expected to start production in 2026.

4. **Labour:**
   - **March 2023:** The facility is projected to directly employ more than 3,000 people, with the potential for up to 30,000 indirect jobs to be created at suppliers and partners in Spain.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **March 2023:** PowerCo, a separate battery company established by the Volkswagen Group, is responsible for the Gigafactory. The ownership structure indicates that PowerCo operates under the Volkswagen Group, but specific details on exclusive ownership or joint ventures were not provided in the article.
