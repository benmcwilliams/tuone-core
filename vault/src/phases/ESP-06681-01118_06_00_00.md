### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Núñez de Balboa, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05-09-2019**: Iberdrola is developing the 500 MW Núñez de Balboa solar plant, which is billed as Europe’s largest solar plant. The project includes advanced technology such as bifacial panels and a battery system.
- **09-04-2020**: The Núñez de Balboa solar park has started commercial operations after completing construction in December 2019.
- **21-05-2021**: Iberdrola has built apiaries at the Núñez de Balboa solar power plant, hosting 105 beehives to promote ecological stability and enhance crop yields in surrounding areas.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05-09-2019**: The Núñez de Balboa project is part of a €300 million investment by Iberdrola, which received project financing from the European Investment Bank and Spain’s Instituto de Crédito Oficial.
- **03-04-2020**: Despite the COVID-19 pandemic, Iberdrola announced plans to invest €10 billion across its business in 2020, which includes renewable projects like Núñez de Balboa.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05-09-2019**: Iberdrola submits plans for the Núñez de Balboa solar plant, which is part of a larger 2.2 GW project pipeline in Spain.
- **09-04-2020**: The Núñez de Balboa solar park begins commercial operations.
- **14-04-2020**: The facility is officially connected to the Spanish grid on 6 April 2020, marking a significant milestone in its operational status.

#### 4. Labour
- **03-04-2020**: During the COVID-19 pandemic, Iberdrola temporarily halted construction on several projects, including Núñez de Balboa, but did not foresee long-term impacts on its renewable investment plans, indicating a focus on job preservation.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **21-05-2021**: The Núñez de Balboa project is developed by Iberdrola in collaboration with Ecoenergías del Guadiana, indicating a joint venture structure for this facility.
