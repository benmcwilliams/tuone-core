### Evolutionary Timeline of the ACS Facility in Chiprana, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **30-11-2017**: ACS announced plans to build a 549 MW photovoltaic (PV) solar facility on 2,369 hectares of land across the municipalities of Escatrón and Chiprana. This facility is set to be Europe’s largest solar power plant upon completion, consisting of 12 substations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **30-11-2017**: The project is estimated to cost €330 million. The Spanish government classified the investment as “of public interest for the region,” which allows for expedited administrative procedures for project authorization.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **30-11-2017**: The project was selected in Spain’s second renewable energy auction, where 3.9 GW of solar power was allocated. ACS secured the largest share of solar projects in this auction, totaling around 1.55 GW of assigned capacity. The facility is required to start delivering power by 2020.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility will be owned by special purpose vehicles under the control of Cobras Concesiones SL, which is a unit of Cobra, the renewable energy arm of ACS. This indicates that ACS has a controlling interest in the facility through its subsidiary.
