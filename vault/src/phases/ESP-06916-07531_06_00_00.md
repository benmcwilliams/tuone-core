### Evolutionary Timeline of the ACS Facility in Escatrón, Spain

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** November 2017
   - **Details:** ACS plans to build a 549 MW photovoltaic (PV) facility on 2,369 hectares of land across Escatrón and Chiprana. The project will consist of 12 substations and aims to be the largest solar power plant in Europe upon completion.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** November 2017
   - **Details:** The total investment for the facility is €330 million. The project has been classified as “of public interest for the region” by the Government of Aragón, which will expedite the administrative procedures for its authorization.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** November 2017
   - **Details:** The project was selected in Spain’s second renewable energy auction, where 3.9 GW of solar power was allocated. The facility is required to start delivering power by 2020.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** Not specified in the article.
   - **Details:** The article does not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss related to the facility.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** November 2017
   - **Details:** The facility will be owned by special purpose vehicles under the control of Cobras Concesiones SL, which is a unit of Cobra, the renewable energy arm of ACS. This indicates that ACS has a controlling interest in the facility through its subsidiary.
