### Evolutionary Timeline of the Juwi Facility in Mula, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2012**: The project was launched by Juwi, aiming to develop a 450 MW solar photovoltaic (PV) plant on a 1,088-hectare site in Mula, Murcia.
- **07-06-2017**: The project received approval from Spain’s National Commission on Financial Markets and Competition (CNMC) for the 450 MW solar project.
- **02-10-2017**: Spain’s Ministry of Energy granted final approval for Juwi to build the 450 MW solar capacity, including a substation and a transmission line to connect to the national grid.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2016 - 2017**: Juwi implemented actions to secure financial backing for the project, although specific investment amounts were not disclosed.
- **2013**: The government of Murcia designated the project as a "Strategic Project," indicating its potential economic benefits for the region.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Mid-2015**: The project received initial approval from Spain’s Ministry of Environmental Affairs.
- **07-06-2017**: CNMC approved the project after reviewing Juwi's financial capacity.
- **02-10-2017**: Final approval was granted by the Ministry of Energy, allowing construction to proceed, pending further authorizations.

#### 4. Labour
- **07-06-2017**: It was estimated that around 200 jobs would be created during the design, promotion, and engineering phases, with an additional 1,000 jobs during the construction phase.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **02-10-2017**: The project is owned by Promosolar Juwi 17, S.L.U., a special purpose vehicle created by Juwi, indicating that Juwi has a significant ownership stake in the facility, although the exact ownership structure (full ownership or joint venture) is not explicitly stated in the articles.
