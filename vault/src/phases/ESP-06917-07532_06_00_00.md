### Evolutionary Timeline of the ACS Group Facility in Mula, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05-09-2018**: The Mula PV plant, built by ACS Group and its subsidiary Cobra, is announced as Europe's largest PV project with a capacity of 500MW. The facility is located in the municipality of Mula, covering 1,000 hectares, and is expected to produce over 750 million kWh of electricity annually, enough to supply approximately 450,000 people. The project includes the installation of 238 central PV inverters and 167MW of multicrystalline PV modules from Trina Solar.

- **2019**: The facility begins commercial operations with an installed capacity of 494MWp, making it one of the largest single-site solar projects in Europe.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **06-09-2023**: Qualitas Energy raises €280 million to refinance debts associated with the Mula PV plant. This funding is recognized as 'green loans' and is aimed at supporting renewable power generation. The refinancing involves collaboration with several banks and financial advisors, indicating ongoing financial support for the facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05-09-2018**: Announcement of the Mula PV plant project, with expectations for it to be fully operational by the end of 2019.
- **2019**: The facility officially begins commercial operations.
- **06-09-2023**: Refinancing of debts is completed, marking a significant milestone for the project.
