### Evolutionary Timeline of the Qualitas Energy Facility in Mula, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2019**: The Mula photovoltaic solar plant began commercial operations with an installed capacity of **494 MWp**, making it one of the largest single-site solar projects in Europe.
- **09-02-2023**: Qualitas Energy signed a **10-year power purchase agreement (PPA)** for the output from the Mula solar plant, which will sell energy to a global energy company.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05-09-2023**: Qualitas Energy closed a refinancing operation worth approximately **€280 million** for the Mula PV plant. This refinancing was classified as a "green" loan under the Green Loan Principles, supporting renewable power generation.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **31-12-2024**: Qualitas Energy announced the sale of its **100% equity interest** in the Mula solar PV plant to the Spanish subsidiary of China Three Gorges. The transaction is expected to be completed in the **first quarter of 2025**.

#### 4. Labour
- There are no specific mentions of job creation or job loss associated with the investments or expansions at the Mula facility in the provided articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **2019 - 2024**: The Mula solar plant was owned jointly by **Qualitas Energy** and **Northleaf Capital Partners**. Following the sale in 2024, the facility will be fully owned by the **Spanish subsidiary of China Three Gorges**.
