## ESP-06917-08129

### Evolutionary Timeline of Trina Solar Limited Facility in Mula, ESP

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 10-08-2018
   - **Details:** Trina Solar Limited announced the supply of 167 MW worth of TSM-PE14H multicrystalline PV modules to a project in Mula, Murcia, which is set to become Spain's and Europe's largest PV plant with a total capacity of 500 MW. The supply deal includes 496,000 modules with a reported power output of 340W. The supply is scheduled for the second half of 2018 and the first quarter of 2019.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 10-08-2018
   - **Details:** The project is being developed without government incentives, as Spain had closed its feed-in-tariff scheme for large-scale solar by the time the project was launched in 2012. The project is backed by Promosolar Juwi 17, S.L.U., a special purpose vehicle authorized to build the plant.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 10-08-2018
   - **Details:** The project was initially approved by Spain's National Commission on Financial Markets and Competition (CNMC) in June 2015, with the Ministry of Environmental Affairs giving the green light in July 2015. The construction of the plant and a substation on a 1,088-hectare site in Mula is part of the project, which also includes building a transmission line to connect to the national grid operator, Red Eléctrica de España.

4. **Labour:**

   - **Details:** The article does not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss related to the facility.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 10-08-2018
