### Evolutionary Timeline of Acciona Energy Facility in Almendralejo, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2021**: Construction of the 125 MW solar plant, named Extremadura I, II, and III, began in Almendralejo, Badajoz.
- **January 2023**: The facility was commissioned, marking it as Acciona's largest photovoltaic (PV) project in Spain. The plant consists of over 230,000 solar panels and is expected to generate approximately 257 GWh of clean electricity annually, enough to power around 65,000 homes and avoid the emission of about 112,000 tons of CO2 per year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2023**: Acciona invested approximately €80 million (around $87 million) in the construction of the Extremadura I, II, and III solar complex.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2021**: Construction of the solar plant commenced.
- **January 2023**: The 125 MW solar plant was officially commissioned.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2023**: As part of the 'Colabora Almendralejo' project, Acciona initiated a program to connect unemployed young people with local companies, providing free training in sustainability and innovation. A total of 25 young people are expected to benefit from this initiative.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned exclusively by Acciona Energía, as there is no mention of joint ventures or partnerships in the provided articles.
