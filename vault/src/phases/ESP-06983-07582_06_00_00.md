## ESP-06983-07582

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Atlantic Copper Facility in Punta del Sebo, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/2024**: Atlantic Copper and Alter Enersun announced an agreement to build a 30 MWp photovoltaic plant for self-consumption. The plant is expected to produce approximately 53 GWh of electricity annually, allowing Atlantic Copper to cover part of its energy demand with 100% renewable sources. This initiative aims to enhance Atlantic Copper's decarbonization and energy efficiency objectives, increasing the percentage of low-carbon electricity consumption from 66% to 82%.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12/2024**: The construction of the photovoltaic plant involves an investment of 16 million euros by Alter Enersun. This investment is part of a long-term strategy to secure fixed-price electricity supply contracts, reducing dependence on the volatile electricity market.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2024**: The photovoltaic plant is planned to be operational by 2026. This project represents one of the significant industrial self-consumption developments in Spain.

#### 4. Labour
- **12/2024**: Atlantic Copper generates around 2,500 direct, indirect, and induced jobs, indicating the facility's substantial impact on local employment. The new photovoltaic plant is expected to further support the company's sustainability goals, potentially leading to job stability or growth in the renewable energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **12/2024**: Atlantic Copper SLU is a Huelva-based company and the largest producer of copper and sulphuric acid in Spain. The facility is not exclusively owned by Atlantic Copper, as the photovoltaic plant is a joint venture with Alter Enersun, which is owned by CL Grupo Industrial and Senergy Group (Seu Global Energía).
