### Evolutionary Timeline of the Endesa Facility in Balearic Islands, ESP

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** February 2021
   - **Details:** Endesa plans to develop renewable hydrogen projects in the Balearic Islands, which will include a total of 25MW of electrolysers paired with 59.5MW of renewable capacity, primarily solar. The overall goal is to produce green hydrogen as part of a broader strategy to transition to renewable energy sources.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** February 2021
   - **Details:** The total investment for the green hydrogen projects across Spain, including the Balearic Islands, is more than €2.9 billion. This investment is part of a larger initiative to develop 23 green hydrogen projects powered by nearly 2GW of solar and wind capacity.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** February 2021
   - **Details:** Endesa has presented plans to the Spanish government for the development of electrolysers with a total capacity of 340MW across various projects, including those in the Balearic Islands. The projects are part of a national hydrogen roadmap aiming for 4GW of installed electrolysis capacity by 2030.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** February 2021
   - **Details:** The development of these hydrogen projects is expected to create 620 jobs during the construction phase and 320 jobs in operations and maintenance over the next 20 years across all projects, including those in the Balearic Islands.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** February 2021
   - **Details:** Endesa is owned by the Italian utility Enel, indicating that the facility in the Balearic Islands is not under exclusive ownership but is part of a larger corporate structure.

This timeline captures the key developments related to the Endesa facility in the Balearic Islands, focusing on its technology, investments, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure as of February 2021.
