## ESP-07000-02167

### Evolutionary Timeline of the BayWa Facility in Alcalá de Guadaíra, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2018**: BayWa r.e. announced plans for a 175 MW solar power plant named Don Rodrigo in Alcalá de Guadaíra. The facility is designed to include a 220 kV transmission line and is expected to generate around 300 GWh per year, covering the annual consumption of approximately 93,000 households.
- **January 2019**: The facility was reported to have been completed and operational, marking its status as a fully functional solar power plant.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2018**: The project required an investment of around €102 million. The facility was granted a fiscal incentive and was exempt from 95% of the local construction tax.
- **April 2018**: BayWa secured a 15-year Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) with Norway-based Statkraft for the sale of power generated by the facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2018**: An agreement was signed between BayWa and the municipality of Alcalá de Guadaíra for the construction of the solar facility.
- **April 2018**: Preliminary work on the facility began, with construction and grid-connection expected to be finalized by the end of the year.
- **January 2019**: The facility was reported as completed and operational.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2018**: The project was expected to create around 275 direct and indirect jobs during its construction phase, with a commitment to hire mainly local workforce.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2019**: BayWa completed the construction of the Don Rodrigo solar plant and subsequently announced its sale to Meag, an asset management company belonging to Munich Re, indicating that BayWa did not retain full ownership of the facility post-completion.
