### Evolutionary Timeline of the CTME Facility in Miranda de Ebro, ESP

1. **Technology and Operations**
   - **Date**: 08-03-2021
   - **Details**: The CTME facility is involved in a pioneering project aimed at developing comprehensive technology for recycling components of old wind turbines. This includes a cutting system for on-site material recycling and an advanced recycling system for composite materials, particularly focusing on wind turbine blades and nacelle lids. The project anticipates the recycling of approximately 21,000 tons of blades from around 3,600 wind turbines that are being decommissioned.

2. **Investment and Development**
   - **Date**: 08-03-2021
   - **Details**: The project has a total budget of €9.77 million, which is being developed through a public-private partnership consortium involving the Regional Energy Agency of Castilla y León (EREN), Fundación Patrimonio Natural de Castilla y León (FPNCYL), and companies like Reciclalia SL. The project has been submitted to the Government of Spain for funding under the call for Expressions of Interest aimed at promoting the circular economy.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**
   - **Date**: 08-03-2021
   - **Details**: The project has been officially announced and is in the planning stages, with the intention to start up a plant in the Environmental Business Park of Soria, followed by a second component transformation plant. The project is part of a broader initiative to enhance the recycling capabilities for wind turbine components in Castilla y León and potentially across Spain and the EU.
