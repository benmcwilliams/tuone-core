### Evolutionary Timeline of the Navantia-Windar Facility in Puerto Real, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2022**: Navantia-Windar announced plans to manufacture 62 wind turbine jacket foundations for the Dieppe le Tréport wind farm offshore France. The project is significant as it represents the largest contract awarded to Navantia Seanergies and Windar to date. The jackets will weigh around 1,000 tonnes each. Additionally, Navantia Seanergies will construct two substation jackets in Puerto Real, one for the Dieppe le Tréport wind farm and another for the Îles d’Yeu et Noirmoutier.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2022**: The capacity reservation agreement with Ocean Winds indicates a significant investment in the facility, although specific financial figures are not disclosed. The fabrication works are set to begin in the first quarter of 2023 and will last approximately two years.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2022**: The announcement of the capacity reservation agreement marks a key milestone for the facility, with fabrication works scheduled to commence in Q1 2023. The project is expected to provide sustainable electricity to approximately 850,000 people by 2026.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2022**: The project is expected to create additional employment opportunities for Windar Renovables, which will manufacture 248 piles at its facilities in Avilés as part of the contract.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2022**: The facility is operated by the Navantia-Windar consortium, but specific details regarding exclusive ownership or joint ventures are not provided in the article. However, it is noted that the facility is part of a larger collaboration with Ocean Winds, which is a joint venture involving multiple companies.
