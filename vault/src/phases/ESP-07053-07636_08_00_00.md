### Evolutionary Timeline of the Capital Energy Facility in Arredondo, ESP

1. **Technology and Operations**
   - **March 2021**: Capital Energy announced the development of the Ribota wind farm, which will have an installed power capacity of 51 megawatts (MW). The facility will consist of 12 wind turbines capable of supplying clean energy to over 56,000 homes and will prevent the emission of approximately 54,300 tons of CO2 annually.

2. **Investment and Development**
   - **March 2021**: The development of the Ribota wind farm involves an investment of around 36 million euros. This investment is part of Capital Energy's broader strategy, which includes 17 renewable energy projects in Cantabria with a combined capacity of nearly 690 MW and a total investment of approximately 650 million euros.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**
   - **March 2021**: The Industry and Energy Area of the Government Delegation in Cantabria submitted the request for prior administrative authorization for the Ribota wind farm, marking a significant milestone in the project's development.

4. **Labour**
   - **March 2021**: The construction of the Ribota wind farm is expected to create approximately 295 jobs during its construction phase, with 10 jobs anticipated for the operational phase. Additionally, the project will have an annual economic impact on local finances of around 266,500 euros.

5. **Ownership Structure**
   - **March 2021**: Capital Energy is the sole owner of the Ribota wind farm, as it is part of the company's portfolio of renewable energy projects in the region. The facility will share electricity evacuation infrastructures with another park, Garma Blanca, which is also owned by Capital Energy.
