## ESP-07085-02520

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fotowatio Facility in Trujillo, ESP

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2018**: Fotowatio Renewable Ventures (FRV) announced the construction of the La Solanilla Solar Farm, a 50 MW photovoltaic (PV) facility. The plant will consist of 151,500 PV modules of 330 Wp, mounted on a horizontal single-axis solar tracking system, and will include 27 inverters. The facility will cover an area of 161.2 hectares and is expected to generate approximately 103 GWh of clean energy annually, enough to supply electricity to 25,000 homes.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2018**: FRV secured a financing agreement with Banco Sabadell for the construction of the La Solanilla Solar Farm. While the specific amount of financing was not disclosed, this agreement is crucial for the project's development and reflects FRV's commitment to renewable energy initiatives.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2018**: FRV confirmed that construction of the La Solanilla Solar Farm would begin by the end of 2018. The project was selected by the Spanish government in a previous auction and is required to start delivering power by the end of 2019.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2018**: The construction phase of the La Solanilla Solar Farm is expected to create approximately 300 jobs locally. Once operational, the facility will maintain five permanent jobs.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The facility is owned by Fotowatio Renewable Ventures (FRV), which is a unit of UAE-based Abdul Latif Jameel Energy. There is no indication of joint ownership or partnerships mentioned in the articles, suggesting that FRV has full ownership of the facility.
