### Evolutionary Timeline of the Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners Facility in Monegros, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date**: 09-07-2020
  - **Details**: The Monegros facility consists of 12 wind farms with a total production capacity of 487 MW. The facility will deploy 129 GE 3.8-130 wind turbines, supplied and installed by GE Renewable Energy. The wind farms are expected to generate 12 terawatt-hours of renewable power under power purchase agreements (PPAs) over a 10-year period from commercial operations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date**: 09-07-2020
  - **Details**: Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (CIP) closed a non-recourse long-term project financing credit facility for the Monegros portfolio, with around €380 million committed by six Spanish and European banks. The financing has been validated as a Green Loan under the Green Loan Principles 2018. The total investment is part of the €3.5 billion energy infrastructure fund, Copenhagen Infrastructure 3.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date**: 09-07-2020
  - **Details**: The Monegros wind farms are on track to reach commercial operation over the next 12 months, with first power already achieved. The project remains on time and on budget despite challenges posed by Covid-19.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date**: 09-07-2020
  - **Details**: The article does not specify exact job creation or job loss figures related to the Monegros facility. However, it mentions that construction management is provided by Blue Power Partners and balance of plant works are executed by leading Spanish contractors, indicating potential job creation in the local construction sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date**: 09-07-2020
  - **Details**: The Monegros portfolio was acquired by CIP in the development phase through two transactions in 2019 from Forestalia Renovables. The facility is managed in cooperation with Forestalia, indicating a joint venture or partnership structure rather than exclusive ownership by CIP.
