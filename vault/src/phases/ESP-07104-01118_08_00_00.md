## ESP-07104-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Aragón, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2021**: Iberdrola commissioned the Herrera II wind complex in Castilla y León, which includes three farms (La Huesca, Valdesantos, and Orbanejoa) with a total installed capacity of 63 MW, consisting of 14 wind turbines. The facility is designed to generate enough electricity to supply approximately 60,000 households annually and prevent the release of 50,000 tons of CO2 per year. The wind turbines installed are the SG 4.5-145, which are the largest onshore wind turbines in Spain, with a unit power of 4.5 MW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2021**: Iberdrola invested €70 million in the development of the Herrera II wind complex. The company plans to invest over €2 billion in developing more than 2,400 MW of renewable energy in the region by 2025.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2021**: The Herrera II wind complex was commissioned, marking a significant milestone in Iberdrola's renewable energy projects in Spain. The company has also announced plans for additional renewable projects in the region, including wind and photovoltaic installations.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2021**: The development of the Herrera II wind complex created approximately 800 jobs during the construction phase, with a focus on employing local companies for civil works.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2023**: Iberdrola sold a 49% stake in its 1.27 GW renewable energy portfolio, which includes assets in Aragón, to Norges Bank Investment Management (NBIM). Despite this sale, Iberdrola retains a 51% stake, maintaining control and management of the assets, and will continue to provide operations and maintenance services for the portfolio.
