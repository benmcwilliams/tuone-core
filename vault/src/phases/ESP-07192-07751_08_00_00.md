### Evolutionary Timeline of the EnergyLoop Facility in Cortes, ESP

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Facility Purpose:** EnergyLoop is established to recycle wind turbine blades and manage waste components from wind farms.
   - **Production Capacity:** The facility is designed to handle the recycling of blades from 139 wind turbines, which includes 417 blades and over 800 tons of material.
   - **Operational Status:** Civil works on the facility have been completed, and it is expected to begin operations in early 2025.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Investment Background:** EnergyLoop was created in 2022 through a collaboration between Iberdrola Ventures Perseo and FCC Ámbito, focusing on innovative and sustainable waste management solutions.
   - **Grants and Subsidies:** The wind farms (La Isabela and Molar de Molinar) from which the blades will be sourced received grants from the Circular Repowering programme, financed by the Recovery and Resilience Mechanism.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Contract Award:** On December 13, 2024, Iberdrola awarded EnergyLoop the contract for recycling the blades from its repowering project in Spain.
   - **Operational Timeline:** The facility is set to start operations in early 2025.

4. **Labour:**

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Ownership Details:** EnergyLoop is a joint venture created by Iberdrola Ventures Perseo and FCC Ámbito, indicating that it is not solely owned by one entity but rather a collaboration between the two companies.
