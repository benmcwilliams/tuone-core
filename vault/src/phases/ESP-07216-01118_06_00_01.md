### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Caceres, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2019**: Iberdrola announced plans to build three solar plants in the Extremadura region, including two 50MW projects (Majada Alta and Santa Antonio) in Cedillo, Caceres, and an additional facility with a capacity of up to 300MW. The total combined capacity of these projects is 400MW, with the two Cedillo plants consisting of 300,000 solar panels. These plants are expected to be operational by 2020.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2021**: The Francisco Pizarro project, which is part of Iberdrola's developments in Caceres, is a €300 million investment. This project is expected to create significant employment opportunities, contributing to the local economy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2019**: Announcement of the construction of the two 50MW solar plants (Majada Alta and Santa Antonio) and the up to 300MW facility in Cedillo, Caceres.
- **2020**: The two Cedillo plants are expected to become operational.
- **2022**: The Francisco Pizarro project, the largest PV plant planned for Europe, is set to be operational. It will supply renewable electricity under a 10-year power purchase agreement (PPA) with Danone starting in April 2022.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2019**: The construction of the two Cedillo plants is expected to involve up to 200 workers.
- **January 2021**: The Francisco Pizarro project is projected to contribute to the employment of more than 1,200 workers.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: Iberdrola owns the facility exclusively, as indicated by the announcements and developments related to the projects.
