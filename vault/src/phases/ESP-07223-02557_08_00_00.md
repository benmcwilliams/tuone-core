### Evolutionary Timeline of the Endesa Facility in Aldeavieja, Spain

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **July 2024:** Endesa begins the largest repowering of the Aldeavieja wind farm, which involves replacing 22 existing wind turbines (each with 660 kW capacity, totaling 14.52 MW) with 4 new generation turbines (each with 6 MW capacity, totaling 24 MW). This upgrade is expected to nearly double the annual production from 32.5 GWh to 64.1 GWh.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **July 2024:** The total investment for the repowering project is €34.1 million, with a maximum aid of €6.51 million granted by the Institute for Energy Diversification and Saving (IDAE) under the EU's Recovery, Transformation and Resilience Plan.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **July 2024:** Announcement of the start of repowering works at the Aldeavieja wind farm.
   - **October 2025:** The facility is expected to begin its testing phase, with full operation anticipated by the end of the same month.

4. **Labour:**
   - **July 2024:** Approximately 50 specialized workers will be involved in the repowering project, with a focus on hiring local labor. Endesa will also conduct training courses in operation and maintenance for local personnel to prepare them for future roles in the facility.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - Endesa operates the facility through its renewable energy subsidiary, Enel Green Power Spain. The ownership structure is not explicitly stated as a joint venture or exclusive ownership, but it is implied that Endesa has significant control over the facility's operations through its subsidiary.
