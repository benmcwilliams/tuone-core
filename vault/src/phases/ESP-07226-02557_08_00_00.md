### Evolutionary Timeline of the Endesa Facility in Campillo de Altobuey, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2021**: Endesa, through its renewable energy unit Enel Green Power España (EGPE), began construction of the Campillo Wind Cluster, which consists of three wind farms: Campillo I, II, and III. 
  - **Campillo I**: Installed capacity of approximately 76.8 MW.
  - **Campillo II and III**: Each with an installed capacity of 91.2 MW.
  - **Total Production**: The three facilities are expected to produce approximately 660 GWh per year, significantly exceeding the annual consumption of a city like Cuenca.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2021**: Endesa announced an investment of around 256 million euros for the construction of the Campillo Wind Cluster.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2021**: Construction of the Campillo Wind Cluster commenced. The project includes the installation of four electrical substations and three high-voltage lines. 

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2021**: The construction of the wind farms is expected to employ around 350 people, primarily from the local area, in alignment with Endesa's Shared Value Creation plan.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Endesa, through its subsidiary Enel Green Power España, is responsible for the construction and operation of the Campillo Wind Cluster, indicating full ownership of the facility.
