### Evolutionary Timeline of the Enel Facility in Motilla del Palancar, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **02-10-2019**: Enel Green Power began construction of the 51MW Cuenca wind farm in Motilla del Palancar, featuring 17 turbines, each with a capacity of 3MW. The facility is expected to generate approximately 144 GWh of electricity annually.
- **18-08-2020**: The wind farm was connected to Spain's main grid, confirming its operational capacity to generate over 144 GWh per year, which will offset around 95,000 tonnes of carbon emissions annually.
- **19-11-2021**: The facility was officially grid-connected, reaffirming its production capacity and environmental benefits.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **02-10-2019**: The total investment for the wind farm was reported at €55 million.
- **18-08-2020**: The investment for the project was updated to €57 million ($68 million), as part of Enel's broader strategy to decarbonize its generation mix by 2050, with a total investment plan of €3.8 billion ($4.5 billion) to achieve 10.2 GW of renewable energy capacity by 2022.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **02-10-2019**: Announcement of the start of construction for the 51MW wind farm.
- **18-08-2020**: Confirmation of the wind farm's connection to the national grid.
- **19-11-2021**: Official announcement of the facility being grid-connected and operational.

#### 4. Labour
- **18-08-2020**: Enel implemented a Creating Shared Value (CSV) plan, which included a 90-hour training course on maintenance and operation of renewable energy plants, attended by 17 local participants. The plan also aimed to improve local employability and included commitments to hire local labor and services during construction.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned by Enel Green Power, which operates through its Spanish subsidiary, Endesa. There is no indication of joint ownership or partnerships mentioned in the articles, suggesting that Enel has full ownership of the facility.
