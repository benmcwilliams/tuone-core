### Evolutionary Timeline of the Nordex Facility in Motilla del Palancar, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2020**: Nordex begins construction of its first Spanish concrete tower factory in Motilla del Palancar, designed to manufacture two concrete units each week. The facility will produce 20-metre long segments for 120-metre towers.
- **August 2020**: Operations at the factory are scheduled to start. The facility is expected to supply concrete towers for the Gecama wind farm and other projects in Spain.
- **December 2020**: The factory officially starts production, capable of producing 50 concrete tower segments per week, equating to two towers with hub heights of 120 metres. The facility is designed to be mobile, allowing it to be relocated closer to future wind farm projects.
- **April 2021**: The facility continues to supply concrete towers for various wind projects, including an undisclosed 50 MW project in northern Spain, utilizing the concrete towers manufactured in Motilla del Palancar.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2020**: The construction of the facility represents a significant investment by Nordex in the region, aimed at reducing logistics costs by manufacturing towers closer to wind farm sites.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2020**: Groundbreaking for the facility.
- **August 2020**: Expected start of operations.
- **December 2020**: Official commencement of production at the factory.
- **April 2021**: Ongoing supply of concrete towers for various wind projects.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2020**: The facility is expected to create around 300 jobs directly, with an additional 200 jobs anticipated through local component suppliers.
- **December 2020**: The workforce at the factory is reported to be 250 employees, with plans to increase to 300.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Nordex Group owns the facility exclusively, as it manufactures concrete towers solely for its own requirements and projects.
