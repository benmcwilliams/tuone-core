## ESP-07239-02557

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Endesa Facility in Totana, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date: 02-07-2018**: Endesa announced plans to construct an 84.9 MW solar park in Totana, Murcia, consisting of 248,000 PV modules. The project is part of a larger 339 MW solar project pipeline awarded to Endesa in a renewable auction held by the Spanish government in July 2017.
- **Date: 03-07-2018**: The facility's construction is set to begin after the summer of 2018, with plans to install PV systems at the site to replace diesel generators during construction. Once completed, these panels will be donated to the municipality for public use.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date: 02-07-2018**: Endesa plans to invest €59 million (approximately US$68.8 million) in the construction of the solar park in Totana.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date: 02-07-2018**: Announcement of the project and investment details were made, with construction expected to start in summer 2018.
- **Date: 03-07-2018**: Confirmation of the project details and construction timeline, with completion expected by November 2019.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date: 02-07-2018**: Endesa, through its subsidiary Enel Green Power España, committed to encouraging the hiring of local labor and subcontracting services from local companies during the development of the solar park.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: Endesa is a subsidiary of the Italian utility Enel, indicating that the facility is not under exclusive ownership but rather part of a larger corporate structure. The facility is developed under the auspices of Enel Green Power España, a subsidiary focused on renewable energy projects.
