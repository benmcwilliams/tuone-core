### Evolutionary Timeline of the GE Vernova Facility in Les Coves de Vinromà, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date: May 2024**  
  GE Vernova's facility in Les Coves de Vinromà is set to produce blades for 125 wind turbines (GE Vernova 6.1-158) as part of a contract with Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (CIP) for the Teruel wind project. The total installed power for this project is approximately 760 MW, which is expected to be the largest wind farm in Spain.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date: May 2024**  
  The contract signed between CIP and GE Vernova is valued at over €700 million for the delivery, installation, and commissioning of the wind turbines. The overall investment for the Teruel wind project is projected to be nearly €1 billion, with an additional €1.4 billion allocated for operation and maintenance throughout the project's lifespan.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date: May 2024**  
  The project is awaiting the Administrative Authorization for Construction (AAC) to commence installation work. The construction phase is expected to begin shortly after this authorization is granted.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date: May 2024**  
  The Teruel project is anticipated to have a significant socioeconomic impact, contributing to the maintenance of over 3,540 jobs in the region. This includes job creation linked to the manufacturing of wind turbines at the GE Vernova facility and other suppliers.
