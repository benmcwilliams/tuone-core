## ESP-07386-03140

### Evolutionary Timeline of the LM Wind Power Facility in Ponferrada, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2017**: LM Wind Power, acquired by GE, is set to produce wind turbine blades at its Ponferrada plant for GE's 1.2GW wind farm projects in Spain, in collaboration with Forestalia Group. The facility is part of a broader supply chain that includes nacelles produced in Germany and towers manufactured in Spain.
- **March 2022**: LM Wind Power produces the first prototype of a 100% recyclable wind turbine blade, measuring 62 meters, as part of the Zebra project. This project aims to demonstrate the feasibility of thermoplastic wind turbine blades, enhancing the sustainability of blade production.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2022**: LM Wind Power collaborates with Endesa, PreZero Espana, and Reciclalia Composite to establish the first wind blade recycling plant in Spain, with a construction budget of €8.5 million. This facility will utilize excess fibreglass from the Ponferrada plant for recycling.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2017**: Announcement of LM Wind Power's involvement in supplying blades for GE's wind farms in Spain.
- **April 2020**: LM Wind Power resumes operations at the Ponferrada plant after a temporary closure due to COVID-19, implementing safety measures to protect employees.
- **March 2022**: Production of the first recyclable blade prototype is completed at the Ponferrada facility.
- **January 2022**: Announcement of the partnership to build a recycling facility, with construction set to begin in 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2020**: LM Wind Power implements safety measures to protect its workforce during the COVID-19 pandemic, ensuring job security for at-risk employees.
- **January 2022**: The new recycling facility is expected to create 30 direct jobs upon becoming operational in early 2024.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2017**: LM Wind Power is noted to be a subsidiary of GE, indicating that the facility in Ponferrada is part of GE's broader operations in renewable energy, suggesting full ownership by GE.
