### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Castille - La Mancha, ESP

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2021**: Iberdrola began commissioning the Herrera II wind complex in Castilla y León, Spain. The facility consists of three wind farms (La Huesca, Valdesantos, and Orbanejoa) with a total installed capacity of 63 MW, comprising 14 wind turbines. The wind complex is designed to generate enough electricity to supply approximately 60,000 households annually and prevent the release of 50,000 tons of CO2 per year. The wind turbines installed are the SG 4.5-145, which are the largest and most powerful onshore wind turbines in Spain, with a unit power of 4.5 MW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2021**: Iberdrola invested €70 million in the development of the Herrera II wind complex. The company plans to invest over €2 billion in developing more than 2400 MW of renewable energy in the region by 2025.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2021**: The Herrera II wind complex was officially commissioned, marking a significant milestone in Iberdrola's renewable energy projects in Spain. The facility is part of a broader investment cycle that includes multiple renewable energy projects in Castilla y León.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2021**: The development of the Herrera II wind complex created approximately 800 jobs during the construction phase, with a significant portion of the work entrusted to local companies. Additionally, the broader investment plan in the region is expected to stimulate industry and create jobs for around 18,000 people.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify any joint ventures or partnerships; therefore, it can be inferred that Iberdrola owns the Herrera II wind complex exclusively.
