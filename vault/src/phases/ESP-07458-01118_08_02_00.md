## ESP-07458-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Fene, ESP

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **06/2021**: Iberdrola signs a framework agreement with Navantia and Windar for the establishment of an XXL monopile factory at the Fene shipyard. The factory will produce monopiles over 100 meters in length and weighing up to 2,500 tons. The facility is expected to start operations in August 2022 and will manufacture monopiles for offshore wind farms from 2023 to 2025.
- **07/2021**: The new facility will incorporate steel-curving capabilities for steel tubes with a diameter of up to 16 meters, enhancing the production capabilities of the Fene shipyard.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **06/2021**: The framework agreement is valued at €400 million, securing advance supply for 130 monopiles for future offshore wind projects. This investment is part of Iberdrola's broader strategy to expand its offshore wind capacity.
- **07/2021**: The agreement follows a previous €350 million contract for the construction of jackets and monopiles for the Saint Brieuc offshore wind project in France, indicating a strong financial commitment to offshore wind development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **06/2021**: Announcement of the framework agreement and the establishment of the XXL monopile factory at the Fene shipyard.
- **08/2022**: Expected start of operations for the new monopile facility.
- **2023-2025**: Planned production period for the monopiles.

#### 4. Labour
- **06/2021**: The initiative is expected to generate several thousand jobs through local contractors in Spain and France, contributing to employment in the renewable energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **06/2021**: The facility is a joint venture between Iberdrola and the Navantia-Windar consortium, indicating that Iberdrola does not own the facility exclusively but collaborates with these manufacturers for its operations.
