### Evolutionary Timeline of the Navantia Facility in Fene, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2020**: Navantia's facility in Fene is confirmed to produce jackets for the Saint-Brieuc offshore wind farm, with a contract for 62 jackets and corresponding piles. The facility will be involved in the construction and assembly of these components.
- **June 2021**: The Fene shipyard completes its 100th jacket for the offshore wind industry, marking significant production progress for the Saint-Brieuc project.
- **July 2021**: A €400 million agreement is signed for the manufacture and supply of 130 XXL monopile foundations, with plans to set up a new factory for these monopiles at the Fene shipyard. The new facility will enhance production capabilities, allowing for the bending of thick sheet metal up to 16 meters in diameter.
- **August 2022**: The new monopile facility at the Fene shipyard is expected to start operations.
- **August 2023**: The first batch of monopiles for the Moray West offshore wind farm is loaded out from the new plant in Fene.
- **June 2024**: The facility produces its first monopile for the East Anglia Three offshore wind project.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2020**: A €350 million contract is awarded for the Saint-Brieuc project, leading to the establishment of a new factory in France and significant employment opportunities.
- **July 2021**: The €400 million framework agreement with Iberdrola for the monopile factory is confirmed, which includes investments for new technology and facilities at the Fene shipyard.
- **August 2022**: The new monopile facility is set to begin operations, enhancing the shipyard's production capabilities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2020**: Contract signed for the Saint-Brieuc project, leading to the establishment of the facility's production capabilities.
- **June 2021**: Completion of the 100th jacket for the Saint-Brieuc project.
- **July 2021**: Announcement of the new monopile factory to be built at the Fene shipyard.
- **August 2022**: Expected start of operations at the new monopile facility.
- **August 2023**: First batch of Moray West monopiles loaded out from the new facility.
- **June 2024**: Production of the first monopile for the East Anglia Three project.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2020**: The contract for the Saint-Brieuc project is expected to create around 1,000 jobs in Galicia and Asturias, with over 1,100 jobs in France.
- **June 2021**: The jackets project is projected to generate nearly 2,000 jobs across Fene, Avilés, and the Brest facility.
- **July 2021**: The new monopile factory is anticipated to further boost employment opportunities in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is operated by a joint venture between Navantia and Windar, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by a single company.
