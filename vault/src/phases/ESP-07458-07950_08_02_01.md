### Evolutionary Timeline of the Navantia-Windar Facility in Fene, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2019**: Navantia-Windar begins construction of five floating foundations for the Kincardine wind farm in Scotland, with assembly and delivery at the Fene yard. The project includes the construction of a 50MW capacity wind farm.
- **June 2020**: Navantia-Windar secures a contract to manufacture and assemble 62 jacket foundations for the Saint-Brieuc wind farm in France, marking their largest offshore wind contract to date.
- **July 2021**: Announcement of a new factory for XXL monopiles at the Fene shipyard, following a EUR 400 million framework agreement with Iberdrola for future offshore wind farms.
- **November 2022**: Navantia-Windar signs a capacity reservation agreement to manufacture 62 jackets for the Dieppe le Tréport wind farm in France, with fabrication starting in early 2023.
- **April 2023**: Navantia-Windar secures an order for 45 monopiles for the East Anglia Three offshore wind farm in the UK, marking the largest contract for this type of foundation for the facility.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2020**: The contract for the Saint-Brieuc wind farm is valued at EUR 350 million, contributing to the facility's growth in offshore wind capabilities.
- **July 2021**: The framework agreement with Iberdrola for the XXL monopile factory is valued at EUR 400 million, indicating significant investment in expanding production capacity.
- **April 2023**: The investment in the monopile factory adaptation is reported to be EUR 36 million, enhancing the facility's manufacturing capabilities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2019**: Construction of floating foundations for Kincardine begins.
- **2021**: Construction of the Saint-Brieuc wind farm is scheduled to begin.
- **2023**: Fabrication for the Dieppe le Tréport wind farm is set to start in Q1, with a duration of approximately two years.
- **June 2023**: Production for the East Anglia Three monopiles is scheduled to start.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2020**: The Saint-Brieuc contract is expected to create around 1,000 jobs in Spain and 1,100 jobs in France.
- **July 2021**: The new XXL monopile factory is anticipated to create additional jobs, although specific numbers are not detailed.
- **April 2023**: The monopile order for East Anglia Three is expected to create around 450 jobs.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Navantia-Windar facility operates as a joint venture between Navantia and Windar, indicating that it is not exclusively owned by either company but rather a collaborative partnership.
