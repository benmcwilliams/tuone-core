### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Villalba de Guardo, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/2023**: Iberdrola announced plans to build a 350MW solar photovoltaic (PV) plant in Villalba de Guardo, with over 630,000 solar modules. The facility is expected to generate enough energy to power 180,000 homes annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12/2023**: The construction of the solar farm will involve an investment of €217 million ($233.6 million).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2023**: Iberdrola secured a favorable environmental impact statement (EIS) for the solar farm in Villalba de Guardo, marking a significant milestone in the project’s development.

#### 4. Labour
- **12/2023**: The project is expected to involve up to 850 industrial suppliers during peak construction periods, indicating potential job creation in the local area.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **01/2023**: Iberdrola sold a 49% stake in its 1.27GW renewable energy portfolio to Norges Bank Investment Management (NBIM), retaining a 51% stake and continuing to control and manage the assets. This indicates that while Iberdrola maintains majority ownership, it has entered into a joint venture arrangement for its broader renewable portfolio, which includes the Villalba de Guardo facility.
