### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Villarreal, ESP

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2021**: Iberdrola announced plans for a solar-plus-hydrogen power project called GREENH2SKER at Porcelanosa’s factory in Villarreal. The project aims to replace up to half of the natural gas used in the production process with green hydrogen by installing an electrolyser powered by a solar PV system. The system will also utilize excess waste heat from production lines.
- **May 2021**: Iberdrola is collaborating with Mitsubishi Power to develop green hydrogen production facilities, which may include the Villarreal project as part of a broader initiative to decarbonize industrial processes.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2021**: The GREENH2SKER project was presented to the European Innovation Fund Small Scale Projects programme, indicating potential funding and support for the initiative.
- **April 2021**: Iberdrola announced a new business unit focused on hydrogen, which will utilize 100% green energy in the electrolysis process, aligning with the development of the Villarreal facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: Announcement of the GREENH2SKER project at Porcelanosa’s factory.
- **May 2021**: Iberdrola confirmed ongoing partnerships and projects, including the Villarreal facility, as part of its strategy to establish a green hydrogen project portfolio.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2021**: While specific job creation numbers for the Villarreal facility were not detailed, Iberdrola's broader hydrogen initiatives, including the Villarreal project, are expected to contribute to job creation in the renewable energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2021**: The facility is part of a partnership between Iberdrola and Porcelanosa, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership. Iberdrola is not the sole owner of the facility, as it is working in conjunction with Porcelanosa and potentially other partners like Sacmi.
