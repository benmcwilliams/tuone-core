### Evolutionary Timeline of the Plenitude Facility in Villarino de los Aires, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2024**: Plenitude has initiated the construction of a 220MW solar plant in Villarino de los Aires, Salamanca, Spain. The facility will cover an area of approximately 286 hectares and will be equipped with 365,300 bifacial solar modules. It is projected to generate over 400,000 MWh (or 400 GWh) of electricity annually. The plant is expected to be completed by 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2024**: The construction of the solar facility is being supported by Sterling & Wilson and other local firms. The project is part of Plenitude's broader strategy to enhance its renewable energy footprint in Spain, where it currently operates over 440MW of installed capacity across various regions.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2024**: The cornerstone laying ceremony for the solar plant was held, marking the official start of construction. The facility is set to connect to the national transmission grid via a 3.2km underground medium-voltage line.
