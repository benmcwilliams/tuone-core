### Evolutionary Timeline of the Repsol Facility in Ciguñuela, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **07/2023**: Repsol began producing its first power at the La Serna wind farm, part of the PI wind complex in Ciguñuela, with an initial capacity of 175 MW. The complex consists of seven wind farms, which will collectively reach a total installed capacity of 175 MW once fully completed.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **03/2023**: Repsol announced the acquisition of three wind farms (150 MW) and two solar plants (100 MW) from ABO Wind, enhancing its renewable portfolio in Spain to a total of 3,200 MW. These projects are at an advanced stage of development and are expected to be operational between 2024 and 2025.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **07/2023**: The grid connection of the first turbines at the La Serna wind farm was completed, marking the start of power production.
- **03/2023**: The acquisition of additional renewable assets from ABO Wind was confirmed, which will share interconnection infrastructure with the PI facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **03/2023**: The new projects acquired from ABO Wind are expected to supply electricity to nearly 172,000 homes and avoid the emission of 266,000 tons of CO2 per year, indicating potential job creation during the development phase, although specific job numbers were not mentioned.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Repsol owns the facility exclusively, as indicated by the acquisition of the wind farms and solar plants from ABO Wind, which will be developed under Repsol's management.
