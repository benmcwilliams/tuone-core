### Evolutionary Timeline of Siemens Gamesa Facility in Aoiz, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2017**: Siemens Gamesa secured an order for 22 turbines totaling 64MW for two wind farms in Spain. The blades for these turbines were to be manufactured at the Aoiz factory.
- **September 2018**: The Aoiz factory was confirmed as a key production site for blades for multiple turbine orders totaling 233MW for various wind farms in Spain.
- **December 2018**: The Aoiz facility continued to produce blades for a contract with Norvento for 10 turbines totaling almost 35MW.
- **February 2019**: The Aoiz factory was involved in producing blades for a total of 200MW of wind capacity across several regions in Spain.
- **March 2020**: The Aoiz facility was temporarily closed due to COVID-19, representing about 10% of Siemens Gamesa's capacity, which was between 600 to 800 megawatts annually.
- **July 2020**: Siemens Gamesa announced the closure of the Aoiz factory, stating it was not competitive for producing blades for large turbine models.
