### Evolutionary Timeline of Siemens Gamesa Facility in Ágreda, Spain

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2017**: Siemens Gamesa's facility in Ágreda is responsible for assembling nacelles for wind turbines. The facility is set to produce nacelles for 36 turbines (G132 3.3MW) for two wind farms in Turkey, marking the first order for the 3.3MW platform in Europe.
- **December 2017**: The facility continues to produce nacelles, this time for 22 turbines (G126-2.625MW and G132-3.465MW) for two wind farms in Spain.
- **April 2020**: The facility resumes operations after a temporary shutdown due to COVID-19, indicating its importance in the production chain.
- **March 2021**: The facility is set to assemble nacelles for 30 SG 5.0-145 turbines for a 150 MW contract with Elawan Energy.
- **March 2021**: The facility will also produce nacelles for 24 SG 5.0-145 turbines for a 120 MW contract with Repsol.
- **January 2022**: The facility is tasked with manufacturing nacelles for 14 SG 6.6-170 turbines and two SG 5.0-145 turbines for the Iglesias wind project.
- **December 2022**: The facility will assemble nacelles for 10 SG 5.0-145 turbines for a 52 MW project with Capital Energy.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: The contracts signed with Elawan Energy and Repsol are part of a broader investment strategy to bolster Siemens Gamesa's presence in the Spanish wind energy market, which is expected to grow significantly.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2017**: Announcement of orders for wind farms in Turkey, with nacelles to be produced in Ágreda.
- **December 2017**: Secured orders for wind farms in Spain, with nacelles produced in Ágreda.
- **April 2020**: Resumption of production at the facility after COVID-19 lockdown.
- **March 2021**: Contracts signed for significant turbine supply, including a 150 MW project with Elawan Energy and a 120 MW project with Repsol.
- **January 2022**: Agreement with Iberdrola for the first deployment of the 5.X platform in Spain.
- **December 2022**: New contract with Capital Energy for a 52 MW project.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2021**: The new contracts are expected to boost activity at the Ágreda facility, contributing to job stability and potential job creation as production ramps up to meet the demands of new contracts.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Siemens Gamesa owns the facility in Ágreda exclusively, as indicated by the consistent references to the facility as part of Siemens Gamesa's operations without mention of joint ventures or partnerships in ownership.
