### Evolutionary Timeline of the Port of Tallinn Facility, Tallinn, Estonia

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2021**: The Port of Tallinn aims to become a hydrogen hub, focusing on developing a green energy ecosystem in the industrial parks of Muuga Harbour and Paldiski South Harbour. The facility plans to build a hydrogen terminal to support industries that do not produce energy themselves but wish to participate in the green revolution.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2021**: The Port of Tallinn is actively seeking EU backing for its hydrogen hub initiative and is collaborating with hydrogen players from the Netherlands and the Port of Hamburg to attract local and international investors.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2021**: The Port of Tallinn publicly announces its ambition to establish itself as a hydrogen hub in Estonia, indicating a strategic move towards becoming a key player in the Baltic Sea's green infrastructure.
- **April 2023**: The Port of Tallinn signs a letter of intent with Poland's Port of Gdynia Authority to cooperate on hydrogen management, aiming to create new value chains and economic opportunities through hydrogen-based fuels.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2023**: The establishment of the hydrogen hub is expected to create new job opportunities as the Port of Tallinn collaborates with various stakeholders, including the Estonian Hydrogen Association, to develop the hydrogen economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2023**: The Port of Tallinn is involved in partnerships with various entities, including the Estonian Hydrogen Association, Alexela, Eesti Energia, and the University of Tartu, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership of the hydrogen hub initiative.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic moves of the Port of Tallinn facility in its evolution towards becoming a significant player in the green hydrogen sector in Estonia.
