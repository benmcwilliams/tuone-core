### Evolutionary Timeline of the Evecon Facility in Kirikmäe, Estonia

**1. Technology and Operations:**
- **Date:** 04-10-2024
  - The Kirikmäe solar park, developed by Evecon, has a production capacity of **77 MW** and is spread over **110 hectares**. It is noted to be more than twice the size of Estonia’s largest solar facility. The park will cover the estimated annual energy needs of **35,000 households** and will consist of **117,600 solar panels** with a power output of **655–665 W** each, manufactured by **Canadian Solar**.

**2. Investment and Development:**
- **Date:** 04-10-2024
  - The Kirikmäe solar park is part of a joint venture called the **Baltic Renewable Energy Platform (BREP)**, established in **December 2022** between Evecon and Mirova. This joint venture is focused on financing, constructing, and operating a portfolio of more than **100 MWp** of solar parks in Estonia.

**3. Project Status and Milestones:**
- **Date:** 04-10-2024
  - The Kirikmäe solar park has been officially opened, marking a significant milestone in the region's renewable energy landscape. The project is part of a larger initiative that includes the opening of the **Imavere** and **Lohu mets** solar parks, which collectively add over **100 MW** of production capacity to the local market within a week.
