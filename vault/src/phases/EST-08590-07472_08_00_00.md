### Evolutionary Timeline of the Nordex Facility in Nord Pärnu, Estonia

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10/2023**: Nordex received an order to supply 38 N163/6.X wind turbines for the Sopi I, Sopi II, and Tootsi wind farms, with a total production capacity of 255 MW. This project will be the most powerful wind project in the Baltic states to date, projected to produce approximately 700 GWh of electricity annually, enough to power around 200,000 homes. The turbines are scheduled to come online by the end of 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10/2023**: The order from Enefit Green represents a significant investment in renewable energy infrastructure in Estonia, marking a return of Nordex to the Estonian market after a long absence since their last installation of an 18 MW wind farm in 2004.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **10/2023**: The contract for the wind farm was awarded to Nordex by Enefit Green, following a public tender won by Nordex at the end of 2022. The construction of the Sopi-Tootsi wind farm group is currently underway, with completion and operational status expected by the end of 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **10/2023**: While specific job creation numbers related to this project were not mentioned, the establishment of the wind farm is expected to contribute positively to local employment in the renewable energy sector, particularly in construction and maintenance roles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **10/2023**: The facility is part of a partnership where Nordex supplies the technology and turbines, while Enefit Green, a subsidiary of the Eesti Energia Group, is the primary developer and operator of the wind farms.
