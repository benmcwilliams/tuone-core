### Evolutionary Timeline of the Evecon Facility in Pärnu County, Estonia

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2024**: Evecon, in partnership with Mirova, completed the construction of the Kirikmäe solar park, which has a production capacity of **77.53 MW**. This facility is noted as the largest solar park in the Baltics at the time of its completion, doubling the capacity of the previous largest operational solar PV plant in Estonia. The park consists of **117,600 solar panels** provided by Canadian Solar.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2024**: The construction of the Kirikmäe solar park was part of a broader investment strategy by the joint venture Baltic Renewable Energy Platform (BREP), established in **2022** between Evecon and Mirova, aimed at financing, building, and operating a portfolio of over **100 MWp** of solar PV in Estonia.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04-10-2024**: The Kirikmäe solar park reached operational status, marking a significant milestone in the development of solar energy in the Baltic region.
- **November 2024**: Following the operational status of the Kirikmäe solar park, another project was announced by Sunly, which would surpass Evecon's facility in size, indicating a competitive landscape in solar energy development in Estonia.
