## FIN-00443-00073

### Evolutionary Timeline of BASF Facility in Harjavalta, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2018**: BASF officially selects Harjavalta as the location for its first battery materials manufacturing facility aimed at the European automobile market. The facility is designed to produce precursors for cathode materials, with a planned production capacity to supply materials for approximately 300,000 electric vehicles (EVs) annually. 
- **February 2020**: BASF announces that the Harjavalta facility will create precursors that will be processed into cathode materials at a new plant in Brandenburg, Germany, expected to start production in 2022.
- **March 2020**: BASF, along with Fortum and Nornickel, signs a letter of intent to establish a battery recycling cluster in Harjavalta, integrating recycling processes with the production of battery materials.
- **June 2020**: BASF confirms the construction of the Harjavalta facility is underway, with production still targeted for 2022, aiming to supply materials for 400,000 battery electric vehicles (BEVs) per year.
- **April 2021**: Nornickel announces plans to increase nickel production at its nearby refinery, which will support the Harjavalta facility's operations.
- **April 2024**: BASF puts plans for the Harjavalta facility on hold due to ongoing permitting issues, leading to potential indefinite layoffs for employees.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2018**: BASF announces a multi-year investment plan of approximately €400 million for the Harjavalta facility, which is part of a broader strategy to establish a European battery supply chain.
- **March 2020**: The investment in the Harjavalta facility is reiterated as part of a larger initiative supported by the European Commission, which has promised state aid totaling €3.2 billion for battery production projects across Europe.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2018**: Announcement of the Harjavalta facility's establishment.
- **February 2020**: Confirmation of the facility's role in the European battery supply chain.
- **June 2020**: Official start of construction at the Harjavalta site.
- **April 2024**: Plans for the facility are put on hold due to permitting issues, with negotiations for employee layoffs initiated.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2024**: BASF announces potential indefinite layoffs for employees at the Harjavalta site due to the lengthy permitting process and uncertainty regarding the facility's future.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2018**: BASF is identified as the primary owner of the facility, with a long-term supply agreement established with Nornickel for critical materials (nickel and cobalt) necessary for battery production. There is no indication of joint ownership or partnerships affecting the ownership structure of the Harjavalta facility itself.

This timeline captures the key developments and challenges faced by the BASF facility in Harjavalta, Finland, highlighting its role in the European battery materials market and the impact of regulatory hurdles on its operations.
