### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fortum Facility in Harjavalta, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2019**: Fortum achieves a lithium-ion battery material recycling rate of over 80% using a low-carbon hydrometallurgical recycling process. This process allows for the recovery of cobalt, lithium, manganese, and nickel from batteries.
- **March 2020**: Fortum, along with BASF and Nornickel, signs a letter of intent to establish a battery recycling cluster to serve the electric vehicle market, enhancing the recycling capabilities at the Harjavalta facility.
- **November 2020**: Fortum announces a new patented method for recycling lithium from rechargeable batteries, although specific details on efficiency improvements are not disclosed.
- **January 2021**: Fortum expands its operations with a new mechanical processing plant in Ikaalinen, which will complement the existing hydrometallurgical pilot plant in Harjavalta.
- **June 2021**: Fortum announces plans to build a new hydrometallurgical recycling plant in Harjavalta, with an investment of €24 million, aimed at recycling a significant portion of end-of-life electric vehicle batteries in Europe.
- **April 2023**: Fortum opens the new large-scale battery recycling plant in Harjavalta, which is the largest recycling plant in Europe in terms of capacity and the first commercial-scale facility for hydrometallurgical recycling.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2020**: The establishment of the battery recycling cluster involves collaboration with BASF and Nornickel, enhancing investment in the recycling infrastructure.
- **June 2021**: Fortum announces a €24 million investment for the new hydrometallurgical plant in Harjavalta.
- **April 2023**: The new plant is completed, supported by previous investments and grants from the EU’s Innovation Fund and Business Finland.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2019**: Fortum achieves an 80% recycling rate.
- **March 2020**: Letter of intent signed for the battery recycling cluster.
- **November 2020**: Announcement of a new lithium recycling method.
- **January 2021**: Expansion of operations with a new mechanical processing plant in Ikaalinen.
- **June 2021**: Announcement of the new hydrometallurgical plant in Harjavalta.
- **April 2023**: Completion and opening of the new large-scale battery recycling plant.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2021**: The new mechanical recycling plant in Ikaalinen is expected to employ up to 20 people.
- **April 2023**: The new facility in Harjavalta is projected to create approximately 30 jobs.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Fortum owns the facility exclusively, having acquired Crisolteq, which developed the hydrometallurgical recycling technology used at the Harjavalta site.
