### Evolutionary Timeline of the Steady Energy Facility in Helsinki, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2020**: Development of the LDR-50 district heating small modular reactor (SMR) begins at VTT Technical Research Centre of Finland. The reactor is designed to have a thermal output of 50 MW and operate at around 150°C and below 10 bar (145 psi).
- **07-06-2024**: Announcement of the construction of a pilot plant to serve as a full-scale operational model of the LDR-50 SMR, which will use an electric element for heat production instead of nuclear fuel. The pilot plant will be used for testing operational features and establishing supply chains.
- **Expected Timeline**: Construction of the first operational LDR-50 district heating plant is expected to begin by **2028**, with the first unit operational by **2030**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07-06-2024**: The pilot investment for the SMR is estimated to be between EUR 15–20 million (USD 16–22 million).
- **20-07-2024**: Steady Energy and Kuopion Energia enhance cooperation, initiating an environmental impact assessment for potential plant locations, which will refine suitable sites for the plant.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **07-06-2024**: Final evaluations of potential sites for the pilot plant are underway, with a decision expected by the end of summer 2024. Proposed locations include Salmisaari caves in Helsinki.
- **10-09-2024**: Helen launches the first phase of its nuclear program aimed at constructing a small nuclear power plant for producing heat for Helsinki, with a completion target for the initial phase by **2026**.
- **12-09-2024**: Helen aims to have a nuclear power plant operational in Helsinki before **2035**, with ongoing site mapping and supplier evaluations.

#### 4. Labour
- **20-07-2024**: The development of the LDR-50 SMR is expected to create jobs, particularly in the construction and operational phases, although specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2023**: Helen signs a letter of intent with Steady Energy for the construction of up to 10 SMRs for district heating, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership. Steady Energy, spun out from VTT, is the developer of the LDR-50 technology, while Helen is a municipal energy company involved in the project.
