## FIN-00503-00280

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fortum Facility in Ikaalinen, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2021**: Fortum announced the expansion of its electric vehicle battery recycling business with a new mechanical processing plant in Ikaalinen. The facility is designed to mechanically crush discarded batteries and separate metals to produce "black mass," which will be sent to the hydrometallurgical plant in Harjavalta for further processing. The initial production capacity is set at approximately 3,000 tonnes of used batteries annually, equivalent to around 10,000 electric car batteries.
- **September 2023**: Fortum announced plans to expand its mechanical processing capacity at the Ikaalinen facility by investing in a new type of mechanical shredder. This expansion is expected to increase the annual capacity to 5,000 tonnes of electric car batteries, enhancing the quality of the "black mass" produced for the hydrometallurgical process.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2021**: Fortum received grants from Business Finland as part of the EU’s Innovation Fund for low-carbon technologies, which included support for the mechanical recycling plant in Ikaalinen.
- **September 2023**: Fortum was awarded a €4.5 million grant from Business Finland to support the expansion of mechanical processing capabilities at the Ikaalinen facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2021**: The new mechanical recycling plant in Ikaalinen was expected to open.
- **April 2023**: The hydrometallurgical recycling plant in Harjavalta, which processes the black mass from Ikaalinen, commenced operations.
- **September 2023**: Fortum announced the development of a pilot plant for new methods of producing battery materials, although the location and operational start date were not specified.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2021**: The new mechanical recycling plant in Ikaalinen was expected to employ up to 20 people.
- **June 2021**: The new hydrometallurgical plant in Harjavalta was projected to create approximately 30 jobs, indirectly benefiting the Ikaalinen facility by increasing demand for its output.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Fortum operates the facility in Ikaalinen as part of its battery recycling business, indicating full ownership of the facility without any joint ventures mentioned in the articles.
