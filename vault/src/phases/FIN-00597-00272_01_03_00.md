## FIN-00597-00272

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Finnish Minerals Group Facility in Kotka, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/2023**: Finnish Minerals Group signs a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) for a cell production plant in Kotka, with potential production capacities of 27 GWh and 40 GWh. The facility will assemble components such as cathodes, anodes, electrolytes, and separators.
- **07/2023**: Plans for a cathode active material (CAM) plant in Kotka are announced, with an initial production capacity of 50,000 tonnes per year and potential for future expansion. This plant will be part of a larger cluster of battery material plants in Europe.
- **08/2023**: The joint venture with Beijing Easpring for the cathode materials factory is confirmed, with production capacity aimed at meeting the needs of over 750,000 electric vehicles annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07/2023**: The initial investment for the CAM plant is projected at approximately €774 million. This investment is part of a broader strategy to enhance the European battery value chain and reduce carbon footprints.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2023**: The environmental impact assessment (EIA) process for the cell production plant is initiated, with a conclusion expected by summer 2024.
- **07/2023**: The project for the CAM plant has received approval from the Board of Directors, moving into detailed planning and permitting phases.
- **08/2023**: Preparatory work at the Keltakallio industrial site began at the end of 2021, with site levelling and preparation continuing through summer 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **08/2023**: The establishment of the joint venture and the construction of the plants are expected to bring new economic activity and job creation to the Kymenlaakso region, although specific job numbers have not been disclosed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **08/2023**: The joint venture for the cathode materials factory will see Beijing Easpring owning 70% and Finnish Minerals Group owning 30%. Finnish Minerals Group's battery company, Finnish Battery Chemicals Oy, is wholly owned by Finnish Minerals Group, which will also own the cell production facility exclusively, pending the outcome of the EIA.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic moves of the Finnish Minerals Group facility in Kotka, Finland, highlighting its focus on advancing clean technology in the battery materials sector.
