### Evolutionary Timeline of the Finnish Minerals Group Facility in Kotka, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/2023**: Finnish Minerals Group signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) for a new cell production plant in Kotka. The facility is proposed to have two production capacity variants: 27 GWh and 40 GWh per year. The plant will assemble components such as cathodes, anodes, electrolytes, and separators.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12/2023**: The estimated investment costs for a cell production capacity of 10 GWh are around one billion euros. This investment is tied to the potential construction of the new plant in Kotka, pending a positive environmental impact assessment.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2023**: The next steps include conducting an environmental impact assessment (EIA) for the proposed plant, with a “reasoned conclusion on the EIA report” expected by summer 2024. The site for the plant has been offered by the city of Kotka, covering approximately 140 hectares in the Keltakallio industrial estate.

#### 4. Labour
- **12/2023**: Specific job creation or job loss details have not been disclosed yet, as the project is still in the planning and assessment phase.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **12/2023**: The facility will be operated by Finnish Battery Chemicals Oy, which is wholly owned by Finnish Minerals Group. The ownership structure indicates that Finnish Minerals Group will have full ownership of the facility once it is established.
