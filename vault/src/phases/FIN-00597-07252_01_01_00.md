## FIN-00597-07252

### Evolutionary Timeline of the CarbonScape Facility in Kotka, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Planned Production**: CarbonScape, in collaboration with Stora Enso, plans to build a biographite demonstration plant in Kotka, southern Finland. The facility aims to produce biographite as a sustainable alternative to mined graphite for lithium-ion batteries.
- **Production Capacity**: The demonstration plant is designed to produce larger biographite samples for original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) and battery cell makers, facilitating product qualification and operator training.
- **Updates**: The facility represents a significant scaling up of CarbonScape's biographite production technology, which has been optimized at a pilot plant in New Zealand since 2018.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Funding Secured**: In September 2023, CarbonScape secured $18 million (NZ$29.08 million) in funding to support its biographite commercialization efforts.
- **Future Investments**: The company is also in the process of designing a commercial biographite plant, with data from the demonstration facility expected to support its global expansion.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Announcement**: The plans for the biographite demonstration plant were announced on September 16, 2024.
- **Construction Timeline**: Construction is expected to start in 2025, with sample production anticipated to begin in 2026.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The establishment of the demonstration plant is expected to create jobs, particularly in training operators and engineers in a large industrial setting, although specific job numbers have not been disclosed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture**: The facility is a collaboration between CarbonScape and Stora Enso, with Stora Enso being an investor in CarbonScape. The ownership structure indicates a joint venture rather than exclusive ownership by CarbonScape.

This timeline captures the key developments and plans for the CarbonScape facility in Kotka, Finland, highlighting its focus on sustainable battery technology and the strategic partnerships involved.
