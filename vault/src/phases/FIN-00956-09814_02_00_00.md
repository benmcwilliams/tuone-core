### Evolutionary Timeline of the Quantitative Heat Oy Facility in Salo, Finland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility is involved in the production of geothermal heat through the drilling of geothermal wells. 
   - As of July 2024, there are three geothermal wells drilled: two at a depth of 2000 meters and one at 1600 meters. 
   - The project aims to have a total of six geothermal heat wells with a combined output of up to 14 GWh per year, sufficient to meet the annual heating needs of approximately 700 single-family homes.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The project received EUR 3 million in energy support from the Ministry of Economic Affairs and Employment, which initiated the geothermal heat well project.
   - A decision was made in June 2024 to invest in a third phase of the project, which includes the construction of three additional geothermal wells.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Winter 2023:** A third geothermal well was drilled to a depth of 2000 meters.
   - **June 2024:** Announcement of the investment in the third phase of the project.
   - **July 2024:** Construction work for the additional wells has begun, with completion expected by summer 2025.
