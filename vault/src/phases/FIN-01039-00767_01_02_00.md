### Evolutionary Timeline of the Terrafame Facility in Sotkamo, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2021**: Terrafame received environmental approval for its battery materials factory in Sotkamo, primarily producing nickel sulfate and cobalt sulfate. The factory is designed to produce nickel sulfate for one million electric car batteries annually and enough cobalt sulfate for 300,000 batteries.
- **June 2021**: Production ramp-up commenced at the battery materials factory, confirming the facility's capacity to meet the previously stated production goals.
- **June 2024**: Terrafame's uranium recovery plant began operations, with plans to produce approximately 200 tonnes of uranium per year by 2026. This facility utilizes low concentrations of natural uranium as a by-product of the existing mining operations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2018**: The Terrafame Board of Directors made a significant investment decision to move downstream in the processing chain to become a producer of battery chemicals, leading to the establishment of the battery materials factory.
- **February 2020**: The Finnish government granted a permit for large-scale uranium recovery, which was part of Terrafame's broader investment strategy in sustainable mining practices.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: Production at the battery materials factory was scheduled to start.
- **June 2021**: The factory began its production ramp-up.
- **June 2024**: The uranium recovery plant was commissioned following regulatory approvals.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2021**: The ramp-up of production at the battery materials factory is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers were not detailed in the articles. The uranium recovery plant's operations are also anticipated to contribute to job creation in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Terrafame is 70% owned by Finnish Minerals Group, which is wholly owned by the State of Finland. This indicates that Terrafame does not have exclusive ownership of the facility but operates under a state-owned structure.
