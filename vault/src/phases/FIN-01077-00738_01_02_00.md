### Evolutionary Timeline of Stora Enso Facility in Sunila, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2015**: Stora Enso begins producing lignin at the Sunila production site.
- **July 2021**: Stora Enso starts the production of Lignode, a bio-based material for battery anodes, as part of a project to ensure sustainable development for battery manufacturing. The facility's annual lignin production capacity is expanded to 50,000 tonnes, making Stora Enso the largest kraft lignin producer globally.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2021**: Stora Enso announces a €10 million investment to expand and innovate its manufacturing capacity at the Sunila site, facilitating the production of Lignode.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2021**: Launch of the pilot plant for Lignode production at the Sunila facility.
- **July 2022**: Stora Enso enters a joint development agreement with Northvolt to develop wood-based batteries using lignin-based hard carbon sourced from Nordic forests. This partnership aims to create sustainable batteries with an anode made from renewable materials.
