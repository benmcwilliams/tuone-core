### Epsilon Advanced Materials Facility Timeline - Vaasa, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2022**: Epsilon Advanced Materials, in collaboration with Finnish Minerals Group, announced plans to build a production plant for anode material in Vaasa, Finland, with an initial production capacity of 10 GWh of finished lithium-ion batteries, expanding to 50 GWh in a second phase. The facility is intended to produce almost 50,000 tons of anode material per year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2022**: The announcement included plans for a feasibility study to analyze the economic and technical viability of the project, although specific investment amounts were not disclosed at this stage.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2022**: A letter of intent was signed between Epsilon Advanced Materials and Finnish Minerals Group, with plans to submit relevant applications to the coordinating authority in early 2023. The feasibility study was set to begin shortly after the announcement.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2022**: No specific job creation or job loss figures were mentioned in the announcement, but the establishment of the facility is expected to create jobs in the region as production ramps up.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2022**: The facility is a joint venture between Epsilon Advanced Materials and Finnish Minerals Group, as indicated by the signed letter of intent.
