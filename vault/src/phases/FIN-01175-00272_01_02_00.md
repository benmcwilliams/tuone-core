### Evolutionary Timeline of the Finnish Minerals Group Facility in Vaasa, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2022**: Finnish Minerals Group, in collaboration with Epsilon Advanced Materials, announced plans to build a production plant for nearly 50,000 tons of anode material per year in Vaasa. The initial phase aims to produce anode material corresponding to 10 GWh of finished lithium-ion batteries, with a second phase planned to expand production to the equivalent of 50 GWh of batteries.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2022**: The announcement of the feasibility study for the anode material plant indicates potential investments, although specific financial details are not provided at this stage.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2022**: A letter of intent was signed between Finnish Minerals Group and Epsilon Advanced Materials to explore the construction of the anode material facility. The feasibility study is set to begin shortly, with applications to be submitted to the coordinating authority in early 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2022**: While specific job creation figures are not mentioned, the establishment of the anode material facility is expected to contribute to job opportunities in the Vaasa region, particularly in the context of the growing battery value chain.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2022**: The facility is a collaboration between Finnish Minerals Group and Epsilon Advanced Materials, indicating a joint venture structure for the anode material production facility in Vaasa.

### Summary
The Finnish Minerals Group facility in Vaasa is in the early stages of development, focusing on the production of anode materials for lithium-ion batteries. The collaboration with Epsilon Advanced Materials marks a significant step in establishing a facility with a planned capacity of 50,000 tons per year. The project is currently undergoing feasibility studies, with expectations of contributing to local job creation and enhancing the European battery supply chain.
