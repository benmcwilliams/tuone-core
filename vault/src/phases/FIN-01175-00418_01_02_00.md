## FIN-01175-00418

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Johnson Matthey Facility in Vaasa, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2021**: Johnson Matthey announced plans to construct a new factory in Vaasa for the production of nickel-rich eLNO cathode materials, with an annual output capacity of 30,000 tonnes. This facility is intended to complement existing production in Konin, Poland.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2021**: The construction of the Vaasa plant is part of a strategic partnership with the Finnish Minerals Group (FMG), which aims to develop the Finnish battery ecosystem. FMG will support Johnson Matthey in two key areas: treatment of sodium sulphate and pre-treatment of nickel and cobalt sulphates.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: Announcement of the construction of the Vaasa facility, with construction expected to begin later in 2021.
- **November 2021**: Johnson Matthey announced its withdrawal from the battery materials business, which included the planned facility in Vaasa. The company cited insufficient potential returns and high capital intensity as reasons for this decision.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2021**: Following the announcement of withdrawal from the battery materials business, Johnson Matthey indicated that it would commence consultations with employees regarding the proposed closure of the Battery Materials business, which would likely lead to job losses.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2021**: The facility was to be developed in partnership with the Finnish Minerals Group, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by Johnson Matthey.

### Summary
The Johnson Matthey facility in Vaasa, Finland, was initially planned to produce nickel-rich eLNO cathode materials with a capacity of 30,000 tonnes annually, supported by a partnership with the Finnish Minerals Group. However, in November 2021, Johnson Matthey announced its withdrawal from the battery materials business, leading to uncertainty regarding the future of the facility and potential job losses. The facility was intended to be a joint venture rather than solely owned by Johnson Matthey.
