### Evolutionary Timeline of the Wärtsilä Facility in Vaasa, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2021**: Wärtsilä, in collaboration with Vaasan Sähkö, EPV Energia, and the City of Vaasa, announced a project to utilize emissions-free hydrogen for power generation, industry, and transportation. The project aims to create a Power-to-X-to-Power system that will produce hydrogen from renewable energy through electrolysis, with the potential for future expansions in hydrogen production and storage capacity.

- **July 2021**: Wärtsilä began testing thermal balancing engines using pure hydrogen, with expectations to develop a power plant concept capable of running on 100% hydrogen by 2025. Full-scale engine tests were conducted in Vaasa to optimize engine parameters for hydrogen and ammonia.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2021**: The project initiated by Wärtsilä and its partners aims to explore funding possibilities throughout the year to support the hydrogen project in Vaasa. Specific investment amounts were not detailed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2021**: A letter of intent was signed among Wärtsilä and its partners to cooperate on the hydrogen project, marking the official start of the initiative.

- **July 2021**: Wärtsilä reported on the progress of its engine testing and the development of hydrogen-based energy solutions, indicating ongoing advancements in the project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
