### Evolutionary Timeline of CNGR Finland Oy Facility in Hamina, Finland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility in Hamina is planned to produce precursor cathode active material (pCAM) as part of a larger battery material cluster in Europe. 
   - The initial production capacity is designed to meet the cathode material needs of over 750,000 full electric vehicles per year.
   - There is potential for future expansion of the production capacity.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The project has an initial investment of approximately €774 million, which is tied to the establishment of the pCAM plant in Hamina.
   - The facility is part of a joint venture between Finnish Minerals Group (owning 40%) and CNGR Advanced Material, indicating significant financial backing for the project.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - The project was announced on 31-07-2023, with the approval of the Board of Directors marking a significant milestone for the expansion overseas.
   - Preparatory works at the Keltakallio industrial site began at the end of 2021, with site levelling and preparation continuing through summer 2023.
   - The project is currently moving into the detailed planning and permitting phase, where construction and environmental permit applications will be submitted.

4. **Labour:**
   - Specific job creation or job loss details have not been provided in the article, but the establishment of the facility is expected to contribute positively to local employment opportunities as the project progresses.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is a joint venture between Finnish Minerals Group and CNGR Advanced Material, with Finnish Minerals Group holding a 40% stake. This indicates that CNGR Advanced Material does not own the facility exclusively.
