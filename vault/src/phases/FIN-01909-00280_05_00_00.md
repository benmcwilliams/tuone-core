### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fortum Facility in Olkiluoto, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2023**: The Finnish Government granted Fortum an operating licence for the life extension of the Loviisa nuclear power plant, which includes the Olkiluoto facility. The Olkiluoto plant consists of three units: Olkiluoto 1 and 2 (880 MW each) and Olkiluoto 3 (1.6 GW). The operating licence allows energy production at the Loviisa plant until the end of 2050, while the Olkiluoto plant continues to contribute significantly to Finland's nuclear energy capacity.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2023**: Fortum announced a two-year feasibility study in collaboration with Westinghouse Electric Company to explore new nuclear build opportunities in Finland and Sweden. This study will assess the prerequisites for constructing new reactors, including small modular reactors (SMRs) and conventional large reactors. Specific investment decisions related to this study will be made at a later stage.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2023**: The Finnish Government approved the life extension of the Loviisa nuclear power plant, which is relevant to Fortum's operations at the Olkiluoto facility. The new operating licence extends the operational timeline for the Loviisa units until 2050 and for the Olkiluoto facility until 2055 for decommissioning preparations.
