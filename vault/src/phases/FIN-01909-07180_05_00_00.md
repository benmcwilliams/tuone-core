### Evolutionary Timeline of the TVO Facility in Olkiluoto, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: The Olkiluoto 3 (OL3) nuclear reactor, the largest in Europe, entered regular production after extensive delays. It has a capacity of 1,600 megawatts (MW) and is expected to produce around 14% of Finland's electricity. The reactor is designed to operate for at least the next 60 years.
- **October 2023**: OL3 accounts for approximately 14% of Finland's overall power demand. The two existing reactors (Olkiluoto 1 and 2) have been modernized and currently operate at a capacity of 890 MW each, with plans for further uprating to about 970 MW.
- **November 2023**: OL3 experienced an unexpected outage due to a turbine problem but was expected to return to production shortly thereafter.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2023**: TVO has invested about EUR 50 million annually in the modernization of Olkiluoto 1 and 2, which has allowed for power uprates from their original capacities. Additionally, an uprating of 80 MW for both units is being investigated.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2021**: OL3 attained first criticality.
- **March 2022**: OL3 was connected to the Finnish power grid.
- **April 2023**: Regular electricity production commenced after 18 years of construction and testing.
- **May 2023**: OL3 officially entered commercial operation.
- **November 2023**: OL3 faced an outage due to a turbine issue, with plans for a partial restart shortly thereafter.
