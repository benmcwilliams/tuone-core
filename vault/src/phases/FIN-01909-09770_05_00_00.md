### Evolutionary Timeline of the Posiva Facility in Olkiluoto, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2016**: Construction work on the used fuel encapsulation plant and final disposal facility began at Olkiluoto.
- **Mid-2020s**: The repository is expected to begin operations.
- **August 2024**: Posiva announced the start of a trial run for the final disposal facility, which includes the testing of equipment and systems without used fuel. The trial run involves depositing four canisters in a final disposal tunnel and retrieving a damaged canister.
- **Ongoing**: The final disposal system includes a sealed iron-copper canister, bentonite buffer, and other safety structures, with operations expected to last for about 100 years before closure.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2015**: The government granted Posiva a construction licence for the project, indicating prior investment and planning stages.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **30 December 2021**: Posiva submitted its application for an operating licence to the Ministry of Economic Affairs and Employment.
- **May 2022**: STUK (Finland's Radiation and Nuclear Safety Authority) began its review of Posiva's application after determining sufficient material was provided.
- **September 2023**: STUK announced that its safety assessment would take longer than expected, leading to a request for an extension of the deadline for its opinion on the application to the end of 2024.
- **January 2024**: STUK requested an extension for its opinion on the operating licence application until the end of 2025 due to incomplete materials necessary for the safety assessment.
