### Evolutionary Timeline of the Posiva Oy Facility in Olkiluoto, Finland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **June 2024:** Completion of the manufacture of the Onkalo Buffer Installation System (BIS). The BIS consists of several devices including an installation device, a transfer device, and a transport container, designed to facilitate the disposal of used nuclear fuel at a depth of about 450 meters. The system is intended to ensure that bentonite fills the space between the canister and the surrounding rock, acting as a barrier to water movement and providing stability over time.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **December 2013:** Posiva Oy submitted its construction license application for the repository project to the Ministry of Employment and the Economy, following extensive studies of the rock at Olkiluoto and the Onkalo underground laboratory.
   - **November 2015:** The Finnish government granted a construction license for the repository project, allowing for further development and investment in the facility.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **2000:** The site for Posiva's repository at Eurajoki, near the Olkiluoto nuclear power plant, was selected.
   - **2001:** The Finnish parliament approved the decision-in-principle for the repository project.
   - **2016:** Construction work on the repository began after receiving the construction license.
   - **Mid-2020s:** The repository is expected to begin operations.

### Technology and Operations
- "The disposal system consists of a tightly sealed iron-copper canister, a bentonite buffer enclosing the canister, a tunnel backfilling material made of swellable clay, the seal structures of the tunnels and premises, and the enclosing rock."
