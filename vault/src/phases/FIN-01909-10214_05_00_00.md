### Evolutionary Timeline of Teollisuuden Voima Oyj Facility in Olkiluoto, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: The Olkiluoto 3 (OL3) nuclear power plant began commercial production on April 16, 2023, after over a decade of delays. It is the largest nuclear power plant in Europe and the third largest globally, with a production capacity of 1600 Megawatts (MW). The plant is expected to supply approximately 15% of Finland's electricity demand.
- **December 2021**: OL3 attained first criticality.
- **March 2022**: The reactor was connected to the grid.
- **September 2022**: The plant operated at full capacity for the first time.
- **May 2023**: The EPR entered commercial operation.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2005**: Construction of OL3 began under a fixed-price turnkey contract with the Areva-Siemens consortium. The project faced significant delays and cost overruns, with the final price estimated at around $11 billion, three times the initial estimate.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 16, 2023**: Regular electricity production commenced.
- **May 1, 2023**: Official commercial operation began.
- **April 2023**: TVO issued an acceptance certificate to the Areva-Siemens consortium for OL3.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Teollisuuden Voima Oyj (TVO) is 27% owned by Fortum and 57% owned by Pohjolan Voima Oy, which is primarily owned by pulp and paper manufacturers UPM Oyj and Stora Enso Oyj.
