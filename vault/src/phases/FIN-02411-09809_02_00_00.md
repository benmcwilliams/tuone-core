### Evolutionary Timeline of the QHeat Facility in Vantaa, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2023**: The QHeat facility, which is Finland's first geothermal heating plant, began operations. It consists of three heating wells approximately 800 meters deep, with an estimated annual energy production of 2,600 MWh, sufficient to meet the heating demand of about 130 private houses. The geothermal heat wells are designed to help Vantaan Energia transition to renewable energy sources and reduce carbon emissions significantly.

- **October 2023**: QHeat developed a second geothermal well for energy storage associated with the Lounavoima waste incineration plant. The first well, drilled to 1600 meters, has been in test use for over a year. The new well drilling aims to expand the energy storage capacity to a total of six wells, which will provide enough heat to supply 10 GWh per year, catering to the annual heat demand of 400-500 detached houses. The drilling technology has advanced, allowing for deeper wells (up to 2000 meters), which can produce significantly more energy compared to traditional shallower wells.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: The facility received investment aid for the geothermal heating project, which is part of Vantaan Energia's broader strategy to reduce its carbon footprint and achieve carbon neutrality by 2030.

- **October 2023**: The development of the second geothermal well involved the use of the GM2000 drilling rig, a new technology designed to enhance drilling efficiency and minimize downtime. This investment in advanced drilling technology is expected to improve the overall energy output from geothermal sources.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2023**: The QHeat facility officially started operations, marking a significant milestone as the first geothermal heating plant in Finland.

- **October 2023**: The drilling of the second geothermal well was completed, with plans to expand the energy storage facility to include a total of six wells. The first well has been operational for over a year, and the new wells are expected to be operational by winter 2023.
