### Evolutionary Timeline of Vantaan Energia Facility in Vantaa, Finland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **March 2023:** The Vantaan Energia facility began operations of the first geothermal heating plant in Finland. The plant consists of three heating wells approximately 800 meters deep, with an estimated annual energy production of 2,600 MWh, sufficient to meet the heating demand of about 130 private houses. The geothermal heat wells are designed to significantly reduce carbon emissions, with geothermal heat emissions being up to 95% lower than those from fossil fuels.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **March 2023:** Vantaan Energia has made significant investments aimed at reducing its carbon footprint in district heating. The company is focused on transitioning to renewable energy sources and phasing out fossil fuels in energy production as part of its climate objectives, targeting carbon neutrality by 2030. Specific investment aid for the geothermal project was previously reported, although exact figures were not disclosed.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **March 2023:** The geothermal heating plant officially started operations. This marks a significant milestone as it is the first instance of geothermal heat being utilized in a district heating network in Finland. The completion of drilling for the first thermal well was also noted in prior reports.
