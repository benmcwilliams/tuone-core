### Evolutionary Timeline of Kauhanummi Oy Facility in Palloneva, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date: September 2021**  
  The facility is part of a consortium planning to build a **500 MW solar power plant** in Palloneva, utilizing **500 hectares** of abandoned peatland. The project aims to achieve a **levelized cost of energy** estimated at less than **€0.04/kWh**. Additionally, the consortium intends to construct a **45 MW wind power plant** in the area.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date: September 2021**  
  The total investment for the solar and wind facilities is projected to be **€400 million**. The consortium is actively seeking to secure **power purchase agreements (PPAs)** from local utilities and large energy-intensive companies to support the financial viability of the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date: September 2021**  
  The project is currently under review by local authorities, with the aim to complete the permitting process by **2022 or the first half of 2023**. If successful, construction is expected to start in **2023**, with completion anticipated in **2024**.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date: September 2021**  
  While specific job creation figures are not mentioned, the development of the solar and wind facilities is expected to generate employment opportunities during the construction phase and potentially in ongoing operations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date: September 2021**  
  Kauhanummi Oy is part of a **joint venture** within an international consortium that includes De Vrije Wind B.V. and Kaskisten Tuulivoima Oy. The facility is not owned exclusively by Kauhanummi Oy but is shared among the consortium members, with Kauhanummi Oy owned by **Marko Nummijärvi**.
