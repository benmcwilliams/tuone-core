### Evolutionary Timeline of the ATP Palloneva Facility in Palloneva, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **29-09-2021**: Announcement of a 500 MW solar power plant project in Palloneva, developed by ATP Palloneva Oy. The facility will occupy 500 hectares of abandoned peatland and aims to have a levelized cost of energy (LCOE) estimated at less than €0.04/kWh. The project is also considering the potential for green hydrogen production and energy storage solutions.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **29-09-2021**: The total investment for the solar and a planned 45 MW wind power plant is projected to be €400 million. The consortium is actively seeking additional investors and power purchase agreements (PPAs) to secure financing for the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **29-09-2021**: The project is under review by local authorities, with the permitting process expected to be completed in 2022 or the first half of 2023. If successful, construction could start in 2023, with completion anticipated in 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **29-09-2021**: While specific job creation figures are not mentioned, the construction and operation of a large-scale solar facility typically imply significant job opportunities during both phases.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **29-09-2021**: The facility is developed by ATP Palloneva Oy, a special purpose vehicle (SPV) that includes De Vrije Wind B.V. (which owns a third of the SPV) and Finnish companies Kaskisten Tuulivoima Oy and Kauhanummi Oy. This indicates a joint venture ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by a single entity.

### Summary
The ATP Palloneva facility is a significant clean technology project in Palloneva, Finland, focusing on solar energy production with a capacity of 500 MW. The project is backed by a consortium of companies and is in the permitting phase, with substantial investments planned. The ownership structure is a joint venture, indicating collaboration among multiple stakeholders.
