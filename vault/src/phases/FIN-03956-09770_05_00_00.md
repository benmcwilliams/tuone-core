### Evolutionary Timeline of the Posiva Facility in Eurajoki, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2000**: The site for Posiva's repository at Eurajoki, near the Olkiluoto nuclear power plant, was selected.
- **2001**: The Finnish parliament approved the decision-in-principle for the repository project.
- **2013**: Posiva submitted its construction licence application to the Ministry of Employment and the Economy.
- **2015**: The government granted a construction licence for the repository project.
- **2016**: Construction work on the repository began.
- **2023 (April)**: A trial run at the repository commenced, testing the final disposal facility's operations without used fuel. The trial involves depositing four canisters in a final disposal tunnel and sealing it with bentonite clay and a concrete plug. This trial run is a comprehensive test of the facility's systems and equipment.
- **Mid-2020s**: The repository is expected to begin operations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2013**: The construction licence application indicates significant investment in the facility's development.
- **2021 (December)**: Posiva submitted an application for an operating licence for the used fuel encapsulation plant and final disposal facility, indicating ongoing investment in operational readiness.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2000**: Site selection for the repository.
- **2001**: Approval of the decision-in-principle by the Finnish parliament.
- **2013**: Submission of the construction licence application.
- **2015**: Granting of the construction licence.
- **2016**: Start of construction work.
- **2021 (December)**: Application for an operating licence submitted.
- **2023 (April)**: Start of the trial run at the repository.
- **2024 (expected)**: Final decision on the operating licence application by the government, pending a positive opinion from STUK.

#### 4. Labour
- **2023 (April)**: The trial run is described as a significant step for Posiva's operations, indicating potential job creation as the facility moves towards actual final disposal operations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Ownership**: The Posiva facility is jointly owned by Finnish nuclear utilities Fortum and TVO.
