### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fortum Facility in Loviisa, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **1977-1981**: The Loviisa nuclear power plant, comprising two VVER-440 type pressurized water reactors, began commercial operations with Unit 1 in 1977 and Unit 2 in 1981.
- **02/2023**: The Finnish Government granted Fortum an operating license for the life extension of the Loviisa plant, allowing energy production until the end of 2050.
- **04/2023**: Fortum received a new operating license for its low and intermediate-level waste (LLW/ILW) disposal facility at Loviisa, extending its operation until 2090.
- **06/2023**: Fortum and Westinghouse began exploring new build opportunities for nuclear energy in Finland and Sweden, including the potential for small modular reactors (SMRs).
- **09/2024**: Fortum successfully loaded new fuel from Westinghouse into the Loviisa reactor, marking a significant milestone in diversifying fuel supply away from Russian sources.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05/2024**: Fortum announced plans to build a hydrogen production pilot plant in Loviisa, with a total R&D cost of around €17 million. Construction is set to begin in summer 2024, with commissioning expected in late 2025.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **02/2023**: The Finnish Government approved the life extension of the Loviisa nuclear plant.
- **04/2023**: New operating license granted for the LLW/ILW disposal facility.
- **06/2023**: Fortum and Westinghouse initiated a feasibility study for new nuclear builds.
- **05/2024**: Announcement of the hydrogen production pilot plant project.
- **09/2024**: Successful loading of Westinghouse fuel into the Loviisa reactor.

#### 4. Labour
- **05/2024**: The hydrogen production pilot plant is expected to create jobs related to its construction and operation, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Fortum operates the Loviisa facility exclusively, with no mention of joint ventures or shared ownership in the articles reviewed. The facility is fully owned by Fortum, which also manages the associated waste disposal facilities.
