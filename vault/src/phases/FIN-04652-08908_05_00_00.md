### Evolutionary Timeline of Fortum Power and Heat Oy Facility in Loviisa, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2023**: The Finnish government granted a new operating license to Fortum Power and Heat Oy for its two Loviisa reactors, which are twin 507-MWe VVER-440/V213 units. This license extends their operational life by an additional 20 years, allowing operations until 2050. Fortum projects that Loviisa will produce up to 170 terawatt-hours of electricity from 2030 to 2050.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2022**: Fortum announced its decision to apply for license renewals to support Finland’s and Europe’s carbon neutrality targets and to enable the building of a reliable, competitive, and sustainable energy system. This decision is tied to the ongoing assessment of the economic viability of new nuclear construction in Finland and/or Sweden.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2020**: Fortum initiated an environmental impact assessment procedure at the Loviisa plant with the aim of operating the units until 2050.
- **March 2022**: Application for license renewals was submitted.
- **February 2023**: The new operating license was granted, allowing operations to continue until 2050.
