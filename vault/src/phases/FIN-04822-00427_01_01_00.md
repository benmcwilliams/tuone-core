### Evolutionary Timeline of the Keliber Facility in Kokkola, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **29-11-2022**: Keliber announced plans to mine spodumene and build a lithium hydroxide refinery at Kokkola Industrial Park (KIP). The refinery aims to produce battery-grade lithium hydroxide, with a planned annual production capacity of 15,000 tonnes starting in 2025. The facility will utilize a caustic soda-based process for sustainable and energy-efficient production. A concentrator plant is also planned to be built approximately 60 kilometers away as an intermediate processing step.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **29-11-2022**: The article does not specify exact investment amounts but indicates that the construction of the refinery is a significant milestone in Keliber's lithium project, which is moving into the implementation phase. The project is expected to contribute positively to the Finnish battery cluster and the Kokkola region.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **29-11-2022**: Preliminary work for the construction of the lithium hydroxide refinery is expected to start in the same year. The refinery is scheduled to ramp up production in 2025. The project is contingent upon obtaining the necessary environmental permits.

#### 4. Labour
- **29-11-2022**: The project is expected to create job opportunities and enhance the vitality of the Kokkola region, although specific job creation numbers are not provided.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **29-11-2022**: Keliber is a subsidiary of Sibanye-Stillwater, a multinational mining and metals company. The Finnish Minerals Group, a state-owned company, is the second-largest shareholder in Keliber. This indicates a joint ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by Keliber.
