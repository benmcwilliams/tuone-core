### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hycamite Facility in Kokkola, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2021**: Hycamite opens a new hydrogen facility in Kokkola, utilizing a novel methane pyrolysis technology that breaks down methane from natural gas and biogas into hydrogen and solid carbon. This technology is based on research from the University of Oulu and aims to support the hydrogen industry and battery production. The facility is preparing to start an industrial pilot plant in the Kokkola Industrial Park (KIP), with large-scale hydrogen production planned for the next few years.

- **02/2023**: Hycamite's technology is highlighted for its ability to produce low-carbon or carbon-negative hydrogen, requiring only 13% of the energy needed for traditional electrolysis. The facility's production is environmentally friendly, with no atmospheric emissions, and the solid carbon produced can be used in various applications, including Li-ion batteries.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **02/2023**: Hycamite secures up to €10 million in capital loans from the Climate Fund to accelerate the development of its facility. This funding is aimed at increasing the production capacity of zero-emission hydrogen and industrial-grade solid carbon.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones

- **02/2023**: Progress in the preparation of the Carbon Storage Facility (CSF) is noted, indicating advancements towards the operational goals of the facility.
