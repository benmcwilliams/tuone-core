### Evolutionary Timeline of the Prime Capital Facility in Kristinestad, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2022**: Prime Capital, through its Prime Green Energy Infrastructure Fund (PGEIF), announced a joint venture with CPC Finland to develop a green hydrogen and e-methane plant with an electrolyser capacity of **200 MW** close to Kristinestad. The facility is part of a larger project with a total generating capacity of over **1 GW**. Construction is scheduled to start in **2024**, with commissioning expected in **2025**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2022**: The joint venture with CPC Finland includes the provision of **600 MW** of renewable energy projects to meet at least **80%** of the plant's electricity demand. The investment is part of a broader strategy to tap into the growing demand for hydrogen and synthetic fuels in Europe, particularly in light of geopolitical events such as the Ukraine war.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2022**: Prime Capital entered into a joint venture with CPC Finland to develop the green hydrogen/e-methane plant. The permitting process for the plant has already begun, with construction set to start in **2024** and commissioning planned for **2025**.
