### Evolutionary Timeline of ABB Facility in Porvoo, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production**: The ABB facility in Porvoo produces wiring accessories and installation materials specifically for the smart building market in the Nordics.
- **Carbon-Neutral Operations**: As of October 2024, the facility has achieved carbon-neutral status by utilizing 100% renewable energy sources. 
- **Energy Efficiency Improvements**: The facility implemented various technologies, including a geothermal system, energy recycling, solar technologies, and electric vehicle (EV) charging stations. Total energy efficiency increased by 21%, and CO2 emissions were reduced by 636 metric tons.
- **Energy Recovery Systems**: The facility achieved 93% energy recovery by utilizing waste heat from air compressors and cooling networks. A rotary screw compressor was installed to capture heat that would otherwise dissipate into the atmosphere.
- **Geothermal System**: A geothermal system was installed to store ground heat for winter use, reducing district heating needs by 844 MWh thermal per year and cutting CO2 emissions by an additional six metric tons annually.
- **Photovoltaic System**: A 375 kW ground-based photovoltaic system was installed, generating 238 MWh per year and reducing emissions by 59 metric tons of CO2 annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Renewable Energy Contract**: The facility utilized a renewable energy contract from its energy supplier, which was a key aspect of achieving carbon neutrality.
- **OPEX-Financing Model**: ABB’s local real estate organization provided an OPEX-financing business model to support the facility's transformation.
