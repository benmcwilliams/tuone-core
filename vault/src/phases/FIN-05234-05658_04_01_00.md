### Evolutionary Timeline of the Neste Facility in Porvoo, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2022**: Neste's Porvoo refinery began using 100% renewable electricity, achieved through various power purchase agreements (PPAs) with wind and hydropower suppliers.
- **May 2023**: Neste announced plans to construct a 120 MW renewable hydrogen electrolyser at the Porvoo facility, aimed at replacing hydrogen produced from fossil feedstocks. This project is part of a broader initiative to transform the refinery into the most sustainable in Europe by 2030.
- **October 2024**: Neste decided not to proceed with the 120 MW electrolyser project due to challenging market conditions and financial performance, following the completion of the basic engineering phase.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2022**: Neste received €27.7 million in public funding from Business Finland for its renewable hydrogen projects at the Porvoo refinery.
- **May 2023**: The electrolyser project received the Important Project of Common European Interest (IPCEI) status from the European Commission, which facilitated additional funding from the Finnish government amounting to $30.5 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Early 2025**: Neste is set to start receiving green electricity from Ilmatar's wind farms under a PPA, which will supply approximately 200 GWh annually, covering about 17% of the refinery's electricity consumption.
- **Mid-2022**: Power deliveries from the Mastokangas wind farm began, contributing approximately 215 GWh annually, which corresponds to 18% of the refinery's electricity needs.
- **January 2022**: The Porvoo refinery achieved its target of using 100% renewable electricity.
