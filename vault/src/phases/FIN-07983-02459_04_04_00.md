### Evolutionary Timeline of the Sunfire Facility in Tampere, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2024**: Sunfire announced it will deliver 50 MW of electrolyzer capacity to Ren-Gas's e-methane plant in Tampere, Finland. The delivery will consist of five 10 MW pressurized alkaline electrolysis modules. The facility is designed to produce e-methane, providing around 200 GWh of transport fuel and 180 GWh of district heating. The project is currently in the feasibility stage and has received an environmental permit.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2024**: Sunfire claimed to have become one of the best-capitalized electrolyzer manufacturers in the industry after securing €500 million ($528 million) in grants and new investments. This financial backing supports their ongoing projects, including the one in Tampere.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2024**: Sunfire was selected by Ren-Gas to supply the electrolyzer modules for the Tampere facility. The project is set to begin construction in 2025, with expectations for commercial operation in 2027.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2024**: While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned, the announcement of the facility's development and the involvement of Sunfire suggests potential job creation in the region, particularly in the construction and operational phases of the plant.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2024**: The facility is a project of Ren-Gas, with Sunfire supplying the electrolyzer technology. There is no indication of joint ownership; thus, it can be inferred that Sunfire does not own the facility exclusively but is a key technology provider for Ren-Gas's project.
