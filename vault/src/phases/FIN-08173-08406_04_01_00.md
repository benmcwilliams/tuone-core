### Evolutionary Timeline of the Blastr Facility in Inkoo, Finland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** November 2024
   - **Details:** Blastr is developing a facility in Inkoo, Finland, expected to produce 2.5 million tonnes of ultra-low CO2 steel annually once fully operational. The facility will focus on producing hot-rolled coil-grade steel with CO2 emissions of less than 500kg CO2e per tonne, significantly lower than the conventional steelmaking process which emits 2,200kg CO2e per tonne.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** November 2024
   - **Details:** The facility is part of a strategic partnership with Interfer Edelstahl, which aims to promote green steel. The partnership indicates a commitment to invest in and develop green steel production, although specific investment amounts tied to the facility's expansions were not disclosed.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** November 2024
   - **Details:** A Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) was signed between Blastr and Interfer Edelstahl to deliver 150,000 tonnes of green hydrogen-derived steel annually. Discussions for a binding agreement are set to begin in early 2025. This partnership follows a similar agreement with Knauf Interfer to explore supplying 100,000 tonnes of hydrogen-based steel per year to Central Europe.
