### Evolutionary Timeline of the Smart Windpower Facility in Pyhajoki, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date**: May 2021
  - The Smart Windpower facility is involved in the development of the Puskakorpi wind farm, which will feature 16 Cypress turbines supplied by GE Renewable Energy, with a total production capacity of 88MW. The wind farm is expected to generate enough renewable energy to power approximately 55,000 households in Finland.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date**: May 2021
  - The project was co-developed by Smart Windpower, Mincovest, and GE Renewable Energy, and was acquired by Foresight Energy Infrastructure Partners (FEIP). The financing for the project includes long-term project finance debt, with Societe Generale acting as the sole lender. The project is part of Foresight's first onshore wind investment in Finland.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date**: May 2021
  - Financial close was reached, allowing the project to proceed to construction. The commercial operations of the wind farm are expected to commence in late 2022, with the installation of the first onshore wind turbine anticipated to begin in early 2022.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date**: May 2021
  - While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned, the involvement of experienced contractors such as Suvic and Ramboll suggests that the project will contribute to local employment opportunities during the construction phase.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date**: May 2021
  - The facility is not exclusively owned by Smart Windpower; it is a co-development project involving multiple partners, including Mincovest and GE Renewable Energy, and is ultimately owned by Foresight Energy Infrastructure Partners (FEIP).
