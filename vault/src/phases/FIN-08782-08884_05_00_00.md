### Fennovoima Facility Timeline - Pyhäjoki, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/2022**: The Hanhikivi I nuclear power plant was planned to feature a 1,200MW AES-2006 pressurized water reactor, with a maximum thermal power of 3,200MW and a projected service lifetime of 60 years. The facility was intended to have a production capacity of 1.2GW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **03/2022**: Fennovoima terminated its engineering, procurement, and construction (EPC) contract with Rosatom due to increased risks and delays associated with the Ukraine war. This decision indicates a significant shift in investment strategy and development plans for the facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2022**: Despite initial plans to secure a construction license and begin building the Hanhikivi I plant, the future of the project became uncertain following the Russian invasion of Ukraine. The Finnish government indicated that it would be "absolutely impossible" to grant permission for construction.
- **03/2022**: Fennovoima officially scrapped the EPC contract with Rosatom, marking a critical milestone in the project's status and indicating a halt in progress.

#### 4. Labour
- **03/2022**: The termination of the EPC contract was expected to have a significant impact on Fennovoima's employees and the supply chain in the Pyhäjoki region. The CEO acknowledged the negative impacts and emphasized the company's commitment to support its employees during this transition.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **12/2022**: Fennovoima is a Finnish-Russian consortium, with Finnish companies (including Outokumpu, Fortum, and SSAB) holding a two-thirds stake in the project, while the remaining interest is held by Rosatom’s subsidiary, RAOS Voima. This indicates a joint venture ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by Fennovoima.
