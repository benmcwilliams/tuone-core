### Evolutionary Timeline of the RAOS Project Facility in Pyhäjoki, Finland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 12-04-2022
   - **Details:** The RAOS Project facility is focused on developing the Hanhikivi I nuclear power plant, which will feature a 1.2GW AES-2006 pressurized water reactor. The reactor has a maximum thermal power of 3,200MW and a projected service lifetime of 60 years. Preparatory works, including cabling and excavation, have begun at the site.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 12-04-2022
   - **Details:** The project is commissioned by Fennovoima, a Finnish-Russian consortium, with Finnish companies (Outokumpu, Fortum, and SSAB) holding a two-thirds stake. The remaining interest is held by Rosatom’s subsidiary RAOS Voima. However, the future of the project is uncertain due to potential withdrawal of Finnish stakeholders and the impact of geopolitical events.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 12-04-2022
   - **Details:** The project is currently in a state of uncertainty regarding government permits for construction, especially following Russia's invasion of Ukraine. The Finnish Economic Affairs Minister indicated that it would be "absolutely impossible" for the government to approve the construction. Fennovoima had anticipated securing a construction license in 2022 to begin building in 2023, but this is now in jeopardy.
