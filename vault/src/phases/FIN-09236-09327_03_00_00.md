### Evolutionary Timeline of the Kemijoki Oy Facility in Taivalkoski, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Established**: 1954
- **Current Production**: Kemijoki Oy operates 20 hydropower plants generating approximately 4,000–5,000 GWh of electricity annually, contributing over 40% of the balancing power produced in Finland.
- **Recent Developments**: 
  - **March 2023**: Initial implementation of the HYCAP (HydroPower Capacity) project at the Kurkiaska power plant, which involves the combined use of electricity storage and power plants to respond to short-term fluctuations in electricity demand and production.
  - **2023**: The Taivalkoski power plant is in the permitting process for a state-of-the-art floating hydraulic fishway to facilitate fish migration past dams.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2023**: The Nordic Investment Bank (NIB) provided a 15-year loan to support Kemijoki Oy’s energy storage technology and other hydropower investments. This funding is aimed at enhancing the company's operational performance and promoting biodiversity.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2023**: HYCAP project initiated at the Kurkiaska power plant.
- **2023**: Ongoing development of migration solutions for all power plants south of Rovaniemi, including the permitting process for the fishway at the Taivalkoski power plant.
