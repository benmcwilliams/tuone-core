### Evolutionary Timeline of the Vattenfall Facility in Korsnäs, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2022**: Vattenfall and Metsähallitus announced plans to build a 1.3 GW offshore wind farm in Korsnäs, which is expected to generate 5 TWh of clean energy annually. The facility is designed to produce fossil-free electricity sufficient for over two million apartments or around 250,000 electrically heated individual houses.
- **December 2024**: The capacity of the Korsnäs wind park is updated to be between 1.3 GW and 2.5 GW, with potential annual fossil-free electricity production increased to 5–7 TWh. The project is currently in the planning phase, with geophysical surveys and environmental impact assessments being conducted.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2022**: The offshore wind facility may entail an investment of between €2 billion and €3 billion. This investment is part of Finland's broader goal to achieve net zero emissions by 2035.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2022**: Announcement of the joint venture between Vattenfall and Metsähallitus to develop the Korsnäs offshore wind farm.
- **December 2024**: The project is in its critical planning phase, with construction expected to begin in the 2030s. A multi-stage permitting process must be completed before construction can start.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2024**: The development of the offshore wind farm is expected to create numerous jobs and opportunities for various industries beyond the energy sector, including services, research, safety, and logistics. Vattenfall is actively engaging with the local community to discuss the potential benefits and job opportunities arising from the project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2022**: The facility is a joint venture between Vattenfall and Metsähallitus, with Vattenfall being a leading offshore wind developer in Europe. Metsähallitus manages the land and water areas of the Finnish state and plays a key role in the project.
