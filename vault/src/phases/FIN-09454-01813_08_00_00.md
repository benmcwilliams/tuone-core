### Evolutionary Timeline of the Neoen Facility in Mutkalampi, Finland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2020**: Neoen announced plans for a wind farm in Mutkalampi with an initial capacity of 250MW, which was later increased to 404MW, making it one of the largest in Finland. The facility will supply clean energy to multiple international companies, including Google, Heineken, Philips, Signify, and Nouryon.
- **April 2021**: The project was confirmed to consist of 69 wind turbines designed for efficiency and minimal noise. The facility is expected to be the largest onshore wind farm in Finland, contributing to 2% of the country's annual energy production.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2021**: Neoen reached financial close for the 404MW Mutkalampi project, securing a €290 million non-recourse senior debt facility from MEAG, alongside financing from Neoen’s own capital. The total projected investment for the wind farm is €478 million, excluding financing costs.
- **April 2021**: Neoen also secured €38 million in VAT funding from Swedish bank SEB.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2020**: Neoen signed a 126MW power purchase agreement (PPA) with a consortium including Heineken, Philips, Signify, and Nouryon, set to begin in Q3 2023.
- **April 2021**: The project reached financial close, and site preparation work commenced. The first stage of energy production is scheduled to start by the end of 2022, with the second stage expected in Q3 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2021**: The project is expected to create jobs during the construction phase, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2020**: The facility is co-developed with Prokon and is owned exclusively by Neoen.
