### QHeat Facility in Koskelo, Finland - Evolutionary Timeline

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production**: The QHeat facility in Koskelo utilizes a deep geothermal energy system, akin to a ground-source heat pump but at greater depths, allowing for higher temperatures to be achieved. The facility is designed to heat buildings by pumping cold water into a well and bringing hot water back to the surface.
- **Production Capacity**: The facility can heat up to four apartment buildings. Future plans include drilling deeper wells (up to 2,000 meters) to expand heat supply capabilities.
- **Updates**: The facility is part of a broader initiative to replace coal-fired heating systems in Finland, with the potential to heat the entire city of Helsinki using fewer wells due to the deeper drilling technology.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investments**: The construction of the Koskelo Geothermal Power Plant was financed partly by NREP Oy, the property owner, and supplemented with public subsidies. NREP is a significant Nordic real estate investor and developer.
- **Subsidies**: The project received public funding, which is common for geothermal projects in Finland, indicating a reliance on both private investment and government support.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Commissioning**: The facility was fully commissioned in January 2020.
- **Future Plans**: There are intentions to drill deeper wells (2,000 meters) to enhance the heat supply, although specific timelines for these expansions were not detailed in the article.

#### 4. Labour

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The facility is not exclusively owned by QHeat; it is a joint venture with NREP Oy, which owns the property and has invested in the project. This partnership indicates a collaborative approach to developing the geothermal energy infrastructure.
