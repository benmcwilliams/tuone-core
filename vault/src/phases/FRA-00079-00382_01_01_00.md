## FRA-00079-00382

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Imerys Facility in Beauvoir, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2022**: Imerys announces plans for the "Emili" project at the Beauvoir site, aiming to mine approximately 34,000 tonnes of lithium annually starting in 2028. The project is expected to support the production of lithium-ion batteries for around 700,000 electric vehicles each year. The facility will utilize underground mining methods to minimize environmental impact and aims to achieve lower CO2 emissions compared to traditional lithium mining operations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2022**: The estimated development and construction costs for the Emili project are around 1 billion euros, although this is a preliminary estimate. The project is supported by the French government, emphasizing its environmental and climate benefits, which will reduce reliance on lithium imports.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2022**: Imerys is currently conducting technical studies at the Beauvoir site, with plans to complete a laboratory phase and an industrial pilot phase before starting production and commercialization within five years. The mining operation is projected to have a lifespan of at least 25 years, with potential extensions to 30 or 35 years.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2022**: The Emili project is expected to create approximately 1,000 direct and indirect jobs across two sites: the mine itself and a nearby plant for purifying minerals and converting them into lithium hydroxide.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2022**: Imerys is the sole owner of the facility, as it operates the existing mine site and is fully responsible for the development and execution of the Emili project.

- "The concentration is said to be 0.9 to 1 per cent – that means you have to mine almost 100 tonnes of rock to get one tonne of lithium."
- "Imerys claims to want to mine lithium with less than half the CO2 emissions of typical existing hard rock lithium operations in the world."
