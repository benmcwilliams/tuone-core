## FRA-00113-01424

### Evolutionary Timeline of the ACC Facility in Bordeaux, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2021**: ACC (Automotive Cells Company) is positioned to produce battery cells and modules, with an initial target capacity of 48 GWh by 2030. The facility in Bordeaux serves as a research and development center, while a pilot site in Nersac is set to start production by the end of 2021. Future mass production is planned in two gigafactories located in Douvrin, France, and Kaiserslautern, Germany, with an initial capacity of 8 GWh.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2021**: The total investment in ACC has increased to over 7 billion euros, attributed to the entry of Mercedes-Benz and associated expansion plans. The project benefits from financial support of 1.3 billion euros from French and German authorities, and it has been approved as an Important Project of Common European Interest (IPCEI).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2021**: Mercedes-Benz acquires a stake in ACC, becoming an equal shareholder alongside Stellantis and Total, each holding 33% of the shares. This partnership aims to enhance ACC's production capabilities and expand its network across Europe. The announcement includes plans for the construction of additional factories to meet the increased capacity targets.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2021**: The entry of Mercedes-Benz into the ACC partnership is expected to create several tens of thousands of jobs in battery cell production in Germany alone, highlighting the potential for significant job creation in the broader European battery production sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2021**: ACC operates as a joint venture with equal ownership among Mercedes-Benz, Stellantis, and Total, each holding 33% of the shares. This collaborative ownership structure is aimed at leveraging the strengths of each partner in the battery production landscape. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic moves related to the ACC facility in Bordeaux, France, as of September 2021.
