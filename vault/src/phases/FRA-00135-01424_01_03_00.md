## FRA-00135-01424

### Evolutionary Timeline of the ACC Facility in Bruges, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Production Capacity**: The facility in Bruges is part of ACC's broader operations, which include a gigafactory in Douvrin, France, with a current production capacity of over 13 GWh per year, expected to be completed by the end of 2024. ACC aims to upgrade the French and German sites to 40 GWh each by 2030, contributing to a total capacity of at least 120 GWh across multiple locations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Secured**: On **February 12, 2024**, ACC secured a loan of €4.4 billion, marking one of Europe’s largest-ever debt raisings in the battery production industry. This funding is aimed at the construction of three gigafactories and R&D, with additional capital injections from shareholders Stellantis, Total, and Mercedes-Benz.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Ownership Structure Change**: Following the capital increase tied to the loan, by the end of March 2024, the ownership structure will be as follows: Stellantis will own 45%, Mercedes-Benz 30%, and Saft (a subsidiary of Total) 25%. This change reflects the commitment of these companies as leading shareholders and customers of ACC’s battery modules.

#### 4. Labour
- **Employment**: ACC employs more than 1,500 people across its operations in France, Germany, and Italy, indicating significant job creation associated with its facilities and expansions.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture**: ACC was created as a joint venture by Total and Stellantis in 2020, with Mercedes-Benz joining in September 2021. The facility in Bruges is part of this joint venture structure, with shared ownership among the three companies.
