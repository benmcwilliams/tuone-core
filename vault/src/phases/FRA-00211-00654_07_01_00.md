### Evolutionary Timeline of the Renault Group Facility in Cléon, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2018**: Renault announces plans to triple the manufacturing capacities for electric motors at the Cléon factory, with the next generation of electric motors set to begin production in 2021.
- **July 2022**: A new production line for electric motors is commissioned at the Cléon plant, initially producing the ‘ePT-160kW’ motor with a capacity of 120,000 motors per year, which could be doubled to 240,000 in the future.
- **2024**: Production of the ‘ePT-100-kW’ motor for the electric Renault 5 is set to begin, contributing to a total production capacity of over one million units at the Cléon plant.
- **2027**: Introduction of a new-generation electric drive system with 200 kW of power, developed in partnership with Valeo, is planned for production at Cléon.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2018**: Renault announces a €1 billion investment in electric mobility, which includes the tripling of production capacities for electric motors at Cléon.
- **July 2022**: A €620 million investment is made for the new production line for electric motors at Cléon, reinforcing its role as a center for electric mobility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2018**: Announcement of the tripling of electric motor production capacity at Cléon.
- **April 2022**: Production of the Mégane E-Tech Electric is paused due to semiconductor shortages, affecting operations at Cléon.
- **July 2022**: Commissioning of a new production line for electric motors at Cléon.
- **October 2023**: Announcement of the E7A motor prototype, with series production expected to begin in 2027.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2021**: An agreement with trade unions results in 1,700 voluntary departures but is offset by plans to hire 2,500 new employees by 2024, indicating a net job increase.
- **April 2022**: The production halt at Cléon leads to short-time work for employees due to supply chain issues.
- **July 2022**: The expansion of electric drive production is expected to increase job opportunities at Cléon.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2023**: Renault officially spins off its EV division, Ampere, which includes the Cléon facility. However, Ampere remains 100% owned by Groupe Renault, indicating that the ownership structure has not changed despite the operational separation.
