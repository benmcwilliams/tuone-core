### Evolutionary Timeline of the Plastic Omnium Facility in Dettingen, FRA

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2020**: Plastic Omnium, in collaboration with ElringKlinger, established the EKPO Fuel Cell Technologies joint venture, focusing on the development, large-scale production, and marketing of fuel cell stacks. The facility in Dettingen is set to produce components for cars, commercial vehicles, buses, trucks, and other mobility applications. 
- **March 2021**: The EKPO Fuel Cell Technologies joint venture officially commenced operations. The Dettingen site has an initial production capacity of 10,000 fuel cell stacks per year, along with key components such as bipolar plates and media modules. Plans for future investments aim to increase production capacity and innovation.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2020**: The acquisition of ElringKlinger Fuelcell Systems Austria GmbH (EKAT) by Plastic Omnium was valued at 15 million euros, complementing its global hydrogen strategy. 
- **March 2021**: The joint venture aims to drive investments to accelerate innovation and extend the commercial pipeline, although specific investment amounts were not detailed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2020**: Announcement of the joint venture EKPO Fuel Cell Technologies between ElringKlinger (60% ownership) and Plastic Omnium (40% ownership).
- **March 2021**: Official start of operations for EKPO Fuel Cell Technologies, with a focus on fuel cells for commercial vehicles and buses, and plans for expansion into passenger cars and special vehicles.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2020**: The joint venture will involve more than 150 employees from ElringKlinger, contributing to the facility's operations and expertise in fuel cell technology.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2020**: The facility operates under a joint venture structure, with ElringKlinger holding 60% and Plastic Omnium holding 40% of EKPO Fuel Cell Technologies.
