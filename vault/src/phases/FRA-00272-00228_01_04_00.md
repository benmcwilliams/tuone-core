## FRA-00272-00228

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Envision AESC Facility in Douai, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2021**: Envision AESC plans to build a battery factory at Renault’s Douai site with an initial production capacity of **9 GWh by 2024**, expanding to **24 GWh by 2030**, and a target of **43 GWh** by 2030.
- **June 2021**: Renault confirms the construction of a "Gigafactory" at Douai, with the same capacity targets as mentioned above.
- **October 2021**: The planned capacity is revised down to **30 GWh by 2029** due to a change in location for the factory.
- **November 2021**: The factory is confirmed to be built on a car park next to Renault’s Douai assembly plant instead of a wooded area, leading to the decision to construct **four smaller plants** totaling approximately **30 GWh**.
- **October 2023**: The European Investment Bank grants €450 million to support the factory, which will produce **9 GWh** initially, with plans to expand to **24-30 GWh by 2030**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2021**: The investment volume for the Douai facility is reported to be around **€2 billion**.
- **November 2021**: Envision AESC plans to invest **€800 million** in the Douai facility.
- **October 2023**: The total investment for the project is estimated at **€1.3 billion**, with the EIB loan covering about one-third of this amount.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2021**: Initial announcement of the battery factory at Douai.
- **June 2021**: Official confirmation of the factory by Renault.
- **October 2021**: Announcement of the downsized capacity and new construction plans.
- **November 2021**: Confirmation of the new location and construction plans for four smaller plants.
- **October 2023**: EIB funding announcement for the factory, with construction expected to begin by the end of 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2021**: The project is expected to create approximately **4,500 direct jobs** in France by 2030.
- **November 2021**: The factory is projected to employ up to **1,200 people** initially.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2021**: Envision AESC is a subsidiary of the Envision Group, which acquired AESC from Nissan in 2018. Nissan retains a **20% minority stake** in AESC.
- **October 2023**: AESC (formerly Envision AESC) continues to operate under the ownership structure established post-acquisition, reaffirming its commitment to battery production for Renault and potentially other automotive manufacturers in the future.

This timeline captures the key developments and changes regarding the Envision AESC facility in Douai, France, focusing on its technology, investments, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure.
