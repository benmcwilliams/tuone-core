## FRA-00272-00228

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Envision AESC Facility in Douai, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2021**: Envision AESC plans to build a battery factory at Renault’s Douai site, targeting an annual production capacity of **24 GWh by 2027** and **43 GWh by 2030**. The factory will primarily supply batteries for Renault's future electric models, including the Renault 5.
- **November 2021**: The planned capacity is revised down to **30 GWh by 2029** due to a change in location and construction plans, which will now involve four smaller plants instead of one large facility.
- **July 2024**: The facility is under construction, with the first production expected to support the launch of the Renault 5.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2021**: The investment for the battery factory is estimated at around **2 billion euros**. French government aid for the EV projects could amount to **200 million euros**.
- **November 2021**: Initial investment for the revised plans is around **800 million euros**.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2021**: Envision AESC's plans for the Douai factory are reported, with official confirmation from Renault shortly after.
- **November 2021**: The project is officially revised to include smaller plants, with construction expected to begin by the end of 2024.
- **July 2024**: Construction is ongoing, with the facility expected to be operational soon.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2021**: The project is expected to create approximately **4,500 jobs** by 2030 in the Douai region, contributing to local economic revitalization.
- **November 2021**: The revised plans indicate that the initial workforce could be up to **1,200 employees**.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2021**: Envision AESC is a subsidiary of the Envision Group, which acquired Nissan's battery subsidiary AESC in 2018. Nissan retains a **20% minority stake** in Envision AESC. The Douai facility is part of a partnership with Renault, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership.
