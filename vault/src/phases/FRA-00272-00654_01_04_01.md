### Evolutionary Timeline of the Renault Group Facility in Douai, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2018**: Renault announced a €1 billion investment to expand the development and production of electrified vehicles at the Douai factory, which will become the second EV manufacturing facility in France.
- **July 2018**: The Douai factory is set to produce the new electric Zoe model, with retooling planned for August 2018 to meet demand.
- **April 2020**: Renault designated the Douai factory to build a new all-electric compact SUV based on the CMF-EV platform, expected to launch in spring 2021.
- **June 2021**: Renault ElectriCity was established, aiming for an annual production of 400,000 electric vehicles by 2025, with Douai producing the Mégane E-Tech Electric and the upcoming Renault 5.
- **April 2022**: Production of the Mégane E-Tech Electric was temporarily halted due to semiconductor shortages.
- **July 2023**: Renault began pilot production of the Renault 5 EV, with series production set for 2024 at the Douai plant.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2021**: Renault announced the creation of a battery factory by Envision AESC at the Douai site, with an investment of €2 billion, targeting a capacity of 9 GWh by 2024 and 24 GWh by 2030.
- **September 2021**: Renault confirmed a €550 million investment to retool the Douai plant for electric vehicle production, including the Mégane E-Tech Electric and Renault 5.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2018**: Announcement of the €1 billion investment for electrification.
- **August 2018**: Retooling of the Douai factory for the new Zoe model.
- **Spring 2021**: Launch of the all-electric compact SUV (unnamed at the time).
- **June 2021**: Establishment of Renault ElectriCity, integrating Douai, Maubeuge, and Ruitz plants.
- **April 2022**: Temporary halt of Mégane E-Tech Electric production.
- **2024**: Series production of the Renault 5 is set to begin.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2021**: The establishment of Renault ElectriCity is expected to create 700 new jobs by 2025, with 350 jobs in Douai.
- **December 2021**: A three-year plan was agreed upon with trade unions, resulting in 1,700 voluntary departures but offset by 2,500 new hires by 2024.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2021**: Renault ElectriCity was created as an umbrella company for electric vehicle production, consolidating operations at Douai, Maubeuge, and Ruitz under Renault Group's ownership.
- **November 2023**: Renault officially spun off its EV division, Ampere, which remains 100% owned by Renault Group, including the Douai facility.

This timeline captures the key developments and changes at the Renault Group facility in Douai, France, focusing on its transition towards electric vehicle production and the associated investments, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure.
