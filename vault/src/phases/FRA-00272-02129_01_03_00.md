## FRA-00272-02129

### Evolutionary Timeline of the AESC Envision Facility in Douai, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Initial Capacity**: The facility is set to launch with a production capacity of **9 GWh** for battery cells, primarily for Renault, starting in **2025**.
- **Future Expansion**: Plans for gradual expansion of the facility's capacity to between **24-30 GWh by 2030** were announced, with earlier ambitions for a capacity of **43 GWh** being scaled back due to site redesigns.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Funding**: On **October 13, 2023**, the European Investment Bank (EIB) granted AESC **€450 million** to support the Douai facility. This includes **€337.2 million** as a direct loan and **€112.8 million** in indirect loans to commercial banks. The total investment for the project is estimated at **€1.3 billion**.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Construction and Production Timeline**: The facility is expected to begin production in **2025**. The project is part of the Renault ElectriCity industrial cluster, which aims to produce **400,000 electric vehicles per year** starting in **2025**.
- **Site Redesign**: The facility's location was changed to a car park next to Renault's Douai assembly plant instead of a wooded area, which contributed to the reduction in planned capacity.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The establishment of the Douai facility is expected to create new jobs with high added value, although specific numbers have not been disclosed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: AESC, which was formerly known as Envision AESC, is a majority-owned subsidiary of the Chinese company Envision. The company was originally founded as a joint venture with Nissan holding a 51% stake, but Envision took over majority ownership in 2018. The ownership structure remains unchanged despite the rebranding back to AESC in 2023.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic decisions regarding the AESC facility in Douai, France, highlighting its role in the broader context of electric vehicle production and battery supply in Europe.
