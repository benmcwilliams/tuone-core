## FRA-00274-00697

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Saft Facility in Douvrin, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2020**: Announcement of pilot production for battery cells at Saft's facility in Nersac, France, with plans for series production in Douvrin starting in **2023**. The Douvrin facility is expected to have a production capacity of **24 GWh**.
- **February 2020**: Confirmation that the Douvrin facility will produce battery cells in conjunction with the Opel site in Kaiserslautern, Germany, leading to a combined production capacity of **48 GWh** across both sites.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2020**: Total announced an investment of approximately **200 million euros** for the pilot production project in Nersac, which is part of the broader investment strategy for the Douvrin facility.
- **February 2020**: The total investment for the battery cell production project, including Douvrin and Kaiserslautern, is reported to be nearly **5 billion euros**.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2020**: Official go-ahead for the battery cell pilot production in Nersac, with Douvrin's series production scheduled to start in **2023**.
- **February 2020**: The joint venture, Automotive Cell Company (ACC), between Saft and PSA is confirmed, aimed at developing electric vehicle battery production in Europe.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2020**: The project is expected to create around **2,000 jobs** at the Kaiserslautern site, with implications for job creation at the Douvrin facility as well, although specific numbers for Douvrin are not detailed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2020**: Saft is part of a **joint venture** with PSA (now part of Stellantis) called Automotive Cell Company (ACC), which will manage the battery cell production activities. Initially, Saft holds a **50% stake** in the joint venture, which is expected to reduce to **33%** during the commercial production phase.
