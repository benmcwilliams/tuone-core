### Evolutionary Timeline of the Comau Facility in Douvrin, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2022**: The Automotive Cells Company (ACC) selected Comau to develop an integrated battery module production line for its planned Gigafactory in Douvrin, France. The production lines are designed for both 8- and 16-cell modules, enabling ACC to produce up to 8-gigawatt hours annually by the end of 2024. The facility is intended to eventually ramp up production capacity to 40 GWh.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2022**: The partnership between ACC and Comau signifies a significant investment in the development of battery production technology in Europe, aiming to establish a local battery ecosystem and reduce reliance on Asian sourcing.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2022**: ACC announced the selection of Comau for the development of the battery production line, marking a key milestone in the establishment of the Gigafactory in Douvrin. The project is part of ACC's broader strategy to become a leader in automotive battery cells and modules in Europe.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2022**: While specific job creation figures were not mentioned in the article, the establishment of the Gigafactory and the development of the production line by Comau are expected to create jobs in the region as the facility ramps up production.
