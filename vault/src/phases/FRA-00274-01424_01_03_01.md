## FRA-00274-01424

### Evolutionary Timeline of the ACC Facility in Douvrin, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2020**: The ACC facility is part of a joint venture established by PSA (now Stellantis) and Total (Saft) to produce lithium-ion battery cells for electric vehicles. Initial plans indicated a production capacity of 8 GWh, with a target to increase to 48 GWh by 2030.
- **April 2021**: Stellantis announces that the Douvrin facility will start production by the end of 2023, with a total capacity of 50 GWh expected from both Douvrin and Kaiserslautern plants.
- **September 2021**: Mercedes-Benz acquires a stake in ACC, increasing the target capacity to at least 120 GWh by the end of the decade.
- **May 2023**: The facility is inaugurated with an initial production capacity of 13 GWh, expected to ramp up to 40 GWh by 2030.
- **March 2024**: ACC announces plans to build additional production blocks, aiming for a total capacity of 65 GWh across its facilities.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2020**: The ACC project receives financial support of €1.3 billion from French and German authorities as part of an IPCEI project.
- **November 2021**: The total investment for the ACC project is reported to exceed €7 billion, bolstered by Mercedes-Benz's entry into the joint venture.
- **February 2024**: ACC secures a loan of €4.4 billion to finance the construction of its gigafactories, with additional capital injections from its shareholders.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2023**: The ACC facility in Douvrin is officially opened, with production expected to commence later in the year.
- **March 2024**: Construction work on the Kaiserslautern and Termoli plants is paused to reassess the direction of battery technology and market demand.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2023**: The Douvrin facility is expected to create at least 600 jobs by 2025 as part of its ramp-up in production.
- **March 2024**: The announcement of a pause in construction at the Kaiserslautern and Termoli sites raises concerns about potential job impacts, although specific figures are not provided.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2021**: Following Mercedes-Benz's entry, the ownership structure of ACC is reported as Stellantis (45%), Mercedes-Benz (30%), and Saft (25%).
- **March 2024**: The ownership structure is confirmed to remain the same after the recent capital increase and loan acquisition.
