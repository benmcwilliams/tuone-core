## FRA-00274-01424

### Evolutionary Timeline of the ACC Facility in Douvrin, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2020**: The ACC facility is part of a joint venture established by PSA (now Stellantis) and Total (Saft) to produce lithium-ion battery cells for electric vehicles. Initial plans indicate a production capacity of 8 GWh, with a target to increase to 48 GWh by 2030.
- **September 2021**: Mercedes-Benz acquires a stake in ACC, raising the target capacity to at least 120 GWh by the end of the decade. The Douvrin facility is set to produce battery cells using NMC (nickel-manganese-cobalt) technology.
- **March 2022**: Plans are confirmed to upgrade the Douvrin facility's capacity from 24 GWh to 40 GWh.
- **May 2023**: The Douvrin facility is inaugurated, with an initial production area of over 60,000 square meters and a planned capacity of 40 GWh by 2030. The first battery cells are expected to be produced by the end of 2023.
- **October 2023**: ACC announces plans to build additional production blocks at Douvrin, aiming for a total capacity of 65 GWh.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2020**: The ACC project receives financial support of €1.3 billion from French and German authorities, with a total investment projected to exceed €5 billion.
- **September 2021**: Following Mercedes-Benz's entry, the total investment estimate rises to over €7 billion.
- **April 2022**: ACC places a major order with Umicore for cathode materials, indicating a long-term supply agreement that supports the facility's production goals.
- **February 2024**: ACC secures a loan of €4.4 billion to finance the construction of its gigafactories, including Douvrin, and to support R&D efforts.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2023**: The ACC facility in Douvrin is officially opened, marking the start of operations, although full commissioning is set for later in the year.
- **End of 2023**: Production of the first battery cells is expected to commence.
- **End of 2024**: The facility aims to reach an annual capacity of over 13 GWh.
- **2025-2026**: Production is expected to ramp up further with the addition of new production lines.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2022**: The Termoli plant in Italy, part of the ACC expansion, is expected to create around 2,000 jobs by 2030, with some workers potentially retrained for positions at the Douvrin facility.
- **May 2023**: The Douvrin facility is projected to create several thousand jobs as it ramps up production.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2020**: ACC is established as a joint venture between Stellantis (PSA and Opel), Total (Saft), and later Mercedes-Benz, with an initial ownership structure of 33% each for Stellantis and Total, and 34% for Mercedes.
- **March 2024**: Following a capital increase, the ownership structure shifts to Stellantis holding 45%, Mercedes-Benz 30%, and Saft 25%.
