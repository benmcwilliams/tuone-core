## FRA-00274-02453

### Evolutionary Timeline of Stellantis Group Facility in Douvrin, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2021**: Stellantis announced plans for a battery factory in Douvrin, which is part of a broader strategy to increase electrified vehicle production. The facility is expected to start production by the end of 2023, contributing to an annual production capacity of 50 GWh when combined with another factory in Kaiserslautern, Germany.
- **June 2023**: The ACC (Automotive Cells Company) gigafactory in Douvrin was inaugurated with an initial production capacity of 13 GWh, which is projected to rise to 40 GWh by 2030. This facility will produce high-performance lithium-ion batteries with a minimal CO2 footprint.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2021**: The Douvrin battery factory project received financial support of €1.3 billion from French and German authorities as part of an Important Project of Common European Interest (IPCEI).
- **June 2023**: Stellantis announced plans to secure approximately 400 GWh of battery manufacturing capacity by 2030 across Europe and North America, with the Douvrin facility being a key component of this strategy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: Announcement of the Douvrin battery factory as part of Stellantis's electrification plans.
- **End of 2023**: Planned start of production at the Douvrin facility.
- **June 2023**: Inauguration of the ACC gigafactory in Douvrin, marking a significant milestone in battery production.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2023**: Stellantis announced that by 2025, at least 600 employees will join the battery manufacturing plant in Douvrin as part of its upskilling and reskilling program, which includes a 400-hour training course for employees.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2021**: The Douvrin facility is operated by the Automotive Cells Company (ACC), a joint venture between Stellantis and Total (Saft). This indicates that Stellantis does not have exclusive ownership of the facility but shares it with its partners.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Stellantis Group facility in Douvrin, France, focusing on its technology, investments, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
