## FRA-00276-01010

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Dubai, FRA

1. **Technology and Operations**
   - **Date**: August 2019
   - **Details**: The facility is part of the Hatta hydroelectric power project, which will produce 250 MW of hydropower using pumped storage technology. The project will utilize water stored in an upper reservoir at the Hatta Dam and will incorporate solar-powered pumps from the Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum Solar Park. The design allows for the water to be reused repeatedly to generate power, with the system capable of reaching 80% of its generation capacity within 90 seconds of startup.

2. **Investment and Development**
   - **Date**: August 2019
   - **Details**: The Dubai Electricity and Water Authority (DEWA) awarded a construction contract valued at Dh1.437 billion (approximately $391 million) for the Hatta hydroelectric project. This investment is part of the Dubai Clean Energy Strategy 2050, which aims to provide 75% of Dubai’s total power generation from clean energy sources by 2050.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**
   - **Date**: August 2019
   - **Details**: The construction contract for the Hatta hydroelectric power plant was awarded to a consortium that includes STRABAG Dubai LLC, STRABAG AG, Andritz Hydro, and Ozkar. EDF is consulting on the project, which is scheduled to be commissioned in early 2024.

4. **Labour**
   - **Date**: August 2019
   - **Details**: DEWA is supporting job creation for UAE nationals as part of the project, which is also aligned with the Hatta Comprehensive Development Plan aimed at developing the area as a tourist center.

5. **Ownership Structure**
   - **Date**: August 2019
   - **Details**: The facility is not exclusively owned by EDF; it is part of a consortium that includes multiple companies, indicating a joint venture structure for the project. EDF's role is primarily as a consultant in the development of the facility.
