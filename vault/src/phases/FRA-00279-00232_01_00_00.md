## FRA-00279-00232

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Eramet Facility in Dunkirk, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2023**: Eramet and Suez announced plans to build a battery recycling facility in Dunkirk, consisting of two stages: 
  - **Stage 1**: A dismantling plant with a processing capacity of 50,000 tonnes of battery modules per year, scheduled to begin operations in **2025**.
  - **Stage 2**: A hydrometallurgical plant to extract and refine metals (nickel, cobalt, lithium) from the black mass, scheduled to open in **2027**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2023**: Eramet received a grant of **80 million Euros** from the European Union and the BPI to partially finance pre-industrial studies, construction of the facilities, and operating costs for the first ten years.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2022**: Initial announcement of the facility's plans by Eramet and Suez.
- **September 2023**: Confirmation of the facility's construction plans and operational timelines.
- **October 2024**: Eramet and Suez suspended the plans for the battery recycling plant due to a lack of ramp-up of battery factories, which affected the supply of raw materials necessary for the recycling process.

#### 4. Labour
- No specific job creation or job loss details were provided in the articles regarding the Eramet facility in Dunkirk.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is a **joint venture** between Eramet and Suez, as indicated by their collaboration on the project.
