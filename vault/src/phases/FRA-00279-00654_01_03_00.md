### Evolutionary Timeline of the Renault Group Facility in Dunkirk, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: Renault Group entered into a commercial partnership with Verkor for the supply of battery cells. The facility is targeted to produce 12 GWh of battery cells annually, sufficient to power over 100,000 Renault vehicles. The overall production target for the gigafactory is 16 GWh. The battery cells will be used in Renault's premium electric cars, including the Alpine brand.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: The partnership with Verkor is part of Renault's strategy to secure the entire value chain for its upcoming Ampere unit, which focuses on electric vehicles and software. Specific financial details of the partnership were not disclosed, but it is noted that this collaboration is crucial for the development of the gigafactory in Dunkirk.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: The partnership with Verkor marks a significant milestone for the Dunkirk facility, as it is now recognized as the primary location for Verkor's gigafactory. The first electric car utilizing Verkor cells is expected to roll off the production line at the Alpine plant in Dieppe in 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2023**: Renault's Ampere unit, which is expected to employ around 10,000 people, is part of the broader strategy to enhance the electric vehicle ecosystem in France. The establishment of the gigafactory in Dunkirk is anticipated to contribute to job creation in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2023**: The facility in Dunkirk is associated with a commercial partnership between Renault Group and Verkor. While Renault is the largest client for the gigafactory, the ownership structure specifics regarding whether Renault exclusively owns the facility or if it is a joint venture with Verkor have not been detailed in the available information.
