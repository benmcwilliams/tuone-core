## FRA-00279-00697

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Saft Facility in Dunkirk, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2020**: Total announced the construction of France’s largest battery storage project in Mardyck, Dunkirk, with a storage capacity of 25MW/25MWh. Saft provided its Intensium Max 20 High Energy battery solution, consisting of 11 integrated 2.3 MWh containers.
- **January 2021**: The 25MW/25MWh battery energy storage system (BESS) became operational, providing grid-balancing services to RTE. The system was built on the site of a former oil refinery owned by Total.
- **December 2021**: TotalEnergies commissioned a new battery with an installed capacity of 61 MW and a total storage capacity of 61 MWh at the Etablissement des Flandres facility in Dunkirk. This system was made up of 27 2.5 MWh containers designed and assembled by Saft.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2020**: The estimated cost of the Mardyck project was reported to be 13 million Euros ($14.5 million U.S.). This project was part of Total's strategy to enhance renewable energy storage solutions.
- **February 2020**: The project that led to the 61 MW battery was secured through a tender held by RTE, indicating a competitive investment environment for energy storage solutions.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2020**: Announcement of the Mardyck battery storage project.
- **January 2021**: The 25MW/25MWh battery system was completed and began operations.
- **December 2021**: The commissioning of the 61 MW/61 MWh battery system marked a significant milestone in the facility's evolution.
