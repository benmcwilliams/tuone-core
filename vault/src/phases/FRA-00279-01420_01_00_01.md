## FRA-00279-01420

### Evolutionary Timeline of the TotalEnergies Facility in Dunkirk, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2021**: The facility launched its first battery energy storage system (BESS) with a capacity of **25 MW** and **25 MWh** of storage. This system was designed to provide grid-balancing services to the national transmission system operator, RTE.
- **December 2021**: The facility expanded its operations by commissioning a larger battery system with an installed capacity of **61 MW** and **61 MWh** of storage. This new system consists of **27 containers**, each with a capacity of **2.5 MWh**. The project aims to enhance the national grid's electricity supply, particularly during winter months.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2021**: The total investment for the initial 25 MW battery energy storage system was reported to be **€15 million** (approximately **$18.2 million**). This investment was part of TotalEnergies' strategy to enhance grid stability and participate in the Frequency Containment Reserves (FCR) market.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2021**: The first phase of the battery storage project (25 MW) was completed and became operational.
- **February 2020**: TotalEnergies secured the project through a tender held by RTE, which laid the groundwork for the subsequent expansions.
- **December 2021**: The facility successfully commissioned the expanded battery system (61 MW), marking it as the largest battery storage project in France.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- TotalEnergies owns the facility exclusively. The battery energy storage systems were developed in collaboration with Saft, a subsidiary of TotalEnergies, which indicates a full ownership structure of the facility by TotalEnergies.
