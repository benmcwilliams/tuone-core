### Evolutionary Timeline of the Renault Group Facility in Flins, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2016**: The Flins facility produced its 50,000th Renault Zoe, indicating a successful production line for this electric vehicle (EV).
- **June 2018**: Renault announced plans to double the manufacturing capacity for the Zoe at the Flins factory, increasing production from 220 to 440 vehicles per day.
- **March 2020**: Production at the Flins plant was suspended due to the COVID-19 pandemic, affecting the manufacturing of the Zoe and other electric vehicles.
- **September 2021**: The Refactory was opened at the Flins site, transforming it into a circular economy hub focused on retrofitting, recycling, and refurbishing vehicles.
- **July 2022**: The Refactory was highlighted as a center for circular economy skills, with plans to refurbish 45,000 vehicles annually and repair batteries.
- **March 2024**: Production of the Renault Zoe is set to cease, making way for new electric models, including the Renault 5, which will be produced in Douai.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2018**: Renault announced a €1 billion investment to expand the development and production of electrified vehicles in France, including the Flins facility.
- **July 2022**: Renault established a new company, "The Future is Neutral," to lead the circular economy initiatives at the Flins site, aiming for significant investments in recycling and refurbishment.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2016**: The Flins facility reached a production milestone with the 50,000th Renault Zoe.
- **June 2018**: Announcement of plans to double production capacity for the Zoe.
- **March 2020**: Temporary closure of the Flins plant due to the COVID-19 pandemic.
- **September 2021**: Opening of the Refactory at Flins, marking a shift towards a circular economy model.
- **March 2024**: Scheduled end of Zoe production at the Flins facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2020**: The COVID-19 pandemic led to the suspension of production, affecting approximately 18,000 employees across Renault's French plants, including Flins.
- **May 2020**: Renault announced a restructuring plan that included cutting 4,600 jobs in France, although they aimed to avoid layoffs through retraining and voluntary redundancies.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Renault Group owns the Flins facility exclusively, with no mention of joint ventures or partnerships regarding ownership.
