### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ineos Facility in Hambach, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2020**: The facility, previously owned by Daimler, was confirmed to be sold to Ineos. Daimler had been producing the Smart EQ electric vehicles at this site. Ineos plans to continue producing the Smart for Daimler until the end of the model cycle, after which the facility will focus on manufacturing the Grenadier off-road vehicle. The part of the plant designated for the EQB electric model is now available for Grenadier production.

- **03/2024**: The last Smart Fortwo rolled off the production line, marking the end of Smart vehicle production at the facility. Ineos will now focus solely on building its own combustion engine vehicles.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2020**: Ineos's acquisition of the Hambach plant was described as a “unique opportunity” to purchase a modern automotive manufacturing facility. The deal was part of Ineos's strategy to build the Grenadier off-road vehicle, although specific investment amounts were not disclosed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2020**: Daimler confirmed the sale of the Hambach plant to Ineos, with the transition expected to occur in the coming weeks.
- **03/2024**: The production of the Smart Fortwo officially ended, transitioning the facility's focus to Ineos's own vehicle production.

#### 4. Labour
- **09/2020**: The transition to Ineos was stated to have secured employment for a total of 1,300 employees at the site, as Ineos committed to continuing production of the Smart vehicles until the end of their model cycle.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **09/2020**: Ineos acquired the Hambach facility from Daimler, indicating full ownership of the site. The facility was previously part of a joint venture involving Daimler and Geely, but Ineos now operates it independently for its own vehicle production.
