## FRA-00430-00521

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mercedes-Benz Facility in Hambach, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2018**: Daimler announces plans to use the Hambach facility to construct a Mercedes compact model of the EQ brand, with an investment of around €500 million. The factory has been producing the electric Smart Fortwo and is preparing to become a hub for electric vehicle production.
- **March 2019**: The Hambach plant continues to produce the Smart EQ Fortwo and is set to produce a compact electric model from Mercedes-Benz, presumably the EQB.
- **July 2020**: Daimler plans to sell the Hambach plant, which has been producing Smart electric vehicles. The future production of the EQB is uncertain.
- **December 2020**: Daimler confirms the sale of the Hambach plant to Ineos, which will continue to produce Smart vehicles until the end of the model cycle.
- **April 2024**: Production of the Smart Fortwo ends after more than 25 years, with the last model rolling off the production line.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2018**: Daimler announces a €500 million investment for the construction of a Mercedes compact EQ model at the Hambach facility.
- **March 2019**: The investment is confirmed to support the production of the EQ model alongside Smart vehicles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2018**: Announcement of the investment and plans for the EQ model production.
- **March 2019**: Confirmation of ongoing production of Smart vehicles and plans for the EQ model.
- **July 2020**: Announcement of the planned sale of the Hambach plant.
- **December 2020**: Official confirmation of the sale to Ineos.
- **April 2024**: The last Smart Fortwo is produced, marking the end of production at the facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2018**: The facility has been operational since 1997 and employs around 1,600 people.
- **December 2020**: After the sale to Ineos, it is confirmed that 1,300 employees will remain at the site under the new management.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2018**: Daimler owns the facility exclusively.
- **December 2020**: The facility is sold to Ineos, marking a shift from exclusive ownership by Daimler to new ownership while continuing to produce Smart vehicles under contract.

This timeline captures the key developments and changes at the Mercedes-Benz facility in Hambach, France, focusing on its transition towards electric vehicle production and the eventual sale of the facility.
