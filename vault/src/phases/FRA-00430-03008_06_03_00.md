## FRA-00430-03008

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Holosolis Facility in Hambach, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2023**: Holosolis announces plans to construct a new solar module manufacturing facility in Hambach, France, with a production capacity of **5 GW**. The facility aims to start commercial operations in **2027**.
- **August 2023**: Holosolis collaborates with Fraunhofer ISE to build Europe’s largest PV factory, focusing on technology selection and factory planning.
- **October 2023**: The facility is set to produce modules based on **tunnel oxide passivated contact (TOPCon)** cells, with a ramp-up to full capacity expected by **2027**.
- **April 2024**: Holosolis files for a construction permit, with plans to start production of photovoltaic panels and cells by **mid-2026**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2023**: Holosolis announces an investment of **€710 million** (approximately **$772.1 million**) for the new facility.
- **October 2023**: Heraeus Group acquires an undisclosed stake in Holosolis to support the development of the 5 GW manufacturing plant, providing silver metallisation pastes essential for solar cell production.
- **April 2024**: Holosolis and Carbon both request construction permits for their respective 5 GW solar panel factories, each expected to invest over **€600 million** (approximately **$641.3 million**).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2023**: Announcement of the new factory in Hambach.
- **April 12, 2024**: Holosolis files for a building permit.
- **July 2024**: The French authorities designate Holosolis' solar factory project as a “project of national interest,” streamlining administrative processes for approvals.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2024**: Holosolis CEO Jacob Boom-Wichers states that the facility will ultimately produce more than **10 million panels** to serve France and Europe, indicating potential job creation, although specific job numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2023**: Holosolis is identified as a spinoff of EIT InnoEnergy, with partnerships involving IDEC Group and TSE. The ownership structure indicates a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership, as Holosolis is supported by multiple stakeholders, including Heraeus Group.
