## FRA-00430-03330

### Evolutionary Timeline of the REC Group Facility in Hambach, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2020**: REC Group announced plans to build a heterojunction solar module factory in Hambach, with an initial production capacity of 2 GWp per year, scaling up to 4 GWp per year by 2025. The facility would cover almost 148,000 m² and include on-site production of cells, assembly of modules, quality control, and logistics.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2020**: The planned investment for the facility was €680 million. This investment was aimed at producing solar modules compliant with French regulations for large-scale PV plants, focusing on low carbon footprints.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2020**: The French National Commission of Public Debate validated the launch of a public consultation for the project, which began on December 14, 2020, and was scheduled to end on February 8, 2021.
- **June 2021**: The start of construction was expected.
- **December 2022**: REC Group announced the cancellation of the factory project due to "various market changes," despite having previously planned to invest €680 million.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2020**: The project was expected to create approximately 1,800 jobs, particularly during the construction phase, in a region affected by economic downturns due to factory closures.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2021**: REC Group, a unit of ChemChina, was acquired by Indian industrial group Reliance for $771 million. However, the ownership structure regarding the Hambach facility was not explicitly detailed in the articles, but REC was initially planning to own the facility.

### Summary
The REC Group facility in Hambach, France, was planned to be a significant solar module manufacturing site with a total investment of €680 million, aiming for a production capacity of 4 GWp per year by 2025. The project was expected to create 1,800 jobs but was ultimately canceled in December 2022 due to market changes. The facility was to be owned by REC Group, which was acquired by Reliance in late 2021.
