## FRA-00430-04008

### HoloSolis Facility Evolution Timeline - Hambach, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2023**: HoloSolis plans to build Europe’s largest PV factory in Hambach, France, with a production capacity of 5 GW per year, producing 10 million solar modules annually. The factory is expected to start operations in 2025 and reach full capacity by 2027. The facility will focus on TOPCon PV cell and panel production and is also working on next-generation solar panels, including perovskite-silicon tandem cells.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2023**: Heraeus, a German metallization paste maker, acquires an unspecified stake in HoloSolis and will provide its metallization technologies for the factory. 
- **11/2023**: Armor Group acquires a 20% stake in HoloSolis, marking its entry into perovskite-silicon tandem panel production. This investment follows Armor's recent shift from organic solar module production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2023**: Announcement of the planned factory in Hambach, with a target opening in 2025 and full operational capacity by 2027.
- **11/2023**: Confirmation of the factory's focus on TOPCon technology and the involvement of Heraeus and Armor Group in the project.

#### 4. Labour
- **09/2023**: The factory is expected to create approximately 1,700 jobs at full capacity in 2027, contributing to local employment and energy sovereignty.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **09/2023**: HoloSolis is a joint venture founded by three entities: EIT Innoenergy, IDEC Group, and TSE. 
- **11/2023**: Following the investments, Heraeus and Armor Group hold stakes in HoloSolis, indicating a shared ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership.
