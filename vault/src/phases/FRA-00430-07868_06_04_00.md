### Evolutionary Timeline of the Heraeus Group Facility in Hambach, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **October 2023:** Heraeus Group, in partnership with Holosolis, announced plans for a photovoltaic (PV) module assembly plant in Hambach, France. The facility will produce modules based on tunnel oxide passivated contact (TOPCon) cells. The plant is set to open in 2025, with a ramp-up to full capacity of 5GW expected by 2027.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **October 2023:** Heraeus acquired an undisclosed stake in Holosolis to support the development of the 5GW manufacturing plant. This investment is part of a strategic move to strengthen their position in the European PV market, which is crucial for CO2 reduction.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **May 2023:** Plans for the assembly plant were initially announced, with Holosolis collaborating with IDEC Group and TSE for the project.
   - **September 2023:** Holosolis announced a research partnership with Fraunhofer ISE to assist in technology selection and planning for the factory during the concept design and construction phases.

4. **Labour:**
   - **October 2023:** While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned, the establishment of the facility is expected to contribute to job creation in the region, particularly in the context of the growing European PV manufacturing sector.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **October 2023:** Heraeus holds an undisclosed stake in the facility through its partnership with Holosolis, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership.
