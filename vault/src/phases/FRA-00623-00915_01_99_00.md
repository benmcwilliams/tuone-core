### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ampere Facility in Lardy, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2024**: Ampere announces a shift from exclusively using nickel-cobalt-manganese (NMC) batteries to incorporating lithium iron phosphate (LFP) batteries in its electric vehicles (EVs). This change is aimed at reducing costs and improving profit margins. The first models equipped with LFP batteries from CATL or LG Energy Solutions (LGES) are expected to launch in early 2026.
- **March 2024**: Ampere announces the construction of an Innovation Battery Cell Laboratory in Lardy, set to open in 2025. This facility will focus on developing prototypes and evaluating battery cells, although Ampere will not produce its own cells.
- **Future Plans**: Ampere aims to produce around one million electric vehicles for the Renault brand by 2031, with a total of seven electric models planned by that year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2024**: Ampere's strategy to adopt LFP technology is part of a broader initiative to localize the entire value chain around LFP technology in Europe, which is expected to enhance competitiveness and reduce costs by approximately 20% starting in 2026.
