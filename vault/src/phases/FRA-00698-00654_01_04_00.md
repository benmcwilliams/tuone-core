## FRA-00698-00654

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Renault Group Facility in Maubeuge, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2018**: Renault announces plans to invest in the Maubeuge facility to produce the next generation of the Kangoo model family, including the electric Kangoo Z.E.
- **June 2021**: The Maubeuge plant is included in the newly created Renault ElectriCity umbrella company, which aims to produce 400,000 electric vehicles per year by 2025. The Maubeuge plant will specifically produce the new electric Kangoo starting in 2022.
- **June 2022**: Renault and Minth announce a joint venture to produce battery housings at the Ruitz plant, supplying the Maubeuge facility with battery housings for electric models.
- **October 2022**: The electric Kangoo E-Tech Electric is highlighted at the Paris Motor Show, showcasing its electric motor and battery specifications.
- **September 2023**: Renault introduces the Grand Kangoo E-Tech Electric, a long version of the Kangoo, which will also be produced at the Maubeuge plant.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2018**: Renault announces a €1 billion investment in electric mobility, which includes funding for the Maubeuge facility.
- **June 2021**: Renault ElectriCity is established, aiming to enhance operational efficiency and competitiveness in electric vehicle production.
- **July 2024**: Renault invests €450 million in the Maubeuge facility to set up a new battery assembly line as part of the ElectriCity initiative.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2018**: Announcement of investment in the Maubeuge facility for the Kangoo model family.
- **June 2021**: Creation of Renault ElectriCity, with Maubeuge as a key production site.
- **June 2022**: Joint venture with Minth for battery housing production begins.
- **October 2022**: Launch of the electric Kangoo E-Tech Electric.
- **September 2023**: Introduction of the Grand Kangoo E-Tech Electric, with production set to start in early 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2021**: Renault plans to create 700 new jobs across the Douai, Maubeuge, and Ruitz plants by 2025, with 350 jobs specifically allocated to Maubeuge.
- **December 2021**: An agreement with trade unions results in 1,700 voluntary departures but is offset by 2,500 new hires by 2024, indicating a net job increase.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2023**: Renault officially spins off its EV division, Ampere, which includes the Maubeuge facility. However, Ampere remains 100% owned by the Renault Group, maintaining the ownership structure of the facility. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and changes at the Renault Group facility in Maubeuge, France, focusing on its evolution in technology, investment, project milestones, labor dynamics, and ownership structure.
