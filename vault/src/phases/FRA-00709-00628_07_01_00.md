### Evolutionary Timeline of the Punch Powertrain Facility in Metz, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2018**: Announcement of a joint venture named Punch Powertrain PSA e-transmissions between PSA and Punch Powertrain, to be based at the PSA factory in Metz. The facility plans to manufacture up to 600,000 electrified powertrains of the e-DCT type with a 48-volt configuration by 2022. Initially, the facility will produce the current electric dual-clutch transmission Hybrid DT2 until the new production lines are established.
- **June 2022**: Update on the Metz plant's focus on producing dual-clutch transmissions (eDCT) for hybrid vehicles as part of the e-transmissions joint venture. The plant's annual production capacity is expected to increase to about 600,000 eDCT transmissions by 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2018**: PSA announced an investment of 80 million euros into the joint venture with Punch Powertrain to support the establishment and operation of the facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2018**: The joint venture is announced and is awaiting official permission to proceed.
- **June 2022**: The Metz plant is confirmed to be transforming to manufacture powertrain components for electrified vehicles, with production of eDCT transmissions ramping up.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2018**: The cooperation between PSA and Punch Powertrain is expected to create 400 jobs at the Metz facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2018**: The facility operates as a joint venture between PSA and Punch Powertrain, indicating that it is not under exclusive ownership by either company but rather a collaborative effort.
