### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Montpellier, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility was part of a joint venture called Montpellier Horizon Hydrogen, which aimed to install an electrolyzer capable of producing 800 kg of hydrogen per day, along with a 2.8 MW solar power plant, hydrogen storage, and distribution stations for refueling buses.
   - The project was intended to support a fleet of hydrogen fuel cell-powered buses, with plans for 51 buses to be deployed across four future bus routes.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The total cost for the hydrogen fuel cell buses was estimated at €29 million, with funding contributions from regional and national transportation authorities and the European Union.
   - The project was initially supported by financial investments, but the operational costs were not adequately addressed, leading to the project's cancellation.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **January 2022:** The city of Montpellier announced the cancellation of the hydrogen bus contract, which was part of the Montpellier Horizon Hydrogen project. The decision was made by the new city president Michaël Delafosse, citing high operational costs compared to battery-powered buses.
   - The project had originally planned for the first 21 hydrogen buses to enter service in 2023, with an additional 30 buses expected in 2025.

4. **Labour:**
   - The cancellation of the project implies potential job losses associated with the planned operations and maintenance of the hydrogen facility and the bus fleet, although specific job creation or loss figures were not mentioned in the article.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility was part of a joint venture between Energies du Sud and Hynamics, a subsidiary of EDF Group. Therefore, EDF did not own the facility exclusively but was involved in a collaborative partnership for the project.
