## FRA-00765-02652

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Armor Facility in Nantes, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2020**: Armor achieved a record efficiency of 26% for its flexible and semi-transparent ASCA organic photovoltaic film under low light conditions (1000 lux). This technology is primarily used for energy-intensive applications, such as powering small connected objects indoors. The facility has installed its ASCA organic photovoltaic film to monitor ambient temperature and humidity in real-time on the production line.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2023**: Armor Group announced the acquisition of a 20% stake in HoloSolis, a French solar panel maker, marking its entry into perovskite-silicon tandem panel production. This strategic move follows the decision to abandon organic solar module production. The investment aligns with Armor's goal of enhancing energy sovereignty and creating non-relocatable jobs.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2023**: Armor Group sold its organic solar panel manufacturing facilities in Kitzingen, Germany, to Hering Group, indicating a shift in focus from organic photovoltaics to new technologies. The company is also involved in the upcoming HoloSolis project, which plans to open a TOPCon PV cell and panel factory in Hambach, France, expected to be operational by 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2023**: The new factory planned by HoloSolis is expected to employ 1,700 people at full capacity by 2027, contributing to job creation in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2023**: Armor Group holds a 20% stake in HoloSolis, indicating a joint venture approach for its new solar panel production strategy, while previously owning the organic solar panel manufacturing facilities exclusively before their sale. 

This timeline captures the significant developments and strategic shifts at the Armor facility in Nantes, France, focusing on its technological advancements, investments, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure.
