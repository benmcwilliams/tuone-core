## FRA-00770-00697

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Saft Facility in Nersac, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2020**: The Saft facility in Nersac is set to begin pilot production of battery cells, with operations scheduled to start in mid-2021. This pilot plant is part of a larger European battery consortium, which includes plans for series production in Douvrin, France, and Kaiserslautern, Germany, starting in 2023 and 2024, respectively. The technology aims for maximum energy efficiency and a lower CO2 balance compared to competitors.
- **April 2021**: The facility's pilot production is confirmed to be a precursor for series cell production. The overall production capacity for the planned battery cell factories in Douvrin and Kaiserslautern is expected to reach 50 GWh, with the Nersac facility contributing to the development of battery cells and modules.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2020**: Total announces an investment of approximately 200 million euros for the pilot production at the Saft facility. The overall investment for the battery cell production project, which includes the facilities in Nersac, Douvrin, and Kaiserslautern, is reported to be nearly five billion euros, supported by 1.3 billion euros from French and German authorities through an IPCEI project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2020**: Official go-ahead for pilot production is announced, with operations expected to commence in mid-2021.
- **April 2021**: Confirmation that the pilot production at Nersac is part of a broader strategy for electrification and battery production, with plans for further battery cell factories in Europe and North America.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2020**: The project is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers related to the Nersac facility are not detailed. The overall project, including the facilities in Douvrin and Kaiserslautern, is anticipated to create around 2,000 jobs at the latter site.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2020**: Saft, a subsidiary of Total, is involved in a joint venture with PSA (now Stellantis) called Automotive Cell Company (ACC). Initially, Saft holds a 50% stake in the joint venture, which is expected to decrease to 33% during the commercial production phase. This indicates that the facility is not solely owned by Saft but is part of a collaborative effort with PSA.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic initiatives surrounding the Saft facility in Nersac, France, highlighting its role in the European battery production landscape.
