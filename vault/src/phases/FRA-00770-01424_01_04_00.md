## FRA-00770-01424

### Evolutionary Timeline of the ACC Facility in Nersac, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2020**: Pilot production in Nersac was initiated, prior to the official establishment of ACC.
- **September 2021**: Mercedes-Benz acquires a stake in ACC, aiming for a production capacity increase from 48 GWh to at least 120 GWh by the end of the decade. The facility in Nersac is part of the production network that includes future gigafactories in Douvrin and Kaiserslautern.
- **February 2024**: ACC secures a loan of €4.4 billion to fund the construction of three gigafactories in France, Germany, and Italy, with the current production capacity at Douvrin set to exceed 13 GWh per year by the end of 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2021**: Following Mercedes-Benz's entry, total investment in ACC rises to over €7 billion, with financial support from French and German authorities amounting to €1.3 billion for the project.
- **February 2024**: ACC secures a €4.4 billion loan, marking one of Europe’s largest debt raisings in the battery industry, to support the construction of additional production blocks.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2020**: Pilot production in Nersac begins.
- **September 2021**: Mercedes-Benz becomes an equal shareholder in ACC, alongside Stellantis and Total.
- **February 2024**: ACC announces plans for additional gigafactories and secures significant funding for expansion.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2021**: The entry of Mercedes-Benz is expected to create several tens of thousands of jobs in battery cell production in Germany, with implications for job creation in France as well.
- **February 2024**: ACC employs over 1,500 people across France, Germany, and Italy, indicating ongoing job creation as the facility expands.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2021**: With the entry of Mercedes-Benz, the ownership structure becomes: Stellantis (33%), Mercedes-Benz (33%), and Total (33%).
- **February 2024**: Following a capital increase, ownership changes to Stellantis (45%), Mercedes-Benz (30%), and Saft (25%), reflecting the evolving investment landscape and commitment from the shareholders.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones of the ACC facility in Nersac, France, highlighting its technological advancements, financial backing, project progress, labor implications, and ownership changes.
