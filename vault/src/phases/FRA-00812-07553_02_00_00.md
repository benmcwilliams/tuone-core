## FRA-00812-07553

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Alec Facility in Nouvelle-Aquitaine, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Focus**: The Alec facility is involved in the development and promotion of geothermal energy, specifically targeting both surface and deep geothermal energy systems.
- **Capacity Goals**: The regional strategy aims for a geothermal energy output of 3,500 GWh by 2030, with significant potential for replacing energy consumption in the tertiary and residential sectors.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Plans**: The Nouvelle-Aquitaine region has allocated a budget of EUR 196 million for renewable energy development from 2022 to 2028, with a priority on geothermal energy.
- **State Support**: In 2024, the French state, through ADEME, financed 71 feasibility studies and 31 investment projects, providing a total of EUR 7.9 million in aid to support geothermal initiatives.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Action Plan**: In late 2023, France’s Ministry of Energy Transition published a national action plan for geothermal energy, which includes a regional action plan for Nouvelle-Aquitaine (2024-2027).
- **Regional Ecosystem Development**: The establishment of a regional ecosystem around the Avenia geothermal cluster is underway, with various stakeholders involved in promoting geothermal energy.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The regional action plan and investments in geothermal energy are expected to create jobs, particularly in training stakeholders and developing the geothermal sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The article does not specify the ownership structure of the Alec facility, but it indicates collaboration among various stakeholders, including the Local Energy and Climate Agency (Alec) and other regional entities.

### Summary
The Alec facility in Nouvelle-Aquitaine is focused on advancing geothermal energy production, with significant investments and a structured action plan aimed at achieving substantial output by 2030. The facility is part of a broader regional initiative to develop renewable energy, particularly geothermal, with state support and a collaborative ecosystem involving multiple stakeholders.
