## FRA-00812-07620

### Evolutionary Timeline of the CRER Facility in Nouvelle-Aquitaine, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The CRER facility is involved in the development and promotion of geothermal energy, focusing on both surface and deep geothermal technologies. The regional strategy aims for a geothermal energy output of 3,500 GWh by 2030, with significant potential for replacing energy consumption in the tertiary and residential sectors.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - A budget of EUR 196 million has been allocated for renewable energy development in the Nouvelle-Aquitaine region from 2022 to 2028, with a priority on geothermal energy projects. In 2024, the State, through ADEME, financed 71 feasibility studies and 31 investment projects, providing a total of EUR 7.9 million in aid.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - The regional action plan for geothermal energy was officially published at the end of 2023, outlining 27 priority actions and 23 complementary actions to accelerate geothermal energy development. The partnership agreement to implement this plan was signed in 2024, marking the beginning of structured efforts to enhance the geothermal sector in Nouvelle-Aquitaine.

4. **Labour:**
   - The action plan emphasizes the training of stakeholders in the geothermal sector, which is expected to create job opportunities as the regional ecosystem for geothermal energy develops.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The CRER facility is part of a collaborative effort involving multiple stakeholders, including the French Agency for Ecological Transition (ADEME), the prefecture, and the Nouvelle-Aquitaine region. It does not appear to be owned exclusively by a single company but is part of a broader regional initiative to promote geothermal energy.

This timeline reflects the ongoing efforts and strategic planning surrounding the CRER facility in Nouvelle-Aquitaine, highlighting its role in advancing geothermal energy in the region.
