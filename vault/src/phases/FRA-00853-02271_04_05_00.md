### Evolutionary Timeline of the HYSETCO Facility in Paris, France

1. **Technology and Operations**
   - **12/2020**: The HYSETCO facility is part of a project to install 11 GWh of electrolyzed hydrogen storage capacity across ten locations in Paris. Each location is expected to feature 20-50 MW of grid-connected electrolyzer capacity and 100-500 MWh of hydrogen gas storage capacity. The facility aims to support a fleet of 50,000 hydrogen-powered taxis by 2030.

2. **Investment and Development**
   - **12/2020**: The project is estimated to require a capital investment of €1 billion, with operating costs projected at €15 million. This investment is part of a broader initiative to utilize decarbonized and green electricity sources for hydrogen production through electrolysis.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**
   - **12/2020**: The project is outlined in a draft version of the ten-year plan by the European Network of Transmission System Operators for Electricity (ENTSO-E), with a commissioning date set for 2022. The project is one of 26 energy storage schemes identified in the ENTSO-E plan.

4. **Labour**
   - **12/2020**: While specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the article, the establishment of the facility and the conversion of the taxi fleet to hydrogen fuel-cell electric vehicles are expected to create jobs in the region.

5. **Ownership Structure**
   - **12/2020**: HYSETCO is identified as a joint venture involving Société du Taxi Électrique Parisien (STEP), Toyota, French energy services company Idex, and Parisian industrial gases supplier Air Liquide. This indicates that the facility is not solely owned by one entity but is a collaborative effort among multiple stakeholders.
