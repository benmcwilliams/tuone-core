## FRA-00853-02445

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Société du Taxi Électrique Parisien Facility in Paris, FRA

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/2020**: The Société du Taxi Électrique Parisien (STEP) proposed a project to convert a fleet of 50,000 taxis to hydrogen-powered fuel-cell electric vehicles. The project includes the installation of 11 GWh of electrolyzed hydrogen storage capacity across ten locations in Paris, each featuring 20-50 MW of electrolyzer capacity and 100-500 MWh of hydrogen gas storage capacity. The project aims to provide a total of 10 GWh of electric storage and a grid re-electrification potential of 5 GWh.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12/2020**: The estimated capital required for the project is €1 billion, with operating costs projected at €15 million. This investment is part of a broader strategy to prevent the curtailment of up to 48 GWh of renewable energy generation by 2030.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2020**: The project was outlined in a draft version of the ten-year plan produced by the European Network of Transmission System Operators for Electricity (ENTSO-E), with a commissioning date set for 2022. The project is one of 26 energy storage schemes identified in the ENTSO-E plan.

#### 4. Labour
- **12/2020**: While specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the article, the scale of the project suggests potential job creation related to the construction and operation of the hydrogen production and storage facilities, as well as the conversion of the taxi fleet.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **12/2020**: The project is proposed by Société du Taxi Électrique Parisien, which is part of a joint venture known as HYSETCO, established with partners including Toyota, French energy services company Idex, and Parisian industrial gases supplier Air Liquide. This indicates that STEP does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a collaborative effort with multiple stakeholders.
