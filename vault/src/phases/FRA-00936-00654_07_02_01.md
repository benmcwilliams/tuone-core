## FRA-00936-00654

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Renault Group Facility in Ruitz, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2021**: Renault Group launches Renault ElectriCity, an umbrella company for electric vehicle production at its Douai, Maubeuge, and Ruitz plants. The target is to produce 400,000 electric vehicles per year by 2025. The Ruitz plant will produce electric components in the future.
- **06/2022**: Renault and Minth announce a joint venture to assemble battery housings at the Ruitz plant, with a planned capacity of 300,000 battery housings per year by 2025. This will support the Douai and Maubeuge plants.
- **11/2023**: The facility is now part of the newly established EV division, Ampere, which aims to produce around one million electric vehicles by 2031.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **06/2021**: Renault announces a comprehensive strategy to cover the entire battery electric vehicle (BEV) value chain, indicating significant investments in electric vehicle production and technology.
- **06/2022**: The joint venture with Minth for battery housing production represents a strategic investment to enhance local production capabilities and reduce carbon emissions.
- **11/2023**: Ampere, the new EV division, is launched with a focus on reducing costs and increasing production efficiency, indicating ongoing investment in the Ruitz facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2021**: Launch of Renault ElectriCity, marking the beginning of a focused electric vehicle production strategy.
- **06/2022**: Announcement of the joint venture with Minth for battery housing production at the Ruitz plant.
- **11/2023**: Official founding of Ampere, which includes the Ruitz facility as part of its operational structure.

#### 4. Labour
- **09/2021**: Renault plans to create 700 permanent jobs by 2025 across the three plants, including Ruitz.
- **12/2021**: An agreement with trade unions indicates that while 1,700 workers will leave voluntarily, 2,500 new hires are planned by 2024, ensuring a net job increase.
- **11/2023**: Ampere employs over 11,000 people, with a significant portion dedicated to the Ruitz facility as part of the ElectriCity hub.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **11/2023**: Although Ampere is now an independent division, it remains 100% owned by the Renault Group, maintaining the ownership structure of the Ruitz facility within the larger corporate framework.
