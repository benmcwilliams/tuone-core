### Evolutionary Timeline of the Symbio Facility in Saint-Fons, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2019**: Symbio was founded as a joint venture between Faurecia and Michelin, focusing on fuel cell manufacturing for the European market.
- **August 2022**: Symbio expanded its operations into the US market.
- **October 2022**: Symbio announced the HyMotive project, aiming to accelerate the industrialization and mass production of fuel cell systems in its gigafactory under construction in Saint-Fons. The production capacity is targeted to reach 100,000 fuel cell systems per year by 2028.
- **December 2022**: Symbio planned to produce 50,000 fuel cells per year at its Saint-Fons factory, with production expected to start in the second half of 2023.
- **July 2023**: Stellantis officially joined Symbio as a third shareholder, with plans to enhance production capabilities and support the hydrogen fuel cell versions of its vehicles.
- **December 2023**: The SymphonHy gigafactory was inaugurated, marking it as Europe’s largest integrated site for hydrogen fuel cell production. The facility is designed to reach a production capacity of 50,000 fuel cells by 2026.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2022**: Symbio announced a €1 billion investment over seven years through the HyMotive project, supported by the French government and the European Union.
- **December 2023**: The HyMotive project is expected to generate 1,000 jobs and support the deployment of sustainable hydrogen mobility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2022**: Announcement of the HyMotive project to industrialize fuel cell systems.
- **December 2022**: Confirmation of plans to start production of 50,000 fuel cells per year in the second half of 2023.
- **July 2023**: Stellantis officially became a shareholder, enhancing the joint venture's capabilities.
- **December 2023**: Inauguration of the SymphonHy gigafactory, which began operations with the first 100% fuel cell system produced in October 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2022**: The HyMotive project is expected to create 1,000 jobs as part of its development.
- **December 2023**: The gigafactory is designed to support a significant workforce, with over 450 engineers already involved.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2019**: Symbio was established as a joint venture between Faurecia and Michelin.
- **July 2023**: Stellantis joined as a third shareholder, resulting in an equal ownership structure where Faurecia, Michelin, and Stellantis each hold 33.3% of Symbio.
