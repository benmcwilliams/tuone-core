### Evolutionary Timeline of the Stellantis Group Facility in Saint-Fons, FRA

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2022**: Stellantis announced plans to become the third shareholder in the Symbio fuel cell joint venture, which focuses on producing hydrogen fuel cells. Symbio aims to produce 50,000 fuel cells per year at its Saint-Fons factory, with production scheduled to start in the second half of 2023. Additionally, Symbio plans to increase its production capacity to 100,000 fuel cell systems per year by 2028.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2022**: Stellantis is negotiating to acquire a “substantial stake” in Symbio, which is expected to close in the first half of 2023. The exact size of the stake has not been disclosed, but it is part of Stellantis' strategic plan to expand hydrogen offerings in Europe and the USA.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2022**: Stellantis confirmed its intention to acquire an equal stake in Symbio, which will result in each shareholder (Stellantis, Faurecia, and Michelin) owning 33.3% of the joint venture. The transaction is expected to close in the third quarter of 2023. Production at the Saint-Fons facility is set to begin in the second half of 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2022**: As part of the expansion of production capacity to 100,000 fuel cell systems per year by 2028, Symbio plans to create 1,000 additional jobs in France.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2022**: Following the completion of the transaction, Stellantis will hold a one-third stake in the Symbio joint venture alongside Faurecia and Michelin, indicating a joint ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership.
