### Evolutionary Timeline of the Nidec Facility in Trémery, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **June 2022:** The Trémery facility, previously the largest diesel engine plant, began the transformation to manufacture electric motors as part of the Emotors joint venture with Nidec. The production capacity is expected to exceed one million units annually by 2024. The first electric motor produced is named M3, designed to operate at 400 volts with an output of 115 kW.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **June 2022:** The transformation of the Trémery plant is part of Stellantis's broader strategy to support its global EV battery strategy and achieve its Dare Forward 2030 targets. Specific investment amounts were not detailed in the article, but the emphasis on creating a new global value chain indicates significant financial commitment.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **June 2022:** Stellantis announced the start of production of electric drive systems at the Trémery facility. The first electric motor (M3) is set to debut in late 2022 in various Stellantis BEV models, including the DS 3 E-Tense, Peugeot e-308, Jeep Avenger, Opel Mokka Electric, and Astra Electric.

4. **Labour:**
   - **June 2022:** The transformation and ramp-up of production at the Trémery facility are expected to impact local employment positively, as Stellantis CEO Carlos Tavares highlighted the collaboration with social partners and local stakeholders to support employees during this transition.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **June 2022:** The facility operates under a joint venture model, specifically the Emotors joint venture between Stellantis and Nidec, indicating that Nidec does not exclusively own the facility but shares ownership with Stellantis.
