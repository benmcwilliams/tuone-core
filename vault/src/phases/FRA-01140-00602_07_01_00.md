### Evolutionary Timeline of the PSA Group Facility in Trémery, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2019**: PSA inaugurated an assembly line for electrified drive trains at the Trémery plant. The initial production capacity was set at 120,000 units for the following year (2020) and 180,000 units for 2021. The facility aims to produce a comprehensive range of state-of-the-art electric motors for all-electric and hybrid vehicles. The long-term goal is to reach a production capacity of 900,000 electric motors per year through the Nidec-PSA joint venture.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2019**: The establishment of the new assembly line for electrified drive trains involved significant investment, although specific financial figures were not disclosed. The Nidec-PSA joint venture, which is a 50-50 partnership between PSA and Nidec, was also mentioned as a key factor in enabling the production capacity increase.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2019**: The assembly line for electrified drive trains was inaugurated, marking a significant milestone in the facility's evolution. The installation process took almost ten months, and the plant is now positioned to produce all types of powertrains in parallel.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2019**: The Trémery plant employs around 4,000 employees, making it the largest private employer in Lorraine. The regional president emphasized the commitment to safeguarding jobs and creating a sustainable and environmentally friendly company model.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2019**: The facility is part of the PSA Group, which has a joint venture with Nidec (Nidec-PSA) for the production of electric motors. Each partner holds a 50% stake in the joint venture, indicating that PSA does not have exclusive ownership of the electric motor production aspect of the facility.
