### Evolutionary Timeline of the Stellantis Group Facility in Trémery, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2022**: Stellantis announced the transformation of the Trémery plant, previously the largest diesel engine plant, to manufacture electric motors as part of the Emotors joint venture with Nidec. Production of electric motors (M3) is set to begin in late 2022, with a production capacity expected to exceed one million units annually by 2024. The M3 motor operates at 400 volts with an output of 115 kW.
- **May 2023**: The new 115 kW electric drive system, developed in collaboration with Nidec, is integrated into Citroën's ë-C4 and ë-C4 X models, showcasing the facility's capability to produce advanced electric powertrains.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2018**: The Emotors joint venture between Stellantis and Nidec was established, marking the beginning of investments aimed at transitioning the Trémery facility towards electric vehicle components.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2022**: Stellantis confirmed the start of production of electric drive systems at the Trémery facility as part of the Emotors joint venture. The first electric motor (M3) is expected to debut in late 2022.
- **January 2023**: The M3 motor is initially used in several Stellantis models, including the new DS 3 E-Tense, Peugeot e-308, Jeep Avenger, and Opel Mokka Electric.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2022**: Stellantis CEO Carlos Tavares emphasized the importance of collaboration with local stakeholders and employees as the facility ramps up production for electrified vehicles, indicating a focus on job retention and creation in the context of the transition to electric vehicle manufacturing.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **2018-Present**: The Trémery facility operates under a joint venture (Emotors) between Stellantis and Nidec, indicating that Stellantis does not exclusively own the facility but shares ownership with its partner. 

This timeline captures the key developments and transformations at the Stellantis Group facility in Trémery, France, as it evolves from traditional diesel engine production to a hub for electric vehicle components.
