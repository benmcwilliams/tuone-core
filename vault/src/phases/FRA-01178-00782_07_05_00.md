## FRA-01178-00782

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Toyota Facility in Valenciennes, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2018**: Toyota announces an investment of €300 million to upgrade its facility in Valenciennes, enabling it to produce 300,000 vehicles annually using the new Toyota New Global Architecture (TNGA). The facility primarily manufactures the hybrid Yaris and will incorporate new equipment and technologies to support this transition.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2018**: The total cumulative investment in the Toyota Motor Manufacturing France (TMMF) plant reaches €1.4 billion, with the latest €300 million investment aimed at enhancing production capabilities and environmental performance. This investment is supported by incentives from the French State and local authorities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2020**: Following the COVID-19 pandemic, Toyota announces the gradual resumption of production at its Valenciennes facility, starting on April 22, 2020, after a temporary shutdown due to the pandemic.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2018**: The investment program includes converting 300 temporary contracts into permanent positions and creating an additional 400 permanent jobs when full production capacity is achieved.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned exclusively by Toyota, as indicated by the references to Toyota Motor Manufacturing France (TMMF) in the articles. There is no mention of joint ventures or shared ownership.
