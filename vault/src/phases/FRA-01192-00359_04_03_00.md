### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hopium Facility in Vernon, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2021**: Hopium unveiled the prototype of its hydrogen-powered sedan, the Alpha O, which is designed to be produced on an industrial scale. The vehicle boasts an output of over 500 hp (approximately 370 kW) and a top speed of 230 km/h, with a range exceeding 1,000 kilometers on a single tank of fuel.
- **September 2022**: Hopium announced plans for an industrial site in Normandy, specifically in Vernon, for the development and production of the Machina hydrogen sedan. The facility will initially have a production capacity of 20,000 vehicles per year and will include a research and development center.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2022**: The establishment of the Vernon facility is part of Hopium's ambition to develop hydrogen-powered sedans in France. The company has not disclosed specific investment amounts but emphasizes the project's significance for industrial renewal in the region.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2021**: Prototype of the Alpha O unveiled, with pre-orders for the first 1,000 units opened.
- **September 2022**: Official announcement of the construction of the industrial site in Vernon, with commissioning planned for the end of 2024 and first deliveries of the Machina scheduled for the end of 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2022**: The Vernon facility is expected to create more than 1,500 jobs upon completion, contributing significantly to local employment.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2022**: Hopium is identified as the owner of the facility, with no mention of joint ventures or partnerships regarding the ownership structure.
