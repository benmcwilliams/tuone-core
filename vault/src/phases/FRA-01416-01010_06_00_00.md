### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Abu Dhabi, FRA

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility is part of the Al Dhafra solar project, which is the world's largest solar power project with a production capacity of 2GW. It is expected to generate enough electricity for approximately 160,000 homes across the UAE. The project is a significant contributor to the UAE's goal of increasing its renewable energy capacity to 9GW by 2025.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The Al Dhafra solar project is developed by a consortium that includes Abu Dhabi National Energy Company (Taqa), Masdar, EDF Renewables, and JinkoPower. Taqa holds a 40% stake, while Masdar, EDF Renewables, and JinkoPower each hold a 20% stake. The project benefits from competitive solar tariffs, with costs at Dh4.85 fils per kilowatt hour.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - The Al Dhafra solar project is expected to become fully operational in 2022. The project is part of a broader initiative to increase the UAE's renewable energy capacity, which was at 2.3GW at the end of 2020.

4. **Labour:**

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is not exclusively owned by EDF; it is part of a joint venture. Taqa owns 40% of the project, while EDF Renewables, Masdar, and JinkoPower each own 20%.
