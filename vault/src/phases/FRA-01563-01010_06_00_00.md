### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Fessenheim, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2019**: France selected its first batch of photovoltaic (PV) projects to be deployed near EDF's Fessenheim nuclear station, which is set for decommissioning. The total planned capacity for this initial selection is **62.5MW**, with three larger projects of **22.2MW**, **15.54MW**, and **10.24MW**, alongside nine smaller projects ranging from **149kW to 4.83MW**. The overall program aims to install **300MW** of solar capacity in the vicinity of the nuclear station.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2019**: The solar repowering scheme was launched with an investment of **€250 million (approximately US$273 million)** in state funding. This funding is aimed at supporting the addition of **200MW** of ground-mounted solar, **75MW** of rooftop systems, and **25MW** of smaller PV systems.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2019**: The Environment Ministry announced the winners of the first tender for solar projects near Fessenheim, marking a significant milestone in the transition from nuclear to solar energy in the region. The first twelve projects were deemed competitive and would have succeeded in national solar auctions.
