### Evolutionary Timeline of the CSFD Facility in Fessenheim, FRA

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 02-07-2018
   - **Details:** The CSFD facility at the Fessenheim nuclear site is set to produce a total of 300 MW of solar energy. This includes 200 MW allocated for ground-mounted photovoltaic (PV) plants (ranging from 500 kW to 30 MW) and 100 MW for rooftop solar systems (not exceeding 8 MW). The project will be implemented in three phases across late 2018, mid-2019, and late 2019.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 02-07-2018
   - **Details:** The projects will benefit from a feed-in tariff (FIT) structure, with ground-mounted PV plants receiving a premium FIT ranging from €50/MWh to €70/MWh, and rooftop projects receiving between €70/MWh and €100/MWh, depending on their size. Additionally, projects partially financed through crowdfunding will receive an extra bonus of €3/MWh.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 02-07-2018
   - **Details:** The CSFD announced plans to build solar plants at the Fessenheim nuclear site, which is scheduled for decommissioning by 2020. The tender for the solar projects will be executed in three phases: 
     - **Phase 1:** Late 2018 - 40 MW for ground-mounted, 15 MW for rooftop (500 kW to 8 MW), and 5 MW for rooftop (100 kW to 500 kW).
     - **Phase 2:** Mid-2019 - 80 MW for ground-mounted, 30 MW for rooftop (500 kW to 8 MW), and 10 MW for rooftop (100 kW to 500 kW).
     - **Phase 3:** Late 2019 - 80 MW for ground-mounted, 30 MW for rooftop (500 kW to 8 MW), and 10 MW for rooftop (100 kW to 500 kW).
