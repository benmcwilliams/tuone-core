### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fessenheim Facility, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2019**: The French government announced plans to replace the decommissioned Fessenheim nuclear power plant with a solar facility capable of producing 300 MW of solar energy. The project will include 200 MW of ground-mounted solar installations and 100 MW of rooftop solar systems.
- **September 2019**: The first round of the solar tender for 300 MW was completed, with 12 winning bids representing 62.8 MW of generation capacity. The average price for the winning projects was set at €66.05/MWh.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2019**: The French government committed €250 million to the solar project at Fessenheim, which is part of a broader initiative to transition the region's economy following the nuclear plant's closure.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2019**: The European Commission approved the French government's tender mechanism for the solar project, allowing for the construction of solar plants at the Fessenheim site.
- **January 2019**: The French government officially launched the tender for the solar project, which is divided into three tranches.
- **September 2019**: The first procurement round for the solar tender was completed, with plans for two additional rounds scheduled for January and July 2020.
