### Evolutionary Timeline of the Siemens Facility in Saillat-sur-Vienne, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **02/2020**: Siemens, in collaboration with Engie and other partners, announced the development of a €15.2 million power-to-X-to-power hydrogen storage facility in Saillat-sur-Vienne. The facility will produce and store electrolyzed hydrogen using renewable energy and water. The hydrogen will be mixed with natural gas to power an upgraded 12 MW Siemens SGT-400 industrial gas turbine, which previously generated steam for local manufacturing and will now generate electricity to be fed back into the grid.
- **2021**: Installation of the hydrogen production, storage, and supply facility at the pilot demonstration site is scheduled.
- **2022**: Installation of the gas turbine for natural gas/hydrogen mixtures and initial demonstration of the advanced pilot plant concept is planned.
- **2023**: Pilot demonstration with up to 100% hydrogen for carbon-free energy production from stored excess renewable energy is set to occur.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **02/2020**: The project has a total budget of €15.2 million, with €10.5 million funded by the European Commission under its Horizon 2020 Framework Program for Research and Innovation.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05/2020**: The project was officially launched, marking the start of engineering development.
- **02/2020**: The project was announced, detailing the collaboration among Siemens, Engie, and other partners, and the planned upgrades to the existing infrastructure.

#### 4. Labour
- **02/2020**: The project is expected to contribute to job creation through the establishment of the hydrogen production and storage facility, although specific job numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **02/2020**: The facility is part of a consortium involving multiple stakeholders, including Siemens, Engie, and others, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by Siemens.
