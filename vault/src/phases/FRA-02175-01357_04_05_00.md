## FRA-02175-01357

### Evolutionary Timeline of the University of Duisburg-Essen Facility in Saillat-sur-Vienne, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Project Launch:** The facility will implement the world's first industrial-scale power-to-X-to-power demonstrator with an advanced hydrogen turbine, utilizing an existing Siemens SGT-400 industrial gas turbine.
   - **Production Capacity:** The facility aims to produce electricity and thermal energy by converting stored hydrogen, with the goal of operating on up to 100% hydrogen.
   - **Demonstration Campaigns:** The facility will be powered by a mix of natural gas and hydrogen, ultimately transitioning to a fully hydrogen operation.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Total Budget:** The project has a total budget of approximately €15.2 million.
   - **EU Contribution:** The European Union will contribute €10.5 million under the Horizon 2020 program to support the project.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Official Launch Date:** The project was officially launched on May 1, 2020.
   - **Phases of Development:**
     - **May 2020:** Contract finalization and start of engineering development.
     - **2021:** Installation of the hydrogen production, storage, and supply facility at the pilot demonstration site.
     - **2022:** Installation of the gas turbine for natural gas/hydrogen mixtures and initial demonstration of the advanced pilot plant concept.
     - **2023:** Pilot demonstration with up to 100% hydrogen for carbon-free energy production from stored excess renewable energy.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Job Creation:** The project is expected to create jobs related to the construction and operation of the hydrogen production and storage facility, although specific numbers are not provided in the article.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Stakeholders:** The facility is part of a consortium made up exclusively of European companies and bodies, including Engie Solutions, Siemens Gas and Power, Centrax, and several universities, including the University of Duisburg-Essen. The ownership structure indicates a collaborative effort rather than exclusive ownership by a single entity.
