### Evolutionary Timeline of the Engie Facility in Saillat-sur-Vienne, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2020**: The facility is set to implement the HYFLEXPOWER project, which involves upgrading an existing Siemens SGT-400 industrial gas turbine to generate electricity and thermal energy from a mix of natural gas and hydrogen, with the goal of achieving up to 100% hydrogen operation. The facility will produce and store electrolyzed hydrogen using renewable energy.
- **2021**: Installation of the hydrogen production, storage, and supply facility at the pilot demonstration site is scheduled.
- **2022**: The gas turbine will be installed for natural gas/hydrogen mixtures, and initial demonstrations of the advanced pilot plant concept will take place.
- **2023**: The pilot demonstration aims to operate with up to 100% hydrogen, contributing to carbon-free energy production from stored excess renewable energy.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2020**: The total budget for the HYFLEXPOWER project is approximately €15.2 million, with €10.5 million funded by the European Commission under the Horizon 2020 Framework Program for Research and Innovation.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 1, 2020**: The HYFLEXPOWER project is officially launched, marking the start of engineering development.
- **2021**: Installation of the hydrogen production and storage facility is planned.
- **2022**: Installation of the gas turbine and initial demonstrations are scheduled.
- **2023**: Final commissioning of the facility is expected, with pilot demonstrations utilizing up to 100% hydrogen.
