## FRA-02175-03230

### Evolutionary Timeline of the National Technical University of Athens Facility in Saillat-sur-Vienne, France

1. **Technology and Operations**:
   - **May 2020**: The HYFLEXPOWER project was officially launched, marking the beginning of the world's first industrial-scale power-to-X-to-power demonstrator with an advanced hydrogen turbine at the Smurfit Kappa site. The facility aims to upgrade an existing Siemens SGT-400 industrial gas turbine to convert stored hydrogen into electricity and thermal energy. The goal is to demonstrate a power supply that can operate on up to 100% hydrogen, contributing to a carbon-free energy mix.

2. **Investment and Development**:
   - **Total Budget**: The project has a total budget of approximately €15.2 million, with €10.5 million funded by the European Union under the Horizon 2020 program. This investment supports the development of hydrogen production and storage facilities, as well as the upgrade of the gas turbine.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**:
   - **May 2020**: Official launch of the HYFLEXPOWER project.
   - **2021**: Installation of the hydrogen production, storage, and supply facility at the pilot demonstration site.
   - **2022**: Installation of the gas turbine for natural gas/hydrogen mixtures and initial demonstration of the advanced pilot plant concept.
   - **2023**: Pilot demonstration with up to 100% hydrogen for carbon-free energy production from stored excess renewable energy.

4. **Labour**:
   - The articles do not specify exact job creation or job loss figures related to the project. However, the involvement of multiple stakeholders and the construction of new facilities suggest potential job creation during the installation and operational phases.

5. **Ownership Structure**:
   - The facility is not exclusively owned by the National Technical University of Athens. It is part of a consortium involving multiple European companies and institutions, including Engie Solutions, Siemens Gas and Power, and others, indicating a collaborative ownership structure for the project.
