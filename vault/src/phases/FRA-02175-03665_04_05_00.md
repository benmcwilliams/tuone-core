## FRA-02175-03665

### Evolutionary Timeline of the University College London Facility in Saillat-sur-Vienne, France

1. **Technology and Operations**
   - **May 2020**: The HYFLEXPOWER project was officially launched, marking the beginning of the world's first industrial-scale power-to-X-to-power demonstrator with an advanced hydrogen turbine at the Smurfit Kappa site. The facility aims to upgrade an existing Siemens SGT-400 industrial gas turbine to convert stored hydrogen into electricity and thermal energy. The goal is to test an entirely green hydrogen-based power supply, ultimately aiming for up to 100% hydrogen operation.

2. **Investment and Development**
   - **Total Budget**: The project has a total budget of approximately €15.2 million, with €10.5 million funded by the European Union under the Horizon 2020 program. This investment is aimed at developing and demonstrating new technologies for the power-to-X-to-power cycle.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**
   - **May 2020**: Official launch of the HYFLEXPOWER project.
   - **2021**: Installation of the hydrogen production, storage, and supply facility at the pilot demonstration site.
   - **2022**: Installation of the gas turbine for natural gas/hydrogen mixtures and initial demonstration of the advanced pilot plant concept.
   - **2023**: Pilot demonstration with up to 100% hydrogen for carbon-free energy production from stored excess renewable energy.

4. **Labour**
   - The articles do not specify exact job creation or job loss figures related to the project. However, the involvement of multiple stakeholders suggests potential job creation in engineering, construction, and operations related to the facility's upgrades and new technology implementations.

5. **Ownership Structure**
   - The facility is operated by Engie Solutions, which is responsible for producing energy at the Smurfit Kappa site. The project involves a consortium of European companies and bodies, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership by University College London or any single entity.
