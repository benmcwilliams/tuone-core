### Evolutionary Timeline of Siemens Gas and Power Facility in Saillat-sur-Vienne, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2020**: Official launch of the HYFLEXPOWER project, which aims to demonstrate the world's first industrial-scale power-to-X-to-power hydrogen gas turbine. The facility will upgrade an existing Siemens SGT-400 industrial gas turbine to convert stored hydrogen into electricity and thermal energy.
- **2021**: Installation of the hydrogen production, storage, and supply facility at the pilot demonstration site.
- **2022**: Installation of the gas turbine for natural gas/hydrogen mixtures and initial demonstration of the advanced pilot plant concept.
- **2023**: Pilot demonstration with up to 100% hydrogen for carbon-free energy production from stored excess renewable energy.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Total Project Budget**: Approximately €15.2 million.
- **EU Contribution**: €10.5 million funded entirely by the European Union under the Horizon 2020 program.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **02-06-2020**: Announcement of the project, detailing its objectives and the stakeholders involved.
- **01-05-2020**: Official launch of the project, marking the start of engineering development.
- **2021**: Start of installation for the hydrogen production and storage facility.
- **2022**: Installation of the gas turbine and initial demonstrations.
- **2023**: Planned pilot demonstration with 100% hydrogen operation.

### Siemens Gas and Power Facility in Saillat-sur-Vienne, FRA

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- "The implementation of this project, the first industrial-scale power-to-X-to-power demonstrator with an advanced hydrogen turbine, will be launched at Smurfit Kappa PRF’s site."
- "The purpose of this project is to prove that hydrogen can be produced and stored from renewable electricity and then added up to 100% to the natural gas currently used with combined heat and power plants."
- "The installed demonstrator will be used to store excess renewable electricity in the form of green hydrogen."
- "During two demonstration campaigns, the facility will be powered by a mix of natural gas and hydrogen, ultimately aiming for up to 100% hydrogen operation."

#### 2. Investment and Development
- "The project’s total budget is close to €15.2 million, of which €10.5 million will be contributed entirely by the European Union under the Horizon 2020 program."

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- "Officially launched on 1 May 2020, the project will last 4 years and will be split into several phases: May 2020: Contract finalization and start of engineering development."
- "2021: Installation of the hydrogen production, storage and supply facility at pilot demonstration site."
- "2022: Installation of the gas turbine for natural gas/hydrogen mixtures and initial demonstration of advanced pilot plant concept."
