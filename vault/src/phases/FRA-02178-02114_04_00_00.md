### Evolutionary Timeline of the Teréga Facility in Carresse-Cassaber, France

1. **Technology and Operations**
   - **July 2020**: Teréga, in collaboration with Hydrogène de France, announced plans for a pilot hydrogen storage project named HyGéo. The facility will utilize disused salt caverns in Carresse-Cassaber, with a production capacity of 1.5 GWh. The project is designed to be deployed quickly and will explore various uses of hydrogen, including power-to-power, power-to-mobility, power-to-industry, and power-to-gas.

2. **Investment and Development**
   - **July 2020**: The HyGéo project has a total investment of €13.5 million. This funding is aimed at conducting a feasibility study and preparing for the construction of the facility.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**
   - **July 2020**: A memorandum of understanding was signed between Teréga and Hydrogène de France to initiate the feasibility study for the HyGéo project.
   - **2022**: Construction of the facility is scheduled to begin.
   - **2024**: The facility is expected to commence commercial operations.

5. **Ownership Structure**
