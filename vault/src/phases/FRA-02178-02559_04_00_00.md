### Evolutionary Timeline of the HDF Facility in Carresse-Cassaber, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **July 2020:** HDF and Teréga launched the HyGéo project, which focuses on the deployment of a hydrogen energy mass storage system. The facility will utilize an abandoned geological cavern for hydrogen storage, aiming to store approximately 1.5 GWh of energy, equivalent to the annual consumption of 400 households. The project will incorporate high-power fuel cells supplied by HDF to produce electricity from the stored hydrogen.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **July 2020:** The total budget for the HyGéo pilot site is estimated at €13.5 million. This investment aligns with the French Government's Multi-year Energy Programme (PPE), which encourages the reuse of salt caverns for hydrogen storage.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **July 2020:** The first phase of the HyGéo project commenced with a feasibility study to characterize the site, validate the cavern's capacity for hydrogen storage, and assess the economic relevance of this storage method.
   - **2022:** Engineering and construction studies are scheduled to begin, with operations expected to start in 2024 following the completion of the feasibility study.

4. **Labour:**

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **July 2020:** The facility is a collaborative project between HDF and Teréga, indicating a joint venture rather than exclusive ownership by HDF.
