## FRA-02178-04306

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Teréga Facility in Carresse-Cassaber, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **July 2020:** Teréga, in collaboration with HDF, launched the HyGéo project, which focuses on the deployment of a hydrogen energy mass storage system. The facility will utilize an abandoned geological cavern for storing approximately 1.5 GWh of "green" hydrogen, equivalent to the annual consumption of 400 households. The project aims to integrate renewable energies into the electricity mix and is part of the hydrogen sector development strategy.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **July 2020:** The total budget for the HyGéo pilot site is estimated at €13.5 million. This investment is aligned with the French Government's Multi-year Energy Programme (PPE), which encourages the reuse of salt caverns for hydrogen storage.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **July 2020:** The first phase of the HyGéo project commenced with a feasibility study to characterize the site and validate the cavern's capacity for hydrogen storage.
   - **2022:** Engineering and construction studies are scheduled to begin following the completion of the feasibility study.
   - **2024:** The facility is expected to be operational after the completion of the engineering and construction phases.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
