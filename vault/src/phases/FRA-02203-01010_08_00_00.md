### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Normandy, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2023**: EDF Renewables and Maple Power were awarded the project to develop the Centre Manche 1 offshore wind farm, which will have a planned capacity of **1 gigawatt (GW)**. The facility is expected to supply electricity equivalent to the annual consumption of **more than 1.5 million people**. The wind farm will be located **32 km off the coast** of the departments of Manche and Calvados in Normandy.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: The project is part of the French government's target to commission around **50 offshore wind farms by 2050**, with a total capacity of **40 GW**. The Centre Manche 1 project is a significant step towards achieving France's carbon neutrality objectives.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2023**: The project company, **Eoliennes en Mer Manche Normandie**, was selected by the French Ministry of Energy Transition following the fourth offshore wind tender launched in **January 2021**. The commissioning of the Centre Manche 1 wind farm is expected by **2030**.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2023**: While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned, the development of the Centre Manche 1 project is expected to contribute positively to local employment during the construction and operational phases, aligning with the broader goals of the energy transition.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2023**: The Centre Manche 1 project is owned by a consortium comprising **EDF Renewables** and **Maple Power**. Maple Power is a joint venture between **Enbridge** and the **Canada Pension Plan Investment Board**. Thus, the facility is not exclusively owned by EDF but is a collaborative effort with shared ownership.
