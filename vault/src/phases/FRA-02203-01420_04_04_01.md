### Evolutionary Timeline of TotalEnergies Facility in Normandy, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2021**: TotalEnergies, in collaboration with Air Liquide, aims to decarbonize hydrogen production at its Normandy facility. The facility has a hydrogen production capacity of 255 tons per day. Air Liquide will operate this unit under a long-term contract and connect it to its hydrogen network to optimize performance and develop a low-carbon hydrogen network.
- **November 2022**: Air Liquide will build and operate a new hydrogen production unit at TotalEnergies' Normandy platform, with a capacity of over 20,000 tons per year, recycling residual biogas from the Grandpuits biorefinery. This unit will primarily produce renewable, low-carbon hydrogen for sustainable aviation fuel.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2022**: An investment of more than €130 million is announced for the new hydrogen production unit at the Normandy facility. This investment is part of a long-term contract with Air Liquide.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2021**: TotalEnergies and Air Liquide begin development studies for a carbon capture and storage (CCS) project at the Normandy facility, aiming to reduce emissions by 650,000 tons per year by 2030.
- **September 2023**: TotalEnergies and Air Liquide sign an agreement for the long-term supply of green and low-carbon hydrogen to TotalEnergies' refining and petrochemical platform in Normandy. Air Liquide will build the Normand'hy electrolyzer with a total electrical capacity of 200 MW.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2021**: The articles do not specify job creation or loss directly related to the investments or expansions at the Normandy facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2021**: TotalEnergies retains ownership of the facility while Air Liquide operates the hydrogen production unit under a long-term contract. The collaboration indicates a partnership but does not specify joint ownership.
