### Evolutionary Timeline of the Air Liquide Facility in Normandy, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2021**: Air Liquide takes over and operates TotalEnergies' hydrogen production unit in Normandy, with a production capacity of **255 tons per day**. The facility will be connected to Air Liquide's hydrogen network to optimize performance and develop a low-carbon hydrogen network.
- **November 2022**: Air Liquide announces plans to build and operate a hydrogen production unit at TotalEnergies' Normandy platform, with a capacity of **more than 20,000 tons per year**, utilizing residual biogas from the Grandpuits biorefinery.
- **September 2023**: Air Liquide will build and operate the **Normand'hy electrolyzer** with a total electrical capacity of **200 MW**. This electrolyzer will receive around **700 GWh/year** of renewable and low-carbon power from TotalEnergies.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2022**: Air Liquide commits to an investment of **over €130 million** (approximately $133.6 million) for the hydrogen production unit at TotalEnergies' Normandy platform.
- **November 2024**: Air Liquide announces an additional investment of **€50 million** ($52 million) for a new hydrogen packaging and delivery supply chain in the Normandy industrial basin.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2021**: Announcement of the collaboration between Air Liquide and TotalEnergies to decarbonize hydrogen production in Normandy.
- **November 2022**: Launch of development studies for a carbon capture and storage (CCS) project at the Normandy facility.
- **September 2023**: Agreement signed for the long-term supply of green and low-carbon hydrogen to TotalEnergies' refining and petrochemical platform in Normandy.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2021**: The articles do not specify job creation or loss directly related to the facility's operations or expansions.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2021**: Air Liquide operates the hydrogen production unit under a **long-term contract** with TotalEnergies, indicating a collaborative operational structure rather than exclusive ownership.
- **November 2022**: Air Liquide has a **40% stake** in H2V Normandy, which aims to build a 200 MW electrolysis unit in Le Havre, indicating a joint venture aspect in the ownership structure.
