### Evolutionary Timeline of the Maple Power Facility in Normandy, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2023**: Announcement of the Centre Manche 1 offshore wind project, which will have a planned capacity of **1 gigawatt (GW)**. The facility is designed to meet the annual electricity needs of **more than 1.5 million people**. It will be located **32 km off the coast** of the departments of Manche and Calvados in Normandy. The project is part of the French government's target to commission around **50 offshore wind farms by 2050**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: The project is awarded to Eoliennes en Mer Manche Normandie, a consortium comprising EDF Renewables and Maple Power, following a tender launched by the French Ministry of Energy Transition in **January 2021**. This project is part of a broader initiative to achieve **40 GW** of offshore wind capacity in France by 2050.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2023**: The Centre Manche 1 project is officially announced as the winner of the offshore wind tender. The facility is expected to be commissioned by **2030**. This marks the **fifth offshore wind project** in France and the **third in Normandy** for the consortium of EDF Renewables and Maple Power.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2023**: While specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the articles, the development and construction of the Centre Manche 1 project are expected to contribute positively to local employment opportunities, aligning with the commitment to local integration and consultation with stakeholders.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2023**: The Maple Power facility is a **joint venture** between **Enbridge** and the **Canada Pension Plan Investment Board**. The project is developed in collaboration with **EDF Renewables**, which is a subsidiary of the EDF Group.
