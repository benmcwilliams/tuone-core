### Evolutionary Timeline of Bouygues Énergies & Services Facility in Sarlat, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2021**: Bouygues Énergies & Services established a solar-powered hydrogen facility at the Bordeaux Area Control Centre (ACC) in Sarlat, Nouvelle-Aquitaine. The facility features a 19.8 kW photovoltaic (PV) system that powers a telecommunications antenna. The system includes:
  - Two fuel cell systems with a capacity of 2.2 kW each.
  - A 5 kW electrolyzer.
  - Lithium nickel-cobalt-aluminum oxide (NCA) batteries with a storage capacity of 23.4 kW.
  - The facility is designed to reduce non-renewable primary energy consumption by 78% and greenhouse gas emissions by 79% compared to conventional diesel generators.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2021**: The total construction cost of the facility was €680,000, with specific investments of €110,000 for the electrolyzer and €180,000 for the fuel cell system. The project was developed with support from the Direction des Services de la navigation aérienne (DSNA), the French government agency responsible for air traffic control.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2021**: The Be Greener hydrogen pilot project was officially announced and constructed, aimed at powering the site's radio communication antenna, which previously relied on an off-grid power generator.
