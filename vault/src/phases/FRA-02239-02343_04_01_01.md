## FRA-02239-02343

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Lhyfe Facility in Le Croisic, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **07-06-2021**: Lhyfe announces the development of the world's first offshore green hydrogen production plant, which will be powered by electricity from a floating wind turbine. The facility will be linked to the Floatgen wind turbine at the SEM-REV demonstration site off the coast of Le Croisic. The production capacity is expected to range from 10 to several hundred megawatts.
- **23-09-2022**: Lhyfe launches the Sealhyfe offshore hydrogen production pilot project, capable of producing up to 400 kg of renewable green hydrogen per day, equivalent to 1 MW of power. The project will initially operate near the shore for six months before moving 20 kilometers offshore.
- **08-06-2023**: The Sealhyfe platform officially starts producing offshore hydrogen, marking a significant milestone in the project.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **29-10-2021**: Lhyfe and Plug Power sign a memorandum of understanding to develop green hydrogen generation plants throughout Europe, targeting a total hydrogen capacity of 300 MW by 2025 and a 1 GW production site.
- **08-06-2023**: The Hydrogen Offshore Production for Europe (HOPE) project consortium, including Lhyfe, secures a €20 million grant from the European Commission to demonstrate large-scale offshore renewable hydrogen production and pipeline transport.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **19-07-2021**: DNV begins a study to develop the offshore hydrogen production site, with a targeted start-up date of 2022.
- **03-08-2021**: DNV continues its safety study for the offshore hydrogen production facility, emphasizing the project's innovative nature and its potential impact on the industry.
- **23-09-2022**: The Sealhyfe pilot project is officially launched, with plans for real-world testing.
- **20-06-2023**: The Sealhyfe platform begins hydrogen production, marking the operational start of the world's first offshore hydrogen production facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **29-10-2021**: The partnership with Plug Power and the development of the hydrogen generation plants are expected to create jobs in the region, although specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Lhyfe is the primary developer of the facility, collaborating with various partners such as Plug Power, Chantiers de l’Atlantique, and Centrale Nantes. The ownership structure appears to be primarily held by Lhyfe, with joint ventures for specific projects and collaborations for technology and infrastructure development.
