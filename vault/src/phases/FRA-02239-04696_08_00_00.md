## FRA-02239-04696

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Centrale Nantes Facility in Le Croisic, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **07/2021**: Announcement of the world's first offshore green hydrogen production plant, powered by electricity from a floating wind turbine, to be installed at the SEM-REV demonstration site off the coast of Le Croisic. The facility will utilize an electrolyser on GEPS Techno’s floating platform, connected to the Floatgen wind turbine. The production capacity is expected to range from ten to several hundred megawatts, with large-scale industrial deployment targeted for 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07/2021**: DNV is engaged to lead a study into the development of the offshore hydrogen production site, indicating a commitment to identifying environmental, safety, and operational risks associated with the project. This suggests a significant investment in research and development to ensure the project's feasibility and safety.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **07/2021**: The project is set to have a targeted start-up date in 2022, with ongoing studies to ensure safety and operational readiness.
- **08/2021**: DNV continues its involvement by conducting a process safety study to identify risks associated with the offshore hydrogen production facility, reinforcing the project's commitment to safety and regulatory compliance.

#### 4. Labour
- **No specific job creation or job loss details** were mentioned in the articles, but the involvement of multiple organizations (Lhyfe, DNV, and Centrale Nantes) suggests potential job creation related to the development and operation of the facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is a collaborative project involving **Centrale Nantes** and **Lhyfe**, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by a single entity. DNV's involvement further emphasizes the collaborative nature of the project.
