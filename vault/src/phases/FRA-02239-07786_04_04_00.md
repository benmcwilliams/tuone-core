### Evolutionary Timeline of the Floatgen Facility in Le Croisic, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **2018:** The Floatgen facility, featuring a 2 MW floating wind turbine, was installed at the SEM-REV demonstration site in Le Croisic. The turbine consists of a Vestas V80 wind turbine mounted on a Damping Pool foundation developed by Ideol (now BW Ideol). It began delivering electricity to the French grid in the same year.
   - **2020:** Floatgen achieved a production record, generating 14% more electricity than in 2019.
   - **2022 (Scheduled):** An offshore hydrogen production plant powered by electricity from the Floatgen wind turbine is set to go online. This facility will be the world’s first offshore green hydrogen production plant, linked to the Floatgen wind turbine.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **2021:** The project for the offshore hydrogen production facility is being developed by Lhyfe in collaboration with Chantiers de l’Atlantique. The electrolyser will be installed on GEPS Techno’s floating platform at the SEM-REV site, with plans for a capacity ranging from ten to several hundred megawatts, potentially built in Saint-Nazaire as early as 2024.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **07-06-2021:** Announcement of the offshore hydrogen production plant scheduled to be operational in 2022. The project is a collaboration between Lhyfe and Chantiers de l’Atlantique, with a focus on validating offshore hydrogen production technology before large-scale deployment in 2024.
