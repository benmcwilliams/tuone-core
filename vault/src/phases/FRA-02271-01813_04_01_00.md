### Evolutionary Timeline of the Neoen Facility in Saucats, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **11-01-2021**: Announcement of the Horizeo project, which will feature a 1 GW solar plant, battery storage, a green hydrogen production unit, a data center, and an agrivoltaic facility. The project aims to demonstrate the maturity and competitiveness of solar energy for direct supply to industrial companies.
- **12-01-2021**: The project is confirmed to be the largest solar-plus-storage facility in Europe, with a total investment of €1 billion. The electricity generated will be sold through power purchase agreements (PPAs) to unspecified customers.
- **24-08-2021**: The project management report is validated by France's National Commission for Public Debate (CNDP). The solar plant will cover over 900 hectares and is expected to produce abundant and competitive electricity.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12-01-2021**: The total investment for the Horizeo project is estimated at €1 billion, with specific allocations: €650 million for the solar plant, €40 million for the green hydrogen production unit, €20 million for the battery storage unit, and €10 million for the agrivoltaic facility.
- **24-08-2021**: The investment breakdown is reiterated, emphasizing the significant financial commitment to various components of the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **11-01-2021**: Initial announcement of the Horizeo project.
- **12-01-2021**: Confirmation of the project details and its expected completion in 2026.
- **24-08-2021**: Public debate on the project begins, scheduled to run from September 9, 2021, to January 9, 2022, with multiple public meetings and workshops planned.

#### 4. Labour
- **12-01-2021**: While specific job creation numbers are not mentioned, the scale of the project suggests potential job creation during construction and operation phases, particularly in the renewable energy and agricultural sectors.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **11-01-2021**: The facility is a joint venture between Engie and Neoen, with both companies collaborating on the development and operation of the Horizeo project. There is no indication of exclusive ownership by either company.

### Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: The Horizeo project is described as a "1 GW solar-plus-storage project" (Article 1, 11-01-2021) and includes a "40 MW Li-ion battery storage unit" (Article 3, 24-08-2021).
- **Technological Features**: The facility will feature "battery storage and supply a green hydrogen production plant" (Article 1, 11-01-2021) and will also include "an agrivoltaic facility" (Article 2, 12-01-2021).
- **Location and Size**: The project is planned to cover "more than 900 hectares" (Article 3, 24-08-2021) and is located "a few kilometers from the current largest photovoltaic park in France, the 300 MW Cestas project" (Article 4, 24-08-2021).

### Investment and Development

- **Investment Breakdown**: The solar plant represents "the largest part of the investment — €650 million" (Article 3, 24-08-2021), while the data center investment is "estimated at €20 million" (Article 4, 24-08-2021), and the green hydrogen production unit is "estimated at €40 million" (Article 3, 24-08-2021).
