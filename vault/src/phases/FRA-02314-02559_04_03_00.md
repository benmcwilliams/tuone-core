### Evolutionary Timeline of HDF Facility in Blanquefort, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05-12-2023**: HDF Energy announced that the first mass production plant for high-power fuel cells in Blanquefort will be delivered in January 2024. The facility is scheduled to come on stream in summer 2024, with mass production expected in 2025. The plant will produce modular 1.5 MW fuel cells designed in partnership with Ballard Power Systems, targeting heavy maritime and rail mobility sectors, as well as electricity production for public power grids.
- **07-06-2024**: The facility was inaugurated, marking it as the first in the world to produce fuel cells larger than 1 MW. HDF Energy plans to scale up production to 1 GW by 2030, with a product range from 1 MW to 10 MW.
- **28-06-2024**: HDF Energy confirmed that the European Commission approved financial support for the facility under the Important Projects of Common European Interest (IPCEI), specifically for the development and industrialization of high-power hydrogen fuel cells.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **28-06-2024**: The French government will provide financing for the facility, with a grant worth up to €172 million dedicated to the development of high-power hydrogen fuel cells. This funding is part of the €1.4 billion ($1.52 billion) Hy2Move phase of the IPCEI.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05-12-2023**: Announcement of the facility's delivery in January 2024 and operational start in summer 2024.
- **07-06-2024**: Official inauguration of the facility.
- **28-06-2024**: Confirmation of European Commission approval for financial support.
