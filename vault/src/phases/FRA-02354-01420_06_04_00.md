## FRA-02354-01420

### Evolutionary Timeline of the TotalEnergies Facility in Porcelette, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2012**: The facility, named SunPower Manufacturing De Vernejoul, was commissioned. It produces photovoltaic modules specifically for residential and commercial rooftop markets with a production capacity of 44 megawatts peak (MWp), expected to yield around 150,000 modules annually. The modules utilize Maxeon solar cells with a median efficiency of 20%.

- **July 2021**: The facility is set to start producing frameless, glass-free solar roofing products known as Maxeon Air solar panels. These panels are designed for roofs that cannot accommodate conventional modules, with a reported weight of 6 kg/m² and a conversion efficiency of 20.9%. Maxeon aims to install 1.4 GWp of these Air modules over the next decade.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2021**: The facility is to receive an unspecified portion of a €118.6 million low-carbon innovation fund from the EU to support the production of the new Maxeon Air solar panels. The exact amount of funding will be determined in the final quarter of 2021.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2012**: Commissioning of the SunPower Manufacturing De Vernejoul facility.
- **July 2021**: Announcement of plans to produce Maxeon Air solar panels at the Porcelette facility, with production expected to begin in the first quarter of 2022.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2012**: Approximately 80 workers were employed at the facility, with a preference for local residents.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is controlled by TotalEnergies through its shareholder, SunPower, indicating a joint ownership structure where TotalEnergies has significant control over operations.
