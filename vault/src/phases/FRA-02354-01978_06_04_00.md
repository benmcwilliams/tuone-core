## FRA-02354-01978

### Evolutionary Timeline of the SunPower Facility in Porcelette, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2012**: The SunPower Manufacturing De Vernejoul facility in Porcelette is commissioned. It has a production capacity of 44 megawatts peak (MWp) and is designed to produce approximately 150,000 high-efficiency solar modules annually, specifically for the residential and commercial rooftop markets. The modules utilize SunPower's Maxeon solar cells, which have a median efficiency of 20%.

- **February 2017**: SunPower supplies 64.4 MW of its E-Series solar panels for seven solar PV projects in France, with a significant portion of the modules manufactured at the Porcelette facility.

- **July 2021**: Maxeon Solar Technologies, controlled by TotalEnergies and a shareholder of SunPower, announces plans to produce frameless, glass-free solar roofing products at the Porcelette facility. The new product, Maxeon Air solar panels, is expected to have a conversion efficiency of 20.9% and aims to meet an estimated demand of over 4 GW in Europe.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2021**: The facility is set to receive funding from a €118.6 million EU low-carbon innovation fund to support the production of the new Maxeon Air solar panels. The exact amount of funding is to be determined in the final quarter of the year.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2012**: The commissioning of the SunPower Manufacturing De Vernejoul facility is announced, marking the start of operations in Porcelette.

- **February 2017**: SunPower's collaboration with La Compagnie du Vent is highlighted, with the supply of 64.4 MW of solar panels for multiple projects.

- **July 2021**: Announcement of the new product line (Maxeon Air solar panels) and the associated EU funding application.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2012**: The facility employs approximately 80 workers, with a preference for local residents. 

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is a joint venture between TotalEnergies and SunPower, with TotalEnergies being the controlling shareholder of Maxeon Solar Technologies, which operates the facility.
