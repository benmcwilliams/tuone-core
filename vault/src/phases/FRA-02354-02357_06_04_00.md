## FRA-02354-02357

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Maxeon Facility in Porcelette, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2021**: Maxeon Solar Technologies announced plans to produce its frameless, glass-free "Maxeon Air" solar panels at the Porcelette facility. The company estimated an unmet demand of over 4 GW for this product in Europe, with plans to install 1.4 GWp over the next decade. The facility was set to produce these panels, which weigh 6 kg/m² and have a conversion efficiency of 20.9%.
- **October 2022**: Maxeon announced the closure of the Porcelette facility, which had been upgraded to produce the Air rooftop modules. The plant was originally capable of producing 150,000 solar panels annually with a 44 MWp production line, but was in the process of implementing a new line for the Air modules with a maximum capacity of 16 MWp per year before its closure.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2021**: Maxeon was named a recipient of a portion of a €118.6 million EU low-carbon innovation fund to support the development of its solar technology at the Porcelette facility. The exact amount of funding was to be determined in the final quarter of 2021.
- **October 2022**: The company had received approximately €5 million from the EU and an additional €400,000 to relaunch the manufacturing facility, but ultimately decided not to participate in these subsidy programs.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2021**: Announcement of plans to produce Maxeon Air solar panels at the Porcelette facility, with production expected to start in the first quarter of 2022.
- **October 2022**: Maxeon officially shut down the Porcelette facility, marking the end of its operations in Europe.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2022**: Following the closure of the Porcelette facility, Maxeon stated that it would continue to employ almost twice the number of people in France compared to those who worked at the Porcelette plant, indicating a shift in workforce rather than outright job loss.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **2012 - October 2022**: Maxeon Solar Technologies owned the Porcelette facility exclusively since its acquisition in 2012. The facility was part of Maxeon's operations under the SunPower brand outside the US until its closure.
