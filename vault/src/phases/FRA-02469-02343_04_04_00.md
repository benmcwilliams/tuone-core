### Evolutionary Timeline of the Lhyfe Facility in Saint-Nazaire, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2022**: Lhyfe, in collaboration with Chantiers de l’Atlantique, announced plans to develop the world’s first offshore renewable hydrogen production demonstrator, named Sealhyfe. The facility aims to produce renewable hydrogen using wind power, with a minimum capacity of 100 MW planned for future installations.
- **September 2022**: The Sealhyfe project was officially launched, with a production capacity of up to 400 kg of renewable green hydrogen per day, equivalent to 1 MW of power. The project began as an 18-month pilot, initially operating near the shore before moving 20 kilometers offshore.
- **June 2023**: The Sealhyfe platform reached its start-of-production milestone after eight months of testing at the Port of Saint-Nazaire. The platform is equipped with a 1 MW electrolyzer supplied by Plug and is designed to operate autonomously.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2022**: The Sealhyfe project was developed in collaboration with multiple partners, including Chantiers de l’Atlantique, the Port of Saint-Nazaire, Kraken Subsea Solutions, Pays de la Loire Region, and the French energy and environment agency ADEME. Specific investment amounts were not disclosed, but the collaboration indicates significant financial backing for the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2022**: Announcement of the memorandum of understanding between Lhyfe and Chantiers de l’Atlantique to develop offshore hydrogen production platforms.
- **September 2022**: Launch of the Sealhyfe pilot project, marking the start of the world's first offshore renewable green hydrogen production demonstrator.
- **June 2023**: Completion of the first phase of testing at the Port of Saint-Nazaire, leading to the transition to offshore production conditions.
