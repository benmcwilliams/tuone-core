### Evolutionary Timeline of Chantiers de l’Atlantique Facility in Saint-Nazaire, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2022**: Chantiers de l’Atlantique collaborates with Lhyfe to develop the world’s first offshore renewable hydrogen production demonstrator, aiming for a minimum capacity of 100 MW. The project is set to launch in September 2022 off the coast of Saint-Nazaire.
- **April 2023**: Chantiers de l’Atlantique is contracted to build the electrical substation for the 496 MW Îles d’Yeu and Noirmoutier offshore wind farm, which will feature 62 Siemens Gamesa 8 MW wind turbines. The substation is a critical component for connecting the wind farm to the grid.
- **April 2023**: The facility is also involved in the construction of the electrical substation for the Dieppe Le Tréport offshore wind farm, further solidifying its role in offshore wind energy infrastructure.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2022**: The partnership with Lhyfe for offshore hydrogen production indicates a significant investment in renewable energy technology, although specific financial figures are not disclosed.
- **April 2023**: The Îles d’Yeu and Noirmoutier offshore wind farm represents an investment of approximately €2.5 billion, with Chantiers de l’Atlantique playing a key role in the construction of the substation.
- **April 2023**: The Dieppe Le Tréport offshore wind farm involves an investment of around €2.7 billion, with Chantiers de l’Atlantique again responsible for the substation construction.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2022**: The offshore renewable hydrogen production demonstrator is set to launch.
- **April 2023**: Final Investment Decision (FID) is made for the 496 MW Îles d’Yeu and Noirmoutier offshore wind farm, with construction expected to start soon.
- **April 2023**: FID is also reached for the Dieppe Le Tréport offshore wind farm, with construction commencing in 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2022**: The collaboration with Lhyfe is expected to create jobs related to the development of offshore hydrogen production platforms, although specific numbers are not provided.
- **April 2023**: The Îles d’Yeu and Noirmoutier project could create up to 1,600 direct jobs in France.
- **April 2023**: The Dieppe Le Tréport project is expected to create 1,300 direct jobs during its construction phase.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Chantiers de l’Atlantique is involved in joint ventures for the offshore wind projects, specifically with companies like Lhyfe for hydrogen production and various partners for the offshore wind farms. The ownership structure for the facility itself is not explicitly stated, but it operates as a key player in collaborative projects rather than as an exclusive owner.
