## FRA-02469-04937

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Enbridge Facility in Saint-Nazaire, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2020**: GE Renewable Energy produced the first nacelle for the 480 MW Saint-Nazaire offshore wind farm, which consists of 80 Haliade 150-6 MW wind turbines. The nacelles are assembled at the Montoir-de-Bretagne production site. The wind farm is expected to be fully operational in 2022.
- **November 2022**: The Saint-Nazaire offshore wind farm officially began operations. It has the capacity to meet 20% of Loire-Atlantique’s annual electricity consumption and can generate enough energy for 700,000 people.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2019**: Construction works for the project began, with significant investments from EDF Renewables, Enbridge, and CPP Investments. The project is part of France's strategy to increase renewable energy sources.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2019**: Construction commenced on the Saint-Nazaire offshore wind farm.
- **October 2020**: DEME Offshore completed a subsea drill tailored for the project, indicating progress in the installation of foundations.
- **November 2022**: The project entered the operational phase, marking the completion of construction and the start of energy production.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2020**: The project is expected to create 1,000 local jobs during the construction phase, with over 500 GE Renewable Energy employees involved in the nacelle assembly.
- **November 2022**: During the construction phase, the project employed an average of 2,300 people in France, including 1,200 in the Pays de la Loire region. Post-construction, around 100 people will be employed for operation and maintenance activities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Saint-Nazaire offshore wind farm is developed jointly by EDF Renewables and Enbridge, with CPP Investments also involved. The ownership structure indicates a partnership rather than exclusive ownership by Enbridge.
