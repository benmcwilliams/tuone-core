### Evolutionary Timeline of the Atlantique Offshore Energy Facility in Saint-Nazaire, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** September 2020
   - **Details:** Atlantique Offshore Energy began construction of the Fécamp offshore wind farm electrical substation. The facility is designed to transform up to 500MW of offshore wind energy. Atlantique Offshore Energy is responsible for the topside and jacket-type foundation design, manufacturing, and commissioning.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** September 2020
   - **Details:** The construction of the Fécamp offshore wind farm electrical substation involves a consortium that includes EDF Renouvelables, Enbridge, and WPD Offshore. The project mobilizes significant resources, including 400 workers, indicating substantial investment in the facility's development.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** September 2020
   - **Details:** The construction of the Fécamp offshore wind farm electrical substation officially started. The delivery of the substation is scheduled for 2022. Additionally, the same consortium is also constructing the Saint-Nazaire offshore wind farm electrical substation, which is set to be commissioned in 2021.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** September 2020
   - **Details:** The construction of the Fécamp offshore wind farm electrical substation mobilizes 400 people, indicating job creation associated with this project.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** September 2020
   - **Details:** The facility operates under a consortium model, with Atlantique Offshore Energy as a key partner alongside GE Grid Solutions and SDI, indicating that it does not have exclusive ownership of the facility but is part of a joint venture for this project.
