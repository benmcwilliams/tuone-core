## FRA-02469-07963

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ocean Winds Facility in Saint-Nazaire, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2024**: The facility is involved in the pre-assembly of components for a 496 MW offshore wind farm, specifically the Îles d’Yeu and Noirmoutier project. The facility will utilize Siemens Gamesa 8 MW turbines, with a total capacity of nearly 500 MW, capable of powering approximately 800,000 people annually. The offshore work is already underway, with more than half of the monopiles installed.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2024**: The total estimated investment for the offshore wind farm project is EUR 2.5 billion. This investment is associated with the construction and operational phases of the facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2024**: The first turbine blades for the offshore wind farm arrived at the Saint-Nazaire Port on December 5, 2024. The project is expected to span two and a half years, with offshore installation work starting in the spring of 2025. 

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2024**: The construction project is projected to create 1,600 direct jobs in France, including around 100 positions in port-related professions.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2024**: The facility is part of a consortium known as Éoliennes en Mer des Îles d’Yeu et de Noirmoutier (EMYN), which includes Ocean Winds, Sumitomo Corporation, Banque des Territoires, and Vendée Energie. This indicates that Ocean Winds does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a joint venture.
