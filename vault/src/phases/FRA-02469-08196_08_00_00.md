### Evolutionary Timeline of the Éoliennes en Mer des Îles d’Yeu et de Noirmoutier (EMYN) Facility in Saint-Nazaire, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2024**: The facility is developing the Îles d’Yeu and Noirmoutier offshore wind farm, which will utilize Siemens Gamesa 8 MW turbines. The total production capacity of the facility is nearly 500 MW, sufficient to power approximately 800,000 people annually. The first turbine blades, measuring 81 meters in length, arrived at the port of Saint-Nazaire for pre-assembly.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2024**: The total estimated investment for the offshore wind farm is EUR 2.5 billion. This investment is associated with the construction project that spans two and a half years.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2024**: The first components of the offshore turbines began arriving at the Saint-Nazaire Port in mid-November, with the first shipment of turbine blades arriving on December 5, 2024. The offshore work is currently underway, with more than half of the monopiles already installed at the site. The project is expected to start transporting components offshore in the spring of 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2024**: The construction project is projected to create 1,600 direct jobs in France, including around 100 positions in port-related professions.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2024**: The facility is developed by a consortium known as Éoliennes en Mer des Îles d’Yeu et de Noirmoutier (EMYN), which includes Ocean Winds, Sumitomo Corporation, Banque des Territoires, and Vendée Energie. This indicates that the facility is not under exclusive ownership but rather a joint venture among multiple stakeholders.
