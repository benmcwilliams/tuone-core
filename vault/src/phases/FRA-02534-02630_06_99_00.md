### Evolutionary Timeline of the Alliaverre Facility in Amiens, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2022**: Alliaverre announced plans to establish a solar glass plant in Amiens, France, with a production capacity of 600 MW per year (50 MW per month). The facility will produce solar-grade glass for three market segments: 2 mm glass for glass/glass modules, 3.2 mm glass for classic modules, and solar tiles. The plant will feature fully automated operations, including cutting, coating, and tempering of flat glass.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2022**: The company is finalizing a €5 million ($5.2 million) investment in collaboration with a French investment fund to support the establishment of the new production facility. Additionally, Alliaverre is seeking regional aid to help offset the higher manufacturing costs in Europe, which are approximately 30% more expensive than in Asia.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2022**: Alliaverre's general manager announced the intention to build the solar glass plant by the end of 2022 or early 2023, aiming to reduce delivery times for European customers and address rising shipping costs and manufacturing constraints in Asia.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2022**: While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned, the establishment of the new facility is expected to create jobs in the Amiens region, contributing to local employment opportunities in the clean technology sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2022**: The article does not specify whether Alliaverre will have full ownership of the facility or if it will be a joint venture. However, it indicates that the company is independently moving forward with the establishment of the plant in Amiens.
