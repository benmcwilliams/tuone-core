## FRA-02598-00110

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Bosch Facility in Vénissieux, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09-12-2010**: Bosch Solar Energy announces plans to set up a photovoltaic (PV) module assembly plant in Vénissieux, transitioning from a diesel systems location. The facility aims to produce resource-conserving photovoltaic products to meet growing market demand.
- **15-03-2012**: Production begins on a new 150 megawatt (MW) crystalline photovoltaic module manufacturing line. At peak production, the facility is expected to produce 2,000 units daily, totaling 600,000 annually. The production lines are flexible, allowing for five different types of mono- and poly-crystalline modules.
- **26-09-2012**: A second PV module line is opened, maintaining the capacity at 150 MW and allowing for the same production output as the first line.
- **01-10-2012**: The facility faces a temporary idling of production for two weeks due to overcapacity and declining governmental support, despite being recognized as the largest crystalline silicon module plant in France.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09-12-2010**: Initial investment in the facility is implied through the establishment of the PV module assembly plant, although specific financial details are not disclosed.
- **15-03-2012**: The commencement of production indicates significant investment in the new manufacturing line, although exact figures are not provided.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09-12-2010**: Announcement of the establishment of the PV module assembly plant.
- **15-03-2012**: Start of production at the new facility.
- **26-09-2012**: Opening of a second production line.
- **01-10-2012**: Announcement of a temporary idling of production due to market conditions.
- **12-03-2014**: Bosch announces it is in advanced talks to sell the Vénissieux facility as part of its exit from the solar industry.

#### 4. Labour
- **15-03-2012**: The establishment of the PV module facility helps retain 200 jobs at the site, which were previously at risk due to the decline in diesel system production.
- **01-10-2012**: The facility continues to employ approximately 200 workers despite the temporary idling of production.
- **12-03-2014**: The potential sale of the facility raises concerns about job security, although Bosch aims to secure as many jobs as possible through the sale process.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **09-12-2010**: Bosch is the sole owner of the facility as it transitions from diesel systems to solar energy production.
- **12-03-2014**: Bosch is in advanced talks to sell the facility, indicating a shift in ownership structure is imminent as the company exits the solar market.
