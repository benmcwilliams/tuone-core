## FRA-02623-02735

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Carbon Facility in Fos-sur-Mer, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2023**: Carbon announces plans to build a solar production facility in Fos-sur-Mer, with an annual production capacity of 5 GW of solar cells and 3.5 GW of PV modules. The facility will utilize TOPCon and IBC technologies.
- **April 2024**: Carbon requests construction permits for the 5 GW solar panel factory, confirming the planned capacity remains unchanged.
- **May 2024**: Carbon reveals plans to start pilot production at the facility in fall 2025, utilizing a 500 MW pilot production line for TOPCon panels.
- **September 2024**: Carbon acquires Photowatt, integrating it into its industrial project, which includes a 500 MW solar panel assembly unit and the 5 GW facility in Fos-sur-Mer.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: The investment for the facility is set at €1.5 billion ($1.6 billion), financed through equity, public funds, and bank financing.
- **May 2024**: The total investment for the facility is updated to approximately €1.7 billion ($1.8 billion).
- **September 2024**: Carbon announces an additional €40 million investment to support Photowatt's activities and the establishment of a 500 MW module production unit.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2023**: Carbon selects a 60-hectare site in Fos-sur-Mer for the factory, entering the public consultation phase.
- **April 2024**: Construction permits are requested, with a public inquiry planned for summer 2024.
- **Fall 2025**: Pilot production is expected to commence at the facility.
- **End of 2026**: Full operations are anticipated to begin at the facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2024**: Carbon announces plans to create 3,000 jobs at the gigafactory in Fos-sur-Mer and an additional 200 jobs at the new R&D center in Istres.
- **September 2024**: The acquisition of Photowatt is expected to create 200 new jobs at its Bourgoin-Jallieu site by 2026.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2023**: Carbon is identified as the sole owner of the facility, with no mention of joint ventures or partnerships regarding the ownership of the Fos-sur-Mer facility. The acquisition of Photowatt indicates a strategy to integrate operations but does not alter the ownership of the Fos-sur-Mer facility itself.
