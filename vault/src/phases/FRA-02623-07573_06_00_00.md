### Evolutionary Timeline of the ArcelorMittal Méditerranée Facility in Fos-sur-Mer, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2017**: ArcelorMittal Méditerranée, in collaboration with EDF Energies Nouvelles, commissioned a 12MW solar plant named La Fossette at the Fos-sur-Mer industrial and port complex. The solar plant covers a 15-hectare area and consists of over 45,000 photovoltaic panels. It is designed to generate electricity equivalent to the annual consumption of approximately 7,400 inhabitants.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2017**: The development of the La Fossette solar plant involved the utilization of ArcelorMittal's industrial land, indicating a strategic investment in renewable energy production. Specific financial details regarding the investment were not disclosed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2017**: The La Fossette solar project was officially commissioned, marking the completion of the construction phase which lasted 10 months. The project was developed and built by EDF Energies Nouvelles on land owned by ArcelorMittal.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2017**: The construction of the La Fossette solar plant involved the employment of up to 40 individuals over the 10-month construction period.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2017**: The facility is owned by ArcelorMittal Méditerranée, which made its land available for the solar project, indicating full ownership of the facility by ArcelorMittal.
