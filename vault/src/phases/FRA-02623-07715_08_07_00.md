## FRA-02623-07715

### Evolutionary Timeline of Eiffage Métal Facility in Fos-sur-Mer, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **03-10-2022**: Eiffage Métal's facility in Fos-sur-Mer is involved in assembling floating foundations for the Provence Grand Large floating offshore wind farm, which will feature three Siemens Gamesa 8 MW wind turbines. The facility collaborates with Smulders, its Belgian subsidiary, for this project.
- **24-05-2023**: The first floating foundation has been loaded out from Eiffage Métal’s factory, equipped with an anchoring system and submersible buoys, capable of supporting wind turbines generating 8.4 MW.
- **25-08-2023**: The first of the three Siemens Gamesa 8 MW wind turbines has been installed at the Provence Grand Large project, with the floating foundations assembled at Eiffage Métal’s site.
- **13-10-2023**: The floating offshore wind farm is reported to be nearing completion, with all three turbines installed and the project expected to produce 24 MW of electricity, sufficient for 45,000 inhabitants.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- No specific investment or subsidy details were mentioned in the articles regarding the Eiffage Métal facility itself. However, the overall Provence Grand Large project is owned by Parc Eolien Offshore de Provence Grand Large, a subsidiary of EDF Renouvelables, and Enbridge Eolien France 2 S.à.r.l (EEF2).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **03-10-2022**: Announcement of the contract for the floating foundations for the Provence Grand Large project, with operations expected in 2023.
- **24-05-2023**: The first floating foundation is loaded out from Eiffage Métal’s facility.
- **25-08-2023**: The first turbine is installed at the floating offshore wind project.
- **13-10-2023**: The project is reported to be complete, with commissioning planned for early 2024.
- **21-11-2024**: The project starts delivering power to the grid, marking its operational status.

#### 4. Labour
- **31-10-2024**: Over 300 people are mobilized at Eiffage Métal’s industrial site in Fos-sur-Mer for the construction of floating foundations for the Les Eoliennes Flottantes du Golfe du Lion (EFGL) project, indicating job creation associated with ongoing operations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Eiffage Métal facility is part of the larger Provence Grand Large project, which is owned by Parc Eolien Offshore de Provence Grand Large (a subsidiary of EDF Renouvelables) and Enbridge Eolien France 2 S.à.r.l (EEF2). There is no indication of exclusive ownership of the facility by Eiffage Métal; it operates in collaboration with its subsidiary Smulders for specific projects. 

This timeline captures the key developments and operational milestones of the Eiffage Métal facility in Fos-sur-Mer, focusing on its role in the Provence Grand Large floating offshore wind project.
