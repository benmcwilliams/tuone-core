### Evolutionary Timeline of Dracula Technologies Facility in Valence, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2023**: Dracula Technologies announces the construction of its organic photovoltaic (OPV) module factory, named the "Green MicroPower Factory," in Valence, France. The facility is designed to produce OPV devices using digital printing technology, with a production capacity of up to 150 million cm² of OPV modules annually. The first shipments are scheduled to start in 2024.
- **September 2024**: The Valence plant officially inaugurates its OPV module production line on September 12, with the same production capacity of 150 million cm². The company aims to quadruple this capacity to 600 million cm² by 2026 through increased shifts and new production lines.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2023**: The total investment for the Valence facility is reported to be €15 million. This includes €5.5 million from a fundraising effort involving Banque des Territoires, the Auvergne Rhône-Alpes sovereign fund, and Semtech. Additionally, €1.6 million in grants from the EIC Accelerator program and loans contribute to the financing. The French government has also provided €5 million as part of the France 2030 “First Factory” tender.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2023**: Announcement of the construction of the Green MicroPower Factory in Valence, with production expected to start in early 2024.
- **September 2024**: Official inauguration of the OPV module production line at the Valence facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2023**: Dracula Technologies plans to hire over 100 additional employees, aiming to reach more than 250 employees by 2030.
- **September 2024**: The facility starts with around 40 employees, with plans to recruit approximately 100 more by 2026, including cleanroom operators and engineers.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify any joint ventures or partnerships regarding the ownership of the Valence facility, implying that Dracula Technologies owns the facility exclusively.
