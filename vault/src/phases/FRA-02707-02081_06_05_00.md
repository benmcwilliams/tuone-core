### Evolutionary Timeline of the Enel Facility in Cadarache, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2021**: A demonstration unit equipped with 36 DC/DC maximum power point trackers (MPPT) converters was put into operation at the CEA demonstrator site in Cadarache, France. This technology aims to improve energy efficiency in solar power plants by reducing the impact of installation site faults and increasing overall power output. The converters have a maximum power of 500 W at the module level, allowing for an increase in the number of panels in a row while maintaining a low output voltage of less than 1,500 V DC.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **06-10-2022**: Enel Green Power and the National Solar Energy Institute (INES) announced their collaboration to develop the DC/DC MPPT technology, which is intended for applications in large ground-based power plants. The specific financial investments related to this collaboration were not detailed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **06-10-2022**: Enel Green Power reported that the addition of DC/DC MPPT converters to a pilot PV plant has resulted in a 1% energy production gain. The facility in Cadarache is part of this pilot project, and the results from the demonstration unit will inform the deployment of a larger 1 MW power plant planned in Catania, Italy.
