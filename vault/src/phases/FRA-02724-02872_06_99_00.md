### Evolutionary Timeline of the Envie 2E Aquitaine Facility in Saint-Loubès, France

1. **Technology and Operations**
   - **Date**: October 2022
   - **Details**: The Envie 2E Aquitaine facility inaugurated a solar module recycling center in Saint-Loubès, France. The facility utilizes a hot blade delamination process, allowing it to process 4,000 tons of solar panels per year. This innovative technology enables the recovery of flat glass and isolation of the polymer layer containing the cells, achieving a recycling rate of 95% of the materials used in solar panels.

2. **Investment and Development**
   - **Date**: October 2022
   - **Details**: The facility was established with an investment of €2 million (approximately $1.9 million). This investment was part of a broader initiative by the French recycling group Soren, which launched a tender in February 2021 to set up three new facilities for solar panel recycling in France.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**
   - **Date**: October 2022
   - **Details**: The facility was officially inaugurated in October 2022, marking it as the first of its kind in Europe for solar module recycling. The project was selected following a call for tenders by Soren, and it represents a significant milestone in the recycling of solar panels.

4. **Labour**
   - **Date**: October 2022
   - **Details**: The facility is expected to create 25 jobs as a result of its operations, contributing to local employment in the Saint-Loubès area.

5. **Ownership Structure**
   - **Date**: October 2022
   - **Details**: The facility is operated by Envie 2E Aquitaine in collaboration with Soren, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by a single entity.
