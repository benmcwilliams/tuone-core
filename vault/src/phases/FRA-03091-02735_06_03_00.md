### Evolutionary Timeline of the Carbon Facility in Bourgoin-Jallieu, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** September 2024
   - **Details:** Carbon has acquired Photowatt, which will be integrated into Carbon's industrial project. The facility will focus on producing solar panels with a planned production capacity of 500 MW for photovoltaic module assembly. Additionally, there are plans for a larger manufacturing facility with a capacity of 5 GW for photovoltaic cells and 3.5 GW for modules by 2026-27. Photowatt will also serve as a pilot facility for testing and refining module production.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** September 2024
   - **Details:** Carbon announced a €40 million ($44.6 million) investment to support the activities at the Bourgoin-Jallieu site, specifically for the creation of the 500 MW photovoltaic module production unit, which is set for completion by the end of 2025. 

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** September 2024
   - **Details:** The acquisition of Photowatt by Carbon is a strategic move to meet the growing demand for Made-in-France solar panels, particularly in the residential market. The project includes the establishment of the Carbon Lab, a research center aimed at scaling production by 2027.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** September 2024
   - **Details:** The acquisition is expected to secure jobs at the Photowatt site, which currently employs 170 workers. EDF Renewables, the previous owner, indicated that Carbon's acquisition would provide new momentum to sustain the company’s activity and help secure jobs.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** September 2024
   - **Details:** Carbon has acquired Photowatt from EDF Renewables, indicating that Carbon now owns the facility. The acquisition marks a shift from EDF's ownership to Carbon's full ownership, allowing for direct management and operational control of the facility.
