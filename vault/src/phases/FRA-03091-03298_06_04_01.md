### Evolutionary Timeline of the Photowatt Facility in Bourgoin-Jallieu, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **1979**: Photowatt was founded, initially producing solar cells for spatial applications.
- **2012**: Photowatt was acquired by EDF, a French energy giant.
- **2021**: The facility faced potential closure as EDF considered liquidating its solar module production due to financial difficulties.
- **2022**: Photowatt unveiled new bifacial solar modules based on G12 wafers, with a production capacity of 120 MW at the Bourgoin-Jallieu plant. The new modules, with power outputs ranging from 600 W to 660 W, were set to be available from early 2023, with 40 MW of orders already booked.
- **2024**: A draft agreement was reached for Photowatt's acquisition by Carbon, which plans to integrate it into a larger solar PV manufacturing project. The facility is expected to serve as a pilot for testing and optimizing production processes.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2024**: The proposed acquisition by Carbon includes an investment plan of €40 million (approximately US$44 million) to build a module assembly plant with an annual capacity of 500 MW.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2021**: Reports emerged about EDF considering the closure of the Bourgoin-Jallieu facility due to financial challenges.
- **May 2022**: Photowatt announced the launch of new bifacial solar modules, adapting the plant to handle 210 mm wafers.
- **September 2024**: A draft agreement for the acquisition of Photowatt by Carbon was reported, with plans for integration into a larger manufacturing project.

#### 4. Labour
- **2024**: The acquisition by Carbon is expected to create 200 new jobs at the Bourgoin-Jallieu site by 2026, enhancing the workforce in response to the growing demand for solar panels.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **2012**: Photowatt was acquired by EDF, making it a subsidiary of the energy group.
- **2024**: A draft agreement indicates that Carbon is set to acquire Photowatt, which would change the ownership structure from EDF to Carbon.
