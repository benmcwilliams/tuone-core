## FRA-03103-03008

### Holosolis Facility Evolution Timeline - Moselle, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Announcement of Gigafactory**: On **May 17, 2023**, Holosolis announced plans to build a solar photovoltaic (PV) module gigafactory in Moselle, France. The facility is set to be Europe’s largest, with a production capacity of **5GW per year**, equating to **10 million photovoltaic modules annually**. The factory is designed to incorporate the latest photovoltaic technologies, focusing on energy efficiency and low carbon footprint.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Support and Partnerships**: Holosolis was founded by EIT InnoEnergy and is supported by the IDEC Group and French solar power producer TSE. The project aims to enhance Europe’s manufacturing and energy security, contributing to the European Solar Industrial Alliance's goal of achieving **30GW of annual capacity by 2025**.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Production Start Date**: The facility is expected to start production in **2025**. The site spans **50 hectares**, allowing for future expansions as new technologies and manufacturing capacities are integrated.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The gigafactory is projected to create approximately **1,700 jobs** upon its operational launch.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: Holosolis is a startup created by EIT InnoEnergy, indicating that it is likely under full ownership of Holosolis, with support from its partners but no indication of joint ventures or shared ownership structures.
