### Evolutionary Timeline of the Veolia Facility in Rousset, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2017**: Veolia announced the opening of France's first solar module recycling facility at its site in Rousset, Bouches-du-Rhône. The facility is operated by Veolia’s unit, Triade Electronique. Initially, it was set to process 1,400 tonnes of end-of-life photovoltaic (PV) modules per year, with an expected increase to 4,000 tonnes by 2021. The facility focuses on recovering secondary raw materials such as glass, aluminum, and copper, contributing to the circular economy.

- **May 2020**: The facility was reported to have a processing capacity of up to 4,000 tonnes annually, achieving a 95% recovery rate of materials from the PV panels.

- **August 2020**: The facility was highlighted for its exceptional recycling rate of 94.7% for crystalline silicon-based PV modules, emphasizing its role in the management of solar waste and compliance with EU regulations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2017**: The establishment of the facility was part of a 4-year contract secured by Veolia from PV Cycle for the treatment of end-of-life photovoltaic modules. Specific investment amounts were not disclosed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2017**: Announcement of the facility's opening and its operational commencement for recycling PV modules.
- **2021**: The facility was expected to reach its full operational capacity of processing 4,000 tonnes of materials.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles did not specify exact job creation or job loss figures related to the establishment or operation of the facility. However, the opening of the facility likely contributed to job creation in the recycling sector, given the nature of the operations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Veolia operates the facility through its unit, Triade Electronique, indicating that Veolia has full ownership of the facility. There is no mention of joint ventures or partnerships in the context of ownership.
