### Evolutionary Timeline of TotalEnergies Facility in Channay, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2021**: TotalEnergies commissioned a 240 kW agrivoltaic demonstrator plant in Channay, utilizing vertical bifacial panels and Next2Sun mounting structures. The facility integrates agricultural production with renewable electricity generation.
- **January 2023**: The facility's power output was updated to 237 kW, with the same vertical bifacial photovoltaic panels. The project includes various crops, such as wheat and aromatic herbs, and features a spacing of 12 meters between modules.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2021**: The Channay facility received €50,000 in crowdfunding, with 42% of the funds sourced from local residents. This investment was part of a broader collaboration with various agricultural and environmental organizations.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2021**: The 240 kW agrivoltaic demonstrator plant was officially energized.
- **January 2023**: TotalEnergies announced the results of the first harvest from the Channay facility, indicating successful agricultural yield increases and environmental benefits.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2023**: The project has contributed to job creation through its collaborations with local agricultural cooperatives and environmental consultancies, although specific job numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- TotalEnergies appears to have full ownership of the Channay facility, as it is developed in partnership with Next2Sun and other local agricultural entities, but no joint venture or shared ownership structure was mentioned.
