### Evolutionary Timeline of the Arverne Facility in Alsace, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2018**: Arverne was founded by Pierre Brossollet, an oil engineer, to develop integrated solutions for geothermal energy and lithium extraction.
- **2023**: The facility focuses on both deep and surface geothermal energy. It operates with three surface drilling rigs and five deep drilling units, with plans to order four additional rigs following the IPO. The facility aims to extract heat and low-carbon lithium from geothermal waters in northern Alsace, with production expected to start in **2027**. The deep geothermal energy projects target depths of 1500 to 3000 meters, while surface geothermal energy involves drilling to 200 meters for heat pump systems.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2023**: Arverne announced its intention to go public, aiming to raise over EUR 150 million through the IPO. The operation values the company at over EUR 300 million. The funds will be used to finance projects, particularly in lithium extraction, which is expected to exceed one billion euros in investment needs.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2023**: Announcement of the IPO and plans for lithium extraction and geothermal energy projects. The company is preparing for production to begin in **2027**.

#### 4. Labour
- **2023**: The company employs nearly 150 people and anticipates significant growth in turnover, projecting revenues of 200 to 350 million euros by **2027** and over one billion euros by **2030**. The expansion and development of the facility are expected to create additional jobs, although specific job creation figures have not been detailed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2023**: Arverne is the parent company and holds more than 60% ownership of Lithium de France, along with two subsidiaries focused on geothermal energy (2gré and DrillHeat). The company is backed by investments from Crédit Mutuel Equity, Ademe Investissement, and Sycomore AM, indicating a structure that includes significant external investment but retains majority ownership within the Arverne group.
