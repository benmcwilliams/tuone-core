### Evolutionary Timeline of the Voltec Solar Facility in Dinsheim-sur-Bruche, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Production**: The facility operates two production lines, each with a capacity of 250 MW, primarily producing PERC technology solar panels.
- **Future Plans**: 
  - Launch of a new product range using TOPCon cells in spring 2024.
  - Development of four-junction (4T) perovskite-silicon technology in collaboration with the Institut Photovoltaïque d’Île-de-France (IPVF).
  - Plans to establish a pilot production line by the end of 2023 and a 200 MW industrial demonstrator by 2025.
  - Capacity expansion to 1 GW by 2027 and 5 GW by 2030.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Funding Secured**: Voltec Solar has secured €9.3 million ($10.1 million) from Ademe, France’s environmental agency, to accelerate the production of perovskite-silicon tandem solar panels.
- **Investment Estimates**: 
  - The pilot line will require an investment of €15 million.
  - The industrial demonstrator will require €50 million.
  - Total estimated investment for the project is around €1 billion by 2030.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Key Dates**:
  - **End of 2023**: Establishment of the pilot production line.
  - **2025**: Launch of the first 200 MW industrial demonstrator.
  - **2027**: Increase factory capacity to 1 GW.
  - **2030**: Target capacity of 5 GW.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: Specific job creation figures have not been detailed in the article, but the expansion and new technology initiatives are expected to create new employment opportunities as production capacity increases.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture**: Voltec Solar and IPVF have created a joint venture named France PV Industrie to implement the project, indicating that the facility is not solely owned by Voltec Solar but involves collaboration with IPVF.
