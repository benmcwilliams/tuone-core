### Evolutionary Timeline of the Air Liquide Facility in Provence, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10/2020**: Air Liquide, in collaboration with Engie SA, announced the "HyGreen Provence" project aimed at producing renewable hydrogen through water electrolysis. The facility is set to have an electrolysis capacity ramping up to **250 megawatts** by **2026**, which will produce approximately **20,000 tons of clean hydrogen** annually. The project will utilize solar farms to power the hydrogen production, contributing to the reduction of CO2 emissions in the local refining and petrochemical complex.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10/2020**: The project is expected to require government subsidies to be financially viable, as producing green hydrogen is currently about three times more expensive than traditional methods. The French government is anticipated to present a more ambitious plan for hydrogen investment in the near future, building on a previous commitment of **100 million euros** for hydrogen vehicle subsidies.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **10/2020**: The announcement of the HyGreen Provence project marks a significant milestone in the development of large-scale hydrogen production in France. The project is designed to be connected to a salt-cavern storage site and the gas grid, with plans to fuel local fleets of electric buses and trucks.

#### 4. Labour

#### 5. Ownership Structure
