### Evolutionary Timeline of the Araymond Facility in Southern France, FRA

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2024**: The Coeur de Savoie floating solar power plant was inaugurated, featuring a 260 kW demonstrator utilizing a new aluminum floating structure. This technology minimizes its footprint on water and material needs. The floats and anchors were assembled on-site using a mobile micro-factory, which allows for the manufacturing of aluminum floats directly at the site, reducing transport impacts and on-site storage needs. The HeliosFloat technology, developed in partnership with HeliosLite, is protected by an international patent and is marketed under an exclusive 11-year licensing agreement with Araymond.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2024**: The project was funded by Enercoop Aura, which views floating photovoltaics as an opportunity for regional development and aims to offer low-cost electricity to consumers. The project aims to optimize costs for future deployments targeting larger installations of 1 MW or more.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2024**: The Coeur de Savoie floating solar power plant was officially inaugurated, marking the completion of the demonstrator project. The facility is expected to monitor operation and maintenance costs while working towards optimizing the solution for larger deployments.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2024**: While specific job creation or loss figures were not mentioned, the project is expected to create opportunities for local development and may involve job creation related to the operation and maintenance of the floating solar power plant.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2024**: The facility is a joint project involving Araymond, HeliosLite, and Enercoop Aura. Araymond does not own the facility exclusively but is a key partner in the joint venture, particularly in the development and commercialization of the HeliosFloat technology.
