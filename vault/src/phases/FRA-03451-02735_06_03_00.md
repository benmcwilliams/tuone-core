## FRA-03451-02735

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Carbon Facility in Istres, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **02/2024**: Carbon announces plans to build an R&D center in Istres, which will include technical platforms and operational equipment to enable the scale-up of production. The facility will focus on the marketing of tunnel oxide passivated contact (TOPCon) cells and modules, and will also develop TOPCon back-contact (TBC) cell technology and disruptive technologies like perovskites. The facility will be located near the planned gigafactory in Fos-sur-Mer, which is expected to have a production capacity of 5 GW for ingots and cells, and 3.5 GW for modules.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **02/2024**: Carbon announces a total investment of €33 million (approximately $35.4 million) for the R&D center, which will be developed over several phases. This project benefits from support through the French government’s France 2030 program. The company plans to allocate 3% of its turnover to R&D, translating to around €45 million (approximately $48.4 million) annually.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **02/2024**: Carbon reveals plans for the R&D and training facility, named “CARBON Lab,” to be built on a 13.9-hectare area of currently unused industrial buildings in Istres. The facility is expected to be operational alongside the gigafactory, which is anticipated to be commissioned in late 2026.

#### 4. Labour
- **02/2024**: The establishment of the CARBON Lab is expected to create 200 jobs in the metropolitan area of Istres, in addition to the 3,000 jobs anticipated at the gigafactory in Fos-sur-Mer.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **02/2024**: The articles do not specify the ownership structure of the facility, but it is implied that Carbon is the primary entity responsible for the development and operation of the R&D center in Istres, as it is a direct investment by the company.
