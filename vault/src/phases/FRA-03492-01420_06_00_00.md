### Evolutionary Timeline of the TotalEnergies Facility in Châteauneuf-les-Martigues, FRA

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2021**: TotalEnergies and Engie announced the Masshylia project, which will be France's largest renewable hydrogen production site. The facility will feature a 40 MW electrolyser capable of producing 5 tons of green hydrogen per day, with plans to expand production capacity to 15 tons per day in the future. The project integrates innovative technologies for hydrogen production and storage, powered by solar farms with a total capacity exceeding 100 MW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2021**: The Masshylia project has applied for subsidies from French and European authorities to support its development, including the AMI and IPCEI funding mechanisms. The project is recognized for its potential to reduce CO2 emissions and enhance the economic viability of renewable hydrogen.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2021**: TotalEnergies and Engie signed a cooperation agreement to design, develop, build, and operate the Masshylia project. The partners aim to begin construction in 2022, with production expected to start in 2024, pending necessary financial support and public authorizations.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2021**: While specific job creation figures were not mentioned in the articles, the project is expected to contribute positively to local employment through construction and operational phases, as indicated by the support from regional institutions.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2021**: The facility is a joint venture between TotalEnergies and Engie, indicating that it is not solely owned by TotalEnergies but rather a collaborative effort between the two companies.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic plans for the TotalEnergies facility in Châteauneuf-les-Martigues, highlighting its role in advancing renewable hydrogen production in France.
