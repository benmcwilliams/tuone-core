## FRA-03889-01010

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Al Dhafra, FRA

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2020**: The Al Dhafra solar project was awarded to a consortium including EDF, with a production capacity of **2 GW**. This facility is designed to increase Abu Dhabi's solar power capacity to approximately **3.2 GW** once operational.
- **November 2023**: The facility was inaugurated, spanning over **20 square kilometers** and utilizing almost **4 million solar panels** with innovative bi-facial technology to maximize energy yield. It generates enough electricity to power nearly **200,000 homes** and displaces **2.4 million tonnes of carbon emissions** annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2020**: The consortium submitted a record bid of **$0.0135/kWh** for the project, which was recognized as a significant achievement in solar power cost competitiveness.
- **November 2023**: The project was developed in partnership with Abu Dhabi National Energy Company (TAQA), Masdar, and JinkoPower, indicating substantial investment from these entities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2020**: The project was awarded, with plans for financial closure in the third quarter of 2020 and partial generation expected to begin in the first half of **2022**.
- **November 2023**: The facility was officially inaugurated, marking the completion of the project.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2023**: The construction of the Al Dhafra solar facility created approximately **4,500 jobs** during its peak construction phase.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2020**: The ownership structure of the facility is as follows: **TAQA** owns **60%** (combined with Masdar), while **EDF** and **JinkoPower** each hold a **20%** stake in the project. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones of the EDF facility in Al Dhafra, highlighting its technological advancements, investment details, project status, labor impact, and ownership structure.
