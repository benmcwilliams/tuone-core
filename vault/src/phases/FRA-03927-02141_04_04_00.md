### Evolutionary Timeline of the Air Liquide Facility in Le Havre, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **September 2021:** Air Liquide agreed to take over and operate TotalEnergies' hydrogen production site in Normandy, which has a production capacity of 255 tons per day (t/d). The facility will be connected to Air Liquide's hydrogen network to optimize performance. The site will also explore the addition of carbon capture and storage (CCS) technology, specifically using Air Liquide's Cryocap technology, which can capture up to 90% of CO2 emissions from hydrogen production while increasing hydrogen output by up to 20%.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **September 2021:** Air Liquide acquired a 40% stake in H2V Normandy, which plans to build a 200MW electrolysis unit in Le Havre. This project is intended to be part of a low-carbon hydrogen network in conjunction with the TotalEnergies site and the Port Jerome facility.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **September 2021:** Development studies for adding CCS to the hydrogen production site were announced, with the goal of reducing emissions by 650,000 tons per year by 2030. The timeline for implementation will depend on the outcomes of these studies and the progression of EU carbon prices.
