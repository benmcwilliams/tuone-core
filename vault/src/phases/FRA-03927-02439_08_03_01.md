### Evolutionary Timeline of Siemens Gamesa Facility in Le Havre, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2019**: Siemens Gamesa filed a construction permit application for a facility in Le Havre to assemble offshore wind turbines and components, specifically for the Le Treport, Saint Brieuc, and Noirmoutier wind farms, totaling nearly 1.5GW capacity.
- **December 2019**: Final agreements were signed for the construction of the facility, which will produce blades, nacelles, and generators on a 36-hectare site, with construction expected to start in mid-2020 and commissioning by the end of 2021.
- **May 2020**: Siemens Gamesa secured a contract to supply 62 SG 8.0-167 DD offshore wind turbines for the 496MW Saint-Brieuc project, with components produced at the Le Havre facility.
- **June 2020**: The facility is confirmed to manufacture SWT-7.0-154 turbines for the 500MW Fécamp project, with construction set to begin in summer 2020.
- **February 2021**: Siemens Gamesa announced the start of hiring for the facility, with 210 jobs related to blade and nacelle production.
- **March 2021**: The facility is confirmed to be the first in the world to manufacture all main offshore wind turbine components under one roof, with operations expected to begin in early 2022.
- **March 2022**: Production of nacelles and IntegralBlades commenced at the facility, marking it as the largest industrial renewable energy project in France.
- **February 2024**: The facility produced components for the 496MW Îles d’Yeu and Noirmoutier offshore wind farm, which is expected to be commissioned in 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2019**: Financial support from various local and regional authorities was confirmed to aid the development of the site.
- **February 2021**: A €12 million contract was awarded for construction works related to the Siemens Gamesa facility.
- **March 2021**: A €64.9 million investment was announced for upgrading the quay to handle heavy components for the facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2019**: Application for construction permit filed.
- **December 2019**: Key agreements signed for the facility.
- **Mid-2020**: Construction of the facility began.
- **End of 2021**: Commissioning of the facility was expected.
- **March 2022**: Production of nacelles and blades commenced.
- **February 2024**: First components for the Îles d’Yeu and Noirmoutier project were produced.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2019**: Announcement of 750 jobs to be created at the facility.
- **February 2021**: 210 jobs announced for the facility, focusing on blade and nacelle production.
- **March 2022**: A total of 750 direct and indirect jobs were created, with 500 positions filled by early 2022.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Siemens Gamesa owns the facility exclusively, with no mention of joint ventures or partnerships in the context of ownership.
