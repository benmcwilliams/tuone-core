## FRA-03927-02580

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Saipem Facility in Le Havre, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production**: The facility is involved in the design, construction, and installation of concrete gravity-based foundations for the Fecamp offshore wind farm.
- **Production Capacity**: The foundations contract is valued at €552 million, with Saipem responsible for 40.5% of the contract.
- **Updates**: The foundations will be built at the Bougainville shipyard in the Grand Port Maritime in Le Havre and will be transported to the offshore wind farm.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investments**: The total capital cost for the Fecamp offshore wind farm is estimated at €2 billion, with the majority financed through non-recourse project-level debt.
- **Subsidies**: The project is underpinned by a 20-year power purchase agreement granted by France in June 2018.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Announcement**: On 02-06-2020, it was announced that the consortium, including Saipem, confirmed the contract for the supply and installation of foundations for the Fecamp offshore wind farm.
- **Construction Start**: Construction of the Fecamp offshore wind farm is set to kick off in June 2020.
- **Commissioning**: The project is scheduled for commissioning in 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The construction of the Fecamp project will create over 1,400 local jobs in total, with 100 full-time positions during the 25-year lifetime of the project. The manufacturing of the gravitational foundations will provide work for around 600 people at the Grand Port Maritime site. Additionally, Siemens Gamesa's new plant in Le Havre will create 750 jobs.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The ownership of the Fecamp offshore wind farm is shared among the consortium members: EDF Renewables (35%), Enbridge (35%), and WPD (30%). Saipem is a contractor and does not own the facility exclusively.
