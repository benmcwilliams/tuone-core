## FRA-03927-07612

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Bouygues Facility in Le Havre, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **02/2020**: Bouygues, as part of a consortium with Saipem and Boskalis, confirmed a contract for the supply and installation of foundations for the 500MW Fecamp offshore wind farm. The foundations will be constructed at the Bougainville shipyard in the Grand Port Maritime in Le Havre. The project involves the design, construction, and installation of 71 concrete gravity-based structures for the offshore wind farm.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **02/2020**: The total capital cost for the Fecamp offshore wind farm is estimated at €2 billion, with the majority financed through non-recourse project-level debt. The foundations contract is valued at €552 million, with Bouygues and Saipem each holding a 40.5% share and Boskalis holding 19%.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **02/2020**: The construction of the Fecamp offshore wind farm is set to kick off in February 2020, with commissioning scheduled for 2023. The project is underpinned by a 20-year power purchase agreement granted by France in June 2018.

#### 4. Labour
- **02/2020**: The construction of the Fecamp project is expected to create over 1,400 local jobs in total, with 100 full-time positions during the 25-year lifetime of the project. Additionally, the manufacturing of gravitational foundations at the Grand Port Maritime site will provide work for around 600 people. Siemens Gamesa's new plant in Le Havre is set to create 750 jobs.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **02/2020**: The ownership structure of the Fecamp offshore wind farm involves a consortium where EDF Renewables owns 35%, Enbridge owns 35%, and WPD owns 30%. Bouygues, as part of the consortium, is responsible for the foundations but does not own the facility exclusively.
