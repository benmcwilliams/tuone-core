### Evolutionary Timeline of the Air Liquide Facility in Port-Jérôme, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **01-07-2022**: Air Liquide, in partnership with Siemens Energy, announced the Normand’Hy electrolyser project, which will have an initial capacity of 200 MW. The facility is set to produce renewable hydrogen, with commissioning scheduled for 2025.
- **20-09-2023**: It was confirmed that the Port-Jérôme facility will produce 28,000 tons of renewable hydrogen annually starting in 2026, serving both the industrial and mobility sectors. The project aims to save up to 250,000 tons of CO2 emissions each year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **01-07-2022**: The joint venture between Siemens Energy (74.9% ownership) and Air Liquide (25.1% ownership) was announced, aimed at scaling up electrolyser production in Europe. The companies plan to apply for funding under the EU’s Innovation Fund and other schemes to support hydrogen projects.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **01-07-2022**: Announcement of the Normand’Hy project and the joint venture for electrolyser production.
- **20-09-2023**: Siemens Energy confirmed the supply of 12 electrolyzers with a total capacity of 200 MW to the Normand’Hy project, marking a significant milestone in the project’s development.

#### 4. Labour
- **20-09-2023**: The project is expected to contribute to the decarbonization of the Normandy industrial basin, which may lead to job creation in the renewable hydrogen sector, although specific job numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **01-07-2022**: The facility operates under a joint venture structure, with Siemens Energy holding 74.9% and Air Liquide holding 25.1%. This indicates that Air Liquide does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a collaborative partnership.
