### Evolutionary Timeline of the TRIG Facility in Occitanie, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Hyd’Occ facility will produce renewable hydrogen, with an initial production capacity of up to 3,000 tonnes per year. It aims to deliver zero-carbon mobility solutions for various transport sectors and industry. Once operational, it will be the largest renewable hydrogen production facility in France, generating 40% of the country's hydrogen.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The project was launched with an investment of €60 million (approximately $65.4 million) in February 2023. The facility aligns with the Occitanie region’s €150 million green hydrogen energy transition plan.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **February 2023:** Investment of €60 million announced for the Hyd’Occ facility.
   - **2025:** The facility is expected to be commissioned.

4. **Labour:**

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The Hyd’Occ facility is developed by the Hyd’Occ project company, a subsidiary of Qair France. TRIG is involved as a renewable energy investment company providing electricity through a power purchase agreement (PPA) from its onshore wind farms in the region.
