## FRA-04377-01010

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Lazer, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2019**: EDF announces the development of its first floating solar project at the Lazer hydroelectric reservoir, with a planned capacity of **20 MW**. The project will cover **24 hectares**, utilizing three-quarters of the water surface.
- **February 2021**: Construction begins on the floating photovoltaic power plant, which will double the production capacity of the existing hydroelectric plant. The facility will occupy **24.5 hectares** and is expected to be commissioned in **spring 2022**.
- **June 2023**: The floating solar plant is officially commissioned, featuring **50,000 solar panels** and providing enough electricity to meet the annual needs of approximately **12,500 people**. The facility is designed to adapt to changing water levels without affecting its operation.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2018**: The project is selected in a tender by the French Energy Regulatory Commission (CRE).
- **2021**: A participatory funding campaign raises **€179,000** from local residents to support the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2019**: Announcement of the project.
- **February 2021**: Construction begins, with geotechnical studies and anchor installations planned for spring 2021.
- **Spring 2022**: The facility is expected to be commissioned.
- **June 2023**: The Lazer floating solar power plant is inaugurated and becomes operational.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2021**: Approximately **40 people** are expected to be employed on-site during the **13-month construction period**.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned exclusively by **EDF Renewables**, a subsidiary of the state-controlled utility **EDF**.
