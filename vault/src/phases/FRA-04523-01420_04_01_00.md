## FRA-04523-01420

### Evolutionary Timeline of TotalEnergies Facility in Chateauneuf-les-Martigues, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2018**: TotalEnergies completed an 8 MW solar plant at its biorefinery, Plateforme Total de La Mède, located in Chateauneuf-les-Martigues. This solar facility utilizes 17,000 solar modules from SunPower and covers approximately 50% of the energy needs of the biorefinery, which is set to produce biodiesel primarily from waste oils and partially from vegetable oils, operational by 2019.
  
- **January 2021**: TotalEnergies and Engie announced plans to build France’s largest renewable hydrogen production site at the La Mède biorefinery. The facility will feature a 40 MW electrolyser capable of producing 5 tonnes of green hydrogen per day, with the potential to expand to 15 tonnes per day. This project aims to support the biofuel production process and reduce CO2 emissions by 15,000 tonnes annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2021**: The Masshylia project, which includes the hydrogen production facility, has applied for subsidies from French and European authorities to support its development. The project is part of a broader commitment to renewable energy and sustainability by TotalEnergies and Engie.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2018**: Completion of the 8 MW solar plant.
- **January 2021**: Announcement of the Masshylia project for renewable hydrogen production.
- **2022**: Construction of the hydrogen production facilities is planned to begin, following the completion of an advanced engineering study.
- **2024**: Target date for the start of hydrogen production at the facility.

#### 4. Labour

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- TotalEnergies operates the facility in partnership with Engie for the hydrogen production project, indicating a joint venture structure for this specific initiative. TotalEnergies has a significant stake in the solar sector through its ownership of SunPower (57%) and EREN RE (23%).
