### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Saint-Brieuc, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2020**: Iberdrola awarded a contract to Navantia-Windar for the construction and assembly of 62 jackets and their corresponding piling for the Saint-Brieuc offshore wind farm, which will have a capacity of 496 MW and is expected to supply clean energy to approximately one million people.
- **July 2022**: Iberdrola installed a substation for the offshore wind farm, which will collect energy from the 62 turbines. The substation is expected to be operational in 2023.
- **January 2023**: Completion of the first phase of construction, with 65% of the piles and 40% of the offshore wind platforms installed.
- **July 2023**: The Saint-Brieuc wind farm began supplying power to the national electricity grid, marking a significant milestone in its operational status.
- **December 2023**: All 62 turbines were installed, completing the construction phase of the facility.
- **September 2024**: The facility was inaugurated, confirming its operational status and capacity to generate 2,000 GWh of clean energy annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2020**: Iberdrola announced an investment of €2.4 billion for the Saint-Brieuc offshore wind farm.
- **January 2023**: The total investment for the project was reiterated as nearly €2.5 billion, with plans for further investments in France amounting to €4 billion by 2025.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2020**: Contract awarded for the construction of jackets and piling.
- **July 2022**: Substation installation announced.
- **January 2023**: First phase of construction completed.
- **July 2023**: First power generated and connected to the grid.
- **December 2023**: Completion of turbine installations.
- **September 2024**: Official inauguration of the facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2020**: The contract with Navantia-Windar is expected to create 2,000 direct jobs, with over 1,100 in France.
- **July 2022**: The project has created 1,250 jobs in Avilés and Fene, with expectations to generate at least 2,750 jobs in total.
- **September 2024**: The construction phase supported 1,700 jobs, including 500 local positions.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2020**: The facility is developed by Iberdrola through its wholly-owned subsidiary, Ailes Marines, indicating full ownership of the project.
