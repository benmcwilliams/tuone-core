### Evolutionary Timeline of the Siemens Gamesa Facility in Saint-Brieuc, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2023**: The Saint-Brieuc Offshore Wind Farm, with a total production capacity of 496 MW, will consist of 62 Siemens Gamesa 8 MW wind turbines. This facility is notable for being the first offshore wind project in France to utilize jacket foundations for the installation of wind turbines.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: The project is developed by Ailes Marines, a wholly-owned subsidiary of Iberdrola. Specific investment amounts are not detailed in the article, but the project represents a significant investment in offshore wind technology in France.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2023**: Construction activities resumed, including pile installation operations for the remaining 22 wind turbine positions and the installation of 38 jacket foundations. The installation of the jackets began on July 1, 2023, with the first four units placed shortly thereafter. The first 8 MW wind turbines are expected to be installed in mid-2023, with commissioning occurring progressively from the second half of the year.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2023**: The project is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers are not provided in the article. The involvement of multiple vessels and companies indicates a significant labor force engaged in various aspects of the construction and installation processes.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2023**: The facility is developed by Ailes Marines, which is a wholly-owned subsidiary of Iberdrola. There is no indication of joint ventures or shared ownership in the information provided.
