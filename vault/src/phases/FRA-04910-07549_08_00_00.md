## FRA-04910-07549

### Ailes Marines Facility Evolution Timeline (Saint-Brieuc, France)

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2020**: Ailes Marines selected GE Renewable Energy’s Grid Solutions to provide the main electrical equipment for the offshore substation of the Saint-Brieuc Bay offshore wind farm. The substation will collect electricity from 62 Siemens Gamesa 8 MW wind turbines and increase voltage from 66,000 to 225,000 volts to limit energy losses during transfer to the coast. The facility is designed to have a total capacity of 496 MW.

- **March 2023**: Construction resumed on the Saint-Brieuc offshore wind farm, which will consist of 62 Siemens Gamesa 8 MW wind turbines. The installation of the jackets for the turbines began on July 1, 2022, with the first turbines expected to be installed in mid-2023. The project is the first large-scale offshore wind farm in Brittany to obtain all necessary government permits.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2020**: The partnership with GE’s Grid Solutions for the electrical fitting of the offshore substation is part of the project’s development, contributing to the green economy and job maintenance in France.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2020**: Announcement of GE Renewable Energy’s involvement in the project and the expected commissioning tests for the electrical substation in 2022.
  
- **March 2023**: Resumption of construction activities, including pile installation for the remaining 22 wind turbine positions and the placement of 38 jacket foundations. The inter-array cable installation began in January 2023, with ongoing operations expected to mobilize up to thirty ships.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2020**: The project is expected to maintain skilled jobs in France, as stated by Ailes Marines' President, highlighting the role of the green economy in creating sustainable jobs.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2020**: Ailes Marines is a company 100% owned by Iberdrola, responsible for the construction, installation, and operation of the Saint-Brieuc Bay offshore wind farm.
