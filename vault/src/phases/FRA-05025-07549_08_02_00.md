## FRA-05025-07549

### Evolutionary Timeline of Ailes Marines Facility in Brest, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **07-09-2020**: Ailes Marines, 100% owned by Iberdrola, is responsible for the construction, installation, and operation of the Saint-Brieuc offshore wind farm, which will feature 62 Siemens Gamesa 8 MW wind turbines. The wind farm has a total production capacity of 496 MW. The facility will include an offshore substation designed to collect and transport electricity produced by the wind farm.
- **26-03-2021**: Ailes Marines selected the port of Brest for the assembly of the turbine towers for the Saint-Brieuc offshore wind farm. The assembly will involve the collaboration of French company Foure Lagadec and Spanish company Haizea Wind.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **26-03-2021**: Ailes Marines announced an investment of €11.8 million to fund the construction of a new assembly plant at the port of Brest, which is part of the industrial plan to support the Saint-Brieuc project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **07-09-2020**: Contracts were finalized with first-tier suppliers for the components of the Saint-Brieuc wind farm, with construction work for the offshore substation already underway. The sail away of the topside and jacket is scheduled for spring/summer 2022, and the wind farm is expected to be fully commissioned in 2023.
- **26-03-2021**: Construction of the new assembly plant in Brest is expected to start in early 2022, with commissioning scheduled for later that year. The assembly of the turbine towers will take approximately eight months.

#### 4. Labour
- **07-09-2020**: The project is expected to mobilize 250 jobs over two years for the manufacture of the foundations for the wind farm.
- **26-03-2021**: The construction of the new assembly plant will create at least 35 new jobs, including mechanical assemblers, welders, industrial electricians, and logistics experts. This is in addition to the 250 jobs already created.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **07-09-2020**: Ailes Marines is 100% owned by Iberdrola, indicating full ownership of the facility.
