## FRA-05025-07795

### Foure Lagadec Facility Timeline - Brest, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility is set to assemble sections of 62 turbine towers for the Saint-Brieuc offshore wind farm, which is owned by Ailes Marines (Iberdrola). The assembly is part of an industrial plan that includes a tower manufacturing facility at the port of Le Havre.
   - The assembly of the turbine towers is expected to take approximately eight months.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The industrial program proposed by Foure Lagadec and Haizea Wind includes the creation of new industrial capacity funded with €11.8 million from Ailes Marines.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **March 2021:** Announcement of the selection of the port of Brest as the assembly site for the turbine towers.
   - **Early 2022:** Construction of the new assembly plant is expected to start.
   - **Late 2022:** Commissioning of the assembly plant is scheduled.

4. **Labour:**
   - The project will create at least 35 new jobs during the construction period, including positions for mechanical assemblers, welders, industrial electricians, and logistics experts. Recruitment is expected to start in mid-2022.
   - This is in addition to the 250 jobs already created by Navantia-Windar and Breton subcontractors for the manufacture of the foundations for the Saint-Brieuc project.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is a collaboration between Foure Lagadec and Haizea Wind, indicating a joint venture structure for the assembly operations, rather than exclusive ownership by Foure Lagadec.
