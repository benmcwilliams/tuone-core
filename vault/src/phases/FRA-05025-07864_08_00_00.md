## FRA-05025-07864

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Haizea Wind Facility in Brest, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2021**: Haizea Wind, in partnership with French company Foure Lagadec, is selected to assemble 62 turbine towers for the Saint-Brieuc offshore wind farm at the port of Brest. The assembly is part of an industrial plan designed by Siemens Gamesa, which includes a tower manufacturing facility at the port of Le Havre.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: The industrial program for the Saint-Brieuc project includes the creation of new industrial capacity funded with €11.8 million from Ailes Marines. This investment is aimed at supporting the assembly of the turbine towers in Brest.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2021**: Announcement of the selection of the port of Brest for the assembly of the turbine towers. 
- **Early 2022**: Construction of the new assembly plant is expected to start.
- **End of 2022**: Commissioning of the assembly plant is scheduled.
- **Mid-2022**: Recruitment for at least 35 new jobs is expected to begin, with the assembly of the turbine sections projected to take approximately eight months.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2021**: The project is expected to create at least 35 new jobs during the construction period, including positions for mechanical assemblers, welders, industrial electricians, and logistics experts. This is in addition to the 250 jobs already created by Navantia-Windar and Breton subcontractors for the manufacture of the foundations for the Saint-Brieuc project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2021**: The facility is a joint venture between Haizea Wind and Foure Lagadec, with Siemens Gamesa and Ailes Marines also involved in the project. There is no indication of exclusive ownership by any single company.
