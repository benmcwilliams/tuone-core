## FRA-05025-07946

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Navantia Facility in Brest, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2021**: Navantia's facility in Brest began receiving components for the jacket foundations for the Saint-Brieuc offshore wind project. The facility is involved in the construction of jackets, which are 74 meters high. The facility is part of a larger project that includes the construction of 62 jackets for the 496MW Saint-Brieuc offshore wind project.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2021**: Iberdrola signed a framework agreement with Navantia and Windar for future offshore wind power plants worth €400 million. This agreement secures advance supply on components for the coming years and positions Navantia-Windar for contracts involving the manufacture of 130 XXL monopile foundations, which will be produced at the Navantia shipyard in Fene, Spain. The investment includes the adaptation of facilities for manufacturing monopiles, enhancing the capabilities of the shipyard.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2021**: Completion of the 100th jacket for the offshore wind industry at Navantia's Fene shipyard, with ongoing construction of jackets for the Saint-Brieuc project.
- **July 2021**: Announcement of the framework agreement with Iberdrola, which includes plans for a new XXL monopile factory at the Navantia shipyard in Fene, expected to be operational within 13 months.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2021**: The jackets project is expected to generate nearly 2,000 jobs, primarily in Fene, Aviles, and the Navantia-Windar facility in Brest. The agreement with Iberdrola is anticipated to create several thousand additional jobs in local contractors in both Spain and France.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility in Brest is part of a joint venture between Navantia and Windar, specifically for the production of jacket components for offshore wind projects. The ownership structure indicates that Navantia does not own the facility exclusively but operates it in partnership with Windar.
