## FRA-05025-07950

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Navantia-Windar Facility in Brest, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2020**: Navantia-Windar was awarded a contract for the construction and assembly of 62 jackets and their corresponding piling for the Saint-Brieuc offshore wind farm. This facility will produce components necessary for offshore wind turbines, specifically jackets that support the turbines.
- **October 2020**: Production of components began in Brest, with the facility expected to manufacture over 35,000 tonnes of steel, including more than 1,500 steel elements for the jackets.
- **February 2021**: The facility received its first jacket foundation components manufactured in Brest, contributing to the production of 186 units planned for the project.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2020**: The contract awarded to Navantia-Windar was valued at €350 million, marking it as the largest offshore wind contract for the joint venture to date. Iberdrola is investing €2.4 billion in the Saint-Brieuc offshore wind farm, with a total expected investment of up to €4 billion in the French market over the next four years.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2020**: Contract signed for the Saint-Brieuc offshore wind farm, with a planned operational date in 2023.
- **October 2020**: Production of components commenced in Brest.
- **February 2021**: The facility reported the completion of over 30 units of jacket components, with production ongoing.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2020**: The contract is expected to create 2,000 direct jobs, with over 1,100 jobs in France and nearly 1,000 in Galicia and Asturias. Additionally, the establishment of the facility in Brest has contributed to the creation of more than 250 jobs in the region.
- **February 2021**: The local manufacturing hub in Brest has been instrumental in job creation and training, enhancing local industry capabilities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility operates as a joint venture (UTE) between Navantia and Windar, specifically established for the Saint-Brieuc offshore wind farm project. There is no indication of exclusive ownership by either company, as it is a collaborative effort.
