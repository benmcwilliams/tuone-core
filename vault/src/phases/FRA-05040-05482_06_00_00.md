### Evolutionary Timeline of the Kyotherm Facility at Malteries Franco-Suisses, FRA

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2017**: Kyotherm entered into a binding agreement with Malteries Franco-Suisses for the sale of heat at a price of 26 EUR/MWh plus inflation over 20 years. This agreement marked the first milestone in building the Solar Heat for Industrial Processes (SHIP) system.
- **Expected Solar Yield**: The facility is expected to yield between 8 to 9 GWh of solar energy annually, with a specific solar yield of 561 to 632 kWh/m².
- **Production Capacity**: The annual heat demand from the malt business is 80 GWh, with the SHIP system designed to provide hot air between 50 °C and 85 °C.
- **Commissioning Date**: The commissioning of the SHIP system is scheduled for **May 2020**. Upon commissioning, it will be the largest SHIP system in Europe.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Total CAPEX**: The total capital expenditure (CAPEX) for the project, which includes the solar field, storage tank, and integration into the factory’s hot air system, is EUR 5.8 million.
- **Funding Structure**: The financing structure includes 10% equity at the start of construction, with bank refinancing planned for later.
- **Ademe Grant**: The project received a grant from Ademe, France’s national energy agency, covering 60% of the CAPEX, which corresponds to 20 EUR/MWh over 20 years.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Binding Agreement**: The binding agreement for heat sale was established in **September 2017**.
- **Construction and Commissioning**: The construction of the SHIP system is set to be completed with commissioning scheduled for **May 2020**.
- **Recognition**: Kyotherm won the 2019 SHC Solar Award, highlighting the significance of the project.
