### Evolutionary Timeline of the NewHeat Facility in Malteries Franco-Suisses, FRA

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2017**: Kyotherm entered into a binding agreement with Malteries Franco-Suisses for the sale of heat at a set price of 26 EUR/MWh plus inflation over 20 years. This agreement marked the first milestone in building the Solar Heat for Industrial Processes (SHIP) system.
- **Expected Solar Yield**: The SHIP system is expected to yield 8 to 9 GWh annually, with a specific solar yield of 561 to 632 kWh/m².
- **Production Capacity**: The facility will have a field size of 14,252 m² and a solar storage tank of 3,000 m³, designed to meet an annual heat demand of 80 GWh for the malt business.
- **Commissioning Date**: The commissioning of the installation is scheduled for **May 2020**, at which point it will be the largest SHIP system in Europe.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Total CAPEX**: The total capital expenditure (CAPEX) for the project is EUR 5.8 million, which includes the solar field, storage tank, and integration into the factory’s hot air system.
- **Funding Structure**: The financing structure includes 10% equity at the start of construction, with bank refinancing planned for a later stage.
- **Ademe Grant**: The project received a grant from Ademe, France’s national energy agency, covering 60% of the CAPEX, equivalent to 20 EUR/MWh over 20 years.
