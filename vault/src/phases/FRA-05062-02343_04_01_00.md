### Evolutionary Timeline of the Lhyfe Facility in Sealhyfe, FRA

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2022**: The Sealhyfe facility began operations with a 1 MW electrolyzer supplied by Plug, marking the world's first offshore hydrogen production facility. The facility is designed to produce hydrogen from renewable energy sources, specifically offshore wind.
- **October 2023**: The facility demonstrated its ability to operate an industrial-scale production unit in isolated conditions, confirming the reliability of the electrolysis technology in harsh offshore environments. The project aimed to optimize production processes for future large-scale sites, with plans to scale up to ten times and then 100 times greater capacity.
- **End of 2024**: Lhyfe aims to achieve a production capacity of up to 22 tonnes of green hydrogen per day.
- **2026**: The company plans to ramp up production capacity to 80 tonnes per day.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2024**: The Sealhyfe project results are being incorporated into the HOPE project, which received a €20-million grant from the European Commission and an additional €13-million grant from the Belgian government. This project aims to scale up production to 10 MW and produce up to 4 tonnes of green hydrogen per day by 2026.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2022 - November 2023**: The Sealhyfe facility underwent a 14-month trial period, during which it was tested for system responsiveness, robustness, and optimization in extreme offshore conditions.
- **October 2023**: The facility successfully withstood five significant storms, confirming the durability of its equipment and maintaining production capacity.
