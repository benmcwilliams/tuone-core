### Evolutionary Timeline of the Engie Facility in Durance-Luberon-Verdon, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2019**: Engie and Air Liquide announced the HyGreen Provence project, which aims to produce 10,440 tons of green hydrogen annually by 2027. The facility will generate 1.3 TWh of solar electricity from a total capacity of 900 MW. The project includes the production and storage of green hydrogen on an industrial scale, utilizing solar and other renewable energy sources.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2019**: The project is positioned as a significant investment in renewable energy, although specific financial details regarding investments or subsidies were not disclosed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2019**: The cooperation agreement between Engie and Air Liquide was signed to kickstart the HyGreen Provence project. The first phase of the project is expected to begin in late 2021, with the final development phase slated for completion in 2027. In 2021, 120 MW of solar capacity is planned to be installed, generating 170 GWh of solar electricity annually, with 10% allocated for the electrolysis of 278 tons of hydrogen.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2019**: While specific job creation or loss figures were not mentioned, the project is expected to contribute to local employment through its development and operational phases, particularly in the context of the 25 municipalities involved in the Durance-Luberon-Verdon area.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2019**: The facility is a joint project between Engie and Air Liquide, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by either company.
