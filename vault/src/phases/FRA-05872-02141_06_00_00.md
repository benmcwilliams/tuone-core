### Evolutionary Timeline of the Air Liquide Facility in Durance-Luberon-Verdon, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2019**: Air Liquide, in collaboration with Engie, announced the "HyGreen Provence" project aimed at producing green hydrogen on an industrial scale. The facility is expected to generate 10,440 tons of green hydrogen annually by 2027, powered by a solar capacity of 900 MW, which will produce 1.3 TWh of solar electricity each year. The project will also involve the storage of hydrogen in underground salt caverns.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2019**: The project is positioned as a significant investment in renewable energy, although specific financial figures regarding investments or subsidies were not disclosed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2019**: The project was officially announced, with plans to begin operations in late 2021 and reach full capacity by 2027. The initial phase includes the installation of 120 MW of solar capacity in 2021, which is expected to generate 170 GWh of solar electricity annually, with 10% allocated for the electrolysis of 278 tons of hydrogen.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2019**: While specific job creation figures were not mentioned, the project is expected to contribute to local employment through its development and operational phases, particularly in the context of the 25 municipalities involved in the Durance-Luberon-Verdon area.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2019**: The facility is a joint venture between Air Liquide and Engie, indicating that it is not solely owned by Air Liquide but rather a collaborative effort between the two companies.

This timeline captures the key developments and plans for the Air Liquide facility in Durance-Luberon-Verdon, focusing on its technological ambitions, operational goals, and collaborative nature.
