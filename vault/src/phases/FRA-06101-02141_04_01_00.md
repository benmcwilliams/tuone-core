### Evolutionary Timeline of the Air Liquide Facility in La Mède, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2024**: Air Liquide announced plans to build a new hydrogen production unit at TotalEnergies' La Mède biorefinery. The facility will have a production capacity of **25,000 tons per year** of renewable hydrogen, which will be produced from recycled biogenic by-products instead of fossil hydrocarbons. The hydrogen produced will primarily be used for the production of biofuels and Sustainable Aviation Fuels (SAF). The facility is expected to be operational by **2028**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2024**: The project is part of a **€150 million investment** aimed at reducing the site’s annual CO2 emissions by **130,000 tonnes**. This investment is linked to the establishment of the hydrogen production unit, which aligns with the broader decarbonization goals of the Provence-Alpes-Côte-D’azur region.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2024**: Air Liquide confirmed the construction of the hydrogen production unit at the La Mède site, with an operational target set for **2028**. This marks a significant milestone in the facility's evolution towards becoming a low-carbon hydrogen production center.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Air Liquide will **build, own, and operate** the new hydrogen production unit at TotalEnergies' La Mède site, indicating **full ownership** of the new facility by Air Liquide.
