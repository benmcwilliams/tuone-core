## FRA-06218-07945

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Naval Energies Facility in Brittany, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **01-12-2017:** Naval Energies, formerly known as DCNS Energies, is involved in the production of tidal turbines and floating wind systems. The facility is on track to build a tidal turbines assembly plant in Cherbourg. The company is part of a consortium developing a 24MW floating wind farm in the Groix area in Brittany.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **01-12-2017:** The absence of tenders for commercial tidal energy projects in France has impacted the company, indicating a slower market growth. However, the company continues to pursue its floating wind business and has participated in international projects, such as the US Aqua Ventus project in Maine.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **01-12-2017:** Naval Energies is still on track to build its tidal turbines assembly plant in Cherbourg despite job cuts and market challenges. The company is also involved in developing a floating offshore wind system in partnership with GE.

4. **Labour:**
   - **01-12-2017:** Naval Energies announced a reduction of 100 jobs out of 260, with 30 employees being transferred to its parent company, Naval Group, and 70 employees losing their jobs completely. This job loss is attributed to the lack of commercial tidal energy project tenders in France.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **01-12-2017:** Naval Energies was formed as a joint venture between DCNS and a fund managed by Bpifrance at the beginning of 2017. The company changed its name from DCNS Energies to Naval Energies in June 2017, following the rebranding of DCNS Group to Naval Group. 

This timeline captures the key developments and challenges faced by the Naval Energies facility in Brittany, France, as of December 2017.
