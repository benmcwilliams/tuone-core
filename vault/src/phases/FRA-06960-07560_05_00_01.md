### Evolutionary Timeline of the Andra Facility in Morvilliers, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **2003:** The Cires facility (Centre Industriel de Regroupement, d'Entreposage et de Stockage) was opened in Morvilliers, designed to receive 650,000 cubic metres of very-low-level radioactive waste (VLLW), primarily weakly contaminated rubble, earth, and scrap metal.
   - **2007:** Construction of longer disposal cells (176 metres) began, increasing the capacity of each cell to 25,000 cubic metres.
   - **2010:** Further optimizations allowed for a disposal capacity of 27,000 cubic metres per cell through steepening slopes and deepening cells.
   - **2016:** Additional optimizations raised the height of the above-ground part of the repository, increasing the storage capacity of each cell to about 30,000 cubic metres.
   - **2023:** Andra applied for environmental permission to increase the current authorized storage capacity to over 900,000 cubic metres by utilizing the third tranche, which could handle an additional 250,000-300,000 cubic metres of waste.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **2023:** Andra submitted an environmental authorisation application to the Aube department, which includes an impact study and risk study for the proposed capacity increase. The project aims to optimize storage without changing the repository's footprint.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **April 2023:** Andra submitted the environmental authorisation application on April 7, with a review process expected to take over a year. The goal is to have the first disposal cell of tranche 3 operational before the end of tranche 2, with construction potentially starting in 2024-2025.
