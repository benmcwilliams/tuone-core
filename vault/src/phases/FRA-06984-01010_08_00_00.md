## FRA-06984-01010

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Fécamp, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2020**: Construction begins on the Fécamp offshore wind farm electrical substation, which will transform up to 500 MW of offshore wind energy. The facility will collect and transform electricity produced by 71 wind turbines to transfer it to the shore network.
- **June 2022**: The Fécamp offshore wind farm maintenance base is inaugurated. This facility will serve as a construction base until the end of 2023 and will then become the center for the operation and maintenance of the 500 MW offshore wind farm.
- **July 2023**: The first four wind turbines are loaded out from the port of Cherbourg, marking the installation phase of the 71-turbine Fécamp offshore wind farm, which is scheduled for commissioning in the winter of 2023-2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2020**: The construction of the electrical substation involves a workforce of 400 people and is part of a consortium including EDF Renouvelables, Enbridge, and WPD Offshore. Specific investment amounts are not detailed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2020**: Start of construction for the Fécamp offshore wind farm electrical substation.
- **June 2022**: Inauguration of the Fécamp offshore wind farm maintenance base.
- **July 2023**: Installation of the first wind turbine components begins, with commissioning expected in winter 2023-2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2022**: Approximately 100 jobs are created for the operation and maintenance of the wind farm, including roles for maintenance technicians and operations managers. Recruitment for these positions is ongoing and expected to conclude in 2023.
- **July 2023**: The project has mobilized around 2,000 people at peak times during its three-year development, including over 1,200 at the gravity foundations site and up to 1,000 at the Siemens Gamesa factory for blade and nacelle manufacturing.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2023**: The Fécamp offshore wind farm is developed by Eolien Maritime France (EMF), a joint venture that includes EDF Renouvelables, EIH S.à.rl (owned by Enbridge Inc. and CPP Investments), and Skyborn. This indicates that EDF does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a joint venture.
