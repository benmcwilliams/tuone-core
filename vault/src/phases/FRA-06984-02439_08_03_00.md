### Evolutionary Timeline of the Siemens Gamesa Facility in Fécamp, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2021**: Siemens Gamesa Renewable Energy (SGRE) announced that the 497-MW Fécamp offshore wind power project will utilize 71 SWT-7.0-154 offshore wind turbines. The facility in Le Havre is responsible for producing the nacelles and blades for this project.
- **2022**: Manufacturing of blades and nacelles for the Fécamp project commenced at the Le Havre facility. Tower sections are produced in Spain. The project is located 13-24 kilometers off the coast of Fécamp.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: The Fécamp offshore wind project is part of a broader investment strategy by Siemens Gamesa, which includes multiple offshore wind projects in France. Specific investment amounts for the Fécamp project were not detailed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2021**: SGRE was awarded the contract for the Fécamp offshore wind project, which is part of a series of offshore installations planned off the French coast.
- **June 2023**: The project is reported to be on track for operational status by the end of 2023. The gravity-based foundations and substation are already in place, and inter-array cable installation is underway.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2021**: The establishment of the facility in Le Havre and the Fécamp project is expected to create local jobs and provide value to the community for years to come. However, specific job creation numbers were not provided in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2021**: The Fécamp offshore wind project is being developed by Eolien Maritime France (EMF), a joint venture that includes EDF Renewables, Enbridge Inc., CPP Investments, and Skyborn Renewables. Siemens Gamesa is a key technology provider but does not exclusively own the facility.
