### Evolutionary Timeline of the Enbridge Facility in Fécamp, France

1. **Technology and Operations**:
   - **Date**: September 2020
   - **Details**: The facility will transform up to 500MW of offshore wind energy. It is part of the Fécamp offshore wind farm, which consists of 71 wind turbines. The electrical substation will collect and transform the electricity produced by these turbines to transfer it to the shore network, while ensuring remote control of the farm.

2. **Investment and Development**:
   - **Date**: September 2020
   - **Details**: The construction of the electrical substation is being undertaken by Atlantique Offshore Energy in partnership with GE Grid Solutions and SDI. The project is commissioned by a consortium that includes EDF Renouvelables, Enbridge, and WPD Offshore. Specific investment amounts are not detailed in the article.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**:
   - **Date**: September 2020
   - **Details**: Construction of the Fécamp offshore wind farm electrical substation has officially started. The first steel sheet for the substation was cut at Chantiers de l’Atlantique shipyard in Saint-Nazaire. The delivery of the substation is scheduled for 2022.

4. **Labour**:
   - **Date**: September 2020
   - **Details**: The construction of the substation mobilizes 400 people, indicating job creation associated with this project.

5. **Ownership Structure**:
   - **Date**: September 2020
   - **Details**: The facility is part of a consortium that includes Enbridge, EDF Renouvelables, and WPD Offshore, indicating that Enbridge does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a joint venture.
