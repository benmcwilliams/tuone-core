### Evolutionary Timeline of the Eolien Maritime France Facility in Fécamp, FRA

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: Siemens Gamesa Renewable Energy awarded Global Wind Service (GWS) the contract for complete pre-assembly for the 497 MW Fécamp offshore wind farm. The facility will feature 71 Siemens Gamesa 7 MW wind turbines. The project includes the pre-assembly of tower components and assembly of the HSBM tool on the nacelles. 
- **2022**: Siemens Gamesa began manufacturing blades and nacelles for the Fécamp project at its facility in Le Havre, while tower sections are produced in Spain. The gravity-based foundations and substation are already in place at the construction site, located 13-24 kilometers off the coast of Fécamp.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: The project is being developed by Eolien Maritime France (EMF), a joint venture that includes EDF Renouvelables, EIH S.à.rl (owned by Enbridge Inc. and CPP Investments), and Skyborn Renewables. Specific investment amounts were not detailed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: GWS started mobilizing for the project, with the first vessel load-out expected in May 2023. The wind farm is anticipated to be operational by the end of 2023.
- **June 2023**: Siemens Gamesa selected LD Tide to provide a crew transfer vessel for the operation and maintenance of the wind farm, indicating ongoing preparations for operational readiness.
