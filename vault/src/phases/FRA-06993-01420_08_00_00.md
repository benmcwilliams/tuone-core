## FRA-06993-01420

### Evolutionary Timeline of the TotalEnergies Facility in Gruissan, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2020**: TotalEnergies became a 20% shareholder in the EolMed floating wind farm pilot project, which has a total capacity of 30 MW. The project will consist of three Vestas 10 MW wind turbines installed on Ideol’s Damping Pool floating foundations, located 18 kilometers off the coast of Gruissan at an average depth of 60 meters.
- **March 2021**: Bureau Veritas was awarded a contract for project certification, ensuring reliability and sustainability for the EolMed project.
- **May 2021**: BW Ideol signed contracts worth over EUR 4 million for engineering and licensing related to the EolMed project.
- **September 2021**: Prysmian Group and Asso.subsea secured a contract for the development of an export submarine power cable system for the EolMed project, valued at EUR 30 million.
- **June 2023**: JDR Cable Systems was awarded a contract to supply, test, and terminate the 66 kV dynamic inter-array cables for the EolMed project.
- **April 2024**: Mooreast secured an order to supply proprietary anchors for the EolMed project, marking it as the largest of the first three floating wind energy projects in France.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: The project reached financial close after securing EUR 85 million in financing from the European Investment Bank (EIB).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2021**: The EolMed project was fully consented.
- **April 2023**: Construction of the floating foundations began in Port-La Nouvelle.
- **Mid-2024**: The EolMed project is scheduled to be commissioned.
- **June 2025**: Installation of the floating turbines at the project site is expected to start, with final commissioning anticipated in the third quarter of 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- Specific job creation or job loss details related to the EolMed project have not been explicitly mentioned in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The EolMed project is co-owned by Qair and TotalEnergies, with TotalEnergies holding a 20% stake. The project is developed in collaboration with BW Ideol and Vestas.
