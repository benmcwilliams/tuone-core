### EnBW Facility in Mediterranean Sea, FRA - Evolutionary Timeline

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** June 2022
   - **Details:** EnBW, in partnership with Shell, is participating in France’s AO6 floating offshore wind tender for two projects, each with a capacity of 250MW. The project is named Moulins du Leonis and aims to leverage Shell’s experience from the Groix Belle Ile pilot project and technological advancements from projects in the UK and Scotland.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** June 2022
   - **Details:** The partnership between Shell and EnBW, along with Banque des Territoires, is focused on the development of the Moulins du Leonis project. Specific investment amounts have not been disclosed, but the companies are preparing for a significant financial commitment as they aim to reach Final Investment Decision (FID) by 2027-2028.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** June 2022
   - **Details:** The pre-qualification phase for the Mediterranean tender closed on May 23, 2022, with results expected in the summer of 2022. The tender award is anticipated in autumn 2023, with the expectation to reach FID by 2027-2028.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** June 2022
   - **Details:** No specific job creation or job loss figures have been reported yet, as the project is still in the tender phase.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** June 2022
   - **Details:** The facility is a joint venture between Shell (through its subsidiary Eolfi) and EnBW (through its subsidiary Valeco), indicating that EnBW does not own the facility exclusively.
