### Evolutionary Timeline of the DEME Facility in Port-La Nouvelle, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2024**: The facility is involved in the assembly of wind turbine components for the Les Eoliennes Flottantes du Golfe du Lion (EFGL) floating offshore wind farm. The components include towers, nacelles, and 80-meter-long blades for Vestas V164-10.0 MW wind turbines. The facility is preparing for the assembly of these turbines on floating foundations, with the floating foundations expected to be delivered in April 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2022**: An agreement was signed between the EFGL consortium (comprising Ocean Winds and the Banque des Territoires) and Euroports for port services during the construction of the wind farm at Port-La Nouvelle. This contract includes logistical support for the assembly of wind turbines on floating foundations.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2022**: The export cable connecting the pilot wind farm to the onshore power grid was installed by DEME’s French subsidiary, SDI, under the responsibility of the grid operator Réseau de Transport d’Électricité (RTE).
- **October 2024**: Arrival of wind turbine components at the Port-La Nouvelle wind terminal, with final assembly scheduled for late spring 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2024**: Over 300 people are mobilized at Eiffage Métal’s industrial site in Fos-sur-Mer for the construction of the floating foundations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2023**: DEME became part of the world-wide CS WIND group, indicating a change in ownership structure that may affect the facility's operations and strategic direction.
