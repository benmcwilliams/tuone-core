## FRA-06999-06608

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Principle Power Facility in Port-La Nouvelle, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2024**: The facility is involved in the pilot project for the 30 MW EFGL (Les Eoliennes Flottantes du Golfe du Lion) floating offshore wind farm, which includes three 10 MW floating wind turbines supported by Principle Power’s WindFloat semi-submersible floating foundations. The project is located more than 16 kilometers offshore from Leucate, Aude, and Le Barcarès, Pyrénées-Orientales.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2022**: An agreement was signed between EFGL, a consortium comprising Ocean Winds and the Banque des Territoires, and Euroports for port services during the construction of the wind farm at Port-La Nouvelle. This agreement indicates investment in logistical support for the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2024**: The FWN Arctic vessel delivered the last sets of blades for the wind project’s three V164 wind turbines, which are manufactured by Vestas in Denmark. The blades are currently stored at the Port-La Nouvelle wind terminal.
- **October 2024**: New components for the wind turbines, including towers and nacelles, arrived at the wind terminal in Port-La Nouvelle. The floating foundations are under construction at Eiffage Métal’s industrial site in Fos-sur-Mer, with delivery expected in April 2025. Final assembly of the wind turbines is scheduled for late spring 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2024**: Over 300 people are mobilized at Eiffage Métal’s industrial site for the construction of the floating foundations, indicating job creation associated with the project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is part of the EFGL consortium, which includes Ocean Winds and the Banque des Territoires. Therefore, it is not exclusively owned by Principle Power but is part of a joint venture arrangement.
