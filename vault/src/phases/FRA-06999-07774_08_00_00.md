## FRA-06999-07774

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Euroports Facility in Port-La Nouvelle, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2022**: Euroports is contracted to provide port services during the construction of the Eoliennes Flottantes du Golfe du Lion (EFGL) floating wind farm, which will consist of three 10 MW Vestas V164 wind turbines mounted on WindFloat semi-submersible floating foundations.
- **August 2024**: The last sets of blades for the wind turbines arrive at the Port-La Nouvelle wind terminal, with the blades measuring 80 meters in length and weighing 34 tonnes each.
- **October 2024**: Additional components, including towers and nacelles for the wind turbines, are delivered to the wind terminal in Port-La Nouvelle. The construction of the floating foundations is ongoing at Eiffage Métal’s industrial site in Fos-sur-Mer.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2022**: The EFGL floating wind project secures financing from KfW IPEX-Bank, Crédit Agricole CIB, Banco Santander, Mizuho Bank, European Investment Bank (EIB), and the Danish Export Credit Agency (EKF) for a total capacity of 30 MW.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2022**: The final investment decision for the EFGL project is reached, allowing the developers, Ocean Winds and Banque des Territoires, to sign contracts with industrial and financial partners.
- **End of 2023**: The wind farm is scheduled to be commissioned and will operate for 20 years.
- **March 2023**: Work begins on connecting the wind farm to the national grid.
- **April 2025**: The floating foundations are expected to be delivered for final assembly at the Port-La Nouvelle wind quay.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2024**: Over 300 people are mobilized at Eiffage Métal’s industrial site for the construction of the floating foundations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is part of a consortium involving Ocean Winds and Banque des Territoires, indicating that Euroports does not own the facility exclusively but is involved in a contractual agreement for port services during the construction phase.
