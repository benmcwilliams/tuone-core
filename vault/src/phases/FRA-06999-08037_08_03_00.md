### Evolutionary Timeline of the Réseau de Transport d’Électricité Facility in Port-La Nouvelle, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2024**: Components for the wind turbines destined for the Les Eoliennes Flottantes du Golfe du Lion (EFGL) floating offshore wind farm have arrived at the wind terminal in Port-La Nouvelle. The facility is involved in the assembly of Vestas V164-10.0 MW wind turbines, with a total production capacity of 30 MW for the pilot project, which includes three 10 MW floating wind turbines supported by semi-submersible floating foundations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2022**: An agreement was signed between the EFGL consortium (comprising Ocean Winds and the Banque des Territoires) and Euroports for port services during the construction of the wind farm at Port-La Nouvelle. This indicates investment in logistical support and infrastructure development for the facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2022**: The export cable connecting the pilot wind farm to the onshore power grid was installed by DEME’s French subsidiary SDI, under the responsibility of Réseau de Transport d’Électricité (RTE).
- **August 2024**: The 80-metre-long blades for the wind turbines arrived at the port.
- **October 2024**: The wind turbine components (towers and nacelles) were delivered and unloaded at the new wind terminal in Port-La Nouvelle. The floating foundations are expected to be delivered in April 2025, with final assembly scheduled for late spring 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2024**: Over 300 people are mobilized at Eiffage Métal’s industrial site in Fos-sur-Mer for the construction of the floating foundations, indicating job creation associated with the project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is part of the EFGL consortium, which includes Ocean Winds and the Banque des Territoires. This suggests that the facility is not under exclusive ownership by a single company but is a joint venture.
