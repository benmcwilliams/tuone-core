## FRA-07008-02167

### Evolutionary Timeline of the BayWa Facility in Saubens, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2021**: BayWa r.e. is developing an agrivoltaic project on a 27-hectare site in Saubens, Haute-Garonne. The facility will integrate solar energy production with agricultural activities. The project includes 20 hectares dedicated to solar panels, which will be used by a sheep farmer (7-8 sheep per hectare, totaling 150 sheep) and areas for market gardening and red fruits. The solar panels will be elevated to allow light penetration and will be designed to optimize energy efficiency.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: The land for the agrivoltaic project will be made available free of charge for a period of 20 years. BayWa r.e. is also offering to finance agricultural equipment and provide technical and administrative support, including training for farmers interested in organic farming and permaculture.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2021**: BayWa r.e. has identified two partners (a beekeeper and a sheep farmer) for the project and is seeking additional partners to utilize the remaining land. The project is in the planning and partnership development stage.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2021**: The project aims to create job opportunities for market gardeners and young farmers interested in cultivating red fruits and engaging in organic farming practices.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2021**: The ownership structure is not explicitly detailed in the article, but it is implied that BayWa r.e. is the primary developer of the facility, potentially indicating full ownership or a significant stake in the agrivoltaic project.
