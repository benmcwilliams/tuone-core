### Evolutionary Timeline of the CNR Facility in Sablons Dike, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2021**: The CNR facility at Sablons dike was commissioned with a production capacity of 104 kW. This facility features vertical bifacial photovoltaic (PV) panels mounted 3 meters high, installed over a length of 350 meters along a canal. It is part of CNR's "Photovoltaïque Grand Linéaire" innovation program, which aims to demonstrate the technical and economic viability of large-scale vertical PV installations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2021**: The initial investment for the pilot project at Sablons dike is part of a broader initiative by CNR to explore the potential of vertical PV installations along its 400 km of dikes. Specific investment amounts for the Sablons project were not disclosed, but it is part of a larger strategy to enhance land value while preserving its other uses.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2021**: The first pilot facility at Sablons dike was officially commissioned.
- **2023**: Construction is expected to commence on a second demonstrator project, which will be a 1.5 km long system on the ViaRhôna dike, featuring East-West oriented panels.
- **2025**: A third project, anticipated to be the first full-scale demonstrator of a large linear photovoltaic park, is planned to be developed with a length of 10 to 20 km.
