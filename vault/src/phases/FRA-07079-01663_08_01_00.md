### Evolutionary Timeline of the GE Facility in Cherbourg, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2018**: The LM Wind Power blade factory in Cherbourg officially opened, specializing in the production of 107-meter blades for the Haliade-X 12MW offshore wind turbine.
- **April 2019**: Production began at the Cherbourg facility, with five blades delivered for the Haliade-X prototype test phases.
- **October 2020**: The facility received component certification for the 107-meter blade after successful testing.
- **October 2021**: GE launched a second 107-meter blade production line at the Cherbourg factory, with plans for an additional hall for post-moulding operations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2018**: GE invested over €120 million in the Cherbourg and Saint-Nazaire factories to support the production of the Haliade-X turbines.
- **October 2021**: Further investments were announced for upgrading the Cherbourg plant, including the construction of an additional hall for finishing blades.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2018**: Inauguration of the LM Wind Power blade factory in Cherbourg.
- **June 2019**: The first 107-meter blade was produced at the Cherbourg facility.
- **November 2019**: The factory had produced five blades for the Haliade-X prototype.
- **March 2021**: GE announced plans to recruit 300 new employees to meet growing demand.
- **October 2021**: Launch of the second 107-meter blade production line and plans for site expansion.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2018**: The factory opened with an initial workforce of over 260 employees.
- **July 2019**: GE announced plans to double the workforce to nearly 550 employees.
- **March 2021**: A recruitment drive for 300 new staff was initiated, increasing the workforce to over 450 employees.
- **October 2021**: The facility aimed to recruit an additional 200 employees, targeting a total of 800 employees.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Cherbourg facility is owned exclusively by GE Renewable Energy, as part of its operations under LM Wind Power, which is a subsidiary of GE.
