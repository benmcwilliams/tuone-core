### Evolutionary Timeline of the LM Wind Power Facility in Cherbourg, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2018**: The LM Wind Power facility in Cherbourg, France, officially opened as the first wind turbine blade manufacturing site in France.
- **November 2018**: The facility completed its recruitment plans for 2018, employing over 100 people. The factory was on track to start the prototyping phase in January 2019.
- **January 2019**: Production of five 107-meter blades for the Haliade-X 12MW offshore wind turbine commenced.
- **June 2019**: The first 107-meter blade was produced at the Cherbourg facility, marking it as the largest single-component ever built.
- **August 2020**: The facility produced the first 107-meter blade, which was installed on the Haliade-X prototype in Rotterdam in October 2019.
- **March 2021**: The facility had produced the first offshore wind turbine blade longer than 100 meters and was preparing for increased production capacity.
- **October 2021**: A second 107-meter blade production line was launched at the Cherbourg factory, with plans for further upgrades and an additional hall for post-moulding.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2019**: GE Renewable Energy announced plans to recruit 200 new employees to support the ramp-up of production for the Haliade-X project.
- **March 2021**: GE announced a recruitment drive for 300 new staff to meet growing demand from the offshore wind sector, indicating significant investment in workforce development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2018**: Recruitment for the facility was completed, and the factory was set to begin prototyping in January 2019.
- **January 2019**: The facility began production of blades for the Haliade-X turbine.
- **June 2019**: The first 107-meter blade was rolled out.
- **October 2019**: The first Haliade-X prototype was installed in Rotterdam.
- **August 2020**: The 107-meter blade received component certification from TUV Nord after extensive testing.
- **October 2021**: The second 107-meter blade production line was launched.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2018**: Over 100 employees were hired at the facility.
- **August 2019**: Plans were announced to hire an additional 200 employees, bringing the total to 320.
- **March 2021**: A recruitment drive for 300 new employees was initiated, increasing the workforce to over 450.
- **October 2021**: The facility aimed to reach a total of 800 employees with ongoing recruitment efforts.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The LM Wind Power facility in Cherbourg is owned by GE Renewable Energy, indicating full ownership of the facility by the parent company.
