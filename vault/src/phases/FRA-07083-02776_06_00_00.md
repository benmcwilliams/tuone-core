### Ciel & Terre Facility Evolution Timeline - Laguna Lake, FRA

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **March 2019:** The facility is identified as the Philippines' first and largest floating solar (FPV) testbed, with an initial production capacity of 20.5 kW. The testbed aims to demonstrate the resilience of FPV technology against typhoons using a screw piling method for anchoring. The project utilizes solar modules from Trina Solar, with a mix of aluminium-framed and frameless panels. Future phases will test additional modules from various manufacturers.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **March 2019:** The project is a collaboration between SunAsia Energy and its subsidiary NorteSol Energy, with support from Ciel et Terre, a French floating solar pioneer. An agreement was signed with the Laguna Lake Development Authority (LLDA) to pilot FPV solar technology and assess its feasibility, particularly during the typhoon season.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **March 2019:** The first segment of the floating PV testbed is completed, and the project is under construction. The results from this testbed are expected to be shared at a floating solar conference in June 2019, with plans to scale up to utility-size FPV systems pending favorable outcomes.

4. **Labour:**
   - The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the facility's development.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is owned by NorteSol Energy, a subsidiary of SunAsia Energy. There is no indication of joint ownership or partnerships beyond the collaboration with Ciel et Terre for expertise in installation processes.
