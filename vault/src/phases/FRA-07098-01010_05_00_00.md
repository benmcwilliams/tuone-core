### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Brennilis, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **1972:** The Brennilis facility began operation as a prototype 75 MWe gas-cooled heavy water reactor.
   - **1992:** The first phase of decommissioning was completed, which involved the removal of all fuel and the dewatering of its systems.
   - **2005:** The second phase of decommissioning was completed, which included the dismantling of equipment and all buildings except the reactor building.
   - **September 26, 2023:** EDF obtained the "complete dismantling" decree, allowing for the dismantling of the reactor, cleaning up civil engineering, demolition of the reactor building, and final rehabilitation of the site. The dismantling of the reactor's peripheral circuits is set to begin later in 2023.
   - **Expected Completion:** The facility is projected to be fully dismantled by 2041.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **2024:** The dismantling process is expected to cost €850 million ($950 million). This investment is fully supported by EDF.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**

   - **2005:** Completion of the second decommissioning phase.
   - **September 26, 2023:** EDF received authorization for complete dismantling.
