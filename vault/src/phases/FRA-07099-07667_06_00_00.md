### Evolutionary Timeline of the Céléwatt Facility in Carayac, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2020**: Céléwatt, in collaboration with engineering company Mécojit, began construction of a 250 kW solar park in Carayac. The facility utilizes mounting structures made from untreated raw oak wood sourced from local forests, marking a unique approach in solar park construction. The project aims to promote local employment and utilize regional natural resources.
- **April 2021**: The construction of the solar park was completed, and the facility became operational. The solar park features 746 monocrystalline panels sourced from Talesun in China, mounted on 600 oak structures. The design is innovative, as it is the first of its kind globally to use untreated wood for solar panel support.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2020**: The project was developed without public financial support. The energy produced is sold to the French energy cooperative Enercoop at a rate of €0.08/kWh. The focus on local resources and employment was a key aspect of the investment strategy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2020**: Announcement of the project and commencement of construction.
- **April 2021**: The solar park was energized and began producing electricity, with less than 18 months from project approval to the first kilowatt-hour produced.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2020**: The project aimed to create local employment opportunities by sourcing materials and labor from the surrounding region. The use of local oak wood and collaboration with local companies contributed to job creation.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2020**: Céléwatt is identified as a cooperative, indicating a collaborative ownership structure. The facility is not mentioned as a joint venture, suggesting that Céléwatt holds exclusive ownership of the facility, although it collaborates with Mécojit for engineering expertise.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones of the Céléwatt facility in Carayac, France, highlighting its innovative approach to solar energy production and commitment to local resources and employment.
