### Evolutionary Timeline of the Enercoop Facility in Carayac, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2021**: The Enercoop facility, developed by French company Céléwatt, launched a 250 kW ground-mounted solar park in Carayac. The solar panels are mounted on structures made of raw oak wood sourced from local forests, marking a first in France and globally for this type of mounting structure. The project utilized 746 monocrystalline panels delivered from China by Talesun.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2021**: The project was supported by Enercoop, which will purchase the generated power at a price of €0.08/kWh. The investment details specific to the solar park's construction and the financial backing from Enercoop were not disclosed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: Céléwatt completed the construction of the solar park and began producing electricity. The project took less than 18 months from the filing of the project approval request to the first kilowatt-hour produced, demonstrating the feasibility of community and regional renewable energy initiatives.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2021**: The project aimed to promote local employment by utilizing local resources for the construction of the solar park, although specific job creation numbers were not mentioned in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2021**: The facility is associated with Céléwatt, which appears to have full ownership of the solar park, with Enercoop acting as a power purchaser rather than a co-owner or joint venture partner.
