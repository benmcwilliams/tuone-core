### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mécojit Facility in Carayac, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2021**: The Mécojit facility, developed by French engineering company Mécojit, produced a 250 kW ground-mounted solar park in Carayac. This facility utilizes a unique mounting structure made from raw oak wood sourced from local forests, marking a first in France and globally. The solar park consists of 746 monocrystalline panels delivered from Talesun, China.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2021**: The project was supported by French energy cooperative Enercoop, which will purchase power from the facility at a rate of €0.08/kWh. The project emphasizes local employment and the use of regional natural resources.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: The construction of the solar park was completed, and it was energized, with the first kilowatt-hour produced shortly after the final checks by Mécojit and connection by Enedis, the French grid operator. The timeline from project approval request to production was less than 18 months.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2021**: The project aimed to promote local employment through the use of local resources, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2021**: The facility is operated by Céléwatt, a French cooperative, with the engineering concept developed by Mécojit. The ownership structure details regarding exclusive ownership or joint ventures were not explicitly mentioned in the articles.
