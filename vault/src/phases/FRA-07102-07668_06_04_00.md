### Evolutionary Timeline of the DAS Solar Facility in Mandeure, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2024**: DAS Solar announced plans to build a 3 GW solar panel factory in Mandeure, France. The facility will feature three production lines within a 51,000 sqm area, repurposing a former automotive subcontractor site. The company aims to expand into the full photovoltaic (PV) supply chain, including solar cell manufacturing and subcontracting for cables, connectors, and wafers.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2024**: DAS Solar will invest €109 million (approximately $115.3 million) in the establishment of the Mandeure facility. This investment is part of the company's strategy to meet the strong demand for locally manufactured solar products in the European Union.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2024**: The project was officially announced by Vice President Shi Si during a press briefing organized by the Pays de Montbéliard Agglomération (PMA). DAS Solar set up DAS Solar France in September 2024 to facilitate the project, with production expected to start by 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2024**: While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned in the articles, the establishment of the facility is expected to contribute to local employment opportunities, as it will be DAS Solar's first factory in Europe, adding to its existing workforce of 8,900 employees across 14 sites in China.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2024**: The articles do not specify whether DAS Solar will have exclusive ownership of the facility or if it will involve joint ventures or partnerships. However, the establishment of DAS Solar France suggests a direct investment and operational control by DAS Solar in the Mandeure facility.
