### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ocean Winds Facility in Leucate, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2022**: Ocean Winds, a joint venture between EDP Renewables and Engie, announced the final investment decision for the **Eoliennes Flottantes du Golfe du Lion (EFGL)** project, which will consist of three **10 MW** floating wind turbines. The project is designed to cover the annual electricity needs of over **50,000** coastal residents and is characterized by advanced floating wind technology.
- **March 2023**: The laying of the export cable began to connect the EFGL wind farm to the French national grid. This connection will be both underwater and underground at **63,000 volts** over nearly **20 kilometers**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2022**: Major financial backing was secured for the EFGL project from KfW IPEX-Bank, Crédit Agricole CIB, Banco Santander, Mizuho Bank, the European Investment Bank (EIB), and the Danish Export Credit Agency (EKF). This financing is significant as it marks one of the world’s first commercially financed floating offshore wind farms.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2022**: Final investment decision reached, allowing the project to enter the implementation phase.
- **June 2022**: Financing agreements were finalized, enabling the project to move forward.
- **October 2022**: Maersk Supply Service was contracted to transport and install the three 10 MW wind turbines, with offshore operations scheduled for Q4 2023.
- **March 2023**: The laying of the export cable commenced, marking a significant step in connecting the wind farm to the national grid.
- **End of 2023**: The wind farm is scheduled to be commissioned.

#### 4. Labour
- Specific job creation or job loss details related to the EFGL project have not been explicitly mentioned in the articles. However, the involvement of various contractors and financial institutions suggests potential job creation in construction and operational phases.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The EFGL project is a **joint venture** between **Ocean Winds** and **La Banque des Territoires**, indicating that Ocean Winds does not own the facility exclusively but shares ownership with its partner.
