## FRA-07108-07671

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Dalkia Facility in Évry-Courcouronnes, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **May 2023:** The geothermal heating network was inaugurated, connected to two geothermal wells drilled to a depth of approximately 1600 meters. The facility extracts water from the Dogger aquifer, with a temperature of around 70 degrees Celsius. The geothermal heating plant increased the share of renewable and recovered energy in the urban heating network from 55% to 77%. The project aims to reduce CO2 emissions by up to 11,000 tonnes annually.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The project was co-developed by Dalkia, the Grand Paris Sud agglomeration, and the city of Évry-Courcouronnes. It received funding support from the State, Ademe (the Ecological Transition Agency), and the Île-de-France region.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **2021:** Drilling of the geothermal wells was completed after three months of continuous operations.
   - **May 2023:** The geothermal heating network was officially inaugurated.

4. **Labour:**
   - The articles do not specify exact job creation or job loss figures related to the geothermal heating network project.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - Dalkia is involved in a co-development partnership for the facility, indicating a joint venture structure with the Grand Paris Sud agglomeration and the city of Évry-Courcouronnes, rather than exclusive ownership.
