## FRA-07130-01010

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Al Henakiyah 2, FRA

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: The Al Henakiyah 2 facility is designed to produce 400 MW (AC) of solar energy.
- **Updates**: As of October 2024, the facility is part of a competitive tender process for solar projects in Saudi Arabia.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Bids Submitted**: In October 2024, the EDF Renouvelables consortium, in partnership with SPIC Huanghe Hydropower Development Co., Ltd. and Korea Electric Power Corporation (KEPCO), submitted a bid of $0.0140/kWh for the Al Henakiyah 2 project. Another consortium, including Masdar and Saudi energy company Nesma Renewable Energy, offered a higher bid of $0.0151/kWh.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Tender Announcement**: On 23-10-2024, the Saudi Power Procurement Co. (SPPC) announced the shortlisted bidders for the fifth round of the National Renewable Energy Program (NREP), which includes the Al Henakiyah 2 project.
- **Bidding Phase**: The project is currently in the bidding phase, with various consortia competing for the contract.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: Specific job creation figures related to the Al Henakiyah 2 facility have not been disclosed in the articles. However, the development of such facilities typically leads to job creation during both the construction and operational phases.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The facility is part of a consortium bid involving EDF Renouvelables, SPIC Huanghe Hydropower Development Co., and KEPCO. The exact ownership structure post-bid acceptance has not been detailed in the articles, but it indicates a joint venture approach rather than exclusive ownership by EDF.

### Summary
The EDF facility in Al Henakiyah 2, FRA, is a 400 MW solar project currently in the bidding phase as part of Saudi Arabia's renewable energy tender program. The facility is being developed through a consortium that includes EDF Renouvelables, indicating a joint venture ownership structure. The project is expected to contribute to job creation, although specific figures have not been provided.
