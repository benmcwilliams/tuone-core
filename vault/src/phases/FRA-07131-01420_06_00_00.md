### Evolutionary Timeline of the TotalEnergies Facility in Al Masa’a, FRA

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Project Capacity**: The Al Masa’a facility is designed to produce 1,000 MW (AC) of solar energy.
- **Production Type**: The facility is part of the Saudi Arabian government's National Renewable Energy Program (NREP) and focuses on photovoltaic (PV) solar energy generation.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Bidding Details**: In the fifth round of the Saudi Arabian renewable energy tender program, TotalEnergies, in partnership with Al Jomaih Energy and Water Company, submitted a bid of $0.0136/kWh for the Al Masa’a project. This bid was part of a competitive process involving multiple consortia.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Announcement Date**: On 23-10-2024, the Saudi Power Procurement Co. (SPPC) announced the shortlisted bidders for the Al Masa’a project, confirming its status as a key project within the NREP.
- **Bidding Consortiums**: The shortlisted bidders for the Al Masa’a project included:
  - SPIC Huanghe Hydropower Development-EDF Renouvelables consortium (bid: $0.0131/kWh)
  - Al Jomaih Energy and Water Company in partnership with TotalEnergies (bid: $0.0136/kWh)

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: While specific job creation figures were not mentioned in the articles, the development of a large-scale solar facility like Al Masa’a typically involves significant employment opportunities during both the construction and operational phases.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture**: TotalEnergies does not own the Al Masa’a facility exclusively. It is part of a joint venture with Al Jomaih Energy and Water Company, indicating a collaborative investment and operational structure for the project.
