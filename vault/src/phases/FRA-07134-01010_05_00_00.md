### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Blayais, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The EDF facility in Blayais operates 900 MWe pressurized water reactors, which are part of the existing fleet of 32 reactors that came into commercial operation between 1977 and 1988. The facility is involved in the Grand Carénage life extension programme aimed at upgrading its plants to improve performance and extend operational life beyond 40 years.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - EDF has planned to invest approximately EUR 55 billion by 2025 under the Grand Carénage programme, which was revised to EUR 45 billion in 2018 and further adjusted to EUR 49.4 billion in 2020. A green bilateral loan of EUR 1 billion was secured in November 2022 with Credit Agricole CIB to support these investments.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - The Grand Carénage life extension programme was announced in 2011. In February 2021, the Autorité de Sûreté Nucléaire (ASN) set conditions for the continued operation of EDF's 900 MWe reactors beyond 40 years. The first reactor licensed to operate beyond 40 years was unit 1 of the Tricastin nuclear power plant in August 2023. The periodic safety reviews for the reactors at the Blayais facility are scheduled to run until 2031.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
