## FRA-07143-01010

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Civaux 2, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Civaux 2 facility is part of EDF's nuclear power generation fleet. It has been identified that the facility, along with Civaux 1, has experienced issues related to stress corrosion in the safety injection system's pipe welds. As of April 2023, EDF is implementing a revised inspection program to address these issues, with a focus on checking 90% of the identified priority welds by the end of 2023 and completing all checks by the first quarter of 2024.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - No specific financial investments or subsidies related to expansions or capacity increases have been mentioned in the articles. However, the ongoing inspection and repair strategy indicates a commitment to maintaining and ensuring the operational integrity of the facility.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **December 2021:** Maintenance checks on Civaux 1 revealed corrosion issues, prompting checks on Civaux 2.
   - **Mid-January 2022:** Similar faults were discovered at Civaux 2, leading to the decision to replace affected parts and extend the shutdown of the plant.
   - **December 2022:** EDF submitted an inspection and repair strategy to the French nuclear safety regulator (ASN) for the years 2023-2025.
   - **March 2023:** ASN acknowledged EDF's amended strategy for inspections, which includes tighter checks on repaired welds.
   - **April 2023:** ASN approved EDF's revised inspection schedule, which aims to check priority welds by the end of 2023.

### Technology and Operations
- "In December 2021, maintenance checks on the primary circuit of Civaux 1 revealed corrosion near the welds on pipes of the safety injection system."
- "Checks were then carried out on the same equipment at unit 2, revealing similar defects."
- "EDF decided to replace the affected parts, requiring an extended shutdown of the plant."
- "In mid-January 2022, EDF announced that similar faults on the safety injection system pipe welds to those discovered at Civaux 1 had been found at Civaux 2."

### Investment and Development
- "In December 2022, EDF submitted to ASN an inspection and repair strategy for all of its reactors with regard to the risk of stress corrosion cracking, for the years 2023-2025."
- "EDF amended this strategy in early March to take into account the discovery of such cracks on welds that had been repaired when the reactors were built."

### Project Status and Milestones
- "EDF's revised schedule provides in particular for an acceleration of the inspections of the welds of the RIS and RRA systems which were the subject of repairs at the time of the construction of the reactors."
- "EDF said 90% of the repaired welds it identified as a priority due to their repair conditions will be checked before the end of 2023, and all of these welds will be checked by the first quarter of 2024."
