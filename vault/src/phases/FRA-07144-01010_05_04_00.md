### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Cordemais, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **January 2022:** EDF initiated a project to convert the Cordemais thermal power plant from coal to biomass, specifically wood pellets, to produce power.
   - **September 2024:** EDF announced the scrapping of the biomass conversion project due to it not meeting “technical and economic conditions.” The facility will cease electricity production by 2027. Instead, EDF's subsidiary Framatome plans to construct a prefabrication plant on the site to manufacture secondary piping systems for EPR2 nuclear reactors.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **September 2024:** The feasibility studies for the new prefabrication plant are underway, although specific investment amounts related to this new project have not been disclosed.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **January 2022:** Start of the biomass conversion project.
   - **September 2024:** Announcement of the cancellation of the biomass project and the plan to construct a prefabrication plant for EPR2 reactors. The facility is set to stop electricity production in 2027.

4. **Labour:**
   - **September 2024:** The new prefabrication plant is expected to create approximately 100 jobs at the outset, increasing to 200 at peak operation.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is owned by EDF, with its subsidiary Framatome involved in the new prefabrication plant project. There is no indication of joint ownership or partnerships mentioned in the articles.
