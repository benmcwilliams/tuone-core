### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Cruas-Meysse, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2024**: Unit 2 of the Cruas-Meysse nuclear power plant was restarted using its first full core of recycled uranium fuel (RepU). This marked a significant step in reviving France's domestic uranium reprocessing industry. The facility is certified to use Enriched Reprocessed Uranium (ERU), with a goal to reuse RepU in 1.3 GW reactors by 2027 and achieve over 30% RepU usage in the French nuclear fleet by the 2030s.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2011**: EDF announced the Grand Carénage life extension programme, planning to invest around EUR 55 billion by 2025 to upgrade its plants, including the Cruas-Meysse facility, to improve performance and extend operational life beyond 40 years.
- **2020**: The investment cost for the Grand Carénage programme was adjusted to EUR 49.4 billion.
- **November 2022**: EDF secured a green bilateral loan worth EUR 1 billion from Credit Agricole CIB to support the life extension of its reactors, aligning with European green investment criteria.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 5, 2024**: Cruas 2 was restarted with its first entirely recycled uranium fuel load, marking a historic milestone in the facility's operations.
- **2018**: The uranium reprocessing sector was resumed after being suspended in 2013, with EDF's efforts to recycle used nuclear fuel assemblies gaining momentum.
