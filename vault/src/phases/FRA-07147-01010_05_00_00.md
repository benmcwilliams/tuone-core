### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Dampierre, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Dampierre facility operates 900 MWe pressurized water reactors, which are part of EDF's fleet of 32 reactors that began commercial operation between 1977 and 1988. The facility is included in the Grand Carénage life extension programme aimed at upgrading plants to improve performance and extend operational life beyond 40 years.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - EDF's Grand Carénage programme was initially planned with an investment of around EUR55 billion by 2025, later revised to EUR45 billion in 2018, and adjusted to EUR49.4 billion in 2020. A green bilateral loan of EUR1 billion was secured in November 2022 with Credit Agricole CIB to support these investments.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - The Autorité de Sûreté Nucléaire (ASN) set conditions in February 2021 for the continued operation of EDF's 900 MWe reactors beyond 40 years, allowing for an additional 10 years of operation. The fourth periodic safety reviews for each reactor, including Dampierre, are scheduled to run until 2031, with public inquiries planned for the measures.
