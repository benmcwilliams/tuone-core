## FRA-07150-01010

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Fecamp, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **02/2020**: The Fecamp offshore wind farm, with a production capacity of 500 MW, is announced. The project will utilize 71 Siemens Gamesa 7.0-154 turbines, to be installed between 13km and 22km off the coast. The project is scheduled for commissioning in 2023.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **02/2020**: The total capital cost of the Fecamp project is estimated at €2 billion, primarily financed through non-recourse project-level debt. The project is supported by a 20-year power purchase agreement granted by France in June 2018.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **02/2020**: The consortium of EDF Renewables, Enbridge, and WPD reaches financial close, marking the start of construction for the Fecamp offshore wind farm. Construction is set to begin immediately following the announcement.

#### 4. Labour
- **02/2020**: The construction of the Fecamp project is expected to create over 1,400 local jobs, with 100 full-time positions anticipated during the 25-year operational lifetime of the project. Additionally, Siemens Gamesa's new plant in Le Havre, which will manufacture turbine components, is expected to create 750 jobs, while the manufacturing of gravitational foundations will provide work for around 600 people.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **02/2020**: The ownership structure of the Fecamp project is as follows: EDF Renewables owns 35%, Enbridge owns 35%, and WPD owns 30%.
