## FRA-07150-06329

### Evolutionary Timeline of the WPD Facility in Fecamp, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date**: 02-06-2020
  - The Fecamp offshore wind farm is a 500MW facility located off the northwest coast of France. It will utilize 71 Siemens Gamesa 7.0-154 turbines, which will be installed between 13km and 22km offshore. The project is scheduled for commissioning in 2023.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date**: 02-06-2020
  - The total capital cost of the Fecamp project is estimated at €2 billion, primarily financed through non-recourse project-level debt. The project is supported by a 20-year power purchase agreement granted by France in June 2018.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date**: 02-06-2020
  - The consortium of EDF Renewables, Enbridge, and WPD reached financial close on the project, with construction set to begin in June 2020. The project is expected to be commissioned in 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date**: 02-06-2020
  - The construction of the Fecamp project is expected to create over 1,400 local jobs, with 100 full-time positions established during the 25-year operational lifetime of the project. Additionally, Siemens Gamesa's new plant in Le Havre will create 750 jobs, and the manufacturing of gravitational foundations will provide work for around 600 people.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date**: 02-06-2020
  - The ownership structure of the Fecamp offshore wind farm is as follows: EDF Renewables owns 35%, Enbridge owns 35%, and WPD owns 30%. This indicates a joint venture rather than exclusive ownership by any single company.
