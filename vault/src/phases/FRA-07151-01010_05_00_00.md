### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Flamanville, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2007**: Construction of the Flamanville 3 European Pressurised Reactor (EPR) began. The reactor is designed to produce approximately 1,600 MW (1.6 GW) of electricity.
- **2012**: The reactor was originally scheduled to enter service.
- **2013**: The dome of the reactor building was completed in July, and the reactor vessel was installed in January.
- **2024 (March)**: EDF announced plans to begin fuel loading in March 2024, with a connection to the grid anticipated in mid-2024.
- **2024 (September)**: The reactor achieved first criticality and began the divergence process, marking the initiation of a stable nuclear reaction at low power.
- **2024 (December)**: The Flamanville EPR was connected to the national grid for the first time, marking it as France's largest reactor.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2004**: The project was initially estimated to cost €3 billion.
- **2022**: By the end of 2022, costs had escalated to an estimated €13.2 billion due to repeated delays and technical issues.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2024**: The French Nuclear Safety Authority (ASN) confirmed a further delay in the reactor's commissioning, which had been pushed back from its original schedule.
- **April 2024**: ASN completed a consultation process for authorizing the commissioning of the reactor.
- **September 2024**: The reactor was loaded with fuel and began the divergence process.
- **December 2024**: The Flamanville EPR was officially connected to the grid, marking a significant milestone after 17 years of construction.
