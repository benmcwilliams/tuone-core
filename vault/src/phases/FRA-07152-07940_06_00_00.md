### Evolutionary Timeline of the Nanosolar Facility in Gabardan, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2011**: Nanosolar supplied approximately 6MW of CIGS (Copper Indium Gallium Selenide) photovoltaic panels for two installations, with 3MW installed in Gabardan, France. This marked the first utility-scale deployment of Nanosolar panels in France. The panels included various efficiencies: 1MW at ~8% efficiency (160W), 1MW at ~8.5% efficiency (170W), and 1MW at ~10% efficiency (200W).

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2011**: The project in Gabardan was part of a partnership with EDF Energies Nouvelles and its US subsidiary, enXco. The construction was executed by ColSun, a joint venture between EDF EN and Belectric, indicating a collaborative investment in the facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2011**: The 3MW installation in Gabardan was commissioned, marking a significant milestone for Nanosolar in the French market.
- **November 2011**: The Oregon project, which was part of the same supply agreement, was also commissioned, showcasing Nanosolar's expanding footprint in the US and France.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Nanosolar does not own the Gabardan facility exclusively; it operates in partnership with EDF Energies Nouvelles and its joint venture with Belectric, indicating a collaborative ownership structure for the project.
