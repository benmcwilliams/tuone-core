### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Gravelines, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Gravelines facility operates 900 MWe pressurized water reactors, which are part of EDF's fleet of 32 reactors that began commercial operation between 1977 and 1988. The facility is involved in the production of nuclear energy and is undergoing upgrades to improve performance and extend operational life beyond 40 years.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - EDF's Grand Carénage life extension programme was initiated in 2011, with an initial investment plan of approximately EUR55 billion by 2025. This investment was revised to EUR45 billion in 2018 and further adjusted to EUR49.4 billion in 2020. A green bilateral loan of EUR1 billion was secured in November 2022 to support these investments.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - In February 2021, the French nuclear safety regulator, ASN, set conditions for the continued operation of EDF's 900 MWe reactors beyond 40 years, allowing for an additional 10 years of operation. The periodic safety reviews for these reactors are scheduled to run until 2031, with specific measures being applied to each reactor, including those at Gravelines.
