### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Penly, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Production**: The Penly facility currently operates two 1300 MWe class pressurized water reactors, Unit 1 (operational since 1990) and Unit 2 (operational since 1992).
- **Future Expansion**: EDF plans to construct two new EPR2 reactors at the Penly site, with preparatory work expected to begin in mid-2024. The construction of these reactors is projected to take approximately seven years, with commissioning anticipated in 2035.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Plans**: The construction of the new EPR2 reactors is part of a broader initiative by EDF to reinvigorate the nuclear sector in France. The French government has also indicated plans to support the nuclear industry, including a buyout of EDF to facilitate the construction of up to 14 new reactors by 2050.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Permitting Process Initiation**: As of July 2023, EDF has begun the permitting process for the new EPR2 reactors at Penly, following a national public debate that occurred from October 2022 to February 2023.
- **OSART Mission**: An Operational Safety Review Team (OSART) from the IAEA conducted a review of the Penly plant from September 4 to September 21, 2023, assessing operational safety and providing recommendations for improvement.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The construction of the new EPR2 reactors is expected to create approximately 30,000 jobs per year during the construction phase and over 10,000 jobs during the operational phase.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: EDF is a state-owned power company, and it operates the Penly facility. The facility is not mentioned as being part of a joint venture, indicating that EDF has full ownership of the site.
