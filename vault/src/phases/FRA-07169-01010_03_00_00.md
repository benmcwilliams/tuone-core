## FRA-07169-01010

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Romanche-Gavet, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2020**: The Romanche-Gavet hydroelectric plant was commissioned on October 9, 2020, after ten years of construction. It has a production capacity of 97 megawatts (MW) and is designed to increase power output by 40% along the La Romanche river. The facility replaces six old plants and five old dams, contributing to the renewable energy supply equivalent to the annual consumption of 230,000 inhabitants in the cities of Grenoble and Chambéry.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2020**: EDF Group committed €400 million to the construction of the Romanche-Gavet facility. Notably, 94% of the contracts for the project were awarded to French companies, with 28% located in the Auvergne Rhône-Alpes region. Additionally, 75% of the funding for the structure came from green bonds, supporting the energy transition.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2020**: The facility was officially inaugurated and commissioned on October 9, 2020. This marked the completion of a significant hydropower project in France, emphasizing EDF's commitment to renewable energy.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2020**: During peak construction periods, 306 workers were employed on the project. Furthermore, 74 individuals were provided with job opportunities as a result of the project, contributing to local employment.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Romanche-Gavet facility is owned exclusively by EDF Group, which operates and maintains the plant with a dedicated team of approximately 20 technicians and engineers from EDF Hydro Alpes.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones associated with the EDF facility in Romanche-Gavet, France, highlighting its significance in the renewable energy landscape.
