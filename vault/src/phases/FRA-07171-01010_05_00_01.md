### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Saint-Laurent, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The EDF facility in Saint-Laurent operates pressurized water reactors, specifically the 900 MWe design. These reactors are part of EDF's broader fleet, which includes 32 operating 900 MWe reactors that were commissioned between 1977 and 1988. The facility is involved in the Grand Carénage life extension programme aimed at upgrading its plants to improve performance and extend operational life beyond 40 years.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - EDF has committed to a significant investment under the Grand Carénage programme, initially estimated at EUR55 billion by 2025, later revised to EUR45 billion in 2018, and adjusted to EUR49.4 billion in 2020. A green bilateral loan of EUR1 billion was secured in November 2022 to support these investments, which are aligned with the European taxonomy for green investments.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - The Autorité de Sûreté Nucléaire (ASN) set conditions in February 2021 for the continued operation of EDF's 900 MWe reactors beyond 40 years, allowing for an additional 10 years of operation. In August 2023, unit 1 of the Tricastin nuclear power plant became the first French reactor licensed to operate beyond 40 years, indicating progress in the life extension efforts.
