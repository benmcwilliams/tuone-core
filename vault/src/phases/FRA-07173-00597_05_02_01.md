### Evolutionary Timeline of the Orano Facility in Tricastin, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2010**: The Georges Besse II (GB-II) uranium enrichment plant was officially opened.
- **April 2011**: GB-II began commercial operation.
- **2016**: GB-II reached its full production capacity of 7.5 million separative work units (SWU).
- **March 2023**: The DN30-X package was licensed by the US Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC) for transporting enriched uranium.
- **July 2023**: Orano announced the development of the 30B-X cylinder for transporting uranium enriched up to 20%, with two versions for different enrichment levels.
- **October 2023**: Orano announced plans to invest €1.7 billion to extend the GB-II plant by adding four new modules, increasing capacity by more than 30%, with production expected to start in 2028.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2023**: Orano's board approved a €1.7 billion investment to extend the GB-II plant, responding to customer demands for increased uranium enrichment capacity.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2023**: Orano confirmed the decision to extend the GB-II plant, with production ramp-up expected to start in 2028 and reach nominal production by 2030.
- **March 2023**: Licensing for the DN30-X package was completed in the US, with French licensing expected in the second half of 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2023**: The extension of the GB-II plant is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers were not disclosed. The project is anticipated to support the supply of low-carbon energy to the equivalent of 120 million households annually.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Orano operates the GB-II facility under its ownership structure, with support from Japanese and Korean shareholders in the Tricastin enrichment company SETH (Société d'Enrichissement du Tricastin Holding) for the expansion project.
