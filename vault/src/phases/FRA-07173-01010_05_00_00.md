### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Tricastin, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2023**: Unit 1 of the Tricastin nuclear power plant was cleared to operate for an additional ten years, becoming the first French power reactor licensed to operate beyond 40 years. The reactor, which has a production capacity of 900 MWe, was originally connected to the grid on 31 May 1980 and entered commercial operation on 1 December 1980. The safety improvements planned by EDF will be applied during the fourth periodic safety reviews, scheduled to run until 2031.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2024**: EDF announced a green financing initiative to support the extended operation of its reactors, including those at Tricastin. The Grand Carénage life extension programme, initiated in 2011, aims to invest approximately EUR 45 billion by 2025 to upgrade plants for continued operation beyond 40 years. This investment includes safety upgrades in response to the Fukushima Daiichi accident.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2021**: The Autorité de Sûreté Nucléaire (ASN) set conditions for the continued operation of EDF's 900 MWe reactors beyond 40 years, which includes Tricastin 1.
- **June 29, 2023**: ASN adopted a decision setting the requirements for the continued operation of Tricastin 1.
- **August 10, 2023**: ASN confirmed that the safety improvements planned by EDF for Tricastin 1 would allow for its continued operation for an additional ten years.
