### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDPR Facility in Dieppe - Le Tréport, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **March 2022:** The Dieppe – Le Tréport offshore wind farm is being developed by the Éoliennes en Mer Dieppe Le Tréport consortium, which is a joint venture between Engie and EDP Renewables (EDPR). The facility will consist of 62 Siemens Gamesa 8 MW wind turbines, with a total production capacity of 496 MW. Once operational, it is expected to generate an average of 2,000 GWh of electricity annually, sufficient to power approximately 850,000 people.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **March 2022:** The project includes a contract valued at around EUR 100 million awarded to Nexans for the supply and installation of the export cable necessary for connecting the wind farm to the onshore power grid. This contract is part of the broader investment in the infrastructure required for the wind farm.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **March 2022:** The installation of subsea cables is scheduled to begin in the second half of 2024. The commissioning of the wind farm is slated for July 2024.
