## FRA-07189-01626

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Engie Facility in Îles d’Yeu and Noirmoutier, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Project**: Îles d’Yeu and Noirmoutier offshore wind farm
- **Production Capacity**: 496 MW
- **Output**: Expected to generate enough clean electricity for nearly 800,000 people annually.
- **Location**: 11 km off the Island of Yeu and 16 km off the Island of Noirmoutier.
- **Construction Timeline**: Initial offshore operations anticipated in summer 2023, with installation processes expected to start in 2024 and completion in approximately 2.5 years (by 2026).

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Total Investment**: €2.5 billion for the development of the offshore wind farm.
- **Development Company**: Eoliennes en Mer des îles d’Yeu et de Noirmoutier (EMYN), a consortium including Ocean Winds, Sumitomo, Banque des Territoires, and Vendée Energie.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Final Investment Decision (FID)**: Reached in April 2023, marking a key milestone for the project.
- **Construction Start**: Expected to begin in spring 2024 with maritime works including monopile foundations, electrical substation construction, and submarine cable installation.
- **Turbine Installation**: Expected in 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The project could create up to 1,600 direct jobs in France, significantly contributing to the country’s offshore wind energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The facility is developed through Ocean Winds, a 50/50 joint venture between Engie and EDP Renewables. Engie does not own the facility exclusively but is a significant partner in the joint venture.
