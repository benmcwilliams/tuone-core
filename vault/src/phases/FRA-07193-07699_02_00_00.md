### Evolutionary Timeline of the ENGIE Solutions Facility in Champs-sur-Marne, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **April 2020:** ENGIE Solutions confirmed the successful completion of two geothermal wells drilled to a depth of 1,900 meters. The facility is designed to produce green heat sufficient to heat the equivalent of 10,000 homes in Champs-sur-Marne and Noisiel. The geothermal resource will provide water at 70 degrees Celsius with a flow rate of 350 cubic meters per hour. Construction of the geothermal heating plant and the associated network is set to begin in 2020, with the aim of supplying heat by the end of 2021.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **April 2020:** The total investment for the geothermal heating project is EUR 40 million. The Ile-de-France region is contributing EUR 4 million (10% of the investment), while ADEME is providing EUR 6 million in financial support as part of the Chaleur Fund to promote deep geothermal energy in eastern Paris.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **December 2019:** The geothermal drilling campaign commenced.
   - **April 2020:** Completion of the drilling phase and confirmation of geothermal resource parameters. Announcement of the start of construction for the heating network and geothermal heating plant, with a target operational date by the end of 2021.
