### Evolutionary Timeline of the GéoMarne Facility in Champs-sur-Marne, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2020**: GéoMarne successfully completed the drilling of two deviated wells reaching a depth of 1,900 meters. The geothermal resource confirmed includes water at 70 degrees Celsius with a flow rate of 350 cubic meters per hour. This geothermal heating project aims to provide green heat sufficient for heating approximately 10,000 homes in Champs-sur-Marne and Noisiel.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2020**: The total investment for the geothermal heating network is EUR 40 million. The Ile-de-France region contributes EUR 4 million (10% of the investment), while ADEME provides EUR 6 million as part of the Chaleur Fund to support the development of deep geothermal energy in eastern Paris.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2019**: The geothermal drilling campaign commenced.
- **April 2020**: Completion of the drilling phase and confirmation of geothermal resources. Construction of the geothermal heating plant and installation of equipment is set to begin, with the aim to supply heat by the end of 2021.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2020**: The project has engaged local citizens through crowdfunding, which reached EUR 1 million, indicating community involvement and potential job creation related to the construction and operation of the facility.
