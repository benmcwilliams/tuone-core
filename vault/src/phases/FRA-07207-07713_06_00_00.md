## FRA-07207-07713

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Eiffage Group Facility in Huatacondo, FRA

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Huatacondo solar park is a 98 MW solar power plant located in northern Chile, specifically in the Tarapacá region, 100 kilometers south of Pozo Almonte. 
   - The facility was developed to consist of 49 individual blocks of 2 MW each, covering an area of 242 hectares. 
   - Eiffage Group assumed the Engineering, Procurement, and Construction (EPC) duties for the plant.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The total investment for the Huatacondo solar project was approximately $196 million. 
   - The project was initially developed by AustrianSolar Chile SpA before being acquired by Sojitz Corporation, a Japanese-led consortium.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - The facility was commissioned and originally owned by AustrianSolar Chile SpA. 
   - The acquisition by Sojitz Corporation was facilitated by Oppenhoff & Partner, a German law firm, which provided legal and contractual advice.

4. **Labour:**
   - The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the Huatacondo facility.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility was originally owned by AustrianSolar Chile SpA. Following the acquisition, it is now owned by a Japanese consortium led by Sojitz Corporation. Eiffage Group is involved in the project as the EPC contractor but does not own the facility.
