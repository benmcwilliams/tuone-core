### Enerparc Facility in Marville, France - Evolutionary Timeline

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** September 2021
   - **Details:** The Enerparc facility, in collaboration with TSE, officially inaugurated France's second-largest photovoltaic (PV) plant with a total installed capacity of 152 MW. The facility is built on 155 hectares of a former NATO military airport and utilizes 364,000 PV modules, including bifacial types. It is capable of generating approximately 160 GWh/year, which meets about 90% of the annual electricity consumption of the nearby city of Verdun.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** September 2021
   - **Details:** TSE invested €65 million in the project, which included €57 million in debt financing and the remainder in equity from shareholders. This investment was crucial for the development and commissioning of the facility.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** May 2021
   - **Details:** The western half of the plant, developed by TSE, was commissioned.
   - **Date:** September 2021
   - **Details:** The eastern half of the plant, developed by Enerparc, was commissioned, marking the official inauguration of the facility.
