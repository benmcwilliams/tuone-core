## FRA-07280-01626

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Engie Facility in Curbans, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **01-06-2018**: Engie inaugurated a 24 MW solar park in Curbans, which is part of the southern French region of Alpes-de-Haute-Provence. The facility is designed to produce nearly 32 million kWh annually, sufficient to cover the electricity consumption of over 13,000 people. The solar park consists of 71,200 PV panels spread across 50 hectares and utilizes innovative 1-axis trackers to optimize energy production.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **01-06-2018**: The total investment for the solar park in Curbans was €28.6 million (approximately $33.3 million). This investment was part of Engie's strategy to enhance its renewable energy portfolio.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **01-06-2018**: The Curbans solar park was officially commissioned. The project had begun construction in 2016, with initial commissioning planned for the end of 2017. The project was developed and built by Engie Green, which will also operate the facility.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the Curbans facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Engie owns the facility exclusively, as it was developed and built by Engie Green, a subsidiary of Engie.

### Summary
The Engie facility in Curbans, France, is a 24 MW solar park that was inaugurated on June 1, 2018, following an investment of €28.6 million. It produces approximately 32 million kWh annually and is designed to serve over 13,000 people. Engie Green is responsible for the development, construction, and operation of the facility, which is fully owned by Engie.
