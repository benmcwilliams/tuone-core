### Evolutionary Timeline of the Engie Facility in Le Chesnay-Rocquencourt, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** Expected by October 2025
   - **Details:** The facility will produce geothermal heating, supplying stable and renewable heat to approximately 7,500 households, the Parly 2 shopping center, Mignot hospital, the town hall, the swimming pool, and the Nouvelle France condominium. The geothermal heating plant will involve drilling a borehole to a depth of 1,500 meters to access geothermal water at a temperature of 61 degrees Celsius. Plans also include drilling two additional wells to extend geothermal heat supply to neighboring towns, potentially benefiting an additional 8,000 households.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** January 2023
   - **Details:** A capital of EUR 5.3 million has been allocated to establish GeomY3, a joint company formed by Engie Solutions, the Yvelines departmental council, the municipality, and the current operator of the local boiling room and district heating network.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** End of 2023
   - **Details:** Work on the first borehole is expected to commence at the end of 2023. The geothermal heating project is anticipated to be operational by October 2025.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Details:** The articles do not specify exact job creation or job loss figures related to this facility. However, the project is expected to create jobs associated with the construction and operation of the geothermal heating plant.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Details:** The facility is not solely owned by Engie; it is a joint venture involving Engie Solutions, the Yvelines departmental council, the municipality, and the current operator of the local heating network.
