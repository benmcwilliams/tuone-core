### Evolutionary Timeline of the GeomY3 Facility in Le Chesnay-Rocquencourt, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Production:** The GeomY3 facility is focused on geothermal heating, aiming to supply renewable heat to the region.
   - **Production Capacity:** The facility is expected to supply heat to 7,500 households, including the Parly 2 shopping center, Mignot hospital, town hall, swimming pool, and Nouvelle France condominium. Plans for expansion could increase this capacity to serve an additional 8,000 households in neighboring towns.
   - **Updates:** The first borehole will be drilled to a depth of 1,500 meters, targeting geothermal water at a temperature of 61 degrees Celsius.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Investment:** A total capital of EUR 5.3 million has been allocated to set up the GeomY3 company, which is a joint venture.
   - **Subsidies:** The Yvelines departmental council has provided land for the construction of the geothermal heating plant.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Announcement Date:** The project was announced on January 28, 2023.
   - **Construction Start:** Work on the first borehole is expected to commence at the end of 2023.
   - **Production Start:** The facility is projected to begin supplying heat by October 2025.
   - **Future Plans:** There are plans to drill two additional wells to expand the geothermal heating supply to neighboring towns.

4. **Labour:**

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Ownership:** The GeomY3 facility is a joint venture involving Engie Solutions, the Yvelines departmental council, the municipality, and the current operator of the local boiling room and district heating network. Therefore, it is not exclusively owned by a single company.
