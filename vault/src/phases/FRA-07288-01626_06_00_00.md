### Evolutionary Timeline of the Engie Facility in Montane, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 01-06-2018
   - **Details:** Engie inaugurated a 24 MW solar park in Montane, France, featuring 71,200 PV panels across 50 hectares. The facility is designed to produce nearly 32 million kWh annually, sufficient to cover the electricity consumption of over 13,000 people. The solar park consists of two plants: Montane Sud and Montane Nord. It employs innovative 1-axis trackers to optimize solar panel orientation and includes an experimental battery storage system to manage electricity production and distribution.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 01-06-2018
   - **Details:** The total investment for the Montane solar park was €28.6 million (approximately $33.3 million). The project was developed and built by Engie Green, which also operates the facility.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 01-06-2018
   - **Details:** The solar park was inaugurated on this date, with construction having started in 2016. The project was initially planned for commissioning by the end of 2017 but was completed in 2018. The facility was developed following successful bids in calls for tenders organized by France's Energy Regulatory Commission (CRE) in 2015.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 01-06-2018
   - **Details:** Engie Green relied on local companies for the development of the plant, particularly in the electricity, track layout, and construction sectors. However, specific job creation or loss figures were not provided in the article.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 01-06-2018
   - **Details:** Engie owns the facility exclusively, as it was developed, built, and is operated by Engie Green, a subsidiary of Engie.
