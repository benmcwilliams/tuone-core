### Evolutionary Timeline of the TotalEnergies Facility in Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **January 2021:** Total and Engie announced plans to develop France’s largest green hydrogen facility at a Total biorefinery in Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur. The facility will feature a 40MW electrolyser capable of producing 5 tonnes of green hydrogen per day. The design includes an innovative management solution for hydrogen production and storage, optimizing the integration of solar energy from multiple PV plants.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **January 2021:** The project, named Masshylia, has applied for subsidies from both French and European authorities to support its development. The French government has committed to investing €7 billion in green hydrogen production by 2030 as part of its coronavirus recovery package.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **January 2021:** Total and Engie aim to begin construction of the facility in 2022, following the completion of an advanced engineering study. The target for starting production is set for 2024. The facility has the potential to expand its capacity to produce up to 15 tonnes of green hydrogen per day in future phases.

4. **Labour:**
   - **January 2021:** While specific job creation or loss figures were not mentioned in the article, the development of the facility is expected to contribute to job creation in the region, particularly in the context of the broader hydrogen strategy and investments in renewable energy.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **January 2021:** The facility is a joint project between Total and Engie, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by Total.
