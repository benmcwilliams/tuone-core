### Evolutionary Timeline of the Engie Facility in Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** January 2021
   - **Details:** Engie, in collaboration with Total, announced the development of France’s largest green hydrogen facility, named Masshylia. The facility will feature a 40MW electrolyser capable of producing 5 tonnes of green hydrogen per day. The project is designed to integrate solar energy from over 100MW of solar projects and includes innovative management solutions for hydrogen production and storage. The electrolyser has the potential to be expanded to produce up to 15 tonnes of green hydrogen per day in future phases.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** January 2021
   - **Details:** The project has applied for subsidies from both French and European authorities to support its development. The French government has committed to investing €7 billion in green hydrogen production by 2030 as part of its coronavirus recovery package.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** January 2021
   - **Details:** The construction of the facility is planned to begin in 2022, following the completion of an advanced engineering study. The aim is to start production in 2024.

4. **Labour:**

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** January 2021
   - **Details:** The facility is a joint project between Engie and Total, indicating a joint venture ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by either company.
