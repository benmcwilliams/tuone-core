### Evolutionary Timeline of the Sillia Facility in Eastern France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Current Production Capacity:** As of July 2017, the Sillia facility has a production capacity of 50MW/year, utilizing three independent production lines for both poly and monocrystalline solar modules.
   - **Expansion Plans:** Recom plans to expand the facility's production capacity from 50MW to 150MW by the end of 2017.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Investment for Expansion:** Recom's acquisition of Sillia includes a commitment to upgrade the facility's production capacity, which indicates a significant investment in modern equipment and infrastructure to facilitate the expansion.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Acquisition and Expansion Announcement:** Sillia was acquired by Recom in June 2017 after going into administration in March 2017. The expansion to 150MW was announced in the same article, with a target completion date by the end of 2017.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Job Security for Existing Employees:** Recom has guaranteed the existing commitments made to the factory’s 44 employees for the next two years following the acquisition. Employees at the facility are also eligible for payments from Bosch, the previous owner, ranging from €23,000 to €150,000.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Ownership Status:** Recom owns the Sillia facility following its acquisition in June 2017. However, it is noted that Recom will not operate the 150MW facility in eastern France that was previously acquired from Bosch.
