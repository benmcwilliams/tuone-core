### Evolutionary Timeline of the GSM Facility in Saint-Maurice-la-Clouère, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** October 2020
   - **Details:** The facility produces a 2.7 MW floating photovoltaic (PV) array located on a flooded quarry in Saint-Maurice-la-Clouère, Nouvelle-Aquitaine, France. The plant is designed to generate more than 3.3 GWh of electricity annually. The solar panels are fully removable and recyclable.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** October 2020
   - **Details:** The total cost of the floating PV project is €3 million (approximately $3.5 million). The project received €150,000 in crowdfunding from local residents, which was part of a broader incentive scheme for projects with crowdfunding support.

   - **Date:** October 2020
   - **Details:** The floating PV array was completed by GSM, with the project being built on an old quarry owned by GSM. The construction involved various contractors, including Bouygues Energies Services for electrical engineering and installation.
