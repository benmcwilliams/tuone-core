### Evolutionary Timeline of the Navantia Windar Facility in Port of Brest, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2024**: The Navantia Windar consortium was responsible for constructing and assembling jacket foundation components for the 496MW Saint-Brieuc offshore wind farm at the Port of Brest. The facility contributed to the production of foundations and transition pieces for the offshore wind project, which features 62 turbines, each with an 8MW capacity, capable of generating 2,000 GWh of clean energy annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2024**: The Saint-Brieuc offshore wind farm was inaugurated with a total investment of €2.4 billion ($2.6 billion). This investment was crucial for the development and operationalization of the facility, marking it as one of France's most powerful offshore wind farms.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2012**: Development of the Saint-Brieuc offshore wind farm began.
- **July 2023**: The Saint-Brieuc offshore wind farm generated its first electricity, marking a significant milestone in the project timeline.
- **September 2024**: The facility was officially inaugurated and became fully operational.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2024**: The construction phase of the Saint-Brieuc offshore wind farm supported approximately 1,700 jobs, including 500 local positions. This involvement highlighted the facility's contribution to local employment and the broader European job market.
