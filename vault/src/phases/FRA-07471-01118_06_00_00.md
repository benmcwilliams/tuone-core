## FRA-07471-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Kurtzenhouse, FRA

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2023**: Iberdrola has been awarded a 25MW floating solar power project in Kurtzenhouse, Alsace. The facility will occupy approximately 13.5 hectares of the 28 hectares available on the site and is expected to produce about 27GWh of green electricity per year. The project will be developed, built, and operated by Iberdrola on the basin of the Bischwiller gravel pit.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2023**: Iberdrola plans to invest significantly in renewable energy projects in France, with a total investment of around €4 billion ($43 billion) by 2025. This investment is part of a broader strategy that includes the development of floating photovoltaic solar projects on active industrial sites.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2023**: Iberdrola began environmental and technical studies to determine the most suitable technological solution for the floating solar project. These studies are expected to be completed by early 2024.
- **Spring 2024**: The application for a building permit is expected to be submitted.
- **Late Summer 2026**: The commissioning of the floating solar power plant is anticipated.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2023**: Iberdrola has indicated that it will choose local companies to build the plant, which may contribute to job creation in the region during the construction phase.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2023**: The floating solar power plant will be developed, built, and operated by Iberdrola, while part of the plant will be owned by the municipality of Kurtzenhouse. This indicates a joint ownership structure between Iberdrola and the local municipality.
