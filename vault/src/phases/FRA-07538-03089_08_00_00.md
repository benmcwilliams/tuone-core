### Evolutionary Timeline of the Jan De Nul Facility in Southern Brittany, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2024**: Jan De Nul, in partnership with Hellenic Cables, was awarded a framework agreement to supply and install export cables for two floating wind farms off the coast of Southern Brittany, with a total capacity of 750 MW. The facility will be responsible for the design, manufacturing, and protection of three high-voltage alternating current (HVAC) 225 kV cable systems, spanning approximately 150 kilometers. Jan De Nul will also embed the cables in the seabed.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2024**: The framework agreement with France’s transmission system operator (TSO) Réseau Transport d’Electricité (RTE) is part of an engineering, procurement, construction, and installation (EPCI) contract expected to be finalized between 2025 and 2026. The submarine cables will be produced at Hellenic Cables' expanded facility in Corinth, Greece, with delivery anticipated between 2028 and 2030.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2024**: The announcement of the framework agreement marks a significant milestone for Jan De Nul, as it is their second important framework agreement following a previous one with TenneT for a 2 GW program. This agreement with RTE adds to their portfolio of projects with multiple transmission system operators.
