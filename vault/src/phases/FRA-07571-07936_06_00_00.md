### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mécojit Facility in Capdenac-Gare, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2020**: The Mécojit facility, in collaboration with the French cooperative Céléwatt, is involved in the construction of a 250 kW solar park in Carayac, southwestern France. This solar park is notable for its innovative use of untreated, unprocessed oak wood structures sourced from local forests, promoting local employment and utilizing regional natural resources. The project is designed to adapt to the natural movements of the wood and aims to optimize the longevity of the installation.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2020**: The solar park project is being developed without public support, indicating a self-funded initiative by Céléwatt and Mécojit. The energy produced will be sold to the French energy cooperative Enercoop at a price of €0.08/kWh.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2020**: Construction of the solar park is announced, with completion scheduled for 2021. The project is described as a pioneering effort in using raw wood for solar panel support structures, marking a significant milestone in sustainable construction practices.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2020**: The project is reported to have created local employment opportunities by sourcing materials and labor from the surrounding region, specifically mentioning the involvement of local companies in the construction process.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2020**: The facility is a collaborative effort between Céléwatt and Mécojit, indicating a joint venture rather than exclusive ownership by either company. The partnership leverages the strengths of both organizations in engineering and cooperative energy production.
