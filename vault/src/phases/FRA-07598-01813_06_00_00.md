## FRA-07598-01813

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Neoen Facility in Cestas, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2015**: The Cestas solar park, developed by Neoen, is connected to the grid. It is the largest solar park in Europe with a production capacity of 300 MW, capable of supplying electricity to approximately 250,000 people (or 150,000 when heating is included). The facility spans 250 hectares and consists of 25 separate plants of 12 MW each. 

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2015**: The project was developed with an investment of EUR 360 million (approximately US$404 million). The electricity generated is sold under a 20-year power purchase agreement with EDF at a price of EUR 105/MWh.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2015**: The Cestas solar park is officially connected to France’s high voltage network, Réseau de transport d’électricité (RTE).
- **February 2016**: The Cestas solar park is credited with contributing to a 25% increase in France's PV output in 2015, highlighting its significance in the national energy landscape.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2015**: At the peak of construction, the project employed 250 workers who were involved in the installation of solar panels.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: Neoen exclusively owns and operates the Cestas solar park, making it a fully owned facility without joint ventures or partnerships in its operational structure.

### Summary
The Neoen facility in Cestas, France, has evolved since its inception in 2015 as Europe's largest solar park, with a capacity of 300 MW. It has seen significant investment, job creation during its construction phase, and has played a crucial role in increasing France's solar energy output. Neoen maintains full ownership of the facility, ensuring its operational independence.
