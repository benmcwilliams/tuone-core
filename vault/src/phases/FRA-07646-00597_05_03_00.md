### Evolutionary Timeline of the Orano Facility in La Hague, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2023**: Orano collaborates with Japan's Federation of Electric Power Companies (FEPC) on research and development for the reprocessing of used mixed oxide (MOX) fuel. This partnership aims to establish reprocessing technology for used MOX fuel, which is crucial for Japan's nuclear power generation strategy.
- **March 2024**: Orano's La Hague facility has reprocessed over 40,000 tonnes of used nuclear fuel, demonstrating its capacity to recycle significant amounts of nuclear material. The facility's technology is also being utilized in a pilot project in the US aimed at recycling 100 tonnes of used nuclear fuel annually.
- **March 2024**: The Cruas-Meysse nuclear power plant in France restarts using a full core of recycled uranium fuel processed at La Hague, marking a significant milestone in France's uranium reprocessing efforts.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2024**: French government officials announce a sustainability/resilience program to extend the life of the La Hague facility beyond 2040.
