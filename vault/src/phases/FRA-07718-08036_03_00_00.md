### Evolutionary Timeline of the Réseau de Transport d’Electricité Facility in La Martyre, France

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility is part of the Celtic Interconnector project, which will feature a 700MW high-voltage submarine cable linking Ireland and continental Europe. The project includes the installation of converter stations in both Ireland and France to facilitate electricity transfer. The interconnector will have the capacity to import and export enough electricity to power approximately 450,000 homes.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The Celtic Interconnector project is being funded in part by the European Union, classified as a Project of Common Interest (PCI). The project has secured financing agreements worth €800 million ($839.6 million) with the European Investment Bank (EIB), Danske Bank, Barclays, and BNP Paribas. Additionally, the project received €530.7 million from the Connecting Europe Facility (CEF) in 2019.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - On **November 28, 2022**, EirGrid and RTE signed technical and financial agreements for the Celtic Interconnector project. The project is expected to become operational by **2026**.
