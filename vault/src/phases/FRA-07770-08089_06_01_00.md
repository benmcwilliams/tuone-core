## FRA-07770-08089

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Solon SE Facility in Silicium de Provence, FRA

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility was intended to produce polysilicon with a planned production capacity of 3,000 metric tons (MT). However, the project faced significant challenges and was unable to commence operations due to financial difficulties.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - Solon SE incurred a €52 million impairment charge related to its investment in the Silicium de Provence facility. This included a €39 million write-down on the carrying value of its equity investment and a €13 million charge on a shareholder loan. The financial crisis hindered further funding necessary for the construction of the plant.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - The Silicium de Provence S.A.S. (SilPro) project was placed under administration in April 2009 after failing to secure additional funding. The facility was never completed, and the project was effectively canceled due to these financial issues.

4. **Labour:**
   - There is no specific information regarding job creation or job loss directly associated with the Silicium de Provence facility, but the failure of the project likely resulted in job losses related to the halted construction and operational plans.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - Solon SE held a 48% stake in SOL Holding AG, which owned 70% of SilPro. Therefore, Solon did not have exclusive ownership of the facility; it was part of a joint venture with other investors, including Dutch group Econcern and Photon Power Industries (PPI), among others.

### Summary:
The Solon SE facility in Silicium de Provence was intended to be a polysilicon production plant with a capacity of 3,000 MT. However, due to the financial crisis, the project was unable to secure necessary funding and was placed under administration in April 2009, leading to a significant impairment charge for Solon SE. The facility was never completed, and Solon SE did not have exclusive ownership, sharing it with other investors.
