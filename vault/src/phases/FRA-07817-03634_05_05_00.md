### Evolutionary Timeline of the Tractebel Facility in Tours, France

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2023**: Tractebel, a subsidiary of Engie, is involved in the Nuward SMR project, which includes the development of a 340 MWe small modular reactor (SMR) plant with two pressurized water reactors (PWRs) of 170 MWe each. The facility is focused on conceptual design studies for various components, including the turbine hall and water intake systems.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: EDF created a wholly-owned subsidiary named Nuward to support the SMR project. The basic design phase of the Nuward SMR is expected to be completed by 2025, indicating significant investment in the development of this clean technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2019**: The Nuward project was launched by the French Alternative Energies and Atomic Energy Commission (CEA), EDF, Naval Group, and TechnicAtome.
- **March 2023**: The basic design phase of the Nuward SMR project commenced, with a Design and Safety options file scheduled for submission to the French Nuclear Safety Authority in July 2023.
- **2025-2030**: Detailed design and formal application for a new nuclear facility are planned, with certification and supply chain development expected during this period.
- **2030**: The first concrete for the first unit in France is scheduled to be poured, with construction anticipated to take about three years.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2023**: Approximately 50 engineers from Tractebel are currently working on the Nuward SMR project, with expectations to double this number in the coming years. By 2024, the core team for Nuward is expected to grow to about 150, with a total workforce of over 600 including partner staff.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2023**: Tractebel operates as a subsidiary of Engie and collaborates with EDF and other partners on the Nuward project. The facility is not exclusively owned by Tractebel but is part of a joint effort involving multiple stakeholders in the nuclear sector.
