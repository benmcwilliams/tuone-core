### Evolutionary Timeline of Fortescue Metals Group Facility in Banbury, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **June 2023:** Fortescue Metals Group announces the construction of a new battery and electric powertrain production facility in Banbury, Oxfordshire, UK. The facility will focus on producing electric/zero-emission powertrain systems for the off-road sector, including trucks and trains. It will feature automated battery module and pack assembly, as well as assembly of power conversion and power systems. The facility will cover more than 13,500 square meters.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **June 2023:** The establishment of the facility is part of Fortescue's strategy to significantly expand its UK manufacturing capability. The company has made investments in this facility, although specific financial figures are not disclosed in the article.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **June 2023:** The facility is set to officially open in 2024. A prototype build is expected to be completed by July or August 2023, with the first mining haul truck module scheduled to roll off the production line in August 2023.

4. **Labour:**
   - **June 2023:** The new facility is projected to create more than 120 jobs in the region.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **June 2023:** Fortescue Metals Group owns the facility exclusively. The company acquired Williams Advanced Engineering (WAE), which will play a role in the facility's operations, but the ownership remains with Fortescue.
