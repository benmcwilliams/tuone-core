### Evolutionary Timeline of the HyProMag Facility in Birmingham, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **03/2020**: HyProMag, a spin-off from the University of Birmingham, is involved in the RaRE project, which aims to integrate recycled rare earth magnets into electric vehicles. The facility utilizes a patented process called Hydrogen Processing of Magnet Scrap (HPMS) to extract neodymium-iron-boron (NdFeB) alloy powders from scrap materials. The project aims to scale up the HPMS process to produce new rare earth magnets for electric vehicles.
- **05/2023**: HyProMag announces the construction of a large-scale ‘short loop’ recycling facility at Tyseley Energy Park in Birmingham. The facility will have a minimum production capacity of 100 tonnes per year, focusing on supplying neodymium/iron/boron magnets and alloys for various markets, including automotive and electronics.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **03/2020**: The British government provides £1.9 million in funding for the RaRE project, with project partners contributing an additional £700,000.
- **05/2023**: The construction of the new recycling facility is part of a $5.3 million (£4.3 million) project funded by the Industrial Strategy Challenge Fund known as Driving the Electric Revolution, delivered by UK Research and Innovation.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **03/2020**: The RaRE project is launched, aiming to develop a continuous supply chain for recycled rare earth magnets.
- **05/2023**: HyProMag begins construction of the large-scale recycling facility at Tyseley Energy Park, with production expected to start later in the year in the UK and in Germany in 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **05/2023**: The announcement of the new facility and its construction is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **05/2023**: Maginito is set to acquire HyProMag, positioning it as a key player in the UK’s rare earth magnet recycling sector. This indicates a shift in ownership structure, moving from a university spin-off to being part of a larger company.
