## GBR-00109-00114

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Britishvolt Facility in Blyth, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2020**: Britishvolt and AMTE Power announced plans for a battery cell factory with a capacity of 30-35 GWh, initially planned for St. Athan, Wales.
- **December 2020**: Britishvolt confirmed Blyth, Northumberland as the new location for the factory, with a planned capacity of 30 GWh, producing over 300,000 lithium-ion batteries annually.
- **January 2021**: Britishvolt partnered with Siemens for automation and electrification solutions, aiming to start production by the end of 2023.
- **April 2021**: Britishvolt purchased land in Blyth and submitted a planning application, with plans to start construction later that year.
- **June 2021**: Britishvolt announced a phased construction plan, with the factory to be built in three phases of 10 GWh each, reaching full capacity by 2027.
- **September 2021**: Preliminary work began on the Blyth factory, with production still scheduled for 2023.
- **January 2022**: Britishvolt announced plans to produce 30 GWh of battery capacity per year, with the factory expected to employ around 3,000 people.
- **July 2022**: Britishvolt revised its capacity plans to 38 GWh, indicating growth in production capabilities.
- **October 2022**: Reports indicated that construction was delayed until mid-2025 due to funding issues and rising energy costs.
- **January 2023**: Britishvolt filed for insolvency, leading to the layoff of most employees and casting doubt on the completion of the Blyth factory.
- **April 2024**: Reports emerged that the planned site for the battery factory might be sold to a company intending to build a data center, indicating the potential cancellation of the project.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2020**: Britishvolt announced a total investment of £2.6 billion for the Blyth facility, marking it as one of the largest industrial investments in the North East of England.
- **January 2022**: Britishvolt secured $2.3 billion (£1.7 billion) in funding for the gigafactory, with an in-principle offer of £100 million from the UK government’s Automotive Transformation Fund.
- **July 2022**: The UK government confirmed additional funding from the Automotive Transformation Fund, although the exact amount was not disclosed.
- **October 2022**: Britishvolt sought a £30 million advance from the government, which was refused, leading to financial difficulties.
- **January 2023**: Britishvolt announced it was in talks to sell a majority stake to secure funding for the Blyth factory.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2020**: Blyth was confirmed as the location for the battery factory.
- **April 2021**: Land purchase and planning application submitted.
- **September 2021**: Preliminary work commenced.
- **October 2022**: Construction was reported to be delayed until mid-2025.
- **January 2023**: Britishvolt filed for insolvency, halting progress on the factory.
- **April 2024**: Plans for the factory were likely to be canceled as the site was considered for sale to a data center developer.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2020**: The project was expected to create 3,500 jobs directly and an additional 10,000 to 15,000 in the supply chain.
- **December 2020**: The factory was projected to employ around 3,000 skilled workers.
- **January 2023**: Following the insolvency filing, most of the approximately 300 employees were laid off.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2020**: Initially a partnership with AMTE Power, Britishvolt later moved to operate independently.
- **January 2023**: Britishvolt was in talks to sell a majority stake to secure its future, indicating a shift in ownership structure.
- **April 2024**: The site was under consideration for sale to a new owner, potentially ending Britishvolt's plans for the factory.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and challenges faced by the Britishvolt facility in Blyth, GBR, highlighting its ambitious plans, significant investments, and eventual financial struggles leading to insolvency.
