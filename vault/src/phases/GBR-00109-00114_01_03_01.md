## GBR-00109-00114

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Britishvolt Facility in Blyth, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2020**: Britishvolt and AMTE Power announced plans for a battery cell factory with a capacity of 30-35 GWh, initially planned for St. Athan, Wales.
- **December 2020**: Britishvolt confirmed Blyth, Northumberland as the new location for the factory, with a planned capacity of 30 GWh, producing over 300,000 lithium-ion batteries annually.
- **January 2021**: Britishvolt partnered with Siemens for automation and electrification solutions, aiming to start construction in summer 2021 and production by the end of 2023.
- **April 2021**: Britishvolt purchased land in Blyth and submitted a planning application, with plans to start construction later that year.
- **June 2021**: Preliminary work began on the factory site, with production still scheduled for 2023.
- **January 2022**: Britishvolt announced plans to produce 30 GWh of battery capacity per year, with the factory expected to create around 3,000 direct jobs and 5,000 in the supply chain.
- **August 2022**: Reports indicated that the factory's construction was delayed until mid-2025 due to rising energy costs and funding issues.
- **January 2023**: Britishvolt filed for insolvency, leading to the layoff of most employees and uncertainty about the factory's future.
- **February 2023**: Recharge Industries acquired Britishvolt, with plans to shift focus from automotive batteries to stationary energy storage and high-performance sports car batteries.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2020**: Initial investment volume for the factory was projected at £1.2 billion.
- **December 2020**: Total investment for the Blyth facility was announced at £2.6 billion, marking it as one of the largest industrial investments in North East England.
- **January 2022**: Britishvolt secured a £100 million grant from the UK government’s Automotive Transformation Fund, paving the way for £1.7 billion in private financing.
- **August 2022**: The UK government confirmed a £100 million funding commitment for the project.
- **January 2023**: Britishvolt sought a £30 million advance from the government, which was refused, leading to financial distress.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2020**: Blyth was confirmed as the location for the battery factory.
- **April 2021**: Land purchase and planning application submitted.
- **September 2021**: Preliminary work commenced on the factory site.
- **January 2022**: Construction was underway, with production expected to start in late 2023.
- **August 2022**: Reports indicated that the project was put on "life support" due to funding issues.
- **January 2023**: Britishvolt filed for insolvency.
- **February 2023**: Recharge Industries acquired Britishvolt, with plans to change the factory's focus.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2020**: The factory was expected to create 3,500 jobs directly and up to 15,000 in the supply chain.
- **January 2022**: The factory was projected to employ approximately 3,000 skilled workers.
- **January 2023**: Most employees were laid off due to insolvency.
- **February 2023**: Employees were transferred to Recharge Industries as part of the acquisition deal.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2020**: Britishvolt was initially a start-up with plans for a joint venture with AMTE Power.
- **January 2022**: Britishvolt was operating independently, focusing on its own gigafactory.
- **January 2023**: Britishvolt filed for insolvency and was acquired by Recharge Industries, which plans to retain the Britishvolt name but shift its operational focus.

This timeline captures the key developments and changes in the Britishvolt facility in Blyth, GBR, highlighting its evolution from planning to insolvency and acquisition.
