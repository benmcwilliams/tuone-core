## GBR-00109-00645

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Recharge Industries Facility in Blyth, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **06-02-2023**: Recharge Industries announces plans to continue the construction of a battery factory in Blyth, Northumberland, originally intended by Britishvolt. The facility aims to manufacture lithium-ion batteries for electric vehicles, with a focus on sourcing materials free from China and Russia to mitigate supply chain issues.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **06-02-2023**: Britishvolt was initially promised $120 million (£100 million) in public funding by the UK government, but an advance of £30 million was refused due to failure to meet construction milestones. Recharge Industries' acquisition of Britishvolt's assets includes the factory site, but specific investment details from Recharge Industries post-acquisition are not disclosed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **06-02-2023**: Recharge Industries successfully acquires Britishvolt, which had collapsed, and plans to proceed with the construction of the gigafactory.
- **10-03-2023**: Recharge Industries aims to start producing batteries for energy storage by 2025, with a future focus on high-performance sports car batteries.
- **16-04-2024**: Reports indicate that the plans for the battery cell factory in Blyth are likely to face cancellation, with Northumberland County Council considering selling the site to a company intending to build a large data center instead.

#### 4. Labour
- **06-02-2023**: Britishvolt had promised to create 3,000 new skilled jobs in the Northeast area following the factory's construction. However, almost all of the 232 staff members lost their jobs when Britishvolt collapsed. The future job creation potential under Recharge Industries remains uncertain due to the project's instability.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **06-02-2023**: Recharge Industries, owned by the US investment firm Scale Facilitation, acquires Britishvolt, indicating a change in ownership structure from Britishvolt to Recharge Industries. The facility is now under the ownership of Recharge Industries, but the long-term viability of this ownership is in question due to ongoing financial difficulties and project uncertainties.

### Summary
The Recharge Industries facility in Blyth, GBR, has undergone significant changes since its acquisition of Britishvolt in early 2023. Initially aimed at producing lithium-ion batteries for electric vehicles, the project has faced financial challenges and potential cancellation, raising concerns about job creation and the future of battery manufacturing in the UK.
