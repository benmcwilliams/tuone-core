## GBR-00109-00645

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Recharge Industries Facility in Blyth, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **06-02-2023**: Recharge Industries announces plans to continue the construction of a battery factory in Blyth, Northumberland, originally intended by Britishvolt. The factory is set to manufacture batteries for electric vehicles.
- **27-02-2023**: Following the acquisition of Britishvolt, Recharge Industries indicates a shift in focus for the Blyth facility from producing battery cells for major car manufacturers to initially focusing on batteries for stationary energy storage and later for high-performance sports cars.
- **10-03-2023**: Recharge Industries aims to start producing batteries at the Blyth site for energy storage by 2025, with plans to eventually focus on high-performance sports car batteries.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **06-02-2023**: Britishvolt had previously been promised $120 million (£100 million) in public funding by the UK government, but an advance of £30 million was refused due to unmet construction milestones.
- **27-02-2023**: The acquisition of Britishvolt's assets by Recharge Industries is completed, saving the planned battery cell factory in Blyth.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **06-02-2023**: Recharge Industries announces its acquisition of Britishvolt and plans to continue the construction of the Blyth facility.
- **27-02-2023**: The acquisition is officially completed, and plans for the Blyth factory are confirmed to be saved.
- **10-03-2023**: Recharge Industries states that the first products from the manufacturing facility are expected to come to market by the end of 2025.
- **21-11-2023**: Reports emerge that Recharge Industries may face bankruptcy proceedings, casting doubt on the future of the Blyth facility.
- **16-04-2024**: Media reports indicate that plans for the battery cell factory in Blyth are likely to be canceled, with the site potentially being sold to a company intending to build a data center.

#### 4. Labour
- **06-02-2023**: Britishvolt had promised to create 3,000 new skilled jobs in the Northeast area following the factory’s construction. However, almost all of the 232 staff members lost their jobs prior to the acquisition.
- **27-02-2023**: The remaining employees of Britishvolt are expected to transfer to Recharge Industries as part of the acquisition deal.
- **21-11-2023**: Reports indicate that only 26 employees from Britishvolt joined Recharge, and most have since left, raising concerns about job security.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **06-02-2023**: Recharge Industries, owned by the US investment firm Scale Facilitation, acquires Britishvolt, indicating a change in ownership structure.
- **27-02-2023**: The acquisition is confirmed, and Recharge Industries retains the name Britishvolt but plans to change the factory's focus.
- **21-11-2023**: Concerns arise regarding Recharge Industries' financial stability and its ability to maintain ownership of the Blyth facility.

This timeline captures the key developments and challenges faced by the Recharge Industries facility in Blyth, GBR, highlighting its evolution from acquisition to potential cancellation of the planned battery cell factory.
