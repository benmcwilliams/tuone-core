### Evolutionary Timeline of the GE Facility in Blyth, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2019**: GE Renewable Energy announced the testing and R&D of the Haliade-X 12MW wind turbine at the ORE Catapult facility in Blyth. The nacelle and 107-meter blade were to be tested to enhance performance ahead of production in 2021.
- **November 2019**: The nacelle of the Haliade-X 12MW offshore wind turbine arrived at the ORE Catapult for rigorous testing, marking it as the second nacelle assembled, with the first in Rotterdam.
- **June 2020**: The Haliade-X 12MW prototype received a provisional type certificate from DNV GL, confirming its design met high safety and quality standards. Testing of the blade continued at Blyth and in the US.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2019**: GE announced an investment of nearly £15m (€17m) in testing and R&D activities for the Haliade-X in the UK, primarily at the Blyth facility.
- **November 2019**: Continued investment of approximately £15m in testing and R&D activities was reiterated, emphasizing the importance of the Blyth facility in the global testing program.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2019**: Announcement of the Haliade-X testing program in the UK, with plans for extensive testing to validate performance and reliability.
- **November 2019**: Arrival of the nacelle at the ORE Catapult in Blyth for testing.
- **June 2020**: Achievement of a provisional type certificate for the Haliade-X, with ongoing testing activities at Blyth and plans for full certification in the coming months.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2019**: The partnership with ORE Catapult and the testing program was expected to create jobs and generate economic benefits in the local supply chain, although specific job creation numbers were not provided.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility in Blyth is operated by GE Renewable Energy in partnership with ORE Catapult, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership. GE is primarily responsible for the Haliade-X technology testing, while ORE Catapult provides the testing infrastructure and expertise.
