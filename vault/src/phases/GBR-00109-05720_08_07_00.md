### Evolutionary Timeline of the ORE Catapult Facility in Blyth, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2017**: ORE Catapult operates a 15MW wind turbine and a 3MW marine drivetrain test facility. They are procuring a Power Analyser System to connect with the Grid Emulation System (eGrid) for measuring electrical power quality.
- **December 2017**: ORE Catapult announces plans to establish a wind turbine bearing test facility at Blyth, focusing on research and product development for wind turbine bearings.
- **March 2018**: ORE Catapult prepares to publish contract notices for the wind turbine bearing test facility, which will be phased as funding becomes available.
- **July 2018**: ORE Catapult seeks contractors for the wind turbine bearing test facility, valued at GBP 6 million, expected to be completed by September 2019.
- **November 2020**: ORE Catapult tests LM Wind Power’s 107-meter blade for GE Renewable Energy’s Haliade-X turbine, marking a significant achievement in blade testing.
- **November 2020**: ORE Catapult invests in a prototype blade facility using additive manufacturing techniques to reduce production costs and explore new materials.
- **May 2023**: ORE Catapult opens the DARE Centre, a £3 million facility for testing and commercializing digital and robotic products for offshore renewable energy.
- **August 2023**: ORE Catapult collaborates with GE Vernova to enhance offshore wind turbine performance through smart technological solutions.

#### 2. Investment and Development

- **May 2023**: A GBP 4 million agreement is signed with GE Vernova for research collaboration on turbine performance.
