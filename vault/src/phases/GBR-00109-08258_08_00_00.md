### Evolutionary Timeline of the Advance Northumberland Facility in Blyth, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2020**: Advance Northumberland, in collaboration with the Offshore Renewable Energy Catapult (OREC), announced the development of a new offshore turbine test facility in Blyth. The facility will be designed to host one large prototype turbine for testing at a time. The facility is expected to be operational by 2022.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2020**: Advance Northumberland is developing the £32 million Northumberland Energy Park, which includes the creation of a new deep water dock and preparation of nearly 200 hectares of quayside-linked area for the offshore wind sector. This investment is part of a broader strategy to enhance the region's capabilities in supporting offshore energy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2020**: A planning application for the offshore turbine test facility is being prepared for submission in autumn 2020, with the first turbine installation anticipated in 2022.
- **October 2020**: Phase one of the Northumberland Energy Park is expected to be available from spring 2021.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2020**: The development of the offshore turbine test facility is projected to pave the way for hundreds of new jobs in the region, as stated by Advance Northumberland chair Richard Wearmouth.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify the ownership structure of the facility, but it is indicated that Advance Northumberland is a key partner in the development alongside OREC, suggesting a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership.
