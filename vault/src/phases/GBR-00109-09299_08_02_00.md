## GBR-00109-09299

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Joule Challenge Facility in Blyth, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** October 2023
   - **Details:** The Joule Challenge facility focuses on developing composite-based components for next-generation offshore wind turbines, specifically targeting the Technology Readiness Level (TRL) of composite towers for 20MW floating offshore wind turbines. The facility aims to design and manufacture a 12-meter composite tower, which will be tested at the Offshore Renewable Energy (ORE) Catapult’s testing facility in Blyth. The project emphasizes the benefits of using composite materials over steel, including a significant mass reduction of 1500 tonnes and a cost reduction of over £1 million per tower.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** October 2023
   - **Details:** The Joule Challenge has secured a £4.7 million investment from the UK government, specifically from the Department for Energy Security and Net Zero’s £1 billion Net Zero Innovation Portfolio (NZIP). This funding is part of an 18-month phase aimed at addressing manufacturing challenges associated with composite materials in offshore wind turbine production.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** October 2023
   - **Details:** The Joule Challenge has entered a new design phase, focusing on the development and testing of composite turbine towers. This phase is crucial for proving the technology at scale and is expected to deliver significant advancements in the offshore wind industry.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** October 2023
   - **Details:** The project is anticipated to create and maintain a wide range of job opportunities within the UK supply chain, as it aims to provide turbine Original Equipment Manufacturers (OEMs) with a cost and mass competitive product.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** October 2023
   - **Details:** The Joule Challenge is a collaborative programme involving multiple stakeholders, including the UK government, offshore wind industry experts, and the composite supply chain. It is delivered by the Offshore Renewable Energy (ORE) Catapult and the National Composites Centre (NCC), indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by a single company.
