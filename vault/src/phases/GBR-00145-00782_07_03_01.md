### Evolutionary Timeline of the Toyota Facility in Burnaston, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2017**: Toyota announced plans to invest £240 million in its Burnaston factory to prepare for the production of vehicles on its new TNGA platform, which underpins models like the Prius and Toyota C-HR. This update indicates a shift towards more advanced vehicle production capabilities.
- **May 2020**: Production of the new Corolla Trek hybrid began at the Burnaston facility, although deliveries were delayed due to the COVID-19 pandemic.
- **December 2022**: Toyota announced the development of a fuel cell version of the Hilux pick-up truck, with plans to set up a pilot production line at the Burnaston plant. The first prototypes were expected to be produced in 2023.
- **October 2023**: Toyota unveiled the prototype hydrogen fuel cell-powered Hilux at the Burnaston plant, marking a significant milestone in their zero-carbon strategy.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2017**: The £240 million investment was earmarked for the Burnaston facility to enhance production capabilities for new vehicle platforms.
- **December 2022**: The Hilux fuel cell project received £11.3 million in funding, with £5.6 million from the UK government and £5.7 million from the Advanced Propulsion Centre (APC).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2017**: Announcement of the £240 million investment for the Burnaston facility.
- **May 2020**: Production of the Corolla Trek hybrid commenced at Burnaston.
- **December 2022**: Announcement of the Hilux fuel cell project and plans for a pilot production line.
- **October 2023**: Unveiling of the hydrogen fuel cell Hilux prototype at the Burnaston plant.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2023**: The Hilux project is expected to create new jobs and upskill existing employees in hydrogen-related technologies, contributing to the local job market and supporting the UK’s automotive supply chain.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: Toyota Manufacturing UK (TMUK) operates the Burnaston facility as a fully owned subsidiary of Toyota. The facility has been Toyota's first fully owned manufacturing business in Europe since its establishment in 1992.
