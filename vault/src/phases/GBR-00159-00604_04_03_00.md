### Evolutionary Timeline of the Panasonic Facility in Cardiff, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2023**: Panasonic announced plans to retrofit its Cardiff manufacturing factory to create a RE100 demonstration production facility. The facility will utilize 21 hydrogen fuel cell generators (total output of 105 kW), 290 kW of solar PV panels, and 1 MWh of battery energy storage. The goal is to operate the site, which manufactures microwave ovens and small appliances, entirely on renewable energy.
- **December 2024**: The facility was inaugurated, showcasing a system powered entirely by renewable energy, integrating hydrogen fuel cells, PV generators, and storage batteries. The factory consumes approximately 1 GWh of power annually, with peak demand of 280 kW. The facility aims for an energy efficiency of 95% by utilizing heat generated from the fuel cells for heating and hot water.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2023**: Panasonic announced an investment of around €130 million (£113 million) over two years for the retrofit of the Cardiff facility, pending planning consent. This investment is aimed at launching the RE100 demonstration production facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2023**: Announcement of the retrofit project and investment allocation.
- **December 2024**: Official inauguration of the RE100 facility, marking the completion of the retrofit and the start of operations powered entirely by renewable energy.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2023**: The Welsh government minister for the Economy, Vaughan Gething, expressed gratitude for Panasonic's ongoing commitment to the workforce in Cardiff, indicating that the investment would support job creation and contribute to the Welsh economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify any joint ventures or partnerships regarding ownership; therefore, it can be inferred that Panasonic owns the facility exclusively.
