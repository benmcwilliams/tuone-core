### Evolutionary Timeline of the Jaguar Land Rover Facility in Castle Bromwich, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05/2019**: Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) announces plans to manufacture a range of new electrified vehicles at the Castle Bromwich plant, starting with the all-electric successor to the Jaguar XJ luxury sedan. The facility will utilize a 90.2 kWh battery, allowing for ranges of up to 470 kilometers. JLR plans to create an electric car development cluster around the plant, including a battery assembly center in Hams Hall and electric drive unit manufacturing at the Wolverhampton Engine Manufacturing Center.
- **03/2020**: JLR confirms the conversion of Castle Bromwich into a production center for electric vehicles, with plans to produce the Jaguar XJ, J-Pace, and Road Rover models on the new Modular Longitudinal Architecture (MLA) platform.
- **04/2021**: JLR expands the I-Pace range in the UK with new special editions, indicating ongoing production scaling at Castle Bromwich. The facility is set to manufacture the upcoming J-Pace and Road Rover models.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **03/2020**: JLR announces an investment of around £1 billion in the Castle Bromwich and Solihull plants to convert them for the production of MLA electric cars.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **07/2020**: Production of the internal combustion engine version of the Jaguar XJ ceases, with plans for the electric version delayed to October 2021 due to the COVID-19 pandemic and spending restrictions.
- **04/2023**: JLR confirms that the stamping facilities at Castle Bromwich will be expanded to support the production of bodywork for next-generation electric vehicles.
