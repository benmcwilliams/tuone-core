### Evolutionary Timeline of Geothermal Engineering Limited Facility in Cornwall, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10-03-2023**: Geothermal Engineering Limited (GEL) announced plans to build the UK’s first deep geothermal power plant at the United Downs site in Cornwall, aiming to provide 25MW of baseload electricity and 100MWh of heat energy by 2028, sufficient to power 70,000 homes.
- **15-05-2023**: GEL is developing the Langarth Deep Geothermal Heat Network, which will supply zero-carbon heat for nearly 4,000 homes and businesses. The project involves drilling to a depth of 5,275 meters into granite rocks to harness geothermal heat.
- **15-11-2023**: GEL announced plans to produce lithium as a by-product of its geothermal projects, targeting 100 tons annually by the end of 2024 and increasing to at least 1,000 tons by 2026. The company aims to produce over 12,000 tons of lithium per year by 2030.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10-03-2023**: GEL secured $18 million (£15 million) in funding for the development of the geothermal plant, with contributions from Kerogen Capital ($14.5 million) and Thrive Renewables ($3.5 million).
- **15-05-2023**: The UK Government awarded GEL $27 million (£22 million) for the Langarth Deep Geothermal Heat Network as part of a $114 million (£91 million) package from the Green Heat Network Fund.
- **15-11-2023**: GEL received £1.8 million from the Automotive Transformation Fund (ATF) Scale up Readiness programme to support its lithium production initiatives.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **10-03-2023**: Announcement of the geothermal plant's development with operations expected to begin in 2024.
- **15-05-2023**: Funding awarded for the Langarth Deep Geothermal Heat Network, with heat supply expected to commence in 2026.
- **15-11-2023**: Plans to start lithium production by the end of 2024, with significant increases in production capacity planned for subsequent years.

#### 4. Labour
