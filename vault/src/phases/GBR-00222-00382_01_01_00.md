## GBR-00222-00382

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Imerys Facility in Cornwall, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2023**: Imerys, in partnership with British Lithium, aims to create the UK’s first integrated producer of battery-grade lithium carbonate. The facility plans to achieve an annual production capacity of **20,000 tonnes** of lithium carbonate by the end of the decade, sufficient for producing **500,000 electric car batteries** annually. The lithium deposit on Imerys’ land is estimated at **161 million tonnes** with a lithium oxide content of **0.54%**. The facility will include a quarry, processing plant, and conversion plant on the brownfield site.
  
#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2023**: Imerys acquires an **80% stake** in British Lithium, forming a joint venture. The financial details of the acquisition are undisclosed, but it is part of a strategic move to leverage British Lithium's processing technology and pilot plant.
  
#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2023**: Announcement of the joint venture between Imerys and British Lithium to extract lithium from Cornwall. The drilling program and pre-feasibility study are underway.
- **November 2023**: Imerys and British Lithium continue to develop their plans for lithium extraction, with the aim of making Cornwall a leading lithium hub in the UK.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2023**: Imerys employs **1,100 people** in the UK, with **830** based in Cornwall. The joint venture and planned expansions are expected to create additional jobs, although specific numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2023**: Imerys holds an **80% stake** in the joint venture with British Lithium, which retains a **20% stake**. This indicates a **joint venture ownership structure** rather than full ownership by Imerys.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic moves related to the Imerys facility in Cornwall, focusing on its operations, investments, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure.
