### Evolutionary Timeline of the British Lithium Facility in Cornwall, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2022**: British Lithium successfully extracts lithium from granite for the first time on a pilot scale, producing five kilograms of lithium carbonate per day. The extraction process involves crushing and grinding granite, followed by calcination at lower temperatures and acid-free leaching and purification. The company plans to build a large plant with a production capacity of 21,000 tonnes of battery-grade lithium carbonate per year, located near the granite quarry.

- **June 2023**: British Lithium continues to develop its proprietary extraction process in collaboration with Imerys, aiming for an annual production of 20,000 tonnes of lithium carbonate by the end of the decade.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2023**: Imerys acquires an 80% stake in British Lithium, forming a joint venture to create the UK’s first integrated producer of battery-grade lithium carbonate. The joint venture aims to leverage Imerys' mineral resources and infrastructure in Cornwall, alongside British Lithium's processing technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2022**: British Lithium announces the successful operation of its pilot plant and outlines plans for a larger production facility.
  
- **June 2023**: The joint venture between Imerys and British Lithium is officially formed, with plans to extract lithium from Cornwall and achieve significant production targets by 2030.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2023**: The joint venture aims to create jobs in the region of Cornwall as part of its development plans, although specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2023**: British Lithium is no longer exclusively owned by its original founders, as Imerys acquires an 80% stake, resulting in an 80:20 joint venture structure. British Lithium retains a 20% ownership and contributes its technology and pilot plant to the joint venture.
