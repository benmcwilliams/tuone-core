## GBR-00225-00055

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Aston Martin Facility in Coventry, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2021**: Coventry City Council announced intentions to develop plans for a battery 'gigafactory' at Coventry Airport, which would not only produce but also recycle batteries. The facility aims to support the automotive industry, particularly with Jaguar Land Rover's transition to electric vehicles.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2021**: The UK government pledged up to £500 million (approximately €573 million) in stimulus funding for the establishment of a gigafactory in the UK, highlighting the importance of battery production for the automotive sector.
- **July 2021**: The planning application for the gigafactory was submitted, with expectations of stimulating private investment of up to £2 billion (about €2.3 billion) in the region.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2021**: Announcement of the joint venture to prepare for the gigafactory's construction, with plans to apply for preliminary planning permission by the end of 2021.
- **July 2021**: Submission of the planning application for the gigafactory, which is expected to create 6,000 new jobs. A decision on the project is anticipated before the end of the year.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2021**: The joint venture is projected to create more than 4,000 new jobs in the region.
- **July 2021**: The planned gigafactory is expected to create 6,000 new jobs, further emphasizing the facility's potential impact on local employment.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2021**: The facility is being developed through a joint venture involving Coventry City Council, the metropolitan region, and Coventry Airport, indicating that it is not exclusively owned by Aston Martin but rather a collaborative effort to attract investment and facilitate the establishment of the gigafactory.

This timeline captures the key developments related to the Aston Martin facility in Coventry, focusing on its technological, investment, project, labor, and ownership aspects as reported in the articles.
