## GBR-00225-00076

### Evolutionary Timeline of the BMW Facility in Coventry, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2021**: Coventry City Council announced intentions to develop plans for a battery 'gigafactory' at Coventry Airport, aiming for production to start by 2025. The facility is expected to produce and recycle batteries, with an initial production capacity not specified but anticipated to be significant given the scale of a gigafactory.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2021**: The UK government pledged up to £500 million (approximately €573 million) in stimulus funding to support the establishment of a gigafactory in the UK.
- **July 2021**: The planning application for the gigafactory was submitted, with expectations of stimulating private investment of up to £2 billion (about €2.3 billion).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2021**: Announcement of plans to create a gigafactory in Coventry, with a joint venture being formed to facilitate the project.
- **July 2021**: Submission of the planning application for the gigafactory, which is set to cover 530,000 square meters. A decision on the project is expected before the end of the year.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2021**: The joint venture is projected to create over 4,000 new jobs in the region.
- **July 2021**: The gigafactory is expected to create 6,000 new jobs, further emphasizing the project's potential impact on local employment.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2021**: The facility is being developed through a joint venture involving Coventry City Council, the metropolitan region, and Coventry Airport, indicating that it will not be exclusively owned by BMW but rather a collaborative effort to attract an experienced manufacturer for the gigafactory.

This timeline captures the key developments related to the BMW facility in Coventry, GBR, focusing on its technological, investment, project, labor, and ownership aspects as reported in the articles.
