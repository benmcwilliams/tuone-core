## GBR-00225-00239

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Eve Energy Facility in Coventry, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2024**: Eve Energy plans to establish a battery factory at Coventry Airport with an initial production capacity of **20 GWh** annually, which is expected to expand to **60 GWh**. This facility aims to become the largest battery factory in the UK, surpassing the AESC factory in Sunderland (35 GWh) and the Tata project in Somerset (40 GWh).

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2024**: Eve Energy is in discussions to invest approximately **£1.2 billion** in the Coventry facility. The project is contingent on securing subsidies amounting to several hundred million pounds, which are currently under negotiation.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2024**: The project is reported to be at an advanced stage, with Eve Energy's plans for the Coventry factory being part of the broader initiative for the UK Centre for Electrification in the West Midlands. The West Midlands Gigafactory, a public-private joint venture, is also involved in these discussions.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2024**: The establishment of the facility is projected to create up to **6,000 jobs** in the region, contributing significantly to local employment.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2024**: The facility is part of the West Midlands Gigafactory joint venture, which involves Coventry City Council and Coventry Airport. While Eve Energy is a key player in this venture, the exact ownership structure regarding exclusivity is not detailed in the articles.

This timeline captures the key developments related to the Eve Energy facility in Coventry, highlighting its technological ambitions, investment plans, project status, potential job creation, and ownership structure as of March 2024.
