## GBR-00225-00439

### Evolutionary Timeline of the LEVC Facility in Coventry, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2017**: LEVC (London EV Company) unveils the TX electric taxi with a range extender, aiming to produce 24,000 units annually at its Coventry facility, up from a previous capacity of 2,000 cabs.
- **January 2018**: The TX electric taxis are launched on London’s streets, marking the beginning of their operational phase.
- **July 2019**: LEVC reports that its electric black taxis have achieved a 10% market share in London, with 2,500 TX vehicles now in operation across the UK.
- **January 2023**: LEVC announces plans to transition to a fully electric vehicle manufacturer, with intentions to increase production capacity to 20,000 units per year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2017**: Geely, LEVC's parent company, has invested £325 million into the facility to support its growth and expansion into new markets.
- **July 2019**: Since Geely's acquisition in 2014, over £500 million has been invested in LEVC to develop it into an electric vehicle manufacturer.
- **January 2023**: Geely plans a major investment to support LEVC's transformation into a pure electric vehicle manufacturer, although specific amounts are not disclosed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2017**: LEVC launches the TX electric taxi and opens order books for UK customers.
- **January 2018**: The TX electric taxis officially start operating in London.
- **July 2019**: LEVC celebrates the rollout of its 2,500th TX vehicle, marking significant sales growth.
- **February 2021**: Coventry city council announces plans for a battery gigafactory, which could support LEVC's operations.
- **January 2023**: LEVC announces its strategy to become a fully electric vehicle manufacturer, with plans for new commercial vehicle models.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2017**: The workforce at the Coventry facility grows from 100 to approximately 700 employees due to the expansion and increased production capacity.
- **October 2022**: LEVC lays off 140 employees as part of restructuring efforts under new management, indicating challenges faced during the COVID-19 pandemic.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **2013**: Geely acquires full control of LEVC, transitioning it from the London Taxi Company to a subsidiary focused on electric vehicles. LEVC operates as a wholly-owned subsidiary of Geely, indicating full ownership of the Coventry facility. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and changes at the LEVC facility in Coventry, highlighting its evolution in technology, investment, project milestones, labor dynamics, and ownership structure.

### LEVC Facility in Coventry, GBR - Contrasting Summary

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: 
  - As of July 2017, LEVC's new factory in Coventry was projected to produce "24,000 EV a year" (Article 1).
  - By January 2023, it was stated that LEVC could build "3,000 taxis per year in a single-shift operation," with potential to increase to "20,000" (Article 5).
  
- **Product Range**: 
  - The facility produces the TX range-extender electric-hybrid taxis, which allow for "full electric mode for up to 80 miles" (Article 3).
  - LEVC is also discussing a range of commercial vehicle and passenger car models on a common electric platform (Article 5).

#### 2. Investment and Development

  - There are plans for a "major investment" by Geely to transition LEVC into a pure electric vehicle manufacturer, with specific amounts to be announced later (Article 5).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones

  - The TX electric taxis were launched on London’s streets in January 2018 (Article 3).
  
- **Expansion Plans**: 
  - In July 2017, LEVC's CEO mentioned plans to expand into "new markets" beyond the UK (Article 2).
  - By February 2021, Coventry was planning to develop a battery gigafactory, which could support LEVC's operations (Article 4).

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: 
  - The workforce at LEVC grew from "about 100 to about 700 employees" since Geely's acquisition (Article 1).
