## GBR-00225-00988

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Coventry Airport Facility, Coventry, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2021**: Coventry City Council announced plans to develop a battery production hub at Coventry Airport, aiming to establish a gigafactory that would produce and recycle batteries. The facility is expected to cover 530,000 square meters.
- **July 2021**: The planning application for the gigafactory was submitted, with the facility projected to create 6,000 new jobs.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2021**: The UK government pledged up to £500 million (approximately €573 million) in stimulus funding to support the establishment of a gigafactory in the UK.
- **July 2021**: The initiators of the project anticipated that the construction of the gigafactory would stimulate private investment of up to £2 billion (about €2.3 billion).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2021**: Announcement of intentions to develop plans for a gigafactory at Coventry Airport, with a target for production to start by 2025.
- **July 2021**: Submission of the planning application for the gigafactory, with a decision expected before the end of the year.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2021**: The joint venture partnership aimed to create more than 4,000 new jobs in the region.
- **July 2021**: The projected job creation increased to 6,000 new jobs as part of the gigafactory's operations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2021**: The facility is being developed through a public-private partnership (PPP) involving Coventry City Council, the metropolitan region, and Coventry Airport, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by a single company.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Coventry Airport facility, focusing on its planned gigafactory for battery production and recycling.
