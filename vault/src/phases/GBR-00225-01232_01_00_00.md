## GBR-00225-01232

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Pivot Power Facility in Coventry, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2021**: Pivot Power announced plans for a 50MW battery storage facility in Coventry, which is part of its Energy Superhub network aimed at delivering up to 2GW of transmission-connected battery storage across the UK. The facility will store enough electricity to power over 100,000 homes for two hours and will connect to the National Grid’s Bustleholme substation to balance supply and demand.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2024**: The Greenpower Park initiative was launched in Coventry, which is expected to attract £2.5 billion in foreign investment. This initiative will include the West Midlands Gigafactory joint venture, which plans to establish a 60 GWh battery cell factory, indicating significant investment in the region's clean technology infrastructure.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2021**: Construction for the Coventry facility was scheduled to begin in early 2022, as part of Pivot Power's broader strategy to replicate elements of its Energy Superhub Oxford project.
- **March 2024**: The official launch of Greenpower Park in Coventry, which will host various clean energy initiatives, including Pivot Power's energy hubs.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2024**: The Greenpower Park initiative is projected to create approximately 6,000 jobs, indicating significant employment opportunities linked to the clean technology sector in Coventry.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2021**: Pivot Power is identified as a subsidiary of EDF, indicating that the facility in Coventry is not owned exclusively by Pivot Power but is part of a larger corporate structure under EDF.
