## GBR-00225-01346

### Evolutionary Timeline of the UK Battery Industrialisation Centre (UKBIC) in Coventry, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2019**: The UK Battery Industrialisation Centre (UKBIC) is announced with a planned production capacity to support testing and development of new battery technologies. The facility is designed to provide an open-access 18,500 square metre space for battery development, with the first operational equipment being a Module and Pack assembly line capable of producing 50 modules and 2.5 packs per 8-hour shift.
- **February 2021**: The Module and Pack assembly line becomes operational, marking a significant step towards the facility's goal of supporting mass production of battery technologies.
- **July 2021**: The UKBIC officially opens, providing services to various sectors including electric vehicles, rail, aerospace, and energy storage. The facility covers the entire battery production process from electrode manufacturing to testing.
- **January 2022**: UKBIC collaborates with Britishvolt to develop high nickel electric vehicle sample cells for mass production, indicating its role in supporting advanced battery technology development.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2019**: The UK government announces an additional £28 million investment in the UKBIC, raising the total funding to £80 million.
- **July 2021**: The total investment in the facility is confirmed at £130 million, with co-funding from the West Midlands Combined Authority as part of the Faraday Battery Challenge.
- **December 2023**: UKBIC secures an additional £38 million in funding to enhance its scale-up capabilities, building on previous commitments.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2019**: Initial funding and plans for the UKBIC are announced, with a target opening date set for 2020.
- **February 2021**: The Module and Pack assembly line is operational, marking the first major milestone in the facility's development.
- **July 2021**: The UKBIC is officially opened by Prime Minister Boris Johnson, signifying its readiness to support the UK battery sector.
- **January 2022**: UKBIC begins a partnership with Britishvolt to develop next-generation battery cells.
- **December 2023**: UKBIC announces further funding to upgrade its capabilities, indicating ongoing development and expansion.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2019**: The facility is expected to create 100 new jobs upon opening, with the potential to unlock 10,000 jobs at full capacity.
- **July 2021**: The facility employs over 80 staff, with plans for further job creation as partnerships with industry and research organizations develop.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The UK Battery Industrialisation Centre is a national facility co-funded by the UK government and the West Midlands Combined Authority. It operates under a governance consortium that includes Coventry City Council, the Coventry and Warwickshire Local Enterprise Partnership, and WMG (University of Warwick). The facility is not owned exclusively by a single company but is a collaborative effort involving multiple stakeholders. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones of the UK Battery Industrialisation Centre in Coventry, highlighting its role in advancing battery technology and supporting the UK's transition to zero-emission vehicles.
