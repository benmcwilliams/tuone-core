## GBR-00225-01378

### Evolutionary Timeline of the West Midlands Gigafactory Facility in Coventry, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2021**: The West Midlands Gigafactory is envisioned to have a production capacity of **60 GWh** of lithium-ion batteries, with production slated to start in **2025**. The facility will be located on a site of nearly **500,000 square meters** and aims to serve the automotive and energy storage industries.
- **January 2022**: The facility is confirmed to be powered by **100% onsite solar energy** and will also include capabilities for recycling used batteries. The project emphasizes a **net zero transport and logistics strategy** to minimize environmental impact.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2021**: The total investment required for the facility is estimated at **£2.5 billion** (approximately **€3 billion**). The project is still in search of investors and partners from the global battery manufacturing sector.
- **January 2022**: The facility continues to seek the **£2.5 billion** investment, with local planning committees approving the outline plans, indicating a step forward in securing funding and support.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2021**: Initial planning applications for the Gigafactory were submitted.
- **October 2021**: Outline plans for the Gigafactory were revealed, targeting a production start in **2025**.
- **January 2022**: Local planning committees officially approved the outline plans for the Gigafactory, confirming the project's progression towards construction.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2021**: The facility is projected to create up to **6,000 new jobs** directly, with additional thousands expected in the wider supply chain across Coventry, Warwickshire, and the surrounding region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2021**: The West Midlands Gigafactory is a **public-private joint venture** between **Coventry City Council** and **Coventry Airport Ltd**. There is no indication of exclusive ownership by a single entity, as the project is designed to attract various investors and partners from the battery manufacturing industry.
