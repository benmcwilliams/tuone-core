## GBR-00252-00782

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Toyota Facility in Deeside, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2016**: Toyota Manufacturing UK announced that it would build next-generation hybrid engines for a new Toyota crossover at its Deeside plant in North Wales. This facility was set to produce engines for a vehicle based on the C-HR concept, aimed at competing with the Nissan Juke.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2016**: The announcement of the hybrid engine production at the Deeside facility was accompanied by an additional investment of £7 million, which included a £700,000 grant from the Welsh government.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2016**: Announcement made regarding the production of hybrid engines for the new crossover at the Deeside plant.
- **July 2022**: A project began to develop a hydrogen fuel cell version of the Hilux pick-up truck, with the potential for mass production at the Deeside facility after initial prototypes were developed at the Burnaston plant.
- **October 2023**: Toyota unveiled a prototype hydrogen fuel cell-powered Hilux, developed in a joint project with consortium partners, indicating significant progress in the project initiated in 2022.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2023**: The development of the hydrogen fuel cell Hilux project is expected to create new jobs and upskill existing employees in hydrogen-related technologies, contributing to the skills base within Toyota and its supply chain.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: Toyota Manufacturing UK operates the Deeside facility as part of its fully owned manufacturing operations in the UK, which began in 1992. The facility is dedicated to producing hybrid engines and is part of Toyota's broader strategy for hybrid and hydrogen technologies.
