### Evolutionary Timeline of the Toyota Facility in Derby, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **02-12-2022**: Toyota announced the development of a fuel cell version of its Hilux pick-up truck at its plant in Derbyshire, UK. A pilot production line will be established for this project, which is part of a broader initiative to create a zero-emission solution in the pick-up truck market. The project will utilize second-generation fuel cells from the Toyota Mirai.
- **05-09-2023**: Toyota unveiled the prototype hydrogen fuel cell electric vehicle (FCEV) Hilux, which is expected to have a driving range of over 600 km. This prototype was developed in collaboration with consortium partners and supported by UK government funding.
- **11-06-2024**: The Hilux hydrogen FCEV began real-world testing. Toyota built ten prototypes at its Derby facility, with five deployed for field testing and five for consumer engagement and media demonstrations. The prototypes feature three hydrogen tanks with a total capacity of 7.8 kg, allowing for a range of approximately 600 km.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **02-12-2022**: The project received a total investment of £11.3 million, with £5.6 million from the UK government and £5.7 million from the Advanced Propulsion Centre (APC). This funding is part of a larger £73 million investment by the UK government and APC in various clean technology projects.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **02-12-2022**: Announcement of the project and establishment of a pilot production line at the Derby facility.
- **05-09-2023**: Unveiling of the prototype hydrogen fuel cell electric Hilux at the Derby plant.
- **11-06-2024**: Commencement of real-world testing for the Hilux hydrogen FCEV, marking a significant milestone in the project.

#### 4. Labour
- **02-12-2022**: The project is expected to enhance the skills base within Toyota UK and its supply chain, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned exclusively by Toyota, as indicated by the references to Toyota Motor Manufacturing UK and its consortium partners involved in the project. There is no mention of joint ventures or shared ownership in the articles.
