## GBR-00262-00032

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Altilium Facility in Devon, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2022**: Altilium opened its Electric Vehicle (EV) Battery Recycling Technology Centre in Devon, which utilizes a processing line to gather data for material handling, scalability, and product quality. The facility produces cathode active materials (CAM) from recycled battery materials using the EcoCathode process.
- **2023**: Altilium's tests confirmed that the recycled CAM produced at its ACT1 facility in Devon matches the performance of commercial materials, achieving comparable charge rates and cycle performance to high nickel NMC 811 batteries.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2022**: Altilium received £3 million in funding from the UK government to support its recycling initiatives, including the development of its facilities.
- **2023**: The company completed a six-month feasibility study funded in part by the UK government’s Automotive Transformation Fund, which highlighted the need for 150,000 metric tons of CAM annually by 2030.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2022**: The EV Battery Recycling Technology Centre in Devon was officially opened.
- **2023**: Altilium announced a cooperation contract with Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) to test pouch cells produced from recovered materials at its Devon facility.
- **2024**: Altilium and Connected Energy began developing sustainable business models for reusing and recycling EV batteries, with processing of batteries from Connected Energy starting at the Devon facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **2022**: The funding from the UK government is expected to create 200 full-time jobs as part of the expansion of Altilium's operations, including the facility in Devon.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Altilium Metals owns the facility in Devon exclusively, as there is no mention of joint ventures or partnerships regarding ownership of the facility itself.
