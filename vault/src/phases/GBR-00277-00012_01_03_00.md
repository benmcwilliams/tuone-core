## GBR-00277-00012

### Evolutionary Timeline of AMTE Power Facility in Dundee, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **07-08-2022**: AMTE Power announces Dundee as the preferred site for its battery cell production facility, referred to as a "MegaFactory." The proposed plant at the Michelin Scotland Innovation Parc (MSIP) aims to produce battery cells with a capacity of 0.5 GWh, expected to be operational by the third quarter of 2025. This facility will complement their existing production in Thurso, Caithness.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07-08-2022**: While the financing for the Dundee facility remains uncertain, AMTE Power has discussed potential support with Scottish Enterprise to advance the development. The company is actively seeking funding to support the establishment of the MegaFactory.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **07-08-2022**: AMTE Power names Dundee as the preferred site for the MegaFactory.
- **09-10-2022**: AMTE Power signs an agreement with the UK Battery Industrialisation Centre (UKBIC) to manufacture up to 60,000 ultra-high-power cells annually, with production scheduled to start in January 2023. This agreement is part of their commercialisation plans while they develop the MegaFactory.
- **19-04-2023**: AMTE Power considers moving the proposed factory to the US due to competitive subsidies available there, which raises concerns about the future of the Dundee project.

#### 4. Labour
- **19-04-2023**: The proposed factory in Dundee is expected to create 215 on-site jobs and an additional 800 jobs across the supply chain. However, the potential move to the US raises uncertainty about these job opportunities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **07-08-2022**: The articles do not specify the ownership structure of the Dundee facility, but AMTE Power is described as the primary company behind the proposed MegaFactory, indicating that they aim for full ownership of the facility.

This timeline captures the key developments and challenges faced by AMTE Power in establishing its facility in Dundee, highlighting the technological ambitions, investment needs, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership considerations.
