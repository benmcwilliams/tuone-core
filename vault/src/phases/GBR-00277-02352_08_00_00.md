### Evolutionary Timeline of the MHI Vestas Facility in Dundee, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2022**: Vestas was selected as the preferred supplier for the Inch Cape offshore wind farm, which will feature 72 V236-15.0 MW turbines. The facility is designed to produce a total capacity of 1.1 GW, making it one of the largest offshore projects in Scotland. The Port of Dundee was announced as the pre-assembly site for the project.

- **January 2024**: The project is advancing towards full construction, with the assembly and fit-out of the offshore substation platform and jacket foundation underway. Vestas is responsible for the pre-assembly of the wind farm's components at the Port of Dundee.

- **December 2024**: The specialist pre-assembly and marshalling of the wind farm’s 72 turbines, including nacelles, blades, and towers, will be undertaken by Vestas at the Port of Dundee.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2024**: Forth Ports secured a £50 million private investment for the Port of Leith to enhance marine access and infrastructure for the Inch Cape project. This investment is part of a broader strategy to support offshore wind infrastructure in Scotland.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2022**: Vestas was announced as the preferred turbine supplier for the Inch Cape offshore wind farm, marking a significant milestone in the project's development.

- **January 2024**: The project is moving towards full construction, with the selection of Chinese suppliers for monopile foundations and the commencement of assembly work.

- **November 2023**: Construction began on the assembly and fit-out of the offshore substation platform and jacket foundation.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2024**: The investment at the Port of Leith is expected to create up to 50 new and upskilled green energy jobs to support the Inch Cape project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is part of a joint venture known as Inch Cape Offshore, which is a collaboration between Red Rock Power and ESB. Vestas does not own the facility exclusively but is a key supplier and operator involved in the project.
