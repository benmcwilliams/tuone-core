## GBR-00291-00032

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Altilium Facility in Eastern Europe, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2023**: Altilium Metals announced plans for an EV battery recycling plant in Teesside, northeast England, intending to produce 30,000 metric tons of cathode active materials (CAM) per year from end-of-life batteries and waste from battery factories. The facility will have a processing capacity of approximately 50,000 tons of 'black mass' and will be capable of processing various battery chemistries, including LFP, NMC, and LCO.
- **October 2023**: Altilium confirmed that its facility in Eastern Europe will begin processing battery waste in 2024, with a capacity to recycle battery waste from over 24,000 vehicles annually. The Teesside plant is projected to process waste from over 150,000 EVs per year once operational.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: Altilium received £3 million in funding from the British government to support the development of its recycling plant in Teesside. The funding is part of a broader initiative to enhance the UK's battery recycling capabilities.
- **October 2023**: Altilium entered a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with ABB to explore automation and digital solutions for its recycling plants, including the Eastern Europe facility. This partnership aims to enhance operational efficiency and scalability.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2023**: Altilium announced the approval from the UK Environment Agency to recycle 'black mass' on a large scale and completed a six-month feasibility study funded by the UK government.
- **October 2023**: The facility in Eastern Europe is set to start processing battery waste in 2024, while the Teesside plant is planned to begin operations in 2026.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2023**: The development of the Teesside facility is expected to create 200 full-time jobs as part of the investment and expansion efforts in the UK.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify the ownership structure of the facility in Eastern Europe, but it is implied that Altilium Metals is the owner of the facility, as it is developing and planning its operations independently.
