## GBR-00296-01995

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Tata Chemicals Facility in Ellesmere Port, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 11-01-2024
   - **Details:** Tata Chemicals is set to utilize hydrogen produced at the new blue hydrogen production facility being developed by Essar Energy Transition (EET) Hydrogen at the Stanlow Manufacturing Complex. The facility will consist of two plants (HPP1 and HPP2), with HPP1 having an initial capacity of 350MW and HPP2 targeting 1,000MW. The overall target capacity for the hydrogen hub is over 4,000MW by 2030. The hydrogen produced will enable Tata Chemicals and other local manufacturers to switch from fossil fuels to low carbon energy.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 11-01-2024
   - **Details:** The hydrogen hub is part of the HyNet industrial decarbonisation cluster, which aims to significantly reduce carbon emissions in the North West of England. The project is expected to reduce carbon emissions by 2.5 million tonnes annually.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 11-01-2024
   - **Details:** Plans for the hydrogen production facility have been approved by Cheshire West & Chester Council. Construction for HPP1 is expected to start in 2024, with low carbon hydrogen production anticipated to begin by 2027.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 11-01-2024
   - **Details:** The project is expected to support UK jobs by providing opportunities for local manufacturers, including Tata Chemicals, to decarbonise their processes.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 11-01-2024
   - **Details:** The hydrogen hub is a joint venture between Essar Oil UK and hydrogen specialist Progressive Energy, indicating that Tata Chemicals does not own the facility exclusively but will benefit from the hydrogen produced there. 

This timeline captures the key developments related to the Tata Chemicals facility in Ellesmere Port, focusing on its integration with the new hydrogen production hub.
