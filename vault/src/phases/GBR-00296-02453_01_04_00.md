### Evolutionary Timeline of Stellantis Group Facility in Ellesmere Port, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **06/2021**: Stellantis announced the conversion of the Vauxhall plant in Ellesmere Port into a production site for battery-electric commercial vehicles and passenger car models. The facility will produce models including the Opel Combo-e Cargo, Peugeot e-Partner, and Citroën ë-Berlingo, with production scheduled to start at the end of 2022.
- **09/2023**: Stellantis officially began production at its first all-electric vehicle plant in Ellesmere Port, manufacturing the Combo Electric for Opel/Vauxhall, along with the Peugeot e-Partner, Citroën ë-Berlingo, and Fiat E-Doblò. The plant is set to produce passenger car models from 2024, including the Opel/Vauxhall Combo Life Electric and Peugeot e-Rifter.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **06/2021**: Stellantis announced a £100 million investment (approximately €117 million) to adapt the body shop, modernize final assembly, and set up on-site assembly for battery packs. The investment aims to support the plant's transition to electric vehicle production and achieve CO2-neutral production by the mid-decade.
- **05/2023**: Stellantis confirmed a $126 million investment made in 2021 to convert the plant into an entirely electric manufacturing location.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **06/2021**: Announcement of the facility's conversion to electric vehicle production.
- **12/2022**: Stellantis announced the launch of a new maritime logistics service to facilitate the shipping of production components to Ellesmere Port.
- **09/2023**: Official start of production at the all-electric vehicle plant, marking it as Stellantis' first EV-only manufacturing site globally.

#### 4. Labour
- **06/2021**: Stellantis indicated that the investment would secure thousands of jobs across the region in the supply chain, although specific job creation figures were not provided.
- **09/2023**: While Stellantis did not disclose specific employment figures, the transition to electric vehicle production was emphasized as a means to secure jobs in the automotive sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **06/2021**: Stellantis operates the facility, which is part of its ownership of the Vauxhall, Peugeot, and Citroën brands. The articles do not indicate any joint ventures or shared ownership; thus, it can be inferred that Stellantis has full ownership of the facility.
