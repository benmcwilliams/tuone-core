## GBR-00296-03930

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Essar Facility in Ellesmere Port, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05-11-2020**: The facility at Essar Oil UK's Stanlow refinery in Ellesmere Port is set to develop the UK’s first Low Carbon Hydrogen Plant, with a production capacity of 3 TWh of low carbon hydrogen. The facility will capture over 95% of the carbon used in the process, equating to 600,000 tonnes of CO2 per annum.
- **25-02-2022**: Vertex Hydrogen, a joint venture between Essar Oil UK and Progressive Energy, announces plans for the UK’s largest hydrogen hub at the Stanlow Manufacturing Complex. The plant will utilize technology from Johnson Matthey to achieve over 85% thermal efficiency and ~97% carbon capture.
- **11-01-2024**: Plans for a 1.35GW blue hydrogen production facility are approved, consisting of two plants (HPP1 and HPP2). HPP1 will have a capacity of 350MW, while HPP2 will target 1,000MW, with an overall target capacity of over 4,000MW by 2030.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05-11-2020**: The UK Government awards £7.5m ($12m) to support the development of the Low Carbon Hydrogen Plant at the Stanlow refinery. This funding will also cover the Front-End Engineering Design (FEED) of the plant.
- **25-02-2022**: The FEED of the hydrogen hub is funded by the Government’s BEIS hydrogen supply competition, supporting the development of the facility.
- **11-01-2024**: The hydrogen hub project is described as the largest low carbon hydrogen project in the UK, with plans to significantly reduce carbon emissions in the North West.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05-11-2020**: Announcement of the Low Carbon Hydrogen Plant project at the Stanlow refinery.
- **25-02-2022**: Vertex Hydrogen announces the development of the hydrogen hub as part of the HyNet cluster.
- **11-01-2024**: Cheshire West & Chester Council approves plans for the 1.35GW hydrogen production facility, with construction expected to start in 2024 and hydrogen production anticipated by 2027.

#### 4. Labour
- **11-01-2024**: The hydrogen hub project is expected to create jobs and support UK jobs as it enables local industrial and power generation businesses to transition to low carbon energy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **25-02-2022**: Vertex Hydrogen is identified as a joint venture between Essar Oil UK and Progressive Energy, indicating that Essar does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a collaborative effort for its development.
- **11-01-2024**: The hydrogen hub is described as a joint venture between Essar Oil UK and Progressive Energy, confirming the ongoing joint ownership structure.
