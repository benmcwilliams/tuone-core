### Evolutionary Timeline of the Waste2Tricity Facility in Ellesmere Port, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Technology**: Waste2Tricity (W2T) utilizes DMG technology developed by Powerhouse Energy PLC to convert unrecyclable plastic into low-cost hydrogen and electricity. This process can handle mixed waste plastic in an untreated, unsorted, and contaminated form, producing clean energy with a very low carbon footprint.
- **Production Capacity**: The facility aims to be the first-of-a-kind plastics-to-hydrogen plant in the UK, located at the Protos site near Ellesmere Port.
- **Updates**: The next stage of development will focus on enabling the technology to produce hydrogen for a distributed hydrogen network and syngas for electricity generation.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment**: W2T is in discussions with financial institutions and high-net-worth individuals to fund the development of the plant. They are raising £1 million pre-IPO with plans to become a public company by late 2019 or early 2020.
- **Subsidies**: Specific subsidies are not mentioned, but the company is actively seeking financial backing for the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Planning Application**: A planning application for the development of the plant at Ellesmere Port is expected to be submitted in June 2019.
- **Operational Timeline**: Subject to planning approval, the plant aims to be operational by early 2020.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The articles do not specify the exact number of jobs created or lost, but the establishment of the facility is expected to generate employment opportunities in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: Waste2Tricity is the exclusive developer of the DMG technology in the UK and Southeast Asia, including Japan and South Korea.
