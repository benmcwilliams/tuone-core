## GBR-00296-04370

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Waste2Tricity Limited Facility in Ellesmere Port, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Technology**: The facility utilizes PowerHouse Energy's DMG® (Distributed Modular Gasification) technology to convert unrecyclable plastics into hydrogen. This technology aims to provide a local source of clean and low-cost hydrogen, which can be used to power buses, Heavy Goods Vehicles (HGVs), and eventually hydrogen cars.
- **Production Capacity**: Each facility in the proposed network is expected to generate £500,000 per annum in license fees, indicating a significant operational scale.
- **Updates**: The facility is part of a broader initiative to develop 11 waste plastic to hydrogen facilities across the UK, with planning permission already approved for the first facility at Peel’s Protos site near Ellesmere Port.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment**: The acquisition of Waste2Tricity Limited by PowerHouse Energy Group plc (PHE) is a significant investment aimed at advancing the hydrogen market and addressing plastic waste. The collaboration with Peel L&P Environmental Limited is also a key part of this development strategy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Acquisition Announcement**: On 02-07-2020, PowerHouse Energy announced its conditional agreement to acquire Waste2Tricity Limited, marking a pivotal moment in the facility's evolution.
- **Planning Permission**: Planning permission has been granted for the first facility at the Protos site, indicating progress towards construction and operational readiness.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: While specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the article, the establishment of the facility and the network of 'Plastic Parks' is expected to create employment opportunities in the region, particularly in the context of waste management and hydrogen production.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: Following the acquisition, Waste2Tricity Limited will become part of PowerHouse Energy Group plc, indicating a shift to a joint ownership structure where Waste2Tricity operates under the umbrella of PHE. Key personnel from Waste2Tricity will transition to consultancy roles within PHE, suggesting a collaborative approach moving forward.
