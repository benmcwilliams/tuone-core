## GBR-00355-00412

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Jaguar Land Rover Facility in Gaydon, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2024**: Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) announced plans to generate over 25% of its UK electricity from renewable energy projects, primarily solar. The Gaydon facility is part of this initiative, which aims to produce almost 120MW of renewable energy at peak capacity across three sites, including Gaydon. Planning has been granted for an 18.2MW ground-mounted solar array at the Gaydon headquarters. The facility will utilize a combination of rooftop and ground-mounted solar panels, as well as solar carports, to enhance its self-generated energy capability by 16%.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2024**: The renewable energy projects, including the solar array at Gaydon, are part of JLR's global renewable strategy, which aims to increase self-generated energy to 36.4% of its global consumption by 2030. Specific investment amounts for the Gaydon solar project have not been disclosed, but it is part of a broader commitment to sustainability and energy diversification.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2024**: JLR announced the solar energy project, with all three facilities (including Gaydon) expected to be operational by the end of 2026. The planning for the solar array at Gaydon has already been granted.
- **April 2024**: JLR partnered with Allye Energy to develop a battery energy storage system (BESS) using second-life batteries from Range Rover vehicles, which will be utilized at the Gaydon facility for powering electric vehicle (EV) charging.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2024**: The partnership with Allye Energy and the development of the BESS may lead to job creation related to the installation and maintenance of the new energy systems, although specific job numbers have not been mentioned in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: Jaguar Land Rover owns the Gaydon facility exclusively. There are no indications of joint ventures or shared ownership in the articles provided.
