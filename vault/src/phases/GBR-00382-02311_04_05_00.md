## GBR-00382-02311

### Evolutionary Timeline of the ITM Power Facility in Glasgow, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2020**: Announcement of a 10 MW electrolyzer planned on the outskirts of Glasgow as part of the Green Hydrogen for Scotland initiative, aimed at producing hydrogen for heavy-duty vehicles.
- **April 2021**: Planning application submitted for a 20 MW electrolyzer, which would be the largest in the UK, capable of generating 8 tonnes of green hydrogen per day.
- **November 2021**: The Whitelee green hydrogen project is confirmed to produce between 2.5 to 4 tonnes of green hydrogen per day, enough to fuel 225 buses traveling between Glasgow and Edinburgh.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2021**: The project receives £9.4 million in funding from the UK Government to support the development of the hydrogen storage facility, which will be integrated with the Whitelee Windfarm.
- **November 2021**: An additional £2.25 million in funding is allocated to support the development of hydrogen skills and standards in the UK.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2020**: Initial announcement of the Green Hydrogen for Glasgow project.
- **April 2021**: Submission of planning application for the 20 MW electrolyzer.
- **November 2021**: Confirmation of funding and project details, including the production capacity and operational goals.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2021**: The project is expected to create high-skilled jobs in Glasgow, contributing to the local economy and supporting the UK's transition to a hydrogen economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is developed in partnership between ITM Power, BOC, and ScottishPower, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by any single entity.
