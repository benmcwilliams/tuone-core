## GBR-00382-02438

### Evolutionary Timeline of the ScottishPower Facility in Glasgow, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/04/2021**: ScottishPower announced its Green Hydrogen for Scotland project, targeting the installation of up to 40MW of solar energy and 50MW of battery storage. The facility will include a 20MW electrolyser, which will be the largest in the UK, capable of producing 8 tonnes of green hydrogen per day.
- **22/11/2021**: The Whitelee green hydrogen project aims to produce between 2.5 to 4 tonnes of green hydrogen daily, sufficient to fuel 225 buses traveling between Glasgow and Edinburgh. This facility will utilize renewable energy from the Whitelee Windfarm.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **22/11/2021**: The UK Government awarded £9.4 million in funding to the Whitelee green hydrogen project, which is part of the broader initiative to develop the UK's largest electrolyser. This funding is intended to support the project’s development and the creation of high-skilled jobs.
- **25/11/2021**: An additional £2.25 million was allocated to support the development of hydrogen skills and standards in the UK, enhancing the workforce needed for hydrogen technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/04/2021**: Planning application submitted for the Green Hydrogen for Scotland project.
- **22/11/2021**: Announcement of the £9.4 million funding following the COP26 climate summit, highlighting the project's significance in the UK's energy transition.
- **25/11/2021**: Confirmation of the project’s status as the UK’s largest power-to-hydrogen energy storage project, with ongoing development alongside the Whitelee Windfarm.

#### 4. Labour
- **22/11/2021**: The project is expected to create high-skilled jobs in Glasgow, contributing to the local economy and supporting the decarbonisation of the transport sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is developed in partnership with ITM Power and BOC, alongside ScottishPower’s Hydrogen division. While ScottishPower is a key player in the project, it is not the sole owner, indicating a collaborative ownership structure involving multiple stakeholders.
