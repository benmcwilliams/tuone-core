## GBR-00382-02576

### Evolutionary Timeline of the BOC Facility in Glasgow, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2020**: The facility is part of the Green Hydrogen for Scotland initiative, featuring a planned 10 MW electrolyzer to produce hydrogen for heavy-duty vehicles. This project aims to fuel commercial vehicles with sustainable hydrogen within two years.
- **04/2021**: Scottish Power targets up to 40 MW of solar energy and 50 MW of battery storage to support the hydrogen production, with plans for a 20 MW electrolyzer that would generate 8 tonnes of green hydrogen per day.
- **11/2021**: The facility is confirmed to produce between 2.5 to 4 tonnes of green hydrogen daily, enough to fuel 225 buses traveling between Glasgow and Edinburgh. The electrolyzer will be the largest in the UK.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **11/2021**: The project receives £9.4 million in funding from the UK Government to support the development of the hydrogen facility, emphasizing its role in decarbonizing the transport sector.
- **11/2021**: An additional £2.25 million is allocated to support the development of hydrogen skills and standards in the UK, enhancing the workforce for the hydrogen economy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2020**: Announcement of the Green Hydrogen for Scotland project, with BOC as a key partner.
- **04/2021**: Planning application submitted for the solar and battery storage components, alongside the electrolyzer.
- **11/2021**: Confirmation of funding and project details following the COP26 climate summit, marking a significant milestone in the facility's development.

#### 4. Labour
- **11/2021**: The project is expected to create high-skilled jobs in Glasgow, contributing to the local economy and supporting the transition to a net-zero future.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is developed as a partnership involving BOC, Scottish Power, and ITM Power, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by any single entity.

### Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: The facility is reported to produce "2.5 to 4 tonnes of green hydrogen a day" (Article 3, 22-11-2021) and "enough zero-carbon fuel for 225 buses travelling to and from Glasgow and Edinburgh each day" (Article 5, 25-11-2021).
- **Electrolyzer Size**: The facility will feature the "UK’s largest electrolyser" (Article 4, 22-11-2021) and a "20MW electrolyser" (Article 2, 12-04-2021).

### Investment and Development
- **Funding Amounts**: The project received "almost £10 million in UK Government funding" (Article 3, 22-11-2021) and "£9.4 million" (Article 4, 22-11-2021), with a total of "£10 million" earmarked (Article 5, 25-11-2021).
- **Additional Funding**: An additional "£2.25 million in new government funding" is allocated for developing hydrogen skills and standards (Article 5, 25-11-2021).

### Project Status and Milestones
- **Planning Application**: A planning application for the "20MW electrolyser" was submitted in early 2021 (Article 2, 12-04-2021).
- **Project Announcements**: The project was first announced in September 2020 (Article 1, 18-09-2020) and is expected to commercially supply hydrogen before 2030 (Article 2, 12-04-2021).

### Labour
- **Job Creation**: The project is expected to "yield high-skilled jobs" (Article 3, 22-11-2021) and "create highly-skilled jobs" (Article 4, 22-11-2021).

### Ownership Structure
- **Partnership**: The facility is developed in partnership with "ITM Power and BOC, in conjunction with ScottishPower’s Hydrogen division" (Article 4, 22-11-2021), indicating a joint venture rather than exclusive ownership by any single company.
