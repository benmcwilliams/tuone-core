## GBR-00428-00274

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ford Facility in Halewood, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2021**: Ford announced plans to manufacture electric drive systems at the Halewood transmission plant starting mid-2024. The facility will produce "power units" (electric motors and single-speed gearboxes) for electric cars and commercial vehicles, with all production intended for export.
- **December 2022**: Ford increased its investment in Halewood to boost production capacity from 250,000 to 420,000 units annually. The facility is expected to equip 70% of Ford's electric vehicles sold in Europe by 2026.
- **February 2024**: Ford opened a new EV powertrain test laboratory at its UK headquarters, which will support the development of components for vehicles like the E-Transit Courier and Ford Puma Gen-E. The Halewood plant is integral to Ford's European electrification strategy.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2021**: Ford announced an investment of 273 million euros for the conversion of the Halewood plant, supported by unspecified British government subsidies.
- **December 2022**: Ford added an additional £150 million to the previous investment, bringing the total investment to £380 million to scale production capacity.
- **February 2024**: Ford confirmed a total investment of £380 million in the Halewood facility to support the production of electric vehicle components.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2021**: Announcement of the decision to convert Halewood for electric vehicle component production, with production set to start in mid-2024.
- **December 2022**: Confirmation of increased production capacity and investment, with a target to equip 70% of Ford's electric vehicles in Europe by 2026.
- **February 2024**: Opening of the EV powertrain test laboratory, further supporting the facility's role in Ford's electric vehicle strategy.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2021**: The Halewood plant employed 710 people at the time of the announcement. It was reported that approximately 500 jobs would remain after the transition to electric vehicle component production.
- **December 2022**: Ford stated that 500 staff members were being upskilled in EV component assembly at the facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Ford owns the Halewood facility exclusively, as indicated by the company's direct investments and operational plans for the plant. There is no mention of joint ventures or shared ownership in the articles.
