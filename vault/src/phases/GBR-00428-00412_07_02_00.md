### Evolutionary Timeline of the Jaguar Land Rover Facility in Halewood, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) announced plans to convert the Halewood plant into a purely electric production facility, focusing on the production of the all-electric Range Rover, set to launch in 2025. The plant will utilize the new Electrified Modular Architecture (EMA) platform.
- **March 2024**: JLR confirmed that the Halewood facility will be part of a broader initiative to generate over 25% of its UK electricity from renewable sources, primarily solar, with plans to produce almost 120MW of renewable energy across three sites, including Halewood.
- **September 2024**: JLR announced a £250 million investment to upgrade the Halewood facility, which will feature new electric vehicle (EV) build lines, 750 autonomous robots, and cloud-based plant management systems.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: JLR committed to investing £15 billion over five years in its electric future, which includes the conversion of the Halewood plant.
- **August 2024**: JLR increased its planned investment to £18 billion, with a significant portion allocated to electric mobility, including the Halewood facility.
- **September 2024**: The £250 million investment specifically aimed at transforming the Halewood site for producing medium-sized electric luxury SUVs was announced.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: JLR announced the conversion of the Halewood plant to an all-electric facility, with the first all-electric Range Rover to be produced there, launching in 2025.
- **March 2024**: JLR's renewable energy projects, including solar installations at Halewood, were confirmed to be operational by the end of 2026.
- **September 2024**: The investment for upgrading the Halewood facility was detailed, marking a significant milestone in its transformation.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2023**: The transition to an all-electric production facility is expected to create new jobs related to electric vehicle production, although specific job creation numbers were not disclosed.
- **September 2024**: The investment in upgrading the Halewood facility is anticipated to support job retention and creation in the context of new technologies and production lines.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: Jaguar Land Rover operates the Halewood facility as a wholly-owned subsidiary under the Tata Group, with no indications of joint ventures or shared ownership reported in the articles.
