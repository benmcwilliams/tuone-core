## GBR-00472-00480

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Lotus Facility in Hethel, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2019**: Lotus announces plans to produce a fully electric hypercar, the Evija, with a production target of 130 units. The facility has a theoretical production capacity of 5,000 vehicles per year, which could be expanded to 10,000 with a two-shift operation.
- **October 2020**: Lotus begins working on a Lightweight Electric Vehicle Architecture (LEVA) project, aiming to develop new BEV chassis and powertrain concepts.
- **November 2020**: Lotus confirms plans for an all-electric SUV, codenamed Lambda, set for market launch in 2022, with a range of at least 580 kilometers and an output of 550 kW.
- **April 2021**: Lotus announces that the Emira will be the last internal combustion engine sports car, transitioning to an all-electric lineup by 2028. The Hethel facility is being modernized to support production of up to 10,000 vehicles per year.
- **August 2021**: Lotus reveals plans to release four electric models by 2026, with the first being an electric SUV (Type 132) in 2022. The Evija hypercar and Emira will continue to be produced at Hethel.
- **February 2022**: Lotus plans to produce 10,000 electric sports cars annually at Hethel by 2028, while the majority of production will shift to a new plant in Wuhan, China.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2020**: The LEVA project is funded by the British government through the Advanced Route to Market Demonstrator program, indicating public investment in Lotus's electric vehicle development.
- **April 2021**: Geely announces a £2 billion investment program to support Lotus's transition to electric vehicles and expand production capabilities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2019**: Announcement of the Evija hypercar and plans for future electric models.
- **October 2020**: Launch of the LEVA project.
- **November 2020**: Confirmation of the electric SUV project (Lambda).
- **April 2021**: Announcement of the Emira as the last combustion engine model and plans for a complete transition to electric vehicles by 2028.
- **August 2021**: Announcement of four new electric models to be released by 2026.
- **February 2022**: Plans for 10,000 electric sports cars to be produced at Hethel by 2028.
- **July 2022**: Production of the Eletre SUV begins at the new plant in Wuhan, China, while Hethel continues to focus on sports car production.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2021**: The investment program is expected to triple production capacity, which may lead to job creation at the Hethel facility as it modernizes and expands operations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Since 2017**: Lotus has been majority-owned by Geely, which influences its strategic direction towards electrification and expansion. The Hethel facility remains under Lotus's control, but there are indications of potential production shifts to China for higher-volume models. 

This timeline encapsulates the evolution of the Lotus facility in Hethel, GBR, focusing on its transition towards electric vehicle production and the strategic investments made under Geely's ownership.
