#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2019**: The Royal Holloway University of London is involved in the MIMRee project, which focuses on developing autonomous robotic inspection and repair technologies for offshore wind farms. The project aims to utilize drones, crawling robots, and autonomous vessels to conduct operations and maintenance missions, significantly reducing turbine downtime and operational costs.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2019**: The MIMRee project is funded by Innovate UK with a total investment of £4 million. This funding supports the development of technologies that could save an average of £26 million over the lifetime of offshore wind projects.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2019**: The MIMRee project is officially announced, with a duration of two years. The project will involve collaboration among eight industry and academic partners, including Royal Holloway University of London, which will focus on creating a human-machine interface for data analysis and intervention.

#### 4. Labour
