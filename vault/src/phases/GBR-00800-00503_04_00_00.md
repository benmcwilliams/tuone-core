### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mahle Facility in Northampton, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2021**: Mahle Powertrain (MPT) announced the construction of a new test centre for batteries at its headquarters in Northampton. This facility will include an assembly area for battery modules and three climatic test chambers for complete battery packs, focusing on battery development, validation, and optimization of charging and discharging processes.
- **July 2022**: Mahle Powertrain completed two new test sites in Northampton, which allow for the safe testing of new vehicles, batteries, and hydrogen technologies under extreme simulated conditions. The Vehicle Development Centre (VDC) focuses on battery development and validation, with capabilities to simulate various climatic conditions.
- **November 2023**: The Northampton facility was noted to support the testing and verification of next-generation electric and hydrogen vehicles, with the capacity to test battery packs up to 1 MW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: Mahle announced a total investment of €12 million for the construction of five new test and development facilities, including the one in Northampton.
- **July 2022**: Over the last five years, Mahle Powertrain invested £15 million in the Northampton facility, which included £3.6 million from the UK Government's Local Growth Fund and SEMLEP (South East Midlands Local Enterprise Partnership).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2021**: Announcement of the new battery test centre in Northampton.
- **July 2022**: Official opening of the Vehicle Development Centre in Northampton, which includes advanced testing capabilities for vehicles and batteries.
- **November 2023**: Recognition of the Northampton facility's expanded capabilities for testing battery packs and support for electric and hydrogen vehicle development.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2022**: The facility employs 160 skilled technicians and engineers, highlighting job creation as a result of the investments made in the facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Mahle Powertrain operates as a subsidiary of the Stuttgart-based supplier Mahle. The articles do not indicate any joint ventures or shared ownership; thus, it can be inferred that Mahle Powertrain has full ownership of the Northampton facility.
