### Evolutionary Timeline of the Anesco Facility in Northampton, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2017**: Anesco's facility in Northampton, along with two other solar farms in Chesterfield and Stratford-upon-Avon, was granted permission by Ofgem to receive Renewable Obligation Certificates (ROCs) for electricity supplied to onsite batteries. Each solar farm has a capacity of 5 MW and is supported by a 1.1 MWh battery. This decision allows the facility to stabilize the grid by storing and releasing electricity during peak times.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2017**: The approval from Ofgem is seen as a significant development for Anesco, potentially leading to further investments in battery storage capabilities across its existing solar sites. Anesco has indicated plans to discuss with investors the opportunity to add battery capability to its 101 solar sites.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2017**: The announcement of Ofgem's decision marks a milestone for Anesco, as it is the first instance in the UK where solar+storage plants can retain ROCs for both exported and stored electricity. This could lead to a transformation in the energy storage landscape in the UK.
