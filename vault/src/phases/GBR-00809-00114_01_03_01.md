## GBR-00809-00114

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Britishvolt Facility in Northumberland, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2022**: Britishvolt announced plans to build a gigafactory in Northumberland, with a production capacity of over 300,000 EV battery packs annually. The first phase of production was expected to start in Q4 2023 or early 2024.
- **June 2022**: Britishvolt's gigaplant was projected to have a total capacity of over 38 GWh by the end of the decade, aimed at producing batteries for the automotive industry and contributing to the UK's carbon net zero target by 2050.
- **June 2022**: The company began developing large format 4690 battery cells, with initial prototyping in Germany before scaling up in the UK.
- **February 2023**: Following the acquisition by Recharge Industries, the focus of the facility shifted from producing battery cells for major car manufacturers to batteries for stationary energy storage and high-performance sports cars.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2022**: Britishvolt received backing from the UK government and Glencore, among others, to support the development of the gigafactory.
- **June 2022**: A multi-million-pound contract was extended with WMG, University of Warwick, to assist in battery cell development and optimization.
- **February 2023**: Recharge Industries acquired Britishvolt, saving the planned battery cell factory and indicating a shift in focus for future investments.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2022**: Britishvolt signed a memorandum of understanding with Aston Martin to develop high-performance battery cell technology.
- **June 2022**: Britishvolt announced the extension of its contract with WMG and the development of the gigaplant.
- **February 2023**: Recharge Industries completed the acquisition of Britishvolt, with plans to start work on the facility within 6 to 12 months and to bring products to market by the end of 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2022**: The gigaplant was expected to create around 3,000 direct skilled jobs and over 5,000 jobs in the associated supply chains.
- **February 2023**: Following the acquisition, it was reported that remaining employees would transfer to Recharge Industries, with the potential for up to 8,000 local jobs to be created once the factory is operational.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2022**: Britishvolt was an independent company building the gigafactory.
- **February 2023**: Britishvolt was acquired by Recharge Industries, which is a US-Australian company, indicating a change in ownership structure from independent to being part of a larger conglomerate. The facility will continue to operate under the Britishvolt name but with a new strategic focus.
