### Evolutionary Timeline of the Tesla Facility in Oxford, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2021**: Tesla, in partnership with Wenea, Fastned, and Pivot Power, announced the installation of Europe’s most powerful EV charging hub in Oxford. The facility will feature:
  - 12 Tesla Superchargers with a capacity of 250kW each.
  - 10 Fastned chargers with a capacity of 300kW each, capable of adding 300 miles of range in just 20 minutes.
  - 16 Wenea charging points with a capacity of 7-22kW.
  - A total of 38 fast and ultra-rapid EV chargers at the site.
  - An onsite energy generation capacity of 10MW, connected to the national electricity grid via a 50MW hybrid battery.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2021**: The total investment for the project is £41 million ($57.9 million), with £10 million ($14.1 million) provided by the UK government through UKRI’s Prospering from the Energy Revolution programme.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2021**: Announcement of the project as part of the Energy Superhub Oxford initiative, which aims to accelerate the rollout of EV infrastructure and reduce CO2 emissions by 10,000 tonnes annually starting from 2021. The site is the first of up to 40 similar sites planned across the UK.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2021**: The project is expected to contribute to job creation in the local area, although specific numbers related to job creation or loss were not detailed in the article.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2021**: The facility is a collaborative project involving multiple partners, including Tesla, Wenea, Fastned, and Pivot Power, indicating a joint venture rather than exclusive ownership by Tesla.
