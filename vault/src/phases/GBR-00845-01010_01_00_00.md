## GBR-00845-01010

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Oxford, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2021**: The Energy Superhub Oxford is announced as the most powerful EV charging hub in Europe. It will feature 38 fast and ultra-rapid EV chargers, including:
  - 10 chargers at 300kW (Fastned)
  - 16 charging points at 7-22kW (Wenea)
  - 12 chargers at 250kW (Tesla)
- The facility will have a total energy generation capacity of 10MW, powered by a hybrid battery system (50MW) using lithium-ion and vanadium flow technology. The site is designed to charge up to 14 EVs simultaneously and is part of a larger initiative to support the expected 36 million EVs in the UK by 2040.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2021**: The project has a total investment of £41 million ($57.9 million), with £10 million ($14.1 million) provided by the UK government through UKRI’s Prospering from the Energy Revolution programme. This funding is aimed at accelerating the rollout of EV infrastructure and reducing CO2 emissions.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2021**: Announcement of the Energy Superhub Oxford project, which is part of a broader initiative to create up to 40 similar sites across the UK.
- **August 2021**: The facility is set to coincide with the launch of the UK’s first Zero Emission Zone in Oxford, allowing EVs to access the zone for free.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2021**: The project is expected to contribute to job creation in the local area as part of the broader initiative to develop EV infrastructure and support the green economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2021**: The facility is developed through a partnership involving EDF Renewables (via its subsidiary Pivot Power), Tesla, Fastned, and Wenea. While EDF is a key player in the project, it is not the sole owner, indicating a joint venture structure among the partners involved in the Energy Superhub Oxford.
