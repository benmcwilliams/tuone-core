### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mini Facility in Oxford, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2017**: The Oxford facility is identified as a potential site for the production of the electric Mini, with plans to start production in 2019. The final decision regarding this production is pending.
- **July 2019**: The Mini Cooper SE, the first production electric car from Mini, is launched. The facility is set to manufacture the Cooper SE alongside combustion engine variants on a single production line.
- **January 2022**: The facility begins offering retrofitting services for classic Mini models to convert them into all-electric vehicles as part of the Mini Recharged project. The electric drive system provides up to 90 kW of power and a range of approximately 160 kilometers.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2019**: Significant investments are made to enhance the flexibility of production lines at the Oxford facility to accommodate the manufacturing of electric vehicles, although specific investment amounts are not disclosed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2017**: Announcement of the potential for electric Mini production at the Oxford facility, with a decision expected by the end of September 2017.
- **July 2019**: Official launch of the Mini Cooper SE, marking the start of electric vehicle production at the facility.
- **January 2022**: Launch of the Mini Recharged project, allowing for the retrofitting of classic Mini models to electric drive.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2019**: The transition to electric vehicle production is expected to create new job opportunities at the Oxford facility, although specific job creation numbers are not provided.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned exclusively by BMW, as it is part of the Mini brand under the BMW Group. There are no indications of joint ventures or shared ownership related to the Oxford facility.
