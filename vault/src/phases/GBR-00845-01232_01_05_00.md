## GBR-00845-01232

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Pivot Power Facility in Oxford, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2021**: Pivot Power, in partnership with Tesla, Wenea, and Fastned, announced the installation of Europe’s most powerful EV charging hub in Oxford. The facility will feature 38 fast and ultra-rapid EV chargers, powered by a 10MW energy generation capacity. The chargers will include:
  - 10 chargers at 300kW (Fastned)
  - 16 charging points at 7-22kW (Wenea)
  - 12 chargers at 250kW (Tesla)
- The facility is designed to charge 14 EVs simultaneously and is part of the Energy Superhub Oxford initiative, which aims to support the charging infrastructure for an estimated 36 million EVs by 2040.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2021**: The project is part of a £41 million ($57.9 million) investment, with £10 million ($14.1 million) provided by the UK government through UKRI’s Prospering from the Energy Revolution programme. This funding is aimed at accelerating the rollout of EV infrastructure and reducing CO2 emissions.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2021**: Announcement of the project and partnership with various stakeholders to develop the EV charging hub.
- **December 2021**: The facility is part of a broader network of Energy Superhub projects, with the Oxford site being the first of up to 40 planned across the UK.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2021**: The project is expected to create jobs related to the installation and operation of the EV charging infrastructure, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2021**: Pivot Power is a subsidiary of EDF Renewables, indicating that the facility is not owned exclusively by Pivot Power but is part of a larger corporate structure under EDF.
