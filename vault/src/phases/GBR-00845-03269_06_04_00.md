## GBR-00845-03269

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Oxford Photovoltaics Facility in Oxford, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **06-03-2018**: Oxford Photovoltaics (PV) relocated to a new 25,000 sq ft headquarters and R&D facility in Oxford. This site consolidates all UK operations and enhances R&D and commercialization programs, including a pilot line in Brandenburg, Germany. The facility focuses on developing perovskite photovoltaic solutions, which aim to improve the efficiency and economics of silicon photovoltaic technology.

- **07-01-2019**: Oxford PV achieved a certified efficiency record of 28% for its 1 cm² perovskite-silicon tandem cell, indicating significant advancements in their technology. The company is focused on pushing the efficiency of their solar cells beyond 30%.

- **11-09-2020**: Oxford PV is targeting commercial production of perovskite-silicon tandem cells by mid-2021, showcasing their commitment to scaling up production and industrial processes.

- **26-05-2023**: The company set a new global record for commercially available solar cells with an efficiency of 28.6%. The focus has shifted towards ramping up production and collaborating with solar module partners for assembly into solar panels.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **26-05-2023**: Oxford PV criticized the UK government for lacking incentives for clean energy investment compared to the US and EU, which could impact future investments and expansions. The company is considering production sites in Germany and the US due to better incentives.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **06-03-2018**: Relocation to a new R&D facility in Oxford.
- **11-09-2020**: Announcement of plans for commercial production of tandem cells by mid-2021.
- **26-05-2023**: Announcement of a global efficiency record for solar cells and ongoing discussions about potential production sites outside the UK.

#### 4. Labour
- **26-05-2023**: The Climate Change Committee (CCC) report indicated that up to 725,000 net jobs could be created in the UK’s renewable energy sector with active reskilling. However, the lack of investment in workforce skills may hinder job creation in clean technologies.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Oxford PV is a spin-out company established by the University of Oxford in 2010. The facility in Oxford is part of their operations, which also includes a production facility near Berlin, Germany. The ownership structure indicates that Oxford PV operates independently but has historical ties to the University of Oxford.
