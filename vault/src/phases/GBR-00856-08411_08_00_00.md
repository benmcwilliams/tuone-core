### Evolutionary Timeline of the Blue Gem Wind Facility in Pembrokeshire, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** March 2023
   - **Details:** Blue Gem Wind, a collaboration between Simply Blue Group and TotalEnergies, has received permission to construct the Erebus floating wind farm. The facility will consist of seven 14MW wind turbines on floating platforms, with a total production capacity of 100MW. This facility is designed to power approximately 93,000 homes. The Erebus project is the first phase of a larger initiative that aims to eventually power four million homes. The facility is expected to begin operations by 2026.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** March 2023
   - **Details:** The Welsh government has granted Section 36 consent, allowing Blue Gem Wind to bid for funding in the UK’s Contracts for Difference (CfD) scheme. This funding mechanism is intended to stabilize revenue for renewable energy projects by offering a fixed price for the electricity produced, which is crucial given the variable nature of electricity prices.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** March 2023
   - **Details:** The Welsh government has granted full planning consent for the Erebus floating wind farm, marking it as the largest floating wind farm in the UK to secure such consent to date. The project is on track to begin operations by 2026.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** March 2023
   - **Details:** While specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the article, the Welsh First Minister emphasized the potential for job creation and green energy benefits for local communities as a result of the Erebus project.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** March 2023
   - **Details:** The Blue Gem Wind facility is a joint venture between Simply Blue Group and TotalEnergies, indicating that it is not under exclusive ownership by a single company.
