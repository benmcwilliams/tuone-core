## GBR-00860-01734

### Evolutionary Timeline of Invinity Energy Systems Facility in Perth, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2020**: Invinity Energy Systems supplied four vanadium flow batteries (VFBs) for a combined solar power and battery storage facility developed by Scottish Water Horizons near Perth’s Waste Water Treatment Works. The facility includes 2,520 solar panels with a combined generating capacity of just over 1MW and the batteries capable of storing up to 0.8MWh of energy. This system allows for approximately 94% of the renewable power generated to be used on-site.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2020**: The project, which includes the solar panels and battery storage, represents a £2 million ($2.6 million) investment by Scottish Water Horizons, marking it as the largest single solar energy investment by the company to date.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2020**: Scottish Water announced the installation of the solar and battery storage facility, which is expected to be operational in 2021. The project was completed by Scottish Water Horizons and included the installation of electric vehicle charging facilities.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2020**: The project is expected to support the creation of green jobs as part of the broader initiative to transition to renewable energy and achieve net-zero carbon emissions by 2040.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2020**: The facility is part of a project developed by Scottish Water Horizons, a subsidiary of Scottish Water, indicating that Invinity Energy Systems does not own the facility exclusively but rather supplies technology (the vanadium flow batteries) for the project.
