## GBR-00860-01922

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Scottish Water Facility in Perth, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2020**: Scottish Water began the installation of its first combined solar power and battery storage facility near Perth’s Waste Water Treatment Works at Sleepless Inch on the River Tay. The project includes 2,520 solar panels with a combined generating capacity of just over 1MW and four vanadium flow batteries (VFBs) capable of storing up to 0.8MWh of energy. The system is designed to provide around 25% of the electricity needed for water treatment, significantly reducing the carbon footprint by approximately 160 tonnes of CO2 annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2020**: The project represents a £2 million ($2.6 million) investment, marking the largest single solar energy investment by Scottish Water Horizons to date. The investment supports the transition to renewable energy and aims to help Scottish Water achieve net-zero carbon emissions by 2040.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2020**: Announcement of the project and commencement of installation activities.
- **2021**: The facility is expected to become operational, although the exact date of production start is not specified in the articles.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2020**: The project is expected to support the creation of green jobs and contribute to a green recovery from COVID-19, as highlighted by the Environment and Climate Change Secretary.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned by Scottish Water, specifically through its commercial subsidiary, Scottish Water Horizons. There is no indication of joint ventures or external ownership in the articles provided.
