### Evolutionary Timeline of the Altilium Facility in Plymouth, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date: 04-12-2024**: Altilium has started processing lithium-ion battery waste at its new ACT 2 recycling plant in Plymouth. The facility spans about 1,700 square meters and can process 300 kg of black mass waste per day, equivalent to one EV car battery. This facility showcases Altilium’s advanced EcoCathode process, which will later be used on an industrial scale in the planned ACT 4 plant in Teesside. The process involves black mass leaching to extract key metals, which are then recycled into high-nickel precursor cathode active materials (P-CAM) and cathode active materials (CAM).

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date: 07-03-2024**: Altilium has secured more than £6 million in backing from UK government innovation awards, including grants from the Faraday Battery Challenge and the Automotive Transformation Fund. This funding is part of a £30-million collaborative project with Nissan, which has received £15 million in grant funding from the Advanced Propulsion Centre (APC).
