## GBR-00876-02393

### Evolutionary Timeline of Oxis Facility in Port Talbot, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12-06-2019**: Oxis Energy announces plans to build a production plant in Port Talbot for manufacturing electrolyte and cathode material for lithium-sulfur (Li-S) cells. The facility aims to produce precursors for Li-S cells on an industrial scale, targeting production capacity to support millions of units from 2020 to 2030.
- **12-05-2020**: Oxis Energy plans to start production in Port Talbot in 2021, initially producing electrolyte and cathode material for 500,000 Li-S cells, with options for future expansion.
- **21-04-2021**: Oxis Energy announces the development of quasi solid-state batteries, with production of chemical composites to occur at the Port Talbot facility. The first generation of these batteries is expected to have an energy density of 450 Wh/kg, with plans to increase to 550 Wh/kg by autumn 2023.
- **22-04-2021**: The facility in Port Talbot is confirmed as the production site for the chemical composition of both Quasi and Solid-State Li-S cells.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12-06-2019**: Oxis Energy receives support from the Welsh Government and the Development Bank of Wales for the commercialization of mass production of lithium-sulfur cells.
- **12-05-2020**: The modernization of the Brazilian plant, which will utilize materials produced in Port Talbot, is set to cost around $50 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12-06-2019**: Announcement of the lease for the Port Talbot site for 15 years.
- **12-05-2020**: Planned start of production in Port Talbot in 2021.
- **21-04-2021**: Oxis Energy aims to deliver the first generation of quasi solid-state Li-S cells by summer 2022, with commissioning of the Brazilian plant expected in 2023.
- **21-05-2021**: Oxis Energy faces bankruptcy, jeopardizing the future of the Port Talbot facility and its operations.

#### 4. Labour
- **21-05-2021**: The company is reported to be on the verge of bankruptcy, leading to the redundancy of the majority of its 60 staff across its sites, including Port Talbot.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **12-06-2019**: Oxis Energy is the sole owner of the facility, having signed a 15-year lease for the site in Port Talbot.
