## GBR-00933-00418

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Johnson Matthey Facility in Royston, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2022**: Johnson Matthey announced the construction of a factory for PEM fuel cell components at its existing Royston site. The facility is expected to have an annual production capacity of **3 GWh**, starting in the **first half of 2024**. The company refers to this facility as a ‘gigafactory’ and indicates potential for future expansion.
- **February 2023**: The planned Royston facility's production capacity is expected to increase from **2 GWh to 5 GWh** with the addition of the new 3 GWh capacity.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2022**: Johnson Matthey announced an investment of **80 million euros** in the Royston plant, supported by the British government through the **Automotive Transformation Fund (ATF)**. This investment is aimed at meeting the growing demand for fuel cell technology in the UK.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2022**: Announcement of the construction of the PEM fuel cell component factory at the Royston site.
- **February 2023**: Confirmation of the production capacity and operational timeline for the Royston facility, with production expected to start in the **first half of 2024**.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2022**: The new hydrogen gigafactory is expected to create **hundreds of jobs** at the Royston facility, contributing to local employment and the hydrogen economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned **exclusively** by Johnson Matthey, as there are no indications of joint ventures or partnerships regarding the ownership of the Royston facility. However, Johnson Matthey has entered into a strategic partnership with Plug Power for supply contracts and a joint plant in the USA, but this does not affect the ownership of the Royston facility.
