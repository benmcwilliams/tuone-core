### Evolutionary Timeline of the Jaguar Land Rover Facility in Solihull, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2019**: Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) announced the introduction of the Modular Longitudinal Architecture (MLA) platform, which will accommodate all-electric drives, plug-in hybrids, and mild hybrids. The facility in Solihull will produce vehicles based on this platform, including a large premium sedan and a large SUV.
- **April 2023**: JLR specified that the Solihull facility will manufacture a new four-door GT Jaguar, built on a proprietary architecture named JEA, with a range of up to 700 km and pricing starting at £100,000. This model is expected to be available for sale in 2024, with deliveries in 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: JLR announced a £15 billion investment over the next five years to support its electric future, which includes launching new electric platforms and preparing plants for component production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2019**: JLR revealed plans for future electric models and the MLA platform, indicating that production would begin at the Solihull plant.
- **April 2023**: JLR confirmed the production of the next-generation medium-size luxury SUV architecture, which will be fully electric, and the four-door GT Jaguar at the Solihull facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2019**: JLR's plans to produce electrified vehicles, including PHEV versions of the Range Rover and Range Rover Sport, at the Solihull plant were expected to create jobs, although specific numbers were not mentioned.
- **April 2023**: The commitment to electric vehicle production and the expansion of facilities may lead to job creation, although specific figures were not provided.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: Jaguar Land Rover is a subsidiary of Tata Motors, indicating that the facility in Solihull is not under exclusive ownership but part of a larger corporate structure.
