## GBR-01055-00055

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Aston Martin Facility in St. Athan, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2016**: Aston Martin announces plans to set up a production site in St. Athan, Wales, to build the luxury sports car DBX, with an all-electric variant planned.
- **September 2018**: The facility is referred to as Aston Martin's "Home of Electrification," with plans to produce the Rapide E (the brand's first electric car) starting in late 2019, and the Lagonda electric car brand by 2021.
- **March 2019**: The Lagonda All-Terrain electric SUV concept is introduced, with production scheduled to begin in St. Athan in 2022.
- **June 2019**: Production of pre-series vehicles for the DBX SUV begins at the St. Athan plant, with plans for the Rapide E to follow.
- **January 2020**: Reports emerge that the production of the Rapide E may be canceled, with the model being kept as a research project instead.
- **March 2021**: Aston Martin announces plans to manufacture all-electric models at its UK plants from 2025, including an electric SUV at St. Athan.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2018**: Aston Martin partners with Williams Advanced Engineering and Unipart to produce batteries for electric vehicles at the St. Athan facility, as part of the UK’s push for low carbon technology.
- **March 2021**: The company confirms that it will rely on hybrid powertrains for electrification until 2025, indicating a shift in investment focus.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2016**: Announcement of the St. Athan facility.
- **September 2018**: Construction is underway, with production expected to start in late 2019.
- **June 2019**: Official start of production at the St. Athan plant for the DBX SUV.
- **March 2021**: Confirmation that the Lagonda brand's electric models are postponed to 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2019**: The workforce at the St. Athan facility grows to over 100 employees, with plans to increase to over 700 by the first quarter of 2020.
- **June 2019**: Aston Martin employs 200 people at the St. Athan plant, with an expectation to add 550 more jobs when production reaches full capacity, along with 3,000 jobs at nearby suppliers.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2021**: Aston Martin is primarily owned by Lawrence Stroll, who led a bailout for the company in early 2020. The facility is fully owned by Aston Martin, with no mention of joint ventures or external ownership.

This timeline captures the key developments and changes at the Aston Martin facility in St. Athan, highlighting its evolution in technology, investment, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure.
