## GBR-01076-00228

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Envision AESC Facility in Sunderland, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **03-08-2018**: Envision Group acquires Nissan's battery subsidiary, Automotive Energy Supply Corporation (AESC), which includes the Sunderland battery factory with an existing capacity of 1.9 GWh.
- **04-11-2020**: Envision AESC operates the Sunderland facility with a capacity of 1.9 GWh and explores plans for a new battery 'gigafactory' adjacent to the Nissan plant.
- **27-05-2021**: Plans for a new battery factory at the Sunderland site are confirmed, with an initial capacity of 6 GWh expected to open in 2024, with potential future expansions to 18-20 GWh.
- **01-07-2021**: Nissan and Envision AESC officially confirm the construction of a new battery factory with an initial capacity of 9 GWh, aiming for 25 GWh by 2030, and potentially up to 35 GWh.
- **15-12-2022**: Envision AESC announces the foundation laying for the new gigafactory, which will have an annual capacity of 12 GWh upon opening in 2025, with future expansion plans to 25 GWh by 2030 and up to 35 GWh.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04-11-2020**: A feasibility study sponsored by the UK government investigates the construction of the new gigafactory.
- **29-05-2021**: Nissan and Envision AESC announce a joint investment of over £1 billion for the new battery plants in the UK and Japan.
- **01-07-2021**: Envision AESC commits to an initial investment of £450 million for the gigafactory, with plans to increase this to £1.8 billion in later phases.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **03-08-2018**: Acquisition of AESC by Envision Group is finalized.
- **27-05-2021**: Announcement of the new battery factory at Sunderland.
- **01-07-2021**: Official confirmation of the new battery factory and its specifications.
- **15-12-2022**: Groundbreaking for the new gigafactory at Sunderland.

#### 4. Labour
- **04-11-2020**: AESC Envision cuts 130 jobs due to the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic.
- **01-07-2021**: The EV36Zero project is expected to create 6,200 jobs, including 750 new jobs at Envision AESC's battery plant.
- **15-12-2022**: Envision AESC anticipates employing up to 4,500 staff at the new facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **03-08-2018**: Envision Group acquires AESC but Nissan retains a 25% minority stake in the new business.
- **27-05-2021**: Envision AESC operates the Sunderland facility under its ownership, with Nissan as a minority stakeholder.
