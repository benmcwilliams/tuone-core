## GBR-01076-00228

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Envision AESC Facility in Sunderland, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **03-08-2018**: Envision Group acquires Nissan's battery subsidiary, Automotive Energy Supply Corporation (AESC), which includes the Sunderland battery factory with an existing capacity of 1.9 GWh.
- **04-11-2020**: Envision AESC operates with a capacity of 1.9 GWh in Sunderland, with plans for a new battery ‘gigafactory’ at the site.
- **27-05-2021**: Plans for a new battery factory at Sunderland are confirmed, with an initial capacity of 6 GWh, potentially increasing to 18-20 GWh.
- **07-07-2021**: Nissan and Envision AESC announce the new battery factory will have an initial capacity of 9 GWh, with plans to expand to 25 GWh by 2030, and potentially up to 35 GWh.
- **17-06-2021**: Envision AESC begins construction of the new battery factory, expected to open in 2024 with an initial capacity of 12 GWh, later expanding to 25 GWh by 2030 and up to 38 GWh.
- **26-01-2024**: The UK Infrastructure Bank grants a £200 million loan to support the construction of the new battery factory, now confirmed to have a capacity of 15.8 GWh.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05-07-2021**: Envision AESC announces an initial investment of £450 million for the new gigafactory, with plans to increase this to £1.8 billion in later phases.
- **17-06-2021**: Nissan announces a £1 billion initiative (EV36Zero) that includes investments in the Sunderland facility and the new battery factory.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **03-08-2018**: Acquisition of AESC by Envision Group finalized.
- **04-11-2020**: Feasibility study for the new gigafactory initiated.
- **27-05-2021**: Confirmation of the new battery factory's construction at Sunderland.
- **07-07-2021**: Official confirmation of the new battery factory's specifications and capacity.
- **17-06-2021**: Construction of the new factory begins.
- **17-03-2023**: Envision AESC lays the foundation for the new gigafactory.
- **26-01-2024**: UK Infrastructure Bank provides a loan to support the factory's construction.

#### 4. Labour
- **07-07-2021**: The EV36Zero project is expected to create 6,200 jobs, including 900 new jobs at Nissan and 750 at Envision AESC.
- **17-03-2023**: The new factory is projected to create 1,000 jobs.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **03-08-2018**: Envision Group acquires AESC but Nissan retains a 20% stake in the company.
- **27-05-2021**: Envision AESC operates the Sunderland facility, with Nissan as a minority stakeholder.
