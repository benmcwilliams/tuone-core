## GBR-01076-00577

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Nissan Facility in Sunderland, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2016**: Nissan invests £26.5 million to modernize its battery assembly plant for the Leaf and e-NV200, aiming to make it the largest in Europe.
- **May 2016**: The Sunderland facility produces its 50,000th Leaf, with production of electric cars and battery assembly starting in 2013.
- **June 2016**: Nissan commissions a solar plant at the Sunderland factory, consisting of 19,000 solar panels with a capacity of 4.75 MW, meeting 7% of the battery plant's energy needs.
- **December 2017**: Production of the 2018 Leaf begins at Sunderland, with the facility set to produce all units for the European market.
- **March 2020**: Production is temporarily halted due to the COVID-19 pandemic.
- **June 2020**: Production resumes with enhanced safety measures after a three-month shutdown.
- **November 2021**: Nissan announces the EV36Zero project, a £1 billion initiative to create an electric vehicle manufacturing ecosystem at Sunderland, including a new battery factory.
- **July 2021**: Nissan confirms that Envision AESC will build a battery factory at Sunderland with an initial capacity of 9 GWh, growing to 25 GWh by 2030.
- **November 2023**: Nissan commits to producing electric versions of the Qashqai and Juke, along with a successor to the Leaf, at Sunderland.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2016**: £26.5 million investment for modernization, with £9.7 million from the UK Advanced Propulsion Centre.
- **November 2021**: Announcement of a £1 billion investment for the EV36Zero project, which includes battery production and electric vehicle manufacturing.
- **November 2023**: Nissan announces plans for a third gigafactory and up to £3 billion investment in Sunderland, including £1.12 billion for immediate operations.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2016**: Investment for modernization announced.
- **May 2016**: Production milestone of 50,000 Leaf achieved.
- **June 2016**: Solar plant commissioned.
- **December 2017**: Production of the 2018 Leaf begins.
- **March 2020**: Temporary production halt due to COVID-19.
- **June 2020**: Resumption of production.
- **November 2021**: Launch of the EV36Zero project.
- **July 2021**: Confirmation of a new battery factory.
- **November 2023**: Confirmation of production for electric Qashqai, Juke, and Leaf successor.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2016**: Investment ensures that approximately 300 jobs remain at the lithium-ion battery plant.
- **November 2021**: The EV36Zero project is expected to create 6,200 jobs, including over 900 new jobs at Nissan and 750 at the new battery plant.
- **November 2023**: The announcement of three new electric models and a third gigafactory is expected to lead to significant job creation.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: Nissan has maintained ownership of the Sunderland facility, with a minority stake in the battery subsidiary AESC, which operates the battery plant on-site. The facility is not a joint venture but is part of Nissan's broader strategy within the Renault-Nissan-Mitsubishi alliance.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic decisions made by Nissan regarding its Sunderland facility, highlighting its evolution as a significant player in the electric vehicle market in Europe.
