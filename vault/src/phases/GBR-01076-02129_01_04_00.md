### Evolutionary Timeline of the AESC Envision Facility in Sunderland, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2012**: AESC opened its first battery cell production facility in Sunderland, with an initial capacity of 1.9 GWh per year.
- **2021**: AESC announced plans to expand the Sunderland facility to a total capacity of 9 GWh, with future plans to increase this to 35 GWh.
- **2022**: Construction began on a second battery factory at the Nissan plant in Sunderland, with an initial annual capacity of around 12 GWh, later updated to nearly 16 GWh.
- **2023**: AESC confirmed that the new battery plant would supply batteries for the next-generation Nissan Leaf, which is set to have 30% more energy density than previous models.
- **2025**: The new battery factory is expected to start operations, supporting the production of electric vehicles including the next-gen Leaf, Qashqai, and Juke.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2024**: The UK Infrastructure Bank granted AESC a loan of £200 million to support the construction of the 15.8 GWh gigafactory in Sunderland.
- **November 2023**: Nissan announced a total investment of up to £2 billion in the UK for the production of three electric models and the new battery factory.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2021**: Nissan secured the future of its Sunderland plant and announced plans to localize battery production for the Leaf e+ in Sunderland.
- **July 2021**: AESC finalized its deal with Nissan to convert the Sunderland plant into a battery factory and announced plans for an EV model to be built there.
- **August 2023**: AESC and Tata Motors were reported to be preparing for a partnership, potentially involving battery cell production in the UK.
- **January 2024**: AESC began construction of the new battery factory at the Nissan plant.
- **March 2025**: Series production of the next-gen Leaf is expected to commence.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2024**: AESC announced that the new battery factory in Sunderland is expected to create approximately 1,000 jobs.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **2018**: AESC was sold to Envision Group by Nissan, which retained a 20% stake in the company. AESC operates the Sunderland facility as a subsidiary of Envision, while Nissan remains a significant stakeholder and customer.
