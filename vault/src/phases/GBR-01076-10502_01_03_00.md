### Evolutionary Timeline of AESC Facility in Sunderland, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2012**: AESC opened its first battery cell production facility in Sunderland, with an initial capacity of 1.9 GWh per year.
- **2021**: AESC announced plans to expand the Sunderland facility to a total capacity of 9 GWh, with future expansion plans to reach 35 GWh.
- **2022**: Construction began on a second battery factory at the Sunderland site, which is expected to have an initial capacity of 12 GWh, later expanded to 15.8 GWh.
- **2023**: AESC confirmed that the new battery plant would supply batteries for the next-generation Nissan Leaf, which is set to have improved energy density and efficiency.
- **2025**: The new battery factory is expected to start operations, with the capacity to power approximately 100,000 EVs.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2024**: The UK Infrastructure Bank granted AESC a £200 million loan to support the construction of the new gigafactory in Sunderland, indicating an expansion of AESC's plans.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2021**: Nissan secured the future of its Sunderland plant and announced plans to localize battery production for the Leaf e+ model in Sunderland.
- **July 2021**: AESC finalized a deal with Nissan to convert the Sunderland plant into a battery factory, with plans for an EV model to be built there.
- **November 2023**: Nissan confirmed plans to produce three electric models in Sunderland, including the next-generation Leaf.
- **August 2024**: Nissan plans to launch test production of the next-gen Leaf at the Sunderland plant.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2024**: AESC expects to create approximately 1,000 jobs at the new battery factory in Sunderland.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **2018**: AESC was sold to Envision Group, but Nissan retains a 20% stake in the company. AESC operates the Sunderland facility as a supplier to Nissan, not as an owner.
