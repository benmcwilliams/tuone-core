### Evolutionary Timeline of the British Solar Renewables Facility in Swindon, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2016**: The facility, a 60.9 MW solar park, was built by British Solar Renewables on a 172-acre site near Swindon, Wiltshire. It is one of the largest photovoltaic plants in the UK and is designed to supply renewable energy to a UK bank under a 15-year power purchase agreement (PPA).

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2016**: The solar park was acquired by Rockfire Capital from developer WELink Energy. The main investors in the solar park include Warrington, Newham, and Thurrock Borough Councils, marking one of the first instances of UK local authorities investing in cleantech bonds. However, specific financial details of the deal were not disclosed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2015**: The Bradenstoke solar PV power plant, which is part of British Solar Renewables' portfolio, was commissioned. It has a capacity of 69.8 MW and is noted as England's largest solar PV power plant. A five-year PPA was signed with Shell Energy Europe Ltd for energy off-take from this facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
