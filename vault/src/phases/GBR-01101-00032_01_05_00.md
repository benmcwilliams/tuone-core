## GBR-01101-00032

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Altilium Facility in Teesside, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Initial Announcement**: Altilium Metals plans to establish a large EV battery recycling facility in Teesside, which will be one of the largest in Europe. The facility aims to process 50,000 tons of 'black mass' and produce 30,000 metric tons of cathode active materials (CAM) annually, meeting around 20% of the UK's CAM demand by 2030. (Article 1)
- **Production Capacity**: The facility is designed to process battery waste from over 150,000 electric vehicles per year. (Article 2)
- **Technology Development**: Altilium's proprietary EcoCathode process is capable of recovering over 95% of critical metals from spent batteries, including lithium, nickel, and cobalt. (Article 12)
- **Recent Advancements**: Altilium has successfully processed lithium iron phosphate (LFP) batteries and high-nickel NMC batteries, demonstrating the ability to recycle various battery chemistries. (Article 9)

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Government Funding**: Altilium received £3 million in funding from the UK government to support the establishment of the Teesside facility. (Article 2)
- **Additional Investments**: SQM Lithium Ventures increased its investment in Altilium by $9.43 million, bringing total investment to $12 million, aimed at accelerating the scale-up of UK and European activities. (Article 8)
- **Collaborative Projects**: Altilium is part of a £30 million collaborative project with Nissan, which includes £15 million in grant funding from the Advanced Propulsion Centre. (Article 7)

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Site Selection**: The area around Middlesbrough was chosen as the preferred site for the recycling facility, with the exact location to be confirmed in early 2023. (Article 2)
- **Feasibility Study**: A six-month feasibility study was completed, funded in part by the UK government’s Automotive Transformation Fund, confirming the need for 150,000 metric tons of CAM annually by 2030. (Article 3)
- **Partnerships**: Altilium signed a non-binding MOU with Marubeni to develop EV battery recycling businesses in the UK. (Article 4)
- **Operational Plans**: The Teesside facility is expected to start operations by 2026, processing battery waste and producing CAM. (Article 6)

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The establishment of the Teesside facility is expected to create 200 full-time jobs, with hundreds more during construction. (Article 2)
- **Collaborative Efforts**: Partnerships with companies like Enva and Connected Energy are anticipated to enhance job creation in the recycling sector. (Articles 10, 11)

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: Altilium Metals is the sole owner of the planned facility in Teesside, with no mention of joint ventures or partnerships affecting ownership. (General context from multiple articles)

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic moves made by Altilium Metals regarding its facility in Teesside, highlighting its commitment to advancing battery recycling technology and contributing to the UK's circular economy for electric vehicle materials.
