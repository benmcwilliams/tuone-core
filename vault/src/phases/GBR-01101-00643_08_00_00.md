## GBR-01101-00643

### Evolutionary Timeline of the RWE Facility in Teesside, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05-11-2020**: RWE is constructing the Triton Knoll offshore wind farm, which will have a maximum installed capacity of 857 MW. The facility will install and operate 90 MHI Vestas V164-9.5 MW turbines, marking a significant technological advancement in offshore wind energy. The project is located over 32 km off the Lincolnshire coast and is designed to power over 800,000 UK homes.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05-11-2020**: A multi-million pound investment was made to transform Able Seaton Port into a specialist assembly and construction facility for the Triton Knoll project. This investment is aimed at establishing wind turbine handling facilities and enhancing local infrastructure to support future offshore wind projects.

- **17-11-2020**: RWE signed a preferred bidder agreement with Prysmian for the supply and installation of HVDC export cables for the 1.4 GW Sofia project, valued at over £200 million. This project is part of RWE's broader investment strategy in offshore wind energy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05-11-2020**: The first turbine blades for the Triton Knoll project arrived at Able Seaton Port, marking a significant milestone in the construction process. The project is set to begin installation early the following year.

- **17-11-2020**: RWE's Sofia project is progressing towards a final investment decision in early 2021, with onshore construction expected to start in the following year.

- **20-04-2023**: RWE announced a partnership with Kellas Midstream to evaluate large-scale green hydrogen production in Teesside, indicating a new phase of development in clean energy technology.

#### 4. Labour
- **05-11-2020**: The transformation of Able Seaton Port and the Triton Knoll project is expected to create job opportunities for local technicians and workers, with a focus on utilizing local suppliers and companies.

- **20-04-2023**: The collaboration with Kellas Midstream is anticipated to create good-quality, well-paid jobs in the cleaner energy sector, contributing to the local economy and the region's goal of becoming a decarbonised industrial cluster by 2040.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **05-11-2020**: The Triton Knoll offshore wind farm is jointly owned by RWE, J-Power, and Kansai Electric Power, with RWE managing the construction and long-term operation and maintenance of the facility on behalf of its partners. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones of the RWE facility in Teesside, highlighting its role in advancing clean technology and renewable energy in the region.
