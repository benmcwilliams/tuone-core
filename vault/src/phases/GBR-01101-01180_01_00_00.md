### Evolutionary Timeline of the Marubeni Facility in Teesside, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **07/2023**: Altilium Metals, in partnership with Marubeni Corporation, is developing an electric vehicle (EV) battery recycling facility in Teesside. The facility is designed to process scrap from over 150,000 EVs annually and aims to produce more than 20% of the UK's planned battery-ready cathode active material (CAM) by 2030. This facility is positioned to be one of the largest battery recycling projects in Europe.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07/2023**: The partnership between Altilium Metals and Marubeni includes plans to secure necessary feedstocks for the recycling process, such as end-of-life batteries and scrap from battery factories. While specific investment amounts are not detailed, the collaboration is expected to enhance the growth and infrastructure development for battery recycling in the UK.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **07/2023**: Altilium Metals and Marubeni signed a non-binding Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) to cooperate on the development of the EV battery recycling facility in Teesside. This marks a significant milestone in the establishment of the facility, with plans to further develop the proprietary technology for producing CAM from recycled batteries.

#### 4. Labour
- **07/2023**: The development of the facility is anticipated to create jobs, although specific numbers are not provided in the articles. The collaboration aims to build infrastructure that supports job creation in the green technology sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **07/2023**: The facility is a result of a partnership between Altilium Metals and Marubeni Corporation, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by either company. Marubeni's involvement is expected to leverage its expertise in battery materials and recycling.
