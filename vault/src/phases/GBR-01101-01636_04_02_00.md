### Evolutionary Timeline of the Equinor Facility in Teesside, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2022**: Equinor's H2H Saltend project is selected to proceed as one of the first large-scale hydrogen projects in the UK. The facility will produce low carbon hydrogen with a capacity of 600 megawatts. It aims to reduce CO2 emissions by nearly one million tonnes annually and is part of the Zero Carbon Humber scheme. Additionally, Equinor is planning a second facility with a capacity of 1.2 gigawatts in the Humber region, which, combined with H2H Saltend, could contribute to 18% of the UK Government's target of 10GW hydrogen production capacity by 2030.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: Kellas Midstream and RWE announce a partnership to evaluate large-scale green hydrogen production in Teesside. This initiative is part of the East Coast Cluster, which has received government backing for its carbon capture elements. Kellas is also developing the H2NorthEast blue hydrogen project, which aims to produce over 1GW of hydrogen by 2030.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2022**: The H2H Saltend project is confirmed to be operational by 2026/2027, marking a significant milestone in the UK's hydrogen production efforts.
- **April 2023**: The partnership between Kellas and RWE signifies ongoing developments in hydrogen production in Teesside, with plans for phased development based on customer demand.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2022**: Equinor's projects, including H2H Saltend, are expected to preserve existing jobs and create new ones, contributing to local supply chain opportunities in the Teesside area.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2022**: Equinor is involved in the Net Zero Teesside Power project in partnership with bp, indicating a joint venture structure for this specific facility. However, the H2H Saltend project is primarily led by Equinor, suggesting a more dominant ownership role in that specific initiative.
