## GBR-01101-01663

### Evolutionary Timeline of the GE Facility in Teesside, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10-03-2021**: GE Renewable Energy announces plans to build a new offshore blade manufacturing plant in Teesside, which will produce blades for the Haliade-X units. The facility is expected to have a production capacity that supports the Dogger Bank offshore wind farm.
- **31-05-2021**: Plans for the facility are submitted, detailing a 78,000 square meter site with a new 1km heavy lift quay. The first blade is expected to roll off the production line in 2023.
- **07-09-2021**: GE secures planning approval for the Teesside plant, which will produce 107-meter long offshore wind turbine blades. Recruitment for the plant is scheduled to begin in mid-2022.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10-03-2021**: The UK government designates Teesside as a free port and awards up to £20 million in funding to the Teesworks project, which is crucial for GE's decision to invest in the facility.
- **31-05-2021**: The construction of the facility is set to begin in October 2021, with an investment that is expected to create up to 2,250 jobs during construction.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **10-03-2021**: Announcement of the facility's development.
- **31-05-2021**: Submission of plans for the facility.
- **07-09-2021**: Planning approval secured for the facility.
- **01-06-2021**: Ground remediation and clearance works are underway, with construction expected to start in October 2021.
- **09-07-2022**: GE announces the cancellation of the blade factory project due to a lack of offshore wind orders.

#### 4. Labour
- **10-03-2021**: The facility is expected to create approximately 750 direct jobs and 1,500 indirect jobs, totaling around 3,000 jobs in the region.
- **31-05-2021**: Up to 2,250 jobs are anticipated to be created during the construction phase of the facility.
- **09-07-2022**: The cancellation of the facility means the anticipated jobs will not materialize.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **10-03-2021**: GE Renewable Energy is the primary company behind the facility, with LM Wind Power (a GE Renewable Energy business) set to operate the plant. There is no indication of joint ownership or partnerships mentioned in the articles.

This timeline captures the key developments and changes regarding the GE facility in Teesside, GBR, from its announcement to its eventual cancellation.
