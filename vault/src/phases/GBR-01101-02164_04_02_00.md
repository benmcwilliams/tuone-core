## GBR-01101-02164

### Evolutionary Timeline of BP's Hydrogen Facility in Teesside, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2021**: BP announced plans to build H2Teesside, aiming for a production capacity of up to 1 gigawatt (GW) of blue hydrogen, which would contribute to 20% of the UK’s hydrogen target of 5 GW by 2030. The facility will capture up to 2 million tonnes of CO2 annually and is linked to the Net Zero Teesside (NZT) project.
- **June 2021**: BP confirmed that H2Teesside would be integrated with the Net Zero Teesside and North Endurance Partnership carbon capture and storage (CCS) projects.
- **September 2021**: BP reiterated its target for H2Teesside to produce 1 GW of hydrogen by 2030, with production expected to start in 2027.
- **March 2022**: BP announced plans for HyGreen Teesside, a new hydrogen production facility that could deliver up to 500 tonnes of hydrogen production by 2030, with an initial phase of 60 MW expected to start by 2025.
- **May 2022**: BP and its partners advanced to the design phase for H2Teesside, confirming it as the largest blue hydrogen project in the UK.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: BP began a feasibility study for H2Teesside, exploring technologies for carbon capture.
- **June 2021**: BP indicated that it would seek to minimize state subsidization for the project while aiming for an 8-10% return on investment.
- **September 2021**: BP, ADNOC, and Masdar announced plans to develop 2 GW of low-carbon hydrogen across hubs in the UK and UAE, which includes H2Teesside.
- **March 2023**: BP's Net Zero Teesside Power project was greenlit by the UK government, with a final investment decision expected in 2024.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2021**: Announcement of H2Teesside project.
- **June 2021**: Confirmation of integration with CCS projects.
- **September 2021**: Design phase initiated for H2Teesside.
- **March 2022**: Announcement of HyGreen Teesside project.
- **March 2023**: Greenlight for Net Zero Teesside Power project.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2021**: BP's hydrogen projects are expected to support thousands of jobs in the Teesside area, contributing to local economic regeneration.
- **July 2021**: Tees Valley Mayor Ben Houchen stated that the hydrogen projects would create good-quality, well-paid jobs for local people.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2021**: BP is the primary operator of the H2Teesside facility, with partnerships established with Northern Gas Networks and other industrial players for hydrogen supply and infrastructure development.
- **September 2021**: BP's collaboration with ADNOC and Masdar indicates a joint venture approach for the development of hydrogen projects, including H2Teesside.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic moves made by BP regarding its hydrogen facility in Teesside, highlighting its technological ambitions, investment strategies, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure.
