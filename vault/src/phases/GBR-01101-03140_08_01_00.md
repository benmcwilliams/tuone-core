## GBR-01101-03140

### Evolutionary Timeline of the LM Wind Power Facility in Teesside, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2021**: GE Renewable Energy submitted plans for a new offshore turbine manufacturing facility in Teesside, covering 78,000 square meters. The facility will include a new 1km heavy lift quay and is expected to produce blades for offshore wind turbines, specifically serving the Dogger Bank Wind Farm.
- **Expected Production Start**: The first blade is anticipated to roll off the production line in **2023**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2021**: The facility's development is supported by the designation of Teesside as the UK’s largest Freeport, which is expected to attract significant investment. The construction phase is set to begin in **October 2021**.
- **Investment in Equipment**: A £1 million investment was made for a 95-tonne excavator to aid in land preparation.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2021**: Plans for the facility were officially submitted, marking a significant milestone in the project.
- **October 2021**: Construction is scheduled to commence.
- **2023**: The facility is expected to begin production with the first blade.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2021**: The construction of the facility is projected to create up to **2,250 jobs**. Once operational, it is estimated to generate **750 direct highly skilled jobs** and nearly **1,500 indirect jobs** related to the supply chain.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned by **GE Renewable Energy**, which operates through its subsidiary **LM Wind Power**. There is no indication of joint ownership or partnerships mentioned in the articles, suggesting that GE Renewable Energy has full ownership of the facility.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and projections for the LM Wind Power facility in Teesside, highlighting its role in the offshore wind energy sector and its anticipated impact on local employment and the economy.
