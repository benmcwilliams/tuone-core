### Evolutionary Timeline of Aker Solutions Facility in Teesside, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2023**: The facility is part of the Net Zero Teesside Power (NZT Power) project, which is a commercial-scale gas-fired power station with an 860MW combined-cycle power plant. It integrates carbon capture, utilization, and storage (CCUS) technology, designed to capture 95% of its emissions, equating to around two million tonnes of CO2 annually. The project aims to support the UK government's goal of fully decarbonizing its power system by 2035.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: The UK government greenlit the NZT Power project, with a final investment decision expected in 2024. The project is anticipated to provide an annual gross benefit of up to £450 million ($557 million) for the region. The Northern Endurance Partnership is developing the necessary transportation and storage infrastructure for the captured CO2.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2023**: The NZT Power project was officially approved by the UK government.
- **Expected Completion**: The facility is projected to be completed by around 2027.

#### 4. Labour
- The project is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers have not been detailed in the available information. The overall economic benefit of the project is highlighted, indicating a positive impact on local employment.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is part of a partnership approach involving multiple companies. BP operates the NZT Power project, while Aker Solutions (Aker Carbon Capture) is involved in the carbon capture technology. The project also includes collaboration with Siemens Energy and Doosan Babcock for construction and integration of technologies. This indicates a joint venture rather than exclusive ownership by Aker Solutions.
