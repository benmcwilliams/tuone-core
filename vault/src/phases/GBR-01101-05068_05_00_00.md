#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date**: 11-12-2024
  - The facility is part of the Net Zero Teesside Power project (NZT Power), which is a pioneering gas-fired power station that incorporates carbon capture and storage (CCUS) technology.
  - The plant is expected to produce up to **742 MW** of low-carbon electricity, sufficient to power **one million UK homes**.
  - The project aims to capture up to **2 million tonnes of CO₂** annually.

  - The consortium, which includes Technip Energies, GE Vernova, and Balfour Beatty, is set to deliver a combined cycle plant with an integrated carbon capture system.

#### 4. Labour
