### Kellas Midstream Facility Evolution Timeline - Teesside, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2022**: Kellas Midstream announced plans for a £750 million investment in the H2NorthEast project, a blue hydrogen initiative. The facility aims to produce 1 gigawatt (GW) of low carbon blue hydrogen, starting with 355 megawatts (MW) by 2027 and scaling up to 1GW by 2030. The project will utilize existing infrastructure at the Central Area Transmission System (CATS) terminal in Teesside.
- **April 2023**: Kellas partnered with RWE to evaluate large-scale green hydrogen production in Teesside. This initiative is part of a phased development aligned with customer demand, complementing the existing blue hydrogen project.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2022**: Kellas Midstream committed to a £750 million investment for the H2NorthEast blue hydrogen project. This investment is expected to support the production of low carbon hydrogen and contribute to the UK’s net zero emissions target by 2050.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2022**: The H2NorthEast project was in the concept select stage, with a final investment decision anticipated in 2024. The project was highlighted during a stakeholder engagement session at the Riverside Stadium in Middlesbrough.
- **April 2023**: A memorandum of understanding (MoU) was signed between Kellas and RWE to explore green hydrogen production, indicating ongoing development and collaboration in hydrogen initiatives in the region.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2022**: The H2NorthEast project is expected to create around 100 jobs and allow almost 20 workers to upskill. Additionally, hundreds more jobs are anticipated during the construction phase and across the supply chain, contributing to local employment and economic growth.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2022**: Kellas Midstream is described as owning, managing, and operating critical energy infrastructure, including the CATS terminal in Teesside. The company is involved in the H2NorthEast project, which is part of its broader strategy to expand into hydrogen production. The partnership with RWE indicates collaboration but does not specify joint ownership of the hydrogen production facilities.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic initiatives of the Kellas Midstream facility in Teesside, GBR, focusing on its hydrogen production capabilities and associated impacts on investment, employment, and ownership structure.
