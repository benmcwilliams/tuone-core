### Evolutionary Timeline of the Wilton Universal Group Facility in Teesside, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** October 2021
   - **Details:** Protium announced plans to build a 40 megawatt (MW) green hydrogen project at the Wilton Universal Group’s site in Teesside. The facility will include up to 40MW of electrolysis capability, developed in two phases, along with hydrogen storage. The project aims to support local manufacturers in transitioning from natural gas and diesel energy sources.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** March 2021
   - **Details:** The UK government announced a £3 million investment to develop Tees Valley as the UK’s first hydrogen transport hub, which is expected to create up to 5,000 new jobs. This investment is part of the broader initiative to support the green hydrogen project at the Wilton Universal Group facility.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** October 2021
   - **Details:** Protium's green hydrogen production facility is due for completion in 2026, subject to planning and engineering works. The project is positioned to be the largest green hydrogen facility in the North East of England.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** October 2021
   - **Details:** The construction of the hydrogen production facility is expected to create jobs during the construction phase, as well as sustained employment during its operation.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** October 2021
   - **Details:** The facility is being developed by Protium at the Wilton Universal Group’s site, indicating that Wilton Universal Group is the owner of the site where the facility will be built, while Protium is responsible for the development of the hydrogen project.
