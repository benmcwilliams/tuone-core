### Evolutionary Timeline of AMTE Power Facility in Thurso, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2020**: AMTE Power's facility in Thurso, Scotland, is noted for producing specialized lithium-ion battery cells in small series, with a planned production capacity of 1 to 5 GWh per year by 2023.
- **February 2021**: AMTE Power claims its Thurso facility has the second-largest cell manufacturing capacity in the UK and is exploring potential for upscaling.
- **June 2021**: The facility is involved in Project ULTRA, preparing two of its battery cells for large-scale manufacture and testing with automotive manufacturers.
- **August 2022**: AMTE Power confirms ongoing development of its "Ultra High Power" cells, which are undergoing scale-up and testing trials at the UK Battery Industrialisation Centre (UKBIC).
- **October 2022**: AMTE Power signs an agreement with UKBIC to manufacture up to 60,000 "ultra-high-power cells" annually over an initial 24-month period, with production scheduled to start in January 2023.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2021**: AMTE Power announces plans for an AIM listing to raise around £7 million, adding to approximately £9.1 million of equity finance since its establishment in 2013, and £5.8 million awarded from the public sector.
- **June 2021**: AMTE Power leads Project ULTRA, funded with £5 million from the UK Government’s Advanced Propulsion Centre, receiving £2.3 million over three years.
- **October 2022**: The agreement with UKBIC is part of AMTE's strategy to commercialize its products and ramp up production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2020**: Initial plans for the Thurso facility are outlined, with production expected to start in 2023.
- **February 2021**: AMTE Power announces its intention to list on the AIM, indicating growth and future plans.
- **June 2021**: AMTE Power is appointed to lead Project ULTRA, marking a significant milestone in its development.
- **October 2022**: Production of ultra-high-power cells at UKBIC is set to begin in January 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2020**: The Thurso facility is expected to create around 50 jobs initially, with potential for more as production scales up.
- **February 2021**: AMTE Power's expansion plans and AIM listing are expected to support job creation as the company grows.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2020**: AMTE Power is identified as a UK developer and manufacturer of battery cells, indicating full ownership of the Thurso facility. There is no mention of joint ventures or partnerships affecting ownership.
