### Evolutionary Timeline of the LionVolt Facility in Thurso, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations**:
   - **January 2024**: LionVolt, a Dutch solid-state battery start-up, acquired a production line in Thurso, Scotland, to produce battery cells using its proprietary 3D technology. The facility will focus on manufacturing solid-state batteries based on lithium metal, aimed at applications in electric vehicles and aviation.

2. **Investment and Development**:
   - **2021**: LionVolt completed a seed financing round of four million euros, which supports its growth and development initiatives, including the establishment of the Thurso facility.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**:
   - **January 2024**: The conversion of the Thurso site for battery cell production is underway. LionVolt aims to bring the production process to market maturity in Scotland, complementing its pilot line facility at the Brainport Industries Campus (BIC) in Eindhoven.

4. **Labour**:
   - **January 2024**: LionVolt announced that it would be taking on a highly qualified team with years of experience in battery cell production as part of the facility's operations.

5. **Ownership Structure**:
   - **January 2024**: LionVolt is the sole owner of the Thurso facility, having acquired it to enhance its manufacturing capabilities and support its growth ambitions.
