### Evolutionary Timeline of Eurocell Facility in Western Europe, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2022**: Eurocell announced plans to build its first European gigafactory in Western Europe, aiming to start manufacturing battery cells at scale within 12 months. The facility will produce two types of batteries: 
  - **Ultra-Fast Charging (UFC) Battery**: Features a lithium-titanate-oxide anode and a lithium-nickel-manganese-oxide cathode, designed for safety and longevity (over 5,000 cycles).
  - **Ultra-High Capacity (UHC) Battery**: Utilizes a silicone-graphite composite anode, claiming to outperform conventional lithium-ion batteries by 150%.
- **Production Capacity**: The facility is expected to produce more than 40 million battery cells per year by 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2022**: Eurocell announced an initial investment of £600 million (€716 million) in two phases for the gigafactory. The investment is contingent on government support and investment.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2022**: Announcement of the gigafactory project, with plans to begin production by early 2023 for existing customers. The facility's construction will occur simultaneously with the initial production phase.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2022**: The establishment of the facility is projected to create hundreds of direct and indirect jobs, facilitating the transfer of skills from Korean battery experts to the local workforce.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2022**: The articles do not specify the ownership structure of the facility, but it is implied that Eurocell is the primary entity behind the project, seeking potential sites and government support for its establishment.
