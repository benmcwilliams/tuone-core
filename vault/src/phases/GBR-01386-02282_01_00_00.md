#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2020**: Highview Power, in partnership with Carlton Power, announced plans to build a cryogenic energy storage facility in Carrington Village. The facility will utilize liquid air as the storage medium and is expected to provide long-duration energy storage, with a production capacity of 50 MW and a minimum storage capacity of 250 MWh. The facility aims to support grid services and integrate renewable energy.
- **July 2021**: Highview Power confirmed the construction of the 50 MW liquid-air energy-storage (LAES) facility, which will be one of Europe’s largest battery-storage systems upon completion. The facility is designed to provide clean, reliable, and cost-efficient long-duration energy storage primarily from renewable sources.
