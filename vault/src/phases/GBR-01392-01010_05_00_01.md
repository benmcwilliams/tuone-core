## GBR-01392-01010

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Suffolk, GBR (Sizewell C)

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Capacity**: The Sizewell C nuclear power project is designed to generate approximately 3.2GW of electricity, sufficient to power around six million homes.
- **Construction Plans**: The project is planned to be built adjacent to the existing Sizewell B and the decommissioned Sizewell A plants. Construction is expected to begin once the project receives a Nuclear Site Licence and necessary permits.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2022**: The UK Government announced £100m ($134m) in funding support for the Sizewell C project to bolster investor confidence and support development.
- **September 2023**: The UK Government increased funding by an additional £341m ($431m) to prepare the site for construction, procure key components, and expand the workforce. Cumulatively, the government has invested £700m ($868.13m) into the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2022**: Announcement of £100m funding support for Sizewell C.
- **July 2022**: The UK Government granted development consent for the Sizewell C nuclear power plant, marking a significant milestone in the project’s progress.
- **September 2023**: The UK Government began a private investment process for Sizewell C, indicating a move towards securing additional funding and partners for the project.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2022**: The project is expected to create up to 10,000 jobs across the UK, including 1,500 apprenticeships.
- **September 2023**: The development is projected to provide 10,000 jobs nationwide, reinforcing the project's potential economic impact.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The Sizewell C project is a joint venture involving the UK Government, the Sizewell C Company, and EDF, which holds a majority stake and is the lead developer of the project.
