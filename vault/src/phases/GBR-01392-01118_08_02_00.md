### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Suffolk, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2022**: Iberdrola commenced construction of the **1,400 MW East Anglia Three offshore wind farm** located off the coast of Norfolk, UK. This facility is part of the East Anglia Hub macro-complex, which includes East Anglia One North and East Anglia Two. The wind farm will cover an area of up to **305 square kilometres** and will feature more than **100 wind turbines**, each up to **247 metres high**. The facility is expected to supply power for **1.3 million British homes**.
- **June 2024**: The first **monopile foundation** for the East Anglia Three project was produced by Navantia Seanergies. The project will utilize **95 Siemens Gamesa 14+ MW wind turbines** and is expected to start delivering electricity in **2026**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2024**: Iberdrola announced a commitment to **double its UK investment from £12 billion to £24 billion** by 2028, which includes a **£4 billion allocation for the East Anglia Two wind farm**. This investment is part of a broader strategy to enhance renewable energy projects in the UK.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2022**: Construction officially began on the East Anglia Three offshore wind farm.
- **June 2024**: The production of monopiles for the project commenced, marking a significant milestone in the construction process.
- **Expected Start of Electricity Delivery**: The facility is anticipated to start delivering electricity in **2026**.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2022**: The construction of the East Anglia hub is projected to create up to **7,000 jobs** in the region, contributing significantly to local employment in the green energy sector.
