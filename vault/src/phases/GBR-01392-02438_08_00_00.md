### Evolutionary Timeline of the ScottishPower Facility in Suffolk, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/08/2022**: ScottishPower Renewables, a subsidiary of Iberdrola, began construction of the **1.4 GW East Anglia III (EA III)** offshore wind farm, part of the **3.3 GW East Anglia Hub**. The facility will generate enough electricity to power **1.3 million homes**. The project includes the construction of an onshore converter station at Bramford, Suffolk, in collaboration with Siemens Energy and Aker Solutions.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12/08/2022**: The East Anglia Hub project involves a total investment of **€7.7 billion ($7.9 billion)**. The project was awarded a **Contract for Difference (CfD)** in **July 2022** from the UK Government, supporting the UK's target of installing **50 GW of offshore wind capacity by 2030**.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/08/2022**: Construction of the East Anglia III offshore wind farm officially began.
- **11/01/2024**: The first transition piece for the East Anglia III project was transferred for completion, with a total of **95 transition pieces** to be constructed by Windar Renovables. The project is expected to start delivering electricity in **2026**.
