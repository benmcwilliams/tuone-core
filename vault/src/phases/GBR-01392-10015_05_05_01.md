## GBR-01392-10015

### Evolutionary Timeline of Sizewell C Company Facility, Suffolk, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Project Overview**: Sizewell C is planned to feature two EPR (European Pressurized Reactor) nuclear reactors, with a total electricity generation capacity of approximately 3.2 GW, enough to power around six million homes.
- **Engineering Design**: Ongoing development of the plant's engineering design is mentioned, indicating active preparations for the facility.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Funding Announcements**:
  - **August 2023**: The UK government announced an additional funding of £341 million for Sizewell C, building on a previous £870 million investment. This funding is aimed at supporting preparation works, including the construction of onsite training facilities for 1,500 apprenticeships and community investments.
  - **September 2023**: The UK government has invested approximately £700 million into the project to date, with ongoing efforts to secure private investment for further development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Joint Venture Formation**: The facility is a joint venture between the UK Government, Sizewell C Company, and EDF, with EDF as the lead developer.
- **Environmental Permits**: In March 2023, the UK’s Environment Agency granted necessary environmental permits for the plant.
- **Development Consent Order**: A planning application was submitted in May 2020 and granted in July 2022, allowing the project to move forward.
- **Private Investment Process**: In September 2023, the UK Government announced the opening of a private investment round for the project, indicating a new phase in funding and development.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The project is expected to create thousands of jobs, with specific mention of training facilities for 1,500 apprenticeships as part of the preparation works. The overall construction phase is anticipated to provide significant employment opportunities, although specific numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership Changes**: Initially, EDF held an 80% stake in the project, with China General Nuclear (CGN) holding 20%. However, in November 2022, the UK government acquired a 50% stake, leading to CGN exiting the project. The current ownership structure is thus a joint venture between the UK Government, Sizewell C Company, and EDF.
