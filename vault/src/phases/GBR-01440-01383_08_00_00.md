### Evolutionary Timeline of the Wykes Facility at Chelveston Renewable Energy Park, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2020**: Wykes operates a solar photovoltaic (PV) plant with a capacity of 60MW and a wind generation capacity of 26MW using GE’s 2.85MW onshore wind turbines. 
- **August 2020**: Wykes contracts GE Renewable Energy to deliver a 25MW energy storage system, which will be integrated with the solar plant. This will increase the solar capacity by an additional 60MW, bringing the total solar capacity to 120MW and the overall energy generation capacity of the Chelveston Renewable Energy Park to 146MW (120MW solar + 26MW wind).

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2020**: The project involves the installation of GE’s Reservoir Energy Storage technology, marking it as the first DC-coupled battery energy storage system in the UK integrated with a solar plant. This technology is expected to enhance energy output, optimize costs, and improve system reliability.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2020**: Announcement of the contract with GE Renewable Energy for the energy storage system and the planned increase in capacity.
- **September 2020**: Confirmation of the project details, including the integration of the energy storage system with the solar PV plant, and the establishment of the facility as the UK’s first direct-DC-coupled solar deployment.
