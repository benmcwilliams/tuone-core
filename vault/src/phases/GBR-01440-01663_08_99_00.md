### Evolutionary Timeline of the GE Facility in Chelveston Renewable Energy Park, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2020**: GE Renewable Energy secured a contract to deliver a 25MW energy storage system to Wykes’ solar plant at Chelveston Renewable Energy Park. The facility initially had a solar capacity of 60MW and a wind generation capacity of 26MW using GE’s 2.85MW onshore wind turbines. 
- **August 2020**: With the installation of GE’s Reservoir Energy Storage technology, the solar capacity will increase by an additional 60MW, bringing the total solar capacity to 120MW and the overall energy generation capacity of the park to 146MW from both wind and solar sources. This project will feature the UK’s first DC-coupled battery energy storage system integrated with a solar plant, allowing for shared power conversion equipment between solar panels and batteries.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2020**: The project is part of a broader initiative by the UK government to incorporate energy storage systems into renewable energy sources, aligning with efforts to reduce greenhouse gas emissions by 2050. Specific investment amounts were not disclosed in the articles.

- **September 2020**: Confirmation of the project details, including the integration of the 25MW energy storage system with the existing solar and wind capacities at Chelveston Renewable Energy Park.
