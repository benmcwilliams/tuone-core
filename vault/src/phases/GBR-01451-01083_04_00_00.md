## GBR-01451-01083

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Gravitricity Facility in Edinburgh, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10/2020**: Gravitricity is developing a £1 million gravity energy storage system, with a 250kW demonstrator featuring a 16-meter high lattice tower. The system uses two 25-ton weights to store and release energy, capable of going from zero to full power in less than a second. The aim is to confirm modeling for a full-scale 4MW project later in 2021, with potential for scaling up to 12,000 tonnes in the future.
- **05/2021**: Plans are revealed to add hydrogen and heat storage capabilities to the gravity energy system. The company aims to use purpose-built shafts as pressurized energy stores for hydrogen, enhancing the system's functionality and economic viability.
- **01/2023**: Gravitricity signs a memorandum of understanding with VSL Systems UK to develop purpose-built underground hydrogen storage solutions, termed FlexiStore. Each store can hold up to 100 tonnes of green hydrogen, with plans for a scale demonstrator in the next two years.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10/2020**: Gravitricity has raised over £1.5 million through crowdfunding and received a £640,000 grant from Innovate UK to support technology development.
- **01/2023**: A £300,000 feasibility study conducted with ARUP confirms the technical and commercial feasibility of storing large amounts of compressed hydrogen in underground lined rock shafts.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **10/2020**: Fabrication of the gravity energy storage system begins, with completion expected in December 2020. Testing is planned for Spring 2021.
- **05/2021**: Gravitricity is currently operating its 250kW demonstrator and is scaling up to commence work on their first 4-8MW scheme later in 2021.
- **01/2023**: Plans for a scale demonstrator of the underground hydrogen storage system are set to be developed in the next two years, with discussions ongoing for pilot project sites.

#### 4. Labour
- **05/2021**: The expansion into hydrogen storage is expected to create additional job opportunities, particularly in the construction and engineering sectors, as Gravitricity plans to build multiple shafts and systems.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify any joint ventures or partnerships regarding ownership of the facility. Gravitricity appears to be operating independently in its developments, although it collaborates with other companies like VSL Systems UK for specific projects.
