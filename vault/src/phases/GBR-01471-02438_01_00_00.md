## GBR-01471-02438

### Evolutionary Timeline of the ScottishPower Facility in Eaglesham Moor, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2008**: The Whitelee Onshore Wind Farm began producing clean, renewable energy, with an initial capacity of 539 megawatts (MW) from 215 wind turbines.
- **2012**: The facility underwent two expansion phases, reaching its current size and installed power.
- **2020**: A 50 megawatt-hour (MWh) battery storage system was announced to be installed at the Whitelee wind farm, aimed at enhancing energy management and participation in the UK’s frequency regulation market. The battery was expected to come online by the end of 2020, with compliance testing scheduled for early 2021.
- **Future Plans**: A green hydrogen plant is planned to be installed at Whitelee, with a capacity to generate 3,000 tonnes of hydrogen per year, supporting emission-free transportation.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2020**: The Scottish government approved plans for the battery storage system in June 2019, which was part of a broader investment strategy by ScottishPower and its parent company, Iberdrola.
- **2023**: The British Government allocated over €11 million (£9.4 million) for the construction of the green hydrogen plant from the energy innovation portfolio of the Department for Business, Energy and Industrial Strategy (BEIS).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2000**: Development of the Whitelee wind farm began.
- **2002**: Permission for construction was requested.
- **2006**: Approval for construction was obtained.
- **January 2008**: The first phase of the wind farm began supplying power to the grid.
- **May 2009**: The Whitelee wind farm was officially opened to the public.
- **End of 2020**: The battery storage system was expected to come online.
- **2023**: Announcement of the green hydrogen project, with government funding secured.

#### 4. Labour
- **2009**: The opening of the Whitelee Visitor Centre, which contributes to local tourism and job creation in the region.
- **Future Plans**: The green hydrogen plant is expected to support jobs related to its construction and operation, particularly in the transportation sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Whitelee Onshore Wind Farm is owned exclusively by ScottishPower Renewables, a subsidiary of the Iberdrola Group.
