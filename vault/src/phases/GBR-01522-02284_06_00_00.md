### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hive Facility (Cleve Hill Solar Park) in Kent, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05/2020**: Hive Energy, in collaboration with Wirsol Energy, is planning the Cleve Hill Solar Park, which will be a subsidy-free solar plant with a production capacity of 350 MW. The facility will include onsite energy storage and will utilize PV panels with an east-west orientation. The project will connect to the nearby Cleve Hill Substation.

- **05/2020**: The Cleve Hill Solar Park is set to become the largest solar power plant in the UK, featuring 880,000 solar panels and occupying 364 hectares (900 acres) of farmland. The facility aims to generate enough electricity to power 91,000 homes and will include unspecified battery storage capabilities.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05/2020**: The Cleve Hill Solar Park is being developed entirely without subsidies, meaning that no taxpayer assistance will be provided to the developers, Hive Energy and Wirsol Energy. The project is expected to generate significant revenue for local councils, estimated at £1 million annually.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05/2020**: Hive Energy and Wirsol Energy are awaiting final approval from the U.K. Department for Business, Energy and Industrial Strategy for the Cleve Hill Solar Park, with an expected decision by the end of May 2020.

- **05/2020**: The project receives final approval from the UK Planning Inspectorate Office, marking a significant milestone after three years of planning. 

#### 4. Labour
- **05/2020**: The project is anticipated to create jobs, although specific numbers related to job creation or loss have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **05/2020**: The Cleve Hill Solar Park is a joint venture between Hive Energy and Wirsol Energy, indicating that the facility is not owned exclusively by either company but rather collaboratively developed.
