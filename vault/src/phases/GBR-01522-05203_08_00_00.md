### Evolutionary Timeline of the HYRO Facility in Kent, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 12-04-2023
   - **Details:** The HYRO facility in Kent, as part of a joint venture with Kimberly-Clark, will produce clean hydrogen using electrolyzers powered by renewable energy sources (wind and solar). The facility will have a combined production capacity of 22.5 MW, aimed at powering Kimberly-Clark's factories and significantly reducing carbon emissions.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 12-04-2023
   - **Details:** HYRO aims to invest £3 billion ($3.7 billion) into green hydrogen plants across the UK, which includes the modifications at the Kent facility to facilitate the transition from gas to clean hydrogen.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 12-04-2023
   - **Details:** The partnership between HYRO and Kimberly-Clark was announced, marking the beginning of the project to switch power supply from gas to clean hydrogen at the Kent facility. The installation of electrolyzers and hydrogen-ready boilers is part of this initiative.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 12-04-2023
   - **Details:** The partnership is expected to create jobs as the transition to clean fuels is implemented, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the article.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 12-04-2023
   - **Details:** The HYRO facility operates as a joint venture between Octopus Energy Generation and RES, indicating that it is not under exclusive ownership by a single company.
