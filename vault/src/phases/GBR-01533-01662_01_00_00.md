### G2 Energy Facility Timeline - Bristol, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The G2 Energy facility is involved in the construction of a battery energy storage project known as the Feeder Road battery in Bristol. 
   - The facility has a production capacity of 20MW.
   - The site was originally intended for a diesel-fuelled STOR plant in 2020, but plans were changed due to community opposition, leading to the current battery storage technology.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - Thrive Renewables has invested £29 million in clean energy infrastructure in the Bristol area, which includes the Feeder Road battery project.
   - An additional investment of approximately £100,000 is planned for upgrades to enhance the site's biodiversity.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - The Feeder Road battery project was acquired by Thrive Renewables in March 2021.
   - As of March 2023, the facility is currently being commissioned, with construction completed prior to this date.

4. **Labour:**
   - The project aims to create new green jobs as part of the community ownership initiative, although specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the article.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is not exclusively owned by G2 Energy; it is part of a collaborative effort involving Thrive Renewables, G2 Energy (EPC), and other partners. Thrive Renewables is offering local residents the opportunity to co-own the asset, indicating a shared ownership model.
