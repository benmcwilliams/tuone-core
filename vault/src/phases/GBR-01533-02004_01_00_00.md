## GBR-01533-02004

### Evolutionary Timeline of Thrive Renewables Facility in Bristol, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2021**: Thrive Renewables acquired the Feeder Road battery storage site in Bristol, which was originally intended for a diesel-fuelled STOR plant. The planning application for the diesel plant was rejected due to community opposition.
- **March 2023**: The Feeder Road battery energy storage project was completed and commissioned, featuring a production capacity of 20MW and a total energy storage capacity of 30MWh, capable of transferring energy for 1.5 hours.
- **January 2024**: Thrive Renewables officially announced the opening of the Feeder Road facility, marking it as their largest battery energy storage system to date.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: Thrive Renewables invested £29 million in clean energy infrastructure in Bristol, which includes the Feeder Road battery project. This investment also encompasses the 8.2MW Avonmouth wind farm and a £4 million loan to Community Energy for developing a community-owned onshore wind turbine.
- **March 2023**: An additional investment of approximately £100,000 was allocated for landscape upgrades to enhance the site's biodiversity.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2021**: Acquisition of the Feeder Road site.
- **March 2023**: Completion and commissioning of the Feeder Road battery energy storage project.
- **January 2024**: Official announcement of the facility's opening and operational status.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2023**: The project is expected to create new green jobs as part of Thrive's commitment to local community involvement and the clean energy transition.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2023**: Thrive Renewables is the owner of the Feeder Road facility and has offered a 20% co-investment opportunity to the local Bristol Energy Cooperative, making it the first commercial battery owner to provide shared ownership to the community. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones of the Thrive Renewables facility in Bristol, highlighting its technological advancements, investments, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
