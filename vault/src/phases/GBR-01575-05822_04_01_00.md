## GBR-01575-05822

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Port of Tilbury Facility, Tilbury, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **08/2023**: The Port of Tilbury, in collaboration with RWE and Mitsui, is developing a green hydrogen project. This includes a small-scale 'proof of concept' demonstrator project aimed at producing green hydrogen to decarbonise port equipment by transitioning from fossil fuels. An initial study for a 10 megawatt green hydrogen plant is also underway, with plans to scale up to over 100 megawatts over a ten-year period. The hydrogen produced will be utilized for port operations and supplied to surrounding industries.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **08/2023**: The project is part of a memorandum of understanding (MoU) between RWE, Mitsui, and the Port of Tilbury, indicating a commitment to invest in green hydrogen technology as part of the UK’s strategy to achieve Net Zero by 2050.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **08/2023**: Announcement of the MoU for the development of two green hydrogen projects at the Port of Tilbury, marking the initiation of the project and the transition of the site from a coal-fired power station to a green hydrogen production facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **08/2023**: The project is expected to create new job opportunities in the local area, contributing to the development of skilled green jobs as part of the transition to a low-carbon economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **08/2023**: The facility is a collaborative project involving RWE, Mitsui, and the Port of Tilbury, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by any single entity. 

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic initiatives related to the Port of Tilbury facility as of August 2023, focusing on its transition towards green hydrogen production.
