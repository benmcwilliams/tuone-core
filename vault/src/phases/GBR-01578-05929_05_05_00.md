### Evolutionary Timeline of the Rolls-Royce SMR Facility in Sheffield, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2024**: The Rolls-Royce SMR facility will produce prefabricated and pre-tested modules for nuclear power plants. The facility is part of the first phase of the Rolls-Royce SMR Module Development Facility, located within the University of Sheffield's Advanced Manufacturing Research Centre's Factory 2050. The design of the Rolls-Royce SMR is a 470 MWe small pressurized water reactor, intended to provide consistent baseload generation for at least 60 years. Approximately 90% of the SMR modules will be constructed in factory conditions, which aims to reduce on-site assembly activities and project risks, potentially shortening build schedules.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2024**: The initial phase of the facility is valued at GBP 2.7 million (USD 3.4 million), as part of a broader investment package exceeding GBP 15 million aimed at de-risking the SMR program. Additionally, Rolls-Royce SMR has received GBP 210 million in UK government funding as part of Phase 2 of the Low-Cost Nuclear Challenge Project, along with GBP 280 million in private capital to accelerate the SMR design and regulatory process.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2024**: The announcement of the facility's establishment marks a significant milestone for Rolls-Royce SMR. The facility is expected to refine production, testing, and digital manufacturing approaches. The aim is to achieve a final investment decision by 2029, following the completion of the Generic Design Assessment regulatory process.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2024**: While specific job creation numbers are not mentioned, the establishment of the facility and its operations are expected to contribute positively to job creation in the South Yorkshire region, as indicated by the partnership with the University of Sheffield.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2024**: The facility is established as a partnership between Rolls-Royce SMR and the University of Sheffield, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership by Rolls-Royce SMR.
