### AESC Envision Facility Evolution Timeline - Cranfield, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility is involved in the development and advancement of EV battery reuse, recycling, and energy balancing technology. 
   - AESC, in collaboration with Altilium and Nissan, aims to produce high nickel chemistry cathode active materials (CAM) through the upcycling of materials from spent Nissan Leaf batteries and production scrap.
   - The facility will contribute to a circular approach to managing EV batteries, with a target to produce 30,000 MT of battery-ready CAM by 2030, which will meet 20% of the UK's expected needs.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The project is part of a £30-million collaborative initiative, with £15 million awarded in grant funding from the Advanced Propulsion Centre (APC).
   - Altilium has secured over £6 million in backing from UK government innovation awards, including grants from the Faraday Battery Challenge and the Automotive Transformation Fund.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - The collaborative project was announced in March 2024, focusing on EV battery recycling and material upcycling.
   - Altilium is constructing its first mini-commercial plant in Plymouth and has plans for a larger facility in Teesside, which will be one of the largest EV battery recycling facilities in Europe.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
