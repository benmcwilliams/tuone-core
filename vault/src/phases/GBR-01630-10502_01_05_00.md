### Evolutionary Timeline of the AESC Facility in Cranfield, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility is involved in the development and advancement of EV battery reuse, recycling, and energy balancing technology. 
   - AESC, in collaboration with Altilium and Nissan, aims to establish a comprehensive circular approach to managing EV batteries, focusing on upcycling materials from spent Nissan Leaf batteries and production scrap to produce high nickel chemistry cathode active materials (CAM).
   - The facility is expected to produce 30,000 MT of battery-ready CAM, which will meet approximately 20% of the UK's requirements by 2030.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The project is part of a £30-million collaborative initiative, with £15 million awarded in grant funding from the Advanced Propulsion Centre (APC).
   - Altilium has secured over £6 million in backing from UK government innovation awards, including grants from the Faraday Battery Challenge and the Automotive Transformation Fund.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - The project was announced on 07-03-2024, marking the beginning of the collaborative effort between Altilium, Nissan, and AESC.
   - Altilium is currently constructing its first mini-commercial plant in Plymouth, with plans for a larger facility in Teesside, which will be one of the largest EV battery recycling facilities in Europe.
