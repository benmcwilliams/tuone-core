### Evolutionary Timeline of the Alcemi Facility in Coalburn, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2023**: Alcemi received planning permission to develop a 500MW/1,000MWh energy storage facility in Coalburn, Scotland. This facility is designed to support the national transmission system and is one of the largest energy storage projects in Europe.
- **December 2023**: Construction began on the 500MW/1GWh Battery Energy Storage System (BESS) project, named Coalburn 1. The project will occupy 15.8 hectares and is expected to reduce reliance on fossil fuels during peak demand, thereby lowering carbon emissions.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2023**: The project is developed in partnership with Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (CIP) through its Flagship Funds. The planning permission marks a significant step towards securing investment for the project.
- **December 2023**: CIP confirmed a final investment decision (FID) for the Coalburn 1 project, indicating a commitment to proceed with construction and development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2023**: Planning permission granted for the 500MW energy storage facility.
- **December 2023**: Construction officially commenced on the Coalburn 1 project, with a 'Notice To Proceed' issued. The operation of the facility is anticipated to begin in Q4 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2023**: While specific job creation figures were not mentioned, the construction and operation of the facility are expected to create local economic opportunities and jobs, as indicated by the partnership's commitment to engaging with local communities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2023**: The facility is developed as a partnership between Alcemi and Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (CIP). This indicates a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by Alcemi.
