## GBR-01668-08144

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Urenco Facility in Capenhurst, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2009**: Urenco's board approved the construction of the Tails Management Facility (TMF) at Capenhurst, which includes a tails deconversion facility and associated storage and processing facilities.
- **2010**: Regulatory and planning approval was received to build the TMF.
- **2014**: Operations at the TMF were anticipated to begin by the end of 2015, but this was later revised to 2017 and then to 2018 due to construction delays.
- **June 2019**: The TMF officially opened.
- **2023**: Urenco announced plans to develop HALEU (High-Assay Low-Enriched Uranium) enrichment capability at the Capenhurst site, with a target production capacity of 10 tonnes per year by 2031.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2024**: The UK government awarded Urenco £196 million to build the HALEU enrichment facility at Capenhurst.
- **January 2024**: The UK Department for Energy Security and Net Zero announced a £10 million investment to develop skills and sites for advanced nuclear fuels, including £9.5 million specifically for Urenco to develop LEU+ and HALEU enrichment capabilities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2014**: Anticipation of TMF operations beginning by the end of 2015.
- **June 2019**: Official opening of the TMF.
- **May 2024**: Announcement of the new HALEU facility to be built at Capenhurst, with a production target of 10 tonnes per year by 2031.
- **December 2024**: Ongoing development of transport capabilities for HALEU materials, co-funded by the UK government.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2024**: The new HALEU facility is expected to support around 400 jobs at the Capenhurst site.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Urenco is jointly owned by the UK government, the Dutch government, and two German utilities, E.ON S.E. and RWE AG, with each holding a one-third stake in the company.
