## GBR-01706-02580

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Saipem Facility in Fife, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Saipem facility is involved in the engineering, procurement, construction, and installation (EPCI) of steel foundation jackets for the Neart na Gaoithe offshore wind farm. 
   - The wind farm has a production capacity of 450MW, featuring 54 Siemens Gamesa 8MW wind turbine generators.
   - The facility is expected to generate sufficient clean energy to power approximately 375,000 households in the UK and eliminate over 400,000 tonnes of carbon dioxide emissions annually.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The Neart na Gaoithe project was secured by EDF Renewables UK in May 2018 through a competitive bidding process, indicating significant investment in the project.
   - In November 2019, ESB acquired a 50% share in the project, further indicating financial backing and development support.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - Onshore construction of the Neart na Gaoithe facility began in November 2019.
   - Offshore work commenced in August 2020.
   - Saipem completed its work on the installation of the foundation jackets in October 2023.
   - The wind farm is anticipated to be fully commissioned by the summer of 2025, with turbine installation and commissioning expected to be completed in the coming months.

4. **Labour:**
   - The operations and maintenance base currently employs 27 staff, primarily local residents.
   - Over the 25-year lifespan of the wind farm, it is projected that around 50 jobs will be generated at the facility.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is not exclusively owned by Saipem; it is part of the Neart na Gaoithe project, which is jointly owned by EDF Renewables UK and ESB, with EDF holding a 50% stake and ESB holding the other 50%.
