## GBR-01706-08396

### Evolutionary Timeline of the BiFab Facility in Fife, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Focus**: BiFab specializes in the fabrication of jackets for offshore wind farms.
- **Production Capacity**: The facility was lined up to supply eight jackets for the Neart na Gaoithe (NNG) offshore wind farm, which has a capacity of 450MW.
- **Updates**: As of October 2020, BiFab was unable to secure the contract for the NNG project due to the lack of assurances from the Scottish government.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment**: The Scottish government has invested a total of £37.4 million in BiFab through a combination of equity and loan facilities, which resulted in a 32.4% equity stake in the company. Additionally, a loan facility of £15 million was provided to support working capital.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Contract Status**: As of October 2020, BiFab was in discussions with EDF Renewables and Saipem regarding the NNG project but had not secured a firm contract. The Scottish government’s refusal to provide assurances made the contract award challenging.
- **Project Timeline**: The NNG offshore wind farm is scheduled to go online in 2022.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Impact**: The GMB union criticized the Scottish government’s decision, indicating that it jeopardized jobs in the Scottish supply chain for offshore wind. However, specific job creation or loss figures were not provided in the article.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The Scottish government holds a 32.4% equity stake in BiFab, indicating that the facility is not under full ownership by BiFab but rather has a minority shareholder in the form of the Scottish government.
