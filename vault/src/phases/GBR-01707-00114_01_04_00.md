## GBR-01707-00114

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Britishvolt Facility in Cambois, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2022**: Britishvolt announced plans for a gigafactory in Cambois, Northumberland, which will be the UK’s first large-scale battery plant. The facility is expected to produce batteries with a collective capacity of 38GWh annually, sufficient for approximately 300,000 electric vehicle battery packs by 2027.
- **August 2022**: Reports indicated that the gigafactory project was put on "life support" due to funding challenges, but Britishvolt stated that enabling works were progressing ahead of schedule, focusing on design and optimizing construction processes.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2022**: Britishvolt received £100 million in funding from the UK government to support the gigafactory project.
- **June 2022**: The company announced an investment of over £200 million in a new test facility in the West Midlands, which will support the gigafactory's operations and help develop new battery technologies.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2022**: Announcement of the gigafactory in Cambois, with an expected opening in late 2023 or early 2024.
- **August 2022**: Reports of the project being slowed down until February 2023, focusing on securing further funding and addressing power supply infrastructure.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2022**: The West Midlands test facility is expected to create 150 jobs.
- **June 2022**: The same number of jobs (150) is anticipated for the test facility, which will support the gigafactory's operations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify whether Britishvolt owns the facility exclusively or if it is a joint venture. However, it is indicated that Britishvolt is leading the development of the gigafactory and associated facilities, suggesting a strong ownership stake in the project.
