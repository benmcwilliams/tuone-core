### Evolutionary Timeline of the JDR Facility in Cambois, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2022**: JDR received approval from Northumberland County Council to build the first export cable factory in the UK, a £130 million subsea wire manufacturing plant in Cambois. The facility will be capable of end-to-end production of high voltage cables and will feature advanced manufacturing technology, including a 47.5-metre high vertical laying-up machine tower and a 45-metre high catenary continuous vulcanisation tower. The facility will cover 69,000 square meters and is expected to be operational by early 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2022**: The facility is part-funded by a grant from the BEIS Offshore Wind Manufacturing Investment Support (OWMIS) scheme, with additional funding sourced from financial institutions and UK Export Finance through an Export Development Guarantee. The total investment for the facility is £130 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2022**: Northumberland County Council approved the plans for the facility.
- **11/2022**: JDR officially broke ground on the construction of the facility, marking the start of the construction phase. The construction is expected to create 171 local jobs during this phase.
- **Early 2024**: The facility is projected to be completed and operational.

#### 4. Labour
- **09/2022**: Upon completion, the facility is expected to create 207 local jobs and safeguard 270 jobs at JDR’s existing facilities. During the construction phase, 171 local jobs are being provided.
