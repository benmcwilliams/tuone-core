## GBR-01722-02282

### Evolutionary Timeline of Highview Facility in Carrington, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2024**: Highview Power announces the construction of the UK’s first commercial-scale liquid air energy storage (LAES) plant in Carrington, Manchester. The facility will have a storage capacity of **300MWh** and an output of **50MW** per hour for six hours. This facility is set to become one of the largest long-duration energy storage (LDES) plants globally.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2024**: Highview Power secures a **£300 million** investment for the Carrington facility. This funding comes from the UK Infrastructure Bank, Centrica, and a consortium of investors including Rio Tinto, Goldman Sachs, KIRKBI, and Mosaic Capital. Centrica contributes **£70 million** specifically to support the project and accelerate technology deployment across the UK.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2024**: Construction of the Carrington facility is set to commence immediately, with an operational target date of **early 2026**. Highview Power also announces plans for four larger 2.5GWh facilities across the UK, with a total projected investment of **£3 billion**.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2024**: The construction of the Carrington facility is expected to support over **700 jobs** during the construction phase and within the supply chain.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2024**: Highview Power is the primary developer of the Carrington facility, with Centrica acting as a strategic partner and investor. The ownership structure indicates a collaborative investment approach rather than exclusive ownership by Highview Power.
