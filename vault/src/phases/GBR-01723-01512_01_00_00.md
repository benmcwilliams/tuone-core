## GBR-01723-01512

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Centrica Facility in Lincolnshire, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2022**: Centrica announced the conversion of a decommissioned gas-fired power station in Lincolnshire into a battery storage facility. The facility will have a production capacity of 50 megawatts and will store 100 megawatt hours of electric energy. It is designed to provide energy for 11,000 households and will store energy from 43 onshore wind farms in Lincolnshire. Full operations are expected to begin in 2023 and will run for a 25-year period.
- **October 2022**: Centrica initiated a trial project to inject hydrogen into a 49-megawatt gas-peaking plant in Lincolnshire. The project aims to reduce carbon intensity by mixing hydrogen with natural gas, with an initial target of 3% hydrogen in the gas mix, increasing to 20% incrementally. The long-term vision is to transition to 100% hydrogen.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2022**: Funding for the hydrogen injection trial is partially provided by the Net Zero Technology Centre, which is supported by the U.K. and Scottish governments. This indicates a commitment to developing hydrogen technology within the facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2022**: Construction of the battery storage facility commenced.
- **Q3 2023**: The hydrogen injection trial is set to begin.
