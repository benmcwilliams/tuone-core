## GBR-01724-01512

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Centrica Facility in Rough, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **01/03/2021**: Centrica announces plans to convert the disused Rough natural gas storage site to store hydrogen. The conversion requires an investment of approximately £650 million ($908 million). The facility, which closed in 2017 after over three decades of operation, had been plagued by technical issues and was deemed too expensive to repair without government funding. The facility's main function was to stabilize gas prices during winter demand surges.

- **09/06/2022**: Centrica submits an application to reopen Rough as a gas storage facility, assessing its role in supporting the UK's energy independence in the short to medium term. The company also believes that Rough could play a significant role in the hydrogen economy in the long term. The reopening application is made to the North Sea Transition Authority (NSTA).

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **01/03/2021**: The conversion to hydrogen storage is estimated to require an investment of £650 million. 

- **09/06/2022**: Plans to convert Rough for hydrogen storage could cost around £2 billion, highlighting the need for state support for the project to be viable.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **01/03/2021**: Centrica is in talks with the UK government regarding the conversion of Rough to hydrogen storage, indicating a potential future project.

- **09/06/2022**: Centrica applies to reopen Rough, which is the UK’s largest gas storage facility, amid concerns over energy supply for the upcoming winter. The application is part of ongoing discussions with the UK government about the site's future.

- **30/06/2022**: Reports confirm that Centrica has submitted an application to the NSTA to reopen Rough, aligning with new European gas storage regulations aimed at ensuring adequate gas supply for the winter.

#### 4. Labour
- **01/03/2021**: The conversion project is expected to create several thousand jobs during the construction phase, as stated by Centrica's CEO Chris O’Shea.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **01/03/2021**: The articles do not specify any changes in ownership structure, indicating that Centrica retains ownership of the Rough facility. The facility was previously a significant asset for Centrica before its closure in 2017. 

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic shifts regarding the Centrica facility in Rough, GBR, focusing on its transition from a natural gas storage site to a potential hydrogen storage facility.
