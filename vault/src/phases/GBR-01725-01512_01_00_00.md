### Evolutionary Timeline of the Centrica Facility in Aberdeenshire, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2023**: Centrica announced the development of a 65MW two-hour battery storage plant in Abernethy, Scotland, which will be its largest battery asset to date. This facility will store renewable energy generated by offshore wind farms in the North Sea and is designed to discharge energy up to four times a day. The plant is expected to connect to the grid in **2028** and has the capacity to power up to **130,000 homes for one hour**. Additionally, a **30MW battery storage plant** in Aberdeenshire is scheduled to go live in **mid-2024**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2023**: The development of the 65MW battery storage plant is part of Centrica's broader ambition to deliver around **1GW of low-carbon energy assets**. Specific investment amounts for the Aberdeenshire facility have not been disclosed, but the project reflects Centrica's commitment to enhancing energy independence in the UK.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2023**: Centrica secured development rights for the 65MW battery storage project in Abernethy, Scotland. The project is part of a larger initiative to manage grid capacity and is set to connect to the grid in **2028**. The 30MW battery storage plant in Aberdeenshire is scheduled to begin operations in **mid-2024**.
