### Evolutionary Timeline of the Diageo Facility in Highlands, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **May 2021:** Diageo is involved in discussions with ScottishPower and other whisky industry leaders to explore the use of green hydrogen to power their distilleries in the Highlands. This indicates a shift towards utilizing clean energy sources in their operations.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **May 2021:** The Port of Cromarty Firth (PCF) signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with Gen2 Energy to import green hydrogen from Norway. This initiative is part of a broader effort to establish a green hydrogen hub in the Highlands, which could benefit Diageo and other distillers by providing a reliable clean energy source.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **May 2021:** The establishment of a "world-leading" green hydrogen hub on the Cromarty Firth was announced as part of the North of Scotland Hydrogen Programme (NSHP), which includes Diageo as a partner. This marks a significant milestone in the region's transition to clean energy.

4. **Labour:**
   - **May 2021:** The development of the green hydrogen hub is expected to create skilled jobs and business opportunities in Scotland, which would likely include job creation at Diageo's facility as they transition to using green hydrogen.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **May 2021:** Diageo is a partner in the North of Scotland Hydrogen Programme, indicating that while they are involved in the initiative, they do not exclusively own the facility or the hydrogen hub, as it is a collaborative effort with other companies and organizations.

This timeline captures the key developments related to Diageo's facility in the Highlands, focusing on their involvement in the transition to green hydrogen as a clean energy source.
