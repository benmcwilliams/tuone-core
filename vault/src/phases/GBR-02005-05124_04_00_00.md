### Evolutionary Timeline of the Glenmorangie Facility in Highlands, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **May 2021:** Glenmorangie, along with other distillers such as Whyte & Mackay and Diageo, is involved in exploring ways to power their distilleries in the Highlands with green hydrogen. This initiative is part of a broader effort to establish a green hydrogen hub in the Cromarty Firth area.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **May 2021:** The Port of Cromarty Firth (PCF) signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with Norwegian firm Gen2 Energy to import green hydrogen. This partnership is expected to support the establishment of a green hydrogen hub, which will provide security of supply for businesses, including Glenmorangie, aiming to switch to green hydrogen by mid-2023.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **March 2021:** Plans to establish a "world-leading" green hydrogen hub on the Cromarty Firth were announced by the North of Scotland Hydrogen Programme (NSHP), which includes Glenmorangie as a partner. This initiative aims to position the north of Scotland at the center of the green hydrogen revolution.

4. **Labour:**
   - **May 2021:** The establishment of the green hydrogen hub is expected to create skilled jobs and business opportunities in Scotland, benefiting the local economy and workforce.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **May 2021:** Glenmorangie is part of a collaborative effort with other distillers and the Port of Cromarty Firth, indicating that it does not exclusively own the facility but is involved in a partnership within the NSHP framework.

This timeline highlights the Glenmorangie facility's involvement in the emerging green hydrogen sector, showcasing its commitment to sustainable energy solutions and collaboration with other industry players.
