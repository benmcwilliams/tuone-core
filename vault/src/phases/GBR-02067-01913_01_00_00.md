### Evolutionary Timeline of SSE Facility in Ferrybridge, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2023**: SSE Renewables announced the construction of a 150MW/300MWh battery energy storage system (BESS) at the site of the former Ferrybridge coal-fired power plant, which was decommissioned in 2016. This facility will be SSE's second BESS project.
- **August 2023**: Groundbreaking for the Ferrybridge BESS project took place, with the facility expected to supply 300MWh of flexible capacity to the UK’s national grid. The project is designed to operate for two hours at a time.
- **June 2024**: The first batteries arrived at the Ferrybridge site, marking a significant construction milestone. A total of 136 battery units will be installed, and the project is set for completion later in 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2023**: The Ferrybridge BESS project is part of SSE Renewables’ £25bn net zero acceleration programme, indicating significant investment in low-carbon technologies.
- **August 2023**: SSE Renewables is developing multiple battery storage projects, including the Ferrybridge site, as part of its broader strategy to enhance grid stability and support renewable energy integration.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2023**: SSE Renewables reached a final investment decision to build the Ferrybridge BESS project, with construction expected to begin in late May 2023.
- **August 2023**: Groundbreaking for the Ferrybridge BESS project was officially announced.
- **June 2024**: The project reached a construction milestone with the arrival of the first battery units, with installation continuing until late June.

#### 4. Labour
