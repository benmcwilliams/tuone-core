## GBR-02073-02438

### Evolutionary Timeline of the ScottishPower Facility in Whitelee, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2021**: ScottishPower, a subsidiary of Iberdrola, announced plans to build a green hydrogen plant at the Whitelee wind farm, which has a capacity of 539 MW. The facility will feature the UK's largest electrolyzer at 20 MW, a hybrid solar energy system, and a battery storage system with a maximum capacity of 50 MW. The plant aims to produce up to 8 tons of green hydrogen per day.
- **June 2021**: ScottishPower submitted a planning application for the green hydrogen facility, which will be engineered and operated by BOC, with the electrolyzer delivered by ITM Power. The project is part of the "Green Hydrogen for Scotland" initiative, aiming to supply hydrogen to the commercial market before 2023.
- **August 2022**: ScottishPower confirmed that the 20 MW electrolyzer at Whitelee is expected to be operational in 2023, producing up to 8 tons of green hydrogen daily.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2021**: The project is part of a broader initiative to develop green hydrogen production facilities across Scotland, supported by various partnerships.
- **August 2022**: The project secured nearly £10 million in government backing as part of the Net Zero Hydrogen Fund, which aims to support low-carbon hydrogen production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: Planning application submitted for the green hydrogen facility at Whitelee.
- **June 2021**: Planning application for the facility was officially lodged with East Ayrshire Council.
- **August 2022**: The facility is expected to be operational in 2023, with the hydrogen production capacity confirmed.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2021**: The project is expected to create jobs related to the construction and operation of the green hydrogen facility, contributing to local employment and supporting the transition to a zero-emission vehicle fleet in Glasgow.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- ScottishPower, as a subsidiary of Iberdrola, is the primary owner of the facility. The project is developed in partnership with BOC and ITM Power, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones related to the ScottishPower facility in Whitelee, focusing on its technological advancements, investments, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
