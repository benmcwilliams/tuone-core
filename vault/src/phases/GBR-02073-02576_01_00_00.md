### Evolutionary Timeline of the BOC Facility in Whitelee, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **April 2021:** ScottishPower submitted a planning application for the UK's largest electrolyser, a 20MW unit, to be part of a green hydrogen facility near the Whitelee windfarm. The facility will also include a combined solar and battery energy storage scheme with capacities of up to 40MW and 50MW, respectively. The facility aims to produce up to 8 tonnes of green hydrogen per day, sufficient to fuel over 550 buses for daily trips between Glasgow and Edinburgh.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **April 2021:** The project is part of the Green Hydrogen for Scotland partnership, which includes ScottishPower, BOC, and ITM Power. The UK government announced a £3 million investment to develop hydrogen transport hubs, which supports the broader initiative for green hydrogen production.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **April 2021:** The planning application for the green hydrogen facility was submitted, marking a significant step towards the establishment of the facility. A decision on the planning application is expected in autumn 2021.

4. **Labour:**
   - **April 2021:** The facility is expected to support job creation in the region, particularly in the hydrogen mobility and refuelling sectors, as it aims to assist local authorities and industries in achieving a zero emissions vehicle fleet by 2029.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **April 2021:** The facility is part of a partnership (Green Hydrogen for Scotland) involving ScottishPower, BOC, and ITM Power, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by a single company. BOC will engineer and operate the facility, while ScottishPower will provide the renewable energy sources.
