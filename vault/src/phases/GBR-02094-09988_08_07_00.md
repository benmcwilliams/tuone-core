### Evolutionary Timeline of the Severfield Facility in York, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** December 2024
   - **Details:** Severfield, the largest steel fabricator in the UK, has entered into its first contract for an offshore renewable energy project, specifically for Ørsted’s Hornsea 3 wind farm. The facility will produce integral components including Suspended Internal Platforms (SIPs), boat landings, and anode cages, which are essential for the foundations of the wind turbines.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** December 2024
   - **Details:** The project is part of a multi-million pound investment aimed at boosting British manufacturing and supporting the clean energy sector. This investment is expected to enhance the manufacturing capabilities of the facility and contribute to the UK's goal of delivering clean power by 2030.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** December 2024
   - **Details:** Severfield has been selected as a supplier for the Hornsea 3 project, marking a significant milestone as it is the company's first involvement in offshore renewables. The facility will collaborate with Hutchinson Engineering for final assembly at Teesworks Port in Teesside.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** December 2024
   - **Details:** The work on the Hornsea 3 project is expected to support over 300 jobs across Severfield and its partners, reinforcing the facility's role in local job creation within the North of England.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** December 2024
   - **Details:** Severfield operates the facility as a full owner, engaging in partnerships with other companies like JDR Cable Systems and Smulders for specific projects, but retains exclusive ownership of its operations in York.
