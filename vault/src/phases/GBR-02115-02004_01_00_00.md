### Evolutionary Timeline of the Thrive Renewables Facility in Avonmouth, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **January 2024:** Thrive Renewables opened its largest battery energy storage system (BESS) to date, a 30MWh facility located on Feeder Road in Bristol. The facility has an overall capacity of 20MW and can transfer energy for a duration of 1.5 hours. This facility is part of Thrive's broader commitment to renewable energy in Bristol, contributing to the city's transition to renewable electricity.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **January 2024:** Thrive Renewables has invested a total of £29 million across three clean energy projects in Bristol, which includes the BESS and a community-owned onshore wind turbine in Avonmouth. The company aims to double its generation capacity to over 180MW within the next five years.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **March 2023:** Thrive Renewables offered Bristol Energy Cooperative a co-investment of 20% into the BESS project, marking it as the first commercial battery owner to provide shared ownership to the local community.
   - **2020:** The site was originally designated for a diesel-fuelled short-term operating reserve (STOR) plant, but this project was shelved due to a planning appeal from local residents, allowing Thrive to proceed with its renewable energy project.
