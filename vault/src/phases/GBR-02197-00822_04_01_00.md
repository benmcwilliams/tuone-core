## GBR-02197-00822

### Evolutionary Timeline of Vattenfall Facility in Aberdeen, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2018**: Vattenfall officially opened the 93 MW Aberdeen Bay wind farm, also known as the European Offshore Wind Deployment Centre (EOWDC). The facility features eleven MHI Vestas wind turbines, including two 8.8 MW turbines and nine 8.4 MW turbines, installed on suction bucket jacket foundations.
- **May 2022**: Vattenfall announced the Hydrogen Turbine 1 (HT1) project, which aims to develop the world’s first hydrogen-producing offshore wind turbine. The project will integrate an electrolyzer directly onto an existing operational turbine, with a goal of producing enough hydrogen daily to power a hydrogen bus for 24,000 kilometers. The project is expected to commence operations by early 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2018**: Vattenfall and the EU invested €3 million to study the environmental impact of offshore wind farms on marine life as part of the EOWDC project.
- **May 2022**: Vattenfall was awarded £9.3 million in innovation funding from the UK government’s Net Zero Innovation Portfolio to support the HT1 project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2017**: Production and delivery of 21 anchors for the wind turbine foundations were completed by Marketex Offshore Constructions for the Aberdeen Bay wind farm.
- **July 2018**: The Aberdeen Bay wind farm exported its first power.
- **September 2018**: The EOWDC was officially opened.
- **May 2022**: Vattenfall announced the HT1 project, with plans to install hydrogen production equipment on an existing turbine, aiming for first production by 2025.
- **June 2023**: Vattenfall expects to receive final permissions for the HT1 project in Q3 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2018**: The EOWDC is expected to create considerable job opportunities in the region, contributing to the local economy and diversifying the energy-based economy of northeast Scotland.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Vattenfall owns the Aberdeen Bay wind farm (EOWDC) exclusively, as there is no mention of joint ventures or partnerships regarding ownership in the provided articles.
