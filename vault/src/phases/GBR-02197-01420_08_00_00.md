### Evolutionary Timeline of TotalEnergies Facility in Aberdeen, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2021**: TotalEnergies opened its UK Offshore Wind Hub in Aberdeen, Scotland. This facility is part of the company's existing offshore operations and is focused on transitioning staff from oil and gas to offshore wind. The facility is involved in the development of the West of Orkney Windfarm, a proposed 2 GW offshore wind project.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2021**: TotalEnergies, in partnership with Macquarie’s Green Investment Group and Renewable Infrastructure Development Group (RIDG), announced a GBP 140 million initiative to develop the Scottish supply chain and harbour infrastructure related to the West of Orkney Windfarm project. This investment includes GBP 105 million in direct commitments from the partners, with the potential for additional matched funding from third parties.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2021**: The announcement of the West of Orkney Windfarm project was made, with plans to enhance local supply chains and infrastructure. The project is still in the proposal stage, pending selection in the ScotWind leasing round.
- **December 2024**: Delmar Systems was awarded a contract to supply mooring lines for the Culzean floating wind project, which is being developed by TotalEnergies. This project involves a pilot floating wind turbine designed to produce electricity for the nearby Culzean gas platform.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2021**: The establishment of the Offshore Wind Hub is expected to facilitate the transition of staff from oil and gas to offshore wind, indicating potential job creation in the renewable energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2021**: TotalEnergies is indicated to have full ownership of the facility, as it is part of the company's existing offshore operations in Aberdeen. The partnerships formed for specific projects (like the West of Orkney Windfarm) do not imply shared ownership of the facility itself.
