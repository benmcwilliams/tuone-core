## GBR-02197-02132

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Aberdeen City Council Hydrogen Facility in Aberdeen, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **02-03-2021**: Aberdeen City Council (ACC) operates two hydrogen refuelling stations, the only ones in Scotland, which provide both 350 and 750 bar hydrogen. The stations utilize electrolysers to produce clean hydrogen from water, powering a fleet of fuel-cell buses and cars. The TECA conference facility is powered by the largest solid-oxide fuel cell installation in the UK, producing around 1.2 MW of electricity and 1.4 MW of heat from hydrogen sourced from an on-site anaerobic digester.
- **11-06-2021**: ACC launched a new fleet of 15 hydrogen double-decker buses, the world's first, which have already completed over 100,000 miles since their introduction in January 2021. Plans for a hydrogen hub to reduce hydrogen costs to parity with conventional fuels were also approved.
- **05-10-2023**: A joint venture with BP aims to establish a hydrogen hub that will produce enough hydrogen to power 25 buses daily and support a range of private vehicles. The facility will be powered by a solar farm.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **02-03-2021**: The Scottish government committed £100 million over five years to support the hydrogen sector as part of its Hydrogen Action Plan.
- **11-06-2021**: The new fleet of hydrogen buses was funded through a mix of EU, Scottish Government, and ACC funding.
- **05-10-2023**: BP was awarded a £215 million procurement contract to assist in delivering three phases of the hydrogen project with ACC.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2014**: Initial hydrogen project began, leading to the launch of ten EU-backed zero-emissions buses in 2015.
- **January 2020**: The first cohort of hydrogen buses retired after clocking 1.2 million miles.
- **January 2021**: The new fleet of hydrogen double-decker buses began operation.
- **11-06-2021**: ACC approved the creation of a hydrogen hub.
- **25-06-2021**: ACC announced the search for a joint venture partner to build the hydrogen hub.
- **05-10-2023**: Planning applications submitted for the hydrogen hub project in collaboration with BP.

#### 4. Labour
- **02-03-2021**: Local workforce upskilling initiatives were highlighted, with North East Scotland College training technicians in fuel cell vehicles and refuelling stations.
- **11-06-2021**: The hydrogen hub is expected to create skilled jobs, particularly to replace those lost in the oil and gas sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **25-06-2021**: The hydrogen hub is being developed as a joint venture between Aberdeen City Council and BP, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by ACC.
