## GBR-02197-02164

### Evolutionary Timeline of the BP Hydrogen Facility in Aberdeen, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2021**: BP was selected as the preferred bidder to help establish a green hydrogen production hub in Aberdeen, aiming to create Scotland's first scalable facility for green hydrogen production. The facility is expected to produce over 800 kg of green hydrogen per day, sufficient to fuel 25 buses and a similar number of other fleet vehicles.
- **February 2022**: BP received approval to become a joint venture partner with Aberdeen City Council (ACC) for the hydrogen hub, which is planned to be developed in three phases. The first phase involves a £215 million investment to set up the facility.
- **March 2022**: The joint venture, named BP Aberdeen Hydrogen Energy Limited, was officially formed to oversee the development of the hydrogen hub.
- **June 2023**: Planning documents indicated that the facility would be located on a 123-acre site, with a solar farm powering the hydrogen production facility. The production capacity is expected to increase as the project develops.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2021**: The project was announced with an estimated cost of £215 million, with the Scottish Government earmarking £15 million for its development.
- **February 2022**: BP and ACC committed £3 million for initial design work on the hydrogen hub.
- **March 2022**: The project received additional funding from the UK government as part of a broader initiative to support hydrogen projects, with BP and ACC's joint venture being one of the beneficiaries.
- **February 2024**: The Aberdeen Hydrogen Hub was awarded a share of £21 million from the UK government’s Net Zero Hydrogen Fund, aimed at increasing hydrogen production capacity.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2021**: BP was selected as the preferred bidder for the hydrogen hub project.
- **February 2022**: Approval was granted for BP to partner with ACC, marking a significant milestone in the project’s development.
- **March 2022**: The joint venture was officially established, and initial design work commenced.
- **June 2023**: Planning documents were submitted, detailing the proposed site and operational plans for the hydrogen hub.
- **February 2024**: A final investment decision was anticipated, with construction expected to begin by the end of 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2021**: A study commissioned by ACC estimated that the hydrogen hub could create up to 700 skilled jobs in the industry by 2030 if an export market is established.
- **February 2022**: The project was expected to lead to thousands of high-value jobs in Aberdeen and the surrounding region as it develops.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2022**: The facility is a joint venture between BP and Aberdeen City Council, with both parties expected to share ownership and responsibilities equally (50-50 ownership).
