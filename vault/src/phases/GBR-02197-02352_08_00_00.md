### Evolutionary Timeline of the MHI Vestas Facility in Aberdeen, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **07-09-2018**: The facility is part of the European Offshore Wind Deployment Centre (EOWDC), which includes two MHI Vestas V164 8.8MW turbines and nine 8.4MW turbines, totaling a production capacity of 93MW. The project also tests innovative technologies such as suction bucket jacket foundations and 66kV cabling.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07-09-2018**: Vattenfall and the EU invested €3 million to study the environmental impact of offshore wind farms on marine life as part of the EOWDC project.

- **25-05-2022**: The UK Government awarded £9 million to Environmental Resources Management (ERM) to develop hydrogen projects, including the Dolphyn project, which aims to produce green hydrogen using floating turbines.

- **11-10-2022**: The UK Government provided £8.62 million in funding to advance the Dolphyn hydrogen project, which is set to utilize floating wind technology for hydrogen production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **07-09-2018**: The EOWDC was officially opened, marking the start of operations for the world’s most powerful offshore wind turbines in Scotland.

- **25-05-2022**: The Dolphyn green hydrogen project moved into the commercial demonstration phase, with plans for a 10 MW unit to be operational by summer 2025.

- **11-10-2022**: Principle Power secured FEED work for the Dolphyn project, focusing on the floating foundation element, and confirmed the 10 MW demonstrator unit's completion timeline.

#### 4. Labour
