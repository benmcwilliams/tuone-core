## GBR-02197-02428

### Evolutionary Timeline of the SGN Facility in Aberdeen, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2020**: The Scottish Government announced a Hydrogen Policy Statement aiming to generate 5 GW of renewable and low carbon hydrogen by 2030, with a long-term goal of 25 GW by 2045. SGN is involved in the Aberdeen Vision initiative, exploring hydrogen use from the Acorn project at the St Fergus terminal to decarbonise gas transmission networks.
- **November 2021**: SGN published a roadmap to make Aberdeen the first city powered entirely by hydrogen by the end of the decade. The plan includes utilizing existing gas infrastructure to decarbonise industry and transport, with hydrogen produced and stored at St Fergus. The initial hydrogen blend in the gas network would start at 2%, increasing to 20% and eventually 100%.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2020**: The Scottish Government pledged £100 million over five years to support the hydrogen sector, which includes initiatives like the Aberdeen Hydrogen Hub, a collaboration involving SGN, Scottish Enterprise, and Opportunity North East.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2020**: Aberdeen was selected as the site for the Dolphyn project, the world’s first offshore floating facility to produce green hydrogen.
- **November 2021**: SGN launched the preliminary engineering phase of the Aberdeen Vision pipeline, which will connect blue hydrogen production at St Fergus and offshore green hydrogen production to Aberdeen.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2020**: The Scottish Government's investment in the hydrogen sector is expected to create new jobs in hydrogen across the north-east, contributing to economic recovery and job creation.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: SGN is the primary company involved in the hydrogen initiatives in Aberdeen, including the Aberdeen Vision project. There is no indication of joint ownership or partnerships affecting the ownership structure of the facility. 

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic initiatives related to the SGN facility in Aberdeen, highlighting its role in the hydrogen economy and the broader goals of the Scottish Government.
