### Evolutionary Timeline of the Principle Power Facility in Aberdeen, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2022**: Principle Power secured front-end engineering and design (FEED) work for the Dolphyn hydrogen project, a floating wind-to-hydrogen scheme. The project aims to produce green hydrogen from seawater using floating offshore wind power, with a modular design integrating electrolyser facilities and a wind turbine on a semi-submersible platform based on WindFloat technology. The initial demonstrator unit is planned to have a capacity of **10 MW** and is expected to be operational by **summer 2025**. Following this phase, the project could scale up to a total capacity of **4 GW**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2022**: The Dolphyn project was awarded **£8.62 million** (approximately **$9.78 million**) in funding from the UK government through the Low Carbon Hydrogen Supply 2 Competition to progress its development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2022**: Principle Power was chosen to advance the FEED for the 10 MW wind-to-hydrogen demonstrator project off the coast of Aberdeen. The project is part of the UK’s Hydrogen coast strategy, which aims to generate over **10,000 new jobs by 2030** and **100,000 by 2050**.
- **October 2023**: Discussions at the FOW23 event highlighted the challenges facing the floating offshore wind sector, including the failure of Allocation Round 5 to attract bids, raising questions about UK energy policy. Principle Power is focusing on establishing a production line for foundations, indicating ongoing development efforts.

#### 4. Labour
- The UK’s Hydrogen coast strategy, which includes the Dolphyn project, is expected to create over **10,000 jobs by 2030** and **100,000 by 2050**, although specific job creation numbers directly tied to the Principle Power facility have not been detailed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify the ownership structure of the facility, but it is indicated that Principle Power is collaborating with Environmental Resources Management (ERM) on the Dolphyn project, suggesting a partnership rather than exclusive ownership.
