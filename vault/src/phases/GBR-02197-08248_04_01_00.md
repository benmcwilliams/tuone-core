### Evolutionary Timeline of the Aberdeen Renewable Energy Group (AREG) Facility in Aberdeen, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production**: The AREG facility focuses on the production of green hydrogen, utilizing electrolysers to convert water into clean hydrogen. 
- **Capacity**: The facility operates two hydrogen refuelling stations in Aberdeen, which offer both 350 and 750 bar hydrogen to the public. Additionally, it features the largest solid-oxide fuel cell installation in the UK, with three fuel cells each producing approximately 440 kW of electricity and 460 kW of heat.
- **Updates**: The facility has integrated solid-oxide fuel cells to power and heat the TECA conference facility and associated hotels, utilizing hydrogen produced from an on-site anaerobic digester.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment**: The Scottish government has committed £100 million over the next five years to support the hydrogen sector as part of its Hydrogen Action Plan, which aims to position Scotland as a leading hydrogen nation.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Milestones**: 
  - In October 2020, the facility unveiled fifteen hydrogen double-decker buses, marking a significant step in expanding the hydrogen fleet in the city.
  - The facility is actively involved in training programs and educational initiatives, including the launch of the UK’s first postgraduate MSc degree in energy transition systems and technologies by the University of Aberdeen in January 2021.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The facility recognizes the need for upskilling the local workforce to maintain hydrogen systems. North East Scotland College is training technicians in fuel cell vehicles and refuelling stations, indicating a focus on job creation in the hydrogen sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The article does not specify the ownership structure of the AREG facility, but it mentions Councillor Philip Bell as a director of AREG, indicating that it is likely a collaborative effort involving local government and stakeholders in the hydrogen sector.

### Summary
The AREG facility in Aberdeen is at the forefront of hydrogen technology, focusing on green hydrogen production and solid-oxide fuel cell applications. Supported by significant government investment, the facility is expanding its operations and workforce training initiatives, positioning Aberdeen as a leader in the hydrogen economy.
