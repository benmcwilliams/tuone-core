## GBR-02197-08352

### Evolutionary Timeline of BP Aberdeen Hydrogen Energy Limited Facility in Aberdeen, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2022**: BP Aberdeen Hydrogen Energy Limited (BPAHEL) announced plans for a scalable green hydrogen production facility, aiming to produce green hydrogen powered by a solar farm. The facility is expected to be operational by 2024, with a production capacity of over 800 kilograms of green hydrogen per day, sufficient to fuel 25 buses and a similar number of other fleet vehicles.
- **June 2023**: Planning approval was granted for the first phase of the hydrogen hub, which will include a hydrogen production facility on Hareness Road and a solar farm on the former Ness landfill site, spanning a total area of 123 acres.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2022**: The total investment for the entire three-phase project is estimated at £215 million. The Scottish Government had previously earmarked £15 million for the development in 2021.
- **June 2023**: The project is expected to stimulate demand for hydrogen and support the development of a hydrogen supply chain, contributing to the local economy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2022**: Public consultation was launched for the first phase of the project, with plans to submit a planning application following community feedback.
- **June 2023**: The local authority's planning committee approved the proposals for the first phase of the Aberdeen Hydrogen Hub, paving the way for construction and production to begin.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2023**: The development of the hydrogen hub is anticipated to create jobs and investment opportunities in the Aberdeen economy, contributing to the local community and supporting the transition to a net-zero city.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2022**: BP Aberdeen Hydrogen Energy Limited is a 50-50 joint venture between BP and Aberdeen City Council, with both parties involved in the development and operation of the facility. The local authority has not confirmed how costs will be split between the two partners.
