### Evolutionary Timeline of the Environmental Resources Management Facility in Aberdeen, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2022**: The Dolphyn green hydrogen project, developed by Environmental Resources Management (ERM), moves into the commercial demonstration phase with plans to produce green hydrogen using floating turbines. The initial production capacity is set at 10 MW for a demonstrator unit, expected to be operational by summer 2025. The project aims to scale up to a multi-gigawatt capacity, potentially supplying up to 25% of the UK’s future low-carbon hydrogen needs by the early 2030s.
- **October 2022**: The Dolphyn project is confirmed to utilize a modular design that integrates electrolyser facilities and a wind turbine on a semi-submersible platform, based on WindFloat technology. The demonstrator unit is planned to be located approximately 15 km off Aberdeen.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2022**: The UK Government awards £9 million to ERM to support the development of hydrogen projects, including the Dolphyn project.
- **October 2022**: An additional £8.62 million in UK Government funding is announced to progress the Dolphyn project, further solidifying its financial backing.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2022**: Announcement of the Dolphyn project moving to the commercial demonstration phase.
- **June 2022**: Offshore Design Engineering (ODE) is awarded a front-end engineering design (FEED) contract for the Dolphyn project, covering topside equipment and facility design.
- **October 2022**: Principle Power secures FEED work for the floating wind-to-hydrogen scheme, marking a significant milestone in the project’s development.
- **June 2023**: The project is highlighted as a key initiative in the emerging hydrogen economy in north-east Scotland, with ongoing preparations for the demonstrator unit's construction.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2022**: The UK’s Hydrogen Strategy, which includes the Dolphyn project, is expected to support over 9,000 jobs by 2030 and over 100,000 by 2050, indicating significant job creation potential linked to the project.
