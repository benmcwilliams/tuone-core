### Kellas Midstream Facility Evolution Timeline (Aberdeen, GBR)

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **April 2022:** Kellas Midstream announced plans for a major blue hydrogen project named H2NorthEast, which will be based around their CATS (Central Area Transmission System) terminal at Teesside. The project aims to produce low carbon hydrogen, starting with a capacity of 355 megawatts (MW) in 2027 and scaling up to 1 gigawatt (GW) by 2030. The hydrogen production will utilize steam methane reforming with carbon capture, usage, and storage (CCUS) technology, achieving a carbon capture efficiency of 97% and offsetting two million tonnes of carbon annually.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **April 2022:** Kellas Midstream plans to invest £750 million in the H2NorthEast project. This investment is part of their strategy to expand into hydrogen production and contribute to the UK’s net zero emissions target by 2050.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **April 2022:** The H2NorthEast project is currently in the concept select stage, with a final investment decision expected in 2024. The project has already secured a handful of offtake agreements with nearby companies.

4. **Labour:**
   - **April 2022:** The H2NorthEast project is expected to create around 100 jobs and allow almost 20 workers to upskill. Additionally, hundreds more jobs are anticipated to be created during the construction phase and across the supply chain.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **April 2022:** Kellas Midstream is involved in the H2NorthEast project, with Blackrock private equity infrastructure investors mentioned as one of Kellas’ owners, indicating that Kellas does not have exclusive ownership of the facility.
