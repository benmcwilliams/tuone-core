## GBR-02197-09665

### Osbit Facility Evolution Timeline - Aberdeen, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **March 2024:** The Floating Wind Innovation Centre (FLOWIC) officially opened on 18 March 2024. This facility is designed to accelerate the development of floating offshore wind technology in the UK. It features several advanced technologies, including:
     - A large-scale dynamic cable flex fatigue rig, installed by Osbit, for testing the strength and reliability of dynamic subsea cables.
     - A large-scale anchor test rig for dynamic anchoring systems.
     - A floating offshore wind scale motion simulator (Hexapod) for dynamic testing of structures and connections.
     - A virtual reality studio for engineers to simulate scenarios related to floating wind turbine construction.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The FLOWIC was developed with a total investment of GBP 9 million (approximately EUR 10.5 million), funded by the Scottish Government and Innovate UK.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **March 2024:** The FLOWIC was officially opened, marking a significant milestone in the commercialisation of floating offshore wind technology in the UK.

4. **Labour:**
   - The establishment of the FLOWIC is expected to create jobs as supply chain businesses transition towards offshore wind, contributing to the local economy and supporting the Just Transition from oil and gas.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is delivered by ORE Catapult in collaboration with ETZ Limited, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by Osbit or any single entity. 

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Osbit facility in Aberdeen, focusing on its role in advancing floating offshore wind technology.
