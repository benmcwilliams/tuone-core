## GBR-02197-09974

### Seaway 7 Facility in Aberdeen, GBR - Evolutionary Timeline

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date: 08-11-2022**: Seaway 7 signed contracts to supply and install inter-array cables for the Moray West Offshore Wind Farm, which has a production capacity of 882 MW. The project involves the installation of approximately 125 kilometers of 66 kV subsea cables, connecting 60 Siemens Gamesa SG 14-222 DD wind turbines to the offshore substation.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date: 08-11-2022**: The contract for the Moray West project is valued between USD 50 million and USD 150 million. This contract supports JDR's decision to open a new GBP 130 million subsea cable manufacturing facility in Cambois, Northumberland, which is expected to be operational in 2024.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date: 08-11-2022**: Seaway 7 was awarded the contract for the Moray West offshore wind farm, with the project management being led from their Aberdeen office. The contract was finalized following a Letter of Award issued in early August 2022.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date: 08-11-2022**: The Moray West project is expected to secure additional jobs at Seaway 7's Aberdeen office, as stated by Lloyd Duthie, Vice President – UK and Asia for Seaway 7. This indicates a growth in the skilled workforce to meet industry needs in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date: 08-11-2022**: The Moray West offshore wind farm is developed and majority-owned by Ocean Winds, with Lithuania’s Ignitis Group holding a minority share. Seaway 7 operates under a contract for the project but does not own the facility exclusively.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Seaway 7 facility in Aberdeen, focusing on its role in the Moray West Offshore Wind Farm project.
