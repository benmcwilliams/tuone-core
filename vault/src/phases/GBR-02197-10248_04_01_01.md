## GBR-02197-10248

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Tractabel Facility in Aberdeen, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2022**: The Tractabel facility is involved in the Dolphyn green hydrogen project, which aims to produce green hydrogen using floating turbines. The project includes the construction of a 10 MW commercial demonstrator unit, expected to be operational by summer 2025. Following this, there are plans for a multi-gigawatt project that could meet up to 25% of the UK's future low-carbon hydrogen needs by the early 2030s.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2022**: The UK Government awarded £8.62 million to support the development of the Dolphyn project, which includes the Tractabel facility. This funding is part of the efforts to progress the construction of the demonstrator project capable of producing green hydrogen from seawater using floating offshore wind power.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2022**: Announcement of the Dolphyn project moving into the commercial demonstration phase, with the 10 MW unit planned to be located 15 km off Aberdeen.
- **October 2022**: Confirmation of the FEED work awarded to Offshore Design Engineering (ODE) and consortium partners, including Tractabel, for the Dolphyn project, marking a significant milestone in the project's development.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2022**: The overall Hydrogen Coast strategy, which includes the Dolphyn project, is expected to generate more than 10,000 new jobs by 2030 and over 100,000 by 2050, indicating potential job creation associated with the Tractabel facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify the ownership structure of the Tractabel facility in Aberdeen, but it is mentioned that Tractabel is part of a consortium with other partners, including ODE and Vestas, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership. 

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Tractabel facility in Aberdeen, focusing on its role in the Dolphyn green hydrogen project.

### Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: The facility is set to produce a 10 MW commercial demonstrator unit by summer 2025 (Article 1). Following this, there are plans for a multi-gigawatt project that could provide “up to 25% of the UK’s future low-carbon hydrogen needs” (Article 1). However, the potential scaled-up capacity is mentioned as 4 GW in another article (Article 2).
- **Technology Description**: The project utilizes floating turbines to produce green hydrogen at scale (Article 1). It employs a modular design that integrates electrolyser facilities and a wind turbine on a moored semi-submersible platform, based on WindFloat technology (Article 2).

### Investment and Development
- **Government Funding**: The UK Government awarded £9 million to ERM for hydrogen projects, including Dolphyn (Article 1). In contrast, another article states that £8.62 million in UK Government funding was awarded to progress the development (Article 2).

### Project Status and Milestones
- **Construction and Operation Timeline**: Both articles confirm that the 10 MW demonstrator unit is slated for completion and operation by summer 2025 (Article 1 and Article 2). However, the first article mentions the project moving to a commercial demonstration phase, while the second article emphasizes the transition to FEED as a significant milestone.

### Labour
- **Job Creation**: The project is expected to contribute to generating more than 10,000 new jobs by 2030 and over 100,000 by 2050 as part of the UK’s Hydrogen coast strategy (Article 2). There is no mention of job creation in the first article.

### Ownership Structure
- **Ownership Details**: The articles do not provide explicit information on whether Tractabel owns the facility exclusively or if it is part of a joint venture. However, both articles mention Tractabel as a consortium member alongside ODE and Vestas, indicating a collaborative effort rather than exclusive ownership.
