### Evolutionary Timeline of RWE's Sofia Offshore Wind Farm, Dogger Bank, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2021**: RWE's Sofia Offshore Wind Farm is set to utilize Siemens Gamesa's 14 MW turbines, marking the first installation of this turbine model globally. The project will consist of 100 turbines and is expected to generate enough energy to power approximately 1.2 million UK homes.
- **February 2022**: The onshore converter station for the wind farm is scheduled for completion in mid-2025. The project will also feature an offshore converter station and extensive electrical infrastructure.
- **December 2022**: The wind farm will consist of 100 Siemens Gamesa 14 MW wind turbine generators, with operations expected to commence in 2026.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: The total investment for the Sofia Offshore Wind Farm is reported to be £3 billion (approximately $4.06 billion).
- **February 2022**: Hellenic Cables is contracted to supply around 360 km of inter-array cables, with production set to begin in 2022 and completed in 2023.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2021**: RWE announces the contract with GE Renewable Energy and Sembcorp Marine for the HVDC transmission system, with construction expected to begin onshore in 2021 and offshore in 2023.
- **February 2022**: Construction of the onshore converter station begins, with completion expected in mid-2025.
- **December 2022**: Asso.subsea is contracted for boulder clearance and pre-trenching services along the inter-array cable routes, with operations scheduled for the second to third quarter of 2023.
- **May 2023**: Specialist Marine Consultants (SMC) signs a contract to deliver offshore resources and services during the project's offshore construction, which is set to commence in 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2022**: The construction of the onshore converter station is expected to bring significant economic benefits to the northeast of England, although specific job creation numbers are not detailed.
- **May 2023**: SMC's involvement in the project indicates potential job creation in offshore construction and maintenance roles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- RWE exclusively owns the Sofia Offshore Wind Farm, as indicated by the consistent references to RWE as the owner and developer throughout the articles.
