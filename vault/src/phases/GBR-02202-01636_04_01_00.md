## GBR-02202-01636

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Equinor Facility in Dogger Bank, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **30-10-2019**: Equinor and SSE Renewables announced the development of the Dogger Bank offshore wind farms, with a total capacity of 3.6GW, divided into three phases of 1.2GW each (Creyke Beck A, B, and C). The facility will utilize HVDC technology for efficient power transmission.
- **04-05-2021**: The Dogger Bank project unveiled plans for the first unmanned HVDC offshore substation, which will have a capacity of 1.2GW, marking a significant advancement in offshore wind technology.
- **23-12-2021**: The project confirmed the use of UK-made steel from Tata Steel for the foundations of the wind turbines, emphasizing local manufacturing and sustainability.
- **14-05-2023**: The project announced the debut of electron beam welding technology for monopile foundations, which is expected to enhance efficiency and reduce costs significantly.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **01-10-2020**: Hellenic Cables was awarded a contract to supply 650 km of array cables for Dogger Bank A and B, contingent on reaching financial close, which was expected in late 2020.
- **02-12-2021**: Financial close was reached for Dogger Bank C, securing £3bn in financing, contributing to a total investment of around £9bn for the entire project.
- **19-06-2023**: The project partners signed contracts for feasibility studies on large-scale green hydrogen production from the proposed Dogger Bank D phase, indicating ongoing investment in renewable energy technologies.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **30-10-2019**: Announcement of the joint venture between Equinor and SSE Renewables for the Dogger Bank project.
- **01-10-2020**: Anticipation of financial close for Dogger Bank A and B.
- **04-05-2021**: Confirmation of the unmanned HVDC platform design.
- **02-12-2021**: Financial close on Dogger Bank C.
- **11-02-2023**: Early scoping work began for the potential fourth phase, Dogger Bank D, which could add 1.32GW of capacity.
- **19-06-2023**: The world's largest offshore wind jack-up vessel, Voltaire, arrived in the UK for turbine installation.

#### 4. Labour
- **23-12-2021**: The project has created or supported over 3,000 jobs in the UK supply chain, with ongoing opportunities for local businesses.
- **20-01-2022**: The project announced a series of supply chain events to showcase operations and maintenance opportunities, further supporting job creation in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **30-10-2019**: Dogger Bank Wind Farms is a 50:50 joint venture between Equinor and SSE Renewables. 
- **04-05-2021**: Dogger Bank A and B are jointly owned by SSE Renewables (40%), Equinor (40%), and Eni (20%). Dogger Bank C is a 50:50 joint venture between SSE Renewables and Equinor.
- **02-12-2021**: Following a sell-down transaction, the ownership structure for Dogger Bank C is confirmed as SSE Renewables (40%), Equinor (40%), and Eni (20%).
