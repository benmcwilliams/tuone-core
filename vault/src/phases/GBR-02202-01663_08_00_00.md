## GBR-02202-01663

### Evolutionary Timeline of the GE Facility in Dogger Bank, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Dogger Bank Wind Farm is being developed in three phases: Dogger Bank A, B, and C, with a total capacity of 3.6 GW (1.2 GW per phase). 
   - The facility will utilize GE Renewable Energy turbines, each rated at 13 MW, capable of powering a home for two days with a single rotation.
   - The wind farm is located 130 km off the North East coast of England and is expected to be completed by 2026.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The project is a joint venture involving SSE Renewables (40%), Equinor (40%), and Eni (20%) for phases A, B, and C.
   - Significant investments are being made in the UK supply chain, particularly in the production of steel by Tata Steel, which is crucial for the construction of the wind farm.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - As of December 2021, the project is in the construction phase, with contracts awarded for the wind turbine foundations to fabrication companies Sif and Smulders in November 2020.
   - The completion of the project is anticipated in 2026.

4. **Labour:**
   - The Dogger Bank Wind Farm is creating or supporting over 3,000 jobs in the UK supply chain, providing opportunities for various companies involved in the project.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is not owned exclusively by GE; it is part of a joint venture with SSE Renewables and Equinor, with Eni holding a stake in the final phase.
