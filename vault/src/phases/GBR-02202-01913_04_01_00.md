## GBR-02202-01913

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Dogger Bank Facility (SSE Renewables, Dogger Bank, GBR)

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **30-10-2019**: SSE and Equinor announce the development of the 3.6GW Dogger Bank offshore wind farms, consisting of three 1.2GW phases (A, B, and C). The facility will utilize HVDC technology for the first time in the UK offshore wind market, connecting to the UK power grid.
- **04-05-2021**: The Dogger Bank project unveils the first unmanned HVDC offshore substation, with a capacity of 1.2GW, marking a significant advancement in offshore wind technology.
- **15-05-2023**: The project announces the use of electron beam welding technology for monopile foundations, which is expected to significantly reduce costs and emissions compared to traditional methods.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **15-04-2020**: SSE outlines a roadmap to achieve the UK’s 40GW offshore wind target by 2030, emphasizing the importance of the Dogger Bank project in this goal.
- **02-12-2021**: Financial close is reached for Dogger Bank C, securing £3bn in financing, contributing to a total investment of around £9bn for the entire project.
- **18-01-2022**: SSE and Equinor award contracts for the feasibility studies of a potential green hydrogen production facility as part of the Dogger Bank D project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **30-10-2019**: Announcement of the Dogger Bank project and the selection of ABB and Aibel for the HVDC technology.
- **01-10-2020**: Hellenic Cables awarded a contract for the supply of 650 km of array cables for Dogger Bank A and B.
- **04-05-2021**: The unmanned HVDC platform is announced, set to be installed in 2023.
- **02-12-2021**: Financial close on Dogger Bank C is achieved.
- **12-05-2023**: Announcement of the debut of electron beam welded foundations at Dogger Bank.
- **12-05-2023**: Naming ceremony for the Grampian Tyne service operation vessel, which will support O&M at Dogger Bank.
- **19-06-2023**: The Voltaire jack-up vessel arrives in the UK to install 277 turbines for the project.

#### 4. Labour
- **15-04-2020**: SSE's roadmap indicates that the Dogger Bank project will create significant job opportunities as part of the UK's offshore wind expansion.
- **06-12-2021**: The project is reported to have created or supported over 3,000 jobs in the UK supply chain.
- **12-05-2023**: The project is expected to create thousands of jobs and contribute over £1 million to coastal communities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **30-10-2019**: Dogger Bank is established as a 50:50 joint venture between SSE Renewables and Equinor.
- **04-05-2021**: Dogger Bank A and B are jointly owned by SSE Renewables (40%), Equinor (40%), and Eni (20%). Dogger Bank C is a 50:50 joint venture between SSE and Equinor.
- **02-12-2021**: Following a transaction, the ownership structure remains SSE (40%), Equinor (40%), and Eni (20%) for Dogger Bank A and B, while Dogger Bank C is also a joint venture with the same ownership distribution.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones of the Dogger Bank facility, highlighting its technological advancements, investments, project status, labor impacts, and ownership structure.
