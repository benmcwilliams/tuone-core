## GBR-02202-02220

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Eni Facility in Dogger Bank, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04-05-2021**: The Dogger Bank Wind Farm is set to utilize the first unmanned high voltage direct current (HVDC) offshore substation with a capacity of 1.2GW. This facility will be part of a larger 3.6GW project, which will be the largest offshore wind farm in the world upon completion in 2026. The HVDC technology is chosen for its efficiency in transmitting electricity over long distances, minimizing losses.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **23-12-2021**: Eni acquires a 20% stake in Dogger Bank C, a joint venture with SSE Renewables and Equinor, who each hold 40%. This investment is part of the overall development of the wind farm, which is expected to provide green energy for six million homes in the UK.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04-05-2021**: Announcement of the unmanned HVDC platform and its significance in the Dogger Bank project.
- **23-12-2021**: Confirmation of Eni's stake in Dogger Bank C, solidifying its role in the project.
- **19-01-2022**: Contract awarded to Hellenic Cables for the supply of inter-array cables for Dogger Bank C, marking a significant milestone in the project's supply chain.
- **20-01-2022**: Announcement of a series of supply chain events to showcase opportunities during the operations and maintenance phase of the project.
- **15-02-2022**: Proserv is contracted to deliver a cable monitoring system for Dogger Bank A and B, enhancing operational capabilities.
- **08-03-2022**: SMC is awarded a contract for marine coordination services for all three phases of the Dogger Bank project, indicating ongoing construction activities.

#### 4. Labour
- **23-12-2021**: The Dogger Bank Wind Farm is reported to be creating or supporting over 3,000 jobs in the UK supply chain, highlighting the project's significant impact on local employment.
- **20-01-2022**: The project is expected to continue creating opportunities for local suppliers and companies, further contributing to job creation in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **04-05-2021**: The Dogger Bank A and B phases are a joint venture between SSE Renewables (40%), Equinor (40%), and Eni (20%). Dogger Bank C is a 50:50 joint venture between SSE Renewables and Equinor, with Eni holding a 20% stake. This structure indicates that Eni does not own the facility exclusively but is a significant partner in the joint ventures.
