## GBR-02206-01842

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ørsted Facility in Immingham, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2019**: Ørsted announced the development of a new learning centre at MODAL’s training complex in Immingham, designed to support safety training for the Hornsea 2 construction team. The facility aims to enhance safety standards across various industries in the Humber region.
- **August 2020**: Ørsted opened the Thrive safety training centre, a £1.4 million purpose-built facility at the MODAL training centre. The facility is designed to induct personnel working on the Hornsea 2 offshore wind project and includes an immersive training programme focused on transforming safety behaviours and attitudes.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2020**: Ørsted invested £1.4 million in the Thrive facility, developed in partnership with Active Training Team (ATT). This investment is aimed at creating a high-quality training environment for safety induction and ongoing training for the Hornsea 2 project and other industries.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2019**: Announcement of the new safety training centre for Hornsea 2.
- **August 2020**: Official opening of the Thrive safety training centre, with the initial rollout of the training programme for Hornsea 2 personnel.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2020**: The opening of the Thrive facility resulted in the creation of over 60 new jobs and contracts in the region, including roles for production crew, actors, and construction personnel, all hired locally to support the project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is developed and operated by Ørsted in partnership with Active Training Team (ATT). There is no indication of joint ownership; Ørsted appears to have full operational control over the facility for its intended purpose. 

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Ørsted facility in Immingham, focusing on its role in supporting the Hornsea 2 offshore wind project and enhancing safety training in the region.
