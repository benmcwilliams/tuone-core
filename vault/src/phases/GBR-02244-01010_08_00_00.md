### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Celtic Sea, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2022**: EDF Renewables announced the Gwynt Glas floating offshore wind project, which will span 1,500 square kilometers in the Celtic Sea. The facility is planned to generate up to 1GW of green energy, enough to power approximately 920,000 homes. 

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2022**: A memorandum of understanding (MoU) was signed between Gwynt Glas Offshore Wind Farm, Associated British Ports, and Port of Milford Haven. This partnership aims to prepare the region for floating wind farms, indicating a commitment to developing the necessary infrastructure for the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2022**: EDF Renewables began consulting with fisheries to determine the optimal location for the Gwynt Glas project, marking the initial phase of project development.
- **November 2022**: The MoU with South Wales ports was established, indicating progress in planning and collaboration for the floating offshore wind project.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2022**: The collaboration with South Wales ports is expected to create job opportunities related to the development of manufacturing, assembly, load-out, and servicing infrastructure, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2022**: The Gwynt Glas project is a joint venture between EDF Renewables UK and DP Energy, indicating that the facility is not owned exclusively by EDF but rather in partnership with another entity. 

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the EDF facility in the Celtic Sea, highlighting its technological ambitions, collaborative efforts, and ownership structure.
