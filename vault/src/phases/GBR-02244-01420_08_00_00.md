## GBR-02244-01420

### Evolutionary Timeline of the TotalEnergies Facility in Celtic Sea, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **March 2020:** The facility, known as the 96MW Erebus floating wind farm, is being developed off the coast of Wales in the Celtic Sea. The project will utilize Principle Power’s WindFloat technology, which is designed for water depths of 70 meters.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **March 2020:** TotalEnergies (then Total) acquired an 80% stake in the Erebus project, marking its first investment in the offshore wind sector. This acquisition is part of Total's broader strategy to expand its renewable energy portfolio, targeting 25GW of new renewable electricity generation capacity by 2025.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **March 2020:** TotalEnergies and Simply Blue Energy submitted an application to the UK Crown Estate for the Erebus project. The project is intended as a demonstration for a potential fleet of floating offshore wind farms in the area, with expectations for completion and commissioning by the middle of the decade.

4. **Labour:**
   - **March 2020:** Simply Blue Energy has been engaging with the local supply chain around the Celtic Sea to involve local companies in the Erebus project, indicating potential job creation opportunities in the region.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **March 2020:** TotalEnergies holds an 80% stake in the Erebus project through a joint venture with Simply Blue Energy, named Blue Gem Wind. This indicates that TotalEnergies does not have exclusive ownership of the facility but is a major stakeholder in the joint venture. 

This timeline captures the key developments related to the TotalEnergies facility in the Celtic Sea, highlighting its technological focus, investment strategies, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure.
