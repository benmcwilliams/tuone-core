### Evolutionary Timeline of the ERM Facility in Celtic Sea, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **May 2022:** The ERM Dolphyn project, a floating wind-to-hydrogen facility, entered the commercial demonstration phase. The facility will produce hydrogen using electrolysis, desalination, and hydrogen production on a floating wind platform. The initial production capacity is set at 10 MW for the demonstration unit, which is expected to be deployed by summer 2025. Following the demonstration, there are plans for a multi-gigawatt project that could meet up to 25% of the UK's future low-carbon hydrogen demand in the 2030s.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **May 2022:** The project received GBP 8.6 million in funding from the UK Government’s Department for Business, Energy and Industrial Strategy (BEIS) as part of the Net Zero Innovation Portfolio Low Carbon Hydrogen Supply 2 Competition to support its demonstration phase.

   - **March 2022:** ERM signed an agreement with Source Energie to jointly develop projects in the Celtic Sea, with the first project planned at the 300 MW Dylan site, expected to be operational by the end of 2028.
