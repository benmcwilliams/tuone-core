### Evolutionary Timeline of the DP Energy Facility in Celtic Sea, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2022**: The Gwynt Glas floating offshore wind project, a joint venture between EDF Renewables and DP Energy, is proposed to generate up to **1GW of green energy**. The facility is expected to power approximately **920,000 homes** and contribute to the Crown Estate’s goal of achieving **4GW of renewable capacity** in the Celtic Sea.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2022**: A memorandum of understanding (MoU) is signed with Associated British Ports and Port of Milford Haven to prepare the region for floating wind farms. This collaboration aims to develop the necessary infrastructure for manufacturing, assembly, load-out, and servicing of the floating offshore wind facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2022**: EDF Renewables begins consulting with fisheries to determine the location for the Gwynt Glas project, which will span **1,500 square kilometres** in both English and Welsh waters.
- **November 2022**: The MoU is established to facilitate the development of the Gwynt Glas project, indicating progress in planning and collaboration with local ports.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2022**: The collaboration with South Wales ports is expected to create opportunities for job creation in the region, particularly in the manufacturing and servicing sectors related to the floating offshore wind project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Gwynt Glas facility is a **joint venture (JV)** between **EDF Renewables UK** and **DP Energy**, indicating that neither company has exclusive ownership of the facility.
