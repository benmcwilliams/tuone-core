### Evolutionary Timeline of the Offshore Design Engineering Facility in Celtic Sea, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2022**: Offshore Design Engineering (ODE) won a contract to provide topside Front-End Engineering Design (FEED) services for the ERM Dolphyn project, which combines electrolysis, desalination, and hydrogen production on a floating wind platform. The project aims to deploy a 10 MW commercial demonstration unit by summer 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2022**: The ERM Dolphyn project received GBP 8.6 million in funding from the UK Government’s Department for Business, Energy and Industrial Strategy (BEIS) as part of the Net Zero Innovation Portfolio Low Carbon Hydrogen Supply 2 Competition.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2022**: The ERM Dolphyn project entered the commercial demonstration phase, with ODE responsible for FEED engineering. Following this phase, a multi-gigawatt project is anticipated to meet up to 25% of the UK’s future low-carbon hydrogen demand in the 2030s.
- **March 2022**: ERM signed an agreement with Source Energie to jointly develop projects in the Celtic Sea, with the first project planned at the 300 MW Dylan site, expected to be operational by the end of 2028.
