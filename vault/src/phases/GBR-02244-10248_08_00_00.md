### Evolutionary Timeline of the Tractabel Facility in Celtic Sea, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** May 2022
   - **Details:** The Tractabel facility is part of the ERM Dolphyn project, which focuses on floating wind-to-hydrogen technology. The facility will produce low-carbon hydrogen through a combination of electrolysis, desalination, and hydrogen production on a floating wind platform. The initial production capacity is set at 10 MW for the commercial demonstration unit, expected to be deployed by summer 2025.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** May 2022
   - **Details:** The project received GBP 8.6 million in funding from the UK Government’s Department for Business, Energy and Industrial Strategy (BEIS) as part of the Net Zero Innovation Portfolio Low Carbon Hydrogen Supply 2 Competition. This funding supports the demonstration phase of the project.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** May 2022
   - **Details:** The ERM Dolphyn project has entered the commercial demonstration phase. The project is expected to lead to a multi-gigawatt development that could meet up to 25% of the UK’s future low-carbon hydrogen demand in the 2030s. An agreement was signed in March 2022 with Source Energie to jointly develop projects in the Celtic Sea, with the first project planned at the 300 MW Dylan site, operational by the end of 2028.
