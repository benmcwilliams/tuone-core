## GBR-02256-02206

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Element 2 Facility in the U.K., GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2021**: Element 2 announced a partnership with Ways2H, a joint venture between U.S.-based Clean Energy Enterprises and Japan Blue Energy. This collaboration aims to develop facilities that convert municipal solid waste and sewage sludge into hydrogen. The facilities will supply Element 2’s network of hydrogen refueling stations, contributing to the production and distribution of renewable hydrogen fuel for transport across the U.K., Ireland, and continental Europe. The plan includes establishing as many as 40 sites, each capable of providing between 500 kg to 1 ton of renewable hydrogen fuel per day.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2021**: Element 2's CEO, Tim Harper, highlighted that the hydrogen supply agreement with Ways2H is a significant step in their strategy to deliver 2,000 hydrogen refueling stations across the U.K., Ireland, and Europe by 2030. This indicates a commitment to substantial investment in infrastructure to support hydrogen fuel distribution.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2021**: The first project under the partnership with Ways2H is expected to take place in Scotland, marking the initial step in Element 2's ambitious expansion plan for hydrogen refueling stations.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2021**: While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned, the establishment of 2,000 hydrogen refueling stations and the associated facilities for hydrogen production imply potential job creation in construction, operation, and maintenance of these facilities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2021**: The partnership with Ways2H indicates a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership. Element 2 is working in conjunction with Ways2H to develop the necessary infrastructure for hydrogen production and distribution, suggesting a joint venture model for this aspect of their operations.
