### Evolutionary Timeline of the Shell Facility in North West England, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility is part of the North West Hydrogen Alliance (NWHA), which focuses on developing a hydrogen economy in the region. It is involved in hydrogen production and Carbon Capture Storage and Utilisation (CCUS) projects, particularly through the HyNet initiative.
   - The NWHA aims to establish the world’s first net-zero carbon industrial cluster by 2040, indicating a commitment to innovative hydrogen technologies.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The NWHA is bidding for over £130 million of Government funding to support hydrogen projects, which are essential for achieving the UK’s ambitious carbon reduction targets.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - The NWHA was launched in November 2018 and has since become a key player in the hydrogen economy in the North West. The facility is actively working on hydrogen projects, with plans for significant developments in the coming years.

4. **Labour:**
   - The development of a major hydrogen cluster in the North West is projected to create nearly 6,000 jobs and contribute £17 billion in Gross Value Added for the region, while also saving 1 million tonnes of CO2 annually.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
