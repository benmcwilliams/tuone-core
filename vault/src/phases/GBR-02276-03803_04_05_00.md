### Evolutionary Timeline of the Atkins/SNC-Lavalin Facility in North West England, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility is part of the North West Hydrogen Alliance (NWHA), which focuses on developing a hydrogen economy in the North West of England. The facility is involved in hydrogen production and is linked to the HyNet project, which is a leading hydrogen and Carbon Capture Storage and Utilisation (CCUS) initiative in the UK.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The NWHA is bidding for over £130 million of Government funding to establish the world’s first net-zero carbon industrial cluster by 2040. This funding is crucial for the development of hydrogen projects in the region.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - The NWHA was launched in November 2018 and has since established itself as a key player in the hydrogen economy. The facility is actively involved in ongoing hydrogen projects and is part of a collaborative effort to develop a hydrogen infrastructure in the North West.

4. **Labour:**
   - The development of a major hydrogen cluster in the North West is projected to create nearly 6,000 jobs and contribute £17 billion in Gross Value Added for the region, while also saving 1 million tonnes of CO2 annually.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is part of a collaborative partnership within the NWHA, which includes several major companies and organizations such as BOC, Cadent, Costain, INOVYN, Peel Environmental, Shell, and the University of Chester. Therefore, it is not exclusively owned by Atkins/SNC-Lavalin but is part of a joint effort with multiple stakeholders.
