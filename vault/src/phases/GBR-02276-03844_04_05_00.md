### Evolutionary Timeline of the Cadent Facility in North West England, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Cadent facility is part of the North West Hydrogen Alliance (NWHA), which focuses on developing a hydrogen economy in the North West of England. The facility is involved in hydrogen production and is part of the HyNet project, which includes hydrogen and Carbon Capture Storage and Utilisation (CCUS) initiatives.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The NWHA is advocating for over £130 million of Government funding to establish the world’s first net-zero carbon industrial cluster by 2040. This funding is aimed at supporting hydrogen projects, which are crucial for achieving the UK’s carbon reduction targets.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - As of November 2020, the NWHA has been actively working on hydrogen projects and has established itself as a key player in the region's hydrogen economy since its launch in November 2018. The facility is part of ongoing efforts to develop a hydrogen economy that could significantly contribute to the UK’s decarbonisation goals.

4. **Labour:**
   - The development of a major hydrogen cluster in the North West is projected to create nearly 6,000 jobs and contribute £17 billion in Gross Value Added for the region, highlighting the potential for job creation linked to the Cadent facility's operations.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The Cadent facility is part of a collaborative effort within the NWHA, which includes several partners such as Atkins/SNC-Lavalin, BOC, Costain, INOVYN, Peel Environmental, Shell, and the University of Chester. This indicates that Cadent does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a joint initiative with multiple stakeholders.
