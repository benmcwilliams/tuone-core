## GBR-02276-04335

### Evolutionary Timeline of the University of Chester Facility in North West England, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The University of Chester is part of the North West Hydrogen Alliance (NWHA), which focuses on developing a hydrogen economy in the North West of England. The facility is involved in hydrogen projects, particularly in the context of the HyNet project, which is a leading hydrogen and Carbon Capture Storage and Utilisation (CCUS) initiative.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The NWHA is advocating for over £130 million of Government funding to establish the world’s first net-zero carbon industrial cluster by 2040. This funding is aimed at supporting hydrogen projects in the region, which includes the University of Chester's involvement.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - The NWHA was launched in November 2018 and has since been working on hydrogen projects. As of May 2020, the NWHA is actively seeking government support to further develop hydrogen initiatives in the North West, emphasizing the importance of hydrogen in achieving net zero carbon emissions.

4. **Labour:**
   - The development of a major hydrogen cluster in the North West, as highlighted by the NWHA, is projected to create nearly 6,000 jobs and contribute significantly to regional economic growth, with an estimated £17 billion in Gross Value Added for the area.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The University of Chester is a partner in the NWHA, which includes various stakeholders such as private sector companies and regional leaders. The facility does not operate in isolation but is part of a collaborative effort to develop the hydrogen economy in the region.

### Summary:
The University of Chester facility is integral to the North West Hydrogen Alliance, focusing on hydrogen production and related technologies. The facility is part of a broader initiative seeking substantial government investment to establish a net-zero carbon industrial cluster, with significant job creation and economic benefits projected for the region. The facility operates collaboratively within a partnership framework rather than as an exclusive owner.
