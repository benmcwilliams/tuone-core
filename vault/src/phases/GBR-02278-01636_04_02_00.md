### Evolutionary Timeline of the Equinor Facility in Humber, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2021**: Equinor announced plans to construct a blue hydrogen plant in the Humber region, which is part of the Hydrogen to Humber (H2H) project. The facility is designed to reform natural gas into hydrogen while capturing over 95% of its CO2 emissions. The initial phase of the H2H project will feature a 600-megawatt (MW) auto thermal reformer (ATR).
- **June 2022**: Equinor, in partnership with SSE Thermal, acquired Triton Power, which includes the Saltend Power Station. The Saltend facility has an installed capacity of 1.2 gigawatts (GW) and aims to transition to using 30% hydrogen by 2027, with a goal of reaching 100% hydrogen power thereafter.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2022**: The acquisition of Triton Power was valued at £341 million, which is part of the broader strategy to transition to hydrogen and enhance the decarbonization efforts in the Humber region.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2021**: Announcement of the H2H project in Humber, with plans for construction and operation of the blue hydrogen plant.
- **June 2022**: Completion of the acquisition of Triton Power, marking a significant step towards the development of hydrogen capabilities at the Saltend Power Station.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2022**: The transition to hydrogen at the Saltend Power Station is expected to create new job opportunities as the facility adapts to cleaner energy production, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2022**: Equinor and SSE Thermal established a 50:50 joint venture for the ownership of Triton Power, which includes the Saltend Power Station. This indicates that Equinor does not own the facility exclusively but shares ownership with SSE Thermal.
