## GBR-02278-02311

### Evolutionary Timeline of ITM Power Facility in Humber, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2022**: ITM Power announced the development of a 5 MW ‘Gigastack’ hydrogen platform aimed at producing green hydrogen. This platform is 2.5 times larger than previous iterations and is designed to operate flexibly with lower capital costs and a smaller system footprint. The goal is to eventually develop larger 100 MW electrolyser systems to meet the UK’s net zero target by 2050. 
- **August 2023**: The Gigastack project, which was to utilize a 100 MW electrolyser powered by electricity from Ørsted’s Hornsea 2 wind farm to produce green hydrogen for the Phillips 66 Humber refinery, was put on pause due to the need for further project maturation and supply chain development.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2022**: ITM Power secured a £9.3 million government award from the Department for Business, Energy and Industrial Strategy (BEIS) to support the Gigastack project and accelerate the commercial deployment of the 5 MW platform. This funding is part of the net zero innovation portfolio.
- **Future Plans**: ITM Power plans to build a £55 million facility with the capacity to produce 1.5 GW of electrolysers per annum, expected to be fully operational by the end of 2023.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2022**: Announcement of the £9.3 million government award and the development of the 5 MW ‘Gigastack’ hydrogen platform.
- **August 2023**: Orsted and Phillips 66 announced the pause of the Gigastack project, indicating that further project maturation and supply chain development are necessary before proceeding.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2022**: ITM Power's CEO indicated that the government’s focus on green hydrogen would create jobs, new supply chains, and high-tech exports, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
