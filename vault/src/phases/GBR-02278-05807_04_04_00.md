## GBR-02278-05807

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Phillips 66 Facility in Humber, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Project**: Gigastack green hydrogen project
- **Technology**: Utilizes a 100 MW electrolyser from ITM Power, powered by electricity from Hornsea 2 wind farm.
- **Purpose**: Intended to produce green hydrogen for the Phillips 66 Humber refinery and serve as a blueprint for scaling up electrolyser technology in the UK.
- **Current Status**: As of August 2023, the project has been put on pause due to the need for further project maturation and supply chain development.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Funding**: The Gigastack project was previously awarded a £9.3 million government contract to accelerate its development.
- **Future Plans**: The project aims to contribute to the UK’s goal of achieving 10 GW of low-carbon hydrogen generation by 2030, with the first 1 GW expected to be operational or under construction by 2025.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Announcement**: On 16-08-2023, Phillips 66 and Orsted announced the decision to pause the Gigastack project and withdraw from the UK Government Hydrogen Business Model (HMB) funding process.
- **Reason for Pause**: The companies cited the need for further project maturation and supply chain development before proceeding.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation/Loss**: The articles do not specify any direct job creation or loss associated with the Gigastack project at this time.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The facility is a joint initiative between Phillips 66 and Orsted, indicating a joint venture structure for the Gigastack project.
