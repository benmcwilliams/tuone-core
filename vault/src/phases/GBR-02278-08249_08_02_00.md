### Evolutionary Timeline of the Able UK Facility in Humber, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2020**: SeAH Steel, a South Korean company, agrees to establish a monopile factory at Able UK's planned Able Marine Energy Park (AMEP). The facility will have an initial production capacity of 162,000 tonnes, which is expected to increase to 320,000 tonnes.
- **March 2021**: The AMEP facility is set to feature 1,348 meters of deepwater quays and 217 hectares of laydown area for the manufacture, storage, and laydown of large offshore wind components.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2020**: The UK government announces a £160 million investment to upgrade ports and factories for the offshore wind sector, which will support the development of the AMEP.
- **March 2021**: The AMEP facility secures funding through the government’s Offshore Wind Manufacturing Investment scheme, which is crucial for the £500 million hub port's development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2020**: A memorandum of understanding is signed between SeAH Steel and Able UK for the monopile factory at AMEP.
- **March 2021**: The project receives government backing, marking a significant milestone in its development.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2020**: The establishment of the SeAH monopile factory is projected to create around 400 new jobs.
- **March 2021**: The AMEP project is expected to facilitate up to 3,000 jobs and significant international inward investment in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2020**: Able UK is identified as the sole partner with SeAH Steel for the monopile facility, indicating that Able UK has exclusive ownership of the facility in collaboration with SeAH.
