### Evolutionary Timeline of Equinor's Facility in Humber Region, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05-11-2021**: Equinor announced the development of the **H2H Saltend** low carbon hydrogen production plant, with an initial capacity of **600 megawatts**. This facility is designed to produce hydrogen from natural gas in combination with carbon capture and storage. It is one of the world's first at-scale facilities of its kind.
- **23-06-2022**: Equinor submitted a proposal for a second facility, **H2H Production 2**, aiming for a capacity of **1.2 gigawatts** by **2028**. Together, these projects could deliver a combined hydrogen production capacity of **1.8 gigawatts**, contributing significantly to the UK’s hydrogen production goals.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **23-06-2022**: Equinor submitted a funding bid of **£16.4 million** (approximately **$19.97 million**) to the UK government's **Net Zero Hydrogen Fund** for the H2H Production 2 project. This bid includes **50% private funding** from Equinor, indicating a strong commitment to the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05-11-2021**: Announcement of the H2H Saltend project, marking the start of Equinor's hydrogen initiatives in the Humber region.
- **23-06-2022**: Submission of the funding bid for H2H Production 2, with a planned final investment decision expected in **2025**.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
