### Evolutionary Timeline of the Harbour Energy Facility at St Fergus Gas Terminal, Aberdeenshire, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2021**: Harbour Energy, in partnership with Storegga and Shell, announced plans to establish a 20 MW blue hydrogen production facility at the St Fergus gas terminal. This facility is part of the Acorn project, which aims to utilize North Sea natural gas to produce blue hydrogen while capturing and storing associated CO2 emissions.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2021**: The partnership between Storegga, Shell, and Harbour Energy is described as a significant investment in one of the largest and most mature U.K. carbon capture and storage (CCS) and hydrogen projects. Specific investment amounts were not disclosed in the article.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: The announcement of the partnership and the intention to develop the blue hydrogen production facility marks a significant milestone in the project timeline. The facility is part of the broader Acorn project, which aims to start producing hydrogen and storing CO2 by 2030.
