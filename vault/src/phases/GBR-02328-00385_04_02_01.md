## GBR-02328-00385

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ineos Facility in Grangemouth, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2021**: Ineos announced plans to invest over €1.17 billion at its Grangemouth refinery, focusing on blue hydrogen production and carbon capture and storage (CCS) to capture at least 1 million tonnes of CO2 per annum by 2030.
- **January 2022**: Ineos issued a tender for the design of a "world-scale" carbon capture-enabled hydrogen production plant at Grangemouth, aiming to displace hydrocarbon fuels with low-carbon hydrogen.
- **April 2022**: Ineos began a trial to transport hydrogen through an 18-mile decommissioned pipeline in Scotland, in partnership with SGN, to assess the feasibility of repurposing existing natural gas networks for hydrogen.
- **May 2022**: A 29-kilometre hydrogen distribution network trial was initiated, using a decommissioned pipeline from Grangemouth to Granton, funded by Ofgem.
- **September 2022**: Ineos awarded a contract to Atkins for the design of the hydrogen production plant, with plans to operationalize it by 2030.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2021**: Ineos committed to investing over €1.17 billion in the Grangemouth facility, which includes funding for blue hydrogen production and CCS.
- **January 2022**: Ineos confirmed a £1 billion investment to reduce the environmental impact of the Grangemouth refinery and achieve net zero by 2045.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2021**: Announcement of the investment plan for blue hydrogen production.
- **January 2022**: Tender issued for the design of the hydrogen production plant.
- **April 2022**: Initiation of hydrogen transport trials through a decommissioned pipeline.
- **May 2022**: Launch of a hydrogen distribution network trial.
- **September 2022**: Contract awarded to Atkins for the design of the hydrogen production plant, with operational plans set for 2030.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2022**: Ineos anticipated the creation of "several hundred" jobs during the construction of the hydrogen plant, although the operational workforce was not yet determined.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Ineos owns the Grangemouth facility exclusively, as indicated by the company's direct investments and project announcements related to the site.

This timeline captures the significant developments and strategic initiatives undertaken by Ineos at its Grangemouth facility, focusing on hydrogen production and sustainability efforts.
