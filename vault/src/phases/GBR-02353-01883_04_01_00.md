## GBR-02353-01883

### Evolutionary Timeline of the RES Facility in Orkney, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** October 2021
   - **Details:** A proposal was made to transform the Flotta terminal in Orkney into a hydrogen production hub, leveraging the abundant offshore wind resources in the North Sea. The facility aims to produce green hydrogen, which is seen as a critical alternative energy source for Scotland's energy transition.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** October 2021
   - **Details:** The project is part of a consortium involving RES, Octopus Energy, and Offshore Wind Power Limited, which includes Macquarie’s Green Investment Group, TotalEnergies, and Renewable Infrastructure Development Group (RIDG). The consortium is actively seeking investments to develop the hydrogen facility and associated offshore wind farms.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** October 2021
   - **Details:** The proposal for the green hydrogen hub was submitted, and initial assessments, including cable routing and nearshore geophysical surveys, have been completed to understand the practical aspects of project delivery.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** October 2021
   - **Details:** The project is expected to create new jobs, particularly for those transitioning from the oil and gas industry, as it aims to bring significant development to Orkney.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** October 2021
   - **Details:** The facility is not owned exclusively by RES; it is part of a joint venture with other partners, including Octopus Energy and members of the Offshore Wind Power Limited consortium.

This timeline captures the initial stages of the RES facility's development in Orkney, highlighting its focus on green hydrogen production and the collaborative nature of its ownership and investment structure.
