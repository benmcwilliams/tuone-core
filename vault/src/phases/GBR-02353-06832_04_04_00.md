### Evolutionary Timeline of the EMEC Facility in Orkney, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10-11-2020**: EMEC announced the deployment of an Invinity Energy Systems flow battery at its tidal energy test site on Eday. This system, consisting of eight battery modules, will enhance the facility's hydrogen production capabilities by providing continuous power from tidal energy. The facility's hydrogen production plant utilizes a 670 kW electrolyser, aiming for significant annual green hydrogen generation.

- **10-10-2022**: EMEC concluded the concept design for a new 100 MW floating offshore wind test and demonstration site located 20 km west of Orkney. This site will feature six berths for floating wind turbines, with four berths grid-connected and two reserved for hydrogen generation. The design aims to support the development of floating wind technologies in high-energy conditions.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10-11-2020**: The flow battery project received £1.8 million in funding from the Scottish Government, facilitated by Highlands and Islands Enterprise (HIE). This investment is aimed at integrating hydrogen production with renewable energy systems.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **10-11-2020**: Announcement of the flow battery project, expected to go live in 2021.
- **10-10-2022**: Completion of the concept design for the 100 MW floating offshore wind test facility, with plans to secure a lease for the site.

#### 4. Labour
