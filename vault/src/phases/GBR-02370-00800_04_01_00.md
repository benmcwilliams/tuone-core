## GBR-02370-00800

### Uniper Facility in Flotta, GBR - Evolutionary Timeline

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility is proposed to produce green hydrogen on an industrial scale, utilizing electricity generated from a planned offshore wind project, the West of Orkney Windfarm, with a capacity of 2 GW. The Flotta Terminal is identified as an ideal location for this production due to its favorable wind resources and proximity to shipping routes.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The project is part of a consortium that includes Macquarie’s Green Investment Group (GIG), TotalEnergies, Renewable Infrastructure Development Group (RIDG), and Repsol Sinopec, with Uniper also involved. The consortium is exploring investments and subsidies necessary for the development of the green hydrogen production facility.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - As of October 2021, the consortium has completed cable routing assessments and nearshore geophysical surveys to understand the practical aspects of project delivery. They have submitted a proposal to Crown Estate Scotland as part of the ScotWind leasing round.

4. **Labour:**
   - The project aims to retain and upskill the current workforce at the Flotta Terminal while creating long-term skilled jobs during both the construction phase and hydrogen operations.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The Flotta facility is not exclusively owned by Uniper; it is part of a consortium that includes multiple stakeholders, with Repsol Sinopec being the terminal owner and Uniper as a partner in the hydrogen production initiative. 

This timeline reflects the current status and future plans for the Uniper facility in Flotta, focusing on the development of green hydrogen production in collaboration with other companies and stakeholders.
