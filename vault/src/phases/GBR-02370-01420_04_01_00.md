## GBR-02370-01420

### Evolutionary Timeline of the TotalEnergies Facility in Flotta, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/2021**: A consortium including TotalEnergies, Macquarie’s Green Investment Group (GIG), and Renewable Infrastructure Development Group (RIDG) proposed to use electricity from a planned offshore wind project to power the production of green hydrogen on an industrial scale at the Flotta Terminal. The project aims to develop a hydrogen production facility that could supply hydrogen both nationally and internationally.

- **04/2022**: Repsol Sinopec Resources UK (RSRUK) confirmed plans to create a new hydrogen hub facility at Flotta, indicating ambitions to turn the site into an industrial-scale hub for processing low carbon fuel. The first hydrogen production is expected by 2029.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04/2022**: Funding for the hydrogen project is anticipated to be secured within the year, with the consortium (including TotalEnergies) selected to build a significant wind farm as part of the ScotWind leasing round.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2021**: The Offshore Wind Power Limited (OWPL) consortium submitted a proposal for the ScotWind leasing round, which includes plans for a 2 GW offshore wind project named the West of Orkney Windfarm.

- **04/2022**: RSRUK and CNOOC confirmed ongoing operations at the Flotta Terminal, ensuring continued oil exports until the end of field life in the 2030s. The facility has been operational since 1976 and is undergoing a transition towards hydrogen production.

#### 4. Labour
- **04/2022**: The Flotta Terminal employs approximately 150 people. The transition to a hydrogen hub is expected to support hundreds of jobs as the terminal is repurposed for new operations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **04/2022**: The Flotta Terminal is owned by Repsol Sinopec, with TotalEnergies as part of a consortium (OWPL) collaborating on the hydrogen production project. The facility has changed ownership multiple times since its inception, with RSRUK currently operating it. 

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic shifts at the TotalEnergies facility in Flotta, highlighting its transition from traditional oil operations to a focus on renewable hydrogen production.
