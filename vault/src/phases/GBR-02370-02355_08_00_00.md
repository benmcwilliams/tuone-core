### Evolutionary Timeline of Macquarie’s Green Investment Group Facility in Flotta, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12-10-2021**: A consortium led by Macquarie’s Green Investment Group (GIG), along with TotalEnergies and Renewable Infrastructure Development Group (RIDG), proposed to use electricity from a planned offshore wind project to power a green hydrogen production facility at the Flotta Terminal in Orkney. The facility aims to produce green hydrogen on an industrial scale, leveraging the proposed 2 GW offshore wind project named the West of Orkney Windfarm.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12-10-2021**: The consortium's proposal for the green hydrogen production facility is supported by local stakeholders, including the European Marine Energy Centre (EMEC) Hydrogen. The project is contingent on securing a market for green hydrogen and discussions with the Scottish and UK governments regarding necessary mechanisms for realization.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12-10-2021**: The Offshore Wind Power Limited (OWPL) consortium has completed cable routing assessments and nearshore geophysical surveys to understand the practical aspects of project delivery. The proposal was submitted during the ScotWind leasing round, indicating an early stage of project development.

#### 4. Labour
- **12-10-2021**: The project aims to retain and upskill the current workforce at the Flotta Terminal while creating long-term skilled jobs during both the construction phase and hydrogen operations. This indicates a focus on job creation as the facility transitions to a diversified energy hub.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **12-10-2021**: The Flotta Terminal is owned by Repsol Sinopec, and the consortium (GIG, TotalEnergies, and RIDG) is collaborating with Repsol Sinopec and Uniper for the development of the green hydrogen production plans. This suggests a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by Macquarie’s GIG.
