## GBR-02370-02390

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Offshore Wind Power Limited Facility in Flotta, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2021**: Offshore Wind Power Limited (OWPL), a consortium including Macquarie’s Green Investment Group, TotalEnergies, and Renewable Infrastructure Development Group (RIDG), proposed a hydrogen production facility at the Flotta terminal. This facility aims to utilize offshore wind resources in the North Sea to produce green hydrogen, leveraging the terminal's existing infrastructure.
- **April 2022**: Plans were confirmed to develop the Flotta facility into an industrial-scale hub for processing low carbon fuel, with ambitions to support hydrogen production by 2029.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2022**: OWPL was selected to build a significant wind farm as part of the ScotWind leasing round, indicating a commitment to secure funding for the hydrogen project within the year.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2021**: The proposal for the hydrogen hub was announced, marking the initial step towards establishing the facility.
- **April 2022**: Confirmation of plans to create a hydrogen hub at Flotta, with expectations for the first hydrogen production by 2029.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2022**: The Flotta terminal employs approximately 150 people, with the potential for hundreds of additional jobs as the facility transitions from oil to hydrogen production.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2022**: The facility is part of a consortium (Offshore Wind Power Limited) that includes multiple stakeholders: Macquarie’s Green Investment Group, TotalEnergies, and RIDG. This indicates a joint venture rather than exclusive ownership by a single company. 

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic plans for the Offshore Wind Power Limited facility in Flotta, highlighting its transition towards renewable energy production and the collaborative nature of its ownership.
