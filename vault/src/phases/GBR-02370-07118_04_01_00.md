## GBR-02370-07118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Repsol Sinopec Facility in Flotta, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12-10-2021**: A consortium including Repsol Sinopec is exploring the development of a green hydrogen production facility at the Flotta Terminal in Orkney. This facility aims to utilize electricity from a proposed 2 GW offshore wind project, the West of Orkney Windfarm, to produce green hydrogen on an industrial scale.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12-10-2021**: The consortium, which includes TotalEnergies and Renewable Infrastructure Development Group (RIDG), is in discussions with the Scottish and UK governments to explore necessary mechanisms for the project. Specific investment amounts or subsidies have not been detailed in the article.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12-10-2021**: The Offshore Wind Power Limited (OWPL) consortium has completed cable routing assessments and nearshore geophysical surveys as part of the project preparation. The proposal for the green hydrogen production facility is part of the ScotWind leasing round.

#### 4. Labour
- **12-10-2021**: The project is expected to retain and upskill the current workforce at the Flotta Terminal, with the potential creation of long-term skilled jobs during both the construction phase and hydrogen operations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **12-10-2021**: The Flotta Terminal is owned by Repsol Sinopec, which is collaborating with other partners in the consortium for the development of the green hydrogen facility. The ownership structure appears to be exclusive to Repsol Sinopec, although they are working in partnership with other companies for this specific project.
