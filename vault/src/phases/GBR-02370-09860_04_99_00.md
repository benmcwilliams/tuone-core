### Evolutionary Timeline of Repsol Sinopec Resources UK Facility in Flotta, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **1976**: The Flotta Terminal began operations, initially expected to last for about 15 years.
- **2022**: Repsol Sinopec Resources UK (RSRUK) confirmed plans to continue exporting oil to the Flotta Terminal from the Golden Eagle, Piper, and Claymore fields until the end of field life in the 2030s. 
- **Future Plans**: RSRUK is exploring the creation of a new hydrogen hub facility at Flotta, aiming to repurpose the site for processing low carbon fuel, with ambitions to support hundreds of jobs.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2022**: Discussions are ongoing between RSRUK and CNOOC to explore growth areas, including renewed drilling and third-party tie-backs. Funding for the hydrogen project is expected to be secured within the year, with the first hydrogen production anticipated by 2029.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2022**: Confirmation of continued operations and plans for a hydrogen hub at Flotta, with ongoing discussions about growth areas and drilling programs completed at the Golden Eagle, Piper, and Claymore fields.

#### 4. Labour
- **2022**: The Flotta facility employs 150 people, with potential to support hundreds more jobs as the terminal is repurposed for hydrogen production.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Current Ownership**: The Flotta facility is owned by Repsol Sinopec Resources UK, which acquired it from Talisman Energy. The facility has changed hands multiple times since its inception, with the most recent ownership being under RSRUK.
