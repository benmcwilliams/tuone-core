### Evolutionary Timeline of the SSE Facility in North Sea, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2022**: Seajacks UK signed contracts with Dogger Bank Wind Farm to utilize its NG2500X class vessel for accommodating technicians during the offshore substation platform hook-up and commissioning phases of the 3600MW project. The vessel will be optimized for substation work, increasing its accommodation capacity to 140 persons.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2022**: The upgrade works on the Seajacks vessel will be conducted at PD Ports Teesport UK facility, maximizing local and UK content. This indicates a commitment to local investment and development in the facility's operations.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: SSE Renewables engaged Kent for a front-end engineering design (FEED) study for the Berwick Bank offshore wind farm, which will generate over 4GW of clean energy from more than 300 turbines. This project is a significant milestone in the development of the facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2023**: The Berwick Bank offshore wind farm project is expected to create 4,600 jobs in Scotland and 9,300 jobs across the UK, contributing £8.3bn ($10.33bn) to the economy. This indicates a substantial positive impact on local and national employment due to the facility's development.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2022**: The Dogger Bank Wind Farm is a joint venture between SSE Renewables, Equinor, and Eni Plenitude, indicating that SSE does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a collaborative ownership structure.
