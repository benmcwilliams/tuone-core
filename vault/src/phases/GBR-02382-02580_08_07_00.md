### Evolutionary Timeline of the Saipem Facility in North Sea, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2021**: Saipem, in collaboration with Harland & Wolff, is set to fabricate and load-out eight jacket foundations for the 450MW Neart na Gaoithe offshore wind farm. The Methil facilities, recently acquired from BiFab, will be utilized for this purpose. This marks a significant operational engagement in the offshore wind sector in the North Sea.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2021**: The contract signed between Neart na Gaoithe owners (EDF Renewables and ESB) and Saipem, along with InfraStrata, indicates a strategic investment in local fabrication capabilities. The Methil facility's geographical proximity to the North Sea is highlighted as a key factor in this investment, aimed at enhancing the local supply chain and supporting the UK government's renewable energy goals.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: The contract for the fabrication of the jacket foundations is officially announced, with work scheduled to commence on **1 July 2021**. This project is positioned as a significant milestone for Saipem in the North Sea, showcasing the potential for local fabrication in the renewables market.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2021**: The contract is expected to create approximately **290 direct and indirect jobs** in Scotland, particularly benefiting the local workforce at the Methil facility. This job creation is framed as a positive development for the Scottish supply chain and the local economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2021**: The facility is operated by Harland & Wolff, which is owned by InfraStrata. Saipem is involved as a tier one service provider, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership of the facility. The partnership suggests a joint venture-like structure for the execution of the Neart na Gaoithe project.
