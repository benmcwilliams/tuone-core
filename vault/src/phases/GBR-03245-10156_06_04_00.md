### Evolutionary Timeline of the Sunsolar Facility in Oldbury, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations**
   - **June 2012**: Sunsolar announced plans to construct a 30MW solar PV manufacturing plant in Oldbury. The facility will produce both polycrystalline and monocrystalline PV modules, with outputs ranging from 200W to 250W. Initially, modules will be manufactured using cells purchased from Europe, but there are plans to install cell manufacturing machinery by early 2013, aiming for full UK-based production by that time.

2. **Investment and Development**
   - **June 2012**: The project received a grant of US$7.85 million (£5 million) from the Government fund, supported by Sandwell Council and the Regional Growth Fund. Sunsolar committed an additional US$7.85 million for the development of the factory.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**
   - **June 2012**: Announcement of the construction of the facility, with plans to open by the end of October 2012. The management team is also seeking a 10-acre site for a purpose-built PV manufacturing and R&D facility, anticipating outgrowing the initial site within 18 months.

4. **Labour**
   - **June 2012**: The establishment of the facility is expected to create new jobs and utilize local suppliers, contributing positively to the local economy and addressing the decline in manufacturing within the Midlands.

5. **Ownership Structure**
   - **June 2012**: The articles do not specify any joint ventures or partnerships, indicating that Sunsolar appears to have full ownership of the facility.
