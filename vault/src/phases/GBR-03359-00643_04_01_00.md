### Evolutionary Timeline of RWE Facility in Pembroke, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04-10-2023**: RWE initiated a study to investigate the quayside offload and launch of two types of floating offshore wind platforms (steel and concrete) in the Celtic Sea. This study aims to maximize local supply chain opportunities and will inform activities at the Port of Pembroke.
- **24-04-2024**: RWE announced plans to develop a green hydrogen production facility adjacent to Pembroke Power Station, with a capacity of approximately 110 MW. The facility is expected to produce two metric tonnes of hydrogen per hour.
- **19-12-2024**: Planning approval was granted for the Pembroke Green Hydrogen project, which will build 100 MW of electrolyser capacity. The project aims to supply nearby industries through a 1.5 km pipeline.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **27-02-2024**: The Pembroke Green Hydrogen Phase 2 project was included in a UK government funding announcement, receiving a share of £21 million aimed at increasing hydrogen capacity across the UK.
- **01-03-2024**: The UK government announced a total investment of over $26.5 billion in hydrogen projects, including the Pembroke Green Hydrogen project, which is expected to contribute to the UK's hydrogen capacity.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04-10-2023**: RWE announced its participation in the Crown Estate’s UK Round 5 seabed auction to secure at least 1 GW of floating offshore wind in the Celtic Sea.
- **24-04-2024**: RWE launched a community consultation for the green hydrogen production facility, inviting local feedback on the proposals.
- **19-12-2024**: RWE received planning approval for the 100 MW green hydrogen plant, marking a critical step towards project realization.

#### 4. Labour
- **24-04-2024**: Richard Little, Pembroke Net Zero Centre Director, emphasized that the green hydrogen generation project aims to safeguard local jobs and provide clean energy alternatives to support local industry.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned by RWE, which is pursuing various projects including the green hydrogen facility adjacent to Pembroke Power Station. There is no indication of joint ventures or shared ownership in the provided articles.
