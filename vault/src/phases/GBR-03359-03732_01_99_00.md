### Evolutionary Timeline of the Xlinks Facility in Pembroke, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2021**: Xlinks announced plans to build a 10.5 GW renewable energy complex in Morocco, consisting of 7 GW of solar capacity and 3.5 GW of wind capacity, along with 5 GW/20 GWh of battery storage. The facility will connect to the UK grid via a 3,800 km high voltage direct current (HVDC) transmission line, which will include four separate cables, making it the longest subsea power transmission link in the world. The project is expected to deliver 26 TWh of firm and flexible power to the UK annually, contributing to 7.5% of the UK's electricity demand.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2021**: Xlinks expects to invest approximately £18 billion in the project. The company is in discussions with the UK government regarding a contract for difference (CfD) scheme to sell the generated power at a price of around £0.048 ($0.067)/kWh.
- **May 2022**: UK investment firm Octopus Energy signed an agreement with Xlinks to develop the facility, although the financial commitment from Octopus Energy is noted to be below 0.1% of the total proposed financial requirements.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: Initial announcement of the project and its specifications, including the timeline for the first 1.8 GW cable to be operational by early 2027, with a second cable expected two years later.
- **April 2023**: The UK government began evaluating the project as part of its energy security strategy, indicating interest in the potential contribution of the Xlinks project to the UK's energy independence.
