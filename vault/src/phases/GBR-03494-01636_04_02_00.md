## GBR-03494-01636

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Equinor Facility in Hull, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **02-07-2020**: Equinor announced plans to build a large “blue” hydrogen facility in Hull, UK, as part of the Hydrogen to Humber Saltend project (H2H Saltend). The facility will utilize carbon capture, utilization, and storage (CCUS) technology to produce hydrogen from natural gas, with a production capacity of 600MW using an auto thermal reformer. The facility aims to significantly reduce carbon emissions in the area by 900,000 tonnes per year and has the potential to develop a large-scale hydrogen network.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **02-07-2020**: Equinor has not disclosed specific investment amounts for the H2H Saltend project but is part of a bid for UK government co-funding, which was launched on 23 June 2020. The UK government pledged $995m (£800m) for carbon capture schemes, which includes support for the Humberside project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **02-07-2020**: Equinor plans to make a final investment decision in 2023, with the facility expected to begin operations in 2026. The project is part of a broader initiative to create a zero-carbon industrial cluster in the Humber region.

- **29-06-2022**: Equinor, in partnership with SSE Thermal, agreed to acquire Triton Power Holdings for £341m ($413m). This acquisition includes the Saltend Power Station, a 1.2GW combined-cycle gas turbine facility, which will be used as a platform to develop low-carbon projects, including hydrogen and carbon capture opportunities.

#### 4. Labour
- **29-06-2022**: The acquisition of Triton Power will result in the transfer of 82 employees to the joint ownership of SSE Thermal and Equinor, indicating job retention and potential future job creation as the facility transitions to low-carbon projects.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **29-06-2022**: Following the acquisition of Triton Power, Equinor will jointly own and operate the Saltend Power Station with SSE Thermal, indicating a joint venture ownership structure for the facility. Prior to this, Equinor was planning the H2H Saltend project independently, but the acquisition marks a shift to shared ownership in the context of broader decarbonization efforts.
