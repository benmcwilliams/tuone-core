### Evolutionary Timeline of Siemens Gamesa Facility in Hull, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2016**: Siemens Gamesa opened its blade manufacturing facility at Alexandra Dock in Hull, UK, with the capability to produce 75-meter long blades.
- **February 2018**: The facility began producing blades for Ørsted’s Hornsea Project One and later for Hornsea Project Two, which will utilize the SG 8.0-167 DD turbines.
- **January 2019**: Siemens Gamesa leased a storage area at King George Dock for turbine blades destined for Hornsea 1.
- **February 2019**: Production of the first wind turbine blade for the 714MW East Anglia 1 offshore wind farm commenced at the Hull facility.
- **August 2021**: Siemens Gamesa announced plans to expand its Hull facility to accommodate the production of longer blades (over 100 meters).
- **February 2023**: The facility started producing 108-meter long B108 IntegralBlades for the SG 14-222 DD offshore wind turbines.
- **December 2023**: The first 108-meter blades for the Moray West offshore wind farm were produced at the Hull facility.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2021**: Siemens Gamesa announced a planned expansion of its Hull facility, supported by a £186 million investment to double the size of the manufacturing plant.
- **November 2021**: The UK government selected Siemens Gamesa for grant funding to support the expansion of its offshore blade plant in Hull.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2018**: Siemens Gamesa was selected as the exclusive supplier for Ørsted’s Hornsea Project Two.
- **January 2019**: Siemens Gamesa began transporting blades for Hornsea 1 from Hull.
- **February 2019**: The first blade for East Anglia 1 was completed.
- **August 2021**: Siemens Gamesa announced plans to expand its Hull facility to produce next-generation offshore wind turbine blades.
- **February 2023**: Production of the first 108-meter blades commenced.
- **December 2023**: The first 108-meter blades for Moray West were produced.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2019**: The Hull facility employed over 1,100 people.
- **August 2021**: The expansion plans were expected to create 200 new jobs at the Hull facility.
- **October 2021**: The workforce at the Hull facility increased to over 1,300 employees after recruiting more than 600 new staff in the past year.
- **August 2024**: A pay deal was secured for around 300 workers at the Hull facility, reflecting ongoing labor negotiations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Siemens Gamesa owns the Hull facility exclusively, as it is part of its operations under Siemens Energy.
