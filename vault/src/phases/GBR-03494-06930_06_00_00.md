### Evolutionary Timeline of the Gridserve Facility in Hull, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2019**: Gridserve announced plans to construct a 25.7 MW solar farm near Hull, which will supply renewable energy to Warrington Borough Council. The facility will utilize bifacial solar modules combined with a single-axis tracker, marking a significant advancement in solar technology in the UK.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2019**: Warrington Borough Council agreed to invest £62.34 million for the construction of two solar projects, including the Hull facility. The council expects to save up to £2 million annually on energy bills from the Hull solar farm.

- **December 2019**: The construction of the Hull solar farm was completed and the project was handed over to Warrington Borough Council. The facility is expected to supply all of the council's energy needs.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2019**: While specific job creation numbers were not detailed, the project is part of Warrington's broader 'invest to save' program, which is expected to generate an operating surplus that can be reinvested into local services, potentially leading to job creation indirectly.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2019**: The Hull solar farm will be owned by Warrington Borough Council upon completion, with Gridserve responsible for the operation and maintenance of the facility over its lifetime. This indicates a partnership model rather than exclusive ownership by Gridserve.
