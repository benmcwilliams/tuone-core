### Evolutionary Timeline of Triton Power Facility in Hull, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Facility Production:** Triton Power operates the Saltend Power Station, which is a 1.2GW combined-cycle gas turbine (CCGT) facility and combined heat and power (CHP) power station located on East Yorkshire’s Humber Estuary.
   - **Future Plans:** SSE Thermal and Equinor, upon acquiring Triton Power, expressed intentions to explore hydrogen and carbon capture opportunities at Saltend to support decarbonisation efforts.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Acquisition Investment:** SSE Thermal and Equinor agreed to acquire Triton Power Holdings for £341 million ($413 million), with the investment shared equally between the two companies. This acquisition is aimed at supporting the decarbonisation of the UK's power system.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Acquisition Announcement:** On June 29, 2022, it was announced that SSE Thermal and Equinor would acquire Triton Power from Energy Capital Partners, with the deal expected to close in September 2022, pending approval from UK National Security Filing and EU Merger Control.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Job Transfer:** The acquisition will result in the transfer of Triton Power’s 82 employees to SSE Thermal and Equinor.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Joint Ownership:** After the acquisition, SSE Thermal and Equinor will jointly own and operate Triton Power’s portfolio, indicating a joint venture structure for the facility.
