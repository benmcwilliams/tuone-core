## GBR-03497-00418

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Johnson Matthey Facility in Saltend Chemicals Park, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2023**: The H2H Saltend project is announced as a 600MW low-carbon hydrogen production plant with carbon capture capabilities. The facility aims to reduce emissions at Saltend Chemicals Park by up to one third by replacing natural gas with low-carbon hydrogen in several industrial facilities. The plant is designed to capture approximately 890,000 tonnes of CO2 per year.
- **March 2023**: Johnson Matthey (JM) signs a technology license for its LCH technology with Equinor for the H2H Saltend project. The facility aims for a carbon capture rate of over 95%, positioning it as one of the largest carbon capture and storage-enabled initiatives in the UK.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2023**: The H2H Saltend project is part of the broader Zero Carbon Humber scheme, which aims to create infrastructure for hydrogen transport and carbon capture in the region. Specific investment amounts are not detailed in the articles, but the project is part of a larger initiative to decarbonize the Humber region.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2023**: Equinor awards a front-end engineering design (FEED) contract for the H2H Saltend project to Linde Engineering. The plant is scheduled to be operational by 2027.
- **March 2023**: Johnson Matthey's LCH technology is confirmed as a key component of the H2H Saltend project, moving JM closer to its goal of securing additional large-scale projects.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific details on job creation or job loss related to the H2H Saltend project. However, the project is expected to create opportunities in the local economy by establishing a low-carbon hydrogen economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify the ownership structure of the H2H Saltend facility. However, it is indicated that Johnson Matthey is collaborating with Equinor and Linde Engineering, suggesting a partnership rather than exclusive ownership.

### Summary
The Johnson Matthey facility at Saltend Chemicals Park is part of the H2H Saltend project, which aims to produce low-carbon hydrogen and capture significant amounts of CO2. The project is a collaboration involving Equinor and Linde Engineering, with Johnson Matthey providing critical technology. The facility is set to be operational by 2027, contributing to the decarbonization of the Humber region.
