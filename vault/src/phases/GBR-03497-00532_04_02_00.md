### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mitsubishi Facility in Saltend Chemicals Park, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: The Saltend Chemicals Park, where Mitsubishi operates, manufactures around 2 million tonnes of high-value products annually, including chemicals, fuels, and construction materials.
- **Upcoming Projects**: The Hydrogen to Humber (H2H) project is set to establish a 600-megawatt (MW) auto thermal reformer (ATR) to convert natural gas to hydrogen, which will enable customers across the park to switch to hydrogen as a fuel source. This project aims to significantly reduce emissions from the park.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Plans**: The H2H project is part of a broader initiative to decarbonize the energy supplies and products at the Saltend site. The project is expected to receive further investment decisions in 2023, contingent on the results of the UK government’s CCS cluster sequencing process.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Project Announcement**: The H2H project was announced in June 2022, marking a significant step towards establishing a hydrogen and carbon capture facility at Saltend.
- **Construction Timeline**: The engineering and construction phase for the H2H project is anticipated to occur from 2024 to 2026, with production and carbon capture expected to begin between 2026 and 2027.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The development of the H2H project and the potential for new inbound investors at the Saltend site are expected to create job opportunities, although specific numbers have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The Saltend Chemicals Park is owned by px Group, which acquired full ownership in 2018. Mitsubishi is one of the resident companies at the park, but the articles do not specify whether Mitsubishi has exclusive ownership of its operations within the facility.
