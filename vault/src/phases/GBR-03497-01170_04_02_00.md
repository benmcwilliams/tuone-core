## GBR-03497-01170

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Linde Facility at Saltend Chemicals Park, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2023**: Linde Engineering was awarded a front-end engineering design (FEED) contract for the H2H Saltend project, a 600MW low-carbon hydrogen production plant with carbon capture capabilities. The plant is designed to capture 890,000 tonnes of CO2 per year and aims to reduce emissions at the Saltend Chemicals Park by up to one third by replacing natural gas with low-carbon hydrogen in various industrial facilities. The plant is scheduled to be operational by 2027.

- **March 2023**: Johnson Matthey signed a technology license for its LCH technology to be used in the H2H Saltend project, which aims for a carbon capture rate of over 95%. This project is part of a larger ambition to establish the Humber region as an international hub for low-carbon hydrogen.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2023**: The contracts awarded to Linde Engineering and BOC for the H2H Saltend project were based on their participation in a design competition, indicating a significant investment in the development of low-carbon hydrogen infrastructure in the region.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2023**: Announcement of the FEED contract for H2H Saltend, marking a significant milestone in the project’s development.
- **March 2023**: Johnson Matthey's licensing agreement for LCH technology further solidified the project's technological foundation.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2023**: The project is expected to create job opportunities in the region as it aims to support local industries and contribute to the development of a low-carbon economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is part of a collaborative effort involving Equinor, Linde Engineering, BOC, and Johnson Matthey. While specific ownership details are not provided, it indicates a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by Linde.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Linde facility at Saltend Chemicals Park, highlighting its role in advancing low-carbon hydrogen production in the UK.
