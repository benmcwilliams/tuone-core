## GBR-03497-01636

### Evolutionary Timeline of Equinor's Facility at Saltend Chemicals Park, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **02-07-2020**: Equinor announces plans to build the Hydrogen to Humber Saltend (H2H Saltend) project, a large-scale “blue” hydrogen facility at Saltend Chemicals Park. The facility will utilize a 600MW auto thermal reformer to produce hydrogen from natural gas while employing carbon capture, utilization, and storage (CCUS) to eliminate CO₂ emissions.
- **03-07-2020**: The facility is described as the largest of its kind globally, with hydrogen produced being sent to chemical plants and a nearby power station, aiming to reduce emissions by nearly 900,000 metric tons of CO₂ annually.
- **07-06-2022**: The project is confirmed to be part of a wider initiative to decarbonize the Humber region, with plans for a 30% hydrogen-natural gas blend at the Triton Power plant once H2H is operational.
- **15-08-2022**: H2H Saltend is selected to proceed as one of the first large-scale hydrogen projects in the UK, with operations expected to start in 2026/2027.
- **31-01-2023**: Linde Engineering is awarded a front-end engineering design (FEED) contract for the H2H Saltend project, which is set to be operational by 2027 and will capture 890,000 tonnes of CO₂ annually.
- **14-03-2023**: Johnson Matthey signs a technology license with Equinor for the H2H Saltend project, aiming for a carbon capture rate of over 95%.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **02-07-2020**: Equinor plans to apply for UK government co-funding for the H2H Saltend project, with a final investment decision expected in 2023.
- **15-08-2022**: The project is part of the UK Government's ambition to deploy CCUS at scale, with the East Coast Cluster selected for support, indicating potential government investment.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **02-07-2020**: Announcement of the H2H Saltend project.
- **15-08-2022**: H2H Saltend is selected to proceed in the Phase-2 cluster sequencing process, moving towards operational status in the mid-2020s.
- **31-01-2023**: FEED contract awarded to Linde Engineering, marking a significant step in project development.
- **14-03-2023**: Johnson Matthey's technology license awarded, further solidifying project plans.

#### 4. Labour
- **15-08-2022**: Equinor's senior vice president states that the project will help preserve existing jobs and create new ones, indicating a positive impact on local employment.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **03-07-2020**: The Saltend Chemicals Park is owned by px Group, which operates the site. Equinor is collaborating with px Group for the H2H Saltend project, indicating a partnership rather than exclusive ownership.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to Equinor's facility at Saltend Chemicals Park, highlighting its technological ambitions, investment strategies, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
