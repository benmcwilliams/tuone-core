## GBR-03497-09216

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ineos Acetyls Facility in Saltend Chemicals Park, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: The Saltend Chemicals Park, where Ineos Acetyls operates, manufactures around 2 million tonnes of high-value products annually, including chemicals and fuels. Ineos Acetyls specifically operates a hydrogen reformer on-site.
- **Updates**: The facility is set to benefit from the Hydrogen to Humber (H2H) Saltend project, which proposes to build a 600 MW low carbon hydrogen production plant with carbon capture and storage (CCS). This project aims to reduce CO2 emissions by nearly one million tonnes annually, representing a 30% reduction in the facility's total current emissions.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investments**: The H2H Saltend project has received backing from various industrial operators, including Ineos Acetyls, which is committed to supporting the development of low carbon hydrogen as part of the Zero Carbon Humber initiative. The project is part of a broader strategy to decarbonize the Humber region, which is the UK's most carbon-intensive industrial cluster.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Key Announcements**: 
  - **January 2022**: Equinor entered the H2H Saltend project into the second phase of the UK Government's funding competition, with formal plans submitted for the hydrogen production plant.
  - **June 2022**: The H2H project was highlighted as a significant step towards decarbonizing the Saltend Chemicals Park, with plans for construction and operation timelines indicating production and carbon capture beginning around 2026-2027.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The H2H Saltend project is expected to create significant job opportunities as it progresses, contributing to the local economy and supporting the transition to a low-carbon industrial base.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The Saltend Chemicals Park is owned by px Group, which acquired it in 2018. Ineos Acetyls operates within this facility but does not own it exclusively; it is one of several companies utilizing the park's infrastructure for their operations.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic initiatives surrounding the Ineos Acetyls facility in Saltend Chemicals Park, emphasizing its role in the transition to a low-carbon economy.
