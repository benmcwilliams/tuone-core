## GBR-03497-10257

### Evolutionary Timeline of Triton Power Facility, Saltend Chemicals Park, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Facility Type**: Triton Power operates a Combined Cycle Gas Turbine (CCGT) combined heat and power (CHP) plant.
- **Production Capacity**: The facility provides electricity, heat, and steam for various processes across the Saltend Chemicals Park.
- **Future Developments**: Triton Power plans to transition to a 30% hydrogen-natural gas blend once the Hydrogen to Humber (H2H) project is operational.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **H2H Project**: The Hydrogen to Humber (H2H) project, which will be the world's largest hydrogen and carbon capture plant, is set to significantly impact the operations at Triton Power. The initial phase includes a 600 MW auto thermal reformer (ATR) to convert natural gas to hydrogen, with completion expected to reduce emissions from the park by nearly 900,000 tonnes of CO2 per year.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Announcement of H2H Project**: The H2H project was announced in June 2022, marking a significant step towards decarbonization at the Saltend site.
- **Construction Timeline**: Equinor, the company behind H2H, expects to make a final investment decision in 2023, with engineering and construction planned from 2024 to 2026, and production and carbon capture starting around 2026-2027.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The development of the H2H project and the transition to hydrogen will likely create new job opportunities, although specific numbers have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The Saltend Chemicals Park, including the Triton Power facility, is owned by px Group, which acquired full ownership in 2018. This marks px Group's first full ownership of an asset, indicating a strategic shift in their operational model.
