### Evolutionary Timeline of the px Group Facility at Saltend Chemicals Park, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: The Saltend Chemicals Park manufactures approximately 2 million tonnes of high-value products annually, including chemicals, fuels, and construction materials.
- **Upcoming Projects**: The facility is set to host the Hydrogen to Humber (H2H) project, which will feature a 600-megawatt (MW) auto thermal reformer (ATR) to convert natural gas to hydrogen. This project aims to decarbonize energy supplies and integrate clean hydrogen as a feedstock for existing operations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Ownership Change**: px Group acquired full ownership of Saltend Chemicals Park in 2018 from BP, marking its first full ownership of an asset.
- **H2H Project Investment**: Equinor is expected to make a final investment decision on the H2H project in 2023, with construction anticipated to begin in 2024 and production starting around 2026-2027.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **H2H Project Announcement**: The H2H project was announced in June 2022, with plans for significant emissions reductions and the establishment of a hydrogen market.
- **Construction Plans**: A 22-acre area has been cleared for H2H construction, with an additional 25 acres earmarked for the plant itself. The project is part of a broader initiative to create a CO2 and hydrogen pipeline network in the region.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation Potential**: The H2H project and the anticipated expansions at Saltend are expected to create new job opportunities, although specific numbers have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Full Ownership**: px Group owns the Saltend Chemicals Park exclusively, having acquired it from BP in 2018. This marks a significant milestone for px Group as it expands its operational footprint in the clean technology sector.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic initiatives at the px Group facility in Saltend Chemicals Park, highlighting its role in the transition to cleaner energy and the establishment of a hydrogen economy in the UK.
