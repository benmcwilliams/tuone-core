## GBR-03498-00418

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Johnson Matthey Facility in Ellesmere Port, Cheshire, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Focus**: The facility is involved in the production of low carbon hydrogen as part of the HyNet North West industrial decarbonisation cluster.
- **Production Capacity**: The initial production capacity is set to be 3 TWh/y of low carbon hydrogen starting in 2025, with plans to develop another facility to manufacture twice that amount, aiming for a total capacity of more than 9 TWh/y.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Total Investment**: The project is backed by a total investment of £750 million (US$1.02 billion).
- **Government Funding**: The UK Government provided £7.5 million for a related engineering and development program, which is expected to be completed in Q2 of 2021.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Announcement Date**: The memorandum of understanding (MoU) for the joint delivery of the low carbon hydrogen production hub was signed on 15-01-2021.
- **Expected Production Start**: Hydrogen production is expected to commence in 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The project is anticipated to create and safeguard jobs in the North West England and North East Wales regions, contributing to the establishment of a hydrogen economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture**: The facility is part of a joint venture between Essar Oil UK and Progressive Energy, with Johnson Matthey also involved as a partner providing low carbon hydrogen technology. The specific ownership structure regarding Johnson Matthey's stake is not detailed, but it is indicated that they are a supporting partner in the project.
