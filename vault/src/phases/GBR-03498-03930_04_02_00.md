## GBR-03498-03930

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Essar Facility in Ellesmere Port, Cheshire, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2021**: Essar Oil UK, in partnership with Progressive Energy, announced plans to develop the UK’s first low carbon hydrogen production hub at Essar’s Stanlow refinery. The hub is set to produce an initial capacity of 3 TWh/y of low carbon hydrogen starting in 2025, with plans to develop an additional facility to manufacture twice that amount, aiming for a total capacity of over 9 TWh/y. This production will utilize natural gas and fuel gases from the refinery, with carbon dioxide being captured and stored offshore.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2021**: The project is backed by a total investment of £750 million (US$1.02 billion). Additionally, the UK Government provided £7.5 million for a related engineering and development program, which is expected to be completed by Q2 of 2021.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2021**: A memorandum of understanding (MoU) was signed between Essar Oil UK and Progressive Energy to jointly deliver the hydrogen production hub. The project is part of the HyNet North West industrial decarbonisation cluster, which is currently under development.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2021**: The project is expected to create a hydrogen economy across North West England and North East Wales, with the potential to reduce emissions and create and safeguard jobs in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2021**: The facility is a joint venture between Essar Oil UK and Progressive Energy, indicating that Essar does not own the facility exclusively but is a supporting company in the partnership. 

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic plans for the Essar facility in Ellesmere Port, highlighting its role in advancing low carbon hydrogen production in the UK.
