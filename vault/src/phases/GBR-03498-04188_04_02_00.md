## GBR-03498-04188

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Progressive Energy Facility in Ellesmere Port, Cheshire, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2021**: Progressive Energy, in partnership with Essar Oil UK, announced plans to develop the UK’s first low carbon hydrogen production hub at Essar’s Stanlow refinery. The facility is set to produce 3 TWh/y of low carbon hydrogen starting in 2025, with plans for a subsequent facility to double that capacity, aiming for a total manufacturing capacity of over 9 TWh/y. This production will contribute to the UK’s goal of achieving 5 GW of low carbon hydrogen by 2030.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2021**: The project is backed by a total investment of £750 million (US$1.02 billion). Additionally, the UK Government provided £7.5 million for a related engineering and development program, which is expected to be completed by Q2 of 2021.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2021**: A memorandum of understanding (MoU) was signed between Essar Oil UK and Progressive Energy for the joint delivery of the hydrogen production hub. The project is part of the HyNet North West industrial decarbonisation cluster, which is currently under development.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2021**: The project is expected to create a hydrogen economy across North West England and North East Wales, with the potential to create and safeguard jobs in the region, as stated by Chris Manson-Whitton, Director at Progressive Energy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2021**: The facility is a joint venture between Essar Oil UK and Progressive Energy, with both companies collaborating on the development and operation of the hydrogen production hub.
