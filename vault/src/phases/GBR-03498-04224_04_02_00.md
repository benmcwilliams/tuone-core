## GBR-03498-04224

### Evolutionary Timeline of the SNC-Lavalin Facility in Ellesmere Port, Cheshire, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Production Focus:** The facility is set to produce low carbon hydrogen as part of the UK’s first low carbon hydrogen production hub.
   - **Production Capacity:** Initially, the hub will produce 3 TWh/y of low carbon hydrogen starting in 2025, with plans to develop another facility to manufacture twice that amount, aiming for a total capacity of more than 9 TWh/y.
   - **Decarbonization Efforts:** The hydrogen production will utilize natural gas and fuel gases from Essar’s Stanlow refinery, with carbon dioxide being captured and stored offshore.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Total Investment:** The project has a total investment of £750 million (US$1.02 billion).
   - **Government Funding:** The UK Government provided £7.5 million to support a related engineering and development program, which is expected to be completed in Q2 of 2021.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **MoU Signing:** On January 15, 2021, Essar Oil UK and Progressive Energy signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) to jointly deliver the low carbon hydrogen production hub.
   - **Engineering Development:** SNC-Lavalin has advanced engineering efforts for the project, which received funding from the UK Government’s hydrogen supply competition. An investment decision is expected shortly after the completion of the engineering program in Q2 2021.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Job Creation:** The project is expected to create and safeguard jobs in the North West England and North East Wales regions as it develops a hydrogen economy.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Joint Venture:** The facility is part of a joint venture between Essar Oil UK and Progressive Energy, with SNC-Lavalin and Johnson Matthey as supporting partners. The ownership is not exclusive to any single company but rather a collaborative effort among multiple stakeholders.
