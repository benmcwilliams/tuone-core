### Evolutionary Timeline of the Great British Nuclear Facility in Wylfa, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date: May 2024**: The Wylfa site in Anglesey has been designated as the preferred location for the UK’s third mega-nuclear power station. This facility is expected to produce clean and reliable power sufficient for approximately six million homes over a 60-year lifespan. The project aims to enhance the UK's energy independence and contribute to the goal of sourcing up to a quarter of electricity needs from domestic nuclear power by 2050.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date: May 2024**: The UK government is engaging with global energy firms to explore building the new power plant, which is anticipated to bring significant investment to the area. The project is part of the UK’s largest expansion of nuclear energy in 70 years, with plans to quadruple nuclear capacity to up to 24GW by 2050.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date: May 2024**: The government has initiated discussions with potential partners for the construction of the new nuclear power station at Wylfa. This marks a significant step in reviving Wylfa's nuclear history and is the first time since the 1960s that land has been acquired for new nuclear developments in the UK.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date: May 2024**: The project is expected to create thousands of well-paid jobs and boost the local economy. The Secretary of State for Energy Security and Net Zero highlighted the potential for high-quality job creation as a significant benefit of the Wylfa project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date: May 2024**: Great British Nuclear (GBN) has agreed to purchase the Wylfa site, indicating that GBN will have ownership of the facility. GBN is also tasked with delivering the world’s fastest small modular reactor competition, further solidifying its role in the development of nuclear energy at this site.
