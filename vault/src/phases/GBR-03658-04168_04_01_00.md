## GBR-03658-04168

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Peel Environmental Facility at Protos Site, Ellesmere Port, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2019**: Peel Environmental announced the development of the first waste plastic to hydrogen facility at the Protos site, utilizing the DMG® (Distributed Modular Gasification) technology developed by PowerHouse Energy. This facility aims to convert unrecyclable plastics into hydrogen, which can be used as a low-carbon transport fuel.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2019**: The total investment for the development of 11 waste plastic to hydrogen facilities across the UK is £130 million. The Protos site is the first location for this initiative, indicating a significant financial commitment to clean technology and waste management.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2019**: A Collaboration Agreement was signed between Peel Environmental, Waste2Tricity, and PowerHouse Energy. The partnership is set to submit a planning application for the Protos site in the coming weeks, marking the initial step towards construction and operation of the facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2019**: While specific job creation figures were not mentioned in the article, the development of the facility is expected to create local employment opportunities in the clean technology sector, particularly in the areas of waste management and hydrogen production.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2019**: Peel Environmental is part of Peel L&P, indicating that the facility is under the full ownership of Peel L&P, with collaboration from Waste2Tricity and PowerHouse Energy for development and technology implementation.
