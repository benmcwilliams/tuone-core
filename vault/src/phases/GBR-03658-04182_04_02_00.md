## GBR-03658-04182

### Evolutionary Timeline of the PowerHouse Energy Facility at Protos Site, Ellesmere Port, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2019**: The facility at the Protos site is set to utilize the pioneering DMG® (Distributed Modular Gasification) technology developed by PowerHouse Energy. This technology is designed to produce hydrogen from unrecyclable plastics, addressing both waste management and hydrogen production for low-carbon transport fuels.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2019**: A collaboration agreement was signed between Peel Environmental, Waste2Tricity, and PowerHouse Energy, representing a total investment of £130 million for the development of 11 waste plastic to hydrogen facilities across the UK, with the first facility located at the Protos site.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2019**: The partnership announced plans to submit a planning application for the first facility at the Protos site in the coming weeks, marking the initial step towards construction and operation.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2019**: While specific job creation figures were not mentioned, the project is expected to contribute to local employment through the development of the facility and the associated infrastructure.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2019**: The facility is part of a collaboration involving Peel Environmental (part of Peel L&P), Waste2Tricity, and PowerHouse Energy. The ownership structure appears to be a partnership rather than exclusive ownership by any single entity, with each party contributing different resources and expertise to the project.

### Technology and Operations
- **Production Technology**: "Using a ‘UK first’ advanced thermal treatment technology the network of facilities could transform the way waste plastics are dealt with nationally."
- **Hydrogen Production**: "The pioneering DMG® (Distributed Modular Gasification) technology developed by Powerhouse Energy produces a local source of hydrogen from unrecyclable plastics."
- **Environmental Impact**: "This clean and low-cost hydrogen could be used to power buses and Heavy Goods Vehicles (HGVs), before being rolled out to hydrogen cars, helping to reduce air pollution and improve air quality on our roads."

### Investment and Development
- **Total Investment**: "Peel Environmental – part of Peel L&P – has signed a Collaboration Agreement... which would see 11 waste plastic to hydrogen facilities – representing an investment of £130 million – developed across the UK."
- **Commitment from Peel L&P**: "This contractual agreement represents a substantial commitment from Peel L&P to the wider deployment of DMG® Energy Recovery Technology in the UK."

### Project Status and Milestones
- **First Facility Location**: "This follows the announcement that the first facility will be located at the company’s 54-acre Protos site near Ellesmere Port in Cheshire."
- **Planning Application**: "The partnership is due to submit a planning application for the first site – at Peel Environmental’s Protos site near Ellesmere Port in Cheshire – in the coming weeks."

### Labour
- **Job Creation Potential**: "We’re pioneering this solution in the North West but local authorities across the country could benefit from a more sustainable way to treat waste plastic, whilst also creating a local source of low carbon transport fuel which could help them meet their climate change targets."

### Ownership Structure
- **Partnership Structure**: "Peel Environmental will provide real estate and infrastructure support, Waste2Tricity is the developer and PowerHouse Energy is the technology and engineering services provider."
