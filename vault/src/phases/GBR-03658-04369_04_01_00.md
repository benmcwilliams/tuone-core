## GBR-03658-04369

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Waste2Tricity Facility at Protos Site, Ellesmere Port, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date: August 2019**
  - The facility will utilize the pioneering DMG® (Distributed Modular Gasification) technology developed by PowerHouse Energy. This technology is designed to convert unrecyclable plastics into hydrogen, providing a local source of clean and low-cost hydrogen that can be used for various transportation applications, including buses and Heavy Goods Vehicles (HGVs).

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date: August 2019**
  - Peel Environmental, part of Peel L&P, announced a significant investment of £130 million to develop a network of 11 waste plastic to hydrogen facilities across the UK, with the first facility located at the Protos site. This investment aims to address the issue of waste plastics and contribute to the hydrogen economy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date: August 2019**
  - A Collaboration Agreement was signed between Peel Environmental, Waste2Tricity, and PowerHouse Energy to develop the facility at the Protos site. The partnership is set to submit a planning application for the facility in the coming weeks.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date: August 2019**
  - While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned in the article, the development of the facility is expected to contribute to local employment opportunities as it aims to provide a sustainable solution for waste plastic treatment and hydrogen production.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date: August 2019**
  - The facility is part of a collaboration involving multiple parties: Peel Environmental provides real estate and infrastructure support, Waste2Tricity is the developer, and PowerHouse Energy supplies the technology and engineering services. The ownership structure appears to be a partnership rather than exclusive ownership by Waste2Tricity.
