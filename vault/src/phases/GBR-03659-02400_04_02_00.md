### Evolutionary Timeline of the Peel L&P Facility in Protos, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2019**: Peel L&P Environmental announced the commitment to the next stage of development for the Protos Energy Park, specifically the FMG® plant. The facility is designed to process **35 tonnes per day** of waste plastics to produce **up to 2 tonnes of hydrogen per day**. This marked a significant enhancement in the performance of the DMG® process.
- **August 2020**: The front-end engineering design (FEED) phase was completed, confirming the facility's design and engineering aspects. The total project cost was estimated at **£20 million**. The facility will utilize **distributed modular generation (DMG)** technology developed by PowerHouse Energy Group.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2020**: The estimated project cost for the Protos facility was confirmed at **£20 million** during the completion of the FEED phase. Peel L&P Environmental has plans to develop **11 facilities** across the UK, with the option for exclusive rights to Powerhouse Energy technology, potentially leading to **over 70 facilities** in total.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2019**: Announcement of the commitment to the next stage of development for the Protos facility.
- **August 2020**: Completion of the FEED phase, marking a significant step towards operational readiness.
- **June 2022**: The Protos facility was confirmed to be the first project to begin construction as part of Peel NRE’s broader strategy to roll out waste plastic to hydrogen facilities across the UK.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2020**: The development of the Protos facility is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers were not detailed in the articles. The overall initiative to roll out multiple facilities across the UK suggests a significant potential for job creation in the clean technology sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure

The articles do not specify joint ventures or partnerships that would dilute this ownership, suggesting that Peel L&P Environmental has exclusive rights to the facility's operations.
