### Evolutionary Timeline of the Shell Facility in St Fergus, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2021**: The Acorn project, located at the St Fergus gas plant, is highlighted as a key initiative in the UK’s Hydrogen Strategy. It aims to repurpose North Sea oil and gas infrastructure for carbon capture and storage (CCS) and to produce low-carbon hydrogen. The Scottish Cluster, which includes Acorn, is expected to provide 1.3 GW of low-carbon hydrogen production by 2030, scaling up to 3.7 GW by 2050.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2021**: The UK Government's Hydrogen Strategy outlines a target of 5 GW of low-carbon hydrogen production capacity by 2030, with an anticipated £4 billion in investment and over 9,000 jobs created across the sector.
- **January 2023**: The Acorn CCS project is reliant on government funding to deliver an industrial-scale CCS development, which is projected to create over 20,000 jobs in its first phase. However, delays in the Track 2 funding process from the UK Government pose risks to investment continuity.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2021**: The Acorn project is recognized as part of the UK’s first Hydrogen Strategy, marking a significant milestone in the development of hydrogen infrastructure.
- **January 2023**: Scottish energy secretary Michael Matheson warns of potential investor withdrawal from the Acorn CCS project due to ongoing delays in the government’s funding process, which could impact the timeline for the project.

- **January 2023**: The Acorn CCS project is expected to create over 20,000 jobs in its first phase, highlighting the significant employment potential tied to the project.
