### Evolutionary Timeline of the Storegga Facility in St Fergus, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2021**: The Acorn project at St Fergus is highlighted as a key initiative for low-carbon hydrogen production, aiming for 1.3 GW of hydrogen production by 2030 and scaling up to 3.7 GW by 2050. The project is part of the Scottish Cluster, which is critical for hydrogen adoption in the UK.
- **April 2022**: The Acorn project is confirmed to be a significant component of the Scottish Cluster, focusing on carbon capture and storage (CCS) and blue hydrogen production from natural gas.
- **June 2023**: Plans for a blue hydrogen production scheme at St Fergus are reiterated, with a proposed first phase including a 200 MW hydrogen plant, contingent on the success of the Acorn CCS component.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2021**: The UK Government's Hydrogen Strategy aims for 5 GW of low-carbon hydrogen production capacity by 2030, with the Acorn project identified as a key contributor.
- **March 2022**: Concerns are raised about the lack of government backing for CCS and hydrogen, which could impact investment in the Acorn project.
- **January 2023**: Scottish Energy Secretary warns of potential investor withdrawal from the Acorn project due to delays in government funding and the Track 2 process for CCS development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2021**: The Acorn project is mentioned as part of the UK’s first Hydrogen Strategy, with expectations for significant hydrogen production.
- **March 2022**: Acorn is noted as a reserve project in the CCS process, missing out on initial funding but expected to compete in the second tranche.
- **June 2023**: The project is still awaiting support via Track 2, with hopes to produce hydrogen by 2027, although delays are a concern.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2022**: The Acorn project is projected to support 20,600 jobs at its peak in 2031, with an average of over 15,000 jobs annually through to 2050.
- **June 2023**: The project is expected to create significant employment opportunities, although the exact numbers may vary based on the project's progress and local labor market conditions.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2021**: Storegga is identified as the lead developer of the Acorn CCS project, which is a joint initiative involving Harbour Energy and Shell, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by Storegga.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones related to the Storegga facility at St Fergus, focusing on its role in the hydrogen economy and carbon capture initiatives in the UK.
