### Evolutionary Timeline of the Acorn Facility in St Fergus, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations**:
   - The Acorn facility, located at the St Fergus gas plant near Peterhead, is focused on developing an industrial-scale carbon capture and storage (CCS) system. It is a key component of the "Scottish Cluster," which aims to store industrial emissions underground.

2. **Investment and Development**:
   - The Acorn project is reliant on government funding, particularly from the £1bn CCS development fund from Westminster. However, there have been delays in the Track 2 process, which is crucial for securing this funding.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**:
   - As of January 2023, the project is facing significant timeline delays, which have raised concerns among investors about potential withdrawal from the project. The UK Government is expected to provide an update on the Track 2 process in Spring 2023, but no specific confirmation on funding for Acorn has been made.

4. **Labour**:
   - The first phase of the Acorn project is projected to create over 20,000 jobs, although the timeline for this job creation is uncertain due to the ongoing delays.

5. **Ownership Structure**:
   - The Acorn facility is a collaborative project involving multiple partners, including Harbour Energy, Shell, North Sea Midstream Partners, and project leader Storegga. It is not owned exclusively by any single company but operates as a joint venture among these partners. 

This timeline reflects the current status and challenges faced by the Acorn facility as of January 2023, highlighting the importance of government support and the potential impact of delays on investment and job creation.
