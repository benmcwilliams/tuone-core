## GBR-03930-02141

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Air Liquide Facility in Runcorn, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production**: The Air Liquide facility in Runcorn produces hydrogen, specifically harnessing waste hydrogen as a by-product from an industrial chlor-alkali plant.
- **Future Updates**: From 2023, the hydrogen production will transition to a greener method, utilizing electrolysis powered by a direct connection to an offshore wind farm.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Funding**: The facility has been supported by significant funding for hydrogen initiatives. Specifically, around £6 million ($8.38 million) from Transport for London (TfL), over £5 million ($7 million) from European bodies (including the Fuel Cells and Hydrogen Joint Undertaking and the Innovation and Networks Executive Agency), and £1 million ($1.4 million) from the Office of Zero Emission Vehicles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Key Announcements**: The hydrogen produced at the Runcorn facility is being utilized to fuel the first hydrogen-powered double-decker buses in London, which were launched on June 23, 2021. 
- **Construction and Production Starts**: The facility's hydrogen production supports the new hydrogen refueling station completed by Nel Hydrogen, which is part of the infrastructure for the hydrogen bus fleet.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: While specific job creation numbers related to the Runcorn facility were not detailed in the articles, the overall investment in hydrogen infrastructure and the introduction of hydrogen buses are expected to contribute positively to job creation in the sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The articles do not specify whether Air Liquide owns the Runcorn facility exclusively or if it is part of a joint venture. However, it is implied that Air Liquide is the primary producer of hydrogen for the buses.

### Summary
The Air Liquide facility in Runcorn plays a crucial role in the production of hydrogen for the UK's first hydrogen-powered double-decker buses, with plans for greener production methods in the future. The facility has received substantial funding to support its operations and the broader hydrogen initiative in the UK.
