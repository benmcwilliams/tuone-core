### Evolutionary Timeline of the Toyota Facility in Derbyshire, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** July 2011
   - **Details:** Toyota Motor Manufacturing UK partnered with British Gas to install a 4.6MW solar power plant at its facility in Derbyshire. The installation consists of 17,000 Sharp 245W monocrystalline panels covering an area of 90,000m². The solar power generated is intended to supply electricity for the production of Auris, Auris Hybrid, and Avensis cars, with an expected output sufficient to manufacture 7,000 vehicles annually.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** July 2011
   - **Details:** The total cost of the solar power installation is £10 million, funded by British Gas. Toyota will benefit from the energy produced without incurring installation costs, as British Gas will handle the installation and receive feed-in tariff payments.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** July 2011
   - **Details:** The solar power plant installation is part of Toyota's 'Sustainable Plant' vision, aiming to align manufacturing operations with environmental sustainability. The project is expected to be completed before the August 1 feed-in tariff deadline set by the UK Government.
