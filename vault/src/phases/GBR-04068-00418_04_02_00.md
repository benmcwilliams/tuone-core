### Evolutionary Timeline of the Johnson Matthey Facility in Tees Valley, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** October 2023
   - **Details:** Johnson Matthey (JM) is utilizing its Low Carbon Hydrogen (LCH) technology for the H2Teesside project. This technology combines a gas-heated reformer with an autothermal reformer (GHR-ATR), which is noted for having the lowest natural gas usage commercially available and the capability to capture up to 99% of carbon dioxide produced. The facility aims to deliver the lowest levelized cost of hydrogen (LCOH) and the most carbon-efficient technology for low carbon (blue) hydrogen production.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** October 2023
   - **Details:** The H2Teesside project, along with NZT Power and BOC Teesside Hydrogen, has been selected by the UK Government as a Track-1 Capture Project. This selection allows the project to proceed to negotiations for government funding from the Department for Energy Security & Net Zero (DESNZ) as part of the East Coast Cluster's Phase 2 for Carbon Capture, Utilization, and Storage (CCUS).
