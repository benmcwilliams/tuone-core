### Evolutionary Timeline of the British Solar Renewables Facility in Bradenstoke, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2015**: The Bradenstoke solar plant, developed by British Solar Renewables (BSR), was commissioned with a production capacity of 69.8 MW, comprised of 269,000 solar PV modules. It generates approximately 65 GWh of energy annually.
- **January 2018**: The facility is noted as England's largest solar PV power plant and the UK's second largest, with a 50 MW AC export capacity.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2018**: The Bradenstoke plant receives a subsidy in the form of 1.4 Renewable Obligations Certificates (ROCs) per MWh, which supports its operational viability in a competitive energy market.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 31, 2015**: The Bradenstoke solar plant was officially commissioned.
- **January 18, 2018**: A five-year power purchase agreement (PPA) was signed between BSR and Shell Energy Europe for the off-take of energy from the Bradenstoke plant.
