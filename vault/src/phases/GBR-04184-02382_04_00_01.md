## GBR-04184-02382

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Northern Gas Networks Facility in Buxton, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05-07-2019**: The Northern Gas Networks (NGN) facility at the Health and Safety Executive’s Science and Research Centre in Buxton was unveiled as the world’s first 100% hydrogen testing facility. The facility is designed to conduct controlled tests to establish safety evidence for a hydrogen gas network, aiming to support the conversion of the UK gas grid from natural gas to hydrogen. The facility is part of the H21 project, which plans to deliver clean energy to nearly 4 million homes and 40,000 businesses in the North of England by 2034.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05-07-2019**: Ofgem awarded £9 million to Northern Gas Networks through the Network Innovation Competition to support the establishment of the hydrogen testing facility.
- **03-12-2019**: The H21 project received an additional £6.8 million for phase two of its hydrogen network research, which will involve simulating network operations and further testing at the Buxton facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05-07-2019**: The H21 100% hydrogen testing facility was officially launched.
- **03-12-2019**: Phase one of the H21 project, focusing on leakage testing, was reported to be ongoing, with completion expected in January 2020 and a full report due in Summer 2020. Phase two was announced to involve further operational simulations and public engagement.

#### 4. Labour
- **05-07-2019**: The H21 project is expected to create and sustain thousands of high-quality jobs as it progresses towards the goal of a hydrogen-based gas grid, contributing to the UK’s decarbonisation efforts.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is led by Northern Gas Networks in collaboration with partners including Cadent, SGN, Wales & West Utilities, and others. It is not exclusively owned by Northern Gas Networks but is part of a joint initiative involving multiple stakeholders in the gas industry.
