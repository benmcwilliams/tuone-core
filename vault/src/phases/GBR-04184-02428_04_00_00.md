### SGN Facility in Buxton, GBR - Evolutionary Timeline

1. **Technology and Operations**
   - **Date**: 05-07-2019
   - **Details**: The SGN facility in Buxton is part of the world’s first 100% hydrogen testing facility. It is designed to conduct controlled tests to establish safety evidence for a hydrogen gas network, aiming to prove that it is as safe as the current natural gas grid. The facility is integral to the H21 project, which seeks to convert the UK gas networks to carry 100% hydrogen, potentially impacting nearly 4 million homes and 40,000 businesses in the North of England by 2034.

2. **Investment and Development**
   - **Date**: 05-07-2019
   - **Details**: Ofgem awarded £9 million to Northern Gas Networks through the Network Innovation Competition to support the establishment of the hydrogen testing facility. This investment is part of a broader initiative to innovate and decarbonize heating in the UK.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**
   - **Date**: 05-07-2019
   - **Details**: The facility was officially unveiled as the world’s first 100% hydrogen testing facility. This milestone marks a significant step in the H21 project, which aims to facilitate the transition to a hydrogen-based gas grid.

4. **Labour**
   - **Date**: 05-07-2019
   - **Details**: The project is expected to create and sustain thousands of high-quality jobs as it supports the transition to a hydrogen economy and the associated technological innovations.

5. **Ownership Structure**
   - **Date**: 05-07-2019
   - **Details**: The facility is part of a collaborative effort involving multiple partners, including Northern Gas Networks, Cadent, SGN, and Wales & West Utilities, indicating that it is not solely owned by SGN but is part of a joint venture among these companies.
