## GBR-04184-03844

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Cadent Facility in Buxton, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations**
   - **Date**: 05-07-2019
   - **Details**: The Cadent facility in Buxton is part of the world’s first 100% hydrogen testing facility. It is designed to conduct controlled tests to establish safety evidence for a hydrogen gas network, aiming to prove that it is as safe as the current natural gas grid. The facility is part of the H21 project, which aims to support the conversion of the UK gas networks to carry 100% hydrogen, with the potential to deliver clean energy to nearly 4 million homes and 40,000 businesses in the North of England by 2034.

2. **Investment and Development**
   - **Date**: 05-07-2019
   - **Details**: Ofgem awarded £9 million to Northern Gas Networks through the Network Innovation Competition to support the H21 hydrogen testing facility. This investment is aimed at facilitating innovation in decarbonisation of heating.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**
   - **Date**: 05-07-2019
   - **Details**: The facility was officially unveiled as the world’s first 100% hydrogen testing facility. This milestone marks a significant step towards the UK's goal of achieving net-zero carbon emissions by 2050.

4. **Labour**
   - **Date**: 05-07-2019
   - **Details**: The project is expected to create and sustain thousands of high-quality jobs as it supports the transition to a hydrogen-based gas grid and contributes to the decarbonisation of heating.

5. **Ownership Structure**
   - **Date**: 05-07-2019
   - **Details**: The facility is part of a collaborative project led by Northern Gas Networks (NGN) in partnership with Cadent, SGN, Wales & West Utilities, the Health and Safety Executive (HSE) Science and Research Centre, and DNV-GL. This indicates a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by Cadent.
