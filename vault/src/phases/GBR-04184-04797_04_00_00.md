### Evolutionary Timeline of the DNV-GL Facility in Buxton, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05-07-2019**: The DNV-GL facility in Buxton was unveiled as the world’s first 100% hydrogen testing facility. The facility is designed to conduct controlled tests to establish safety evidence for a hydrogen gas network, aiming to prove that it is as safe as the current natural gas grid. The facility is part of the H21 project, which aims to support the conversion of the UK gas networks to carry 100% hydrogen, potentially impacting nearly 4 million homes and 40,000 businesses in the North of England by 2034.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05-07-2019**: Ofgem awarded £9 million to Northern Gas Networks through the Network Innovation Competition to support the establishment of the hydrogen testing facility. This investment is part of a broader initiative to innovate and decarbonize heating in the UK.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05-07-2019**: The facility was officially launched, marking a significant milestone in hydrogen technology development. The announcement highlighted the importance of the facility in achieving the UK’s Net Zero targets and the potential for large-scale hydrogen conversion.

#### 4. Labour
- **05-07-2019**: The project is expected to create and sustain thousands of high-quality jobs as it supports the transition to a hydrogen-based gas grid and the broader hydrogen economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is part of a collaborative project involving multiple partners, including Northern Gas Networks, Cadent, SGN, Wales & West Utilities, the Health and Safety Executive (HSE) Science and Research Centre, and DNV-GL. This indicates a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by DNV-GL.
