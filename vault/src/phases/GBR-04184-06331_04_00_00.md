## GBR-04184-06331

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Wales & West Utilities Facility in Buxton, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05/2019**: The facility at the Health and Safety Executive’s Science and Research Centre in Buxton was unveiled as the world’s first 100% hydrogen testing facility. It is designed to conduct controlled tests to establish safety evidence for a hydrogen gas network, aiming to prove it is as safe as the current natural gas grid. The facility is part of the H21 project, which seeks to support the conversion of the UK gas networks to carry 100% hydrogen.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05/2019**: Ofgem awarded £9 million to Northern Gas Networks through the Network Innovation Competition to support the establishment of the H21 hydrogen testing facility. This investment is aimed at facilitating the transition to low carbon forms of heating and supporting the decarbonisation of the gas grid.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05/2019**: The H21 hydrogen testing facility was officially launched, marking a significant milestone in the UK’s efforts to explore hydrogen as a clean energy source. The facility is expected to play a crucial role in determining the feasibility of converting millions of homes from natural gas to hydrogen.

#### 4. Labour
- **05/2019**: The project is anticipated to create and sustain thousands of high-quality jobs as it supports the innovation and implementation of hydrogen technology across various sectors, including transportation and industry.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **05/2019**: The facility is part of a collaborative effort involving multiple partners, including Northern Gas Networks, Cadent, SGN, Wales & West Utilities, the HSE Science and Research Centre, and DNV-GL. This indicates that the facility is not owned exclusively by Wales & West Utilities but is part of a joint venture among several organizations focused on hydrogen technology development. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones related to the Wales & West Utilities facility in Buxton, highlighting its role in advancing hydrogen technology and contributing to the UK's decarbonisation goals.
