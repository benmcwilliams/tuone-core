## GBR-04203-00968

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Carlton Power Facility in Polmaise, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2023**: Carlton Power announced the development of the Stirling Green Hydrogen scheme, which will include a 10 MW electrolyser capable of producing approximately 1,000 tonnes of green hydrogen annually. The facility will utilize electricity generated primarily from wind and solar power. The project is designed to allow for additional capacity as demand from energy-intensive users in the area increases.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2023**: The Stirling Green Hydrogen scheme is part of Carlton Power's broader strategy to secure government support through the Hydrogen Business Model and Net Zero Hydrogen Fund. The project is expected to receive financial backing from the UK Government, although specific investment amounts have not been disclosed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2023**: Carlton Power and Superglass signed a partnership agreement to support the development of the Stirling Green Hydrogen scheme. The project is contingent on obtaining planning consent and a final investment decision (FID) within the next 12-18 months, with aspirations to have the hub operational by 2026.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2023**: The Superglass manufacturing facility, which is located near the proposed hydrogen hub, employs 200 people. The introduction of the hydrogen facility is expected to create opportunities for local businesses to transition to hydrogen, potentially leading to job creation in the region as the hydrogen economy develops.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2023**: The Stirling Green Hydrogen facility will be owned by the Green Hydrogen Energy Company (GHECO), a joint venture between Carlton Power and Schroders Greencoat. This indicates that Carlton Power does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a collaborative ownership structure.
