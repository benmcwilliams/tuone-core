### Evolutionary Timeline of the Cementation Skanska Facility in Bentley Works, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2022**: Cementation Skanska, in collaboration with ULEMCo and the Building Research Establishment (BRE), initiated the ZECHER project (Zero Carbon Hydrogen Construction Equipment for Real-world use). This project aims to develop the world’s first dual-fuel hydrogen and diesel piling machine. The project will convert a Soilmec SR30 rotary and CFA piling rig at the Bentley Works facility to explore the viability of hydrogen fuel for construction site decarbonisation. The rig currently operates on diesel, consuming 100 litres per day and emitting 262 kg of CO2.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2022**: The ZECHER project received a grant of £418,613 from Phase 1 of the UK government’s Red Diesel Replacement programme to support the development of the dual-fuel technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2022**: Announcement of the ZECHER project, which includes the physical conversion of the piling rig and aims to demonstrate a reduction of up to 50% in CO2 emissions during its operation.
