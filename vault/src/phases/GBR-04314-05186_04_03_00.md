### H2Terminals Facility Timeline - Thames Estuary, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **03/2024**: H2Terminals, in collaboration with Lloyd's Register and other partners, announced plans for a project that involves the floating production and transport of hydrogen. The facility will convert green hydrogen generated on a floating island into its liquid state for transportation to a terminal on the Thames Estuary. The hydrogen will then be converted to electricity and hot water on floating barges, with byproducts including liquid nitrogen and oxygen.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **03/2024**: The project is part of the Thames Estuary Growth Board action plan, which aims to attract investment and development to the area. H2Terminals is set to offer thirty-year Power Purchasing Agreements to industry and utility companies, indicating a significant investment in the hydrogen production capacity aligned with the UK government's target for low-carbon hydrogen production by 2030.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **03/2024**: An MoU was signed between H2Terminals and Lloyd's Register to collaborate on the project, which includes the development of a wind, wave, and solar energy plant located 100 kilometers offshore. The project is positioned as a sustainable logistics network along the River Thames.

#### 4. Labour
- **03/2024**: Gerry Wilkinson, CEO at H2Terminals, highlighted the project's potential to deliver jobs and investment opportunities to the Thames Estuary region, although specific job creation numbers were not mentioned.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **03/2024**: The article does not specify the ownership structure of the H2Terminals facility, but it indicates collaboration with multiple partners, including Lloyd's Register, HiDROGEN, and D3IM, suggesting a joint venture or partnership model rather than exclusive ownership.
