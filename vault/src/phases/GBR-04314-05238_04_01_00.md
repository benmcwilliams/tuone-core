### Evolutionary Timeline of the HiDROGEN Facility in Thames Estuary, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **03/2024**: The HiDROGEN facility is involved in the production and transport of green hydrogen. The facility will utilize a floating production system located 100 kilometers offshore, where green hydrogen will be generated and converted into its liquid state for transportation. The hydrogen will be shipped to a terminal on the Thames Estuary, where it will be converted to electricity and hot water, along with byproducts like liquid nitrogen and oxygen. The facility will also incorporate battery storage on floating barges for electricity during transit.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **03/2024**: The project is part of the Thames Estuary Growth Board action plan, which aims to attract investment and development to the region. The UK government has set ambitious targets for low-carbon hydrogen production capacity by 2030, indicating potential future investments tied to these goals.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **03/2024**: An MoU was established between Lloyd's Register, H2 Terminals, HiDROGEN, and D3IM to collaborate on the project. This includes assessing technology, developing asset designs, and defining assurance programs for floating hydrogen production and energy export systems. The project is positioned as a significant step towards creating a sustainable logistics network along the River Thames.

#### 4. Labour
- **03/2024**: The project is expected to deliver jobs and investment opportunities to the Thames Estuary region, as stated by Gerry Wilkinson, CEO at H2 Terminals. Specific job creation numbers were not detailed, but the emphasis on job opportunities suggests a positive impact on local employment.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **03/2024**: The ownership structure of the HiDROGEN facility is not explicitly detailed in the article. However, it is indicated that the facility is part of a collaborative effort involving multiple partners, including Lloyd's Register, H2 Terminals, and D3IM, suggesting a joint venture or partnership model rather than exclusive ownership by HiDROGEN.
