## GBR-04314-05511

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Lloyd's Register Facility in Thames Estuary, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **03/2024**: Lloyd's Register (LR) announced a collaboration with h2terminals and other partners for a UK hydrogen project. The facility will focus on the floating production and transport of hydrogen, with plans to develop a wind, wave, and solar energy plant located 100 kilometers offshore. The green hydrogen produced will be converted to liquid form and transported to a terminal on the Thames Estuary, where it will be converted to electricity and hot water on floating barges. The project includes the development of asset designs and qualification of containment systems for floating hydrogen production.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **03/2024**: The project is part of the Thames Estuary Growth Board action plan, which aims to attract investment and development to the region. The UK government has set ambitious targets for low-carbon hydrogen production capacity by 2030, indicating potential future investments tied to these goals.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **03/2024**: An MoU was signed to formalize the collaboration and define an assurance program for floating hydrogen production and energy export systems. LR awarded consulting company Ricardo an approval in principle (AiP) for the design of a multi-megawatt containerized fuel cell power plant solution as part of the Sustainable Hydrogen Powered Shipping (sHYpS) project. Additionally, LR granted an AiP to H2SITE for its ammonia to hydrogen technology.

#### 4. Labour
- **03/2024**: The project is expected to create jobs and investment opportunities in the Thames Estuary region, as stated by Gerry Wilkinson, CEO at H2Terminals. The demand for green hydrogen is projected to increase significantly, which will likely lead to further job creation in the sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **03/2024**: The facility is part of a collaborative effort involving multiple partners, including Lloyd's Register, H2Terminals, HiDROGEN, and D3IM. The ownership structure appears to be a joint venture rather than exclusive ownership by Lloyd's Register. 

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Lloyd's Register facility in the Thames Estuary, GBR, as of March 2024.
