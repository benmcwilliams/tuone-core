## GBR-04382-01010

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Sizewell, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04-11-2022**: The Sizewell C nuclear power facility is proposed to have a capacity of 3.2 GW, enough to power approximately six million UK households. Development consent was granted in July 2022.
- **21-02-2023**: EDF indicated that hydrogen production could be a potential byproduct at Sizewell C, which is designed to feature two EPR reactors.
- **25-07-2023**: The UK government announced a £170 million investment to accelerate preparations for Sizewell C, which is expected to generate low-carbon energy for six million homes and avoid nine million tonnes of CO₂ emissions annually.
- **19-09-2023**: The search for private investment in Sizewell C was launched, with the project expected to produce around 3.2 GW of electricity.
- **21-12-2023**: The project is confirmed to be operational by mid-2034, with both reactors expected to be online.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04-11-2022**: The UK government planned to acquire a 20% stake in Sizewell C, with EDF also purchasing an equal stake, anticipating further investments from private investors.
- **25-07-2023**: The UK government injected £170 million into Sizewell C to support construction preparations.
- **31-08-2023**: Additional funding from the Department for Energy Security and Net Zero was announced to ramp up activities at the site, including training facilities for apprentices.
- **10-01-2024**: The UK government committed an additional £1.3 billion to Sizewell C, marking the largest investment to date, aimed at early construction works.
- **24-01-2024**: The government confirmed a further £1.3 billion investment to keep Sizewell C on schedule and support local community initiatives.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04-11-2022**: Development consent for Sizewell C was granted in July 2022.
- **25-07-2023**: The project was confirmed to be the first nuclear power plant in the UK in 30 years, with construction expected to create up to 10,000 jobs.
- **19-09-2023**: The formal search for private investment was launched.
- **21-12-2023**: The High Court ruled the approval of Sizewell C lawful, allowing the project to proceed.
- **24-01-2024**: A Development Consent Order was issued, allowing construction to begin.

#### 4. Labour
- **25-07-2023**: Sizewell C is expected to create 900 permanent jobs and thousands of jobs during maintenance outages, with up to 10,000 jobs during the construction phase.
- **31-08-2023**: The project will support the training of 1,500 apprentices through onsite facilities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **04-11-2022**: Initially, EDF held an 80% stake in Sizewell C, with China General Nuclear (CGN) holding 20%. However, the UK government later acquired a 50% stake in November 2022, leading to CGN's exit from the project.
- **24-01-2024**: The UK government solidified its position as the majority shareholder in Sizewell C with ongoing investments.
