## GBR-04382-02198

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Energy Facility in Sizewell, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2024**: EDF Energy announced the commencement of the formal construction phase for the Sizewell C project. The facility will feature two EPRs (European Pressurized Reactors) with a total production capacity of 3.2 GW of electricity, sufficient to power approximately six million homes. The design is a replica of the Hinkley Point C plant currently under construction in Somerset.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2024**: A funding package of GBP 250 million (USD 318 million) was announced for the local community, which will be released in phases during the construction. This includes GBP 23 million for community projects, GBP 100 million for environmental initiatives, GBP 12 million to support local tourism, and GBP 12 million for a housing fund aimed at boosting private housing and tourist accommodation.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2020**: EDF Energy submitted a Development Consent Order (DCO) for the Sizewell C plant.
- **July 2022**: The project received permission from the Planning Inspectorate to proceed with construction.
- **September 2023**: The UK government, Sizewell C, and EDF initiated an equity raise process to attract private investors for the project. A final investment decision (FID) is anticipated later in 2024.
- **January 2024**: Formal construction commenced, marking a significant milestone for the project.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2024**: The Sizewell C project is expected to create 1,500 apprenticeships and support thousands of jobs across the country. At least one-third of the peak construction workforce of 7,900 is projected to come from the local area. The project aims to invest in local skills to ensure a talent pipeline for both the construction and operation of Sizewell C, as well as for future nuclear initiatives in the UK.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify the ownership structure of the Sizewell C facility, but it is implied that EDF Energy is the primary company involved in the project, with potential private investors being sought through the equity raise process.
