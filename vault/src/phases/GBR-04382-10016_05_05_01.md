### Evolutionary Timeline of Sizewell C Ltd Facility in Sizewell, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: Sizewell C is designed to feature two EPRs (European Pressurized Reactors) producing a total of 3.2 GW of electricity, sufficient to power approximately six million homes for at least 60 years.
- **Updates**: The facility aims to replicate the design of the Hinkley Point C plant, with the goal of achieving quicker construction and lower costs based on lessons learned from that project.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Initial Investment**: In November 2022, the UK government announced an investment of GBP 679 million (USD 845 million) to become a 50% partner with EDF in the Sizewell C project.
- **Additional Funding**: In summer 2023, a further GBP 511 million was allocated to the project to facilitate progress towards a final investment decision.
- **Infrastructure Funding**: In January 2024, GBP 1.3 billion of government funding was approved for necessary infrastructure work, including roads and rail lines.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Nuclear Site Licence**: On May 8, 2024, the Office for Nuclear Regulation (ONR) issued a nuclear site licence for Sizewell C, marking a significant milestone in the project. This licence allows Sizewell C Ltd to request permission to start nuclear-related construction.
- **Earthworks**: As of May 2024, earthworks are underway at the site, indicating progress towards construction.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The project is expected to create numerous jobs during the construction phase, although specific job numbers have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: Sizewell C Ltd is a standalone company that is majority-owned by the UK government. Initially, EDF held an 80% stake in the project, with China General Nuclear (CGN) holding 20%. However, due to changing geopolitical circumstances, EDF plans to retain only a minority stake (maximum of 20%) in the final investment decision.
