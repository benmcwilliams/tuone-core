## GBR-04384-02198

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Energy Facility in Heysham, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Production**: The facility is involved in a project to demonstrate solid oxide electrolysis (SOEC) integrated with nuclear heat and electricity to produce low-carbon hydrogen. This technology aims to improve hydrogen production efficiency by 20% compared to conventional electrolysis.
- **Future Plans**: The project will utilize hydrogen as fuel for asphalt production, a first of its kind globally. The facility is divided into two nuclear power plants: Heysham 1 (operational since 1983, expected to shut in 2026) and Heysham 2 (operational since 1988, expected to end in 2028).

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Funding**: In November 2022, the UK Department for Business Energy and Industrial Strategy awarded almost GBP 400,000 for a feasibility study of the Bay Hydrogen Hub - Hydrogen4Hanson project. 
- **Additional Funding**: On September 15, 2023, a further GBP 6.1 million was announced for the project, which will be matched by project partners, bringing total funding to over GBP 15 million for the development of hydrogen production, distribution, and end-use technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Feasibility Study**: Conducted with initial funding in November 2022.
- **Funding Announcement**: September 15, 2023, announced additional funding for project development.
- **Future Construction**: A decision on construction and supporting works at Heysham 2 is expected to be made in early 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The project is anticipated to contribute to job creation in the nuclear and hydrogen sectors, although specific numbers have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: EDF Energy owns the facility, which is part of a consortium that includes Hanson, National Nuclear Laboratory (NNL), and Vulcan Burners for the hydrogen project. The facility operates under EDF's management, indicating full ownership by EDF Energy.

This timeline captures the key developments and future plans for the EDF Energy facility in Heysham, GBR, focusing on its role in advancing clean technology through nuclear-generated hydrogen production.
