### Evolutionary Timeline of MHI Vestas Facility in Isle of Wight, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2015**: MHI Vestas begins manufacturing 82-meter-long offshore wind turbine blades at the Isle of Wight factory.
- **April 2018**: The facility produces blades for the V164 offshore wind turbines with a nameplate capacity of up to 9.5MW, expected to produce around 150 blades yearly.
- **October 2018**: MHI Vestas announces plans to increase capacity by adding a second blade mould at the Isle of Wight facility.
- **January 2019**: The second manufacturing line at the Isle of Wight factory is set to open.
- **June 2020**: MHI Vestas signs a contract to supply 114 V164-10MW turbines for the Seagreen offshore wind farm, with blades supplied from the Isle of Wight.
- **March 2022**: Vestas announces the introduction of a new manufacturing line for the V174-9.5MW turbine blades at the Isle of Wight facility.
- **December 2024**: Vestas announces plans to repurpose the Isle of Wight factory for onshore wind blade production due to declining demand for offshore blades.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2018**: MHI Vestas announces an investment of £42 million in the Isle of Wight facility, which is expected to create 1,100 new jobs and provide significant economic benefits to the region.
- **2020**: Vestas has invested close to £1 billion with UK suppliers, including over £100 million per year since 2017.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2018**: Williams Shipping begins transporting blades from the Isle of Wight factory.
- **September 2018**: Vestas relocates testing activities to facilitate further expansion of MHI Vestas' offshore wind business.
- **October 2018**: MHI Vestas starts a major recruitment drive to support the expansion of the Isle of Wight facility.
- **December 2018**: MHI Vestas announces the arrival of a new blade mould and plans to start production on a second manufacturing line.
- **March 2019**: MHI Vestas announces the creation of nearly 400 new jobs due to recent investments.
- **June 2020**: MHI Vestas secures a supply contract for the Seagreen project, further solidifying its operations in the UK.
- **March 2022**: Production of V174 blades begins at the Isle of Wight facility.
- **December 2024**: Vestas announces the repurposing of the Isle of Wight factory for onshore wind blade production.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2018**: MHI Vestas plans to hire hundreds of new workers, increasing the workforce from over 300 to support the expansion.
- **November 2018**: The expansion is expected to create 1,100 new jobs.
- **March 2019**: Nearly 400 new highly skilled jobs are created as a result of the investment.
- **December 2024**: The transition to onshore production will sustain approximately 300 manufacturing jobs, but 300 jobs are at risk of redundancy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- MHI Vestas is a joint venture between Mitsubishi Heavy Industries and Vestas Wind Systems, indicating that the facility is not under exclusive ownership but rather a collaborative effort between the two companies.
