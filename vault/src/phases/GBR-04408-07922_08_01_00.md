### Evolutionary Timeline of MHI Vestas Offshore Wind Facility in Isle of Wight, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production**: The facility produces offshore wind turbine blades, specifically for the V164-9.5 MW turbines.
- **Production Capacity**: The Triton Knoll project will utilize 90 turbines, each capable of generating 9.5 MW, contributing to a total capacity of 857 MW for the offshore wind farm.
- **Updates**: The first 15 turbine blades manufactured at the Isle of Wight facility were delivered to Able Seaton Port for the Triton Knoll project.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investments**: A multi-million pound investment was made to transform Able Seaton Port into a specialist assembly and construction facility for the Triton Knoll project, which includes the establishment of a full turbine logistics and pre-assembly hub by MHI Vestas.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date**: November 2020
  - **Milestone**: The first turbine blades produced at the Isle of Wight facility were delivered to Able Seaton Port, marking a significant step in the Triton Knoll offshore wind farm project.
- **Construction Start**: The transformation of Able Seaton Port began in November 2019 to prepare for the Triton Knoll project.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The establishment of the turbine logistics and pre-assembly hub at Able Seaton Port has led to the involvement of local suppliers and technicians, indicating job creation in the region. Specific job numbers were not mentioned in the article.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: MHI Vestas Offshore Wind is involved in the Triton Knoll project, which is jointly owned by RWE, J-Power, and Kansai Electric Power. RWE manages the construction and long-term operation of the wind farm on behalf of its partners, indicating that MHI Vestas does not have exclusive ownership of the facility but is a key supplier for the project.
