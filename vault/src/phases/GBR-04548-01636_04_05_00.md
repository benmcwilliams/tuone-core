## GBR-04548-01636

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Equinor Facility in Aldbrough, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2021**: SSE and Equinor announced plans for the Aldbrough Hydrogen Storage facility, which aims to be one of the world’s largest hydrogen storage facilities. The facility will initially have a capacity of at least 320 GWh, significantly larger than any existing facility globally. The project involves upgrading existing caverns or creating new ones to store low-carbon hydrogen.
- **January 2022**: Contracts were awarded to Atkins and ERM for the feasibility study and environmental assessments for the hydrogen storage facility. The facility is expected to be operational by early 2028.
- **March 2024**: The Aldbrough Hydrogen Pathfinder Project is set to integrate a salt cavern storage facility (20 GWh), an electrolytic hydrogen production facility (35 MWe), and a low-carbon hydrogen power production through an Open Cycle Gas Turbine (OCGT) with a capacity of up to 50 MWe. The project aims to produce and store hydrogen at a rate of 4,000 kg/h.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2023**: The Aldbrough Hydrogen Project was selected by the UK Government for the final stage of the Net Zero Hydrogen Fund, which supports the development of new low-carbon hydrogen production. This selection indicates significant governmental backing for the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2021**: Announcement of the Aldbrough Hydrogen Storage facility plans.
- **January 2022**: Awarding of key contracts to Atkins and ERM, marking an important milestone in the project’s development.
- **March 2023**: An Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) Scoping Report was published.
- **August 2023**: The project was selected for the final stage of the Net Zero Hydrogen Fund.
- **2024**: Construction works are expected to begin, taking around 36 months, with production anticipated to start in 2028.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2021**: The project is expected to create and safeguard jobs in the region as it develops the hydrogen storage capacity, contributing to the local economy and supporting the transition to a low-carbon economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Aldbrough facility is co-owned by SSE Thermal and Equinor, indicating a joint venture structure for the hydrogen storage project. The existing Aldbrough Gas Storage facility has been operational since 2011 and is also co-owned by the same companies.
