## GBR-04551-02431

### Evolutionary Timeline of the SSE Thermal Facility in Keadby, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2021**: SSE Thermal and Equinor announced plans for the Keadby Hydrogen Power Station, proposed to be the world’s first 100% hydrogen-fired power station. This facility is part of a broader initiative to develop hydrogen production and storage capabilities in the Humber region, which is crucial for decarbonizing power generation and heavy industry.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2022**: SSE Thermal and Equinor acquired Triton Power for £341 million, which includes the Saltend Power Station. This acquisition is part of their strategy to transition to hydrogen, with plans to incorporate 30% hydrogen into the Saltend Power Station by 2027, eventually aiming for 100% hydrogen operation.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2021**: Announcement of the Keadby Hydrogen Power Station as part of the broader hydrogen initiative in the Humber region.
- **June 2022**: Completion of the acquisition of Triton Power, which includes the Saltend Power Station, marking a significant step towards the hydrogen transition.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2021**: SSE Thermal indicated that the development of the Aldbrough Hydrogen Storage facility and the Keadby Hydrogen Power Station would safeguard and create crucial jobs in the region, although specific job numbers were not detailed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2022**: The acquisition of Triton Power by SSE Thermal and Equinor established a 50:50 joint venture for the ownership of the Saltend Power Station, indicating a collaborative approach to the facility's operations and future developments.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic moves related to the SSE Thermal facility in Keadby, focusing on its transition towards hydrogen technology and the collaborative efforts with Equinor.
