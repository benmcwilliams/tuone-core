### Evolutionary Timeline of the KBR Facility in Stanlow, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **September 2023:** KBR is awarded a contract for the engineering design of a second hydrogen plant (HPP2) at the Stanlow complex. This facility will utilize "blue" hydrogen process technology and has a planned capacity of up to 1,000MW. Together with the first plant (HPP1), the total capacity for hydrogen production at the Stanlow complex will reach 1,350MW. Production is expected to commence in 2026.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **September 2023:** HPP2 has received funding from the Net Zero Hydrogen Fund, indicating financial support for its development. HPP1 has already been selected as one of the two initial large-scale hydrogen projects by the UK Government, which suggests significant governmental investment and interest in the project.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **September 2023:** KBR's contract for HPP2 marks a significant milestone in the development of the hydrogen facility at Stanlow. HPP1 is already in the process of moving into bilateral negotiations with the UK Government, indicating progress in its development.

4. **Labour:**
   - The articles do not specify job creation or job loss directly associated with the HPP2 project or the overall facility at this time.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is part of a joint venture between Essar Energy and Progressive Energy (now rebranded as EET Hydrogen). KBR is providing engineering and technology services but does not own the facility. The ownership structure indicates that the facility is not exclusively owned by a single company but is a collaborative effort within the Essar Energy Transition portfolio.
