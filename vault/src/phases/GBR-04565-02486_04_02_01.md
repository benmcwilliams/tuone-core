### Evolutionary Timeline of the Vertex Hydrogen Facility in Stanlow, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2022**: Vertex Hydrogen is overseeing the design, development, and construction of the UK’s first large-scale, low-carbon hydrogen plant, which will produce more than 1 GW of hydrogen. The facility will utilize waste gases from the Stanlow refinery and natural gas, incorporating carbon capture and storage (CCS) technology.
- **February 2023**: Vertex Hydrogen aims to expand its capacity to 3.8 GW of blue hydrogen for the UK market.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2022**: The hydrogen project represents a £1 billion investment as part of the HyNet plan, with Kent awarded a major contract for the project.
- **February 2023**: Essar Group announced a $2.4 billion green investment program in the UK, which includes investments in blue and green hydrogen, ammonia, biofuels, and the decarbonization of refineries. This investment is part of the Essar Energy Transition (EET) program, which aims to create a leading energy transition hub in the North West of England.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2022**: The hydrogen plant is expected to begin production in 2026.
- **February 2023**: Essar Group announced plans for the construction of a carbon capture and storage (CCS) facility at the Stanlow refinery, with an investment of $430 million, aiming to operate by 2027.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2023**: The investments made through the EET program are expected to create highly-skilled job opportunities, contributing to a sustainable future for the Stanlow facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2022**: Vertex Hydrogen is a joint venture between Essar Energy and Progressive Energy, indicating that the facility is not owned exclusively by one company but rather shared between these two entities.
