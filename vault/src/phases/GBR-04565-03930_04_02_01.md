## GBR-04565-03930

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Essar Facility in Stanlow, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05-11-2020**: Essar Oil UK is involved in the development of the UK’s first Low Carbon Hydrogen Plant at the Stanlow refinery, which will produce 3 TWh of low carbon hydrogen annually. The facility aims for a carbon capture rate of over 95%, capturing 600,000 tonnes of CO2 per year.
- **26-05-2021**: Essar receives £7.2 million to build the UK’s first refinery furnace that can be entirely fuelled by hydrogen, with construction starting in 2021 and completion expected by September 2023. The furnace will reduce CO2 emissions by 11% annually.
- **11-02-2022**: Plans are unveiled for a new £45 million hydrogen-fuelled furnace at the Stanlow refinery.
- **27-06-2022**: A hydrogen-powered furnace arrives at the Port of Liverpool, set to be installed at Stanlow, which will run on 100% hydrogen by 2026, replacing three existing furnaces and reducing emissions by 242,000 tonnes of CO2 annually.
- **27-02-2023**: Essar announces a $3.6 billion investment plan, with $2.4 billion allocated to the Stanlow site, aiming for a 75% reduction in carbon emissions by 2030.
- **13-07-2024**: Essar Energy Transition launches EET Hydrogen Power, Europe’s first hydrogen-ready combined heat and power plant at Stanlow, with a capacity of 125 MW and a projected reduction of 740,000 tonnes of CO2 per year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05-11-2020**: The UK government awards £7.5 million to the HyNet project, which includes the hydrogen plant at Stanlow.
- **26-05-2021**: The UK government provides £7.2 million to support the hydrogen furnace project at Stanlow.
- **27-02-2023**: Essar announces a $2.4 billion investment in the UK, focusing on the Stanlow site as part of its energy transition strategy.
- **13-07-2024**: The investment into EET Hydrogen Power is part of a broader $3 billion energy transition initiative at Stanlow.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05-11-2020**: Announcement of the Low Carbon Hydrogen Plant project at Stanlow.
- **26-05-2021**: Construction of the hydrogen furnace begins, with completion expected by September 2023.
- **27-06-2022**: Arrival of the hydrogen-powered furnace at the Port of Liverpool for installation at Stanlow.
- **27-02-2023**: Announcement of the $3.6 billion investment plan, with a focus on hydrogen and decarbonisation projects.
- **13-07-2024**: Launch of EET Hydrogen Power, with construction expected to complete in 2027.

#### 4. Labour
- **27-02-2023**: The investment plans are expected to create and protect highly skilled job opportunities in the North West of England as part of the decarbonisation efforts.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Essar Oil UK exclusively owns the Stanlow facility, as indicated by its involvement in all projects and investments related to the site. The facility is part of the Essar Energy Transition (EET) initiative, which consolidates various Essar ventures focused on low carbon energy.
