## GBR-04565-08853

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Essar Energy Facility in Stanlow, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2022**: Announcement of the HyNet project, which includes the development of the UK’s first large-scale, low-carbon hydrogen plant. The facility will produce more than 1 GW of hydrogen by capturing waste gases from the Stanlow refinery and natural gas. The hydrogen plant is expected to begin production in **2026**.
- **September 2023**: A second hydrogen plant, referred to as HPP2, is planned at the Stanlow complex. Together with HPP1, the total capacity is projected to be up to **1,350 MW**. HPP1 is set to produce around **1 GW** of hydrogen annually, with production also commencing in **2026**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2022**: The hydrogen plan represents a **£1 billion investment** by the joint venture of Essar Energy and Progressive Energy (now EET Hydrogen).
- **September 2023**: HPP2 has received funding from the **Net Zero Hydrogen Fund**, supporting its development alongside HPP1.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2022**: Vertex Hydrogen (joint venture of Essar Energy and Progressive Energy) is awarded a contract for the design work of the hydrogen project at HyNet.
- **September 2023**: KBR is awarded a contract for engineering design work for HPP2 at the Stanlow complex. HPP1 has been selected as one of the two initial large-scale hydrogen projects by the UK Government, moving into the next stage of negotiations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The hydrogen project is a **joint venture** between Essar Energy and Progressive Energy (now EET Hydrogen). Initially, Vertex Hydrogen was a subsidiary of Essar Oil UK but has transitioned to a sister company within the Essar Energy Transition portfolio.
