## GBR-04823-02198

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Energy Facility in Sizewell C, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2023**: The Environment Agency granted environmental permits for Sizewell C, which is expected to host two EPR reactor units producing 3.2 GWe, similar to the Hinkley Point C plant.
- **November 2022**: EDF stated that construction of Sizewell C remained subject to a Final Investment Decision (FID) that depended on achieving certain key stages, particularly the ability to raise necessary financing.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2023**: The UK government made available an additional £341 million ($434 million) to help prepare the site for construction, on top of the existing £870 million investment from the Department for Energy Security and Net Zero (DESNZ) Capital Budgets.
- **January 2024**: A further £1.3 billion of government funding was approved to enable necessary infrastructure work, such as roads and rail lines, to continue pending a FID.
- **September 2023**: The UK government launched a pre-qualification for potential investors as the first stage of an equity raise process.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2020**: EDF Energy submitted a development consent order (planning application) for Sizewell C.
- **July 2022**: The development consent order was granted.
- **March 2023**: Environmental permits were granted, allowing the project to progress.
- **End of 2024**: The UK government aims to reach FID, subject to receiving relevant approvals.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2024**: Julia Pyke and Nigel Cann, joint managing directors at Sizewell C, indicated that the subsidy scheme enhances the ability to deliver key works, which may include job creation related to developing local infrastructure and progressing significant earthworks onsite.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2023**: Sizewell C Ltd, a standalone company majority-owned by the UK government, signed a deal with EDF Energy to purchase the freehold of the land for the new power plant. This indicates a joint ownership structure where the UK government holds a majority stake while EDF Energy is a key partner in the project.
