## GBR-04835-05293

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hygen Energy Facility in Bradford, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2023**: The proposed Bradford Hydrogen Production Facility is set to generate approximately 16 tonnes of hydrogen per day, enough to power all the buses in Bradford. The facility will be located on the Northern Gas Networks Birkshall site and will include solar panels for electric charging points and an on-site skills and training center.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2023**: Northern Gas Networks submitted an application for £6.8 million into the BEIS Net Zero Hydrogen Fund to help develop the site, which has been shortlisted for funding. This funding will cover 20% of the project costs, with the remaining funding sourced from the private sector.
- **December 2023**: The project received backing from the Hydrogen Production Business Model / Net Zero Hydrogen Fund, which will support its development into one of the largest low carbon hydrogen production centers in the UK.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2023**: The facility is in the proposal stage, with public consultations planned for September 2023. A planning application is expected to be submitted to Bradford Council later in 2023, with construction anticipated to start in mid-2024 and operations aimed for 2025.
- **December 2023**: The project is confirmed as a joint venture between Hygen and N-Gen, with significant implications for local decarbonization efforts.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2023**: It is projected that around 30 jobs could be created during the construction phase of the facility. Once operational, the facility is expected to contribute to the local economy significantly.
- **December 2023**: The project could create over 9,000 high-quality jobs by 2030, contributing to Bradford's economic growth.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2023**: The facility is a joint venture between Hygen and N-Gen, indicating that Hygen does not own the facility exclusively but shares ownership with N-Gen.
