## GBR-04835-05626

### Evolutionary Timeline of the N-Gen Facility in Bradford, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2023**: The proposed N-Gen facility aims to produce approximately **16 tonnes of hydrogen per day**, sufficient to power all the buses in Bradford. The facility will be located on the Northern Gas Networks Birkshall site and will include refuelling facilities for hydrogen-powered and electric vehicles.
- **December 2023**: The facility is referred to as the **Bradford Low Carbon Hydrogen (BLCH) production facility**, which will utilize renewable energy to produce hydrogen, positioning it as one of the largest low carbon hydrogen production centers in the UK.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2023**: Northern Gas Networks submitted an application for **£6.8 million** into the BEIS Net Zero Hydrogen Fund to help develop the site, which has been shortlisted for funding. This funding is expected to cover **20% of the project funding**, with the remainder sourced from the private sector.
- **December 2023**: The project received backing from the **Hydrogen Production Business Model / Net Zero Hydrogen Fund**, indicating government support for its development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2023**: The facility is in the proposal stage, with a planning application expected to be submitted to Bradford Council later in 2023. If approved, construction could commence in **mid-2024**, with the facility anticipated to be operational by **2025**.
- **December 2023**: The project is confirmed as a joint venture between Hygen and N-Gen, with significant implications for local decarbonization efforts, potentially equivalent to removing **800 diesel buses** from West Yorkshire roads.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2023**: It is projected that around **30 jobs** could be created during the construction phase of the facility. 
- **December 2023**: The project is expected to create over **9,000 high-quality jobs** by **2030**, contributing significantly to the local economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2023**: The facility is a **joint venture** between **Hygen** and **N-Gen**. Hygen is responsible for building, owning, and operating commercial-scale hydrogen production facilities, while N-Gen focuses on energy development and decarbonization solutions. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and projections related to the N-Gen facility in Bradford, highlighting its significance in the transition to low carbon energy solutions.
