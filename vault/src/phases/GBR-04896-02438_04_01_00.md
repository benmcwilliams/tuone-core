### Evolutionary Timeline of the ScottishPower Facility in Felixtowe, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations**
   - **Date**: 10-08-2022
   - **Details**: ScottishPower, a subsidiary of the Iberdrola group, is planning to build a green hydrogen plant at the port of Felixtowe. The facility will produce 14,000 tonnes of renewable hydrogen (H2) per year in its first phase, with the potential to double its capacity in the future. The plant will utilize an electrolyser to split water into hydrogen and oxygen using electricity from renewable sources. It will supply hydrogen to vehicles and machinery at the port, capable of fuelling up to 1,300 green hydrogen trucks and providing clean fuel for trains transporting goods to the port. The facility may also be used for the production of green ammonia or ethanol.

2. **Investment and Development**
   - **Date**: 10-08-2022
   - **Details**: The project is backed by an investment of approximately €170 million (£150 million) aimed at contributing to the decarbonisation of the largest freight port in the UK.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**
   - **Date**: 10-08-2022
   - **Details**: The facility is expected to be operational in 2026. The project is being developed in partnership with Hutchison Ports and will be located on brownfield land within the port, similar in size to a football pitch.
