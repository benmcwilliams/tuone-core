### Evolutionary Timeline of InterGen Facility at Rocksavage Power Plant, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **June 2021:** InterGen announced plans to collaborate with HyNet North West to develop a zero-carbon power station at the Rocksavage Power Plant. The facility aims to blend hydrogen with natural gas, with the goal of transitioning to 100% net zero power generation by 2028. This would make Rocksavage the first plant in the UK to blend hydrogen with natural gas.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **June 2021:** The partnership with HyNet North West is part of a broader initiative to decarbonize the North West and North Wales region, replacing fossil fuels with clean hydrogen. Specific investment amounts were not detailed in the article, but the project is expected to significantly contribute to regional clean growth and job creation.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Mid-2020s:** The project is expected to begin in the mid-2020s, with the first stage of the hydrogen network anticipated to be available at Runcorn. This will allow InterGen to modify the existing generating plant to start blending hydrogen with natural gas.

4. **Labour:**
   - **June 2021:** The HyNet North West project is expected to safeguard existing jobs and create thousands of new employment opportunities in the region, although specific job creation numbers were not provided.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **June 2021:** The article does not specify the ownership structure of the Rocksavage facility, but it indicates that InterGen is the operator of the facility in collaboration with HyNet North West for the hydrogen blending project. 

This timeline captures the key developments and plans for the InterGen facility at Rocksavage Power Plant as of June 2021, focusing on its transition towards a zero-carbon power generation model.
