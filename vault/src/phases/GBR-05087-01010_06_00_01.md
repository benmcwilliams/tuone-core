## GBR-05087-01010

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Buckinghamshire, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 06-09-2024
   - **Details:** EDF Renewables is developing the Rosefield Solar Farm project, which will include solar panels and battery storage. The project is classified as a nationally significant infrastructure project (NSIP) and is located between Buckinghamshire and Aylesbury. While the exact planned generation capacity has not been publicly clarified, EDF has a grid connection agreement with National Grid at the East Claydon Substation that allows for the export of up to 500MW of electricity to the grid. The final capacity will depend on the development's design, which is still under consultation.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 06-09-2024
   - **Details:** The project is co-owned by EDF Renewables and PS Renewables. The consultation process for the project includes changes that reduce the area proposed for solar panels by almost 40% while increasing the area set aside for nature fivefold. The project is part of a broader effort to secure development consent and funding through the UK government’s Contracts for Difference scheme.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 06-09-2024
   - **Details:** The second consultation for the Rosefield Solar Farm project has opened, running for eight weeks starting from 18 September 2024. This follows an initial consultation in September 2023. The feedback from this consultation will inform the final proposals for the development consent order (DCO) application.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
