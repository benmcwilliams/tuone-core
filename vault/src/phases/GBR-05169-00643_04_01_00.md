### Evolutionary Timeline of the RWE Facility in Port of Tilbury, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **08/2023**: RWE, in collaboration with Mitsui and the Port of Tilbury, announced the development of a green hydrogen project. The facility will initially focus on a small-scale 'proof of concept' demonstrator project aimed at producing green hydrogen to decarbonise port equipment. An initial study for a 10 megawatt green hydrogen plant is also part of this initiative, with plans to scale up to over 100 megawatts over a ten-year period.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **08/2023**: The project is part of a memorandum of understanding (MoU) between RWE, Mitsui, and the Port of Tilbury, indicating a commitment to invest in green hydrogen technology as part of the UK’s strategy to achieve net-zero emissions by 2050.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **08/2023**: The MoU was signed to initiate two parallel work streams: the proof of concept for green hydrogen production and the feasibility study for the 10 megawatt plant. The project aims to transform land previously used for coal-fired power generation into a facility for green hydrogen production.

#### 4. Labour
- **08/2023**: The project is expected to create new job opportunities in the local area, contributing to the development of skilled green jobs as part of the transition to a low-carbon economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **08/2023**: The facility is a collaborative project involving RWE, Mitsui, and the Port of Tilbury, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by any single entity.
