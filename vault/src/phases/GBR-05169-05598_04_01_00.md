## GBR-05169-05598

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mitsui Facility in Port of Tilbury, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date**: 08-07-2023
  - **Details**: Mitsui, in collaboration with RWE and the Port of Tilbury, is developing a green hydrogen project. The initial phase includes a small-scale 'proof of concept' demonstrator project aimed at producing green hydrogen to decarbonise port equipment. Additionally, there is an initial study for a 10 megawatt green hydrogen plant, with plans to scale up to over 100 megawatts over a ten-year period. The facility will be located on land previously occupied by a coal-fired power station.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date**: 08-07-2023
  - **Details**: The project is part of a memorandum of understanding (MoU) between RWE, Mitsui, and the Port of Tilbury, focusing on developing green hydrogen production as part of the UK’s strategy to achieve Net Zero by 2050. Specific investment amounts are not detailed in the article.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date**: 08-07-2023
  - **Details**: The MoU was signed to initiate two parallel work streams: the proof of concept project and the study for the 10 megawatt hydrogen plant. The project aims to support the Port of Tilbury's commitment to carbon neutrality by 2032 and Net Zero by 2042.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date**: 08-07-2023
  - **Details**: The project is expected to create new job opportunities in the local area, contributing to the development of a low-carbon economy and skilled green jobs, as highlighted by Dehenna Davison MP and other stakeholders.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date**: 08-07-2023
  - **Details**: The facility is being developed as a collaborative project under a joint venture framework involving Mitsui, RWE, and the Port of Tilbury, indicating that Mitsui does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a partnership. 

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic initiatives related to the Mitsui facility at the Port of Tilbury as of July 2023.

### Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: The facility is developing an initial study into a "10 megawatt green hydrogen plant" and aims to scale up development "over a ten year period upwards of 100 megawatts."
- **Production Focus**: The facility will produce green hydrogen for "decarbonising items of port equipment by switching from fossil fuels to hydrogen."

### Investment and Development
- **Investment Goals**: Mitsui aims to achieve "net zero-emissions status by 2050" and "halve its greenhouse gas (GHG) impact by 2030," indicating a commitment to sustainable investment in the facility.

### Project Status and Milestones
- **Project Initiation**: The project is part of a "recently signed memorandum of understanding (MoU)" between RWE, Mitsui, and the Port of Tilbury, indicating a collaborative effort to develop the hydrogen facility.

### Labour
- **Job Creation**: The project is expected to create "exciting new careers for people," contributing to local job opportunities as part of the Freeport initiative.

### Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture**: The facility is not solely owned by Mitsui; it is a collaborative project involving RWE and the Port of Tilbury, indicating a joint venture structure.
