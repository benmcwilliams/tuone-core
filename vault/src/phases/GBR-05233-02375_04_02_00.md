## GBR-05233-02375

### Evolutionary Timeline of Neptune Energy Facility at Theddlethorpe Gas Terminal, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/05/2021**: Neptune Energy proposed the DelpHYnus project, which involves the development of a combined carbon capture and blue hydrogen production facility at the former Theddlethorpe Gas Terminal site. The project aims to transport and store CO2 for the South Humber industrial area and produce blue hydrogen. The company has completed subsurface and well screening work to identify suitable storage locations and assess the feasibility of the development.

- **12/07/2021**: Neptune Energy signed agreements to progress the blue hydrogen and CO2 transport and storage scheme. The project is expected to deliver an operational carbon capture and storage (CCS) hub by 2025 and large-scale blue hydrogen generation as early as 2027. The development will repurpose existing pipelines for CO2 transport, aiming to keep costs low and reduce environmental impacts.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12/07/2021**: Neptune Energy entered into a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with an unnamed industrial gas generation organization for the blue hydrogen plant development. Additionally, it signed an MoU with PX Group, which will operate a new power plant for the blue hydrogen facility and act as an investor. The project is positioned to align with UK government targets for net-zero emissions.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/05/2021**: Neptune submitted an application to the Oil and Gas Authority (OGA) for a carbon storage licence, which is expected to be awarded within six months. The project is in the appraisal phase, with a focus on developing a detailed technical proposal.

- **12/07/2021**: The project is on track to establish an operational CCS hub by 2025 and commence large-scale blue hydrogen production by 2027.

#### 4. Labour
- **12/07/2021**: The development of the DelpHYnus project is expected to create jobs in the South Humber area, particularly in the construction and operation of the blue hydrogen plant and associated infrastructure.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **12/05/2021**: Neptune Energy is the primary operator of the facility, with plans to collaborate with various partners for the development of the blue hydrogen plant and CO2 transport and storage solutions. The ownership structure appears to be primarily under Neptune Energy, with partnerships for specific operational aspects.
