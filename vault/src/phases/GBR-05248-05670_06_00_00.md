### Evolutionary Timeline of NextEnergy Capital Facility in Strensham, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2020**: NextEnergy Capital (NEC) announced plans for the Strensham solar facility, which has a production capacity of **40MW**. This facility is part of a larger initiative that includes another site in Llanwern, South Wales, totaling **115MW** across both projects.
- **April 2021**: The Strensham facility was successfully constructed and energized within **seven months**, covering **141 acres** across two parcels of land. 

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2020**: NEC secured a **£100 million** non-recourse debt financing from Santander to fund the construction of the Strensham facility, along with the Llanwern site. The total constructed value for Strensham was approximately **£25 million**.
- **August 2022**: NEC announced the first close of its NextPower UK ESG fund at **£327 million**, aimed at investing in new-build subsidy-free solar assets, including the operational Strensham facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2020**: Announcement of the Strensham project as part of a broader subsidy-free solar initiative.
- **April 2021**: The Strensham solar facility was energized, marking its operational status.
- **August 2022**: The Strensham facility was highlighted as one of the seed assets in the NextPower UK ESG fund, which aims to double the amount of subsidy-free solar power in the UK.
