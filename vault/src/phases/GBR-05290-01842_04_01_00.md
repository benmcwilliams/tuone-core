### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ørsted Facility in Yorkshire, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Focus**: The facility is involved in the production of green hydrogen through electrolysis, utilizing electricity generated from Ørsted’s 1.3 GW Hornsea Two wind farm located off the Yorkshire coast.
- **Production Capacity**: The facility is part of the ITM Gigastack Hydrogen Project, which aims to develop a 100 MW industrial-scale electrolysis system.
- **Updates**: A 5 MW pilot project has already been operational, demonstrating the feasibility of green hydrogen production.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Commitment**: In an open letter to the UK government, Ørsted, along with other energy companies, expressed willingness to invest £1.5 billion in hydrogen projects, contingent on the development of a clear government strategy for green hydrogen.
- **Government Support**: The UK government announced a £7.5 million support package for the study phase of the clean energy project, alongside a broader £70 million package for the hydrogen industry.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Announcement of Collaboration**: Ørsted partnered with ITM Power and Phillips 66 to advance the ITM Gigastack Hydrogen Project, which was highlighted in the article published on 10-11-2020.
- **Pilot Project**: The 5 MW pilot project is already operational, indicating progress towards larger-scale production.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation Potential**: The green hydrogen sector is expected to create jobs, particularly in industrial sectors such as steelmaking, which can utilize hydrogen to replace fossil fuels as a feedstock. The overall green hydrogen industry in the UK could sustain up to 120,000 jobs by 2050, as per a report by the Offshore Wind Industry Council.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The facility is part of a collaborative effort involving Ørsted, ITM Power, and Phillips 66, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by Ørsted.
