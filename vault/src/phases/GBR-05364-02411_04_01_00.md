### Evolutionary Timeline of the Powerhouse Energy Group Facility in Rothesay Dock, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2022**: The facility at Rothesay Dock is designed to produce sustainable hydrogen from non-recyclable plastics, with a production capacity of 13,500 tonnes per year. The hydrogen generated will serve as a clean fuel for heavy goods vehicles (HGVs), buses, and cars. Plans include a linked hydrogen refuelling station on-site.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2022**: The facility has received approval for a £20 million investment aimed at supporting the UK's energy transition to Net Zero. This investment is part of a broader initiative to increase hydrogen production in the UK.

- **09/2022**: The West Dunbartonshire facility was approved by the West Dunbartonshire Council, marking it as the second waste plastic to hydrogen plant in the UK. Construction is expected to take around 15 months, with the project being part of a larger rollout of Powerhouse Energy's technology across the UK.
