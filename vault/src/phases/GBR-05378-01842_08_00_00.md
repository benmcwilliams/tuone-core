## GBR-05378-01842

### Ørsted Facility at Humber Refinery, GBR - Evolutionary Timeline

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Project**: Gigastack
- **Technology**: The facility aims to produce green hydrogen using a 100 MW electrolyser system powered by renewable energy from the Hornsea Two offshore wind farm. The hydrogen is intended to replace hydrocarbon-based fuels in industrial-scale fired heaters at the Phillips 66 Humber Refinery.
- **Production Capacity**: The Gigastack project is designed to produce hydrogen as part of a larger initiative to integrate offshore wind energy with hydrogen production.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Status**: As of August 2023, Ørsted and Phillips 66 have paused the Gigastack project, indicating that further project maturation and supply chain development are necessary before proceeding with investments. They had aimed for a final investment decision by Q2 2023, but this has been delayed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Withdrawal Announcement**: On 18 August 2023, Ørsted and Phillips 66 announced the withdrawal of the Gigastack project from the UK’s first electrolytic allocation round (HAR1). This decision was made to allow for further development and maturation of the project.
- **Future Plans**: The partners expressed intentions to continue working with the UK government and stakeholders to realize the potential of green hydrogen, although no specific new timelines were provided following the withdrawal.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation/Loss**: The articles do not specify any immediate job creation or loss related to the withdrawal of the Gigastack project. However, the project was expected to contribute to job creation in the hydrogen production sector once operational.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The Gigastack project is a partnership between Ørsted and Phillips 66, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by either company.

### Summary
The Ørsted facility at Humber Refinery is focused on producing green hydrogen through the Gigastack project, which has been paused as of August 2023 to allow for further development. The project is a joint venture with Phillips 66, and while it aims to integrate offshore wind energy into hydrogen production, it has withdrawn from the current funding allocation round to reassess its approach.
