## GBR-05378-05807

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Phillips 66 Facility in Humber Refinery, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10/2020**: Phillips 66, in partnership with Ørsted and ITM Power, announced the development of a 100 MW industrial-scale electrolysis system as part of the ITM Gigastack Hydrogen Project. This project aims to convert electricity from Ørsted’s Hornsea Two wind farm into green hydrogen, which will be used as feedstock at the Humber Refinery, replacing grey hydrogen produced from fossil fuels.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10/2020**: The UK government announced a £7.5 million support for the study phase of the Gigastack project. Additionally, a £70 million package for the hydrogen industry was announced in February 2020, indicating ongoing governmental support for hydrogen initiatives.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **08/2023**: Phillips 66 and Ørsted withdrew their Gigastack project from the UK’s first electrolytic allocation round (HAR1). The project aimed for a final investment decision by Q2 2023 and a commercial operation date in 2025. The withdrawal was attributed to the need for further project maturation and supply chain development.

#### 4. Labour
- **10/2020**: The development of green hydrogen projects, including the Gigastack initiative, is expected to contribute to job creation in the UK, particularly in sectors that can utilize hydrogen to replace fossil fuels, such as steel production and aviation.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **10/2020 - 08/2023**: The Gigastack project is a collaboration between Phillips 66, Ørsted, ITM Power, and Element Energy, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by Phillips 66.

This timeline captures the key developments and changes related to the Phillips 66 facility in Humber Refinery, focusing on its technological advancements, investments, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure.
