## GBR-05379-00712

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Shell Facility in Killingholme, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12-04-2022**: Shell and Uniper announced plans for a blue hydrogen production facility at Uniper's Killingholme site in North Lincolnshire. The facility is designed to have a production capacity of up to 720 megawatts, which is sufficient to heat more than half a million homes annually. The technology will utilize gas reformation combined with carbon capture and storage (CCS) to mitigate emissions. The project aims to capture approximately 1.6 million metric tonnes of carbon dioxide per year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12-04-2022**: Shell plans to invest up to £25 billion in the UK energy system over the next decade, with more than 75% allocated for low- and zero-carbon technology, including hydrogen and CCS. The UK Government is also investing £360 million in innovative energy technologies to support hydrogen production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12-04-2022**: The Humber Hub Blue project, which includes the Killingholme facility, passed the eligibility criteria for Phase-2 of the UK Government’s cluster sequencing process. Successful projects are expected to take final investment decisions from 2024, with operations anticipated to start in 2027.
- **13-04-2022**: Shell and Uniper confirmed they would proceed with process design studies and site development activities, aiming to reach front end engineering and design by 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **12-04-2022**: The project is expected to create high-quality jobs in the Humberside region, contributing to economic growth and supporting the decarbonization of the UK’s largest industrial cluster.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **12-04-2022**: The facility is a joint venture between Shell and Uniper, as indicated by their cooperation agreement to progress the hydrogen production facility.
