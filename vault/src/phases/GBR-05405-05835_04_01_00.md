### Evolutionary Timeline of the Powerhouse Energy PLC Facility in Chester, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Technology**: The facility utilizes DMG (Distributed Modular Generation) technology to convert unrecyclable plastic into low-cost hydrogen and electricity. This process operates with a very low carbon footprint and does not require sorting or washing of mixed waste plastic.
- **Production Capacity**: The facility aims to produce hydrogen for transport fuel and electricity for export, with the potential to monetize plastic waste.
- **Updates**: The technology has been demonstrated at a small scale at the University of Chester Energy Centre, with plans for a larger facility at the Protos site near Ellesmere Port.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment**: Waste2Tricity (W2T) is in discussions with financial institutions and high net worth individuals to fund the first-of-a-kind plant at Protos. They are raising £1 million pre-IPO, aiming to become a public company by late 2019 or early 2020.
- **Subsidies**: No specific subsidies mentioned, but the project has garnered interest from the Japanese Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry (METI), indicating potential support for hydrogen infrastructure development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Announcement**: The project was announced in July 2019, highlighting the technology's potential and the establishment of a demonstration plant at the University of Chester.
- **Construction Start**: A planning application for the development of the plant at the Protos site was expected to be submitted in June 2019, with hopes for operational status by early 2020, pending planning approval.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: Specific job creation figures were not mentioned in the article, but the establishment of the facility and its operations are expected to create employment opportunities in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: W2T has exclusive rights to use the DMG technology in the UK and Southeast Asia, including Japan and South Korea. The facility is being developed under a Special Purpose Vehicle, Waste2Tricity (Protos) Ltd, indicating a collaborative approach rather than full ownership by Powerhouse Energy PLC alone.
