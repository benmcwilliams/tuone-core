## GBR-05551-02264

### Evolutionary Timeline of H2 Green Facility in Inverness, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2021**: H2 Green announced plans to build a green hydrogen production facility at the former gas holder site of SGN in Inverness. The facility is expected to produce **8 tonnes of hydrogen per day**, servicing **800 heavy goods vehicles** and cutting **30,000 tonnes of emissions annually**.
- **March 2023**: The facility is targeting first production in **2025** and will scale from **6 megawatts to 24 megawatts**, eventually peaking at **10 tonnes of green hydrogen per day**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: H2 Green received a **£500,000 grant** from the UK Government's Net Zero Hydrogen Fund to support Front-End Engineering Design (FEED) work and post-FEED studies for the facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2021**: Plans for the facility were unveiled.
- **Q2 2022**: SGN began preparing the Inverness site for development, including the removal of the existing gas holder.
- **March 2022**: H2 Green signed a memorandum of understanding with Highland Council to create a network of green hydrogen hubs across the Highlands.
- **March 2023**: The facility was awarded funding and targeted first production in **2025**.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2021**: The facility is expected to create around **10 jobs** initially, with potential for wider economic benefits in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2021**: H2 Green, a subsidiary of Getech, has made a legally binding services agreement with SGN for the development of the facility. The ownership of the site will be transferred from SGN to an operating company owned by H2 Green, indicating **full ownership** by H2 Green.
