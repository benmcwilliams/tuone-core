### Evolutionary Timeline of the Shell Facility at Killingholme Power Station, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2022**: Shell and Uniper announced plans to develop a blue hydrogen production plant at Killingholme Power Station, with a production capacity of up to 720MW. The facility will produce low carbon hydrogen through steam reforming fossil gas, incorporating carbon capture and storage (CCS) to mitigate emissions. The project aims to capture approximately 1.6 million metric tonnes of carbon annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2022**: The UK government has indicated support for the Humber Hub Blue project as part of its industrial decarbonisation support programme. The government is investing £360 million in innovative energy technologies to achieve its hydrogen production targets, which include delivering 10GW of low carbon hydrogen by 2030.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2022**: Shell and Uniper plan to collaborate on process design studies and site development activities, aiming to progress to the front end engineering and design stage by 2023. The government expects projects in the second phase of its support programme to be operational by 2027, with final investment decisions anticipated in 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2022**: The project is expected to create high-quality jobs in the Humberside region, contributing to the local economy and helping to level up the area as part of the UK’s broader decarbonisation efforts.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2022**: The facility is a joint venture between Shell and Uniper, indicating that neither company has exclusive ownership of the facility.
