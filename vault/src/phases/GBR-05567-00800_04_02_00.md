## GBR-05567-00800

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Uniper Facility at Killingholme Power Station, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **April 2022:** Uniper, in collaboration with Shell, announced plans to develop a blue hydrogen production plant at Killingholme Power Station. The facility aims to produce up to 720MW of hydrogen through steam reforming fossil gas, incorporating carbon capture and storage (CCS) to mitigate emissions. The project is expected to capture approximately 1.6 million metric tonnes of carbon annually.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **April 2022:** The UK government is investing £360 million in innovative energy technologies, which includes support for the Humber Hub Blue project as part of its industrial decarbonisation support programme. The project is part of the UK’s broader aim to achieve 10GW of low carbon hydrogen production by 2030.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **April 2022:** The Humber Hub Blue project is in the planning stages, with Shell and Uniper collaborating on process design studies and site development activities. They aim to progress to the front end engineering and design stage by 2023. The government anticipates that projects in the second phase of its support programme will be operational by 2027, with final investment decisions expected in 2024.

4. **Labour:**
   - **April 2022:** The project is expected to create high-quality jobs in the Humberside region, contributing to the local economy and supporting the UK government’s energy security strategy.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **April 2022:** The facility is a joint venture between Uniper and Shell, indicating that it is not solely owned by Uniper but rather a collaborative effort between the two companies.
