## GBR-05654-06156

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Superglass Facility in Stirling, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2019**: Superglass completed a £37 million upgrade to its manufacturing facility, which produces approximately 60,000 tonnes of glass mineral wool insulation annually. The facility employs 200 people.
- **July 2023**: Superglass entered into a partnership with Carlton Power to evaluate the technical and supply elements for a proposed green hydrogen facility. The Stirling Green Hydrogen scheme will include a 10 MW electrolyser expected to produce around 1,000 tonnes of green hydrogen annually, with potential for additional capacity as demand increases.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2023**: The Stirling Green Hydrogen scheme is part of Carlton Power's initiative to develop hydrogen projects in Scotland, which are shortlisted for financial support through the UK Government's Hydrogen Business Model and Net Zero Hydrogen Fund. The project aims to reduce Superglass' reliance on natural gas and support local decarbonisation efforts.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2023**: Superglass and Carlton Power announced their partnership to develop the Stirling Green Hydrogen scheme. The project is expected to be operational by 2026, pending planning consent and financial support from the UK Government. Planning consent and a final investment decision (FID) are anticipated within the next 12-18 months.

#### 4. Labour
- **2019**: The Superglass facility employs 200 people. The partnership with Carlton Power and the development of the hydrogen facility are expected to create opportunities for job growth in the area, particularly for energy-intensive users.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2023**: The Stirling Green Hydrogen facility will be owned by the Green Hydrogen Energy Company (GHECO), a joint venture between Carlton Power and Schroders Greencoat. Superglass does not own the hydrogen facility exclusively but is a key partner in its development.
