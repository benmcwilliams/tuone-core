### Evolutionary Timeline of Vattenfall's Facility in Aberdeen Bay, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **07-09-2018**: Vattenfall officially opened the 93.2MW Aberdeen Bay offshore wind farm, also known as the European Offshore Wind Deployment Centre (EOWDC). The facility consists of two MHI Vestas V164 8.8MW turbines and nine 8.4MW turbines, supported by 11 suction bucket foundations. The wind farm began exporting power in early July 2018.
- **16-04-2019**: Vattenfall applied to extend the operational lease of the wind farm from 22 to 25 years to align with the design life of the wind farm’s components. This extension aims to enhance local job creation and increase fossil-free electricity generation.
- **20-05-2022**: Vattenfall received £9.3 million in funding to develop a hydrogen-producing offshore wind turbine, named Hydrogen Turbine 1 (HT1), which will be integrated with an existing turbine at the Aberdeen Bay facility. The project aims for first hydrogen production in 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **16-04-2019**: Vattenfall and the EU invested €3 million to study the environmental impact of offshore wind farms on local wildlife, including dolphins and birds.
- **20-05-2022**: The UK Government awarded Vattenfall £9.3 million from the Net Zero Innovation Portfolio to support the development of the hydrogen-producing offshore wind turbine.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **07-09-2018**: Official opening of the Aberdeen Bay offshore wind farm.
- **16-04-2019**: Application submitted to extend the operational lease for the wind farm.
- **20-05-2022**: Announcement of the Hydrogen Turbine 1 project, with plans for first production in 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **16-10-2020**: A report from Oxford Brookes University indicated that the Aberdeen Bay wind farm could create between 40 and 50 full-time equivalent jobs annually through operations and maintenance activities. The facility is expected to generate £5 million annually for the local economy over its 20-year lifespan.
