### Evolutionary Timeline of the Loganair Facility at Glasgow Airport, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2024**: The facility is set to become a hydrogen production, storage, and distribution hub at Glasgow Airport. The initial focus will be on producing gaseous hydrogen from a solar farm on-site, with plans to produce hydrogen on-site from a 7.5MW solar farm by 2025. By 2030, the solar farm capacity is expected to increase to a maximum of 20MW. The facility aims to serve various modes of transport in the aviation sector, including ground-handling vehicles and buses.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2024**: The project is supported by Ikigai Capital, which has conducted a comprehensive feasibility study. The ambition is to produce hydrogen at costs below £10 per kilogram, with a best-case scenario of £4.16/kg, making it competitive with other fuel sources. The project is part of Scotland's broader initiative to develop hydrogen production capacity, aiming for 5GW by 2030 and 25GW by 2045.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2024**: The hydrogen hub project at Glasgow Airport is announced, with plans for phased development focusing initially on gaseous hydrogen production. The project is designed to be replicable at other locations globally.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2024**: The development of hydrogen infrastructure at Glasgow Airport is expected to create thousands of future jobs in Scotland, contributing to the decarbonisation of aviation.
