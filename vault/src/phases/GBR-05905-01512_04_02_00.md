### Evolutionary Timeline of the Centrica Facility in Easington, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **03-11-2022**: Centrica and Equinor signed a co-operation agreement to explore the development of a hydrogen production hub at the Easington gas terminal. The facility aims to utilize existing infrastructure to produce both blue and green hydrogen, with potential production capacity of an additional 1GW of hydrogen. The facility is also linked to the Rough gas storage facility, which has a capacity of 100 billion cubic feet (bcf), with 30 bcf available for winter 2022/23.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **03-11-2022**: The partnership between Centrica and Equinor is part of a broader investment strategy to develop a hydrogen hub, leveraging existing resources and infrastructure. Specific investment amounts were not disclosed, but the project is positioned to enhance the economic landscape of the Humber region.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **03-11-2022**: Announcement of the co-operation agreement between Centrica and Equinor to develop the hydrogen hub at Easington. The project is part of the Zero Carbon Humber partnership, which aims to create regional hydrogen and CO2 pipelines.

#### 4. Labour
- **03-11-2022**: The development of the hydrogen hub is expected to safeguard many existing jobs at the Easington gas terminal while also creating new roles as the facility transitions to hydrogen production.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **03-11-2022**: The facility is operated by Centrica, but the development of the hydrogen hub is a joint initiative with Equinor, indicating a collaborative ownership structure for this specific project. 

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic moves regarding the Centrica facility in Easington, focusing on its transition towards hydrogen production and the collaborative efforts with Equinor.
