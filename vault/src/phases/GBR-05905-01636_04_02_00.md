### Evolutionary Timeline of the Equinor Facility in Easington, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **03-11-2022**: Equinor and Centrica announced plans to explore the development of a hydrogen production hub at the Easington gas terminal. The proposed hub aims to produce both blue hydrogen (paired with carbon capture) and green hydrogen (powered by renewables). The facility could potentially produce an additional 1 GW of hydrogen, with around 200 MW of off-take demand.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **03-11-2022**: The partnership between Equinor and Centrica is part of a broader strategy to leverage existing infrastructure at the Easington terminal, which currently handles a significant portion of the UK’s gas supply. Specific investment amounts were not disclosed, but the project is positioned to enhance the region's energy capabilities and support the transition to low-carbon energy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **03-11-2022**: The announcement of the cooperation agreement marks the initial milestone for the hydrogen hub project. The facility is identified as a key location within the Zero Carbon Humber partnership, which aims to create regional hydrogen and CO2 pipelines.

#### 4. Labour
- **03-11-2022**: The development of the hydrogen hub is expected to safeguard many existing jobs at the historical gas terminal while also creating new roles in the region. This aligns with the commitment from both Equinor and Centrica to invest in the local economy and workforce.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **03-11-2022**: The facility is operated by Centrica, with Equinor as a partner in a cooperation agreement. This indicates a joint venture approach rather than full ownership by either company.
