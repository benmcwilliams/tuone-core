## GBR-06026-01636

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Equinor Facility in Saltend, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2021**: Equinor announced the development of the H2H Saltend project, a 600 MW low carbon hydrogen production plant that will utilize natural gas in combination with carbon capture and storage (CCS). The facility aims to produce hydrogen for local industries and is part of a broader strategy to decarbonize the Humber region.
- **February 2024**: Planning approval was granted for the H2H Saltend project, confirming its status as one of the first large-scale hydrogen production facilities in the UK. The project is expected to reduce emissions at Saltend by up to one third and will capture and store approximately 900,000 tonnes of CO2 annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2021**: Equinor announced the awarding of pre-FEED study contracts for the H2H Saltend project, indicating significant investment in the development phase. The project is part of a larger ambition to achieve 1.8 GW of hydrogen production capacity in the region.
- **February 2024**: The UK government announced a £21 million investment into hydrogen projects, which includes support for the H2H Saltend project as part of its strategy to ramp up hydrogen production to meet net-zero targets.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2021**: Initial announcement of the H2H Saltend project.
- **November 2021**: Contracts awarded for the pre-FEED study, marking a significant step in project development.
- **January 2022**: Equinor entered the H2H Saltend project into a UK government funding competition, seeking further support for the project.
- **February 2024**: Planning permission was granted by the East Riding of Yorkshire Council, allowing the project to move forward.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2024**: The project is expected to create new jobs and opportunities in the Humber region as it progresses, contributing to local economic growth and the transition to a net-zero economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Equinor is a key player in the H2H Saltend project, collaborating with SSE Thermal. The facility is part of a joint effort to decarbonize the Humber region, indicating a partnership rather than exclusive ownership by Equinor.
