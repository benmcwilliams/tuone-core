### Evolutionary Timeline of RWE Facility at Pembroke Power Station, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2021**: RWE announced plans to produce green hydrogen at the Pembroke Power Station site in Wales. The initial phase involves investigating the feasibility of installing a 100 MW electrolyser to produce green hydrogen from local and grid-connected renewable energy. The project has the potential to scale up to several gigawatts and is linked to floating offshore wind in the Celtic Sea, positioning it as one of the UK's largest green hydrogen plants in development.

- **February 2022**: RWE's Pembroke Net Zero Centre (PNZC) was launched to explore various decarbonisation technologies, including carbon capture and storage and hydrogen feasibility. The feasibility study by Jacobs is part of this initiative, focusing on the integration of green hydrogen production with nearby renewable energy sources and the wider grid.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2021**: RWE signed a contract with Jacobs for a feasibility study to investigate the production and supply of hydrogen at Pembroke Power Station. This study is expected to cost and involve significant investment, although specific financial details were not disclosed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2021**: RWE announced the feasibility study contract with Jacobs, which is set to complete in March 2022. This study is the first major step towards establishing large-scale hydrogen infrastructure at Pembroke.

- **February 2022**: The completion of Jacobs' feasibility study is scheduled for March 2022, marking a significant milestone in the development of hydrogen production capabilities at the facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2022**: The development of the hydrogen project is expected to create jobs in South Wales, particularly in the context of decarbonization efforts and the establishment of hydrogen infrastructure, although specific job creation numbers were not provided.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2021**: RWE is the sole owner of the Pembroke Power Station facility, as indicated by the company's direct involvement in the hydrogen production plans and the establishment of the Pembroke Net Zero Centre.
