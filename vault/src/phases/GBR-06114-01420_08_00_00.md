### Evolutionary Timeline of the TotalEnergies Facility in Orkney Islands, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2021**: TotalEnergies, in collaboration with Macquarie’s Green Investment Group and Scottish developer RIDG, announced a bid for a 2GW offshore wind farm named the West of Orkney Windfarm. This facility is designed to power more than 2 million homes and is currently under assessment by Crown Estate Scotland. The project includes plans for extensive site investigations and a grid connection agreement with National Grid, enabling generation to commence in 2029.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2021**: The consortium has committed to enhancing port infrastructure in Caithness and Orkney through memorandums of understanding with local authorities, which will involve funding collaborative design and supply studies and investing in improving harbour infrastructure.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2021**: The West of Orkney Windfarm bid was officially announced, marking the beginning of the project’s development phase. The project is currently being assessed, with a target generation start date set for 2029.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2021**: The project is expected to create sustainable long-term skilled local jobs, particularly through the development of port infrastructure and operations related to the offshore wind farm.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2021**: The facility is part of a consortium involving TotalEnergies, Macquarie’s Green Investment Group, and Scottish developer RIDG, indicating a joint venture ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by TotalEnergies.
