## GBR-06194-01420

### Evolutionary Timeline of the TotalEnergies Facility in Hartlepool, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 03-11-2021
   - **Details:** The facility is involved in the marshalling operations for the Seagreen project, which is a 1075MW offshore wind farm. The project will utilize Vestas V164-10.0 MW turbines, with the Able Seaton Port in Hartlepool serving as the pre-assembly site for turbine parts, including blades, nacelles, and supporting towers. The facility is expected to support approximately 100 full-time employees for the installation of wind turbines and related operations.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 03-11-2021
   - **Details:** The Seagreen project has a total investment of £3 billion (€3.5 billion). The project is significant as it is set to be Scotland's largest renewable energy project and will contribute to the UK supply chain, particularly with 87% of the turbine blades being manufactured in the UK.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 03-11-2021
   - **Details:** The installation of turbine foundations has commenced, and the campaign to fit turbine parts is expected to begin in late 2021. First power generation is anticipated in spring 2022, with full commissioning of the 114-turbine wind farm expected in 2023.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 03-11-2021
   - **Details:** The facility is projected to create approximately 100 full-time jobs, including roles for technicians, crane personnel, supervisors, quality inspectors, and warehouse workers.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 03-11-2021
   - **Details:** The facility is part of a joint venture between TotalEnergies and SSE Renewables for the Seagreen project.
