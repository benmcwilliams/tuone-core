### Evolutionary Timeline of the Cavendish Nuclear Facility in Hartlepool, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date: 08-04-2024**: The Cavendish Nuclear facility, in collaboration with X-Energy, is set to establish a 12-reactor plant in Hartlepool by the early 2030s. The facility will utilize X-Energy's proprietary Xe-100 advanced modular reactor (AMR) design. The reactors are projected to generate 3.2GW of electricity, sufficient to supply six million homes, or alternatively produce 8GW of high-temperature heat and steam for zero-carbon manufacturing and industrial processes.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date: 08-04-2024**: The UK Government awarded £3.34m ($4.22m) from the Future Nuclear Enabling Fund (FNEF) to Cavendish Nuclear and X-Energy to advance their plans for the AMRs. X-Energy will match this investment with an additional £3.34m. The funding will be used to tailor deployment plans to the UK context, including assessments of domestic manufacturing capabilities and supply chain opportunities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date: 08-04-2024**: The collaboration between X-Energy and Cavendish Nuclear is in the planning stages for the establishment of the 12-reactor plant. They are exploring the licensing process for the Xe-100 AMR design with UK nuclear regulators and have initiated supply chain analyses with infrastructure construction company Kier and other partners.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date: 08-04-2024**: The project is expected to create jobs and contribute to long-term energy security in the UK through the deployment of advanced nuclear technology, although specific job creation numbers have not been detailed in the article.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
