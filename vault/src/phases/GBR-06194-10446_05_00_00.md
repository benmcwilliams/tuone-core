### Evolutionary Timeline of the X-Energy Facility in Hartlepool, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date: 08-04-2024**: X-Energy, in collaboration with Cavendish Nuclear, is advancing plans for advanced modular reactors (AMRs) in Hartlepool. The facility aims to establish a 12-reactor plant, which will be the first of 40 X-Energy Xe-100 small modular reactors across the UK. The reactors are projected to generate 3.2GW of electricity, sufficient to supply six million homes, or alternatively produce 8GW of high-temperature heat and steam for zero-carbon manufacturing and industrial processes.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date: 08-04-2024**: The UK Government awarded £3.34m ($4.22m) from the Future Nuclear Enabling Fund (FNEF) to X-Energy and Cavendish Nuclear to support the development of AMRs. X-Energy will match this investment with an additional £3.34m. The funding will be utilized to tailor deployment plans to the UK context, including assessments of manufacturing capabilities and supply chain opportunities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date: 08-04-2024**: The collaboration between X-Energy and Cavendish Nuclear is in the planning stages for the establishment of the 12-reactor plant in Hartlepool, with a vision for completion by the early 2030s. The companies are exploring the licensing process for the Xe-100 AMR design with UK nuclear regulators.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date: 08-04-2024**: X-Energy's project is expected to create jobs and contribute to long-term energy security in the UK through the deployment of clean, reliable advanced nuclear power.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date: 08-04-2024**: The facility is a collaboration between X-Energy and Cavendish Nuclear, indicating a joint venture structure for the development of the Hartlepool facility.
