### Evolutionary Timeline of MHI Vestas Facility in Fawley, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2018**: MHI Vestas repurposed part of a decommissioned oil-fired power plant in Fawley into a painting and logistics facility for offshore wind turbine blades. The facility is designed to handle the V164 offshore wind turbines, which have a nameplate capacity of up to 9.5MW. The Fawley facility is expected to produce around 150 blades annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2018**: The establishment of the Fawley facility is part of MHI Vestas' ongoing investment in the UK wind energy sector, which is noted to be beneficial for the local area.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2017**: MHI Vestas announced the repurposing of the Fawley facility.
- **April 2018**: Williams Shipping began transporting MHI Vestas’ 82-metre-long offshore wind turbine blades from the Isle of Wight to the Fawley facility for painting and logistics.
- **June 2021**: The Fawley facility is confirmed to be involved in the production of blades for the Seagreen offshore wind farm project, with 99 out of 114 sets of blades being produced in the UK.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2021**: Vestas ramped up its workforce to over 1,500 employees across the UK, which includes production, blade technology research and development, construction, and service roles. This increase is attributed to the demand for blades for various offshore wind projects, including Seagreen.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
