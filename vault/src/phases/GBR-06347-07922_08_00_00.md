### Evolutionary Timeline of MHI Vestas Offshore Wind Facility in Fawley, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05-11-2020**: MHI Vestas Offshore Wind produces V164-9.5 MW turbines, which are the largest turbines installed in commercial projects globally. The facility in Fawley is involved in the finishing of turbine blades manufactured at the Isle of Wight. The Triton Knoll offshore wind farm, which utilizes these turbines, has a maximum installed capacity of 857 MW and will consist of 90 turbines.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05-11-2020**: A multi-million pound investment was made to transform Able Seaton Port into a specialist assembly and construction facility for the Triton Knoll project, which MHI Vestas is supplying. This investment is aimed at enhancing the local offshore wind supply chain and infrastructure.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05-11-2020**: The first turbine blades for the Triton Knoll project arrived at Able Seaton Port, marking a significant milestone in the project’s logistics and assembly phase. The project is being constructed by RWE, with MHI Vestas supplying the turbines.

#### 4. Labour
- **05-11-2020**: The establishment of the turbine logistics and pre-assembly hub at Able Seaton Port has led to the involvement of local suppliers and technicians, indicating job creation in the region. MHI Vestas has prioritized UK companies for the delivery of the project, contributing to local employment.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **05-11-2020**: The Triton Knoll project is jointly owned by RWE, J-Power, and Kansai Electric Power, with RWE managing the construction and long-term operation. MHI Vestas is a supplier for the project but does not own the facility exclusively.
