## GBR-06400-01636

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Equinor Facility at Port of Tyne, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2020**: Equinor and SSE Renewables announced the construction of an operations and maintenance (O&M) hub at the Port of Tyne for the 3,600 MW Dogger Bank offshore wind farm. The facility will include office space and a warehouse, supporting the operation of the wind farm for over 25 years.
- **March 2021**: The design for the O&M base was completed, and a planning application was submitted. The facility will include environmentally friendly features such as EV chargers and solar panels.
- **November 2021**: Construction officially began on the 3,400 square meter O&M base, which will support over 200 jobs and will be operational by the end of Q4 2022.
- **June 2023**: A naming ceremony for the Grampian Tyne service operation vessel (SOV) was held at the Port of Tyne base, marking the readiness of the facility to support operations and maintenance work for the offshore wind farm.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2020**: The total capital investment for the Dogger Bank project is approximately £9 billion, with investments expected between 2020 and 2026.
- **December 2020**: An option agreement was signed between Port of Tyne and Dogger Bank Wind Farm for the O&M base, allowing for development rights for up to 35 years.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2020**: Construction of the Dogger Bank wind farm began.
- **May 2020**: Announcement of the O&M hub at Port of Tyne.
- **December 2020**: Completion of the preliminary phase for the O&M base agreement.
- **March 2021**: Planning application for the O&M base submitted.
- **November 2021**: Groundbreaking for the O&M base.
- **Q4 2022**: Expected completion of the O&M base, ahead of the wind farm operations starting in 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2020**: The project is expected to generate over 200 direct jobs in the region, with recruitment activities ramping up in early 2022.
- **March 2021**: It was specified that 120 of the 200 jobs will be related to wind turbine operation and maintenance.
- **November 2021**: Over 200 people will be based at the O&M facility or offshore to operate and maintain the wind farm.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2020**: The Dogger Bank Wind Farm is a 50:50 joint venture between Equinor and SSE Renewables, with Eni joining later. Equinor is responsible for the operation of the wind farm and the construction of the O&M base.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones related to the Equinor facility at the Port of Tyne, highlighting its role in the Dogger Bank offshore wind farm project.
