### Evolutionary Timeline of the GE Facility in Port of Tyne, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **December 2020:** An agreement was signed for an operations and maintenance (O&M) base at Port of Tyne for the Dogger Bank Wind Farm, which is set to be the world’s largest offshore wind farm with a capacity of 3.5 GW. The facility will support the O&M operations for the wind farm throughout its 35-year lifespan. GE Renewable Energy’s Service team will be co-located with the Dogger Bank Operational and Maintenance team at this base.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **December 2020:** The Port of Tyne is in the process of approving construction bids for initial works, which include a new access road, improved drainage facilities, and upgrades to the quay. These initial works are expected to be completed by July 2021, with the full construction phase commencing in the third quarter of 2021.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **December 2020:** The agreement for the O&M base was finalized, marking the completion of the preliminary phase. Initial construction works are set to begin early in 2021, with the facility expected to be operational by 2023 when the wind farm operations commence.

4. **Labour:**
   - **December 2020:** The construction and operation of the O&M base are projected to create a total of 200 long-term jobs, with 120 of these jobs specifically related to wind turbine operation and maintenance.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **December 2020:** The O&M base is part of a 50:50 joint venture between Equinor and SSE, with GE Renewable Energy's Service team being co-located at the facility. The ownership of the facility is not exclusively held by GE, as it is part of the joint venture arrangement for the Dogger Bank Wind Farm.
