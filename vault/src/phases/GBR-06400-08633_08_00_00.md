### Evolutionary Timeline of the Dogger Bank Wind Farm Facility in Port of Tyne, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **December 2020:** An agreement was signed between Port of Tyne and Dogger Bank Wind Farm for the establishment of an operations and maintenance (O&M) base for the world’s largest offshore wind farm, which has a production capacity of 3.5 GW. The O&M base will operate for up to 35 years throughout the wind farm's lifetime. Initial works for the facility are expected to commence in early 2021, with the full building phase starting in the third quarter of 2021.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **December 2020:** The construction works at Port of Tyne include improvements such as a new access road, enhanced drainage facilities, enabling works for electricity infrastructure, and a quay upgrade. These initial works are set to be completed by July 2021.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **December 2020:** The agreement for the O&M base marks the completion of the preliminary phase, with initial works expected to start early in 2021 and full construction to begin in Q3 2021. The facility is scheduled to be operational for offshore service vessels when the wind farm operations begin in 2023.

4. **Labour:**
   - **December 2020:** The construction of the O&M base is expected to create a total of 200 long-term jobs, with 120 of these jobs specifically related to wind turbine operation and maintenance. GE Renewable Energy’s Service team will be co-located with the Dogger Bank Operational and Maintenance team at the base.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **December 2020:** Dogger Bank Wind Farm is a 50:50 joint venture between Equinor and SSE, with plans for Eni to join the partnership. The joint venture retains full rights to develop the O&M facility for a specified period.
