### Evolutionary Timeline of the Equinor Facility in Peterhead, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2022**: Equinor and SSE Thermal acquired Triton Power, which includes the Saltend Power Station with an installed capacity of 1.2 GW. The facility aims to transition to hydrogen, targeting 30% hydrogen power by 2027 and eventually 100% hydrogen thereafter. The hydrogen will potentially be sourced from Equinor’s Hydrogen to Humber (H2H) project, which will reform natural gas into hydrogen with carbon capture and storage (CCS).
- **December 2022**: The Hywind Scotland floating wind farm, owned by Equinor, has been operational since 2017. It has a capacity of 30 MW and generates enough electricity to power approximately 34,000 UK homes. The facility has achieved a capacity factor of 54% and employs unique operational methods to maintain the floating turbines.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2022**: The acquisition of Triton Power was valued at £341 million, indicating a significant investment in transitioning to hydrogen and enhancing low-carbon solutions in the UK.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2017**: The Hywind Scotland project was commissioned, marking the world's first floating offshore wind farm.
- **June 2022**: Announcement of the acquisition of Triton Power and plans for hydrogen integration at Saltend Power Station.
- **December 2022**: Hywind Scotland celebrated five years of operation, showcasing its successful performance and operational advancements.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2022**: The Hywind Scotland project has implemented a unique operations and maintenance model that involves upskilling onshore wind technicians for offshore maintenance, indicating job creation in specialized roles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2022**: Equinor and SSE Thermal will jointly own Triton Power in a 50:50 joint venture, pending regulatory approvals.
- **December 2022**: Equinor exclusively owns the Hywind Scotland facility, reinforcing its position as a leader in floating wind technology.
