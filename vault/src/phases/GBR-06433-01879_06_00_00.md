### Evolutionary Timeline of the Quinbrook Facility in Cleve Hill, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: Construction commenced on the Cleve Hill solar park, which is designed to have a total capacity of 373MW, including a 150MW battery storage component. This facility is set to be the UK’s largest solar and battery storage project.
- **Projected Output**: The facility is expected to supply enough renewable energy to power 102,000 UK homes annually and reduce carbon emissions by more than 142,000 tons in its first year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2022**: Quinbrook secured a contract for difference during the UK’s allocation round 4, which is crucial for the financial viability of the project.
- **October 2024**: Quinbrook signed long-term power offtake agreements with Tesco and Shell, which cover the entire solar power generation capacity of the park. The agreement with Tesco is a 15-year power purchase agreement (PPA) covering 65% of the expected solar generation, while Shell has a ten-year route-to-market agreement for the remaining 35%.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2020**: Development consent for the Cleve Hill project was granted.
- **April 2023**: Construction of the Cleve Hill solar park officially began.
- **Early 2025**: Operational commencement of the facility is anticipated.

#### 4. Labour
- The Cleve Hill project has the potential to create approximately 2,300 jobs and generate over £143 million ($186 million) in local benefits throughout its lifetime in Kent.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Quinbrook Infrastructure Partners is the owner of the Cleve Hill facility, indicating full ownership of the project without any joint ventures mentioned in the articles.
