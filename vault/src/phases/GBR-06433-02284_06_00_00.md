## GBR-06433-02284

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Cleve Hill Facility (Hive Energy and Wirsol Energy, Cleve Hill, GBR)

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2020**: The Cleve Hill solar farm is approved to have a production capacity of up to **350MW**. It will incorporate approximately **880,000 solar panels** and a significant energy storage system of **350MW/700MWh**. This facility is set to be the largest solar park in the UK, generating enough electricity to power over **91,000 homes** and reducing CO2 emissions by **68,000 tonnes annually**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2020**: The development value of the Cleve Hill project is reported to be **£450 million** (approximately **US$554 million**). This investment is tied to the construction of the solar and storage facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 16, 2018**: The application for the Cleve Hill project is submitted to the Planning Inspectorate.
- **December 14, 2018**: The application is accepted for examination.
- **February 28, 2020**: A recommendation is made to the Secretary of State following a six-month examination process.
- **May 28, 2020**: The UK government officially approves the Cleve Hill solar farm project.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2020**: The project is expected to create a significant number of skilled jobs, contributing to the local economy and supporting the green recovery in the wake of the coronavirus crisis. However, specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2020**: The Cleve Hill facility is developed as a **joint venture** between **Hive Energy** and **Wirsol Energy**. There is no indication of exclusive ownership by either company.
