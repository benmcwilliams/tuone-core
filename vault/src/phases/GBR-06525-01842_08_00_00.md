## GBR-06525-01842

### Evolutionary Timeline of Ørsted's Hornsea 3 Facility, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2023**: Ørsted awarded NKT a contract for the delivery of a high-voltage DC on- and offshore export cable system for Hornsea 3. The project includes two circuits of approximately 170 km of 320 kV DC offshore cable, 50 km of 320 kV DC onshore cable, and four circuits for a 1.5 km route of 400 kV AC onshore cable. The facility will have a generation capacity of 2,852 MW and is located approximately 160 km off the Yorkshire coast. Upon completion, the combined capacity of Hornsea 1, 2, and 3 will exceed 5 GW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2023**: Ørsted made the final investment decision for the Hornsea 3 offshore wind farm, confirming its commitment to the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2023**: Announcement of the contract with NKT for the export cable system.
- **May 2024**: Balmoral Comtec awarded a multi-billion-pound contract for cable protection systems for Hornsea 3. The project is expected to be completed by the end of 2027.
- **December 2024**: Ørsted announced contracts with JDR Cable Systems, Severfield, and Smulders for various components and services for Hornsea 3, reinforcing the project's progress and local supply chain involvement.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2024**: The work on Hornsea 3 is expected to support over 300 jobs across the three partners involved in the supply chain, highlighting the project's contribution to local employment.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Ørsted is the sole owner of the Hornsea 3 facility, as indicated by the contracts and investments made directly by the company for the project.
