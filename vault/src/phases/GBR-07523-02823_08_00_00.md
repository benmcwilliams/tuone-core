### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDPR Facility in Moray Firth, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **November 2024:** A planning application was submitted for the Caledonia offshore wind farm, which aims to generate 2GW of power in the outer Moray Firth. The project is designed to include up to 140 turbines, each with a maximum capacity of 25MW. The facility will utilize established fixed foundation technology optimized for cost, similar to the Moray East and Moray West projects.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **November 2024:** The development of the Caledonia offshore wind farm follows three years of intensive work, indicating significant investment in the early stages to ensure a high-quality application for consent. The project is part of a larger strategy to enhance generation capacity in the Moray Firth region.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **November 2024:** The submission of the planning application marks a significant milestone in the development of the Caledonia offshore wind farm, which has been in development since 2021. The site was awarded in 2022 through the ScotWind process.

4. **Labour:**
   - **November 2024:** While specific job creation figures are not mentioned, the project director emphasized the importance of frontloading investment in the early stages, which typically correlates with job creation during the construction and operational phases of such large-scale projects.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **November 2024:** The Caledonia offshore wind farm is developed by Ocean Winds, a 50:50 joint venture between EDP Renewables (EDPR) and Engie. This indicates that the facility is not solely owned by EDPR but is part of a joint venture arrangement. 

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the EDPR facility in Moray Firth, focusing on the Caledonia offshore wind farm project.
