### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ocean Winds Facility in Moray Firth, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2022**: Ocean Winds places an order for 14 monopiles for its 882 MW Moray West offshore wind farm, which will consist of 60 Siemens Gamesa SG 14-222 DD wind turbines. The monopiles will be produced in a new XXL monopile factory in Spain.
- **April 2023**: Global Energy Group begins fabrication of J-tube frames for the Moray West project, marking a significant milestone in the project. The wind farm is expected to generate enough energy to power up to 640,000 homes annually.
- **February 2024**: The first Siemens Gamesa 108-metre-long wind turbine blades are shipped from Hull to the Moray West site, with installation planned to start in 2024. The project is currently under construction, with foundation installation underway.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2022**: The order for monopiles represents an investment of over €36 million in the new factory in Spain.
- **November 2022**: JDR announces a GBP 130 million investment in a new subsea cable manufacturing facility in Northumberland, which will support the Moray West project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2022**: Ocean Winds awards the monopile contract, marking the start of the Moray West project.
- **April 2023**: The first steel is cut for J-tube frames, indicating progress in the fabrication phase.
- **February 2024**: The first turbine blades are shipped, with construction ongoing and first power expected to be generated later in the year.
- **July 2024**: Moray West achieves first power generation.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2022**: The monopile contract is expected to generate 280,000 hours of work, with an average of 150 direct jobs created per month.
- **April 2023**: The fabrication of J-tubes creates 50 local jobs at Global Energy Group's facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Ocean Winds is a joint venture between EDP Renewables and Engie, which means it does not have exclusive ownership of the Moray West facility. The project is majority-owned by Ocean Winds, with a minority share held by Lithuania’s Ignitis Group.
