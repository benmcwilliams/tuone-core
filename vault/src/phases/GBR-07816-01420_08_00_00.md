### Evolutionary Timeline of the TotalEnergies Facility at Seagreen Offshore Wind Farm, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2022**: The Seagreen Offshore Wind Farm, with a production capacity of 1,075 MW, is noted as the world’s deepest fixed-bottom offshore wind farm, featuring 114 V164-10.0 MW turbines. The facility is designed to provide enough green energy to power over 1.6 million homes.
- **October 2023**: All Vestas turbines at the Seagreen Offshore Wind Farm are reported to be fully operational. The facility is recognized as Scotland’s largest wind farm, with its deepest foundation installed at a record depth of 58.7 meters below sea level.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2022**: The Seagreen Offshore Wind Farm is part of a £3 billion joint venture between TotalEnergies (51%) and SSE Renewables (49%). This investment underscores the scale and significance of the project in the renewable energy sector.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2022**: Certex UK wins a contract for inspection and maintenance work at the Seagreen Offshore Wind Farm, indicating ongoing operational preparations and support for the facility.
- **October 2023**: The facility officially enters full commercial operation, marking a significant milestone in its development and operational readiness.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2023**: The operational status of the Seagreen Offshore Wind Farm is expected to create job opportunities and boost local supply chains, as emphasized by Scotland’s First Minister, who highlights the commitment to maximizing economic opportunities and creating jobs through the development of local supply chains.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2022**: The Seagreen Offshore Wind Farm is established as a joint venture, with TotalEnergies holding a 51% stake and SSE Renewables holding 49%. This indicates that TotalEnergies does not own the facility exclusively but is a majority partner in the joint venture.
