### Evolutionary Timeline of the Seagreen Offshore Wind Farm Facility

**Facility Overview:**
- **Company:** SSE Renewables (49%) and TotalEnergies (51%)
- **Location:** Offshore, Angus, Scotland, UK
- **Country:** United Kingdom
- **Type:** Offshore Wind Farm
- **Capacity:** 1075 MW (1.1 GW)
- **Turbines:** 114 V164-10.0 MW turbines

---

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2022:** The Seagreen Offshore Wind Farm is described as the world’s deepest fixed-bottom offshore wind farm, with a production capacity of 1075 MW. It is designed to provide enough green energy to power over 1.6 million homes.
- **October 2023:** All Vestas turbines at the Seagreen Offshore Wind Farm are reported to be fully operational. The facility is noted for having the deepest foundation installed at a record 58.7 meters below sea level.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2022:** The Seagreen Offshore Wind Farm is part of a £3 billion joint venture between TotalEnergies and SSE Renewables, indicating significant investment in the project.
- **November 2022:** PD&MS Group is awarded a three-year balance of plant (BoP) statutory inspection framework agreement, which includes options for a two-year extension, further indicating ongoing investment in operational support.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2022:** Certex UK wins a contract for inspection and maintenance work at the Seagreen Offshore Wind Farm, marking a significant operational milestone.
- **November 2022:** PD&MS Group wins a contract to support inspections at the Seagreen Offshore Wind Farm, further solidifying operational capabilities.
- **October 2023:** The facility officially enters full commercial operation, marking a significant milestone in its development.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2023:** The operational phase of the Seagreen Offshore Wind Farm is expected to create jobs and boost local supply chains, as emphasized by Scotland’s First Minister, who highlights the commitment to a just transition for workers and maximizing economic opportunities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2022:** The Seagreen Offshore Wind Farm is confirmed as a joint venture, with TotalEnergies holding a 51% stake and SSE Renewables holding 49%. This indicates that the facility is not under exclusive ownership by either company.
