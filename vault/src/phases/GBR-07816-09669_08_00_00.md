### Evolutionary Timeline of PD&MS Group Facility at Seagreen Offshore Wind Farm, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** November 2022
   - **Details:** PD&MS Group has been awarded a three-year balance of plant (BoP) statutory inspection framework agreement for the Seagreen Offshore Wind Farm, which has a capacity of 1.1GW. The facility will perform topside statutory inspections on power generating equipment, ensuring safety, compliance, and operational efficiency. The work will involve inspections across all wind turbine generators and the offshore substation, focusing on jacket foundations and transition pieces.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** November 2022
   - **Details:** The contract with SSE Renewables includes options for a two-year extension, indicating a commitment to ongoing investment in the facility's operational capabilities. The emphasis on local technicians for inspections also suggests a strategic investment in regional resources to enhance cost and environmental efficiencies.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** November 2022
   - **Details:** PD&MS Group's contract marks a significant milestone as it positions the company within the operational phase of Scotland's largest offshore wind farm, which is a joint venture between TotalEnergies and SSE Renewables. The facility is expected to deliver enough green energy to power over 1.6 million homes.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** November 2022
   - **Details:** The contract will embed PD&MS’ inspection technicians into the Seagreen team, indicating job creation within the local workforce. The strategy of utilizing regionally based technicians is aimed at enhancing local employment opportunities while also meeting local content targets.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** November 2022
   - **Details:** The Seagreen Offshore Wind Farm is a joint venture between TotalEnergies and SSE Renewables, with PD&MS Group providing inspection services rather than owning the facility.
