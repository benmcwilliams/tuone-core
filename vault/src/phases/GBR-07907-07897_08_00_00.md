## GBR-07907-07897

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Innogy Facility in Grimsby, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2019**: Innogy established a base at Grimsby’s Royal Dock for the 857MW Triton Knoll offshore wind farm. This facility serves as the headquarters for offshore construction, managing hundreds of offshore technicians and crews, with operations set to begin in the first quarter of 2020.
- **June 2020**: The construction of the operations and maintenance (O&M) base was completed. The £3.4 million facility includes an open plan office, control room, meeting rooms, canteen, gym, and a warehouse with a Hazmat store and workshop area. It is designed to support the long-term maintenance of the offshore wind farm for the next 25 years.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2020**: The O&M base was constructed at a cost of £3.4 million, indicating a significant investment in the facility to support the Triton Knoll project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2019**: Announcement of the establishment of the construction base at Grimsby’s Royal Dock for the Triton Knoll offshore wind farm.
- **June 2020**: Completion of the O&M base construction, with final fit-out works ongoing.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2020**: The facility is set to create a new local team, including the recruitment of new apprentices, indicating job creation as a result of the facility's establishment and operations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2020**: The Triton Knoll project is owned by Innogy, J-Power, and Kansai Electric Power. Innogy is responsible for managing the construction and long-term operation and maintenance of the project on behalf of its partners, indicating a joint ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by Innogy.
