### Evolutionary Timeline of the Innogy Facility in Grimsby, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04/2019**: The facility is confirmed as the home for Triton Knoll’s offshore construction and operations and maintenance activities. It will be constructed across nearly four acres at Grimsby’s Royal Dock. The facility will support the management of the Triton Knoll offshore wind farm, which has a production capacity of 857 MW, consisting of 90 MHI Vestas 9.5 MW turbines, two substations, and over 100 km of cabling.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04/2019**: The establishment of the facility is part of a broader investment strategy linked to the Triton Knoll offshore wind project, which aims to enhance the offshore wind sector in the UK. The facility will enable significant investment into coastal communities and businesses.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04/2019**: A port lease is signed with ABP in Grimsby for the construction of the facility. Tendering for a contractor to build the facility is underway, expected to conclude later in the summer of 2019. The facility will serve as a long-term base for construction and operations and maintenance activities for the Triton Knoll project.

#### 4. Labour
- **04/2019**: The facility aims to recruit new and existing technicians, with the first up to 20 roles expected to be filled by the end of 2019. This recruitment is part of the broader commitment to job creation in the offshore wind sector, which has the potential to create up to 27,000 high-skilled jobs in UK coastal communities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **04/2019**: The Triton Knoll offshore wind farm is jointly owned by innogy (59%), J-Power (24%), and Kansai Electric Power (16%). Innogy manages the construction of the wind farm on behalf of the partnership, indicating that the facility is not exclusively owned by innogy but is part of a joint venture.
