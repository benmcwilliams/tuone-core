### Evolutionary Timeline of the Triton Knoll Facility in Grimsby, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04/2019**: Triton Knoll confirmed Grimsby’s Royal Dock as the home for its offshore construction and operations and maintenance facility. The facility will be constructed across nearly four acres and will manage the construction and 20-25 year operations and maintenance activities of the Triton Knoll offshore wind farm, which spans over 145 km². The wind farm will consist of 90 MHI Vestas 9.5 MW turbines, two substations, and over 100 km of cabling.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04/2019**: The project signed a port lease with ABP in Grimsby, indicating a commitment to invest in the local area and support the offshore wind industry. The facility aims to enhance investment into coastal communities and businesses.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04/2019**: Announcement of the establishment of the Triton Knoll facility at Grimsby’s Royal Dock, with tendering for a contractor to build the facility underway. The first roles are expected to be filled by the end of the year.

#### 4. Labour
- **04/2019**: Triton Knoll aims to recruit new and existing technicians, with the first up to 20 roles expected to be in place by the end of 2019. This recruitment is part of the broader commitment to create high-skilled jobs in UK coastal communities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **04/2019**: The Triton Knoll offshore wind farm is jointly owned by innogy (59%), J-Power (24%), and Kansai Electric Power (16%). Innogy manages the construction of the wind farm on behalf of the partnership.
