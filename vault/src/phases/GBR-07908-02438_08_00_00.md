### Evolutionary Timeline of the ScottishPower Facility in Lowestoft, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2018**: ScottishPower Renewables (SPR) begins development work at the Port of Lowestoft for the East Anglia ONE offshore wind farm, which has a production capacity of 714 MW. The facility will support the operations and maintenance of the wind farm, which includes 102 Siemens 7MW wind turbines. The facility is designed to support both the construction phase and the operational lifespan of over 30 years.
- **July 2020**: The East Anglia ONE wind farm is completed and begins generating power.
- **September 2021**: Siemens Gamesa extends its servicing contract for the East Anglia ONE wind farm from five to 15 years, indicating ongoing operations and maintenance from the Lowestoft base.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2018**: SPR announces a GBP 2.5 billion investment for the East Anglia ONE offshore wind farm, with an additional GBP 10 million awarded for development work at the Port of Lowestoft.
- **December 2019**: A GBP 5.6 million contract is awarded to James Fisher Marine Services for maintenance work at the East Anglia ONE project, further indicating investment in operational capabilities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2018**: Construction begins on the operations and maintenance base at the Port of Lowestoft.
- **September 2019**: East Anglia ONE generates its first power.
- **July 2020**: The East Anglia ONE wind farm is fully commissioned.
- **September 2021**: Siemens Gamesa's contract extension for servicing the wind farm is announced.
- **March 2024**: Labour Shadow Chancellor Rachel Reeves visits the facility, highlighting ongoing developments and future projects, including East Anglia THREE.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2018**: It is projected that around 100 full-time jobs will be created at the Lowestoft port when East Anglia ONE is completed.
- **December 2019**: The maintenance contract with James Fisher Marine Services is expected to create around 20 new jobs at the operations and maintenance base.
- **March 2024**: The facility supports thousands of jobs across the supply chain, with ongoing recruitment of apprentices and graduate trainees.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned exclusively by ScottishPower Renewables, which is a subsidiary of Iberdrola. There are no indications of joint ventures or shared ownership in the articles reviewed.
