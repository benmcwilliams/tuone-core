### Evolutionary Timeline of the Associated British Ports Facility in Lowestoft, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2018**: Construction begins on the operations and maintenance base for the East Anglia ONE offshore wind farm at the Port of Lowestoft. The facility will support the 714MW wind farm, which includes 102 Siemens 7MW wind turbines, set to be fully commissioned in 2020. The operations and maintenance building will include specialist offices and warehousing.
- **November 2024**: Completion of the Lowestoft Eastern Energy Facility (LEEF), a £35 million infrastructure project designed to support operations and maintenance (O&M) activities and construction phase requirements for the offshore wind sector. Key features include 345m of quayside with three deep-water berths, 8 acres of operational and storage space, and infrastructure for crew transfer vessels.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2018**: Contracts valued at approximately £10 million awarded for development work at the Port of Lowestoft to support the East Anglia ONE offshore wind farm.
- **March 2021**: Announcement of the LEEF project, requiring an initial investment of around £25 million to upgrade marine facilities at Lowestoft’s Outer Harbour.
- **November 2024**: The total investment for the LEEF project is confirmed at £35 million, supported by the Town’s Fund.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2018**: Work begins on the East Anglia ONE base.
- **March 2021**: Announcement of the LEEF project, aimed at enhancing the port's capabilities for the offshore energy sector.
- **November 2024**: Official completion of the LEEF, marking a significant milestone in the port's evolution as a hub for offshore wind and energy markets.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2018**: The East Anglia ONE project is expected to create around 100 full-time jobs at the port upon completion, along with thousands of contractor and supply chain jobs contributing to the local economy.
- **March 2021**: The LEEF project is anticipated to attract supply chain companies, potentially creating new jobs and contributing to the local economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned by Associated British Ports (ABP), which operates the Port of Lowestoft. There is no indication of joint ventures or shared ownership in the provided articles; ABP appears to have full ownership of the facilities discussed.
