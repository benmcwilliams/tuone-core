### Evolutionary Timeline of the Orbis Energy Facility in Lowestoft, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Lowestoft Eastern Energy Facility (LEEF) is set to provide state-of-the-art port infrastructure to support the offshore energy industry, particularly focusing on operations and maintenance (O&M) activities for offshore wind farms. 
   - The facility will feature 360 meters of berthing space for simultaneous use by three Service Operation Vessels (SOVs) and will include up to 8 acres of flexible storage and marshalling area, along with 5,000 square feet of new office space with direct quayside access.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The first phase of the LEEF project will require an investment of approximately £25 million. This investment is aimed at upgrading marine facilities at Lowestoft’s Outer Harbour to meet the demands of the growing offshore energy sector.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **March 2021:** Announcement of the LEEF project by Associated British Ports (ABP), detailing plans for significant upgrades to the Port of Lowestoft to support the offshore energy sector and contribute to the UK’s net zero greenhouse gas emissions goal.

4. **Labour:**

The project aims to attract supply chain companies to Lowestoft, potentially leading to a renaissance in the energy cluster and further job creation.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is developed by Associated British Ports (ABP), which is responsible for the ownership and operation of the Lowestoft Eastern Energy Facility. There is no indication of joint ventures or shared ownership mentioned in the article.
