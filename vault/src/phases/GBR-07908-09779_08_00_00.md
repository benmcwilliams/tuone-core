### Evolutionary Timeline of the PowerPark Facility in Lowestoft, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Lowestoft Eastern Energy Facility (LEEF) is set to enhance marine facilities at the Port of Lowestoft, focusing on offshore energy capabilities. The facility will support operations and maintenance (O&M) activities for offshore wind farms and provide quayside support for construction activities.
   - The project will deliver 360 meters of berthing space for simultaneous use by three Service Operation Vessels (SOVs) and will include up to 8 acres of flexible storage and marshalling area, along with 5,000 square feet of new office space with direct quayside access.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The first phase of the LEEF project will require an investment of approximately £25 million. This investment is aimed at upgrading the existing quayside infrastructure to meet the demands of the offshore energy industry.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - The announcement of the LEEF project was made on March 25, 2021. The project is part of a five-year development plan by Associated British Ports (ABP) to enhance the Port of Lowestoft's capabilities in the Southern North Sea energy sector.

4. **Labour:**
   - The LEEF project is expected to create new jobs and contribute to the local economy. The Port of Lowestoft currently supports over 580 local jobs and contributes £30 million annually to the local economy. The project aims to attract supply chain companies, potentially leading to further job creation in the area.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is developed and owned by Associated British Ports (ABP), which is responsible for the upgrades and enhancements at the Port of Lowestoft. There is no indication of joint ventures or shared ownership mentioned in the article.
