### Evolutionary Timeline of the Lowestoft Eastern Energy Facility (LEEF), Lowestoft, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** March 2021
   - **Details:** The Lowestoft Eastern Energy Facility (LEEF) is set to provide significant upgrades to marine facilities at Lowestoft’s Outer Harbour. It will support offshore energy capabilities, particularly for operations and maintenance (O&M) activities related to offshore wind farms. The facility will feature 360 meters of berthing space for simultaneous use by three Service Operation Vessels (SOVs) and will include up to 8 acres of flexible storage and marshalling area, along with 5,000 square feet of new office space with direct quayside access.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** March 2021
   - **Details:** The first phase of the LEEF project will require an investment of approximately £25 million. This investment is aimed at enhancing the port's infrastructure to meet the demands of the growing offshore energy sector.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** March 2021
   - **Details:** The announcement of the LEEF project marks the beginning of a significant development phase for the Port of Lowestoft, which is expected to take place over the next five years. The project is part of a broader masterplan for the port to enhance its capabilities in supporting the offshore energy industry.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** March 2021
   - **Details:** The LEEF project is anticipated to create new jobs and support the local economy, which currently contributes £30 million annually and supports over 580 local jobs. The project aims to attract supply chain companies to Lowestoft, potentially leading to a renaissance in the local energy cluster.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
