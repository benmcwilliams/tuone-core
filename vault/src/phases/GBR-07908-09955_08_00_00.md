### Evolutionary Timeline of the Scottish & Southern Energy Facility in Lowestoft, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2021**: The Lowestoft Eastern Energy Facility (LEEF) is announced, aimed at upgrading marine facilities at Lowestoft’s Outer Harbour to support the offshore energy sector. The facility will provide state-of-the-art port infrastructure for operations and maintenance (O&M) activities and construction support for offshore wind farms. It will feature 360 meters of berthing space for simultaneous use by three Service Operation Vessels (SOVs) and up to 8 acres of flexible storage and marshalling area.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: The first phase of the LEEF project will require an investment of approximately £25 million. This investment is part of a broader strategy to enhance the port's capabilities to meet the demands of the growing offshore energy industry.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2021**: The announcement of the LEEF project marks a significant milestone for the Port of Lowestoft, indicating the start of a major development initiative aimed at enhancing the port's infrastructure to support offshore wind energy operations.

#### 4. Labour

The development of LEEF is anticipated to attract supply chain companies, further increasing job opportunities in the area.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
