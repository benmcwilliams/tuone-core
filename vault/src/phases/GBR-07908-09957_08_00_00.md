### Evolutionary Timeline of the Scottish Power Renewables Facility in Lowestoft, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2021**: The Lowestoft Eastern Energy Facility (LEEF) is announced, aimed at upgrading marine facilities at Lowestoft’s Outer Harbour to support the offshore energy sector. The facility will provide state-of-the-art port infrastructure for operations and maintenance (O&M) activities and construction support for offshore wind farms. The project will include 360 meters of berthing space for three Service Operation Vessels (SOVs) and up to 8 acres of flexible storage and marshalling area.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: The first phase of the LEEF project is expected to require an investment of around £25 million. This investment is part of a broader strategy to enhance the port's capabilities to meet the demands of the growing offshore energy industry.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2021**: Announcement of the LEEF project by Associated British Ports (ABP), which is set to develop the facility over the next five years. The project is part of a masterplan for the port to support the offshore energy sector and contribute to the UK’s net-zero greenhouse gas emissions goal.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2021**: The LEEF project is anticipated to create new jobs and support the local economy, which already benefits from the port contributing £30 million annually and supporting over 580 local jobs. The project aims to attract supply chain companies to Lowestoft, potentially leading to a renaissance in the energy cluster and further job creation.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2021**: The facility is developed by Associated British Ports (ABP), which operates the Port of Lowestoft. There is no indication of joint ownership or partnerships mentioned in the article, suggesting that ABP is the primary entity responsible for the facility's development. Scottish Power Renewables operates an O&M building at the port, supporting the East Anglia ONE offshore wind farm, but the ownership of the LEEF facility itself lies with ABP.
