## GBR-07908-10435

### Evolutionary Timeline of Windcat Workboats Facility in Lowestoft, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - Windcat Workboats operates a fleet of 46 crew transfer vessels (CTVs) primarily serving the European offshore wind sector. 
   - The facility is set to introduce the Hydrocat, the world’s first hydrogen-powered CTV, expected to be operational by June 2021. 
   - Plans are in place to investigate the construction of hydrogen refueling stations in dedicated Windcat ports.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - Compagnie Maritime Belge (CMB) is acquiring Windcat Workboats for £32.8 million in cash, along with assuming approximately £20.4 million of outstanding debt. This transaction is part of CMB's strategy to diversify into the offshore wind market and develop hydrogen-powered vessels.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **December 2020:** Announcement of the acquisition of Windcat Workboats by CMB, with the transaction expected to close on or before January 12, 2021.
   - **June 2021:** Expected operational date for the Hydrocat, the first hydrogen-powered CTV.

4. **Labour:**
   - Windcat employs approximately 180 shore-based and sea-going personnel. The acquisition by CMB is expected to enhance business growth, which may lead to job creation in the future.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - Windcat Workboats was previously owned by Seacor Marine Holdings and is transitioning to full ownership by Compagnie Maritime Belge (CMB) following the acquisition.
