## GBR-07928-02352

### Evolutionary Timeline of the MHI Vestas Facility in Able Seaton Port, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04/2020**: MHI Vestas begins preparations at Able Seaton Port for the pre-assembly of 90 V164-9.5 MW turbines for the 857 MW Triton Knoll offshore wind farm. The facility will handle turbine components including 89-meter-high towers, 80-meter-long blades, and 375-tonne nacelles. The site will provide a full package of offshore wind farm capabilities, including turbine handling.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04/2020**: MHI Vestas and Innogy jointly invest in the infrastructure works at Able Seaton Port to support the Triton Knoll project. The investment aims to enhance regional competitiveness and support local businesses.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04/2020**: Construction work begins at Able Seaton Port to prepare for the pre-assembly hub for MHI Vestas hardware.
- **10/2020**: MHI Vestas officially sets up operations at Able Seaton Port, with components for the Triton Knoll turbines expected to arrive shortly. Installation of the turbines is scheduled to start in early 2021.

#### 4. Labour
- **04/2020**: The project is expected to create jobs, with a project team of around 50 people being established at the site. Local suppliers and technicians are also engaged in the preparations, indicating a focus on local employment.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility at Able Seaton Port is operated by MHI Vestas in collaboration with Innogy and Able UK, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by MHI Vestas.
