## GBR-07928-07897

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Innogy Facility in Able Seaton Port, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** February 2020
   - **Details:** Work commenced at Able Seaton Port for the pre-assembly hub for MHI Vestas hardware for the 857MW Triton Knoll offshore wind farm. The facility will handle the pre-assembly of 90 MHI Vestas turbines, including 89-meter-high turbine towers, 80-meter-long turbine blades, and 375-tonne nacelles. The site will provide a full package of offshore wind farm capabilities, including turbine handling, in addition to existing operations related to offshore foundations and major vessels.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** February 2020
   - **Details:** Innogy and MHI Vestas are jointly investing in the infrastructure works at the Teesside facility. The investment aims to enhance regional and UK businesses' competitiveness in the global market, with a focus on the growth of offshore wind capacity.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** February 2020
   - **Details:** Preparatory works began for the construction of 140 square meters of heavy-duty quayside at Able Seaton Port. This includes establishing new drainage, upgrading the internal electricity supply, and plans for new infrastructure such as access roads, office buildings, and accommodation for a project team of around 50 people.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** February 2020
   - **Details:** The project is expected to create jobs in the region, with a project team of approximately 50 people being established at the facility.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** February 2020
   - **Details:** The facility is not owned exclusively by Innogy; it is a joint investment with MHI Vestas, indicating a collaborative ownership structure for the operations at Able Seaton Port.
