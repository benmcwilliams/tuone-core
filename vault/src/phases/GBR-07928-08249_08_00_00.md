## GBR-07928-08249

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Able UK Facility in Able Seaton Port, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2019**: Able UK began the transformation of Able Seaton Port (ASP) to establish a 140,000 m² handling facility with specialist quayside for offshore wind turbine components. This facility was specifically prepared for the Triton Knoll offshore wind farm project.
- **May 2020**: The facility received its first offshore wind turbine components, marking its first use for such purposes. The Triton Knoll project involves the installation of 90 MHI Vestas V164-9.5 MW turbines, which are among the largest turbines in commercial projects globally.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2020**: A multi-million pound investment was made to transform Able Seaton Port into a specialist assembly and construction facility for the Triton Knoll project. This investment was aimed at enhancing the port's capabilities for future offshore wind projects.
- **September 2021**: The total planned investment for the Triton Knoll project was reported to be approximately £2 billion, which includes the infrastructure developments at Able Seaton Port.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2021**: Triton Knoll generated its first power and delivered it to the grid, marking a significant milestone in the project timeline.
- **September 2021**: The final wind turbine for the Triton Knoll project was installed, with turbine commissioning expected to be completed in 2021 and final project completion anticipated in 2022.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2020**: The establishment of the turbine logistics and pre-assembly hub at Able Seaton Port involved local suppliers and technicians, indicating job creation in the region. Companies such as Global Wind Service, Dawson, and Boston Energy were involved in the project, contributing to local employment.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2021**: Triton Knoll is jointly owned by RWE (59%), J-Power (25%), and Kansai Electric Power (16%). RWE manages the construction and long-term operation of the wind farm on behalf of its partners, indicating a joint venture ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by Able UK.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones related to the Able UK facility at Able Seaton Port, focusing on its role in the Triton Knoll offshore wind farm project.
