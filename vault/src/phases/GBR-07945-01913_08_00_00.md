### Evolutionary Timeline of the SSE Facility in Shetland, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2020**: SSE received approval from Ofgem to construct a 600MW HVDC interconnector between mainland Scotland and the Shetland Isles, which is expected to be completed by Q1 2024. This interconnector is crucial for the construction of the 443MW Viking wind farm on Shetland's main island. The Viking wind farm is projected to be the UK’s largest onshore wind farm in terms of annual electricity output, capable of producing enough energy to power almost half a million homes.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2020**: SSE announced a significant investment of £580 million in the Viking wind farm project. This investment is tied to the construction of the wind farm and the associated interconnector.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2020**: SSE took a positive final investment decision on the Viking wind farm, which was a condition for Ofgem's approval of the interconnector. Construction of the wind farm is scheduled to begin in August 2020 and is expected to be completed in 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2020**: The construction of the Viking wind farm is expected to create approximately 400 jobs at peak construction and an additional 35 full-time operations and maintenance jobs throughout its operational life. The project aims to maximize local employment opportunities and support the Shetland and wider Scottish supply chains.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2020**: The Viking Energy partnership, which includes SSE Renewables, has limited investment from the Shetland Charitable Trust and developers of the Burradale Wind Farm near Lerwick. This indicates that SSE does not have exclusive ownership of the facility but is part of a partnership structure.
