## GBR-07953-00822

### Evolutionary Timeline of Vattenfall Facility in Great Yarmouth, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **03-05-2018**: Vattenfall announces plans to locate its offshore wind operations base at Peel Ports Great Yarmouth. The facility will support the development of the 1.8GW Norfolk Vanguard and 1.8GW Norfolk Boreas wind farms, located 47 and 73 kilometers off the Norfolk coast, respectively. The facility is expected to employ up to 150 skilled technicians for maintenance over a minimum of 25 years.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **03-05-2018**: The agreement with Peel Ports Great Yarmouth is expected to trigger significant investment from the offshore wind power supply chain, potentially creating thousands of jobs in the region. The commitment to a 25-year presence is seen as a catalyst for further investment in the local supply chain.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **03-05-2018**: Vattenfall and Peel Ports Great Yarmouth are in the process of finalizing their agreement, which is expected to be completed by summer 2018. This agreement will solidify Vattenfall's operations base at the facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **03-05-2018**: The establishment of the operations base is projected to create up to 150 skilled technician jobs for maintenance of the wind farms. Additionally, construction works on the onshore infrastructure are expected to create around 400 jobs.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **03-05-2018**: Vattenfall is in the process of finalizing an agreement with Peel Ports Great Yarmouth, indicating a partnership rather than exclusive ownership. The facility will serve as an operations base for Vattenfall's offshore wind projects, suggesting a joint venture or collaborative arrangement with Peel Ports.

### Summary
The Vattenfall facility in Great Yarmouth is set to become a significant operations base for offshore wind projects, with a commitment to long-term investment and job creation in the region. The partnership with Peel Ports indicates a collaborative approach to developing the facility, which is expected to play a crucial role in the growth of the offshore wind industry in East Anglia.
