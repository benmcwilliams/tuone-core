### Evolutionary Timeline of the ScottishPower Facility in Great Yarmouth, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2020**: ScottishPower Renewables, in partnership with Macquarie’s Green Investment Group, completed the installation of 102 SWT-7.0-154 turbines for the East Anglia 1 offshore wind project, with a total capacity of 714 MW. The project involved pre-assembling turbine components at Peel Ports Great Yarmouth, which included 75-metre fiberglass blades, tower sections, and nacelles. The facility played a crucial role in the construction and marshalling activities for this project.

- **December 2024**: ScottishPower Renewables announced plans for the East Anglia Two offshore wind farm, which will utilize Siemens Gamesa's SG 14-236 DD turbines. The project is expected to generate up to 960 MW of renewable energy and is set to commence offshore construction in 2027, with operations starting in 2028.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2020**: ScottishPower Renewables co-invested in Peel Ports Great Yarmouth to prepare the port for construction and marshalling activities related to the East Anglia 1 project. This investment facilitated the assembly and installation of turbine components.

- **December 2024**: Siemens Gamesa secured a contract worth over GBP 1 billion for the supply of turbines for the East Anglia Two project, indicating significant financial investment in the facility's future operations.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2019**: Installation work for the East Anglia 1 project began.
- **April 2020**: Completion of the installation of all turbines for the East Anglia 1 project was announced.
- **November 2024**: Announcement of the reservation agreement for the East Anglia Two project, confirming the use of Great Yarmouth for pre-assembly of turbine components.

#### 4. Labour
