## GBR-08013-01420

### Evolutionary Timeline of TotalEnergies Facility in Culzean, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2024**: TotalEnergies launched a pilot project to install a 3 MW floating wind turbine located 2 km west of the Culzean gas platform, which is 220 km off the eastern coast of Scotland. The turbine is expected to supply approximately 20% of the platform's power requirements, supplementing existing gas turbine electricity generation.
- **November 2024**: Fabrication of the floating wind turbine foundation commenced, marking the transition from planning to tangible progress.
- **December 2024**: Delmar Systems was selected to provide the complete mooring systems for the floating wind turbine, which will be moored to the seabed using a modular, light semi-submersible floater hull designed by Ocergy.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2024**: The pilot project was selected in Crown Estate Scotland’s Innovation and Targeted Oil & Gas (INTOG) leasing round, which supports the integration of offshore wind energy with oil and gas platforms.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2024**: Announcement of the pilot project for the floating wind turbine.
- **November 2024**: Foundation fabrication began, with the first plate cut on November 19.
- **December 2024**: Continued progress on the project with the awarding of contracts for mooring systems and the introduction of specialized mooring lines.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify job creation or job loss directly related to the pilot project at this time.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- TotalEnergies owns 49.99% of the Culzean field, while BP owns 32% and JX Nippon holds 18.01%. The floating wind pilot project is owned and developed exclusively by TotalEnergies.
