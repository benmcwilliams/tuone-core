## GBR-08013-08305

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Archer Wind Facility in Culzean, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/2024**: Archer Wind has ordered TP-Products’ bolted connections for the floating wind foundation for the Culzean pilot project. The facility will produce a single 3 MW floating wind turbine, which is designed to cover approximately 20% of the Culzean gas platform's energy needs.
- **11/2024**: Fabrication of the foundation for the 3 MW floating wind turbine commenced on November 19, marking the transition from planning to tangible progress.
- **12/2024**: Delmar Systems is contracted to provide the complete mooring systems for the floating wind turbine, which will be moored to the seabed using a modular, light semi-submersible floater hull designed by Ocergy.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2023**: The Culzean floating wind pilot project was selected in Crown Estate Scotland’s Innovation and Targeted Oil & Gas (INTOG) leasing round, indicating a significant investment in the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2024**: The Culzean Floating Wind Pilot Project is scheduled to be fully operational by the end of 2025. The project aims to demonstrate the viability of hybrid power generation on offshore facilities and test a promising floater design for future floating offshore wind projects.

#### 4. Labour
- **No specific job creation or job loss details** were mentioned in the articles regarding the Archer Wind facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is **owned and developed by TotalEnergies**, with Archer Wind contracted to deliver the floating foundation. Archer Wind is a company owned by the Norwegian oil and gas service provider Archer, indicating that Archer Wind does not exclusively own the facility but operates under a contract with TotalEnergies.
