### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ardersier Port Facility in Ardersier, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **08-12-2021**: Work has commenced on Europe’s first fully circular energy transition facility at Ardersier Port. The facility will repurpose disused oil rigs to manufacture floating offshore wind farm foundations. It will also include a £300 million ‘green steel’ mill and a concrete production plant, all powered by resources from the facility. Once operational, it will be the UK’s largest floating wind foundation fabrication facility.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **08-12-2021**: The first phase of the project involves a £20 million operation to dredge 2.5 million cubic metres of sand from the site over nine months. The dredged sand will be utilized as aggregate in the concrete production plant.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **08-12-2021**: The project has officially started, marking a significant milestone in the establishment of the facility. An agreement has been reached with floating wind company BW Ideol for exclusive access to the port for creating concrete foundations for their floating wind units.

#### 4. Labour
- **08-12-2021**: The project is expected to support the UK’s Green Industrial Revolution, which will likely lead to job creation in the green energy sector, although specific job numbers have not been detailed in the article.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **08-12-2021**: The facility is owned by Steve Regan, who is identified as the owner of Ardersier Port. There is no indication of joint ownership or partnerships mentioned in the article, suggesting full ownership by Regan.
