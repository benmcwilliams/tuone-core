## GBR-08019-02438

### Evolutionary Timeline of the ScottishPower Facility in Cromarty Firth, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05-03-2021**: ScottishPower, in partnership with the North of Scotland Hydrogen Programme (NSHP), announced plans to establish a green hydrogen hub in Cromarty Firth. The facility aims to produce and store green hydrogen using electrolysers powered by renewable energy sources, particularly from nearby wind farms.
- **23-07-2021**: A feasibility study identified Cromarty Firth as the ideal location for the UK’s largest green hydrogen electrolyser, with a capacity of 35 MW, capable of producing up to 14 tonnes of green hydrogen per day by 2024. The facility is designed for phased development to meet local and international demand.
- **05-05-2022**: ScottishPower and Storegga announced a partnership to develop a green hydrogen production plant in Cromarty Firth, expected to produce up to 20 tonnes of hydrogen daily by 2024, with potential expansions up to 300 MW.
- **30-06-2022**: Plans for a 50 MW green hydrogen plant were announced, aimed at providing hydrogen for heating processes in local whisky distilleries, with future expansion potential to 1 GW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **30-06-2022**: ScottishPower and Storegga supported the bid for Green Freeport status for Cromarty Firth, which could attract over £1 billion in long-term investment and accelerate the development of a larger-scale hydrogen plant.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05-03-2021**: Launch of the North of Scotland Hydrogen Programme, marking the beginning of the project.
- **23-07-2021**: Completion of a feasibility study confirming the suitability of Cromarty Firth for a large electrolyser, with plans for phased development.
- **05-05-2022**: Announcement of the first green hydrogen production plant, expected to be operational by 2024.
- **30-06-2022**: Support for Green Freeport status to expedite investment and development timelines.

#### 4. Labour
- **30-06-2022**: The development of the green hydrogen facility is expected to create high-quality green jobs and supply chain opportunities in the region, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **05-05-2022**: The facility is a joint development between ScottishPower and Storegga, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by ScottishPower.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones related to the ScottishPower facility in Cromarty Firth, highlighting its role in advancing green hydrogen technology and operations in the region.
