### Evolutionary Timeline of the Storegga Facility in Cromarty Firth, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2021**: A feasibility study identifies the Port of Cromarty Firth as the ideal location for the UK’s largest green hydrogen electrolyser, with a proposed capacity of 35 MW, capable of producing up to 14 tonnes of green hydrogen per day by 2024. The facility is designed to decarbonise local whisky distilleries and can be expanded to meet growing demand.
- **May 2022**: ScottishPower and Storegga announce a partnership to develop a green hydrogen production plant in Cromarty Firth, with an initial production capacity of 20 tonnes of hydrogen per day, and potential expansion to 300 MW in modular phases.
- **March 2023**: The project is confirmed to be capable of producing 20 tonnes of hydrogen per day, with plans to utilize the 29 MW Beinn Tharsuinn wind farm to produce an average of 11,000 kg of hydrogen daily.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2022**: The Cromarty Hydrogen Project seeks Green Freeport status, which could attract over £1 billion in long-term investment and accelerate the development of a larger-scale plant by up to 10 years.
- **March 2023**: The project receives government support under hydrogen expansion plans, further solidifying its financial backing.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2021**: The feasibility study is published, marking the initial step towards the development of the hydrogen facility.
- **May 2022**: The partnership between ScottishPower and Storegga is announced, with plans for the first plant to be operational by 2024.
- **March 2023**: The project is confirmed to be on track for hydrogen production to begin in Q4 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2023**: The development is expected to create new employment opportunities, helping to retain young people in the Highlands and supporting a just transition as fossil fuels are phased out.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2022**: The facility is a joint venture between ScottishPower and Storegga, indicating that it is not exclusively owned by either company but rather a collaborative effort to develop the hydrogen production capacity in the Cromarty Firth region.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones related to the Storegga facility in Cromarty Firth, highlighting its technological advancements, investment strategies, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
