## GBR-08019-04815

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Diageo Facility in Cromarty Firth, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05/2021**: Diageo, along with other partners, launched the North of Scotland Hydrogen Programme (NSHP) aimed at establishing a green hydrogen hub in Cromarty Firth. The facility will produce green hydrogen using electrolysers powered by renewable energy sources, specifically targeting the decarbonization of local distilleries, including Diageo's operations.

- **07/2021**: A feasibility study identified the Port of Cromarty Firth as the ideal location for the UK’s largest green hydrogen electrolyser, with a proposed capacity of 35 megawatts (MW) and the ability to produce up to 14 tonnes of green hydrogen per day. The facility is planned to be developed in phases, with the first phase expected to be operational by 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07/2021**: The feasibility study for the green hydrogen facility was backed by significant partners, including ScottishPower and Diageo, indicating a collaborative investment approach. The project aims to prepare for a final investment decision by 2023, with the first phase of the electrolyser expected to be operational by 2024.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05/2021**: Announcement of the North of Scotland Hydrogen Programme, marking the beginning of the project to establish a green hydrogen hub in Cromarty Firth.

- **07/2021**: Completion of a feasibility study confirming the suitability of the Cromarty Firth for the green hydrogen electrolyser, with plans for phased development and a target for the first hydrogen production by 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **05/2021**: The development of the green hydrogen hub is expected to create significant economic opportunities and job creation in the Highlands, as stated by Energy Minister Paul Wheelhouse. However, specific job creation numbers related to Diageo's facility have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **05/2021**: Diageo is part of a partnership in the North of Scotland Hydrogen Programme, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership of the facility. The partnership includes other distillers and energy companies, suggesting a joint venture approach to the development of the hydrogen hub.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic initiatives related to the Diageo facility in Cromarty Firth, focusing on its role in the emerging green hydrogen economy.
