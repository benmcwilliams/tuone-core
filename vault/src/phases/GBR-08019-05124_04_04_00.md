## GBR-08019-05124

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Glenmorangie Facility in Cromarty Firth, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05/2021**: A plan was launched to establish a green hydrogen hub at the Cromarty Firth, involving Glenmorangie and other partners. The hub aims to produce and store green hydrogen using electrolysers powered by renewable energy sources, particularly from nearby wind farms.
- **07/2021**: A feasibility study identified the Port of Cromarty Firth as the ideal location for the UK’s largest green hydrogen electrolyser, with a proposed capacity of 35 megawatts (MW) and the ability to produce up to 14 tonnes of green hydrogen per day. The facility is designed to decarbonise local whisky distilleries, including Glenmorangie.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05/2021**: The North of Scotland Hydrogen Programme (NSHP) was initiated, with funding from ScottishPower and the participating drinks companies, including Glenmorangie, to conduct a feasibility study on using hydrogen as an alternative fuel source for distilleries.
- **07/2021**: The feasibility study proposed a phased development for the hydrogen facility, with the first phase expected to be operational by 2024, leading to potential expansions to meet growing energy demands.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05/2021**: Announcement of the North of Scotland Hydrogen Programme, marking the beginning of the project aimed at establishing a green hydrogen hub.
- **07/2021**: Completion of the feasibility study confirming the viability of the hydrogen production plant, with plans for detailed engineering and community engagement to follow, preparing for a final investment decision by 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **05/2021**: The project is expected to create significant economic opportunities and job creation in the Highlands as it develops the green hydrogen hub, although specific job numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **05/2021**: Glenmorangie is part of a partnership (North of Scotland Hydrogen Programme) that includes other distillers and energy companies, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership of the facility. The facility is being developed in collaboration with ScottishPower, Whyte & Mackay, Diageo, and the Port of Cromarty Firth.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic initiatives related to the Glenmorangie facility in Cromarty Firth, focusing on its role in the emerging green hydrogen economy.
