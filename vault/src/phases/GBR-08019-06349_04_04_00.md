### Evolutionary Timeline of the Whyte & Mackay Facility in Cromarty Firth, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05/2021**: A plan was launched to establish a green hydrogen hub in Cromarty Firth as part of the North of Scotland Hydrogen Programme (NSHP). The facility aims to produce and store green hydrogen using electrolysers powered by renewable energy sources, particularly from nearby wind farms. The feasibility study initiated in this month focuses on using hydrogen to replace fossil fuels in the distilleries of Whyte & Mackay, Glenmorangie, and Diageo.

- **07/2021**: A feasibility study identified the Port of Cromarty Firth as the ideal location for the UK’s largest green hydrogen electrolyser, with a proposed capacity of 35 MW, capable of producing up to 14 tonnes of green hydrogen per day. The study suggests a phased development, with the first phase expected to be operational by 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07/2021**: The project is backed by significant partners, including ScottishPower and the distilleries involved, indicating a collaborative investment approach. The feasibility study is a precursor to a final investment decision expected by 2023, which will include detailed engineering and community engagement.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05/2021**: Announcement of the North of Scotland Hydrogen Programme, marking the beginning of the project aimed at establishing a green hydrogen hub.
  
- **07/2021**: Completion of the feasibility study confirming the viability of the green hydrogen production facility, with plans for phased development starting with the electrolyser by 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **05/2021**: The project is expected to create significant economic opportunities and job creation in the Highlands, although specific job numbers have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **05/2021**: The facility is part of a partnership involving multiple companies, including Whyte & Mackay, Glenmorangie, Diageo, and ScottishPower, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by any single entity.
