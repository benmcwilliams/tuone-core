### Evolutionary Timeline of the OCF Consortium Facility in Cromarty Firth, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2022**: The OCF consortium proposed the establishment of a floating 'super wind hub' assembly and production facility as part of their bid for Green Freeport status. This facility aims to stimulate component manufacture in Scottish and UK ports, enhancing local production capabilities in the renewable energy sector.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2022**: The OCF consortium's bid for Green Freeport status is projected to attract over £2.5 billion in new private sector investment into the UK’s green energy sector. This investment is tied to the establishment of the super wind hub facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2022**: The OCF consortium submitted its bid for Green Freeport status to the UK government, marking a significant milestone in the development of the facility. The bid is described as “compelling and logical,” indicating a strong foundation for future project advancements.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2022**: The OCF consortium claims that the Green Freeport initiative could create up to 25,000 jobs in the region, highlighting the potential for significant employment opportunities linked to the development of the facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2022**: The OCF consortium, launched in 2020, includes multiple stakeholders such as the ports of Cromarty Firth, Nigg, and Inverness, as well as Inverness Airport. It is backed by various public sector organizations and academic bodies, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by a single entity.
