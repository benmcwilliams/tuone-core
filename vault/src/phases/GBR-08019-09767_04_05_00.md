## GBR-08019-09767

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Port of Cromarty Firth Facility, Cromarty Firth, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05/03/2021**: The Port of Cromarty Firth (PCF) announced plans to establish a "world-leading" green hydrogen hub as part of the North of Scotland Hydrogen Programme (NSHP). The facility aims to produce and store green hydrogen using electrolysers powered by renewable energy sources, particularly from nearby wind farms.
- **24/05/2021**: PCF signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with Norwegian firm Gen2 Energy to import green hydrogen from Norway, enabling distribution by road, rail, and sea. This partnership is expected to support the establishment of a green hydrogen hub and provide security of supply for businesses aiming to switch to hydrogen by mid-2023.
- **05/08/2021**: A report identified the Cromarty Firth as the best location for a marshalling and assembly hub for the offshore wind industry, highlighting its existing facilities and capacity for expansion. The port has invested over £50 million in its facilities to support offshore wind developments.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05/08/2021**: The report emphasized the need for port alliances and clustering to support the offshore wind industry, indicating ongoing investments in infrastructure to enhance capacity and capabilities at the port.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05/03/2021**: Launch of the North of Scotland Hydrogen Programme (NSHP) to develop green hydrogen facilities in the Cromarty Firth area.
- **24/05/2021**: Signing of the MoU with Gen2 Energy marks a significant milestone in the creation of a hydrogen value chain between Norway and the UK.
- **05/08/2021**: The independent Port Enhancement Study confirmed Cromarty Firth's potential for supporting future offshore wind projects, indicating readiness for upcoming developments.

#### 4. Labour
- **24/05/2021**: The MoU with Gen2 Energy is expected to create skilled jobs and business opportunities in Scotland as the region transitions to clean energy sources.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Port of Cromarty Firth operates as a Trust Port, meaning it is not owned by private shareholders; instead, all profits are reinvested into the port's development. This structure allows for significant investment in expanding facilities and capabilities to support green hydrogen and offshore wind initiatives. 

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic initiatives at the Port of Cromarty Firth, focusing on its evolution as a clean technology facility dedicated to green hydrogen and offshore wind energy.
