### Evolutionary Timeline of the Siemens Facility in New Deer, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2019**: Siemens is the lead contractor for the construction of the onshore substation for the 950MW Moray East offshore wind farm. The facility will include three SVC Plus advanced STATCOMs and will supply three offshore substation platform topsides.
- **October 2019**: Siemens delivered the first of three 12-meter long transformers to the onshore substation at New Deer. These transformers are essential for changing the voltage of the power brought onshore from 220,000 volts to 275,000 volts for grid transmission. Installation of all transformers is expected to be completed by the first quarter of 2020, with all work at the substation anticipated to finish by the end of the first quarter of 2021.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2019**: Siemens has placed multi-million pound contracts with various UK companies for the construction of the transmission infrastructure for the Moray East offshore wind farm, indicating significant investment in local infrastructure and development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2018**: Construction work began on the Moray East project, with Siemens responsible for the onshore transmission infrastructure.
- **March 2019**: Work commenced on the onshore substation site.
- **October 2019**: The first transformer was delivered to the New Deer facility, marking a significant milestone in the project.
- **2020 (Q1)**: Anticipated completion of the installation of all three transformers.
- **2021 (Q1)**: Expected completion of all work at the onshore substation site.
- **2022**: The Moray East offshore wind farm is scheduled to be fully operational.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2019**: Approximately 130 people were reported to be working on the development of the onshore substation at New Deer, indicating job creation as a result of the project.
