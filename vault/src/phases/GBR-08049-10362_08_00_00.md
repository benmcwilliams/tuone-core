## GBR-08049-10362

### Evolutionary Timeline of the VolkerInfra Facility in New Deer, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2019**: VolkerInfra, in partnership with TFKable, won a contract to provide onshore high voltage cable for the 950MW Moray East offshore wind farm. The facility is responsible for the installation works connecting the wind farm to the National Grid at the onshore substation in New Deer, Aberdeenshire. The project includes three 220kV circuits and multiple 66kV connections for offshore transformer modules.
- **June 2019**: The first onshore cable sections were installed by VolkerInfra and TFKable. The cables produced are 220kV high voltage wires, with the next phase of production ongoing.
- **July 2019**: VolkerInfra continued its role in providing and installing high voltage onshore export cables, which stretch 35km underground from near Whitehills to New Deer.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2019**: The Moray East project received a 15-year Contract for Difference with a strike price of GBP 57.5/MWh, indicating significant financial backing for the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2019**: Contract awarded to VolkerInfra and TFKable for the Moray East project.
- **June 2019**: Installation of the first onshore cable sections commenced.
- **July 2019**: Continued progress on the construction of the onshore substation and installation of high voltage cables.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2019**: The project is noted to be one of the largest construction projects in Scotland, with a focus on involving local companies in the supply chain, which is expected to create job opportunities in the area.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify the ownership structure of the facility in New Deer, but it is implied that VolkerInfra is a key contractor working on the project, likely in a collaborative capacity with TFKable and Siemens, rather than having exclusive ownership.
