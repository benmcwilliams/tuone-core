### Evolutionary Timeline of the SSE Facility in Port of Nigg, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations**:
   - **Production Capacity**: The facility, named Nigg Offshore Wind (NOW), is designed to produce up to 135 offshore wind towers per year, along with transition pieces, suction buckets, and bespoke tubular structures for the floating offshore wind market.
   - **Facility Specifications**: The factory will be 450 meters long and cover an area of 38,000 m². It will utilize advanced robotics and welding inspection technology originally developed for the offshore oil and gas industry.

2. **Investment and Development**:
   - **Total Investment**: The facility is backed by a total investment of £110 million ($146 million).
   - **Specific Investments**: SSE Renewables will provide £15 million ($20 million) in debt financing. Additionally, Mainstream Renewable Power will contribute £5 million ($6.4 million) in debt and act as a strategic partner for the facility's long-term development. The facility is also expected to receive funding support from the Scottish Government and the UK Government through various schemes.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**:
   - **Announcement Date**: The facility was announced on 07-12-2021, marking a significant milestone in the development of offshore wind manufacturing in Scotland.
   - **Construction and Operational Timeline**: While specific construction start and production start dates are not provided in the article, the announcement indicates a commitment to developing the facility to meet the growing demand for offshore wind energy.

4. **Labour**:
   - **Job Creation**: The facility is expected to create more than 400 direct long-term, high-value jobs. It will also provide opportunities for regional staff historically employed in the oil and gas industry to be re-trained and upskilled at the Nigg Skills Academy.

5. **Ownership Structure**:
   - **Ownership**: The facility is a collaboration between Global Energy Group (GEG) and Haizea Wind Group, with SSE Renewables acting as a strategic backer. The ownership structure suggests a joint venture rather than exclusive ownership by a single company.
