### Evolutionary Timeline of the ScottishPower Facility in Port of Nigg, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **11/2021**: A feasibility study was launched to explore the potential use of green hydrogen at the Port of Nigg. The study aims to identify processes and plants that could be powered by green hydrogen, which is produced using renewable energy sources. The facility has previously supported the construction of two of Scotland’s largest offshore wind farms, Beatrice and Moray East, and has been awarded the marshalling, storage, and logistics base for the Seagreen project.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **11/2021**: The Scottish Government announced an investment of an additional £100 million over the next five years to support the establishment of a hydrogen sector in Scotland, which is expected to create new jobs and economic growth.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **11/2021**: The agreement between ScottishPower and Global Energy Group was announced to investigate the generation of green hydrogen at the Port of Nigg. This project is part of a broader initiative to lower carbon emissions and support the UK’s offshore wind ambitions.

#### 4. Labour
- **11/2021**: The Scottish Government emphasized that the development of the hydrogen sector could lead to new job creation in Scotland, although specific job numbers related to the Port of Nigg facility were not detailed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **11/2021**: The facility is owned by Global Energy Group, which is collaborating with ScottishPower on the green hydrogen initiative. The ownership structure indicates a partnership rather than exclusive ownership by ScottishPower.
