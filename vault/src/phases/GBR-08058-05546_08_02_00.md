## GBR-08058-05546

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mainstream Facility in Port of Nigg, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations**:
   - **Production Capacity**: The facility, known as Nigg Offshore Wind (NOW), is designed to produce up to 135 offshore wind towers per year, along with transition pieces, suction buckets, and bespoke tubular structures for the floating offshore wind market.
   - **Facility Specifications**: The factory will be 450 meters long and cover an area of 38,000 m². It will utilize advanced robotics and welding inspection technology originally developed for the offshore oil and gas industry.

2. **Investment and Development**:
   - **Total Investment**: The facility represents a total investment of £110 million ($146 million).
   - **Financial Backing**: It will receive £15 million ($20 million) in debt from SSE Renewables, with additional senior debt from Sequoia Economic Infrastructure Income Fund. Mainstream Renewable Power will contribute £5 million ($6.4 million) in debt.
   - **Government Support**: The facility is expected to receive funding support from the Scottish Government via Highlands and Islands Enterprise and the UK Government through the offshore wind manufacturing investment support scheme.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**:
   - **Announcement Date**: The facility was officially announced on 07-12-2021, marking a significant milestone in the development of offshore wind manufacturing in Scotland.

4. **Labour**:
   - **Job Creation**: The facility is projected to create more than 400 direct long-term, high-value jobs. Additionally, regional staff from the oil and gas industry will have opportunities for retraining and upskilling at the Nigg Skills Academy.

5. **Ownership Structure**:
   - **Ownership**: The facility is a collaboration involving Global Energy Group (GEG) and Haizea Wind Group, with Mainstream Renewable Power acting as a strategic partner. The specific ownership structure details regarding exclusivity or joint ventures were not explicitly stated in the article. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and characteristics of the Mainstream facility at Port of Nigg, highlighting its role in advancing offshore wind manufacturing in the UK.
