### Evolutionary Timeline of the Haizea Facility in Port of Nigg, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date**: 07-12-2021
  - The Haizea facility, known as Nigg Offshore Wind (NOW), is set to be the UK’s largest offshore wind tower manufacturing facility. It will be a 450-meter-long, 38,000m² factory capable of rolling steel plates to produce towers weighing over 1,000 tonnes each. The facility is designed to supply components for both fixed and floating offshore wind markets and will incorporate advanced robotics and welding inspection technology.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date**: 07-12-2021
  - The facility will be built with a total investment of £110 million ($146 million). Financial backing includes £15 million ($20 million) in debt from SSE Renewables, additional senior debt from Sequoia Economic Infrastructure Income Fund, and £5 million ($6.4 million) from Mainstream Renewable Power. The facility is also expected to receive funding support from the Scottish Government and the UK Government through various schemes.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date**: 07-12-2021
  - The announcement of the facility marks a significant milestone in the development of offshore wind manufacturing in Scotland. The project is subject to achieving financial close, and once operational, it will be capable of producing up to 135 towers per year, along with other components for the offshore wind market.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date**: 07-12-2021
  - The facility is projected to create more than 400 direct long-term, high-value jobs. It will also provide opportunities for regional staff historically employed in the oil and gas industry to be re-trained and upskilled at the Nigg Skills Academy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date**: 07-12-2021
  - The facility is a collaboration between Global Energy Group (GEG) and Haizea Wind Group, indicating a joint venture structure for ownership and operation.
