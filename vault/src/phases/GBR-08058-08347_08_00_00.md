### BMS Heavy Lift Facility Evolution Timeline - Port of Nigg, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The BMS Heavy Lift facility at the Port of Nigg is involved in the onshore assembly and offshore installation of wind turbine components. The facility supports the Seagreen Wind Energy Limited project, which is Scotland’s largest and the world’s deepest fixed-bottom offshore wind farm.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The facility has benefited from the development of the new purpose-built East Quay, which was unveiled in July 2022. This area offers 160,000m² of adjacent project areas for laydown, storage, and assembly yards, enhancing the operational capacity of the facility.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - In December 2022, it was announced that Global Port Services, in collaboration with BMS Heavy Lift, secured contracts to support the pre-assembly construction of wind turbine components for the Seagreen offshore wind project. This project is expected to run through to summer 2023.

4. **Labour:**
   - The pre-assembly construction project is expected to support more than 100 skilled jobs at the Port of Nigg, indicating a positive impact on local employment due to the facility's operations.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The BMS Heavy Lift facility is part of a collaborative effort with Global Port Services, which is owned by Global Energy Group alongside the Port of Nigg.
