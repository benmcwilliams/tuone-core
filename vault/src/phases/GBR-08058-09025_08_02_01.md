### Evolutionary Timeline of Global Energy Group Facility at Port of Nigg, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **07-06-2019**: Global Energy Group's Port of Nigg is selected by DEME Offshore as the staging port for jacket foundations for the 950MW Moray East offshore wind farm. The facility has been significantly upgraded with £90m invested since 2011, including £50m on port infrastructure and £40m on heavy equipment.
- **28-01-2021**: An £18.9m redevelopment project for the East Quay at Port of Nigg is announced, with £8.3m in funding from Highlands and Islands Enterprise (HIE). The project aims to enhance the port's capacity for offshore wind activities.
- **11-02-2021**: A feasibility study is launched to explore the use of green hydrogen at Port of Nigg, aiming to reduce carbon emissions and support offshore component manufacturing.
- **08-12-2021**: Global Energy Group finalizes terms with Haizea Wind Group to build a £110 million offshore wind tubular rolling facility at Port of Nigg, capable of producing up to 135 towers per year.
- **04-07-2022**: The new East Quay is unveiled, increasing the port's deep-water quayside capacity to over 1200 meters, enhancing its capabilities for future floating offshore wind projects.
- **10-04-2023**: Production of J-tube frames for the 882MW Moray West Offshore Wind Farm begins at Port of Nigg, marking a significant milestone in the facility's operations.
- **12-09-2023**: The first transition pieces for the Moray West project arrive at Port of Nigg, further solidifying its role in offshore wind operations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07-06-2019**: Global Energy Group has invested £90m in the Port of Nigg since taking over in 2011, with ongoing investments to enhance infrastructure and equipment.
- **28-01-2021**: HIE approves £8.3m in funding for the East Quay redevelopment, part of an £18.9m project aimed at boosting the local economy through renewable energy development.
- **08-12-2021**: The offshore wind tubular rolling facility at Port of Nigg receives financial backing of £15 million from SSE Renewables and £5 million from Mainstream Renewable Power, alongside support from the Scottish Government.
- **16-02-2024**: The Port of Nigg West Quay Deepwater Expansion project progresses to Stage 2 of SOWEC’s Strategic Investment Model, confirming industry confidence in the port's strategic role.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **07-06-2019**: Port of Nigg is chosen as the staging port for the Moray East offshore wind farm.
- **28-01-2021**: Construction on the East Quay is set to begin in April 2021, expected to take 14-16 months.
- **04-07-2022**: The new East Quay is officially opened.
- **10-04-2023**: Fabrication of J-tube frames for Moray West begins.
- **12-09-2023**: The first transition pieces for Moray West arrive at the port.

#### 4. Labour
- **28-01-2021**: The East Quay development is expected to create up to 39 new jobs in the local area.
- **08-12-2021**: The new offshore wind tower manufacturing facility is projected to create over 400 direct long-term jobs.
- **10-04-2023**: The fabrication of J-tubes for Moray West supports 50 local jobs.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Global Energy Group (GEG) owns the Port of Nigg exclusively. The facility has been developed under GEG's management since its acquisition in 2011, with ongoing investments and partnerships to enhance its capabilities in the offshore wind sector.
