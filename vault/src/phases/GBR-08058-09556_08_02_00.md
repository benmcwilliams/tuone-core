## GBR-08058-09556

### Evolutionary Timeline of Nigg Offshore Wind Facility, Port of Nigg, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date**: 07-12-2021
  - **Facility Overview**: The Nigg Offshore Wind (NOW) facility is a £110 million ($146 million) offshore wind tubular rolling factory located at Port of Nigg in the Scottish Highlands. 
  - **Production Capacity**: The facility is designed to roll steel plates to produce up to 135 offshore wind towers per year, along with transition pieces, suction buckets, and bespoke tubular structures for both fixed and floating offshore wind markets. The factory spans 450 meters and covers an area of 38,000 m².
  - **Technology**: The facility will incorporate advanced robotics and welding inspection technology, originally developed for the offshore oil and gas industry.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date**: 07-12-2021
  - **Investments**: The facility will receive financial backing of £15 million ($20 million) in debt from SSE Renewables, with additional senior debt from Sequoia Economic Infrastructure Income Fund. Mainstream Renewable Power will contribute £5 million ($6.4 million) in debt and will act as a strategic partner for the facility's long-term development.
  - **Government Support**: The facility is expected to receive funding support from the Scottish Government via Highlands and Islands Enterprise and the UK Government through the offshore wind manufacturing investment support scheme.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date**: 07-12-2021
  - **Announcement**: The establishment of the Nigg Offshore Wind facility was officially announced, marking a significant milestone in the development of offshore wind manufacturing capabilities in Scotland.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date**: 07-12-2021
  - **Job Creation**: The facility is projected to create more than 400 direct long-term, high-value jobs. Additionally, regional staff from the oil and gas industry will have opportunities for retraining and upskilling at the Nigg Skills Academy to operate the machinery required for rolling the large structures.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date**: 07-12-2021
  - **Ownership**: The facility is a collaboration between Global Energy Group (GEG) and Haizea Wind Group, indicating a joint venture structure for the operation of the facility. GEG is the primary developer, with strategic partnerships and investments from SSE Renewables and Mainstream Renewable Power.
