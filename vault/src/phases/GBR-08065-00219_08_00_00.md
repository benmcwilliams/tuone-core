## GBR-08065-00219

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EnBW Facility in Port of Leith, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2022**: EnBW and BP signed a reservation and collaboration agreement with Forth Ports for the Port of Leith to serve as the marshalling port for the 2.9 GW Morven offshore wind farm. The facility will provide a bespoke offshore wind berth and a significant land bank for the laydown and marshalling of offshore wind farm components. The facility is expected to be operational by the end of 2023.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2022**: Forth Ports announced a £50 million (€59 million) investment into the new offshore wind hub at the Port of Leith, which is part of the Morven project. This investment aims to support the facility's transformation into a renewables hub and enhance its capacity for local content manufacturing.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2022**: The agreement between BP, EnBW, and Forth Ports marks a significant milestone in the development of the Morven offshore wind project, which was awarded during the ScotWind leasing round earlier in the year. The project is expected to generate enough power for over three million homes once completed.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2022**: The establishment of the offshore wind hub at the Port of Leith is projected to create up to 1,000 direct jobs and around 2,000 indirect jobs. Additionally, BP and EnBW plan to create up to 120 new direct jobs at their operational center in Scotland, contributing approximately £40 million per year to the local economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2022**: The facility at the Port of Leith is owned and operated by Forth Ports, while EnBW and BP are collaborating on the Morven offshore wind project. There is no indication of joint ownership; rather, the facility serves as a marshalling port for the project, implying that EnBW and BP do not own the facility exclusively. 

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the EnBW facility at the Port of Leith, highlighting its role in the Morven offshore wind project and its anticipated impact on the local economy and job market.
