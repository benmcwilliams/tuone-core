### Evolutionary Timeline of the Forth Ports Facility in Port of Leith, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2021**: Forth Ports announced plans for the creation of Scotland’s largest renewable energy hub at the Port of Leith, featuring a bespoke riverside marine berth capable of accommodating the world’s largest offshore wind installation vessels. The facility will include a heavy lift capability of up to 100 tonnes per square metre and a 57-hectare cargo handling site for lay down, assembly, supply chain, and manufacturing opportunities.
- **July 2022**: Forth Ports signed a reservation and collaboration agreement with BP and EnBW for the Port of Leith to serve as the marshalling port for the Morven offshore wind project, which is expected to have a generating capacity of 2.9 GW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2021**: A private investment of £40 million was announced for the development of the renewable energy hub at the Port of Leith, aimed at supporting Scotland's energy transition and achieving net-zero greenhouse gas targets by 2045.
- **July 2022**: Forth Ports confirmed that the £50 million investment into the new offshore wind hub would see the facility operational by the end of 2023.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2021**: Announcement of the £40 million investment and plans for the renewable energy hub.
- **July 2022**: Signing of the collaboration agreement with BP and EnBW for the Morven offshore wind project, marking the Port of Leith as a key operational site.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2021**: The development of the renewable energy hub is projected to support up to 1,000 high-quality, long-term direct jobs and about 2,000 indirect jobs.
- **July 2022**: The offshore wind hub is expected to create up to 1,000 direct jobs and 2,000 indirect jobs, with BP and EnBW's operational centre in Scotland contributing to job creation.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2021**: Forth Ports is identified as the owner and operator of the Port of Leith, indicating full ownership of the facility. The investment is backed by Forth Ports’ shareholders.
