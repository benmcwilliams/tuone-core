## GBR-08159-07621

### Evolutionary Timeline of CS Wind Facility in Campbeltown, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2017**: CS Wind's Campbeltown facility, which produces wind turbine towers, faced a significant reduction in workforce due to a slowdown in the UK onshore wind sector. The facility's production capacity was impacted as the workforce was reduced from 154 to 100 employees.
- **July 2017**: The facility began delivering turbine towers for Community Windpower’s 31MW Sanquhar wind farm project, which included nine Vestas V112 3.45MW machines. This order helped fill a production gap at the Campbeltown plant.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2020**: Local politicians and union representatives criticized CS Wind for not securing new contracts or investments for the Campbeltown facility, despite its potential to contribute to the growing wind turbine market in Scotland. There were calls for the company to either seek orders or allow new ownership to take over the facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2017**: Announcement of workforce reduction due to market conditions.
- **July 2017**: Start of deliveries for the Sanquhar wind farm project.
- **August 2020**: Public criticism of CS Wind's inaction regarding securing contracts for the Campbeltown facility, with calls for the company to either bid for contracts or vacate the site.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2017**: The workforce was reduced from 154 to 100 employees, resulting in a job loss of approximately 54 positions due to the restructuring of the facility.
- **August 2020**: Ongoing concerns about job security as the facility had not secured new contracts, leading to fears of further job losses or the potential for the facility to remain unproductive.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2020**: The facility is owned by CS Wind, a South Korean company. However, there were discussions about the potential for new ownership if CS Wind did not actively seek contracts or work for the facility. The site is ultimately under the oversight of Highlands and Islands Enterprise, which has been in contact with CS Wind regarding its plans for the facility.

This timeline captures the key developments and challenges faced by the CS Wind facility in Campbeltown, highlighting its operational status, labor implications, and ownership considerations over the years.
