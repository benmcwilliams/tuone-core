### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ørsted Facility in Hornsea 2, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2018**: Ørsted's Hornsea 2 offshore wind farm is awarded a Contract for Differences (CfD) for a capacity of 1386MW, marking it as a significant clean energy project in the UK.
- **June 2019**: The project is confirmed to feature Siemens Gamesa 8.4MW turbines, with plans for 165 turbines to be operational by 2022.
- **July 2020**: Ørsted announces the hiring of CHC Helicopter for crew transfers during the construction phase, indicating ongoing operational preparations.
- **October 2020**: The first foundation is installed, marking a significant milestone in the construction of the wind farm.
- **April 2022**: NKT completes the export cable connection for the 1320MW Hornsea 2, which will connect the wind farm to the National Grid.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2018**: Ørsted secures a €150m deal with Nexans for the supply of over 200km of high voltage cables for the nearshore section of Hornsea 2.
- **June 2019**: Bladt Industries is contracted to supply 135 transition pieces for the project, indicating further investment in infrastructure.
- **October 2020**: Ørsted signs a multi-million pound deal with Pict Offshore for a new lifting system, showcasing investment in innovative technology for safety and efficiency.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2018**: Hornsea 2 is awarded a CfD, marking the start of its development.
- **June 2019**: Contract awarded for transition pieces, with delivery scheduled from mid-2020 to mid-2021.
- **July 2020**: CHC Helicopter is contracted for aerial transportation during construction.
- **October 2020**: Monopile installation begins, with a total of 165 monopiles and transition pieces planned.
- **April 2022**: The export cable is commissioned, with the project expected to be fully operational later that year.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2020**: The contract with Pict Offshore for the Get Up Safe system leads to an increase in their workforce, indicating job creation linked to the project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Ørsted is the sole owner of the Hornsea 2 facility, as indicated by the contracts and partnerships formed for the project, with no mention of joint ventures or shared ownership in the articles.

### Summary
The Hornsea 2 facility, owned exclusively by Ørsted, has evolved significantly since its CfD award in October 2018. It is set to become the world's largest offshore wind farm, with a capacity of 1386MW, featuring 165 Siemens Gamesa turbines. The project has seen substantial investments in infrastructure and technology, with various contracts awarded for cabling, transition pieces, and innovative safety systems. The construction phase has progressed with the installation of foundations and export cables, aiming for full operational status in 2022. Job creation has also been noted as a result of the project's development.
