### Evolutionary Timeline of the Moray West Offshore Wind Farm Facility (Ocean Winds, Moray, GBR)

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2021**: Moray West offshore wind farm selected Siemens Gamesa to supply offshore wind turbines, featuring the latest Direct Drive technology. The project is designed to have a capacity of 850 MW, with plans for 85 wind turbines and two offshore substations.
- **March 2022**: The project scope includes the supply of 62 transition pieces by Lamprell, with a total capacity of 860 MW confirmed.
- **April 2023**: Financial close achieved for the 882 MW Moray West project, with construction set to begin.
- **September 2023**: Agreement signed with Global Energy Group for marshalling transition pieces at the Port of Nigg.
- **April 2024**: Installation of Siemens Gamesa’s 14 MW wind turbines scheduled to start, with the project expected to generate first power in 2024 and be fully operational by 2025.
- **November 2024**: All 60 Siemens Gamesa turbines installed, marking a significant milestone in the project’s timeline.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2021**: Siemens Gamesa announced a planned expansion of its Hull facility to support the Moray West project, which is part of a larger investment in the UK offshore wind supply chain.
- **April 2023**: Ocean Winds secured a total investment of €7.4 billion for multiple offshore wind projects, including Moray West.
- **November 2024**: The project is expected to inject over £800 million into the local Scottish economy throughout its lifespan.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2021**: Siemens Gamesa selected as the preferred bidder for turbine supply.
- **March 2022**: Lamprell signed a capacity reservation agreement for transition pieces, with a contract value of over $200 million.
- **April 2023**: Financial close reached, allowing construction to commence.
- **September 2023**: Transition pieces to be marshalled at the Port of Nigg.
- **April 2024**: Wind turbine installation campaign scheduled to run from April to October.
- **November 2024**: Final turbine installation completed, with the project entering the commissioning phase.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2021**: Siemens Gamesa announced plans to add 200 jobs at its Hull facility due to the Moray West project.
- **November 2024**: The project is expected to create over 1,500 Full Time Equivalent (FTE) years in Scotland, with more than 60 long-term operational roles based in Buckie.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2021**: Ocean Winds, a 50-50 joint venture between EDP Renewables (EDPR) and Engie, is the majority owner of the Moray West project, with Lithuania’s Ignitis Group holding a minority share.
