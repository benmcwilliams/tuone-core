### Evolutionary Timeline of Dajin Heavy Industry Facility in Moray West, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **06/2022**: Dajin Heavy Industry won a contract to supply 48 XXL monopile foundations for the 860MW Moray West offshore wind farm. The monopiles will weigh about 2000 tonnes each and have a diameter of 10 metres. The company has a current production capacity of 1 million tonnes per year and is planning to expand to 2 million tonnes with new facilities in Europe and China, focusing on next-generation foundations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **06/2022**: The contract with Ocean Winds for the Moray West project prompted Dajin Heavy Industry to invest in expanding its facilities, including a new 100-hectare fabrication yard in southern China and a planned foundation facility in Europe.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **06/2022**: Steel cutting for the first monopile has already started, marking the beginning of production for the Moray West project.
- **10/2023**: The first XXL monopile was installed at the offshore construction site, approximately 22.5 kilometers from the Caithness coastline.

#### 4. Labour
- **06/2022**: Dajin Heavy Industry expressed its commitment to maximizing local content for every project, indicating potential job creation in the local economy due to the offshore wind industry.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **06/2022**: Dajin Heavy Industry is a fabricator that is not exclusively owning the facility but is working under a contract with Ocean Winds, which is a 50:50 joint venture between EDP Renewables and Engie.
