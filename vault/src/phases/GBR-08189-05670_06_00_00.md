### Evolutionary Timeline of NextEnergy Capital Facility in Llanwern, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2020**: NextEnergy Capital (NEC) announces the construction of the Llanwern solar facility, which has a production capacity of **75MW**. This facility is recognized as the **largest solar farm in the UK** at that time. The facility is part of a broader initiative to develop **subsidy-free solar projects** in the UK, alongside another project in Strensham (40MW), bringing the total capacity of both projects to **115MW**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2020**: NEC secures a **non-recourse debt financing** of **£100 million** (approximately **US$123 million**) from Santander to fund the construction of the Llanwern and Strensham solar projects. The constructed value of the Llanwern site is expected to exceed **£60 million**. The financing is part of NEC's strategy to develop a new subsidy-free private fund, **NextPower UK ESG**, aimed at expanding solar capacity in the UK.

- **August 2022**: NEC announces the first close of its **NextPower UK ESG fund** at **£327 million**, targeting new-build subsidy-free utility-scale solar assets. The fund aims to double the amount of subsidy-free solar power in the UK and has a hard cap of **£1 billion**, potentially delivering around **2GW** of generating capacity.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Q4 2020**: Both the Llanwern and Strensham solar farms are expected to be connected to the grid, with Power Purchase Agreements in place for both projects.

- **August 2022**: The Llanwern facility is confirmed to be operational, contributing to the total installed capacity of **115MW** from both Llanwern and Strensham.
