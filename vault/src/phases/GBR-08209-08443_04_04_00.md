### Evolutionary Timeline of the Bruichladdich Facility in Islay, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2021**: Bruichladdich Distillery announced plans to decarbonise its distillation process using a dynamic combustion chamber (DCC) technology, patented by Jericho Energy Ventures. This technology aims to eliminate operational emissions by switching from fossil fuels to sustainable hydrogen energy. The project, named HyLaddie, is expected to demonstrate the capability of hydrogen in the distillation process by 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2021**: The project secured £2.65 million in funding from the Green Distilleries Competition, part of the UK Government's Net Zero Innovation Portfolio. This funding will facilitate the installation of the DCC and a zero-emission hydrogen boiler at the distillery.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2021**: Announcement of the HyLaddie project, which includes the installation of innovative hydrogen combustion technology at the operational Victorian distillery. The project is set to navigate the complexities of integrating new technology into an existing facility.
- **Summer 2023**: Completion of Phase 2 of the project is anticipated, which involves the production of hydrogen and oxygen from a larger commercial-scale electrolyser located offsite, in conjunction with a new renewable energy project on Islay.
