## GBR-08210-08446

### Evolutionary Timeline of Budweiser Brewing Group Facility in Magor, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** October 2021
   - **Details:** Budweiser Brewing Group announced plans to decarbonise its operations at the Magor brewery by using green hydrogen. This facility is one of the largest breweries in the UK. The project aims to build the first large-scale hydrogen generation system at a brewery, which will fuel production and logistics assets, including Heavy Goods Vehicles (HGVs) and forklift trucks. The facility will utilize existing wind and solar assets to manufacture green hydrogen.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** October 2021
   - **Details:** The partnership with Protium, a UK-based green hydrogen energy company, is aimed at exploring the deployment of zero-emission green hydrogen at the Magor brewery. The project is expected to create a commercial platform for the hydrogen economy, leveraging long-term zero emissions off-take agreements.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** Expected operational by 2024
   - **Details:** The project is set to be commercially operational by 2024, with the Hydrogen Production Facility including a battery, hydrogen storage unit, and a hydrogen refuelling station for HGVs. It is projected to save approximately 15,500 tonnes of CO2e per year from 2027.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** October 2021
   - **Details:** The project is expected to create new jobs and opportunities in the area, as highlighted by Lesley Griffiths, Minister for Rural Affairs and North Wales.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** October 2021
   - **Details:** Budweiser Brewing Group is collaborating with Protium for this project, indicating a partnership rather than exclusive ownership of the hydrogen production facility. The specifics of ownership regarding the hydrogen facility itself are not detailed, but Budweiser is the primary operator of the brewery.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic initiatives at the Budweiser Brewing Group facility in Magor, focusing on its transition towards sustainable operations through the integration of green hydrogen technology.
