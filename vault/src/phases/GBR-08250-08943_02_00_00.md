### Evolutionary Timeline of the GEL Facility in United Downs, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2023**: GEL is set to complete power production at the United Downs plant by 2024. The facility will produce 2 MWe of baseload electricity and up to 10 MWth of zero carbon heat, which will be utilized in a large housing development at Langarth. The facility is recognized as the UK’s first integrated deep geothermal power and heat project. GEL has successfully drilled and tested two wells at the site, including a production well drilled 5km underground. Future geothermal projects in Cornwall are anticipated to deliver 5 MWe of baseload power and 20 MWth of renewable heat energy each.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2023**: GEL received £15 million in funding from Kerogen Capital through its energy transition platform, CelerateX, to support the completion of power production at the United Downs plant and to prepare new geothermal sites in Cornwall. This investment is part of a broader initiative to initiate 25 MWe of renewable electricity and 100 MWth of renewable heat across GEL’s portfolio by 2028.
- **01/2020**: Thrive Renewables made its first commercial investment of £3 million into United Downs to support final testing of wells at the site.
- **2021**: Thrive Renewables invested an additional £0.47 million to fund planning applications for additional potential geothermal sites.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2023**: Announcement of the completion timeline for power production at the United Downs plant by 2024.
- **2019-2020**: Successful drilling and testing of two wells at United Downs, including a production well.

#### 4. Labour
