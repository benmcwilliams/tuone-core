### Evolutionary Timeline of the ScottishPower Facility in East Anglia, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2021**: ScottishPower Renewables announced the East Anglia Hub program, which includes the East Anglia ONE North, TWO, and THREE projects. The facility will utilize over 200 next-generation 14+MW Siemens Gamesa wind turbines. The initial capacity for East Anglia THREE is 1,400 MW, with planning applications for East Anglia ONE North (800 MW) and East Anglia TWO (900 MW) under examination.
- **November 2024**: East Anglia TWO is confirmed to generate up to 960 MW, enough to power nearly one million homes. The project will utilize 64 SG 14-236 DD* offshore wind turbines, with blades manufactured in Hull.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2021**: The East Anglia Hub program is valued at GBP 6.5 billion, with ScottishPower aiming to optimize the projects ahead of the Contracts for Difference auction.
- **November 2024**: ScottishPower announced a £4 billion investment specifically for the East Anglia TWO offshore wind farm. Additionally, the company committed to doubling its UK investment from £12 billion to £24 billion between 2024 and 2028.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2021**: Planning consent for East Anglia THREE is secured, while planning applications for East Anglia ONE North and East Anglia TWO are under review. Construction is expected to start in 2023, with completion anticipated in 2026.
- **November 2024**: The turbine supply agreement with Siemens Gamesa is formalized, valued at over £1 billion, marking a significant milestone for the East Anglia TWO project.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2024**: The manufacturing of turbine blades at Siemens Gamesa’s factory in Hull is expected to secure around 1,300 jobs in the region, with over 600 new employees recruited in the past year.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2024**: Siemens Gamesa is identified as the fully owned wind business of Siemens Energy, indicating that ScottishPower operates the facility in collaboration with Siemens Gamesa for turbine supply and installation, but the ownership structure specifics regarding exclusivity are not detailed in the articles.
