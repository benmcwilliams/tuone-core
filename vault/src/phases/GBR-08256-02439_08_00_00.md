### Evolutionary Timeline of the Siemens Gamesa Facility in East Anglia, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** February 2021
   - **Details:** Siemens Gamesa has been selected as the preferred bidder to supply and install over 200 next-generation 14+MW wind turbines for the East Anglia Hub programme, which includes the East Anglia ONE North, TWO, and THREE projects. The initial power generation capacity planned is 1,400 MW from East Anglia THREE, with additional capacities of 800 MW and 900 MW from East Anglia ONE North and East Anglia TWO, respectively.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** February 2021
   - **Details:** The East Anglia Hub programme has a total investment of GBP 6.5 billion. The projects are part of the UK government's Ten Point Plan aiming for 40 GW of offshore wind generation by 2030. The collaboration between ScottishPower Renewables and Siemens Gamesa aims to optimize the projects ahead of the next Contracts for Difference auction.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** February 2021
   - **Details:** The East Anglia Hub projects are in the planning phase, with East Anglia THREE having secured planning consent. Planning applications for East Anglia ONE North and East Anglia TWO are under examination. Construction is expected to commence in 2023, with completion anticipated in 2026.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** February 2021
   - **Details:** The development of the East Anglia Hub is expected to create job opportunities and support local economic investment across the East of England. Siemens Gamesa and ScottishPower Renewables plan to work with local authorities and colleges to maximize positive outcomes for local communities, including internships and project-based work for young people.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** February 2021
   - **Details:** The facility is part of a collaborative effort between Siemens Gamesa and ScottishPower Renewables, which is a subsidiary of Iberdrola. The ownership structure indicates a partnership rather than exclusive ownership by Siemens Gamesa.
