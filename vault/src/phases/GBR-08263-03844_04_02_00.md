### Evolutionary Timeline of the Cadent Facility in North-West England, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Focus**: The Cadent facility is involved in the production of blue hydrogen, which is derived from natural gas with emissions captured through carbon capture and storage (CCS). This technology aims to meet decarbonization targets while providing a low-carbon alternative to natural gas.
- **Operational Context**: The facility is part of the HyNet industrial cluster in North-West England and North Wales, which has been selected for government funding to develop a mini hydrogen economy, including hydrogen production, CCUS capability, and transport infrastructure.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Government Support**: The UK Government has backed the HyNet project, which Cadent is a partner in, as part of its Hydrogen Strategy. This strategy aims to create a significant hydrogen economy in the UK, potentially worth £900 million and creating over 9,000 jobs in the coming years, with projections rising to £13 billion and 100,000 jobs by 2050.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Funding Announcement**: In October 2021, the HyNet project was selected for Track 1 funding by the UK Government, marking a significant milestone for the facility's development.
- **Ongoing Development**: The facility is actively working on establishing the necessary infrastructure for hydrogen production and distribution, as well as addressing the challenges of fuel switching to hydrogen.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The development of the hydrogen economy, particularly through the HyNet project, is expected to create a substantial number of jobs, with estimates suggesting over 9,000 high-quality jobs in the near future and potentially 100,000 by 2050.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Partnership**: Cadent is a partner in the HyNet project, indicating that it is not the sole owner of the facility but is involved in a collaborative effort with other stakeholders to develop the hydrogen production and infrastructure.

### Summary
The Cadent facility in North-West England is focused on producing blue hydrogen as part of the HyNet industrial cluster, which has received government backing and funding. The project is expected to create thousands of jobs and is part of a broader strategy to develop a low-carbon hydrogen economy in the UK. Cadent operates as a partner in this initiative, contributing to the establishment of necessary infrastructure and technology for hydrogen production.
