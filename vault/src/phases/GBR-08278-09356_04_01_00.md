### Evolutionary Timeline of the Kraft Heinz Facility in Wigan, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09-02-2024**: Kraft Heinz announced plans to develop a 20MW green hydrogen plant at its Kitt Green manufacturing facility in Wigan. The facility currently produces 250,000 tonnes of food annually, including Heinz's baked beans. The green hydrogen plant is expected to produce around 2,000 tonnes of hydrogen annually, translating into 80GWh of capacity. This production will meet 50% of the manufacturing plant’s annual natural gas demand and reduce carbon emissions by an estimated 16,000 tonnes.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09-02-2024**: The development of the green hydrogen plant is contingent upon securing local planning and government funding, specifically through the Hydrogen Production Business Model (Hydrogen Allocation Round 2 – HAR2). The project is seen as a significant step in Kraft Heinz's efforts to reduce carbon emissions and align with consumer preferences for sustainable products.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09-02-2024**: Kraft Heinz signed an agreement with Carlton Power to explore the potential development of the green hydrogen plant. The project is in the exploratory phase, pending local planning and funding approvals.

#### 4. Labour
- **09-02-2024**: The announcement does not specify job creation or loss directly related to the green hydrogen project, but it indicates a potential for future job creation as the facility expands its operations to include hydrogen production.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **09-02-2024**: Kraft Heinz is pursuing the development of the facility in partnership with Carlton Power, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership. The facility is primarily owned by Kraft Heinz, but the project involves a joint effort with Carlton Power for the hydrogen plant development.
