### Evolutionary Timeline of the Cerulean Winds Facility in UK Central North Sea, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date**: 02-08-2022
  - **Details**: Cerulean Winds has partnered with Ping Petroleum UK to establish a facility that will utilize clean energy from floating offshore wind to power a Floating Production & Storage vessel at Ping Petroleum’s Avalon site. The project aims to demonstrate the use of floating offshore wind in decarbonizing oil and gas production. The facility will feature one of the largest floating wind turbines built in the UK, which will provide the majority of the power required by the production and storage vessel.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date**: 02-08-2022
  - **Details**: The project is expected to attract an investment of between £80-100 million, aimed at development and enhancing the UK’s renewable energy supply chain. The project was enabled by a grant from the UK Government’s Department of Business, Energy and Industrial Strategy (BEIS) through the Floating Offshore Wind Demonstration Programme, which supports innovation and optimization of the technology to be deployed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date**: 02-08-2022
  - **Details**: The project is set to begin production in 2025. The development concept has already been cleared by the North Sea Transition Authority (NTSA). The agreement signed between Cerulean Winds and Ping Petroleum marks a significant milestone in the establishment of one of the UK’s first oil and gas facilities powered mainly by offshore wind.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date**: 02-08-2022
  - **Details**: The project is expected to secure jobs and new skills in the North Sea oil and gas sector, contributing to the commitments of the North Sea Transition Deal, which aims to reduce emissions from oil and gas production.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date**: 02-08-2022
  - **Details**: Ping Petroleum UK acquired a 100% stake in the Avalon site in August 2021. The partnership with Cerulean Winds indicates a collaborative approach, but specific ownership details regarding the floating wind turbine and its operations have not been explicitly stated in the article.
