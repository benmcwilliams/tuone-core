### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hive Facility in North Kent Coast, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 09-11-2017
   - **Details:** A joint venture between Wirsol and Hive Energy proposed the Cleve Hill Solar Farm, which aims to produce a solar power capacity of "in excess of 350 MW." This facility will incorporate battery storage technology to provide balancing services to the grid and is designed to utilize an east/west panel orientation for more consistent energy generation throughout the day.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 09-11-2017
   - **Details:** The project is expected to bring in approximately GBP 27.25 million ($35.6 million) in investment over its lifetime. It is also projected to generate more than GBP 1 million per year for the local government. The developers have stated that the project will be built without any government subsidies.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 09-11-2017
   - **Details:** The Cleve Hill Solar Farm project is classified as a 'Nationally Significant Infrastructure Project' and will require a Development Consent Order (DCO) from the Secretary of State for Business, Energy and Industrial Strategy. The submission of the DCO application is scheduled for later in 2018, following two consultation phases and a full environmental impact assessment.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 09-11-2017
   - **Details:** While specific job creation or loss figures were not mentioned in the article, the project is expected to have a positive economic impact on the local area, potentially leading to job creation during the construction and operational phases.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 09-11-2017
   - **Details:** The facility is a joint venture between Wirsol and Hive Energy, operating under the name Cleve Hill Solar Farm Ltd. This indicates that the facility is not owned exclusively by either company but rather shared between the two developers.
