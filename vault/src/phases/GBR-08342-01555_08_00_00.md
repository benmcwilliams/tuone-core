## GBR-08342-01555

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners Facility in Beatrice, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **July 2019:** The Beatrice offshore wind farm, with a production capacity of 588 MW, was officially opened. It consists of 84 turbines, each with a capacity of 7 MW, supplied by Siemens Gamesa. The facility is located 13 km off the coast of Caithness in the North Sea and was completed in June 2019.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The Beatrice project represents a significant investment by its partners, including SSE Renewables (40%), Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (35%), and Red Rock Power Limited (25%). The total economic contribution of the project is estimated at over £2 billion over its lifetime, with £2.4 billion covering development, construction, and 25 years of operation.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **June 2019:** Completion of the Beatrice offshore wind farm.
   - **July 2019:** Official opening of the facility by Prince Charles, marking it as the world's fourth largest offshore wind farm.

4. **Labour:**
   - The Beatrice facility is expected to create an average of 800 jobs annually during its operational phase, with 370 of those jobs located in Scotland. The project has also contributed £460 million to the Scottish economy during its development and construction phases.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The Beatrice offshore wind farm is not exclusively owned by Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners; it is part of a joint venture with SSE Renewables (40%) and Red Rock Power Limited (25%).
