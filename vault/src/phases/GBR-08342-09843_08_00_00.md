## GBR-08342-09843

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Red Rock Power Limited Facility in Beatrice, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **July 2019:** The Beatrice offshore wind farm, with a production capacity of 588 MW, was officially opened. It consists of 84 turbines, each with a capacity of 7 MW, supplied by Siemens Gamesa. The facility is located 13 km off the coast of Caithness in the North Sea and was completed in June 2019.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **July 2019:** The Beatrice project represents a significant investment by its owners, with a total economic contribution of over £2 billion expected over its lifetime. The development and construction phases contributed £460 million to the Scottish economy and £1.3 billion to the wider UK economy.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **June 2019:** Completion of the Beatrice offshore wind farm.
   - **July 2019:** Official opening of the facility by Prince Charles, marking it as the world's fourth largest offshore wind farm.

4. **Labour:**
   - **July 2019:** The Beatrice facility is expected to create an average of 800 jobs annually during its operational phase, with 370 of those jobs located in Scotland.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **July 2019:** The ownership of the Beatrice wind farm is shared among three partners: SSE Renewables (40%), Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (35%), and Red Rock Power Limited (25%). Therefore, Red Rock Power Limited does not own the facility exclusively; it is part of a joint venture.
