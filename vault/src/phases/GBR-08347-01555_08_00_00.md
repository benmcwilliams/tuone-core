### Evolutionary Timeline of the Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners Facility in Scrabster, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **December 2021:** The Pentland Floating Offshore Wind Farm (PFOWF) project is initiated, with plans for an initial single 8.6MW turbine demonstrator to showcase new floating wind technology. The second stage will involve a larger array of up to 10 turbines, collectively capable of generating up to 100MW. The construction of the array is expected to commence in 2024, with operations starting in 2026.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **December 2021:** Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (CIP) has entered the ScotWind process as part of a consortium with SSE Renewables and Marubeni Corporation, indicating significant investment in the development of offshore wind projects in Scotland.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **December 2021:** A memorandum of understanding (MoU) is signed between the Pentland Floating Offshore Wind Farm project and Scrabster Harbour Trust, focusing on the development of operations and maintenance (O&M) requirements and facilities. The project is set to utilize Scrabster's facilities for various stages, including commissioning and deployment of wind measurement equipment.

4. **Labour:**
   - **December 2021:** The Pentland project is expected to bring work to Scrabster and the surrounding area, contributing to local job creation through the development and operational phases of the wind farm.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **December 2021:** The PFOWF is managed by Copenhagen Offshore Partners (COP) on behalf of Highland Wind Limited, which is majority owned by Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (CIP) through one of its funds. This indicates that CIP has a significant ownership stake in the facility, although it is not exclusively owned by them.
