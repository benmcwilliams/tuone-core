### Evolutionary Timeline of the Copenhagen Offshore Partners Facility in Scrabster, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2021**: The Pentland Floating Offshore Wind Farm (PFOWF) project is initiated, which will initially consist of a single 8.6MW turbine demonstrator. This project aims to showcase new floating wind technology with significant potential for localization in Scotland. The second stage will involve a larger array of up to 10 turbines, collectively capable of generating up to 100MW. Construction for this array is expected to commence in 2024, with operations starting in 2026.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2021**: The memorandum of understanding (MoU) signed with Scrabster Harbour Trust indicates a commitment to develop operations and maintenance (O&M) requirements and facilities for the PFOWF project. Investments in local infrastructure, such as the St Ola Pier and deep-water basin, are highlighted as enhancing Scrabster's position to support the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2021**: The MoU marks a significant milestone in the development of the PFOWF project, which is expected to bring work to Scrabster and establish it as the O&M facility for the project. The project is planned in two stages, with the first stage's turbine deployment expected in 2024 and the larger array operational by 2026.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2021**: The PFOWF project is anticipated to create direct job opportunities in Scrabster and the surrounding area, as indicated by the local supply chain and socioeconomic study being conducted in collaboration with the University of Highlands and Islands and Xodus.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2021**: The PFOWF project is managed by Copenhagen Offshore Partners (COP) on behalf of Highland Wind Limited, which is majority owned by Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (CIP) through one of its funds. This indicates that the facility is not exclusively owned by COP but is part of a larger ownership structure involving CIP.
