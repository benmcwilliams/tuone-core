### Evolutionary Timeline of the Highland Wind Limited Facility in Scrabster, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2021**: The Pentland Floating Offshore Wind Farm (PFOWF) project is initiated, with plans for an initial single 8.6MW turbine demonstrator to showcase new floating wind technology. The second stage will involve a larger array of up to 10 turbines, collectively capable of generating up to 100MW. The construction of the array is expected to commence in 2024, with operations starting in 2026. Upon completion, it will be the largest floating wind farm in the world.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2021**: The article mentions that the project is supported by investments and infrastructure developments at Scrabster, including the recent opening of the St Ola Pier and a deep-water basin, enhancing the facility's operational capabilities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2021**: A memorandum of understanding (MoU) is signed between the Pentland Floating Offshore Wind Farm project and Scrabster Harbour Trust, focusing on the development of operations and maintenance (O&M) requirements and services. The project is in its early stages, with vessel mobilization and demobilization activities already taking place for surveys and equipment deployment.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2021**: The project is expected to bring work opportunities to Scrabster and the surrounding area, contributing to local job creation through the development of O&M facilities and services.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2021**: Highland Wind Limited is majority owned by Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (CIP) through one of its funds. Copenhagen Offshore Partners (COP) is managing the development activities on behalf of Highland Wind Limited.
