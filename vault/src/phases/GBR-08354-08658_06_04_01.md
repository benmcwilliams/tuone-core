## GBR-08354-08658

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Dyesol Facility in Shotton, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **06-11-2008**: Dyesol opened its photovoltaic accelerator facility in Shotton, North Wales, in partnership with Corus. The facility focuses on the commercialization of Dye Solar Cell (DSC) technology, which can be integrated into steel sheeting building products. The technology is designed to offer lower manufacturing costs and higher electricity output in various light conditions.
- **23-11-2011**: Dyesol and Tata Steel progressed to constructing a 20m² DSC roof array at the Sustainable Building Envelope Centre (SBEC) in Shotton. The DSC modules are designed to produce electricity even in low light and can replace conventional building materials, aiming for grid parity with lower-cost materials.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10-08-2009**: The joint BIPV project between Dyesol and Corus received £5 million in support from the Welsh Assembly Government (WAG) to advance the commercial manufacturing of DSC on steel. This funding is tied to the project's fifth development stage, which includes production trials and acceptance testing.
- **23-11-2011**: The project received an additional £5 million technology grant from the Welsh Assembly, contributing to the £11 million project aimed at enhancing DSC technology and production capabilities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **06-11-2008**: Opening of the photovoltaic accelerator facility in Shotton.
- **10-08-2009**: The project entered its fifth stage of development, with expectations to complete the industrialization phase by May 2010.
- **23-11-2011**: Completion of the DSC roof array project in June 2011, marking a significant milestone in the development of high-performance DSC technology.

#### 4. Labour
- **10-08-2009**: The joint project at the Shotton facility employed a 30-person technology team dedicated to bringing the BIPV product to market.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **06-11-2008**: Dyesol operates the facility in partnership with Corus, indicating a joint venture structure for the development and commercialization of DSC technology. Dyesol is the exclusive supplier of DSC materials to Corus for this project.
