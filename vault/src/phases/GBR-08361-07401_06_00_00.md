## GBR-08361-07401

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Cubico Sustainable Investments Facility in Frodsham, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date: 08-11-2024**: Cubico Sustainable Investments announced the launch of the second phase of consultation for the **Frodsham Solar** project, which aims to develop a **130MW solar farm**. The facility is expected to generate enough clean electricity to power over **40,000 homes annually** by **2029**. The project will also include a **Battery Energy Storage System (BESS)** to store and supply electricity as needed.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date: 12-11-2024**: The project is part of a larger initiative to align with **Net Zero North West’s** strategic vision and support local councils in reaching net zero by **2045**. The specific investment details related to the solar farm's capacity have not been disclosed, but the project is part of a broader investment strategy by Cubico, which acquired **Peel Cubico Renewables** in **January 2024**, securing a **50% stake** in the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date: 12-11-2024**: The second consultation phase for the **Frodsham Solar NSIP** project is set to last until **19 December 2024**. The first consultation phase was conducted in **June 2023**. An application for consent is planned for **Spring 2025**, with the project anticipated to be operational by **2029**.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date: 08-11-2024**: Cubico has committed to providing a **Community Fund** of over **£2 million** throughout the project's life, which will support local initiatives, including training and employment opportunities for local residents. However, specific job creation or loss figures related to the project have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date: 12-11-2024**: The facility was initially developed as a **joint venture** between Peel Cubico Renewables and Cubico Sustainable Investments. However, following the acquisition of Peel's stake in **January 2024**, Cubico now holds **full ownership** of the Frodsham Solar project.
