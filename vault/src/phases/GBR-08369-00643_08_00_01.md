## GBR-08369-00643

### Evolutionary Timeline of the RWE Facility in Triton Knoll, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2020**: RWE completed undersea work on the Triton Knoll offshore wind farm, which has a production capacity of 857MW. The facility includes 90 turbine foundations and two 50km-long power export cables. It is designed to power over 800,000 UK homes.
- **September 2021**: The final wind turbine was installed, marking a significant milestone in the construction of the facility. Each of the 90 Vestas 9.5 MW turbines can power a typical home in the UK for 29 hours with just one rotation.
- **January 2022**: RWE completed turbine commissioning at the 860MW Triton Knoll offshore wind farm. Full operations were expected in the first quarter of 2022.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2020**: The total investment in the Triton Knoll project was approximately $2.64 billion (£2 billion), with RWE owning 59%, J-Power 25%, and Kansai Electric Power 16%. 
- **January 2022**: The investment for the Triton Knoll project was reported to be around €2.4 billion, which includes construction and grid connection. RWE committed to investing €50 billion gross through to 2030 to expand its green generation capacity, with around €18 billion earmarked for the UK.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2017**: The UK Department for Business, Energy & Industrial Strategy awarded a contract for difference (CfD) to Triton Knoll.
- **February 2021**: The project generated its first power and delivered it to the grid.
- **January 2022**: RWE announced the completion of turbine commissioning, with full operations expected in the first quarter of 2022.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2022**: The expansion of the wind farm’s operations and maintenance base into the new ‘Grimsby Hub’ facility was expected to create around 60 new local jobs, strengthening RWE's relationship with the region’s businesses and communities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2020**: Triton Knoll is jointly owned by RWE (59%), J-Power (25%), and Kansai Electric Power (16%). RWE leads the construction, operation, and maintenance of the wind farm on behalf of its project partners. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones of the RWE facility in Triton Knoll, GBR, highlighting its technological advancements, investments, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
