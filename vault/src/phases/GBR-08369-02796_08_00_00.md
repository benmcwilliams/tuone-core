### Evolutionary Timeline of the DEME Facility in Triton Knoll, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** September 2021
   - **Details:** The Triton Knoll offshore wind farm consists of 90 state-of-the-art Vestas 9.5 megawatts (MW) wind turbines. Each turbine is capable of generating enough electricity to power a typical UK home for 29 hours with just one rotation. Once fully operational, the facility will generate enough green electricity for over 800,000 UK homes. The project remains on schedule for turbine commissioning in 2021 and final completion in 2022.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** September 2021
   - **Details:** The total planned investment for the Triton Knoll project amounts to approximately £2 billion. The investment has also led to the development of infrastructure at Able Seaton Port, which has been upgraded to serve as a turbine assembly and installation facility.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** February 2021
   - **Details:** Triton Knoll generated its first power and delivered it to the grid. Turbine installation commenced in January 2021 and was completed on schedule by September 2021, with the final turbine being installed.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** September 2021
   - **Details:** The investment in Triton Knoll has resulted in the development of infrastructure at Able Seaton Port, which includes accommodation and office units for workers, indicating job creation related to the project.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** September 2021
   - **Details:** Triton Knoll is jointly owned by RWE (59%), J-Power (25%), and Kansai Electric Power (16%). RWE leads the construction and long-term operation and maintenance of the wind farm on behalf of the project partners.
