## GBR-08369-09230

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Innogy Renewables UK Ltd Facility in Triton Knoll, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date**: 08-10-2018
  - **Details**: The Triton Knoll offshore wind farm, developed by Innogy Renewables UK Ltd, is planned to have an installed capacity of approximately 860MW. It will consist of 90 MHI Vestas 9.5MW wind turbines, scheduled to be operational in 2021. The facility will utilize a 220kV land export cable to connect to the UK grid, transmitting power over a distance of around 57km.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date**: 08-10-2018
  - **Details**: Nexans was awarded a contract worth over EUR 35 million to supply the land export cable and accessories for the Triton Knoll project. This investment is critical for the onshore electrical system that will deliver wind power from the turbines to UK households.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date**: 08-10-2018
  - **Details**: The project entered full onshore construction with the announcement of the contract with Nexans. The first cable drums are scheduled for delivery in the first quarter of 2019, with jointing works planned for the second quarter of 2019. Commissioning and testing of the cable system are expected to take place in summer 2020.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date**: 08-10-2018
  - **Details**: To support the Triton Knoll project, Nexans will strengthen its local UK workforce, including project, site, and QHSE managers, as well as chief jointers. This indicates job creation associated with the project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date**: 08-10-2018
  - **Details**: The Triton Knoll offshore wind farm is developed by Innogy Renewables UK Ltd in partnership with J-Power and Kansai Electric Power, indicating a joint venture ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by Innogy.
