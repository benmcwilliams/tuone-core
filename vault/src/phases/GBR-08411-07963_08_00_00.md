### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ocean Winds Facility in Invergordon, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2023**: Ocean Winds selected the Port of Cromarty Firth (POCF) for marshalling 62 monopiles for the 882 MW Moray West offshore wind project. The monopiles, weighing up to 2,000 tonnes, are among the largest in the offshore wind sector. The facility at Invergordon was recently expanded to accommodate this project, featuring two new berths and a 372-metre quayside, with a total laydown area exceeding 90,000 square metres.
- **November 2023**: The final batch of eight XXL monopiles was delivered by Dajin Heavy Industry for the Moray West project. The offshore construction began in October 2023, with the first monopile installation occurring at the project site approximately 22.5 kilometres from the Caithness coastline. The wind farm will consist of 60 Siemens Gamesa 14.7 MW wind turbines and two offshore substations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2023**: Ocean Winds signed a multi-million-pound contract with POCF for the marshalling of monopiles, indicating significant investment in the facility's capabilities to support the Moray West project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2023**: Announcement of the marshalling deal for the Moray West project, with monopile arrivals expected in the second quarter of 2023 and installation completion anticipated approximately one year later.
- **July 2023**: The first shipment of XXL monopiles arrived in Invergordon, marking a key milestone in the project timeline.
- **October 2023**: Offshore construction commenced with the installation of the first monopile.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2023**: The expansion of the POCF facilities and the marshalling contract for Moray West is expected to contribute to job creation in the local supply chain, although specific job numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Ocean Winds is a 50-50 joint venture formed by EDP Renewables and ENGIE, with a minority stake held by UAB Ignitis Renewables. This indicates that Ocean Winds does not have exclusive ownership of the facility but operates as a joint venture.
