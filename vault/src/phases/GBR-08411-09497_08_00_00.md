### Evolutionary Timeline of Moray Offshore Windfarm West Facility in Invergordon, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Project Overview**: The Moray Offshore Windfarm West is an offshore wind project located in the outer Moray Firth off Inverness, Scotland. It has a total generating capacity of 882 MW and will consist of 60 wind turbines, each with a capacity of 14.7 MW.
- **Production Capacity**: Once operational, the facility is expected to power approximately 650,000 households in Scotland for the next 25 years.
- **Marshalling Operations**: The facility has selected the Port of Cromarty Firth (POCF) for marshalling all 62 monopiles required for the project. The monopiles are among the largest in the offshore wind sector, weighing just under 2,000 tonnes.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Contractual Agreement**: On January 25, 2023, Moray Offshore Windfarm West signed a multi-million-pound contract with POCF to utilize the newly expanded facilities at Invergordon for marshalling operations. This agreement is expected to support further investments in the offshore wind sector.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Marshalling Timeline**: The monopiles are scheduled to start arriving in Invergordon in the second quarter of 2023, with installation expected to be completed approximately one year later.
- **Construction Phase**: The project is in the construction phase, with significant activities planned at the POCF's Quay West facility, which has been developed to accommodate large-scale offshore wind projects.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The marshalling operations at POCF are anticipated to contribute positively to the local supply chain and create job opportunities in the area, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the article.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture**: The Moray Offshore Windfarm West is developed by Ocean Winds, which is a 50-50 joint venture between EDP Renewables and ENGIE, with a small minority stake held by UAB Ignitis Renewables. This indicates that the facility is not under exclusive ownership but rather a collaborative effort among multiple stakeholders.
