## GBR-08412-01626

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Engie Facility in Fraserburgh, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility serves as the Operations and Maintenance (O&M) port for the Moray East offshore wind farm project, which has a production capacity of 950 MW. 
   - The O&M port will include a two-storey building, a berthing facility for wind farm support vessels, a pontoon facility for crew transfer vessel (CTV) access, and a storage and unloading area, covering approximately 1,880 m².

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The Moray East project was awarded a tariff of GBP 57.50/MWhr under a Contract for Difference (CfD) by the UK Government in 2017, which indicates a significant investment and subsidy for the project.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - On June 19, 2018, it was announced that Moray Offshore Windfarm (East) Ltd selected Fraserburgh as the preferred O&M port for the Moray East project. 
   - The facility is scheduled for commissioning in 2022, with construction of the necessary quayside O&M buildings and facilities planned.

4. **Labour:**
   - The article does not provide specific details regarding job creation or job loss associated with the facility.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The Moray Offshore Windfarm East is a joint venture company with ownership distributed as follows: EDP Renewables (56.7%), ENGIE (23.3%), and Diamond Generating Europe Limited (DGE) (20%). Therefore, ENGIE does not own the facility exclusively but is a part of a joint venture.
