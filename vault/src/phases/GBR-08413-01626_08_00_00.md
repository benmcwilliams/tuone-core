### Evolutionary Timeline of the Engie Facility in Moray East, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2018**: The Moray East offshore wind farm, developed by EDPR, Engie, and Mitsubishi Corp, is set to produce 950MW of renewable energy. The project will feature 100 MHI Vestas 9.5MW turbines.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2019**: Global Energy Group has invested £90 million at the Port of Nigg since acquiring it in 2011, with £50 million allocated for port infrastructure and £40 million for heavy plant and equipment. An additional £10-£15 million is planned for expanding quayside availability and adjacent yard space to support the Moray East project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2018**: Moray East is awarded a Contract for Differences (CfD) as part of the second allocation round, marking a significant milestone in its development.
- **June 2019**: DEME Offshore selects the Port of Nigg as the staging port for the jacket foundations of the Moray East offshore wind farm, indicating progress in the construction phase.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2019**: The contract awarded to Global Energy Group for the staging of jacket foundations is expected to create jobs and enhance local employment opportunities, although specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2018**: The Moray East project is developed as a joint venture involving EDPR, Engie, and Mitsubishi Corp, indicating that Engie does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a collaborative ownership structure.
