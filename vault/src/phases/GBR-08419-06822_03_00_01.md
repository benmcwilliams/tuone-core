### Evolutionary Timeline of Drax Facility in Argyll, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2020**: Drax Group’s Cruachan hydroelectric pumped storage plant began supplying critical system stability support services to the National Grid. The facility utilizes reversible turbines to pump water from Loch Awe to an upper reservoir, acting as a giant battery to store energy for peak demand.
- **March 2023**: Drax announced plans to expand the Cruachan facility with a new underground pumped hydro facility that will have a capacity of 600MW, enhancing the existing operations.
- **July 2023**: The Scottish Government approved the construction of the new pumped storage hydro plant, which will effectively double the total generation capacity of the site to over 1GW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2023**: The construction of the new facility is expected to cost approximately £500 million ($645.79 million) and is part of a broader £7 billion investment plan by Drax in clean energy technologies from 2024 to 2030.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2020**: Drax began a six-year partnership with the National Grid ESO to provide system stability services.
- **March 2023**: Drax hired Studio Pietrangeli to enhance the design of the new pumped storage expansion.
- **July 2023**: The Scottish Government approved the development of the new pumped storage hydro plant.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2023**: Drax indicated that the construction of the new facility would support almost 1,000 jobs across the supply chain.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify any joint ventures or partnerships regarding ownership; it is implied that Drax Group owns the Cruachan facility exclusively.
