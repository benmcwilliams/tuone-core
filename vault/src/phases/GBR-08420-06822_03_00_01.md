### Evolutionary Timeline of the Drax Facility in Ben Cruachan, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2021**: Drax Group initiated the planning process for a new underground pumped hydro storage power station, aiming to double the generating capacity at the Cruachan plant. The new facility will add 600MW to the existing 440MW, bringing the total capacity to over 1GW. The new power station will utilize reversible turbines to store energy from wind farms and release it when demand increases.
- **September 2023**: The expansion project, known as Cruachan 2, was confirmed to involve the construction of a new 600MW capacity facility adjacent to the existing plant. The project aims to connect to the grid by 2030 and will optimize the use of existing assets, including the Cruachan Reservoir.
- **July 2024**: Drax appointed Voith Hydro to advance the plans for the 600MW underground pumped hydro energy storage project. Exploratory ground works and seismic surveys are underway to gather geological data for the construction.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2023**: The Cruachan 2 expansion project is estimated to cost £500 million. The project is contingent upon the UK government establishing a financial stabilization mechanism for large-scale energy storage projects, which has been lacking since 1984.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2021**: Drax announced the commencement of the planning process for the expansion.
- **July 2023**: The Scottish government approved the expansion project.
- **September 2023**: Cowi was selected as an advisor for the Cruachan expansion, indicating progress in the project’s development.
- **July 2024**: Drax signed a partnership with Voith Hydro to move forward with the project, with ongoing exploratory works.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2021**: The expansion project is expected to support almost 900 jobs in rural areas across Scotland during the construction phase.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify any joint ventures or partnerships regarding ownership; it is implied that Drax Group owns the facility, including the planned expansions.
