### Evolutionary Timeline of the Drax Facility at Cruachan Power Station, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2020**: The Cruachan pumped storage project began supplying critical system support services under a six-year contract with National Grid ESO. The facility has four generating units and provides inertia to the grid without generating electricity, enabling more renewable energy sources like wind and solar to be integrated into the power system.
- **July 2023**: Drax Group secured development consent for a new 600MW underground pumped storage hydro facility at Cruachan, which will expand the site's overall capacity to over 1GW. This facility is expected to be operational by 2030.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2023**: Drax announced a £500 million investment for the construction of the new underground pumped storage hydro facility at Cruachan. This investment is part of a broader £7 billion strategic investment plan in clean energy technologies from 2024 to 2030.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2020**: Drax began its contract with National Grid ESO to provide system support services.
- **July 2023**: Development consent was granted for the new 600MW underground pumped storage hydro facility, marking a significant milestone in Drax's plans to enhance the Cruachan site.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2023**: The expansion project is expected to support nearly 1,000 jobs across the supply chain during the construction phase of the new facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Drax Group operates the Cruachan Power Station, indicating that the company has full ownership of the facility.
