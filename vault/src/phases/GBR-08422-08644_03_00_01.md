### Evolutionary Timeline of Drax Group Facility at Cruachan Power Station, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2020**: Cruachan Power Station began supplying critical system support services under a world-first power system stability contract with National Grid ESO. The facility operates four generating units and one unit will provide inertia to the grid without generating electricity, enhancing the integration of renewable energy sources.
- **July 2023**: Drax Group secured development consent for a new 600MW underground pumped storage hydro (PSH) plant at Cruachan, which will expand the facility's overall capacity to over 1GW. This project is part of a strategic investment plan in clean energy technologies.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2023**: The expansion of Cruachan Power Station is projected to cost £500 million. This investment is part of a broader £7 billion strategic investment plan by Drax in clean energy technologies from 2024 to 2030. The project is contingent upon the establishment of a financial stabilisation mechanism from the UK government.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2020**: Announcement of the six-year contract with National Grid ESO for system support services, marking a significant operational milestone for Cruachan Power Station.
- **July 2023**: Development consent was granted for the new underground PSH plant, with expectations for it to be operational by 2030.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2023**: The expansion project is expected to support almost 1,000 jobs across the supply chain during construction, highlighting the facility's role in job creation in the local economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Drax Group owns the Cruachan Power Station facility exclusively, as indicated by the references to Drax Group as the owner and operator in the articles.
