### Evolutionary Timeline of Dwr Cymru Welsh Water Facility at Craig Goch Dam, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: The Craig Goch Dam is part of the Elan Valley system, which has a combined storage capacity of 99,500Ml and supplies up to 364Mld (Megalitres per day) to Birmingham and 5Mld to the local area.
- **Updates**: Essential maintenance work has been ongoing since autumn 2021 to protect and future-proof the dam, including the reinstatement of the original 36” diversion pipe and installation of new hydraulic valves.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment**: Dwr Cymru Welsh Water is investing £1.8 billion in its water and sewerage network between 2020 and 2025, which includes the maintenance and upgrades at Craig Goch Dam.
- **Specific Projects**: The current maintenance project at Craig Goch involves multiple contractors and is focused on improving the dam's drawdown facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Autumn 2021**: Initiation of essential maintenance work at Craig Goch Dam.
- **Phase One Completion**: Initial works to remove silt and inspect the 36” valve were completed before Christmas 2022.
- **Phase Two Start**: Work is scheduled to recommence on May 15, 2023, with a drawdown of reservoir levels to facilitate further construction.
- **Anticipated Completion**: The project is expected to be completed by January 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The maintenance work involves multiple contractors, including Morgan Sindall and Whitland Engineering, which likely contributes to job creation in the local area, although specific job numbers are not mentioned.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The Craig Goch Dam is owned and operated exclusively by Dwr Cymru Welsh Water, which is a not-for-profit company owned on behalf of its customers. It has been owned by Glas Cymru since April 2001, specifically formed to acquire and own Welsh Water.
