### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Dungeness, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Status**: Dungeness B power station is currently in the defuelling stage as of June 2021. Dungeness A was decommissioned in 2006. Both facilities are not producing power.
- **Future Prospects**: There is interest in installing Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) at Dungeness, which are smaller and faster to construct than conventional nuclear reactors. The site is considered ideal for such developments due to existing infrastructure and workforce.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Government Support**: In July 2023, the government body Great British Nuclear initiated a competition for companies developing nuclear technology, including SMRs, to register interest for government support. EDF has expressed interest in potential future developments at the Dungeness site.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Defuelling Stage**: Dungeness B entered the defuelling stage in June 2021.
- **Decommissioning**: Dungeness A was decommissioned in 2006.
- **Future Development Discussions**: Ongoing discussions regarding potential future uses for the Dungeness site were confirmed by Andrew Bowie MP, the nuclear minister, in September 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **Workforce Engagement**: The local workforce has been described as engaged and enthusiastic about future developments, indicating potential job creation opportunities linked to the installation of SMRs.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: EDF, the French energy giant, owns the Dungeness site and is open to discussions regarding future development opportunities. They are positioned as custodians of the land around the site.
