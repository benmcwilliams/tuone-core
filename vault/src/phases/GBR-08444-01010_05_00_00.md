## GBR-08444-01010

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility at Hinkley Point, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Initial Announcement (2007)**: EDF announced the Hinkley Point C nuclear project, initially expected to start operations in 2017.
- **Cost Estimates (2016)**: By the time the project was confirmed, the budget estimate rose to £24.8 billion.
- **Current Capacity**: The facility is designed to produce 3.2 GW of power, enough to supply six million homes.
- **Production Start Date**: Initially set for 2017, the expected start date has been pushed to 2029, with the first reactor now expected to come online in 2029 and the second in 2030.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Cost Increases**: As of February 2023, the estimated cost of the project rose to £32.7 billion due to inflation. By January 2024, costs were projected to be between £31 billion and £35 billion based on 2015 values.
- **Funding Structure**: EDF is primarily financing the project, with China’s General Nuclear Power Group (CGN) holding a 33% stake. EDF can request additional capital from CGN if costs exceed their equity cap.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Milestone Achievements**: In December 2023, the installation of the dome on the first reactor was completed, marking a significant construction milestone.
- **Delays**: The project has faced multiple delays, with the latest updates indicating potential operational dates of 2029 or 2031, significantly later than the original schedule.
- **Regulatory Changes**: The UK government is exploring new nuclear projects and has committed to increasing nuclear capacity to 24 GW by 2050.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: A new engineering subsidiary, EDF EPR Engineering, was created in September 2023, employing 600 staff to enhance project efficiency.
- **Impact of Delays**: The ongoing delays and cost overruns have raised concerns about job stability and future employment opportunities related to the project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture**: EDF holds the majority stake in the Hinkley Point C project, while CGN retains a 33% stake. This indicates a joint venture ownership structure rather than full ownership by EDF.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and challenges faced by the EDF facility at Hinkley Point, highlighting its evolution in technology, investment, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure.
