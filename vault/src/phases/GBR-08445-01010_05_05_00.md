## GBR-08445-01010

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility at Hinkley Point C, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2018**: Construction of Hinkley Point C began. The facility is composed of two European Pressurized Reactors (EPR) with a total capacity of 3.2 GW (1630 MWe each), expected to generate low-carbon electricity for at least 60 years.
- **January 2024**: The operational start date for Unit 1 was revised to 2030, with costs estimated between £31 billion and £34 billion based on 2015 values, significantly higher than initial estimates.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2019**: The overall costs for Hinkley Point C increased from the original £19.5 billion to between £21.5 billion and £22.5 billion.
- **February 2024**: EDF announced further cost increases, with the total expected to rise to between £31 billion and £34 billion, and completion pushed back to at least 2029.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2019**: EDF revealed that Hinkley Point C was running years behind schedule, with an expected online date of 2025 or 2026.
- **May 2022**: The expected operational date for Unit 1 was revised to 2027.
- **January 2024**: The "base case" for Unit 1's operation was set for 2030, with costs revised from £26 billion to between £31-34 billion.
- **February 2024**: EDF pushed back the start date to at least 2029, with a new estimated cost of £31-34 billion.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2024**: Hinkley Point C has created 900 permanent jobs expected to last for over 60 years. Additionally, 1320 apprentices have been trained, and 7885 individuals have received training in various skills, contributing to a 10% productivity increase in the nearby town of Bridgwater.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **2024**: EDF holds a majority stake in Hinkley Point C, while China’s General Nuclear Power Group (CGN) holds a 33% stake. EDF is responsible for most of the construction costs, and under the contract, any cost overruns fall entirely on EDF, as the UK government does not cover additional costs.
