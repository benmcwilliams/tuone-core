## GBR-08445-02198

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Energy Facility at Hinkley Point C, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2018**: Construction of Hinkley Point C began. The facility is designed to consist of two EPR (European Pressurized Reactor) units, each with a capacity of 1630 MWe, totaling 3.26 GW of electricity production capacity.
- **February 2023**: The first of two 500-tonne reactor pressure vessels (RPVs) was delivered to the site, marking a significant milestone in the construction process.
- **May 2024**: The first reactor pressure vessel was installed, a major milestone as it is the first RPV installed in the UK in over 30 years. The installation allows for the completion of the primary circuit for unit 1.
- **Projected Dates**: The first reactor is scheduled to connect to the grid in 2027, with the second reactor expected to follow in 2028. However, the operational date for unit 1 has been revised to 2030.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2024**: EDF announced that the estimated cost of Hinkley Point C has increased from GBP 26 billion to between GBP 31-34 billion (in 2015 prices). The UK government does not provide funding for this project, and all additional costs are the responsibility of EDF and its partners.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2018**: Construction officially commenced.
- **February 2023**: Delivery of the first reactor pressure vessel.
- **May 2024**: Installation of the first reactor pressure vessel.
- **2027**: Expected connection of the first reactor to the grid.
- **2030**: Revised operational date for unit 1.

#### 4. Labour
- **Ongoing**: Hinkley Point C is projected to create 25,000 employment opportunities during its construction phase. As of the latest reports, over 1,000 apprentices have already been trained, contributing to local job creation and skills development.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- EDF Energy holds a majority stake in Hinkley Point C, with China General Nuclear Power Group (CGN) holding a 33% stake. The financial burden of any additional costs related to the plant's construction falls entirely on EDF and its partners, with no financial support from the UK government.
