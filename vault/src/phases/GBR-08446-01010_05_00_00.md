### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Hunterston B, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **1976**: Hunterston B commenced operations with two Advanced Gas-cooled Reactors (AGRs), Reactor 3 and Reactor 4, with a production capacity of 490 MWe each.
- **January 2022**: Reactor 4 was taken offline due to cracks found in the core, marking the end of operations for the facility after 46 years.
- **May 2022**: Work began to defuel Reactor 3, which had its generating lifespan extended to over 45 years.
- **September 2023**: Defueling of Reactor 3 was completed, and preparations to start defueling Reactor 4 were announced, with the aim to have all used fuel sent to Sellafield by mid-2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2021**: The UK government and EDF agreed on improved arrangements for the decommissioning of the AGRs, aiming to shorten the time for safe fuel removal as plants come offline. Specific investment amounts were not detailed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **07-01-2022**: EDF announced the closure of Hunterston B, marking the end of its operational life.
- **May 2022**: Initiation of defueling operations for Reactor 3.
- **22-09-2023**: Completion of defueling for Reactor 3 and announcement of upcoming defueling for Reactor 4, with a target for all used fuel to be sent to Sellafield by mid-2025.

#### 4. Labour
- The closure of Hunterston B in January 2022 resulted in job losses, although specific numbers were not provided in the articles. The facility had previously provided stable employment for thousands in the North Ayrshire area.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- EDF was the sole operator of the Hunterston B facility. However, following the decommissioning process, ownership is set to transfer to the Nuclear Decommissioning Authority (NDA) in 2026, with Magnox responsible for the long-term decommissioning.
