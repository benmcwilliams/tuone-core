## GBR-08446-02198

### Evolutionary Timeline of EDF Energy Facility in Hunterston B, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 1976**: Reactor 3 of the Hunterston B plant, which comprises two 490 MWe advanced gas-cooled reactors (AGRs), came online.
- **March 1977**: Reactor 4 started operations.
- **November 2021**: Reactor 3 was taken offline after exceeding its initial expected lifespan of 25 years, operating for over 45 years.
- **January 2023**: Reactor 4 was shut down, marking the end of operations for both reactors.
- **2022**: The facility entered the defueling phase, with nuclear fuel being removed from the reactors and transported to Sellafield for storage. Defueling of Reactor 3 was completed in September 2022, while defueling of Reactor 4 is expected to be completed in 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2021**: The UK government and EDF agreed on improved arrangements for the decommissioning of the AGR plants, aiming to shorten the time for safe fuel removal and facilitate the transfer of ownership to the Nuclear Decommissioning Authority (NDA).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2023**: EDF Energy submitted an Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) for the decommissioning works to the Office for Nuclear Regulation (ONR).
- **September 2024**: ONR approved EDF Energy’s application to start the decommissioning of Hunterston B following a public consultation and assessment of the EIA.

#### 4. Labour
- The EIA identified socioeconomic effects on the regional employment market, indicating job losses for workers at Hunterston B during the decommissioning phases.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- EDF Energy initially held full ownership of the facility. After the completion of defueling in 2025, the nuclear site license will transfer from EDF Energy to Nuclear Restoration Services (NRS), a subsidiary of the Nuclear Decommissioning Authority, which will then be responsible for the decommissioning of the site.
