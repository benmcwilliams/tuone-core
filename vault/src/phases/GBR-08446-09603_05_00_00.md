## GBR-08446-09603

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Nuclear Restoration Services Facility in Hunterston B, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Reactor Details**: The Hunterston B plant consists of two 490 MWe advanced gas-cooled reactors (AGRs), Reactor 3 and Reactor 4. 
  - **Reactor 3**: Came online in February 1976, initially expected to operate for 25 years but extended to over 45 years; taken offline in November 2021.
  - **Reactor 4**: Started up in March 1977 and was shut down in January 2023.
- **Defueling Phase**: Following the shutdown, nuclear fuel has been removed from the reactors and transported to Sellafield for storage. Defueling of Reactor 4 is expected to be completed in 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Decommissioning Approval**: The UK Office for Nuclear Regulation (ONR) approved EDF Energy’s application to start decommissioning Hunterston B, which includes an Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) that outlines mitigation measures for environmental impacts and socioeconomic effects on the local employment market.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Decommissioning Approval Date**: The decommissioning of Hunterston B was approved on 19 September 2024, following a public consultation and assessment by ONR.
- **Environmental Impact Assessment Submission**: EDF Energy submitted the EIA to ONR on 1 December 2023.
- **Defueling Completion**: Defueling of Reactor 4 is expected to be completed by 2025, after which the nuclear site license will transfer from EDF Energy to Nuclear Restoration Services (NRS).

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Impacts**: The decommissioning process is expected to have socioeconomic effects on the regional employment market, with workers at Hunterston B being released from their roles during various phases of the project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership Transition**: Initially, EDF Energy was responsible for the decommissioning of the Hunterston B facility. Following the completion of defueling, ownership and responsibility for decommissioning will transfer to Nuclear Restoration Services (NRS), a subsidiary of the Nuclear Decommissioning Authority (NDA).
