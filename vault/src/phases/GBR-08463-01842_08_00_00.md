### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ørsted Facility in London Array, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Operational Since**: The London Array offshore wind farm has been operational since **2013**.
- **Production Capacity**: The facility has a total installed capacity of **630 MW**, powered by **175 Siemens Gamesa 3.6 MW turbines**. It generates enough clean electricity to power approximately **500,000 British homes**.
- **Maintenance and Operations**: In **2022**, RWE won a ten-year operations and maintenance (O&M) services contract for the facility, set to begin in **early 2023**. This contract was part of a competitive tender conducted by London Array Limited on behalf of its shareholders.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Ownership Changes**: Ørsted (formerly Dong Energy) originally owned a **50% stake** in the London Array. In **2014**, it sold a **25% stake** to Caisse de dépôt et placement du Québec (CDPQ) for **£644 million**. In **July 2023**, Ørsted announced a deal to sell its remaining **25% stake** for **£717 million** to Schroders Greencoat, marking its exit from the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Commercial Operations Start**: The London Array began commercial operations in **2013**.
