### SeAH Wind Facility Evolution Timeline - Teesworks, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2022**: SeAH Wind announced plans to relocate its monopile factory to Teesworks, with a production capacity of 100 to 150 monopiles annually for offshore wind turbines, expected to start operations in 2026.
- **September 2023**: The facility is projected to be the world's largest monopile manufacturing plant, capable of producing up to 200 monopiles per year, with monopiles weighing up to 3,000 tonnes and measuring up to 120 meters in length.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2022**: SeAH Wind's investment in the facility is estimated between £200-300 million.
- **December 2022**: Construction of two concrete batching plants was completed at the site, costing £3 million, to supply concrete for the monopile factory.
- **September 2023**: A £100 million deal was struck with British Steel for the supply of steel for the facility, which is part of the £450 million investment in the monopile manufacturing facility.
- **February 2024**: Firth Steels secured a contract to supply profiled steel for the facility, contributing to the overall investment of £650 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2022**: SeAH Wind signed a binding agreement to relocate its factory to Teesworks and planned to file a planning application by late March 2022.
- **September 2022**: SeAH Wind signed contracts to supply monopile foundations for Ørsted’s Hornsea 3 offshore wind project, marking the first confirmed contract for the new factory.
- **December 2022**: Construction of the monopile factory commenced, with significant progress reported.
- **September 2023**: Major construction milestones were achieved, including the erection of steelwork for the facility.
- **February 2024**: The project continued to progress rapidly, with the first sections of the building envelope being installed.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2022**: The facility is expected to create around 750 direct jobs and 1,500 indirect jobs through supply chains.
- **December 2022**: Approximately 200 people were engaged in the construction project, with 80% hired from the local area.
- **September 2023**: The workforce on-site increased to nearly 400, with expectations of more jobs as construction progressed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2022**: SeAH Wind is identified as the owner of the facility, with no mention of joint ventures or partnerships affecting ownership.
