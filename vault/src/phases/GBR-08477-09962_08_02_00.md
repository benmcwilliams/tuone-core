### Evolutionary Timeline of SeAH Wind Ltd Facility in Teesworks, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2022**: SeAH Wind Ltd announced plans to build a GBP 200-300 million XXL monopile manufacturing facility at Teesworks, shifting from the initially planned Able Marine Energy Park. The facility is expected to produce between 100 and 150 monopiles per year, with operations commencing in 2026.
- **June 2022**: Planning approval was secured for the GBP 300 million facility, which will feature a 40-meter high and over 800-meter long building, providing 1.13 million square feet of factory space. The monopiles will be up to 120 meters long, 15.5 meters in diameter, and weigh approximately 3,000 tonnes.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2022**: SeAH Wind secured government funding in July 2021 for the facility, which is part of a broader investment strategy to support the UK offshore wind industry.
- **June 2022**: The facility's construction is part of a GBP 107 million investment in the South Bank Quay, which will facilitate the transportation of monopiles directly from the factory.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2022**: Announcement of the facility's location change to Teesworks and the intention to begin construction in July 2022.
- **June 2022**: Site works are scheduled to commence in July 2022, with overall completion anticipated in 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2022**: The facility is expected to create 750 direct jobs and an additional 1,500 jobs in the supply chain and during construction.
- **June 2022**: Reiteration of job creation figures, with 750 roles once the factory is fully operational in 2026.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2022**: SeAH Wind Ltd is a subsidiary of South Korea’s SeAH Steel Holdings, indicating that the facility is not owned exclusively but is part of a larger corporate structure.
