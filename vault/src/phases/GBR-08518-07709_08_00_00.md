### Edda Wind Facility Timeline - Montrose Port, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** March 2023
   - **Details:** The Edda Wind facility operates the Edda Brint, a Service Operation Vessel (SOV) designed for offshore wind operations. The vessel is 82 meters long and can accommodate up to 60 personnel. It features a motion-compensated gangway system for landing on offshore facilities and is prepared for future zero-emission operations with hybrid hydrogen power capability. The SOV will support the 1075MW Seagreen offshore wind farm, which is part of a larger 1140MW project.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** March 2023
   - **Details:** The Edda Brint has secured a 15-year contract with Vestas for operation and maintenance work at the Seagreen offshore wind farm. This investment is part of the broader development of the Seagreen project, which aims to generate around 5,000 GWh of renewable energy annually.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** March 2023
   - **Details:** The Edda Brint arrived at Montrose Port and is set to begin operations for the Seagreen offshore wind farm in the coming weeks. This marks a significant milestone in the Seagreen project, which is a joint venture between SSE Renewables and TotalEnergies.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** March 2023
   - **Details:** The Edda Brint will serve as a platform for technicians to perform operational and maintenance tasks on the offshore wind turbines, which may lead to job creation in the offshore wind sector, although specific job numbers were not mentioned.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** March 2023
   - **Details:** The Edda Wind facility operates under a contract with Vestas for the Seagreen project, which is a joint venture between SSE Renewables and TotalEnergies. Therefore, Edda Wind does not own the facility exclusively but is involved in the operational aspect through its SOV.
