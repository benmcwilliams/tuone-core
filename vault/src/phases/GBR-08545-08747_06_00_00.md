## GBR-08545-08747

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Elements Green Facility at Great North Road Solar and Biodiversity Park, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Production Capacity:** The facility is planned to be a 1GW ground-mounted solar PV plant.
   - **Innovative Approach:** The project will incorporate agrivoltaics, allowing for sheep grazing alongside solar energy production. Initially, a flock of almost 4,000 sheep will graze the fields, with potential expansion to 9,000 during lambing.
   - **Ecological Management:** Approximately 850 acres will be dedicated to ecological management, including the planting of 50,000 trees, creation of 25km of hedgerows, and establishment of wildflower meadows.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Partnerships:** Elements Green has formed partnerships with organizations such as the RSPB, Sherwood Forest Trust, Nottinghamshire Wildlife Trust, and Trent Rivers Trust to enhance biodiversity and ecological management as part of the development.
   - **Cost Savings:** The grazing of sheep is expected to save the project £5 million in mowing costs over its 40-year lifespan.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Current Status:** As of December 2024, the project is in the pre-application stage, having completed its first community consultation in February 2024.
   - **Future Plans:** An application for the project is expected to be submitted between April and June 2025, with the development anticipated to come online in 2027.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Job Creation:** The grazing partnership with local farmers is expected to support traditional farming practices, although specific job creation numbers have not been detailed in the article.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Ownership:** The facility is being developed exclusively by Elements Green, with no mention of joint ventures or shared ownership in the provided information.
