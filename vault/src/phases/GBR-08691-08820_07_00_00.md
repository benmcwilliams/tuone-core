### Enginius Facility Timeline - Bordesley Green Campus, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Enginius facility specializes in hydrogen-powered municipal vehicles, particularly focusing on training for the automotive industry to support the transition to hydrogen-fueled vehicles. 
   - The facility includes a 450m² ATEX-compliant workshop equipped with vehicle lifts and an EV training area, designed to meet industry specifications for safety and ventilation.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The establishment of the hydrogen vehicle training workshop was supported by funding secured in December 2022 through a competitive bidding process. This investment was aimed at enhancing the skills of the existing automotive workforce to meet the growing demand for hydrogen vehicles.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - The facility was officially launched in February 2024, marking the transition from a vision to a reality through a partnership between South & City College Birmingham (SCCB) and Enginius.
   - The facility will also serve as a research and development center, with plans for a Level 4 course focused on diagnosing faults on hydrogen vehicles.

4. **Labour:**
   - The new training facility is expected to create a major pipeline for jobs for young people and facilitate the upskilling of the automotive workforce in the region, particularly in response to the government's plans to phase out petrol and diesel vehicles.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - Enginius is part of FAUN Zoeller, which indicates that the facility is not owned exclusively by Enginius but is part of a larger corporate structure under FAUN Zoeller.
