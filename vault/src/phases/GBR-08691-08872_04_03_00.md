### FAUN Zoeller Facility Evolution Timeline - Bordesley Green Campus, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Focus**: The facility specializes in hydrogen-powered municipal vehicles, particularly the BLUEPOWER e-base refuse collection vehicle.
- **Facility Features**: The 450m² workshop is ATEX-compliant, equipped with robust ventilation, safety systems, vehicle lifts, and an EV training area. It serves as both a training and research facility for hydrogen vehicle technology.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Initial Investment**: The funding for the facility was secured in December 2022 through a competitive bidding process, aimed at supporting the automotive industry's transition to hydrogen fuel.
- **Partnerships**: A pioneering partnership was formed between South & City College Birmingham (SCCB) and Enginius, a subsidiary of FAUN Zoeller, to enhance training capabilities in hydrogen vehicle maintenance.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Launch Date**: The hydrogen vehicle training facility was officially unveiled on February 12, 2024.
- **Future Plans**: There are plans for a Level 4 course focused on diagnosing faults on hydrogen vehicles, indicating ongoing development and expansion of training programs.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The facility is expected to create a major pipeline for jobs, particularly for young people and those seeking to upskill in the automotive workforce, addressing the need for trained engineers in the hydrogen vehicle sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: FAUN Zoeller, through its subsidiary Enginius, is involved in the facility, but the facility itself is operated by South & City College Birmingham, indicating a collaborative partnership rather than exclusive ownership.
