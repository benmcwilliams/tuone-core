## GBR-08691-10075

### Evolutionary Timeline of South & City College Birmingham Facility in Bordesley Green Campus, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2022**: The facility was conceptualized with a focus on training for hydrogen-fueled vehicles, including light vehicles, PSVs, and HGVs. The facility includes a 450m² ATEX-compliant workshop equipped with vehicle lifts and an EV training area.
- **February 2024**: The facility officially launched, featuring a BLUEPOWER e-base refuse collection vehicle for realistic training. The workshop is designed to support the automotive sector's transition to alternatively fueled vehicles, particularly hydrogen.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2022**: Funding was secured through a competitive bidding process to develop the hydrogen vehicle training workshop. The investment aims to support the automotive industry's upskilling efforts in response to the growing demand for hydrogen vehicles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2022**: Initial announcement of the facility's development following funding acquisition.
- **February 2024**: Official launch of the hydrogen vehicle training workshop at the Bordesley Green Campus.

#### 4. Labour
- The facility aims to create a major pipeline for jobs for young people and upskill the existing automotive workforce in the region. The training provided will focus on Levels 3 and 4 qualifications, enhancing the skill set of current workers and supporting students in higher education.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned and operated by South & City College Birmingham (SCCB), in partnership with Enginius, part of vehicle manufacturer FAUN Zoeller. This indicates a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership, as the college works with industry partners to enhance training and development.
