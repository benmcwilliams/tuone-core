## GBR-08692-01636

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Equinor Facility in Dogger Bank C, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Capacity and Production**: Dogger Bank C has a production capacity of 1,200 MW and is expected to generate approximately 6,000 GWh annually. Once completed, the entire Dogger Bank project will have an annual capacity of 18 TWh, enough to power six million UK homes.
- **Construction Leadership**: SSE Renewables is leading the construction of all three phases of the Dogger Bank Wind Farm, while Equinor will operate the facility upon completion.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Total Investment**: The total investment for the Dogger Bank Wind Farm is approximately £9 billion ($12 billion), with around £3 billion ($4 billion) allocated specifically for phase C, including offshore transmission.
- **Financial Close**: Financial close for Dogger Bank C was reached on December 6, 2021.
- **Stake Sale**: On November 2, 2021, SSE and Equinor announced the sale of a combined 20% stake in Dogger Bank C to Eni for £140 million ($186 million), with each company selling a 10% stake.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Financial Close**: December 6, 2021 - Financial close on Dogger Bank C was achieved.
- **Stake Acquisition**: February 11, 2022 - Eni acquired a 20% stake in Dogger Bank C, resulting in SSE and Equinor each holding a 40% stake.
- **Completion Timeline**: The entire Dogger Bank project is expected to be completed by March 2026.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The construction of Dogger Bank C is expected to create a significant number of direct and indirect jobs, particularly linked to the planned GE blade manufacturing plant in Teesside.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Ownership**: After the stake sale to Eni, the ownership structure of Dogger Bank C consists of SSE Renewables and Equinor each holding a 40% stake, while Eni holds a 20% stake. Thus, the facility is not exclusively owned by any single company but is a joint venture among the three entities.
