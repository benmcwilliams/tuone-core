### Evolutionary Timeline of the SSE Facility in Dogger Bank C, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **06-12-2021**: SSE Renewables and Equinor reached financial close on Dogger Bank C, which has a production capacity of **1,200 MW** and is expected to generate around **6,000 GWh** of electricity annually. The entire Dogger Bank project, comprising three phases, will produce enough clean electricity to supply **5% of the UK’s demand**, equivalent to powering **six million UK homes**. SSE is leading the construction across all three phases, with Equinor set to operate the wind farm post-completion.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **06-12-2021**: The total investment for Dogger Bank Wind Farm is approximately **£9 billion ($12 billion)**, with around **£3 billion ($4 billion)** allocated specifically for phase C, including offshore transmission.
- **11-02-2022**: Eni acquired a **20% stake** in Dogger Bank C for **£140 million ($186 million)**, with SSE and Equinor each selling a **10% stake** to Eni. The proceeds from this sale will support SSE's Net Zero Acceleration Programme.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **06-12-2021**: Financial close for Dogger Bank C was announced, marking an important milestone in the project.
- **11-02-2022**: The project is expected to be completed by **March 2026**. SSE continues to lead the construction efforts.

#### 4. Labour
- **06-12-2021**: The ramp-up of construction for phase three is expected to create a significant number of direct and indirect jobs, particularly linked to the planned GE blade manufacturing plant in Teesside.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **11-02-2022**: After the sale of stakes to Eni, the ownership structure is as follows: SSE Renewables and Equinor each hold a **40% stake**, while Eni holds a **20% stake** in Dogger Bank C. This indicates a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by SSE.
