## GBR-08692-02220

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Eni Facility in Dogger Bank C, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date: 06-12-2021**
  - Dogger Bank C has a production capacity of **1,200 MW** and is expected to generate approximately **6,000 GWh** of electricity annually. 
  - The entire Dogger Bank project, which includes three phases, will produce enough clean electricity to supply **5% of the UK’s demand**, equivalent to powering **six million UK homes**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date: 06-12-2021**
  - The total investment for the Dogger Bank Wind Farm is approximately **£9 billion ($12 billion)**, with around **£3 billion ($4 billion)** allocated specifically for phase C, including offshore transmission.
  
- **Date: 11-02-2022**
  - Eni acquired a **20% stake** in Dogger Bank C for a total consideration of **£136 million ($184 million)**, with **£68 million ($92 million)** paid to both SSE Renewables and Equinor for their respective 10% stakes.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date: 06-12-2021**
  - Financial close on Dogger Bank C was reached, marking an important milestone in the project’s development.
  
- **Date: 11-02-2022**
  - The project is expected to be completed by **March 2026**. SSE continues to lead the construction, while Equinor will operate the facility upon completion.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date: 06-12-2021**
  - The construction of Dogger Bank C is expected to create a significant number of direct and indirect jobs, particularly linked to the planned GE blade manufacturing plant in Teesside.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date: 11-02-2022**
  - After the acquisition, the ownership structure of Dogger Bank C is as follows: SSE Renewables and Equinor each hold a **40% stake**, while Eni holds a **20% stake**. This indicates that Eni does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a joint venture with SSE and Equinor.
