### Evolutionary Timeline of the Thames Water Facility at Queen Elizabeth II Reservoir, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2016**: Construction began on Europe’s largest floating solar PV array at the Queen Elizabeth II reservoir. The facility will produce a total of 6.3 MWp of solar energy, covering approximately 10% of the reservoir. The energy generated will be utilized to power a nearby water treatment facility. The installation will consist of over 23,000 solar panels mounted on more than 61,000 floats and secured with 177 anchors.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2016**: The floating solar project is designed to help Thames Water achieve its goal of self-generating one-third of its power by 2020. The project qualifies for a 1.5 ROC (Renewable Obligation Certificate) rate under the UK renewable obligation scheme, which is higher than the 1.3 ROC rate for ground-mounted systems. This financial support is crucial for the project's viability and aligns with Thames Water's sustainability goals.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2016**: The project was officially announced, and construction commenced. It is scheduled for completion by the end of March 2016. This project marks a significant milestone as it is the first floating solar installation for Lightsource, the UK's largest PV developer.
