### Evolutionary Timeline of the Equinor Facility at Saltend Power Station, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2022**: Equinor and SSE Thermal acquired Triton Power, which includes the Saltend Power Station. The facility has an installed capacity of 1.2 gigawatts (GW) and operates using a combined cycle gas turbine (CCGT) that primarily uses natural gas. The facility is intended to provide electricity during periods of low output from renewable sources like solar and wind. The goal is to transition the power plant to use 30% hydrogen by 2027, with a long-term aim of achieving 100% hydrogen power thereafter. The hydrogen for this transition may be sourced from Equinor’s Hydrogen to Humber (H2H) project, which aims to reform natural gas into hydrogen with carbon capture and storage (CCS).

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2022**: The acquisition of Triton Power, including Saltend Power Station, was completed for £341 million. This investment is part of a broader strategy to transition to hydrogen and reduce carbon emissions in the Humber area, which is known for high CO2 emissions.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2022**: The acquisition of Triton Power was announced, and preparations to transition Saltend Power Station to hydrogen began. The project is subject to approval by the UK National Security Filing and EU Merger Control.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify any direct job creation or job loss associated with the acquisition or the transition to hydrogen at Saltend Power Station.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2022**: Equinor and SSE Thermal will own the Saltend Power Station in a 50:50 joint venture following the acquisition of Triton Power.
