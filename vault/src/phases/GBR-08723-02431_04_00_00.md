### Evolutionary Timeline of the SSE Thermal Facility at Saltend Power Station, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** June 2022
   - **Details:** The Saltend Power Station has an installed capacity of 1.2 gigawatts (GW) and operates using a combined cycle gas turbine (CCGT) that primarily utilizes natural gas. The facility is transitioning to incorporate hydrogen, with plans to use 30% hydrogen power by 2027 and ultimately aim for 100% hydrogen power thereafter. The hydrogen is expected to be sourced from Equinor’s Hydrogen to Humber (H2H) project, which will reform natural gas into hydrogen while capturing over 95% of its CO2 emissions.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** June 2022
   - **Details:** Equinor and SSE Thermal acquired Triton Power for £341 million, which includes the Saltend Power Station. This acquisition is part of their strategy to transition to hydrogen and enhance low carbon solutions in the UK.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** June 2022
   - **Details:** The acquisition of Triton Power, including Saltend Power Station, was announced, and the transition to hydrogen is set to begin, pending approval from the UK National Security Filing and EU Merger Control. The H2H project, which is part of this initiative, is billed as the world’s largest hydrogen production facility, with an initial phase comprising a 600-megawatt auto thermal reformer (ATR).

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** June 2022
