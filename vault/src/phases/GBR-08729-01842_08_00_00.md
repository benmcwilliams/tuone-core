### Evolutionary Timeline of the Orsted Facility in Hornsea 1, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05-07-2019**: Orsted installed the 100th turbine of the 174 Siemens Gamesa 7MW turbines at the Hornsea 1 offshore wind farm, which has a total production capacity of 1.2GW. The project is expected to be fully commissioned by 2020.
- **16-05-2022**: Hornsea 1 entered full commercial operation in December 2019, featuring 174 Siemens 7MW turbines. The facility benefits from a 15-year Contract for Difference at a price of £175.25 per megawatt-hour.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **16-05-2022**: Greencoat UK Wind announced it would acquire a 12.5% stake in Hornsea 1 from Global Infrastructure Partners for approximately £400 million. This acquisition is expected to complete in the third quarter of 2022.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05-07-2019**: Orsted reported that 100 of the 174 turbines were installed, with all units expected to be in place by late summer 2019.
- **09-08-2019**: Hornsea 1 experienced a technical fault that led to a loss of 800MW of load during a UK power outage.
- **03-01-2020**: Orsted agreed to pay £4.5 million to Ofgem for its role in the power outage incident on 9 August 2019.
- **16-05-2022**: The acquisition of Greencoat UK Wind's stake in Hornsea 1 is expected to complete in the third quarter of 2022.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **16-05-2022**: Hornsea 1 is currently 50% owned by Orsted and 50% by Global Infrastructure Partners (GIP). Greencoat UK Wind is acquiring a 12.5% stake, which will be part of GIP's ownership structure. Orsted continues to provide operation, maintenance, and management services for the wind farm.
