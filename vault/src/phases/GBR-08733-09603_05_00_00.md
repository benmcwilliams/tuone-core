## GBR-08733-09603

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Nuclear Restoration Services Facility in Sizewell A, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: Sizewell A previously operated two 210 MWe Magnox gas-cooled reactors, which were shut down on December 31, 2006, after 40 years of low carbon electricity generation.
- **Current Operations**: The facility is currently focused on decommissioning activities, specifically the demolition of the turbine hall and electrical annexe. As of August 2024, significant progress has been made in removing redundant equipment, with over 5,500 tonnes of metal recycled.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Financial Aspects**: The recycling of waste materials from the decommissioning process is expected to generate an income of between £3-4.5 million ($3.8-5.8 million). Specific investments related to the demolition project have not been detailed in the article.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Planning Consent**: On August 21, 2024, UK Nuclear Restoration Services received planning consent to demolish the turbine hall and electrical annexe at Sizewell A.
- **Demolition Timeline**: The demolition is expected to clear an area the size of a professional football pitch for future use by March 2025. Preparatory work began in July 2022, with extensive enabling works completed prior to the demolition stage.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation and Work Hours**: The decommissioning project has involved significant labor, including:
  - 25,000 work hours to clean areas of asbestos.
  - 45,000 work hours to remove scaffolding and other materials.
  - 2,498 tonnes of metal removed and recycled in 2023/4.
  - 35.6 miles of cabling removed.
  The article does not specify net job creation or loss resulting from these activities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The facility is owned by UK Nuclear Restoration Services (NRS), with no mention of joint ventures or shared ownership in the article.
