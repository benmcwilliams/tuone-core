### Evolutionary Timeline of the Essar Facility in Ellesmere, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/2024**: Essar Energy Transition (EET) is targeting to produce its first blue hydrogen at the Ellesmere plant within two years, aiming for production by 2026 at the latest. The facility's initial phase, known as HPP1, will have a production capacity of 350 MW. EET plans to expand this capacity significantly, with discussions of a second phase that could be three times the size of the first.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12/2024**: EET is in the final stages of discussions with the UK government to reach a final investment decision (FID) by September 2024. This agreement is crucial for determining the project's costs and operational expenditures, although it is currently non-binding. The company is also working on securing carbon capture and storage (CCS) technology in collaboration with Eni, which is essential for the blue hydrogen production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2024**: EET submitted the HPP1 proposal nine months prior, indicating progress towards the establishment of the facility. The next steps involve determining the strike price and conducting due diligence, leading up to the anticipated FID in September 2024.
