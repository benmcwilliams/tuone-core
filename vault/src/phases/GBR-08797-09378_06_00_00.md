### Evolutionary Timeline of the Lark Energy Facility in Wymeswold, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2013**: The Wymeswold facility, a 33MWp solar power plant, was completed and connected to the grid. It produces approximately 30,650 MWh of electricity annually, sufficient to power around 8,000 households. The installation utilizes Trina Solar modules and LTi inverters across a 78-hectare site.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2013**: The facility was sold for £43.7 million (approximately US$70 million) to Foresight Solar Fund. This sale did not include cash and accrued revenues of £1.3 million (US$2.08 million). The investment reflects the facility's status as the largest solar farm in the UK at the time.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2013**: The facility was finished and connected to the grid.
- **November 2013**: The facility was sold to Foresight Solar Fund, marking a significant milestone in its ownership and investment history.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2014**: Concerns were raised regarding the safety of workers at solar sites, including Wymeswold, due to adverse weather conditions (flooding) that affected construction timelines. Lark Energy's CEO, Jonathan Selwyn, highlighted the risks faced by workers trying to meet the Renewable Obligation Certificate (ROC) deadline amidst challenging conditions.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2013**: The facility was sold to Foresight Solar Fund, indicating that Lark Energy no longer holds ownership of the facility. Prior to the sale, Lark Energy was involved in the development and construction of the plant in conjunction with SAG Solarstrom.
