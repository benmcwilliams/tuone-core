### Evolutionary Timeline of Fortune Hydro AG Facility in Snowdonia, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** July 2023
   - **Details:** Fortune Hydro AG, in collaboration with Voith Small Hydro, acquired the 450 MW Dorothea Lakes Pump Storage project. This facility will integrate wind, solar, and hydroelectric power, allowing for the storage of electricity produced during low demand for use during peak demand. The facility aims to provide 450 MW of flexible energy storage capability, replacing fossil fuel generation and enabling more renewable electricity.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** July 2023
   - **Details:** The total strategic investment in renewable energy for the Dorothea Lakes project is £800 million (US$1 billion), with £500 million ($644.8 million) allocated specifically for project construction. It is noted that 70% of the project cost will be dedicated to civil works.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** July 2023
   - **Details:** The acquisition of the Dorothea Lakes Pump Storage project was announced, marking a significant milestone in the development of the facility. The project is positioned to contribute to the Welsh Government's goal of achieving net zero emissions by 2050.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** July 2023
   - **Details:** The project is expected to create approximately 600 jobs during the construction phase and an additional 20 jobs for the operational phase, which is projected to last 100 years.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** July 2023
   - **Details:** Fortune Hydro UK will own and manage the Dorothea Lakes Pump Storage project. The technology will be provided by Voith, and once operational, the plant will be run by Green Highland, a Scottish operator, under a service level agreement.
