## GBR-08808-09054

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Green Highland Facility in Snowdonia, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** July 2023
   - **Details:** The facility, known as the Dorothea Lakes Pump Storage project, will have a production capacity of 450 MW. It is designed to integrate wind, solar, and hydroelectric power, allowing for the storage of electricity generated during low demand periods for use during peak demand. The facility aims to replace fossil fuel generation and enable more renewable electricity.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** July 2023
   - **Details:** The total investment for the project is £800 million (US$1 billion), with £500 million ($644.8 million) allocated specifically for construction. It is noted that 70% of the project cost will be dedicated to civil works, which will provide significant local employment opportunities.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** July 2023
   - **Details:** The acquisition of the Dorothea Lakes Pump Storage project was announced, marking the beginning of the project. The facility is expected to generate substantial economic development and new business opportunities in the local community.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** July 2023
   - **Details:** The project is expected to create 600 jobs during the construction phase and an additional 20 jobs for the operational phase, which is projected to last 100 years.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** July 2023
   - **Details:** Fortune Hydro UK will own and manage the pump storage project. The technology will be provided by Voith, and Green Highland, a Scottish operator, will run the plant under a service level agreement once it is built and operational.
