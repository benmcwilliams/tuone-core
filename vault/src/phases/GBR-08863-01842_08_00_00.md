### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ørsted Facility in Hornsea One, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Operational Status**: Hornsea One is the world’s largest offshore wind farm, developed by Ørsted, and became fully operational in January 2020. 
- **Production Capacity**: The facility has a total capacity of 1.2 GW, featuring 174 Siemens Gamesa 7 MW wind turbines, generating enough energy to power over one million homes in the UK.
- **Subsidy**: Hornsea One has an inflation-linked Contract-for-Difference (CfD) subsidy with 13 years remaining.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2022**: The Renewables Infrastructure Group (TRIG) and Equitix announced their acquisition of a 7.8% stake in Hornsea One from Global Infrastructure Partners (GIP), with the transaction expected to finalize by mid-2022.
- **May 2022**: Greencoat UK Wind agreed to acquire a 12.5% stake in Hornsea One from GIP for approximately GBP 400 million, expected to complete in Q3 2022.
- **August 2022**: Greencoat UK Wind completed the acquisition of the 12.5% stake from GIP, confirming the investment and its impact on their portfolio.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2020**: Hornsea One became fully operational.
- **March 2022**: TRIG and Equitix announced their stake acquisition.
- **May 2022**: Greencoat UK Wind announced its intention to acquire a stake.
- **August 2022**: Greencoat UK Wind completed its acquisition of the 12.5% stake.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- As of August 2022, Hornsea One is jointly owned by Ørsted (50%) and Global Infrastructure Partners (50%). Following the acquisitions by TRIG, Greencoat UK Wind, and Octopus Energy Generation, the ownership structure has become more diversified, with these entities holding minority stakes in the facility.
