## GBR-08910-02439

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Siemens Gamesa Facility in Nigg, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2023**: Siemens Gamesa is set to supply 60 SG 14-222 DD wind turbines for the Moray West offshore wind farm, which has a total capacity of 882 MW. The wind turbines will be installed on monopile foundations, with specific dimensions of approximately 90 meters in length, a maximum diameter of 10 meters, and a maximum weight of 2,000 tonnes. The offshore installation works are scheduled to begin later in 2023, with the wind farm expected to be fully operational by 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Ongoing**: The Port of Nigg has benefitted from over GBP 120 million in investments from Global Energy Group (GEG). This investment supports the development of necessary infrastructure and facilities to accommodate increasing demand for offshore wind projects.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2022**: A contract was signed for the manufacturing and delivery of transition pieces (TPs) for the Moray West project by Lamprell, which is part of the wind farm's development.
- **September 2023**: Moray West signed an agreement with GEG to use the Port of Nigg for marshalling all 62 transition pieces prior to installation. The TPs are expected to start arriving at the port later in 2023, with installation commencing shortly thereafter and completion anticipated in 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **Ongoing**: The facility's involvement in the Moray West project and other offshore wind projects is expected to create job opportunities, although specific job creation numbers have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: The Port of Nigg is owned and operated by Global Energy Group (GEG). There is no indication that Siemens Gamesa has any ownership stake in the facility; rather, they are a supplier for the wind turbines used in projects supported by the port. 

This timeline captures the key developments and operational aspects of the Siemens Gamesa facility in Nigg, GBR, as related to the Moray West offshore wind project and the broader context of offshore wind energy development in the region.
