### Evolutionary Timeline of the Haizea Facility in Nigg, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **December 2021:** Haizea Wind Group, in collaboration with Global Energy Group (GEG), signed an agreement to establish the Nigg Offshore Wind (NOW) factory at the Scottish Port of Nigg. This facility is set to be the largest offshore wind tower factory in the UK, with a production capacity of up to 135 towers per year, along with other structures such as transition pieces, suction buckets, and bespoke tubular structures for the floating offshore wind market.
   - **Expected Production Start:** Commercial production is anticipated to commence in 2023.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **February 2022:** The NOW factory is backed by a financial investment of GBP 15 million (approximately EUR 18 million) in debt from SSE Renewables, making it the largest single UK-based strategic backer of the project. The total investment for the facility is GBP 110 million.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **January 2022:** Construction works for the NOW factory were scheduled to begin.
   - **February 2022:** Site preparation, construction, and commissioning of the plant are expected to take around 18 months.

4. **Labour:**
   - **February 2022:** The construction and operation of the NOW factory are projected to support 1,248 full-time equivalent (FTE) job years across the supply chain during the building phase. Once operational, the facility will create over 400 direct FTE jobs in manufacturing, providing reskilling opportunities for regional oil and gas employees.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **February 2022:** The facility is a joint venture between Haizea Wind Group and Global Energy Group (GEG), with SSE Renewables as a significant financial backer.
