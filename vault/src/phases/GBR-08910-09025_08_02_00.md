## GBR-08910-09025

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Global Energy Group Facility in Nigg, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2021**: Global Energy Group (GEG) and Haizea Wind Group signed an agreement to build the Nigg Offshore Wind (NOW) factory at the Port of Nigg, which is set to be the largest offshore wind tower factory in the UK. The facility will produce up to 135 towers per year, along with other structures for the offshore wind industry, including transition pieces and suction buckets. Commercial production is expected to start in 2023.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2021**: The NOW factory received financial backing of GBP 15 million from SSE Renewables, marking it as the largest single UK-based strategic backer for the project.
- **June 2022**: The Opportunity Cromarty Firth (OCF) consortium submitted a bid for Green Freeport status, which could attract over £2.5 billion in new private sector investment in the UK’s green energy sector.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2022**: Construction works for the NOW factory were scheduled to begin.
- **February 2022**: The construction team for the NOW factory was gathered, with site preparation and construction expected to take around 18 months.
- **Mid-2023**: The facility is expected to start commercial production.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2022**: The construction and commissioning of the NOW factory is projected to support 1,248 full-time equivalent (FTE) job years across the supply chain. Once operational, the factory will provide over 400 direct FTE jobs in manufacturing, offering reskilling opportunities for regional oil and gas employees.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: Global Energy Group owns the facility, with no indication of joint ventures or shared ownership mentioned in the articles.
