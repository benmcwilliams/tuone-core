### Evolutionary Timeline of the Haizea Facility in Nigg Energy Park, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: The facility is designed to produce up to 135 offshore wind turbine towers annually, along with other structures such as transition pieces, suction buckets, and bespoke tubular structures for the floating offshore wind market.
- **Facility Specifications**: The Nigg Offshore Wind (NOW) factory will span 450 meters and cover 38,000 square meters, capable of rolling steel plates to manufacture towers weighing over 1000 tonnes each.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Total Investment**: The facility represents a £110 million investment, with additional costs for rolling machinery robotics and a new blast and paint shop, bringing the total to between £110 million and £120 million.
- **Funding Sources**: The project will receive £15 million in debt funding from SSE Renewables, with additional senior debt from Sequoia Economic Infrastructure Income Fund and £5 million from Mainstream Renewable Power. The UK government will provide grant funding through the Offshore Wind Manufacturing Investment Support programme, subject to due diligence.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Announcement Date**: The project was publicly announced on March 12, 2021.
- **Construction Timeline**: Construction is set to begin in January 2022, contingent upon reaching financial close by the end of 2021, and is expected to take 18 months to complete.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The facility is projected to create over 400 direct long-term, high-value jobs in the local area, contributing to the local economy and supporting the transition to a greener future.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture**: The facility is a joint venture between Global Energy Group (GEG) and Haizea Wind Group (HWG), indicating that it is not solely owned by either company but rather a collaborative effort.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones related to the Haizea facility at Nigg Energy Park, highlighting its significance in the offshore wind industry and its impact on local employment and investment.
