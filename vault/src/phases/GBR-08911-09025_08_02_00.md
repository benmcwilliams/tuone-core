## GBR-08911-09025

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Global Energy Group Facility in Nigg Energy Park, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **03-12-2021**: Global Energy Group (GEG) and Haizea Wind Group announced plans to build a new turbine tower factory at Nigg Energy Park. The facility, named Nigg Offshore Wind (NOW), will be a 450-meter-long, 38,000-square-meter factory capable of rolling steel plates to produce towers weighing over 1000 tonnes each, along with other structures for the offshore wind industry. The factory is designed to produce up to 135 towers annually, as well as transition pieces, suction buckets, and bespoke tubular structures for both fixed and floating offshore wind markets.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **03-12-2021**: The facility will receive grant funding through the UK government’s £160 million Offshore Wind Manufacturing Investment Support programme, pending due diligence. The total cost of the facility is estimated between £110 million and £120 million. The project will also receive £15 million in debt funding from SSE Renewables, with additional senior debt from Sequoia Economic Infrastructure Income Fund and £5 million from Mainstream Renewable Power.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **03-12-2021**: The construction of the NOW factory is set to begin in January 2022, contingent upon reaching financial close by the end of 2021. The construction is expected to take 18 months to complete.

#### 4. Labour
- **03-12-2021**: The facility is projected to create more than 400 direct long-term, high-value jobs in the local area, contributing to the local economy and supporting the transition to a greener future.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **03-12-2021**: The facility will be developed as a joint venture between Global Energy Group and Haizea Wind Group, indicating that GEG does not own the facility exclusively but shares ownership with Haizea. 

This timeline captures the key developments related to the Global Energy Group facility in Nigg Energy Park as of December 2021, highlighting its technological capabilities, financial backing, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure.
