## GBR-08937-00800

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Uniper Facility at Flotta Terminal, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/2021**: The Offshore Wind Power Limited (OWPL) consortium, which includes Uniper, is studying the development of an industrial-scale green hydrogen project at the Flotta Terminal. The facility aims to produce green hydrogen powered by the proposed West of Orkney Windfarm, which has a planned installed capacity of 2 gigawatts (GW).

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12/2021**: The OWPL consortium is in the pre-Front End Engineering Design (FEED) stage for the Flotta Hydrogen Hub. The project is expected to require significant funding, with estimates suggesting it could form a "multi-billion pound" development. The consortium is awaiting funding and approval, with the first hydrogen production and export anticipated in 2028/2029.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2021**: The OWPL consortium submitted a proposal to Crown Estate Scotland to develop the N1 plan option area west of Orkney as part of the ScotWind leasing process. The results of this process are expected to be released in early 2022.

#### 4. Labour
- **12/2021**: The development of the Flotta Hydrogen Hub is projected to create "hundreds of skilled jobs" during both the construction and operational phases. The facility's transition to green hydrogen production is also expected to involve upskilling the current workforce.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **12/2021**: The Flotta Terminal is owned by Repsol Sinopec, and the development of the hydrogen hub is a collaborative effort involving Uniper and other partners in the OWPL consortium. The facility is not exclusively owned by Uniper but is part of a joint venture with Repsol Sinopec and other stakeholders. 

This timeline captures the key developments and plans for the Uniper facility at Flotta Terminal, highlighting its transition towards green hydrogen production and the collaborative efforts involved in this initiative.
