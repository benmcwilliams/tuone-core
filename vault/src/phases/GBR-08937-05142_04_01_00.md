## GBR-08937-05142

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Green Investment Group Facility at Flotta Terminal, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Focus**: The facility is set to produce green hydrogen using offshore wind power.
- **Production Capacity**: The proposed West of Orkney Windfarm, which will supply power to the Flotta Hydrogen Hub, has a planned installed capacity of 2 gigawatts (GW), capable of powering more than two million homes.
- **Future Plans**: The first hydrogen production and export is expected to commence in 2028/2029.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Potential**: The offshore wind and hydrogen schemes have the potential to form a “multi-billion pound” development, although specific investment figures are not yet available.
- **Funding and Approval**: The consortium is currently at the pre-Front End Engineering Design (FEED) stage, with hopes to secure funding and project approval in the following year (2022).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Announcement Date**: The plans for the green hydrogen facility were announced on **12-10-2021**.
- **Current Status**: The project is in the pre-FEED stage, with a memorandum of understanding signed with local authorities to progress the project.
- **Future Milestones**: The results of the ScotWind leasing process are expected to be released in early 2022, which will be crucial for the project's advancement.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The project is anticipated to create “hundreds of skilled jobs” during both the construction and operational phases of the Flotta Hydrogen Hub.
- **Job Loss Considerations**: Repsol Sinopec, the facility's owner, mentioned potential “organisational changes” at the Flotta oil terminal, indicating possible staff reductions, although they aim to minimize job losses and explore redeployment opportunities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The Flotta Terminal is owned by Repsol Sinopec, and the green hydrogen project is being developed in partnership with a consortium that includes TotalEnergies, Macquarie’s Green Investment Group, and the Renewable Infrastructure Development Group (RIDG). The facility is not exclusively owned by the Green Investment Group but is part of a collaborative effort with multiple stakeholders.
