## GBR-08937-07118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Repsol Sinopec Facility at Flotta Terminal, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/2021**: The Offshore Wind Power Limited (OWPL) consortium, which includes Repsol Sinopec, announced plans for an industrial-scale green hydrogen project at the Flotta Terminal. The facility aims to produce green hydrogen powered by the proposed West of Orkney Windfarm, which has a planned installed capacity of 2 gigawatts (GW).

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12/2021**: The OWPL consortium is in the pre-Front End Engineering Design (FEED) stage for the Flotta Hydrogen Hub. The project is expected to require significant funding, with estimates suggesting it could form a "multi-billion pound" development. The consortium is working on securing funding and approvals in the following year.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2021**: The OWPL consortium submitted a proposal to Crown Estate Scotland to develop the N1 plan option area west of Orkney as part of the ScotWind leasing process. The results of this process are expected to be released in early 2022. The first hydrogen production and export are anticipated to begin in 2028/2029.

#### 4. Labour
- **12/2021**: The development of the Flotta Hydrogen Hub is projected to create "hundreds of skilled jobs" during both the construction and operational phases. Repsol Sinopec also indicated that it would explore redeployment opportunities for existing staff at the Flotta oil terminal, which has been operational since 1976.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **12/2021**: Repsol Sinopec is identified as the owner of the Flotta Terminal, which is involved in the partnership with OWPL and Uniper for the development of the green hydrogen facility. The facility is part of a strategic roadmap for Repsol Sinopec to transition towards clean energy while maintaining its conventional oil and gas operations. 

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic plans for the Repsol Sinopec facility at Flotta Terminal, highlighting its transition towards green hydrogen production and the associated economic impacts.
