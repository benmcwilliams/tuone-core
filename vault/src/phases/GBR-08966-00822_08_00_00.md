### Evolutionary Timeline of the Vattenfall Facility in South Kyle, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2020**: The South Kyle facility is proposed as a joint venture between Vattenfall and Greencoat, featuring 50 turbines with a production capacity of 240MW, enough to power approximately 170,000 homes. The facility is expected to generate £38 million for the local economy.
- **June 2023**: Vattenfall officially inaugurates the South Kyle onshore wind farm, confirming its capacity to generate enough electricity to power 187,000 homes. The facility is now part of Vattenfall’s 5.5GW onshore wind portfolio, with 500MW capacity in the UK.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2020**: The total investment for the South Kyle wind farm is reported to be £320 million. The project is expected to contribute significantly to the local economy.
- **June 2023**: Vattenfall announces a power purchase agreement for 15 years following the commissioning of the facility, indicating ongoing financial commitments to the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2020**: Construction is delayed due to Covid-19, with an initial operation start planned for Q1 2023. The timeline for construction remains uncertain.
- **October 2020**: Preparatory works begin on site, with expectations for the wind farm to start generating electricity by early 2023.
- **June 2023**: The South Kyle wind farm is inaugurated, marking the completion of the project and its readiness to generate electricity.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2020**: At the peak of construction, the project is expected to require at least 150 workers on site, indicating significant job creation during the construction phase.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2023**: The facility is owned by Greencoat UK Wind, with Vattenfall managing the asset on behalf of Greencoat for a minimum of ten years. This indicates a joint ownership structure where Vattenfall does not exclusively own the facility.
