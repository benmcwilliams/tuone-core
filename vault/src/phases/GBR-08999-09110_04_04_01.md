## GBR-08999-09110

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hanson Facility in Criggion, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2024**: The Hanson facility in Criggion is set to implement a full-scale net zero asphalt project that will utilize nuclear-derived hydrogen for asphalt production. This project aims to demonstrate zero-emission asphalt production, replacing the current fossil fuel mix. The facility will incorporate a newly-built solid oxide electrolysis cell (SOEC) electrolyser to generate hydrogen, which is expected to increase hydrogen production efficiency by over 20% compared to traditional methods.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2024**: The project receives a commitment of £6.1 million (US$7.4 million) from the UK’s Department for Energy Security and Net Zero. A consortium including EDF, National Nuclear Laboratory (NNL), and Vulcan Burners will match this funding, bringing the total investment to £15 million (US$18.2 million). This funding follows a previous award of nearly £400,000 (US$486,000) for a feasibility study related to the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2024**: The project is announced, with plans to develop a final design for hydrogen production, distribution, and end-use technology. The goal is to switch to net zero hydrogen asphalt production at the Criggion facility. The project is positioned as a groundbreaking development, being the first of its kind to demonstrate hydrogen as a fuel at an asphalt plant.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2024**: The project is expected to contribute to job safeguarding in the region, as indicated by comments from EDF UK Research & Development, highlighting the potential for nuclear power to support the decarbonization of the asphalt industry.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2024**: The facility is owned by Hanson, which is a business unit of Heidelberg Materials. There is no indication of joint ownership or partnerships affecting the ownership structure of the Criggion facility.
