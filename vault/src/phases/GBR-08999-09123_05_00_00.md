## GBR-08999-09123

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Heidelberg Materials Facility in Criggion, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2024**: The Criggion asphalt plant, operated by Hanson (a business unit of Heidelberg Materials), is set to implement a full-scale net zero asphalt project. This project aims to demonstrate zero-emission asphalt production using hydrogen derived from nuclear energy. The facility will utilize a newly-built solid oxide electrolysis cell (SOEC) electrolyser to generate hydrogen, replacing the current fossil fuel mix used in asphalt production.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2024**: The project receives a commitment of £6.1 million (US$7.4 million) from the UK’s Department for Energy Security and Net Zero. A consortium, including EDF, National Nuclear Laboratory (NNL), and Vulcan Burners, will match this funding, bringing the total investment to £15 million (US$18.2 million). This funding is part of the Bay Hydrogen Hub initiative, which aims to develop hydrogen production and distribution technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2024**: The project is in the initial stages, focusing on developing the final design for hydrogen production, distribution, and end-use technology. The goal is to switch to net zero hydrogen asphalt production at the Criggion facility. The project is groundbreaking as it will be the first demonstration of hydrogen as a fuel at an asphalt plant globally. The feasibility study for this project was previously awarded nearly £400,000 (US$486,000) in funding.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2024**: The project is expected to contribute to job safeguarding in the region, as it aims to decarbonize the asphalt industry, which is carbon-heavy. The development of this technology is seen as a way to support jobs while transitioning to a more sustainable production method.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2024**: The Criggion facility is operated by Hanson, which is a business unit of Heidelberg Materials. There is no indication of joint ownership; thus, it can be inferred that Heidelberg Materials has full ownership of the facility.
