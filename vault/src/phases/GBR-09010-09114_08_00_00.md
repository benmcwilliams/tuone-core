## GBR-09010-09114

### Evolutionary Timeline of Harland and Wolff Facility in Belfast, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2019**: Harland and Wolff is involved in assembling jacket foundations for the 714MW East Anglia 1 offshore wind farm. The facility is responsible for assembling 18 jackets, while the remaining 42 jackets and 182 piles are fabricated in the UAE.
- **August 2019**: The facility completes the assembly of the 18 jackets for the East Anglia 1 wind farm.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2019**: InfraStrata acquires Harland and Wolff for £5.25 million. This acquisition is part of a strategic move to bring in-house engineering and fabrication requirements for the Islandmagee Gas Storage Project, enhancing UK energy security.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2019**: Harland and Wolff is actively involved in the East Anglia 1 project, with the project on track for installation in 2019.
- **June 2019**: Dolphin Drilling, the parent company of Harland and Wolff, completes a financial restructuring, and the Belfast yard is put up for sale.
- **August 2019**: Administrators are appointed to Harland and Wolff due to insolvency, and the facility enters administration.
- **December 2019**: InfraStrata completes the acquisition of Harland and Wolff, securing the future of the facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2019**: The appointment of administrators raises concerns about the futures of 123 staff members at Harland and Wolff, with some employees protesting due to uncertainty regarding job security.
- **August 2019**: A period of unpaid temporary lay-off for some of the facility’s staff is extended as efforts to secure the site’s future continue.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2019**: InfraStrata acquires Harland and Wolff, indicating that the facility is now fully owned by InfraStrata after being under administration. The acquisition is executed through a newly incorporated special purpose vehicle, which is 100% owned by InfraStrata.
