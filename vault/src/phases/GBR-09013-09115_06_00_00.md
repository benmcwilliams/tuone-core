### Evolutionary Timeline of Harworth Group Facility in Kellingley, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **December 2016:** Harworth Group announced the construction of a 5 MW solar PV plant at the site of the former Kellingley Colliery. The solar farm is expected to deliver enough clean energy to power 20,000 local households. This facility marks Harworth's 10th solar installation in the U.K. 

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **December 2016:** Harworth Group secured a 31-year lease for the solar project. The planning consent for the Kellingley Solar Farm Limited was obtained in July 2015, prior to the coal mine's closure.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **December 2015:** The Kellingley Colliery, the last deep coal mine in the U.K., closed permanently on December 18, 2015.
   - **March 2017:** The solar farm is expected to be brought online, following the commencement of construction shortly after the announcement in December 2016.

4. **Labour:**
   - The Kellingley Colliery previously employed over 2,000 staff at its peak. The transition to the solar farm represents a shift in employment dynamics, although specific job creation or loss related to the solar facility was not detailed in the article.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
