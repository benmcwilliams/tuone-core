## GBR-09016-09120

### Hecate Independent Power Facility Timeline

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** May 2021
   - **Details:** Hecate Independent Power (HIP) announced plans to build up to 10GW of fixed and floating offshore wind capacity in the North Atlantic, with an initial 2GW targeted for commissioning off the southern and eastern coasts of Iceland by early 2025. Each wind farm, referred to as a "pod," will consist of 1GW of turbines connected to the UK via dedicated subsea cables. The facility aims to provide renewable electricity during periods when existing British offshore wind projects are not generating power.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** May 2021
   - **Details:** The total project cost is estimated at £21 billion (€24 billion). For the initial 2GW pilot phase, an investment of £2.9 million is expected in 2021, increasing to an additional £144 million through 2025. A new £200 million factory for cable production is planned for an unnamed port in the north-east of England.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** May 2021
   - **Details:** HIP lodged four connection applications with National Grid for an initial 4GW of grid connections to the UK’s 400kV electricity transmission system. The project is positioned to be the first captive wind farm in overseas territorial waters for the UK.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** May 2021
   - **Details:** The initial 2GW generation capacity is expected to create 15,000 new jobs in the UK. Additionally, up to 500 new jobs are anticipated in southern and eastern Iceland associated with the pilot phase.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** May 2021
   - **Details:** Hecate Independent Power is a joint venture between Hecate Wind (a wholly-owned subsidiary of Hecate Holdings, a US company) and Independent Power Corporation, a UK developer of conventional power plants.
