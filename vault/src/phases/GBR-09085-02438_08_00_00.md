## GBR-09085-02438

### Evolutionary Timeline of the ScottishPower Facility in East Anglia Three, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** March 2023
   - **Details:** ScottishPower has awarded a £1.3 billion contract to Siemens Gamesa for the supply of 95 wind turbines, each with a capacity of 14.7 MW, for the East Anglia Three (EA3) offshore wind farm. The facility will have a total production capacity of 1.4 GW and is expected to generate enough clean energy to power 1.3 million UK homes annually. Once completed, EA3 will become the second largest wind farm in the world.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** March 2023
   - **Details:** ScottishPower expects to invest more than £2 billion ($2.46 billion) to support the construction and operation of the East Anglia project. The contract awarded to Siemens Gamesa is part of this investment. Additionally, the UK government has introduced measures allowing businesses to write off the full cost of equipment and machinery in the first year of investment, which supports this project.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** July 2022
   - **Details:** Onshore construction of the East Anglia Three project began in July 2022. The project is part of the East Anglia zone and is expected to support the UK’s commitment to net-zero and energy security.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** March 2023
   - **Details:** During the two-year construction phase, the project is expected to support more than 2,300 jobs. Additionally, over 100 jobs are anticipated to be created for the operational life of the wind facility. ScottishPower has already awarded contracts worth almost £70 million ($86.24 million) to UK-based companies, contributing to local job creation.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** March 2023
   - **Details:** The East Anglia Three offshore wind farm is owned exclusively by ScottishPower, a subsidiary of Iberdrola.
