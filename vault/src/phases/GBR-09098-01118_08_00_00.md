### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Whitelee Park, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2008**: Whitelee Onshore Wind Farm begins producing clean, renewable energy. It has 215 wind turbines with a total installed capacity of 539 megawatts, making it the largest onshore wind farm in the UK.
- **2012**: The facility reaches its current size and installed power after undergoing two expansion phases, which included 140 Siemens turbines and 75 Alstom turbines.
- **2023**: Iberdrola announces plans to install a green hydrogen plant at Whitelee Park, capable of generating 3,000 tonnes of hydrogen per year from onshore wind energy.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2023**: The British Government allocates over €11 million (£9.4 million) to the construction of the green hydrogen plant at Whitelee Park from the energy innovation portfolio of the Department for Business, Energy and Industrial Strategy (BEIS).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2000**: Development of the Whitelee wind farm begins.
- **2002**: Permission for construction is requested.
- **2006**: Approval for construction is obtained.
- **January 2008**: The first phase of the wind farm begins supplying power to the electricity grid.
- **May 2009**: Whitelee wind farm is officially opened to the public.
- **2015**: A new operations center is inaugurated at the Whitelee wind farm.
