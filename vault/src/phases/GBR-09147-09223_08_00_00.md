### Evolutionary Timeline of the Inland and Coastal Facility at Eyemouth Harbour, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility is designed to serve as the Operations and Maintenance (O&M) base for the Neart na Gaoithe (NnG) Offshore Wind project. 
   - It includes a three-storey main O&M building with a total area of 1,040 square metres and a two-storey storage warehouse of 538 square metres.
   - Inland and Coastal is responsible for designing, manufacturing, and installing a new pontoon to facilitate access for crew transfer vessels and technicians.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The project is part of the NnG Offshore Wind initiative, which has secured a lease with Eyemouth Harbour Trust for a 25-year operational period.
   - Specific financial investments related to the construction of the O&M base and pontoon have not been detailed in the article.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **June 2021:** Muir Construction and Inland and Coastal were contracted to design and construct the O&M base at Eyemouth Harbour.
   - Construction works were expected to start in June 2021, with enabling works already underway by Muir Construction.
   - Fabrication of the pontoon was set to begin in June 2021, with installation planned for later in the year.

4. **Labour:**
   - The O&M base is expected to create up to 50 permanent jobs.
   - Additional socio-economic benefits for the local community include training, retraining, and apprenticeship opportunities.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The NnG project is jointly owned by EDF Renewables UK and ESB, with the facility being part of this joint venture. The article does not specify if Inland and Coastal has any ownership stake in the facility itself.
