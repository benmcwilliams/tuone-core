### Evolutionary Timeline of the Jan De Nul Facility in Able Seaton, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **June 2023:** The facility is associated with the world's largest offshore wind jack-up vessel, Jan De Nul's Voltaire, which has a lifting capacity of 3,200 tonnes. The vessel is designed as an Ultra-Low Emission vessel (ULEv) and is equipped with advanced exhaust filtering systems. It is responsible for the installation of 277 GE Haliade-X offshore wind turbines for the Dogger Bank Wind Farm, which has a total capacity of 3.6GW, making it the largest offshore production facility in the world.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **2020:** Jan De Nul Group signed the final contract for the transport and installation of the GE Haliade-X offshore wind turbines for Dogger Bank A and B.
   - **2021:** A subsequent contract was signed for the transport and installation of 87 GE Haliade-X 14 MW offshore wind turbines for Dogger Bank C.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **June 2023:** The Voltaire vessel docked in Able Seaton, UK, ahead of its first assignment for the Dogger Bank Wind Farm project. The vessel is set to sail out in early July 2023 to install the first turbine.
