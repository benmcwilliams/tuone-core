### Evolutionary Timeline of the Kistos Facility at Hill Top Farm, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** February 2024
   - **Details:** Kistos has acquired the Hill Top Farm gas storage site, which currently has a gas storage capacity of 17.8 million therms. There is an ongoing program to increase this capacity to 21.2 million therms. Hill Top Farm accounts for 3.1% of the UK's total available onshore gas storage capacity and up to 11% of the UK's flexible daily gas capacity. The facility is recognized as strategically important for energy transition and UK energy security.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** February 2024
   - **Details:** Kistos agreed to purchase the Hill Top Farm site from EDF Energy for £25 million. This acquisition aligns with Kistos's strategy to pursue opportunities that support the energy transition and diversify its upstream portfolio.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** February 2024
   - **Details:** Kistos announced the acquisition of the Hill Top Farm and Hole House Farm gas storage sites from EDF Energy. The acquisition is part of Kistos's growth strategy and marks its entry into the gas storage market.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** February 2024
   - **Details:** Kistos expressed intentions to benefit from the existing team's expertise at the Hill Top Farm site, indicating potential job retention and creation as they seek to maximize the potential of the assets and explore further development options.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** February 2024
   - **Details:** Kistos will assume full ownership and operatorship of the Hill Top Farm facility following the acquisition from EDF Energy. This acquisition allows Kistos to diversify its presence across the energy value chain, including a foothold in the midstream market.
