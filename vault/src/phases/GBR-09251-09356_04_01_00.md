### Evolutionary Timeline of the Kraft Heinz Facility in Kitt Green, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Production**: The Kitt Green facility is one of the largest food processing plants in Europe, producing a quarter of a million tons of food annually and processing over 1.3 billion cans of food each year, including staple products like baked beans.
- **Green Hydrogen Initiative**: In February 2024, Kraft Heinz announced a partnership with Carlton Power to explore the creation of a green hydrogen facility at the Kitt Green plant. The proposed facility aims to have a capacity of up to 20MW, which could replace up to 50% of the plant’s gas demand. The project is expected to reduce carbon emissions by 16,000 tonnes per year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Amount**: The green hydrogen project is estimated to cost £40 million. This initiative marks Kraft Heinz's first effort in clean hydrogen production across its global operations.
- **Funding Sources**: The project is contingent on securing financial support from the UK Government’s Hydrogen Allocation Round (HAR2) under the Hydrogen Production Business Model (HPBM).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Announcement Date**: The partnership and project were announced on February 5, 2024.
- **Projected Operation Start**: The hydrogen facility is slated to enter operation in 2026, subject to planning approval and financing over the next 12-18 months.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The project is expected to contribute to the creation of green jobs in the local area, as highlighted by local politicians who emphasized the importance of transitioning to net zero for protecting local employment.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: Kraft Heinz is the sole owner of the Kitt Green facility, as there is no mention of joint ventures or shared ownership in the context of the facility's operations or the hydrogen project.
