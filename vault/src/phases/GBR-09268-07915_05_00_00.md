### Evolutionary Timeline of Last Energy Facility in Llynfi, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2024**: Last Energy is developing the Llynfi Clean Energy Project, which will utilize its PWR-20 reactor technology. This technology is based on a pressurized water reactor with a capacity of 20 MWe or 80 MWt. The facility will consist of several modules that can be assembled on-site, designed for rapid deployment within 24 months. The project aims to serve private industrial customers, including data centers.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2024**: Last Energy announced a capital investment of GBP 300 million (approximately USD 393 million) for the entire project. The financing will be supported by a Letter of Interest from the Export-Import Bank of the United States (EXIM), which will cover the costs for a single power plant installation. The project will not require public funding.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2024**: Last Energy is actively engaging with regulatory bodies and local officials to advance the project. The company is targeting 2027 for the commissioning of the first plant, contingent on a successful planning and licensing process. The project is set to be built on the site of the former Llynfi coal-fired power station, which has been vacant since its decommissioning in 1977.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2024**: The project is expected to create more than 100 full-time local jobs as a result of the investment and development of the facility. Additionally, contracts with local suppliers are projected to exceed GBP 30 million.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2024**: Last Energy is a US-based company that will own and operate the facility exclusively. The company is a spin-off of the Energy Impact Center, focusing on accelerating the clean energy transition through innovative nuclear technology.
