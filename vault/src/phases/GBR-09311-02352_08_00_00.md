### Evolutionary Timeline of the MHI Vestas Facility in Inch Cape, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **December 2024:** MHI Vestas has secured a firm order for the supply, installation, and commissioning of 72 V236-15.0 MW wind turbines for the Inch Cape offshore wind project. The project will generate over 5 terawatt hours of renewable electricity annually, contributing significantly to the UK's energy security and carbon emissions reduction. The site covers 150km² in the North Sea, 15km offshore Angus, with turbines reaching 274m in height. The project includes a 66kV/220kV substation on a jacket foundation and will utilize two 220kV subsea export cables to transmit electricity 85km onshore to Cockenzie, East Lothian.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **December 2024:** The project is part of a broader commitment to quadruple offshore wind capacity in the UK by 2030, aligning with government goals for full decarbonization of electricity by the same year. Specific investment amounts are not detailed in the article.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **December 2024:** The project has reached a significant milestone with the full notice to proceed on the agreement. Construction of the topside and jacket foundation for the offshore substation is underway, with key fabrication occurring at the Smulders yard in Wallsend, UK. Preparatory surveys for the cable route have begun, and cable installation is planned to start in 2025.
