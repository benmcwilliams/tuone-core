### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ocean Winds Facility in Shetland Islands, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2022**: Ocean Winds, in partnership with Mainstream Renewable Power, was awarded a site for a floating offshore wind farm with a capacity of 1.8GW, named the Arven Offshore Wind Farm. This facility is expected to power over two million homes and offset three million tonnes of carbon emissions annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2022**: A seabed lease agreement was signed with Crown Estate Scotland for the Arven Offshore Wind Farm, valued at £36 million ($40.4 million). This fee is part of the investment required to develop the facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2022**: Announcement of the selection of Mainstream Renewable Power and Ocean Winds as the preferred bidders for the offshore wind farm site.
- **November 2022**: Signing of the seabed lease agreement, marking a significant step towards the development of the Arven Offshore Wind Farm.

#### 4. Labour
- No specific job creation or job loss details were mentioned in the articles regarding the facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Ocean Winds and Mainstream Renewable Power will hold equal ownership (50-50 joint venture) in the Arven Offshore Wind Farm project. Ocean Winds itself is a joint venture established in 2020 between EDP Renewables and Engie.
