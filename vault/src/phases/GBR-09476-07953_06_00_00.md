### Evolutionary Timeline of NextEnergy Solar Fund Facility in Staughton, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2019**: The Staughton facility, a 50MW subsidy-free solar plant, was energized. It is located on a former airfield on the Bedfordshire/Cambridgeshire border. The electricity generated is bundled with NextEnergy Solar Fund’s existing portfolio and sold under existing sales contracts. This facility is noted as the UK's largest subsidy-free solar site at the time of its connection to the grid.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2020**: NextEnergy Capital secured a £100 million non-recourse debt financing from Santander to support investment in two subsidy-free projects, which includes the Staughton facility. The total constructed value of the projects is over £60 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2019**: The Staughton solar plant was officially energized.
- **May 2020**: NextEnergy Solar Fund announced plans to start construction on an 8.5MW extension project, indicating ongoing development efforts following the energization of Staughton.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the Staughton facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- NextEnergy Solar Fund (NESF) owns the Staughton facility exclusively, as it is part of their portfolio which includes approximately 754MW across 90 assets in the UK and Italy.
