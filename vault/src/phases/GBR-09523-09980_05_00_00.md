### Evolutionary Timeline of Sellafield Ltd Facility in Sellafield, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2023**: Sellafield Ltd unveiled its AI strategy aimed at improving efficiency and safety across its operations. The strategy includes the use of AI for automation and decision-making, with a goal for full implementation by 2032.
- **August 2023**: The deployment of the Elios 3 drone equipped with LiDAR technology marked a significant milestone in mapping and 3D modeling, enhancing operational efficiency and safety by reducing the need for human intervention in hazardous areas.
- **August 2023**: Waste retrieval operations began at the Pile Fuel Cladding Silo (PFCS), which holds over 3200 cubic meters of intermediate-level waste. This marked the first time waste was retrieved from all four legacy ponds and silos at Sellafield.
- **December 2023**: The Box Encapsulation Plant Product Store (BEPPS) received its first box of waste, marking a significant step in the clean-up efforts at Sellafield. The facility has a capacity to store 6681 waste boxes and can receive up to 9 boxes every 24 hours.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: The Nuclear Waste Services strategy was launched, with Sellafield Ltd leading trials to develop thermal treatment technologies as a proven solution for nuclear waste management. This initiative is part of a broader strategy to reduce waste and improve recycling efforts by 2030.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2023**: Announcement of the AI strategy.
- **August 2023**: Successful testing of the robot grab for waste retrieval from the PFCS.
- **August 2023**: First waste retrievals from the PFCS were achieved.
- **December 2023**: The first box of waste was received by the new BEPPS facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2023**: The initiation of waste retrieval operations and the deployment of drones are expected to enhance safety and efficiency, potentially leading to job creation in the technology and operations sectors as new roles may emerge from these advancements.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Sellafield Ltd operates as a wholly-owned subsidiary of the Nuclear Decommissioning Authority, indicating full ownership of the facility.
