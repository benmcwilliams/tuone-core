### Evolutionary Timeline of Sellafield Ltd. Facility in Sellafield, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **1947**: Development at the Sellafield site began to produce plutonium for defense objectives.
- **1956**: Queen Elizabeth II opened Calder Hall, the world’s first commercial nuclear power station, which generated electricity and produced plutonium.
- **1964**: The Magnox Reprocessing Plant began operations, reprocessing nearly 55,000 metric tons of fuel until its closure in July 2022.
- **2018**: The Thermal Oxide Reprocessing Plant ceased reprocessing after fulfilling its commercial contracts.
- **Current Focus**: The facility is now focused on decommissioning legacy facilities and managing spent fuel and special nuclear materials, with a mission extending over 100 years.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2023**: The UK Alpha Resilience and Capability (ARC) Programme was initiated, involving collaboration among various organizations to enhance skills and capabilities in managing alpha materials. This program includes building a dedicated Glove Box Training Facility near Sellafield to improve training for alpha operators.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2022**: The Magnox Reprocessing Plant completed its reprocessing operations.
- **2018**: The Thermal Oxide Reprocessing Plant ceased operations.
- **2023**: The ARC Programme was launched to address skills shortages and improve training for handling alpha materials.

#### 4. Labour
- **Current Employment**: Sellafield employs approximately 11,000 people directly, with additional support for the supply chain.
- **Future Projections**: The UK nuclear industry anticipates a peak demand for between 98,000 and 105,500 full-time equivalents in the next decade, highlighting the need for effective workforce planning and skills development.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: Sellafield Ltd. operates as a wholly owned subsidiary of the Nuclear Decommissioning Authority (NDA), which oversees the decommissioning and management of nuclear sites in the UK.
