### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ocean Winds Facility in Caithness, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2022**: Ocean Winds awarded a contract to Navantia Seanergies and Windar Renovables for the manufacture and delivery of 14 XXL monopiles for the Moray West wind farm. The monopiles will be approximately 90 meters long, with a diameter between 8 and 10 meters, and a maximum weight of 2,000 tons. This contract marks the first order executed in a new XXL monopile factory being built in Fene, Spain.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2022**: The investment for the new XXL monopile factory is over EUR 36 million. This facility is expected to generate 280,000 hours of work over a ten-month global execution period.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2022**: Material collection for the monopiles began, with production work scheduled to start in December 2022. The Moray West wind farm will feature 60 Siemens Gamesa 14.7 MW wind turbines installed on monopile foundations approximately 14 miles off the coast of Caithness.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2022**: The project is expected to create an average of 150 direct jobs per month during the production phase, contributing to local employment.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Ocean Winds was created in 2019 as a company equally owned by EDPR (EDP Renewables) and ENGIE, a French multinational. This indicates a joint ownership structure for the facility, with both companies having equal stakes in the operations and developments associated with the Moray West wind farm.
