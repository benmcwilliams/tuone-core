## GBR-09573-07963

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ocean Winds Facility at Moray West Offshore Wind Farm, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: The facility commenced production of J-tube frames for the 882 MW Moray West Offshore Wind Farm at the Port of Nigg. These J-tube frames are essential for supporting cables from the wind turbines and exporting power to shore.
- **April 2024**: The first Siemens Gamesa 14.7 MW turbine was installed at the Moray West Offshore Wind Farm. The project involves 60 turbines, each capable of generating up to 14.7 MW, contributing to the total capacity of 882 MW. The blades for these turbines are the longest in UK waters, measuring 108 meters.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: The J-tube fabrication project is described as a multi-million-pound contract, indicating significant investment in the facility's operations.
- **April 2024**: Siemens Gamesa expanded its facility in Hull to double its size to support the production of the 180 B108 blades for the Moray West project, reflecting substantial investment in local manufacturing capabilities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: Steel cutting for the J-tube frames marked a significant milestone in the project, indicating the start of fabrication activities.
- **April 2024**: The installation of the first turbine at the Moray West Offshore Wind Farm was announced, with the turbine installation campaign expected to continue throughout most of 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2023**: The J-tube fabrication project is expected to support 50 jobs at the North Fabrication division of GEG.
- **April 2024**: Siemens Gamesa's expanded facility in Hull employs over 1,000 people, contributing to job creation in the region as part of the Moray West project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Moray West Offshore Wind Farm is developed by Ocean Winds, which is a 50:50 joint venture between ENGIE and EDP Renewables. This indicates that the facility is not under exclusive ownership but rather a joint venture arrangement.
