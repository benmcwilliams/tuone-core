## GBR-09585-09648

### Opportunity Cromarty Firth Facility Timeline (Alness Business Park, GBR)

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility aims to establish a "global centre of excellence" focused on the development of floating wind and green hydrogen technology. It will also provide educational opportunities and retraining for individuals transitioning from other energy sectors, such as oil and gas and nuclear.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The initial funding for the facility's manager position is provided by the National Manufacturing Institute Scotland (NMIS) through the Scottish Government’s £25 million Transition Training Fund. This funding is part of a broader initiative to support skills development in the renewable energy sector.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **June 2021:** The Power House initiative was launched, with the appointment of Mina Hanna as the first manager. The facility is located at North Highland College UHI’s Tern House campus in Alness Business Park.

4. **Labour:**
   - The appointment of Mina Hanna is part of a scheme to recruit 30 graduates who were unable to find work due to the Covid pandemic, indicating job creation in the renewable energy sector.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The Opportunity Cromarty Firth (OCF) group is a collaborative initiative involving around a dozen public and private sector organizations, including the Port of Cromarty Firth, Global Energy Group, Highland Council, and the University of the Highlands and Islands. This suggests a joint venture or collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by a single entity. 

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic focus of the Opportunity Cromarty Firth facility as of June 2021.
