### Evolutionary Timeline of Ørsted's Hornsea II Facility, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2022**: Hornsea II is a 1.3GW offshore wind farm located 89 km offshore from Yorkshire, UK, covering an area of 462 km². It is designed to generate enough clean energy to power over 1.3 million homes annually. The facility features 165 wind turbines and is in the construction phase, expected to come online later in 2022.
- **April 2022**: NKT announced the commissioning of the 220kV high voltage alternative current (HVAC) cable system for Hornsea II, indicating progress in the installation of necessary infrastructure for the wind farm.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2022**: Ørsted agreed to divest a 50% stake in Hornsea II to AXA IM Alts and Crédit Agricole Assurances for £3 billion ($3.9 billion). This transaction is part of Ørsted's strategy to support the UK Government's goal of installing 40GW of offshore wind capacity by 2030.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2022**: Announcement of the divestment of a 50% stake in Hornsea II, with the deal expected to close in the second half of 2022 once the wind farm is fully commissioned.
- **April 2022**: Ongoing construction and installation of wind turbines, with the project expected to come online later in 2022.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2022**: Ørsted's investments in the UK, including Hornsea II, are projected to support over 40,000 jobs in the offshore wind supply chain over the next few years.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2022**: Ørsted will retain a 50% ownership stake in Hornsea II after the divestment, with AXA IM Alts and Crédit Agricole Assurances each acquiring a 25% stake. Ørsted will continue to provide operations and maintenance (O&M) services for Hornsea II for a period of 20 years post-commissioning.
