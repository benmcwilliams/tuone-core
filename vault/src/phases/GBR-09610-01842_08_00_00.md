### Evolutionary Timeline of the Orsted Facility in Humber Estuary, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2022**: Orsted announced a £2.5 million project aimed at restoring biodiversity in the Humber Estuary. This project includes the planting of three hectares of salt marsh, four hectares of seagrass, and the creation of a biogenic reef through the introduction of half a million native oysters. This initiative is part of Orsted's commitment to integrate biodiversity solutions into its offshore wind energy projects.

- **November 2022**: Orsted, in partnership with the Yorkshire Wildlife Trust, initiated plans for what could become the largest seagrass restoration project in the UK and Europe, focusing on restoring up to 74 acres of lost seagrass meadow in the Humber Estuary as part of the Hornsea 4 offshore wind farm. The project aims to enhance marine habitats and support local biodiversity.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2022**: The £2.5 million investment for the biodiversity restoration project was announced, emphasizing Orsted's commitment to integrating environmental considerations into its operations.

- **November 2022**: The seagrass restoration project is funded by the Hornsea 4 offshore wind farm, with plans for a second phase of restoration contingent on securing development consent.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
