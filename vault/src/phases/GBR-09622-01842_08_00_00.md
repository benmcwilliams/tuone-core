### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ørsted Facility in Walney Extension, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Walney Extension offshore wind farm produces a total capacity of 659 MW, utilizing 40 MHI Vestas 8MW turbines and 47 Siemens Gamesa 7MW turbines. The facility is located in the Irish Sea, approximately 19 km from the Walney Island coast in Cumbria.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - Ørsted has invested GBP 1.8 million in the construction of a heliport base to support operations at the Walney Extension offshore wind farm. This investment is aimed at enhancing the logistical capabilities for personnel transfer to and from the wind farm.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **January 2019:** Ørsted awarded a contract to Jones Bros Civil Engineering UK for the design and construction of a heliport base in Barrow-in-Furness. This facility is crucial for supporting helicopter operations related to the wind farm.

4. **Labour:**
   - The construction of the heliport base is expected to create jobs related to the design, construction, and operational support for the Walney Extension offshore wind farm.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - Ørsted owns the Walney Extension facility, having previously operated under the name DONG Energy. There is no indication of joint ventures or shared ownership in the provided information.
