### Evolutionary Timeline of the Pale Blue Dot Energy Facility in Banchory, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date**: March 2021
  - **Details**: Pale Blue Dot Energy is involved in the North of Scotland Hydrogen Programme (NSHP), which aims to establish a green hydrogen hub on the Cromarty Firth. The facility will produce green hydrogen using electrolysers powered by renewable energy sources, particularly from nearby wind farms. The hydrogen produced is intended to power businesses and homes in the Highlands and potentially export to other regions.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date**: March 2021
  - **Details**: The feasibility study for the hydrogen hub is funded by ScottishPower and the whisky companies involved (Glenmorangie, Whyte & Mackay, and Diageo). This study will explore the use of hydrogen as a substitute for fossil fuels in local distilleries and maltings.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date**: March 2021
  - **Details**: The NSHP was officially launched, marking the beginning of the project to develop the green hydrogen hub. A four-month feasibility study was initiated to assess the potential for hydrogen use in local industries.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date**: March 2021
  - **Details**: While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned, the project is expected to create significant economic opportunities and potentially lead to job creation in the region as the hydrogen hub develops and local industries decarbonize.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date**: March 2021
  - **Details**: The facility is part of a partnership involving multiple stakeholders, including ScottishPower, the Port of Cromarty Firth, and several whisky companies, alongside Pale Blue Dot Energy. This indicates a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by Pale Blue Dot Energy.
