### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ping Petroleum UK Facility at Avalon Site, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **August 2021:** Ping Petroleum UK acquired a 100% stake in the Avalon site.
   - **Expected Production Start:** Production is anticipated to begin in **2025**.
   - **Production Capacity:** The field has a total estimated recovery of **23 million barrels of oil**.
   - **Technology Implementation:** The facility will be powered mainly by floating offshore wind, utilizing a large floating offshore wind turbine connected to a Floating Production & Storage vessel.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Investment Amount:** The project is expected to realize between **£80-100 million** in investment.
   - **Funding Source:** The project received a grant through the **Floating Offshore Wind Demonstration Programme** from the UK Government’s Department of Business, Energy and Industrial Strategy (BEIS) to support innovation and technology optimization.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **February 2022:** An agreement was signed between Cerulean Winds and Ping Petroleum UK to establish one of the UK’s first oil and gas facilities powered mainly by offshore wind.
   - **Regulatory Approval:** The proposed development concept has been cleared by the **North Sea Transition Authority (NTSA)**.

4. **Labour:**
   - The project is expected to secure jobs and new skills in the North Sea oil and gas sector, contributing to the commitments of the North Sea Transition Deal.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - Ping Petroleum UK owns the Avalon site **exclusively** with a **100% stake**.
