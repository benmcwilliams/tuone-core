## GBR-09733-03423

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Sharp Facility in Wrexham, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2010**: Sharp announced plans to double the annual production capacity of its Wrexham facility from 250MW to 500MW of crystalline solar cell modules. The expansion was set to begin in December 2010 and aimed for completion by February 2011. This expansion was part of Sharp's strategy to meet increasing global demand for solar cell modules and to enhance local production for the UK market.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2010**: The total cost of the expansion to increase production capacity to 500MW was estimated at around 4 billion yen. This investment was aimed at supporting the growth of the solar market in the UK, particularly in light of the newly implemented UK feed-in tariff.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2011**: The expansion to 500MW production capacity was completed.
- **December 2013**: Sharp announced the closure of the Wrexham facility, with production ceasing in February 2014 due to the "rapid commoditisation" of the solar panel market in the UK and Europe.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2013**: The closure of the Wrexham facility was expected to result in significant job losses, with up to 250 employees facing redundancy. The decision was described as unavoidable due to the uncompetitive nature of the facility in the current market conditions.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Throughout the timeline, Sharp was the sole owner of the Wrexham facility, operating it as part of its international production network for solar cell modules. There is no indication of joint ventures or shared ownership during the facility's operational period. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and changes at the Sharp facility in Wrexham, highlighting its production capacity, investments, project milestones, labor impacts, and ownership structure.
