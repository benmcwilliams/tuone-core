### Evolutionary Timeline of RWE Facility in Camster II, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2024**: RWE is constructing a 36MW extension to the operational Camster wind farm in Caithness. All 10 turbines from Vestas-Celtic Wind Technology Ltd are complete, alongside civil works and electrical infrastructure. Final road capping and borrow pit reinstatement are underway, with electricity generation expected in 2025 following grid connection.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2024**: RWE is making a significant investment as part of its broader ambition to invest €8 billion net into new UK energy infrastructure. The three projects, including Camster II, represent this investment in renewable generation capacity for Scotland. Additionally, RWE is committed to investing £25 million through flexible community benefits packages over the lifetime of these projects.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2024**: The Camster II Onshore Wind Farm project is progressing well, with all turbines completed and final works in progress. Electricity generation is expected to commence in 2025 after grid connection.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2024**: RWE's projects, including Camster II, are creating and supporting local jobs. The company is committed to providing new job roles, including apprenticeships and site managers, across Scotland as part of its investment in the local economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2024**: RWE exclusively owns the Camster II facility as part of its portfolio of three wind projects in Scotland, which also includes Enoch Hill and Strathy Wood.
