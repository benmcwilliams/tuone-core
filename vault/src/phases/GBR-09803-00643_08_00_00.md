## GBR-09803-00643

### Evolutionary Timeline of RWE Facility: Sofia Offshore Wind Farm, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2020**: RWE Renewables announced the development of the Sofia Offshore Wind Farm, which will have a production capacity of 1.4 GW. The project will consist of 100 turbines, each rated at 14 MW, and will be equipped with 108-metre-long turbine blades. The total area of the wind farm will be approximately 593 square kilometres.
- **October 2024**: The first turbine blade was rolled out by Siemens Gamesa for the Sofia Offshore Wind Farm. The offshore installation commenced earlier in the year, and the project’s substation has been installed. The project is expected to be completed in 2026.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2020**: Prysmian Group signed a preferred bidder agreement with RWE for a high voltage submarine and land export cable connection worth over €200 million ($237 million). This investment is crucial for the project's infrastructure.
- **October 2024**: RWE confirmed that over 50% of the investment in the Sofia project is being spent within the UK, indicating a significant local economic impact.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2020**: Announcement of the Sofia Offshore Wind Farm project, with a final investment decision pending.
- **February 2022**: Bureau Technical Services secured a contract for quality and inspection work on the project, indicating progress in the development phase.
- **October 2024**: The first turbine blade was unveiled, marking a significant milestone in the construction of the wind farm. The offshore installation began earlier in the year, with project completion anticipated in 2026.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2024**: Siemens Energy reported that over 1,300 people are employed at their Hull factory, with more than 600 new employees recruited in the past year to support the production of turbine blades for the Sofia project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2020**: The Sofia Offshore Wind Farm is owned exclusively by RWE Renewables, as indicated in the articles.
