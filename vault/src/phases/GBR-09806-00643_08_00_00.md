### Evolutionary Timeline of RWE Facility in Strathy Wood, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2024**: RWE's Strathy Wood Onshore Wind Farm project, with a generation capacity of **63 MW**, began construction with the installation of the first foundations. The target operational date is set for **October 2026**. RJ McLeod Ltd has been awarded the Civil Balance of Plant contract, and Nordex is the turbine supplier.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2024**: RWE announced a significant investment of **€8 billion** net into new UK energy infrastructure, which includes the Strathy Wood project as part of a trio of wind projects in Scotland (Enoch Hill, Camster II, and Strathy Wood). Additionally, RWE is committed to investing **£25 million** through community benefits packages over the lifetime of these projects.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2024**: The Strathy Wood project officially began construction with the installation of foundations. The project is part of a larger initiative that includes Enoch Hill and Camster II, which collectively will have a total installed capacity of **168 MW**. The operational date for Strathy Wood is targeted for **October 2026**.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2024**: RWE's projects, including Strathy Wood, are expected to create and support local jobs. The company has committed to providing new job roles, including apprenticeships and site managers, across Scotland as part of its investment in these projects.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2024**: RWE owns the Strathy Wood facility exclusively as part of its portfolio of renewable energy projects in Scotland, which also includes Enoch Hill and Camster II.

This timeline captures the key developments and commitments related to the RWE facility in Strathy Wood, highlighting its role in advancing renewable energy in Scotland.
