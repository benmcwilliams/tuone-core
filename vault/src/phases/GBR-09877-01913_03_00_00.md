### Evolutionary Timeline of the SSE Facility in Coire Glas, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2020**: SSE announced the Coire Glas project, which is designed to have a production capacity of 1.5 GW and a storage capacity of 30 GWh. The facility is intended to allow for rapid switching between charging and discharging.
- **December 2021**: The project was confirmed to have secured all necessary approvals, with procurement underway. The capacity was expanded to 1.5 GW and 90 GWh of storage. The project involves constructing an upper reservoir with a 500-meter drop to Loch Lochy.
- **May 2023**: A report indicated that the Coire Glas project is part of a broader initiative to double the UK's pumped storage capacity to 7.7 GW, highlighting its importance in improving the electricity network and supporting renewable energy.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2020**: SSE's development manager highlighted the need for a cost-effective financing model for the project, suggesting that a cap and floor system could be beneficial.
- **December 2021**: SSE indicated that the project was stalled due to the need for better financial incentives, emphasizing the importance of a regulated payment mechanism for the project's viability.
- **May 2023**: SSE called for urgent government support to clarify investment frameworks for pumped storage hydro projects, indicating the potential for significant economic benefits and job creation.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2020**: SSE anticipated imminent planning consent for the Coire Glas project.
- **October 2020**: SSE received necessary approvals for the expanded capacity of 1.5 GW.
- **December 2023**: SSE plans to make a final decision regarding the project, aiming to start construction by April 2024.
- **August 2024**: SSE completed exploratory tunneling at the Coire Glas site, marking a significant phase in the project’s development.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2023**: A report indicated that the development of pumped storage projects, including Coire Glas, could create almost 15,000 jobs and generate significant economic benefits for the UK by 2035.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2021**: SSE confirmed that it is developing the Coire Glas project independently but is open to potential equity partnerships and project financing options. This indicates that while SSE is the primary developer, it may consider joint ventures or partnerships in the future.
