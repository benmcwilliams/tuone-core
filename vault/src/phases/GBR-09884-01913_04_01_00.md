## GBR-09884-01913

### Evolutionary Timeline of the SSE Facility in Gordonbush, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04/2022**: SSE Renewables and Siemens Gamesa announced plans to produce green hydrogen at the Gordonbush facility, utilizing a 100 MW onshore wind farm. The facility is designed to produce up to **2,000 tonnes of green hydrogen annually** using Siemens Gamesa’s Renewable Hydrogen Upgrade solution. A battery energy storage system will also be integrated to manage surplus energy from the wind farm's 46 turbines.
- **08/2023**: The production capacity was revised to **1,300 tonnes of green hydrogen annually** in the latest project updates, indicating ongoing refinements to the project.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04/2022**: SSE Renewables submitted a screening request to the Highland Council for the development of the hydrogen facility, with plans for a final application expected in summer 2022. This indicates early stages of investment and development efforts.
- **08/2023**: The Gordonbush Hydrogen project was included in the UK Government's Hydrogen Business Model (HMB) subsidy scheme, which aims to support projects targeting first operation in 2025. This inclusion suggests potential financial backing for the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04/2022**: Announcement of the project and plans for public consultation to gather community feedback.
- **Summer 2022**: Expected submission of a final planning application to the Highland Council for the electrolyser development.
- **08/2023**: The project was invited to the next stage of negotiations under the HMB process, marking an important milestone towards funding and development.

#### 4. Labour
- **04/2022**: SSE Renewables indicated that the development of the green hydrogen facility would support local job creation, although specific job numbers were not detailed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **04/2022**: The facility is a collaboration between SSE Renewables and Siemens Gamesa, indicating a **joint venture** structure for the development of the hydrogen facility. SSE Renewables is the primary developer, leveraging its existing wind farm infrastructure.
