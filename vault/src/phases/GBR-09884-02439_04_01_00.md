## GBR-09884-02439

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Siemens Gamesa Facility in Gordonbush, GBR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **04/2022:** Siemens Gamesa, in collaboration with SSE Renewables, announced plans to produce green hydrogen at the Gordonbush facility. The facility will utilize Siemens Gamesa’s Renewable Hydrogen Upgrade solution, powered by the 100 MW Gordonbush onshore wind farm. The facility is designed to produce up to 2,000 tonnes of green hydrogen annually, using zero carbon electricity from renewable sources through electrolysis. Additionally, a battery energy storage system will be incorporated to manage surplus energy from the wind farm's 46 turbines.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **04/2022:** SSE Renewables submitted a screening request to the Highland Council to consider the development of the green hydrogen facility. A final planning application for the electrolyser development is expected to be submitted in summer 2022, with a separate application for the battery energy storage system anticipated later in the year.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **04/2022:** Announcement of the green hydrogen facility plans, including community and stakeholder consultations to inform project designs. The final planning application is expected in summer 2022.

4. **Labour:**
   - **04/2022:** The project aims to develop a green hydrogen supply chain that supports local job creation, particularly in high carbon sectors looking to decarbonize their energy and fuel supplies.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **04/2022:** The facility is a collaborative project between Siemens Gamesa and SSE Renewables, indicating a joint venture structure for the development of the green hydrogen facility at Gordonbush. 

This timeline captures the key developments and plans for the Siemens Gamesa facility in Gordonbush, GBR, as of April 2022.

### Siemens Gamesa Facility in Gordonbush, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- "The planned facility at Gordonbush, near Brora, would comprise electrolyser and ancillary technology and would be capable of producing up to 2,000 tonnes of green hydrogen a year."
- "Green hydrogen would be produced using Siemens Gamesa’s Renewable Hydrogen Upgrade solution."

#### 2. Investment and Development
- "SSE Renewables recently submitted a screening request to the Highland Council to consider its plans for the development."
- "A final planning application for the proposed electrolyser development is expected to be submitted to the Highland Council in summer."

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- "A local public consultation will also be held in April to gather community and stakeholder feedback on the proposal that will help inform final detailed project designs."
- "A separate application is expected to be made to the Scottish Government Energy Consents Unit later this year for the associated battery energy storage system."

#### 4. Labour
- "Green hydrogen will be key in the decarbonization of hard-to-electrify, and thus hard-to-abate, sectors."
- "This will include working with potential customers from high carbon sectors looking to decarbonise their energy and fuel supplies."

#### 5. Ownership structure
- "As an integrated group, SSE has a view across the whole energy value chain and is uniquely positioned to deliver."
- "We’re excited to work together with SSE on our journey to unlock the green hydrogen revolution and deliver the fuel for the future."
