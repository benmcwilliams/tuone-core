### Evolutionary Timeline of the SSE Facility in Loch Lochy, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Production**: The SSE facility at Loch Lochy is home to the Sloy hydroelectric power station, which has a capacity of 152.5MW. 
- **Future Plans**: SSE Renewables is planning to redevelop the Sloy hydroelectric power station into a pumped hydro storage facility, which could provide up to 25GWh of long-duration electricity storage, capable of delivering flexible renewable energy for up to 160 continuous hours.
- **Coire Glas Project**: SSE is also developing the Coire Glas pumped storage project, which will have a capacity of 1.3GW and is expected to deliver 30GWh of long-duration electricity storage, enough to power three million British homes for up to 24 hours non-stop.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Boost**: In early 2023, SSE announced a £100 million investment to further develop the Coire Glas project, which includes the construction of a major exploratory tunnel as part of site investigation works.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Exploratory Works**: Exploratory tunnelling for the Coire Glas project commenced in December 2022 and was completed in August 2024, marking a significant milestone in the development of the UK's first large-scale pumped storage scheme in 40 years.
- **Future Plans**: SSE aims to make a final investment decision on the Coire Glas project in late 2025 or early 2026, with main construction expected to start in the second half of 2026, contingent on securing an investable cap and floor mechanism.

#### 4. Labour

#### 5. Ownership Structure
