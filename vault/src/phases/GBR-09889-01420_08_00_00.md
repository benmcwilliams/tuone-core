### Evolutionary Timeline of the TotalEnergies Facility in Seagreen, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2021**: The Seagreen offshore wind farm, with a production capacity of 1,075 MW, is under development. The project will consist of 114 Vestas 10 MW turbines, expected to generate approximately 5,000 GWh of renewable energy annually, enough to power over 1.6 million UK homes. 
- **September 2022**: The first turbine was commissioned and connected to the grid, marking a significant milestone in the operational phase of the facility.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2021**: The Seagreen project is a £3 billion joint venture between TotalEnergies (51%) and SSE Renewables (49%). The investment reflects the commitment to renewable energy and the development of offshore wind technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2021**: Briggs Marine is contracted to supply, deploy, maintain, and recover 16 buoys critical for navigation around the Seagreen site. All buoys were installed by early September 2021.
- **September 2022**: The project reached a significant milestone with the commissioning of the first turbine and the expectation that all turbines will be operational in the first half of 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2021**: The contract with Briggs Marine for the installation of navigation buoys is expected to expand operations and create associated employment opportunities in the local area.
- **September 2022**: Certex UK is contracted to provide inspection and maintenance services for the Balance of Plant equipment, indicating ongoing job creation related to the operational support of the wind farm.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2021**: The Seagreen offshore wind farm is a joint venture, with TotalEnergies holding a 51% stake and SSE Renewables holding 49%. This indicates that TotalEnergies does not own the facility exclusively but is a majority partner in the joint venture.
