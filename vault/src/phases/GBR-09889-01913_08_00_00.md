### Evolutionary Timeline of the SSE Seagreen Facility, Seagreen, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2020**: The Seagreen offshore wind farm is announced with a planned capacity of 1,075 MW, capable of generating approximately 5,000 GWh of renewable energy annually, enough to power over 1.6 million UK homes. The project includes 114 Vestas 10 MW turbines.
- **September 2022**: The first turbine is commissioned and connected to the grid, marking a significant operational milestone. Over 50 jacket foundations have been installed at the offshore construction site.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2020**: The Seagreen project is valued at approximately USD 3.8 billion, part of a larger trend where offshore wind power investments surged by 319% in the first half of 2020.
- **September 2021**: The project is identified as a £3 billion joint venture between TotalEnergies (51%) and SSE Renewables (49%).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2020**: Announcement of the Seagreen offshore wind farm project.
- **September 2021**: Briggs Marine is contracted to supply and maintain 16 buoys critical for navigation around the Seagreen site, indicating progress in the project's infrastructure.
- **September 2022**: The first turbine is commissioned, and the project reaches a significant milestone with the completion of over 50 jacket foundations.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2021**: The contract awarded to Briggs Marine for buoy installation and maintenance is expected to expand operations and create associated employment opportunities in the local area.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2021**: The Seagreen offshore wind farm is a joint venture, with TotalEnergies holding a 51% stake and SSE Renewables holding a 49% stake, indicating that SSE does not own the facility exclusively.
