## GBR-09894-01913

### Evolutionary Timeline of the SSE Facility in Tummel Bridge, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2021**: SSE Renewables announced plans to repower the Tummel Bridge Hydro-Electric Power Station, increasing its production capacity from 34MW to a peak of 40MW. The refurbishment will replace the existing twin-runner, horizontal Francis hydro turbines with modern runner technology, expected to enhance efficiency and extend the operational life of the facility by at least 40 years.
- **May 2024**: The first of the two new hydropower turbines was successfully installed and commissioned, marking a significant milestone in the repowering project. The second turbine is expected to be operational by mid-summer 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2021**: SSE Renewables committed to investing nearly £50 million over two years for the repowering of the Tummel Bridge facility. This investment is noted as the largest single refurbishment investment in SSE's hydro fleet in recent times.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Spring 2022**: Refurbishment works commenced at the Tummel Bridge Hydro-Electric Power Station.
- **Late 2023**: The repowering works are expected to be completed, coinciding with the facility's 90th anniversary.
- **May 2024**: The first new turbine was commissioned, with the second expected to be operational by mid-summer 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2021**: The refurbishment project is anticipated to support up to 65 construction jobs at peak, contributing to local job creation in the Scottish regional supply chain.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- SSE Renewables owns the Tummel Bridge Hydro-Electric Power Station exclusively, as there is no mention of joint ventures or shared ownership in the articles.
