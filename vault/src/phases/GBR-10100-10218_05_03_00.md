## GBR-10100-10218

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Terrestrial Energy Facility in Preston, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2023**: Terrestrial Energy announced a contract for a pilot plant at the Springfields reactor fuel manufacturing site in Preston. This facility will support the fuel supply for the Integral Molten Salt Reactor (IMSR) development and is scalable for a fleet of IMSR plants expected to operate in the 2030s. The IMSR technology utilizes molten salt as both fuel and coolant, employing standard assay low-enriched uranium (LEU) for its operations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2023**: The UK government committed GBP2.9 million (USD3.8 million) to establish the pilot plant under its Nuclear Fuel Fund programme. This investment is aimed at advancing the industrial scale-up and commercial supply of enriched uranium fuel for the IMSR.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2023**: Announcement of the contract for the pilot plant at the Springfields site, marking a significant step towards the development of the IMSR technology.
- **October 2024**: Terrestrial Energy signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with Viaro Energy to develop an IMSR nuclear project in the UK. This partnership aims to assess site viability and regulatory requirements, with plans to establish a joint venture for the project. A final investment decision is expected by the end of the decade.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2024**: Upon commencing operations, the proposed IMSR plant is anticipated to create more than 120 permanent jobs, along with numerous construction and supply chain roles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2024**: The facility will be developed as a joint venture (JV) between Terrestrial Energy and Viaro Energy, with Viaro responsible for infrastructure and investment, while Terrestrial Energy will lead the development and procurement of the nuclear technology.
