## GBR-10100-10362

### Evolutionary Timeline of the VolkerInfra Facility in Preston, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2019**: VolkerInfra is contracted to supply and install high voltage onshore cables for the Moray East offshore wind project, which has a total capacity of 950MW. The facility will be responsible for designing, supplying, and installing approximately 330,000 meters of 220kV onshore underground export cables, covering a route length of over 34km. The project includes working with voltages of 400kV, 220kV, and 33kV, and involves the installation of three 220kV circuits and multiple 66kV connections for offshore transformer modules.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2019**: Siemens, as the lead contractor for the Moray East project, places multi-million pound contracts with various UK companies, including VolkerInfra, for the construction of the transmission infrastructure. This indicates significant investment in local companies to support the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2019**: VolkerInfra completes cable route development works, including geotechnical surveys, and begins the installation of high voltage cables for the Moray East project. Production of the 220kV onshore high voltage cables is set to start in the first quarter of 2019.
- **July 2019**: The construction of the onshore substation and related infrastructure is underway, with VolkerInfra actively involved in the installation of the high voltage cables. The Moray East wind farm is scheduled to be fully operational by 2022.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2019**: The involvement of local companies in the project, including those appointed by VolkerInfra for road improvements and horizontal directional drilling works, suggests job creation in the local area as part of the construction efforts for the Moray East project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify the ownership structure of the VolkerInfra facility in Preston, GBR, but it is implied that VolkerInfra operates as a subcontractor under Siemens for the Moray East project, indicating that it does not have exclusive ownership of the facility related to this project. 

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the VolkerInfra facility in Preston, GBR, as it pertains to the Moray East offshore wind project.

### Technology and Operations
- **Article 1 (29-01-2019)**: "VolkerInfra will supply onshore cables and associated infrastructure for EDPR's 950MW Moray East offshore wind farm off the coast of Scotland."
- **Article 2 (15-07-2019)**: "VolkerInfra will provide and install the high voltage onshore export cables for the project which stretch the 35km underground cable route from near Whitehills on the Aberdeenshire coast to the new substation site south of New Deer."

### Investment and Development
- **Article 1 (29-01-2019)**: "Contractor Siemens selected VolkerInfra, following a two-year competitive tender."
- **Article 2 (15-07-2019)**: "Siemens has placed multi-million pound contracts with companies across the UK for the construction of the transmission infrastructure for the Moray East offshore wind farm."

### Project Status and Milestones
- **Article 1 (29-01-2019)**: "The initial design work commenced in late 2017 and the main pre-construction works started in October 2018."
- **Article 2 (15-07-2019)**: "Moray East will comprise 100 MHI Vestas 9.5MW wind turbines mounted on jacket foundations at the site some 22 kilometres off the Aberdeenshire coast in Scotland. The wind farm is scheduled to be fully operational in 2022."

### Labour
- **Article 1 (29-01-2019)**: "VolkerInfra has appointed Scottish companies to deliver road improvements and complete horizontal directional drilling works required for the cable installation."
- **Article 2 (15-07-2019)**: "Firms from the area local to the onshore construction sites have been working on site enabling works ahead of major civil construction works including preparation of the substation site and onshore cable route."

### Ownership Structure
- **Article 1 (29-01-2019)**: "The Moray Offshore Windfarm (East) Limited consortium developing the offshore wind project off Scotland’s east coast is comprised of EDPR (43.3%), Diamond Green Limited (33.4%) and Engie (23.3%)."
- **Article 2 (15-07-2019)**: "Siemens is responsible for the complete construction of the onshore substation, including three SVC Plus advanced STATCOMs."
