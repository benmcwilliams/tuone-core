### Evolutionary Timeline of Tokamak Energy Facility in Culham Campus, near Oxford, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2023**: Tokamak Energy announced plans to build a prototype spherical tokamak, the ST80-HTS, at the UKAEA's Culham Campus by 2026. This facility aims to demonstrate the full potential of high-temperature superconducting magnets and inform the design of its fusion pilot plant, which is intended to deliver electricity to the grid in the 2030s.
- **October 2023**: Tokamak Energy provided initial details of a high-field spherical tokamak plant capable of generating 800 MW of fusion power and 85 MW of net electricity. The design includes an aspect ratio of 2.0, a plasma major radius of 4.25 meters, and a magnetic field of 4.25 Tesla, along with a liquid lithium tritium breeding blanket.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2024**: A five-year program was announced, funded equally by the US Department of Energy, the UK's Department of Energy Security and Net Zero, and Tokamak Energy. This program aims to advance research for future fusion power plants, particularly focusing on coating the inner wall of the ST40 device with lithium to improve fusion conditions.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09-12-2024**: The ST40 upgrade was announced, with expectations for it to be operational by 2027. This upgrade is part of a strategic partnership between the US and UK energy departments to enhance fusion research capabilities.
