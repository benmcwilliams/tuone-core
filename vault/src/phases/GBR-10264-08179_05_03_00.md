### Evolutionary Timeline of the Westinghouse Facility in Springfields, GBR

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2023**: Westinghouse announced plans to expand its fuel offerings at the Springfields facility, focusing on developing new variants of light water reactor fuels, including for the AP1000 and AP300 small modular reactors (SMRs). The facility will also support the production of high-assay low-enriched uranium (HALEU) fuels for advanced reactors.
- **August 2024**: Westinghouse began production of LEU+ ADOPT fuel pellets at the Springfields facility. These pellets contain up to 8% uranium-235 and are designed to improve safety and efficiency in nuclear reactors. The first pellets were produced in collaboration with Southern Nuclear and the US Department of Energy (DOE).

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2023**: The UK government allocated GBP10.5 million to Westinghouse's Springfields plant to support the development of new fuel technologies essential for next-generation nuclear reactors.
- **December 2022**: Prior to the July announcement, GBP13 million was awarded to Westinghouse for design and enabling work to begin new conversion capabilities at the Springfields site, aimed at providing uranium conversion services globally.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
