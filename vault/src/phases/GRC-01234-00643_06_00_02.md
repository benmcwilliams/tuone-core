### Evolutionary Timeline of the RWE Facility in Western Macedonia, Greece

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2023**: RWE and PPC, through their joint venture Meton Energy, announced the development of five solar photovoltaic (PV) projects with a combined capacity of 210 MWp (175 MWac) in Western Macedonia. Construction was set to begin shortly after the announcement, with operations expected by Q2 2024.
- **August 2023**: The companies took final investment decisions for an additional three solar projects (Amynteo Cluster II) with a total capacity of approximately 280 MWp (259 MWac), located in the same region. Construction was scheduled to start in autumn 2023, with completion expected by the end of 2024.
- **February 2024**: RWE and PPC announced plans for a 450 MWp solar plant (Orycheio Dei Amynteo) within the former lignite mine area, marking the last project in their 940 MWp/870 MW (AC) Amynteo portfolio. Construction was set to begin soon, with commissioning expected by the end of 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2023**: The total investment for the initial five solar projects was €180 million, with €90 million financed by the EU’s NextGenerationEU funds. The remaining financing included €54 million in commercial debt and €36 million in shareholders’ equity.
- **August 2023**: The investment for the Amynteo Cluster II projects was €196 million, with €98 million from the EU’s Recovery and Resilience Facility. The remaining financing comprised €59 million in commercial debt and €39 million in shareholders’ equity.
- **February 2024**: The Orycheio Dei Amynteo project had a total budget of €255.4 million, with €127.7 million from NextGenerationEU funds, €76.6 million in commercial debt, and €51.1 million in shareholders’ equity.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2023**: Announcement of the five solar PV projects (Amynteo Cluster I) and commencement of construction expected soon.
- **August 2023**: Final investment decision for the three additional solar projects (Amynteo Cluster II) and construction scheduled to start in autumn 2023.
- **February 2024**: Final investment decision for the 450 MWp solar plant (Orycheio Dei Amynteo) with construction expected to start shortly.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- RWE owns 51% of the joint venture Meton Energy, while PPC holds the remaining 49%. This indicates a joint venture ownership structure for the facility.
