### Evolutionary Timeline of Meton Energy S.A. Facility in Western Macedonia, GRC

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date**: 01-08-2023
  - **Production**: Meton Energy S.A. is developing a portfolio of solar photovoltaic projects, specifically the Amynteo Cluster II, which consists of three solar farms with a total capacity of approximately 280 MWp (259 MWac).
  - **Current Capacity**: In addition to the new projects, the facility is already constructing five large-scale solar projects (Amynteo Cluster I) with a total capacity of 210 MWp (175 MWac).
  - **Future Capacity**: Including all projects under development, Meton Energy S.A. aims to achieve a total capacity of up to 2,000 MWp.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date**: 01-08-2023
  - **Investment**: The total investment for the Amynteo Cluster II is €196 million.
  - **Funding Sources**: 
    - €98 million secured from European Union – NextGenerationEU funds via the Recovery and Resilience Facility (RRF) plan “Greece 2.0”.
    - €59 million in commercial debt financing from Alpha Bank S.A., Eurobank S.A., and National Bank of Greece S.A.
    - €39 million from shareholders’ equity.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date**: 01-08-2023
  - **Milestones**: 
    - Final investment decisions have been made for the construction of the Amynteo Cluster II.
    - Construction is scheduled to start in autumn 2023, with all three projects expected to be fully operational by the end of 2024.
    - The facility is also currently constructing five solar projects (Amynteo Cluster I).

#### 4. Labour
- **Date**: 01-08-2023
  - **Job Creation**: While specific job creation numbers are not mentioned, the construction of multiple large-scale solar projects typically leads to significant job creation in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date**: 01-08-2023
  - **Ownership**: Meton Energy S.A. is a joint venture between RWE Renewables Europe & Australia (51% share) and PPC Renewables (49% share).
