## GRC-01234-05766

### Evolutionary Timeline of the PPC Facility in Western Macedonia, Greece

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2020**: PPC Renewables announced plans for a 200 MW solar project in Western Macedonia, which will be the largest concentration of PV projects under development in Greece. This facility is part of a broader strategy to transition from fossil fuels to renewable energy.
- **January 2023**: PPC and RWE, through their joint venture Meton Energy, decided to develop five solar PV projects with a combined capacity of 210 MWp (175 MWac) in Western Macedonia.
- **August 2023**: The partners announced an additional three solar farms (Amynteo Cluster II) with a total capacity of around 280 MWp (259 MWac) in the same region, bringing the total capacity of projects under development to 490 MW.
- **February 2024**: PPC announced plans for a 550 MW PV plant at a former coal mine in Western Macedonia, which will consist of approximately 950,000 PV panels and is expected to generate 1 TWh of electricity.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2020**: The 200 MW facility in Western Macedonia secured a top-up tariff of €0.04911/kWh, the lowest for a renewables facility in Greece at that time. The indicative budget for the EPC tender was €110.15 million.
- **January 2023**: The total investment for the five solar projects was announced to be €180 million, with €90 million financed by the EU’s NextGenerationEU funds.
- **August 2023**: The investment for the three new solar farms (Amynteo Cluster II) was set at €196 million, with €98 million from the EU’s Recovery and Resilience Facility.
- **February 2024**: The 550 MW PV plant project has a total budget of €368 million, with €184 million from RRF resources and co-financing of €110.4 million from Eurobank and Piraeus Bank.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2020**: PPC Renewables secured contracts for two 15 MW solar projects on the site of the planned 200 MW facility.
- **January 2023**: Final investment decision was made for the five solar PV projects in Western Macedonia, with construction expected to begin shortly.
- **August 2023**: Final investment decisions were made for the three additional solar projects (Amynteo Cluster II), with construction scheduled to start in autumn 2023 and expected to be operational by the end of 2024.
- **February 2024**: Construction plans for the 550 MW PV plant were announced, with commissioning expected by the end of 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2023**: The partnership between RWE and PPC is expected to create jobs in the region as construction begins on the solar projects, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2023**: The joint venture company Meton Energy was established, with RWE holding a 51% stake and PPC Renewables holding 49%. This structure applies to all projects developed under this joint venture, including the solar projects in Western Macedonia.

This timeline captures the significant developments and milestones related to the PPC facility in Western Macedonia, Greece, focusing on its evolution in technology, investment, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
