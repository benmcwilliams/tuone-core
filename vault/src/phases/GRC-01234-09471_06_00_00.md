### Evolutionary Timeline of the Meton Energy Facility in Western Macedonia, Greece

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2023**: Meton Energy, a joint venture between RWE and PPC, announced the development of five solar photovoltaic (PV) projects with a combined capacity of 210MWp (175MWac) in Western Macedonia. Construction was set to begin shortly after the announcement, with operations expected to start by Q2 2024.
- **August 2023**: The joint venture decided to develop an additional 280MW of solar capacity through three solar farms, known as Amynteo Cluster II, with construction expected to start in Autumn 2023 and operations by the end of 2024.
- **February 2024**: Meton Energy secured investment for a new 450MW solar project, Orycheio Dei Amynteo (Amynteo Cluster 3), with construction scheduled to begin soon and commissioning expected by the end of 2025. This brought the total capacity of the Amynteo portfolio to 940MW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2023**: The total investment for the initial five solar projects was €180 million, with €90 million financed by the EU’s NextGenerationEU funds. Additional financing included €54 million in commercial debt and €36 million in shareholders' equity.
- **August 2023**: The investment for the Amynteo Cluster II projects was €196 million, with €98 million from the EU’s Recovery and Resilience Facility, and the rest from commercial debt and shareholders' equity.
- **February 2024**: The Orycheio Dei Amynteo project had a total investment of €255.4 million, with €127.7 million from NextGenerationEU funds, €76.6 million in commercial debt, and €51.1 million in shareholders' equity.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2023**: Final investment decision taken for the first five solar PV projects, with construction expected to start soon.
- **August 2023**: Final investment decision for the Amynteo Cluster II projects, with construction set to begin in Autumn 2023.
- **February 2024**: Final investment decision for the Orycheio Dei Amynteo project, with construction expected to start shortly.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
