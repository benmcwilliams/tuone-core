### Evolutionary Timeline of Cero Development Facility in Thessaly, Greece

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2024**: Cero Development Hellas received approval to add battery energy storage with a capacity of 749 MWh to its existing project for a 370 MW photovoltaic plant located in the municipalities of Domokos and Stylida in Central Greece. This facility, known as the Dristello solar power plant, aims to enhance the efficiency of solar power generation amidst challenges in the Greek electricity grid.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2024**: The Dristello project is part of a broader investment strategy by Cero Development, which includes a 100 MW photovoltaic park in Prosotsani, Drama, valued at EUR 60 million. This project is notable for being covered by Greece's first long-term bilateral power purchase agreement (PPA) for solar power and is also the first renewables project to receive funding from the European Union’s Recovery and Resilience Facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2024**: Cero Development is actively working on multiple solar projects in Greece, including a 269 MW solar power plant with batteries in Malandrino, Phocis, and additional projects of 110 MW and 250 MW in Thessaly. The company has secured licenses for these projects, which are expected to come online by 2026.
