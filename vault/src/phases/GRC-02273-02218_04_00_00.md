### Engie EPS Facility in Agkistro, Greece - Evolutionary Timeline

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2020**: Engie EPS unveiled a hydrogen-based energy storage system in Agkistro, Greece. The facility features a hydrogen “Power-to-Power” system, which includes an electrolyser for converting electricity into hydrogen (Power-to-Gas) and a fuel cell system for converting stored hydrogen back into electricity (Gas-to-Power). The system has a net energy storage capacity equivalent to 500 kWh of hydrogen. This technology aims to support renewable energy storage in isolated and off-grid areas, particularly benefiting a local agri-food processing unit powered by a nearby hydroelectric plant owned by Horizon.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2020**: The project was developed under the EU-H2020 funded initiative titled "Remote area energy supply with multiple options for integrated hydrogen-based technologies." This funding supports the deployment of hydrogen-based technologies in remote areas.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2020**: Engie EPS announced the deployment of its hydrogen-based energy storage solution in Agkistro, marking the completion of the project. The facility is recognized for its innovative approach to renewable energy storage and has been awarded the best renewable energy project in the Innovation section at the European Sustainable Energy Week 2020.
