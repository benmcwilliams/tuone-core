### Evolutionary Timeline of the Terna Energy Facility in Crete, Greece

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 07-05-2021
   - **Details:** Terna Energy announced plans for the Amari facility in Crete, which will be Europe’s largest hybrid power plant. The facility will combine two wind farms with a total capacity of 89.1 MW, a hydroelectric power plant with a capacity of up to 93 MW, and a pump complex of 140 MW, resulting in a total capacity of 322.1 MW. The pumping system will be installed underground at the existing Amari river dam reservoir, featuring three constant speed turbine pumps of 31 MW each and twelve variable speed pumps of 3.2 MW each. The project aims to ensure a constant output of 227 MW, reducing reliance on fossil fuels.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 07-05-2021
   - **Details:** The Amari facility is valued at EUR 280 million. Terna Energy has secured strategic investment status for the project, which is expected to be 70% domestically sourced.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 07-05-2021
   - **Details:** The announcement of the Amari facility marks a significant milestone in Terna Energy's efforts to integrate renewable energy sources in Crete. The project is part of a broader initiative that includes the Amfilochia pumped storage project, which has been recognized as a project of common interest (PCI) by the European Commission.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 07-05-2021
   - **Details:** The construction of the Amari facility is expected to create over 1,000 jobs, with at least 100 permanent positions available once operational.

5. **Ownership Structure:**

   - **Details:** Terna Energy is part of the GEK Terna Group, indicating that the facility is owned by Terna Energy, likely under full ownership as no joint venture or partnership details are mentioned.
