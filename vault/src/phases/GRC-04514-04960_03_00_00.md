## GRC-04514-04960

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Energy and Development Cooperative of Sifnos Facility

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date of Publication: 01-08-2021**
  - The Energy and Development Cooperative of Sifnos is developing a hybrid power plant that combines a wind farm and a pumped storage hydroelectric plant. 
  - The facility will consist of four wind turbines, each with a capacity of 3 MW, totaling 12 MW. 
  - The pumped storage hydroelectric system is proposed to have a capacity of 8.74 MW, designed to store excess energy and provide electricity when wind output is low.
  - The facility aims to produce 100% of the island's electricity from renewable sources, including potential solar power integration.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date of Publication: 01-08-2021**
  - The cooperative has applied for support from the Regulatory Authority for Energy (RAE) of Greece to develop the hybrid power plant.
  - The project is expected to be financially viable, with a payback period of approximately eight years, contingent on government backing.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date of Publication: 01-08-2021**
  - The project is in the planning phase, with a study conducted in collaboration with the Hellenic Mediterranean University of Crete.
  - The facility is designed to meet the entire electricity demand of Sifnos Island, which has less than 3,000 inhabitants.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date of Publication: 01-08-2021**
  - The article does not specify job creation or job loss directly related to the project; however, it implies potential local involvement as residents can become cooperative members.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date of Publication: 01-08-2021**
  - The facility is owned by the Energy and Development Cooperative of Sifnos, which allows local residents to become members with a minimum share of EUR 100. No individual can hold more than 20% of the overall capital, indicating a cooperative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership.
