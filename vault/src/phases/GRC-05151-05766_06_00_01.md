## GRC-05151-05766

### Evolutionary Timeline of the PPC Facility in Amynteo, Greece

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2023**: The Amynteo Cluster II projects, consisting of three solar photovoltaic (PV) parks with a total capacity of 259 MW, were announced. These projects will be built on the site of the former Amynteo opencast lignite mine. The construction is set to begin in autumn 2023, with operations expected to start by the end of 2024. This follows the earlier development of the Amynteo Cluster I, which comprises five solar projects totaling 175 MW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2023**: The total investment for the Amynteo Cluster II is €196 million. This investment will be financed through a combination of €98 million from the EU's NextGenerationEU fund, €59 million in commercial debt financing from Alpha Bank, Eurobank, and the National Bank of Greece, and €39 million in equity from shareholders.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2023**: The final investment decision for the Amynteo Cluster II was made, and construction is scheduled to commence in autumn 2023. The three solar parks are expected to be operational by the end of 2024. The projects are part of a larger initiative where Meton Energy, a joint venture between RWE and PPC, is developing solar farms with a total capacity of up to 2 GW in Greece.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is operated as a joint venture between RWE Renewables and PPC Renewables, known as Meton Energy. Both companies have contributed projects to the joint venture's pipeline, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by either company.
