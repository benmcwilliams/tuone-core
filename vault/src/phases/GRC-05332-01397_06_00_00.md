## GRC-05332-01397

### Evolutionary Timeline of the juwi Facility in Kozani, Greece

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 18-02-2020
   - **Details:** The facility is a utility-scale solar project with a production capacity of 204.3 MWp. The project was initially acquired by juwi in 2017, and after securing necessary permits and approvals, it was sold to Hellenic Petroleum (HELPE) in early 2020. juwi will continue to act as the Engineering, Procurement, and Construction (EPC) and Operations & Maintenance (O&M) contractor for a period of 20 years (10 years each for EPC and O&M). The project is set to break ground in July or August 2020 and is expected to be completed by the end of 2021.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 18-02-2020
   - **Details:** Hellenic Petroleum (HELPE) will invest €130 million (approximately US$140.7 million) into the Kozani PV plant as part of the acquisition deal. The project is also supported by feed-in premium tariffs of €57.73/MWh (US$62.45/MWh) for 20 years, awarded during a technology-neutral auction in April 2019.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 18-02-2020
   - **Details:** The sale of the 204.3 MWp project from juwi to HELPE was announced, with the transaction expected to complete in Q2 2020. The project is scheduled to commence construction in July or August 2020 and aims for completion by the end of 2021.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 18-02-2020
   - **Details:** The project is expected to create approximately 300 jobs, as highlighted by Greece’s Environment and Energy Minister Kostis Hatzidakis during the announcement of the sale.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 18-02-2020
   - **Details:** juwi will not retain any ownership shares in the facility after the sale to Hellenic Petroleum (HELPE), indicating that HELPE will have full ownership of the facility. However, juwi will remain involved as the EPC and O&M contractor.
