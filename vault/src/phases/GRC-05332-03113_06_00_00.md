## GRC-05332-03113

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Juwi Facility in Kozani, Greece

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2020**: Juwi began construction of a 204 MW solar park in Kozani, which is the largest single project for the company to date. The facility will utilize bifacial solar panels and is expected to generate approximately 300 million kilowatt-hours annually, enough to power 75,000 households. The solar park will cover an area of 450 hectares.

- **April 2022**: The 204 MW solar plant was commissioned, making it Europe’s largest bifacial solar park. The facility consists of over 500,000 bifacial modules provided by JinkoSolar and was built in 18 months on complex terrain.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2020**: The total investment for the solar park was reported at €130 million, secured through a tender held by the Greek government.

- **April 2022**: The European Bank for Reconstruction and Development (EBRD) provided three-quarters of the funding for a €100 million Eurobond issued by Hellenic Petroleum for the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2019**: Juwi secured a contract in a tender held by the Greek government for the project, which accounted for nearly half of the total awarded capacity of 437 MW.

- **November 2020**: Construction of the solar park commenced.

- **April 2022**: The solar park was officially commissioned.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the Juwi facility in Kozani.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2020**: Juwi sold the project to Hellenic Petroleum, which is the current owner of the facility. Therefore, Juwi does not exclusively own the facility; it is owned by Hellenic Petroleum.
