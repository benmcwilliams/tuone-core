## GRC-05332-05766

### Evolutionary Timeline of the PPC Facility in Kozani, Greece

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2019**: PPC planned a 200 MW solar plant in Kozani but did not secure a tariff in Greece’s first joint PV-wind tender, missing out due to not splitting the capacity as competitors did.
- **July 2020**: PPC Renewables announced a future 230 MW photovoltaic complex in the Kozani area, divided into three parts, with a 200 MW unit still to select a contractor.
- **December 2020**: PPC Renewables initiated plans for floating solar power plants totaling 50 MW, with the first project to be located on Polyfytos Lake in Kozani.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2020**: The total budget for the 230 MW solar power complex in Kozani was reported to be EUR 131.5 million, with contracts signed for the construction of 15 MW solar parks worth EUR 10 million and EUR 11.5 million for additional units.
- **December 2020**: PPC earmarked EUR 3.4 billion for investments through 2023, with 34% allocated for renewable energy projects, including the floating solar initiative.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2019**: PPC's bid for the 200 MW solar plant in Kozani was unsuccessful in the tender.
- **July 2020**: Works on the first section of the 230 MW photovoltaic complex were scheduled to begin.
- **December 2020**: Applications for 650 MW of new photovoltaic plants were submitted, including the floating solar project in Kozani.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2020**: The construction of the solar power plants is expected to create jobs, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: PPC Renewables, a subsidiary of the Public Power Corporation (PPC), is responsible for the projects in Kozani, indicating that PPC retains full ownership of the facility and its developments. 

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic moves of the PPC facility in Kozani, highlighting its focus on expanding renewable energy capabilities amidst a transition from traditional energy sources.
