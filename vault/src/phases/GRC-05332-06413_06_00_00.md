### Evolutionary Timeline of the juwi Hellas Facility in Kozani, Greece

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** April 2021
   - **Details:** The juwi Hellas facility is constructing the Kozani solar park, which is set to be one of Europe’s largest bifacial solar parks. The facility will utilize bifacial photovoltaic (PV) modules that can generate electricity from both the front and back, enhancing efficiency. Upon completion, the park is expected to produce over 300 million kWh of electricity annually, sufficient to power 75,000 households in Greece.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** April 2021
   - **Details:** The total investment for the Kozani solar park is €136 million. This investment supports the development of the facility and the installation of nearly half a million PV modules.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** April 2021
   - **Details:** The construction process has reached a significant milestone with the installation of the first rows of PV modules. The facility is on schedule to be connected to the grid by February 2022, after which it will be handed over to the future operator, Hellenic Petroleum Group.

4. **Labour:**
