## GRC-05332-09133

### Evolutionary Timeline of Hellenic Petroleum Facility in Kozani, Greece

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2020**: Hellenic Petroleum (HELPE) acquires a 204.3 MWp photovoltaic (PV) project from German developer juwi, marking it as Greece's largest renewable energy venture to date. The project is located near Kozani and is set to utilize either mono- or bifacial solar modules. Construction is expected to begin in July or August 2020 and be completed by the end of 2021.
- **April 2022**: Juwi announces the commissioning of the 204 MW solar plant, which consists of more than 500,000 bifacial PV modules. This facility is noted as Europe’s largest bifacial solar park and is a significant step in the region's transition away from lignite.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2020**: Hellenic Petroleum commits to invest €130 million (US$140.7 million) in the Kozani PV plant.
- **October 2020**: The European Bank for Reconstruction and Development (EBRD) announces it will provide €75 million for the project as part of a €100 million Eurobond reissue by Hellenic Petroleum, aimed at financing 204 MW of solar capacity in the coal mining region.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2020**: The acquisition of the 204.3 MWp project is finalized, with construction expected to start in mid-2020.
- **April 2022**: The solar park is officially commissioned, marking the completion of the project.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2020**: The project is expected to create approximately 300 jobs during construction, with additional positions anticipated during the operational phase, primarily sourced from the local community.
- **October 2020**: The EBRD reiterates that the solar facilities will create 300 construction jobs and “dozens” of operational jobs.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2020**: Hellenic Petroleum acquires full ownership of the Kozani solar project from juwi, which will remain as the EPC (Engineering, Procurement, and Construction) and O&M (Operations and Maintenance) contractor for a period of 20 years (10 years each for EPC and O&M).
