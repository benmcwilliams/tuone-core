## GRC-05417-05766

### Evolutionary Timeline of the PPC Facility in Megalopoli, Greece

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2020**: PPC Renewables, a division of the Public Power Corporation (PPC), announced plans to develop a **50 MW solar plant** in the Megalopoli coal mining region of the Peloponnese peninsula. This facility is intended to be Greece's first **subsidy-free PV project**. The electricity generated will be sold to the parent company, PPC.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2020**: The project is part of PPC's broader strategy to pivot towards renewable energy, moving away from a fossil fuel-driven business model. The purchase tariff for the electricity generated at the Megalopoli facility was still under negotiation at the time of the announcement.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2020**: PPC Renewables secured tenders for the development of the largest solar cluster in Greece, which includes the 50 MW solar plant in Megalopoli. This project marks a significant milestone as it represents the company's first step into unsubsidized renewable energy generation.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2020**: While specific job creation figures were not mentioned in the articles, the establishment of the 50 MW solar plant is expected to contribute to job creation in the renewable energy sector as PPC transitions from coal to solar energy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2020**: The facility will be developed by PPC Renewables, which is a subsidiary of the state-owned Public Power Corporation (PPC). This indicates that PPC has **full ownership** of the facility, as it is being developed under its renewables division.

This timeline captures the key developments related to the PPC facility in Megalopoli, Greece, focusing on its transition towards renewable energy and the establishment of the solar plant.
