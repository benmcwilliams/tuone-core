## GRC-06094-01998

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Terna Energy Facility in North Eastern Greece, GRC

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Established**: Terna Energy was established in 1997 and has become a leader in Greece’s wind energy sector.
- **Current Production Capacity**: The facility owns approximately 1.2 GW of operating onshore wind power capacity, with the Kafireas project on Evia Island being its largest at 330 MW.
- **Solar Capacity**: Terna Energy operates 8.5 MW of solar capacity and is in the process of building an additional 101 MW of photovoltaic (PV) capacity in Greece. The company has a pipeline of 2 GW of solar projects for which it is seeking licenses.
- **Energy Storage**: The company is constructing the Amphilochia pumped hydroelectric energy storage project, which will have a total installed capacity of 680 MW for production and 730 MW for pumping, making it Greece’s largest grid-connected energy storage investment.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment in Energy Storage**: The Amphilochia project is expected to exceed €500 million. Terna Energy has submitted a €120 million loan request to the European Investment Bank for this project.
- **Public Subsidy Support**: In February 2024, Terna Energy was awarded contracts in Greece’s second energy storage tender, which includes a 40 MW project and a 12 MW installation under its Heron subsidiary, receiving public subsidy support for capital and operational expenditure.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Acquisition Announcement**: On June 24, 2024, it was announced that Masdar signed a definitive agreement to acquire initially 67% of Terna Energy's outstanding shares for €3.2 billion, marking a significant milestone in the energy sector.
- **Research Licenses**: In November 2023, Terna Energy was granted one of two research licenses by the Greek government for developing offshore wind power farms in North Eastern Greece.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The articles do not specify exact numbers regarding job creation or loss as a result of the investments or expansions mentioned.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership Change**: As of June 2024, Masdar is set to acquire 67% of Terna Energy, with plans to launch a mandatory tender offer to acquire 100% of the shares, indicating a shift from Terna Energy's previous ownership structure to a majority ownership by Masdar.
