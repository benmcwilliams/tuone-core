### Evolutionary Timeline of the Deutsche Werte Holding Facility in Xanthi, Greece

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** May 2019
   - **Details:** Deutsche Werte Holding (DWH) has become a shareholder in Maximus Terra, a Greek PV specialist. The facility in Xanthi is part of a 200MW solar pipeline, which is split into two 100MW projects (Xanthi and Florina). The expected annual turnover from every 25MW of solar is approximately €2 million (US$2.23 million). DWH is considering either realizing short-term profits or constructing and maintaining the PV parks, which could unlock around €340 million (US$379.75 million) in turnover.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** May 2019
   - **Details:** DWH's investment in Maximus Terra allows them to access a portfolio of 1.36GW worth of renewable assets, which includes wind, biomass, and biogas plants. The investment is aimed at deploying the solar pipeline, particularly the Xanthi project.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** May 2019
   - **Details:** DWH announced its acquisition of a stake in Maximus Terra and indicated that the Xanthi solar project would break ground "very soon." The project is part of a broader strategy to capitalize on attractive conditions in the Greek renewable market.
