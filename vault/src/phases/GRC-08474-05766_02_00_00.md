## GRC-08474-05766

### Evolutionary Timeline of the PPC Facility in Methana, Greece

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 28-06-2020
   - **Details:** PPC Renewables, a subsidiary of the state energy company PPC, is focusing on the utilization of geothermal potential in Methana, among other locations. The facility will produce geothermal energy with a planned capacity of 5 MW in Methana, as part of a broader project that includes three other geothermal fields (Lesvos, Milos-Kimolos-Polyaigos, and Nisyros). The power plants will be supplied with geothermal fluid from drilling operations.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 28-06-2020
   - **Details:** PPC Renewables has partnered with ELECTOR of the ELLAKTOR group to form a special purpose public limited company named “Geothermal Target Two – Single Person SA.” This partnership will involve financing, construction, and management of the geothermal power plants. The strategic partner (ELECTOR) will hold a majority percentage in the special purpose company.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 28-06-2020
   - **Details:** The project is in the decisive stage of utilizing geothermal fields, with plans to start exploratory drilling in the geothermal field of Milos by the end of 2020. The goal is to have the power plant operational by 2025.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 28-06-2020
   - **Details:** The article does not specify job creation or job loss directly related to the Methana facility, but the construction and operation of geothermal plants typically involve local labor for drilling and maintenance.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 28-06-2020
   - **Details:** PPC Renewables retains ownership of the exploitation contracts for the geothermal fields, while the strategic partner ELECTOR will manage the special purpose company. This indicates a joint venture structure for the Methana facility, with PPC maintaining significant control over the operations.
