### Evolutionary Timeline of the PPC Facility in Milos, Greece

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 28-06-2020
   - **Details:** PPC Renewables, a subsidiary of the state energy company PPC, is set to utilize geothermal potential in Milos as part of a project involving four geothermal fields (Milos-Kimolos-Polyaigos, Lesvos, Nisyros, and Methana). The facility will produce geothermal energy with a planned capacity of 5 MW for Milos. The goal is to start exploratory drilling by the end of 2020, with the power plant expected to be operational by 2025.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 28-06-2020
   - **Details:** PPC Renewables has established a special purpose public limited company named “Geothermal Target Two – Single Person SA” to manage the financing, construction, and operation of the geothermal power plants. The strategic partner, ELECTOR, will hold a majority stake in this company.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 28-06-2020
   - **Details:** The international tender for selecting a strategic partner for the geothermal projects was completed, with ELECTOR awarded the position. The first exploratory drilling in Milos is scheduled to begin by the end of 2020, and the power plant is expected to be operational by 2025.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 28-06-2020
   - **Details:** The article does not specify job creation or job loss directly related to the Milos facility, but the construction and operation of the geothermal power plant are likely to create jobs in the local community.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 28-06-2020
   - **Details:** PPC Renewables retains ownership of the exploitation contracts for the geothermal fields, while the special purpose company, in which ELECTOR holds a majority stake, will manage the financing and construction of the power plants. Thus, the facility is not exclusively owned by PPC but operates under a joint venture structure with ELECTOR.
