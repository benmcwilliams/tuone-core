## GRC-08475-08679

### Evolutionary Timeline of the ELECTOR Facility in Milos, Greece

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** June 2020
   - **Details:** The ELECTOR facility, part of the ELLAKTOR group, is set to engage in the energy utilization of geothermal fields, specifically focusing on the Milos-Kimolos-Polyaigos geothermal field. The facility will produce geothermal energy with a planned capacity of 5 MW for Milos, alongside other geothermal fields (8 MW in Lesvos and 5 MW in Nisyros and Methana). The goal is to start exploratory drilling in Milos by the end of 2020, with the power plant expected to be operational by 2025.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** June 2020
   - **Details:** PPC Renewables has established a special purpose public limited company, “Geothermal Target Two – Single Person SA,” to manage the financing, construction, and operation of the geothermal power plants. The strategic partnership with ELECTOR involves a majority stake in this special purpose company, indicating significant investment in the geothermal projects.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** June 2020
   - **Details:** The announcement of the partnership between PPC Renewables and ELECTOR marks a significant milestone in the project. The first exploratory drilling in the geothermal field of Milos is scheduled to begin by the end of 2020, with the aim of having the power plant operational by 2025.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** June 2020
   - **Details:** While specific job creation numbers are not mentioned in the article, the establishment of the special purpose company and the planned construction and operation of the geothermal power plants are expected to create jobs in the region, particularly during the construction phase and ongoing operations.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** June 2020
   - **Details:** The facility is not exclusively owned by ELECTOR. Instead, it operates as a strategic partner with PPC Renewables, which retains ownership of the exploitation contracts. The special purpose company will have ELECTOR entering with a majority percentage, indicating a joint venture structure for the project.
