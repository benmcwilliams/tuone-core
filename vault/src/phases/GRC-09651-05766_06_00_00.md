### Evolutionary Timeline of the PPC Facility in Ptolemaida, Greece

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2020**: PPC Renewables, a division of the Public Power Corporation (PPC), is developing a 200 MW solar project in the Ptolemaida region, which is part of a larger plan to install 2 GW of solar capacity on former coal mines in the area. This facility is part of a broader initiative to transition from lignite to solar energy, aligning with Greece's commitment to phase out coal by 2028.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2020**: The 200 MW solar project in Ptolemaida is part of a larger investment strategy by PPC, which includes securing a €0.04911/kWh top-up tariff for the energy generated. The indicative budget for the engineering, procurement, and construction (EPC) tender for this project is set at €110.15 million, excluding VAT.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2020**: PPC Renewables secured contracts for two smaller 15 MW solar projects on the same site where the 200 MW facility is planned. The smaller projects will receive premium tariffs of €0.06488 and €0.05382/kWh, respectively, under 20-year contracts. The EPC tender for the first of the 15 MW facilities is currently being evaluated, with the second plant's bidding window closing on April 28, 2020.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2020**: While specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the article, the development of the 200 MW solar facility and associated projects is expected to create jobs in the construction and operational phases, contributing to local employment in the Ptolemaida region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2020**: The facility is owned by PPC, a state-owned electric company, indicating full ownership of the facility by the parent company. PPC Renewables operates as a subsidiary focused on renewable energy projects.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic shifts at the PPC facility in Ptolemaida, highlighting its transition towards renewable energy and the establishment of significant solar capacity in the region.
