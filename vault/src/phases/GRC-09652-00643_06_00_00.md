## GRC-09652-00643

### Evolutionary Timeline of the RWE Facility in West Macedonia, Greece

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date: 07-10-2021**: RWE, through its subsidiary RWE Renewables, announced a joint venture with PPC Renewables to co-develop renewable energy projects in Greece, specifically targeting 2 GW of new photovoltaic (PV) generation capacity. The initial focus is on nine solar projects located in the former Amyntaio open pit lignite mine in West Macedonia, with a combined generation capacity of up to 940 MW (870 MWac). The projects are in various stages of development, with operations expected to start between 2023 and 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date: 07-10-2021**: The joint venture is expected to participate in future public tenders in Greece, which offer premium payments under 20-year contracts. The PPC has already secured contracts for three solar farms totaling 230 MW in previous rounds of Greece's renewables tenders, indicating a supportive investment environment for the facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date: 07-10-2021**: The announcement of the joint venture marks a significant milestone in RWE's entry into the Greek power market, confirming the development of solar projects in the West Macedonia region. The projects are part of a broader strategy to phase out coal from electricity generation in Greece by 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date: 07-10-2021**: While specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the announcement, the establishment of the joint venture and the development of solar projects are expected to contribute positively to local employment in the renewable energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date: 07-10-2021**: RWE holds a 51% stake in the joint venture with PPC Renewables, which means that RWE does not own the facility exclusively but is in a partnership with the Greek utility, PPC, which holds the remaining 49%.
