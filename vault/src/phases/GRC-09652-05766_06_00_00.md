### Evolutionary Timeline of the PPC Facility in West Macedonia, Greece

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **07-10-2021**: The PPC facility, through a joint venture with RWE, is set to co-develop solar projects in West Macedonia, specifically in the former Amyntaio open pit lignite mine. The facility will contribute to a total of 2 GW of new photovoltaic (PV) generation capacity, with PPC contributing nine solar projects totaling 940 MW (870 MWac). The projects are in various stages of development, with operations expected to start between 2023 and 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07-10-2021**: The joint venture between RWE (holding 51%) and PPC (holding 49%) aims to leverage both companies' strengths for the development of renewable energy projects. The PPC has previously secured contracts for three solar farms with a total capacity of 230 MW, which are also located in the West Macedonia region. These projects have received premium top-up payments under Greece's renewables tenders.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **07-10-2021**: The announcement of the joint venture marks a significant milestone in the development of renewable energy in Greece, particularly in the transition from coal to solar energy. The projects are expected to begin operations from 2023 to 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **07-10-2021**: While specific job creation numbers are not mentioned, the establishment of the joint venture and the development of solar projects are expected to contribute positively to local employment in the renewable energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **07-10-2021**: The facility operates under a joint venture structure, with RWE holding 51% ownership and PPC holding 49%. This indicates a collaborative approach to the development and operation of the facility, combining resources and expertise from both companies.
