## GRC-09872-04229

### Evolutionary Timeline of the SPI Solar Facility in Evros, Greece

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2011**: SPI Solar announced the development of a 4.4MW fixed-ground-mount solar power plant in Evros, Greece. This facility is SPI's first project in the country and will utilize LDK Solar’s PV modules. 
- **January 2012**: SPI Solar announced a second project in the Evros region, a 2MW facility, bringing the total capacity under development in Greece to 7.4MW within a year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2011**: The 4.4MW project in Evros was acquired by SPI Solar through a 100% limited partnership interest, which was already in early-phase planning with another entity. The Greek Public Power Corporation (PPC) signed a 20-year power purchase agreement (PPA) with SPI for this project.
- **February 2012**: The 2MW facility in Evros also secured a 20-year PPA with the Greek Public Power Corporation.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2011**: Construction for the 4.4MW solar power plant in Evros was scheduled to begin.
- **January 2012**: Announcement of the 2MW facility in Evros, marking continued expansion in the region.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2012**: SPI Solar indicated that local jobs would be created during the construction of the facilities in Greece, contributing to the local economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2011**: SPI Solar acquired a 100% limited partnership interest in the 4.4MW project, indicating full ownership of the facility. The 2MW facility is also expected to be owned and operated by private investors within Greece.
