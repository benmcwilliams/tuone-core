### Evolutionary Timeline of Rimac Automobili Facility in Zagreb, Croatia

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05/2018**: Rimac Automobili partners with Seat to develop the CUPRA e-Racer, an electric touring-class race car. The vehicle features four electric motors generating 300 kW, with a peak of up to 500 kW, capable of accelerating to 270 km/h and sprinting from 0 to 100 km/h in 3.2 seconds. This partnership marks Rimac's transition from supercar manufacturing to becoming a leading EV component supplier.
- **04/2021**: Rimac announces plans for a new state-of-the-art campus in Zagreb, which will include a manufacturing facility for electric sports cars and batteries. The campus will cover 200,000 square meters, with 100,000 square meters built on, and will feature an assembly line and a test track.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04/2021**: The new campus is projected to cost €200 million. This investment is aimed at enhancing Rimac's capabilities as a hypercar manufacturer and a Tier 1 provider of electric performance technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04/2021**: Rimac reveals the design and project plans for the new campus, with construction scheduled to begin in 2021 and operations expected to start in 2023. The facility will combine development, administration, and production.

#### 4. Labour
- **04/2021**: The new Rimac Campus is expected to create over 2,500 jobs, significantly increasing employment opportunities in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **05/2018**: Porsche acquires a 10% minority stake in Rimac Automobili, which is later increased to 24% in March 2021. This indicates that Rimac is not exclusively owned by a single entity but has significant investment from Porsche, alongside other stakeholders like Hyundai.
