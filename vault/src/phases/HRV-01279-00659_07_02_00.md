### Evolutionary Timeline of Rimac Automobili Facility in Zagreb, Croatia

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2018**: Rimac Automobili focuses on high-voltage battery technology, electric powertrains, and HMI Development. The company also produces e-bikes under its subsidiary Greyp Bikes.
- **April 2021**: Rimac announces plans for a new state-of-the-art campus in Zagreb, which will include an assembly line for electric sports cars and a production facility for batteries. The campus will cover 200,000 square meters, with 100,000 square meters built on.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2021**: The new campus is projected to cost around 200 million euros. The facility aims for climate-neutral production, with construction scheduled to begin in 2021 and operations expected to start in 2023.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2018**: Porsche acquires a 10% minority stake in Rimac, marking the beginning of a development partnership.
- **July 2018**: Rimac collaborates with Seat to develop the CUPRA e-Racer, showcasing their technology in electric vehicle racing.
- **April 2021**: Rimac unveils plans for a new campus in Zagreb, with construction set to begin in 2021 and operations to commence in 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2021**: The new Rimac Campus is expected to employ over 2,500 people, significantly increasing job opportunities in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2018**: Rimac Automobili is partially owned by Porsche, which holds a 10% stake. By March 2021, Porsche increased its stake to 24%, indicating a growing partnership. The facility is not exclusively owned by Rimac due to this joint ownership structure with Porsche.
