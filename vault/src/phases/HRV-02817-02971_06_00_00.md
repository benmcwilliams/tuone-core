## HRV-02817-02971

### Evolutionary Timeline of the HEP Facility in Cres, HRV

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05/2018**: HEP announced plans to construct a 6.5 MW solar PV plant named Orlec Trinket on the island of Cres. This facility will be the largest solar installation in Croatia, as previous projects were limited to a maximum of 1 MW. The plant is expected to produce approximately 8.5 GWh of electricity annually, sufficient to meet the needs of around 2,000 local households.
- **12/2019**: The Orlec Trinket East project is confirmed to consist of 13 segments of 500 kW each, covering a total area of 17 hectares. The project is still awaiting the start of construction.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05/2018**: The total investment for the Orlec Trinket project is estimated at approximately HRK 45 million (around $7 million). HEP will finance the project without any subsidies, having conducted a cost-effectiveness analysis that deemed it a sound investment.
- **12/2019**: The project remains valued at HRK 45 million, with plans for the Orlec Trinket West project to add an additional 4.14 MW of capacity.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05/2018**: HEP signed an agreement with Primorje-Gorski Kotar County to develop the 6.5 MW solar PV plant on Cres. This marks the beginning of HEP's venture into ground-mounted solar power plants.
- **12/2019**: Local authorities are still awaiting the start of construction for the Orlec Trinket project, which is expected to bring the total solar capacity on the island to over 10 MW once both the Orlec Trinket East and West projects are completed.

#### 4. Labour
- **05/2018**: The construction of the Orlec Trinket solar plant is anticipated to create jobs, although specific numbers regarding job creation or loss have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **05/2018**: HEP is the sole owner of the Orlec Trinket facility, as it is a state-run utility. The land for the project has been leased from the church, which owns the land on which the plant will be constructed.
