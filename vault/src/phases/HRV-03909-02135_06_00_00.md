## HRV-03909-02135

### Evolutionary Timeline of Acciona Energy Facility in Sibenik-Knin, Croatia

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05/2024**: Acciona Energía is developing the **189MW Promina photovoltaic plant** in Sibenik-Knin, Croatia. This facility will be the largest solar plant in the country upon its commissioning in **2027**. It will consist of over **300,000 photovoltaic modules** generating **320GWh** of clean electricity annually, sufficient to power approximately **69,000 homes** and prevent the emission of around **140,000 tonnes of CO2** per year. Additionally, a **400kV interconnection substation** will be constructed at the site to connect the facility to the national grid.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05/2024**: Acciona Energía secured a **12-year Contract for Difference (CfD)** with the Croatian energy regulator, Hrvatski Operator Trzista Energije (HROTE), as part of a competitive public auction. This contract is part of a broader support program approved by the European Commission aimed at accelerating renewable energy deployment in Croatia.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05/2024**: The Promina solar project is currently under construction, with a completion target set for **2027**. The project is part of Acciona's broader strategy in Croatia, which includes additional wind projects (Opor at **27MW**, Boraja II at **45MW**, and the Jelinak wind complex at **30MW**), which have been operational since **2013**.

### Summary
Acciona Energía is advancing the **189MW Promina photovoltaic plant** in Sibenik-Knin, Croatia, which will be the largest solar facility in the country upon its completion in **2027**. The project is supported by a **12-year CfD** from HROTE and includes the construction of a **400kV interconnection substation**. Acciona aims to significantly contribute to Croatia's renewable energy capacity, targeting around **8%** of the country's total installed clean energy capacity upon completion of this and other projects.
