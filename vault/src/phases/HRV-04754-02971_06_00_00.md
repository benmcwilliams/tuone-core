## HRV-04754-02971

### Evolutionary Timeline of the HEP Facility in Vis, Croatia

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2020**: The solar power plant (SPP) in Vis was commissioned with a capacity of **3.5 MW**. It can produce **5 GWh** of electricity per year. At the time of commissioning, it was the largest solar farm in Croatia.
- **2021**: The facility integrated the **biggest battery storage system** in Croatia, with **1 MW** operational power and **1.44 MWh** capacity, allowing it to operate at full capacity for approximately **1 hour and 26 minutes**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2021**: The solar power plant was developed as a **zero-subsidy project** by Hrvatska elektroprivreda (HEP Group).
- **2023**: An investment of **EUR 2.3 million** was made for the integration of the battery storage system with the solar power plant.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2020**: The Vis solar power plant was commissioned.
- **July 2021**: The City of Vis signed a letter of intent with the H2 association to develop a hydrogen filling station, marking the beginning of plans to produce green hydrogen using electricity from the solar power plant.
- **2023**: Plans for the hydrogen filling station were submitted for public funding, with a target to build and commission the station by **2025**.

#### 4. Labour
- **2023**: The City of Vis plans to spend **EUR 6.7 million** on utility vehicles and vessels that use hydrogen, which may lead to job creation in the local economy related to the hydrogen project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned exclusively by **Hrvatska elektroprivreda (HEP Group)**, a state-controlled energy company.
