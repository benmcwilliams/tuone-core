### Crosco Facility in Bjelovar, Croatia - Evolutionary Timeline

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2023**: The Crosco facility is set to develop the first geothermal district heating project in Croatia. The facility aims to utilize geothermal energy to heat the business zone Veliko Korenovo, with expectations of finding water at a depth of up to 1,500 meters with temperatures between 70-80 degrees Celsius. 
- **March 2024**: Drilling of well Koronevo GT-1 was completed as scheduled, with an expected thermal energy output of approximately 6 MW. This capacity is sufficient to supply heat to seven hectares of greenhouses or over 400 apartments. Plans for five additional boreholes are also in place.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2023**: The project is set to receive funding from the “Energy and Climate Change” programme of the Ministry of Regional Development and EU funds, supported by the Kingdom of Norway.
- **March 2024**: A EUR 3.5 million drilling contract was awarded to Crosco for the completion of well Koronevo GT-1.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Early 2022**: The initiative to develop geothermal district heating in Bjelovar was first reported.
- **End of 2022**: An update indicated that feasibility studies were underway for the planned district heating project.
- **November 2023**: Drilling work for well Koronevo GT-1 commenced.
- **March 2024**: Testing was completed on the geothermal wells, confirming the project's progress and viability.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2024**: The Mayor of Bjelovar, Dario Hrebak, expressed expectations that the positive outcome of the drilling would attract new investments to the region, potentially leading to job creation in the area, particularly in agriculture and greenhouse production.
