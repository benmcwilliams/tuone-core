### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hrvatska elektroprivreda Facility in Benkovac, HRV

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** October 2019
   - **Details:** Hrvatska elektroprivreda (HEP) began construction of the Korlat wind facility near Benkovac, which will have a production capacity of 58MW. The facility will consist of 18 Nordex turbines, each with a capacity of 3.6MW. The annual production is estimated at 170 gigawatt hours, which accounts for approximately 1% of Croatia's annual electricity consumption. The facility is scheduled for commissioning in August 2020.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** October 2019
   - **Details:** The project is part of HEP's broader renewable energy development strategy, which aims to increase total production capacity by 1500MW by 2030, with a significant portion coming from wind and solar power plants. The return on investment for the Korlat wind farm is based on electricity wholesale market prices, as it will be the first new wind farm in Croatia to produce electricity without guaranteed purchase at an incentive price.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** October 2019
   - **Details:** Construction of the Korlat wind facility commenced, with a scheduled commissioning date set for August 2020. The project is significant as it marks the largest renewable energy plant of its kind in Croatia.
