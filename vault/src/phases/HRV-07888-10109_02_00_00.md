### Evolutionary Timeline of the Star Energy Facility in Ernestinovo, HRV

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **August 2023:** The facility is planned to develop a 10-MW geothermal power plant utilizing Organic Rankine Cycle (ORC) technology. The Ernestinovo geothermal license area covers 76 square kilometers in the Pannonian Basin. Three deep exploration wells have been drilled, and 2D seismic data has been collected and processed. The development strategy includes four producing wells and two injection wells, with plans to re-enter two abandoned oil and gas wells.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **August 2023:** Star Energy (formerly IGas Energy plc) acquired the Croatian geothermal developer GT Energy for EUR 1.3 million. Additional costs include EUR 0.1 million for guarantees to the Croatian Hydrocarbon Agency and EUR 0.2 million for ongoing appraisal expenses related to the Ernestinovo license area. This acquisition aligns with Star Energy's strategy to become a geothermal developer, owner, and operator, focusing on electricity generation.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **August 2023:** The acquisition of GT Energy and the strategic shareholders’ agreement with Peninsula International Pte Ltd for joint development, construction, and operation of geothermal projects in Croatia were announced. Joint bids for three licenses were submitted, with results expected by December 2023.

4. **Labour:**
   - **August 2023:** The acquisition and planned development of the facility are expected to bring onboard an experienced team of geological and geothermal professionals, although specific job creation or loss figures were not detailed in the article.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **August 2023:** Star Energy, through its acquisition of GT Energy, is moving towards full ownership of the facility, although it has entered into a strategic shareholders’ agreement with Peninsula International Pte Ltd for joint development efforts.
