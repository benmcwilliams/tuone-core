### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hrvatska elektroprivreda Facility in Kaštelir, HRV

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2019**: Hrvatska elektroprivreda (HEP) announced plans to expand its solar power generation portfolio, which includes the construction of a solar power plant in Kaštelir. The facility is part of a larger initiative to add a total of 350 MW of solar capacity by 2030. The initial construction will involve four photovoltaic (PV) plants with a combined capacity of 11.6 MW, including the one in Kaštelir.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2019**: HEP plans to invest approximately HRK 80 million ($12.1 million) for the construction of the four PV plants, with an annual investment of HRK 150 million in solar projects until 2023. This is part of a broader investment strategy aiming for a total of around HRK 750 million in solar capacity additions.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2019**: The construction contract for the solar project in Kaštelir has been awarded to a consortium consisting of PVI Solar, PVI GmbH, Intecco, and Deling. The project is part of HEP's goal to increase its renewable energy quota from 35% to 50% by 2030.

#### 4. Labour
- **09/2019**: While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned, the construction of the solar plants is expected to contribute to local employment opportunities during the construction phase and potentially in ongoing operations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **09/2019**: The facility in Kaštelir is owned exclusively by Hrvatska elektroprivreda (HEP), a state-owned utility company in Croatia.
