### Evolutionary Timeline of the BMW Facility in Debrecen, Hungary

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2018**: BMW announced plans to construct a factory in Debrecen with an annual production capacity of 150,000 vehicles, capable of manufacturing both combustion and new energy vehicles.
- **March 2022**: BMW revealed that the first models of the ‘New Class’ electric cars, including an SUV and a sedan, are planned for production starting in 2025 at the Debrecen facility.
- **May 2022**: BMW introduced the ‘iFactory’ strategy, aiming for the Debrecen plant to be the world’s first car plant to operate without fossil fuels in its production processes.
- **June 2022**: The foundation stone for the factory was laid, emphasizing its capacity of 150,000 units per year and its status as a CO2-free manufacturing site.
- **November 2022**: BMW announced the construction of assembly lines for high-voltage batteries at the Debrecen plant, which will begin production in parallel with the vehicle production of the New Class in 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2018**: BMW committed to investing approximately 1 billion euros in the Debrecen facility.
- **June 2022**: BMW confirmed that the total investment in the construction and commissioning of the entire plant would exceed 2 billion euros by the end of 2025.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2018**: Announcement of the factory's construction plans.
- **June 2022**: Laying of the foundation stone for the factory.
- **November 2022**: Start of construction for high-voltage battery assembly lines.
- **2025**: Planned start of series production for the first all-electric New Class vehicle.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2018**: BMW projected the creation of around 1,000 jobs at the factory, with additional jobs expected from suppliers and service providers.
- **November 2022**: An additional 500 jobs are expected to be created in the new high-voltage battery assembly by 2025.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility in Debrecen is owned exclusively by BMW, as indicated by the company's direct investment and operational plans for the site.
