## HUN-00249-00120

### Evolutionary Timeline of the CATL Facility in Debrecen, Hungary

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/2022**: CATL announced plans to build a 100 GWh battery plant in Debrecen, Hungary, which will be its second battery cell manufacturing facility in Europe. The facility will cover an area of 221 hectares in the Southern Industrial Park and will produce battery cells and modules for European automakers, including Mercedes-Benz, BMW, Stellantis, and Volkswagen.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12/2022**: CATL revealed an investment of €7.34 billion ($7.6 billion) for the construction of the facility, marking it as the largest greenfield investment in Hungary's history. The investment is part of CATL's global expansion strategy to enhance its production capacity in Europe.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2022**: CATL confirmed that construction of the first production facilities would start later in the year, subject to shareholder approval. The groundbreaking ceremony was anticipated to take place in 2022.
- **11/2024**: CATL plans to start production at the Debrecen facility in the second half of 2024, with an initial capacity of 40 GWh per year, expanding to 100 GWh later.

#### 4. Labour
- **08/2022**: The facility is expected to create numerous jobs, although specific numbers were not detailed in the announcements. The establishment of the plant is anticipated to contribute significantly to local employment in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **08/2022**: CATL is the sole owner of the facility, as it is a greenfield project entirely funded and developed by the company.
