## HUN-00249-00239

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Eve Energy Facility in Debrecen, Hungary

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2022**: Reports emerged that Eve Energy is planning to build a battery plant in Debrecen, Hungary, to supply cylindrical battery cells for BMW's 'New Class' electric cars. The facility is expected to produce large-format round cells with a diameter of 46 mm.
- **January 2023**: Eve Energy officially confirmed plans to build its first European factory in Debrecen, with an annual production capacity of 28 GWh, set to begin operations in 2026.
- **May 2023**: The factory's specifications were reiterated, confirming the production of large cylindrical cells for BMW's New Class electric cars.
- **October 2023**: Groundbreaking for the factory was announced, with a confirmed annual capacity of 28 GWh.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2023**: Eve Energy announced an investment of approximately 400 billion forints (over 1 billion euros) for the Debrecen facility. The Hungarian government is providing support of 14 billion forints (about 38 million euros).
- **October 2023**: The investment details were reiterated during the groundbreaking ceremony, confirming the financial commitment to the facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2022**: Eve Energy signed a non-binding letter of intent with the municipality of Debrecen to acquire 45 hectares for the battery cell production plant.
- **May 2023**: Official confirmation of the factory's construction and its specifications.
- **October 2023**: Groundbreaking ceremony for the factory took place, marking the start of construction.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2023**: The construction of the Debrecen facility is expected to create over 1,000 new jobs in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: Eve Energy is establishing the facility as a wholly-owned operation to supply BMW, with no indication of joint ventures or shared ownership reported in the articles.
