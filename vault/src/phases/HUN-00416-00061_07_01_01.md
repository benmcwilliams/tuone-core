### Evolutionary Timeline of the Audi Facility in Györ, Hungary

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2018**: Audi begins production of electric motors for the e-tron quattro at the Györ facility, marking the start of its electric drive production.
- **July 2019**: Audi announces an expansion of electric motor production in Györ to integrate the next generation of electric motors. Initial production capacity was around 400 electric axle motors per day.
- **November 2023**: Audi starts series production of electric motors for the Premium Platform Electric (PPE) in Györ, producing stators and transmission components in a new production area spanning 15,000 square meters. The facility can produce up to 2,000 engines a day.
- **February 2024**: Preparations begin for the production of the new generation of electric drives called MEBeco for small electric cars, with increased vertical integration in manufacturing.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2021**: Audi announces a double-digit million euro investment in local technical development, focusing on electric drives and transmissions.
- **June 2022**: Audi confirms that the new electric drive family for the MEB ECO platform will be produced in Györ, supported by the Hungarian government.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2018**: Production of electric motors for the e-tron begins.
- **July 2019**: Expansion of electric motor production announced.
- **April 2020**: Production resumes after COVID-19 shutdown, with around 100 employees initially working on engine assembly.
- **November 2023**: Series production of PPE electric motors begins.
- **February 2024**: Preparations for MEBeco production are underway.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2019**: The expansion of electric motor production is expected to create 250 new jobs.
- **November 2023**: Approximately 700 employees are involved in the production of PPE motors, working in three shifts.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Audi owns the facility exclusively, as it is part of Audi Hungaria, which is a subsidiary of the Volkswagen Group.
