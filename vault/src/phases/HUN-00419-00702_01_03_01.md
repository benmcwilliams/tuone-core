## HUN-00419-00702

### Evolutionary Timeline of Samsung Facility in Göd, Hungary

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2017**: Construction of the Samsung SDI battery factory in Göd, Hungary, is completed. The factory initially has an annual production capacity of **30 GWh**.
- **February 2021**: Samsung SDI announces plans to expand the factory's capacity to over **40 GWh**.
- **August 2023**: Samsung SDI plans to expand the facility to produce large cylindrical battery cells (46 mm diameter) for BMW, with the addition of **two production lines** that will add **10 GWh** of capacity per year. The facility will produce cells in the **46120, 4680, and 4695 formats**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2021**: Samsung SDI announces an investment of approximately **740 million euros** (around **900 million USD**) for the expansion of the Göd facility.
- **August 2023**: Samsung SDI commits an additional investment of around **500 billion won** (approximately **345 million euros**) for the expansion to produce cylindrical cells.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2021**: Announcement of the expansion plans for the Göd facility.
- **August 2023**: Confirmation of the expansion to produce cylindrical cells for BMW, with plans to install new production lines.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2023**: The construction of EcoPro BM's cathode materials plant in Debrecen, Hungary, is reported to create **631 jobs**. This plant will supply materials to Samsung SDI’s Göd facility, indirectly supporting job creation in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **2020**: Samsung SDI and EcoPro BM establish a **joint venture** for cathode materials, indicating a collaborative ownership structure for certain aspects of production related to the Göd facility. However, Samsung SDI operates the Göd facility independently as its own manufacturing site.

This timeline captures the key developments and changes at the Samsung facility in Göd, Hungary, focusing on its technological advancements, investments, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure.
