## HUN-00419-00702

### Evolutionary Timeline of Samsung Facility in Göd, Hungary

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2016**: Samsung SDI plans to re-utilize an existing facility near Budapest for EV battery cell production, driven by increasing demand for electric vehicles in Europe.
- **August 2016**: Samsung SDI announces plans to establish a battery plant in Hungary with a production capacity of 50,000 EV battery packs per year, set to open in the second half of 2018.
- **May 2017**: Construction of the battery plant in Göd is completed, with plans for large-scale production to start in the second quarter of 2018, targeting an output of 50,000 EV batteries annually.
- **February 2021**: Samsung SDI announces an expansion of the Göd facility, increasing its annual capacity from 30 GWh to over 40 GWh.
- **August 2023**: Samsung SDI plans to expand the Göd facility to produce large cylindrical battery cells (46 mm diameter) for BMW, investing around 500 billion won (approximately 345 million euros) to install two additional production lines with a capacity of 10 GWh per year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2016**: Samsung SDI commits to invest approximately 358 million dollars in the Göd battery plant.
- **February 2021**: The company announces a further investment of around 740 million euros for the expansion of the Göd facility.
- **August 2023**: An additional investment of around 500 billion won (about 345 million euros) is planned for the expansion to produce cylindrical cells.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2016**: Announcement of plans to establish a battery production facility in Hungary.
- **August 2016**: Confirmation of the battery plant's establishment with a target opening in late 2018.
- **May 2017**: Completion of construction of the Göd facility.
- **February 2021**: Announcement of the expansion of the Göd facility to increase production capacity.
- **August 2023**: Plans to expand the facility to produce cylindrical battery cells for BMW.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2016**: The establishment of the battery plant is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers are not detailed.
- **February 2021**: The expansion of the facility is anticipated to contribute to job creation, but exact figures are not provided.
- **April 2023**: The EcoPro BM plant being built in Debrecen, Hungary, is expected to employ 631 people and will supply cathode materials to Samsung SDI’s Göd facility, indirectly supporting job creation.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: Samsung SDI operates the Göd facility, with indications of a joint venture for cathode materials established with EcoPro BM in 2020. There are considerations for a potential joint venture with BMW for the production of cylindrical cells, but no formal agreement has been confirmed yet.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones related to the Samsung facility in Göd, Hungary, focusing on its technology, investments, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
