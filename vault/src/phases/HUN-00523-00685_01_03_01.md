## HUN-00523-00685

### Evolutionary Timeline of the SK Group Facility in Iváncsa, Hungary

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2021**: SK Innovation announced plans to build a third battery cell factory in Iváncsa, Hungary, with an annual production capacity of **30 GWh**, sufficient to supply batteries for approximately **430,000 electric cars** per year. The facility is expected to be completed by **2028**.
- **March 2022**: The EU Commission confirmed that the plant will provide at least **1,900 direct jobs** and will maintain the planned capacity of **30 GWh**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2021**: SK Innovation announced an investment of **€1.9 billion** (approximately **$2.29 billion**) for the construction of the Iváncsa facility.
- **March 2022**: SK On (the spun-off battery division of SK Innovation) will invest a total of **€1.623 billion** in the project. Additionally, the EU approved state aid amounting to **€209 million** for the construction of the plant.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2021**: Announcement of the new battery cell factory in Iváncsa.
- **Q3 2021**: Construction of the Iváncsa facility is scheduled to begin.
- **March 2022**: The EU Commission confirmed the funding for the plant, affirming the project’s progress.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2022**: The Iváncsa facility is expected to create at least **1,900 direct jobs** upon completion.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2021**: SK Innovation officially spun off its battery business, which is now known as **SK On**. The Iváncsa facility is owned by SK On, indicating that it is part of the newly independent battery division of SK Innovation.

This timeline captures the key developments related to the SK Group facility in Iváncsa, Hungary, focusing on its technology, investments, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure.
