## HUN-00566-00521

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mercedes-Benz Facility in Kecskemét, Hungary

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2016**: Announcement of a new factory in Kecskemét for the production of electric and hybrid cars, with an initial investment of approximately €1 million.
- **June 2018**: Groundbreaking for the first "Full-Flex Plant" in Kecskemét, designed to produce both electric and internal combustion engine vehicles. The facility will cover 382,000 sqm and is expected to start production in 2020.
- **December 2020**: Confirmation that the Kecskemét plant will produce the EQB, a compact electric vehicle, starting in 2021.
- **October 2021**: Series production of the EQB begins at the Kecskemét plant, marking the first electric vehicle produced in Hungary.
- **June 2022**: Announcement of a €2 billion investment in European assembly plants, including Kecskemét, to prepare for new electric vehicle platforms by 2025.
- **March 2023**: Plans to convert the Kecskemét plant for the production of new electric models based on the Mercedes-Benz Modular Architecture (MMA).

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2018**: The Hungarian government supports the investment in the Kecskemét facility with a €37.5 million grant.
- **October 2021**: The Hungarian government provides 15 billion forints (approximately €42 million) in support of a 50 billion forint (about €136 million) investment in the plant.
- **June 2022**: Mercedes announces an investment of over €2 billion in its European production sites, including Kecskemét, to equip them for electric vehicle production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2016**: Announcement of the new factory in Kecskemét.
- **June 2018**: Groundbreaking for the Full-Flex Plant.
- **December 2020**: Decision made to produce the EQB at Kecskemét.
- **October 2021**: Start of series production of the EQB.
- **June 2022**: Announcement of significant investments for future electric vehicle production.
- **March 2023**: Plans for conversion to accommodate new electric platforms.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2018**: The Kecskemét plant is expected to create 2,500 jobs upon completion in 2020.
- **October 2021**: The start of EQB production is expected to contribute to job creation, although specific numbers are not detailed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned exclusively by Daimler AG (Mercedes-Benz), with no mention of joint ventures or partnerships regarding the ownership of the Kecskemét plant.
