## HUN-00589-00685

### Evolutionary Timeline of SK Group Facility in Komárom, Hungary

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2017**: SK Innovation announces plans for a new battery facility in Hungary with a production capacity of 7.5 GWh, which is nearly double that of its facility in Seosan, Korea (3.9 GWh).
- **March 2018**: Construction of the battery cell facility in Komárom officially begins. The facility is set to cover 430,000 sqm and is expected to start production by 2020.
- **February 2019**: SK Innovation announces the construction of a second battery factory in Komárom, which will also have an annual capacity of 7.5 GWh, to be completed in 2021.
- **January 2021**: The first plant in Komárom is completed and operational, while the second plant is under construction with a planned capacity of 9.8 GWh, expected to start production in Q1 2022.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2017**: SK Innovation plans to invest $734 million in the new facility.
- **March 2019**: The investment for the second factory is announced to be $859 million.
- **August 2019**: SK Innovation borrows $657 million to fund expansions, including the Hungarian facilities.
- **July 2021**: SK Innovation receives a €90 million subsidy from the Hungarian government for the second plant, with total investment for this plant estimated at €700 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2018**: Groundbreaking for the first battery cell facility.
- **December 2019**: The first plant in Komárom is completed and begins operations.
- **2021**: The second plant is under construction, with expected completion and start of production in 2022.
- **March 2022**: The EU Commission approves state aid of €209 million for the construction of a third plant in Iváncsa, Hungary, which will have a capacity of 30 GWh.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2022**: The third plant in Iváncsa is expected to create at least 1,900 direct jobs, contributing to local employment.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: SK Innovation operates the facility under its subsidiary SK Battery Kft. There is no indication of joint ventures or shared ownership for the Komárom facilities; SK Innovation appears to have full ownership of the plants.
