## HUN-02352-02353

### Evolutionary Timeline of MOL Group Facility in Százhalombatta, Hungary

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **02/2018**: MOL Group announces its entry into the solar energy business, planning to construct three photovoltaic (PV) plants with a combined capacity of 18.3 MW at its industrial sites in Hungary, including the Danube refinery in Százhalombatta. These projects are intended to meet part of the energy needs of its operations and are scheduled to be operational by the end of 2018.
- **04/2022**: MOL Group, in partnership with Plug Power, announces the construction of a green hydrogen production facility at the Danube Refinery in Százhalombatta. The facility will utilize a 10 MW electrolysis unit and is expected to produce approximately 1,600 tons of clean, carbon-neutral hydrogen annually. The facility is set to be operational in 2023.
- **04/2024**: MOL Group officially inaugurates the 10 MW green hydrogen plant at the Danube Refinery. The plant is designed to gradually replace the natural gas-based production process, which contributes significantly to the company's carbon emissions.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04/2022**: The investment for the green hydrogen facility is reported to be €22 million. This investment aligns with MOL Group's strategy to transition towards more sustainable energy production methods.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **02/2018**: Announcement of the solar PV projects at the Danube refinery and other sites, with a target completion by the end of 2018.
- **04/2022**: Announcement of the green hydrogen production facility in collaboration with Plug Power, with an operational target set for 2023.
- **04/2024**: Inauguration of the green hydrogen plant, with production expected to start in the second half of 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the solar PV projects or the green hydrogen facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- MOL Group operates the facility at Százhalombatta as a wholly-owned entity. The company is primarily owned by the Hungarian government (25.2% stake), with other shareholders including MOL Plc and Mol Investment Kft (9.3%) and OmanOil Budapest (7.1%). However, the facility itself is not described as a joint venture or partnership in the context of its operations.
