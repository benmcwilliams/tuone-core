### Evolutionary Timeline of the EcoSolifer Facility in Csorna, Hungary

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10/2015**: EcoSolifer places an order with Meyer Burger for a 90-100MW heterojunction (HJ) solar cell line, with plans to produce high-efficiency solar modules using amorphous silicon deposition technology on monocrystalline wafers. The facility aims to expand capacity to 500MW in the short term.
- **08/2018**: After a two-year pause due to restructuring, EcoSolifer announces that work on its 100 MWp HJT bifacial PV cell manufacturing plant is back on track, with commissioning expected in Q4 2018 and production starting in Q1 or Q2 2019.
- **03/2020**: EcoSolifer begins commercial production of bifacial heterojunction solar cells at its 100 MW factory, with plans to increase capacity from one shift (14 MW) to three shifts (100 MW) by the end of Q2 2020. Future capacity may be raised to 300 MW depending on market conditions.
- **12/2020**: The facility's annual nameplate capacity is reported to be around 350MW, with plans to expand to approximately 2GW by 2022.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10/2015**: EcoSolifer signs a contract worth CHF 23 million (approx. US$29.5 million) with Meyer Burger for the delivery of HJT equipment, which includes integrated cell performance measurement and sorting technology.
- **12/2020**: Meyer Burger incurs a loss of CHF 1.97 million (US$2.22 million) due to delays in the contract for the HJ solar cell line, but continues to provide support services to EcoSolifer.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **10/2015**: EcoSolifer announces plans for a 100MW HJT solar cell line and aims to begin industrial manufacturing in Q1 2016.
- **08/2018**: The project resumes after a two-year hiatus, with the factory building completed in July 2016.
- **03/2020**: Commercial production of bifacial HJT solar cells begins at the facility.
- **12/2020**: Final acceptance of the installed equipment is achieved, marking a significant milestone in the project.

#### 4. Labour
