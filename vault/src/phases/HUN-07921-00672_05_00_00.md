### Evolutionary Timeline of the Rosatom Facility in Paks, Hungary

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2014**: The Paks II project was launched through an intergovernmental agreement between Hungary and Russia for the construction of two VVER-1200 reactors, with a combined capacity of 2.4 GW.
- **2022**: The construction license for Paks II was issued by Hungary's National Atomic Energy Office in August.
- **2023**: Hungary's Energy Minister announced that the Paks II plant is expected to be completed by 2032, two years later than initially planned.
- **2024**: Preparatory works began, including soil stabilization and the construction of a groundwater cut-off wall. The first concrete is expected to be poured by the end of 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2014**: The project was awarded to Rosatom without a tender, with financing primarily through a Russian state loan covering approximately 80% of the estimated €12.5 billion cost.
- **2023**: Hungary and Russia agreed to amend the financing contract to address delays and changes in the geopolitical landscape, ensuring the project's continuation.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2022**: Construction license for Paks II issued.
- **July 2023**: Groundwater cut-off construction began.
- **August 2023**: The first concrete for the new units is expected to be poured by the end of 2024.
- **November 2024**: Hungary introduced a bill to amend the existing contract with Rosatom to ensure the smooth continuation of the project.

#### 4. Labour
- **2023**: The construction site is expected to employ over 1,000 workers, with numbers increasing to over 10,000 during peak construction periods.
- **2024**: As construction progresses, the number of workers on-site is anticipated to grow significantly.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is a joint venture between Hungary and Russia, with Rosatom being the primary contractor responsible for the construction and operation of the new reactors. Hungary retains ownership of the existing Paks facility and the new units under the intergovernmental agreement.
