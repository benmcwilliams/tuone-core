### Evolutionary Timeline of MVM Paksi Atomerömű Zrt Facility in Paks, Hungary

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Production**: The facility operates four VVER-440 reactors, which produce about half of Hungary's electricity.
- **Design Lifetime**: Originally designed for 30 years, the lifetime was extended in 2005 by an additional 20 years, now set to operate until between 2032 and 2037.
- **Future Plans**: A life extension program has been announced to extend the operating license by another 20 years, marking the beginning of a decade-long process. This includes evaluating and potentially replacing equipment and systems to enhance safety and efficiency.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment for Life Extension**: The revamping of electric and control systems is estimated to cost around EUR 1.5 billion (USD 1.6 billion). The full implementation plan for the life extension is expected to be submitted in 2028.
- **Nuclear Fuel Storage**: Starting in 2024, the facility will hold three years' worth of nuclear fuel on-site, an increase from the previous two years, aimed at reducing uncertainties due to geopolitical conflicts.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Life Extension Announcement**: December 2023 - The CEO announced the initiation of the life extension program to the EU, marking the start of a decade-long process.
- **Paks II Project**: Launched in early 2014, this project involves the construction of two new VVER-1200 reactors, supported by a Russian state loan. The construction license application was submitted in July 2020, and the license was issued in August 2022. Groundworks for Paks II began in 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The life extension and renovation projects are expected to create jobs, although specific numbers were not detailed in the article. The emphasis on a dedicated team of professionals suggests a focus on skilled labor in the ongoing operations and upgrades.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
