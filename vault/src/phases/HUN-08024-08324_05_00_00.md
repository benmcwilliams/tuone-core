### Evolutionary Timeline of the Atomstroyexport Facility in Paks II, Hungary

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Paks II facility is set to produce nuclear energy with a total production capacity of 2.4 GW through two new water-water energetic reactor-1200 power units. 
   - As of August 2023, main-stage construction has begun on these two units, with ongoing work including soil excavation for unit 6 and the construction of a workshop for reinforcement products.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The project is being developed with a significant investment of $13.6 billion (approximately Rbs 1.31 trillion). 

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **August 2022:** Rosatom secured the license to build the Paks II project from the Hungarian Atomic Energy Authority.
   - **August 2023:** Main-stage construction commenced on the two reactor units, with preparations for excavation of the pit for unit 6.
   - **September 2023:** It was announced that the first concrete pour at the facility is expected to take place by early 2025, with ongoing soil excavation and site preparation activities.

4. **Labour:**
   - As many as 1,000 jobs are projected to be created at the Paks II nuclear power plant by 2024, with expectations for this number to increase to 10,000 during the peak construction phase.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is owned by Rosatom, with Atomstroyexport as a subsidiary involved in the construction. There is no indication of joint ownership or partnerships mentioned in the articles.
