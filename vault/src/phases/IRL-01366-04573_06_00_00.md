### Evolutionary Timeline of the BNRG Facility in Dublin, Ireland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** August 2021
   - **Details:** BNRG, in partnership with Neoen, completed financing for three solar farms (Hilltown, Hortland, and Millvale) located in Meath, Kildare, and Wicklow, Ireland. The combined production capacity of these solar plants is 58 MWp. The electricity generated will be sold under power purchase agreements (PPAs) supported by the Irish government via a Contract for Difference (CfD) mechanism valid until 2037. The solar farms are expected to provide enough electricity to power approximately 12,700 homes.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** August 2021
   - **Details:** The total project costs for the solar farms amount to approximately €39 million, excluding financing costs. Funding is provided through a combination of equity from Neoen and BNRG, along with a non-recourse senior debt facility from Société Générale.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** August 2021
   - **Details:** The projects were among the first to complete financial close in Ireland, following their success in the first auction of the Irish government’s Renewable Electricity Support Scheme (RESS 1) in 2020. Construction is underway, with commissioning scheduled for the first half of 2022.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** August 2021
   - **Details:** The article does not specify the exact number of jobs created or lost as a result of this project. However, it mentions a financial contribution to community projects worth around €100,000 annually for local development initiatives during the RESS support period, which may indirectly support local employment.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** August 2021
   - **Details:** The solar farms are jointly owned by Neoen and BNRG. The assets will be fully consolidated in Neoen’s accounts, indicating a partnership rather than exclusive ownership by either company.
