### Evolutionary Timeline of RWE Facility in Dublin Array, Ireland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2021**: RWE's 8.5 MW battery storage facility at Stephenstown, Balbriggan, County Dublin, begins operations. This facility is designed to provide critical system services to stabilize the electricity grid as Ireland transitions to renewable energy.
- **June 2022**: RWE launches its second battery storage facility at Lisdrumdoagh, Co. Monaghan, with a capacity of 60 MW. This facility is aimed at balancing intermittency in electricity generation and providing backup during power outages.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2022**: RWE invests €25 million in the Lisdrumdoagh battery storage project. This investment is part of RWE's broader strategy to expand its battery storage technology business in Ireland.
- **April 2022**: RWE announces a commitment to invest €50 billion gross through to 2030 to expand its renewable generation capacity to 50 GW, with €1.5 billion earmarked for projects in Ireland.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: The Stephenstown battery storage facility becomes operational.
- **June 2022**: The Lisdrumdoagh battery storage facility goes live, marking RWE's second battery project in Ireland.
- **Ongoing**: RWE is developing the Dublin Array offshore wind project in partnership with Saorgus Energy, which will consist of 45 to 61 wind turbines with a capacity of 600-900 MW.
