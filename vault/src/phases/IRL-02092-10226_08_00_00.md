### Evolutionary Timeline of The Renewables Infrastructure Group Facility in Kerry, Ireland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **January 2018:** The Renewables Infrastructure Group (TRIG) acquired the Clahane 1 wind farm in Kerry, which has a production capacity of 41.2MW, featuring 20 Enercon E-70 turbines that have been operational since 2008. Additionally, TRIG purchased the Clahane 2 site, which is under construction and will add another 13.8MW with six further E-70 turbines expected to be operational later in 2018. The total capacity of the facility is thus 55MW.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **January 2018:** The total consideration for the acquisition of both projects (Clahane 1 and Clahane 2) was €72 million. This amount includes a deferred consideration contingent on the completion of certain critical construction milestones at the Clahane 2 site. To support this acquisition and future projects, TRIG increased its revolving acquisition facility from £150 million to £240 million, with £148 million already spent and an additional £23 million allocated for further construction and deferred payments.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **January 2018:** The acquisition of the operational Clahane 1 wind farm and the under-construction Clahane 2 project was announced. The Clahane 2 site is expected to come online later in 2018.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
