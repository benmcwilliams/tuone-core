## IRL-04418-01592

### Evolutionary Timeline of the ESB Facility in Moneypoint, Ireland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2021**: ESB and Equinor announced plans for a floating offshore wind farm off the south-west coast of Ireland, with a total capacity of up to 1.5GW. The first phase, Moneypoint Offshore One, is expected to have a capacity of 400MW, located 16km off the Clare/Kerry coast. The second phase, Moneypoint Offshore Two, aims to add an additional 1.1GW capacity.
- **August 2021**: ESB announced plans to redevelop the Moneypoint site into a renewable energy hub, including a hydrogen facility as part of the Green Atlantic @ Moneypoint initiative.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2021**: The offshore wind project is described as a multi-million Euro investment under a public-private partnership between ESB and Equinor. A foreshore license application was submitted to conduct site investigations, indicating significant preparatory investment.
- **August 2021**: The Green Atlantic @ Moneypoint project is part of ESB's broader investment strategy to transition the facility towards renewable energy, although specific financial details were not disclosed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: The foreshore license application for the offshore wind project was submitted, marking the beginning of formal project development.
- **Expected Timeline**: The coal power plant at Moneypoint is scheduled to cease operations in 2025, with the offshore wind farm expected to connect to the Moneypoint substation, utilizing the existing infrastructure.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2021**: The announcement of the offshore wind project is seen as a potential boost for local employment, especially after approximately 100 workers lost their jobs due to restructuring at the Moneypoint Power Station. The transition to renewable energy is expected to create new job opportunities in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2021**: The facility is a result of a partnership between ESB and Equinor, indicating a joint venture structure for the offshore wind project. ESB retains ownership of the Moneypoint site, while collaborating with Equinor for the development of the offshore wind farm.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic shifts at the ESB facility in Moneypoint, highlighting its transition from coal to renewable energy sources, particularly offshore wind and hydrogen production.
