## IRL-04418-01636

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Equinor Facility in Moneypoint, Ireland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2021**: Equinor and ESB are exploring the development of a floating offshore wind farm off the south-west coast of Ireland, specifically the Moneypoint Offshore Wind Farm. The project aims for a total capacity of up to 1.5GW, with an initial phase (Moneypoint Offshore One) targeting 400MW located 16km off the coast of Clare and Kerry. The second phase (Moneypoint Offshore Two) is expected to add an additional 1.1GW capacity.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2021**: The project is described as a multi-million Euro investment under a public-private partnership between ESB and Equinor. A foreshore license application was submitted to the Department of Housing, Local Government and Heritage in January 2021 to conduct site investigations, which is a crucial step in the development process.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: The foreshore license application is a significant milestone, allowing for preliminary survey work and site investigations for the Moneypoint Offshore One Wind Farm. The project is part of Ireland's long-term decarbonisation strategy, with plans to connect the wind farm to the existing Moneypoint substation after the coal plant's closure in 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2021**: The proposed offshore wind development is expected to revitalize the Moneypoint Power Station, which has faced job losses due to restructuring. Approximately 100 workers lost their jobs in a recent restructuring plan, but the new project is anticipated to create job opportunities in the renewable energy sector, providing a boost to the local economy in West Clare.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2021**: The facility is a joint venture between ESB (Electricity Supply Board) and Equinor, a Norwegian company. This partnership indicates that the facility is not under exclusive ownership by either company but rather a collaborative effort to develop the offshore wind project.
