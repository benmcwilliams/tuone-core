### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mercury Renewables Facility in Firlough, Ireland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2023**: Mercury Renewables proposed a utility-scale integrated wind farm and green hydrogen project known as Firlough, located on the Mayo-Sligo border. The facility is expected to produce approximately **4.5 million kilograms of green hydrogen per year**, starting in **2025**. The project includes a **78MW wind farm** with **13 turbines** that will power the hydrogen production through electrolysis, which splits water into hydrogen and oxygen without emissions or harmful byproducts.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2023**: The total investment proposed for the Firlough project is **€200 million**. This investment is part of a broader national strategy to integrate green hydrogen into Ireland's energy grid, aiming for net-zero carbon emissions by **2050**.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2023**: Mercury Renewables submitted a planning application to **An Bord Pleanála** for the Firlough project. The project has been deemed of “national strategic importance” by the planning authority. The expected operational start date is **2025**, contingent on the assessment timeline by An Bord Pleanála.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2023**: The construction of the Firlough facility is projected to create between **100 to 150 jobs** during the construction phase. Upon completion, the facility is expected to provide an additional **10 to 20 full-time and part-time jobs**.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify the ownership structure of the Firlough facility, but it is implied that **Mercury Renewables** is the primary developer and likely holds exclusive ownership of the project, as it is described as a proposal from the company.
