### Evolutionary Timeline of the Neoen Facility in Kildare, Ireland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 18-08-2021
   - **Details:** Neoen, in partnership with BNRG, completed financial close for the Hilltown, Hortland, and Millvale solar farms in Kildare, Meath, and Wicklow, with a combined capacity of 58 MWp. The solar farms will generate electricity to power approximately 12,700 homes. The engineering, procurement, and construction (EPC) contracts have been awarded to Omexom, and construction is underway, with commissioning scheduled for the first half of 2022.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 18-08-2021
   - **Details:** The total project costs are approximately €39 million, funded through a combination of equity from Neoen and BNRG and a non-recourse senior debt facility from Société Générale. The projects were successful in the first auction of the Irish government’s Renewable Electricity Support Scheme (RESS 1) in 2020.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 18-08-2021
   - **Details:** Financial close was completed for the solar farms, marking a significant milestone as one of the first projects to achieve this in Ireland. Construction is currently underway, with commissioning expected in 1H2022.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 18-08-2021
   - **Details:** The article does not specify exact job creation or loss figures, but it mentions that a financial contribution to community projects will support local development initiatives worth around €100,000 annually for the duration of the RESS support, which ends in 2037.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 18-08-2021
   - **Details:** The solar farms are jointly owned by Neoen and BNRG, indicating a joint venture structure for this facility. The assets will be fully consolidated in Neoen’s accounts.
