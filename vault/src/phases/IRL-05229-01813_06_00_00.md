## IRL-05229-01813

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Neoen Facility in Meath, Ireland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 18-08-2021
   - **Details:** Neoen, in partnership with BNRG, completed financial close for three solar farms (Hilltown, Hortland, and Millvale) in Meath, Kildare, and Wicklow, with a combined capacity of 58 MWp. The solar farms will generate electricity to power approximately 12,700 homes. The engineering, procurement, and construction (EPC) contracts have been awarded to Omexom, and construction is underway with commissioning scheduled for the first half of 2022.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 18-08-2021
   - **Details:** The total project costs are approximately €39 million, funded through a combination of equity from Neoen and BNRG and a non-recourse senior debt facility from Société Générale. The projects were successful in the first auction of the Irish government’s Renewable Electricity Support Scheme (RESS 1) in 2020.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 18-08-2021
   - **Details:** Financial close completed for the solar farms. Construction is currently underway, with commissioning expected in the first half of 2022. The projects are among the first to complete financial close in Ireland under the RESS 1 scheme.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 18-08-2021
   - **Details:** The article does not specify exact job creation or job loss figures, but it mentions that the projects will support local development initiatives worth around €100,000 annually for community projects during the RESS support period, which ends in 2037.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 18-08-2021
   - **Details:** The solar farms are jointly owned by Neoen and BNRG. The assets will be fully consolidated in Neoen’s accounts, indicating that Neoen has a significant stake in the facility.
