### Evolutionary Timeline of the Neoen Facility in Wicklow, Ireland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 18-08-2021
   - **Details:** Neoen, in partnership with BNRG, completed financial close for three solar farms (Hilltown, Hortland, and Millvale) in Wicklow, Meath, and Kildare, with a combined production capacity of 58 MWp. The solar farms are designed to generate electricity that will be sold under power purchase agreements (PPAs) supported by the Irish government through a Contract for Difference (CfD) mechanism valid until 2037. The projects will provide enough electricity to power approximately 12,700 homes.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 18-08-2021
   - **Details:** The total project costs for the solar farms are approximately €39 million, excluding financing costs. Funding is provided through a combination of equity from Neoen and BNRG, along with a non-recourse senior debt facility from Société Générale.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 18-08-2021
   - **Details:** Construction of the solar farms is underway, with commissioning scheduled for the first half of 2022. The projects were among the first to complete financial close under the Irish government’s Renewable Electricity Support Scheme (RESS 1) in 2020.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 18-08-2021
   - **Details:** The article does not specify exact job creation or job loss figures, but it mentions that the projects will support local development initiatives, indicating potential job creation in the community.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 18-08-2021
   - **Details:** The solar farms are jointly owned by Neoen and BNRG, indicating a joint venture structure for this facility. The assets will be fully consolidated in Neoen’s accounts.
