### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ampyx Power Facility in County Mayo, Ireland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2021**: RWE, in partnership with Ampyx Power, announced the establishment of a test center in County Mayo to investigate airborne wind energy technology. The facility will initially test a 150 kW demonstrator system, followed by a larger 1 MW commercial scale system. The airborne wind energy system utilizes a ground-based winch generator and a tethered aircraft-shaped device to produce electricity.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2021**: The test center is supported by the Interreg North-West Europe funding program, which is part of the EU's MegaAWE project aimed at supporting technology development and commercialization in the airborne wind energy sector.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2021**: Planning permission for the test center was granted, and construction of the site infrastructure was expected to begin later that year. The operational lifetime of the test center is projected to be eight years.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2021**: The establishment of the test center is anticipated to create jobs related to the construction and operation of the facility, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2021**: The facility is a collaboration between RWE and Ampyx Power, indicating a partnership rather than exclusive ownership by either company. RWE is the primary developer of the test center, while Ampyx Power provides the technology.
