### Evolutionary Timeline of SSE Facility in Arklow, Ireland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2020**: SSE Renewables proposed the Arklow Bank Phase 2 offshore wind farm, aiming for a production capacity of 520MW with up to 76 turbines. The project is located in shallow waters at the Arklow Bank sandbank in the Irish Sea.
- **June 2022**: SSE secured planning permission for an operations and maintenance (O&M) base for the 800MW Arklow Bank 2 offshore wind farm, which will serve as the onshore support base for the wind farm's operational lifetime.
- **August 2022**: SSE completed offshore ground investigation works for Phase II of the Arklow Bank Wind Park, confirming the project’s capacity of 800MW, which is expected to power 850,000 homes and displace approximately 830 billion kilograms of carbon emissions annually.
- **September 2023**: SSE completed two offshore survey campaigns for the 800MW project, enhancing the understanding of seabed conditions and supporting ongoing design refinement.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2020**: SSE announced an investment of between €1 billion and €2 billion for the development of the Arklow Bank Phase 2 project.
- **August 2022**: The total investment for the Arklow Bank Wind Park is anticipated to be €2.5 billion ($2.49 billion).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2020**: Public consultation for the Arklow Bank Phase 2 project commenced, with plans to submit applications to An Bord Pleanála and the Department of Housing, Planning and Local Government.
- **June 2022**: Planning permission was granted for the O&M base at Arklow Harbour’s South Dock.
- **August 2022**: Completion of offshore ground investigations marked a key milestone, with the project expected to generate its first power in 2028 and be completed by 2029.
- **September 2023**: Completion of offshore survey campaigns, with ongoing work to secure final consent for the project.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2020**: The project is expected to create around 80 full-time jobs for the operation of the wind farm, based out of the new O&M facility.
- **June 2022**: The O&M base will accommodate around 80 long-term direct local jobs, with hundreds more jobs created during the construction phase of the operations base and the wider project.
