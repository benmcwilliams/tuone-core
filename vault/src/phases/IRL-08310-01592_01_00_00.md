1. **Technology and Operations**
   - **Date**: September 2024
   - **Details**: The proposed facility, named Ballynahone Energy Storage, will utilize iron-air battery technology supplied by US-based Form Energy. It is designed to have a production capacity of 10 MW and will be capable of storing 1 GWh of energy, with the ability to discharge energy at full power for up to 100 hours when fully charged.

2. **Investment and Development**
   - **Date**: September 2024
   - **Details**: The facility is a joint venture between Ireland’s state-owned forestry business Coillte and utility ESB. A planning application for the project was submitted to Donegal County Council, indicating a commitment to the development of this clean technology facility.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**
   - **Date**: September 2024
   - **Details**: The planning application for the Ballynahone Energy Storage project was submitted earlier in the month. This marks a significant milestone as it is proposed to be Europe’s first large-scale iron-air battery storage project.
