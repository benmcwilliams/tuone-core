### Evolutionary Timeline of the Shannon Foynes Port Company Facility in Shannon Foynes, Ireland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2022**: Shannon Foynes Port Company announced plans to develop the estuary into an international hub for floating offshore wind. This includes the expansion of the quayside area by linking two existing jetties, which will add 117 meters of jetty set down/storage area. The facility will also include logistics buildings as part of a new 38-hectare port logistics park, with future plans for additional warehousing.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2022**: A total investment of €28 million was announced for the development of the Shannon Foynes Port facility. This investment is co-funded by Shannon Foynes Port Company and the EU’s Connecting Europe Facility. The funding will support the expansion of infrastructure necessary for the offshore renewable energy sector.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2022**: Planning permission and foreshore consents were secured for the new jetty and associated set down area, with construction already underway. Work on the logistics park is set to begin in the third quarter of 2022, with completion expected in the first half of 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2021**: The Irish Department of Transport's policy statement indicated that the development of offshore renewable energy projects, including those at Shannon Foynes, is expected to create new jobs and businesses in the region, particularly in engineering, fabrication, transport, logistics, and other related technologies.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2022**: The facility is owned and operated by Shannon Foynes Port Company, which is leading the investment and development efforts in collaboration with the EU's funding mechanisms. There is no indication of joint ownership or partnerships with other entities for this specific facility.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic initiatives related to the Shannon Foynes Port Company facility, focusing on its role in advancing offshore renewable energy in Ireland.
