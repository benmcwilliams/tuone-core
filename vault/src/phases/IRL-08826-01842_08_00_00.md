### Evolutionary Timeline of the Orsted Facility at Coom Green Energy Park, Ireland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **November 2023:** Orsted and FuturEnergy Ireland received planning approval for the Coom Green Energy Park, which will have a production capacity of 121MW. The facility is designed to power approximately 80,000 homes and aims to displace up to 150,000 tonnes of carbon emissions annually. Additionally, the project includes provisions for 50MW of energy storage.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **November 2023:** The project represents a significant investment by Orsted, although specific financial figures are not disclosed. The facility is part of a 50:50 joint venture between Orsted and FuturEnergy Ireland.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **November 2023:** Planning approval was granted by An Bord Pleanála, marking a critical milestone for the project. The next steps include applying for a grid connection from EirGrid and potentially entering future Renewable Electricity Support Scheme (RESS) auctions or selling power through Corporate Power Purchase Agreements (CPPAs). The facility is expected to be operational by 2027, subject to a final investment decision.

4. **Labour:**
   - **November 2023:** The construction phase of the Coom Green Energy Park is expected to create up to 168 jobs. Once operational, the facility will provide up to 42 long-term positions.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **November 2023:** The Coom Green Energy Park is a joint venture, with Orsted and FuturEnergy Ireland each holding a 50% stake in the project. 

This timeline captures the key developments and details regarding the Orsted facility at Coom Green Energy Park as of November 2023.
