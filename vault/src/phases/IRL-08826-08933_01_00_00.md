### Evolutionary Timeline of the FuturEnergy Ireland Facility at Coom Green Energy Park, Ireland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **November 2023:** The Coom Green Energy Park, a joint venture between Orsted and FuturEnergy Ireland, has been granted planning approval for a capacity of **121MW**. The facility is designed to be an onshore wind development project that can power approximately **80,000 homes** and displace up to **150,000 tonnes of carbon emissions annually**. Additionally, the plans include **50MW of energy storage**.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **November 2023:** The project represents a significant investment by Orsted, although specific financial figures are not disclosed. The facility is expected to contribute to Ireland’s pre-2030 emissions reduction targets and will include a **community benefit fund of approximately €500,000** for the first 15 years once operational.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **November 2023:** Planning approval was granted by An Bord Pleanála, marking a crucial milestone for the project. The next steps involve applying for a grid connection from EirGrid and potentially entering future RESS auctions or selling power through CPPAs. The facility is projected to be operational by **2027**, subject to a final investment decision.

4. **Labour:**
   - **November 2023:** The construction phase of the Coom Green Energy Park is expected to create up to **168 jobs**, with an additional **42 long-term positions** anticipated once the wind farm becomes operational.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **November 2023:** The Coom Green Energy Park is a **50:50 joint venture** between Orsted and FuturEnergy Ireland, indicating shared ownership of the facility.
