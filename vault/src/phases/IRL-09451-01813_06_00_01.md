### Evolutionary Timeline of the Neoen Facility in Ballinknockane, Ireland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2024**: Neoen launched the construction of the Ballinknockane solar farm with an upgraded capacity of **79 MWp**. Initially planned for **61 MWp**, the project will be Neoen's first transmission-connected project in Ireland and the first utility-scale solar farm in County Limerick. The solar farm is scheduled to be energized in **mid-2026** and commissioned in the **first half of 2027**. It is expected to generate enough energy to power **16,000 homes**, preventing the emission of approximately **21,000 tonnes of CO2** annually. The facility will also incorporate agrisolar practices, such as sheep-grazing, and implement biodiversity measures on-site.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2024**: The Ballinknockane solar farm was successful in Ireland’s **RESS 2 auction** in **2022** and will benefit from a **Contract for Difference (CfD)** mechanism valid until **2040**. Neoen's total capacity in operation or under construction in Ireland now reaches **190 MW**, including three solar farms and eight wind farms.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2024**: Construction officially commenced with a notice to proceed given to **Omexom** and **TLI Group** under EPC turnkey contracts for the solar plant and the **110kV onsite substation**, respectively. The project is a significant milestone for Neoen in Ireland, following their success in the **RESS 4 auction** with two new solar projects totaling **170 MWp**.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2024**: While specific job creation numbers were not detailed, the construction and operation of the solar farm are expected to contribute positively to local employment through the engagement of contractors and local initiatives.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
