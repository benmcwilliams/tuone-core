### Evolutionary Timeline of the Neoen Facility in County Limerick, Ireland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2024**: Neoen announced the construction of the Ballinknockane solar farm, which will have a production capacity of **79 MWp**. This facility will be connected to the high-voltage grid in County Limerick and is set to be the first utility-scale solar farm in the area. The project was initially awarded a capacity of **61 MW** but was later increased to **79 MW**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2022**: The Ballinknockane solar farm was awarded through the **RESS 2 tender**, which included a **Contract for Difference (CfD)** mechanism valid until **2040**. The final average price from the tender was set at **€0.09787/kWh**.
- **December 2024**: Neoen appointed **AFRY** as the technical partner for the project, which includes project management support and monitoring of construction and commissioning phases.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2024**: Construction of the Ballinknockane solar farm began, with expectations to be energized by **mid-2026** and fully operational in the **first half of 2027**. This marks a delay of approximately two years from the initial schedule.
- **October 2024**: Neoen indicated that with the Ballinknockane project, it has reached a total production capacity of **190 MW** in Ireland, which includes other solar and wind projects.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2024**: The announcement of the project and its construction phase implies job creation, although specific numbers regarding job creation or loss have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2024**: The Ballinknockane solar farm is **owned and operated entirely by Neoen**, confirming full ownership of the facility.
