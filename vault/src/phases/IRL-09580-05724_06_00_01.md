### Evolutionary Timeline of the Octopus Renewables Infrastructure Trust Facility near Dublin, Ireland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **05-02-2024:** Octopus Renewables Infrastructure Trust (ORIT) acquired a 199MW solar complex near Dublin, consisting of four solar farms. This acquisition increased ORIT’s total operational capacity to 735MW. The solar farms are designed to generate enough green energy to power 50,000 homes and offset 60,000 tonnes of carbon dioxide emissions annually. A fifth solar farm with an additional 42MW capacity is currently under construction, expected to be operational by Q3 2024.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **05-02-2024:** The acquisition of the solar complex was completed for €160m ($172.5m), partially financed through a debt facility of €80.6m from Allied Irish Banks and La Banque Postale. Additionally, ORIT has pre-agreed to extend the existing debt facility by €25.8m for the construction of the fifth solar farm.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **November 2022:** ORIT entered into a 15-year power purchase agreement (PPA) with Microsoft for the off-take of power from the solar farms.
   - **December 2023:** The solar farms began commercial operations, allowing ORIT to finalize the acquisition from Statkraft Ireland.
   - **Q3 2024:** The fifth solar farm with 42MW capacity is expected to be operational.

4. **Labour:**

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - ORIT, a subsidiary of UK-based Octopus Energy, acquired the facility from Statkraft Ireland. The ownership structure indicates that ORIT now fully owns the solar complex, making it the owner of Ireland’s largest solar complex.
