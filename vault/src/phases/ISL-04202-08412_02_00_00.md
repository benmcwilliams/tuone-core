### Evolutionary Timeline of the Blue Lagoon Facility in Svartsengi, Iceland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: The Blue Lagoon facility utilizes geothermal water that has already been used for electricity and hot water production by HS Orka. The facility is part of a larger Resource Park that includes various companies leveraging geothermal resources.
- **Resource Utilization**: The Blue Lagoon's operations are integrated into a circular economy model, where geothermal resources are used efficiently, minimizing waste. The facility benefits from the geothermal water and other resource streams, including CO₂, which is utilized for algae production in cosmetics.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment in Circular Economy**: HS Orka has invested in the development of the Resource Park, which includes the Blue Lagoon and other businesses that utilize geothermal resources. The focus is on creating synergies among the companies to enhance resource efficiency.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Resource Park Development**: The Resource Park, which includes the Blue Lagoon, has been evolving since the late 1970s, with a significant emphasis on circular economy practices. The Blue Lagoon is highlighted as a successful case within this model, showcasing the effective use of geothermal resources.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The establishment and growth of the Blue Lagoon and the Resource Park have contributed to job creation in various sectors, including aquaculture, biotech, and tourism, as multiple companies operate within the park.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: HS Orka is the largest privately owned power producer in Iceland and operates the geothermal power plants that supply resources to the Blue Lagoon. The Blue Lagoon operates as part of the Resource Park, indicating a collaborative model rather than exclusive ownership.

### Summary
The Blue Lagoon facility in Svartsengi, Iceland, is a key player in the country's circular economy, utilizing geothermal resources efficiently. It operates within a Resource Park developed by HS Orka, which emphasizes sustainability and resource synergy. The facility has contributed to job creation and showcases a successful model for utilizing geothermal energy in various applications, including tourism and cosmetics.
