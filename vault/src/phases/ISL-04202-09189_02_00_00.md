### Evolutionary Timeline of the ISTAK Facility in Svartsengi, Iceland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Production Capacity**: As of September 2023, the Svartsengi geothermal plant has an installed power capacity of approximately 66 MW. The ongoing expansion project, named SVA7, aims to increase this capacity to 85 MW by replacing older production units with a new, more efficient production unit.
- **Expansion Details**: The SVA7 project involves significant upgrades, including the construction of a new power station building and the installation of critical equipment such as a generator, cooling tower, and gas suction system. The concrete work for the new station building began in mid-September 2023.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Amounts**: The contract for the SVA7 expansion is valued at ISK 2.6 billion, with a total estimated project cost of ISK 12 billion. Funding for the expansion includes a new USD 61.9 million CAPEX increase facility agreement from existing financiers and additional shareholder funding.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Groundbreaking**: The groundbreaking for the SVA7 expansion project occurred in December 2022, with earthworks completed in spring 2023.
- **Construction Timeline**: The project is expected to be completed and handed over by October 30, 2025. The installation of hardware for the expansion is noted as the largest single contract in the history of the involved construction company, HD ehf.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The expansion project is anticipated to create around 100 jobs at its peak during the construction phase.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The facility is operated by HS Orka, and the construction work is being carried out by ISTAK, indicating that ISTAK is involved in the project but does not own the facility exclusively. The ownership structure suggests a collaborative effort between HS Orka and ISTAK for the expansion project.
