### Evolutionary Timeline of the ON Power Facility in Hellisheidi, Iceland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2020**: The Hellisheidi geothermal power plant, operated by ON Power, utilizes high-temperature geothermal energy to generate electricity and provide hot water for the district heating system in the capital area of Reykjavik. The facility is part of the Hengill geothermal area, which is noted for its significant geothermal resources.
- **July 2023**: The Silverstone carbon capture project commenced construction, aiming to capture 34,000 tonnes of CO2 annually, contributing to Iceland's 2030 carbon neutrality goals. This project will position Hellisheidi as the world’s first near carbon-neutral geothermal power plant.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2023**: ON Power and Carbfix received a grant of EUR 3.9 million from the EU Innovation Fund for the Silverstone carbon capture project, which is expected to enhance the facility's carbon capture capabilities significantly.
