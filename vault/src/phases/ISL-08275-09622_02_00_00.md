### Evolutionary Timeline of the ON Power Facility in Hellisheiði, Iceland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Production Capacity**: ON Power operates the Hellisheiði Geothermal Power Plant, which is part of a broader initiative to utilize geothermal energy efficiently. The facility is involved in producing both electricity and hot water, contributing to Iceland's goal of becoming carbon neutral by 2040.
- **Geothermal Park Development**: ON Power has established the 'Geothermal Park' near the Hellisheiði facility, which spans 103 hectares and is designed to support sustainability-focused companies. The park is still in development but currently hosts three customers, including Climeworks, Vaxa Technologies, and GeoSilica.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Drilling Project**: In June 2024, ON Power announced a collaboration with Iceland Drilling to drill new production wells, including one in Hellisheiði, to compensate for natural subsidence in geothermal systems and meet growing energy demands. The project emphasizes environmentally friendly practices, utilizing an electrified drill rig to save over two million liters of diesel.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Geothermal Park Launch**: The Geothermal Park initiative began approximately ten years ago, with ongoing development and the establishment of partnerships with companies focused on circular economy practices.
- **Drilling Project Announcement**: In June 2024, ON Power confirmed the start of a drilling project set to begin in May 2025, aimed at maintaining energy production levels and supporting future growth.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The ongoing development of the Geothermal Park and the drilling project is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers have not been disclosed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: ON Power is identified as the owner of the Hellisheiði facility, with no mention of joint ventures or shared ownership in the provided articles. The company operates independently to develop its geothermal resources and support sustainability initiatives.
