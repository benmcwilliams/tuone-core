### Evolutionary Timeline of the Carbfix Facility in Nesjavellir, Iceland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2023**: The pilot carbon capture and storage (CCS) plant of Carbfix began operations at the Nesjavellir geothermal power plant. The facility captures all H2S and 98% of CO2 emissions from the geothermal power plant, with an annual capture capacity of 3,000 tons of CO2 and 1,000 tons of H2S, representing approximately 20% of the power plant's annual emissions. The captured CO2 is injected into the basaltic subsurface, where it reacts with the bedrock to form stable carbonate minerals. A full-scale CCS plant is scheduled to become operational by 2030.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2023**: Carbfix and ON Power received a grant of EUR 3.9 million from the EU Innovation Fund for the Silverstone project, which is a separate carbon capture initiative but highlights the ongoing investment in carbon capture technologies associated with Carbfix.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2023**: Announcement of the start of operations for the pilot CCS plant at the Nesjavellir geothermal power plant.
- **July 2023**: Reference to the pilot plant's operations in the context of the Silverstone project, indicating the ongoing development and integration of Carbfix technology.
