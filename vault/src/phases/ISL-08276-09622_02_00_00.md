### Evolutionary Timeline of the ON Power Facility in Nesjavellir, Iceland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2020**: The Nesjavellir geothermal power plant, operated by ON Power, utilizes high-temperature geothermal energy to generate electricity and provide hot water for the district heating system in Reykjavik. The facility has a production capacity of 120 MW.
- **May 2021**: A new production well (NJ-32) was drilled at the Nesjavellir plant, reaching a maximum temperature of 360 degrees Celsius, indicating potential for increased production capacity.
- **March 2023**: A pilot carbon capture and storage (CCS) plant began operations at Nesjavellir, capturing 98% of CO2 emissions and all H2S emissions, with an annual capture capacity of 3,000 tons of CO2 and 1,000 tons of H2S. This pilot project is a precursor to a full-scale CCS plant expected to be operational by 2030.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: The pilot CCS plant was developed as part of the Geothermal Emission Control (GECO) project under the EU Horizon 2020 program, indicating significant investment in research and development for emissions reduction technologies.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2020**: The facility is highlighted for its innovative geothermal energy utilization and ongoing improvements in efficiency and emissions reduction.
- **May 2021**: The successful drilling and testing of the NJ-32 well marked a significant milestone for the facility, with promising initial results.
- **March 2023**: The operational launch of the pilot CCS plant represents a major milestone in reducing the facility's carbon footprint.
- **June 2024**: ON Power signed a contract with Iceland Drilling for the drilling of three new wells at Nesjavellir, aimed at compensating for natural subsidence and meeting future energy demands.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2024**: The new drilling project is expected to maintain job levels and potentially create new jobs in the geothermal sector, as ON Power emphasizes responsible and environmentally friendly practices.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2020**: ON Power is identified as the geothermal power generation company of Reykjavik Energy, indicating that it operates under the umbrella of Reykjavik Energy, but specific details on ownership structure (e.g., full ownership or joint venture) are not explicitly stated in the articles.
