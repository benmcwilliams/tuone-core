## ISL-09015-09120

### Hecate Independent Power Facility Timeline - North Atlantic, ISL

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** May 2021
   - **Details:** Hecate Independent Power (HIP) announced plans to build up to 10GW of fixed and floating offshore wind capacity in the North Atlantic. The initial phase will consist of 4GW of grid connections to the UK’s 400kV electricity transmission system, with each wind farm (or 'pod') having a capacity of 1GW. The first 2GW of generation capacity is targeted to be off the southern and eastern coasts of Iceland, expected to be commissioned in early 2025.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** May 2021
   - **Details:** The total project cost is estimated at £21bn (€24bn). An initial investment of £2.9m for the first 2GW pilot phase is expected in 2021, with an additional £144m investment through 2025. A new £200m factory for cable production is planned for an unnamed port in the north-east of England.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** May 2021
   - **Details:** HIP lodged four connection applications with National Grid for the initial 4GW of grid connections. The project is positioned to be the first captive wind farm in overseas territorial waters for the UK, with a commissioning target for the initial 2GW by early 2025.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** May 2021
   - **Details:** The initial 2GW pilot phase is expected to create 15,000 new jobs in the UK. Additionally, up to 500 new jobs are anticipated in southern and eastern Iceland associated with this phase.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** May 2021
   - **Details:** Hecate Independent Power is a joint venture between Hecate Wind (a wholly-owned subsidiary of Hecate Holdings, US) and Independent Power Corporation (a UK developer of conventional power plants).
