## ISL-09017-09120

### Hecate Independent Power Facility Timeline

**1. Technology and Operations:**
- **Date:** May 2021
  - Hecate Independent Power (HIP) announced plans to build up to 10GW of fixed and floating offshore wind capacity in the North Atlantic, specifically targeting an initial 2GW of generation capacity off the southern and eastern coasts of Iceland. The facility will consist of multiple wind farm "pods," each with a capacity of 1GW, connected to the UK via subsea cables. The expected commissioning date for the initial 2GW is early 2025.

**2. Investment and Development:**
- **Date:** May 2021
  - The total project cost for the entire 10GW initiative is estimated at £21 billion (€24 billion). For the initial 2GW pilot phase, an investment of £2.9 million is expected in 2021, with an additional £144 million anticipated through 2025.

**3. Project Status and Milestones:**
- **Date:** May 2021
  - HIP lodged four connection applications with the National Grid for an initial 4GW of grid connections to the UK’s 400kV electricity transmission system. The project is positioned to be the first captive wind farm in overseas territorial waters for the UK, with a focus on diversifying wind sources to enhance grid stability.

**4. Labour:**
- **Date:** May 2021
  - The initial 2GW pilot phase is projected to create up to 500 new jobs in southern and eastern Iceland. Additionally, the overall project is expected to generate 15,000 new jobs in the UK.

**5. Ownership Structure:**
- **Date:** May 2021
  - Hecate Independent Power is a joint venture between Hecate Wind, a wholly-owned subsidiary of Hecate Holdings (US), and Independent Power Corporation (UK).
