### Evolutionary Timeline of Silicor Materials Facility in Grundartangi, ISL

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **July 2014:** Silicor Materials announced plans to establish a facility in Grundartangi, Iceland, with a production capacity of 19,000 metric tons (MT) of solar-grade silicon. The facility will convert metallurgical-grade silicon (MG-Si) feedstock to purified solar-grade silicon, aiming for a production cost of US$9/kg.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **July 2014:** Silicor Materials is seeking unspecified financing from Arion Bank, one of Iceland's largest banks, and is working with Centra Corporate Finance to raise equity finance for the project. The company is also in discussions with Iceland’s Ministry of Industries and Innovation for location incentive packages.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **July 2014:** Silicor Materials expressed intentions to break ground on the facility later in 2014, with construction and initial production targeted for 2016. The company has obtained heads of terms and letters of intent from Landsvirkjun and Orka Náttúrunnar (ON Power) for power supply.

4. **Labour:**
   - **July 2014:** The facility is expected to initially employ 100 construction workers and around 400 full-time employees once operational.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **July 2014:** The ownership structure of the facility is not explicitly detailed in the article, but it is implied that Silicor Materials is the primary entity behind the establishment of the facility, with potential partnerships for financing and power supply.
