### Evolutionary Timeline of the Baker Hughes Facility in Florence, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2020**: Baker Hughes successfully tested a hybrid hydrogen turbine at its Florence facility, designed for gas networks. This turbine can blend hydrogen with natural gas and is set to be installed at Snam’s compressor station in Istrana by 2021. The turbine is capable of compressing and moving hydrogen fuel blends through Snam’s transmission network.
- **January 2024**: Baker Hughes unveiled a new hydrogen testing facility in Florence, which includes a test bench for full load testing of NovaLT industrial turbines capable of running on blends up to 100% hydrogen. The facility features a 300-bar pressure and 2,450 kg storage capacity, serving as a hub for collaboration in the hydrogen economy.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2024**: The announcement of the new hydrogen testing facility indicates significant investment in expanding capabilities for hydrogen technology validation and development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2020**: Successful completion of testing for the hybrid hydrogen turbine.
- **January 2024**: Unveiling of the new hydrogen testing facility in Florence.
- **April 2024**: Baker Hughes was awarded a contract by Snam to provide three hydrogen-ready gas turbine-driven compressor trains for a new gas compressor station in Sulmona, Italy, as part of the Adriatic Line project.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2020**: The testing and development of hydrogen technologies are expected to contribute to local development and employment opportunities, as stated by Lorenzo Simonelli, Chairman & CEO of Baker Hughes.
