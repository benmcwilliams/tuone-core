## ITA-00681-00266

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ferrari Facility in Maranello, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2022**: Ferrari announces plans to electrify 60% of its models by 2026, with a goal of 80% by 2030. The facility will produce electric motors and battery modules, with an expansion planned for an “e-building” to support this production.
- **July 2022**: Ferrari begins installation of a solar photovoltaic system on the roofs of its factory buildings, which will produce 1,626,802 kWh annually.
- **June 2023**: Ferrari aims to complete the new production facility (e-building) in mid-2024, which will manufacture electric supercars and next-generation powertrains.
- **March 2024**: Ferrari expands its battery partnership with SK On to innovate in battery cell technology, continuing to develop electric and hybrid vehicles.
- **June 2024**: The e-building is inaugurated, marking the start of production for the first electric model and continuing production of internal combustion engine (ICE) vehicles and hybrids. The facility has a total area of 42,500 square meters and can potentially expand annual production capacity to approximately 20,000 cars.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2022**: Investment in the e-building is part of Ferrari's strategic plan to enhance electrification and production capabilities.
- **July 2022**: The solar PV system installation represents a significant investment in renewable energy, aimed at reducing CO2 emissions by 8,500 tonnes over 25 years.
- **March 2024**: The memorandum of understanding with SK On indicates ongoing investment in battery technology development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2022**: Announcement of plans for 60% electrification by 2026 and the introduction of 15 new models.
- **July 2022**: Start of solar PV system installation.
- **June 2023**: Announcement of the e-building's completion target for mid-2024.
- **March 2024**: Signing of the MoU with SK On for battery technology development.
- **June 2024**: Official opening of the e-building, marking a significant milestone in Ferrari's transition to electric vehicles.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2024**: The e-building employs over 300 workers, contributing to job creation as Ferrari expands its production capabilities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Ferrari operates the Maranello facility independently, with no indication of joint ventures or external ownership. The facility is fully owned by Ferrari, which continues to develop its own technologies and production processes. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic initiatives at the Ferrari facility in Maranello, Italy, focusing on its transition towards electrification and sustainable production practices.
