### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fiat Chrysler Facility in Melfi, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09-10-2018**: Fiat Chrysler (FCA) prepares the Melfi plant for the production of the Jeep Renegade plug-in hybrid (PHEV). Pre-production is scheduled to start in 2019, with market launch planned for 2020. The investment includes modernization of the plant and training for workers.
- **29-11-2018**: FCA announces a broader investment plan of over 5 billion euros for Italian facilities, including Melfi, to support electric vehicle production. The Melfi plant is specifically set for the Jeep Renegade PHEV production.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **29-11-2018**: FCA's investment plan includes a total of 9 billion euros for electric vehicles and hybrids by 2022, with specific allocations for the Melfi plant to support the production of the Jeep Renegade PHEV.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **31-10-2019**: FCA and PSA agree on a merger, which may impact the Melfi facility. The merger aims to create synergies in electric mobility and production.
- **16-03-2020**: FCA announces a temporary shutdown of the Melfi plant due to the COVID-19 pandemic, affecting the production of the Jeep Renegade PHEV.

#### 4. Labour
- **31-10-2019**: Reports indicate that over half of the 7,300 employees at the Melfi plant were on short-time work, with estimates suggesting around 10,000 employees may be superfluous due to the merger and restructuring.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **31-10-2019**: Following the merger agreement between FCA and PSA, the ownership structure of the Melfi facility is expected to transition into a 50:50 joint venture between the two companies, although specific details on the ownership of the Melfi plant post-merger are not explicitly stated in the articles.
