## ITA-00704-02453

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Stellantis Group Facility in Melfi, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Production**: The Melfi plant currently produces the Jeep Renegade, Fiat 500X, and the current generation Jeep Compass. The Renegade is also available as a plug-in hybrid.
- **Future Production**: Starting in 2024, the facility will produce five electric vehicle (EV) models based on the STLA Medium platform, including:
  - A DS model (likely the DS 9 Crossback)
  - A successor to the DS 7 Crossback
  - An electric version of the Jeep Compass
  - An electric successor to the Opel Insignia
  - A Lancia model named Aurelia
- **Production Capacity**: The plant is set to increase its electric car production significantly, with the introduction of these new models.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Plans**: Stellantis announced a €2 billion investment in its Italian plants in 2025, which includes the Melfi facility. This investment aims to support the production of new electric and hybrid models.
- **Platform Utilization**: The Melfi plant will utilize the STLA Medium platform for the upcoming electric models, which is designed for C and D segments with battery capacities ranging from 87 to 104 kWh.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2022**: Rumors emerged about the production of four new electric models at the Melfi plant starting in 2024.
- **September 2022**: Stellantis confirmed that the Melfi plant would house the new STLA Medium electric platform.
- **October 2023**: Stellantis announced that the Melfi plant would produce a total of five electric models starting in 2024.
- **February 2024**: The Lancia Ypsilon was unveiled as the brand's first electric model, set to launch in mid-2025, with production planned at the Melfi facility for future models.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The expansion into electric vehicle production is expected to create new jobs, although specific numbers have not been disclosed. The transition to electric models may also lead to job adjustments as production of current models is phased out.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The Melfi facility is fully owned by Stellantis, which was formed from the merger of Fiat Chrysler Automobiles and PSA Group. The plant has been part of the Stellantis production network since the merger.
