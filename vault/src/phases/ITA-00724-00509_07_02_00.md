### Maserati Facility in Mirafiori, Italy - Evolutionary Timeline

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Maserati facility in Mirafiori produces Maserati models, primarily focusing on the electric versions of the GranTurismo and Grecale. The production of the Quattroporte Folgore electric sedan was announced for 2025 but has been halted as of January 2024. The current combustion engine generation of the Quattroporte has been in the market since 2013.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - There are indications of financial pressure on Maserati, as the company has requested suppliers to reduce their prices by six percent, reflecting a need to manage costs amid uncertain developments in their electric vehicle lineup.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **January 2024:** Maserati halts development of the Quattroporte Folgore electric sedan. The electric versions of the GranTurismo and Grecale, initially planned for delivery in 2023, have also faced delays, with the first deliveries of the electric GranTurismo now underway and the Grecale expected to be delivered in the second quarter of 2024.

4. **Labour:**
   - From February 12 to March 3, 2024, approximately 2,250 employees at the Mirafiori plant will be put on short-time work due to weak demand. This includes 1,250 workers involved in the production of the electric Fiat 500 and 1,000 workers manufacturing Maserati models, primarily those with petrol engines.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The Maserati facility in Mirafiori is owned by Stellantis, the parent company of Maserati. There is no indication of joint ventures or shared ownership in the information provided.
