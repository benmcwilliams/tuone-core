## ITA-00724-02453

### Evolutionary Timeline of Stellantis Group Facility in Mirafiori, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2018**: Fiat Chrysler Automobiles (FCA) announced an investment plan of over €5 billion to electrify its Italian production facilities, including the Mirafiori plant, which will produce a compact electric vehicle platform, starting with the electric Fiat 500.
- **July 2019**: FCA confirmed a €700 million investment to produce the electric Fiat 500, with an annual production capacity of 80,000 units starting in Q2 2020.
- **January 2020**: Production lines for the electric Fiat 500 were completed, and pre-series production began.
- **April 2021**: Production of the electric Fiat 500 was reduced from 300 to 210 units per day due to battery supply issues.
- **September 2022**: Stellantis announced plans to produce hybrid transmissions at Mirafiori, with production starting in the second half of 2024.
- **September 2023**: Stellantis opened a battery technology center at Mirafiori, investing €40 million to develop and test battery packs and modules.
- **March 2024**: Stellantis announced plans to produce a more affordable version of the electric Fiat 500, investing €100 million for platform revisions.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2018**: FCA's investment plan included over €5 billion for electrification across Italian plants, including Mirafiori.
- **July 2019**: The €700 million investment for the electric Fiat 500 production line was confirmed.
- **October 2019**: FCA announced an initial investment of €50 million for a battery assembly center at Mirafiori.
- **September 2022**: Stellantis announced further investments for hybrid transmission production.
- **September 2023**: The opening of the battery technology center involved a €40 million investment.
- **March 2024**: Stellantis announced an additional €100 million investment for the Fiat 500's platform revision.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2020**: Production of the electric Fiat 500 was set to begin, but was delayed due to the COVID-19 pandemic.
- **June 2020**: The electric Fiat 500 was officially launched.
- **April 2021**: Production of the electric 500 was reduced due to battery supply issues.
- **September 2022**: Announcement of hybrid transmission production starting in 2024.
- **September 2023**: Opening of the battery technology center.
- **March 2024**: Plans for a more affordable electric Fiat 500 were announced.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2019**: The electric Fiat 500 production was expected to employ around 1,200 people.
- **April 2021**: The reduction in production due to battery supply issues raised concerns about job security for temporary workers.
- **February 2024**: Stellantis announced short-time work for 2,250 employees due to weak demand for the electric Fiat 500 and Maserati models.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: The Mirafiori facility is fully owned by Stellantis, following the merger of FCA and PSA Group, which formed Stellantis in January 2021. The facility operates under the Stellantis brand, focusing on electric and hybrid vehicle production.
