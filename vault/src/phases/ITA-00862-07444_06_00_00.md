### Evolutionary Timeline of the Innovation Network Corporation of Japan (INCJ) Facility in Piedmont, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2013**: The facility, under the joint ownership of Mitsubishi Corporation, INCJ, and Solar Ventures, operates as Solar Holding, which has a photovoltaic (PV) portfolio of 42 MWp across 19 plants located in Piedmont, Sardinia, and Apulia. The energy production capacity is sufficient to meet the energy needs of approximately 20,000 households.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2013**: The acquisition of Solar Holding marks the first investment in Italy for both Mitsubishi and INCJ. The companies plan to acquire an additional 100 – 150 MWp of PV plants in Italy to enhance their presence in the solar market.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2013**: The acquisition of Solar Holding is completed, with Mitsubishi owning 50%, INCJ acquiring 35%, and Solar Ventures holding 15%. This transaction is seen as a strategic move to develop and mature the Italian solar market.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2013**: While specific job creation or loss figures are not mentioned, the partnership aims to leverage local expertise and management capabilities, which may imply potential job creation in the operational phase of the plants.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2013**: The facility is not exclusively owned by INCJ; it is part of a joint venture with Mitsubishi Corporation (50% ownership), INCJ (35% ownership), and Solar Ventures (15% ownership).
