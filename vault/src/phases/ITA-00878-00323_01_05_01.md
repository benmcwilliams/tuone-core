## ITA-00878-00323

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Glencore Facility in Portovesme, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10/2023**: Glencore and Li-Cycle announced plans to modernize the existing Portovesme complex, which has been operational since 1929, to process recycled batteries into new battery materials such as nickel, cobalt, and lithium. The facility is expected to have a processing capacity of 50,000 to 70,000 tonnes of black mass annually, sufficient for producing up to 36 GWh of new lithium-ion batteries.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **11/2022**: Glencore completed a $200 million investment in Li-Cycle through the purchase of a five-year convertible note, establishing a strategic partnership that supports the development of the Portovesme Hub.
- **05/2023**: Glencore and Li-Cycle announced plans for a definitive feasibility study (DFS) for the Portovesme Hub, expected to be completed by mid-2024. The project anticipates competitive long-term financing from Glencore to fund Li-Cycle’s share of the capital investment.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05/2023**: Announcement of the Portovesme Hub project, with plans to commence operations by late 2026 or early 2027, subject to final investment approval.
- **11/2023**: Glencore decided to build a pilot battery recycling plant outside of Italy, but confirmed that the feasibility study for the larger hub project in Portovesme is ongoing and remains focused on the site.

#### 4. Labour
- **05/2023**: The Portovesme Hub is expected to leverage the existing experienced workforce at the metallurgical complex, although specific job creation or loss figures have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **05/2023**: Glencore and Li-Cycle plan to operate the Portovesme Hub through a 50:50 joint venture, indicating that the facility will not be exclusively owned by Glencore but rather shared with Li-Cycle.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic decisions regarding the Glencore facility in Portovesme, Italy, as reported in the articles.

### Technology and Operations
- **Article 1 (10-05-2023)**: "The Portovesme complex, which has been in operation since 1929, will be modernised to process recycled batteries into new battery materials such as nickel, cobalt, and lithium."
- **Article 2 (11-05-2023)**: "The Portovesme Hub would produce critical battery materials, including nickel, cobalt and lithium from recycled battery content."
- **Article 3 (24-11-2023)**: "Glencore has decided to build a pilot project for an electric vehicle (EV) battery recycling plant outside of Italy, but still has its eye on the island of Sardinia for a larger facility."

### Investment and Development
- **Article 2 (11-05-2023)**: "In 2022, Li-Cycle executed long-term commercial agreements with Glencore and also announced that Glencore concurrently completed its previously announced $200-million investment in Li-Cycle through the purchase of a five-year convertible note."
- **Article 3 (24-11-2023)**: "The company also confirmed in the letter its intent to carry on with the restructuring of its existing industrial activities in Portovesme."

### Project Status and Milestones
- **Article 1 (10-05-2023)**: "Subject to final investment approval, the plant is expected to commence operations by the end of 2026 or early 2027."
- **Article 2 (11-05-2023)**: "Subject to a final investment decision, the project will proceed to construction with commissioning of the Portovesme Hub expected to commence in late 2026 to early 2027."
- **Article 3 (24-11-2023)**: "The definite feasibility study is ongoing and remains focused on Portovesme."

### Labour
- **Article 3 (24-11-2023)**: "The CGIL, CISL and UIL labour unions criticized the lack of firm deadlines for the environmental assessment procedure for the pilot plant."

### Ownership Structure
- **Article 1 (10-05-2023)**: "The two companies will operate the facility through a 50:50 joint venture."
- **Article 2 (11-05-2023)**: "Glencore and Li-Cycle also anticipate forming a 50/50 joint venture that would repurpose part of the existing Glencore metallurgical complex in Portovesme."
