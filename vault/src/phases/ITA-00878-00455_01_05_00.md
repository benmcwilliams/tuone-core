## ITA-00878-00455

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Li-Cycle Facility in Portovesme, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10/2023**: Li-Cycle and Glencore announced plans to build the Portovesme Hub, which will utilize Li-Cycle’s hydrometallurgical technology to process black mass from lithium-ion batteries into new battery materials, including nickel, cobalt, and lithium. The facility is expected to have a processing capacity of 50,000 to 70,000 tonnes of black mass annually, sufficient for producing up to 36 GWh of new lithium-ion batteries.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **11/2022**: Glencore completed a $200 million investment in Li-Cycle through a convertible note, which is part of a strategic partnership to support the development of the Portovesme Hub.
- **05/2023**: The companies plan to conduct a definitive feasibility study (DFS) for the Portovesme Hub, expected to be completed by mid-2024. The project will also benefit from competitive long-term financing from Glencore to fund Li-Cycle’s share of the capital investment.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05/2023**: Announcement of the Portovesme Hub project, with a definitive feasibility study to commence within 60 days. The project is expected to begin construction following a final investment decision, with operations anticipated to start by late 2026 or early 2027.
- **09/2023**: Li-Cycle confirmed that the Portovesme Hub is expected to begin processing black mass in the first half of 2024, with full build-out planned for later phases.

#### 4. Labour
- **05/2023**: The Portovesme Hub is expected to create jobs as part of the repurposing of the existing Glencore metallurgical complex, although specific job creation numbers have not been disclosed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **05/2023**: The facility will operate as a 50:50 joint venture between Li-Cycle and Glencore, indicating that neither company has exclusive ownership of the facility.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic decisions regarding the Li-Cycle facility in Portovesme, Italy, highlighting its role in the European battery recycling landscape.

### Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: The Portovesme Hub is expected to process "50,000 to 70,000 tonnes of black mass annually" (Article 1, 10-05-2023) while another source states it will have a capacity of "up to 70,000 tonnes per year" (Article 4, 26-09-2023).
- **Technology Utilization**: The facility will utilize Li-Cycle’s hydrometallurgical technology to extract battery materials (Article 2, 11-05-2023), and it is noted that the Portovesme site will be repurposed from an existing Glencore metallurgical complex (Article 2, 11-05-2023).

### Investment and Development
- **Investment Amounts**: Glencore has previously invested "200 million dollars" in Li-Cycle (Article 2, 11-05-2023) and is providing an additional "75 million dollars" to support Li-Cycle during a financial bottleneck (Article 7, 13-03-2024).
- **Funding Structure**: The project anticipates "competitive long-term financing from Glencore" to fund Li-Cycle’s share of the capital investment (Article 2, 11-05-2023).

### Project Status and Milestones
- **Feasibility Study**: A definitive feasibility study (DFS) is expected to be completed by "mid-2024" (Article 2, 11-05-2023).
- **Operational Timeline**: The Portovesme Hub is expected to commence operations by "the end of 2026 or early 2027" (Article 1, 10-05-2023) while another article mentions that processing is expected to begin in "the first half of 2024" (Article 5, 27-09-2023).

### Labour
- **Job Creation**: The articles do not specify the exact number of jobs created at the Portovesme Hub, but it is implied that the facility will leverage an "experienced workforce" from the existing Glencore site (Article 2, 11-05-2023).

### Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture**: The facility will be operated through a "50:50 joint venture" between Glencore and Li-Cycle (Article 1, 10-05-2023; Article 2, 11-05-2023).
