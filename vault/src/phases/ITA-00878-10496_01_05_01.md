## ITA-00878-10496

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Glencore Li-Cycle Facility in Portovesme, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10/2023**: Glencore and Li-Cycle announced plans to build Europe’s largest battery recycling plant at the Portovesme complex, which has been operational since 1929. The facility will be modernized to process recycled batteries into new battery materials, including nickel, cobalt, and lithium. The expected processing capacity is between 50,000 to 70,000 tonnes of black mass annually, sufficient for producing up to 36 GWh of new lithium-ion batteries.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05/2023**: Glencore and Li-Cycle announced a strategic partnership, with Glencore having previously invested $200 million in Li-Cycle through a convertible note. The project at Portovesme is expected to benefit from Glencore's existing infrastructure, which will enable a cost-efficient development plan.
- **09/2023**: Glencore provided an additional liquidity injection of $75 million to Li-Cycle to support its operations and ongoing projects, including the Portovesme Hub.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05/2023**: Announcement of the feasibility study for the Portovesme Hub, expected to be completed by mid-2024. The final investment decision is anticipated to be made in 2024, with construction expected to commence shortly thereafter.
- **09/2023**: Phase 1 of the Portovesme Hub is expected to begin processing black mass in the first half of 2024, with full operational capacity anticipated by late 2026 or early 2027.

#### 4. Labour
- **05/2023**: The project is expected to create jobs, leveraging the existing experienced workforce at the Portovesme site, although specific job creation numbers have not been disclosed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **05/2023**: The facility will operate as a 50:50 joint venture between Glencore and Li-Cycle, indicating that both companies will share ownership and operational responsibilities for the Portovesme Hub.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic decisions regarding the Glencore Li-Cycle facility in Portovesme, Italy, highlighting its evolution in technology, investment, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure.
