## ITA-00978-00399

### Italvolt Facility Evolution Timeline - Scarmagno, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2021**: Italvolt announces plans to build a gigafactory for electric car battery cells in Scarmagno, Italy, with an initial production capacity of **45 GWh**, potentially expandable to **70 GWh**. The factory will produce MNC (nickel, manganese, cobalt) lithium-ion battery cells with silicon anodes and is considering solid-state cells. The facility will have over **eight production lines**, each with a capacity of **6 GWh**.
- **April 2022**: Italvolt confirms that production is expected to start in **2025**, with mass production aimed for **2027-2028**. The factory will focus on battery cells, not packs, and will include an R&D center.
- **January 2023**: Italvolt enters a strategic collaboration with StoreDot to manufacture ultra-fast charging (XFC) battery cells, marking the first production of XFC cells in Europe.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2021**: Italvolt announces an investment of approximately **4 billion euros** for the factory. The Italian government and local authorities are reported to support the project, although specific funding details are not disclosed.
- **June 2021**: The Italian government earmarks **600 million euros** from the EU reconstruction fund for the factory, which is expected to create **500 new jobs** by **2030**. Total investments, including contributions from industrial and financial partners, are projected to exceed **1 billion euros**.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2021**: Italvolt announces the establishment of the factory in Scarmagno, Piedmont, and signs a purchase contract for the land.
- **September 2021**: Italvolt completes the land purchase for the factory site, which covers **100 hectares**.
- **April 2022**: Italvolt expects to receive building permits by **spring 2023** to commence construction in the second half of **2022**.
- **January 2023**: Italvolt faces reported delays in the project timeline, with no specific date provided for the start of production.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2021**: Italvolt states that the factory is expected to employ **4,000 people**.
- **June 2021**: The announcement of **500 new jobs** by **2030** is made in conjunction with the Italian government's funding commitment.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2021**: Italvolt is described as an independent company founded by Lars Carlstrom, with no mention of joint ventures or external ownership. The company emphasizes its independence and transparency regarding public funding discussions.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones of the Italvolt facility in Scarmagno, Italy, focusing on its technology, investments, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
