### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fiat Chrysler Facility in Turin, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **06/2019**: Fiat previews the all-electric Concept Centoventi and announces plans for a new electric Fiat 500, with production set to begin in 2020. The facility is being prepared for a small electric car platform.
- **10/2019**: Fiat Chrysler announces the construction of a battery assembly center at the Mirafiori plant, with an initial investment of €50 million. The center will support the production of the electric Fiat 500 and future electric models.
- **01/2020**: Maserati begins testing its first all-electric drive system, which will be fitted to models produced in Turin, including the GranTurismo and GranCabrio.
- **02/2020**: Fiat Chrysler starts pre-series production of the electric Fiat 500 at the Mirafiori plant, with an annual production capacity of 80,000 units.
- **02/2020**: The facility is set to become the hub for FCA's electrification strategy in Europe, with plans for a large photovoltaic system and extensive charging infrastructure.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **06/2019**: Fiat Chrysler announces an investment of over €5 billion in the electrification of its Italian plants by 2021, including the Mirafiori facility.
- **10/2019**: The initial investment of €50 million for the battery assembly center is confirmed.
- **02/2020**: An additional investment of approximately €700 million is reported for the production line of the electric Fiat 500.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **06/2019**: Announcement of the electric Fiat 500 and the Concept Centoventi.
- **10/2019**: Announcement of the battery assembly center at Mirafiori.
- **02/2020**: Start of pre-series production for the electric Fiat 500, with market launch scheduled for June 2020.

#### 4. Labour
- **10/2019**: The production of the electric Fiat 500 is expected to create around 1,200 jobs at the Mirafiori plant.
- **02/2020**: The facility's expansion and new production lines are anticipated to contribute to job creation in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Fiat Chrysler Automobiles (FCA) owns the Mirafiori facility exclusively, as it is part of the company's operations in Italy. There is no indication of joint ventures or shared ownership in the articles provided.
