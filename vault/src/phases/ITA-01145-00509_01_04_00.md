## ITA-01145-00509

### Maserati Facility in Turin, Italy - Evolutionary Timeline

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2020**: Maserati began testing its first all-electric drive system, intended for the GranTurismo and GranCabrio models, which are to be produced in Turin. The facility is also involved in building a battery assembly center.
- **February 2020**: Maserati announced plans to electrify all new models, starting with a hybrid version of the Ghibli and the all-electric GranTurismo and GranCabrio, set to begin production in 2021.
- **March 2020**: Production of the hybrid Maserati Ghibli commenced, alongside the pre-series production of the electric Fiat 500 at the Mirafiori plant.
- **January 2024**: Maserati halted development of the Quattroporte Folgore electric sedan, and the delivery of electric GranTurismo and Grecale models, originally planned for 2023, was delayed.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2020**: Maserati announced an investment of 800 million euros for the production of electric vehicles, which includes the GranTurismo and GranCabrio.
- **March 2020**: Fiat Chrysler (FCA) reported a total investment of five billion euros in its Italian plants by 2021, which includes the development of the battery assembly center in Turin.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2020**: Testing of all-electric drive systems began.
- **February 2020**: Production of the hybrid Ghibli started, and plans for the GranTurismo and GranCabrio EVs were confirmed.
- **March 2020**: Pre-series production of the electric Fiat 500 commenced.
- **March 2020**: The premiere of Maserati's first electrified sports car was postponed to September 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic.
- **January 2024**: Maserati announced the halt of development for the Quattroporte Folgore and delays in the electric GranTurismo and Grecale deliveries.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2024**: Due to weak demand, Stellantis announced that approximately 2,250 employees at the Mirafiori plant would be put on short-time work, affecting 1,250 workers on the electric Fiat 500 line and 1,000 workers on Maserati models.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Maserati is a subsidiary of the Italian-American group Fiat Chrysler (now part of Stellantis), indicating that the facility is not under exclusive ownership but rather part of a larger corporate structure.
