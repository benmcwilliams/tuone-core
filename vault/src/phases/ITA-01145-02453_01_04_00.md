## ITA-01145-02453

### Evolutionary Timeline of Stellantis Group Facility in Turin, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2023**: Stellantis opened its first battery technology centre at the Mirafiori plant in Turin. The facility focuses on developing and testing battery packs, modules, cells, and software for future electric car models. It spans 8,000 square meters and includes 32 climate-controlled test cells, making it the largest battery technology centre in Italy and one of the largest in Europe.
- **November 2023**: Stellantis inaugurated the 'Sustainera Circular Economy Hub' at the Mirafiori plant, dedicated to remanufacturing motors, gearboxes, and high-voltage batteries for electric vehicles (EVs), as well as vehicle overhauling and dismantling. The facility covers 73,000 square meters and aims to manage over 50,000 remanufactured parts by 2025, increasing to 150,000 by 2030.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2023**: Stellantis invested €40 million in the battery technology centre to enhance its capabilities for developing and testing battery technologies.
- **November 2023**: An additional investment of €40 million was made for the Sustainera Circular Economy Hub, which aims to industrialize the recovery and sustainable reuse of materials.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2023**: Opening of the battery technology centre.
- **November 2023**: Inauguration of the Sustainera Circular Economy Hub.
- **March 2024**: The Mirafiori plant operated on a one-shift basis due to weak demand for the electric Fiat 500 and Maserati models, affecting production schedules.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2023**: The battery technology centre employs over 100 employees.
- **November 2023**: The Sustainera Circular Economy Hub currently employs 170 upskilled employees, with plans to expand to approximately 550 by 2025.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Stellantis owns the Mirafiori facility exclusively, as indicated by the company's direct investments and operational control over the battery technology centre and the Sustainera Circular Economy Hub.

This timeline captures the significant developments and strategic investments made by Stellantis at its Turin facility, reflecting its commitment to advancing clean technology and sustainable practices in the automotive industry.
