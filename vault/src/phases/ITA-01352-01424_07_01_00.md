## ITA-01352-01424

### Evolutionary Timeline of the ACC Facility in Termoli, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2022**: Stellantis announced plans to convert its existing engine plant in Termoli into a battery factory under the Automotive Cells Company (ACC) joint venture. The facility is expected to have a production capacity of **40 GWh** by 2030.
- **April 2022**: ACC signed a long-term supply agreement with Umicore for cathode materials, indicating the facility's role in supporting battery production for electric vehicles.
- **October 2022**: ACC selected Comau and Siemens for automation and production technology, indicating preparations for the facility's operational setup.
- **March 2023**: ACC's IT infrastructure for the Termoli plant was to be managed by Atos, ensuring operational efficiency for the planned battery production.
- **February 2024**: The facility was still on track for production to start in **2026**, with a capacity of **40 GWh** planned.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2022**: The investment for the Termoli facility was estimated at around **€2 billion**, including **€370 million** in public EU funds from the Corona Recovery and Resilience Facility.
- **February 2024**: ACC secured a loan of **€4.4 billion** to finance the construction of its battery factories, including the one in Termoli, indicating significant financial backing for the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2022**: Announcement of the conversion of the engine plant to a battery factory.
- **2024**: Construction work was paused to switch from nickel-based cell chemistry to more cost-effective technologies due to declining demand for electric vehicles.
- **August 2024**: The Italian government pressured Stellantis to commit to the ACC project, indicating uncertainty about the project's future.
- **September 2024**: Italy withdrew its funding commitment for the Termoli facility, redistributing **€200 million** in EU funds earmarked for the project.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2022**: The transition to battery production was expected to create around **2,000 jobs** by 2030, although there were concerns about layoffs of up to **1,000 employees** during the transition period as engine production was phased out.
- **June 2024**: The pause in construction raised concerns about job security and future employment at the facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2022**: The ACC joint venture is owned by Stellantis, TotalEnergies/Saft, and Mercedes-Benz, with Stellantis being the largest shareholder.
- **February 2024**: Following a capital increase, the ownership structure was reported as **45% Stellantis**, **30% Mercedes-Benz**, and **25% TotalEnergies/Saft**.

This timeline captures the key developments and challenges faced by the ACC facility in Termoli, Italy, highlighting its strategic importance in the European battery production landscape.
