## ITA-01352-02453

### Evolutionary Timeline of Stellantis Group Facility in Termoli, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **08/2021**: Stellantis announces plans to convert its existing engine plant in Termoli into a battery factory as part of its electrification strategy. The facility is expected to support the production of batteries for electric vehicles.
- **03/2022**: Stellantis confirms that the new battery factory in Termoli will have a capacity of 40 GWh, contributing to a total of 120 GWh across three ACC plants in Europe.
- **07/2024**: Construction work at the Termoli battery factory is paused to switch from nickel-based cell chemistry to more cost-effective battery technologies.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **08/2021**: Stellantis announces a €30 billion investment in electrification, which includes the development of the Termoli facility.
- **03/2022**: The investment for the conversion of the Termoli engine plant into a battery factory is part of a larger €2 billion investment plan, which includes €370 million in public EU funds.
- **08/2024**: The Italian government pressures Stellantis to commit to the ACC battery cell factory in Termoli, highlighting the urgency of the investment due to the limited availability of EU funds.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **08/2021**: Announcement of the agreement with the Italian government to develop the Termoli plant into a battery factory.
- **03/2022**: Official confirmation of the plans to convert the engine plant into a battery factory, with production expected to start by 2026.
- **07/2024**: Work on the Termoli battery factory is put on hold to improve plans for the production facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **03/2022**: The conversion of the engine plant is expected to lead to job losses, with up to 1,000 employees potentially laid off before the battery production starts in 2026. However, it is anticipated that around 2,000 jobs will be created by 2030.
- **07/2024**: The union representatives express concerns about temporary layoffs during the transition period from engine production to battery production.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **03/2022**: The battery factory in Termoli will be operated under the Automotive Cells Company (ACC), a joint venture between Stellantis, TotalEnergies, and Mercedes-Benz. Stellantis is the largest shareholder in ACC.

This timeline captures the key developments and changes related to the Stellantis Group facility in Termoli, Italy, focusing on its transition from an engine plant to a battery manufacturing facility as part of the company's broader electrification strategy.
