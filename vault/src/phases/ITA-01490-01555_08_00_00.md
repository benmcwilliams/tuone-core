### Evolutionary Timeline of the Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners Facility in Sardinia, Italy

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Project Name:** Ichnusa Wind Power Project
   - **Production Capacity:** 504 MW
   - **Technology:** Floating wind power
   - **Location:** Approximately 35 km off the coast of Sardinia
   - **Significance:** This facility is part of a larger portfolio of almost 3 GW of floating wind projects in Italy, aimed at contributing to the Italian government's target of 20 GW of operational wind by 2030.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Ownership Structure:** The project is developed by Copenhagen Offshore Partners (COP) on behalf of Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners, with involvement from GreenIT, a joint venture owned 51% by Plenitude (Eni) and 49% by CDP Equity.
   - **Local Supply Chain Development:** The project is designed to support local supply chain development by the second half of the decade.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Milestone Achieved:** On June 12, 2023, COP submitted the Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) for the Ichnusa Wind Power Project to the Italian Ministry of Environment and Energy Security. This marks the project's advancement as Italy's third floating wind project to reach EIA submission and the first off the Sardinian coast.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Job Creation:** While specific job creation figures were not mentioned in the article, the project is expected to support local supply chain development, which may lead to job creation in the region.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Ownership Details:** The facility is not solely owned by Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners; it is part of a joint venture with GreenIT, which includes Plenitude (Eni) and CDP Equity.
