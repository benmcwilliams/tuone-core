### Evolutionary Timeline of the Energy Dome Facility in Sardinia, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10-12-2021**: Energy Dome introduces a modular, scalable system for utility-scale, long-term electricity storage based on compressed CO2. The system can store up to 200 MWh of energy and operates with a round-trip efficiency of up to 80%. The first pilot project in Sardinia is planned with a capacity of 2 MW/4 MWh.
- **29-09-2022**: Energy Dome's first commercial demonstration facility is confirmed to be under construction in Sardinia, utilizing the CO2 Battery technology.
- **06-12-2023**: Announcement of a standard frame 20MW/200MWh CO2 Battery project in Sardinia, designed to supply energy for 10 consecutive hours. The facility is expected to have a Commercial Operation Date in the third quarter of 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10-12-2021**: Energy Dome raises $11 million through a Series A funding round to develop the pilot project in Sardinia.
- **28-04-2023**: Energy Dome closes a €40 million Series B funding round, which will be used to commercialize the technology and expand into key markets.
- **06-12-2023**: Funding commitments for the 20MW/200MWh project in Sardinia include a project-level grant of up to €35 million from Breakthrough Energy Catalyst and €25 million in venture debt financing from the European Investment Bank.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **10-12-2021**: Announcement of the pilot project in Sardinia, with testing for grid electricity storage expected in early 2022.
- **29-09-2022**: Confirmation that the first commercial demonstration facility is under construction in Sardinia.
- **06-12-2023**: Announcement of funding for the construction of the 20MW/200MWh CO2 Battery project, with a projected operational date in Q3 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **10-12-2021**: The pilot project in Sardinia is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers are not detailed in the articles.
- **28-04-2023**: The expansion and commercialization efforts are anticipated to lead to job creation, but exact figures are not provided.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **10-12-2021**: Energy Dome is developing the facility in partnership with Italian utility A2A for the pilot project.
- **29-09-2022**: The facility is under Energy Dome's ownership, with plans for further developments in collaboration with Ørsted for additional projects.
