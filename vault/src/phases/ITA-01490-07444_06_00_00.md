### Evolutionary Timeline of the Innovation Network Corporation of Japan (INCJ) Facility in Sardinia, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2013**: The facility, under the joint ownership of Mitsubishi Corporation, INCJ, and Solar Ventures, is part of Solar Holding, which has a photovoltaic (PV) portfolio of 42 MWp in Italy. The facility's energy production capacity is stated to meet the energy needs of approximately 20,000 households. Solar Holding operates 19 plants across the regions of Piedmont, Sardinia, and Apulia.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2013**: The acquisition of Solar Holding marks the first investment in Italy for both Mitsubishi and INCJ. The companies plan to acquire an additional 100 – 150 MWp of PV plants in Italy, aiming to become one of the largest players in the Italian solar market. INCJ has the capacity to invest up to ¥2 trillion (approximately US$21 million).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2013**: The acquisition of Solar Holding is completed, with Mitsubishi owning 50%, INCJ 35%, and Solar Ventures 15%. This transaction is seen as a strategic move to enhance the development of the Italian solar market.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with this investment or the operations of the facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2013**: The facility is not solely owned by INCJ; it is part of a joint venture where Mitsubishi Corporation holds a 50% stake, INCJ holds 35%, and Solar Ventures holds 15%.
