## ITA-02264-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Sicily, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2024**: Iberdrola signed an agreement with Ib Vogt for the construction of the Fenix project, a 245MW solar photovoltaic (PV) plant in Sicily. The project has the potential for a future expansion of an additional 60MW, bringing the total capacity to 305MW. The facility will utilize bifacial solar panels and is expected to generate approximately 400 GWh of electricity annually, sufficient to power around 140,000 households.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2024**: Iberdrola secured a €100 million ($103 million) green loan from the European Investment Bank (EIB) to finance the construction of the photovoltaic plant in Sicily. The EIB will cover 70% of the project cost, with SACE's Archimede guarantee ensuring favorable market conditions for up to 25 years. The total investment required for the project is estimated at €300 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2024**: Construction of the Fenix project is set to begin.
- **2025**: The facility is expected to become operational.
- **2024**: Iberdrola plans to have a total of 330MW of solar PV under construction in Italy, including the Fenix project, with an additional 40MW expected to start in early 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2024**: The Fenix project is anticipated to create approximately 500 jobs during the construction phase and over 100 permanent jobs during the operational phase.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2024**: Iberdrola is the primary developer of the Fenix project, collaborating with Ib Vogt for construction and future operations and maintenance. There is no indication of joint ownership; thus, it can be inferred that Iberdrola retains full ownership of the facility.
