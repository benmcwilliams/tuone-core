## ITA-02264-01510

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Canadian Solar Facility in Sicily, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2019**: Canadian Solar's project in Sicily is a large-scale solar PV scheme with a planned capacity of **195 MW**. The project is held under Big Fish SPV S.r.l., which Canadian Solar partially owns.
- **March 2020**: Canadian Solar announces a **151 MW** portfolio of 12 unsubsidized solar projects across Italy, including Sicily, utilizing Canadian Solar's HiKu CS3W-425 modules. The projects are expected to generate approximately **287 GWh** of solar power annually once operational.
- **May 2021**: Two additional PV projects in Sicily are planned with a total generation capacity of **12 MW**, set to sell electricity to Axpo Italia under a ten-year PPA.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2019**: Falck Renewables acquires a **70% stake** in Big Fish SPV S.r.l. for **€60,000**, with potential increases based on project capacity.
- **March 2020**: Canadian Solar secures a **€55 million** credit facility from Intesa San Paolo to fund the construction of the **151 MW** portfolio of solar projects.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2019**: The acquisition deal for the 195 MW project is set to complete by the end of July 2019, with plans to apply for building permits in Q4 2019.
- **March 2020**: Construction for the **151 MW** portfolio is expected to begin in the **third quarter of 2020**.
- **May 2021**: The two PV projects in Sicily are planned to sell electricity under a **20-year PPA**.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2020**: Canadian Solar's CEO emphasizes the company's commitment to investing in the Italian solar market, which may imply job creation, although specific job numbers are not mentioned.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2019**: Canadian Solar retains a **30% stake** in Big Fish SPV S.r.l. after the sale of a majority stake to Falck Renewables. This indicates a **joint venture** ownership structure for the 195 MW project.
- **March 2020**: The **151 MW** portfolio is fully owned by Canadian Solar, indicating **full ownership** of these projects.
