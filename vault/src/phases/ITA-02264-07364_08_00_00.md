## ITA-02264-07364

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Renexia Facility in Sicily, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2024**: Renexia, through its subsidiary Renext Solutions, has initiated the design phase for underwater substations as part of the 2.8 GW Med Wind floating offshore wind project. The project will be located off the coast of Trapani, covering an area of 850 square kilometers. The facility aims to generate approximately 9 TWh of electricity annually, sufficient to meet the energy needs of 3.4 million households. The project will utilize 18.8 MW wind turbines, reducing the total number of turbines from 190 to 148.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2024**: The design contract with Aker Solutions for the underwater substations marks a significant investment in the Med Wind project. This contract follows a previous agreement with Technip Energies for the Front-End Engineering Design (FEED) of floating foundations and moorings.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2024**: The design phase for the underwater substations has commenced, following oceanographic campaigns that identified suitable areas for the wind farm. The project is being developed in phases to reach its full capacity of 2.8 GW.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2024**: The construction of the wind farm is expected to create jobs in Sicily over the six years of construction and throughout the 25 years of maintenance activities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2024**: Renexia is the primary developer of the Med Wind project, with its subsidiary Renext Solutions managing the design aspects. There is no indication of joint ownership or partnerships affecting the ownership structure of the facility at this time. 

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Renexia facility in Sicily, focusing on its technological advancements, investments, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
