- **Production Focus**: The facility is involved in the production of green hydrogen through electrolysis powered by utility-scale solar energy.
- **Projected Costs**: The levelized cost of hydrogen (LCOH) is projected to drop to between €0.7 and €1.8/kg by 2030, and further to €0.3 and €0.9/kg by 2050. The current LCOH ranges from €0.031 to €0.081/kWh.

### Technology and Operations
- "The projected cost decrease will be the result of a simultaneous price drop for both electrolysis powered by large-scale PV and the CAPEX of solar itself."
- "The LCOH will decrease to between €0.020 and €0.054/kWh or €0.7 and €1.8/kg by 2030 and €0.010 and €0.027/kWh or €0.3 and €0.9/kg by 2050."

### Investment and Development
- "The average CAPEX for utility-scale solar was calculated under three different scenarios: A fast growth scenario in which it is predicted to fall from around €0.047/kWh currently to €0.027/kWh in 2030 and €0.013/kWh in 2050; a base growth scenario in which this value should drop to €0.031/kWh in 2030 and €0.019/kWh in 2050; and a slow growth scenario where it reaches only €0.036/kWh in 2030 and €0.025/kWh in 2050."
