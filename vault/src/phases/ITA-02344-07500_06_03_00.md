### Evolutionary Timeline of the Solertix Facility in Rome, Italy

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 01-06-2023
   - **Details:** The Solertix facility, originally a research and development startup focused on perovskite cell technology, was acquired by FuturaSun. The facility is part of the Organic Solar Center (CHOSE) at the University of Rome, specializing in third-generation photovoltaic (PV) cell research. FuturaSun aims to develop a stable, commercially viable perovskite product, which is noted for its potential to achieve higher efficiencies than traditional silicon-based cells.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 01-06-2023
   - **Details:** The financial details of the acquisition of Solertix by FuturaSun were not disclosed. However, FuturaSun has announced plans for significant manufacturing capacity expansions in Europe and China, including a 10GW solar cell plant in Huai’an, China, with an investment of US$163 million, and a 2GW module factory in Cittadella, Italy.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 01-06-2023
   - **Details:** The acquisition of Solertix marks a significant milestone for FuturaSun as it seeks to enhance its capabilities in perovskite technology. The facility is positioned to contribute to the development of high-efficiency PV cells, with a focus on overcoming the challenges of material stability and industrial production.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 01-06-2023
   - **Details:** Following the acquisition, FuturaSun plans to double the workforce at the Solertix facility, indicating a positive impact on job creation as the company aims to expand its R&D efforts in perovskite technology.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 01-06-2023
   - **Details:** FuturaSun has acquired Solertix, indicating that the facility is now under full ownership by FuturaSun, transitioning from its previous status as a startup affiliated with the University of Rome.
