## ITA-02485-02582

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Saras Facility in Sarroch, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2021**: Saras, in partnership with Enel Green Power, announced plans to set up a green hydrogen production facility at the Sarroch industrial site. The facility will utilize a **20 MW electrolyzer** powered by renewable energy produced on-site. The hydrogen produced will serve as a raw material for the Saras refinery.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2021**: The collaboration between Saras and Enel Green Power is part of a broader commitment to promote green hydrogen development in Italy. Specific investment amounts were not disclosed, but the project is positioned as a significant step towards decarbonizing the refining processes at the Saras facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2021**: A memorandum of intent was signed between Enel Green Power and Saras to develop the green hydrogen project. This marks the formal initiation of the project, confirming both companies' commitment to advancing green hydrogen technology.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2021**: While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned, the project is expected to contribute to job creation in the region as it develops new technologies and processes related to green hydrogen production.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2021**: The facility is a collaboration between Saras and Enel Green Power, indicating a **joint venture** structure for the green hydrogen project. Saras retains ownership of the Sarroch site, while Enel provides the renewable energy technology and expertise.

This timeline captures the key developments related to the Saras facility in Sarroch, Italy, focusing on its transition towards green hydrogen production and the collaborative efforts with Enel Green Power.

### Technology and Operations
- "A refinery of Italian oil product provider Saras will host a large scale electrolyzer that will produce hydrogen to be used as a raw material in the refinery itself." (Article 1, 17-02-2021)
- "Green energy production should be ensured by a 20 MW electrolyzer powered by renewable energy produced on-site." (Article 1, 17-02-2021)
- "Our Sarroch site is particularly suited–due to its size, location and structural characteristics–to develop and host a green hydrogen production project with clear potential for development and growth." (Article 1, 17-02-2021)
- "Enel Green Power is committed to implementing and developing projects for the production and use of 'green' or 'renewable' hydrogen, produced via electrolysis of water powered exclusively by renewable electricity." (Article 3, 19-02-2021)
