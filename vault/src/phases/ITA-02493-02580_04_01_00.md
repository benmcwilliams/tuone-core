## ITA-02493-02580

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Saipem Facility in Ravenna, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2021**: Saipem unveiled the SUISO technology, which integrates offshore green hydrogen production with oil and gas facilities. This technology combines renewable energy sources (floating wind, floating solar, and marine energy) to power electrolysers on existing offshore platforms for green hydrogen production. The facility is planned to be part of the AGNES energy hub off the coast of Ravenna.
- **October 2021**: The AGNES project was detailed, which will include two offshore wind farms (600MW total capacity), a 100MW floating solar system, and a 50MW hydrogen storage system. The project aims to be the first energy hub in Southern Europe.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2021**: Saipem, in collaboration with QINT’X, applied for a permit to install the AGNES energy hub, indicating a significant investment in renewable energy infrastructure off the coast of Ravenna.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2021**: Announcement of the SUISO technology and its application at the AGNES energy hub.
- **October 2021**: Application for state concession to install the AGNES project, which includes detailed plans for the wind farms and solar systems. The consortium began a wind resource assessment using an existing oil and gas platform, with surveys expected to start in the second quarter of the following year.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture**: The facility is part of a joint venture between Saipem and QINT’X for the development of the AGNES energy hub. There is no indication of exclusive ownership by Saipem.
