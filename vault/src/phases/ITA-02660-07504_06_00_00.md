### Evolutionary Timeline of the SunRay Renewable Energy Facility in Montalto di Castro, Italy

1. **Technology and Operations**
   - **December 2009**: SunRay Renewable Energy completed the first phase of the Montalto di Castro solar power plant, which has a production capacity of 24MW. The facility utilizes 78,720 SunPower solar panels and tracker systems. The project is part of a larger plan for an 85MW development, with aspirations for a full-scale installation reaching 100MW, making it one of the largest solar plants globally.

2. **Investment and Development**
   - **December 2009**: The construction of the 24MW plant involved an investment of €120 million. The project also included the design and construction of a 150MW substation by Terna SpA.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**
   - **September 2009**: Announcement of the 85MW project.
   - **November 30, 2009**: The first phase (24MW) was connected to the grid, completing construction several weeks ahead of schedule.

4. **Labour**
   - **December 2009**: More than 200 local workers were trained in advanced photovoltaic technology as part of the project, contributing to job creation in the community.

5. **Ownership Structure**
   - The facility is developed for SunRay Renewable Energy, indicating that SunRay is involved in the ownership and operational aspects of the facility, although the specific ownership structure (full ownership or joint venture) is not explicitly stated in the article.
