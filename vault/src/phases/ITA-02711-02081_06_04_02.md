## ITA-02711-02081

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Enel Facility in Southern Italy, ITA

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2008**: Enel, in partnership with Sharp Corporation, announced a joint venture to develop photovoltaic (PV) power plants with a total capacity of 189MW by the end of 2012. The facility would utilize thin-film solar cells, with an initial production capacity of 480MW starting mid-2010.
- **January 2022**: Enel Green Power CEO Salvatore Bernabei announced plans to scale up the heterojunction solar module factory in southern Italy from 200MW to 3GW by mid-2024. The factory will feature advanced solar panel technology, including 108 half-cells based on M10 wafers.
- **April 2023**: The facility is identified as the 3GW 3Sun bifacial module facility, which is expected to come online in 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2022**: Enel announced an investment of approximately €600 million for the expansion of its module manufacturing facility in southern Italy, with the European Commission contributing around €118 million to this total.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Spring 2009**: The joint venture between Sharp and Enel was established to operate as an independent power producer.
- **Mid-2010**: The first phase of the thin-film solar cell plant began operations with an annual capacity of 480MW.
- **Mid-2024**: The expansion of the heterojunction solar module factory to 3GW is expected to be completed.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2022**: The expansion of the module manufacturing facility is anticipated to create new jobs, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2008**: The facility was established as a joint venture between Enel and Sharp Corporation, indicating that Enel does not own the facility exclusively but shares ownership with Sharp.
