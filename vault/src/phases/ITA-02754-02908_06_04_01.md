## ITA-02754-02908

### Evolutionary Timeline of FuturaSun Facility in Cittadella, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2021**: FuturaSun introduced the Silk Pro Red module, a building-integrated photovoltaic (BIPV) solar panel with wattages ranging from 230-245 W and efficiency up to 13.45%. This module is designed for architectural applications and includes colored options (red, silver, orange).
- **October 2022**: The company unveiled the FU415-425M ZEBRA Pro module, an all-black n-type interdigitated back-contact (IBC) solar panel with power outputs between 415 W and 425 W and efficiencies up to 21.60%. Plans for a new factory in Italy focusing on IBC panels were announced.
- **March 2023**: FuturaSun announced plans to build a new factory in Cittadella with a production capacity of 2 GW, focusing on high-efficiency solar panels, including IBC and PERC technologies.
- **October 2024**: The FENICE project was introduced, aimed at producing over 7.6 GW of high-efficiency photovoltaic modules in the first 10 years, focusing on n-type and xBC technologies.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: FuturaSun committed to a total investment of €25 million for the new factory in Cittadella, which will enhance the European PV supply chain.
- **October 2024**: The FENICE project involves a total investment of approximately €53 million, with €21 million covered by the EU's Innovation Fund.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2023**: FuturaSun acquired 24,000 square meters of land in Cittadella for the new factory, marking the start of the project to build a 2 GW solar module facility.
- **October 2024**: The FENICE project reached the Grant Agreement Preparation stage, moving towards formalizing €21 million in financing from the EU.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2024**: The FENICE project is expected to directly employ over 250 people once operational, contributing to job creation in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- FuturaSun is the sole owner of the facility in Cittadella, Italy, as indicated by its plans and investments for the new factory and its existing operations.
