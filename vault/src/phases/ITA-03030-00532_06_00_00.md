### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mitsubishi Facility in Apulia, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date**: March 2013
  - **Details**: Mitsubishi Corporation, along with INCJ and Solar Ventures, acquired Solar Holding, which has a photovoltaic (PV) portfolio of 42 MWp in Italy. The facility contributes to energy production that meets the needs of approximately 20,000 households. Solar Holding operates 19 plants across various regions, including Apulia.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date**: March 2013
  - **Details**: The acquisition of Solar Holding marks Mitsubishi's first investment in Italy. The three shareholders plan to acquire an additional 100-150 MWp of PV plants in Italy, aiming to become one of the largest players in the Italian solar market. INCJ has the capacity to invest up to ¥2 trillion (approximately US$21 million).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date**: March 2013
  - **Details**: The acquisition of Solar Holding was completed, establishing a joint venture among Mitsubishi (50% ownership), INCJ (35% ownership), and Solar Ventures (15% ownership). This marks a significant milestone in Mitsubishi's entry into the Italian solar market.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date**: March 2013
  - **Details**: The article does not specify exact job creation or loss figures resulting from this investment, but it implies that the partnership aims to enhance operational capabilities and potentially create jobs in the solar sector through the management of the plants.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date**: March 2013
  - **Details**: Mitsubishi Corporation owns 50% of the facility through its stake in Solar Holding, while INCJ holds 35% and Solar Ventures holds 15%. This indicates a joint venture ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by Mitsubishi.
