### Evolutionary Timeline of the Canadian Solar Facility in Apulia, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2020**: Canadian Solar announced the construction of a 151 MW portfolio of solar projects in Italy, which includes facilities in Apulia. The projects will utilize Canadian Solar's HiKu CS3W-425 modules and are expected to generate approximately 287 gigawatt-hours of solar power annually once operational. This portfolio is noted to be among Italy's first subsidy-free utility-scale PV projects.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2020**: Canadian Solar secured a €55 million financing deal from Intesa Sanpaolo to fund the construction of the 151 MW portfolio, which encompasses 12 projects across Sicily, Apulia, and Lazio. This financing is crucial for the development of these subsidy-free solar projects.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2020**: Canadian Solar announced that the first projects of the 151 MW portfolio are expected to break ground in the third quarter of 2020. The company has been active in the Italian subsidy-free market since 2018 and has a project pipeline of over 900 MW under permitting.
