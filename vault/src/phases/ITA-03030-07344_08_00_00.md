## ITA-03030-07344

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Galileo Facility in Apulia, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2022**: Galileo, in partnership with Hope Group, announced the development of the **Lupiae Maris** project, a **525MW floating wind project** off the coast of Apulia. The project will consist of **35 floating wind turbines**, each with a capacity of **15MW**. The facility will be located several kilometers from the coast, utilizing floating offshore wind foundations to access deeper seabeds and exploit stronger wind resources in the Southern Adriatic Sea.
- **February 2023**: The joint venture (JV) confirmed the development of another project, **Barium Bay**, which will have a capacity of **1.1GW** and will feature **74 wind turbines** capable of generating over **three billion kilowatt-hours** of clean energy. This facility will be located **40km from shore**, stretching from Barium to the north of Barletta.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2023**: The Lupiae Maris project filed its **Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA)** with the Italian Ministry of Environment and Energy Security, marking a significant step towards obtaining planning permission for the facility. The project aims to generate approximately **1.5 billion renewable kWh per year**, sufficient to meet the annual energy needs of over **500,000 families**.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2022**: Announcement of the Lupiae Maris project and the formation of the joint venture with Hope Group.
- **January 2023**: Submission of the EIA for the Lupiae Maris project, a key milestone in the planning process.
- **February 2023**: Announcement of the Barium Bay project, highlighting the ongoing development of the offshore wind facilities in the Adriatic Sea.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2023**: The Barium Bay project is expected to create new job opportunities and capabilities in the local community, contributing to the energy transition in Italy. Specific job creation numbers were not detailed, but the emphasis on local community benefits suggests a positive impact on employment.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2022**: The facility is developed as a **joint venture (JV)** between Galileo and Hope Group, indicating that neither company has exclusive ownership of the Lupiae Maris project. The same ownership structure applies to the Barium Bay project, reinforcing the collaborative approach to developing these facilities.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones related to the Galileo facility in Apulia, Italy, focusing on the Lupiae Maris and Barium Bay projects.
