## ITA-03030-07444

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Innovation Network Corporation of Japan (INCJ) Facility in Apulia, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2013**: The facility, under the joint ownership of Mitsubishi Corporation, INCJ, and Solar Ventures, operates as Solar Holding, which has a photovoltaic (PV) portfolio of 42 MWp across 19 plants located in Italy, including Apulia. The energy production capacity is sufficient to meet the energy needs of approximately 20,000 households.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2013**: The acquisition of Solar Holding marks the first investment in Italy for both Mitsubishi and INCJ. The companies plan to invest in an additional 100-150 MWp of PV plants in Italy, aiming to become one of the largest players in the Italian solar market. INCJ has the capacity to invest up to ¥2 trillion (approximately US$21 million).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2013**: The acquisition of Solar Holding is completed, with Mitsubishi owning 50%, INCJ acquiring 35%, and Solar Ventures holding 15%. This transaction is seen as a strategic move to enhance the development of the Italian solar market.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2013**: The article does not specify job creation or job loss directly associated with this investment, but the expansion plans suggest potential job creation in the solar sector as the companies aim to grow their operations in Italy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2013**: The facility is not exclusively owned by INCJ; it is part of a joint venture where Mitsubishi holds a 50% stake, INCJ holds 35%, and Solar Ventures holds 15%. This collaborative ownership structure indicates a strategic partnership aimed at leveraging each entity's strengths in the solar market.
