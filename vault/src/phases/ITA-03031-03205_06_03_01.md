## ITA-03031-03205

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Midsummer Facility in Bari, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2020**: Midsummer announced plans to set up a 50 MW manufacturing facility in Bari, Italy, focusing on building-integrated photovoltaics (BIPV) using CIGS technology. The facility will utilize Midsummer's Power Mesh Technology (PMT) to enhance cell power output by approximately 10%.
- **November 2021**: The facility is confirmed to be operational, producing thin-film solar roofs primarily for the southern European market.
- **March 2023**: Midsummer developed a 24.9% efficient 4T tandem perovskite-CIGS solar cell, suitable for integration with their production equipment.
- **July 2024**: The facility is described as the largest European thin-film solar cell manufacturing plant, with a production capacity of 50 MW per year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2021**: Midsummer was awarded approximately €38 million (US$44 million) in financial incentives from Italian authorities to support the establishment of the facility, which is part of a total project investment of €66 million (US$76.5 million).
- **June 2024**: Midsummer secured an additional €8 million (US$8.6 million) from Invitalia to further support the development of the facility, building on previous investments.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2020**: Announcement of the factory setup in Bari.
- **October 2021**: Confirmation of financial incentives and readiness to begin filling the facility with equipment and recruiting staff.
- **Summer 2023**: Expected start of production after receiving production certification.
- **July 2024**: All production equipment is installed, and the facility is ready to scale production.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2021**: Midsummer indicated plans to recruit staff for the Bari facility as part of the project development.
- **June 2024**: The second grant from Invitalia is specifically earmarked for hiring personnel in Bari.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2021**: The facility will be wholly owned by Midsummer's subsidiary, Midsummer Italia.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones of the Midsummer facility in Bari, Italy, highlighting its technological advancements, financial backing, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
