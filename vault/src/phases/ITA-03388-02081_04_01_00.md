### Evolutionary Timeline of the Enel Facility in Northern Chile

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **August 2021:** The facility is part of the Haru Oni project, which will utilize green hydrogen derived from 3.4MW of dedicated wind energy to produce green methanol. The pilot project is designed to produce 350 metric tonnes/year of methanol and 130,000 liters/year of gasoline, with plans for scaling up in two subsequent stages. Additionally, it will produce 16 tonnes/year of carbon-neutral LPG.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **August 2021:** The project is backed by a consortium led by Andes Mining and Energy (AME), which includes Enel. A final investment decision for a commercial-scale plant, estimated at $800 million, is expected in the following year. This plant aims for methanol production of 140,000 tonnes/year.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **August 2021:** Civil works for the pilot project commenced, with a formal ground-breaking ceremony scheduled for September 10, 2021. The pilot plant is set to begin operations in May 2022. A final decision on a potential world-scale methanol plant (1.4 million tonnes/year) is anticipated in 2023, with a launch planned for 2026.

4. **Labour:**

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is part of a consortium, indicating that Enel does not own the facility exclusively. It is a joint venture with partners including AME, Siemens, and Chile's state-owned oil company Enap.
