#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04/2024**: Researchers at the Politecnico di Torino investigated the potential of east-west oriented photovoltaic (PV) systems for energy communities. Their findings indicated that these systems, while yielding less energy than south-oriented installations, provide higher shared energy values. The study emphasized the benefits of east-west configurations, such as increased energy production density, reduced wind pressure, and better synchronicity with energy demand.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04/2024**: The research conducted by the Politecnico di Torino was based on a Monte Carlo simulation modeling of an energy community consisting of 60 users with PV systems totaling 150 kW. The study highlighted the importance of net metering schemes for the viability of east-west PV systems, suggesting that investment in such infrastructure is crucial for maximizing shared energy values.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04/2024**: The research paper titled “Shared energy in renewable energy communities: The benefits of east- and west-facing rooftop photovoltaic installations” was published in *Energy Reports*, marking a significant milestone in the understanding of PV system orientations for energy communities.

#### 4. Labour

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **04/2024**: The research indicated that the PV systems analyzed were owned by 30 users within the energy community, suggesting a shared ownership model rather than exclusive ownership by the Politecnico di Torino.
