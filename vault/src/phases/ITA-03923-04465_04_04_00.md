## ITA-03923-04465

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Agire Facility in Mantua, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Project Announcement (12-05-2023)**: The Agire facility is part of the ambitious Hydrogen Valley project in Mantua, which aims to produce over 1,500 tons of renewable hydrogen annually. The facility will utilize a 10 MW electrolyser installed by Sapio, connected to a dedicated photovoltaic park with an installed power of approximately 14MW. The hydrogen production plant will also include a high-pressure hydrogen distribution logistics system and an adjacent hydrogen refueling station.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Funding Details (12-05-2023)**: The Hydrogen Valley project has a total value exceeding €60 million, financed by the National Recovery and Resilience Plan (PNRR) with €30 million and the European Interregional Innovation Investment tender with €9 million. The project is expected to unlock over €30 million in private capital.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Project Launch (12-05-2023)**: The Hydrogen Valley project was officially unveiled, marking the beginning of the initiative aimed at reducing CO2 emissions by over 14,000 tons per year and establishing a comprehensive hydrogen production and distribution network in the Mantua area.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation (12-05-2023)**: The project is anticipated to create new job opportunities as it develops various components of the Hydrogen Valley, including the hydrogen production plant, refueling stations, and associated infrastructure.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture (12-05-2023)**: The Agire facility is part of a collaborative project involving multiple partners, including Sapio, Renhive, and other private companies, universities, and research centers. The project is led by the Province of Mantua, indicating a joint venture rather than exclusive ownership by Agire.

This timeline captures the key developments and details related to the Agire facility in Mantua, Italy, as part of the Hydrogen Valley project.
