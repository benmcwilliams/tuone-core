### Evolutionary Timeline of the Galileo Facility in Brindisi, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2023**: A floating buoy equipped with lidar and other sensors was deployed off the coast of Brindisi to conduct surveys for the construction of the Kailia Energia and Lupiae Maris floating offshore wind farms. This buoy will collect data on wind, weather conditions, and wave motion for a minimum of 12 months, marking the initial operational phase for the facility.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2023**: Galileo announced a joint development agreement with Hope Group to develop a 300MW pipeline of onshore wind power plants in Italy. This agreement is part of a broader strategy to expand renewable energy projects, including the previously mentioned offshore wind farms.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2023**: The deployment of the buoy represents the first activity in the preliminary phases leading to the construction of the Lupiae Maris wind farm.
- **December 2023**: The joint development agreement for the 300MW onshore wind pipeline was announced, indicating ongoing progress in renewable energy projects.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2023**: The collaboration between Galileo and Hope Group is expected to create job opportunities as they develop new renewable energy projects, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2023**: The facility operates as a joint venture between Galileo and Hope Group, indicating that it is not exclusively owned by either company but rather a collaborative effort to develop renewable energy projects in the region.
