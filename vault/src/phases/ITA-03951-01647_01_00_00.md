### Evolutionary Timeline of the Falck Renewables Facility in Foggia, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2021**: Falck Renewables is developing a 41.1 MW solar project in the province of Foggia, Italy. The facility is expected to generate approximately 70 gigawatt-hours of electricity annually. The project includes a 10 MW/20 megawatt-hour storage system designed for the on-site integration of renewable electricity and agricultural production, specifically incorporating rows of olive trees alongside solar panels.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2021**: A corporate power purchase agreement (PPA) was signed with chemicals producer Solvay, covering around 70% of the power produced by the solar plant. This 10-year contract is significant as it represents the electricity needs of four of Solvay’s plants in Italy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2021**: The announcement of the solar project and the signing of the PPA with Solvay marks a key milestone in the development of the facility. The project is characterized as one of the most innovative in renewable energy production.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2021**: The management of the olive grove integrated into the solar project will be entrusted to local specialized operators organized as a social enterprise. This arrangement is expected to create local jobs and contribute to economic growth in the Apulia region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2021**: The facility is owned by Falck Renewables through its subsidiary, Falck Renewables Sviluppo. There is no indication of joint ventures or shared ownership in the provided information, suggesting that Falck Renewables has full ownership of the facility.
