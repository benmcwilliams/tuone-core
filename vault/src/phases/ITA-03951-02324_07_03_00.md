### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iveco Facility in Foggia, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: The Iveco facility in Foggia officially opened, focusing on the production of battery and fuel cell buses, among other products. The plant has a production capacity of up to 1,000 buses per year. The facility will manufacture battery-electric vehicles (BEVs) and hydrogen buses, while also retaining the capacity for combustion engine models. Notably, the chassis and frames for these vehicles will be sourced from other European plants.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: The opening of the Foggia plant follows a strategic decision by Iveco to re-enter bus manufacturing in Italy, which had ceased in 2011. The specific financial investments tied to the establishment of the facility have not been disclosed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: The facility was inaugurated, marking the official start of operations after laying the foundation stone eight months prior. The plant is set to produce various models, including the twelve-meter E-Way solo bus with battery-electric drive and the Iveco Bus Crossway LE Electric.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2023**: The opening of the Foggia facility is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers regarding job creation have not been mentioned in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2023**: Iveco operates the Foggia facility independently, having parted ways with its former parent company, CNH Industrial, in January 2022. The articles do not indicate any joint ventures or shared ownership for this facility.
