### Evolutionary Timeline of the MHI Vestas Facility in Taranto, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2022**: Vestas announced plans to begin serial production of its V236-15.0 MW blades at the Taranto facility starting in the third quarter of 2023. The blades measure 115.5 meters and are designed for the growing European offshore market, complementing existing production for 2MW and 4MW platforms.

- **November 2024**: Vestas inaugurated a new blade production line for the V236-15.0 MW offshore wind turbine at the Taranto factory. This expansion is part of Vestas' strategy to enhance its manufacturing capabilities for offshore wind energy.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2024**: The expansion of the production line in Taranto was highlighted as a significant investment in Vestas' European operations, aimed at supporting the energy transition and job creation. The company expressed gratitude to local authorities and Invitalia for their support, indicating a collaborative effort to scale up clean energy in Europe.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2022**: Announcement of the upcoming production of V236-15.0 MW blades, with production set to start in Q3 2023.
  
- **November 2024**: Inauguration of the new blade production line, marking a milestone in Vestas' expansion of offshore wind globally.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2024**: Following the new production line's inauguration, the workforce at the Taranto facility increased from 700 to 1,600 employees, with expectations to reach 2,000 employees soon. Approximately 1,300 of these positions are dedicated to the V236 production, showcasing significant job creation as a result of the facility's expansion.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify any joint ventures or partnerships; therefore, it can be inferred that Vestas owns the facility exclusively, as there is no mention of shared ownership or joint ventures in the provided information.
