## ITA-04499-02081

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Enel Facility in Tarquinia, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2023**: Enel Green Power commenced construction of a 170 MW agrivoltaic solar project in Tarquinia, which is set to be the largest solar plant in Italy and the biggest agrivoltaic plant in the country. The facility will utilize bifacial photovoltaic modules mounted on trackers, allowing solar energy to be absorbed on both the front and rear surfaces. The plant is expected to generate approximately 280 GWh/year, which will help avoid the emission of around 130,000 tons of CO2 annually and meet the energy needs of approximately 110,000 households.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: The project is part of a broader commitment by Enel Green Power to promote sustainable development and energy transition in the region. The construction of the plant aligns with Italy's increased interest in agrivoltaic projects, supported by a €1.5 billion scheme launched by the Italian Ministry of Agricultural, Food and Forestry Policies to incentivize such projects.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2023**: Enel Green Power officially started construction of the agrivoltaic solar plant in Tarquinia. The construction is expected to last for approximately 13 months, with completion anticipated in mid-2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2023**: The construction of the facility is projected to employ up to 330 people, primarily from local companies in the province of Viterbo, during the estimated 13-month construction period.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2023**: The agrivoltaic solar farm will be built on land owned by a local company, which will collaborate with Enel Green Power to integrate agricultural activities at the site. This indicates a partnership rather than exclusive ownership by Enel.
