## ITA-04581-02881

### Evolutionary Timeline of the European Energy Facility in Troia, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2019**: European Energy acquires the rights to build a 123 MW solar plant in Troia, Apulia, which is the largest Italian PV project to have secured all necessary approvals. The project was initiated in 2011.
- **June 2020**: The facility is completed and connected to the grid with a production capacity of 103 MW. The plant features 275,000 solar modules and is expected to sell power through a bilateral power purchase agreement.
- **January 2022**: The facility is sold to Iren Spa, and it begins selling power to the spot market.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2019**: European Energy secures a 12-year agreement with Axpo Italia for the purchase of 300 MW of solar power, which supports the financial viability of the Troia project.
- **June 2020**: The project is completed under a legal requirement to connect to the grid, following a court ruling that reinstated its approval.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2019**: European Energy acquires ASI Troia FV1, the special purpose company owning the Troia project.
- **June 2020**: The 103 MW solar park is connected to the grid after a year of construction.
- **January 2022**: The facility is sold to Iren Spa for €166 million, which includes the 103 MW solar plant in Troia and a 17.5 MW facility in Bari.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2020**: More than 400 people were involved in the construction of the solar park. Ongoing operations and maintenance jobs will be created for the 30-year lifecycle of the plant.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2019**: European Energy acquires full ownership of the ASI Troia FV1 project.
- **January 2022**: The facility is sold to Iren Spa, indicating a change in ownership from European Energy to Iren Spa.

This timeline captures the key developments and changes related to the European Energy facility in Troia, Italy, focusing on its technology, investments, project milestones, labor impact, and ownership structure.
