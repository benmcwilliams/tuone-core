### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Montefiascone, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2023**: Iberdrola completed its second solar plant in Montefiascone with a production capacity of **7 MW**. This facility is part of Iberdrola's broader strategy to expand its renewable energy portfolio in Italy.
- **Future Plans**: An additional **60 MW** could potentially be added to the Fenix project in Sicily, which would bring the total capacity to **305 MW**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2022**: Iberdrola commissioned its first solar plant in Italy, located in Montalto di Castro, with a capacity of **23 MW**.
- **2024**: Iberdrola plans to have **330 MW** under construction in Italy, with an additional **40 MW** expected to begin in early 2025, reaching a total installed capacity of **400 MW**.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2023**: The Montefiascone solar plant was completed.
- **2024**: Iberdrola announced plans for two additional solar plants, Limes 10 (18 MW) and Limes 15 (36 MW), to begin construction in the first half of the year. Three more projects are scheduled for the second half of the year, with all necessary permits already obtained.

#### 4. Labour
- **2024**: The Fenix project in Sicily is expected to create approximately **500 jobs** during the construction phase and more than **100 stable jobs** during the commercial operation phase. The Montefiascone facility contributes to Iberdrola's overall employment impact in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: Iberdrola owns the Montefiascone facility exclusively, as indicated by its direct involvement in the commissioning and operational management of the solar plant.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones related to the Iberdrola facility in Montefiascone, Italy, highlighting its role in the company's renewable energy strategy in the region.
