## ITA-05465-05905

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Renantis Facility in Manzano, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Installed Capacity**: The La Manganizza agrivoltaic plant will have an installed capacity of **8.16 MW**.
- **Production Output**: Once operational, the facility is expected to produce approximately **12 GWh/year** of energy, sufficient for over **4,000 households**.
- **Technology**: The facility will utilize **over 14,000 double-sided solar PV modules** mounted on **single-axis trackers**, which enhances energy output and minimizes visual impact compared to traditional solar modules.
- **Agricultural Integration**: The site will also feature multiple **beehives**, a **probiotic meadow**, and various native plants, including hedges and melliferous trees, promoting biodiversity and ecological balance.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Total Investment**: The estimated total investment for the project is around **€8 million**.
- **Crowdfunding Campaign**: Renantis launched a crowdfunding campaign on **July 4, 2023**, to support the construction of the facility, allowing local residents to invest amounts between **€200 and €10,000** with a guaranteed interest rate of **6.5%** per year for three years.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Announcement**: The project was officially announced on **July 11, 2023**.
- **Construction Start**: Work on the facility is set to commence in **autumn 2023**.
- **Previous Success**: The crowdfunding campaign follows the successful completion of previous campaigns for other agrivoltaic projects, including the **Terzo di Aquileia** plant, which raised over **€150,000**, exceeding its target by **50%**.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The project is expected to create local job opportunities, particularly in managing the apiary and agricultural activities associated with the facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: Renantis will own the facility, as indicated by the crowdfunding campaign being guaranteed by the company, allowing local investors to participate in the project financially while maintaining full ownership.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and characteristics of the Renantis facility in Manzano, Italy, highlighting its innovative approach to combining renewable energy production with agricultural practices.
