### Evolutionary Timeline of the Solvay Facility in Rosignano, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2021**: Solvay's facility in Rosignano is part of a corporate power purchase agreement (PPA) with Falck Renewables, which covers around 70% of the power produced by a new 41.1 MW solar project in Apulia. This project is expected to generate approximately 70 gigawatt-hours of electricity annually, supporting the energy needs of Solvay's plants in Italy, including Rosignano.
  
- **June 2023**: Solvay announces a partnership with Sapio to construct a green hydrogen production plant at the Rosignano site. The plant will have a capacity of 756 tons of green hydrogen per year and will be powered by a dedicated 9.5 MW photovoltaic system. The project is scheduled for completion by mid-2026 and aims to utilize green hydrogen in the production of peroxide.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2023**: The green hydrogen project at Rosignano has secured a subsidy of €16 million from the Tuscany Region, highlighting the regional government's commitment to sustainable initiatives. This funding is part of the National Recovery and Resilience Plan (PNRR) and supports the establishment of Europe’s first hub for green hydrogen peroxide manufacturing.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2021**: Announcement of the PPA with Falck Renewables for the solar project in Apulia, which will support the energy needs of Solvay's plants, including Rosignano.
  
- **June 2023**: Announcement of the collaboration with Sapio for the Hydrogen Valley Rosignano Project, with plans for the green hydrogen production plant to be completed by mid-2026.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2021**: The integration of the solar project in Apulia is expected to create local jobs and promote economic growth in the region, as the management of the olive grove associated with the solar project will be entrusted to local specialized operators.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2023**: The green hydrogen production plant will be a joint effort between Solvay and Sapio, indicating a collaborative ownership structure for this specific project. The facility in Rosignano is owned by Solvay, but the new hydrogen project involves a partnership with Sapio.
