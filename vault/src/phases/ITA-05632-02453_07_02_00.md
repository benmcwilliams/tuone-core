### Evolutionary Timeline of the Stellantis Group Facility in Cassino, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10-03-2023**: The Stellantis plant in Cassino, Italy, announced its conversion to produce electric vehicles (EVs) based on the STLA Large platform, specifically designed for battery electric vehicles (BEVs). The facility currently produces the Alfa Romeo Giulia, Alfa Romeo Stelvio, and Maserati Grecale, which are based on the Giorgio platform for combustion vehicles. The STLA Large platform supports 800-volt systems and is designed for vehicles ranging from 4.70 to 5.40 meters in length. The Cassino plant is the second Stellantis facility to produce EVs on this platform, following the Windsor assembly plant in Canada.

- **19-12-2024**: Stellantis confirmed that the Cassino plant will manufacture three models based on the STLA Large platform, including the Alfa Romeo Stelvio starting in 2025 and the Alfa Romeo Giulia in 2026. The production will include hybrid and electric versions.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **19-12-2024**: Stellantis announced a €2 billion investment plan for its factories in Italy, which includes the Cassino plant. This investment is aimed at supporting the production of new electric and hybrid models.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones

- **19-12-2024**: Confirmation of the production timeline for the Alfa Romeo Stelvio and Giulia models, with production starting in 2025 and 2026, respectively.
