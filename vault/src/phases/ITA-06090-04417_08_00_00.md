## ITA-06090-04417

### Evolutionary Timeline of the AGNES Facility in Adriatic Sea, Italy

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **2021:** AGNES, in collaboration with Saipem and QINT’X, applied for a concession for an offshore energy hub in the Adriatic Sea, off the coast of Ravenna. The hub consists of two offshore wind farms, Romagna 1 and Romagna 2, with a total capacity of 600 MW.
   - **Romagna 1:** Proposed to be built 22 kilometers off the coast of Lido di Classe, it will feature 25 wind turbines, each with a capacity of 8 MW, covering an area of 85 square kilometers. Additionally, a floating photovoltaic system with a capacity of 100 MWp will be installed next to this wind farm.
   - **Romagna 2:** Planned to have a capacity of 400 MW with up to 50 turbines, also rated at 8 MW each, located approximately 26 kilometers offshore Porto Corsini.
   - The facility will integrate a Power-to-Hydrogen plant and battery storage alongside the wind and solar components.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **2024:** Ramboll was contracted to support AGNES throughout the authorisation process, providing concept design and strategic advisory services, including estimating capital expenditures (CAPEX) and operational expenditures (OPEX). Specific investment amounts are not detailed in the articles.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **2021:** Application for the concession for the offshore energy hub was submitted.
   - **2022:** AGNES contracted Rosetti Marino to prepare the conceptual design for the wind turbines, which included an analysis of alternative foundation options and input for the environmental impact assessment (EIA) and authorisation process.
   - **2024:** Ramboll's involvement indicates that the authorisation process is currently underway and expected to be completed by the end of the year.

4. **Labour:**
   - The articles do not specify job creation or job loss related to the AGNES facility at this time.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The articles indicate that AGNES is working in collaboration with Saipem and QINT’X for the project, suggesting a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership.
