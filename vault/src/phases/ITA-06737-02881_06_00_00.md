### Evolutionary Timeline of the European Energy Facility in Mineo, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2023**: European Energy finalized an agreement to sell a 50% stake in a 163MW solar farm in Mineo, Sicily, to Sosteneo SGR. The facility will occupy 382 hectares and is designed to generate approximately 350 GWh of clean energy annually, sufficient to power around 80,000 households. The solar plant will utilize advanced photovoltaic technologies to maximize energy production.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2023**: The financial terms of the deal for the Mineo solar farm were undisclosed, but the transaction is part of Sosteneo's strategy to invest in greenfield projects that facilitate energy transition in Italy. This agreement is expected to enhance European Energy's investment strategy in the Italian renewable energy market.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 20, 2023**: The agreement for the 163MW solar farm in Mineo was finalized, marking a significant milestone in the development of the facility. This follows a previous agreement in September 2023 for a 68MW solar farm in Ramacca, also in Sicily.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify job creation or job loss directly associated with the Mineo facility. However, the overall development of the solar farms is expected to contribute positively to local employment through construction and operational phases.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2023**: European Energy and Sosteneo will jointly construct and operate the Mineo solar plant on a 50/50 partnership basis. European Energy retains 50% ownership of the facility, indicating a joint venture structure for this project.
