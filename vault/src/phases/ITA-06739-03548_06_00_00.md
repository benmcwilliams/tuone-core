## ITA-06739-03548

### Evolutionary Timeline of the SunEdison Facility in Rovigo, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **11-03-2010**: SunEdison receives approval to develop a 72MW solar power plant in Rovigo, which is set to be the largest solar power plant in Europe. The project is expected to generate power for 16,500 homes.
- **10-01-2011**: The facility is reported to be completed and operational, with a production capacity of 70MW, making it the largest single-operating PV solar power plant in Europe.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **02-11-2010**: First Reserve closes a financial facilities agreement for the Rovigo solar plant at €276 million. This includes a joint venture with SunEdison, where First Reserve purchases the plant from SunEdison.
- **Funding Sources**: The financing agreement involves several banks, including Banco Santander, Unicredit Corporate Banking, Credit Agricole, Dexia Crediop, Natixis, and Societe Generale.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **11-03-2010**: Final approval for the project is granted, with power generation expected to begin in the second half of 2010 and final completion by year-end.
- **02-11-2010**: The sale of the plant to First Reserve is finalized, with SunEdison overseeing operations and maintenance post-sale.
- **10-01-2011**: The facility is recognized as a significant achievement in the solar industry, contributing to the growth of U.S.-based PV project integrators.

#### 4. Labour
- **11-03-2010**: The project is expected to create over 350 local construction jobs during its development phase.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **02-11-2010**: SunEdison initially develops the facility but sells it to First Reserve, establishing a joint venture. After the sale, SunEdison retains operational control, indicating a shift from full ownership to a partnership model with First Reserve.
