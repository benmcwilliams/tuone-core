### Evolutionary Timeline of the Maverick Facility in Molise Offshore Wind Farm, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2023**: Maverick has revised its plans for the Molise Offshore Wind Farm, which will now consist of 70 floating wind turbines instead of the originally planned 120. The turbines will have a capacity of 15MW each, totaling 1GW for the facility. The project will utilize Vestas V236-15.0MW units and will employ two types of floating platforms: Gazelle floaters for the turbines and BW Ideol platforms for the offshore substations. The operational area has been reduced from 295 square kilometers to 219 square kilometers, and the turbines will be installed in waters with depths ranging from 88 to 126 meters.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2023**: The estimated investment for the Molise Offshore Wind Farm is €2.1 billion. The project will connect to the National Transmission Grid (NTG) at the Terna station in Montecilfone.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2023**: Maverick submitted its final project scope to the Italian authorities, marking a significant milestone in the project development. The installation of the turbines is projected to begin between January 2028 and December 2033.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2023**: While specific job creation or loss figures were not mentioned in the article, the construction and operation of the facility are expected to create jobs, particularly in the assembly of the floating platforms at the port of Vasto.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2023**: Maverick is controlled by Green Bridge, indicating that it is not exclusively owned by Maverick but is part of a larger corporate structure under Green Bridge.
