### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ansaldo Nucleare Facility in Rotondella, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Production**: The facility is involved in the cementation of approximately 3 cubic meters of liquid uranium-thorium solution, referred to as the 'final product', resulting from experimental reprocessing operations.
- **Construction of Structures**: The ICPF project includes the construction of a process building for cementing activities and an annexed temporary storage facility, which is already completed.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Contract Award**: On June 20, 2023, a contract was awarded to a consortium comprising Ansaldo Nucleare and Monsud SpA for the construction of the ICPF facility, following a tender launched on December 30, 2022.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Historical Context**: 
  - Between 1968 and 1970, 84 elements of irradiated fuel were shipped to the Rotondella plant.
  - Between 1975 and 1978, a nuclear testing campaign was conducted on 20 of these elements.
  - Preliminary activities for the ICPF plant began in 2004, focusing on the design and qualification of the cementitious matrix.
  - Between 2007 and 2010, a mock-up prototype of the cementation cell was created.
  - Between 2014 and 2017, excavation works and construction of a barrier and foundations for the interim storage facility were implemented.
  - In 2017, Sogin terminated the agreement with the winning contractor due to default.
- **Future Operations**: The entry into operation of the plant is expected to enhance safety levels and significantly increase decommissioning activities at the site, with a projected growth of over 4% in decommissioning operations in 2023.
