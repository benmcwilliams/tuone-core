## ITA-06799-01010

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Loreo, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2009**: EDF Energies Nouvelles announces plans to build a ground-based solar farm in Loreo with a capacity of 12.5 MWp, utilizing First Solar’s CdTe thin film modules as part of a €1 billion solar project financing initiative.
- **November 2010**: The Loreo solar photovoltaic power plant is officially commissioned with a slightly increased capacity of 12.6 MWp. This facility is part of a larger initiative that includes another plant in Priolo, totaling 26.1 MWp across both sites.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2009**: EDF secures €1 billion in project financing, which includes a €500 million loan from the European Investment Bank (EIB) to support solar projects in Italy and France. This financing is crucial for the development of the Loreo facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Early 2010**: Financing for the Loreo solar project is confirmed as part of the €1 billion initiative.
- **November 2010**: The Loreo solar power plant is commissioned, marking it as one of the largest solar energy projects completed by EDF in Italy.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2010**: The commissioning of the Loreo facility is noted to reflect the acceleration of EDF's solar activities in Italy, implying job creation through local development and construction efforts, although specific job numbers are not provided.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2010**: The Loreo facility is reported to be owned 100% by EDF EN Italia, indicating full ownership by EDF Energies Nouvelles.
