### Evolutionary Timeline of the Sogin Facility in Latina, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 1964**: The Latina nuclear power plant, comprising a single 210 MWe Magnox graphite gas-cooled reactor, began operations.
- **December 1987**: The plant was permanently shut down.
- **November 1999**: Sogin took over ownership of the Latina site.
- **June 2020**: Italy's Ministry of Economic Development issued a decree authorizing Sogin to begin the initial phase of decommissioning the Latina plant.
- **2023-2027**: The facility is undergoing decommissioning, which includes the segmentation and extraction of heavily contaminated components and the dismantling of the reactor vessel, expected to be completed by 2027. The dismantling will produce approximately 268,000 tonnes of materials, with 96% earmarked for recycling.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: A tender valued at about EUR10 million was launched for the construction of a facility for the recovery and treatment of approximately 70 tonnes of radioactive Magnox alloy residues at the Latina plant.
- **November 2024**: The initial phase of decommissioning, which includes dismantling six steam generators, is expected to cost EUR270 million (USD284 million).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2020**: Decommissioning authorization received from Italy's Ministry of Economic Development.
- **April 2023**: Tender issued for the removal of the reactor vessel, marking the entry into the final phase of decommissioning.
- **November 2024**: Preparations for dismantling the steam generators are underway, with the project involving complex operations to isolate and safely dismantle the components.
