### Evolutionary Timeline of the Enel Facility in Monterotondo Marittimo, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: A wood chip drying plant, managed by the Best Local Energy Consortium, opens in Monterotondo Marittimo. This facility utilizes geothermal heat supplied by Enel Green Power, which provides hot water at 85 degrees Celsius for drying biomass. The facility is part of a broader initiative to develop value-adding applications for geothermal energy in Tuscany.

- **June 2023**: Planning begins anew for the Monterotondo 2 geothermal power plant. Initially proposed in 2015 with a capacity of 20 MW, the project has been revised to a smaller 5-MW capacity using binary cycle technology due to unsatisfactory natural conditions for the original forecast.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2015**: Initial investment of EUR 110 million was announced for the development of the Monterotondo 2 geothermal power plant with a planned capacity of 20 MW.

- **June 2023**: The decision to construct a smaller 5-MW power plant indicates a shift in investment strategy, although specific new investment figures for the revised project have not been disclosed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: The wood chip drying plant officially opens, marking a significant milestone in utilizing geothermal energy for biomass processing.

- **June 2023**: The authorization process for the Monterotondo 2 geothermal power plant is restarted, following the decision to reduce the plant's capacity. The process includes affixing pre-ordinary expropriation restrictions and declaring public utility for land parcels in the municipality.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2023**: The opening of the wood chip drying plant is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers are not mentioned in the articles.

- **June 2023**: The article does not specify job creation or loss related to the revised plans for the Monterotondo 2 geothermal power plant.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
