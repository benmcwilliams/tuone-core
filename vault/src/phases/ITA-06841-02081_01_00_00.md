## ITA-06841-02081

### Evolutionary Timeline of Enel Facility at Rome-Fiumicino International Airport, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2022**: Enel plans to deploy a 5MW/10MWh second-life battery storage system at Rome-Fiumicino International Airport, complementing a 30MW solar park under development. The storage system will utilize various second-life lithium-ion batteries, primarily based on nickel, manganese, and cobalt chemistry.
- **June 2023**: Enel X has been awarded a tender to build a 22MW solar plant at the airport, which will be the largest self-consumption photovoltaic system installed at a European airport. The solar plant will cover a 340,000 square meter area and will be complemented by a 10MWh storage system based on second-life electric vehicle batteries.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2022**: The EU Innovation Fund is providing €3 million ($3.3 million) for the second-life battery storage project at the airport.
- **June 2023**: The construction of the solar plant and storage system is part of a collaboration with Aeroporti di Roma (ADR), indicating significant investment in renewable energy infrastructure at the airport.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2022**: Announcement of the development of a 30MW solar park and a 5MW/10MWh battery storage system.
- **June 2023**: Work on the 22MW solar plant is scheduled to start by the end of 2023 and is expected to be completed in 2024.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Enel X, a unit of the Enel Group, is collaborating with Aeroporti di Roma (ADR) for the solar plant and storage system, indicating a partnership rather than exclusive ownership. The solar park will be operated by Aeroporti di Roma.
