## ITA-06878-07509

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Terna Facility in Partanna, Italy

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility is involved in the development of a 600 MW high-voltage direct current subsea cable link between Partanna, Sicily, and Cape Bon, Tunisia. The project includes a 200 km transmission line that will reach a maximum depth of 800 meters. The project aims to increase electricity production from renewable sources in Europe and Africa and promote energy diversification.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - On December 14, 2022, it was announced that Terna and its Tunisian counterpart, Société tunisienne de l’électricité et du gaz (STEG), secured €307 million from the Connecting Europe Facility (CEF) to invest in the €850 million subsea cable project.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - The project has been in development since 2003, initially planning a 1 GW power line and a 1.2 GW power plant in Tunisia. However, due to an economic recession in Europe and falling power demand, the project was downsized to a 600 MW subsea line in 2011, and the power plant plans were scrapped. The project became part of Terna’s National Electricity Transmission Grid Development Plan in 2016.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - Terna and STEG created a 50:50 joint venture company, Elmes Etudes Sarl, to oversee pre-construction activities on the power line, indicating that the facility is not exclusively owned by Terna but is a joint venture with STEG.
