## ITA-06884-07489

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Sogin Facility in Garigliano, Italy

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 1964**: The Garigliano boiling water reactor was connected to the grid.
- **1982**: The reactor was shut down.
- **2023**: Sogin announced the tender for the segmentation and extraction of heavily contaminated components and the reactor vessel, which will take place underwater for worker safety. The dismantling of the vessel is expected to conclude in **2027**. Approximately **268,000 tonnes** of materials will be produced from the dismantling, with **96%** (about **258,000 tonnes**) intended for recycling, primarily metals and concrete.
- **July 2024**: Preparatory activities for the removal of the reactor vessel head were completed, including restoring auxiliary systems. The removal of components began, with treated components stored in high integrity containers for future transfer to a national repository. The construction of the DT2 building for interim storage of waste generated from decommissioning is also underway, with completion expected in the **first half of 2026**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: A tender valued at approximately **EUR 36 million (USD 39 million)** was issued for the segmentation and extraction operations of the reactor vessel.
- **July 2024**: Sogin announced the completion of the foundation slab for the new DT2 building, which will house about **1800 cubic metres** of low and medium-level radioactive waste from the decommissioning activities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: Announcement of the tender for the removal of the reactor vessel, marking the entry into the final phase of decommissioning.
- **July 2024**: Progress reported on the dismantling of the reactor vessel, including the removal of the vessel head and the laying of the foundation slab for the DT2 building.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify job creation or job loss directly related to the investments or expansions at the Garigliano facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned exclusively by **Sogin**, a state-owned company established in **1999** to manage the decommissioning of Italy's former nuclear power sites.
