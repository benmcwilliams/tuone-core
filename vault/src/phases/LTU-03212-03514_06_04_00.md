## LTU-03212-03514

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Solitek Facility in Vilnius, Lithuania

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **08/2018**: Solitek plans to increase its solar cell production capacity at its Vilnius facility from 80 MW to 180 MW by the end of 2018. The production will transition from poly BSF cells to bifacial cells, with future plans to manufacture IBC ZEBRA cells. The facility also produces Poly C-Si and Mono C-Si glass-glass modules, with power ratings ranging from 270W to 345W.
- **02/2019**: Solitek partners with Valoe Corporation to manufacture IBC solar cells at the Vilnius facility, starting with an initial production capacity of 60 MW. The production line is expected to be converted for IBC technology.
- **07/2020**: Solitek begins applying circular principles to its production operations, participating in EU projects focused on circular economy models in the solar industry.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **02/2019**: Valoe announces it will invest approximately €5 million to convert the cell production equipment for IBC cells at Solitek’s facility.
- **11/2019**: Valoe must raise €3.5 million from a convertible bond to ensure production starts at the Solitek facility early next year.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **08/2018**: Announcement of the plan to expand cell production capacity from 80 MW to 180 MW.
- **02/2019**: Joint production of IBC solar cells at Solitek’s facility is announced, with production expected to start in the second half of the year.
- **11/2019**: Production at the Solitek facility is expected to start early next year, contingent on securing necessary funding.
- **07/2020**: Solitek ramps up output in response to rising orders in Scandinavia.

#### 4. Labour
- **02/2019**: The partnership with Valoe and the expansion of production capacity are expected to create jobs, although specific numbers are not mentioned.
- **11/2019**: The need for funding to start production raises concerns about job security, indicating potential job losses if funding is not secured.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **02/2019**: Valoe acquires the solar cell production business from Solitek's parent company, Global BOD Group, indicating a joint venture structure for the IBC cell production at the Vilnius facility. Solitek remains a significant player in the facility's operations, but Valoe's involvement suggests a shared ownership model for the IBC production line.
