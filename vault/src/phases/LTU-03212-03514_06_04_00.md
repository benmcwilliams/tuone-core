### Evolutionary Timeline of the Solitek Facility in Vilnius, Lithuania

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2013**: The Solitek facility was established in Vilnius, Lithuania, producing Poly C-Si and Mono C-Si glass-glass solar modules for both regular and building-integrated photovoltaic (BIPV) applications. The modules produced range in power from 270W to 345W.
- **08-02-2018**: Solitek announced plans to increase its solar cell production capacity from 80 MW to 180 MW by the end of 2018. The production will transition from poly BSF cells to bifacial cells, with future plans to manufacture IBC ZEBRA cells.
- **21-02-2019**: A partnership with Valoe Corporation was established to produce interdigitated back contact (IBC) solar cells at the Vilnius facility, starting with an initial production capacity of 60 MW.
- **07-11-2019**: Production at the facility was expected to start early in 2020, contingent on raising €3.5 million through a convertible bond.
- **14-07-2020**: Solitek began applying circular principles to its production operations, participating in EU projects focused on circular economy models in the solar industry.
- **08-11-2021**: The facility prepared to ramp up output due to rising orders in Scandinavia and a new partnership with Convalt Energy to reduce dependence on Asian semiconductors and solar cells.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **21-02-2019**: Valoe Corporation announced it would invest approximately €5 million to convert the cell production equipment for IBC cells at the Solitek facility.
- **07-11-2019**: The need to raise €3.5 million through a convertible bond was highlighted to ensure production could start at the facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **08-02-2018**: Announcement of the expansion of cell capacity and the opening of a new module assembly factory in Ukraine.
- **21-02-2019**: Joint production of IBC solar cells was announced, with production expected to start in the second half of 2019.
- **07-11-2019**: Production was expected to commence early in 2020, pending financial arrangements.
- **14-07-2020**: Solitek began integrating circular manufacturing principles into its operations.
- **08-11-2021**: The facility was set to ramp up output in response to increased orders.

#### 4. Labour
- **21-02-2019**: The partnership with Valoe Corporation and the expansion of production capacity were expected to create job opportunities, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **2013**: Solitek was established as a unit of Global BOD Group, which is the largest ophthalmic prescription lenses and CD/DVD manufacturer in Scandinavia. The ownership structure indicates that Solitek operates under the umbrella of Global BOD Group, suggesting a full ownership model.
