## LTU-03212-03689

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Valoe Facility in Vilnius, Lithuania

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2019**: Valoe Corporation and Solitek announced a joint production of interdigitated back contact (IBC) solar cells at Solitek’s factory in Vilnius, with an initial production capacity of 60 MW.
- **December 2020**: Valoe confirmed the restart of cell manufacturing at the Vilnius site after completing the acquisition of the facility. The production line was equipped with IBC technology, and Valoe aimed to double the capacity to 120 MW in the future.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2019**: Valoe announced it would arrange financing of approximately €5 million for the project, which included a purchase price of about €3.2 million for the cell production business from Solitek.
- **May 2020**: Valoe secured €2.5 million from the Nordic Environment Finance Corporation (Nefco) and a €3 million credit facility from Winance Investment to complete the purchase of the Vilnius facility and fund the installation of IBC production equipment.
- **March 2020**: Valoe extended its €3.5 million convertible bond issuance to raise funds necessary for the factory purchase and equipment integration.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2019**: Announcement of the acquisition of the solar cell production business from Solitek.
- **May 2020**: Completion of the purchase of the Vilnius facility after multiple renegotiations of payment terms.
- **December 2020**: Production of IBC solar cells officially restarted at the Vilnius facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2020**: As production resumed, Valoe anticipated job creation associated with the manufacturing operations at the Vilnius facility, although specific numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2019**: Valoe acquired the solar cell production business from Solitek, indicating a shift towards full ownership of the facility. The ownership structure evolved from a joint venture to Valoe having full control over the operations in Vilnius.

This timeline captures the key developments in the evolution of the Valoe facility in Vilnius, Lithuania, focusing on technology, investment, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure.
