### Evolutionary Timeline of the European Energy Facility in Anyksciai, Lithuania

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2023**: European Energy announced the construction of a **65MW solar PV plant** in Anyksciai, Lithuania. The facility will feature **more than 140,000 solar panels** mounted on tracker systems, allowing for optimal power generation. Once operational, it is expected to generate approximately **90,000MWh annually**, sufficient to power around **24,000 households**. The plant is set to be one of the largest solar farms in the Baltics.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2023**: European Energy has invested over **€460 million** in renewable energy projects in Lithuania to date. The company plans to invest a total of **over €1.6 billion** across various solar and wind projects, aiming for a combined capacity of **1.6GW**. Additionally, they are investing **€250 million** in a power-to-X plant in Lithuania for producing green hydrogen and e-methanol.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2023**: The solar PV project in Anyksciai is expected to be **connected to the grid in the fourth quarter of 2023**. This project is part of a broader initiative by European Energy, which includes plans for two additional solar projects in Jonava and Skuodas, expected to reach commercial operation by **2026**.
