### Evolutionary Timeline of the Glassbel Facility in Klaipeda, Lithuania

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **03-07-2014:** The Glassbel facility is involved in the Smartflex project, which focuses on developing custom building-integrated photovoltaic (BIPV) modules. The facility has established a pilot production line capable of manufacturing photovoltaic modules tailored to architects' specifications. The production process aims to minimize manual operations through automation. The facility's test building, the Glassbel headquarters, features a 200 square meter façade for testing these modules, with modules reaching lengths of up to 3.5 meters and outputs of up to 750Wp.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **03-07-2014:** The Smartflex project is part-funded by the EU, with around €2.9 million (approximately US$3.96 million) allocated from the EU Seventh Framework Programme for Research and Innovation to support the development of the BIPV technology.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **04-2014:** The Smartflex project officially commenced in early April 2014. By July 2014, the project had achieved several milestones, including the completion of the software for architects to design PV modules and the development of the manufacturing process and equipment.
