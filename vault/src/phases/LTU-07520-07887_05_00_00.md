### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ignalina NPP Facility in Ignalina, Lithuania

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2009**: The Ignalina Nuclear Power Plant (INPP) ceased operations, with its two RBMK reactors having provided 70% of Lithuania's electricity until their closure.
- **Ongoing**: The facility is currently undergoing decommissioning, which involves the removal of fuel from the reactors and its placement into dry casks for interim storage. This decommissioning process is expected to last until **2038**.
- **Future Plans**: Lithuania is planning to construct a deep geological repository (DGR) for long-lived radioactive waste, with the repository expected to be constructed and commissioned by **2068**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2021-2030**: Lithuania's Development Programme for the Management of Nuclear Facilities and Radioactive Waste outlines the strategy for managing radioactive waste, including the establishment of interim storage facilities and the future deep geological repository.
- **2022**: A contract was signed with Posiva Solutions Oy for the development of the concept for the geological disposal facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2023**: The IAEA conducted a review of Lithuania's plans for the deep geological disposal site, providing recommendations for improving the site selection process.
- **March 2024**: A public consultation was held in Vilnius regarding the potential locations for the deep repository and the project's stages.
- **Ongoing**: Research for the selection of a deep disposal site is currently underway, with a completion target for the research programme set for **2047**. The repository construction is tentatively planned for **2058-2067**.
