## LTU-07523-05340

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ignitis Group Facility in Moray Firth, LTU

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Moray West Offshore Wind Farm, located in the Moray Firth, is an 882 MW offshore wind project that will utilize 60 Siemens Gamesa SG 14-222 DD wind turbines. The facility will produce renewable energy and includes the installation of inter-array cables (IACs) to connect the turbines to the offshore substation, which will then export power to the onshore connection point at Blackhillock near Keith.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - JDR has announced a new GBP 130 million (approximately EUR 149 million) subsea cable manufacturing facility in Cambois, near Blyth, Northumberland, expected to open in 2024. This facility will support the Moray West project by manufacturing high-voltage subsea cables.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - On 08-11-2022, it was reported that Moray West Offshore Wind Farm signed contracts with Seaway 7 and JDR for the supply and installation of inter-array cables. The project secured a Contract for Difference (CfD) as part of the UK Government’s latest CfD Allocation Round.

4. **Labour:**
   - The Moray West project is expected to create jobs, particularly in Seaway 7’s Aberdeen office, as stated by Lloyd Duthie, Vice President – UK and Asia for Seaway 7. The project aims to develop and grow a skilled workforce to meet industry needs in the region.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The Moray West offshore wind farm is developed and majority-owned by Ocean Winds, with Lithuania’s Ignitis Group holding a minority share in the project.
