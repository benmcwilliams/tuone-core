### Evolutionary Timeline of the ArcelorMittal Facility in Differdange, Luxembourg

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2021**: The facility inaugurated Luxembourg's first floating photovoltaic (PV) plant, utilizing 25,000 solar modules on a former cooling pond. The project is associated with ArcelorMittal Differdange, which operates an electric steel plant in the same municipality. The estimated production capacity of the floating solar plant is between 3 and 4 MW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2019**: The project was tendered by the Luxembourg government, indicating governmental support for the development of renewable energy projects in the region.
- **March 2021**: Construction of the floating PV plant began, marking the start of the investment phase for this renewable energy initiative.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2021**: Construction started on the floating solar facility.
- **November 2021**: The floating PV plant was officially inaugurated and became operational.

#### 4. Labour
- There is no specific information provided in the articles regarding job creation or job loss associated with the floating PV plant project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The floating PV plant is owned and operated by Enovos, a German renewable energy company, while ArcelorMittal provided 5.7 hectares of its water surface for the project. This indicates a collaborative effort rather than exclusive ownership by ArcelorMittal.
