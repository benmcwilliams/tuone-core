### Evolutionary Timeline of the Green Genius Facility in Jekabpils, Latvia

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2023**: Green Genius announced plans to build a 100 MW unsubsidized solar installation in Jekabpils, which would become the largest solar installation in Latvia upon completion.
- **September 2023**: The planned solar park near Jekabpils was updated to a capacity of 120.8 MW, occupying 153 hectares. The facility is expected to generate approximately 245 GWh of electricity annually, enough to power around 82,000 households.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2023**: Green Genius announced an investment of €109 million for the construction of the solar facility in Jekabpils. This investment is part of a larger €179 million investment into two solar projects in the Baltic states, including a 78 MW project in Lithuania.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2023**: Announcement of the project to build a 100 MW solar installation in Jekabpils.
- **September 2023**: Confirmation of the updated project capacity to 120.8 MW and the investment amount. Construction is planned to start by mid-2024, with operations expected to begin in 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2023**: While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned, the construction and operation of the facility are expected to contribute to local employment opportunities in the renewable energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2023**: Green Genius is the sole developer of the facility, indicating full ownership of the project. The company has not indicated any joint ventures or partnerships specifically for the Jekabpils facility.
