### Evolutionary Timeline of the Sunly Facility in Krāslava, Latvia

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date: November 2024**  
  Sunly has commenced construction on a 90MW solar PV plant in Krāslava as part of a larger portfolio of three utility-scale solar parks in Latvia, which collectively will have a capacity of 225MW. The projects are designed as hybrid parks, integrating solar PV with battery energy storage systems (BESS) to enhance energy production predictability and reduce operational costs.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date: November 2024**  
  Sunly secured €300 million (approximately US$326 million) in debt financing from various investors, including Rivage Investment, Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners, and Kommunal Landspensjonskasse, to support the development of its renewables projects in the Baltic region and Poland. This funding is tied to the construction of the 225MW solar PV portfolio, which includes the Krāslava facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date: November 2024**  
  Construction has officially begun on the solar parks, including the Krāslava project. The facility is part of a strategic initiative to prepare for the Baltic states' disconnection from the Russia-controlled BRELL frequency area, scheduled for February 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date: November 2024**  
  While specific job creation figures for the Krāslava facility have not been disclosed, the overall construction of the three solar parks is expected to create jobs in the region, contributing to local employment opportunities in the renewable energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date: November 2024**  
  Sunly is an independent power producer based in Estonia and is developing the Krāslava facility as part of its own portfolio. There is no indication of joint ventures or shared ownership; thus, it can be inferred that Sunly holds full ownership of the facility.
