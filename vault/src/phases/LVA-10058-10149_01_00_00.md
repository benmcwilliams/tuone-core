### Evolutionary Timeline of the Sunly Facility in Valmiera, Latvia

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2024**: Sunly has commenced construction on a 54MW solar park in Valmiera as part of a larger portfolio of three utility-scale solar PV parks in Latvia, which will collectively have a capacity of 225MW. The projects are designed as hybrid parks, integrating solar PV with battery energy storage systems (BESS) to enhance energy production predictability and reduce operational costs.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2024**: Sunly secured €300 million (approximately US$326 million) in debt financing from various investors, including Rivage Investment, Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners, and Kommunal Landspensjonskasse, to support the development of its renewables projects in the Baltic region and Poland.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2024**: Construction has officially begun on the three solar parks, including the 54MW facility in Valmiera. The other two projects include a 90MW solar park in Krāslava and an 81MW solar park in Madona. The construction of these parks is part of a strategic move to prepare for the Baltic states' disconnection from the Russia-controlled BRELL frequency area in February 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2024**: While specific job creation figures were not disclosed, the construction of the three solar parks is expected to contribute to local employment opportunities in the Valmiera region and beyond, as the projects are large-scale developments.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2024**: Sunly is identified as an independent power producer based in Estonia, indicating that it owns the facility exclusively without any mention of joint ventures or partnerships for the Valmiera solar park.
