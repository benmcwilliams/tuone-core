## NLD-00018-04911

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Electriq Global Facility in Amsterdam, NLD

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **10/2023:** Electriq Global announced plans to build a hydrogen powder manufacturing plant in the Port of Amsterdam, scheduled to begin operations in 2026. The facility will utilize Electriq Powder technology, which aims to significantly reduce production costs and lower carbon footprints. The plant will be powered by renewable energy from Zenith Energy Terminals.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **10/2023:** Electriq Global secured a €1.1 million grant from the RVO (Netherlands Enterprise Agency) to support the design and engineering of the hydrogen powder plant. This grant is part of a broader initiative to establish the Port of Amsterdam as a central hub for hydrogen carriers, with a target of handling 1 million tons of hydrogen by 2030.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **10/2023:** The project was officially announced, with plans for construction to begin in the near future, leading to operational status by 2026. The collaboration with the Netherlands Foreign Investment Agency (NFIA) and the local partner 'Amsterdam in business' was highlighted as crucial for bringing the project to the Netherlands.

4. **Labour:**
   - **10/2023:** While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned, the announcement indicated that the project would contribute to the local economy and the hydrogen economy in the Netherlands, suggesting potential job creation as the facility develops.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **10/2023:** The facility will be built on the grounds of Zenith Energy Terminals, indicating a partnership. However, the ownership structure specifics (whether it is a joint venture or if Electriq Global has full ownership) were not explicitly detailed in the article.
