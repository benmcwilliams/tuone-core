## NLD-00018-05819

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Port of Amsterdam Facility, Amsterdam, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2020**: The Port of Amsterdam collaborates with Port of Den Helder and IJmuiden Seaport to assess the economic potential of offshore wind projects in North Holland, indicating a focus on supporting wind farm operations and maintenance (O&M) in the region.
- **October 2022**: The Port of Amsterdam, along with Evos and Hydrogenious, signs a memorandum of understanding (MoU) to develop large-scale hydrogen import facilities. These facilities will include storage and a LOHC dehydrogenation plant with a capacity of 100 to 500 tons of hydrogen per day, aiming for operational readiness before 2028.
- **April 2024**: The Port of Amsterdam signs two agreements to develop a green hydrogen corridor with the Port of Bilbao, focusing on the technical feasibility and commercial viability of a liquid hydrogen corridor and exploring the production of synthetic aviation fuel (SAF) in collaboration with Repsol and KLM.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2022**: The MoU for hydrogen facilities indicates a commitment to invest in infrastructure for hydrogen delivery, with a target of handling at least 1 million tons of LOHC volumes per annum.
- **April 2024**: The agreements with Petronor and EnBW for the green hydrogen corridor suggest ongoing investment in renewable hydrogen infrastructure and production capabilities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2020**: The report on offshore wind projects marks the beginning of the Port of Amsterdam's strategic involvement in renewable energy.
- **October 2022**: The signing of the MoU for hydrogen facilities marks a significant milestone in the Port's transition towards hydrogen as a clean energy source.
- **April 2024**: The signing of two new agreements to develop a green hydrogen corridor represents a further commitment to expanding hydrogen capabilities and infrastructure.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2020**: The report anticipates increased business activity and job creation in North Holland due to the offshore wind projects, particularly in the O&M phase, which is expected to generate significant local employment opportunities.
- **October 2022**: The development of hydrogen facilities is expected to create jobs related to the construction and operation of the new infrastructure.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Port of Amsterdam operates as a public port authority, indicating that it does not have exclusive ownership of the hydrogen facilities but collaborates with private companies (Evos, Hydrogenious, Petronor, etc.) through joint ventures and agreements to develop the necessary infrastructure for hydrogen production and delivery. 

This timeline encapsulates the evolution of the Port of Amsterdam facility, highlighting its strategic initiatives in clean technology and renewable energy, particularly in hydrogen and offshore wind sectors.
