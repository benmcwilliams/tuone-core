### Evolutionary Timeline of the Rolls-Royce SMR Facility in Amsterdam, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2022**: Rolls-Royce SMR signed an exclusive agreement with ULC-Energy to collaborate on the deployment of Rolls-Royce SMR power plants in the Netherlands. The Rolls-Royce SMR is a 470 MWe design based on a small pressurized water reactor, capable of providing consistent baseload generation for at least 60 years. A single SMR can supply electricity for approximately 1.4 million Dutch households.
- **November 2023**: Rolls-Royce SMR announced a joint investigation with ULC-Energy and Topsoe to study hydrogen production using the Rolls-Royce SMR in combination with Topsoe's Solid Oxide Electrolysis Cell (SOEC) technology. This collaboration aims to leverage the operational flexibility of the SMR to produce clean hydrogen more efficiently.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2022**: ULC-Energy signed a memorandum of understanding with US utility Constellation, a minority shareholder in Rolls-Royce SMR Limited, to support the deployment of a fleet of Rolls-Royce SMRs in the Netherlands.
- **November 2023**: The formal planning phase for the project is set to begin, with site selection and contract negotiations planned for 2024, followed by a formal licensing application in 2025 and construction of the first SMR unit starting in 2027.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2022**: Exclusive agreement signed with ULC-Energy for the deployment of SMR power plants.

- **November 2023**: Announcement of collaboration with Topsoe for hydrogen production and confirmation of the planning timeline for the SMR facility.
