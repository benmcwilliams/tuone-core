### ULC-Energy Facility Timeline in Amsterdam, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Establishment**: ULC-Energy was established in 2021 in Amsterdam, focusing on developing nuclear energy projects to support decarbonisation in the Netherlands.
- **Production Capacity**: The facility is set to deploy Rolls-Royce SMR (Small Modular Reactor) power plants, with each SMR designed to have a capacity of 470 MWe, capable of providing electricity for approximately 1.4 million Dutch households.
- **Future Plans**: The first SMR unit's construction is scheduled to begin in 2027, with a start-up date anticipated in the 2030s.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Collaborations**: In August 2022, ULC-Energy signed an exclusive agreement with Rolls-Royce SMR to collaborate on deploying SMR power plants in the Netherlands. Additionally, a memorandum of understanding was signed with US utility Constellation to support the deployment of a fleet of SMRs.
- **Hydrogen Production Study**: In November 2023, ULC-Energy announced a joint investigation with Rolls-Royce SMR and Topsoe to study hydrogen production using high-temperature electrolysis, which is expected to be a competitive solution for clean hydrogen production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Planning Phase**: The formal planning phase for the SMR deployment began in 2023, with site selection and contract negotiations expected in 2024. A formal licensing application is planned for 2025.
- **Construction Timeline**: Construction of the first SMR unit is scheduled to start in 2027.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: While specific job creation numbers have not been detailed, the collaboration with BAM Infra Nederland and the deployment of SMRs are expected to contribute positively to job creation in the construction and energy sectors.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: ULC-Energy is a privately established company and is not mentioned as being in a joint venture for the facility. It operates independently while collaborating with Rolls-Royce SMR and other partners for specific projects.
