### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hydrogenious Facility in Amsterdam, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2022**: Hydrogenious, in collaboration with Evos and the Port of Amsterdam, announced plans to develop large-scale import facilities for hydrogen at the Port of Amsterdam. The facilities will include storage and a Liquid Organic Hydrogen Carrier (LOHC) dehydrogenation plant, with a final release capacity of between 100 and 500 tons of hydrogen per day. The total handled LOHC volumes are projected to be at least 1 million tons per annum.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2022**: The collaboration between Evos, Hydrogenious, and the Port of Amsterdam is part of a broader strategy to enhance hydrogen delivery capabilities, with the objective to have the first plants operational before 2028. Specific investment amounts were not disclosed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2022**: An MoU was signed between Evos, Hydrogenious, and the Port of Amsterdam to realize the hydrogen import facilities. The feasibility study completed by the parties indicated that thermal oil benzyl toluene (LOHC-BT) technology is compatible with the existing infrastructure at the Port of Amsterdam.
