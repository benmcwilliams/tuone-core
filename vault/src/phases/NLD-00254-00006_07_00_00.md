### Evolutionary Timeline of the ABB Facility in Delft, Netherlands

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** December 2020
   - **Details:** ABB unveiled its new E-mobility Innovation Lab in Delft, a research and development center focused on electric vehicle (EV) charging infrastructure. The facility spans 3,600 m² and is designed to house 120 specialists working on next-generation solutions. It features advanced technology for testing compatibility with all vehicle types, including environmental testing rooms for extreme conditions (-40 to +100 degrees Celsius). The facility is also equipped with solar panels and a geothermal heating/cooling system to minimize environmental impact.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** December 2020
   - **Details:** The facility represents a $10 million investment by ABB. Additionally, ABB announced a broader investment of $30 million into a global Center of Excellence and production site for EV charging infrastructure in Italy, indicating a strategic commitment to enhancing its technology leadership in the e-mobility sector.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** December 2020
   - **Details:** The E-mobility Innovation Lab was officially unveiled, marking the start of operations aimed at advancing ABB's portfolio in sustainable mobility and e-mobility solutions.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** December 2020
   - **Details:** The facility is expected to create jobs for 120 specialists, contributing to local employment in the tech and clean energy sectors.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** December 2020
   - **Details:** ABB owns the facility exclusively, as it is a direct investment by the company to enhance its R&D capabilities in e-mobility.
