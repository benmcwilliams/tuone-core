### Evolutionary Timeline of the TU Delft Facility in Delft, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **07-12-2021**: TU Delft researchers developed an ultra-thin silicon layer for heterojunction solar cells, achieving an efficiency of 22.2%. This innovation aims to improve performance by replacing traditional amorphous silicon layers with nanocrystalline silicon layers, which could lead to further enhancements in efficiency.

- **06-01-2023**: Drilling commenced for the Geothermie Delft geothermal heating project, which will serve as a research facility and supply sustainable heat to the TU Delft campus and nearby neighborhoods. The first hot water is expected by mid-2024.

- **21-02-2023**: The EPOS-eNLarge project, which aims to improve understanding of subsurface processes for geothermal energy production, received EUR 17.9 million in funding. This project will complement the geothermal facility at TU Delft.

- **04-05-2023**: The geothermal project is positioned to become one of the largest international research facilities on geothermal energy in urban environments, focusing on data collection for better simulations and risk reduction in geothermal projects.

- **09-12-2023**: Drilling for the Geothermie Delft project was completed, yielding promising results with thermal water at 78 °C, capable of supplying district heating needs for 30 years.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **21-02-2023**: The EPOS-eNLarge project received EUR 17.9 million from the Dutch Research Council (NWO) to enhance geothermal research capabilities.

- **26-04-2023**: The OFFSET project, which involves a floating hydrogen and ammonia facility linked to an offshore wind farm, secured EUR 3 million in funding from the Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO) for research into seawater electrolysis.

- **04-05-2023**: Follow-on investments from the consortium partners (Shell Geothermal, EBN, TU Delft, and Aardyn) enabled the continuation of drilling operations for the geothermal project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12-12-2022**: Drilling operations for the Geothermie Delft project began with a small ceremony.

- **21-06-2023**: A spud event marked the official start of drilling, with actual drilling commencing shortly after.

- **26-06-2023**: Drilling officially began, with plans for two holes to be drilled to a depth of 1.5 kilometers.

- **09-12-2023**: Completion of drilling operations was reported, with 80 core samples collected for further research.

#### 4. Labour
- **06-01-2023**: The geothermal project is expected to create jobs related to drilling and research activities, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

- **04-05-2023**: The geothermal facility is anticipated to enhance research capabilities, potentially leading to job creation in the geothermal energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **06-01-2023**: The Geothermie Delft project is a joint venture involving TU Delft, EBN, Shell, and Aardyn, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by TU Delft.
