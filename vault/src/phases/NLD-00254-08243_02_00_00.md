### Aardyn Facility Evolution Timeline - Delft, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **06-01-2023**: Drilling commenced for the Geothermie Delft geothermal heating project at TU Delft. The facility aims to supply sustainable heat to the campus and nearby neighborhoods, while also serving as a research facility for geothermal studies. The first hot water is expected by mid-2024.
- **27-06-2023**: Official drilling began on June 26, 2023, with plans for two holes to be drilled, reaching a depth of approximately 1.5 kilometers. The facility will provide renewable heat and gather data to improve geothermal project simulations.
- **09-12-2023**: Drilling was completed, with 80 core samples collected. Early results indicated a temperature of 78 °C from the Delft Sandstone formation, with a capacity to extract approximately 400 cubic meters of thermal water per hour. The geothermal resource is projected to meet the district heating needs of Delft for 30 years.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04-05-2023**: The consortium behind the project, which includes Aardyn, TU Delft, Shell, and EBN, provided necessary follow-on investment to support drilling operations. Additionally, the EPOS-eNLarge project received EUR 17.9 million in funding from the Dutch Research Council (NWO) to support monitoring infrastructure for the geothermal project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12-12-2022**: Initial drilling operations began with a small ceremony.
- **21-06-2023**: A spud event marked the official start of drilling operations.
- **26-06-2023**: Actual drilling work commenced.
- **09-12-2023**: Completion of drilling operations was announced.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Geothermie Delft facility is a joint project involving TU Delft, EBN, Shell, and Aardyn, indicating that Aardyn does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a consortium.
