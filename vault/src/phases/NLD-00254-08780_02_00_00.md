## NLD-00254-08780

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Energie Beheer Nederland (EBN) Facility in Delft, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2023**: Drilling officially commenced for the geothermal heating project at TU Delft. The project involves drilling vertically for about one kilometer and then deviating for an additional 500 meters, with two holes planned. The geothermal source aims to provide renewable heat for TU Delft and surrounding districts.
- **December 2023**: Drilling was completed, and a total of 80 core samples were collected. Early results indicated that the water pumped from the Delft Sandstone formation has a temperature of 78 °C, with an extraction capacity of approximately 400 cubic meters of thermal water per hour. The geothermal resource is expected to supply district heating needs for Delft for 30 years, with additional heating provided by a heat pump.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2023**: The consortium behind the project, which includes EBN, Shell Geothermal, TU Delft, and Aardyn, provided necessary follow-on investment to facilitate drilling operations. The EPOS-eNLarge project received EUR 17.9 million in funding from the Dutch Research Council (NWO) to support monitoring infrastructure for the geothermal project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2023**: Announcement of the geothermal project and the consortium's investment, highlighting its role as a research facility.
- **June 2023**: Official start of drilling on June 26, following a spud event on June 21.
- **December 2023**: Completion of drilling for the geothermal project, with promising early results regarding the geothermal resource.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is part of a consortium involving multiple partners: Shell Geothermal, Energie Beheer Nederland (EBN), TU Delft, and Aardyn. Therefore, EBN does not own the facility exclusively; it is a joint venture with other entities.
