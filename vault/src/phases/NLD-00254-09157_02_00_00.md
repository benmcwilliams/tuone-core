### Evolutionary Timeline of the Huisman Facility in Delft, Netherlands

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 12/2022
     - **Details:** Drilling commenced for the Geothermie Delft geothermal heating project at TU Delft. The facility will serve as a research site for geothermal studies and supply sustainable heat to the campus and nearby neighborhoods. The initial drilling equipment is designed for the first 80 meters of soft ground, with plans to drill two holes for water supply and discharge.

   - **Date:** 04/2023
     - **Details:** Plans to deploy a larger drilling rig to drill through harder rocks to a depth of 2.5 kilometers. This phase is expected to take approximately six months.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** Mid-2022
     - **Details:** Huisman was selected to deliver the drilling rig for the Geothermie Delft project, indicating a significant investment in the technology and infrastructure necessary for the geothermal facility.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 06/01/2023
     - **Details:** Official announcement of the commencement of drilling operations for the Geothermie Delft project. The first hot water is anticipated by mid-2024.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 12/2022
     - **Details:** The commencement of drilling operations involved personnel from TU Delft and drilling teams, indicating job creation related to the project, although specific numbers are not provided.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Details:** The Geothermie Delft facility is a joint project involving TU Delft, EBN, Shell, and Aardyn. Therefore, Huisman does not own the facility exclusively but is a key contributor through its provision of drilling technology.
