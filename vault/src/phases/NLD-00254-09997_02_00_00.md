## NLD-00254-09997

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Shell Geothermal Facility in Delft, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2023**: Drilling officially commenced for the geothermal heating project at TU Delft. The project involves drilling vertically for about one kilometer and then deviating for an additional 500 meters, with two holes planned. The geothermal resource tapped under Delfgauw is expected to supply the district heating needs of Delft for 30 years, with an extraction capacity of approximately 400 cubic meters of thermal water per hour at a temperature of 78 °C.
- **December 2023**: Drilling was completed, and a total of 80 core samples were collected to better characterize the subsurface geology and geothermal resource. The geothermal source will also serve as a research facility to gather data and generate more accurate simulations for future geothermal projects.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2023**: The consortium behind the project, which includes Shell Geothermal, EBN, TU Delft, and Aardyn, provided necessary follow-on investment to facilitate drilling operations. Additionally, the EPOS-eNLarge project received EUR 17.9 million in funding from the Dutch Research Council (NWO) to support monitoring infrastructure for the geothermal project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2023**: Announcement of the project progress and investment, highlighting the facility's role in research and data collection for geothermal energy in urban environments.
- **June 21, 2023**: A spud event was held to mark the official start of drilling, attended by over 200 guests.
- **June 26, 2023**: Actual drilling work commenced.
- **December 2023**: Completion of drilling operations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is a collaborative project involving a consortium of partners: Shell Geothermal, Energie Beheer Nederland (EBN), TU Delft, and Aardyn. There is no indication of exclusive ownership by Shell; rather, it is a joint venture among the listed partners.
