### Evolutionary Timeline of the Solarcentury Facility in Eindhoven, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2018**: Solarcentury and Encavis announced the development of a 44 MW solar farm near Eindhoven, claiming it to be the largest PV farm in the Netherlands. The project is expected to produce nearly 32 million kWh per year, covering the annual electricity consumption of over 13,000 people.
- **December 2018**: The Budel Solar Park, with a capacity of 44 MW, was completed and became operational. It was built on a site previously used by the Nyrstar zinc smelter.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2018**: The total investment in the solar park was reported to be €28.6 million. The Dutch government guaranteed a feed-in tariff (FIT) of €0.104/kWh for 15 years, which supports the financial viability of the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2018**: Announcement of the project development by Solarcentury and Encavis.
- **December 2018**: Completion of the Budel Solar Park and commencement of operations.
- **June 2019**: The facility was reported to have raised €250,000 through a crowdfunding campaign, indicating community involvement and support for the project.

#### 4. Labour

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2018**: The Budel Solar Park is co-owned by Solarcentury and Encavis, with Solarcentury retaining a 20% equity share in the farm.
