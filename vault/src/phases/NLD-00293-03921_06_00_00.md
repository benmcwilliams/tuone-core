### Encavis Facility in Eindhoven, NLD - Evolutionary Timeline

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2018**: Encavis, in partnership with Solarcentury, announced the development of a 44 MW solar farm near Eindhoven, which they claimed would be the largest PV farm in the Netherlands. The project is expected to produce nearly 32 million kWh per year, sufficient to cover the annual electricity consumption of over 13,000 people. 
- **November 2018**: The Budel park, as the facility is known, commenced operations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2018**: The total investment for the solar park was reported to be €28.6 million. The Dutch government guaranteed a feed-in tariff (FIT) of €0.104/kWh for 15 years, which supports the financial viability of the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2018**: Construction of the solar farm began, with completion slated for the end of the year.
- **November 2018**: The facility officially began operations.
- **June 2019**: The facility was reported to be operational and had raised €250,000 through a crowdfunding campaign, indicating community involvement and support.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2019**: The project was noted to have engaged local citizens in its funding through a crowdfunding initiative, although specific job creation or loss figures were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2019**: The Budel park is co-owned by Encavis and Solarcentury, with Solarcentury retaining a 20% equity share in the facility. This indicates a joint venture ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by Encavis.
