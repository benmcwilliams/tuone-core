### Evolutionary Timeline of the NAM Facility in Emmen, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2019**: NAM, in collaboration with Enexis and Gasunie, is exploring the potential for hydrogen production using excess solar power in the northeastern Netherlands. The feasibility study includes the assessment of hydrogen production at NAM sites in Emmen and Roswinkel. The proposed electrolyzer capacity is between 20-50 MW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2019**: The feasibility study is being conducted by DNV-GL, focusing on the conditions for converting locally generated sustainable energy into hydrogen and the potential for transporting this hydrogen via existing gas pipelines.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2019**: The study is set to reveal results by mid-January 2020, indicating that the project is in the planning and assessment phase.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2019**: No specific job creation or job loss information is provided in the article regarding the facility at this stage.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2019**: The facility is part of a joint venture (JV) formed by Shell and ExxonMobil, indicating that NAM does not own the facility exclusively but operates under a joint venture structure.
