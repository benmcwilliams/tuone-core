### Evolutionary Timeline of the Shell Facility in Rotterdam, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **01-06-2021**: Shell announces the development of a **200 MW electrolysis-based hydrogen plant** in Rotterdam, expected to produce **50,000 – 60,000 kg of green hydrogen per day**. This facility will be powered by renewable energy from an offshore wind farm.
- **10-01-2022**: Shell signs a contract with thyssenkrupp Uhde Chlorine Engineers for the **Holland Hydrogen I** project, confirming the construction of the **200 MW electrolysis plant**.
- **08-07-2022**: Shell confirms the **final investment decision (FID)** for the Holland Hydrogen I facility, which will be **Europe’s largest renewable hydrogen plant** upon completion in **2025**.
- **20-06-2023**: The **Hollandse Kust Noord offshore wind farm** begins producing electricity, which will be used to power the hydrogen plant in Rotterdam.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **01-06-2021**: The announcement of the hydrogen plant indicates significant investment in green hydrogen technology, although specific investment amounts are not detailed.
- **08-07-2022**: The FID for the Holland Hydrogen I facility marks a commitment to substantial investment in renewable hydrogen infrastructure.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **01-06-2021**: Announcement of the hydrogen plant development.
- **10-01-2022**: Contract signed with thyssenkrupp for engineering and construction.
- **08-07-2022**: FID taken for the Holland Hydrogen I facility, with operations expected to start in **2025**.
- **20-06-2023**: The Hollandse Kust Noord offshore wind farm begins delivering electricity, a critical milestone for the hydrogen plant's operation.

#### 4. Labour
- **01-07-2020**: The report emphasizes that hydrogen projects will create new employment opportunities in the region, although specific job numbers are not provided.
- **01-06-2021**: Shell's hydrogen initiatives are expected to contribute to job creation in the industrial and transport sectors.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Shell is the **exclusive owner** of the hydrogen facility, as indicated by its direct involvement in the development and investment decisions related to the Holland Hydrogen I project. The facility is part of Shell's broader strategy to transition to net-zero emissions by 2050.
