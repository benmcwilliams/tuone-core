### Evolutionary Timeline of the Vattenfall Facility in Rotterdam, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2024**: The Zeevonk project, a joint venture between Vattenfall and Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (CIP), is announced. It will consist of a 2 GW offshore wind farm and a 50 MW floating offshore solar farm located 62 km off the Dutch coast. A significant portion of the electricity generated will be converted into green hydrogen at a large-scale electrolyser plant at the port of Rotterdam. The project is expected to be operational in **2029**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2024**: A 250 MW Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) is secured by CIP with Google for the wind power generated at the Zeevonk project. This agreement is part of the broader investment strategy to support the energy transition and the development of the facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2024**: The Zeevonk project is officially announced, detailing its components and expected operational date. The project is designed to integrate offshore wind, floating solar, and green hydrogen production.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2024**: The partnership between Vattenfall, CIP, and Google is expected to drive local growth and job creation as the project progresses, although specific job numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2024**: The facility is a joint venture between Vattenfall and Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (CIP), indicating that Vattenfall does not own the facility exclusively but shares ownership with CIP.
