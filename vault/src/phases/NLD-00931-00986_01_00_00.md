### Evolutionary Timeline of the Connected Energy Facility in Rotterdam, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2018**: Connected Energy, in collaboration with Engie, launched a grid-connected storage project in Rotterdam utilizing second-life electric vehicle (EV) batteries. The initial phase of the project has a production capacity of **150 kW/90 kWh** and aims to provide grid balancing services to TenneT. The E-Stor system developed by Connected Energy allows for individual control of each battery pack, optimizing their utilization for grid services such as frequency response.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2018**: The project is part of a three-stage rollout, with the second stage expected to have a power rating of **1 MW** and the third stage anticipated to be larger, although specific details were not disclosed. The project is backed by Engie, indicating a significant investment in the development of second-life battery applications.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2018**: The proof of concept for the project was successfully integrated into the distribution system of TenneT, marking the official start of the facility's operations. The second stage is planned for early 2019, followed by the third stage later in 2019.
