### Evolutionary Timeline of the Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners Facility in Rotterdam, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2024**: The Zeevonk project, a joint venture between Vattenfall and Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (CIP), is announced. It will consist of a **2 GW offshore wind farm** and a **50 MW floating offshore solar farm**, located 62 km off the Dutch coast. A significant portion of the electricity generated will be converted into **green hydrogen** at a large-scale electrolyser plant at the port of Rotterdam. The project is expected to be operational by **2029**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2024**: CIP secures a **250 MW Power Purchase Agreement (PPA)** with Google for wind power generated at the Zeevonk project. This agreement is part of a broader strategy to support the energy transition and is indicative of the project's scale and significance in the renewable energy sector.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2024**: The announcement of the Zeevonk project marks a significant milestone, highlighting the collaboration between CIP and Google, and the project's design focused on system integration of offshore wind, floating solar, and green hydrogen. The project is set to move forward with construction expected to begin in the near future, aiming for operational status by **2029**.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2024**: The partnership between CIP and Google is expected to drive local growth and job creation, although specific job numbers have not been detailed in the articles. The project is designed to contribute to the Dutch energy transition, which typically involves job creation in renewable energy sectors.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2024**: The facility is a **joint venture** between Vattenfall and Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (CIP), indicating that CIP does not own the facility exclusively but shares ownership with Vattenfall.
