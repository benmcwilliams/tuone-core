### Evolutionary Timeline of the Eneco Facility in Rotterdam, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2023**: Eneco submitted a planning application for the construction of the "Eneco Electrolyser," an 800MW green hydrogen production plant in Rotterdam’s Europoort industrial area. The plant will utilize renewable electricity from wind farms and solar parks to produce hydrogen, aiming to replace natural gas in industrial and electricity sectors. The plant is expected to produce approximately 80 kilotonnes of hydrogen annually, depending on renewable energy availability and industrial demand.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2019**: Mitsubishi Corporation and Chubu Electric Power Co Inc made a binding offer of €4.1 billion to acquire Eneco, which will enable the company to expand its renewable energy assets. Mitsubishi committed a €1 billion internal loan to finance Eneco’s long-term investments.
- **May 2020**: Eneco and Shell, through their joint venture CrossWind, launched a bid for the Hollandse Kust Noord offshore wind zone, intending to develop a 759MW offshore wind farm to be operational by 2023. This project is part of a broader strategy to create a green hydrogen hub in Rotterdam.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2019**: Announcement of the acquisition deal by Mitsubishi and Chubu, which is subject to approval from the shareholder municipalities.
- **May 2020**: CrossWind's bid for the Hollandse Kust Noord offshore wind zone was announced.
- **November 2023**: Planning application for the Eneco Electrolyser was submitted, with construction expected to start in 2026 and operations to begin in 2029.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2019**: The acquisition by Mitsubishi and Chubu was stated to not change the workforce at Eneco, ensuring job stability during the transition to private ownership.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2019**: Eneco was publicly owned by 44 municipalities in the Netherlands before the acquisition by Mitsubishi Corporation and Chubu Electric Power Co Inc. Following the acquisition, Mitsubishi will become the largest shareholder, while Eneco will remain an integrated and independent Dutch energy company. The facility will be developed in collaboration with Mitsubishi through the Eneco Diamond Hydrogen joint venture.
