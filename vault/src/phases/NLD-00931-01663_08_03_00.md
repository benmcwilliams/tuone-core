## NLD-00931-01663

### Evolutionary Timeline of the GE Facility in Rotterdam, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2019**: GE Renewable Energy announced the installation of the prototype of its 12MW Haliade-X offshore turbine in Rotterdam, in collaboration with the Future Wind joint venture (Sif Holdings and Pondera Development). The prototype is designed for testing and data collection to obtain type certification by 2021.
- **November 2019**: The Haliade-X prototype was installed and began producing power.
- **June 2020**: The Haliade-X 12 MW prototype received a provisional type certificate from DNV GL, confirming its safety and quality standards.
- **October 2020**: The prototype was upgraded to a 13MW version, which is expected to generate 4% more annual energy production than the previous model.
- **October 2021**: The Haliade-X prototype was reported to be operating at 14 MW, further enhancing its power output capabilities.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2019**: The partnership with Future Wind included a 15-year full-service operation and maintenance agreement, indicating significant investment in the facility's operational capabilities.
- **June 2020**: GE Renewable Energy announced that the Haliade-X technology had been selected for multiple large-scale wind power projects in the US and UK, indicating substantial future investments tied to these projects.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2019**: A ground-breaking ceremony was held to mark the installation preparations for the Haliade-X prototype at the Maasvlakte site in Rotterdam.
- **November 2019**: The Haliade-X prototype was successfully installed and began generating power.
- **June 2020**: The prototype received a provisional type certificate, a critical milestone for its commercial viability.
- **October 2020**: The upgraded 13MW version of the Haliade-X began operations.
- **October 2021**: The prototype was reported to be operating at 14 MW.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2020**: The testing and development of the Haliade-X technology, including the nacelle and blade testing, were expected to create jobs and generate economic benefits in the UK, as indicated by the ORE Catapult's statements. However, specific job creation numbers related to the Rotterdam facility were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility in Rotterdam is operated under a joint venture model, specifically through the Future Wind consortium, which includes GE Renewable Energy, Sif Holdings, and Pondera Development. This indicates that GE does not have exclusive ownership of the facility but is a key partner in its operations.
