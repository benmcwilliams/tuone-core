## NLD-00931-02164

### Evolutionary Timeline of the BP Facility in Rotterdam, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **02/2022**: BP, in collaboration with Hydrogen Chemistry Company (HyCC), announced the development of the H2-Fifty project, a 250MW green hydrogen plant in the port area of Rotterdam. This facility aims to produce green hydrogen to replace fossil-based feedstock used at BP’s Rotterdam refinery and other industries in the port area. The project is expected to displace up to 350,000 tons of carbon emissions annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **02/2022**: The H2-Fifty project is backed by the Topsector Energy (TSE), a Dutch government initiative, and has been nominated for the Important Projects of Common European Interest (IPCEI) Hydrogen programme. A final investment decision for the plant is anticipated in the following year.
- **12/05/2022**: BP submitted bids for two offshore wind leases in the Netherlands, with a potential capacity of 1.4GW. BP plans to invest up to €2 billion into clean energy projects in the Netherlands, which includes the integration of offshore wind power with green hydrogen production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **01/07/2020**: A report by the Dutch Research Institute For Transitions (DRIFT) highlighted Rotterdam's potential as a leading hydrogen hub, emphasizing the need for investments in hydrogen production and infrastructure.
- **02/2022**: BP and HyCC signed a joint development agreement for the H2-Fifty project, following a feasibility study that confirmed its critical role in decarbonizing the region's industry.
- **12/05/2022**: BP's bids for the Hollandse Kust (West) offshore wind farm leases were submitted, which include plans for integrating green hydrogen production to support BP's Rotterdam refinery.

#### 4. Labour
- **01/07/2020**: The DRIFT report indicated that hydrogen projects would contribute to job creation in the region, supporting the local economy and climate targets.
- **12/05/2022**: BP announced plans to develop a skills and entrepreneurship incubator to support workforce development in new industries related to clean energy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **02/2022**: BP is developing the H2-Fifty project in a joint venture with HyCC, indicating a collaborative ownership structure for this facility. The specific ownership details for the offshore wind projects are not mentioned, but BP is leading the bids, suggesting a significant stake in the developments.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic initiatives related to BP's facility in Rotterdam, focusing on its evolution in technology, investment, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure.
