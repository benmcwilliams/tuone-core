## NLD-00931-02179

### Evolutionary Timeline of Chiyoda Corporation Facility in Rotterdam, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **03-08-2021**: Chiyoda Corporation, in collaboration with Mitsubishi Corporation, announced a feasibility study for the commercial-scale import of hydrogen using Chiyoda's Spera Hydrogen technology, which involves releasing hydrogen from Methylcyclohexane (MCH). The goal is to import 100 to 200 kilotons per annum (ktpa) of hydrogen by 2025 and 300 to 400 ktpa by 2030.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **03-08-2021**: The feasibility study is part of a broader commercial development initiative involving the Port of Rotterdam Authority and Koole Terminal, focusing on the import of hydrogen from overseas sources to Northern Europe.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **03-08-2021**: The announcement of the feasibility study marks a significant milestone in the project, with the study expected to take one year to complete. This project follows a previous demonstration project completed in 2020, which focused on the long-distance transportation and storage of hydrogen.

#### 4. Labour
- **03-08-2021**: While specific job creation or loss figures were not mentioned in the articles, the expansion of hydrogen import capabilities and the feasibility study suggest potential job creation in the region as the project develops.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **03-08-2021**: The facility is part of a joint venture involving Chiyoda Corporation and Mitsubishi Corporation, indicating that Chiyoda does not own the facility exclusively but is collaborating with other entities for its development.

### Technology and Operations
- "Chiyoda Corporation has developed the Spera Hydrogen technology to release hydrogen from MCH [Methylcyclohexane]. MCH is produced from toluene through hydrogenation process."
- "The ambition of the companies to import 100 to 200 ktpa hydrogen in 2025 and 300 to 400 ktpa in 2030."

### Investment and Development
- "Chiyoda Corporation and Mitsubishi Corporation to conduct a joint feasibility study for a commercial-scale import of hydrogen from overseas sources to one terminal in the port of Rotterdam."
