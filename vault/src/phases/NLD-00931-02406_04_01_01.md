### Evolutionary Timeline of the Port of Rotterdam Facility, Rotterdam, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2019**: The Port of Rotterdam is involved in the North Sea Wind Power Hub (NSWPH) consortium, which explores the feasibility of offshore wind power hubs that may include hydrogen production. The facility could be operational in the 2030s.
- **02/2021**: The Port of Rotterdam is set to host a 100 MW electrolyzer by 2025, with plans to expand to 500 MW.
- **06/2021**: The Port of Rotterdam is developing an 11-hectare site for a green hydrogen plant powered by wind energy, with a capacity of up to 1 GW, expected to be ready around 2028.
- **05/2023**: The Port of Rotterdam plans to import 4.6 million tonnes of hydrogen annually by 2030 and is developing a 200 MW electrolysis plant using electricity from the Hollandse Kust Noord offshore wind farm.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2020**: A memorandum of understanding is signed between Portugal and the Netherlands to develop a strategic export-import value chain for green hydrogen, linking the Sines hydrogen facility in Portugal to the Port of Rotterdam.
- **10/2020**: A pre-feasibility study is completed for exporting green hydrogen from Iceland to the Port of Rotterdam.
- **05/2022**: The Port of Rotterdam Authority announces plans to import renewable energy on a large scale through green hydrogen, strengthening its position as a hydrogen hub in Europe.
- **05/2023**: The Port of Rotterdam and Brazil's Pecém port sign a collaboration agreement to promote green hydrogen production and logistics.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2019**: The NSWPH consortium's assessment phase is completed, recommending a gradual rollout of offshore wind hubs.
- **10/2020**: The MoU with Landsvirkjun for hydrogen export from Iceland is signed.
- **02/2021**: Announcement of the 100 MW electrolyzer project at the Port of Rotterdam.
- **06/2021**: The Port of Rotterdam is developing a site for a green hydrogen plant.
- **05/2023**: The Port of Rotterdam Authority co-signs a declaration of intent with the Port of Pecém for energy projects, including green hydrogen.

#### 4. Labour

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **05/2023**: The Port of Rotterdam Authority co-owns the Port of Pecém, indicating a collaborative ownership structure for international hydrogen projects.
