### Evolutionary Timeline of the Remeha Facility in Rotterdam, Netherlands

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** June 2019
     - A pilot project commenced in Rozenburg near Rotterdam, featuring the world’s first hydrogen-powered domestic boiler. This boiler operates under real conditions and is designed to contribute to the decarbonization of heating systems by burning pure hydrogen generated from renewable energy sources.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** January 2020
     - The project is a joint initiative involving Remeha (a subsidiary of BDR Thermea Group), the network operator Stedin, the municipality of Rotterdam, and the housing association Ressort Wonen. The use of an existing natural gas pipeline for hydrogen transport indicates a significant investment in infrastructure to support hydrogen technology.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** June 25, 2019
     - The hydrogen boiler was put into operation as part of the pilot project.
   - **Date:** January 2020
     - Following the pilot in the Netherlands, plans were announced for another field trial in Great Britain, with over 400 hydrogen boilers to be installed over the next two years.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** January 2020
     - While specific job creation or loss figures were not mentioned, the development and operation of the hydrogen boiler project imply potential job creation related to the installation and maintenance of the new technology.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** January 2020
     - The facility is operated by Remeha, which is a subsidiary of the BDR Thermea Group. The project is a joint initiative, indicating that while Remeha oversees the project, it collaborates with other entities (Stedin, municipality of Rotterdam, and Ressort Wonen) rather than having exclusive ownership.
