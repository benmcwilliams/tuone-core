1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility is involved in the potential establishment of a green hydrogen supply chain, where green hydrogen will be released from liquid organic hydrogen carriers (LOHC) and used primarily in the mobility and industrial sectors. The LOHC technology allows for the bonding of hydrogen molecules to an organic carrier, enabling transport using conventional fuel infrastructure.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - Royal Vopak is a co-investor in a project that includes the construction of the world’s largest plant for storing green hydrogen in LOHC in Dormagen, Germany. The project is supported by funding of €9 million from the state of North Rhine-Westphalia, with an additional €2 million allocated for scientific studies at the Jülich Research Center.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - The project in Dormagen, which is linked to the Royal Vopak facility in Rotterdam, is currently under construction as of March 2021, with commissioning scheduled for 2023. The project aims to integrate the heat energy released during the LOHC hydrogenation process into the steam network, enhancing overall efficiency.
