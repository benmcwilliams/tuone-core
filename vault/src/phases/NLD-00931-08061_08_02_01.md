## NLD-00931-08061

### Evolutionary Timeline of Sif Holding Facility in Rotterdam, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2019**: Sif Holding secured a contract to manufacture 114 monopiles for Vattenfall’s 1.4GW Hollandse Kust (Zuid) offshore wind project. The monopiles will be produced using 113 KTons of steel and stored at the MV2 facility in Rotterdam for 2020-2021.
- **December 2020**: Sif was awarded a contract to supply 70 "TP-less" monopile foundations for the 759MW Hollandse Kust Noord offshore wind farm, with fabrication scheduled to start in 2022 at the MV2 facility.
- **February 2023**: Sif announced a Final Investment Decision to construct the world’s largest monopile foundation manufacturing plant at the Maasvlakte 2 site in Rotterdam. The new facility will increase Sif's total capacity to 500 kilotons per year, with operations expected to start in the second half of 2024.
- **December 2023**: Sif confirmed collaboration with Ecowende for the Hollandse Kust West site VI project, involving the production of 52 transition piece-less monopiles, with a total weight of approximately 70 kilotons, scheduled for production in 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2023**: The construction of the new facility is projected to cost EUR 328 million, funded through various sources including EUR 100 million in advanced factory payments from launching customers, EUR 50 million preferred equity from Equinor, and EUR 50 million common equity from a rights offering underwritten by Egeria.
  
#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2019**: Contract secured for the Hollandse Kust (Zuid) project.
- **December 2020**: Awarded contract for Hollandse Kust Noord project.
- **April 2023**: Construction of the new monopile factory is expected to start.
- **Second half of 2024**: First manufacturing operations from the new facility are scheduled to begin.
- **2025**: Production of monopiles for the Hollandse Kust West site VI project is set to commence.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2023**: The expansion of the facility is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2023**: Sif Holding is primarily owned by Egeria, which is supportive of the expansion plan. The facility operates under Sif's full ownership, with partnerships for specific projects (e.g., joint ventures with Shell and Eneco for certain contracts).
