### Exasun Facility Timeline in The Hague, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2015**: Exasun launched commercial operations with its first pilot production line for black glass modules at its facility in The Hague.
- **2016**: A second production line for black glass modules was installed.
- **07-06-2018**: Exasun announced plans to install a new production line, increasing its production capacity fivefold from approximately 15 MW to 100 MW. The facility produces black glass modules (60 cells, 1×1.6 m) and BIPV modules for waterproof PV-roofs and PV facades, utilizing Metal Wrap-Through back-contact cells with no busbars.
- **2020**: Exasun projected to increase its capacity in phases to more than 1 GW, potentially at other locations in the Netherlands or abroad.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07-06-2018**: Exasun secured funding from the regional government of South Holland through the innovation fund ENERGIIQ, along with financial support from Dutch banks ING and ABN Amro, to facilitate the expansion of its production capacity. The specific amount of funding was not disclosed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **07-06-2018**: Announcement of the new production line installation and capacity expansion plans.
- **Autumn 2018**: Expected start of manufacturing activities for the new production line.
- **22-01-2024**: Exasun filed for insolvency, attributing the decision to soaring production costs and a significant drop in module sales prices due to market conditions.

#### 4. Labour
