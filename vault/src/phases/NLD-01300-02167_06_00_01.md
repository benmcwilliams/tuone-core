### Evolutionary Timeline of the BayWa Facility in Zwolle, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2019**: BayWa r.e. completed the Sekdoorn floating solar installation near Zwolle with a capacity of **14.5 MWp**. This project was part of a larger initiative to expand floating solar technology in Europe.
- **February 2020**: Construction began on the **27.4 MWp Bomhofsplas floating solar project** on a sandpit lake in Zwolle. This project was intended to be the largest floating PV installation outside of China, featuring **73,000 PV modules** and **13 floating transformers**.
- **July 2021**: BayWa r.e. announced the commissioning of two additional floating PV facilities in the Netherlands, bringing their total floating PV portfolio in Europe to **11 projects** with an aggregate capacity exceeding **180 MWp**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2019**: BayWa r.e. indicated plans to invest in further floating solar projects, aiming to add more than **100 MW** of floating PV capacity in Europe in 2020.
- **February 2020**: The Bomhofsplas project was designed to be constructed in an **almost completely carbon-free** manner, utilizing existing solar capacity and a **600 kW battery** for onsite energy needs.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2019**: Completion of the **14.5 MWp Sekdoorn project**.
- **February 2020**: Start of construction on the **27.4 MWp Bomhofsplas project**, expected to be completed within **eight weeks**.
- **July 2021**: Completion of two floating PV plants with a combined capacity of **70.9 MW**, marking significant growth in BayWa's floating solar portfolio.
