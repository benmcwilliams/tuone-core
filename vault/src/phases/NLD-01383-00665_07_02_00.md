### Evolutionary Timeline of the Rivian Facility in Born, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Plans**: Rivian is in talks to potentially produce electric vehicles at the VDL Nedcar production plant in Born, Netherlands. The facility is currently used for assembling vehicles for the BMW Group, but with the expiration of contracts for the Mini Countryman and BMW X1 in 2023, the plant is seeking new customers or ownership.
- **Production Capacity**: The specific production capacity for Rivian at this facility has not been detailed in the articles, but it is implied that Rivian could utilize existing assembly lines previously used by BMW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Discussions**: While the article does not specify the exact investment amount for Rivian in the Netherlands, it mentions that Rivian is considering a significant investment, similar to the “well over a billion pounds” (approximately 1.17 billion euros) proposed for a facility in the UK.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Negotiations**: As of December 2021, the Dutch Ministry of Economic Affairs confirmed that negotiations were ongoing with Rivian regarding the VDL Nedcar plant. A Rivian delegation was expected to visit the Born plant in December 2021.
- **Potential Takeover**: Rivian may take over VDL Nedcar completely or utilize the existing assembly lines for its production, although no definitive agreements have been reached as of the publication date.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Implications**: The VDL Nedcar plant currently employs around 4,500 people. The future of these jobs is uncertain, depending on whether Rivian takes over the plant and how it plans to utilize the workforce.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership Status**: As of the article's publication, it is unclear whether Rivian would own the facility exclusively or enter into a joint venture with VDL Nedcar. The discussions involve the possibility of Rivian taking over the plant completely.

### Summary
The Rivian facility in Born, Netherlands, is in the negotiation phase for potentially taking over the VDL Nedcar plant to produce electric vehicles. The discussions include considerations for significant investments and the future of the existing workforce. The ownership structure remains uncertain, with possibilities of full ownership or a joint venture.
