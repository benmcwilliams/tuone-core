### Evolutionary Timeline of the RWE Facility in Groningen, Netherlands

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility is part of the NortH2 project, which aims to produce green hydrogen using electricity generated from offshore wind. The project plans to achieve a production capacity of 1 GW by 2027, 4 GW by 2030, and over 10 GW by 2040. The infrastructure will include offshore wind farms, an electrolyser plant, pipelines for hydrogen delivery, and underground storage in salt caverns.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The NortH2 project is a collaborative effort involving multiple stakeholders, including Shell, Gasunie, Equinor, and RWE. The final investment decision for the project is planned for 2024, following the Assess Phase, which includes environmental and safety assessments.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - The NortH2 project was launched in February 2020. In December 2020, RWE joined the consortium. As of February 2021, Royal HaskoningDHV was awarded a contract for environmental consultancy services, marking a significant step in the project's development.

4. **Labour:**

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is not exclusively owned by RWE; it is part of a consortium that includes Shell, Gasunie, Equinor, and RWE, indicating a joint venture structure for the NortH2 project.
