### Equinor Facility in Groningen, NLD - Evolutionary Timeline

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility is part of the NortH2 project, which aims to produce green hydrogen using electricity from offshore wind. The project plans to utilize approximately 1 GW of offshore wind capacity by 2027, expanding to 4 GW by 2030, and over 10 GW by 2040. The infrastructure will include offshore wind farms, an electrolyser plant, pipelines for hydrogen delivery, and underground storage in salt caverns.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The NortH2 project is supported by a consortium that includes Shell, Gasunie, Equinor, and RWE, along with support from Groningen Seaports and the Province of Groningen. A final investment decision for the project is planned for 2024, indicating ongoing development and potential future investments.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - The NortH2 project was launched in February 2020. In December 2020, Equinor and RWE joined the consortium. As of February 2021, Royal HaskoningDHV was awarded a contract for environmental consultancy services, marking a significant step in the project's development phase.
