### Evolutionary Timeline of the Gasunie Facility in Groningen, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2019**: Gasunie, in collaboration with Enexis and NAM, is exploring the feasibility of producing hydrogen from excess solar and wind energy in the northeastern Netherlands. The study considers the installation of an electrolyzer with a capacity of 20-50 MW at a clean energy hub in Groningen.
- **March 2020**: Gasunie and Shell announce plans to construct a large green hydrogen plant in Groningen, powered by a new offshore wind farm. The facility aims to produce 800,000 tonnes of hydrogen by 2040, which would significantly contribute to CO2 emissions reduction in the Netherlands.
- **February 2021**: The NortH2 project is launched, aiming to produce green hydrogen using electricity from offshore wind farms, with a target of 1 GW by 2027, scaling up to 10+ GW by 2040. This project involves a consortium including Gasunie, Shell, Equinor, and RWE.
- **July 2023**: Gasunie reports strong interest in hydrogen storage, with plans for the first salt cavern for large-scale hydrogen storage near Groningen to be operational by 2028. The site will eventually have a total capacity of up to 4 GW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2020**: Gasunie and Shell plan to conduct a feasibility study for the green hydrogen plant, indicating the need for additional investments and partnerships to realize the project.
- **February 2021**: The NortH2 project is supported by Groningen Seaports and the Province of Groningen, indicating regional investment and development support.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2019**: A feasibility study is initiated to assess hydrogen production from excess renewable energy in Groningen.
- **March 2020**: Announcement of the construction of a green hydrogen plant in Groningen, with a feasibility study to be completed by the end of 2020.
- **February 2021**: Launch of the NortH2 project, with plans for significant hydrogen production from offshore wind by 2027.
- **July 2023**: Gasunie announces the successful reservation of capacity for hydrogen storage at Zuidwending, with the first cavern expected to be operational by 2028.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2020**: The construction of the green hydrogen plant and the NortH2 project is expected to create jobs, although specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2020**: The green hydrogen plant is a partnership between Gasunie and Shell, indicating a joint venture structure for this facility.
- **February 2021**: The NortH2 project is developed by a consortium that includes Gasunie, Shell, Equinor, and RWE, further indicating a collaborative ownership model.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Gasunie facility in Groningen, focusing on its evolution in technology, investment, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
