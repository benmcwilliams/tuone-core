### Evolutionary Timeline of the Enexis Facility in Groningen, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2019**: Enexis, in collaboration with Gasunie and NAM, is exploring the production of hydrogen using excess solar power in the northeastern Netherlands. The facility is considering the installation of an electrolyzer with a capacity of 20-50 MW to convert locally generated sustainable energy into hydrogen. The hydrogen production aims to mitigate grid constraints and avoid curtailment of clean energy.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2019**: The feasibility study being conducted by Enexis, Gasunie, and NAM is assessing the potential for hydrogen production and the necessary infrastructure, including the use of existing gas pipelines for hydrogen transport. Specific investment amounts or subsidies related to this project have not been detailed in the article.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2019**: A feasibility study is underway to evaluate the conditions for hydrogen production at NAM sites in Emmen and Roswinkel, Drenthe. The results of this study are expected to be revealed by mid-January 2020.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2019**: The article does not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the hydrogen production initiative at the Enexis facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2019**: The facility is part of a joint venture involving Enexis, Gasunie, and NAM (the latter being a joint venture between Shell and ExxonMobil). Therefore, Enexis does not own the facility exclusively; it is part of a collaborative effort with other companies.

This timeline captures the key developments related to the Enexis facility in Groningen, focusing on the exploration of hydrogen production as a solution to grid constraints in the region.
