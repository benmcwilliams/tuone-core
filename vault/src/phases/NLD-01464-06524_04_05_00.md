#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2024:** Researchers at Hanze University of Applied Sciences Groningen proposed a hybrid hydrogen-battery system designed to store surplus renewable electricity. The system includes:
  - A 4.5 kW PEM hydrogen electrolysis system
  - A 0.85 m³ hydrogen storage tank
  - A 0.8 kW purification unit
  - A PEM hydrogen fuel cell
  - A lithium-ion battery
- The system is intended for off-grid households, utilizing rooftop PV power or small wind turbines. The battery serves as a secondary storage unit, primarily used when hydrogen generation is not available.

The research indicates potential for cost reduction by downsizing the electrolyzer.

### Technology and Operations
- "Researchers at the Hanze University of Applied Sciences Groningen in the Netherlands have investigated for the first time how to combine hydrogen production and battery storage with rooftop PV power or small wind turbines in off-grid households."
- "The proposed system is intended for storing surplus solar power, with the battery acting as a primary storage unit only when hydrogen generation is not immediately available."
- "It consists of a 4.5 kW PEM hydrogen electrolysis system, a 0.85 m3 hydrogen storage tank, a 0.8 kW purification unit, a PEM hydrogen fuel cell, and a lithium-ion battery."
- "The analysis demonstrated that, under no circumstances was the electrolyzer able to work at its maximum nominal power, which means it can be downsized, thus reducing the system's costs."

### Investment and Development
