#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date: November 2024**  
  The University of Groningen (UG) collaborates with Stellenbosch University to establish the FUTURA Hydrogen Campus Southern Africa (HyCASA). This facility is dedicated to research and innovation in sustainable energy technologies and green hydrogen production, storage, and utilization.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date: November 2024**  
  The establishment of the HyCASA facility is part of a broader initiative to enhance research and development in the green hydrogen sector, particularly in Southern Africa, which is positioned as a key supplier of green hydrogen to Europe. #### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date: November 2024**  
  The announcement of the collaboration to launch the HyCASA facility was made during the Southern African Sustainable Energy Conference (SASEC) held from November 13-15, 2024. The facility aims to create a dedicated campus with state-of-the-art research facilities.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date: November 2024**  
  The HyCASA initiative includes plans to implement comprehensive skills development programs aimed at equipping professionals and students for the expanding hydrogen sector, which is expected to stimulate economic growth and job creation in the region.

  The facility is a collaborative effort between Stellenbosch University and the University of Groningen, indicating a joint venture ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by either institution.
