## NLD-01750-01557

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Corre Energy Facility in Zuidwending, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2024**: Corre Energy and SemperPower announced a joint venture to develop a battery storage facility at the Zuidwending site. The facility will feature a 320 MW battery with a capacity of 640 MWh, designed to complement Corre's existing compressed air energy storage (CAES) project. The CAES facility will utilize salt caverns and is expected to have a maximum storage discharge duration of approximately 3.5 days, with a target commencement date around the end of 2026.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2024**: The joint venture will initially invest €7 million in the battery project. The total capital expenditure during the construction and installation phase is expected to be around €300 million. The project aims to monetize excess grid capacity and provide a grid balancing solution for the Netherlands, with an estimated reduction of over 70,000 tonnes of carbon dioxide emissions per year.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2023**: Dutch energy supplier Eneco signed a 15-year offtake agreement for the ZW1 CAES project, which is part of the overall development at the Zuidwending site.
- **July 2024**: The announcement of the joint venture and the battery project was made, marking a significant milestone in the development of the facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2024**: The project is expected to bring numerous benefits to the local region, including job creation and a boost to the local economy, although specific job numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2024**: The facility is a 50/50 joint venture between Corre Energy and SemperPower, indicating that Corre Energy does not own the facility exclusively but shares ownership with SemperPower.
