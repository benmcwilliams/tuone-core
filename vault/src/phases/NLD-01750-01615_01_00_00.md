## NLD-01750-01615

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Eneco Facility in Zuidwending, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2024**: A joint venture between Corre Energy and SemperPower announced the development of a 320 MW battery storage facility, which will complement the existing compressed air energy storage (CAES) project in Zuidwending. The battery will have a total capacity of 640 MWh and is designed to provide a two-hour discharge duration. The CAES facility will utilize salt caverns and is planned to have a maximum storage discharge duration of approximately 3.5 days, with a target commencement date around the end of 2026.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2024**: The joint venture between Corre Energy and SemperPower will initially invest €7 million ($7.6 million) into the battery project. This investment is aimed at delivering the 320 MW battery project, which is expected to generate additional revenue for the companies involved.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2023**: Eneco signed a 15-year offtake agreement for the ZW1 CAES project, which is part of the broader energy storage initiative in Zuidwending. This agreement allows Eneco to optimize the entire output of the storage site on behalf of Corre Energy.
- **End of 2026**: Target commencement date for the CAES facility.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the investments or expansions at the Zuidwending facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is developed as a **50/50 joint venture** between Corre Energy and SemperPower. Eneco is involved through a long-term offtake agreement but does not own the facility exclusively.
