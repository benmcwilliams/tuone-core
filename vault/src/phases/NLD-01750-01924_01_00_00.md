## NLD-01750-01924

### SemperPower Facility Evolution Timeline - Zuidwending, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2024**: SemperPower, in collaboration with Corre Energy, announced the development of a battery storage facility at the Zuidwending site. The facility will feature a 320 MW battery with a total capacity of 640 MWh, designed to complement Corre's compressed air energy storage (CAES) project. The battery will serve as a grid balancing solution and is expected to significantly reduce carbon dioxide emissions by over 70,000 tonnes annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2024**: The joint venture between Corre Energy and SemperPower will initially invest €7 million in the battery project. The total capital expenditure during the construction and installation phase is projected to be around €300 million. The financial close for the project is targeted for late 2025.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2024**: The announcement of the joint venture and the battery storage facility was made, marking the beginning of the project development. The battery facility is expected to begin commercial operations in 2026, alongside the CAES project, which is also set to commence around the end of 2026.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2024**: The project is anticipated to create numerous jobs and provide a boost to the local economy, as stated by SemperPower's CEO, Dennis Schiricke. The facility aims to enhance energy security and support regional stakeholders.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2024**: The facility is a 50/50 joint venture between Corre Energy and SemperPower. SemperPower is owned by Return Energy, an energy transition investment platform, which indicates that the facility is not exclusively owned by either company but rather shared equally between them.
