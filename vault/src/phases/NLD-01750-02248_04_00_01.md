### Evolutionary Timeline of the Gasunie Facility in Zuidwending, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2019**: Gasunie announced plans for a 1 MW power-to-gas pilot at its underground storage facility at Zuidwending, aimed at supporting renewable energy integration and hydrogen production.
- **September 2021**: Gasunie began storing hydrogen in its underground storage facility at Zuidwending, with plans to make the cavern fully operational by 2026. The facility is designed to store large quantities of hydrogen efficiently and safely.
- **October 2021**: Gasunie confirmed the successful conversion of a portion of the salt cavern for hydrogen storage, with ongoing testing to ensure the facility's readiness for large-scale hydrogen storage.
- **August 2022**: Gasunie announced plans to establish a total storage capacity of approximately 26 million kilograms of hydrogen, with the first cavern expected to be operational by 2026.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2021**: Gasunie indicated that pre-investments would be necessary to prepare the infrastructure for the planned four caverns by 2030, which would increase the site's total capacity to up to 4 GW.
- **August 2022**: Bilfinger and Gasunie aimed to commission the first hydrogen cavern in the Netherlands by 2026, with plans to inject around 76 million cubic meters of hydrogen gas into each of the four planned caverns.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2019**: Announcement of the 1 MW power-to-gas pilot project at Zuidwending.
- **September 2021**: Initiation of hydrogen storage at Zuidwending, with a target for full operational status by 2026.
- **October 2021**: Successful conversion of a salt cavern for hydrogen storage and ongoing testing.
- **August 2022**: Confirmation of plans to establish a total storage capacity of approximately 26 million kilograms of hydrogen by 2026.

#### 4. Labour

#### 5. Ownership Structure
