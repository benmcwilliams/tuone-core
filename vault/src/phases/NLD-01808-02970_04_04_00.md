### H2arvester Facility Timeline - Lelystad, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2022**: The H2arvester facility, part of a Dutch consortium, developed a mobile agrivoltaic system designed to improve soil quality and biodiversity in agricultural fields. The system can be combined with an electrolyzer for hydrogen generation. Two prototypes are being tested: one in Oude-Tonge and one at the research farm of Wageningen University in Lelystad. The mobile solar arrays are designed to cover up to 10% of an agricultural field without losing its agricultural purpose.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2022**: The project in Oude-Tonge received an investment of €166,000 from local farmer Jacob Jan Dogterom, with InnovationQuarter, the regional economic development agency for Zuid-Holland, subsidizing around half of the project's costs. The facility in Lelystad is part of the broader H2GO Program, which supports the integration of electricity and green hydrogen.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2022**: The H2arvester project was officially inaugurated by Netherlands' Agriculture Minister, Henk Staghouwer. The two pilot systems, including the one in Lelystad, are set to operate for one year to evaluate their impact on agricultural yield and land use.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
