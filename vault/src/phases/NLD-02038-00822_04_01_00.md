### Evolutionary Timeline of the Vattenfall Facility in Eemshaven, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2019**: Vattenfall is exploring the conversion of part of its gas-fired power plant in Eemshaven to run on hydrogen by 2025. This initiative aims to reduce emissions and investigate the potential of using excess renewable electricity to produce clean hydrogen via electrolysis.
- **June 2022**: The Magnum gas-fired power plant, which is hydrogen-ready, is sold to RWE. This plant has been operational since 2013 and can be partially or completely run on hydrogen.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2019**: The hydrogen project at Eemshaven is estimated to cost over €1 billion across the value chain, with a final investment decision expected in 2022, contingent on Dutch government subsidies.
- **June 2022**: Proceeds from the sale of the Magnum plant to RWE will be used by Vattenfall to invest in fossil-free energy projects, including offshore wind and district heating and cooling.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2019**: Announcement of Vattenfall's exploration into hydrogen conversion for its Eemshaven facility, with a target completion date of 2025.
- **June 2022**: RWE agrees to acquire the 1.4GW Magnum gas-fired power plant from Vattenfall, with the deal expected to be completed by September 30, 2022.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2022**: The sale of the Magnum plant to RWE is expected to provide job security and development opportunities for employees, as RWE plans to integrate the plant into its existing operations in Eemshaven.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2022**: Vattenfall sells the Magnum gas-fired power plant to RWE, indicating that Vattenfall no longer holds ownership of this facility. Prior to the sale, Vattenfall had full ownership of the Magnum plant since its operation began in 2013.
