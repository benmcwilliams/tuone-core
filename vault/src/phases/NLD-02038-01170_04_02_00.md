### Evolutionary Timeline of the Linde Facility in Eemshaven, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09-01-2024**: The H2M Eemshaven project is announced, aiming to build a facility that reforms natural gas from the Norwegian continental shelf (NCS) into hydrogen with CO2 capture and storage (CCS). The facility will have a production capacity of 1 GW (235,000 tons) of hydrogen, which is expected to meet 2.3% of Europe’s existing grey hydrogen demand and 0.24% of the European clean hydrogen market forecast for 2050.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09-01-2024**: The project is part of Equinor's strategy to develop hydrogen production in major industrial clusters in Europe by 2035. Linde will co-own and operate the facility, contributing its technology and expertise.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09-01-2024**: Announcement of the H2M Eemshaven project, with plans to start production in late 2028. The facility will be connected to onshore hydrogen pipelines planned in both the Netherlands and Germany.
- **04-12-2024**: Equinor opens a hydrogen offtake process for the H2M Eemshaven project, inviting parties interested in long-term supply agreements to register until January 31, 2025. This step is crucial for securing off-takers for the hydrogen produced.

#### 4. Labour
- No specific job creation or job loss details have been mentioned in the articles regarding the H2M Eemshaven project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **09-01-2024**: Linde will build, co-own, and operate the hydrogen production and carbon capture facility in collaboration with Equinor, indicating a joint venture ownership structure for the facility.
