### Evolutionary Timeline of the TotalEnergies Facility in Eemshaven, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Project Name**: OranjeWind
- **Production Capacity**: Expected annual generation of approximately 3 terawatt hours (TWh), sufficient to supply over one million Dutch households.
- **Components**: The facility will utilize 53 turbines, each with a capacity of 15 megawatts (MW), manufactured by Vestas. Monopiles and secondary steel components will be produced by Sif.
- **Construction Activities**: The Buss Terminal Eemshaven will handle offloading, storage, handling, and pre-assembly of major turbine components and inter-array cabling.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Details**: The articles do not specify exact investment amounts but indicate a significant commitment to the OranjeWind project, which is part of a broader strategy to enhance offshore wind capacity in the region.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Announcement Date**: December 11, 2024
- **Construction Start**: Early 2026
- **Turbine Installation Start**: Early 2027
- **Commissioning Completion**: Expected by the end of 2027
- **Marshalling Area**: A total of 35-40 hectares will be utilized for the OranjeWind and Nordseecluster B projects concurrently.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The articles do not provide specific numbers regarding job creation or loss, but the involvement of Buss Terminal Eemshaven and various contractors suggests potential job opportunities related to construction and operational activities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The facility is a joint project between RWE and TotalEnergies, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by either company.
