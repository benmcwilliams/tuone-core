## NLD-02038-01636

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Equinor Facility in Eemshaven, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2024**: Equinor's H2M Eemshaven project is announced, aiming to build a hydrogen production facility that will reform natural gas from the Norwegian continental shelf (NCS) into hydrogen with carbon capture and storage (CCS). The facility is expected to have a production capacity of 1 GW, producing approximately 235,000 tons of hydrogen annually. The project aims to start production in late 2028.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2024**: Equinor and Linde agree to collaborate on the H2M Eemshaven project, with Linde responsible for building, co-owning, and operating the hydrogen production and carbon capture facility. The project is part of Equinor's strategy to develop hydrogen production in major industrial clusters across Europe by 2035.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2020**: The NortH2 project is announced, which includes the Eemshaven facility as part of a larger initiative to produce green hydrogen using offshore wind. The project aims for a capacity of 10 GW, with 1 GW expected by 2027 and 4 GW by 2030.
- **01/2024**: Equinor opens the hydrogen offtake process for the H2M Eemshaven production project, inviting parties interested in long-term supply agreements to register.

#### 4. Labour
- **09/2024**: The H2M Eemshaven project is expected to contribute to job creation in the region, although specific job numbers have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **09/2024**: The H2M Eemshaven project is a collaboration between Equinor and Linde, indicating a joint venture structure for the facility. Equinor will secure access to carbon transport and storage capacity while Linde will handle the construction and operation of the facility.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Equinor facility in Eemshaven, focusing on its technology, investments, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.

### Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: The H2M Eemshaven project is expected to have a production capacity of 1 GW (235,000 tons) of hydrogen, which will meet 2.3% of Europe’s existing grey hydrogen demand and 0.24% of the European clean hydrogen market forecast for 2050 (Article 3, 09-01-2024).
- **Production Method**: The facility will reform natural gas from the Norwegian continental shelf (NCS) to hydrogen with CO2 capture and storage (CCS), capturing more than 95% of the CO2 (Article 3, 09-01-2024).
- **Updates on Hydrogen Production**: Equinor's plans to export blue hydrogen from Norway to Germany were scrapped in September 2024 due to insufficient demand, inadequate regulatory framework, and high prices (Article 4, 04-12-2024).

### Investment and Development
- **Investment Needs**: The project is expected to require significant investment, with a final investment decision dependent on securing off-takers and possibly government subsidies (Article 4, 04-12-2024).
- **Collaborative Development**: Equinor and Linde are collaborating on the project, with Linde responsible for building, co-owning, and operating the hydrogen production and carbon capture facility (Article 3, 09-01-2024).

### Project Status and Milestones
- **Production Start Date**: Hydrogen production is anticipated to start in late 2028 (Article 3, 09-01-2024).
- **Feasibility Study**: A feasibility study for the NortH2 project, which is also located in Eemshaven, is underway and expected to be completed by July 2021, with plans for 1GW by 2027 and 4GW by 2030 (Article 2, 22-12-2020).

### Labour
- **Job Creation**: Specific job creation figures related to the H2M Eemshaven project have not been detailed in the articles, but the collaboration with Linde suggests potential job opportunities in construction and operation (Article 3, 09-01-2024).

### Ownership Structure
- **Joint Ownership**: The facility is a joint venture between Equinor and Linde, with both companies playing significant roles in its development and operation (Article 3, 09-01-2024).
