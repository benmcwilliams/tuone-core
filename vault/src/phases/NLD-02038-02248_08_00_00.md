## NLD-02038-02248

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Gasunie Facility in Eemshaven, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **30-07-2019**: Vattenfall, in collaboration with Equinor, Mitsubishi Hitachi Power Systems, and Gasunie, is exploring the conversion of part of a gas-fired power plant in Eemshaven to run on hydrogen by 2025. This initiative aims to reduce emissions and investigate the potential of using excess renewable electricity for hydrogen production via electrolysis.

- **27-02-2020**: The NortH2 project is announced, aiming to produce green hydrogen using renewable electricity from a planned offshore wind farm. The initial production capacity is expected to be around 800,000 tonnes of green hydrogen per year by 2040, with the first hydrogen production anticipated in 2027.

- **28-02-2020**: The NortH2 project is further detailed, emphasizing the construction of significant wind farms in the North Sea, with a capacity of 3 to 4 gigawatts by 2030 and 10 gigawatts by 2040. The project will utilize Gasunie's existing natural gas infrastructure for hydrogen storage and transport.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **27-02-2020**: The NortH2 project is expected to require European and national subsidies for its initial phases, which will support the decarbonization of energy in the Netherlands.

- **22-12-2020**: The NortH2 project is highlighted as one of the largest green hydrogen projects globally, with a planned capacity of at least 10GW, aiming to produce 1 million tonnes of hydrogen per year by 2040. The feasibility study is ongoing, with completion expected by July 2021.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **30-07-2019**: Announcement of the hydrogen conversion project at the Eemshaven facility, with a target for operational changes by 2025.

- **27-02-2020**: Official launch of the NortH2 project, with a feasibility study expected to conclude by the end of the year.

- **28-02-2020**: Confirmation of the project’s ambition to produce green hydrogen in Eemshaven, with the first wind turbines planned for installation by 2027.

- **22-12-2020**: The NortH2 project is recognized as a gigawatt-scale initiative, with ongoing feasibility studies and plans for significant offshore wind farm development.

#### 4. Labour
- **27-02-2020**: The NortH2 project is anticipated to create thousands of jobs in the northern Netherlands as it progresses towards establishing a green hydrogen economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **27-02-2020**: The NortH2 project is a consortium involving Shell, Gasunie, and Groningen Seaports, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by any single entity. The project aims to expand its consortium with additional partners to facilitate its development.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones related to the Gasunie facility in Eemshaven, focusing on its transition towards green hydrogen production and the collaborative efforts involved in the NortH2 project.
