### Evolutionary Timeline of the Groningen Seaports Facility in Eemshaven, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2020**: The Groningen Seaports facility is part of the NortH2 project, which aims to construct a purpose-built offshore wind farm with a capacity of up to 10GW to produce green hydrogen. The initial phase targets a production capacity of 800,000 tonnes of green hydrogen per year by 2040, with the first hydrogen production expected in 2027.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2020**: The NortH2 project anticipates requiring European and national subsidies for its initial phases, although specific investment amounts are not detailed. The project is supported by the Province of Groningen and aims to position the region as a center for green hydrogen production in Europe.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2020**: The NortH2 project is officially launched with a feasibility study expected to conclude by the end of 2020. The project plans to install the first offshore wind turbines by 2027, with a phased approach to reach 1GW by 2027 and 4GW by 2030, ultimately achieving 10GW by 2040.
- **December 2020**: The NortH2 project is recognized as one of the world's largest green hydrogen projects, with a planned output of 1 million tonnes of hydrogen per year by 2040.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2020**: The NortH2 project is expected to create thousands of jobs in the northern Netherlands as it develops the green hydrogen industry, contributing to local economic growth and job creation.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2020**: The facility is part of a consortium involving Shell, Gasunie, and Groningen Seaports, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by any single entity. The consortium is actively seeking additional partners to expand the project.
