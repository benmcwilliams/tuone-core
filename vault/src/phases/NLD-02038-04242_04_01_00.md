### Evolutionary Timeline of the Shell Nederland Facility in Eemshaven, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2020**: Shell Nederland, in collaboration with Gasunie and Groningen Seaports, announces the NortH2 project aimed at producing green hydrogen using renewable electricity from a mega offshore wind farm. The initial production capacity is projected to be around 800,000 tonnes of green hydrogen per year by 2040. The facility will include a large electrolyser in Eemshaven to convert wind energy into green hydrogen.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2020**: The NortH2 project anticipates requiring European and national subsidies for its initial phases, which will support the decarbonisation of energy. The project is expected to involve significant investments, although specific amounts are not detailed in the announcement.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2020**: The announcement marks the official kick-off of the NortH2 project, which includes a feasibility study to assess the project's viability. The consortium aims to produce the first hydrogen by 2027, contingent on successful outcomes from the feasibility study, government permits, and final investment decisions.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2020**: The project is expected to create job opportunities throughout the hydrogen production chain, although specific job creation numbers are not provided in the announcement.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2020**: The facility is part of a consortium involving Shell Nederland, Gasunie, and Groningen Seaports, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by Shell Nederland. The consortium is actively seeking additional partners to expand the project.

This timeline captures the key developments related to the Shell Nederland facility in Eemshaven, focusing on the NortH2 project and its implications for technology, investment, project status, labor, and ownership structure.
