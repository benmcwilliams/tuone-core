### Evolutionary Timeline of Ørsted Facility in North Sea Port, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2021**: Ørsted announced the development of the 'SeaH2Land' project, which aims to create one of the world’s largest renewable hydrogen plants with a production capacity of 1GW by 2030. The facility will utilize GW-scale electrolysis to produce renewable hydrogen, which is expected to convert about 20% of the current hydrogen consumption in the region to renewable sources. The facility will be linked to a new 2GW offshore wind farm in the Dutch North Sea to supply the necessary green electricity.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2021**: The project is supported by major industrial companies in the region, including ArcelorMittal, Yara, Dow Benelux, and Zeeland Refinery, which will help develop the required regional infrastructure. The first phase of the SeaH2Land project will involve 500MW of electrolyser capacity, with the second phase scaling up to 1GW, contingent on the establishment of a national hydrogen backbone for flexibility and storage.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: Ørsted's SeaH2Land project was officially announced, outlining a clear vision and roadmap for large-scale renewable hydrogen production linked to offshore wind capacity. The project is in the dialogue phase with regulatory authorities to establish the necessary framework and policies for development.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2021**: While specific job creation figures were not mentioned, the project is expected to contribute to the decarbonisation of the industrial sector in the Dutch-Flemish North Sea Port, which could lead to job creation in the renewable energy and hydrogen sectors as the facility develops.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2021**: Ørsted is developing the facility, indicating that it will have ownership over the project. However, the project involves collaboration with other industrial players in the region, suggesting a partnership model for infrastructure development.
