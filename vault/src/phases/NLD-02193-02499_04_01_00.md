## NLD-02193-02499

### Yara Facility in North Sea Port, NLD - Evolutionary Timeline

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2021**: The SeaH2Land project is announced, aiming to develop a renewable hydrogen production facility with a capacity of 1GW by 2030. This facility will be linked to industrial demand in the Dutch-Flemish North Sea Port cluster, which currently has a hydrogen consumption of 580,000 tonnes per year, primarily from fossil sources. The project envisions converting about 20% of this consumption to renewable hydrogen.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2021**: The SeaH2Land project is supported by major industrial companies, including Yara, which will contribute to the development of the necessary regional infrastructure for sustainable production of steel, ammonia, ethylene, and fuels. The project is contingent upon establishing a regulatory framework to facilitate the development of renewable hydrogen linked to large-scale offshore wind.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: The first phase of the SeaH2Land project is set to unlock 500MW of electrolyser capacity, with plans to scale up to 1GW in the second phase. The project includes the development of a regional cross-border pipeline to connect large-scale hydrogen production and consumption in the North Sea Port area.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2021**: While specific job creation figures are not mentioned, the project is expected to contribute to the decarbonisation of the industrial sector in the region, which may lead to job creation in the renewable energy and hydrogen sectors as the facility develops.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2021**: Yara is part of a consortium with Ørsted and Zeeland Refinery for the development of mid-size renewable hydrogen production at their sites. This indicates a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership of the facility by Yara.

### Summary
The Yara facility in North Sea Port is evolving through the SeaH2Land project, which aims to establish a significant renewable hydrogen production capacity by 2030. The project is supported by major industrial players and is designed to enhance the region's infrastructure for sustainable energy production, with Yara participating in a consortium rather than owning the facility exclusively.
