### Evolutionary Timeline of the Dow Benelux Facility in North Sea Port, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Production**: The Dow Benelux facility is part of the North Sea Port cluster, which is one of the largest production and demand centers of fossil hydrogen in Europe, producing 580,000 tonnes per year.
- **Future Plans**: The facility is involved in the SeaH2Land project, which aims to develop a renewable hydrogen production facility with a capacity of 1GW by 2030. This facility will be linked to a regional cross-border pipeline and will utilize green electricity from large-scale offshore wind farms.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment in Infrastructure**: The SeaH2Land project, supported by major industrial companies including Dow Benelux, aims to develop the necessary regional infrastructure for sustainable production of hydrogen, steel, ammonia, ethylene, and fuels. The project envisions a phased approach, starting with 500MW of electrolyser capacity, scaling to 1GW.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Announcement of SeaH2Land**: The SeaH2Land project was announced in April 2021, with plans to develop a renewable hydrogen production facility by 2030.
- **Regulatory Framework**: The project is contingent upon establishing a regulatory framework to support the development of renewable hydrogen linked to offshore wind capacity.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: While specific job creation figures related to the SeaH2Land project have not been detailed, the overall development of renewable hydrogen infrastructure is expected to create jobs in the region as industrial demand grows and new projects are initiated.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Participation**: Dow Benelux is not the sole owner of the facility but is part of a collaborative effort with other major industrial players in the region, including ArcelorMittal, Yara, and Zeeland Refinery, to develop the SeaH2Land project and the associated infrastructure.

This timeline captures the key developments and future plans for the Dow Benelux facility in North Sea Port, highlighting its role in the transition towards renewable hydrogen production and the collaborative efforts with other industrial entities in the region.
