### Evolutionary Timeline of the RWE Facility in Dutch North Sea, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2022**: RWE and Neptune Energy announced the H2opZee project, aiming to build 300 to 500 MW of electrolyzer capacity to produce green hydrogen using offshore wind. The hydrogen will be transported to land through an existing pipeline with a capacity of 10-12 GW, suitable for future expansions to gigawatt scale.
- **September 2022**: Contracts were awarded to Siemens Gamesa and Dutch engineering firms H2SEA and Enersea for concept engineering work as part of the feasibility study for the H2opZee project. The feasibility study began in June 2022 and is set to run until Summer 2023.
- **July 2024**: RWE and SolarDuck successfully installed the Merganser offshore floating solar pilot project, with a capacity of 0.5 MWp, located approximately 12 kilometers off the coast of Scheveningen. This project aims to test and demonstrate the technology for large-scale deployment of offshore floating solar.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2022**: The H2opZee project is part of RWE's broader investment strategy, which includes a $56 billion plan to double its renewable energy capacity to 50 GW by 2030, with an average of €5 billion allocated annually to hydrogen and other renewable projects.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2022**: Announcement of the H2opZee project and the initiation of a feasibility study in the second quarter of 2022.
- **September 2022**: Progress on the feasibility study with contracts awarded for engineering support.
- **July 2024**: Successful installation of the Merganser offshore floating solar pilot project.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2022**: The H2opZee project is expected to contribute to job creation in the renewable energy sector, although specific job numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2022**: RWE and Neptune Energy are jointly developing the H2opZee project, indicating a joint venture ownership structure for this facility. RWE also collaborates with SolarDuck for the Merganser project, suggesting a partnership model for this initiative.
