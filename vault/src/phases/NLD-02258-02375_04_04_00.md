### Evolutionary Timeline of the Neptune Energy Facility in Dutch North Sea, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2022**: Neptune Energy, in collaboration with RWE, announced the H2opZee project, aiming to build 300 to 500 MW of electrolyzer capacity to produce green hydrogen using offshore wind. The hydrogen will be transported to land through an existing pipeline with a capacity of 10-12 GW, suitable for future expansions to gigawatt scale.
- **September 2022**: Contracts were awarded to Siemens Gamesa, H2SEA, and Enersea for concept engineering work as part of the feasibility study for the H2opZee project. The feasibility study began in June 2022 and is set to run until Summer 2023, focusing on evaluating technical concepts and potential development locations for optimal hydrogen generation and transport.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2022**: The H2opZee project is part of a broader initiative backed by the Dutch government’s Topsector Energie programme, which aims to support the hydrogen economy in the Netherlands.
- **November 2021**: RWE announced a $56 billion investment plan to double its renewable energy capacity to 50 GW by 2030, which includes significant investments in hydrogen production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2022**: The development agreement for the H2opZee project was signed, with plans to start a feasibility study in the second quarter of 2022.
- **September 2022**: The feasibility study commenced, focusing on various technical concepts and potential development locations for the H2opZee project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
