### Summary
The BW Offshore facility in Offshore, NLD, is set to produce green hydrogen and ammonia, with a planned operational date of 2027. It has received a EUR 3 million grant for development and has achieved technical feasibility approval. The ownership structure details are not fully disclosed, but BW Offshore plays a crucial role in the project alongside SwitcH2 and other partners.

**1. Technology and Operations:**
- "The facility will produce hydrogen that can be transported to shore through existing oil and gas pipelines or newly installed Thermoplastic Composite Pipe (TCP)."
- "The produced ammonia can be transported to end-users by shuttle tankers."
- "The large-scale production of green hydrogen faces challenges in intermittent energy supply, transmission of electricity, and high-quality water supply for the electrolysis process."
- "This can be addressed with a floating facility which would use seawater as feed for the electrolysis process."

**2. Investment and Development:**
- "The OFFSET project was selected to receive the EUR 3 million grant by the Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO) under the MOOI scheme (Missiegedregen Onderzoek en Ontwikkeling – Mission driven Research and Development)."

**3. Project Status and Milestones:**
- "Last month, SwitcH2 and BW Offshore announced that the facility secured Approval in Principle (AiP) from DNV, which affirmed the technical feasibility of the design and enabled the project to start the basic design phase."
- "Planned to be linked with an adjacent wind farm by 2027."

**5. Ownership structure:**
