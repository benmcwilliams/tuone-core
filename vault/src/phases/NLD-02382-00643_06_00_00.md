### Evolutionary Timeline of the RWE Facility in North Sea, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2022**: RWE, in collaboration with Neptune Energy, announced the H2opZee project, which aims to produce green hydrogen using offshore wind energy. The project plans to establish 300-500 MW electrolyser capacity in the North Sea. The existing pipeline will transport the hydrogen to land, with a capacity of 10-12 GW, suitable for further roll-out at gigawatt scale.
- **November 2022**: RWE won a tender to build the 700 MW Hollandse Kust West (HKW) VII offshore wind farm, which will generate renewable power sufficient for nearly one million Dutch households. The facility will also integrate an electrolyser for green hydrogen production and other flexible demand solutions, including e-boilers and battery storage.
- **July 2024**: RWE and SolarDuck completed a 0.5 MWp offshore floating solar pilot project in the North Sea, showcasing the capability to withstand extreme offshore conditions.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2022**: The H2opZee project is supported by the Dutch government as part of TKI Wind op Zee, indicating governmental investment and backing for the feasibility study and subsequent phases of the project.
- **November 2022**: The permit for the HKW VII project was awarded by the Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO), which signifies governmental investment in RWE's offshore wind initiatives.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2022**: Announcement of the H2opZee project and the initiation of a feasibility study set to begin in Q2 2022, with the goal of having the project operational before 2030.
- **November 2022**: RWE's successful bid for the HKW VII project marks its entry into the Dutch offshore wind market, with expectations for the facility to come online within five years.
- **July 2024**: Completion of the offshore floating solar pilot project, further diversifying RWE's renewable energy portfolio in the North Sea.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2022**: The HKW VII project is expected to create jobs related to the construction and operation of the offshore wind farm, contributing to local employment in the renewable energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2022**: The H2opZee project is a joint development agreement between RWE and Neptune Energy, indicating a joint venture ownership structure.
- **November 2022**: RWE is the sole owner of the HKW VII project, marking its exclusive ownership of this facility.
