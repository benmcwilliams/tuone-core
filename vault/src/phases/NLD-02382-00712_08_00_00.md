### Evolutionary Timeline of the Shell Facility in North Sea, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date: 25-07-2022**: The facility is involved in the development of floating solar technology as part of a collaboration with RWE and SolarDuck. The pilot project, named Merganser, will have a production capacity of 0.5 megawatt peak (MWp) and aims to integrate offshore floating solar into an offshore wind farm, enhancing energy generation efficiency.
- **Date: 26-04-2023**: The facility is set to host the world’s first grid-connected offshore solar-wind hybrid project. This project will install 0.5 MW of floating solar modules between the turbines of the Hollandse Kust Noord wind park, which has a capacity of 759 MW. The solar farm is expected to be operational by 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date: 26-04-2023**: The contract for the offshore solar-wind hybrid project has been awarded to Oceans of Energy, marking a significant investment in the facility's development. This project is part of a broader initiative to co-locate wind, solar, storage, and green hydrogen generation.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date: 25-07-2022**: Announcement of the Merganser pilot project, which is the first offshore pilot for SolarDuck.
- **Date: 26-04-2023**: Award of the contract to Oceans of Energy for the construction of the first commercial offshore solar project in the North Sea, with completion of the wind project expected by the end of 2023 and the solar farm by 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date: 26-04-2023**: The project is expected to create jobs through the construction and operation of the offshore solar-wind hybrid project, although specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date: 26-04-2023**: The facility is part of the Hollandse Kust Noord project, which is a joint venture between Shell and Dutch energy provider Eneco. This indicates that Shell does not own the facility exclusively but shares ownership with Eneco.
