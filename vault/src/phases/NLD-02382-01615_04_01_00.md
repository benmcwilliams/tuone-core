### Evolutionary Timeline of the Eneco Facility in North Sea, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2022**: The NortH2 consortium, which includes Eneco, RWE, Equinor, and Shell, is investigating the feasibility of producing hydrogen offshore at a multi-gigawatt scale. The aim is to establish a multi-gigawatt wind farm in the North Sea connected to an onshore hydrogen facility, targeting green hydrogen production using electricity from about 4 GW of offshore wind by 2030, and 10 GW by 2040.
- **April 2023**: Oceans of Energy is awarded a contract to build what is claimed to be the “world’s first grid-connected offshore solar-wind hybrid project” at a site in the North Sea, which will include 0.5 MW of floating solar modules installed between the turbines of the Hollandse Kust Noord wind park, a project developed by CrossWind (a joint venture between Eneco and Shell).

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2022**: The NortH2 consortium is exploring investments required for the hydrogen production project, with feasibility studies to be released in January 2023. This includes assessments of technologies and environmental impacts.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2022**: The feasibility study for the NortH2 hydrogen project is set to be released in January 2023, outlining options for investment and technology integration.
- **April 2023**: The contract for the offshore solar-wind hybrid project is awarded to Oceans of Energy, with the wind project expected to be completed by the end of 2023 and the solar farm due to be operational by 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2023**: The development of the hybrid project and the associated wind farm is expected to create jobs, although specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2023**: The Hollandse Kust Noord wind park, where the solar project will be located, is a joint venture between Eneco and Shell, indicating that Eneco does not own the facility exclusively but shares ownership with Shell. 

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Eneco facility in the North Sea, focusing on technology, investment, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
