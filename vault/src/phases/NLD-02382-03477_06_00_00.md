### Evolutionary Timeline of the SolarDuck Facility in North Sea, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2021**: SolarDuck developed a triangular floating PV platform designed to withstand harsh offshore conditions, with a production capacity targeted at 10 MW for standard projects and potential floating complexes of up to 500 MW.
- **July 2022**: SolarDuck was selected as the exclusive provider of offshore floating solar technology for the Hollandse Kust West (HKW) site VII, planning to integrate a 5 MW floating solar plant with energy storage into the offshore wind farm.
- **November 2022**: SolarDuck confirmed the construction of a 5 MW floating PV project at the HKW wind farm, expected to start operations in 2026.
- **July 2024**: A 0.5 MW pilot project was completed 12 km off the Dutch coast, featuring six interconnected platforms designed to withstand extreme offshore conditions.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: SolarDuck secured €350,000 from the Dutch province of Gelderland for its first pilot project.
- **November 2022**: A consortium including SolarDuck received €7.8 million in funding from the Dutch government to develop a pilot project in the North Sea.
- **July 2024**: The completion of the 0.5 MW pilot project was part of the ongoing investment in offshore solar technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2021**: SolarDuck launched its first full-scale demonstrator project on the Waal River, with a total installed capacity of 65 kW.
- **July 2022**: SolarDuck was selected by RWE for the HKW site VII, marking a significant milestone in offshore solar integration.
- **November 2022**: SolarDuck was confirmed to build a 5 MW floating PV project at the HKW wind farm, expected to be operational by 2026.
- **July 2024**: The completion of the 0.5 MW pilot project was reported, demonstrating the technology's viability.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2021**: The development of the floating PV technology and pilot projects is expected to create jobs, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.
- **July 2024**: The pilot project completion indicates ongoing employment opportunities related to the construction and maintenance of offshore solar facilities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2022**: SolarDuck is collaborating with RWE as an exclusive provider for offshore floating solar technology, indicating a partnership rather than full ownership of the projects.
- **November 2022**: The partnership with RWE for the HKW wind farm project further emphasizes a collaborative ownership structure for the floating solar installations.
