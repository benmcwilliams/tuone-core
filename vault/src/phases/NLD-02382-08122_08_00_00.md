### Evolutionary Timeline of the TenneT Facility in North Sea, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2019**: TenneT, as part of the North Sea Wind Power Hub (NSWPH) consortium, completed studies on offshore wind power hubs. The consortium proposed a hub-and-spoke concept for offshore wind farms, suggesting a gradual rollout of 10GW to 15GW hubs to support large-scale offshore wind build-out. The facility could potentially include electrical transmission and hydrogen production, with operations expected to commence in the 2030s.

- **09/2022**: TenneT launched a large-scale high voltage direct current (HVDC) cable tender for offshore grid connections in the North Sea, targeting a total capacity of 2 GW. This initiative is part of TenneT's broader plan to connect 40 GW of new offshore wind capacity in Germany and the Netherlands by 2030.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2022**: TenneT's initiatives, including the 2GW cable tender, are part of a EUR 30 billion investment drive aimed at expanding offshore wind capacity. This investment is crucial for meeting the collective target of installing at least 65 GW of offshore wind energy by 2030 among Germany, the Netherlands, Denmark, and Belgium.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2019**: Completion of the assessment phase for the offshore hub studies, indicating feasibility for future projects.
- **09/2022**: Launch of the 2GW cable tender, marking a significant step in the offshore grid expansion efforts.

#### 4. Labour
- **09/2022**: The launch of the 2GW cable tender is expected to boost investment security and employment opportunities in the offshore market, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- TenneT operates as a cross-border transmission system operator and is involved in joint initiatives with partners such as Energinet, Gasunie, and Port of Rotterdam for the NSWPH project. However, the articles do not specify whether TenneT has exclusive ownership of the facility or if it operates under a joint venture structure.
