## NLD-02382-09634

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Oceans of Energy Facility in North Sea, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2018**: Launch of the pilot project for an offshore PV system.
- **November 2019**: The initial 8.5 kW modular PV system became operational.
- **January 2020**: The capacity of the floating plant was doubled to 17 kW.
- **2020**: Plans announced to expand the facility to 50 kW for a one-year testing phase, followed by expansions to 1 MW and eventually 100 MW.
- **April 2023**: Awarded a contract to build a 0.5 MW floating solar module project between the turbines of the Hollandse Kust Noord wind park, marking the first commercial project for Oceans of Energy.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2019**: The project received financial backing from the Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO).
- **2020**: The facility is part of a broader initiative expected to reach 100 MW of floating solar capacity in the North Sea by 2030, as per a DNV GL report.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12-12-2019**: The pilot project was reported to be operational.
- **17-02-2020**: The facility successfully withstood extreme winter weather conditions, confirming its operational stability.
- **10-12-2020**: The project was highlighted in a report indicating its potential for future expansions and contributions to renewable energy.
- **03-09-2021**: Plans announced for a 3 MW offshore floating PV array.
- **26-04-2023**: Contract awarded for the first commercial offshore solar project, with installation planned for 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **2020**: The expansion and development of the facility are expected to create jobs, although specific numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: Oceans of Energy operates the facility as a spin-off from the Delft University of Technology, indicating full ownership of the pilot project. The commercial project in collaboration with CrossWind (a joint venture between Shell and Eneco) suggests a partnership model for future developments.
