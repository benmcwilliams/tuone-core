### Evolutionary Timeline of the Strohm Facility in North Sea, NLD

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 25-04-2023
   - **Details:** The Strohm facility is part of a floating green hydrogen and ammonia project, titled OFFSET, which will utilize existing wind farms for power. The initial design capacity is set at 200MW, with plans to scale up to 300MW, producing approximately 26 million kilograms of hydrogen per year. The facility will employ non-corrosive thermoplastic composite pipe (TCP) technology for hydrogen storage and offloading.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 25-04-2023
   - **Details:** The Dutch government has announced a funding of $3.3 million (€3 million) for the OFFSET project, which includes Strohm among other partners. The total estimated cost for the project is over $1.1 billion (€1 billion), although additional funding sources for the remaining costs have not been disclosed.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 25-04-2023
   - **Details:** The project is expected to commence production in 2028, with construction planned to start by 2027. The project aims to enhance the accessibility of green fuel and reduce costs associated with its production.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 25-04-2023
   - **Details:** The article does not specify job creation or job loss directly associated with the Strohm facility; however, the scale of the project suggests potential job creation during construction and operation phases.

5. **Ownership Structure:**

   - **Details:** The Strohm facility is part of a collaborative project involving multiple stakeholders, including BW Offshore, SwitcH2, MARIN, Delft University, and Strohm.
