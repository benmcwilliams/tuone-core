### SwitcH2 Facility Evolution Timeline - North Sea, NLD

1. **Technology and Operations**:
   - **Date**: April 2023
   - **Details**: The SwitcH2 facility is designed to produce green hydrogen and ammonia, with an initial production capacity of 200 MW, expected to yield approximately 26 million kilograms of hydrogen per year. Plans are in place to scale up to a capacity of 300 MW by stacking the electrolysis capacity higher. The facility will utilize existing wind farms for power and will incorporate thermoplastic composite pipe (TCP) infrastructure for transporting hydrogen onshore.

2. **Investment and Development**:
   - **Date**: April 2023
   - **Details**: The Dutch government has announced a funding grant of $3.3 million (€3 million) for the OFFSET project, which includes the SwitcH2 facility. The total estimated cost for the design, construction, supplies, operation, and transportation of green fuel is projected to exceed $1.1 billion (€1 billion). Additional funding sources for the remaining costs have not been disclosed.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**:
   - **Date**: April 2023
   - **Details**: The project is set to commence production in 2028, with construction expected to start by 2027. The project has been selected under the Mission Driven Research and Development scheme by the Dutch Ministry of Economic Affairs and Climate Policy.
