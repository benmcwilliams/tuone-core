### Evolutionary Timeline of the Uniper Facility in Maasvlakte, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2021**: Uniper and the Port of Rotterdam Authority announced plans to develop a 100 MW electrolyzer at the Maasvlakte location, with intentions to eventually expand capacity to 500 MW. The facility will produce green hydrogen using power generated from North Sea wind farms. The electrolyzer will be connected to the HyTransport pipeline, linking it to the national hydrogen infrastructure.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2021**: An investment decision for the project is anticipated in 2022. The project aims to position the Maasvlakte location as a significant link in the European hydrogen value chain, with potential for collaboration with other partners in the hydrogen sector.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2021**: The partnership between Uniper and the Port of Rotterdam Authority was formalized, marking the beginning of the project to develop the hydrogen production facility. The project is part of a broader initiative to establish a large-scale hydrogen network across the port complex.

#### 4. Labour
- **09/2021**: While specific job creation figures were not mentioned, the project is expected to contribute to significant employment opportunities in the hydrogen sector, as indicated by the ambition to create a major link in the hydrogen value chain.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **09/2021**: The facility is a collaboration between Uniper and the Port of Rotterdam Authority, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by Uniper.

### Summary
The Uniper facility in Maasvlakte is set to become a key player in the production of green hydrogen, with plans for a 100 MW electrolyzer that could expand to 500 MW. The project is backed by a partnership with the Port of Rotterdam Authority, with an investment decision expected in 2022. The facility aims to leverage renewable energy from North Sea wind farms and is part of a larger initiative to create a hydrogen network in the region.
