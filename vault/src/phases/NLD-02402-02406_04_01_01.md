### Evolutionary Timeline of the Port of Rotterdam Facility in Maasvlakte, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2021**: Announcement of a 100 MW electrolyzer to produce green hydrogen, with plans to expand capacity to 500 MW. The facility will utilize electricity generated from offshore wind farms.
- **04/2023**: Development of an 11-hectare site for a green hydrogen plant capable of generating up to 1 GW of electrolysis. The site is strategically located near high-voltage substations and water pipelines, with plans to connect to regional heat networks. The Port Authority aims for a total of 2.5 GW of electrolysis by 2030.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2021**: The project has successfully completed the pre-qualification process for the European Union's Important Projects of Common European Interest (IPCEI) program, indicating potential for future investment.
- **04/2023**: The Port Authority is consulting with local authorities to amend zoning plans for the hydrogen production site, indicating ongoing development efforts and interest from multiple parties for investment.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2021**: Feasibility study for the 100 MW electrolyzer is underway, with completion expected in the summer of 2021. The project is set to begin construction in 2025.
- **04/2023**: The Port Authority announces plans for a 1 GW hydrogen plant, with construction expected to follow the tender for the IJmuiden Ver Wind Farm, anticipated to be completed by 2028.
