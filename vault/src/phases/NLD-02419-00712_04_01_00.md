### Evolutionary Timeline of the Shell Facility in Port of Rotterdam, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2019**: The Gigawatt Elektrolysefabriek project was launched, aiming to produce green hydrogen at a gigawatt scale from wind and solar parks in the Netherlands. The project plans to develop a gigawatt-scale electrolysis plant at the Port of Rotterdam, with a goal to produce hydrogen on an industrial scale.
- **May 2020**: Shell announced plans to create a green hydrogen hub in the Port of Rotterdam, with an expected production capacity of 50,000-60,000 kg of hydrogen per day and a capacity of around 200 MW. The plant is to be constructed at a dedicated industrial site at Maasvlakte.
- **January 2022**: Shell appointed Thyssenkrupp Uhde Chlorine Engineers to supply electrolysers for the Hydrogen Holland I project, confirming the installation of a 200 MW electrolysis plant. The project is expected to start production in 2024.
- **July 2022**: Shell confirmed that the Holland Hydrogen I facility would be Europe’s largest renewable hydrogen plant, generating up to 60,000 kg of renewable hydrogen daily, utilizing renewable power from the Hollandse Kust (Noord) offshore wind farm.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2019**: The Gigawatt Elektrolysefabriek project is estimated to require an investment of around €1 billion, with a goal to reduce costs to €350 million for a gigawatt-scale green hydrogen plant.
- **May 2020**: The construction of the green hydrogen plant and associated pipeline is part of a series of projects supported by the Port Authority and various partners, aligning with the Dutch government's hydrogen outlook.
- **July 2022**: Shell made a final investment decision to proceed with the Holland Hydrogen I facility, marking a significant commitment to renewable hydrogen production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2019**: Launch of the Gigawatt Elektrolysefabriek project.
- **May 2020**: Announcement of the green hydrogen hub with an operational target of 2023.
- **January 2022**: Confirmation of the supplier for electrolysers and expected start of construction in Spring 2022.
- **July 2022**: Final investment decision made, with operations expected to start in 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2020**: The development of the hydrogen hub is expected to create jobs related to the construction and operation of the facility, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
