### Evolutionary Timeline of the GE Facility in Port of Rotterdam, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2019**: GE Renewable Energy unveiled the Haliade-X 12 MW offshore wind turbine, the world's largest, with a height of 260 meters and a rotor diameter of 220 meters. It is capable of generating enough electricity for 16,000 households. The first nacelle of the Haliade-X was revealed at the Saint-Nazaire production facility, with components being shipped to the Port of Rotterdam for installation and testing.
- **October 2019**: The rotor star of the Haliade-X prototype was nearing completion, with the second of three 107-meter blades installed. The turbine was set to begin trials in the Port of Rotterdam, featuring a rotor that would capture wind to achieve a gross capacity factor of 63%.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2019**: GE Renewable Energy signed an agreement with Future Wind (a joint venture between Pondera Development and SIF Holding Netherlands) to install and test the first prototype Haliade-X at the Port of Rotterdam for a period of 5 years.
