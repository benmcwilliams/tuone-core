### Evolutionary Timeline of the Nouryon Facility in Port of Rotterdam, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2019**: The Gigawatt Elektrolysefabriek project was launched, aiming to develop a gigawatt-scale electrolysis plant at the Port of Rotterdam. This facility is intended to produce green hydrogen from wind and solar energy, marking a significant shift from traditional hydrogen production methods that rely on natural gas. The project aims to scale up current electrolysis capacities from a few megawatts to gigawatt levels to meet the sustainable hydrogen needs of Dutch industry.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2019**: The estimated investment for the gigawatt-scale electrolysis plant is around €1 billion, with a goal to reduce costs to approximately €350 million, making it a competitive alternative to conventional fossil hydrogen technology. The project is supported by TKI Energy & Industry and involves multiple partners, including Nouryon.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2019**: The Gigawatt Elektrolysefabriek project was officially announced, with plans to construct new electrolysis plants between 2025 and 2030. The project aims to determine the requirements for these plants collaboratively among the consortium partners.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2019**: While specific job creation figures were not mentioned in the article, the development of a gigawatt-scale facility is expected to create jobs in construction, operation, and maintenance, contributing to the local economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2019**: The facility is part of a consortium that includes Nouryon, Shell, Yara, OCI Nitrogen, Gasunie, DOW Chemical, Ørsted, and several research institutions. This indicates that Nouryon does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a joint venture with multiple partners.
