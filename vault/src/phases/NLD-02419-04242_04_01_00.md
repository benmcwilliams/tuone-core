### Evolutionary Timeline of the Shell Nederland Facility in Port of Rotterdam, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2020**: Shell Nederland is collaborating with Eneco to develop the Hollandse Kust (noord) offshore wind farm, which will have a capacity of up to 760 MW. The facility aims to produce green hydrogen using electricity from this wind farm, with the hydrogen plant in the Port of Rotterdam expected to have a capacity of around 200 MW. The hydrogen production goal is approximately 50,000 – 60,000 kilograms per day.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2020**: Shell and Eneco have issued guarantees for investments in the construction and operation of the Hollandse Kust (noord) wind farm through their joint venture, CrossWind. The final investment decision for the hydrogen plant has not yet been made, but it is part of a broader strategy to create a green hydrogen hub in the Port of Rotterdam.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2020**: Shell and Eneco submitted a bid for the Hollandse Kust (noord) offshore wind farm in a zero-subsidy round. The wind farm is expected to be operational by 2023. The Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO) will announce the winner of the tender by the end of July 2020.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2020**: While specific job creation numbers are not mentioned, the development of the hydrogen plant and the offshore wind farm is expected to contribute to job creation in the region, particularly in the renewable energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2020**: The facility is part of a joint venture between Shell and Eneco, known as CrossWind, indicating that Shell does not exclusively own the facility but shares ownership with Eneco.
