### Evolutionary Timeline of the Dutch Institute for Sustainable Process Technology Facility in Port of Rotterdam, NLD

1. **Technology and Operations**
   - **March 2019**: The Dutch Institute for Sustainable Process Technology (ISPT) launched the Gigawatt Elektrolysefabriek project aimed at producing green hydrogen at a gigawatt scale. The facility will utilize electrolysis technology to convert electricity from wind and solar parks into hydrogen. The project plans to develop a gigawatt-scale electrolysis plant at the Port of Rotterdam, significantly increasing the current production capacity, which is limited to a few megawatts.

2. **Investment and Development**
   - **March 2019**: The estimated investment for the gigawatt-scale electrolysis plant is around €1 billion, with a goal to reduce costs to approximately €350 million, making it a competitive alternative to conventional fossil hydrogen technology. The project is supported by TKI Energy & Industry and involves a consortium of partners.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**
   - **March 2019**: The Gigawatt Elektrolysefabriek project was officially launched, with plans to determine the requirements for constructing new electrolysis plants between 2025 and 2030.

4. **Labour**
   - **March 2019**: While specific job creation or loss figures were not mentioned in the article, the scale of the project and its industrial nature suggest potential job creation in the construction and operational phases of the facility.

5. **Ownership Structure**
   - **March 2019**: The facility is part of a consortium involving multiple partners, including Nouryon, Shell, Yara, OCI Nitrogen, Gasunie, DOW Chemical, Ørsted, Frames, ECN, the University of Utrecht, and Imperial College London. This indicates that the facility is not owned exclusively by ISPT but is a collaborative effort among various stakeholders.
