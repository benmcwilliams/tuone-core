### Evolutionary Timeline of the ECN Facility in Port of Rotterdam, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2019**: The Gigawatt Elektrolysefabriek project was launched, aiming to produce green hydrogen at a gigawatt scale from wind and solar parks in the Netherlands. The facility plans to develop a gigawatt-scale electrolysis plant at the Port of Rotterdam, which is a significant increase from current electrolysis projects that only have capacities of a few megawatts. The project also aims to produce heat and oxygen as byproducts of water electrolysis.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2019**: The estimated investment for the gigawatt-scale electrolysis plant is around €1 billion, with a goal to reduce costs to €350 million, making it a competitive alternative to conventional fossil hydrogen technology. The project is supported by TKI Energy & Industry.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2019**: The Gigawatt Elektrolysefabriek project was officially launched, with plans to construct new electrolysis plants between 2025 and 2030.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2019**: While specific job creation or loss figures were not mentioned in the article, the scale of the project suggests potential for significant job creation in the construction and operational phases of the facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2019**: The facility is part of a consortium that includes multiple partners such as Nouryon, Shell, Yara, OCI Nitrogen, Gasunie, DOW Chemical, Ørsted, Frames, ECN, the University of Utrecht, and Imperial College London.
