### Evolutionary Timeline of the OCI Nitrogen Facility in Port of Rotterdam, NLD

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **March 2019:** The Gigawatt Elektrolysefabriek project was launched, aiming to produce green hydrogen at a gigawatt scale from wind and solar parks in the Netherlands. The project plans to develop a gigawatt-scale electrolysis plant at the Port of Rotterdam, which is a significant step towards industrial-scale green hydrogen production. The current hydrogen production in the Netherlands is around 800,000 tonnes per year, primarily from natural gas, with the goal of transitioning to sustainable hydrogen production.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **March 2019:** The estimated investment for the gigawatt-scale electrolysis plant is around €1 billion, with a target to reduce costs to €350 million, making it a competitive alternative to conventional fossil hydrogen technology. The project is supported by TKI Energy & Industry and involves multiple partners, including OCI Nitrogen.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **March 2019:** The Gigawatt Elektrolysefabriek project was officially announced, with plans to construct new electrolysis plants between 2025 and 2030. The project aims to ramp up hydrogen production technology by a factor of 1,000 to meet future industrial demands.

4. **Labour:**
   - **March 2019:** While specific job creation or loss figures were not mentioned in the article, the scale of the project and its industrial nature suggest potential job creation in the construction and operational phases of the gigawatt-scale electrolysis plant.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **March 2019:** The facility is part of a consortium that includes OCI Nitrogen along with other partners such as Nouryon, Shell, Yara, Gasunie, DOW Chemical, Ørsted, and research institutions. The ownership structure appears to be a joint venture among these partners, rather than exclusive ownership by OCI Nitrogen.
