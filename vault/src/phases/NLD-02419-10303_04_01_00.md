### Evolutionary Timeline of the University of Utrecht Facility in Port of Rotterdam, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2019**: The Gigawatt Elektrolysefabriek project was launched, aiming to produce green hydrogen at a gigawatt scale from wind and solar parks in the Netherlands. The facility will utilize electrolysis technology to convert electricity from renewable sources into hydrogen, with a target production capacity significantly higher than current projects, which only have capacities of a few megawatts. The goal is to develop an optimal design for a gigawatt-scale electrolysis plant by 2025-2030.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2019**: The estimated investment for the gigawatt-scale electrolysis plant is around €1 billion, with plans to reduce costs to approximately €350 million, making it a competitive alternative to conventional fossil hydrogen technology. The project is supported by TKI Energy & Industry.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2019**: The Gigawatt Elektrolysefabriek project was officially launched, with a consortium of companies and research institutes, including the University of Utrecht, working together to determine the requirements for constructing new electrolysis plants.
