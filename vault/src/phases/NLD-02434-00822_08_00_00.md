### Evolutionary Timeline of Vattenfall Facility in Haringvliet, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2019**: Vattenfall announced plans for its first triple hybrid facility in Haringvliet, consisting of a 38MW solar installation, a 22MW wind component, and a 12MWh storage battery system. The facility aims to optimize energy production by combining solar, wind, and battery storage technologies.
- **March 2022**: The Haringvliet energy park was officially commissioned, featuring a 38MW solar facility, a 22MW wind power complex, and 12 battery containers. The facility is expected to produce approximately 140 gigawatt-hours of electricity annually, enough to power around 40,000 Dutch households.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2019**: The overall investment for the Haringvliet project was reported to be €61 million (approximately US$68 million). Additionally, Vattenfall had previously invested €35 million in 2019 to integrate solar and energy storage into its onshore wind project in Haringvliet.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2019**: Announcement of the project and its components.
- **September 2020**: The facility was expected to be operational by this date.
- **March 2022**: Official opening of the Energypark Haringvliet, marking the completion of the project.
