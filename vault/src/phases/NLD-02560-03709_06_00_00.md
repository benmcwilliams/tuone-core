### Evolutionary Timeline of the Waterschap Rivierenland Facility in Weurt, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2022**: The Waterschap Rivierenland facility is involved in the development of the Hide and Shine Floating Solar (HAS FPV) technology, which is designed for floating photovoltaic (PV) systems. This technology allows solar modules to be submerged partially or fully, providing resilience against storms and harsh weather conditions. The first pilot project at the facility will utilize 50 solar modules to test the system's performance in various weather conditions, particularly during storms.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2022**: The development of the HAS FPV system is a collaborative effort involving multiple Dutch companies, including Easyfix Solar, EQAenergy, and Aveco de Bondt. The project aims to create a more cost-effective solution for floating solar technology, potentially reducing costs related to weather insurance and construction.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2022**: The consortium plans to deploy the first pilot project at the water reservoir in Weurt, near Nijmegen, in the coming weeks. This pilot will focus on researching and developing the system's balancing, floating, and control behavior under different weather conditions.
