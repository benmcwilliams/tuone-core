### Evolutionary Timeline of the Chint Facility in Midden-Groningen, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2019**: Chint announced the start of construction on a 103 MW solar park in Midden-Groningen, which will utilize 430,000 Astronergy solar panels on a 116-hectare site. The project is expected to supply the annual power needs of approximately 35,000 households.
- **November 2020**: Chint completed construction on three additional solar parks in the Netherlands, totaling 152 MW. This includes the Zonnepark Stadskanaal (101 MW), Zonnepark Buinerveen (41 MW), and Zonnepark Ommelanderwijk (5.7 MW). The projects were developed in collaboration with Powerfield.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2019**: Chint acquired the rights to develop the Midden-Groningen solar park from Dutch company Powerfield in 2016. Specific investment amounts for the project were not disclosed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2019**: Construction of the 103 MW solar park in Midden-Groningen officially began.
- **December 2019**: The project was noted as being deployed on a 117-hectare plot of land, indicating a slight adjustment in the reported area.
- **November 2020**: Chint announced the completion of the solar parks, including the one in Midden-Groningen.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2019**: The construction of the Midden-Groningen solar park was part of Chint's strategy to utilize excess panel stock from its halted production in Germany, which resulted in the loss of over 200 jobs at the German facility. However, specific job creation numbers for the Midden-Groningen facility were not mentioned.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2019**: Chint is identified as the owner of the facility, having acquired development rights from Powerfield. There is no indication of joint ventures or shared ownership in the articles.
