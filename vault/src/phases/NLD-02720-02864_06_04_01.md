## NLD-02720-02864

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Energyra Facility in Zaanstad, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2017**: Energyra announced plans to build a solar module factory in Zaanstad with an initial production capacity of 100 MW, capable of producing 350,000 panels annually. The facility will manufacture both mono and polycrystalline modules using technology from the Energy Research Centre of the Netherlands (ECN).
- **April 2018**: Construction of the 100 MW factory commenced, with production activities expected to start by the end of the year. The factory will produce modules based on the previously mentioned technologies.
- **June 2018**: Energyra confirmed that the first production line would be operational by September 2018, with plans to increase capacity to 300 MW by adding a second line in the future.
- **April 2019**: Energyra filed for insolvency, citing delays in equipment delivery and a working capital deficit as reasons for the proceedings.
- **January 2020**: After a rescue package was secured, Energyra announced plans to restart production at the Zaanstad facility, with preparations underway.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2017**: The construction of the factory was expected to require an investment of approximately €21 million.
- **April 2018**: The regional government of North Holland provided undisclosed financial support through its North Holland Sustainable Economy Participation Fund (PDENH).
- **June 2018**: The initial investment backing from North Holland and other investors amounted to €15 million.
- **June 2023**: The Dutch government allocated €412 million to support solar panel manufacturing with a circular economy approach, which may include Energyra's facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2017**: Announcement of the factory's construction.
- **April 2018**: Construction officially began.
- **September 2018**: Expected completion of the first production line and start of manufacturing activities.
- **April 2019**: Energyra filed for insolvency.
- **January 2020**: Agreement reached to restart production at the facility.
- **June 2023**: Government support announced for solar panel manufacturing.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2017**: Energyra expected to employ between 50 and 60 people at the facility.
- **April 2018**: The same employment figures were reiterated as construction progressed.
- **April 2019**: The insolvency proceedings raised concerns about job security, but plans for a controlled restart aimed to retain staff.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2017**: Energyra is identified as the owner of the facility, with no mention of joint ventures or external ownership. The company aimed to maintain full control over the production processes and technology.
