### Evolutionary Timeline of the HyET Solar Facility in Arnhem, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2022**: HyET Solar, in collaboration with Delft University of Technology, is developing a photovoltaic foil technology with a 12.0% conversion efficiency. The facility plans to scale its production capacity to **40 MW**. The solar foil is based on hydrogenated amorphous silicon and nanocrystalline silicon in a tandem cell configuration. The PowerFoil1300HE developed for residential applications has a rated power of **152W/m**.
- **September 2023**: HyET Solar announces plans to scale up its production capacity to **300 MW**. The facility produces light and flexible solar foils using hydrogenated amorphous silicon, with two module types based on single junction and tandem junction designs.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2023**: HyET Solar raises **€29 million ($31 million)**, with **Invest-NL** contributing **€14.5 million** in venture debt financing. This funding is aimed at deploying the **40 MW production line** at the Arnhem facility and is part of a broader strategy to reshore solar production to the Netherlands and Europe.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2022**: Announcement of the collaboration with TU Delft to develop photovoltaic foil technology and plans to expand the factory capacity to **40 MW**.
- **September 2023**: Completion of the funding round to accelerate the establishment of the **40 MW manufacturing facility** in Arnhem.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2022**: The expansion and development of the facility are expected to create jobs, although specific job creation numbers are not mentioned in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2021**: Fortescue Future Industries (FFI) acquires a **60% stake** in HyET Solar, indicating that the facility is not under exclusive ownership by HyET Solar but is partially owned by FFI.
