### H2arvester Facility Timeline - Oude-Tonge, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2022**: The H2arvester facility, a mobile agrivoltaic system, was inaugurated in Oude-Tonge, South Holland. It consists of four solar cars hosting a total of 168 solar panels and an irrigation system. Each solar car measures 12×6 meters and can move at a pace of 10 meters per hour. The system is designed to cover up to 10% of an agricultural field without losing its agricultural purpose. The technology aims to improve soil quality and biodiversity while potentially integrating with an electrolyzer for hydrogen generation.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2022**: Local farmer Jacob Jan Dogterom invested €166,000 in the H2arvester system. Additionally, InnovationQuarter, the regional economic development agency for the Province of Zuid-Holland, subsidized around half of the project's costs. The project is part of the H2GO Program, which supports the combination of electricity and green hydrogen.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2022**: The first prototype of the H2arvester was deployed and inaugurated by Netherlands' Agriculture Minister, Henk Staghouwer. The system is currently in a pilot phase, with testing being conducted for one year to assess its impact on agricultural yield and land use.
