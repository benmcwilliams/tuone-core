## NLD-03093-03310

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Powerfield Facility in Noord-Groningen, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2017**: Powerfield announced plans to build a solar module manufacturing facility in Noord-Groningen with an initial production capacity of between 60 and 150 MW, aiming for full capacity of 300 MW after three years. The facility was expected to produce 1.2 million solar modules per year.
- **May 2018**: Powerfield abandoned its plans to build the 300 MW module factory due to rapidly declining module prices and other market factors. The company decided to source solar modules from Dutch manufacturer Energyra instead.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2017**: Powerfield planned to invest between €15 million ($15.8 million) and €20 million ($21.1 million) in the new facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2017**: Announcement of the project to build a solar module manufacturing facility.
- **May 2018**: Official abandonment of the project due to market conditions.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2017**: The facility was expected to create over 100 jobs initially upon opening.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2017 - May 2018**: Powerfield was planning to own the facility exclusively; however, after abandoning the project, the ownership structure became irrelevant as the facility was never built. The company shifted to sourcing modules from Energyra instead.
