### Evolutionary Timeline of the Siemens Gamesa Facility in Hollandse Kust Noord, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2021**: The Hollandse Kust Noord offshore wind farm is set to feature 69 Siemens Gamesa 11 MW turbines, with a total production capacity of 759 MW. The project is scheduled to be fully operational by 2023.
- **February 2023**: The final TP-less monopile installation is completed, designed to support the Siemens Gamesa SG 11.0-200 DD 11 MW wind turbines. The monopiles weigh between 788 tons and 960 tons each, allowing for faster installation and cost reductions.
- **October 2023**: The final Siemens Gamesa wind turbine is installed, marking a significant milestone in the project. The wind farm is expected to generate at least 3.3 TWh of electricity annually, enough to power approximately one million households.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2021**: The project is developed by CrossWind, a joint venture between Shell and Eneco, which secured the rights to build and operate the wind farm in a zero-subsidy auction in 2020.
- **September 2021**: Buss Terminal Eemshaven is selected as the base port for pre-assembly and installation of components, indicating significant logistical investment in the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2021**: The project is officially announced, with construction activities planned to commence.
- **October 2022**: Van Oord begins installing foundations for the wind farm.
- **February 2023**: Completion of the installation of monopiles.
- **October 19, 2023**: The final wind turbine is installed, completing the construction phase of the wind farm.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2022**: The commencement of foundation installation by Van Oord indicates job creation related to construction and installation activities.
- **October 2023**: The successful installation of all turbines suggests ongoing job opportunities in operations and maintenance as the facility transitions to operational status.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2021**: The facility is developed by CrossWind, a joint venture between Shell and Eneco, indicating that Siemens Gamesa does not own the facility exclusively but is a key partner as the turbine manufacturer. Van Oord serves as the Balance of Plant contractor, further indicating a collaborative ownership and operational structure.
