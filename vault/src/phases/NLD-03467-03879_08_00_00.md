### Evolutionary Timeline of the CrossWind Facility (Hollandse Kust Noord, NLD)

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **07-05-2020**: CrossWind, a joint venture between Shell and Eneco, launched a bid for the Hollandse Kust Noord offshore wind zone, planning to develop a 759 MW offshore wind farm expected to generate nearly 3.3 TWh of clean energy annually, enough to power over one million Dutch homes.
- **30-07-2020**: Van Oord was awarded the contract for the engineering, procurement, construction, and installation of the wind farm’s foundations, turbines, and inter-array cables. The project will feature 69 Siemens Gamesa 11 MW turbines.
- **01-06-2021**: Shell added Worley to the project team for the green hydrogen plant, which will be powered by the wind farm and is expected to produce 50,000-60,000 kg of green hydrogen per day.
- **30-06-2022**: Rovco was contracted to perform subsea investigations for unexploded ordnance (UXO) at the wind farm site, a crucial step for the project's progress.
- **20-10-2023**: The final wind turbine was installed, marking a significant milestone in the project. The wind farm is expected to be fully operational by the end of 2023, generating at least 3.3 TWh of electricity annually.
- **08-08-2024**: Fores Engineering was contracted to assemble a 2.5 MW offshore electrolyser for the hydrogen production facility, which is part of the innovative energy integration plan for the wind farm.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07-05-2020**: Both Shell and Eneco issued guarantees for investments related to the construction and operation of the wind farm.
- **01-06-2021**: The hydrogen plant, powered by the wind farm, is part of Shell's broader strategy to decarbonize its operations and support the heavy transportation industry.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **30-07-2020**: The final investment decision was taken, allowing construction to commence.
- **30-06-2022**: UXO investigations were initiated, a critical step towards the wind farm's construction.
- **20-10-2023**: The installation of the 69th and final wind turbine was completed, with the project on track for full operational status by the end of 2023.
- **30-11-2023**: The project received the Final Operational Notification (FON) certificate, confirming its compliance with Dutch grid code requirements.

#### 4. Labour
- **30-06-2022**: The project is expected to create numerous jobs during the construction phase, although specific job creation figures were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **07-05-2020**: CrossWind is a joint venture between Shell and Eneco, indicating shared ownership of the facility.
