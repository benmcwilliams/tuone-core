### Evolutionary Timeline of the Van Oord Facility at Hollandse Kust Noord, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **30-07-2020**: Van Oord was awarded the contract to construct the 759 MW Hollandse Kust Noord (HKN) offshore wind project, which includes the installation of 69 Siemens Gamesa 11 MW turbines. The project will also feature five technology demonstrations, including green hydrogen production.
- **30-06-2022**: Van Oord began subsea investigation works for UXO (unexploded ordnance) identification and removal, crucial for the installation of foundations and cables.
- **09-02-2023**: Van Oord successfully installed the final monopile, completing the foundation installation for the wind farm. The monopiles weigh between 788 and 960 tonnes each.
- **20-10-2023**: The final wind turbine was installed, marking the completion of the turbine installation phase. The wind farm is expected to generate at least 3.3 TWh of electricity annually, enough to power approximately one million households.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **30-07-2020**: The project was part of a zero-subsidy auction in the Netherlands, indicating significant investment from the CrossWind consortium (Shell and Eneco) to develop the offshore wind farm.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **30-07-2020**: Contract awarded to Van Oord; construction set to start with a Final Investment Decision taken.
- **17-10-2022**: Installation of the first monopile began.
- **09-02-2023**: Completion of monopile installation.
- **20-10-2023**: Installation of the 69th and final wind turbine completed; project on track for full operational status by the end of 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **30-07-2020**: The project is expected to create numerous jobs related to the construction and operation of the offshore wind farm, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **30-07-2020**: The Hollandse Kust Noord project is a joint venture between Shell and Eneco, known as CrossWind, with Van Oord serving as the Balance of Plant contractor. This indicates that Van Oord does not own the facility exclusively but is a key contractor in the project. 

This timeline captures the significant developments and milestones related to the Van Oord facility at Hollandse Kust Noord, highlighting its role in advancing clean energy technology in the Netherlands.
