### Evolutionary Timeline of the Groningen Seaports Facility in Delfzijl, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2020**: Announcement of the NortH2 project, which aims to produce green hydrogen using renewable electricity from a mega offshore wind farm. The project plans to achieve a production capacity of approximately 800,000 tonnes of green hydrogen per year by 2040. The facility will utilize a large electrolyser in the Eemshaven for converting wind energy into hydrogen.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2020**: The NortH2 project is supported by the province of Groningen and anticipates requiring European and national subsidies for its initial phases, which are essential for the decarbonisation of energy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2020**: The consortium, including Gasunie, Groningen Seaports, and Shell Nederland, announces the launch of the NortH2 project. The project is set to begin with a feasibility study in 2020, with the first hydrogen production expected by 2027, contingent on successful outcomes from the study and necessary permits.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2020**: The project is expected to create job opportunities in the region as it develops the green hydrogen facility, contributing to the decarbonisation of the industry and supporting growth in the local economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2020**: The facility is part of a consortium involving multiple partners (Gasunie, Groningen Seaports, and Shell Nederland), indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by any single entity. The consortium is actively seeking additional partners to support the project.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic plans for the Groningen Seaports facility in Delfzijl, focusing on the NortH2 project and its implications for green hydrogen production in the region.
