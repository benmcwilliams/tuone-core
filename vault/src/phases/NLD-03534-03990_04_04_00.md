### Evolutionary Timeline of the HEAVENN Facility in Delfzijl, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **03-07-2020**: The HEAVENN project is launched, aiming to create a fully integrated green hydrogen value chain in the Northern Netherlands by the end of 2025. The facility will utilize a 20 MW electrolyser in Delfzijl, developed under a previous project called “Djewels,” and a planned 10 MW electrolyser in Emmen. The hydrogen produced will be used for various applications, including green methanol production, high-temperature industrial heat, backup power for a data center, and hydrogen-derived e-fuels for aviation.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **03-07-2020**: The HEAVENN project receives a significant European grant of €20 million from the Fuel Cell and Hydrogen Joint Undertaking (FCH-JU) to support its development. This grant is one of the largest ever awarded to a single project under the FCH-JU partnership and is complemented by €70 million in public and private co-funding at the national level.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **03-07-2020**: The official kick-off of the HEAVENN project is announced, marking the beginning of the initiative to develop the hydrogen economy in the Northern Netherlands. The project is expected to be completed by the end of 2025, with the establishment of a comprehensive hydrogen value chain.

#### 4. Labour
- **03-07-2020**: The transition to a hydrogen economy is anticipated to create new job opportunities in the region, particularly in high-tech and skilled positions related to green hydrogen production and its applications. The project aims to provide a solution to job losses resulting from the decline of the natural gas industry in the area.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **03-07-2020**: The HEAVENN project is a collaborative initiative involving 29 public and private parties from 7 European countries, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by a single company.
