### Evolutionary Timeline of the Wirsol Facility in Delfzijl, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2017**: Wirsol announced the commercial operation of the **30 MW Sunport Delfzijl Solar Park**. The facility consists of approximately **120,000 solar modules** and was built over a year. The project is designed to generate around **27,634 megawatt hours of electricity annually**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2017**: The construction of the Sunport Delfzijl Solar Park involved an investment of **€40 million**. The project was developed by Wirsol, showcasing their commitment to expanding renewable energy in the Netherlands.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2017**: The Sunport Delfzijl Solar Park was officially inaugurated and began operations.
- **July 2017**: A **10-year power purchase agreement** was established between Wirsol and Eneco, where Eneco would buy all the electricity generated by the solar plant to supply Google’s data center in Eemshaven, located 32 km away.

#### 4. Labour
- There are no specific mentions of job creation or job loss directly associated with the Sunport Delfzijl Solar Park in the provided articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Sunport Delfzijl Solar Park is **owned exclusively by Wirsol**, a German solar developer. Eneco, a Dutch utility, holds a power purchase agreement with Wirsol but does not own the facility.
