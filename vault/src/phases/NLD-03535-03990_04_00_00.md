### HEAVENN Facility Timeline in Hoogeveen, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **03-07-2020**: The HEAVENN project is introduced as part of the Northern Netherlands' ambition to create a "Hydrogen Valley." The project aims to establish a fully integrated green hydrogen value chain by the end of 2025. It will utilize a 20 MW electrolyser in Delfzijl and a planned 10 MW electrolyser in Emmen for green hydrogen production. In Hoogeveen, the plan includes heating 100 new residential buildings with hydrogen boilers and fuel-cell driven heating systems, with potential future connections to 250-1,200 homes using a blend of natural gas and hydrogen.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **03-07-2020**: The HEAVENN project receives a significant European grant of €20 million from the Fuel Cell and Hydrogen Joint Undertaking (FCH-JU) to support its development. This grant is complemented by €70 million in public and private co-funding at the national level, specifically aimed at developing green hydrogen from renewable electricity.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **03-07-2020**: The HEAVENN project officially kicks off, marking the beginning of its development phase. The project is expected to culminate in a fully operational hydrogen value chain by the end of 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **03-07-2020**: The transition to a hydrogen economy in the Northern Netherlands, including the HEAVENN project, is anticipated to create new job opportunities in the region, particularly in high-tech and skilled positions related to green hydrogen production and infrastructure development. This is seen as a potential solution to job losses in the declining gas industry.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **03-07-2020**: The HEAVENN project is a collaborative initiative involving 29 public and private parties from 7 European countries, indicating that it is not solely owned by one company but rather a joint effort among multiple stakeholders. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic plans for the HEAVENN facility in Hoogeveen, highlighting its role in the broader context of the Northern Netherlands' hydrogen economy.
