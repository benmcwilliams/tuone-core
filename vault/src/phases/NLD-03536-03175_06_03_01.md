### Evolutionary Timeline of the MCPV Facility in Veendam, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10/2024**: MCPV announced plans to build a 4 GW heterojunction (HJT) cell manufacturing plant in Veendam. The facility will produce solar panels using HJT technology with an efficiency of up to 26.5%. Future plans include the production of tandem perovskite-silicon solar modules with efficiencies exceeding 30%.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10/2024**: MCPV raised €4.2 million (US$4.6 million) in financing to support the development of the 4 GW solar cell manufacturing plant. This funding comes from the Dutch Ministry of Economic Affairs, the provincial government of Groningen, and the Dutch regional development agency NOM. The financing is part of a broader public-private cooperation initiative, including support from the National Growth Fund.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **10/2024**: MCPV confirmed the kickstart of the implementation phase for the 4 GW cell factory in Veendam. The company has signed an agreement with Enexis, the local grid operator, to ensure sufficient grid capacity for both initial production lines and future expansions.

#### 4. Labour
- **10/2024**: While specific job creation numbers have not been detailed, the announcement of the facility's development implies potential job creation associated with the construction and operation of the new manufacturing plant.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **10/2024**: MCPV is a subsidiary of SolarNL, indicating that it does not have exclusive ownership of the facility but operates under the umbrella of its parent company.
