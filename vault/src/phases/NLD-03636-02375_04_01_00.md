### Evolutionary Timeline of Neptune Energy Facility at Q13a Platform, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2020**: Neptune Energy joins the European Clean Hydrogen Alliance, supporting the EU's hydrogen strategy. The Q13a platform will utilize electricity from offshore wind turbines to power a hydrogen plant, converting seawater into hydrogen via electrolysis.
- **November 2020**: The PosHYdon project is announced, integrating offshore wind, gas, and hydrogen systems. The hydrogen plant on the Q13a platform is confirmed as part of this initiative, marking it as the first fully electrified platform in the Dutch North Sea.
- **May 2021**: The Q13a platform is noted for its role in the PosHYdon project, which aims to produce hydrogen in an offshore environment, with production expected to start later in 2021.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2020**: The PosHYdon project is supported by partnerships with Gasunie, NOGAT B.V., and Noordgastransport B.V., indicating significant investment in infrastructure for hydrogen production and transport.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2019**: Neptune Energy is selected for the PosHYdon pilot project to create the first offshore green hydrogen plant in the Dutch North Sea.
- **November 2020**: The project is officially announced, with plans for installation on the Q13a platform.
- **Expected Late 2021**: Production of hydrogen is anticipated to begin on the Q13a platform as part of the PosHYdon project.

#### 4. Labour
- **No specific job creation or loss details** are mentioned in the articles regarding the Q13a facility or the PosHYdon project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2020**: The Q13a-A platform is noted to have partners: EBN B.V. (40%) and TAQA Offshore B.V. (10%), indicating that Neptune Energy does not have exclusive ownership of the facility.
