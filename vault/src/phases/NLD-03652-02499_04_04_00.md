## NLD-03652-02499

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Yara Facility in Sluiskil, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **07-10-2020**: Yara, in collaboration with Ørsted, announced the development of a 100MW wind-powered electrolyser plant aimed at producing renewable hydrogen. This hydrogen will replace fossil-based hydrogen in the ammonia production process at Yara’s Sluiskil facility. The project is expected to generate approximately 75,000 tonnes of green ammonia annually, which is about 10% of the capacity of one of the ammonia plants at Sluiskil.

- **20-11-2023**: Yara signed a cross-border CO2 transport and storage agreement with Northern Lights, aiming to cut annual CO₂ emissions from the Sluiskil plant by 800,000 tons. This project involves transporting liquefied CO₂ emitted from ammonia production to permanent storage under the Norwegian Continental Shelf, with operations expected to begin in 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07-10-2020**: Yara and Ørsted are seeking public co-funding to support the development and construction of the 100MW electrolyser facility. The final investment decision for this project is anticipated to be made in late 2021 or early 2022, contingent on sufficient co-funding and a confirmed business case.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **07-10-2020**: Announcement of the joint project between Yara and Ørsted for the electrolyser plant, with plans to significantly reduce CO2 emissions and enhance the sustainability of ammonia production.

- **20-11-2023**: Signing of the CO2 transport and storage agreement marks a significant milestone in Yara's efforts to decarbonize its ammonia production processes.

#### 4. Labour
- **07-10-2020**: The project is expected to create economic activity and jobs in the region, as indicated by the potential for the Netherlands to lead in the green transformation of heavy industry powered by offshore wind.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **07-10-2020**: The facility is owned exclusively by Yara, although it is collaborating with Ørsted for the renewable hydrogen project. The CCS project involves a partnership with Northern Lights for CO2 transport and storage, but Yara maintains full ownership of the Sluiskil facility. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic initiatives undertaken by Yara at its Sluiskil facility, highlighting its commitment to sustainability and innovation in clean technology.
