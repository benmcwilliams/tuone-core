### Evolutionary Timeline of Neptune Energy Facility in Q13a-A, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2021**: Neptune Energy's Q13a-A facility is set to host the PosHYdon project, which aims to validate the integration of offshore wind, gas, and hydrogen. The facility will install a green hydrogen-producing plant that converts seawater into demineralised water and then into green hydrogen via electrolysis. The plant will have a production capacity of 400 kilograms of green hydrogen per day using a 1MW electrolyser.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2021**: The Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO) awarded €3.6 million ($4.2 million) to the PosHYdon project consortium, which includes Neptune Energy. This funding is intended to kickstart the project and support the installation of the green hydrogen production plant at the Q13a-A site.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2021**: The Dutch government officially backs the PosHYdon project, marking it as the world’s first offshore green hydrogen production plant on a working platform. The project aims to integrate three energy systems at the Q13a-A facility, which is located approximately 13 km off the coast of Scheveningen.
