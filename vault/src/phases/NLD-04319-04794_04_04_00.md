### Evolutionary Timeline of the DEME Offshore Facility in Q13a-A, NLD

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** July 2021
   - **Details:** The facility at Q13a-A is set to host the world’s first offshore green hydrogen production plant as part of the PosHYdon project. The plant will utilize a 1MW electrolyser to produce a maximum of 400 kilograms of green hydrogen per day. The production process involves converting seawater into demineralised water and then into green hydrogen through electrolysis, powered by electricity generated from onsite offshore wind turbines.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** July 2021
   - **Details:** The Dutch government, through the Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO), awarded €3.6 million ($4.2 million) in funding to the consortium implementing the PosHYdon project. This funding is intended to kickstart the project and support the completion of the installation.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** July 2021
   - **Details:** The announcement of the funding and the project details marks a significant milestone for the facility, indicating the start of the pilot project aimed at integrating offshore energy systems and producing green hydrogen.
