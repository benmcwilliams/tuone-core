### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hatenboer Facility in Q13a-A, NLD

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** July 2021
   - **Details:** The facility at Q13a-A is set to produce green hydrogen through a pilot project named PosHYdon. It will utilize a 1MW electrolyser capable of producing a maximum of 400 kilograms of green hydrogen per day. The hydrogen production will be powered by electricity generated from onsite offshore wind turbines, with seawater being converted into demineralised water and then into green hydrogen via the electrolysis process.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** July 2021
   - **Details:** The Dutch government, through the Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO), has awarded €3.6 million ($4.2 million) to the consortium involved in the PosHYdon project. This funding is aimed at kickstarting the project and will be supplemented by the consortium's own budget to complete the installation.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** July 2021
   - **Details:** The PosHYdon project was announced, marking the implementation of what is claimed to be the world’s first offshore green hydrogen production plant. The project aims to integrate offshore wind, gas, and hydrogen systems at the Q13a-A facility, which is approximately 13km off the coast of Scheveningen in the Dutch North Sea.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
