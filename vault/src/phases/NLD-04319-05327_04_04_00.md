### Evolutionary Timeline of the IV-Offshore & Energy Facility at Q13a-A, NLD

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** July 2021
   - **Details:** The facility is set to produce green hydrogen through a pilot project named PosHYdon. It will utilize a 1MW electrolyser capable of producing a maximum of 400 kilograms of green hydrogen per day. The production process involves converting seawater into demineralised water, which is then transformed into green hydrogen via electrolysis, powered by electricity generated from onsite offshore wind turbines. The project aims to integrate offshore wind, gas, and hydrogen energy systems.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** July 2021
   - **Details:** The Dutch government, through the Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO), has awarded €3.6 million ($4.2 million) to the consortium involved in the PosHYdon project. This funding is intended to kickstart the project and support the completion of the installation.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** July 2021
   - **Details:** The PosHYdon project was announced as the world’s first offshore green hydrogen production plant, with plans to be implemented at the Q13a-A site operated by Neptune Energy. The project aims to gain insights into the integration of energy systems at sea and the production of hydrogen in an offshore environment.
