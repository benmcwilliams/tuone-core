### Evolutionary Timeline of the Nexstep Facility at Q13a-A, NLD

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** July 2021
   - **Details:** The facility at Q13a-A is set to produce green hydrogen through a pilot project named PosHYdon. The production capacity is defined by a 1MW electrolyser, which is expected to generate a maximum of 400 kilograms of green hydrogen per day. The facility will integrate three energy systems: offshore wind, offshore gas, and offshore hydrogen. The hydrogen production will utilize electricity from onsite offshore wind turbines and will involve the conversion of seawater into demineralised water, followed by electrolysis to produce hydrogen.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** July 2021
   - **Details:** The Dutch government, through the Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO), has awarded €3.6 million ($4.2 million) to support the PosHYdon project. This funding is intended to kickstart the project and assist the consortium in leveraging additional budget for the installation.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** July 2021
   - **Details:** The PosHYdon project was announced as the world’s first offshore green hydrogen production plant. The project aims to gain insights into integrating energy systems at sea and the production of hydrogen in an offshore environment. The facility is located approximately 13km off the coast of Scheveningen in the Dutch North Sea.
