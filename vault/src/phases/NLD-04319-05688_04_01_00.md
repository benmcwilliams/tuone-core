### Evolutionary Timeline of the Noordgastransport Facility (Q13a-A, NLD)

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date**: July 2021
  - The facility is set to produce green hydrogen through a pilot project named PosHYdon. The production capacity is defined by a 1MW electrolyser, which is expected to generate a maximum of 400 kilograms of green hydrogen per day. The facility will integrate offshore wind, gas, and hydrogen systems, utilizing electricity from onsite offshore wind turbines to convert seawater into demineralised water and then into green hydrogen via electrolysis.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date**: July 2021
  - The Dutch government, through the Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO), has awarded €3.6 million ($4.2 million) to the consortium involved in the PosHYdon project. This funding is aimed at kickstarting the project and will be complemented by the consortium's own budget for the completion of the installation.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date**: July 2021
  - The PosHYdon project was announced, marking the initiation of the world's first offshore green hydrogen production plant at the Q13a-A site. The project aims to gain insights into the integration of energy systems at sea and the production of hydrogen in an offshore environment.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date**: July 2021
  - While specific job creation or loss figures were not mentioned in the article, the project is expected to contribute to the development of offshore green hydrogen production, which may lead to job creation in the renewable energy sector as the facility expands and operates.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date**: July 2021
  - The facility is operated by Neptune Energy, and the consortium developing the project includes multiple partners such as Nel Hydrogen, InVesta, Hatenboer, IV-Offshore & Energy, Emerson Automation Solutions, and others, indicating a collaborative effort rather than exclusive ownership by a single entity.
