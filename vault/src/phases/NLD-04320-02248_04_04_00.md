### Evolutionary Timeline of the Gasunie Facility at Q13a-A Platform, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2021**: The Q13a-A platform, operated by Neptune Energy, is set to host the PosHYdon project, which will install a 1MW electrolyser. This facility aims to produce green hydrogen by converting seawater into demineralised water and then into hydrogen via electrolysis. The electrolyser is expected to produce a maximum of 400kg of green hydrogen per day. The platform is noted as the first fully electrified platform in the Dutch North Sea.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2021**: The PosHYdon project secures a grant of €3.6 million from the Dutch government through the Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO). The remaining budget for the project will be funded by consortium partners, which include various companies and organizations.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2021**: The announcement of the PosHYdon project marks the start of activities for the pilot project, which aims to validate the integration of offshore wind, gas, and hydrogen systems in the Dutch North Sea.

#### 4. Labour

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2021**: The Q13a-A platform is operated by Neptune Energy, and the PosHYdon project involves a consortium of multiple partners, including Gasunie, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by a single company.
