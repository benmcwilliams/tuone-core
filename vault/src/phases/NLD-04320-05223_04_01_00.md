### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hatenboer Facility at Q13a-A Platform, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2021**: The Hatenboer facility is part of the PosHYdon project, which aims to develop the world’s first offshore green hydrogen pilot. The facility will install a 1MW electrolyser on the Neptune Energy-operated Q13a-A platform. This electrolyser is expected to produce a maximum of 400kg of green hydrogen per day. The project seeks to validate the integration of offshore wind, gas, and hydrogen energy systems.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2021**: The project secured a grant of €3.6 million from the Dutch government through the Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO) to support the development of the PosHYdon project. The remaining budget will be funded by consortium partners, which include Hatenboer.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2021**: The PosHYdon project was officially announced, marking the start of activities for the pilot installation on the Q13a-A platform. The project aims to gain experience in integrating energy systems at sea and producing hydrogen in an offshore environment.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2021**: While specific job creation or loss figures were not mentioned in the article, the involvement of multiple partners in the consortium suggests potential job creation related to the installation and operation of the electrolyser and the overall project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2021**: The facility is operated by Neptune Energy, with Hatenboer being one of the consortium partners involved in the PosHYdon project. The ownership structure indicates that the facility is not exclusively owned by Hatenboer but is part of a collaborative effort among multiple partners.
