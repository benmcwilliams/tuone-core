### Evolutionary Timeline of the NOGAT Facility at Q13a-A Platform, Netherlands

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** July 2021
   - **Details:** The Q13a-A platform, operated by Neptune Energy, is set to host the PosHYdon project, which will install a 1MW electrolyser. This electrolyser will produce a maximum of 400kg of green hydrogen per day by converting seawater into demineralised water and then into hydrogen via electrolysis. The platform is noted as the first fully electrified platform in the Dutch North Sea, utilizing electricity generated from offshore wind to power the hydrogen production.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** July 2021
   - **Details:** The PosHYdon project received a grant of €3.6 million from the Dutch government through the Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO). The remaining budget for the project will be funded by consortium partners, which include various companies and organizations involved in the development of the pilot.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** July 2021
   - **Details:** The announcement of the PosHYdon project marks the start of activities for the pilot, which aims to validate the integration of offshore wind, gas, and hydrogen systems in the Dutch North Sea. The project is a significant milestone in offshore green hydrogen production, with the aim of gaining experience in integrating energy systems at sea.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** July 2021
   - **Details:** The article does not specify exact job creation or job loss figures related to the PosHYdon project. However, the development of the project is expected to contribute to job creation in the renewable energy sector, particularly in offshore operations and maintenance.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** July 2021
   - **Details:** The Q13a-A platform is operated by Neptune Energy. The PosHYdon project is a consortium effort, indicating that the facility is not owned exclusively by one company but involves multiple partners, including NOGAT, DEME Offshore, Eneco, and others.
