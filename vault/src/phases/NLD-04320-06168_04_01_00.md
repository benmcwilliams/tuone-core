### Evolutionary Timeline of the TAQA Facility at Q13a-A Platform, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2021**: The Q13a-A platform, operated by Neptune Energy, is set to host the PosHYdon project, which will install a 1MW electrolyser. This facility aims to produce green hydrogen by converting seawater into demineralised water and then into hydrogen via electrolysis. The electrolyser is expected to produce a maximum of 400kg of green hydrogen per day. The Q13a-A platform is noted as the first fully electrified platform in the Dutch North Sea.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2021**: The PosHYdon project secures a grant of €3.6 million from the Dutch government through the Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO). This funding is part of the budget for the pilot project, with additional funding provided by consortium partners.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2021**: The PosHYdon project is officially announced, marking the start of activities for the pilot project on the Q13a-A platform. The project aims to validate the integration of offshore wind, gas, and hydrogen energy systems in the Dutch North Sea.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2021**: While specific job creation or loss figures are not mentioned in the article, the involvement of multiple partners in the PosHYdon consortium suggests potential job creation related to the installation and operation of the electrolyser and associated systems.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2021**: The Q13a-A platform is operated by Neptune Energy, and the PosHYdon project involves a consortium that includes TAQA, among other partners. The ownership structure indicates that the facility is not exclusively owned by TAQA but is part of a collaborative effort with multiple stakeholders.
