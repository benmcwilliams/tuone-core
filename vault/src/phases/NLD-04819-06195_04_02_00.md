### Evolutionary Timeline of Tata Steel Facility in Ijmuiden, Netherlands

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **November 2024:** Tata Steel is planning to clean up its Ijmuiden steel plant by introducing direct reduced iron (DRI) in one or more of the plant’s furnaces. The company has ordered two DRI units from Danieli, which are designed to be "hydrogen-ready." These units can produce DRI pellets with a carbon content of just 0.5% through extensive use of hydrogen.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **November 2024:** Tata Steel is collaborating with Norwegian producer Gen2 Energy and the Port of Amsterdam to utilize green hydrogen produced from hydropower in Norway. This initiative involves transporting liquid hydrogen to Amsterdam for conversion back to its gaseous state, which will then be fed into a planned pipeline network for delivery to Tata and other companies.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **November 2024:** Tata Steel has signed a separate agreement with ECOLOG, Horisont Energie, the Port of Amsterdam, OCAP, Norway’s DNB, and ABN AMRO to explore a CO2 corridor. This project aims to capture CO2 emissions from the cleaner steel production process and potentially export them to Norway.
