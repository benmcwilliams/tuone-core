### Evolutionary Timeline of the GroenLeven Facility in Friesland, Netherlands

1. **Technology and Operations**
   - **March 2022**: GroenLeven, in collaboration with Alliander, commissioned the Netherlands’ first solar-powered green hydrogen plant. The facility is powered by a 50MWp solar farm and is designed to produce 100,000 kg of hydrogen per year. The project aims to explore how hydrogen technology can alleviate grid congestion caused by increasing renewable energy sources.

2. **Investment and Development**
   - **March 2022**: The Dutch government announced a record €13 billion (approximately US$14.3 billion) for its 2022 Sustainable Energy Production and Climate Transition Incentive Scheme, which is open to green hydrogen projects linked to solar initiatives. This funding is expected to support the development of the hydrogen plant.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**
   - **March 2022**: The hydrogen plant was officially opened and began operations, with hydrogen production expected to start in June 2022. The project was initially announced in 2020, focusing on testing the hydrogen electrolyser's ability to follow the solar plant's generation profile.
