### Evolutionary Timeline of Vattenfall Facility in Almere, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2021**: Vattenfall announced a pilot agrivoltaic project in Almere, with a production capacity of **0.7 MW**. The project, named **Symbizon**, aims to combine photovoltaic power generation with strip farming techniques. The facility will utilize single-axis trackers and bifacial solar modules placed at a height of 2.3 meters.
- **November 2021**: Vattenfall revealed plans for a **16.8 MW** solar facility in Almere, which will consist of **25,000 bifacial modules** and is expected to generate approximately **18.3 GWh** per year. The facility will also employ solar trackers to enhance energy production while maintaining soil quality and biodiversity.
- **November 2022**: Vattenfall confirmed plans to build the **16.8 MW** solar project along the A6 highway in Almere, with construction expected to begin in **2024** and operations to start in **late 2024 or early 2025**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2021**: The planned **16.8 MW** solar facility is noted to be the first unsubsidized solar park in the Netherlands, indicating a shift towards projects relying on power purchase agreements (PPAs) rather than state subsidies.
