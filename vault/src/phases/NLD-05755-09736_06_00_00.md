### Evolutionary Timeline of the Pfalzsolar Facility in Almere, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2019**: Pfalzsolar announced the construction of a 34MW solar farm in Almere, located in the Dutch province of Flevoland. The facility will cover a 25-hectare site and is designed to generate approximately 30 million kilowatt-hours of electricity annually. The construction is set to utilize an east-west mounting system to optimize land use.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2019**: The project is part of a contract secured by Pfalzsolar from HVC Group, a Dutch waste management company. The financial specifics of the investment were not detailed in the articles, but the facility will benefit from the Dutch SDE+ subsidy program, which provides a feed-in tariff for the electricity generated.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2019**: Construction of the Almere solar park began on March 21, 2019, and was expected to take about four months to complete. This project is noted as Pfalzsolar's largest to date and marks a significant milestone in their collaboration with HVC Group.
