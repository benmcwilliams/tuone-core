### Evolutionary Timeline of the NAM Facility in Roswinkel, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2019**: The NAM facility in Roswinkel is being considered for hydrogen production using excess solar power generation capacity. The feasibility study includes the potential installation of an electrolyzer with a capacity of 20-50 MW. This initiative aims to convert locally generated sustainable energy into hydrogen to avoid curtailment of clean energy in the region.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2019**: The feasibility study for hydrogen production at the NAM facility is being conducted by a partnership involving Dutch transmission system operator Enexis, Gasunie, and NAM. The study assesses the conditions for hydrogen production and the transport of this energy via existing gas pipelines.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2019**: A feasibility study is announced to explore hydrogen production at the NAM facility in Roswinkel, alongside other sites owned by NAM. The results of this study are expected to be revealed by mid-January 2020.

#### 4. Labour
- **No specific job creation or job loss information** is provided in the articles regarding the NAM facility in Roswinkel as of December 2019.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2019**: The NAM facility is part of a joint venture (JV) formed by Shell and ExxonMobil, indicating that NAM does not own the facility exclusively but operates it as part of a partnership.
