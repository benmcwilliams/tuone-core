### Evolutionary Timeline of the TNO Facility in Oostvoornse, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2020**: TNO is establishing a test facility for floating photovoltaic (PV) plants in Oostvoornse. The facility will analyze the impact of wind and waves on floating structures and module yield. The PV arrays at the facility will range up to **50 kW** in size. The focus will be on the integrity of floating structures, assessing module damage, and the impact of waves on light absorption. TNO will monitor the systems for their electrical performance, while partners will study hydrodynamic issues and ecological impacts.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2020**: The facility is part of the **Solar@Sea project**, which is funded by the Dutch government. This project aims to explore concepts suitable for offshore solar applications, indicating a significant investment in research and development for floating PV technologies.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2020**: TNO announced the establishment of the test facility in Oostvoornse. The commissioning of the floating systems is planned for **summer 2020**, after which monitoring will commence. The facility will involve collaboration with partners such as Sabic, Equinor, and the municipality of Westvoorne, and will include systems built by Texel4Trading/SolarFloat (Netherlands), SolarisFloat (Portugal), and Isifloating by Isigenere (Spain).
