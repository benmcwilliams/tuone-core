### Evolutionary Timeline of the RWE Facility in Borssele, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2021**: RWE signed a power purchase agreement (PPA) with ASML for the supply of 250 GWh of green electricity annually. This electricity will come from RWE's portfolio, which includes two new onshore wind farms (Oostpolderdijk and Westereems 3) with a total installed capacity of 16.5 MW, and a solar plant near Borssele. The onshore wind farms are expected to be commissioned in 2021.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12/2021**: The Dutch government earmarked EUR 500 million to support new nuclear build, indicating a significant investment in energy infrastructure, including the Borssele site.
- **12/2022**: The Dutch Council of Ministers designated the Borssele site for two new nuclear reactors and initiated a feasibility study for extending the operation of the existing Borssele nuclear plant beyond 2033.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2022**: The Borssele site was officially designated for new nuclear reactors, and feasibility studies were called for extending the existing plant's operation.
- **06/2024**: The Dutch government began exploratory discussions regarding a potential share transfer of the Borssele plant, which is currently 70% owned by Zeeuwse Energie Houdstermaatschappij (ZEH) and 30% by RWE.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- As of **06/2024**, the ownership structure of the Borssele plant is as follows: 70% owned by Zeeuwse Energie Houdstermaatschappij (ZEH) and 30% owned by RWE. The Dutch government is exploring the possibility of purchasing shares to gain control over the plant's future operations.
