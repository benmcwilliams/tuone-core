## NLD-07889-00712

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Shell Facility in Borssele, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2020**: The Borssele 3&4 project, part of the Blauwwind consortium, began the installation of 77 MHI Vestas V164-9.5MW turbines, with a total production capacity of 732 MW. The project is located 22 km off the coast of Zeeland and is expected to be one of the largest offshore wind farms in the Netherlands.
- **January 2021**: The Borssele III & IV offshore wind farm reached full commissioning, confirming its operational capacity of 731.5 MW, capable of powering approximately 825,000 Dutch households.
- **May 2021**: The facility received certification from Lloyd’s Register, allowing it to officially start operations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **No specific investment amounts or subsidies were detailed in the articles.** However, the successful completion and commissioning of the wind farm indicate significant investment from the consortium partners, including Shell.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2020**: Installation of turbines commenced.
- **July 2020**: The connection to the Borssele Beta platform was completed, facilitating the transport of electricity to the onshore substation.
- **January 6, 2021**: Official commissioning of the wind farm.
- **May 2021**: Certification was awarded, allowing the facility to enter operation.

#### 4. Labour
- **No specific job creation or job loss figures were mentioned in the articles.** However, the successful construction and operation of the facility likely contributed to job creation within the renewable energy sector in the Netherlands.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Borssele 3&4 project is developed by the Blauwwind consortium, which includes multiple partners: Partners Group (45%), Shell (20%), DGE (15%), Eneco Group (10%), and Van Oord (10%). Therefore, Shell does not own the facility exclusively but is a significant partner in a joint venture.
