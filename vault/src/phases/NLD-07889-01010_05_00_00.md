### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Borssele, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2021**: The Dutch government announced plans to place nuclear power at the heart of its climate and energy policy, with a focus on constructing two new reactors at the Borssele location. Each reactor is expected to have a capacity of 1000-1650 MWe, contributing 9-13% of the Netherlands' electricity production by 2035.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2022**: The Dutch cabinet identified Borssele as the most suitable location for the new reactors, emphasizing the role of nuclear energy in achieving a CO2-free energy system.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2023**: The Dutch government announced that KHNP (Korea Hydro & Nuclear Power) would begin a feasibility study in January 2024 to assess the compliance of the reactor design with Dutch regulations, the suitability of the Borssele location, and to estimate costs and timelines for building the reactors. This study is expected to last at least six months, with results anticipated in autumn 2024. This marks the first of three contracts for feasibility studies, with contracts for Westinghouse and EDF to follow.
