## NLD-07889-01842

### Evolutionary Timeline of Ørsted Facility in Borssele, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2018**: Ørsted's Borssele 1 & 2 offshore wind farm is announced to have a total capacity of **752 MW**. The wind farm will consist of **94 Siemens Gamesa 8 MW wind turbines** installed on monopile foundations. The project involves multiple contractors, including EEW OSB for transition pieces and GeoSea for transportation and installation of the monopiles.
- **May 2019**: The **Borssele Alpha** offshore substation platform, weighing **3,650 tonnes**, is prepared for installation. This platform is part of the infrastructure supporting the wind farm.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2018**: Ørsted secures contracts with various suppliers, including EEW OSB, Bladt Industries, and Nexans, as part of the development of the Borssele 1 & 2 project. The contracts represent significant investments in the supply chain for the wind farm.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2018**: Ørsted announces the contract with EEW OSB for the supply of **35 transition pieces** for the Borssele 1 & 2 project. The works are scheduled to begin in **2020**.
- **May 2019**: The topside for the Borssele Alpha offshore substation is ready for installation, indicating progress towards the operational phase of the wind farm, which is expected to come online in **2020**.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2018**: The contract with EEW OSB secures **180 jobs** in Teesside, UK, highlighting the job creation associated with the Borssele 1 & 2 project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Ørsted is the **exclusive owner** of the Borssele 1 & 2 facility, as indicated by the contracts and project announcements related to the wind farm.
