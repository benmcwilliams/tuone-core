### Evolutionary Timeline of the MHI Vestas Facility in Borssele, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2019**: The Borssele III & IV offshore wind farm is under construction, featuring MHI Vestas 9.5 MW turbines. The total capacity of the wind farm is 731.5 MW, with 77 turbines planned for installation.
- **May 2020**: Installation of the MHI Vestas V164-9.5 MW turbines begins at the Borssele 3 & 4 project, with the operation expected to last throughout the year.
- **February 2021**: The Borssele 5 offshore wind farm is commissioned, featuring two Vestas 9.5 MW turbines. This site is designated as an innovation site, incorporating several technological advancements aimed at reducing costs and environmental impacts.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2021**: The Borssele 5 project includes innovative technologies such as a submerged slip joint, which was the first of its kind used commercially on a full-sized offshore wind turbine. This innovation was certified by DNV GL in 2019, indicating prior investment in research and development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2019**: Van Oord is contracted for helicopter services during the construction phase of the Borssele III & IV offshore wind farm.
- **May 2020**: The installation of turbines at the Borssele 3 & 4 project commences, with completion aimed for the end of the year.
- **February 2021**: The commissioning of the Borssele 5 offshore wind farm is completed, marking a significant milestone in the project’s development.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2021**: The completion of the Borssele 5 project is noted to have been achieved on time and within budget, suggesting potential job creation through the construction and operational phases, although specific job numbers are not provided.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2020**: The Borssele 3 & 4 project is developed by the Blauwwind consortium, which includes Partners Group (45%), Shell (20%), DGE (15%), Eneco Group (10%), and Van Oord (10%). This indicates that MHI Vestas does not exclusively own the facility but is part of a consortium structure for the Borssele projects.
