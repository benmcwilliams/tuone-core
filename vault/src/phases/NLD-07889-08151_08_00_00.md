## NLD-07889-08151

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Van Oord Facility in Borssele, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2018**: Van Oord is involved in the Borssele 1 and 2 wind farm project, which has a production capacity of 752 MW. The facility will utilize 94 Siemens Gamesa 8MW wind turbines, with inter-array cables being installed by Van Oord.
- **November 2019**: Van Oord is contracted to execute the Balance of Plant for the Borssele III & IV offshore wind farm, which has a capacity of 731.5 MW and will consist of 77 MHI Vestas 9.5 MW turbines.
- **January 2021**: The Borssele III & IV wind farm is fully commissioned, capable of powering 825,000 households. Van Oord was responsible for the design, engineering, procurement, construction, and installation of the foundations and inter-array cables.
- **February 2021**: Van Oord completes the commissioning of the Borssele 5 offshore wind farm, featuring two Vestas 9.5 MW turbines and several technological innovations aimed at reducing costs and environmental impacts.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2016**: The Blauwwind consortium, which includes Van Oord, is awarded the concession for the Borssele III and IV sites, marking the beginning of significant investment in the offshore wind sector.
- **February 2021**: Van Oord sells a 10% stake in the Blauwwind consortium to Luxcara, indicating a strategic move to recycle capital for future projects.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2018**: Announcement of the Borssele 1 and 2 wind farm project, with commissioning scheduled for the end of 2020.
- **November 2019**: Construction phase begins for the Borssele III & IV offshore wind farm.
- **January 2021**: Official commissioning of the Borssele III & IV wind farm on January 6, 2021.
- **February 2021**: Completion of the Borssele 5 offshore wind farm, designated as an innovation site.
- **May 2021**: Certification awarded to Borssele III & IV, allowing the facility to start operations.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2021**: The successful completion of the Borssele III & IV wind farm construction is noted to have occurred without significant job losses, despite challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic. The project is expected to create jobs in the renewable energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2021**: Van Oord holds a 10% stake in the Blauwwind consortium, which is a joint venture with other partners including Shell, Partners Group, Eneco, and DGE. This indicates that Van Oord does not have exclusive ownership of the facility but is a significant partner in the consortium. 

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Van Oord facility in Borssele, NLD, focusing on its technology, operations, investments, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.

### Technology and Operations
- **Contrasting Production Capacities**: The Borssele III & IV offshore wind farm has a production capacity of 731.5 MW, while the Borssele 1 and 2 wind farm has a capacity of 752 MW.
- **Innovations**: The Borssele 5 offshore wind farm features two Vestas 9.5 MW turbines and includes several technological advances such as a submerged slip joint, which is the first of its kind used on a full-sized offshore wind turbine on a commercial basis.

### Investment and Development
- **Ownership and Stake Changes**: Van Oord sold a 10% stake in the Blauwwind consortium to Luxcara, indicating a shift in ownership structure and investment strategy.

### Project Status and Milestones
- **Commissioning Dates**: Borssele III & IV was officially commissioned on January 6, 2021, while the construction of the Borssele 1 and 2 wind farm was scheduled for completion by the end of 2020.
- **Construction Start Dates**: Construction for Borssele III & IV started in the second half of 2019, while the Borssele 1 and 2 project had its commissioning scheduled for the end of 2020.

### Labour
- **Job Creation**: The articles do not provide specific figures on job creation or loss related to the expansions or investments at the Borssele facilities.

### Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture Details**: The Borssele III & IV wind farm is developed by the Blauwwind consortium, which includes Shell, Partners Group, Eneco, DGE, and Van Oord, indicating a joint venture structure. In contrast, the Borssele 5 project is developed under the Two Towers flag, which also involves Van Oord but with a different partnership structure.
