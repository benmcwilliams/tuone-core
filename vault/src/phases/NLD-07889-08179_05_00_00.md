### Evolutionary Timeline of the Westinghouse Facility in Borssele, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2021**: The Dutch government placed nuclear power at the heart of its climate and energy policy, indicating plans for two new reactors at Borssele with a capacity of 1000-1650 MWe each. These reactors are expected to contribute 9-13% of the Netherlands' electricity production by 2035.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2023**: The Dutch government announced that feasibility studies would be conducted to assess the technical and safety aspects of building new nuclear power stations at Borssele. This includes a contract with KHNP (Korea Hydro & Nuclear Power) to begin a feasibility study in January 2024, with contracts with Westinghouse and EDF to follow.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2023**: The feasibility study by KHNP is set to start in January 2024 and is expected to last at least six months. The first results of these studies are anticipated in the autumn of 2024. This marks a significant step towards the potential construction of new reactors at the Borssele site.
