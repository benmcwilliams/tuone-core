## NLD-07889-08690

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EPZ Facility in Borssele, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Production**: The Borssele facility operates a 485 MWe (net) pressurized water reactor, which has been in operation since 1973 and currently provides about 3% of the Netherlands' electricity.
- **Future Plans**: The Dutch government has designated the Borssele site as the preferred location for the construction of two new nuclear reactors, each with a capacity of 1000-1650 MWe, expected to be completed around 2035. Additionally, there are plans for the development of small modular reactors (SMRs).

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Funding Allocations**: 
  - In April 2023, the Dutch government allocated EUR320 million for nuclear energy development, which includes EUR10 million for extending the operation of the Borssele plant and EUR117 million for studies related to the construction of new reactors.
  - A further EUR65 million is earmarked for building the nuclear knowledge infrastructure and supporting the development of SMRs.
- **Future Financial Support**: The government anticipates cumulative support for new nuclear projects to reach EUR5 billion by 2030.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Current Status**: The Borssele plant is scheduled to close in 2033, but feasibility studies are underway to explore the possibility of extending its operational life.
- **Future Milestones**: The construction of two new reactors is planned for completion around 2035, with formal planning phases starting in 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **Workforce Development**: In January 2024, a joint initiative was announced to enhance the nuclear workforce by developing a new nuclear curriculum in collaboration with educational institutions. This aims to increase interest in careers within the nuclear sector, indicating a focus on job creation in the industry.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Current Ownership**: The Borssele plant is currently 70% owned by Zeeuwse Energie Houdstermaatschappij (ZEH), which is comprised of decentralized authorities in Zeeland, and 30% owned by German utility RWE.
- **Potential Changes**: The Dutch government is exploring the possibility of purchasing shares in the plant to gain decisive control over its extended operation, although no final decision has been made yet.

This timeline captures the key developments and future plans for the EPZ facility in Borssele, highlighting its role in the Netherlands' nuclear energy strategy and the ongoing efforts to enhance its operational capacity and workforce.
