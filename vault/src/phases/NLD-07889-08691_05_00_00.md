### Evolutionary Timeline of the EPZ Borssele Facility in Borssele, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **1973**: The EPZ Borssele facility, which consists of a single pressurized water reactor, began operations. It is the only nuclear power plant in the Netherlands, with a production capacity of 485 MW. As of 2022, it provided approximately 3.3% of the country's electricity.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07-06-2024**: The Dutch government announced plans to earmark €14 billion ($15.2 billion) for a nuclear power construction program, which includes funding for the Borssele facility. Currently, about €4.5 billion ($4.9 billion) is allocated for this program. The government is also studying the feasibility and safety of extending the operational life of the Borssele plant beyond its scheduled license expiration in 2033.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **07-06-2024**: Discussions are ongoing regarding the potential extension of the Borssele nuclear power plant's operational life beyond 2033. The government is considering purchasing a stake in the facility as part of these discussions.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The EPZ Borssele facility is 30% owned by the German utility RWE. The remaining 70% is owned by the Zeeuwse Energie Houdstermaatschappij, a public limited company that includes groups from the province of Zeeland and various municipalities within the province.
