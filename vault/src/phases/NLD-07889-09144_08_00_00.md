### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hitachi Energy-Petrofac Facility in Borssele, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2023**: Hitachi Energy-Petrofac was awarded a framework agreement for offshore grid contracts by TenneT, covering a total of 22GW of transmission capacity. This includes the development of high voltage direct current (HVDC) systems, specifically a world-first 2GW connection at a voltage level of 525kV. The projects will connect various offshore wind farms, including those in Borssele.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: The total value of the contracts awarded to the Hitachi Energy-Petrofac consortium is approximately €13 billion, which is part of a larger €23 billion investment for 11 contracts. Each project under this framework agreement is valued at over €2 billion, with costs split approximately equally between Hitachi Energy and Petrofac.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2023**: The first contract under the Hitachi Energy-Petrofac framework agreement was awarded for the Ijmuiden Ver Alpha project, with the second contract for Nederwiek 1 expected to be awarded later in the year. The projects are part of TenneT's plan to build 40GW of offshore transmission capacity, with commissioning expected between 2029 and 2031.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2023**: The contracts are expected to create more than 200 new positions across the UK, France, and Germany, including roles in the supply chain, although specific job creation details for the Borssele facility were not mentioned.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2023**: The facility is operated as a joint venture between Hitachi Energy and Petrofac, with both companies sharing the responsibilities and financial commitments for the projects awarded under the framework agreement.
