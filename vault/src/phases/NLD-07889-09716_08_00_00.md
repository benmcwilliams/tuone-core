### Evolutionary Timeline of the Partners Group Facility in Borssele, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2020**: The Borssele 3&4 project, developed by the Blauwwind consortium, began the installation of 77 MHI Vestas V164-9.5MW turbines. The total production capacity of the wind farm is 731.5 MW, capable of powering approximately 825,000 Dutch households.
- **January 2021**: The facility reached full commissioning on January 6, 2021, marking the completion of construction. The last turbine was installed in November 2020.
- **May 2021**: The facility received certification from Lloyd’s Register, allowing it to officially start operations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **No specific investment amounts or subsidies** were detailed in the articles, but the successful completion and commissioning of the facility indicate significant financial backing from the consortium partners, including Partners Group.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2020**: Installation of turbines commenced.
- **July 2020**: The connection to the Borssele Beta platform was completed, facilitating the transport of electricity to the onshore substation.
- **January 2021**: Official commissioning of the wind farm.
- **May 2021**: Certification was granted for the facility to enter operation.

#### 4. Labour
- **No specific job creation or job loss figures** were mentioned in the articles, but the successful construction and operation of the facility likely contributed to job creation within the renewable energy sector in the Netherlands.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Borssele 3&4 project is a joint venture under the Blauwwind consortium, which includes Partners Group (45%), Shell (20%), DGE (15%), Eneco Group (10%), and Van Oord (10%). Therefore, Partners Group does not own the facility exclusively but holds a significant stake in the joint venture.
