### Evolutionary Timeline of the BASF Facility in Hollandse Kust Zuid, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2021**: Siemens Gamesa commenced serial production of the SG 11.0-200 DD wind turbines for the 1.5 GW Hollandse Kust Zuid offshore wind farm at its facility in Cuxhaven, Germany. The project involves the production of 140 turbines, with the first five units having undergone pre-serial production in Brande, Denmark. The complete production chain is being monitored for quality assurance.

- **April 2022**: The installation and commissioning of the 140 Siemens Gamesa wind turbines began, with the first turbine installed by Cadeler’s installation vessel, Wind Osprey. The project is expected to be completed by summer 2023, marking Hollandse Kust Zuid as the largest operating offshore wind farm globally.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2021**: BASF acquired a 49.5% stake in the Hollandse Kust Zuid project, partnering with Vattenfall, who holds the development rights for the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2021**: Announcement of the start of serial production of the wind turbines for the Hollandse Kust Zuid project.
- **April 2022**: The first wind turbine was installed, marking a significant milestone in the construction of the offshore wind farm.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2022**: 8.2 Consulting AG was selected to oversee quality surveillance during the commissioning phase, indicating ongoing job creation related to the installation and commissioning of the wind turbines.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2021**: BASF holds a 49.5% stake in the Hollandse Kust Zuid project, indicating a joint venture with Vattenfall, who is the primary developer and operator of the project.
