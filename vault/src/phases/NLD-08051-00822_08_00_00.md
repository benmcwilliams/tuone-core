## NLD-08051-00822

### Evolutionary Timeline of Vattenfall's Hollandse Kust Zuid Facility, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04/2020**: Vattenfall announces the final investment decision for the Hollandse Kust Zuid offshore wind farm, which will have a total capacity of 1500 MW, featuring 140 Siemens Gamesa 11 MW turbines. The facility is expected to begin operations in 2023.
- **10/2020**: Trelleborg is contracted to supply 281 NjordGuard cable protection systems for the project, enhancing the operational efficiency and safety of the wind farm's power cables.
- **03/2021**: Vattenfall signs a long-term Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) with Air Liquide for 100 GWh of renewable electricity per year, starting in 2023.
- **06/2021**: Vattenfall sells a 49.5% stake in the project to BASF for €300 million, with BASF committing around €1.6 billion to the project.
- **08/2021**: Siemens Gamesa begins serial production of the SG 11.0-200 DD wind turbines for the project, with production taking place in Cuxhaven, Germany.
- **04/2022**: Installation and commissioning of the turbines commence, with the first turbine installed by Cadeler's Wind Osprey vessel.
- **06/2023**: The final turbine is installed, marking the completion of the wind farm, which is set to be the largest offshore wind farm in the world.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04/2020**: Vattenfall's investment decision follows the successful bid for two subsidy-free tenders in 2018 and 2019, which allowed the project to proceed without government subsidies.
- **06/2021**: The sale of a 49.5% stake to BASF involves a total commitment of around €1.6 billion, including funding for construction.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04/2020**: Final investment decision made; construction to start in 2021.
- **10/2020**: Contract awarded for cable protection systems.
- **03/2021**: First PPA signed for the project.
- **06/2021**: Stake sale to BASF completed; offshore construction begins in July 2021.
- **04/2022**: Installation of turbines begins.
- **06/2023**: Final turbine installed; project expected to be operational later in 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **04/2020**: Vattenfall emphasizes that the project will create significant economic activity and employment, particularly during economically uncertain times.
- **06/2021**: BASF's involvement is expected to support job creation at its Antwerp Verbund site and other European locations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **06/2021**: Vattenfall sells a 49.5% stake in the project to BASF, indicating a joint ownership structure for the Hollandse Kust Zuid facility, with Vattenfall retaining the majority stake.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones of the Hollandse Kust Zuid facility, highlighting its significance in the offshore wind sector and Vattenfall's commitment to renewable energy.
