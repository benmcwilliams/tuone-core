### Evolutionary Timeline of the Eneco Facility in Egmond aan Zee, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2020**: The CrossWind consortium, consisting of Shell and Eneco, is awarded the tender to develop the Hollandse Kust (noord) offshore wind project. The facility is designed to have an installed capacity of **759 MW**, generating at least **3.3 TWh** of electricity annually. The project is set to be operational by **2023**.
- **December 2022**: The project is confirmed to consist of **69 Siemens Gamesa SG 11.0-200 DD wind turbines**. The installation and commissioning are expected to be completed in the **second half of 2023**.
- **April 2023**: The first turbine is installed, marking a significant milestone in the construction process. The project is expected to generate its first power by the summer of 2023 and become fully operational by the end of the year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2020**: The project is developed **without government subsidies**, indicating a significant investment from the CrossWind consortium (Shell and Eneco) to support the construction and operation of the wind farm.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2020**: Announcement of the CrossWind consortium's selection for the Hollandse Kust (noord) project.
- **October 2022**: Offshore construction begins.
- **February 2023**: The last TP-less monopile is placed on the seabed.
- **April 2023**: The first Siemens Gamesa wind turbine is installed on its foundation.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is a **joint venture** between Shell and Eneco, known as the CrossWind consortium. There is no indication of exclusive ownership by either company.
