### Evolutionary Timeline of the CrossWind Facility in Egmond aan Zee, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2020**: The CrossWind consortium, consisting of Shell and Eneco, announced the Hollandse Kust (Noord) offshore wind project, which will have an installed capacity of **759 MW** and is expected to generate at least **3.3 TWh** of clean energy annually. The project is located **18.5 kilometers** off the west coast of the Netherlands near Egmond aan Zee.
- **October 2022**: Construction commenced with the installation of the first monopile. The project will utilize **69 Siemens Gamesa SG 11.0-200 DD** wind turbines.
- **April 2023**: The first turbine was installed on **April 15**, marking a significant milestone in the construction process. The project is expected to be fully operational by the end of 2023.
- **December 2023**: Van Oord completed the installation of the entire wind farm, making it one of the fastest installed offshore wind sites.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2020**: CrossWind secured the rights to build and operate the Hollandse Kust (Noord) wind farm in a **zero-subsidy auction**, indicating a significant investment commitment without government subsidies.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2020**: Final Investment Decision (FID) was taken, allowing construction to begin.
- **October 2022**: Construction officially started with the installation of the first monopile.
- **April 2023**: The first wind turbine was installed, with expectations to generate power by summer 2023.
- **December 2023**: The installation of the 759 MW wind farm was completed.
