### Evolutionary Timeline of the Vattenfall Facility in IJmuiden Ver Beta, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/2024**: The IJmuiden Ver Beta project, a joint venture between Vattenfall and Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (CIP), is set to comprise a 2 GW offshore wind farm and a 50 MW floating offshore solar farm located 62 kilometers off the west coast of the Netherlands. The project will also include a large-scale electrolyser plant at the Port of Rotterdam with a capacity of 1 GW, designed to convert electricity generated from the offshore wind and solar farm into green hydrogen.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12/2024**: The project is being developed without subsidies, indicating a significant investment from the joint venture partners. Google has signed a power purchase agreement (PPA) for 250 MW of energy capacity from the Zeevonk project, which is part of the IJmuiden Ver Beta initiative, further highlighting the financial backing and commitment to the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **06/2024**: The IJmuiden Ver Beta project was awarded as part of the largest Dutch offshore wind tender, with the Zeevonk joint venture (Vattenfall and CIP) securing the 2 GW area. The project is expected to be operational by 2029.

#### 4. Labour
- **12/2024**: While specific job creation numbers have not been detailed in the articles, the scale of the project, including the offshore wind farm, floating solar farm, and electrolyser plant, suggests significant job opportunities in construction, operation, and maintenance phases.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **12/2024**: The facility is a joint venture between Vattenfall and Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (CIP), indicating that Vattenfall does not own the facility exclusively but shares ownership with CIP.
