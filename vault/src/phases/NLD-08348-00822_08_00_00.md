### Evolutionary Timeline of the Vattenfall Facility in Zeevonk, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2024**: The Zeevonk project is a joint venture between Vattenfall and Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (CIP). It will consist of a **2GW offshore wind farm** and a **50MW floating offshore solar farm**, located **62 km off the Dutch coast**. The project is designed to integrate offshore wind, solar energy, and green hydrogen at scale. A significant portion of the electricity generated will be converted into green hydrogen at a large-scale electrolyser plant at the port of Rotterdam. The project is expected to be operational by **2029**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2024**: Google enters a **250MW Power Purchase Agreement (PPA)** with CIP for the Zeevonk project, committing to purchase wind power generated at the facility for **15 years**. This agreement is part of a broader strategy to achieve **24/7 Carbon Free Energy (CFE)** by **2030**.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2024**: The announcement of the partnership with Google and the details of the project are made public, marking a significant milestone in the development of the Zeevonk project.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2024**: The project is expected to drive local growth and job creation, as stated by Felix Pahl, partner at CIP, emphasizing the project's potential to provide clean energy and deliver attractive returns to investors.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2024**: The Zeevonk project is a **joint venture** between Vattenfall and CIP, indicating that Vattenfall does not own the facility exclusively but shares ownership with CIP.
