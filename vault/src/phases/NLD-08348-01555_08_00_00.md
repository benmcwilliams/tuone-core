### Evolutionary Timeline of the Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners Facility in Zeevonk, NLD

**1. Technology and Operations:**
- **December 2024:** The Zeevonk project is a joint venture between Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (CIP) and Vattenfall. It will consist of a **2GW offshore wind farm** and a **50MW floating offshore solar farm**, located 62 km off the Dutch coast. A significant portion of the electricity generated will be converted into **green hydrogen** at a large-scale electrolyser plant in Rotterdam. The project is designed to integrate offshore wind, solar energy, and green hydrogen.

**2. Investment and Development:**
- **December 2024:** CIP announced a **250MW Power Purchase Agreement (PPA)** with Google for the Zeevonk project, which will allow Google to purchase wind power generated at the facility for its Dutch operations over a period of **15 years**. This partnership is part of a broader strategy to support Google's ambition to operate on **24/7 Carbon Free Energy (CFE)** by 2030.

**3. Project Status and Milestones:**
- **December 2024:** The Zeevonk project is expected to be operational by **2029**. The announcement of the PPA with Google marks a significant milestone in the project's development, highlighting its subsidy-free, GW-scale nature.

**4. Labour:**
- **December 2024:** Felix Pahl, a partner at CIP, indicated that the development of the Zeevonk project is expected to drive **local growth and jobs**, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the announcement.

**5. Ownership Structure:**
- **December 2024:** The Zeevonk project is a **joint venture** between Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (CIP) and Vattenfall, indicating that it is not solely owned by CIP but rather shared with Vattenfall.
