### Evolutionary Timeline of the Neptune Energy Facility in Scheveningen, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2021**: The PosHYdon project was announced, involving the installation of a 1MW electrolyser on the Neptune Energy-operated Q13a-A platform. This platform is notable for being the first fully electrified platform in the Dutch North Sea. The electrolyser is expected to produce a maximum of 400kg of green hydrogen per day, utilizing electricity generated from offshore wind to convert seawater into hydrogen via electrolysis.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2021**: The PosHYdon project secured a grant of €3.6 million from the Dutch government through the Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO). This funding is aimed at enabling the consortium to initiate all activities for the pilot project. The remaining budget for the project will be funded by the consortium partners, which include DEME Offshore, Eneco, and others.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2021**: The announcement of the PosHYdon project marks a significant milestone as it is set to be the world’s first offshore green hydrogen pilot. The project aims to validate the integration of offshore wind, gas, and hydrogen energy systems in the Dutch North Sea.
