### Evolutionary Timeline of the DEME Offshore Facility in Scheveningen, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2021**: The facility is involved in the PosHYdon project, which aims to develop the world’s first offshore green hydrogen pilot. This project will feature the installation of a 1MW electrolyser on the Neptune Energy-operated Q13a-A platform, located approximately 13 km off the coast of Scheveningen. The electrolyser is expected to produce a maximum of 400kg of green hydrogen per day. The project seeks to validate the integration of offshore wind, gas, and hydrogen energy systems.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2021**: The PosHYdon project secured a grant of €3.6 million from the Dutch government through the Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO). This funding is aimed at enabling the consortium to initiate all activities for the pilot project. The remaining budget will be funded by the consortium partners, which include DEME Offshore and Eneco.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2021**: The announcement of the PosHYdon project marks a significant milestone, as it is the first offshore green hydrogen pilot project. The project is set to begin installation activities on the Q13a-A platform, which is noted for being the first fully electrified platform in the Dutch North Sea.
