### Hatenboer Facility Evolution Timeline - Scheveningen, NLD

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** July 2021
   - **Details:** The Hatenboer facility is part of the PosHYdon project, which involves the installation of a 1MW electrolyser on the Neptune Energy-operated Q13a-A platform, located approximately 13 km off the coast of Scheveningen. The electrolyser is designed to produce a maximum of 400 kg of green hydrogen per day by converting seawater into demineralised water and then into hydrogen via electrolysis. The project aims to validate the integration of offshore wind, gas, and hydrogen energy systems.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** July 2021
   - **Details:** The PosHYdon project secured a grant of €3.6 million from the Dutch government through the Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO). The remaining budget for the project will be funded by the consortium partners, which include Hatenboer and other companies.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** July 2021
   - **Details:** The PosHYdon project was officially announced, and the consortium partners began preparations for the pilot project. The project is noted as the world’s first offshore green hydrogen pilot, marking a significant milestone in offshore energy production.

4. **Labour:**

   - **Details:** The article does not specify exact job creation or job loss figures related to the PosHYdon project. However, the development of the facility and the pilot project is expected to contribute to job creation in the renewable energy sector.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** July 2021
   - **Details:** The facility is part of a consortium that includes multiple partners, indicating that Hatenboer does not own the facility exclusively. The consortium comprises various companies, including Nel Hydrogen, InVesta, IV-Offshore & Energy, Emerson Automation Solutions, and others, working collaboratively on the PosHYdon project.
