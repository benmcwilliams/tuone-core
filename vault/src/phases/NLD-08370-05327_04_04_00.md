### Evolutionary Timeline of the IV-Offshore & Energy Facility in Scheveningen, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2021**: The PosHYdon project is announced, involving the installation of a 1MW electrolyser on the Neptune Energy-operated Q13a-A platform, approximately 13 km off the coast of Scheveningen. The facility aims to produce green hydrogen by using electricity generated from offshore wind to convert seawater into hydrogen via electrolysis. The electrolyser is expected to produce a maximum of 400 kg of green hydrogen per day.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2021**: The project secures a grant of €3.6 million from the Dutch government through the Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO) to support the development of the PosHYdon pilot. The remaining budget will be funded by consortium partners, which include IV-Offshore & Energy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2021**: The PosHYdon project is officially announced, marking the start of activities for the pilot project. The consortium partners are identified, and the project is set to validate the integration of offshore wind, gas, and hydrogen energy systems.
