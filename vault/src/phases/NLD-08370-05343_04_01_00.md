### Evolutionary Timeline of the InVesta Facility in Scheveningen, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2021**: The InVesta facility is part of the PosHYdon project, which involves the installation of a 1MW electrolyser on the Neptune Energy-operated Q13a-A platform, located approximately 13 km off the coast of Scheveningen. The electrolyser is designed to produce a maximum of 400kg of green hydrogen per day by converting seawater into demineralised water and then into hydrogen via electrolysis. The project aims to validate the integration of offshore wind, gas, and hydrogen energy systems.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2021**: The PosHYdon project secured a grant of €3.6 million from the Dutch government through the Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO) to support the pilot project. The remaining budget will be funded by the consortium partners, which include InVesta.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2021**: The PosHYdon project was officially announced, marking the start of activities for the pilot installation on the Q13a-A platform. This project is noted as the world’s first offshore green hydrogen pilot.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The InVesta facility is part of a consortium for the PosHYdon project, which includes multiple partners such as Nel Hydrogen, Hatenboer, IV-Offshore & Energy, and Emerson Automation Solutions. The ownership structure is not exclusively held by InVesta, indicating a collaborative approach among various stakeholders in the project.
