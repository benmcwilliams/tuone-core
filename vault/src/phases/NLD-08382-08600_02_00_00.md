### Evolutionary Timeline of Daldrup & Söhne AG Facility in Luttelgeest, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2020**: Daldrup & Söhne AG successfully drilled the first well of a triplet for the geothermal heating plant project in Luttelgeest. The well reached a vertical drilling depth of approximately 1,800 meters, with an initial production volume exceeding the expected yield of 100 liters per second. The temperature of the deep water was recorded at 76 degrees Celsius, which is above the forecasted temperature. This project is notable for the installation of a protective pipe string, which is expected to become a new standard in Dutch geothermal projects.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2020**: The project is being constructed on a turnkey basis for Nature's Heat and ACL, under an EPC (Engineering Procurement Construction) contract. The contract includes integrated coverage of exploration risks through Daldrup's own ART (Alternative Risk Transfer) structure, indicating a strategic investment in the geothermal sector in the Netherlands.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2020**: Drilling of the second well is scheduled to begin, with a total of three wells planned for the project. The completion of the heating system and heat distribution network is expected by spring 2021.
