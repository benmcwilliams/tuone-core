### Evolutionary Timeline of the Solarcentury Facility in Vlagtwedde, Netherlands

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** September 2019
   - **Details:** Solarcentury, in collaboration with PowerField, announced plans to build the Vlagtwedde solar farm, which will have a production capacity of 110 MW. The facility is expected to generate approximately 104 GWh of electricity annually and will consist of 350,000 solar panels spread over 100 hectares. The in-service date for the facility is projected to be late 2020.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** September 2019
   - **Details:** The project is backed by Impax, a British asset manager that owns the site. Specific investment amounts were not disclosed in the article, but the project is significant as it represents the largest solar initiative in the Netherlands at the time.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** September 2019
   - **Details:** The announcement of the project marks a key milestone, with plans for construction and operation set to commence, aiming for an in-service date in late 2020.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** September 2019
   - **Details:** The project includes the establishment of a 24-hectare blueberry farm adjacent to the solar facility, which is intended to provide employment opportunities for the local population, alongside the jobs created by the solar farm itself.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** September 2019
   - **Details:** The facility is not exclusively owned by Solarcentury; it is a collaborative project involving Solarcentury, PowerField, and Impax, indicating a joint venture structure for the ownership of the facility.
