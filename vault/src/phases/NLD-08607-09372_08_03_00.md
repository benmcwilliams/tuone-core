### Evolutionary Timeline of the Lagerwey Facility in Barneveld, NLD

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **January 2018:** Lagerwey announced plans to fast-track its next-generation onshore wind turbine platform following its 100% acquisition by Enercon. The new model, an up to 5MW IEC III class LP4 with 158 or 160-metre rotors, was expected to reach the market in autumn 2018. The facility was also engaged in producing the existing LP4 offering, the IEC II class L136 4.5MW unit.
   - The Barneveld plant was busy building components for 60 L100 2.5MW turbines for the 150MW Adygea 1, 2, and 3 complex in Russia, under a joint venture with NovaWind.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The acquisition by Enercon was a significant investment that enabled Lagerwey to enhance its production capabilities and market presence. The financial backing from Enercon was expected to facilitate the launch of the LP4 platform and support the production of existing models.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **January 2018:** The takeover by Enercon was approved by Lagerwey shareholders on January 1, 2018. The Barneveld facility was set to ramp up production to meet the order for the Russian wind farm project, with the first shipment of pre-assembled nacelle and hub kits scheduled for June 2018.

4. **Labour:**
   - Following the acquisition and the ramp-up in production, Lagerwey planned to hire between 20 and 40 new staff, primarily in assembly and procurement, to support its existing workforce of 120 employees.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - Lagerwey became a wholly-owned subsidiary of Enercon following the acquisition, which was finalized on January 1, 2018. The facility operates as a standalone entity but is expected to benefit from closer cooperation with Enercon in sales and procurement.
