### Evolutionary Timeline of F&S Solar Facility at Schiphol Airport, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2018**: F&S Solar finalized the installation of a **15 MW solar facility** at Amsterdam's Schiphol Airport. The facility was developed under the Netherlands’ SDE+ program for large-scale renewables. The solar park is designed to produce approximately **1,050 kWh per installed kW per year**, which is considered high for northwest European conditions. The facility utilizes **125,000 thin film modules** known for their high efficiency and favorable low-light performance.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2018**: The project was developed with the involvement of **Blue Elephant Energy**, a Hamburg-based investor, and a local company. The development was supported by the **SDE+ program**, which provides incentives for commercial and utility-scale renewable energy projects.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2018**: The solar park was officially completed and operational. The planning work for the project began in **autumn 2016**, indicating a significant lead time for development and regulatory compliance, particularly concerning the orientation of solar modules to prevent glare for pilots.
