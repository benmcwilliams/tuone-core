### Evolutionary Timeline of the GE Facility in Maasvlakte-Rotterdam, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2019**: GE Renewable Energy, in collaboration with GMB Civiel BV, selected Royal HaskoningDHV to design the foundation for the Haliade-X 12 MW wind turbine, which is described as the world’s most powerful offshore wind turbine. The prototype is set to be installed onshore in Maasvlakte-Rotterdam to facilitate testing and data collection.
- **December 2019**: The Haliade-X 12 MW prototype was successfully installed at the port of Maasvlakte-Rotterdam. The turbine has a production capacity of 12 MW, stands 260 meters tall, and features blades that are 107 meters long. It produced 262 megawatt hours of energy in a 24-hour period, enough to power 30,000 households.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2019**: The energy produced by the Haliade-X prototype is being sold by Future Wind, a joint venture between Pondera Development and SIF Holding Netherlands. This indicates a significant investment in the infrastructure and technology associated with the Haliade-X project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2019**: Announcement of the agreement between GE Renewable Energy and Future Wind for the installation of the Haliade-X 12 MW prototype in Maasvlakte-Rotterdam.
- **December 2019**: The prototype installation was completed, marking a significant milestone in the project timeline. The turbine generated its first kilowatt hour in November 2019, and the commercial production is aimed to start in 2021.
