### Evolutionary Timeline of the GE Facility in Rotterdam-Maasvlakte, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2019**: GE Renewable Energy (GERE) announced the rollout of the first 12MW Haliade-X nacelle from its factory in Saint-Nazaire, France. This nacelle is intended for a prototype being built at the Rotterdam-Maasvlakte facility. The Haliade-X is designed to generate 67GWh of power annually and features a 220-meter-diameter rotor.
- **November 2019**: The Haliade-X offshore wind turbine installed in Rotterdam-Maasvlakte produced its first kilowatt-hour of clean power, marking the start of its testing phase to obtain type certification in 2020. This achievement was highlighted as a critical milestone for the team involved.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2019**: GE is investing close to $400 million in the development of the Haliade-X platform, which is crucial for its competitiveness in the offshore wind market.
- **November 2019**: The successful completion of the Haliade-X project was attributed to the support from various entities, including the City of Rotterdam and the Port of Rotterdam, indicating a collaborative investment environment.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2019**: Announcement of the Haliade-X nacelle rollout and its shipment to Rotterdam-Maasvlakte for prototype construction.
- **November 2019**: The Haliade-X turbine began producing electricity and entered the testing phase, with plans to obtain type certification in 2020. This turbine is also set to be deployed in several major offshore wind projects in the US and UK.
