### Evolutionary Timeline of the GE Facility in Wieringerwerf, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2020**: A consortium comprising GE Renewable Energy, LM Wind Power, and TNO announced the construction of the largest wind turbine rotor test rig at LM Wind Power’s WMC Technology Center in Wieringerwerf. The facility is designed to test rotor blades under various conditions, verifying their strength and dynamic behavior under mechanical loads created by larger blades. The construction and commissioning of the test rig are expected to be completed by November 2020.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2020**: The construction of the rotor test rig is part of the three-year STRETCH project, which is partially funded by the Dutch Ministry of Economic Affairs. This funding is aimed at supporting the development of innovative rotor technology to facilitate the design of larger wind turbine rotors.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2020**: Announcement of the construction of the rotor test rig, with completion expected in November 2020. The project is part of a broader initiative to enhance the capabilities of wind turbine technology and support the energy transition.

#### 5. Ownership Structure

The articles indicate that LM Wind Power has plans to utilize the WMC test facilities for rotor hub testing for new GE turbines, as well as for blade testing and providing digital tools and research services to the wind energy industry.
