## NLD-09076-06411

### Evolutionary Timeline of the ib vogt Facility in Borsele, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2018**: ib vogt announced the construction of the Solarpark Scaldia, a solar plant with a production capacity of 54.5 MW, which is noted to be the largest solar plant in the Netherlands. The facility is designed to produce an average of 51,000 MWh of clean energy annually. The plant will utilize 140,000 solar modules arranged in an east-west orientation to optimize land use and energy generation.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2018**: The facility reached financial close on a €41 million (approximately US$47.7 million) debt facility to fund the construction of the solar plant. This financing was provided by HSH Nordbank and is part of the project’s overall financial structure.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2018**: ib vogt acquired the Solarpark Scaldia project from the developer Solarpark Zeeland.
- **May 2018**: Construction of the solar plant commenced.
- **Q3 2018**: The plant is expected to start producing energy, with a commissioning planned by October 2018. The output will be sold under a 17-year Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) with Engie.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify exact job creation or job loss figures related to the construction or operation of the facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- ib vogt owns the facility exclusively, as it is both the EPC contractor and the owner of the Solarpark Scaldia.
