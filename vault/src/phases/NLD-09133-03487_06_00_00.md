### Evolutionary Timeline of the Solarcentury Facility in Westerwolde, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2018**: Solarcentury, in partnership with PowerField, began developing the Vlagtwedde solar farm in Westerwolde, Groningen. The facility is designed to have a production capacity of **110 MW**, which will generate enough power for **30,000 homes** once operational. The site will feature **350,000 solar panels**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2017**: PowerField received an operating grant to develop the Vlagtwedde solar farm, indicating initial financial support for the project.
- **September 2019**: The project was confirmed to be constructed and maintained by Solarcentury, with no specific details on contract value or additional subsidies mentioned in the announcement.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2018**: Announcement of the partnership between Solarcentury and PowerField to develop the solar farm.
- **September 2019**: A deal was signed for the construction of the 110 MW solar park, with an expected operational date in the **second half of 2020**. The facility is projected to produce **104 GWh** of clean energy annually.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2019**: The project includes the planting of a **24ha blueberry farm** as part of the landscape integration, which is expected to provide local employment opportunities in addition to the jobs created by the solar farm itself.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2019**: The facility will be owned by a partnership that includes Solarcentury, PowerField, and London-based asset management firm Impax. This indicates a **joint venture** ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by Solarcentury.
