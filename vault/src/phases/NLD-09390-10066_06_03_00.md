### Evolutionary Timeline of the Solland Solar Facility in Heerlen, Netherlands

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** August 2015
   - **Details:** Solland Solar, a solar cell producer, had a production capacity of 180MW per annum. The company announced plans to expand its capacity to over 300MW as part of a supply deal with Trina Solar, which included options for further expansion after July 2016.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** August 2015
   - **Details:** The expansion of production capacity from 180MW to more than 300MW was tied to a supply agreement with Trina Solar, which aimed to provide multicrystalline solar cells to circumvent US anti-dumping duties on imports from China. This expansion was expected to add 100 jobs at the facility.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** August 2015
   - **Details:** Solland Solar filed for bankruptcy protection, which raised doubts about its ability to fulfill the supply agreement with Trina Solar. A Dutch court appointed an insolvency administrator, and creditors were requested to file claims by the end of September 2015.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** August 2015
   - **Details:** The planned expansion of capacity was expected to create an additional 100 jobs at the solar cell plant.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** August 2015
   - **Details:** Solland Solar is a subsidiary of the Italian industrial firm Pufin Group. The ownership structure indicates that Pufin Group has control over the facility, but it is not exclusively owned by them as it is a subsidiary.
