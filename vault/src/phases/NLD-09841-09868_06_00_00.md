### Evolutionary Timeline of the Rijkswaterstaat Facility in De Slufter, NLD

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** April 2019
   - **Details:** The Rijkswaterstaat facility is planning to deploy a 100 MW floating solar photovoltaic (PV) project named "Zon op de Slufter" at the De Slufter dredging spoil depot, which spans 250 hectares. The floating solar installation aims to utilize the water surface area without disrupting the primary function of the basin, which is for spoil storage.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** April 2019
   - **Details:** The project is part of the Hernieuwbare energie op Rijks(waterstaat)-areaal program, which was launched by the Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO) and Rijkswaterstaat in March 2019. The program aims to make surfaces available for ground-mounted and floating PV projects.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** April 2019
   - **Details:** Rijkswaterstaat has initiated market consultation for the tendering of the 100 MW floating solar project. The ambition is to start the tender procedure for the project at the beginning of 2020, contingent on the results of a feasibility study that is currently underway.
