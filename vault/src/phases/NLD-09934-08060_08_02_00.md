### Evolutionary Timeline of Sif Facility in Maasvlakte 2, Netherlands

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2020**: Sif, in collaboration with Smulders, was awarded a contract to supply 190 monopiles and transition pieces for the Dogger Bank A and B offshore wind farm project, with a total capacity of 2400 MW. Sif is responsible for the fabrication and supply of monopiles and primary steel for transition pieces at its Maasvlakte 2 terminal.
- **May 2023**: Sif, in partnership with SSE Renewables and others, successfully demonstrated the first-ever electron beam welded section for offshore wind turbine monopile foundations, significantly enhancing production speed and reducing costs and emissions.
- **February 2023**: Sif made a final investment decision (FID) to expand its manufacturing facilities at Maasvlakte 2, with an investment of €328 million, aiming to increase total capacity to 500 kilotons per year and produce 200 monopiles annually.
- **August 2024**: The first production line of the expanded facilities was launched, with the first plates already welded and rolled.
- **November 2024**: The first monopile for the Empire Wind 1 project was completed at the new production line.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2023**: Sif announced a €328 million investment for the expansion of its Maasvlakte 2 facility, with €50 million in advance payments from Empire Offshore Wind.
- **January 2024**: Despite the cancellation of the Empire Wind 2 contract, Sif confirmed that financing for the expansion remained secured, with the advance payment for Empire Wind 2 converting into a perpetual bond.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2020**: Awarded contract for Dogger Bank A and B foundations, subject to financial close.
- **May 2023**: Successful demonstration of electron beam welding technology.
- **February 2023**: FID for facility expansion.
- **August 2024**: Launch of the first production line in the expanded facility.
- **November 2024**: Completion of the first monopile for Empire Wind 1.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2024**: The expansion of the facility is expected to create jobs, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.
