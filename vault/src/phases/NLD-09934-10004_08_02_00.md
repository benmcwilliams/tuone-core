### Evolutionary Timeline of Sif Netherlands Facility in Maasvlakte 2, NLD

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2020**: Sif Netherlands, in collaboration with Smulders Projects Belgium, was awarded a contract to manufacture all monopiles and transition pieces for the Dogger Bank A & B offshore wind farm project. Sif is responsible for the fabrication and supply of monopiles and primary steel for transition pieces, as well as marshalling all foundation components at its Maasvlakte 2 Rotterdam terminal. The project involves the production of 190 monopiles and transition pieces for a total capacity of 2.4 GW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2020**: The contract with Dogger Bank Wind Farm is subject to financial close, anticipated in late 2020. This contract represents a significant investment in the facility's operations and capacity, marking it as the largest award ever to Sif.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2020**: The announcement of the contract with Dogger Bank Wind Farm marks a significant milestone for Sif Netherlands, indicating a robust mid-term order pipeline.
- **May 2021**: Production of secondary steel and assembly of transition pieces is scheduled to begin at Smulders’ facilities in Poland and Belgium.
- **September 2021**: Assembly of fully equipped transition pieces is set to start at the Hoboken facility, expected to take approximately ten months.
- **2022-2024**: Foundations will be installed by OHT’s newbuild Alfa Lift.
- **2023**: The Haliade-X 13 MW turbines are scheduled to deliver the first electricity.
- **2026**: Full commissioning of the Dogger Bank Wind Farm is slated.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2020**: The contract and subsequent operations are expected to create job opportunities at the Maasvlakte 2 facility, although specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the article.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2020**: Sif Netherlands operates in a project-specific consortium with Smulders Projects Belgium for the Dogger Bank project. The ownership structure for the facility itself is not explicitly stated, but Sif is a key player in the consortium, indicating a collaborative operational model rather than exclusive ownership.
