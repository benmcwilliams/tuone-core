## NOR-00032-00540

### Evolutionary Timeline of Morrow Batteries Facility in Arendal, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **02-06-2020**: Morrow Batteries announces plans to build a battery cell factory for electric cars in Agder, Norway, with a final production capacity of 32 GWh, divided into four expansion stages of 8 GWh each, with the first stage expected to be completed in 2024.
- **22-05-2021**: The production capacity is revised upwards to 42 GWh, with the factory primarily focused on automotive and marine batteries.
- **27-10-2021**: Morrow Batteries continues to develop its plans for the factory, which is to be powered by hydropower and aims to produce sustainable batteries.
- **08-11-2022**: Morrow Batteries confirms that the factory is under construction and will have a capacity of up to 43 GWh by the end of 2028, with mass production of the first-generation product portfolio planned for 2024.
- **20-08-2024**: Morrow Batteries opens its gigafactory in Arendal, marking it as Europe’s first gigafactory for Lithium Iron Phosphate (LFP) batteries, with an initial production capacity of 1 GWh, aiming for commercial production by the end of the year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **29-12-2020**: Morrow Batteries announces an investment volume of five billion Norwegian crowns (approximately 470 million euros) for the factory, supported by EU Horizon 2020 funds.
- **06-05-2022**: Morrow Batteries secures €100 million in an investment round led by Siemens Financial Services and ABB, to finance the first phase of the factory, which will initially have a production capacity of over 1 GWh per year.
  
#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **29-12-2020**: Location confirmed for the factory at Eyde Energipark in Arendal, with construction scheduled to begin in 2023.
- **22-05-2021**: Construction is expected to start in 2023, with operations planned to begin in the fourth quarter of 2024.
- **12-08-2024**: Morrow Batteries officially opens its gigafactory in Arendal, with test production already started and commercial production expected to commence by the end of the year.

#### 4. Labour
- **29-12-2020**: The factory is expected to employ at least 2,000 workers.
- **02-05-2022**: Morrow Batteries employs more than 100 battery specialists as of this date, indicating job creation as the project progresses.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **02-06-2020**: Morrow Batteries is founded as a consortium involving Agder Energi, Bjørn Rune Gjelstens, and the environmental organization Bellona, indicating a joint ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership.
