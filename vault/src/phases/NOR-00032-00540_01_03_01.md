## NOR-00032-00540

### Evolutionary Timeline of Morrow Batteries Facility in Arendal, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2020**: Morrow Batteries announced plans to build a 32 GWh battery cell factory in Eyde Energipark, Arendal, primarily for the automotive and marine industries. The factory is expected to be the "world’s most sustainable battery factory."
- **May 2021**: The production capacity was revised to 42 GWh, with the first phase expected to be completed in 2024.
- **November 2022**: Morrow Batteries confirmed that the factory would be designed for a capacity of up to 43 GWh by the end of 2028, with operations scheduled to start in 2023.
- **August 2024**: The facility was inaugurated as Europe’s first gigafactory for Lithium Iron Phosphate (LFP) batteries, with an initial production capacity of 1 GWh, capable of producing up to three million battery cells.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2020**: The investment volume for the factory was estimated at five billion Norwegian kroner (approximately 470 million euros), supported by EU’s Horizon 2020 research program.
- **May 2022**: Morrow Batteries secured €100 million in an investment round led by Siemens Financial Services and ABB, aimed at financing the first phase of the factory.
  
#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2020**: Announcement of the factory's location and construction plans.
- **2023**: Construction was scheduled to begin.
- **August 2024**: The factory was officially opened, with test production already started and commercial production expected to commence by the end of the year.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2020**: The factory is expected to create at least 2,000 jobs in Arendal.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Morrow Batteries is a Norwegian company founded in 2020, with significant backing from Agder Energi and Gjelsten Holding, indicating a joint ownership structure involving multiple stakeholders. The company operates independently but collaborates with various partners for material supply and technology development.
