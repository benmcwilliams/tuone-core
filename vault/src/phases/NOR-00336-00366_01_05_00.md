### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hydrovolt Facility in Fredrikstad, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **11-02-2021**: Hydrovolt begins construction of a battery recycling plant in Fredrikstad, Norway, with an initial recycling capacity of 8,000 tonnes of batteries per year. The facility is designed to operate entirely with renewable energy and high automation. Plans are in place to process various types of batteries, including those from electric vehicles and the marine sector.
- **May 2022**: The Hydrovolt facility officially starts operations, with a processing capacity of approximately 12,000 tonnes of battery packs annually (equivalent to around 25,000 electric vehicle batteries). 
- **29-02-2024**: Hydrovolt announces its long-term goals to expand recycling capacity to 70,000 tonnes by 2025 and 300,000 tonnes by 2030, which corresponds to recycling around 150,000 and 500,000 electric vehicle batteries, respectively.
- **03-07-2024**: Hydrovolt announces plans to expand operations to France, with a new facility in Hordain expected to start operations in 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **11-02-2021**: Hydrovolt secures a joint investment of 120 million Norwegian kroner (approximately 11.7 million euros) from Northvolt and Norsk Hydro for the construction of the plant. Additionally, the Norwegian government agency Enova grants Hydrovolt 43.5 million Norwegian kroner (around 4.2 million euros) to support the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **11-02-2021**: Announcement of the construction of the battery recycling plant.
- **May 2022**: Official start of operations at the Hydrovolt facility in Fredrikstad.
- **29-02-2024**: Announcement of collaboration with Fortum for battery recycling and confirmation of operational capacity.
- **03-07-2024**: Announcement of plans to expand operations to France, with operations expected to start in 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2022**: The facility's operational start is expected to create jobs, although specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Hydrovolt is a joint venture between the Swedish battery manufacturer Northvolt and the Norwegian aluminium producer Norsk Hydro, indicating that the facility is not under exclusive ownership but rather a collaborative effort between the two companies.
