### Evolutionary Timeline of the Norsk Hydro Facility in Fredrikstad, Norway

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **11/2021:** Construction of a battery recycling plant commenced. The facility, operated by the joint venture Hydrovolt (between Norsk Hydro and Northvolt), has an initial recycling capacity of 8,000 tonnes of batteries per year. 
   - **Operational Capacity Update:** By the end of 2021, the plant became operational with a capacity to process approximately 12,000 tonnes of battery packs annually, equivalent to about 25,000 electric vehicle (EV) batteries. 
   - **Future Expansion Plans:** Hydrovolt aims to expand its recycling capacity to 70,000 tonnes by 2025 and 300,000 tonnes by 2030, targeting the recycling of 150,000 EV batteries in 2025 and 500,000 in 2030.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **11/2020:** The Norwegian government agency Enova granted Hydrovolt 43.5 million Norwegian kroner (approximately 4.2 million euros) to support the establishment of the facility.
   - **Joint Investment:** Hydrovolt was established with a joint investment of 120 million Norwegian kroner (around 11.7 million euros) from Norsk Hydro and Northvolt.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **11/2021:** The battery recycling plant in Fredrikstad was officially announced to be operational.
   - **Future Milestones:** Plans for future expansions to increase recycling capacity by 2025 and 2030 were outlined.
