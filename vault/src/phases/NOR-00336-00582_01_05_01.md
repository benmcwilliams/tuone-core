### Evolutionary Timeline of the Northvolt Facility in Fredrikstad, Norway

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Initial Production Capacity:** The facility, operated under the joint venture Hydrovolt, began with an initial recycling capacity of 8,000 tonnes of batteries per year upon construction start in November 2021.
   - **Current Production Capacity:** As of the latest update, the facility has increased its capacity to process approximately 12,000 tonnes of battery packs annually, which translates to about 25,000 electric vehicle (EV) batteries.
   - **Future Expansion Plans:** Hydrovolt aims to expand its recycling capacity to 70,000 tonnes by 2025 and 300,000 tonnes by 2030, targeting the recycling of 150,000 EV batteries in 2025 and 500,000 in 2030.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Joint Investment:** Hydrovolt was established as a joint venture between Northvolt and Norsk Hydro, with a total investment of 120 million Norwegian kroner (approximately 11.7 million euros) for the construction of the facility.
   - **Government Support:** The Norwegian government agency Enova provided a grant of 43.5 million Norwegian kroner (around 4.2 million euros) to support the project.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Construction Start:** Construction of the battery recycling plant commenced in November 2021.
   - **Operational Status:** The facility became operational at the end of 2021.
   - **Current Status:** As of the latest update, Hydrovolt is recognized as Europe’s largest recycling plant for electric vehicle batteries.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Job Creation:** The establishment of the Hydrovolt facility is expected to contribute to job creation in the region, although specific numbers related to job creation have not been detailed in the articles.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Joint Venture Ownership:** The facility is owned jointly by Northvolt and Norsk Hydro through the Hydrovolt joint venture. Neither company operates the facility directly; instead, the operation is managed by BatteriRetur, a Norwegian company that will supply the spent batteries for recycling.
