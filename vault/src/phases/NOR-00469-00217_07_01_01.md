## NOR-00469-00217

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Elkem Facility in Herøya, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2020**: Elkem announces plans for a large-scale battery materials plant at Herøya Industrial Park as part of the Northern Recharge project, aimed at producing graphite for lithium-ion battery anodes. The facility is expected to leverage hydropower to reduce CO2 emissions significantly.
- **April 2022**: Elkem's subsidiary Vianode is established to focus on battery materials, with plans for a pilot plant in Herøya capable of producing graphite for over 20,000 electric vehicles annually. A future large-scale plant could produce for over one million vehicles.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2020**: Elkem receives NOK 10 million (approximately EUR 917,900) from Enova for the initial planning of the Northern Recharge plant, marking a significant step towards a final investment decision.
- **April 2022**: The total investment for the pilot plant and preparations for a potential large-scale plant is estimated at around EUR 209 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2020**: Elkem selects Herøya Industrial Park for the potential battery materials plant and announces the construction of a pilot plant in Kristiansand.
- **October 2020**: Elkem progresses towards a final investment decision for the Northern Recharge project in 2021.
- **April 2022**: An investment decision for the Vianode pilot plant in Herøya is expected in the first half of 2022.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2022**: The pilot plant is expected to create around 100 jobs, with potential future expansion increasing employment to approximately 300 positions.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2022**: Elkem retains majority ownership of its subsidiary Vianode with a 40% stake, while Hydro and Altor each acquire a 30% stake, indicating a joint venture structure for the battery materials operations.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic decisions regarding the Elkem facility in Herøya, Norway, focusing on its evolution in technology, investment, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure.
