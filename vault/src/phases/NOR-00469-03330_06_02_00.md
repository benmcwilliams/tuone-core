## NOR-00469-03330

### Evolutionary Timeline of the REC Group Facility in Herøya, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2011**: REC Group operates a multicrystalline wafer production facility in Herøya with an annual capacity of **650 MW**. However, due to weak market conditions, the company announced a **temporary shutdown** of production capacity, affecting approximately **45%** of its wafer capacity in Norway, including the Herøya facility.
- **November 2011**: Production at the Herøya facility is reduced by **60%** due to ongoing weak pricing in the solar market.
- **January 2012**: The facility continues to operate at a reduced capacity, with expectations to produce approximately **105 MW** of multi- and monocrystalline wafers in Norway for the first quarter of 2012.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2016**: REC Group announces plans for **capital expenditure** to upgrade its manufacturing facility in Herøya, although specific investment amounts for Herøya are not detailed. The company is also investing in state-of-the-art equipment for its new block production facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2011**: REC terminates a long-term wafer sales contract and announces a temporary shutdown of production capacity in Norway.
- **October 2011**: The company considers permanent closures of its wafer facilities, including those in Herøya, due to significant negative cash flow.
- **January 2012**: The facility's production is temporarily reduced, and the company expects to produce significantly less than its full capacity.
- **October 2016**: REC Group announces organizational restructuring and job cuts but continues to invest in upgrades at the Herøya facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2011**: The potential closure of facilities, including Herøya, could affect around **700 employees**.
- **January 2012**: The temporary production reduction at Herøya does not specify job losses but indicates ongoing challenges in the workforce.
- **October 2016**: REC trims its global workforce by **3%**, affecting around **65 jobs** due to restructuring in response to market conditions.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2016**: REC Group is no longer a publicly listed company after being acquired by **Elkem Bluestar** in 2015, indicating a change in ownership structure. The facility in Herøya is now under the ownership of Elkem Bluestar through REC Group.

This timeline captures the key developments and changes at the REC Group facility in Herøya, Norway, focusing on technology, investment, project status, labor impacts, and ownership structure.
