### Evolutionary Timeline of the Vianode Facility in Herøya Industrial Park, Porsgrunn, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **11-05-2021**: Vianode is founded as a wholly-owned subsidiary of Elkem for the development and production of anode materials for electric car batteries. The facility aims to produce advanced, sustainable anode materials, targeting a reduction in emissions by over 90% compared to conventional production methods.
- **02-08-2021**: Vianode announces plans to rent an additional building at Herøya Industrial Park, increasing its production capacity for graphite used in electric car batteries from 55,000 tonnes to 60,000 tonnes per year. A "fast-track plant" is to be built to meet strong customer demand and shorten time to market.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **11-05-2021**: Vianode has received an initial funding of 10 million kroner (approximately 1 million euros) from the Norwegian government. The company indicates that further applications for funding related to the large-scale plant will be submitted.
- **22-05-2021**: Vianode is on the shortlist for a grant from the EU Innovation Fund, which would support its development efforts.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **11-05-2021**: The investment decision for a large production facility at Herøya Industrial Park is expected to be made before the end of 2021, with construction potentially starting shortly thereafter.
- **22-05-2021**: The cooperation with Morrow Batteries is established, which is expected to positively influence the decision regarding the plant's construction.

#### 4. Labour
- **No specific job creation or job loss details** are provided in the articles, but the expansion of production capacity and the establishment of a new facility typically imply potential job creation in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **11-05-2021**: Vianode is established as a wholly-owned subsidiary of Elkem, although the company is open to partnerships with external industrial and financial partners.
