### Evolutionary Timeline of the Vianode Facility in Kristiansand, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2022**: Vianode opened the Vianode Technology Center (VTC) in Kristiansand, focusing on research and development of sustainable battery materials, specifically graphite-containing anode materials. The facility aims to produce materials that can be manufactured more cheaply and with lower energy consumption. The current production capacity is not specified, but the facility is designed to support the development of materials for electric vehicles.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2022**: Vianode announced plans to invest approximately 2 billion Norwegian kroner (around €195 million) in an electric car battery materials factory in Norway, which will be located in Herøya.
- **March 2024**: Vianode received a grant of €30 million from Innovation Norway as part of the European battery cell production project (EuBatIn) to support the development of the anode material factory in Herøya. Additionally, Vianode had previously received €90 million from the EU for the same site.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2022**: The Vianode Technology Center (VTC) was officially opened in Kristiansand.
- **March 2024**: The anode material factory in Herøya is expected to be operational in the second half of 2024. The facility in Kristiansand is currently operating as a pilot plant and technology center.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2022**: The VTC started with 16 employees from nine nations, with plans to increase to 20 researchers by the end of 2022 and 25 full-time positions by the end of 2025.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Vianode is a subsidiary of the Norwegian metal producer Elkem, indicating that the facility is not owned exclusively by Vianode but is part of a larger corporate structure under Elkem.
