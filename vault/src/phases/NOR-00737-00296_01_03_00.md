### Evolutionary Timeline of the Freyr Facility in Mo i Rana, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2019**: Freyr announces plans for a battery cell factory in Mo i Rana with an initial production capacity of **32 GWh** per year, aiming to utilize lithium-ion technology for automotive, maritime, and stationary applications.
- **January 2021**: Freyr updates its production capacity goal to **40 GWh** by 2025, and plans to establish "fast-track production plants" with a capacity of **2 GWh** per year.
- **March 2021**: Freyr announces the construction of the **Customer Qualification Plant (CQP)**, which will produce sample cells and is scheduled to start operations in the second half of 2022.
- **June 2022**: The Freyr Board approves the construction of the **Giga Arctic** factory, designed for an annual capacity of **29 GWh**, with production expected to start in the first half of 2024.
- **July 2023**: Freyr begins semi-automated production of its first unit battery cells at the CQP, marking a significant milestone in its operational capabilities.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: Freyr receives a grant of **NKr 142 million** (approximately **$16.4 million**) from Norway's Ministry of Climate and Environment to support the pilot plant.
- **June 2022**: Freyr secures **$1.6 billion** in funding commitments for the Giga Arctic project, including **€400 million** from Eksfin, Norway’s export credit agency.
- **July 2023**: Freyr is awarded a **€100 million** grant from the EU's Innovation Fund to support the Giga Arctic project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2019**: Freyr announces plans for the Mo i Rana facility.
- **October 2020**: Freyr signs a Memorandum of Understanding with Siemens Energy for cell supply.
- **January 2021**: Freyr signs a licensing agreement with 24M Technologies for battery cell production.
- **March 2021**: Freyr receives government funding for the pilot plant.
- **July 2021**: Freyr makes the final investment decision for the CQP.
- **June 2022**: Freyr's Board approves the Giga Arctic factory.
- **January 2023**: Freyr announces a framework agreement with Impact Clean Power Technology for battery cell supply.
- **July 2023**: Freyr starts semi-automated production at the CQP.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2021**: The construction of the CQP and Giga Arctic is expected to create numerous jobs, although specific numbers are not detailed in the articles.
- **January 2023**: Freyr's agreements with customers indicate a growing demand for labor as production ramps up.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2019**: Freyr is identified as the sole owner of the facility, with plans for future joint ventures and partnerships, particularly with 24M Technologies and other suppliers for materials and technology.
