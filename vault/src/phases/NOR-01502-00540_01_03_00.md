### Morrow Batteries Facility Timeline - Agder, Norway

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 02-06-2020
   - **Details:** Morrow Batteries plans to build a battery cell factory for electric cars in Agder, Norway. The facility will have a final production capacity of 32 GWh, achieved through four planned expansion stages of 8 GWh each. The first stage is expected to be completed in 2024. Initially, the factory will produce battery cells based on existing technologies, with future plans to develop and industrialize next-generation lithium-sulfur battery cells.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 02-06-2020
   - **Details:** The project is supported by funds from the EU research programme Horizon 2020. Morrow Batteries aims to create a sustainable battery cell production industry, leveraging local hydroelectric power and waste materials from the Norwegian oil industry.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 02-06-2020
   - **Details:** The project is set to begin construction in 2021, with the first expansion stage expected to be completed in 2024.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 02-06-2020
   - **Details:** If Morrow Batteries captures 2.5% of the European battery cell market, it could create around 10,000 new jobs in Norway. The region is noted for having a skilled workforce due to its history with electrochemical companies.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 02-06-2020
   - **Details:** Morrow Batteries is co-owned by the Norwegian energy group Agder Energi, entrepreneur Bjørn Rune Gjelstens, and the environmental organization Bellona, among other partners. This indicates a joint ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by Morrow Batteries.
