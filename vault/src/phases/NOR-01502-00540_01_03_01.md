## NOR-01502-00540

### Evolutionary Timeline of Morrow Batteries Facility in Agder, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2020**: Morrow Batteries announces plans to build a battery cell factory for electric cars in Agder, with a final production capacity of 32 GWh, divided into four expansion stages of 8 GWh each. The first stage is expected to be completed in 2024.
- **May 2021**: The production capacity expectation is revised to 42 GWh in the final expansion stage.
- **May 2022**: Morrow Batteries orders LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate) cathode material for a pilot plant, indicating a focus on sustainable battery production.
- **November 2022**: Morrow Batteries signs a multi-year development agreement with Celgard for battery separators, confirming the factory's design for a capacity of up to 43 GWh by the end of 2028.
- **August 2024**: Morrow Batteries opens Europe’s first LFP gigafactory in Arendal, with an initial production capacity of 1 GWh, aiming to supply the European battery energy storage market.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2020**: Morrow Batteries announces an investment volume of 5 billion NOK (approximately 470 million euros) for the factory, supported by EU Horizon 2020 funds.
- **May 2022**: The company secures €100 million in investment from Siemens Financial Services, ABB, and other investors to develop the first phase of the factory.
- **August 2024**: The factory is inaugurated, with ongoing investments to enhance production capabilities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2020**: Initial announcement of the factory project.
- **December 2020**: Location confirmed at Eyde Energipark in Arendal, with construction scheduled to begin in 2023.
- **May 2021**: Announcement of a partnership with Vianode for anode materials.
- **October 2021**: Collaboration with Posco Chemical for cathode materials development.
- **May 2022**: Morrow Batteries begins construction of the factory.
- **August 2024**: Official opening of the gigafactory, with test production already initiated.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2020**: The factory is expected to employ at least 2,000 workers.
- **May 2022**: The company employs over 100 battery specialists, indicating job creation as the project progresses.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2020**: Morrow Batteries is founded as a joint venture involving Agder Energi, Bjørn Rune Gjelstens, and the environmental organization Bellona, among others. The ownership structure includes various Norwegian stakeholders and partners, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones of the Morrow Batteries facility in Agder, Norway, highlighting its technological advancements, investments, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
