### Evolutionary Timeline of the Statkraft Facility in Oslo, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09-08-2021**: The Mågeli hydroelectric power plant, operated by Statkraft, is set to supply Deutsche Bahn with almost 190 GWh of green electricity annually for a ten-year period starting in 2023. This contract is facilitated by the Nordlink North Sea cable, connecting the Norwegian and German power grids.

- **04-04-2023**: Statkraft enters into a long-term Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) with Fortescue Future Industries (FFI) to supply renewable power for FFI’s proposed Holmaneset green hydrogen and green ammonia project, which has a planned capacity of 300 MW. The project is currently in the feasibility phase.

- **09-01-2024**: Statkraft announces plans to invest up to $6.6 billion in hydro and wind power, aiming to double its current output from wind farms and add approximately 3 TWh of additional electricity output from hydroelectric plants.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09-01-2024**: Statkraft plans to invest between €1.8 billion and €3 billion on upgrades of hydroelectric plants, €1.2 billion to €2 billion for rehabilitating older dams and plants, and €1 billion for the renewal and construction of onshore wind farms.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09-08-2021**: Announcement of the contract with Deutsche Bahn for the supply of renewable energy starting in 2023.
  
- **04-04-2023**: Signing of the conditional PPA with FFI for the Holmaneset project, pending a positive Final Investment Decision.

- **09-01-2024**: Announcement of significant investment plans to enhance hydro and wind power capabilities.
