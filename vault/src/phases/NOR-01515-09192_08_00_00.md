### Evolutionary Timeline of IWS Fleet Facility in Oslo, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: IWS Fleet, in collaboration with IWS Services and ProCon Wind Energy, launched a novel one-stop Operations and Maintenance (O&M) concept for offshore wind farms. This concept integrates IWS Fleet’s new service vessels, supply chain management from IWS Services, and ProCon’s maintenance skills. The hybrid vessels are designed for zero-emission operations and feature the largest battery pack of 2200 kW-hr and solar charging capabilities. Each vessel can accommodate 120 technicians and is equipped with a motion-controlled crane for stable equipment transfer.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: The facility is under construction with four Commissioning, Service and Operations vessels (CSOV) being built at China Merchants Industry in Hong Kong. Two of these vessels are scheduled for operation in 2023, and the remaining two in 2025. Additionally, there are options for four further sister vessels.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: The new O&M concept was presented at the WindEurope conference held from April 25-27, 2023. This concept aims to provide a comprehensive service package for offshore wind farm operators, addressing the increasing complexity and size of offshore wind projects.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2023**: The introduction of the new O&M concept and the construction of the service vessels are expected to create job opportunities, particularly for skilled technicians who will operate and maintain the vessels and provide services to offshore wind farms.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2023**: Integrated Wind Solutions ASA (IWS) holds 100% ownership of IWS Fleet AS, indicating exclusive ownership of the facility. The companies involved in the O&M concept are majority owned by IWS, which is stocklisted in Oslo.
