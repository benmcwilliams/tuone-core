### Evolutionary Timeline of IWS Services Facility in Oslo, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: IWS Services, in collaboration with ProCon Wind Energy and IWS Fleet, launched a novel one-stop Operations and Maintenance (O&M) concept for offshore wind farms. This concept integrates service vessels, supply chain management, and maintenance skills into a comprehensive service package aimed at reducing costs and carbon footprints. The hybrid vessels feature advanced technology, including the largest battery pack of 2200 kW-hr and solar charging capabilities, designed for zero-emission operations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: The development of the O&M concept is supported by the expertise and resources of the involved companies, particularly IWS Fleet and IWS Services, which are majority owned by Integrated Wind Solutions (IWS). The investment details specific to this O&M concept were not disclosed, but the collaboration indicates significant resource allocation towards enhancing offshore wind maintenance services.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: The O&M concept was announced and set to be presented at the WindEurope conference from April 25-27, 2023. This marks a significant milestone in the facility's operational capabilities, showcasing their innovative approach to offshore wind maintenance.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2023**: The new O&M concept is expected to create job opportunities, as each hybrid vessel can accommodate 120 technicians, indicating potential job creation in the maintenance sector for offshore wind farms.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2023**: Integrated Wind Solutions ASA holds 100% ownership of IWS Fleet AS and 97% ownership of Green Ducklings A/S, while ProCon A/S is 75% owned by IWS. This indicates a strong ownership structure with IWS having significant control over the operations and strategic direction of the facility.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and operational strategies of the IWS Services facility in Oslo, Norway, highlighting its commitment to advancing offshore wind maintenance technology and services.
