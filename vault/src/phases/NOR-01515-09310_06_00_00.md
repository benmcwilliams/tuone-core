### KACO New Energy Facility Timeline - Oslo, Norway

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **June 2019:** KACO new energy's 1,500V inverters are integrated into a 2.5MW photovoltaic (PV) rooftop project near Oslo. This project is notable for being Norway's first 1,500V-based commercial and industrial (C&I) solar scheme. The use of 1,500V technology is expected to lower installation costs by 10-15% due to reduced cabling needs and less reliance on expensive electricians.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **June 2019:** Initial funding for the rooftop project was secured from the Norwegian government's Enova program to cover early expenses. No further state support was required after this initial funding.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **June 2019:** Construction of the 2.5MW rooftop PV unit is underway, with a completion target set for the end of summer 2019.

4. **Labour:**
   - **June 2019:** The project is expected to create jobs, particularly for the installation phase, although specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the article.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is part of a project involving KACO new energy's technology but does not specify exclusive ownership. The rooftop project is built over a cold storage facility owned by Login Eiendom, indicating a collaborative effort rather than full ownership by KACO new energy.
