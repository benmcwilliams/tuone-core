### Evolutionary Timeline of the REMA 1000 Facility in Oslo, Norway

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **June 2019:** A 2.5MW photovoltaic (PV) rooftop solar project is being constructed over a cold storage facility owned by Login Eiendom, which is used by retailer REMA 1000. This project is notable for being Norway's first commercial and industrial (C&I) scheme utilizing 1,500-volt inverters, which are expected to lower installation costs by 10-15% due to reduced cabling and labor requirements.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **June 2019:** Initial funding for the rooftop project was secured from the Norwegian government's Enova program to cover early expenses. No further state support has been required since then.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **June 2019:** Construction of the 2.5MW rooftop solar unit is underway, with an expected completion date by the end of summer 2019.

4. **Labour:**
   - **June 2019:** The use of 1,500V inverters is anticipated to reduce the need for expensive electricians due to less cabling, which may impact job requirements in the installation phase.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is owned by Login Eiendom, which operates the cold storage facility for REMA 1000. There is no indication of joint ownership or partnerships regarding the facility's solar project; thus, it can be inferred that REMA 1000 does not have exclusive ownership of the facility but is a tenant utilizing the space.
