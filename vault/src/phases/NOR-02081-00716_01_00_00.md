### Evolutionary Timeline of the Siemens Facility in West Mira, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2018**: Siemens, in collaboration with Seadrill and Northern Ocean, implemented the world’s first lithium-ion battery solution on the West Mira drilling rig. This facility operates as a sixth-generation, ultra-deep-water semi-submersible rig, utilizing a low-emissions hybrid (diesel-electric) power plant with lithium-ion storage technology. The rig is designed to operate in the Nova Field, approximately 120 km northwest of Bergen, Norway.
- **2020**: The West Mira rig features a hybrid power system with four converter-battery systems, totaling 6-MW power available for dynamic positioning (DP) from a total of 166-MWh installed batteries. The lithium-ion batteries are used to supply power during peak load times, serve as backup to prevent blackouts, and provide power to thrusters in case of machinery loss. The hybrid scheme is expected to reduce diesel engine runtime by 42%, leading to a 15% reduction in CO2 emissions and a 12% reduction in NOx emissions.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2020**: The development of the energy storage solution for the West Mira rig represents a significant investment in clean technology, aimed at reducing emissions in the offshore oil and gas sector. The project is part of a broader trend towards decarbonization in the industry, driven by new International Maritime Organization (IMO) directives.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2018**: Announcement of the implementation of the lithium-ion battery solution on the West Mira drilling rig.
- **2020**: The West Mira rig is recognized as the first rig to be awarded the DNV GL Battery (Power) Class Notation, allowing the batteries to be part of the DP power calculation.
