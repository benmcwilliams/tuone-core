### Evolutionary Timeline of the Equinor Facility in Trondheim, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2021**: Equinor, in partnership with Moss Maritime, announced plans to build a floating solar pilot plant measuring 80m x 80m off the coast of Frøya, near Trondheim. The facility will be the first of its kind situated in rough waters, designed to test how weather conditions affect the installation rather than focusing solely on energy production. The plant will be tested for a minimum of one year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2021**: The project has been in development for three years, indicating significant prior investment in research and development by both Equinor and Moss Maritime. The partnership aims to commercialize the floating solar technology through this pilot project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2021**: Equinor filed an application with the Norwegian Water Resources and Energy Directorate for the pilot plant. The construction is planned to start in late summer 2021, with the facility expected to be operational for testing for at least one year.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2021**: While specific job creation or loss figures were not mentioned, the project is expected to contribute to local employment through construction and operational phases, as indicated by the collaboration with local authorities and institutions.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2021**: The facility is a joint venture between Equinor and Moss Maritime, with Equinor leading the project. There is no indication of exclusive ownership, as the partnership is essential for the development and execution of the pilot plant.

This timeline captures the key developments related to the Equinor facility in Trondheim, focusing on the floating solar pilot project and its implications for technology and renewable energy research.
