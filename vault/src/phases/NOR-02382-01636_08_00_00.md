### Equinor Facility in North Sea, Norway: Evolutionary Timeline

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **11-10-2019**: Equinor ASA announced the Hywind Tampen project, which involves the installation of 11 floating wind turbines in the North Sea, with a total capacity of 88 megawatts (MW). This facility is designed to supply electricity to the Gullfaks and Snorre oil platforms, marking it as the largest floating wind power project in the world.
- **21-11-2022**: The Hywind Tampen facility began producing its first power, with expectations for more turbines to start generating energy by the end of the year. The project aims to meet approximately 35% of the annual power consumption of the Snorre and Gullfaks platforms, significantly reducing carbon dioxide emissions by over 200,000 tonnes annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **11-10-2019**: The total cost of the Hywind Tampen project is estimated at nearly NOK 5 billion (approximately $550 million). The Norwegian government agreed to subsidize up to NOK 2.3 billion, with additional funding from Norway’s NOx Fund amounting to NOK 566 million.
- **13-10-2019**: A final investment decision (FID) was taken for the project, confirming the financial commitments and the project's progression.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **11-10-2019**: Equinor made the final investment decision to proceed with the Hywind Tampen project.
- **21-11-2022**: The facility officially started producing power, marking a significant milestone as the first floating wind farm to power offshore oil and gas installations.
