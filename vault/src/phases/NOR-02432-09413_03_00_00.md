### Evolutionary Timeline of the Lyse Facility in Stavanger, Norway

1. **Technology and Operations**:
   - **Date**: October 2020
   - **Details**: The Lyse facility, in collaboration with Hydro, established a joint hydropower company named Lyse Kraft DA. This company has a normal annual power production capacity of 9.5 TWh. Hydro remains the operator of the assets, which includes Lyse’s fully owned hydropower plants.

2. **Investment and Development**:
   - **Date**: October 2020
   - **Details**: The merger creates a strategic partnership that enhances the operational capacity of both companies. Hydro's total operator responsibility for Norwegian hydropower and wind power increases to 13.6 TWh in a normal year, indicating a significant investment in the hydropower sector.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**:
   - **Date**: October 2020
   - **Details**: The establishment of Lyse Kraft DA marks a significant milestone in the collaboration between Lyse and Hydro, allowing for better resource management and operational efficiency.

4. **Labour**:
   - **Date**: October 2020
   - **Details**: As a result of the merger, thirty-four employees were transferred from Lyse to Hydro. This indicates a shift in workforce management due to the joint operational structure.

5. **Ownership Structure**:
   - **Date**: October 2020
   - **Details**: The ownership of Lyse Kraft DA is split, with Lyse holding 74.4% and Hydro holding 25.6%. This indicates a joint venture rather than exclusive ownership by either company.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and changes at the Lyse facility in Stavanger, Norway, as of October 2020.
