### Evolutionary Timeline of the Equinor Facility in Froya, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2021**: Equinor, in partnership with Moss Maritime (a unit of Saipem), announced plans to build an 80m x 80m floating solar pilot plant off the island of Froya. The plant will be the first of its kind situated in rough waters, with solar panels installed at a height of less than 3 meters above the water surface. The pilot plant is designed to be tested for a minimum of one year to assess how weather conditions affect its performance rather than focusing solely on energy production.
- **May 2021**: Researchers at Sintef tested a prototype of the floating structure intended for the offshore solar plant. The structure was designed to withstand harsh environmental conditions, with an anchoring system that allows it to cope with waves. The goal is to expand the pilot plant into a full-scale research and development facility.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2021**: Equinor announced its intent to enter the solar energy business in February 2017, launching a $197 million fund dedicated to renewables. The partnership with Moss Maritime is part of this broader strategy to develop floating solar technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2021**: Equinor filed an application with the Norwegian Water Resources and Energy Directorate for the floating solar pilot plant. The construction of the plant is set to begin in late summer 2021.
- **May 2021**: The floating structure design was tested in an artificial ocean basin, simulating real-time environmental conditions to ensure the design's viability for the pilot project.
