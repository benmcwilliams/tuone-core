### Evolutionary Timeline of the NorSun Facility in Årdal, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2023**: NorSun operates a solar wafer manufacturing facility in Årdal, Norway, with an annual production capacity of 1 GW. The company plans to expand this capacity by an additional 3 GW.
- **December 2024**: NorSun announces the discontinuation of its manufacturing operations in Årdal due to market challenges, including low-cost imports from China. The facility's production capacity is officially noted as 1 GW before closure.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2023**: NorSun secures NOK 90 million (approximately US$8.5 million) in equity financing to support its expansion plans, which include the construction of a 5 GW wafer manufacturing facility in the US and the expansion of its Årdal facility.
- **December 2024**: The company shifts focus entirely to its US manufacturing project, indicating a strategic pivot away from European operations.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2023**: NorSun announces plans to expand its Årdal facility and build a new 5 GW manufacturing plant in the US, with a final investment decision expected by the end of the year.
- **September 2023**: NorSun temporarily halts production at the Årdal facility and announces layoffs due to market pressures from low-priced Chinese solar modules.
- **December 2024**: NorSun officially closes its Årdal facility, marking the end of its operations in Norway to concentrate on the US project.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2023**: NorSun announces temporary layoffs at the Årdal facility due to production halts, although the specific number of affected employees is not disclosed.
- **December 2024**: The closure of the Årdal facility results in job losses, as the company ceases all operations in Norway.
