## NOR-03105-06175

### Evolutionary Timeline of TECO 2030 Facility in Narvik, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2022**: TECO 2030 signed a contract with ThyssenKrupp for the delivery of the first complete fuel-cell stack production line at the TECO 2030 Innovation Center in Narvik. The facility is set to be the first Gigafactory in Europe for Proton Exchange Membrane (PEM) fuel cells, with an initial production capacity of 120 MW in 2024, increasing to 400 MW in 2025, and targeting 1.6 GW by 2030.
  
#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2022**: The establishment of the facility is part of TECO 2030's broader strategy to develop zero-emission technology for the maritime and heavy industry, although specific investment amounts related to the facility's construction were not detailed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2022**: Announcement of the contract with ThyssenKrupp and the planned installation of the production line equipment in Narvik, with shipment expected in Q1 2024.
- **August 2023**: TECO 2030 confirmed the ongoing development of the facility, reiterating its production capacity goals.
- **December 2024**: TECO 2030 filed for bankruptcy of its Innovation Center subsidiary, which was managing the development of the 1.2 GW manufacturing facility. The bankruptcy was attributed to regulatory delays and missed payments to the landlord.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2024**: The bankruptcy filing indicated potential job losses associated with the closure of the Innovation Center, although specific numbers were not provided in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: TECO 2030 is a spinoff from TECO Maritime Group and operates as a clean tech company focused on developing PEM hydrogen fuel cell stacks and modules. The articles do not indicate any joint ventures or external ownership stakes in the facility, suggesting that TECO 2030 owns the facility exclusively.

### Summary
The TECO 2030 facility in Narvik, Norway, was established with ambitious production goals for PEM fuel cells, aiming to become a leading player in the clean technology sector. However, the project faced significant challenges, leading to a bankruptcy filing in late 2024, which jeopardized its operational future and potential job security for employees.
