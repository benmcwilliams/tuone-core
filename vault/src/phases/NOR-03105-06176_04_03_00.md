## NOR-03105-06176

### Evolutionary Timeline of the TECO 2030 ASA Facility in Narvik, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Focus**: TECO 2030 ASA plans to establish Norway’s first large-scale production facility for hydrogen-based fuel cells.
- **Production Capacity**: The facility will see a gradual increase in production capacity, although specific figures are not detailed in the article.
- **Facility Location**: The factory will be set up in a building that previously housed a plant operated by solar company REC.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Total Investment**: Up to NOK 1 billion (approximately US$116 million) is expected to be invested over a ten-year period.
- **Financing Discussions**: TECO 2030 ASA is in talks with the Norwegian public environmental body ENOVA and other institutions to explore financing options for the giga factory.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Announcement Date**: The project was announced on 12-03-2021.
- **Construction Start**: Work on the site is planned to start in 2021.
- **Production Start**: The first production is expected to commence in 2022.
- **Future Milestones**: TECO 2030 ASA aims to have 100 employees at the factory by the end of 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The project is expected to create up to 500 jobs in Narvik during the development, pilot production, and full-scale industrial production phases.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Facility Ownership**: The facility is leased from Kubera AS, which purchased the building from the state-owned SIVA - The Industrial Development Corporation of Norway. TECO 2030 ASA has a long-term lease agreement with a purchase option for the facility.
