### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fesil Facility in Holla, Norway

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 02-07-2010
   - **Details:** The Fesil facility in Holla produces silicon metal with a production capacity of approximately 50,000 metric tons annually. This capacity accounts for about one third of Wacker Chemie's yearly needs.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 02-07-2010
   - **Details:** Wacker Chemie signed a Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) for the acquisition of the Fesil silicon-metal production site, indicating a significant investment in securing long-term supply and operational independence.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 02-07-2010
   - **Details:** Wacker Chemie announced the signing of a PPA for the acquisition of the Fesil facility, which is subject to approval from Wacker’s supervisory board, Fesil’s board of directors, and antitrust authorities. The closing of the transaction is expected before the end of Q3 2010.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 02-07-2010
   - **Details:** Wacker plans to continue employing the existing workforce at the Holla site following the acquisition, indicating job retention rather than job loss.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 02-07-2010
   - **Details:** The acquisition will result in Wacker Chemie taking full ownership of the Fesil facility, as they plan to integrate it into their silicone value chain. Fesil will then focus on ferrosilicon metal production and other ventures.
