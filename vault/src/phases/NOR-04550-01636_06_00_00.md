### Evolutionary Timeline of the Equinor Facility in Frøya, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2021**: Equinor, in collaboration with Moss Maritime, announced the launch of a floating offshore solar power pilot project off the island of Frøya. The facility is designed to measure 80 meters by 80 meters and will be capable of producing up to 1MW of solar power. The pilot aims to test the technology in rough waters and analyze the effects of weather conditions on energy production.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2021**: Equinor awarded a contract to Nexans for the supply of a 5km power export cable to connect the floating solar platform to the land. This investment is part of the overall development of the pilot project, which is expected to contribute to the commercialization of floating solar technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2021**: Announcement of the pilot project, with plans to deploy it in late summer 2021.
- **July 2021**: Confirmation that the pilot project is scheduled to come online in December 2021, marking it as the world’s first floating solar plant operating in rough offshore waters.
