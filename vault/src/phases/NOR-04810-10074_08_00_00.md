## NOR-04810-10074

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Source Galileo Facility in Hammerfest, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2024**: The GoliatVIND project is set to be the world’s first demonstration plant featuring floating wind turbines and floaters over 15 MW. The facility will produce a total capacity of 75 MW, utilizing five 15 MW wind turbines installed on Odfjell Oceanwind’s Deepsea Star semi-submersible floating foundation. The project aims to establish Norwegian supplier capacity and reduce technical risks associated with floating offshore wind technology.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2024**: GoliatVIND is backed by a consortium including Odfjell Oceanwind, Source Galileo, Kansai Electric Power, and ENEOS Renewable Energy. The project is positioned as a key initiative to secure Norway's competitive edge in floating offshore wind, especially in light of the postponement of other projects like Utsira Nord.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2023**: GoliatVIND submitted a proposal for an Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) programme for the floating offshore wind project outside Hammerfest, Norway.
- **November 2024**: The EIA programme has been approved by the Ministry of Energy, paving the way for the submission of a licence application. The project is expected to commence construction in 2028/2029.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2024**: The project is anticipated to create jobs in the local area, particularly in establishing supplier capacity and supporting the development of floating offshore wind technology in Norway. However, specific job creation numbers have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2024**: The GoliatVIND project is a joint venture involving multiple stakeholders: Odfjell Oceanwind, Source Galileo, Kansai Electric Power, and ENEOS Renewable Energy. Source Galileo does not own the facility exclusively but is a key partner in the consortium.

This timeline captures the significant developments related to the Source Galileo facility in Hammerfest, Norway, focusing on the GoliatVIND project and its implications for the floating offshore wind sector.
