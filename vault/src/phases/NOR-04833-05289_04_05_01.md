### Evolutionary Timeline of the HydrogenPro AS Facility at Takasago Hydrogen Park, Japan

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2023**: An alkaline electrolyzer manufactured by HydrogenPro AS, with a hydrogen production capacity of 1,100 Nm³/h, was put into operation at Takasago Hydrogen Park. This facility is notable for being the world's first with integrated verification capabilities for hydrogen technologies, encompassing production, storage, and utilization.

- **April 2024**: Mitsubishi Heavy Industries (MHI) began operation of a 400 kW class Solid Oxide Electrolysis Cell (SOEC) test module at the facility. This module is designed to achieve an overall efficiency of 90%-HHV and is a significant advancement in high-efficiency hydrogen production technology.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2023**: The hydrogen storage area at Takasago Hydrogen Park was part of a project subsidized by Japan's New Energy and Industrial Technology Development Organization (NEDO), which includes a storage facility with a total capacity of 39,000 Nm³.

- **Future Plans (2024)**: MHI plans to expand the hydrogen storage facility's capacity to 120,000 Nm³, which is three times the current capacity, in preparation for further hydrogen utilization demonstrations.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2023**: The alkaline electrolyzer from HydrogenPro AS was officially put into operation.
  
- **November 2023**: MHI successfully conducted a demonstration operation of 30% hydrogen fuel co-firing at the T-Point 2 gas turbine combined cycle demonstration power plant using hydrogen produced and stored at the park.

- **2024**: MHI plans to conduct a 100% hydrogen-firing demonstration using a small- to mid-sized H-25 gas turbine.
