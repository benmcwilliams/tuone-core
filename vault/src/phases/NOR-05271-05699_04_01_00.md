### Evolutionary Timeline of the Norwegian Hydrogen Facility in Ålesund, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2023**: Norwegian Hydrogen announced plans to establish a green hydrogen production facility in Ålesund, Norway, with an expected annual production capacity of 40,000 tonnes of green hydrogen by 2030. This facility is part of the FjordH2 project, which aims to be one of the largest green hydrogen factories in the Nordic region.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2023**: Fortescue Metals Group invested A$13.5 million in Norwegian Hydrogen, acquiring a 12.5% ownership interest. This investment is aimed at scaling up the green hydrogen value chain and supporting Fortescue's proposed projects in Norway.
- **November 2023**: Norwegian Hydrogen raised approximately NOK 50 million ($4.4 million) from existing and new investors, valuing the company at around NOK 750 million ($66.7 million). This funding is part of their corporate development strategy to further capitalize on the green hydrogen sector.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2023**: Announcement of the Ålesund facility as part of the FjordH2 project, which is positioned to respond to the European Union’s REPowerEU Plan for diversified clean energy sources.
- **March 2023**: Norwegian Hydrogen established a subsidiary, Vireon, to construct hydrogen refueling stations for heavy-duty vehicles in various locations across Norway, Denmark, Sweden, and Finland.
