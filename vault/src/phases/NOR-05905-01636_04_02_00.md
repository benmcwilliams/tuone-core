### Evolutionary Timeline of the Equinor Facility in Easington, NOR

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **June 2023:** Equinor, in collaboration with Centrica Storage and Wood, is working on transforming the Easington gas processing terminal into a hub for low-carbon hydrogen production. The facility aims to integrate hydrogen production with the redevelopment of the Rough gas storage field and a hydrogen fuel switching project at the terminal. The goal is to establish over 1GW of green and blue low-carbon hydrogen capacity.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **June 2023:** The project is part of Centrica's broader strategy to achieve net zero by 2045. Specific investment amounts have not been disclosed, but the development of the Easington low-carbon hub is considered a key component of this strategy.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **June 2023:** The feasibility study for the Easington hydrogen hub is underway, with Wood evaluating development scenarios for both green and blue hydrogen production facilities. The project is in the design phase, following a cooperative agreement established between Centrica and Equinor in 2022.

4. **Labour:**
   - **June 2023:** While specific job creation numbers have not been mentioned, the project is expected to support local employment through the development of the hydrogen hub and associated infrastructure.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **June 2023:** The facility is not solely owned by Equinor; it is part of a cooperative agreement with Centrica Storage, indicating a joint venture or partnership structure for the development of the low-carbon hydrogen hub at Easington.
