### Norge Mining Facility Evolution Timeline - Skaland, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2024**: Norge Mining acquired the Skaland graphite plant, which is noted as Europe’s largest graphite mine. The facility is currently producing graphite, which is essential for battery production. However, the mine has only achieved a purity of 94%, which is below the required 99.9% for battery-grade graphite. Norge Mining aims to enhance the production process to reach the necessary purity levels.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2024**: Norge Mining purchased Skaland Graphite AS for $11.75 million (11.2 million euros). This acquisition is seen as a transformative milestone for the company, which previously focused on phosphate mining rather than graphite.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2024**: The acquisition of the Skaland facility was finalized, marking the beginning of Norge Mining's operations at the site. The company plans to target battery-grade graphite production and optimize operations in collaboration with the existing local team.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2024**: While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned, the acquisition implies a commitment to maintaining and potentially increasing local employment as Norge Mining plans to optimize operations and enhance production capabilities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2024**: Norge Mining now owns the Skaland facility exclusively, having purchased it from the previous owner, Mineral Commodities (MRC). This marks the company's first producing mine, indicating a shift in its operational focus.
