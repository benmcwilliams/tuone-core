### Evolutionary Timeline of the Equinor Facility in Mongstad, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2020**: The facility is involved in the development of hydrogen production, specifically through a new liquid hydrogen (LH2) production plant planned at Mongstad. This plant is part of the HySHIP project, which aims to establish a viable LH2 supply chain and bunkering platform. The facility is expected to support the operation of a new roll-on/roll-off demonstration ferry designed to run on LH2.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2020**: The HySHIP project, which includes the hydrogen production plant at Mongstad, received €8 million (approximately $9.4 million) in EU funding to further the development and commercialization of hydrogen as a carbon-neutral fuel.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2020**: Announcement of the HySHIP project, which includes the establishment of a hydrogen production plant at Mongstad. The project aims to create a supply chain for LH2 and is scheduled to become operational with the ferry by 2024.
- **01/2022**: Equinor unveils the Trollvind project, which aims to provide power for platforms across the Oseberg and Troll fields. This project is linked to the need for power for the hydrogen production facility at Mongstad and is targeting a final investment decision in 2023, with potential startup in 2027.

#### 4. Labour
- **01/2022**: The development of the hydrogen production facility and associated projects like Trollvind is expected to create jobs in the region, although specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **09/2020**: The hydrogen production plant at Mongstad is a collaborative effort involving Equinor, BKK, and Air Liquide, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by Equinor.
