## NOR-07972-09102

### Evolutionary Timeline of Halden Kjernekraft Facility, Halden, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2023**: Halden Kjernekraft was established to investigate the deployment of Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) in response to a power deficit in the Oslo, Akershus, and Østfold regions. The facility aims to explore the potential of SMRs as a solution to the energy needs of the municipality.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2023**: The initiative to investigate SMR deployment was driven by Halden municipality, which has a history of hosting nuclear reactors. Norsk Kjernekraft plans to prepare license applications in accordance with national regulations and international standards, indicating a commitment to structured development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2023**: The Halden municipal council made the decision to establish Halden Kjernekraft.
- **December 2024**: Amentum and Multiconsult were selected to evaluate the potential for SMR deployment, focusing on suppliers, technical standards, and environmental impacts. This marks a significant step in the planning and assessment phase for the proposed nuclear power plant.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2023**: The establishment of Halden Kjernekraft is expected to contribute to job creation, as indicated by local leaders who emphasize the long-term job benefits associated with nuclear reactor operations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2023**: Halden Kjernekraft is structured as a joint venture, with ownership distributed as follows: Halden municipality (20%), Norsk Kjernekraft (40%), and Østfold Energi (40%). This collaborative ownership model reflects a shared commitment to exploring nuclear energy solutions in the region. 

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic decisions regarding the Halden Kjernekraft facility, highlighting its focus on SMR technology and the collaborative efforts of its stakeholders.
