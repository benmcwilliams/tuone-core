## NOR-07972-09575

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Norsk Kjernekraft Facility in Halden, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **11/2023**: Norsk Kjernekraft, in collaboration with Halden municipality and Østfold Energi, announced plans to investigate the deployment of Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) at the Halden facility. The facility aims to explore the potential of SMRs to address the power deficit in the Oslo, Akershus, and Østfold regions, which currently stands at 16 TWh. The facility will focus on producing nuclear power, which is expected to supplement renewable energy sources in the long term.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **11/2023**: The initiative to investigate SMR deployment was prompted by the Halden municipality, which has a history of hosting nuclear reactors. Norsk Kjernekraft plans to prepare license applications in accordance with national regulations and international standards, indicating a commitment to secure financing through collaboration with capital-strong industries and financial players.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **11/2023**: The Halden municipal council made the decision to establish Halden Kjernekraft on November 9, 2023. The facility is in the initial phase of conducting investigations and surveys to inform future decisions regarding the deployment of SMRs.

#### 4. Labour
- **11/2023**: Norsk Kjernekraft's CEO, Jonny Hesthammer, emphasized that the project could contribute to long-term job creation in the region, leveraging the municipality's experience with nuclear reactor operations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **11/2023**: The Halden Kjernekraft facility is jointly owned, with Halden municipality holding a 20% stake, while Norsk Kjernekraft and Østfold Energi each own 40%. This indicates a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by Norsk Kjernekraft.

### Summary
The Norsk Kjernekraft facility in Halden is in the early stages of exploring the deployment of Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) to address regional power deficits. The facility is a joint venture involving Halden municipality, Norsk Kjernekraft, and Østfold Energi, with plans for future investments and job creation linked to the development of nuclear power in the area.
