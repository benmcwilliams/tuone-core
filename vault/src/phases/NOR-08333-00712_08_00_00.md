### Evolutionary Timeline of the Shell Facility in Bergen, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2022**: Shell, along with Equinor, Petoro, TotalEnergies, and ConocoPhillips, is involved in the Trollvind initiative, which aims to develop a 1GW floating offshore wind farm in the Troll oil field area, approximately 65km west of Bergen. The project is expected to generate 4.3 terawatt-hours of electricity annually starting in 2027. The wind farm will provide power to the Troll and Oseberg oil fields and the Kollsnes plant, addressing electricity shortages in the Bergen area.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2022**: The partners in the Trollvind project have indicated that the development will not require additional financial support, with estimates suggesting that the wind farm can deliver power at a competitive rate of less than Nkr 1 (€0.095) per kilowatt-hour. An investment decision for the project is anticipated in 2023.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2022**: The Trollvind project is in the evaluation phase, with a focus on commercial arrangements for selling power to existing oil and gas installations. The partners are working towards an investment decision expected in 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2022**: The project is expected to create opportunities for the supplier industry, providing predictability and a portfolio for offshore wind development, which may lead to job creation in the renewable energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2022**: The facility is part of a joint venture involving Shell, Equinor, Petoro, TotalEnergies, and ConocoPhillips, indicating that Shell does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a collaborative partnership.
