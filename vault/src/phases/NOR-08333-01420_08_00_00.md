### TotalEnergies Facility in Bergen, Norway - Evolutionary Timeline

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2022**: TotalEnergies, along with partners Equinor, Petoro, Shell, and ConocoPhillips, initiated a study for the Trollvind project, which aims to develop a 1GW floating offshore wind farm approximately 65km west of Bergen. The project is expected to generate 4.3 terawatt-hours of electricity annually starting in 2027. The wind farm will provide power to the Troll and Oseberg oil fields and the Kollsnes plant, addressing electricity shortages in the Bergen area.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2022**: The partners indicated that the Trollvind project would not require additional financial support and estimated that it could deliver power at a cost of less than Nkr 1 (€0.095) per kilowatt-hour. An investment decision regarding the project is anticipated in 2023.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2022**: The study for the Trollvind project was announced, with plans for a full-scale floating offshore wind farm. The project is positioned to support Norway's ambition to become a leader in offshore wind energy, leveraging expertise from the oil and gas sector.
