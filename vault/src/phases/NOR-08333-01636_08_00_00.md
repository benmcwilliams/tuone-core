### Evolutionary Timeline of the Equinor Facility in Bergen, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2022**: Equinor, along with partners Petoro, TotalEnergies, Shell, and ConocoPhillips, initiated a study for the **Trollvind project**, which aims to build a **1GW floating offshore wind farm** in the Troll oil field area, approximately 65 km west of Bergen. The project is expected to deliver **4.3 terawatt-hours of electricity annually**, starting in **2027**. The wind farm will provide power to the Troll and Oseberg oil fields and the Kollsnes plant, addressing the electricity grid's needs in the Bergen area.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2022**: The partners indicated that the Trollvind project would not require additional financial support and estimated that it could deliver power at a cost of less than **Nkr 1 (€0.095) per kilowatt-hour**. An investment decision is anticipated in **2023**.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2022**: The announcement of the Trollvind project and its objectives was made, highlighting the need for offshore wind to support electrification and reduce emissions in the oil and gas sector.
- **August 2022**: Further details were provided about the project, emphasizing the urgency due to increasing power demand in Norway and the constraints of existing grid connections. The final investment decision is targeted for **2023**, with potential startup in **2027**.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2022**: The development of the Trollvind project is expected to create job opportunities, particularly in the context of electrifying gas turbines and supporting new industrial developments in the Bergen/Kollsnes area. However, specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2022**: The facility is part of a **joint venture** involving Equinor and its partners (Petoro, TotalEnergies, Shell, and ConocoPhillips). There is no indication that Equinor owns the facility exclusively; rather, it is a collaborative effort among multiple companies.
