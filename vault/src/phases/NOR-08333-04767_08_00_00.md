### Evolutionary Timeline of the ConocoPhillips Facility in Bergen, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2022**: ConocoPhillips, along with partners Petoro, TotalEnergies, and Shell, initiated a study for the Trollvind project, which aims to develop a 1GW floating offshore wind farm in the Troll oil field area, approximately 65km west of Bergen. The project is expected to generate 4.3 terawatt-hours of electricity annually starting in 2027. The wind farm will provide power to the Troll and Oseberg oil fields and the Kollsnes plant, addressing the electricity grid needs in the Bergen area.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2022**: The partners indicated that the Trollvind project would not require additional financial support and estimated that it could deliver power at a cost of less than Nkr 1 (€0.095) per kilowatt-hour. An investment decision for the project is anticipated in 2023.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2022**: The announcement of the Trollvind initiative marks the beginning of the project evaluation phase. The partners are assessing commercial arrangements for the sale of power generated by the wind farm to existing oil and gas installations.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2022**: While specific job creation or loss figures were not mentioned in the article, the project is expected to contribute to the local economy and support the transition to low-carbon energy, which may influence future job opportunities in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
