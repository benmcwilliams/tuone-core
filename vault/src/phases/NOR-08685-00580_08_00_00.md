### Evolutionary Timeline of the Norsk Hydro Facility in Sirdal, Norway

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 02-07-2018
   - **Details:** Norsk Hydro will receive electricity from the 208MW Tonstad wind farm, which is under development. The wind farm will consist of 51 Siemens Gamesa turbines and is designed to supply energy to Norsk Hydro's aluminium plants in Norway.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 02-07-2018
   - **Details:** The Tonstad wind farm is a joint project developed by French utility Engie and Swiss investment firm SUSI Partners. Engie retains a 20% stake in the project, while SUSI Partners, through the SUSI Renewable Energy Fund 2, is the majority investor with an 80% interest.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 02-07-2018
   - **Details:** A 25-year agreement was established for the sale of all output from the Tonstad wind farm to Norsk Hydro, effective from 2020 to 2045. Engie will manage the construction and provide operational support for the wind farm.

4. **Labour:**

   - **Details:** The article does not specify job creation or job loss related to the Tonstad wind farm or the Norsk Hydro facility.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 02-07-2018
   - **Details:** Norsk Hydro does not own the Tonstad wind farm exclusively. The ownership is shared, with Engie holding a 20% stake and SUSI Partners holding an 80% stake through its renewable energy fund. Hydro Energi, a wholly owned subsidiary of Norsk Hydro, will handle the energy produced.
