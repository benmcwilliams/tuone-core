## NOR-08717-01636

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Equinor Facility in Gulen, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05/2019**: Equinor announces the assembly of 11 floating wind turbines, each with a capacity of 8MW, for the 88MW Hywind Tampen offshore wind farm at Wergeland Base in Gulen. The facility will handle the construction of the turbines and necessary infrastructure before they are towed to the North Sea.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **05/2019**: The total capital and development costs for the Hywind Tampen project are estimated at around NOK 5 billion, with NOK 566 million provided by an industry NOx fund.
- **10/2019**: Equinor confirms a total investment of close to NOK 5 billion for the Hywind Tampen project, which includes contracts awarded to various companies for construction and assembly.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05/2019**: Equinor announces that no work will commence until an investment decision is made, expected during 2019.
- **10/2019**: Major contracts worth NOK 3 billion (€322 million) are awarded to Kvaerner, Siemens Gamesa, JDR, and Subsea 7, marking a key milestone in the project. Construction is set to start immediately, with completion expected by Autumn 2022.

#### 4. Labour
- **10/2019**: The project is expected to involve about 250 man-years for Kvaerner employees, with a total of approximately 800 man-years when including other suppliers and public sector contributions.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **10/2019**: Equinor is the primary developer of the Hywind Tampen project, responsible for operation and maintenance of the wind farm, indicating that Equinor has full ownership of the facility in Gulen for this project. 

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Equinor facility in Gulen, Norway, focusing on the Hywind Tampen offshore wind farm project.
