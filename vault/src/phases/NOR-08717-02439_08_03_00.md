## NOR-08717-02439

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Siemens Gamesa Facility in Gulen, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production**: Siemens Gamesa is responsible for supplying turbines for the Hywind Tampen floating wind project, which has a total capacity of 88 MW.
- **Assembly Location**: The turbines will be fabricated at various locations in Europe and then transported to Wergeland Base in Gulen, Norway for assembly.
- **Maintenance**: Siemens Gamesa will also handle the maintenance of the wind turbines through a five-year contract as part of the turbine agreement.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Total Investment**: The total investment for the Hywind Tampen project is close to NOK 5 billion.
- **Contracts**: Siemens Gamesa is part of a larger contract awarded by Equinor, which is worth NOK 3 billion (€322 million) for the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Announcement Date**: The project was announced on 31-10-2019.
- **Construction Timeline**: The project is set to start immediately after the announcement and is scheduled for completion by Autumn 2022.
- **Production Start**: The wind farm is expected to start production at the end of 2022.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The project will involve about 250 man-years for Kvaerner employees, and with other suppliers and public sector involvement, the total will be around 800 man-years.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: Siemens Gamesa is not the exclusive owner of the facility; it is part of a collaborative project involving multiple companies, including Kvaerner, JDR, and Subsea 7, under the contract awarded by Equinor.
