## NOR-08717-09367

### Kvaerner Facility in Gulen, Norway - Evolutionary Timeline

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Production:** The facility is involved in the construction of floating concrete hulls for the Hywind Tampen floating wind project, which has a total capacity of 88MW.
   - **Production Capacity:** Kvaerner's contract includes the engineering, procurement, and construction of 11 floating concrete hulls.
   - **Updates:** The project will involve assembly of the wind turbines at Wergeland Base in Gulen, with the hulls initially constructed at Kvaerner’s facility at Stord.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Investment Amount:** Kvaerner's contract with Equinor is valued at approximately NOK1.5bn, part of a total project investment of close to NOK5bn for the Hywind Tampen project.
   - **Subsidies:** The project is part of a broader initiative to integrate renewable energy into the oil and gas sector, with significant financial backing from Equinor.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Announcement Date:** The contract was announced on 31-10-2019.
   - **Construction Start:** The initial phase of constructing the concrete hulls is set to start immediately after the announcement.
   - **Completion Date:** The project is scheduled for completion in Autumn 2022, with production expected to start at the end of 2022.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Job Creation:** The project will involve about 250 man-years for Kvaerner employees, and approximately 800 man-years when including other suppliers and public sector contributions.
   - **Recruitment:** Kvaerner has started recruiting new concrete specialists in anticipation of increased opportunities in renewable projects.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Joint Venture:** The marine operations for the project will be executed in a 50/50 joint venture between Kvaerner and DOF Subsea. Kvaerner does not own the facility exclusively but is a key contractor in the project.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones related to the Kvaerner facility in Gulen, Norway, as it pertains to the Hywind Tampen floating wind project.
