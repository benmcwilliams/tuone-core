## NOR-08721-09575

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Norsk Kjernekraft Facility in Kollsnes, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2024**: Norsk Kjernekraft identified a 250-acre site adjacent to the Kollsnes industrial area for the potential construction of small modular reactors (SMRs). The site has the capacity for five SMRs, each with a generating capacity of 300 MWe, totaling a potential generation of 12.5 TWh per year, which could account for nearly 10% of Norway's current electricity consumption.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2024**: Norsk Kjernekraft plans to finance the construction of SMR power plants in collaboration with capital-strong industry and solid financial players. The company is preparing license applications in accordance with national regulations and international standards.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2024**: Norsk Kjernekraft announced the compilation of a report to be sent to the Ministry of Oil and Energy for assessment regarding the new site for SMRs. This report will undergo consultation with the municipality, residents, and industry stakeholders. If approved, it will form the basis for an impact assessment.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2024**: While specific job creation figures were not mentioned, the development of SMR power plants is expected to create jobs in the construction and operational phases, particularly in collaboration with power-intensive industries.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2024**: Norsk Kjernekraft has entered into a letter of intent with landowner Rolv Svein Rougnø for the acquisition of the site. The facility is intended to be built, owned, and operated by Norsk Kjernekraft in collaboration with power-intensive industries, indicating a structure of exclusive ownership with potential partnerships for operational support.
