### Evolutionary Timeline of the Equinor Facility in Tampen Area, Norway

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** October 2019
   - **Details:** The facility is part of the Hywind Tampen project, which involves the production of floating wind turbines. The project will feature 11 turbines, each with a capacity of 8 MW, totaling 88 MW. This capacity is expected to meet approximately 35% of the annual power demand for the Snorre A and B, and Gullfaks A, B, and C oil & gas platforms.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** October 2019
   - **Details:** Kvaerner has been awarded a contract worth about NOK 1.5 billion (approximately EUR 146 million) for the engineering, procurement, and construction of the floating concrete substructures for the Hywind Tampen project.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** October 2019
   - **Details:** Kvaerner's project is set to start immediately and is scheduled for completion in autumn 2022. The initial construction of 11 concrete hulls will take place at Kvaerner's facility in Stord, Norway, followed by the towing of these structures to the site at Dommersnes in the Vindafjord for completion.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** October 2019
   - **Details:** The article does not specify job creation or job loss directly related to the Hywind Tampen project, but the scale of the project and the involvement of Kvaerner and DOF Subsea suggest potential job creation in marine operations and construction.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** October 2019
   - **Details:** The project involves a joint venture between Kvaerner and DOF Subsea for the marine operations aspect, indicating that Equinor does not own the facility exclusively but is collaborating with these companies for the project execution.
