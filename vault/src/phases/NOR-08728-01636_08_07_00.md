### Evolutionary Timeline of the Equinor Facility at Wergeland Base, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2023**: Equinor entered into a long-term partnership with Wergeland Group for the fabrication and installation of floating offshore wind foundations and turbines. The Wergeland Base will serve as a factory for offshore wind, enabling safe, efficient, and robust mass production. The first project under this partnership is the Utsira Nord project, which will utilize a concrete floater based on a spar-type design. The Utsira Nord project has a total capacity of 1.5 GW, divided into three sites of 500 MW each.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2023**: The partnership agreement with Wergeland Group is aimed at promoting the development of the Norwegian offshore wind supplier industry, contributing to local value creation and social development. The agreement has an initial duration of five years, with an option to extend.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2023**: Norway opened the application window for the Utsira Nord areas.
- **End of 2023**: Contracts for the Utsira Nord project are expected to be awarded.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2023**: The partnership is expected to contribute to job creation along the Norwegian coast, as stated by Equinor's senior vice president for the Renewables Norway cluster, Siri Espedal Kindem.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2023**: Equinor has a long-term partnership with Wergeland Group, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership. The facility is not owned exclusively by Equinor but involves a joint effort with Wergeland Group as the preferred supplier. 

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic moves related to the Equinor facility at Wergeland Base, highlighting its role in advancing offshore wind technology in Norway.
