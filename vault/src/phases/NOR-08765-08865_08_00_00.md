### Evolutionary Timeline of the Eviny Facility in Høyanger, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2022**: Eviny, in partnership with Hydro and Zephyr, announced plans to build an onshore wind farm between Høyanger and Sunnfjord. The facility is projected to have a total power production capacity of 1TWh annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2022**: The estimated investment for the wind facility is between NOK3bn ($302m) and NOK4bn ($403m). This investment is aimed at supporting the renewable energy needs of Hydro Høyanger and other industrial companies in the region.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2022**: The announcement of the wind project marks the beginning of the development phase. The project is positioned to supply renewable power to Hydro Høyanger, which is crucial for maintaining aluminium production and local jobs.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2022**: The project is expected to safeguard 180 jobs in the local community by ensuring a stable supply of renewable energy for Hydro Høyanger's aluminium production. Additionally, the wind farm is anticipated to create local value and generate yearly income for Høyanger and Sunnfjord municipalities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2022**: The wind farm will be jointly owned and operated by Hydro and Eviny for the long term. Eviny is noted to be a publicly owned company, and it is owned by Statkraft.
