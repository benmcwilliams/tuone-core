### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hydro Facility in Sunnfjord, Norway

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **December 2022:** Hydro, in partnership with Eviny and Zephyr, announced plans to build an onshore wind farm between Høyanger and Sunnfjord. The facility is expected to have a total power production capacity of 1TWh annually.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **December 2022:** The estimated investment for the wind facility is between NOK3bn ($302m) and NOK4bn ($403m).

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **December 2022:** Announcement of the wind project, with intentions to supply power to Hydro Høyanger and other industrial companies in the region. The project is positioned to support Hydro Høyanger's need for more renewable power by 2030.

4. **Labour:**
   - **December 2022:** The project is expected to safeguard 180 jobs in the local community associated with Hydro Høyanger's aluminium production.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **December 2022:** The wind farm will be owned and operated jointly by Hydro and Eviny for the long term.
