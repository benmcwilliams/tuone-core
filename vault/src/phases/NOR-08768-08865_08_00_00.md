### Evolutionary Timeline of the Eviny Facility in Sunnfjord, Norway

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** December 2022
   - **Details:** Eviny, in partnership with Hydro and Zephyr, announced plans to build an onshore wind facility between Høyanger and Sunnfjord. The facility is projected to have a total power production capacity of 1TWh annually.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** December 2022
   - **Details:** The estimated investment for the wind project is between NOK3 billion ($302 million) and NOK4 billion ($403 million). This investment is aimed at supporting the renewable energy needs of Hydro Høyanger and other industrial companies in the region.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** December 2022
   - **Details:** The announcement of the wind project marks the initial milestone. The project is intended to provide clean energy to existing industries and facilitate new industrial development in the region.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** December 2022
   - **Details:** The project is expected to safeguard 180 jobs in the local community related to Hydro Høyanger's aluminium production. Additionally, the development and operation of the wind farm are anticipated to create local value and generate yearly income for Høyanger and Sunnfjord municipalities.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** December 2022
   - **Details:** The wind farm will be owned and operated jointly by Hydro and Eviny for the long term. Eviny is noted to be a publicly owned company, owned by Statkraft, and is the largest power producer in Western Norway.
