## NOR-08900-09007

### Evolutionary Timeline of Geothermal Energy Nordic AS Facility in Longyearbyen, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09-02-2023**: The Longyearbyen local council published an energy plan emphasizing renewable energy development, particularly geothermal heating. The facility is exploring the use of medium-depth geothermal wells (up to 1600 meters) with a closed coaxial collector pipe. The process water from these wells is expected to reach temperatures between 10 to 25 degrees Celsius, which will be enhanced using a heat pump for the district heating network. The report indicates that while deep rock heating for power generation is not economically viable, the geothermal heating technology is attractive due to the high temperature gradient in the region.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09-02-2023**: The geothermal project is noted to be investment-intensive and complex to develop. The Longyearbyen local council plans to collaborate with the University Centre in Svalbard (UNIS) and Store Norske Spitsbergen Kulkompani (SNSK) to further investigate the geothermal potential and develop a technical and economic understanding of the technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09-02-2023**: The energy plan includes recommendations for further investigation and a potential pilot project for geothermal energy. Additionally, the plan discusses the potential for a Borehole Thermal Energy System (BTES) for seasonal heat storage, which has been assessed through simulations and test drilling conducted in 2021.

#### 4. Labour
- **09-02-2023**: The articles do not specify job creation or job loss directly related to the geothermal facility; however, the development of geothermal energy and BTES could imply future job opportunities in the region as the project progresses.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **09-02-2023**: The facility is a collaboration between Geothermal Energy Nordic AS (GTML) and Store Norske Spitsbergen Kulkompani (SNSK), indicating a joint venture rather than exclusive ownership by GTML.
