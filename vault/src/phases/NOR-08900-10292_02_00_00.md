### Evolutionary Timeline of the University Centre in Svalbard Facility in Longyearbyen, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2023**: The Longyearbyen local council (LL) published a comprehensive energy plan focusing on renewable energy development, particularly investigating geothermal heating and seasonal heat storage. The plan includes the potential for medium-depth geothermal wells up to 1600 meters, utilizing a closed coaxial collector pipe. The process water from these wells is expected to reach temperatures between 10 to 25 degrees Celsius, which will be enhanced by a heat pump for district heating.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2023**: The geothermal energy project is noted to be investment-intensive and complex to develop and scale. The Longyearbyen energy plan indicates a need for further technical and economic understanding of geothermal energy, which will involve collaboration with the University Centre in Svalbard (UNIS) and Store Norske Spitsbergen Kulkompani (SNSK). 

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2023**: The energy plan includes recommendations for further investigation and potential pilot projects for geothermal energy. Additionally, the Borehole Thermal Energy System (BTES) has been assessed, with previous test drilling and thermal response tests conducted in 2021, funded by the Svalbard Environment Protection Fund. The results indicated that the thermal properties of the bedrock are suitable for seasonal heat storage.
