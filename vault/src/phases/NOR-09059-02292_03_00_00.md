## NOR-09059-02292

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hydro Facility in Karmøy, Norway

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** October 2020
   - **Details:** Hydro and Lyse merged part of their hydropower production to create Lyse Kraft DA, which has a normal annual power production capacity of 9.5 TWh. Hydro remains the operator of the assets operated by RSK, which was established in 1963 to supply power to Hydro’s aluminum plant at Karmøy. This agreement allows Hydro to increase its total operator responsibility of Norwegian hydropower and wind power to 13.6 TWh in a normal year.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** October 2020
   - **Details:** The merger is strategically important for Hydro as it secures the value of their RSK hydropower assets and contributes to predictability for further industrial development in Norway. Specific financial investments tied to this merger were not detailed in the article.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** October 2020
   - **Details:** The establishment of Lyse Kraft DA was announced, and Hydro resumed operator responsibility for Lyse’s fully owned hydropower plants. This marks a significant milestone in the collaboration between Hydro and Lyse.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** October 2020
   - **Details:** As a result of the merger, thirty-four employees were transferred from Lyse to Hydro, indicating a shift in workforce management due to the new operational structure.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** October 2020
   - **Details:** The new company, Lyse Kraft DA, is owned 74.4% by Lyse and 25.6% by Hydro. Hydro does not exclusively own the facility but retains operational control over the hydropower assets.
