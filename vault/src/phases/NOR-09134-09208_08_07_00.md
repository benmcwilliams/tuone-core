### Evolutionary Timeline of the Implenia Facility in Jelsa, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **11-10-2022**: Implenia and WindWorks Jelsa announced plans to build a facility in Jelsa, Norway, dedicated to producing concrete platforms for floating wind turbines. The facility will cover an area of 800,000 square meters and aims to utilize cost-effective production processes and automation to manufacture a variety of concrete substructures for offshore wind farms.

- **18-08-2023**: The facility's project was further detailed, with plans to produce large concrete or steel structures for floating offshore wind turbines. The facility is expected to have a production capacity of 1GW per year, sufficient to power approximately 4 million homes annually. The project will be executed in multiple phases from 2023 to 2032, maintaining the 800,000 square meter production and assembly area.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **18-08-2023**: An investment and shareholder agreement was signed between Implenia and WindWorks Jelsa, where both companies will hold equal minority shares (around 41% each). The first phase of the project, termed "pre-production," will take place from 2023 to 2026, requiring limited financial investments. Implenia committed to making two small investment tranches in 2023 and 2024, with the first concessions expected to be granted in December 2023.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **11-10-2022**: Announcement of the intention to build the facility and the signing of a memorandum of understanding (MoU) between Implenia and WindWorks Jelsa.
  
- **18-08-2023**: Formalization of the investment and shareholder agreement, marking a significant milestone in the project's development.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify exact job creation or job loss figures related to the facility's development. However, the emphasis on creating a competitive environment and the potential for export opportunities suggests a positive impact on local employment in the renewable energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **18-08-2023**: The ownership structure of the facility indicates that Implenia and NorSea will each hold around 41% of WindWorks Jelsa, making it a joint venture rather than a fully owned facility by Implenia.
