## NOR-09256-09367

### Kvaerner Facility in Dommersnes, Norway - Evolutionary Timeline

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **31-10-2019**: Kvaerner is contracted by Equinor to construct the floating concrete hulls for the Hywind Tampen floating wind project, which has a total capacity of 88MW. The facility will produce 11 floating concrete hulls that will support 8MW turbines, contributing to the energy needs of the Snorre and Gullfaks oil and gas platforms. The construction of the hulls will begin at Kvaerner’s specialized facility at Stord, Norway, and will be completed at the Dommersnes site.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **31-10-2019**: The total investment for the Hywind Tampen project is approximately NOK 5 billion. Kvaerner's contract is valued at around NOK 1.5 billion, which includes engineering, procurement, and construction of the floating concrete hulls.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **31-10-2019**: Kvaerner announces the start of the Hywind Tampen project, with construction expected to be completed by Autumn 2022. The project involves significant collaboration with DOF Subsea for marine operations, executed as a 50/50 joint venture.

#### 4. Labour
- **31-10-2019**: The project is expected to involve about 250 man-years of work for Kvaerner employees, with a total of approximately 800 man-years when including other suppliers and public sector contributions.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **31-10-2019**: Kvaerner operates the facility in a joint venture with DOF Subsea for the marine operations aspect of the Hywind Tampen project, indicating that Kvaerner does not have exclusive ownership of the marine operations but retains full ownership of the construction of the concrete hulls.
