## NOR-09258-09367

### Kvaerner Facility in Stord, Norway - Evolutionary Timeline

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2019**: Kvaerner is awarded a contract by Equinor for the Hywind Tampen floating wind project, which involves the engineering, procurement, and construction of 11 floating concrete hulls for wind turbines. The total capacity of the project is 88MW, which will supply approximately 35% of the annual power demand for the Snorre A and B, Gullfaks A, B, and C oil & gas platforms. The initial construction of the concrete hulls will take place at Kvaerner’s specialized facility in Stord, Norway.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2019**: The total investment for the Hywind Tampen project is close to NOK 5 billion. Kvaerner's contract is valued at approximately NOK 1.5 billion (around €146 million), which includes the construction of the floating concrete substructures and marine operations.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2019**: Kvaerner announces the start of the Hywind Tampen project, with construction expected to be completed by Autumn 2022. The project involves the construction of 11 concrete hulls, which will be towed to the site at Dommersnes in the Vindafjord for completion.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2019**: The project is expected to involve about 250 man-years for Kvaerner employees, with a total of approximately 800 man-years when including other suppliers and public sector contributions. Kvaerner has also started recruiting new concrete specialists in anticipation of future projects.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2019**: Kvaerner operates in a 50/50 joint venture with DOF Subsea for the marine operations aspect of the Hywind Tampen project. The facility in Stord is owned by Kvaerner, but the marine operations are executed in collaboration with DOF Subsea. 

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Kvaerner facility in Stord, Norway, specifically in the context of the Hywind Tampen project.
