## NOR-09495-09575

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Norsk Kjernekraft Facility in Aure, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04-11-2023**: Norsk Kjernekraft proposed a plant in Aure, consisting of several small modular reactors (SMRs) with a total production capacity of approximately 12.5 TWh of electricity annually, which would represent an 8% increase in Norway's power production if fully realized.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04-11-2023**: Norsk Kjernekraft indicated plans for financing the project in collaboration with capital-strong industry and solid financial players, although specific investment amounts were not detailed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04-11-2023**: An agreement of intent was signed to investigate nuclear power in collaboration with several municipalities, including Aure and Heim. A proposal for the plant was submitted to the Ministry of Oil and Energy, which would initiate an environmental impact assessment upon approval.
- **18-05-2024**: Norsk Kjernekraft submitted a proposal to the Ministry of Oil and Energy for an assessment into the construction of the SMR power plant in Aure and Heim.
- **04-07-2024**: The company reiterated its proposal to the Ministry for the assessment of the SMR plant in Aure and Heim, indicating ongoing progress in the project.

#### 4. Labour
- **18-05-2024**: Norsk Kjernekraft emphasized that building SMR power plants would create many jobs, both directly for the operation of the power plant and indirectly for the industries that would develop in connection with it.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **04-11-2023**: Norsk Kjernekraft aims to build, own, and operate the SMR power plants in Norway, indicating a plan for full ownership of the facility in Aure.

### Summary
The Norsk Kjernekraft facility in Aure, Norway, is in the proposal and planning stages for a nuclear power plant utilizing small modular reactors (SMRs). The facility aims to produce 12.5 TWh of electricity annually, contributing significantly to Norway's energy needs. The company is pursuing full ownership of the facility and is actively engaging with local municipalities and the government to advance the project, with job creation being a key benefit highlighted in their plans.

### Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: "The plant is planned to consist of several SMRs, which together will produce around 12.5 TWh of electricity annually" (Article 1, 04-11-2023). Contrastingly, another article mentions "Norsk Kjernekraft says it could build three or four SMRs of 300 MWe capacity each... sufficient to meet the needs of the data centre as well as, for example, a green electrolysis factory for hydrogen" (Article 2, 18-05-2024).
- **Updates on Technology**: "Norsk Kjernekraft has said it intends to initially establish SMRs based on conventional technology" (Article 1, 04-11-2023). However, it also signed a letter of intent with Denmark's Seaborg to investigate the deployment of "Seaborg's compact molten salt reactor in Norway" (Article 1, 04-11-2023).

### Investment and Development
- **Investments**: "Norsk Kjernekraft aims to build, own and operate SMR power plants in Norway in collaboration with power-intensive industry" (Article 1, 04-11-2023). In contrast, another article states, "Financing will take place in collaboration with capital-strong industry and solid financial players" (Article 2, 18-05-2024), indicating a reliance on external partnerships for funding.

### Project Status and Milestones
- **Proposal Submission**: "In November, Norsk Kjernekraft submitted a proposal to the Ministry of Oil and Energy for an assessment into the construction of a power plant based on multiple SMRs in the municipalities of Aure and Heim" (Article 4, 04-07-2024). This contrasts with the statement that "Norsk Kjernekraft has submitted a proposal to Norway's Ministry of Energy for an assessment of the construction of a power plant based on multiple SMRs in the municipality of Øygarden" (Article 6, 29-08-2024), indicating multiple proposals in different locations.
- **Environmental Impact Assessment**: "Once the proposal for the plant has been approved by the Ministry of Oil and Energy, an environmental impact assessment can start" (Article 1, 04-11-2023). However, another article mentions that "the report it has now submitted to the Ministry of Energy describes local conditions for the construction and operation of a nuclear power plant" (Article 3, 17-06-2024), suggesting ongoing assessments in various stages.

### Labour
- **Job Creation**: "The nuclear power plant creates many jobs, both directly for the operation of the power plant, and indirectly for the industry that is being built" (Article 2, 18-05-2024). In contrast, another article states, "A nuclear power plant in Vardø will give Finnmark access to large amounts of stable power, create hundreds of jobs" (Article 3, 17-06-2024), indicating varying job creation estimates based on different project locations.

### Ownership Structure
- **Ownership Claims**: "Norsk Kjernekraft aims to build, own and operate SMR power plants in Norway in collaboration with power-intensive industry" (Article 1, 04-11-2023). However, it is also noted that "a new company, Halden Kjernekraft AS, has also been founded by Norsk Kjernekraft, Østfold Energi and the municipality of Halden" (Article 3, 17-06-2024), indicating joint ventures in some projects.
