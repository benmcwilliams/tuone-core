### Evolutionary Timeline of the Norsk Kjernekraft Facility in Buneset, Øygarden, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2024**: Norsk Kjernekraft proposed the establishment of a nuclear power plant at Buneset, Øygarden. The facility is planned to accommodate up to five Small Modular Reactors (SMRs), each with a generating capacity of 300 MWe. This results in a potential total generation capacity of 12.5 TWh per year, which could cover nearly 10% of Norway's current electricity consumption.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2024**: The proposal includes a study program submitted to the Ministry of Energy to assess the societal and environmental impacts of the construction, operation, and decommissioning of the power plant. The report will be part of the knowledge base for the government's investigation into nuclear power in Norway.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2024**: The formal process to establish the nuclear power plant has been initiated with the submission of the proposal to the Ministry of Energy. This marks the third notification sent by Norsk Kjernekraft regarding potential nuclear power sites in Norway.

#### 4. Labour
- **09/2024**: The establishment of the nuclear power plant is expected to contribute to job creation, particularly in the context of electrifying oil and gas installations and establishing new industries in the Øygarden municipality, which currently faces a power deficit.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **09/2024**: The site for the proposed facility is owned by Rolv Svein Rougnø, a landowner and former mayor of Øygarden. Norsk Kjernekraft has entered into a letter of intent with Rougnø, indicating plans to acquire the site for the construction of the SMR power plants. The ownership structure suggests a potential joint venture or partnership for the development of the facility.
