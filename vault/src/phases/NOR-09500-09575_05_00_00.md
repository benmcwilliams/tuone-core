## NOR-09500-09575

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Norsk Kjernekraft Facility in Heim, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04-11-2023**: Norsk Kjernekraft proposed a small modular reactor (SMR) power plant in Heim, which, if fully realized, will produce approximately 12.5 TWh of electricity annually, representing an 8% increase in Norway's power production. The facility is planned to consist of several SMRs.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04-11-2023**: Norsk Kjernekraft indicated that financing for the project would occur in collaboration with capital-strong industry and solid financial players. The company aims to prepare license applications in accordance with national regulations and international standards.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04-11-2023**: Norsk Kjernekraft entered into an agreement of intent with several municipalities, including Heim, to investigate the deployment of nuclear power. A proposal for the plant was submitted to the Ministry of Oil and Energy, which will initiate an environmental impact assessment upon approval.
- **18-05-2024**: The company submitted a proposal to the Ministry of Oil and Energy for an assessment into the construction of the SMR power plant in Heim and Aure.
- **17-06-2024**: Norsk Kjernekraft reiterated its proposal for the SMR power plant in Heim and Aure, emphasizing the need for environmental impact assessments and local support.
- **04-07-2024**: The company continued to push for the assessment of the SMR plant in Heim, alongside other locations in Norway.

#### 4. Labour
- **04-11-2023**: Norsk Kjernekraft stated that the proposed nuclear power plant would create many jobs, both directly for the operation of the power plant and indirectly for the industries that would be established in connection with it.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **04-11-2023**: Norsk Kjernekraft aims to build, own, and operate the SMR power plants in Norway, indicating a plan for full ownership of the facility in Heim.

This timeline captures the key developments related to the Norsk Kjernekraft facility in Heim, Norway, focusing on its technology, investments, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure.
