### Evolutionary Timeline of the Norsk Kjernekraft Facility in Svartnes, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Proposed Capacity**: In June 2024, Norsk Kjernekraft proposed the construction of a nuclear power plant at Svartnes with a capacity of up to 600 MWe and an annual output of up to 5 TWh, which is stated to be sufficient to triple the power supply in Finnmark.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Proposal**: The report submitted to the Ministry of Energy in June 2024 outlines the local conditions for the construction and operation of the nuclear power plant, indicating that the project is in the early stages of investment planning. Specific investment amounts have not been disclosed yet.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Initial Proposal**: The municipality of Vardø proposed Svartnes as a potential site for the nuclear power plant in April 2023.
- **Agreement for Study**: An agreement between Vardø municipality and Norsk Kjernekraft was established in June 2023 to prepare a report for a study program.
- **Report Submission**: In June 2024, Norsk Kjernekraft submitted a report to the Ministry of Energy detailing the proposed nuclear power plant and its potential impacts.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: Norsk Kjernekraft's CEO indicated that the construction of the nuclear power plant would create hundreds of jobs, contributing to the local economy and providing stable employment opportunities in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: Norsk Kjernekraft aims to build, own, and operate the proposed nuclear power plant, indicating full ownership intentions for the facility.
