## NOR-09505-09575

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Norsk Kjernekraft Facility in Vardø, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2024**: Norsk Kjernekraft announced plans to build small modular reactors (SMRs) in Norway, focusing on off-grid projects. The company aims to produce electricity and heat, with potential applications in heat-intensive industries and carbon capture. They proposed building three to four SMRs of 300 MWe capacity each, totaling 900-1200 MWe (7.5-10 TWh annually) to support local industries, including a data center in Skien.

- **June 2024**: A nuclear power plant is proposed for Svartnes, near Vardø, with a capacity of up to 600 MWe and an annual output of up to 5 TWh, which could triple the power supply in Finnmark. The report submitted to the Ministry of Energy outlines local conditions for construction and operation.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2024**: Norsk Kjernekraft submitted a report to the Ministry of Energy detailing the feasibility of constructing a nuclear power plant at Svartnes, which is part of their broader strategy to develop SMR facilities in collaboration with local industries.

- **August 2024**: Norsk Kjernekraft signed a collaboration agreement with South Korean companies DL Energy and DL E&C to leverage their expertise in constructing nuclear power plants, which could expedite the development process.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: Vardø municipality proposed Svartnes as a potential site for a nuclear power plant to Norsk Kjernekraft.

- **June 2024**: An agreement was made between Vardø municipality and Norsk Kjernekraft to prepare a report for a study program regarding the nuclear power plant.

- **August 2024**: Norsk Kjernekraft submitted a proposal to the Ministry of Energy for an assessment of the construction of a multi-unit SMR nuclear power plant in Vardø.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2024**: Norsk Kjernekraft's CEO stated that the nuclear power plant in Vardø would create hundreds of jobs, both directly for the operation of the plant and indirectly through the establishment of local industries.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: Norsk Kjernekraft aims to build, own, and operate the SMR power plants in Norway, including the proposed facility in Vardø, in collaboration with power-intensive industries. The company is working towards full ownership of the facility while engaging with local municipalities for support and collaboration.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic plans for the Norsk Kjernekraft facility in Vardø, Norway, highlighting its focus on SMR technology, local collaboration, and job creation.
