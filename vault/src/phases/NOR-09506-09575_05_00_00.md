## NOR-09506-09575

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Norsk Kjernekraft Facility in Øygarden, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2024**: Norsk Kjernekraft announced plans to build small modular reactors (SMRs) in Øygarden, with a potential capacity of up to five SMRs, each capable of producing 300 MWe, totaling 900-1200 MWe (7.5-10 TWh annually). The facility aims to provide electricity and heat for local industries, including data centers and hydrogen production.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2024**: Norsk Kjernekraft submitted a proposal to Norway's Ministry of Energy for an assessment of constructing a multi-unit SMR nuclear power plant in Øygarden. This proposal is part of a broader strategy to collaborate with power-intensive industries and secure financing from capital-strong industry players.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2024**: Norsk Kjernekraft initiated work on an impact assessment for a plot of land in Øygarden to evaluate the feasibility of establishing a nuclear power plant.
- **August 2024**: A formal proposal was submitted to the Ministry of Energy for the construction of the SMR facility in Øygarden, following similar proposals for other municipalities.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2024**: Norsk Kjernekraft indicated that the construction and operation of the nuclear power plant would create numerous jobs, both directly at the facility and indirectly through the associated industries that would benefit from the stable power supply.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: Norsk Kjernekraft aims to build, own, and operate the SMR power plants in Norway, including the proposed facility in Øygarden, in collaboration with power-intensive industries. The company is focused on maintaining full ownership of the facility while engaging in partnerships for development and financing.
