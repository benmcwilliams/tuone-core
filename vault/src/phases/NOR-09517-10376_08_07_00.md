### Evolutionary Timeline of the Vårgrønn Facility in Utsira Nord, Norway

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **June 2023:** Equinor, in partnership with Vårgrønn, confirmed the development of a floating offshore wind project at Utsira Nord, which has a total capacity of 1.5 GW. The project will utilize a concrete floater based on a spar-type design, with concrete foundations produced in Norway. The facility at the Wergeland Base will serve as a factory for the mass production of offshore wind components.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **June 2023:** The partnership agreement with Wergeland Group is aimed at enhancing the industrial development of floating offshore wind in Norway. While specific investment amounts were not disclosed, the agreement is expected to promote the development of the Norwegian supplier industry and local value creation.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **March 2023:** Norway opened the application window for the Utsira Nord areas.
   - **June 2023:** Equinor and Vårgrønn announced their partnership and confirmed the floater concept for the Utsira Nord project. The award of contracts for the project is scheduled to take place by the end of 2023.

4. **Labour:**
   - **June 2023:** The partnership is expected to create local job opportunities and promote social development through the establishment of a Norwegian supply chain for offshore wind.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **June 2023:** The facility is a collaboration between Equinor and Vårgrønn, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by either company.
