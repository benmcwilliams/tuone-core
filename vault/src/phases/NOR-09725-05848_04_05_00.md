### Provaris Energy Ltd Facility Evolution Timeline - Fiskå Facility, Norway

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2024**: Provaris Energy Ltd is preparing to restart its prototype tank fabrication and testing program at the Fiskå Facility. This involves the purchase of robotic laser welding equipment and aims to produce small-scale hydrogen storage tanks, which are crucial for improving operational economics for industrial hydrogen users.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2024**: Provaris is finalizing a term sheet for a long-term hydrogen supply agreement with Uniper and Norwegian Hydrogen, targeting a 10-year offtake contract for over 40,000 tonnes per annum of renewable green hydrogen from the Nordics to Germany. This agreement is a critical milestone for Provaris' hydrogen supply chain development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2024**: The completion of the sale of the Fiskå Facility is expected around January 1, 2025. This sale will enable Provaris to secure a lease agreement for part of the facility, facilitating the resumption of its prototype tank program. The finalization of the term sheet for hydrogen supply is also progressing towards execution.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify any direct job creation or job loss associated with the recent developments at the Fiskå Facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2024**: Provaris Energy Ltd is in the process of selling the Fiskå Facility, with the completion of the sale expected by January 1, 2025. Following the sale, Provaris will enter into a lease agreement for a portion of the facility, indicating that they will not own the facility exclusively post-sale but will retain operational capabilities through the lease.
