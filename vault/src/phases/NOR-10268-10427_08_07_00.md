### Evolutionary Timeline of Wind Catching Systems Facility at Mehuken Wind Park, Norway

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** October 2022
   - **Details:** Wind Catching Systems is developing a multi-turbine floating offshore wind power plant named the Windcatcher. The technology aims to maximize energy production per floating structure and features a self-contained maintenance system that reduces the need for specialized vessels. One Wind Catching unit is projected to have the same annual production as five conventional 15 MW offshore wind turbines. The design life of the technology is projected to be 50 years, with significant reductions in maintenance costs compared to conventional solutions.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** October 2022
   - **Details:** Wind Catching Systems received a technology development grant of NOK 22 million (approximately USD 2.1 million) from Enova to support the design, construction, and testing of the Windcatcher. Additionally, General Motors (GM) invested up to USD 10 million in June 2022 as part of a Series A investment round to fund the development and commercialization of the technology. The funding from GM, along with the grant from Enova, is expected to fully finance the turbine development program.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** October 2022
   - **Details:** The pilot project for the Windcatcher is planned to be installed at Mehuken wind park on the Norwegian west coast in 2023. The facility is in the development phase, collaborating with companies such as Zephyr, Aibel, The Institute for Energy Technology (IFE), and DNV.
