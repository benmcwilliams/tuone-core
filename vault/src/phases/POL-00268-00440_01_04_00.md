### Evolutionary Timeline of LG Chem Facility in Dolnośląskie, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2016**: LG Chem begins negotiations to acquire a site in Wroclaw, Poland, for an electric vehicle (EV) battery production facility.
- **October 2016**: Construction of the 42,300 square meter facility starts, with an initial production capacity to produce battery packs for over 100,000 EVs annually.
- **September 2017**: LG Chem announces a further investment of $387 million to expand its Polish factory.
- **November 2018**: LG Chem plans to triple production capacity to produce enough battery cells for 300,000 EVs annually, with an investment of $571 million.
- **November 2019**: The European Bank for Reconstruction and Development lends €250 million for the expansion, aiming for a production capacity of 1 million batteries by 2022.
- **March 2020**: LG Chem secures a €480 million loan from the European Investment Bank to increase production capacity to around 65 GWh.
- **April 2020**: LG Chem announces plans to quadruple production capacity, aiming to produce batteries for 800,000 to 1 million electric cars.
- **December 2020**: LG Chem completes the acquisition of a former TV assembly plant to facilitate further expansion.
- **July 2022**: The facility is reported to have a production capacity of 70 GWh.
- **December 2023**: LG Chem signs a deal to supply 200,000 NCM battery modules for electric buses, indicating ongoing production and technological advancements.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2017**: LG Chem announces a $387 million investment for expansion.
- **November 2018**: A $571 million investment is planned to triple production capacity.
- **November 2019**: The EBRD lends €250 million for expansion.
- **March 2020**: LG Chem secures a €480 million loan from the EIB for further capacity increase.
- **April 2020**: Additional financing of 700 billion won (approximately €550 million) is secured from South Korean banks for expansion.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2016**: Negotiations for site acquisition begin.
- **October 2016**: Construction of the facility starts.
- **Second half of 2017**: Facility opens for production.
- **September 2017**: Announcement of further investment for expansion.
- **November 2018**: Plans to triple production capacity are announced.
- **November 2019**: EBRD loan for expansion is confirmed.
- **March 2020**: EIB loan for capacity increase is secured.
- **April 2020**: Announcement of plans to quadruple production capacity.
- **December 2020**: Acquisition of additional plant for expansion is completed.
- **December 2023**: LG Chem signs a contract for battery module supply.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2019**: The expansion project is expected to create 1,000 new jobs by 2022.
- **March 2020**: By the end of 2022, the facility is projected to employ about 6,000 full-time staff.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- LG Chem owns the facility exclusively, operating it as part of its global network of battery production plants. The facility is described as the largest battery production facility in Europe, indicating full ownership and control over operations.
