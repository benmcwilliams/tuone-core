### Evolutionary Timeline of LG Chem Facility in Dolnośląskie, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2016**: LG Chem begins negotiations to acquire a site in Wroclaw, Poland, for electric vehicle (EV) battery production, driven by increasing demand for EVs in Europe.
- **October 2016**: Construction of the LG Chem facility in Kobierzyce near Wroclaw starts. The facility is designed to produce battery packs for over 100,000 EVs annually, with a range of up to 320 km.
- **September 2017**: LG Chem announces an additional investment of $387 million to expand its Polish factory.
- **November 2018**: LG Chem plans to triple its production capacity to produce batteries for 300,000 EVs annually, with an investment of approximately $571 million.
- **January 2019**: The European Commission authorizes €36 million in investment aid for LG Chem's new plant in Dolnośląskie, with plans to produce battery cells for over 80,000 EVs per year.
- **July 2019**: LG Chem announces plans to expand production capacity to 1 million EV batteries per year by 2022, with a total investment projected at €2.8 billion.
- **November 2019**: The European Bank for Reconstruction and Development lends €250 million for the expansion of the facility, aiming for an annual production capacity of 65-70 GWh by 2022.
- **March 2020**: LG Chem secures a €480 million loan from the European Investment Bank for further expansion, targeting a total capacity of around 65 GWh.
- **April 2020**: LG Chem signs loan agreements for a total of €550 million to finance the expansion of its production capacities in Poland.
- **December 2020**: LG Chem's Wroclaw facility is described as the largest battery production facility in Europe, with a capacity of 70 GWh.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2016**: LG Chem announces a PLN 1.3 billion (approximately $339 million) investment in the facility.
- **September 2017**: Additional investment of $387 million is announced for expansion.
- **November 2018**: LG Chem plans to invest $571 million to triple production capacity.
- **January 2019**: The European Commission approves €36 million in investment aid for the new facility.
- **November 2019**: The European Bank for Reconstruction and Development lends €250 million for expansion.
- **March 2020**: LG Chem secures a €480 million loan from the European Investment Bank for further expansion.
- **April 2020**: LG Chem signs loan agreements totaling €550 million for expansion.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2016**: Initial negotiations for the facility begin.
- **October 2016**: Construction of the facility starts.
- **Second half of 2017**: The facility is expected to open.
- **November 2018**: Plans to triple production capacity are announced.
- **January 2019**: Investment aid from the European Commission is authorized.
- **July 2019**: Plans to expand production to 1 million batteries by 2022 are announced.
- **March 2020**: Expansion plans are further solidified with loans from the European Investment Bank.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2019**: The European Commission states that the investment will create over 700 direct jobs.
- **November 2019**: The expansion is expected to create 1,000 new jobs by 2022.
- **March 2020**: The facility is projected to employ about 6,000 full-time staff by the end of 2022.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2016**: LG Chem is negotiating to acquire the site, indicating plans for full ownership.
- **October 2016**: The facility is confirmed to be owned by LG Chem, which operates multiple plants globally, including in South Korea, China, and the U.S.
