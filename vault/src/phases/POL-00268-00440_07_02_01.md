### Evolutionary Timeline of LG Chem Facility in Dolnośląskie, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2016**: LG Chem begins negotiations to acquire a site in Wroclaw, Poland, for EV battery production, driven by increasing demand for electric vehicles in Europe.
- **October 2016**: Construction of the new factory for EV batteries near Wroclaw starts. The facility is planned to cover 42,300 square meters and produce battery packs for over 100,000 EVs annually.
- **September 2017**: LG Chem announces a further investment of $387 million to expand its Polish factory, which opened in 2016.
- **November 2018**: LG Chem plans to triple production capacity to produce enough battery cells for 300,000 EVs annually, with an investment of $571 million.
- **January 2019**: The European Commission authorizes €36 million in investment aid for LG Chem's new plant in Dolnośląskie, with plans to supply battery cells for over 80,000 EVs per year.
- **November 2019**: LG Chem announces plans to expand production capacity from 100,000 to 1 million EV batteries per year by 2022, aiming for an annual production capacity of 65-70 GWh.

- **December 2020**: LG Chem's production capacity in Wroclaw is reported to be 70 GWh, with the facility described as the largest battery production site in Europe.
