## POL-00527-00521

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Mercedes-Benz Facility in Jawor, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2018**: Mercedes-Benz announces that its new plant in Jawor will source power from the Taczalin wind farm, which has a capacity of 45.1 MW. This facility will utilize CO2-neutral energy for its operations.
- **January 2019**: The company reveals plans to build a battery assembly plant in Jawor, alongside an engine factory that will produce four-cylinder engines for hybrid and conventional vehicles. The battery factory is set to start production in the early 2020s, focusing on batteries for the EQ vehicle lineup.
- **December 2020**: Production of plug-in hybrid batteries begins at the Jawor facility, with plans to expand to include battery systems for the EQA and EQB models starting in 2021.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2019**: Daimler announces an investment of over one billion euros in its global battery production network, which includes the Jawor facility as the ninth location.
- **May 2020**: The company continues to emphasize its commitment to expanding battery production capacity globally, with Jawor identified as a key site for future battery production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2019**: Announcement of the construction of the battery assembly plant in Jawor.
- **December 2020**: Start of production for plug-in hybrid batteries at the Jawor facility.
- **March 2021**: Confirmation that the Jawor plant will also produce battery systems for the EQA and EQB models starting in 2021.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2019**: The engine factory in Jawor is expected to employ around 1,000 people, while the battery assembly plant will create an additional 300 jobs.
- **December 2020**: The production ramp-up at the Jawor facility is anticipated to contribute to job creation in the region as battery production expands.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: The facility is owned exclusively by Mercedes-Benz, as part of its strategy to establish a comprehensive battery production network to support its electrification goals.
