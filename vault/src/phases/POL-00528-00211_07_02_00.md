## POL-00528-00211

### Evolutionary Timeline of the ElectroMobility Poland Facility in Jaworzno, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2020**: ElectroMobility Poland (EMP) announced plans to produce electric vehicles under the brand name Izera, with two models (a compact car and an SUV) presented as prototypes. The factory in Jaworzno is set to produce these vehicles, although specific production capacities were not disclosed.
- **November 2022**: EMP confirmed that the Izera vehicles would be based on Geely's Sustainable Experience Architecture (SEA) platform, marking a significant technological partnership.
- **March 2023**: EMP announced a collaboration with Italian design company Pininfarina to design three electric cars (an SUV, a hatchback, and an estate) based on the SEA platform.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2020**: The Polish government expressed financial support for the project, which is expected to involve an investment of approximately 1.1 billion euros. This investment aims to boost local parts suppliers and the automotive sector.
- **January 2023**: It was reported that the investment would also benefit German companies involved in the planning and construction of the production facilities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2020**: The groundbreaking ceremony for the factory was scheduled for 2021, with production initially planned to start in 2023.
- **January 2023**: Production was postponed to the end of 2025, with construction of the factory now scheduled to begin in early 2024.
- **March 2023**: The groundbreaking ceremony is still planned for 2024, with the first Izera electric cars expected to roll off the production line by the end of 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2020**: The facility is expected to create around 15,000 jobs, including 3,000 at the plant itself and 12,000 among suppliers and subcontractors.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2020**: ElectroMobility Poland is owned by state-owned energy groups PGE, Tauron, Enea, and Energa, indicating a joint ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by a single entity.
