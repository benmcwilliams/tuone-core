### Evolutionary Timeline of the Johnson Matthey Facility in Konin, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2019**: Johnson Matthey announced the construction of a new factory in Konin, Poland, for the production of battery cathode materials. The initial production capacity is set at **10,000 tons per year**, with potential expansion up to **100,000 tons per year** depending on demand.
- **February 2021**: The company confirmed that they are on track to scale up production in Poland as planned, with mass production expected to start in **2022**. The cathode materials will feature a high nickel content, aimed at reducing cobalt levels.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2019**: Johnson Matthey indicated a broader investment strategy, having announced plans to invest **200 million pounds** (approximately **234 million euros**) in expanding its battery materials business, which includes the Konin facility.
- **April 2021**: The company signed long-term supply contracts for raw materials with Nornickel and SQM, which are essential for the production of battery materials at the Konin facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2019**: Announcement of the construction of the Konin facility.
- **February 2021**: Confirmation of scaling up production and readiness for mass production in **2022**.
- **November 2021**: Johnson Matthey announced its withdrawal from the battery materials business, which included plans for the Konin facility. The company stated that it would seek to sell all or part of its battery materials business, leading to potential closure of the facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2021**: Following the announcement of withdrawal from the battery materials business, Johnson Matthey began consultations with employees regarding the proposed closure of the Battery Materials business, which would likely result in job losses.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2019 - November 2021**: Johnson Matthey was the sole owner of the facility in Konin, Poland, as there were no indications of joint ventures or partnerships specifically related to this facility during the timeline. However, the company was exploring strategic partnerships in the broader context of its battery materials business.
