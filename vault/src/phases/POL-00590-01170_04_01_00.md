### Evolutionary Timeline of the Linde Facility in Konin, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: The facility is planned to convert 40 tons of plastic waste into 58 MWh of renewable electricity and clean hydrogen per day.
- **Product Use**: The hydrogen produced will be used locally as fuel for city buses, other heavy-duty vehicles, and passenger cars.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Funding Approval**: The project is subject to funding approval from the EU's Just Transition Fund, which aims to support energy transition in areas traditionally reliant on fossil fuels. The Polish government has committed to match any European funding for the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Announcement**: On **07-09-2021**, it was announced that Linde, Hydrogen Utopia International (HUI), and Powerhouse Energy (PHE) are in the final stages of signing a mutual contract for the development of the facility in Konin. 

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: While specific job creation numbers have not been detailed in the articles, the establishment of the facility is expected to create jobs related to the operation and maintenance of the plant, as well as in the local transportation sector due to the use of hydrogen fuel.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture**: The facility is a joint development project involving Linde, Hydrogen Utopia International (HUI), and Powerhouse Energy (PHE).
