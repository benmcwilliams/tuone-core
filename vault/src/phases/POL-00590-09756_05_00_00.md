### Evolutionary Timeline of the Polska Grupa Energetyczna Facility in Konin, Poland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 04/2023
   - **Details:** A joint special purpose vehicle named PGE PAK Energia Jądrowa SA was established to construct a nuclear power plant in Pątnów, which is part of the Konin region. The plant will feature two APR1400 reactors supplied by Korea Hydro & Nuclear Power (KHNP). The facility aims to contribute to Poland's energy security and environmental goals by providing zero-emission energy.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 04/2023
   - **Details:** The establishment of PGE PAK Energia Jądrowa SA marks a significant step in acquiring financing and preparing a detailed investment schedule for the nuclear power plant project. The joint venture will handle all stages of the project, including environmental studies and obtaining necessary permits.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 04/2023
   - **Details:** The joint venture was officially formed, and the articles of association were signed. This follows a letter of intent signed on 31 October 2022, indicating the commitment to develop the nuclear power plant in the Konin region. The project is part of Poland's broader nuclear energy strategy, which includes plans for up to six reactors by 2040.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 04/2023
   - **Details:** While specific job creation figures were not mentioned in the article, the establishment of the joint venture and the planned construction of the nuclear power plant are expected to create jobs in the region, contributing to local employment opportunities.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 04/2023
   - **Details:** The facility is a joint venture between Polska Grupa Energetyczna (PGE) and ZE PAK, with each company holding 50% of the shares in PGE PAK Energia Jądrowa SA. This indicates a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by one company. 

This timeline captures the key developments related to the Polska Grupa Energetyczna facility in Konin, focusing on the establishment of the joint venture for the nuclear power plant project and its implications for technology, investment, project status, labor, and ownership.
