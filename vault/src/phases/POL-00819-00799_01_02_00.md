## POL-00819-00799

### Evolutionary Timeline of Umicore Facility in Nysa, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **02-06-2018**: Umicore announces the establishment of its first European cathode material factory in Nysa, Poland, aimed at producing cathode materials for electric vehicle (EV) batteries. The factory is expected to create up to 400 jobs and is planned to commence operations by the end of 2020.
- **24-06-2020**: Umicore receives a €125 million loan from the European Investment Bank (EIB) to finance the construction of the Nysa facility.
- **21-09-2022**: The Nysa plant officially opens, with an initial production capacity of 20 GWh by the end of 2023 and plans to expand to 40 GWh in 2024, ultimately aiming for over 200 GWh in the second half of the decade.
- **09-10-2023**: Umicore announces plans for the Ionway joint venture with Volkswagen, which will build a new cathode materials plant adjacent to the existing Nysa facility, further enhancing production capabilities.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **24-06-2020**: Umicore secures a €125 million loan from the EIB for the Nysa facility.
- **12-10-2023**: The Polish government supports the Ionway project with €350 million in cash grants, contributing to the estimated €1.7 billion investment for the new plant.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **02-06-2018**: Announcement of the Nysa facility.
- **21-09-2022**: Official opening of the Nysa plant.
- **09-10-2023**: Announcement of the Ionway joint venture and plans for a new plant in Nysa.

#### 4. Labour
- **02-06-2018**: The Nysa facility is expected to create up to 400 jobs in its first phase.
- **21-09-2022**: The plant employs around 240 people, with expectations to exceed 400 jobs by the end of 2023.
- **12-10-2023**: The Ionway project is expected to create 900 jobs by the end of the decade.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **21-09-2022**: Umicore operates the Nysa facility independently, but the establishment of the Ionway joint venture with Volkswagen indicates a collaborative approach for future expansions and production.
