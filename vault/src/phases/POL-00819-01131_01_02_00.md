### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ionway Facility in Nysa, Poland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **09-10-2023:** Ionway, a joint venture between Volkswagen and Umicore, announced plans to build a cathode materials plant in Nysa, Poland. The facility will produce high-performance cathode materials (CAM) with an initial production capacity of 20 GWh, scaling up to a final capacity of 160 GWh by 2025. The plant will be adjacent to Umicore's existing cathode materials plant, allowing for logistical advantages and access to raw materials.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **09-10-2023:** The Polish government is supporting the Ionway facility with €350 million in cash grants. The total estimated investment for the project is around €1.7 billion, with PowerCo and Umicore expected to raise €1.35 billion themselves by the end of the decade.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **09-10-2023:** The announcement of the facility marks the operational first step for Ionway. Site preparation, planning, and permitting are currently underway, with construction set to begin once the approval process is completed. Production is expected to start in 2025.

4. **Labour:**
   - **09-10-2023:** The Ionway facility is projected to create approximately 900 jobs by the end of the decade.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **09-10-2023:** The Ionway facility is a joint venture between Volkswagen and Umicore, indicating that it is not under exclusive ownership by either company but rather a collaborative effort.
