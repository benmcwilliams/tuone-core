### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fiat Chrysler Facility in Tychy, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **06-08-2020**: Fiat Chrysler Automobiles (FCA) announces a shift in its vehicle platform strategy due to the merger with PSA, indicating that the Tychy facility will transition to using PSA’s CMP platform for B-segment vehicles. This includes future models like the successors to the Fiat Punto and Fiat 500XL, as well as an electric B-SUV from Alfa Romeo.
- **04-01-2021**: FCA confirms plans to invest in the Tychy plant to produce hybrid and electric vehicles for the Jeep, Fiat, and Alfa Romeo brands, with mass production of the first electrified model set to begin in the second half of 2022.
- **05-07-2023**: The facility starts production of the Fiat 600e, an electric crossover based on the e-CMP platform, featuring a 115 kW electric motor and a 54 kWh battery. The first models are expected to be delivered in September 2023. Additionally, the facility is set to produce the Fiat Topolino, a small city EV.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04-01-2021**: FCA announces an investment of 755 million zloty (approximately 166 million euros) in the Tychy facility to support the production of hybrid and electric vehicles, fulfilling a commitment to strengthen operations in Poland.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **06-08-2020**: FCA communicates the decision to stop B-segment developments and transition to PSA platforms, indicating a significant operational shift for the Tychy facility.
- **04-01-2021**: Announcement of the investment and plans for electric vehicle production starting in 2022.
- **05-07-2023**: Production of the Fiat 600e begins at the Tychy facility, marking the start of electric vehicle manufacturing at the site.

#### 4. Labour
- **04-01-2021**: The investment in the Tychy facility is expected to create jobs as FCA expands its operations to include the production of hybrid and electric vehicles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned by Fiat Chrysler Automobiles, which is in the process of merging with PSA to form Stellantis. There is no indication of joint ownership or partnerships affecting the Tychy facility's operations as of the latest articles.
