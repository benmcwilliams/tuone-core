### Evolutionary Timeline of Impact Clean Power Technology Facility (GigafactoryX) in Warsaw, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2022**: Impact Clean Power Technology (ICPT) begins construction of GigafactoryX, a production facility for battery systems aimed at heavy-duty commercial vehicles and stationary energy storage. The initial production capacity is set at **2 GWh per year**, with plans to expand to **5 GWh** in the final stage. The facility will utilize highly automated production lines powered by a photovoltaic system and will incorporate energy storage from second-life battery systems.
- **December 2022**: ICPT signs a supply contract with Pepper Motion to manufacture complete battery systems for electric buses and trucks, emphasizing the robustness and modularity of their battery units.
- **January 2023**: ICPT enters a framework agreement with Freyr to source LFP battery cells, with a projected volume of **10 to 14 GWh** from 2025 to 2030.
- **November 2023**: ICPT announces an extension of its supply contract with Solaris for next-generation battery systems, indicating ongoing production at GigafactoryX.
- **December 2023**: ICPT orders **200,000 battery modules** with NCM cells from LG Energy Solution for use in electric buses, to be assembled at GigafactoryX.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2022**: The construction of GigafactoryX marks a significant investment in battery production technology, although specific financial details regarding the investment are not disclosed in the articles.
- **January 2023**: The agreement with Freyr potentially involves orders valued at **USD 1.8 billion**, indicating substantial financial commitments tied to the battery cell supply.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2022**: Announcement of the construction of GigafactoryX.
- **July 2023**: Assembly work for the automated production line is scheduled to begin.
- **2024**: GigafactoryX is expected to commence operations with an initial production capacity of **2 GWh**.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific details on job creation or job loss associated with the construction or operation of GigafactoryX. However, the establishment of a large-scale battery production facility typically implies job creation in manufacturing and assembly roles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- ICPT is identified as the manufacturer and operator of GigafactoryX, indicating **full ownership** of the facility. There are no mentions of joint ventures or partnerships regarding ownership.
