### Evolutionary Timeline of the ORLEN Facility in Warsaw, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2024**: ORLEN is advancing its hydrogen mobility project as part of the third phase of the ‘Clean Cities – Hydrogen mobility in Poland’ initiative. This phase includes the establishment of an additional 16 publicly available hydrogen refuelling stations across Poland, enhancing the existing infrastructure which already includes eight refuelling stations and an automotive-grade hydrogen production plant in Wloclawek. The facility aims to build an international network of over 100 hydrogen refuelling stations by 2030, with a total electrolyser capacity of about 1 GW, producing over 130,000 tonnes of renewable hydrogen annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2024**: ORLEN received a significant EU grant of €60 million, which is part of a larger €424 million funding allocation for 42 projects under the CEF Alternative Fuels Infrastructure Facility. This funding is aimed at advancing hydrogen infrastructure and is the largest grant awarded under the Connecting Europe Facility for hydrogen initiatives.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2024**: Announcement of the third phase of the hydrogen mobility project, which includes the expansion of hydrogen refuelling stations and the establishment of a new hydrogen production plant in Szczecin.
- **Previous Phases**: The first two phases involved the deployment of eight hydrogen refuelling stations and the establishment of an automotive-grade hydrogen production plant in Wloclawek, supported by PLN 70 million in funding from the EU’s Connecting Europe Facility.
