### Evolutionary Timeline of the Industria Facility in Warsaw, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Goals**: Industria aims to produce 50,000 tonnes of low-carbon hydrogen annually using Rolls-Royce SMR technology as part of the Central Hydrogen Cluster initiative in Poland.
- **Technology Partnership**: On 13 March 2024, Industria signed a letter of intent with Chiltern Vital Group (CVG) to cooperate on the development of small modular reactors (SMRs) and related technologies.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Financial Resources**: Industria's President, Szczepan Ruman, emphasized the need for significant financial resources to achieve their goals, indicating that the cooperation with CVG is aimed at building RR SMR units and related technologies, which will require investment.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Cooperation Agreement**: On 13 March 2024, Industria and CVG signed a letter of intent to collaborate on various aspects, including training, technology exchange, and the development of financing models for SMR projects.
- **Previous Agreement**: In July 2023, Industria signed a letter of intent with the Kostrzyn-Słubicka Special Economic Zone SA (KSSSE) regarding the location of a modular power plant based on Rolls-Royce SMR technology.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation Potential**: The cooperation with CVG is expected to create future employment opportunities in Poland, Great Britain, and internationally, although specific job numbers were not mentioned.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: Industria is a state-owned entity, part of the Industrial Development Agency JSC (IDA). The facility's ownership structure in relation to the SMR technology and partnerships with CVG and Rolls-Royce SMR has not been specified as a joint venture or exclusive ownership.
