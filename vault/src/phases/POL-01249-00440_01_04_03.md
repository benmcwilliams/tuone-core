## POL-01249-00440

### Evolutionary Timeline of LG Chem Facility in Wroclaw, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2016**: LG Chem negotiates to buy a site in Wroclaw for an EV battery production facility, driven by increasing demand for electric vehicles in Europe.
- **October 2016**: Construction of the 42,300 square meter facility begins, with an annual production capacity of battery packs for over 100,000 EVs, targeting a range of up to 320 km. The facility is set to open in the second half of 2017.
- **September 2017**: LG Chem announces a further investment of $387 million to expand its EV battery business in Poland.
- **November 2018**: LG Chem plans to triple production capacity to produce enough battery cells for 300,000 EVs annually, with an investment of $571 million.
- **December 2019**: The European Bank for Reconstruction and Development lends €250 million for the expansion project, aiming for a production capacity of 1 million batteries by 2022.
- **March 2020**: LG Chem secures a €480 million loan from the European Investment Bank to expand production capacity to around 65 GWh by the end of 2022.
- **April 2020**: LG Chem announces plans to quadruple production capacity, aiming to produce batteries for 800,000 to 1 million electric cars.
- **December 2023**: LG Energy Solution signs a deal to supply 200,000 NCM battery modules for electric buses, to be manufactured at the Wroclaw plant.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2017**: LG Chem announces a $387 million investment for expansion.
- **November 2018**: A further investment of $571 million is planned to triple production capacity.
- **November 2019**: The EBRD lends €250 million for the expansion project.
- **March 2020**: LG Chem secures a €480 million loan from the EIB for further expansion.
- **April 2020**: LG Chem announces a total investment of approximately €1.5 billion for the expansion project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2016**: Negotiations to acquire the site in Wroclaw begin.
- **October 2016**: Construction of the facility starts.
- **Second half of 2017**: The facility opens.
- **September 2017**: Announcement of a $387 million investment for expansion.
- **November 2018**: Plans to triple production capacity are announced.
- **December 2019**: EBRD loan for expansion is confirmed.
- **March 2020**: EIB loan for further expansion is secured.
- **April 2020**: Announcement of plans to quadruple production capacity.
- **December 2023**: LG Chem signs a contract to supply battery modules for electric buses.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2018**: The expansion plans are expected to create 1,000 new jobs by 2022.
- **March 2020**: The expansion project is projected to employ about 6,000 full-time staff by the end of 2022.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2020**: LG Chem operates the facility as part of its battery division, which was later spun off as LG Energy Solution in December 2020. The facility remains under the ownership of LG Chem, now operating as LG Energy Solution.
