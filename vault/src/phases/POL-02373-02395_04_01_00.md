### Evolutionary Timeline of PKN Orlen Facility in Trzebinia, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2019**: PKN Orlen announced a partnership with Pesa to develop hydrogen fuel cell (HFC) trains, focusing initially on freight locomotives. The facility produces high-quality hydrogen at a rate of approximately 45 tonnes/hour as a byproduct of oil refining. 
- **2021**: The facility deployed its first hydrogen hub, producing grey hydrogen for transport, with plans to transition to low-carbon hydrogen from renewable sources.
- **2022**: PKN Orlen committed to installing 0.5 GW of new hydrogen production capacity using renewable energy sources and municipal waste conversion by the end of the decade.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2022**: PKN Orlen committed PLN 7.4 billion (€1.63 billion) by 2030 for low- and zero-carbon hydrogen development projects, which includes the establishment of ten hydrogen hubs across Poland, the Czech Republic, and Slovakia.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2019**: Announcement of the partnership with Pesa for HFC train development.
