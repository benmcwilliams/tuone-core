### Evolutionary Timeline of Jabil Facility in Kwidzyn, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2014**: Jabil Poland received two additional ‘Ypsator’ 1722-7 lamination lines from Robert Bürkle, increasing the facility's total nameplate capacity to 800 MW. The new lines added a combined capacity of 240 MW, allowing for high-volume production in a compact space.

- **February 2018**: Jabil announced the closure of its solar PV module production facility in Kwidzyn, which had a production capacity of 700 MW. The decision was attributed to an expected drop in demand for solar modules.

- **July 2018**: Recom acquired Jabil's solar module production lines, planning to restart manufacturing activities with the 700 MW production equipment in the first quarter of 2019.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **No specific investments or subsidies** were mentioned in the articles related to the Jabil facility in Kwidzyn.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2014**: Installation of two Ypsator lamination lines, increasing capacity to 800 MW.
  
- **February 2018**: Announcement of the closure of the solar module manufacturing facility due to insufficient demand.

- **July 2018**: Recom's acquisition of Jabil's production lines, with plans to restart production in early 2019.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2018**: The closure of the facility affected approximately 500 employees. Although they would continue to work in production for other clients, the closure marked a significant job loss in the solar manufacturing sector in Kwidzyn.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2014 - February 2018**: Jabil owned the facility exclusively.
  
- **July 2018**: Following the acquisition by Recom, Jabil no longer owned the facility, marking a transition in ownership to Recom.
