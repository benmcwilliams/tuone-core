### Evolutionary Timeline of Saule Technologies Facility in Wrocław, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2019**: Saule Technologies announced plans for its first prototype production line, targeting the production of flexible, lightweight, semi-transparent, single junction solar substrates with an initial capacity of 40,000 square meters per annum, with plans to expand to 200,000 square meters in 2020.
- **June 2021**: The facility officially inaugurated its new cell factory, which occupies approximately 5,000 m² and hosts a pilot production line. The facility aims to establish a highly efficient industrial line targeting a capacity of 100 MW. The cells produced are lightweight and flexible, suitable for various applications including powering IoT devices and building-integrated photovoltaics (BIPV).
- **November 2021**: Saule Technologies launched a solar-powered electronic shelf label solution for retail, utilizing their flexible perovskite solar cells. This product allows for centralized price changes across retail chains and is designed to last for 10 years.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2021**: Columbus Energy invested €10 million in Saule Technologies, becoming the main shareholder and acquiring priority purchase rights. This investment is part of a broader collaboration that includes a €250 million investment portfolio with MVGM group for commercial real estate management.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Q4 2019**: Expected launch of the prototype production line for perovskite solar substrates.
- **June 2021**: Inauguration of the perovskite solar cell factory in Wrocław, marking the commencement of commercial production of perovskite solar cells.
- **November 2021**: Launch of solar-powered electronic shelf labels, showcasing the application of the technology developed at the facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
