### Evolutionary Timeline of the PKN Orlen Facility in Włocławek, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2020**: PKN Orlen initiated the process of selecting a contractor for a hydrogen hub in Włocławek, which is expected to produce up to 600 kg of refined hydrogen per hour. The facility will include an installation for hydrogen production for transport fuel, logistic infrastructure, and refueling stations. The first stage of distribution will focus on public and freight transport, with the potential for passenger car refueling.

- **February 2022**: PKN Orlen announced that the Włocławek hydrogen hub is due to start producing green hydrogen in the second half of 2023. This facility is part of a broader strategy to develop ten hydrogen hubs by the end of the decade, with a focus on low- and zero-carbon hydrogen production.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2022**: PKN Orlen committed PLN 7.4 billion (€ 1.63 billion) by 2030 for low- and zero-carbon hydrogen development projects, which includes the development of the Włocławek facility. This investment is part of a larger plan to create a network of over 100 hydrogen refueling stations across Poland, the Czech Republic, and Slovakia.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
