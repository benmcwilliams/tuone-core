### Evolutionary Timeline of the Orlen Synthos Green Energy Facility in Włocławek, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: Orlen Synthos Green Energy (OSGE) announced the selection of seven potential sites for the construction of small modular reactors (SMRs) in Poland, including Włocławek. The SMRs will be based on GE Hitachi Nuclear Energy's BWRX-300 technology, for which OSGE holds exclusive rights in Poland.
- **December 2023**: OSGE submitted applications to the Ministry of Climate and Environment for decisions-in-principle for the construction of plants at six locations, including Włocławek. The ministry issued decisions-in-principle for the construction of a total of 24 BWRX-300 reactors at these locations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: OSGE announced a letter of intent for financing from US financial institutions, including EXIM Bank and DFC, indicating significant financial backing for the SMR projects.
- **December 2023**: The decisions-in-principle received from the Ministry of Climate and Environment are seen as a crucial step towards deep decarbonization of the Polish economy, enabling OSGE to launch a program to build a fleet of BWRX-300 reactors.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: OSGE shortlisted seven locations for further geological surveys for SMR construction, including Włocławek.
- **Late April 2023**: OSGE submitted applications for decisions-in-principle for the construction of SMR plants at six locations, excluding Warsaw.
- **December 2023**: The Ministry of Climate and Environment issued decisions-in-principle for the construction of 24 BWRX-300 reactors at the six selected locations.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2023**: OSGE indicated that the construction of SMRs would create jobs, although specific numbers were not detailed in the articles. The company emphasized the importance of local community engagement and the associated benefits of the projects.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2023**: Orlen Synthos Green Energy is identified as a joint venture between Synthos Green Energy (SGE) and PKN Orlen, indicating that the facility is not owned exclusively by one entity but rather through this partnership.
