## POL-04113-02167

### Evolutionary Timeline of the BayWa Facility in Witnica, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2020**: BayWa r.e. broke ground on a 64.6MWp solar farm in Witnica, marking it as Poland's largest solar project at the time. The project is designed to achieve grid parity for solar energy in Poland.
- **January 2021**: The facility is reported to be nearing completion and is equipped with 405-400Wp Mono PERC half-cell modules from JinkoSolar, Huawei string inverters, and Siemens MV transformers. It is expected to come online in the first half of 2021.
- **September 2021**: The facility is completed and connected to the grid, officially recognized as Poland's first subsidy-free, power purchase agreement (PPA)-backed commercial scale solar installation.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2020**: The Witnica project is developed without any subsidy support from the Polish government, relying instead on a long-term power purchase agreement with a local industrial client for financing.
- **January 2021**: BayWa r.e. secured a PPA with HeidelbergCement, marking it as a significant financial milestone for the project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2020**: Groundbreaking of the Witnica solar farm.
- **April 2020**: An extension for an additional 13MWac was arranged for grid connection.
- **Q1 2021**: Full commissioning of the solar farm is slated.
- **September 2021**: The facility is sold to Alternus Energy Group after being completed and connected to the grid.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the Witnica facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2021**: BayWa r.e. sold the 64.4MWp solar farm in Witnica to Alternus Energy Group. Post-sale, BayWa r.e. continues to provide operations and maintenance (O&M) services for the facility, indicating that they no longer own the facility but maintain a service relationship.
