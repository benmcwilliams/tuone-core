### Evolutionary Timeline of the HeidelbergCement Facility in Witnica, Poland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** January 2021
   - **Details:** The facility is associated with a solar power purchase agreement (PPA) for a solar park in Witnica, which is claimed to be Poland's first subsidy-free solar park. The solar farm has a production capacity of 65 MWp and utilizes 405-400Wp Mono PERC half-cell modules from JinkoSolar, Huawei string inverters, and Siemens MV transformers. The solar park is expected to come online in the first half of 2021.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** January 2021
   - **Details:** The project is part of a unique financial model that allows for a fixed price hedge against rising electricity costs, supporting HeidelbergCement's carbon neutrality targets. The investment is significant as it marks a shift towards competitive solar PV power generation in Poland, which traditionally relies heavily on coal.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** July 2020
   - **Details:** BayWa r.e. started construction on the Witnica solar park in July 2020, seven years after the initial development plan was proposed. The project was announced to be operational in the first half of 2021.
