## POL-04453-02092

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Huawei Facility in Kleczew, Poland

1. **Technology and Operations**:
   - **Date**: March 2023
   - **Details**: Huawei will supply its FusionSolar Smart PV Solution to a hybrid renewable energy park in Kleczew, which includes a 193MWp photovoltaic (PV) power plant and a 12MW wind power plant. The total capacity of the facility is 205MW, with an expected annual energy yield of approximately 222GWh from the PV plant and about 47GWh from the wind farm. This facility is noted as the largest renewable energy park in Central and Eastern Europe and the first in Poland to combine PV and wind technologies on such a large scale.

2. **Investment and Development**:
   - **Date**: January 2023
   - **Details**: The Lewandpol Group, the investor in the energy farm, secured a loan agreement worth PLN90 million (approximately US$20.7 million) from a fund partly managed by the state-owned Polish Development Fund. This loan was supplemented by a senior financing package of PLN776 million (approximately US$178.8 million) provided by a consortium of banks including ING, PKO BP, and mBank.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**:
   - **Date**: March 2023
   - **Details**: The announcement of Huawei's involvement in supplying technology for the construction of the hybrid renewable energy park marks a significant milestone in the project. The first phase of construction includes the establishment of the PV and wind power plants.

4. **Labour**:

   - **Details**: The article does not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the investments or expansions at the facility.

5. **Ownership Structure**:
   - **Date**: March 2023
   - **Details**: The facility is not exclusively owned by Huawei. The investor is the Lewandpol Group, and the asset manager is Ergy, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than full ownership by Huawei.
