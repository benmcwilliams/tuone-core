### Equinor Facility in Damnica, Poland - Evolutionary Timeline

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** June 2023
   - **Details:** The Zagórzyca solar plant, Equinor’s second solar facility in Poland, has started test production. It has a production capacity of 60 MW and will produce approximately 61 GWh of power annually, sufficient to supply electricity to around 31,000 Polish households. The facility comprises 111,000 solar panels spread over an area of approximately 55 hectares. 

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 2021 (acquisition)
   - **Details:** Equinor acquired Wento, a Polish renewable power producer, along with its 1.6 GW pipeline of solar projects. This acquisition laid the groundwork for the development of the Zagórzyca solar plant and other projects in Poland.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** June 2023
   - **Details:** The Zagórzyca solar plant was officially inaugurated and began test production. This marked the doubling of Equinor's solar capacity in Poland to around 120 MW, following the commercial production of its first solar plant, Stępień, and with another plant, Lipno, expected to be commissioned in 2024.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** June 2023
   - **Details:** Approximately 500 people were involved in the construction of the Zagórzyca solar plant, indicating significant job creation during the development phase. The use of local contractors contributed to building knowledge and skills within the Polish solar development industry.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** June 2023
   - **Details:** The Zagórzyca solar plant is developed and operated by Wento, which is a 100% subsidiary of Equinor. This indicates that Equinor has full ownership of the facility.
