## POL-04549-06340

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Wento Facility in Damnica, Poland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** June 2023
   - **Details:** The Wento facility, known as the Zagórzyca solar plant, has started test production. It has a production capacity of 60 MW and will produce approximately 61 GWh of power annually, sufficient to supply electricity to around 31,000 Polish households. The facility comprises 111,000 solar panels spread over an area of approximately 55 hectares. This facility is part of Equinor's broader strategy to build a renewable energy portfolio in Poland.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 2021 (acquisition date)
   - **Details:** Equinor acquired Wento and its 1.6 GW pipeline of solar projects in 2021. The Zagórzyca solar plant represents a significant investment in Poland's renewable energy sector, contributing to Equinor's goal of expanding its renewable energy offerings in the country.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** June 2023
   - **Details:** The Zagórzyca solar plant has officially launched and started test production. This marks the second solar plant developed by Wento in Poland, following the first plant, Stępień, which is already in commercial production. Another plant, Lipno, is expected to be commissioned in 2024.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** June 2023
   - **Details:** Approximately 500 people were involved in the construction of the Zagórzyca solar plant. The use of local contractors during construction contributed to building knowledge and skills within the Polish supplier industry.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 2021 (acquisition date)
   - **Details:** The Wento facility is fully owned by Equinor, as Wento is a 100% subsidiary of Equinor.
