### Evolutionary Timeline of the ORLEN Facility in Katowice, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2024**: ORLEN is piloting a hydrogen refuelling station in Katowice as part of its broader initiative to deploy hydrogen infrastructure across Poland. The facility is involved in the production and supply of automotive-grade hydrogen, contributing to the Clean Cities – Hydrogen mobility in Poland project. The company aims to establish a network of over 100 hydrogen refuelling stations by 2030.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2024**: ORLEN received a significant EU grant of €120 million for hydrogen refuelling infrastructure initiatives, with half of this amount allocated to ORLEN's project. This funding supports the third phase of the Clean Cities project, which includes the establishment of additional hydrogen refuelling stations and a new hydrogen production plant in Szczecin.
- **December 2024**: The Clean Cities project has attracted approximately €76 million in non-repayable funding from the EU's Connecting Europe Facility and PLN 20 million from Poland's National Fund for Environmental Protection and Water Management.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2024**: ORLEN announced the launch of hydrogen fuel supplies for public vehicles in Katowice, marking a significant milestone in the Clean Cities initiative.
- **December 2024**: ORLEN has successfully delivered over 90,000 kg of automotive-grade hydrogen to various cities, including Katowice, as part of its commitment to advancing sustainable mobility.

#### 4. Labour
