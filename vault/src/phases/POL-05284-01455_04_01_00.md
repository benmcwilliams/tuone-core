### Evolutionary Timeline of the ArcelorMittal Facility in Kraków, Poland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **November 2024:** ArcelorMittal announced plans to build a hydrogen production plant in Kraków to decarbonise its galvanising operations. The facility will ensure a reliable supply of hydrogen for the galvanising plants and is expected to be capable of using hydrogen by the end of 2026. The current operations include a hot rolling mill, cold rolling mill, galvanising, and sheet metal painting lines, which operate on natural gas. A project to build hydrogen furnaces has been completed to eliminate ammonia in the annealing plant.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **November 2024:** The hydrogen production plant is supported by a total investment of over PLN 100 million ($23.9 million). Additionally, ArcelorMittal has invested almost PLN 2.5 billion ($598 million) in Kraków’s rolling mills and galvanising plants to decarbonise its steel production and processing activities.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **November 2024:** Announcement of the hydrogen plant project in collaboration with Linde Gaz Polska, with plans for completion by the end of 2026.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
