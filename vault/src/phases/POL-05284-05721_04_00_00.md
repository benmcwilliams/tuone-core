### Evolutionary Timeline of the ORLEN Facility in Kraków, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2024**: ORLEN is advancing its hydrogen mobility project as part of the third phase of the ‘Clean Cities – Hydrogen mobility in Poland’ initiative. This phase includes the establishment of an additional 16 publicly available hydrogen refuelling stations across Poland, including one in Kraków. The facility will support hydrogen fuel supplies for public vehicles, city buses, private cars, and trucks. ORLEN aims to build an international network of over 100 hydrogen refuelling stations by 2030, with a total electrolyser capacity of about 1 GW, producing over 130,000 tonnes of renewable hydrogen annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2024**: ORLEN received a significant EU grant of €60 million, part of a total of €424 million allocated to 42 projects under the CEF Alternative Fuels Infrastructure Facility. This funding is aimed at advancing hydrogen infrastructure, including the expansion of the facility in Kraków.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2024**: Announcement of the third phase of the hydrogen mobility project, which includes the construction of additional refuelling stations and a new hydrogen production plant in Szczecin. The project is part of a broader strategy to enhance hydrogen infrastructure in Poland.
