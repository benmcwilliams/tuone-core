### Evolutionary Timeline of the ORLEN Facility in Pila, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2024**: ORLEN is advancing its hydrogen infrastructure as part of the ‘Clean Cities – Hydrogen mobility in Poland’ project. The facility will contribute to the establishment of hydrogen refuelling stations across Poland, including one in Pila. The project aims to deploy a total of 16 publicly available refuelling stations along the Trans-European Transport Network (TEN-T) and establish an automotive-grade hydrogen production plant in Szczecin. The ORLEN Group plans to build an international network of over 100 hydrogen refuelling stations by 2030, with a total electrolyser capacity reaching about 1 GW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2024**: ORLEN received a significant EU grant of €60 million as part of the funding for the third phase of its hydrogen mobility project. This funding is part of a larger €424 million disbursed across 42 projects under the CEF Alternative Fuels Infrastructure Facility. Previous phases of the project had already received PLN 70 million in funding under the EU’s Connecting Europe Facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2024**: Announcement of the third phase of the ‘Clean Cities – Hydrogen mobility in Poland’ project, which includes the establishment of additional hydrogen refuelling stations, including one in Pila.
