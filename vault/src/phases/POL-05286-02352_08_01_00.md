## POL-05286-02352

### Evolutionary Timeline of MHI Vestas Facility in Szczecin, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2022**: Vestas announced plans to establish a new assembly factory in Szczecin, Poland, to produce nacelles and hubs for its flagship offshore wind turbine, the V236-15.0 MW. The factory is expected to start operations in the second half of 2024.
- **January 2024**: Vestas revealed plans to establish a second factory in Szczecin to produce blades for the V236-15.0 MW turbine, expected to start operations in 2026.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: Vestas acquired the bankrupt Polish fabricator ST3 Offshore for PLN 170.5m (€36.3m), which will support the construction of the new assembly factory in Szczecin.
- **February 2023**: Vestas acquired a site in northern Szczecin for the new blade factory.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2022**: Announcement of the new assembly factory in Szczecin.
- **March 2023**: Acquisition of ST3 Offshore to support factory construction.
- **January 2024**: Announcement of the new blade factory in Szczecin.
- **February 2024**: Foundation stone laid for the nacelle and hub assembly factory, with operations expected to begin in 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2022**: The assembly factory is expected to create between 600 and 700 direct jobs.
- **January 2024**: The blade factory is expected to create over 1,000 direct jobs.
- **February 2024**: Combined, the two factories are projected to create more than 1,700 direct jobs by 2026.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Vestas is establishing both the nacelle assembly factory and the blade factory in Szczecin, indicating full ownership of these facilities. The acquisition of ST3 Offshore also suggests a strategy to consolidate operations under Vestas' control.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the MHI Vestas facility in Szczecin, Poland, focusing on technology, investment, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
