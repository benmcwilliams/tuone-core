### Evolutionary Timeline of the ORLEN Facility in Szczecin, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2024**: ORLEN announced plans to establish an automotive-grade hydrogen production plant in Szczecin as part of the third phase of its ‘Clean Cities – Hydrogen mobility in Poland’ project. This facility will utilize a water electrolysis process powered by renewable energy. The overall goal is to build an international network of over 100 hydrogen refuelling stations across Poland, the Czech Republic, and Slovakia by 2030, with a total electrolyser capacity of about 1 GW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2024**: ORLEN received a significant EU grant of €60 million (half of the total €120 million allocated for hydrogen refuelling infrastructure initiatives) to support the third phase of its hydrogen mobility project. This funding is part of the Connecting Europe Facility aimed at advancing the hydrogen economy in Poland.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2024**: Announcement of the establishment of a new hydrogen production plant in Szczecin as part of the expansion of hydrogen infrastructure in Poland. This is part of a broader initiative that includes the deployment of eight hydrogen refuelling stations and plans for an additional 16 stations across the country.
