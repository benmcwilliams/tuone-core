### Evolutionary Timeline of the Windar Facility in Szczecin, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2022**: Windar Renovables announced plans to build an offshore wind turbine tower factory in Szczecin, Poland, with an initial production capacity of 100 towers per year for turbines with outputs between 14 MW and 16 MW.
- **January 16, 2024**: Windar received final approval for the facility, which will now produce towers for the new generation of 20 MW offshore wind turbines.
- **October 14, 2024**: The factory is set to include four production lines for wind towers, including non-standard diameters, and will have a raw material warehouse and assembly halls. The facility is expected to start operations in the first half of 2026.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2022**: Windar announced an investment of EUR 80 million for the construction of the facility.
- **January 16, 2024**: The investment was updated to over EUR 100 million, reflecting the scale and capabilities of the planned facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 16, 2024**: Windar signed a concession agreement with the Szczecin Port Authority for a 17-hectare site for the factory.
- **September 3, 2024**: A construction permit was issued for the facility.
- **October 14, 2024**: Construction is expected to begin soon, with the factory planned to be operational by the first half of 2026.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 16, 2024**: The new facility is expected to create 450 direct jobs and will contribute to local economic development through collaboration with local suppliers.
- **October 14, 2024**: The factory is anticipated to create about 400 jobs, indicating a slight adjustment in job creation estimates.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Windar Renovables is the sole owner of the facility, as indicated by the announcements and agreements made regarding the construction and operation of the factory. There is no mention of joint ventures or partnerships in the ownership structure of the Szczecin facility.
