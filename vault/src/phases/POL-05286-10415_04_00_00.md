### Evolutionary Timeline of the West Pomeranian University of Technology Facility in Szczecin, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2023**: The West Pomeranian University of Technology, in collaboration with Grupa Azoty Police and Ultra Safe Nuclear Corporation (USNC), signed an agreement to develop a nuclear energy research facility based on USNC's Micro-Modular Reactor (MMR) technology. The facility will initially construct a 30 MWt MMR, which will serve as a training, research, and test facility. This MMR is designed to integrate as a zero-carbon generation source into Grupa Azoty Police's existing energy infrastructure.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: The agreement between the three parties indicates a commitment to prepare a comprehensive research program and a plan for the construction, operation, and maintenance of the MMR over the next six months. Specific investment amounts have not been disclosed, but the project aligns with Grupa Azoty's strategy to enhance renewable capacities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 29, 2023**: The tripartite agreement was signed, marking the official start of the project to develop the MMR facility. The first stage involves the construction of the 30 MWt MMR, with plans to integrate nuclear energy into chemical processes and generate steam and hydrogen at Grupa Azoty Police's plant.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify exact job creation or job loss figures related to this facility. However, the collaboration is expected to contribute to the development of a modern nuclear infrastructure and workforce in Poland, indicating potential job creation in the sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is a collaborative project involving three parties: Grupa Azoty Police, Ultra Safe Nuclear Corporation, and the West Pomeranian University of Technology. Therefore, it is not exclusively owned by any single entity but is a joint venture among the three organizations.
