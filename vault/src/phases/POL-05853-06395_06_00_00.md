### Evolutionary Timeline of Zespół Elektrowni Pątnów-Adamów-Konin SA Facility in Brudzew, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2021**: Zespół Elektrowni Pątnów-Adamów-Konin SA (ZE PAK SA) announced plans to build a 70 MW solar plant in Brudzew, Greater Poland Voivodeship. This facility is part of the company's transition from coal to renewable energy sources.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: ZE PAK SA secured a loan of 175 million PLN (approximately $45.5 million) from Polish banks (PKO BP SA, Bank Pekao SA, and mBank SA) to finance the solar project in Brudzew.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2021**: ZE PAK SA secured a 15-year power purchase agreement (PPA) for the 70 MW solar plant, marking a significant milestone in the project’s development. This PPA is with Polkomtel Sp. z o.o., the operator of the Polsat Group.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2021**: While specific job creation figures were not mentioned in the articles, the establishment of the solar plant is expected to contribute to job creation in the renewable energy sector, aligning with Poland's broader shift towards sustainable energy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2021**: ZE PAK SA is primarily owned by the Polish government, which holds a 50% stake. The company is not exclusively owned by a single entity, indicating a shared ownership structure.
