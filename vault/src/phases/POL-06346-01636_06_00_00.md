### Evolutionary Timeline of the Equinor Facility in Zagórzyca, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2023**: The Zagórzyca solar plant, with a production capacity of 60MW, began test production. It is equipped with 111,000 solar panels and spans an area of approximately 55 hectares. The plant is expected to generate 61 gigawatt-hours of clean energy annually, sufficient to power around 31,000 Polish homes. This facility is Equinor's second solar plant in Poland, doubling the company's solar capacity in the country to approximately 120MW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2021**: Equinor acquired Wento, a Polish renewable power producer, along with its pipeline of 1.6GW in solar projects. This acquisition laid the groundwork for the development of the Zagórzyca facility and other solar projects in Poland.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2023**: The Zagórzyca solar plant was inaugurated, marking the start of its test production phase. This facility is part of a broader strategy by Equinor to enhance its renewable energy offerings in Poland, which includes plans for additional solar plants and battery storage solutions.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2023**: Approximately 500 temporary jobs were created during the construction of the Zagórzyca solar plant.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **2021 Onwards**: The Zagórzyca solar plant is developed and operated by Wento, which is a 100% subsidiary of Equinor. This indicates that Equinor has full ownership of the facility.
