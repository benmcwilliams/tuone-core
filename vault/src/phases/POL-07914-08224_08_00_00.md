### Evolutionary Timeline of the AGH University Facility in Ometepe, POL

1. **Technology and Operations**:
   - **Date**: April 2021
   - **Details**: AGH University, in collaboration with an international research group, developed a mathematical model for a hybrid renewable energy system for Ometepe, Nicaragua. The proposed system combines wind and solar power generation with pumped hydro storage. The model aims to optimize the cost of energy (COE) while ensuring a reliable power supply, with solar expected to represent around 70% of the system's installed capacity.

2. **Investment and Development**:
   - **Date**: April 2021
   - **Details**: The study indicated that the COE for the proposed system could range from €0.047/kWh to €0.095/kWh, depending on the reliability of the power supply. The research also explored the potential for producing green hydrogen from surplus energy, although the implementation of a 5 MW electrolyzer was deemed challenging.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**:

### Technology and Operations
- "The proposed model was conceived for an energy system based exclusively on wind and solar power generation coupled with a mix of pumped hydro and battery storage."
- "The pumped hydro unit is expected to provide most of the storage operations and the battery bank is used only if the upper reservoir is full or is not able to operate."
- "Solar should represent around 70% of the system's installed capacity."

### Investment and Development
- "The sensitivity analysis conducted for different combinations of capex and loss of power supply probability (LPSP) estimated that the COE ranges between €0.047/kWh and €0.095/kWh, based on international reference capex values."

### Project Status and Milestones
