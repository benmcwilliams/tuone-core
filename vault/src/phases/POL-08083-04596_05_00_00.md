### Evolutionary Timeline of the Bechtel Facility in Choczewo, Poland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **April 2024:** Bechtel, as part of the Westinghouse-Bechtel consortium, is responsible for conducting geological research for the construction of Poland's first nuclear power plant, which will feature three AP1000 reactors. The facility will be located in the Choczewo municipality, covering an area of approximately 30 hectares. The geological research will involve constructing around 220 research points with depths ranging from 20 to 210 meters. The results of this research will inform the ongoing design process of the power plant.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **November 2022:** The Polish government selected Westinghouse AP1000 reactor technology for the construction of the nuclear power plant at the Lubiatowo-Kopalino site in Choczewo. An agreement for the delivery of the plant was signed in May 2023 between Westinghouse, Bechtel, and PEJ (the special-purpose vehicle responsible for the project).

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **April 2024:** The geological works project has received confirmation from the ministry that it meets the necessary requirements and is consistent with applicable law. The geological research is set to commence in May 2024, with the first stage expected to be completed within the same year. The aim is for the first AP1000 reactor to enter commercial operation by 2033.

4. **Labour:**
   - No specific information regarding job creation or job loss has been mentioned in the articles provided.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is being developed by PEJ, which is 100% owned by the State Treasury of Poland. Bechtel is part of a consortium with Westinghouse, indicating a collaborative ownership structure for the project rather than exclusive ownership by Bechtel.
