### Evolutionary Timeline of the Westinghouse Facility in Choczewo, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2022**: The Polish government selected the Westinghouse AP1000 reactor technology for construction at the Lubiatowo-Kopalino site in Choczewo, Pomerania. The facility will feature three AP1000 reactors.
- **April 2024**: Geological surveys for the nuclear power plant project commenced, with Bechtel conducting geological research on a 30-hectare site. The research involves constructing approximately 220 research points with depths ranging from 20 to 210 meters. The results will inform the ongoing design process for the power plant.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2023**: An agreement was signed between Westinghouse, Bechtel, and Polskie Elektrownie Jądrowe (PEJ) outlining the plan for the delivery of the nuclear power plant.
- **July 2023**: The Ministry of Climate and Environment issued a decision-in-principle for PEJ to construct the plant, marking a significant step in the investment process.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2024**: A symbolic kick-off ceremony was held to mark the start of geological surveys for the nuclear power plant. The fieldwork is expected to be completed by November 2024. The findings will be crucial for the earthworks design and the Location Report necessary for obtaining a construction permit from the National Atomic Energy Agency (PAA).
- **2033**: The target date for the first AP1000 reactor to enter commercial operation.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2024**: The commencement of geological surveys was highlighted as a significant moment for the Polish economy and local community, indicating potential job creation in the supply chain and construction phases of the project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2024**: PEJ, a special-purpose vehicle 100% owned by the State Treasury, is responsible for the construction project of the first nuclear power plant in Poland, indicating that the facility is not exclusively owned by Westinghouse but is part of a collaborative effort involving multiple entities.
