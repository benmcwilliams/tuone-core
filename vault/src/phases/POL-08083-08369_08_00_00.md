### Evolutionary Timeline of the Baltic Power Facility in Choczewo, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2022**: Baltic Power, a joint venture between Polish PKN Orlen and Canadian Northland Power, announced the launch of a tender for the construction of an operations and maintenance (O&M) base for the 1.2 GW offshore wind farm. The facility will include a social and office building, a warehouse, and a trans-shipment quay for crew transfer vessels. The offshore wind farm will consist of approximately 70 wind turbines located about 22 kilometers north of the Baltic coastline.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **06/2021**: Poland’s Energy Regulatory Office awarded a contract for difference (CfD) to the 1.2 GW Baltic Power project, indicating financial backing for the development.
- **09/2022**: The tender for the O&M base construction includes provisions for necessary technical infrastructure, indicating significant investment in the facility's operational capabilities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2021**: PKN Orlen and Northland Power announced that contracts for the Baltic Power project would be awarded within six months.
- **09/2022**: The tender for the O&M base was launched, with the contract expected to start in July 2022 and run until November 2024.
- **05/2022**: Baltic Power initiated a prequalification process for offshore construction services and equipment, with construction of the wind farm planned to begin in 2023 and completion expected in 2026.

#### 4. Labour
- **05/2022**: The prequalification for tendering services and equipment for offshore construction is expected to create job opportunities, particularly for offshore technicians and manpower resources during the construction period from 2024 to 2026.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **09/2022**: The Baltic Power facility is a joint venture between Polish PKN Orlen and Canadian Northland Power, indicating a shared ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by a single entity.
