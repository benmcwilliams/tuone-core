### Evolutionary Timeline of the PEJ Facility in Choczewo, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2024**: PEJ (Polska Elektrownia Jądrowa) is responsible for the construction of Poland's first nuclear power plant, featuring three Westinghouse AP1000 reactors. The facility will be located in the Lubiatowo-Kopalino site in the Choczewo municipality, Pomerania. The first AP1000 reactor is expected to enter commercial operation in **2033**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2024**: The geological research project, conducted by Bechtel as part of a US consortium, is confirmed to meet investment requirements. This research is crucial for the ongoing design process of the nuclear power plant.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2024**: Geological research begins at the proposed site, covering approximately 30 hectares, with about 220 research points to be constructed.
- **August 2024**: PEJ seeks approval for preparatory works on an area of about 300 hectares, which will be conducted in two phases. The first phase involves surveying and fencing the area, as well as vegetation removal with mitigation measures.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2024**: While specific job creation numbers are not mentioned, the preparatory works and eventual construction of the nuclear power plant are expected to create jobs, particularly in the local communities of Choczewo and neighboring areas.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2024**: PEJ is a special-purpose vehicle that is **100% owned by the State Treasury** of Poland, indicating full ownership of the facility.
