## POL-08083-09759

### Evolutionary Timeline of Polskie Elektrownie Jądrowe Facility in Choczewo, POL

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: Polskie Elektrownie Jądrowe (PEJ) submitted an application for a decision-in-principle to construct Poland's first large nuclear power plant, indicating a maximum total installed capacity of up to 3750 MWe using US AP1000 reactor technology. The construction of the first nuclear power plant is scheduled to start in **2026**, with the first reactor expected to be commissioned in **2033**.
- **April 2024**: A kick-off ceremony was held to mark the start of geological surveys at the Lubiatowo-Kopalino site in Choczewo, which will inform the design and construction of the nuclear power plant featuring three Westinghouse AP1000 units.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: The application for the decision-in-principle was submitted following the amended special nuclear act, which facilitates the development of nuclear power facilities in Poland. This act aligns with Poland's Energy Policy until 2040 and the EU's climate policy.
- **April 2024**: The geological surveys are part of the preparatory work for the construction phase, which is crucial for the investment process.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: PEJ submitted the application for a decision-in-principle, marking a significant step towards obtaining necessary approvals for construction.
- **August 2023**: PEJ signed an agreement with the Warsaw University of Technology to develop nuclear skills, indicating progress in workforce preparation for the upcoming project.
- **April 2024**: The IAEA conducted a review mission to assess Poland's readiness for the nuclear power plant project, confirming the structured progress towards construction.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2023**: PEJ committed to organizing paid internships for graduates from the Warsaw University of Technology, indicating job creation and skills development in the nuclear sector as part of the broader nuclear power program.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2023**: PEJ is a special purpose vehicle that is **100% owned by the State Treasury**, indicating full ownership of the facility by the Polish government.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones related to the Polskie Elektrownie Jądrowe facility in Choczewo, Poland, highlighting its technological advancements, investment efforts, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
