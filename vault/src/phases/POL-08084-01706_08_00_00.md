## POL-08084-01706

### Timeline of the Hitachi Facility in Polish Baltic Sea, POL

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 11-01-2023
   - **Details:** Hitachi Energy has been contracted to provide an AC grid connection for the MFW Bałtyk II and MFW Bałtyk III offshore wind farms. These jointly-owned wind farms will have a combined generating capacity of 1,440 MW and are scheduled to generate first power in 2027. Hitachi Energy's responsibilities include delivering the complete control system, telecommunications network, high-voltage equipment for both offshore and onshore substations, and the turnkey delivery of the onshore substation.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 11-01-2023
   - **Details:** The project is part of a larger development program for the Polish offshore wind sector, which aims for a total capacity of 5,900 MW. The final investment decision for both Bałtyk II and III projects is planned for 2024, contingent upon obtaining necessary permits.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 11-01-2023
   - **Details:** The Bałtyk II and III projects were awarded Contracts for Difference (KTR) by the Energy Regulatory Office (ERO) in May 2021. The projects are in the planning phase, with the first power generation expected in 2027.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** 11-01-2023
   - **Details:** The article does not specify job creation or job loss directly related to the Hitachi facility, but it mentions the development of local supply chains and the establishment of a nacelle factory by Vestas in Poland, indicating potential job creation in the region.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** 11-01-2023
   - **Details:** The wind farms (MFW Bałtyk II and III) are jointly owned by Equinor and Polenergia, indicating that Hitachi Energy does not have exclusive ownership of the facility but is a contractor providing essential services for the project.
