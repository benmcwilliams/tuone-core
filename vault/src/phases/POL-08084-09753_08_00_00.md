### Evolutionary Timeline of the Polenergia Facility in Polish Baltic Sea, POL

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2018**: Polenergia, in partnership with Equinor, announced the development of two offshore wind projects, Polenergia Bałtyk II and Polenergia Bałtyk III, with a combined capacity of 1,200 MW (600 MW each). The projects are located 27 km and 40 km off the port of Łeba. 
- **January 2023**: The combined generating capacity of the two wind farms was updated to 1,440 MW, with the first power generation expected in 2027.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2021**: The Energy Regulatory Office (ERO) awarded Contracts for Difference (KTR) for Bałtyk II and III, which are part of the first phase of the development program for the Polish offshore wind sector, totaling 5,900 MW. 
- **2024**: The final investment decision for both projects is planned, contingent upon obtaining necessary permits.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2018**: Announcement of the joint ownership and development of Polenergia Bałtyk II and III with Equinor, including legally binding environmental decisions and grid connection agreements.
- **2025**: Expected start of power delivery from Polenergia Bałtyk III to the Polish grid.
- **2027**: Expected start of power delivery from Polenergia Bałtyk II to the Polish grid.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2018**: Polenergia is jointly owned by Polska Grupa Energetyczna (PGE) and Equinor for the development of the offshore wind projects. PGE announced a tender offer to acquire a 100% stake in Polenergia, indicating a potential shift towards full ownership in the future.
