### Evolutionary Timeline of the Westinghouse Facility in Baltic Coast, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: The facility is planned to construct a nuclear power plant with a capacity of 3.75 GW.
- **Updates**: The project is still in the planning stages, with the first operations expected to start in 2040, as per the latest updates.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Funding Approval**: On January 7, 2025, Poland approved funding of up to 60.2 billion zlotys (approximately $14.7 billion) for the nuclear power plant project. This funding will consist of 30% equity and 70% debt financing.
- **Initial Investment**: In August 2024, Poland announced an investment of 4.6 billion zlotys (about $1.2 billion) from its 2025 budget to kickstart the development of the nuclear power plant.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Environmental Permit**: An environmental permit for the plant was issued in 2023.
- **Construction Timeline**: The construction of the nuclear power plant is expected to be completed by 2036, with operations starting in 2040.
- **Funding Negotiations**: The project is awaiting approval from the European Union, with discussions expected to extend beyond one year.

#### 5. Ownership Structure

### Summary
The Westinghouse facility on the Baltic coast of Poland is set to be the country's first nuclear power plant, with a planned capacity of 3.75 GW. The project has received significant funding approvals and is in the early stages of development, with operations expected to commence in 2040. The facility is a joint venture involving Westinghouse Electric and the state-owned PEJ, reflecting a collaborative approach to modernizing Poland's energy infrastructure.
