### Evolutionary Timeline of Bechtel Facility in Lubiatowo-Kopalino, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2022**: The Polish government selected Westinghouse’s AP1000 pressurized water reactor technology for the construction of the nuclear power plant at the Lubiatowo-Kopalino site.
- **September 2023**: Westinghouse and Bechtel signed a consortium agreement to design and build the nuclear power facility, which will feature three AP1000 reactors, each with a capacity of 1.27 GW.
- **April 2024**: The IAEA conducted a review of Poland's nuclear infrastructure, confirming the project's progress towards construction.
- **May 2024**: Bechtel and Westinghouse estimated the cost of one reactor at $7 billion, with concrete for the first reactor expected to be poured in 2028.
- **December 2024**: The project aims for the first AP1000 reactor to enter commercial operation in 2033.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2023**: An engineering services agreement was signed between PEJ, Westinghouse, and Bechtel, enabling site work to commence.
- **May 2024**: The Polish government announced plans to allocate PLN 60 billion for the nuclear power plant project from 2025 to 2030.
- **December 2024**: PEJ received letters of intent for financing from various international entities, totaling over PLN 95 billion.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2022**: The Polish government selected Westinghouse’s AP1000 technology.
- **July 2023**: The Ministry of Climate and Environment issued a decision-in-principle for PEJ to construct the plant.
- **September 2023**: The consortium agreement was signed, marking a significant milestone in the project.
- **April 2024**: IAEA mission confirmed the project's readiness for the next stages.
- **May 2024**: Geological surveys commenced, marking the start of preparatory works for the construction.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2023**: Bechtel expressed eagerness to partner with the local workforce and suppliers, indicating potential job creation as the project progresses.
- **August 2024**: Bechtel began engaging Polish entrepreneurs in the supply chain, suggesting further job opportunities in the local economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is being developed by Polskie Elektrownie Jądrowe (PEJ), a special purpose vehicle that is 100% owned by the Polish State Treasury. Bechtel and Westinghouse are partners in the project but do not have ownership stakes in the facility itself.
