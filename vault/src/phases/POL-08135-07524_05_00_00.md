### Evolutionary Timeline of the Westinghouse Electric Company Facility in Lubiatowo-Kopalino, Poland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **November 2022:** The Polish government selected the Westinghouse AP1000 reactor technology for the construction of the nuclear power plant at the Lubiatowo-Kopalino site in the Choczewo municipality.
   - **03-09-2024:** The facility is set to produce nuclear energy with plans for three nuclear power units, including infrastructure for inflow basins and cooling water channels.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **03-09-2024:** Poland plans to allocate PLN 4.6 billion ($1.2 billion) in its 2025 budget to start preparations for the nuclear power plant. The total project cost is estimated at PLN 60 billion for the period of 2025-2030, with further funding expected from the US for technology.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **May 2023:** An agreement was signed defining a plan for the delivery of the plant by Westinghouse, Bechtel, and Polish utility Polskie Elektrownie Jądrowe (PEJ).
   - **July 2023:** Poland’s Ministry of Climate & Environment issued a decision-in-principle for PEJ to construct the plant.
   - **03-09-2024:** The symbolic kick-off ceremony for site geological surveys was held, marking the beginning of preparations for the construction of Poland’s first nuclear power plant.

4. **Labour:**
   - **03-09-2024:** Bechtel is in the process of engaging Polish entrepreneurs in the supply chain for the nuclear power plant project, indicating potential job creation through contracts with Polish subcontractors.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **03-09-2024:** The facility is a collaborative project involving Westinghouse Electric Company, Bechtel, and Polish utility Polskie Elektrownie Jądrowe (PEJ), indicating a joint venture rather than exclusive ownership by Westinghouse.
