## POL-08135-08179

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Westinghouse Facility in Lubiatowo-Kopalino, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2022**: The Polish government selected Westinghouse's AP1000 reactor technology for the construction of the nuclear power plant at Lubiatowo-Kopalino.
- **September 2023**: Westinghouse and Bechtel signed a consortium agreement to design and build the nuclear power facility, which will feature three AP1000 reactors, each with a power generating capacity of 1.27 GW.
- **Expected Operations Start**: The nuclear power plant is projected to begin operations in **2033**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2024**: The Polish Nuclear Power Programme timeline indicates that construction is set to begin in **2026**, with the first unit's commissioning targeted for **2033**.
- **December 2024**: PEJ has received letters of intent for financing from various entities, including Bpifrance Assurance Export and Sfil, totaling over PLN 15 billion (USD 3.75 billion). Additional commitments have been made by Export Development Canada and the US International Development Finance Corporation, bringing total financial commitments to over PLN 95 billion.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2023**: The Polish Ministry of Climate and Environment issued a decision-in-principle for Polskie Elektrownie Jądrowe (PEJ) to construct the plant.
- **September 2023**: An engineering services agreement was signed between PEJ and the Westinghouse/Bechtel consortium, allowing site-specific design work to commence.
- **April 2024**: The construction of the first reactor is expected to begin in **2028**, two years later than initially planned.
- **August 2024**: PEJ submitted an application for preparatory works, including surveying and fencing the site, which will be conducted in phases.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2024**: A report by PricewaterhouseCoopers (PwC) projected that the construction phase of six AP1000 units will create over **204,990 person-years** of direct employment in Poland. Once operational, the units are expected to support **16,300 jobs** annually.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is being developed by **Polskie Elektrownie Jądrowe (PEJ)**, which is a special purpose vehicle **100% owned by the State Treasury** of Poland. Westinghouse and Bechtel are partners in the project but do not own the facility.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones related to the Westinghouse facility in Lubiatowo-Kopalino, Poland, focusing on its technology, investments, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
