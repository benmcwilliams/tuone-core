### Evolutionary Timeline of the Polskie Elektrownie Jądrowe Facility in Lubiatowo-Kopalino, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2022**: The Polish government selected the Westinghouse AP1000 reactor technology for construction at the Lubiatowo-Kopalino site.
- **September 2023**: An engineering services contract was signed with Westinghouse and Bechtel to finalize a site-specific design for the plant, which will feature three AP1000 reactors with a total capacity of 3750 MWe.
- **2033**: The first AP1000 reactor is expected to begin operations, although this was later updated to 2036.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2023**: The Polish government announced an intention to allocate PLN 60 billion ($14.5 billion) to fund the plant.
- **December 2024**: PEJ received letters of intent for financing totaling more than PLN 95 billion from various international financial institutions, including the US International Development Finance Corporation and French export credit agencies.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2023**: The Ministry of Climate and Environment issued a decision-in-principle for PEJ to construct the plant.
- **May 2023**: An agreement was signed between Westinghouse, Bechtel, and PEJ outlining the delivery plan for the plant.
- **October 2023**: The Pomeranian Voivode issued a decision determining the location for the nuclear plant, marking a significant milestone.
- **December 2024**: The updated Polish Nuclear Energy Programme indicated a delay in the commercial operation of the first unit to 2036.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2023**: The project is expected to create thousands of jobs for local Polish construction workers and suppliers, as stated by Bechtel's president.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **100% Ownership**: Polskie Elektrownie Jądrowe (PEJ) is a special-purpose vehicle that is 100% owned by Poland's State Treasury, responsible for the construction of the nuclear power plant.
