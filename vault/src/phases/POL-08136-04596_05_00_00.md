### Evolutionary Timeline of the Bechtel Facility in Pomerania, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2023**: Bechtel, in consortium with Westinghouse and Polskie Elektrownie Jądrowe (PEJ), announced the design and construction of Poland's first AP1000 nuclear power plant in Pomerania. The plant is projected to have a capacity of 3750 MWe, with the first reactor expected to be operational by 2033.
- **August 2023**: PEJ confirmed plans for the construction of the nuclear power plant, which will include multiple reactors, with the first unit having a capacity of 1.0-1.6 GWe, and subsequent units to be implemented every 2-3 years.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2024**: A letter of intent was signed with Export Development Canada (EDC) to support the sale of goods and services by Canadian suppliers for the Pomerania plant. This follows earlier financing commitments from the US International Development Finance Corporation and the US Export-Import Bank, which collectively amount to over USD 980 million and PLN 70 billion (USD 17.3 billion) respectively.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2022**: The Polish government selected Westinghouse AP1000 technology for the Pomerania plant.
- **May 2023**: An agreement was signed between Westinghouse, Bechtel, and PEJ to outline the delivery plan for the nuclear power plant.
- **June 2023**: PEJ applied for a general opinion from the PAA President regarding safety analyses, marking a significant step in the licensing process.
- **August 2023**: PEJ submitted an application for a decision on the location of the investment, a key milestone in obtaining administrative approvals for construction.
- **July 2024**: The Ministry of Climate and Environment issued a decision-in-principle for PEJ to construct the plant.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2023**: The Polish government expressed intentions to utilize local content, including Polish enterprises and human resources, in the construction of the nuclear power plant, indicating potential job creation in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: The facility is a project of Polskie Elektrownie Jądrowe (PEJ), which is 100% owned by the Polish State Treasury. Bechtel and Westinghouse are partners in a consortium for the design and construction of the facility, indicating a joint venture structure for this project.
