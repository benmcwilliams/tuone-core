## POL-08136-08179

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Westinghouse Facility in Pomerania, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2023**: Westinghouse and Polskie Elektrownie Jądrowe (PEJ) finalize a contract for pre-design work on Poland's first nuclear power plant (NPP) in Pomerania, utilizing AP1000 reactor technology. The plant will feature six AP1000 pressurized water reactors with a total generation capacity of 6 GW to 9 GW. The first unit is expected to be commissioned in 2032 or 2033, with all units operational by 2040.
- **July 2023**: PEJ applies for a decision-in-principle from the Ministry of Climate and Environment, detailing the project characteristics and confirming the use of Westinghouse AP1000 technology.
- **August 2023**: PEJ submits an application for a siting decision, which is crucial for determining the land necessary for the construction of the nuclear power plant.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2022**: An agreement is signed between Westinghouse, Bechtel, and PEJ for the delivery of the nuclear plant.
- **August 2023**: The Polish government announces an intention to allocate PLN 60 billion to fund the construction of the nuclear power plant.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2022**: The Polish government announces the construction of the first nuclear power plant in Pomerania, confirming a capacity of 3750 MWe using Westinghouse technology.
- **April 2023**: PEJ applies for a general opinion from the National Atomic Energy Agency (PAA) regarding safety analyses, marking a significant step in the administrative approval process.
- **July 2023**: The Ministry of Climate and Environment issues a decision-in-principle for the nuclear power plant, allowing PEJ to proceed with further administrative decisions.
- **August 2023**: PEJ seeks a siting decision, a key milestone for the project.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2023**: PEJ announces plans to collaborate with the Warsaw University of Technology to develop a curriculum and training programs for personnel in the nuclear sector, indicating a focus on job creation and skills development in the industry.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2023**: It is confirmed that PEJ, a special-purpose vehicle, is 100% owned by the State Treasury of Poland, indicating full ownership of the facility by the Polish government.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones related to the Westinghouse facility in Pomerania, Poland, focusing on its technology, investments, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
