### Evolutionary Timeline of the Polskie Elektrownie Jadrowe Facility in Pomerania, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2023**: Polskie Elektrownie Jadrowe (PEJ) has finalized a contract with Westinghouse for pre-design work on Poland's first nuclear power plant (NPP) located in Pomerania. The facility will utilize AP1000 reactor technology and is planned to feature six pressurized water reactors with a total generation capacity of 6 GW to 9 GW. The first unit is expected to be commissioned in 2032 or 2033, with all six units anticipated to be operational by 2040.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2023**: The contract with Westinghouse includes provisions for the development of nuclear and other technologies necessary for the project. This agreement is part of a broader effort by the Polish government to enhance energy security and reduce carbon emissions.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2023**: The contract for pre-design work marks a significant milestone in the project, allowing PEJ to commence initial engineering works ahead of the formal execution contract. This proactive approach is based on lessons learned from other nuclear projects globally.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2023**: The project is expected to create jobs in the nuclear energy sector, particularly through the training of personnel in collaboration with AGH University of Science & Technology in Krakow. This initiative aims to prepare graduates for roles in the nuclear energy field, contributing to Poland's energy security.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2023**: The facility is owned by Polskie Elektrownie Jadrowe (PEJ), which is leading the nuclear power projects in Poland. The project is being developed in partnership with Westinghouse, indicating a collaborative approach rather than exclusive ownership.
