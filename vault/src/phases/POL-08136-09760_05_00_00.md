### Evolutionary Timeline of the Polskie Elektrownie Jądrowe (PEJ) Facility in Pomerania, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2023**: PEJ, in collaboration with Westinghouse and Bechtel, initiated design activities for Poland's first AP1000 nuclear power plant, with plans to potentially sign a construction contract in 2025. The facility aims to begin generating nuclear power by 2033, contributing to Poland's goal of transitioning from coal to nuclear energy.
- **December 2024**: The facility is set to feature three AP1000 reactors, with the design and engineering documentation including essential components such as the nuclear island and turbine island.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2024**: PEJ signed a letter of intent with Export Development Canada (EDC) to support the sale of goods and services by Canadian suppliers, contingent on successful due diligence and credit approval. This follows a previous commitment from the US International Development Finance Corporation to provide over USD 980 million in financing for the project.
- **September 2024**: The Polish government announced an intention to allocate PLN 60 billion to fund the construction of the nuclear power plant.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2021**: PEJ was established as a state-owned entity tasked with developing the investment process for Poland's first nuclear power plant.
- **November 2022**: The Polish government selected Westinghouse AP1000 technology for the Pomerania plant.
- **May 2023**: A formal agreement was signed between Westinghouse, Bechtel, and PEJ to define the principles of cooperation for the design and construction of the nuclear power plant.
- **February 2024**: PEJ and Westinghouse concluded a contract for pre-design work, marking a significant step towards the engineering services contract.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2023**: The project is expected to create jobs in the local economy, with a focus on utilizing Polish enterprises and human resources as part of the construction and operational phases.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2021**: PEJ is a special-purpose vehicle that is 100% owned by Poland's State Treasury, indicating full state ownership of the facility. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones in the evolution of the PEJ facility, highlighting its technological, financial, and operational aspects as it progresses towards becoming Poland's first nuclear power plant.
