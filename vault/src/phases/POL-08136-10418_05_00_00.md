### Evolutionary Timeline of the Westinghouse Electric Co. Facility in Pomerania, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2023**: Westinghouse Electric Co. and Bechtel, in collaboration with the state-owned Polskie Elektrownie Jądrowe (PEJ), announced the initiation of design activities for Poland's first AP1000 nuclear power plant, which will be located in Pomerania. The facility aims to contribute to Poland's goal of generating nuclear power by 2033.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2023**: The consortium formed by Westinghouse, Bechtel, and PEJ is part of a strategic agreement to manage the design and construction of the nuclear power plant. The Polish government has established a framework for investment, with PEJ taking on most of the initial investment risks.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2022**: Poland selected Westinghouse technology for its first large-scale nuclear plant amid a competitive selection process.
- **December 2022**: PEJ signed a cooperation agreement with Westinghouse following the technology selection.
- **February 2023**: Westinghouse and PEJ concluded a contract for pre-design work.
- **May 2023**: The consortium announced a timeline for the project, with construction expected to start in 2026 and the plant operational by 2033.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2023**: The Polish government expressed its commitment to utilizing local content, including Polish enterprises and human resources, in the construction and operation of the nuclear facility, indicating potential job creation in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2023**: The facility will be developed under a consortium model, with Westinghouse and Bechtel collaborating with PEJ, which is 100% state-owned. This indicates a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by Westinghouse.
