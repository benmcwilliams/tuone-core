## POL-08722-01636

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Equinor Facility in Port of Leba, Poland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** May 2021
   - **Details:** Equinor has acquired a site at the Port of Leba to construct, own, and operate an operations and maintenance (O&M) base. This facility will support the Baltyk 1, 2, and 3 offshore wind projects, which have a combined potential capacity of 1440 MW. The O&M base will serve as a logistics hub, including office, storage, and transportation facilities, optimizing safety and operational costs for the offshore wind farms.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** May 2021
   - **Details:** Equinor is making a long-term investment in the Port of Leba as a future hub for offshore wind in Poland. The investment is aimed at creating local value and supporting the development of the Baltyk offshore wind projects.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** May 2021
   - **Details:** Equinor completed the acquisition of the site for the O&M base. The Baltyk 2 and Baltyk 3 projects were awarded contracts for difference (CfD) by Poland’s Energy Regulatory Office on May 4, 2021. Construction of the wind farms is anticipated to begin as soon as 2024.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** May 2021
   - **Details:** The establishment of the O&M base is expected to create local job opportunities, including maintenance technician roles primarily based offshore, as well as office-based teams to support operations from land.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** May 2021
   - **Details:** Equinor will exclusively own the facility at the Port of Leba, although it is jointly developing the Baltyk projects in partnership with Polenergia.
