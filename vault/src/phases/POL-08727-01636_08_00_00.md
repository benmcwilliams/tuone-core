### Equinor Facility in Ustka, Poland - Evolutionary Timeline

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility is involved in the development of two offshore wind farms, MFW Bałtyk II and MFW Bałtyk III, with a combined production capacity of 1,440 MW (two 720 MW wind farms). The wind farms will utilize Siemens Gamesa 14 MW wind turbines.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The projects were awarded Contracts for Difference (CfDs) in May 2021 by Poland’s Energy Regulatory Office (ERO), which indicates a commitment to financial support for the development of these offshore wind farms.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **March 2023:** Geotechnical surveys were completed, involving boreholes up to 55 meters below the seabed. These surveys are crucial for planning and designing the foundations for the wind turbines and offshore substations, as well as for determining the export cable corridor to connect to land transmission infrastructure.

   - The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the facility at this time.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is a joint venture between Equinor and Polenergia, as they are jointly developing the offshore wind projects.
