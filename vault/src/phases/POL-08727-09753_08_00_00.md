### Evolutionary Timeline of the Polenergia Facility in Ustka, Poland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **March 2023:** Polenergia, in partnership with Equinor, is developing the MFW Bałtyk II and MFW Bałtyk III offshore wind farms in the Polish Baltic Sea. These projects will have a combined production capacity of 1,440 MW, with each wind farm featuring 720 MW capacity and utilizing Siemens Gamesa 14 MW wind turbines.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **May 2021:** The MFW Bałtyk II and MFW Bałtyk III projects were awarded Contracts for Difference (CfDs) by Poland’s Energy Regulatory Office (ERO), indicating financial support for the development of these offshore wind farms.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **March 2023:** Completion of geotechnical surveys for the offshore wind projects, which included obtaining data from boreholes up to 55 meters below the seabed. This data is crucial for planning and designing the foundations for the wind turbines and offshore substations.

4. **Labour:**
   - No specific information regarding job creation or job loss has been mentioned in the articles provided.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is a joint venture between Polenergia and Equinor, indicating that it is not exclusively owned by Polenergia.
