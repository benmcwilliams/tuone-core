### Evolutionary Timeline of Ørsted Facility in Osieki Lęborskie, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: Ørsted and PGE announced the appointment of a Polish consortium as the contract engineer for the Baltica Offshore Wind Farm, which includes the onshore connection infrastructure located in Osieki Lęborskie. The project aims to provide 2.5 GW of green energy by 2030, with two stages: Baltica 2 (1.5 GW) and Baltica 3 (1 GW).
- **January 2024**: Ørsted and PGE selected GE Vernova and Polimex Mostostal as the general contractors for the onshore connection for the 1.5 GW Baltica 2 offshore wind farm. The infrastructure will include a 275/400 kV high-voltage transformer substation and onshore export cable lines, with construction starting in 2024 and expected to be operational by 2027.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2024**: The construction of the onshore infrastructure for Baltica 2 is part of a larger investment strategy by Ørsted and PGE to enhance Poland's renewable energy capacity. The total capacity of the Baltica Offshore Wind Farm (including Baltica 2 and Baltica 3) will be 2.5 GW, significantly contributing to Poland's energy transition.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: Agreement signed for the contract engineer for the Baltica Offshore Wind Farm, marking a significant step towards the project's development.
- **January 2024**: Contracts finalized for the construction of the onshore infrastructure for Baltica 2, with construction set to begin in 2024 and the first electricity expected to be fed into the Polish Power System by 2027.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2024**: The construction and operation of the Baltica Offshore Wind Farm, including the onshore infrastructure, are expected to create jobs in the region, although specific job creation numbers have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Ørsted and PGE are joint venture partners in the Baltica Offshore Wind Farm project, with both companies collaborating on the development and construction of the facility. There is no indication of exclusive ownership by either company.
