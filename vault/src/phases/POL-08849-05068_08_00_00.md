### Evolutionary Timeline of the GE Vernova Facility in Osieki Lęborskie, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2024**: GE Vernova, in consortium with Polimex Mostostal, has been selected as the general contractor for the onshore infrastructure of the 1.5 GW Baltica 2 offshore wind farm. The facility will develop a 275/400 kV high-voltage transformer substation and onshore export cable lines necessary for transmitting power to the Polish Power System. Construction is set to begin in 2024, with testing and activation scheduled for 2026 and 2027.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2024**: The contract for the construction of the onshore infrastructure is part of the larger investment in the Baltica offshore wind farm project, which includes both Baltica 2 and Baltica 3, aiming for a combined capacity of 2.5 GW. The completion of these projects is contingent upon a final investment decision.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2024**: GE Vernova and Polimex Mostostal are confirmed as contractors for the onshore infrastructure of the Baltica 2 project. Construction works are planned to start in 2024, with the first electricity expected to be fed into the Polish Power System in 2027.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2024**: While specific job creation numbers are not mentioned, the announcement of the construction project implies potential job creation associated with the development of the onshore infrastructure for the Baltica 2 offshore wind farm.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2024**: GE Vernova is part of a consortium with Polimex Mostostal for the Baltica 2 project, indicating a joint venture structure for this specific facility's operations. GE Vernova is represented in Poland by GE Power Sp. z o.o. 

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the GE Vernova facility in Osieki Lęborskie, Poland, focusing on its role in the Baltica 2 offshore wind farm project.
