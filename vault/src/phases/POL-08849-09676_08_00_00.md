### Evolutionary Timeline of the PGE Group Facility in Osieki Lęborskie, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2024**: PGE Group and Ørsted selected GE Vernova and Polimex Mostostal as the consortium to construct the onshore infrastructure for the 1.5 GW Baltica 2 offshore wind farm. This includes the development of a 275/400 kV high-voltage transformer substation and onshore export cable lines necessary for transmitting power to the Polish Power System. The first construction works are planned to start in 2024, with testing and activation scheduled for 2026 and 2027.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2024**: The announcement of the contract for the construction of the onshore infrastructure indicates significant investment in the Baltica 2 project, which is part of a larger initiative to enhance Poland's renewable energy capacity. The total capacity of the Baltica offshore wind farm, including Baltica 2 and Baltica 3, will be 2.5 GW.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2024**: PGE Group and Ørsted confirmed the selection of contractors for the onshore infrastructure of the Baltica 2 offshore wind farm. Construction is set to commence in 2024, with the goal of operationalizing the facility by the end of 2027. The first electricity from Baltica 2 is expected to be fed into the Polish Power System in 2027.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2024**: While specific job creation numbers have not been detailed, the construction of the onshore infrastructure and the overall development of the Baltica offshore wind farm are expected to create numerous jobs in the region, contributing to local employment.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2024**: The facility is a joint venture between PGE Group and Ørsted, indicating that it is not exclusively owned by one company but rather developed collaboratively to leverage both companies' expertise in offshore wind energy.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones related to the PGE Group facility in Osieki Lęborskie, Poland, focusing on the Baltica 2 offshore wind farm project.
