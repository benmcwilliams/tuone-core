### Evolutionary Timeline of Geotermia Pyrzyce Facility in Pyrzyce, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2019**: The Geotermia Pyrzyce facility utilizes geothermal energy for heating supply, drawing hot water from a depth of 1,640 meters at a temperature of 64 degrees Celsius. The facility has over 20 years of experience in exploiting geothermal waters in the West Pomeranian region and employs innovative methods such as super soft acid treatment to prevent clogging.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2019**: An agreement was signed involving the NFEPWM, Geotermia Pyrzyce, the municipality of Pyrzyce, WFOSiGW in Szczecin, and the Prime Minister’s Plenipotentiary for the Clean Air program. This agreement aims to develop a model program to utilize geothermal resources for local heating systems, indicating potential financial support and investment in the facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 14, 2019**: The project “Pyrzyce Laboratory” was inaugurated, focusing on using geothermal energy to combat low emissions in the municipality. A working team was established to develop a roadmap for reducing low emissions in Pyrzyce.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify exact job creation or job loss figures related to the investments or expansions at the Geotermia Pyrzyce facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Geotermia Pyrzyce facility is not exclusively owned by a single company. It is a collaborative effort involving multiple stakeholders: NFOSiGW, WFOSiGW in Szczecin, and the municipality of Pyrzyce, indicating a joint ownership structure.
