### Evolutionary Timeline of Grupa Azoty Police Facility in Police, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2023**: Grupa Azoty Police, in collaboration with Ultra Safe Nuclear Corporation (USNC) and the West Pomeranian University of Technology, signed an agreement to build a nuclear energy research facility based on USNC's Micro-Modular Reactor (MMR) technology. The first stage involves constructing a 30 MWt MMR, which will serve as a training, research, and test facility. This facility will be integrated with Grupa Azoty Police's existing energy infrastructure, aiming to study and optimize the MMR as a zero-carbon energy source for chemical processes, steam, and hydrogen generation.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: The agreement signifies a commitment to develop a comprehensive research program and a detailed plan for the construction, operation, and maintenance of the MMR. The project aligns with Grupa Azoty's strategy to enhance renewable energy capacities, targeting nearly 380 MW by 2030.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 29, 2023**: The tripartite agreement was signed, marking the official start of the project to develop the MMR facility. The parties will work over the next six months to finalize the research program and construction plans.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2023**: While specific job creation figures were not mentioned, the collaboration is expected to contribute to the development of a modern nuclear infrastructure and workforce in Poland, indicating potential job creation in the nuclear energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2023**: The facility is a collaborative project involving Grupa Azoty Police, USNC, and the West Pomeranian University of Technology, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by Grupa Azoty.
