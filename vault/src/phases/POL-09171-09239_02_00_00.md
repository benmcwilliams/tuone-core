### Evolutionary Timeline of the Institute of Mineral and Energy Economy Facility in Gminą Sękowa, Poland

1. **Technology and Operations**:
   - **Date**: 08-06-2021
   - The facility is involved in a hybrid renewable energy project that utilizes a deep-borehole heat exchanger (DBHE) powered by photovoltaics. The project aims to generate heat for district heating.
   - The original geothermal project, Sękowa GT-1, involved a borehole with a depth of 3 km, which was intended to extract geothermal energy for heating. However, the borehole did not yield the expected geothermal water.
   - The DBHE is designed to extract heat from medium-depth geothermal energy, reaching temperatures of up to 90 degrees Celsius for space heating. The expected water temperature from the DBHE is around 60 degrees Celsius, with a heat demand estimated at approximately 3,768 gigajoules per year.
   - A photovoltaic (PV) system with an area of about 150 m² will power the heat pumps and circulation pump of the DBHE, with excess electricity being fed into the grid.
