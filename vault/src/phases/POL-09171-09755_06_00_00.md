### Evolutionary Timeline of the Polish Ministry of Environment Facility in Gminą Sękowa, POL

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date**: 08-06-2021
  - The facility is based on a hybrid renewable energy project utilizing a deep-borehole heat exchanger (DBHE) and heat pumps powered by photovoltaics. 
  - The original geothermal project, Sękowa GT-1, involved a borehole with a depth of 3 km, intended to extract geothermal energy for district heating. However, the borehole was found unusable as it did not yield water with the expected parameters.
  - The DBHE is designed to extract heat from medium-depth geothermal energy, reaching temperatures of up to 90 degrees Celsius for space heating. The expected water temperature from the DBHE is around 60 degrees Celsius, with a heat demand estimated at approximately 3,768 gigajoules per year.
  - A photovoltaic (PV) system with a surface area of around 150 m² will power the heat pumps and circulation pump of the DBHE, with excess electricity being injected into the grid.
