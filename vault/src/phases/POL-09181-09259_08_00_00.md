### Evolutionary Timeline of the Istrana Facility in Port of Gdańsk, POL

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Production Focus:** The Istrana facility will serve as an offshore wind terminal, specifically designed for the storage, assembly, and offloading of offshore wind components, including wind turbine blades, nacelles, and wind turbine towers.
   - **Production Capacity:** The terminal will be built on 21 hectares of reclaimed land and will feature deep-water berths to accommodate large offshore wind components.
   - **Operational Timeline:** The terminal is expected to be operational by 2026.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Total Investment:** The total investment cost for the offshore wind terminal is EUR 253 million.
   - **Funding Sources:** The Polish government has secured approximately EUR 194 million in support from the European Commission under EU State aid rules, funded through the Recovery and Resilience Facility (RRF). The remaining funds will be provided by Istrana.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Announcement and Selection:** On June 25, 2024, the Port Authority announced the selection of Istrana to build and operate the offshore wind terminal, following a procedure initiated in 2022.
   - **Construction Start:** Construction work on the terminal is anticipated to begin in the summer of 2024.
   - **Lease Agreement:** Istrana will operate the terminal under a lease agreement with the Port Authority until 2055.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Job Creation:** Specific job creation figures have not been detailed in the articles, but the construction and operation of the terminal are expected to generate employment opportunities in the region.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Ownership:** Istrana is owned by Baltic Hub Container Terminal, indicating that it is not an exclusively owned facility but part of a larger corporate structure.
