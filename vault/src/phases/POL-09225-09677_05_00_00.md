### Evolutionary Timeline of PGE PAK Energia Jądrowa Facility in Pątnów, Poland

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **November 2023:** PGE PAK Energia Jądrowa submitted an application for the construction of a nuclear power plant utilizing APR1400 technology. The facility is planned to have two units generating a total of 22 TWh of electricity annually, which will meet approximately 12% of Poland's current electricity demand.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **April 2023:** PGE PAK Energia Jądrowa was formed as a 50/50 joint venture between ZE PAK and Polska Grupa Energetyczna (PGE) to implement the nuclear power project.
   - **November 2023:** The Polish Ministry of State Assets issued a decision-in-principle confirming that the investment project aligns with public interest and state energy policies, allowing the company to proceed with further administrative arrangements.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **October 2022:** A letter of intent was signed by Poland's Ministry of State Assets, South Korea's Ministry of Trade, Industry and Energy, ZE PAK, PGE, and Korea Hydro and Nuclear Power (KHNP) to develop plans for the nuclear power plant in Pątnów.
   - **November 2023:** The issuance of the decision-in-principle marks a significant milestone, enabling the project to move forward towards obtaining necessary permits for construction.

4. **Labour:**
   - **November 2023:** While specific job creation figures were not mentioned, the project is expected to provide employment opportunities in the region, contributing to local economic development.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **April 2023:** PGE PAK Energia Jądrowa is a joint venture, with equal ownership (50/50) between ZE PAK and Polska Grupa Energetyczna (PGE).
