### Evolutionary Timeline of Last Energy Facility in Katowice Special Economic Zone, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2023**: Last Energy announced the development of 10 PWR-20 small modular reactor (SMR) units in the Katowice Special Economic Zone (KSSE). Each unit has a production capacity of 20 MWe, contributing to a total capacity of 200 MWe for the facility. The first plant is expected to be commissioned in 2026. The PWR-20 design is characterized by its modular construction, allowing for rapid deployment and scalability to meet customer energy needs.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: The power purchase agreements (PPAs) signed with KSSE represent over $4.3 billion in electricity sales over the contract's lifetime and include $1 billion in inward energy and infrastructure investment in the zone. This investment is aimed at supporting the development of the facility and enhancing energy infrastructure in the region.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2023**: Last Energy secured PPAs for 10 SMR units in KSSE, marking a significant milestone in the development of nuclear power in Poland. The agreements are part of a larger initiative that includes a total of 34 units across Poland and the UK, with a combined electricity sales value exceeding $18.9 billion. The commissioning of the first plant is targeted for 2026.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2023**: The establishment of the facility is expected to contribute to job creation within the KSSE, which is home to 540 companies and 90,000 industrial jobs. The specific number of jobs created as a result of this investment has not been detailed, but the project is anticipated to enhance the competitiveness of the industrial zone and support long-term employment.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2023**: Last Energy is positioned as the project developer and operator of the facility in KSSE. While the articles do not specify joint ventures or partnerships regarding ownership, Last Energy appears to be pursuing a model of full ownership and operation of the nuclear power plants, leveraging private capital for financing rather than government funding.
