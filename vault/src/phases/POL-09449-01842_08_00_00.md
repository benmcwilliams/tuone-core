### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ørsted Facility in Baltica 2, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: Ørsted, in partnership with PGE Group, announced the development of the Baltica 2 offshore wind project, which will have a production capacity of 1.5GW. The facility will consist of 107 Siemens Gamesa wind turbines, each with a capacity of 14MW. The project is expected to generate enough clean energy to power 2.4 million Polish homes and displace 5.4 million tonnes of carbon emissions annually. The facility is located 40km from the northern coast of Poland and is expected to begin operations in 2027.
- **October 2023**: Ørsted and PGE Group finalized a contract with Steelwind Nordenham for the supply of monopile foundations for the Baltica 2 project. This contract marked the completion of all key component contracts for the offshore part of the facility. The entire Baltica Offshore Wind Farm is planned to reach a total capacity of 2.5GW by the end of the decade.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: The Baltica 2 project is described as the largest offshore wind investment in the Polish part of the Baltic Sea and one of the largest globally, with a total investment capacity of 1.5GW for the first stage. The project is part of Poland's strategy to enhance energy security and develop new green electricity sources.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: Announcement of the wind turbine order to Siemens Gamesa for the Baltica 2 project, with operations expected to start in 2027.
- **October 2023**: Finalization of the last Tier 1 contract for the supply of monopile foundations, indicating advanced preparations for the project. Production of monopiles is set to begin in 2024 and 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2023**: The project is expected to create new industries in Poland, as indicated by the interest from Polish companies to contribute to the project. The Suppliers Day organized by PGE and Ørsted highlighted the potential for job creation and collaboration with local suppliers.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Ørsted and PGE Group are joint partners in the Baltica 2 project, with no indication of exclusive ownership by either company. The collaboration is focused on developing the largest renewable energy project in Poland.
