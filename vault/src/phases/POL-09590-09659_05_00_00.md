### Evolutionary Timeline of the Orlen Synthos Green Energy Facility in Ostrołęka, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: Orlen Synthos Green Energy (OSGE) announced seven potential sites for the construction of small modular reactors (SMRs) in Poland, including Ostrołęka. The SMRs will be based on GE Hitachi Nuclear Energy's BWRX-300 technology, for which OSGE holds exclusive rights in Poland.
- **December 2023**: OSGE submitted applications to the Ministry of Climate and Environment for decisions-in-principle for the construction of plants at six locations, including Ostrołęka. The ministry issued decisions-in-principle for the construction of a total of 24 BWRX-300 reactors at these locations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: OSGE announced that it would utilize funds awarded under the US Project Phoenix to study the site at Ostrołęka. This project aims to support energy security and climate goals through coal-to-SMR power plant conversions.
- **September 2023**: OSGE was awarded funds as part of the Project Phoenix, which is a US government initiative to support energy transitions in central and eastern Europe.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: OSGE shortlisted seven locations for further geological surveys for SMR plants, including Ostrołęka.
- **December 2023**: The Ministry of Climate and Environment issued decisions-in-principle for the construction of 24 BWRX-300 reactors at six locations, marking a significant milestone in the project development process.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2023**: The Project Phoenix initiative aims to retain local jobs through workforce retraining as part of the transition to SMR technology, indicating potential job creation in the Ostrołęka area.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: Orlen Synthos Green Energy is a joint venture between Synthos Green Energy (SGE) and PKN Orlen, indicating that the facility is not under exclusive ownership but rather a collaborative effort between these two companies.
