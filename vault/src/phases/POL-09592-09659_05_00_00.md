### Evolutionary Timeline of the Orlen Synthos Green Energy Facility in Tarnobrzeg Special Economic Zone, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: Orlen Synthos Green Energy (OSGE) announced the selection of seven potential sites for the construction of small modular reactors (SMRs) in Poland, including Tarnobrzeg Special Economic Zone. The SMRs will be based on GE Hitachi Nuclear Energy's BWRX-300 technology, for which OSGE holds exclusive rights in Poland.
- **December 2023**: OSGE submitted applications to the Ministry of Climate and Environment for decisions-in-principle for the construction of plants at six locations, including Tarnobrzeg. The ministry issued decisions-in-principle for the construction of a total of 24 BWRX-300 reactors at these locations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: OSGE announced a letter of intent for financing from US financial institutions, including EXIM Bank and DFC, which indicated a commitment of USD 4 billion to support the construction of the first two power plants developed by OSGE.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: OSGE shortlisted seven locations for further geological surveys for SMR construction, including Tarnobrzeg. The company planned to analyze the feasibility of building the first small modular block at these sites by 2030.
- **December 2023**: The Ministry of Climate and Environment issued decisions-in-principle for the construction of 24 BWRX-300 reactors at six selected locations, marking a significant milestone in the administrative process for nuclear power facility investments.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2023**: OSGE indicated that the construction of SMRs would create job opportunities, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2023**: Orlen Synthos Green Energy is identified as a joint venture between Synthos Green Energy (SGE) and PKN Orlen, indicating that the facility is not solely owned by one entity but rather a collaboration between these two companies.
