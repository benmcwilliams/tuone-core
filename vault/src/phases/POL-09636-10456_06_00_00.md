### Evolutionary Timeline of the ZE PAK SA Facility in Turek, Poland

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2021**: The ZE PAK facility in Turek completed the construction of Poland's largest solar power plant with a production capacity of 70 MW. The plant consists of 155,554 photovoltaic modules, each with an output of 455 W, and includes 306 inverters and 31 transformer stations with a capacity of 2 MVA each. The facility was built on a site previously occupied by the Adamów brown coal mine.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2021**: The total investment for the solar power project was approximately PLN 163.8 million ($40.4 million), which was lower than the initially estimated PLN 200 million ($49.3 million) announced in November 2018 when the project was launched.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2021**: ZE PAK secured a 15-year power purchase agreement (PPA) with Polish telecommunications group Polsat for the solar power generated by the facility.
- **November 2021**: The solar plant officially came online, marking a significant milestone in ZE PAK's transition towards renewable energy sources.
