## PRT-00676-02453

### Evolutionary Timeline of Stellantis Group Facility in Mangualde, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2023**: Stellantis announced plans to manufacture battery-electric vans at the Mangualde facility starting in early 2025. The models to be produced include the Citroën ë-Berlingo, Fiat e-Doblò, Opel Combo-e, and Peugeot e-Partner. The facility will also feature its own battery assembly line. The expected production capacity is nearly 50,000 battery electric vehicles (BEVs) per year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2023**: The transformation of the Mangualde plant is part of the ‘GreenAuto’ project, which has an estimated investment volume of €119 million. The Portuguese government is supporting this initiative with €59.8 million from its Recovery and Resilience Plan.
- **December 2022**: Stellantis signed a power purchase agreement (PPA) for 4,000 MWh of solar power at the Mangualde manufacturing plant, contributing to its renewable energy goals.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: Announcement of the transition to electric vehicle production at the Mangualde facility, marking a significant milestone in its operational evolution.
- **October 2021**: The ‘GreenAuto’ project was launched, which aims to modernize the Mangualde facility for electric vehicle production.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2023**: The transition to electric vehicle production is expected to create 450 new jobs at the Mangualde facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify the ownership structure of the Mangualde facility, but it is implied that Stellantis operates the facility as part of its global manufacturing operations. There is no mention of joint ventures or shared ownership in the provided articles.
