## PRT-00998-00315

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Galp Facility in Setúbal, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2021**: Galp, in a 50:50 joint venture with Northvolt named Aurora, announced plans to build a lithium conversion plant in Setúbal, Portugal. The plant is expected to have an annual production capacity of up to 35,000 tonnes of battery-grade lithium hydroxide, with commercial operations slated to begin in 2026. The plant is positioned to be the largest and most sustainable integrated lithium conversion facility in Europe.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2021**: The estimated investment for the project is around €700 million, with plans to seek subsidies to support the development. 

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2021**: The joint venture Aurora was established, and the location for the lithium hydroxide plant was confirmed to be Setúbal. The commissioning of the plant is expected by the end of 2025, with commercial operations starting in 2026.
- **February 2024**: Galp announced the cancellation of the lithium conversion plant project following Northvolt's exit from the joint venture. Despite efforts to find new partners, Galp stated that the current context and lack of an international partner made it impossible to continue with the project.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2021**: The project was projected to create over 200 direct jobs and more than 3,000 indirect jobs in the region.
- **February 2024**: With the cancellation of the project, the anticipated job creation associated with the facility will not materialize.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2021**: The facility was to be developed as a 50:50 joint venture between Galp and Northvolt under the name Aurora.
- **February 2024**: Following Northvolt's withdrawal, Galp is no longer pursuing the project, indicating that the joint venture is effectively dissolved, and Galp does not exclusively own the facility.

This timeline captures the key developments regarding the Galp facility in Setúbal, highlighting its initial promise and subsequent cancellation due to external financial pressures and partnership challenges.
