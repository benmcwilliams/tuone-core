## PRT-00998-00582

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Northvolt Facility in Setúbal, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2021**: Northvolt, in a 50:50 joint venture with Galp named Aurora, announced plans to build a lithium conversion plant in Setúbal, Portugal. The plant is expected to have an annual production capacity of up to 35,000 tonnes of battery-grade lithium hydroxide, with commercial operations slated to begin in 2026. The facility aims to be the largest and most sustainable integrated lithium conversion plant in Europe.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2021**: The estimated investment for the facility is around €700 million, with plans to seek subsidies to support the project. This investment is tied to the establishment of the plant and its production capacity.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2021**: The location for the lithium hydroxide plant was confirmed to be Setúbal, Portugal. The commissioning of the plant is expected by the end of 2025, with commercial operations starting in 2026.
- **February 2024**: Galp announced the cancellation of plans for the lithium conversion plant following Northvolt's exit from the project. The decision was made due to Northvolt's worsening financial situation and inability to find new partners for the project.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2021**: The project was expected to create up to 1,500 direct and indirect jobs, with more than 200 direct jobs and over 3,000 indirect jobs anticipated in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2021**: The facility was to be developed as a joint venture (50:50) between Northvolt and Galp under the name Aurora. However, following Northvolt's exit in early 2024, the future ownership structure of the facility became uncertain, leading to the project's cancellation.

This timeline captures the key developments related to the Northvolt facility in Setúbal, Portugal, highlighting its intended operations, investments, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure.
