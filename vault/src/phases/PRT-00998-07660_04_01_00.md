## PRT-00998-07660

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Construção e Manutenção Electromecânica (CME) Facility in Setúbal, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: The facility is part of the Green H2 Setúbal Project, which aims to produce green hydrogen with a capacity of 135 kg (1,500 Nm3) per hour. The hydrogen will be produced using electrolyzers powered by a 12 MW solar PV plant.
- **Updates**: The hydrogen produced will be of high purity (>99.999%) and will be suitable for various applications, including sale to off-takers and injection into the Portuguese natural gas distribution network.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investments**: The project has received €5 million in CAPEX support from the EU-funded Operational Programme for Sustainability and Efficient Use of Resources (PO SEUR) to aid in its development.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Tender Announcement**: On March 2022, Hyperion Renewables launched an EPC tender for the construction of the facility.
- **Bidding Process**: As of April 2022, seven companies, including CME in partnership with French electrolyzer producer Elogen, were selected to compete in the second stage of the tender.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: While specific job creation numbers have not been detailed in the articles, the involvement of CME and other companies in the construction and operation of the facility is expected to create jobs in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture**: The facility is not solely owned by CME; it is part of a partnership with Elogen for the production of electrolyzers, indicating a joint venture structure for this project.
