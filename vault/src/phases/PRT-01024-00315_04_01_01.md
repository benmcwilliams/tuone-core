## PRT-01024-00315

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Galp Facility in Sines, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2020**: The H2Sines project is initiated, aiming to create a green hydrogen industrial cluster in Sines. The first phase includes a 10 MW electrolysis pilot plant, with plans to expand to 1 GW of electrolysis capacity by 2030 and potentially 1.5 GW in the long term.
- **June 2021**: Galp announces plans to install a 100 MW electrolyzer at its Sines refinery by 2025, with potential expansion to 1,000 MW.
- **September 2023**: Galp confirms a final investment decision for a 100 MW electrolyzer unit, expected to produce up to 15 ktpa of green hydrogen, with operations starting in 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2021**: Galp estimates a €1 billion investment for the conversion of its refinery to green hydrogen, including solar power generation.
- **December 2021**: A consortium led by EDP and Galp receives a €30 million grant from the EU for the 100 MW green hydrogen project in Sines.
- **September 2023**: Galp announces a total investment of €250 million for the 100 MW electrolyzer project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2020**: The H2Sines project is announced, with a focus on green hydrogen production and export.
- **June 2021**: Galp plans to install a 100 MW electrolyzer by 2025.
- **December 2021**: The consortium for the 100 MW project is selected by the European Commission.
- **March 2023**: Construction for the 100 MW project is expected to begin in 2023, with operations slated for 2025.
- **September 2023**: Final investment decision made for the 100 MW electrolyzer, with a startup planned for 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2021**: The transition to green hydrogen is expected to create jobs in the renewable energy sector, although specific job creation figures are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2021**: Galp is identified as the primary owner of the facility, with plans for joint ventures and collaborations with other companies like EDP, Martifer, and Vestas for the H2Sines project and the 100 MW electrolyzer project. Galp will own 75% of a joint venture with Mitsui for renewable diesel and green hydrogen production.
