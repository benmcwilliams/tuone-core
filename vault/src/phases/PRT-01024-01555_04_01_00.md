## PRT-01024-01555

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners Facility in Sines, Portugal

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **April 2022:** The MadoquaPower2X plant, a collaboration between Madoqua Renewables, Power2X, and Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (CIP), is planned to be built in Sines. The facility will have an electrolysis capacity of **500 MW** and is expected to generate **50,000 tons of green hydrogen** and **500,000 tons of green ammonia** annually. There are plans for future expansions to produce **1 million tons of green ammonia** by the end of the decade.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **April 2022:** The total investment for the project is estimated at **€1 billion (US$1.07 billion)**. The project is expected to be powered by **1 GW of renewable energy**, primarily from wind and solar sources.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **April 2022:** The project is already under development, with a **final investment decision** anticipated by the end of **2023**. Production of green hydrogen is expected to start by **2025**, and the goal is to begin exporting green ammonia by **2026**. Subsequent phases of development are set to start in **2024** and reach full commissioning before **2030**.

4. **Labour:**
   - The articles do not provide specific details regarding job creation or job loss associated with the facility.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is a joint venture involving **Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (CIP)**, **Madoqua Renewables**, and **Power2X**. Therefore, CIP does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a consortium.
