## PRT-01024-01587

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDP Facility in Sines, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2020**: EDP is developing a pilot hydrogen production project at the Ribatejo Combined Cycle Plant, utilizing a 1 MW electrolyzer with a storage capacity of 12 MWh.
- **July 2020**: The H2 Sines project is announced, aiming to create a green hydrogen industrial cluster in Sines. The initial phase will include a 10 MW electrolysis pilot plant, with plans to expand to 1 GW of electrolysis capacity by 2030.
- **December 2021**: A consortium led by EDP and Galp is selected to develop a 100 MW green hydrogen production project in Sines, with construction expected to start in 2023 and operations in 2025.
- **January 2022**: EDP and partners plan to develop a 100 MW hydrogen project in Sines, with funding of €30 million from the EU's Horizon 2020 program.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2020**: The Portuguese government received expressions of interest for hydrogen projects amounting to €16 billion, with Sines identified as a key area for green hydrogen production.
- **November 2021**: EDP announces plans to invest in 1.5 GW of renewable hydrogen by 2030, significantly increasing its initial target.
- **December 2021**: The consortium for the 100 MW project in Sines is awarded a €30 million grant from the European Commission.
- **November 2022**: A Canadian-Portuguese joint venture plans a $1.04 billion hydrogen plant in Sines, including a 500 MW electrolyzer.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2020**: Announcement of the H2 Sines project and the signing of a Memorandum of Understanding among EDP, Galp, and other partners.
- **December 2020**: The H2 Sines project is recognized as one of the world's largest green hydrogen projects, with a planned capacity of 1 GW.
- **2023**: Construction of the 100 MW hydrogen project is expected to begin.
- **2025**: Operations for the 100 MW hydrogen project are anticipated to start, subject to approvals.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2020**: The development of the hydrogen cluster in Sines is expected to contribute to job creation in the region, although specific job numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2020**: EDP is part of a consortium with Galp, Martifer, REN, and Vestas for the H2 Sines project, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership.
- **December 2021**: The consortium for the 100 MW project includes EDP, Galp, and several other partners, further confirming a collaborative ownership model.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the EDP facility in Sines, Portugal, focusing on its evolution in technology, investment, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
