### Engie Facility in Sines, Portugal - Evolutionary Timeline

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04-01-2022**: Engie is part of a consortium developing the GreenH2Atlantic project, which aims to produce 100MW of green hydrogen. The facility will utilize scalable and fast-cycling 8MW electrolyser modules and will be integrated with a local hybrid renewable power plant. The project is set to transition the former coal-fired power plant area into a renewable hydrogen production hub.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04-01-2022**: The GreenH2Atlantic project has received €30 million ($33.9 million) in funding from the European Union’s Horizon 2020 – Green Deal Call research and innovation programme to finance the construction of the 100MW electrolyser plant.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04-01-2022**: Construction works on the GreenH2Atlantic plant are expected to begin in 2023, with operations scheduled to start in 2025, pending necessary approvals.
- **18-07-2022**: Engie, as part of a consortium with Shell and others, is working on establishing a green hydrogen production facility in the Sines port industrial zone. The consortium aims to produce, transport, and store around 100 tonnes of hydrogen per day and has applied for Important Projects of Common European Interest (IPCEI) status from the EU.
