## PRT-01024-02101

### Martifer Facility in Sines, Portugal - Evolutionary Timeline

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2020**: The H2Sines project is initiated, aiming to create a green hydrogen industrial cluster in Sines. The project will start with a 10 MW electrolysis pilot plant, with plans to expand to 1 GW of electrolysis capacity over the decade. Long-term goals include achieving 1.5 GW of renewable electric energy generation capacity to support the electrolysers.
- **January 2022**: A new project, GreenH2Atlantic, is announced, which will develop a 100 MW green hydrogen production facility in Sines. This facility will utilize scalable 8 MW modules and is expected to transition the area from coal-fired power to renewable hydrogen production.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2020**: The H2Sines project is estimated to cost €1.5 billion ($1.84 billion) and is expected to be completed by 2030.
- **January 2022**: The GreenH2Atlantic project receives €30 million ($33.9 million) in funding from the European Union’s Horizon 2020 – Green Deal Call research and innovation programme to support the construction of the 100 MW electrolyser plant.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2020**: The H2Sines project is officially announced, with a memorandum of understanding signed among EDP, Galp, Martifer, REN, and Vestas to study the feasibility of the project.
- **January 2022**: Construction for the GreenH2Atlantic project is expected to begin in 2023, with operations slated to start in 2025, pending approvals.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2022**: The GreenH2Atlantic project is expected to create jobs as it transitions the former coal-fired power plant area into a renewable hydrogen production hub, contributing to local employment and sustainability goals.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2020**: The H2Sines project is a consortium effort involving EDP, Galp, Martifer, REN, and Vestas, indicating a joint venture ownership structure.
- **January 2022**: The GreenH2Atlantic project also involves a consortium including EDP, Galp, Martifer, and other partners, further confirming the joint venture nature of ownership for the facility.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones of the Martifer facility in Sines, Portugal, focusing on its evolution in technology, investment, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
