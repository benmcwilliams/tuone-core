## PRT-01024-02109

### Evolutionary Timeline of the REN Facility in Sines, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2020**: The H2Sines project is initiated, aiming to create a green hydrogen industrial cluster in Sines. The facility will start with a **10 MW electrolysis pilot plant** and plans to expand to **1 GW of electrolysis capacity** by the end of the decade, with a long-term goal of **1.5 GW of renewable electric energy generation** to supply the electrolysers.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2020**: The total expected cost for the H2 Sines project is estimated at **€1.5 billion ($1.84 billion)**. The project is designed to ensure financial feasibility through gradual development and optimization of hydrogen production and consumption.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2020**: A consortium including EDP, Galp, Martifer, REN, and Vestas signs a **Memorandum of Understanding** to study the feasibility of the H2Sines project.
- **August 2020**: The project is confirmed to be in the planning stages, with the first phase involving the installation of the **10 MW electrolysis pilot project**.
- **December 2020**: The H2 Sines project is recognized as one of the **world's largest green hydrogen projects**, with a planned completion date of **2030** for the full **1 GW facility**.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify exact job creation or job loss figures related to the H2 Sines project. However, the project is expected to contribute to the re-industrialization of the Portuguese economy, which may imply potential job creation in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is developed as a **consortium** involving multiple partners: EDP, Galp, Martifer, REN, and Vestas. Therefore, REN does not own the facility exclusively; it is part of a **joint venture** with other companies.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic plans for the REN facility in Sines, Portugal, focusing on the H2Sines project aimed at establishing a significant green hydrogen production capability.
