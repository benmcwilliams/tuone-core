## PRT-01024-02241

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fusion Fuel Facility in Sines, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2022**: Fusion Fuel Green, in collaboration with KEME Energy, announced the installation of a green hydrogen plant in Sines, Portugal. The facility will utilize a proton exchange membrane (PEM) electrolyzer with an electrolysis capacity of 1.22 MW, producing an estimated 77 tons of green hydrogen annually.
- **August 2022**: Fusion Fuel revealed plans for the HEVO-Industria project, which will feature 300 HEVO-solar hydrogen generators, integrating miniaturized PEM electrolyzers with concentrated photovoltaic (CPV) panels. This project will have a total capacity of 6.6 MW and is expected to produce 764 tons of green hydrogen per year.
- **December 2022**: Fusion Fuel announced the allocation of €22.5 million from a €36 million grant for the H2 Hevo-Sines project, which will have an electrolysis capacity of 75 MW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2022**: The initial project in Sines was expected to require a capital investment of €2.54 million.
- **August 2022**: The HEVO-Industria project was set to require a total capital investment of €25 million, with a €10 million grant from the Portuguese government approved under the Recovery and Resilience Plan.
- **December 2022**: Fusion Fuel was approved for €36 million in grant funding for its Sines Green Hydrogen Valley Alliance.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2022**: Announcement of the green hydrogen plant in Sines, with construction expected to begin early the following year and commercial operation targeted for the second half of 2023.
- **August 2022**: Confirmation of the HEVO-Industria project, with plans to integrate hydrogen refueling stations.
- **December 2022**: Expected start of construction for the H2 Hevo-Sines project in 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2022**: The HEVO-Industria project is expected to create jobs related to the construction and operation of the facility, although specific numbers were not disclosed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2022**: Fusion Fuel Green is the primary owner of the facility in Sines, collaborating with KEME Energy for the project. The ownership structure appears to be exclusive to Fusion Fuel, with no mention of joint ventures or partnerships beyond this collaboration.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones of the Fusion Fuel facility in Sines, Portugal, focusing on its technology, investments, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
