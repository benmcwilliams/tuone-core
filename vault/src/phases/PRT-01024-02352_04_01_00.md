## PRT-01024-02352

### Evolutionary Timeline of the MHI Vestas Facility in Sines, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2020**: The H2Sines project is initiated, aiming to create a green hydrogen industrial cluster in Sines. The facility will start with a **10 MW electrolysis pilot plant** and plans to expand to **1 GW of electrolysis capacity** by the end of the decade. Long-term goals include achieving **1.5 GW of renewable electric energy generation capacity** to support the electrolysers.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2020**: The total expected cost for the H2 Sines project is estimated at **€1.5 billion ($1.84 billion)**. This investment is aimed at supporting both domestic consumption and the export of green hydrogen.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2020**: A consortium including EDP, Galp, Martifer, REN, and Vestas announces the feasibility study for the H2Sines project.
- **August 2020**: The project is confirmed to be in the planning stages, with the first phase involving the installation of the **10 MW pilot electrolysis** facility.
- **December 2020**: The H2 Sines project is recognized as one of the **world's largest green hydrogen projects**, with a planned capacity of **1 GW** by 2030.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific details on job creation or job loss related to the H2 Sines project. However, the establishment of a green hydrogen industrial cluster is expected to contribute positively to local employment in the long term.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is developed as a **joint venture** among several companies, including EDP, Galp, Martifer, REN, and Vestas. There is no indication that MHI Vestas has exclusive ownership of the facility; rather, it is part of a collaborative effort among multiple stakeholders.
