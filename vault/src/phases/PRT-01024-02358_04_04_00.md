## PRT-01024-02358

### Evolutionary Timeline of the McPhy Facility in Sines, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **04/2022**: McPhy has been selected as the preferred supplier for the GreenH2Atlantic project in Sines, Portugal. The project involves the development of a 100MW electrolysis plant designed to convert green electricity into over 41 tons of clean hydrogen daily. The electrolyzer will consist of scalable modules, each with a capacity of up to 8MW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04/2022**: The GreenH2Atlantic project has received funding of €30 million ($33.9 million) from the European Union’s Horizon 2020 – Green Deal Call research and innovation programme. This funding is specifically allocated for the construction of the 100MW electrolyser plant.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04/2022**: Construction works on the GreenH2Atlantic plant are expected to begin in 2023, with operations anticipated to start in 2025, pending approval from relevant authorities. The project aims to transition the former coal-fired power plant area in Sines into a renewable hydrogen production hub.

#### 4. Labour
- **04/2022**: While specific job creation figures are not mentioned, the project is expected to contribute to local employment opportunities as it transitions the site from coal to renewable hydrogen production.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **04/2022**: The GreenH2Atlantic project is a collaborative effort involving 13 partners, including EDP, Galp, Engie, Bondalti, Martifer, Vestas Wind Systems, Efacec, and McPhy. This indicates that McPhy does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a consortium developing the project.
