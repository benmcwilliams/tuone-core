### Evolutionary Timeline of Madoqua Renewables Facility in Sines, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2022**: Madoqua Renewables, in partnership with Power2X and Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (CIP), announced the development of the MadoquaPower2X facility in Sines. The facility will have an electrolysis capacity of **500 MW** and is expected to produce **50,000 tons of green hydrogen** and **500,000 tons of green ammonia** annually. There are plans for future expansions to potentially increase ammonia production to **1 million tons** by the end of the decade.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2022**: The total investment for the MadoquaPower2X project is **€1 billion (US$1.07 billion)**. The project is expected to be powered by **1 GW of renewable energy**, primarily from wind and solar sources. The final investment decision is anticipated by the end of **2023**.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2022**: The project is already under development, with production of green hydrogen expected to start by **2025** and the goal to begin exporting green ammonia by **2026**. Subsequent phases of development are set to start in **2024** and reach full commissioning before **2030**.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify exact job creation or job loss figures related to the MadoquaPower2X facility. However, the scale of the project suggests potential job creation during both the construction and operational phases.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The MadoquaPower2X facility is a joint venture involving **Madoqua Renewables**, **Power2X**, and **Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners (CIP)**. There is no indication that Madoqua Renewables owns the facility exclusively; it is part of a consortium.
