## PRT-02473-02557

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Endesa Facility in Pego, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2022**: Endesa announced plans for a hybrid facility in Pego, which will consist of:
  - A 365 MW photovoltaic (PV) unit
  - A 264 MW wind farm
  - 168 MW of battery storage
  - A 500 kW electrolyzer to utilize surplus power that cannot be stored by the batteries.
- The facility is designed to allow approximately 6,000 hours of annual production, exceeding the operational capacity of conventional thermal plants. This hybrid renewable energy plant will replace a 628 MW coal power plant in Pego.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2022**: The project is estimated to require an investment of approximately €600 million. This investment is tied to the construction of the hybrid facility integrating renewable sources and storage.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2022**: Endesa won Pego's fair transition competition, securing a connection permit to link to the 224 MegavoltAmpere (MPA) network for the installation of the hybrid facility. The coal power plant is expected to be closed in the same year.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2022**: Endesa stated that workers from the old coal facility will be integrated into the operation of the new hybrid plant, indicating a transition of employment rather than job loss.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Endesa is a subsidiary of the Italy-based utility Enel. The facility in Pego is operated by Endesa Generación Portugal, indicating that Endesa has full ownership of the facility.
