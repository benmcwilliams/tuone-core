## PRT-02518-02135

### Evolutionary Timeline of Acciona Energy Facility in Moura, Portugal

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Production:** The facility was a solar panel manufacturing plant.
   - **Production Capacity:** The plant maintained manufacturing activities for 10 years, employing an average of more than 100 workers.
   - **Closure:** The facility was closed in January 2019 due to economic unviability in a competitive market dominated by Chinese manufacturers.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Initial Investment:** The factory project was initiated with an investment of €10 million, linked to the construction of a 45 MW Solar Photovoltaic Plant in Amareleja, Moura.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Start of Operations:** The facility began operations in 2008, following an agreement with the municipality to keep it operational for 10 years.
   - **Closure Announcement:** On January 10, 2019, Acciona announced the closure of the plant, citing inability to compete after the EU removed minimum import prices for Chinese photovoltaic products.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Job Creation:** The facility employed more than 100 workers during its operational period.
   - **Job Loss:** The closure in January 2019 resulted in job losses for the employees at the plant.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Ownership:** Acciona owned the facility, having acquired the company created by the Chamber of Moura to build and manage the plant. The facility was not a joint venture; it was fully owned by Acciona.
