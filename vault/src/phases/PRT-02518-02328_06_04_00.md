### Jinko Solar Facility in Moura, Portugal - Evolutionary Timeline

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility was initially a solar panel manufacturing plant operated by Jinko Solar, producing photovoltaic (PV) modules. 
   - The production capacity specifics are not detailed in the articles, but it was noted that the facility had been operational for 10 years before its closure in 2019.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The facility was established with an investment of €10 million, linked to the construction of a 45 MW Solar Photovoltaic Plant in Amareleja, Moura.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **September 2018:** Jinko Solar announced the conclusion of its activity at the Moura facility, transferring production to plants in Asia.
   - **January 2019:** Acciona, the company managing the facility after Jinko, announced the closure of the plant due to economic unviability in a competitive market dominated by Chinese manufacturers.

4. **Labour:**
   - The facility employed more than 100 workers during its operational period. The closure in January 2019 led to job losses, with Acciona entering talks with union representatives to define the conditions of employee departures.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility was not exclusively owned by Jinko Solar; it was managed by Acciona after Jinko abandoned the project. Acciona had previously acquired the company created by the Chamber of Moura to build and manage the plant.
