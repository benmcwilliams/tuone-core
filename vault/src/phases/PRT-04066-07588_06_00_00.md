## PRT-04066-07588

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Axpo Ibéria Facility in Évora, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2018**: Construction began on the Vale de Moura solar park, a 28.8 MW (DC) photovoltaic (PV) plant located near Évora, in Portugal's Alentejo region. The facility is notable for being the first solar plant built without any subsidies in the Iberian Peninsula. It is expected to occupy 55 hectares and produce an annual energy output of over 52 GWh.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2018**: The project received a financing agreement from Portugal’s Banco BPI. The power generated will be sold to Axpo Ibéria under a 10-year Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) signed in January 2018. The largest shareholder of the project is Mirova, a unit of the French financial group Natixis.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2018**: Announcement of the start of construction for the Vale de Moura project.
- **End of 2018**: Construction is expected to be completed by the end of the year.
- **Beginning of 2019**: The start of commercial operations is scheduled.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the construction or operation of the facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Axpo Ibéria does not own the facility exclusively; it is a joint venture with Mirova, which is the largest shareholder of the Vale de Moura project. Axpo Ibéria is responsible for purchasing the power generated by the facility through the PPA.
