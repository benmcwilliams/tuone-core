## PRT-04399-02823

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDPR Facility in Penela, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2024**: EDP Renewables inaugurated a new hybrid energy project in Penela, combining a photovoltaic plant (Monte de Vez) with the existing São João Wind Farm. The wind farm has a capacity of 22.8 MW, while the photovoltaic plant has a capacity of 21 MWp. The combined production capacity of the facility is now 79 GWh of renewable energy per year, sufficient to supply electricity to 23,000 families. The facility utilizes 36,000 bifacial solar panels alongside 13 wind turbines, enhancing energy production efficiency through the complementarity of wind and solar energy.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2024**: The hybrid project is part of EDP's broader investment strategy in the central region of Portugal, where the company has over 280 MW of wind energy in operation. EDP plans to implement an additional 700 MW in similar hybrid projects in the coming years.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2024**: The facility was officially inaugurated, marking it as Portugal's second hybrid park that integrates wind and solar energy in the same location. The project is a significant milestone in EDP's efforts to accelerate the energy transition in the region.
