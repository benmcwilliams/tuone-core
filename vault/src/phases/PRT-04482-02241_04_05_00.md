## PRT-04482-02241

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fusion Fuel Facility in Évora, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date: Late 2021**: The H2Évora plant began continuous operation. It is the first successfully commissioned solar-to-green hydrogen facility in Portugal.
- **Production Capacity**: The facility is designed to produce an estimated 15 tons of green hydrogen per year using 15 HEVO-solar hydrogen generators, which integrate miniaturized proton exchange membrane (PEM) electrolyzers with high-efficiency concentrated photovoltaic (CPV) panels.
- **Additional Features**: The facility includes a 200 kW FCwave fuel cell module from Ballard Power, which converts green hydrogen into electricity for sale to the grid during peak demand. It also has hydrogen purification, compression, and storage systems.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date: August 2022**: Fusion Fuel secured a €10 million ($9.9 million) grant to develop the 6.6 MW HEVO-Industria green hydrogen project in Sines, Portugal, which will feature 300 solar hydrogen generators.
- **Date: January 2023**: The Portuguese government announced a €40 million investment as part of its national hydrogen strategy to support hydrogen projects, including the H2Évora facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date: 10-11-2022**: The successful commissioning of the H2Évora plant was announced, marking it as a significant milestone in Portugal's renewable energy landscape.
- **Date: January 2023**: The Portuguese government unveiled plans for a green hydrogen tender, indicating ongoing support and future opportunities for the facility.
