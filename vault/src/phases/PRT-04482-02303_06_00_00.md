### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hyperion Renewables Facility in Évora, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **03-11-2020**: Hyperion Renewables, in collaboration with Endesa, is planning to build a 257 MW solar-plus-storage power plant in Évora. The facility will consist of 571,111 solar panels and a 10 MW storage system with two hours of autonomy. The estimated production capacity is 400,000 MWh/year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **03-11-2020**: The project is being developed through a direct agreement with the Portuguese transmission system operator (REN). The facility is in an advanced phase of licensing, with all rights secured, indicating significant investment and development efforts.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **03-11-2020**: The project has received favorable approval from the Évora city council for both the solar park and the evacuation line connecting to the future REN substation in Divor, approximately 2 km away. The project is expected to be one of the next to receive authorization from the transmission system operator.

#### 4. Labour
- **03-11-2020**: The article does not specify job creation or job loss related to this facility, but the development of such a large-scale project typically implies potential job creation during construction and operation phases.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **03-11-2020**: The facility is a joint venture between Hyperion Renewables and Endesa, indicating that Hyperion does not own the facility exclusively.
