## PRT-04482-02557

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Endesa Facility in Évora, Portugal

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **03-11-2020:** Endesa, in collaboration with Hyperion Renewables, announced plans to build a 257 MW solar-plus-storage power plant in Évora. The facility will consist of 571,111 solar panels and a 10 MW storage system with two hours of autonomy. The estimated production capacity is 400,000 MWh/year.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **03-11-2020:** The project is part of a broader initiative where the Portuguese government has approved several large plants totaling 3,500 MW to enter into direct agreements with the transmission system operator, REN. Specific investment details for the Évora facility were not disclosed in the articles.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **03-11-2020:** The project is reported to be in an advanced phase of licensing, with all rights secured. The Évora city council has given favorable approval for both the solar park and the evacuation line connecting to the future REN substation in Divor, approximately 2 km away. The project is expected to be one of the next to receive authorization from the transmission system operator.

4. **Labour:**
   - The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the facility's development.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is a joint venture between Endesa and Hyperion Renewables, as indicated by the collaboration mentioned in the articles. There is no indication of exclusive ownership by either company.
