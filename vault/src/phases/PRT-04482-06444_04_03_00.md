## PRT-04482-06444

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ballard Power Facility in Évora, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Late 2021**: The H2Évora plant began continuous operation. It is a grid-connected pilot project that produces an estimated 15 tons of green hydrogen per year. The facility features 15 HEVO-solar hydrogen generators, which utilize miniaturized proton exchange membrane (PEM) electrolyzers attached to high-efficiency concentrated photovoltaic (CPV) panels. Additionally, it includes a 200 kW FCwave fuel cell module supplied by Ballard Power, which converts green hydrogen into electricity for sale to the grid during peak demand. The facility also has hydrogen purification, compression, and storage systems.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2022**: Fusion Fuel secured a €10 million ($9.9 million) grant to develop the 6.6 MW HEVO-Industria green hydrogen project in Sines, Portugal, which will feature 300 solar hydrogen generators. This investment is separate from the H2Évora facility but indicates ongoing financial support for hydrogen projects in Portugal.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **10-11-2022**: The successful commissioning of the H2Évora plant was announced, marking it as the first successfully commissioned solar-to-green hydrogen facility in Portugal. The facility is now connected to the Portuguese electric grid.
