### Evolutionary Timeline of the Neoen Facility in Azambuja, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2022**: Neoen began construction on a solar park in Azambuja, which is set to be one of Portugal's largest solar facilities with a total capacity of **272 MW**. This facility consists of two solar farms: the **68 MW Torre Bela Solar Farm**, which is fully owned by Neoen, and the **204 MW Rio Maior Solar Farm**, which is co-owned with Aura Power. The facility is expected to produce approximately **500 GWh** of electricity annually once operational. The commissioning of the solar park is scheduled for **early 2024**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2022**: The project is supported by two **15-year power purchase agreements (PPAs)** with the Portuguese State, which will purchase **80%** of the renewable energy produced. These agreements are expected to commence in **2025**. The construction is being managed by Aldesa, a major Spanish construction group, under an Engineering, Procurement, and Construction (EPC) turnkey contract.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2022**: Construction officially began on the solar park, with a target commissioning date of **early 2024**. The facility is designed to be built in plots averaging **20 hectares**, with green corridors to enhance biodiversity.
