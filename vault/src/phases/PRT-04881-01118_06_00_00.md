### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Alcochete, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09-02-2022**: Iberdrola began construction on the Alcochete solar complex, which includes two photovoltaic plants: Alcochete I with an installed capacity of 232.89 MW and Alcochete II with 12.72 MW. Both plants utilize bifacial technology, enhancing efficiency by allowing solar panels to capture sunlight from both sides. The total capacity of the Alcochete complex is 46 MW, contributing to a larger portfolio of solar projects awarded in the 2019 auction.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **31-01-2023**: Iberdrola announced plans to invest an additional €3 billion in wind and solar energy in Portugal, which includes the completion of the Alcochete solar complex. This investment is part of a broader strategy to enhance renewable energy infrastructure in the country.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **End of 2022**: Construction of the Alcochete solar complex (46 MW) was completed. This complex is part of Iberdrola's broader efforts in the Setúbal district, which also includes the Conde (13.5 MW) and Algeruz II (27 MW) facilities.
- **20-07-2024**: Iberdrola announced the installation of two new photovoltaic plants in Portugal, further expanding its renewable energy portfolio, which now totals 1,435 MW across various projects, including the Alcochete complex.

#### 4. Labour
- **09-02-2022**: During the construction phase of the Alcochete solar complex, it was reported that 500 jobs would be created at peak construction periods. This job creation is part of Iberdrola's commitment to supporting local economies through renewable energy projects.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify any joint ventures or partnerships for the Alcochete facility, indicating that Iberdrola likely holds full ownership of the solar complex.
