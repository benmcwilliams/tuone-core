## PRT-04913-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Támega River, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2022**: Iberdrola commissioned its first solar farm in Portugal, the **12.72MW Alcochete 2 PV plant**. This facility is part of a larger portfolio of projects awarded in solar auctions in 2019, totaling **187MW**. The plant features over **50,500 fixed single-facial photovoltaic modules**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2022**: The Alcochete 2 PV plant involved an investment of **€17.8 million**. This investment is part of Iberdrola's broader strategy to expand its renewable energy capacity in Portugal.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2022**: The Alcochete 2 PV plant was officially commissioned. It was one of three projects from the 2019 auction expected to enter commercial operation in 2022, alongside the **13.51MW Conde project** and the **32.89MW Alcochete 1 project**. The remaining projects from the auction, including **Montechoro 1 (11.57MW)**, **Montechoro 2 (24.95MW)**, and **Carregado (64.1MW)**, are in the process of obtaining construction permits and are scheduled for commissioning in **2023**.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2022**: The construction of the Alcochete 2 PV plant generated **more than 200 jobs** during peak construction periods, all of which were filled by local workers.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify any joint ventures or partnerships; therefore, it can be inferred that **Iberdrola owns the facility exclusively**.

### Summary
The Iberdrola facility in the Támega River region of Portugal has evolved significantly since its commissioning in August 2022. The facility, primarily focused on solar energy production, represents a substantial investment in renewable energy, contributing to local job creation and forming part of a larger strategy to enhance Iberdrola's renewable energy portfolio in the country.
