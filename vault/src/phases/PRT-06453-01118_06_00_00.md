### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Algarve, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Estoi Solar Plant**: The facility in Algarve is set to include the Estoi solar plant, which will have a production capacity of **83MW** and will also feature battery storage. This plant is expected to come online in **2024**.
- **Additional Projects**: In early **2023**, construction will begin on the **Montechoro I and II projects** in Paderne (Albufeira) with a combined capacity of **37MW**, and the **Carregado project** in Alenquer (Lisbon) with a capacity of **64MW**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- Iberdrola plans to invest an additional **€3 billion** in wind and solar energy in Portugal over the coming years, which includes the development of the Estoi solar plant and other projects.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Environmental Approvals**: On **31-01-2023**, Iberdrola secured environmental approvals for the **Fernando Pessoa solar project** near Sines, which will be one of Europe’s largest solar plants with a capacity of **1200MW**, expected to be operational by **2025**.
- **Construction Timeline**: The Estoi solar plant is scheduled to come online in **2024**, while the Montechoro projects will begin construction in early **2023**.

#### 4. Labour
- The construction of the Fernando Pessoa project is expected to create up to **2500 jobs**, mostly local, contributing significantly to the local economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Iberdrola is the **exclusive owner** of the facility, with no mention of joint ventures or partnerships for the Estoi solar plant. However, the Fernando Pessoa project will have **Prosolia Energy** as a partner.
