### Evolutionary Timeline of the CTIEC Facility in Algarve, Portugal

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility, known as the Solara4 plant, is a solar power project with a production capacity of 221 MW. It is being constructed using 661,500 photovoltaic modules supplied by the Chinese manufacturer Jetion Solar. The construction of the facility began in March 2017.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The Solara4 project is being developed by the Irish company Welink, which has secured funding from Investec and Kommunalkredit funds. The project is notable for being described as the largest facility in Europe built without public subsidies.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **March 2017:** Construction of the Solara4 plant began.
   - **December 2020:** The project was reported to be the largest solar power project under construction in Portugal.
   - **March 2021:** A total of 6 Covid-19 cases were detected among workers at the construction site, leading to a partial halt in construction activities. The mayor of Alcoutim indicated that there have been previous delays in project completion, but no specific completion date was provided.

4. **Labour:**
   - The article mentions that the construction site had workers from both sides of the Iberian border, indicating job creation in the region. However, specific job creation numbers or losses due to the Covid-19 outbreak were not detailed.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The facility is not exclusively owned by CTIEC. It is being developed by Welink, with CTIEC serving as the EPC (Engineering, Procurement, and Construction) turnkey contractor. The project also involves other companies such as Portwind Lda, Imenergy, Iprocel, and B.E.T. Green Energy.
