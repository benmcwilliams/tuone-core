### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iprocel Facility in Algarve, PRT

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The facility, known as the Solara4 plant, is a solar power project with a production capacity of 221 MW. It is located near the Vaqueiros district in the Algarve region of Portugal. The project involves the installation of 661,500 photovoltaic modules sourced from the Chinese manufacturer Jetion Solar.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The Solara4 project is noted as the largest facility in Europe built without public subsidies. It is being developed by the Irish company Welink, which has secured funding from Investec and Kommunalkredit funds. 

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - Construction of the Solara4 project began in March 2017. As of March 2021, the project was partially halted due to a Covid-19 outbreak among workers, with six positive cases reported. The mayor of Alcoutim indicated that there have been previous delays in the completion of similar projects, and he could not provide a specific completion date.

4. **Labour:**
   - The Covid-19 outbreak at the construction site resulted in a partial halt of work, affecting the workforce. However, specific job creation or job loss figures related to the project were not detailed in the article.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The Solara4 facility is not exclusively owned by Iprocel; it is a collaborative project involving multiple companies, including Portwind Lda, Imenergy, Iprocel, and B.E.T. Green Energy, under the development of Welink.
