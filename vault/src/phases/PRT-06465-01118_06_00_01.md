## PRT-06465-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Santiago do Cacém, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2021**: Iberdrola, in partnership with Prosolia, announced plans to build a solar facility in Santiago do Cacém with an initial capacity of 1.14 GW, utilizing Tesla batteries.
- **January 2023**: The project was updated to a capacity of 1.2 GW, now named the Fernando Pessoa solar project, which is expected to be one of Europe’s largest solar plants, set to come online in 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2021**: The total investment for the solar project in Portugal was announced at €850 million, part of a larger investment plan that includes four solar parks in Spain.
- **January 2023**: Iberdrola announced plans to invest an additional €3 billion in wind and solar energy projects in Portugal, which includes the Fernando Pessoa project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2021**: Initial announcement of the solar project in Santiago do Cacém.
- **January 2023**: Secured environmental approvals for the Fernando Pessoa project, confirming its status as the largest PV plant in Europe.
- **Expected Online**: The facility is projected to begin operations in 2025.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2023**: The construction of the Fernando Pessoa project is expected to create up to 2,500 jobs, primarily for local workers.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2021**: Iberdrola holds a 50% stake in the facility, indicating a joint venture with Prosolia for the development of the solar project.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Iberdrola facility in Santiago do Cacém, Portugal, focusing on its technological advancements, investments, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.

### Technology and Operations
- **Capacity Discrepancy**: The initial announcement in September 2021 stated that the facility would have a capacity of **1.14 GW** and employ Tesla batteries. However, a later article from January 2023 reported that the capacity has been updated to **1.2 GW**, with no mention of the Tesla batteries in the latest announcement.
- **Production Start Date**: The facility is expected to come online in **2025** according to multiple sources.

### Investment and Development
- **Investment Amount**: Iberdrola and Prosolia plan to jointly invest **€850 million** in the construction of the solar facility. Additionally, Iberdrola has announced plans to invest an extra **€3 billion** in wind and solar energy in Portugal over the coming years.

### Project Status and Milestones
- **Environmental Approvals**: As of January 2023, Iberdrola secured environmental approvals for the project, which was originally announced in **September 2021**.
- **Construction Timeline**: The project is expected to begin commercial operations in **2025**.

### Labour
- **Job Creation**: The construction of the facility is expected to create up to **2,500 jobs**, mostly local, during the construction phase.

### Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture**: Iberdrola holds a **50% stake** in the facility, indicating a joint venture with Prosolia.
