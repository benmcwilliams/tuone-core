## PRT-06465-08001

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Prosolia Facility in Santiago do Cacém, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2021**: Prosolia, in collaboration with Iberdrola, announced plans to build a solar facility in Santiago do Cacém with an initial capacity of **1.14 GW**. The facility will utilize **Tesla batteries** for energy storage.
- **February 2023**: The capacity of the solar facility was updated to **1.2 GW**, and it was confirmed that the project would be named the **Fernando Pessoa solar facility**. This facility is expected to be the **largest PV plant in Europe** and is projected to begin commercial operations in **2025**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2021**: The joint investment for the construction of the solar facility was set at **€850 million**. This investment is part of a larger plan that includes four solar parks in Spain with a combined capacity of **360 MW**.
- **February 2023**: Iberdrola announced plans to invest **€3 billion** in solar, wind, and storage projects in Portugal by the end of the year, which includes the Fernando Pessoa project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **September 2021**: The project was announced and is currently under review by local environmental authorities.
- **February 2023**: The project secured an **environmental permit** from Portuguese authorities, allowing construction to proceed. The project is expected to be operational by **2025**.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2023**: During the construction phase of the Fernando Pessoa project, it is expected to create up to **2,500 jobs** in the region. 

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2021**: The facility is a **joint venture** between Iberdrola and Prosolia, with Iberdrola holding a **50% stake** in the project.
