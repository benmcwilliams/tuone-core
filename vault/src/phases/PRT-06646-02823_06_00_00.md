### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDPR Facility in Sabugal, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2023**: EDP Renewables (EDPR) connected its first international hybrid plant to the grid, combining an 8.4MW solar farm (Mina de Orgueirel) with an 11MW wind park (Mosteiro) in Sabugal. The solar plant utilizes over 17,000 bifacial solar panels, while the wind park consists of eight turbines, including a more powerful machine as part of an over-equipment project. The hybrid complex has a production capacity of up to 39.5GWh per year, sufficient to provide renewable energy for over 30,000 people and prevent approximately 18,000 tonnes of CO2 emissions annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2023**: The establishment of the hybrid facility reflects EDPR's commitment to energy transition and investment in hybridization of parks in its portfolio, indicating ongoing investment strategies in both Portugal and other regions with strong development potential.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2023**: The Mina de Orgueirel solar farm was officially opened and connected to the grid, marking the commissioning of EDPR's first hybrid wind/solar park on the Iberian Peninsula.
