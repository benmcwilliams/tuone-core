## PRT-07040-07623

### Evolutionary Timeline of the CTIEC Facility in Vaqueiros, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **06-03-2017**: Announcement of the development of a 220MW solar plant named Solara 4 in Vaqueiros, Algarve. The facility is expected to produce enough energy for approximately 200,000 homes. The project is noted as the largest privately funded photovoltaic (PV) project in Europe.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **07-03-2017**: The total investment for the Solara 4 project is approximately €200 million (around $211.8 million). The project is financed by CTIEC, and there are ongoing discussions regarding Power Purchase Agreements (PPAs) with interested parties.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **06-03-2017**: Construction is set to begin before the end of Q1 2017, with the facility expected to start feeding electricity to the grid by mid-2018.
- **07-03-2017**: Confirmation that construction will commence by the end of Q1 2017, with completion scheduled for mid-2018.

#### 4. Labour
- **06-03-2017**: The construction phase is projected to create 200 jobs, with up to 600 jobs at peak construction times. Once operational, the facility will provide 30 permanent operation and maintenance jobs.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **06-03-2017**: The facility is a collaboration between UK-based WElink Energy and China Triumph International Engineering (CTIEC), indicating a joint venture structure for the project. CTIEC is responsible for the engineering, procurement, and construction (EPC) services.
