### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDP Facility in Salamonde, Portugal

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 05-04-2022
   - **Details:** EDP, through its renewables unit EDPR, won a contract to build a floating photovoltaic (PV) plant with a capacity of 100 MW at the Salamonde dam. The project is part of a larger procurement exercise for floating PV projects in Portugal, totaling 263 MW across seven locations. The facility will also have the potential to support up to 154 MW of renewable energy, including an additional 14 MW of solar and 70 MW of wind capacity, although the latter two are not included in the auction contract.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 05-04-2022
   - **Details:** EDP agreed to a negative bid of –€4/MWh for a 15-year contract under a contract for difference (CfD) regime. This means EDP will pay €4 for each megawatt-hour generated by the floating solar plant over the contract period. The grid connection capacity awarded is for a duration of 30 years.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 05-04-2022
   - **Details:** The floating PV plant is expected to come online in 2025. The project was part of the first auction for floating PV projects in Portugal, which concluded in late November 2021.
