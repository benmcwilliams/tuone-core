### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDP Facility in Tabuaço, Portugal

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** 05-04-2022
   - **Details:** EDP, through its renewables unit EDPR, won a contract to build a floating photovoltaic (PV) plant with a capacity of 100 MW at the Tabuaço site. The project is part of a larger procurement exercise for floating PV projects in Portugal, totaling 263 MW across seven different dams. The facility will also have the potential to support up to 154 MW of renewable energy, including an additional 14 MW of solar and 70 MW of wind capacity, although these additional capacities are not included in the contract for difference (CfD).

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** 05-04-2022
   - **Details:** EDP agreed to a negative bid of –€4/MWh for a 15-year contract, which allows for a 30-year grid connection for the floating array and other renewable projects. This indicates a significant investment in the facility, although specific monetary figures for the Tabuaço project alone were not disclosed.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** 05-04-2022
   - **Details:** The floating PV plant at Tabuaço is expected to come online in 2025. The project was part of the first auction for floating PV projects in Portugal, which concluded in late November 2021.
