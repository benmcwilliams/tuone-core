### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Alto Tâmega, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10-08-2023**: The Alto Tâmega Hydroelectric Power Plant is reported to have a production capacity of **160 MW**. It is the last component of the Tâmega complex, which includes two other plants: Gouvães (880 MW) and Daivões (118 MW). The construction of the dam is completed, and electromechanical assemblies are expected to finish by the end of 2023.
- **15-08-2023**: The facility's dam is confirmed to be fully completed, standing at **104.5 m** high, with a concrete volume of **220,000 m³** and a crest length of **335 m**. The filling of the Alto Tâmega reservoir, covering **468 ha** with a volume of **132 hm³**, is set to begin.
- **22-01-2024**: One of the two turbines at the Alto Tâmega Hydroelectric Plant is connected to the grid, marking a significant step towards full commissioning. The second turbine is expected to connect in February 2024, with commercial operation anticipated by the end of March 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10-08-2023**: The Tâmega hydroelectric complex represents one of the largest energy initiatives in Portugal's history, with a total investment exceeding **€1.5 billion** and an installed capacity of **1,158 MW** across all three plants.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **10-08-2023**: The construction of the Alto Tâmega Hydroelectric Power Plant is progressing well, with civil works nearly finished and electromechanical assemblies on schedule.
- **15-08-2023**: The closure of the river diversion tunnel is reported, with a concrete plug to be built to ensure the tunnel's permanent closure.
- **22-01-2024**: The first turbine is connected to the grid, with the second turbine's connection scheduled for February 2024. Full commercial operation of the plant is expected by the end of March 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **10-08-2023**: During the peak of construction, the facility employed nearly **1,000 workers** on-site, indicating significant job creation associated with the project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify the ownership structure of the Alto Tâmega facility, but it is implied that Iberdrola is the primary company responsible for the project, managing all aspects of its development and construction.
