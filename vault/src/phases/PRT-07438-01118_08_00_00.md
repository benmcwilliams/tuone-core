### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Braga, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09-02-2022**: Iberdrola began construction on four solar farms in Portugal, totaling 86 MW. The plants include:
  - Algeruz 2: 27.35 MW
  - Conde: 13.51 MW
  - Alcochete 1: 232.89 MW
  - Alcochete 2: 12.72 MW
  - Notably, Alcochete 1 and 2 will utilize bifacial technology, enhancing efficiency by allowing electricity generation from both sides of the panels.
- **16-06-2024**: Iberdrola announced plans for a new wind farm in the districts of Vila Real and Braga, with a capacity of 274 MW, which will be the largest hybridization project in Portugal, combining wind and hydro energy.
- **10-12-2024**: Iberdrola received a production license for the 274 MW wind project, which will integrate with the existing Tâmega hydroelectric complex. The project will include 38 turbines, each with a capacity of 7.2 MW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10-12-2024**: The wind project is set to involve an investment of €350 million. This investment is part of Iberdrola's broader commitment to renewable energy, having invested over €2 billion in Portugal over the last 20 years.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09-02-2022**: Construction of the solar farms commenced, with completion expected within the same year.
- **16-06-2024**: Iberdrola obtained environmental authorization for the 274 MW wind power plant.
- **10-12-2024**: The production license for the wind project was granted, marking a significant milestone towards construction.

#### 4. Labour
- **09-02-2022**: During the construction of the solar farms, it was reported that 500 jobs would be created at peak construction periods.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify any joint ventures or partnerships for the facilities mentioned, indicating that Iberdrola likely holds full ownership of the solar and wind projects in Braga, Portugal.
