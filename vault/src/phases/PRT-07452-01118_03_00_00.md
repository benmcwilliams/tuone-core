### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Daivões, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2022**: The Daivões hydroelectric plant, with an installed capacity of **118 MW**, became operational as part of the Tâmega Hydroelectric Complex, which includes two other plants: Gouvães (880 MW) and Alto Tâmega (160 MW).
- **2024**: The Tâmega Hydroelectric Complex is set to achieve a total installed capacity of **1158 MW** by the end of March 2024, with the full commissioning of the Alto Tâmega plant.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2022**: The Tâmega Hydroelectric Complex, which includes the Daivões plant, is part of a larger project involving a total investment of over **€1.5 billion**.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2022**: The Daivões plant was commissioned and began operations.
- **January 2024**: One of the two turbines at the Alto Tâmega Hydroelectric Plant was connected to the grid, marking progress towards full commissioning of the Tâmega Hydroelectric Complex.
- **February 2024**: The second turbine at the Alto Tâmega plant is expected to be connected to the grid.
- **March 2024**: The Alto Tâmega plant is anticipated to enter commercial operation, completing the commissioning of the entire Tâmega Hydroelectric Complex.

#### 4. Labour
- **2022**: The construction of the Tâmega Hydroelectric Complex, which includes the Daivões plant, generated significant employment, although specific job numbers for Daivões alone were not detailed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: Iberdrola owns the Daivões facility as part of the Tâmega Hydroelectric Complex, which is fully owned by the company.

### Technology and Operations
- "The Tâmega Hydroelectric Complex is made up of three power stations: the Alto Tâmega Hydroelectric Power Station has an installed capacity of 160MW, the 880MW Gouvães Pumped Storage Power Station and the 118MW Daivões Power Station." (Article 2, 22-01-2024)
- "The last two [Gouvães and Daivões] have been in operation since 2022." (Article 2, 22-01-2024)
- "By the end of March 2024, this plant will be in commercial operation and the entire Tâmega Hydroelectric Complex will be operating with a total installed capacity of 1158MW." (Article 2, 22-01-2024)

### Investment and Development

### Project Status and Milestones
