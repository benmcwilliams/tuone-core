### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Gouvães, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **2022**: The Gouvães Pumped Storage Power Station, with an installed capacity of **880MW**, became operational as part of the Tâmega Hydroelectric Complex, which also includes the Daivões Power Station (118MW) and the Alto Tâmega Power Station (160MW).
- **March 2024**: The entire Tâmega Hydroelectric Complex, including Gouvães, is expected to be fully operational with a total installed capacity of **1158MW**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2022**: The Tâmega Hydroelectric Complex, which includes the Gouvães facility, represents a significant investment of over **€1.5 billion**.
- **2024**: The project is noted for increasing Portugal's hydroelectric storage capacity by **33%**, with a significant increase in energy storage volume compared to previous years.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2022**: Gouvães Pumped Storage Power Station began operations.
- **January 2024**: One of the two turbines at the Alto Tâmega Hydroelectric Plant was connected to the grid, marking progress towards full commissioning of the Tâmega Hydroelectric Complex.
- **February 2024**: The second turbine at the Alto Tâmega is expected to be connected to the grid.
- **March 2024**: The entire Tâmega Hydroelectric Complex, including Gouvães, is anticipated to be in commercial operation.

#### 4. Labour
- **2022**: The construction of the Tâmega Hydroelectric Complex, which includes Gouvães, generated over **200 jobs** during peak construction periods, all filled by local workers.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: Iberdrola owns the Gouvães facility as part of the Tâmega Hydroelectric Complex, which is a wholly owned initiative by the company.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones related to the Iberdrola facility in Gouvães, Portugal, highlighting its operational capacity, investments, project status, labor impact, and ownership structure.

### Technology and Operations
- **Article 1 (16-08-2022)**: "Iberdrola has commissioned the Daivões (conventional plant) and Gouvães (pumped-storage plant) hydroelectric plants, which are part of the Támega River gigabattery, one of the largest energy storage facilities in Europe with 1158MW of capacity."
- **Article 2 (22-01-2024)**: "The Tâmega Hydroelectric Complex is made up of three power stations. The Alto Tâmega Hydroelectric Power Station has an installed capacity of 160MW, the 880MW Gouvães Pumped Storage Power Station and the 118MW Daivões Power Station."
- **Article 4 (24-01-2024)**: "The Tâmega Hydroelectric Complex comprises three power plants: the Alto Tâmega plant, boasting an installed capacity of 160MW; the Gouvães pumped storage plant (880MW); and the Daivões plant (118 MW), both of which have been operational since 2022."
