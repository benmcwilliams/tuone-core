## PRT-07486-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Ribeira de Pena, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2024**: Iberdrola connected one of the two turbines at the **160MW Alto Tâmega hydroelectric power plant** to the Portuguese electricity grid. This is part of the **1.16GW Tâmega hydroelectric complex**, which also includes the **880MW Gouvães pumped storage power plant** and the **118MW Daivões power plant**. The complex is designed to provide an energy reserve of **40 million kilowatt hours**, sufficient to power **11 million people for a day**. The full commissioning of the Alto Tâmega plant is expected by the end of March 2024.
  
- **December 2024**: Iberdrola received approval to construct the **largest wind farm in Portugal** with an installed capacity of **274 MW** and a production capacity of **601 GWh per year**. This wind farm will be integrated into the Tâmega Power Plant System, utilizing existing infrastructure in Ribeira de Pena.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2024**: The Tâmega hydroelectric complex was built with a total investment of **€1.5 billion** (approximately **$1.6 billion**).
  
- **December 2024**: The wind farm project is expected to require an investment of **close to €350 million**. This project is part of Iberdrola's broader commitment to renewable energy, having invested over **€2 billion** in renewable energies in Portugal over the last 20 years.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2024**: Connection of the first turbine at the Alto Tâmega hydroelectric power plant to the grid marks a significant milestone towards full commissioning.
  
- **March 2024**: The Alto Tâmega power plant is expected to commence commercial operations, completing the Tâmega hydroelectric complex's operational capacity.
  
- **December 2024**: Iberdrola obtained the production license for the wind farm, marking a step forward in the construction of the largest wind farm in Portugal.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2024**: The wind farm project is anticipated to create approximately **700 jobs** during peak construction phases, focusing on civil engineering, wind turbine assembly, substations, and transmission lines. The Tâmega hydroelectric complex is projected to create up to **3,500 direct jobs** and **10,000 indirect jobs** during its construction phase, with a significant portion of these jobs benefiting local communities.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify any joint ventures or partnerships; therefore, it can be inferred that **Iberdrola owns the facility exclusively**.
