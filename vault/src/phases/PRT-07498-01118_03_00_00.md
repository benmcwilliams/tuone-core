## PRT-07498-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Tâmega, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2022**: Iberdrola connected the first power unit at its Tâmega hydroelectric and pumped storage giga battery plant, which has a total capacity of 1,158 MW and can generate 1,766 GWh annually. The facility consists of three reservoirs: Gouvães, Daivões, and Alto Tâmega.
- **July 2022**: The Tâmega Giga Battery was officially opened, capable of storing 40 million kWh, enough to supply energy to 11 million people for 24 hours. The Gouvães and Daivões plants were operational, while the Alto Tâmega facility is expected to be commissioned in 2024.
- **June 2024**: Iberdrola announced plans for a new 274 MW wind power plant to be integrated into the Tâmega Electroproduction System, marking the facility as the largest hybridization project in Portugal.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2022**: The Tâmega complex was developed with an investment of over €1.5 billion ($1.67 billion).
- **July 2022**: Iberdrola's chairman announced plans to double the investment in Portugal to €3 billion, which will be allocated to new solar and wind projects in the region.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2022**: The first 220 MW turbine at the Gouvães hydroelectric power plant was synchronized to deliver clean power to the grid.
- **July 2022**: The Tâmega Giga Battery was officially opened, marking it as one of Europe’s largest pumped storage facilities.
- **June 2024**: Iberdrola obtained environmental authorization for the new wind power plant, with construction expected to begin in early 2025.
- **December 2024**: Iberdrola obtained a production license for the wind farm, which will be integrated into the Tâmega Electroproduction System.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2022**: The construction of the Tâmega complex created 3,500 direct jobs and 10,000 indirect jobs, with 20% of the jobs coming from neighboring municipalities.
- **December 2024**: The wind farm project is expected to create around 700 jobs in civil engineering, turbine assembly, substations, and transmission lines.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: Iberdrola owns the Tâmega facility exclusively, with no mention of joint ventures or partnerships in the provided articles.
