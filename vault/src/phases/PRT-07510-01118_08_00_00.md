## PRT-07510-01118

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Iberdrola Facility in Vila Real, Portugal

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2024**: Iberdrola announced plans to build a new wind farm in Vila Real and Braga, with a production capacity of 274 MW, capable of supplying electricity to 128,000 homes. This facility will be the largest hybridization project in Portugal, combining wind and hydro energy.
- **December 2024**: Iberdrola received a production license for the 274 MW wind project, which will include the installation of 38 Enventus V172 turbines, each with a capacity of 7.2 MW. The project is expected to produce 601 GWh of electricity annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2024**: The total investment for the wind farm is reported to be €350 million. This project is part of a broader investment strategy, with Iberdrola having invested over €2 billion in renewable energies in Portugal over the last 20 years.
- **December 2024**: The project is integrated into the existing Tâmega hydropower plant, which has a total investment of more than €1.5 billion.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2024**: Iberdrola obtained the necessary environmental authorization to proceed with the wind farm project.
- **December 2024**: The production license was granted by the Portuguese Directorate-General for Energy and Geology, marking a significant milestone towards the construction of the facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2024**: The project is expected to create jobs through the involvement of local companies in the development of infrastructure and construction of the substation and power lines. Specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ongoing**: Iberdrola is the sole owner of the facility, as indicated by the absence of any mention of joint ventures or partnerships in the articles.
