## PRT-07535-08086

### Evolutionary Timeline of the SolarisFloat Facility in Oostvoornse, PRT

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date**: April 2020
  - The SolarisFloat facility in Oostvoornse is established as a test facility for floating photovoltaic (PV) plants. The facility will analyze the impact of wind and waves on floating structures and module yield.
  - The PV arrays at the facility will range up to **50 kW** in size. The facility will focus on assessing module damage, the impact of waves on light absorption, and different mooring systems.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date**: April 2020
  - The facility is part of the **Solar@Sea project**, which is funded by the Dutch government. This project aims to explore offshore solar concepts and develop reliable designs for floating PV systems.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date**: April 2020
  - TNO (The Netherlands Organisation for Applied Scientific Research) is coordinating the establishment of the testing facility. The plan is to commission the systems during the summer of 2020, after which monitoring will commence. However, there are uncertainties regarding potential delays due to the coronavirus crisis.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date**: April 2020
  - The article does not specify job creation or job loss directly associated with the establishment of the SolarisFloat facility. However, the involvement of multiple partners (Sabic, Equinor, and the municipality of Westvoorne) suggests potential job opportunities related to the project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date**: April 2020
  - The facility is a collaborative effort involving multiple partners, including TNO, Sabic, Equinor, and the municipality of Westvoorne. SolarisFloat (Portugal) is one of the selected companies to build the floating systems, indicating that the facility is not under exclusive ownership but rather a joint venture with various stakeholders involved.
