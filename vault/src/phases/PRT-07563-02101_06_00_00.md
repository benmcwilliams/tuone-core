### Martifer Facility in Avalades, Portugal - Evolutionary Timeline

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Date:** September 2012
   - **Details:** The Martifer facility in Avalades is one of the largest photovoltaic (PV) solar plants in Portugal, covering an area of over 41 hectares. It employs 66,080 solar modules with a production capacity of 15.6 MWp. The facility is part of a broader expansion strategy by Martifer Solar, which aims to increase its renewable energy project portfolio.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Date:** September 2012
   - **Details:** At the end of the first half of 2012, Martifer Solar had approximately €235 million worth of projects in its backlog, indicating significant investment in expanding its PV power plant projects, including the Avalades facility.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Date:** September 2012
   - **Details:** The Avalades solar plant was completed as part of two major projects in Portugal, which also included the Ferreiras solar plant. The construction of these plants involved a total of 200 workers, with 120 specifically for the Avalades project.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Date:** September 2012
   - **Details:** The construction of the Avalades facility created 120 jobs. Additionally, ongoing operation and maintenance services will be provided by more than 10 personnel.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Date:** September 2012
   - **Details:** The Avalades facility was developed, built, and operated for a fund managed by BNP Paribas Clean Energy Partners, indicating that Martifer Solar is not the exclusive owner of the facility but is a key partner in its operation.
