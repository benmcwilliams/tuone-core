## ROU-04135-05790

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Parapet Facility in Tulcea, ROU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10/2023**: The Parapet facility, in collaboration with Blue Line Energy, completed the construction of a 1.1 MW photovoltaic power plant within the Beştepte wind park. This facility is notable for being the first hybrid project in Romania to produce energy from renewable sources. The total installed capacity of the hybrid plant is now 7.25 MW, which includes the existing wind component that has been operational since 2014. The facility is designed to generate a total annual power output of 24 GWh, significantly contributing to carbon dioxide emissions reduction by 20,400 tonnes per year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10/2023**: The investment for the photovoltaic power plant project is approximately EUR 10 million. This investment supports the hybridization of the facility, which aims to enhance energy production stability and efficiency.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **10/2023**: The hybrid solar and wind park project was announced as completed, with the photovoltaic system currently undergoing technological trials. The integration of the photovoltaic system into the existing infrastructure is in progress.

#### 4. Labour
- **10/2023**: The construction of the photovoltaic plant resulted in an increase in job opportunities during both the construction phase and the operational period of the facility. Various regional, national, and international service and equipment providers were involved, contributing to local job creation.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **10/2023**: The facility is a joint project between Parapet and Blue Line Energy. Parapet is a construction company specializing in solar energy, while Blue Line Energy is a renewable energy producer and ESRE license holder. The ownership structure indicates a collaborative approach to the development and operation of the facility. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones of the Parapet facility in Tulcea, Romania, highlighting its significance in the renewable energy sector.
