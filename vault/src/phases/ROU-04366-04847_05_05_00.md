### Evolutionary Timeline of the E-INFRA Facility in Doicești, Romania

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2023**: The facility is set to produce energy through a small modular reactor (SMR) plant, which will be the first NuScale SMR outside of the USA. The plant is expected to have a production capacity of up to 462 megawatts (MWe).
- **August 2023**: An 80 MW photovoltaic park has been commissioned as part of the Doicești energy complex. This park will complement the future SMR plant, demonstrating the integration of nuclear and renewable energy sources.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2023**: The project is supported by a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) among several companies, including Nuclearelectrica and Samsung C&T, indicating significant investment and collaboration for the development of the SMR technology in Doicești.
- **August 2023**: The photovoltaic park is built on a site that reuses ash deposits from the former coal-fired power plant, showcasing sustainable development practices.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2023**: Announcement of the MoU for the development of the NuScale VOYGR power plants at the Doicești site.
- **August 2023**: Commissioning of the 80 MW photovoltaic park, marking a significant milestone in the development of the Doicești energy complex.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2023**: The SMR plant is projected to create 190 permanent jobs directly, with the potential for thousands more in related sectors as companies seek to leverage the clean energy source.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2023**: E-INFRA, a Romanian holding company that includes Nova Power & Gas, is involved in the project, indicating a collaborative ownership structure with multiple partners, including Nuclearelectrica and Samsung C&T.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones of the E-INFRA facility in Doicești, Romania, highlighting its focus on clean energy production through innovative technologies and sustainable practices.
