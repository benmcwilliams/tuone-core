## ROU-04366-05042

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fluor Facility in Doicești, Romania

1. **Technology and Operations**:
   - **Date**: July 2024
   - **Details**: The facility is set to produce energy using small modular reactor (SMR) technology, specifically utilizing NuScale technology. The project aims for an installed capacity of 462 MW, comprising six modules, each with a capacity of 77 MW. This facility will be located at the former coal plant site in Doicești.

2. **Investment and Development**:
   - **Date**: July 2024
   - **Details**: The project is expected to receive significant financial backing, with the US International Development Finance Corporation considering providing up to USD 1 billion in financing. This investment is crucial for the development and deployment of the SMR project.

3. **Project Status and Milestones**:
   - **Date**: July 2024
   - **Details**: Fluor signed a FEED 2 contract with RoPower Nuclear to provide design and engineering services for the SMR project. This contract will lead to an updated cost estimate and schedule, along with necessary safety and security analyses for a final investment decision.

4. **Labour**:
   - **Date**: July 2024
   - **Details**: The project is projected to create nearly 200 permanent jobs, 1,500 construction jobs, and 2,300 manufacturing and component assembly jobs. Additionally, there will be jobs related to facility operation and maintenance over the 60-year lifespan of the facility.

5. **Ownership Structure**:
   - **Date**: July 2024
   - **Details**: The facility is part of a joint venture involving multiple stakeholders, including RoPower Nuclear, Nuclearelectrica, Nova Power & Gas (part of the E-INFRA group), Fluor, Samsung C&T Corporation, and Sargent & Lundy. Fluor is not the exclusive owner of the facility but plays a significant role in its development.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Fluor facility in Doicești, Romania, as of July 2024.
