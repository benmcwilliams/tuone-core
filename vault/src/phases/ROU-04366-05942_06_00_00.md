### Evolutionary Timeline of the S.N. Nuclearelectrica SA Facility in Doicești, ROU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2023**: The Doicești – Șotânga photovoltaic park was commissioned with an installed capacity of **80 MW**. This facility is part of the broader Doicești energy complex, which will also include the first small modular reactor (SMR) plant in Europe. The photovoltaic park utilizes over **123,000 bifacial monocrystalline panels** and occupies more than **115 hectares**. It is designed to produce green energy and will supply power for the future SMR plant's operations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2023**: The development of the Doicești energy complex, including the photovoltaic park and the future SMR plant, is positioned as a sustainable solution that reuses land from the former Doicești coal-fired power plant. The project is expected to attract investments and enhance energy production capacity in Romania.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2023**: The commissioning of the **80 MW photovoltaic park** marks a significant milestone in the development of the Doicești energy complex. The project is part of a larger initiative to integrate nuclear and renewable energy sources.

#### 4. Labour
- **July 2023**: The development of the Doicești energy complex is anticipated to create a **real industrial hub** in the area, which is expected to attract new investors and generate socio-economic benefits for the local community. However, specific job creation or loss figures were not detailed in the article.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **July 2023**: The facility is part of a collaborative project involving S.N. Nuclearelectrica SA and RoPower, indicating a **joint venture** approach to the development of the energy complex, particularly the SMR technology in Romania.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic initiatives surrounding the S.N. Nuclearelectrica SA facility in Doicești, highlighting its role in advancing clean energy solutions in Romania.
