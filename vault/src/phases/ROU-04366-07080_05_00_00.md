## ROU-04366-07080

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Nuclearelectrica Facility in Doicești, Romania

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2023**: The facility is set to deploy a NuScale VOYGR-6 small modular reactor (SMR) power plant with a production capacity of 462 MWe, utilizing six modules, each with an installed capacity of 77 MWe. This facility will be the first NuScale SMR outside of the USA.
- **July 2024**: The project continues to progress with the signing of a FEED 2 contract, which will provide design and engineering services necessary for the implementation of the SMR project.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2023**: The NuScale Energy Exploration Centre (E2 Centre) is opened at the University Politehnica of Bucharest, funded by the US Department of State under the FIRST programme, supporting the development of nuclear energy in Romania.
- **July 2024**: The US International Development Finance Corporation expresses interest in providing up to USD 1 billion in financing for the Doicești SMR project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2023**: A memorandum of understanding (MoU) is signed between Nuclearelectrica and partners to support the development of NuScale VOYGR power plants at the former coal-fired plant site in Doicești.
- **July 2024**: The FEED 2 contract is signed between RoPower Nuclear and Fluor, marking a significant step in the project’s development.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2023**: The NuScale VOYGR-6 power plant is estimated to create approximately 200 permanent jobs, 1,500 construction jobs, and 2,300 jobs in production and component assembly.
- **July 2024**: The project is projected to create nearly 200 permanent jobs, 1,500 construction jobs, and 2,300 manufacturing and component assembly jobs over the facility's 60-year lifespan.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2023**: Nuclearelectrica is involved in a joint venture with RoPower Nuclear (which includes Nova Power & Gas) for the development of the SMR project at Doicești. Nuclearelectrica is a key partner in this initiative, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership.
