### Evolutionary Timeline of the Nofar Energy Facility in Ratesti, Romania

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2021**: Nofar Energy, in partnership with Econergy, acquired a ready-to-build 155MW solar farm in Ratesti, Romania. This facility is set to be the largest solar plant in Romania and is part of Econergy's 1.5GW solar pipeline in the country. 
- **November 2023**: The Ratesti solar project began commercial operations, with expectations to supply electricity to the grid imminently. The project construction cost was reported at €102 million.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2021**: The total investment for the Ratesti project was announced to be €100 million, with both Econergy and Nofar Energy each acquiring a 50% stake from Portland Trust.
- **November 2023**: Econergy closed a €60 million financing facility with Raiffeisen Bank International to support the Ratesti project. This financing is notable as it is the first in Romania based on electricity merchant sales without a power purchase agreement (PPA).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Q4 2021**: Construction of the Ratesti solar project was scheduled to begin.
- **Q4 2022**: Commercial operations were initially expected to start.
- **November 2023**: The project officially began commercial operations, marking a significant milestone in its development.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the Ratesti facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Ratesti facility is equally owned by Econergy and Nofar Energy, with each company holding a 50% stake in the project.
