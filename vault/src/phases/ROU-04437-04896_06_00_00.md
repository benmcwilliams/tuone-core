### Evolutionary Timeline of the Econergy Facility in Ratesti, Romania

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2021**: Econergy and Nofar Energy acquired a ready-to-build 155MW solar farm in Ratesti, Romania, from Portland Trust. This facility is set to be the largest solar plant in Romania and is part of Econergy's 1.5GW solar pipeline in the country.
- **Q4 2021**: Construction is scheduled to begin.
- **Q4 2022**: Commercial operations were expected to start.
- **November 2023**: The Ratesti solar project began supplying electricity to the grid, marking it as the first project connected from a portfolio of 2.2GW in various stages of development.
- **January 2024**: The facility is reported to provide green electricity to over 100,000 households annually.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2021**: The total investment for the Ratesti project is €100 million, with Econergy and Nofar Energy each holding a 50% stake.
- **November 2023**: Econergy closed a €60 million financing facility with Raiffeisen Bank International to support the Ratesti project, bringing the total investment in construction to €102 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2021**: Acquisition of the Ratesti project completed.
- **Q4 2021**: Construction expected to start.
- **Q4 2022**: Anticipated start of commercial operations.
- **November 2023**: Project connected to the electricity grid.
- **January 2024**: Official announcement of the commencement of commercial operations.

#### 4. Labour
- Specific job creation or loss figures related to the Ratesti project have not been detailed in the articles. However, the project is expected to contribute to the local economy through its operations and management.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2021**: The Ratesti facility is jointly owned by Econergy and Nofar Energy, each holding a 50% stake in the project.
