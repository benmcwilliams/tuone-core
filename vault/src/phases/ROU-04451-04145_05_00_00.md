## ROU-04451-04145

### Evolutionary Timeline of the NuScale Power Facility in Bucharest, Romania

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2023**: The NuScale Energy Exploration Centre (E2 Centre) was opened at the University Politehnica of Bucharest, featuring a control room simulator for the NuScale VOYGR small modular reactor (SMR) power plant. This is the first E2 Centre established outside the USA, aimed at training future nuclear experts and operators. The VOYGR-6 power plant, which is planned for deployment, will have a capacity of 462 MWe.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2021**: NuScale Power and Nuclearelectrica signed a teaming agreement to deploy the 462 MWe NuScale VOYGR-6 power plant in Romania by the end of the decade. 
- **2022**: Nuclearelectrica received $1.2 million in funding from the US Trade & Development Agency (USTDA) to evaluate potential sites for SMRs, including the Doicești site, which is the preferred location for the VOYGR-6 power plant.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2020**: An intergovernmental agreement between the USA and Romania was signed to cooperate on Romania's nuclear energy program.
- **May 2023**: The E2 Centre was officially opened, marking a significant milestone in the collaboration between NuScale, Nuclearelectrica, and the University Politehnica of Bucharest.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2023**: The NuScale VOYGR-6 power plant is estimated to create approximately 200 permanent jobs, 1,500 jobs during construction, and 2,300 jobs in production. This development is expected to significantly contribute to job creation in Romania and the US.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **2021 Onwards**: The facility is a collaboration between NuScale Power and Nuclearelectrica, indicating a joint venture structure for the deployment of the VOYGR-6 power plant. Nuclearelectrica is a state-owned nuclear power corporation in Romania, and the partnership aims to leverage both entities' strengths in advancing nuclear technology in the region.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones related to the NuScale Power facility in Bucharest, Romania, highlighting its technological advancements, investment efforts, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
