## ROU-04625-05042

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Fluor Facility in Cernavoda, Romania

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Production**: The Cernavoda nuclear power plant, operated by Societatea Nationala Nuclearelectrica, currently produces 1.4 GW of electricity with two operational units, meeting 20% of Romania’s electricity demand.
- **Expansion Plans**: Plans to add units 3 and 4 to the facility, which will almost double its power output, were announced. The expansion is part of Romania's efforts to enhance sustainability and energy security.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Phase**: In November 2024, Fluor and its joint venture partners received a limited notice to proceed with the design phase of units 3 and 4. This includes providing essential information for a final investment decision, which encompasses engineering and construction plans, revised cost estimates, and initial nuclear safety assessments.
- **Support for Refurbishment**: A memorandum of understanding was signed in early 2024 for the refurbishment of Unit 1 and the construction of new units 3 and 4, backed by the Italian export credit agency, SACE.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Key Announcements**: On July 23-24, 2024, U.S. Secretary of Energy Jennifer Granholm highlighted the modernization plans for the Cernavoda nuclear power plant during her visit to Romania, which includes collaboration with Fluor and Sargent & Lundy.
- **Limited Notice to Proceed**: The limited notice to proceed for the design phase of units 3 and 4 was granted in November 2024, during the COP29 climate change conference.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: While specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles, the expansion and modernization efforts are expected to create jobs in the construction and operational phases of the new units.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture**: The facility is part of a joint venture led by Fluor, which includes partners AtkinsRéalis, Sargent & Lundy, and Ansaldo Nucleare. The facility is operated by Societatea Nationala Nuclearelectrica, indicating that Fluor does not have exclusive ownership but is involved in a collaborative effort for its expansion and modernization.
