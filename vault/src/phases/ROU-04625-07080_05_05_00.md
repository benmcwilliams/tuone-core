### Evolutionary Timeline of the Nuclearelectrica Facility in Cernavoda, Romania

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **1996**: Unit 1 of the Cernavoda nuclear power plant began commercial operation.
- **2007**: Unit 2 commenced commercial operation.
- **2023**: A 30-month modernization project for Unit 1 was announced, aimed at extending its operational life to 60 years, with a budget of €45 million.
- **2023**: Plans for the construction of Units 3 and 4 were confirmed, with expected commercial operation in 2030 and 2031, respectively. These units will add approximately 10 TWh of clean energy to Romania's energy system.
- **2023**: A tritium removal facility, the first in Europe and third globally, was announced to enhance safety and environmental protection.
- **2024**: The refurbishment of Unit 1 was confirmed to extend its operational life until 2054.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: Nuclearelectrica awarded a €45 million contract for the modernization of Unit 1.
- **June 2023**: A support agreement was signed, indicating state guarantees for financing the construction of Units 3 and 4, with an estimated cost of €7 billion.
- **September 2023**: Canada committed C$3 billion (approximately $2.2 billion) in funding for nuclear energy projects, specifically for the construction of Units 3 and 4.
- **October 2023**: A consortium was formed for the refurbishment of Unit 1, with a contract valued at $1.85 billion expected to be finalized in 2024.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2021**: Phase 1 of the Units 3 and 4 project began, focusing on preparatory work.
- **March 2023**: The modernization project for Unit 1 was announced.
- **June 2023**: Contracts for the construction of Units 3 and 4 were signed, with a completion timeline of 2030 and 2031.
- **October 2023**: A contract for the tritium removal facility was signed, with an estimated completion duration of 50 months.
- **February 2025**: Refurbishment works for Unit 1 are scheduled to commence.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2023**: The modernization of Unit 1 is expected to preserve existing jobs and create new ones, contributing to local economic development.
- **November 2024**: The construction of Units 3 and 4 is projected to create over 19,000 well-paid jobs.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Nuclearelectrica is the sole operator of the Cernavoda nuclear power plant, with no indication of joint ventures or shared ownership in the articles reviewed. The company has engaged in partnerships with various international firms for specific projects, but retains full ownership of the facility.
