## ROU-04625-07386

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ansaldo Nucleare Facility in Cernavoda, Romania

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **1996**: Unit 1 of the Cernavoda nuclear power plant began operations, utilizing CANDU 6 reactor technology.
- **2007**: Unit 2 commenced operations, also based on CANDU 6 technology.
- **2017**: A refurbishment project for Unit 1 was initiated, aimed at extending its operational life by 30 years.
- **2023**: Ansaldo Nucleare was appointed to supply engineering services for a €45 million modernization project for Unit 1, which is part of a life extension program.
- **2024**: A memorandum of understanding (MOU) was signed for the refurbishment of Unit 1 and the construction of new Units 3 and 4, which are expected to be operational by 2030 and 2031, respectively.
- **2024**: The facility's total output is expected to increase significantly with the addition of Units 3 and 4, which will almost double the current capacity of 1.4 GW.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2023**: The modernization project for Unit 1 was awarded to SNC-Lavalin, with a budget of €45 million.
- **2024**: The refurbishment and expansion project is potentially financed up to €2 billion, supported by the Italian export trade agency SACE.
- **2024**: A €1.9 billion EPC contract was signed for the refurbishment of Unit 1, involving multiple international partners.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2017**: Refurbishment project for Unit 1 commenced.
- **February 2022 - 2026**: Ongoing second phase of the refurbishment project, focusing on financial resources and contract negotiations.
- **2024**: MOU signed for refurbishment and construction of Units 3 and 4.
- **February 2025**: Refurbishment works for Unit 1 are scheduled to commence.
- **2030 & 2031**: Expected commercial operation dates for Units 3 and 4.

#### 4. Labour
- **2023**: The refurbishment of Unit 1 is expected to preserve high-quality jobs and create new ones, contributing to local employment and the community.
- **2024**: The expansion project is anticipated to generate a new generation of nuclear specialists and further job creation in the region.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Current Status**: The Cernavoda nuclear power plant is operated by Societatea Nationala Nuclearelectrica (SNN), which is 82.49% state-owned. Ansaldo Nucleare is involved as a key partner in the refurbishment and expansion projects, but does not own the facility exclusively. The collaboration includes other international partners such as Candu Energy and Korea Hydro & Nuclear Power.
