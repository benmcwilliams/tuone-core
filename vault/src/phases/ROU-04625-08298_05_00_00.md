## ROU-04625-08298

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Ansaldo Facility in Cernavoda, Romania

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Cernavoda Nuclear Power Plant**: The facility consists of two Candu pressurized heavy water reactors, with Unit 1 having gone into commercial operation in 1996 and Unit 2 in 2007. The plant is currently undergoing a refurbishment project for Unit 1, which began in 2017 and is in its second phase (February 2022 - 2026). This refurbishment includes the replacement of key reactor components and aims to extend the operating life of Unit 1 to 60 years.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Contract Value**: A contract valued at USD 1.85 billion is expected to be concluded within the first half of 2024 for the refurbishment and life extension of Unit 1. 
- **Specific Contracts**: In July 2022, Candu Energy signed a contract worth USD 47 million for engineering and early procurement for the retubing of the reactor core. In March 2023, a two-year agreement worth USD 65 million was signed for pre-project work related to the lifetime extension of Cernavoda 1.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Refurbishment Phases**: The refurbishment project for Unit 1 is divided into three phases:
  - **Phase 1**: Began in 2017.
  - **Phase 2**: Ongoing from February 2022 to 2026, focusing on financial resources, contract negotiations, and obtaining necessary approvals.
  - **Phase 3**: Scheduled for 2027 to 2029, will involve the shutdown of Unit 1 and all required work for its recommissioning.
- **Consortium Formation**: On October 14, 2023, a multinational consortium was formed, including Candu Energy, Ansaldo, and KHNP, to manage the refurbishment project.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The formation of the consortium and the refurbishment project are expected to create jobs, although specific numbers have not been disclosed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture**: The facility is not exclusively owned by Ansaldo; it is part of a consortium with Candu Energy and KHNP, indicating a joint venture structure for the refurbishment project.
