## ROU-04625-08497

### Evolutionary Timeline of Candu Energy Facility in Cernavoda, Romania

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **1996**: Unit 1 of the Cernavoda Nuclear Power Plant (NPP) began commercial operation, followed by Unit 2 in 2007. Both units are CANDU-6 reactors with a capacity of 650 MWe each.
- **2017**: The refurbishment project for Unit 1 commenced, which is designed to extend its operational life by 30 years.
- **2022-2026**: The second phase of the refurbishment project is ongoing, focusing on financial resources, contract negotiations, and obtaining necessary approvals.
- **2025**: Refurbishment works are expected to commence in February, aiming to ensure Unit 1 operates safely until 2054.
- **2030-2031**: Scheduled commercial operation for new units 3 and 4, which are also CANDU-6 reactors.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2023**: A consortium formed including Candu Energy, Ansaldo Nucleare, and Korea Hydro & Nuclear Power (KHNP) for the refurbishment of Unit 1, with a contract valued at USD 1.85 billion expected to be concluded in the first half of 2024.
- **February 2024**: Ansaldo Nucleare signed a memorandum of understanding for the refurbishment and expansion of Cernavoda NPP, potentially financed up to €2 billion (approximately USD 2.16 billion).
- **December 2024**: A €1.9 billion (USD 1.97 billion) EPC contract was signed for the refurbishment of Unit 1, pending approval from the Canadian government and Nuclearelectrica shareholders.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2022**: Candu Energy signed the first contract for the refurbishment of Cernavoda 1, worth USD 47 million.
- **March 2023**: Candu Energy signed a two-year agreement worth USD 65 million for pre-project work for the lifetime extension of Cernavoda 1.
- **December 2024**: The EPC contract for Unit 1 refurbishment was signed, covering detailed design, procurement, and construction of necessary infrastructure.

#### 4. Labour
- The refurbishment and expansion projects are expected to create jobs and support the development of a new generation of nuclear specialists, contributing to Romania's socio-economic development.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Nuclearelectrica, the state-owned nuclear power producer, is the primary owner of the Cernavoda facility, holding an 82.49% stake. The refurbishment and expansion projects involve a consortium that includes Candu Energy (a subsidiary of AtkinsRéalis), Ansaldo Nucleare, and KHNP, indicating a collaborative ownership structure for the refurbishment efforts.
