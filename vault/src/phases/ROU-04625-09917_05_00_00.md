## ROU-04625-09917

### Evolutionary Timeline of SN Nuclearelectrica Facility in Cernavoda, Romania

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Production**: The facility operates Romania's only nuclear power plant (NPP) at Cernavoda, which has two commercially operational Candu 6 pressurized heavy water reactors.
- **Future Plans**: There are plans to implement the Cernavoda 3&4 project, which aims to complete two additional Candu 6 reactors. Additionally, a NuScale VOYGR-6 power plant with a capacity of 462 MWe is planned for deployment by the end of the decade.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Funding**: SN Nuclearelectrica received $1.2 million from the US Trade & Development Agency (USTDA) to identify and evaluate potential sites for small modular reactors (SMRs).
- **Collaborations**: The facility is part of a multinational consortium with NuScale and RoPower, supported by the US Foundational Infrastructure for Responsible Use of Small Modular Reactor Technology (FIRST) capacity building program.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **E2 Centre Launch**: On May 18, 2023, the NuScale Power Energy Exploration Centre (E2 Centre) was launched at the Polytechnic University of Bucharest, marking a significant step in US-Romania nuclear cooperation.
- **SMR Development**: In 2021, NuScale and SNN signed a collaboration agreement to accelerate the implementation of the first SMR in Europe. Front-end engineering and design work for the Doicesti site has already begun.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The SMR project at Doicesti is expected to create thousands of jobs in Romania and the US, contributing to the clean energy transition and strengthening European energy security.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: SN Nuclearelectrica is a Romanian state-owned company that operates the facility. It collaborates with NuScale and RoPower, indicating a joint venture approach for future projects, particularly regarding the development of SMRs.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and future plans for the SN Nuclearelectrica facility in Cernavoda, Romania, highlighting its role in advancing nuclear technology and energy security in the region.
