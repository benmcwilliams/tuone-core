## ROU-04807-05809

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Photon Energy Facility in Aiud, Romania

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2022**: Photon Energy began construction of a solar farm in Aiud, Alba County, with a total capacity of **5 MW**. The facility is designed to cover **6.6 hectares** and will be equipped with approximately **8,700 solar panels**. It is expected to deliver around **6.8 GWh** of renewable energy annually to the grid managed by Distributie Energie Electrică Romania. 
- **May 2023**: Photon Energy announced the commissioning of two additional solar power plants in Romania, including the Aiud facility, which contributed to a combined generation capacity of **9.5 MWp**. The total number of solar power plants operated by Photon Energy reached **94**, with a combined capacity of **113.1 MWp**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2022**: Photon Energy is developing a total of **236 MWp** of solar projects in Romania, with **115 MWp** in advanced stages. The Aiud facility is part of this broader investment strategy aimed at expanding their independent power producer (IPP) portfolio to over **120 MWp** by the end of 2022.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2022**: Construction of the Aiud solar farm commenced, with an operational target set for the **fourth quarter of 2022**.
- **May 2023**: The Aiud facility was officially commissioned, marking a significant milestone in Photon Energy's expansion in the Romanian renewable energy market.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the Aiud facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The Aiud solar farm is owned and operated by **Holloway Solar SRL**, a special-purpose company that is **wholly owned** by Photon Energy Group. This indicates that Photon Energy has **full ownership** of the facility.
