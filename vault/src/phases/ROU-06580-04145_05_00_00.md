## ROU-06580-04145

### Evolutionary Timeline of the NuScale Power Facility in Doicesti, Romania

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2023**: NuScale Power, in collaboration with Romania's state nuclear power corporation SN Nuclearelectrica and RoPower Nuclear, announced plans to deploy the first NuScale six-module VOYGR-6 power plant in Romania, targeting an installed capacity of 462 MWe. The facility will replace a former coal plant in Doicesti, Dambovita County.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2023**: A global public-private commitment of up to $275 million was announced at the G7 Summit to advance the deployment of the NuScale VOYGR in Romania. This funding will support various project phases, including procurement of long lead materials and regulatory analyses.
- **April 2024**: The project received additional support from the US Export-Import Bank (EXIM) and the International Development Finance Corporation (DFC), with potential backing of up to $3 billion and $1 billion, respectively, for project deployment.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2023**: NuScale and RoPower began conducting a Phase 1 Front-End Engineering & Design (FEED) study to analyze the preferred site for the SMR deployment in Doicesti.
- **April 2024**: The Phase 1 FEED study received approval from the International Atomic Energy Agency, marking a significant milestone in the project’s development.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2024**: The SMR project is expected to create nearly 200 permanent jobs, 1,500 construction jobs, and 2,300 manufacturing and component assembly jobs, along with additional facility operation and maintenance jobs over the 60-year life of the facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2023**: The project is a joint venture between SN Nuclearelectrica and Nova Power & Gas, with RoPower Nuclear as the project development company. NuScale Power collaborates with these entities but does not exclusively own the facility.
