## ROU-06580-05924

### Evolutionary Timeline of the RoPower Facility in Doicesti, ROU

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2024**: The RoPower facility in Doicesti is part of Romania's Small Modular Reactor (SMR) project, aiming for an installed capacity of 462 MWe, utilizing six 77 MWe NuScale modules. The facility will replace an existing thermal power plant.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2023**: The project received USD 275 million in funding from the USA and multinational public-private partners. This funding is designated for procurement of long lead materials, Phase 2 Front End Engineering and Design work, project management expertise, site characterization, regulatory analyses, and development of site-specific schedules and budgets.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2024**: The project is in the Front End Engineering and Design Phase 1, which has received approval from the International Atomic Energy Agency. The project is actively preparing for the deployment of Romania's first SMR project.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2024**: The project is expected to create nearly 200 permanent jobs, 1500 construction jobs, and 2300 manufacturing and component assembly jobs, along with additional facility operation and maintenance jobs over the 60-year lifespan of the facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2024**: The RoPower facility is a joint venture between Nuclearelectrica and Nova Power and Gas, indicating that it is not exclusively owned by a single company.
