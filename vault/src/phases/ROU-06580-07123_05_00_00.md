## ROU-06580-07123

### Evolutionary Timeline of RoPower Nuclear Facility in Doicesti, Romania

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date: 11-04-2024**: The RoPower Nuclear facility is set to utilize NuScale technology to develop a small modular reactor (SMR) project with an installed capacity of 462 MW, consisting of six modules, each with a capacity of 77 MW. The facility aims to replace a thermal power plant at the Doicesti site.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date: 18-05-2023**: The project is supported by significant US investments and partnerships, including a $7 billion energy investment project facilitated by the US Exim Bank. The US Trade & Development Agency (USTDA) provided $1.2 million in funding to Nuclearelectrica for site evaluation.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date: 18-05-2023**: The establishment of the NuScale Power Energy Exploration Centre (E2 Centre) at the Polytechnic University of Bucharest marks a significant milestone in the project, supporting the development of SMRs in Romania.
- **Date: 11-04-2024**: The IAEA approved the site selection process for the Doicesti facility, allowing the project to move forward to the next phase of site evaluation and application for a site license.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date: 11-04-2024**: The SMR project is projected to create approximately 200 permanent jobs, 1,500 construction jobs, and 2,300 manufacturing and component assembly jobs, along with additional jobs for facility operation and maintenance over its 60-year lifespan.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date: 18-05-2023**: RoPower Nuclear is a joint venture established between Romanian nuclear utility SN Nuclearelectrica (SNN) and the private company Nova Power & Gas, indicating that the facility is not under exclusive ownership by a single entity but rather a collaborative effort between these two companies.
