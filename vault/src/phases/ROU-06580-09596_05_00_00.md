## ROU-06580-09596

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Nova Power & Gas Facility in Doicesti, Romania

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date**: May 2023
  - The facility is set to produce energy using a NuScale Power Corporation VOYGR small modular reactor (SMR) plant. The specific configuration being considered is the VOYGR-6, which has a production capacity of 462 MWe. The project aims to replace an old coal-fired power plant in Doicesti with this modern nuclear technology.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date**: May 2023
  - The facility received funding of up to USD275 million to advance the deployment of the NuScale SMR plant. This includes a Letter of Interest from the US Export-Import Bank (EXIM) for up to USD99 million, aimed at supporting various phases of project development, including procurement of materials and regulatory analyses. Additional potential financial support of up to USD3 billion and USD1 billion from EXIM and the US International Development Finance Corporation, respectively, was also mentioned.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date**: May 2023
  - The project is currently in the Front End Engineering and Design (FEED) phase, with a phase 1 study already underway to analyze the preferred site for the VOYGR-6 SMR power plant. The new funding will support the phase 2 study, which will initiate the process of securing necessary authorizations and licenses for the project. The project is expected to be operational as soon as 2029.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date**: May 2023
  - While specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the article, the involvement of Nova Power & Gas in this significant project suggests potential job creation associated with the construction and operation of the new SMR facility, particularly as it replaces an old coal plant.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date**: May 2023
  - The facility is part of a joint venture between Nova Power & Gas and Nuclearelectrica, with the latter being a Romanian state-owned company. This indicates that Nova Power & Gas does not own the facility exclusively but is a key partner in the project alongside Nuclearelectrica.
