#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2023**: The proposed hydropower plant, Turnu Magurele-Nikopol, is expected to have an installed power capacity of over 800 MW, with the capacity divided equally between Romania and Bulgaria. The facility aims to utilize the Danube River as a shared natural resource for energy production.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **August 2023**: The projected cost for the Turnu Magurele-Nikopol hydropower plant was over €2 billion in 2018, with current inflation likely increasing this figure. Romania's Energy Minister mentioned potential funding sources, including the EU's Modernisation Fund and transborder cooperation funds, although specific investments have not yet been confirmed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2023**: The project is still in the planning phase, with political support from both Romania and Bulgaria. However, environmental concerns have been raised regarding the impact on local ecosystems and existing projects, such as the Fast Danube navigation improvement initiative.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2023**: Local residents express optimism about the project, highlighting that it could create jobs and generate tax revenue for local budgets. The construction of the hydropower plants is expected to require numerous specialists and a significant workforce, which could revitalize the local economy.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **August 2023**: The ownership structure of the facility has not been explicitly detailed in the articles. However, it is implied that the project is a joint initiative between Romania and Bulgaria, as the capacity and benefits are shared between the two countries.
