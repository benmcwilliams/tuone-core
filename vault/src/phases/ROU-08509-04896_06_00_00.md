### Evolutionary Timeline of the Econergy Facility in Parau, Romania

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2024**: Econergy commenced commercial operations at a 92MW solar park in Parau, Romania. The facility began supplying electricity to the grid. Econergy provided engineering, procurement, and construction management services during the construction process and will continue to provide asset management services for the next 10 years.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2024**: Econergy and Rivage Investment, a French asset manager, announced a €150 million debt financing agreement to support their projects, including the Parau facility. 
- **April 2024**: Econergy secured a €60 million financing agreement with Raiffeisen Bank International AG to support its 155MW Ratesti solar project, which is part of its broader investment strategy in Romania.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2024**: The Parau solar park began commercial operations and connected to the grid. This marked a significant milestone in Econergy's expansion in Romania, contributing to a total installed capacity of 247MW across its projects in the country.
- **June 2024**: Econergy sold a 214MW solar PV project in Romania to an undisclosed Balkan conglomerate, indicating a strategic shift in its project portfolio while maintaining a pipeline of about 2GW of projects in Romania.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the Parau facility or other projects.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2024**: The Parau solar park is jointly owned by Econergy and RGreen, a French renewable energy investment fund. This indicates a joint venture structure for the facility, rather than full ownership by Econergy.

### Summary
The Econergy facility in Parau, Romania, represents a significant step in the company's expansion within the renewable energy sector. With a 92MW solar park now operational, joint ownership with RGreen, and ongoing asset management commitments, Econergy is positioning itself as a key player in Romania's energy landscape. The facility is part of a larger strategy that includes substantial financing agreements and a robust project pipeline aimed at increasing renewable energy capacity in the region.
