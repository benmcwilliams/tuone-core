### Evolutionary Timeline of the Econergy Facility in Rătești, Romania

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09-11-2021**: Econergy and Nofar Energy announced the acquisition of a 155 MW solar project in Rătești, which will be the largest solar installation in Romania. The facility is designed to generate 219.19 TWh per annum and will utilize Canadian Solar monocrystalline bifacial and monofacial panels with output classes of 655 W and 660 W.
- **06-11-2023**: The Rătești PV Park was unveiled, confirming its status as Romania's largest PV facility. The plant spans 170 hectares and is expected to be connected to the electricity grid by the end of November 2023.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09-11-2021**: The total investment for the Rătești solar project is reported to be €100 million (approximately US$116 million). By the time of the 2023 article, the construction cost was updated to €102 million (approximately US$109 million).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09-11-2021**: Construction was scheduled to start in Q4 2021, with commercial operations expected to begin in Q4 2022.
- **06-11-2023**: The facility was nearing grid connection, with an announcement that it would be connected to the grid in the following weeks.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific details regarding job creation or job loss associated with the Rătești facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **09-11-2021**: The project is a joint venture between Econergy and Nofar Energy, with each company holding a 50% stake in the facility. Econergy is responsible for EPC (Engineering, Procurement, and Construction) and O&M (Operations and Maintenance) negotiations, construction management, as well as PPA (Power Purchase Agreement) and project finance management.
