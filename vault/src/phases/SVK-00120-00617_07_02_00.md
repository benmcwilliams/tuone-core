### Evolutionary Timeline of the Porsche Facility in Bratislava, Slovakia

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2023**: Porsche announced that it will build the upcoming battery-electric Cayenne at the Volkswagen Slovakia plant in Bratislava. The facility is already producing combustion engine and plug-in hybrid variants of the Cayenne. This marks the plant's transition to manufacturing its first all-electric SUV. The production of the electric Cayenne is expected to start around 2026.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2023**: While Porsche did not disclose specific investment amounts for the upgrade of the Bratislava facility, it indicated that the site is preparing for the start of series production of the extensively upgraded new Cayenne. 

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2023**: Announcement made regarding the production of the all-electric Cayenne at the Bratislava facility, confirming its role in Porsche's electric vehicle strategy.
- **November 2021**: Discussions began regarding the production of the electric Cayenne, with indications that Porsche was considering the Bratislava plant as a preferred location for its production.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2021**: The potential production of the electric Cayenne at the Bratislava facility is part of Porsche's broader strategy to maintain job security at the site, especially in light of the withdrawal from the VW Group’s Artemis project, which was expected to produce around 25,000 vehicles annually.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not provide explicit details about the ownership structure of the facility in Bratislava. However, it is implied that Porsche operates within the Volkswagen Group framework, suggesting a level of shared ownership or operational control typical of multi-brand sites within the group.
