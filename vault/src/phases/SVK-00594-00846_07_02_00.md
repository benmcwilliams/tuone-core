### Evolutionary Timeline of the Volvo Group Facility in Košice, Slovakia

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **02/2022**: Volvo Cars announced plans to build a new factory in Slovakia designed to manufacture up to 250,000 all-electric vehicles per year. The plant will utilize the advanced Scalable Product Architecture 2 (SPA2) platform, specifically for electric vehicles. 
- **11/2023**: Volvo confirmed the order of two 9,000-tonne Giga presses from Idra for the new plant, which will be among the largest casting machines in Europe, aimed at enhancing production efficiency for electric vehicles.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **02/2022**: Volvo Cars is investing approximately 1.2 billion euros in the Košice plant, with around 20% potentially financed by government subsidies. This investment marks a significant commitment to expanding electric vehicle production in Europe.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **02/2022**: Announcement of the new electric car plant in Slovakia, with construction scheduled to begin in 2023 and series production expected to start in 2026.
- **11/2023**: Confirmation of the Giga presses order, indicating progress in the facility's setup and readiness for production.

#### 4. Labour
- **02/2022**: While specific job creation numbers were not detailed, the establishment of the new plant is expected to create numerous jobs in the region, contributing to local employment.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **02/2022**: The facility will be owned exclusively by Volvo Cars, which is a subsidiary of the Geely Group. This marks the company's fifth car plant in Slovakia, reinforcing its commitment to electric vehicle production in Europe.
