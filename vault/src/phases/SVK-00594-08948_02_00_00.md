## SVK-00594-08948

### Evolutionary Timeline of the GEOTERM KOSICE Facility in Kosice, Slovakia

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **December 2024:** Announcement of a geothermal heating project in Kosice with an installed thermal output target of 30 MWt by July 2028. This output is expected to cover about a quarter of the total energy supply of the Kosice heating plant. The project will involve drilling three new geothermal wells to complement the existing three wells at the Svinica-Durkov geothermal center.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **December 2024:** The project will receive EUR 56.2 million from the European Commission’s Just Transition Fund. This funding is part of a broader strategy to enhance geothermal energy utilization in Slovakia and is categorized as a “national priority project.”

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **January 2025:** The project is set to officially start, with a planned duration of 43 months for implementation. The contract for the project was signed by the Ministry of Investments, Regional Development and Informatization (MIRRI) of the Slovak Republic with GEOTERM KOSICE and MH Teplarensky holding.

4. **Labour:**
   - **December 2024:** The project is expected to support job creation and contribute to the development of the local economy, although specific job numbers have not been detailed.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **December 2024:** The facility is operated by GEOTERM KOSICE in partnership with MH Teplarensky holding, indicating a joint venture structure for the project.
