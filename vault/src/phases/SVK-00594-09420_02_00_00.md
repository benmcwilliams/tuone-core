## SVK-00594-09420

### Evolutionary Timeline of the MH Teplarensky Facility in Kosice, Slovakia

1. **Technology and Operations**:
   - **December 2024**: Announcement of a geothermal heating project in Kosice, aiming for an installed thermal output of 30 MWt by July 2028. This project will cover about a quarter of the total energy supply of the Kosice heating plant. The project involves drilling three new geothermal wells to complement the existing three wells at the Svinica-Durkov geothermal center.

2. **Investment and Development**:
   - **December 2024**: The project will receive EUR 56.2 million from the European Commission’s Just Transition Fund. This funding is part of a broader strategy to enhance geothermal energy utilization in Slovakia and is categorized as a “national priority project.”

3. **Project Status and Milestones**:
   - **January 2025**: The geothermal heating project is set to commence, with a planned duration of 43 months. The contract for the project was signed by the Ministry of Investments, Regional Development and Informatization (MIRRI) of the Slovak Republic with implementing partners GEOTERM KOSICE and MH Teplarensky.

4. **Labour**:
   - **December 2024**: The project is expected to support job creation and contribute to the development of the local economy, although specific job numbers are not detailed in the announcement.

5. **Ownership Structure**:
   - The facility is part of a collaborative effort between MH Teplarensky and GEOTERM KOSICE, indicating a joint venture or partnership structure for the geothermal heating project, rather than exclusive ownership by MH Teplarensky.
