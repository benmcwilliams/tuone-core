### Evolutionary Timeline of the Jaguar Land Rover Facility in Nitra, Slovakia

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2017**: Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) announces that its facility in Nitra, Slovakia, currently under construction, will also be used to assemble batteries. This indicates a future capability to manufacture electric cars and plug-in hybrids.
- **June 2018**: JLR confirms that the Nitra facility is capable of producing both internal combustion engine (ICE) vehicles and plug-in hybrid vehicles. The facility is designed to accommodate a flexible platform for both petrol and electric models.
- **October 2023**: JLR announces plans to convert the Nitra plant for battery electric vehicle (BEV) production. The first electric vehicles are expected to roll off the production line before the end of the decade. The facility will continue to manufacture the Defender and Discovery models as ICE vehicles until their electric counterparts are introduced.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2019**: JLR invested €1.3 billion in the construction of the Nitra plant, which features a modern production system, including Kuka’s Pulse Carrier system, enhancing efficiency by reducing handling times by 30% compared to conventional systems.
- **October 2023**: JLR announces a total investment of £15 billion over the next five years for the group’s electric future, which includes the conversion of the Nitra plant for electric vehicle production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2017**: Announcement of the construction of the facility in Nitra, with plans for battery assembly.
- **June 2018**: Confirmation that production of the Land Rover Discovery will start in the autumn of 2018 at the Nitra facility.
- **October 2023**: Announcement of the conversion of the Nitra plant for BEV production, with plans to introduce nine all-electric models by 2030.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2023**: JLR's Executive Director of Industrial Operations highlights the plant's workforce of 5,000 skilled employees, indicating job stability and confidence in the facility's future as it transitions to electric vehicle production.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility in Nitra is owned exclusively by Jaguar Land Rover, as there is no mention of joint ventures or partnerships in the provided articles.
