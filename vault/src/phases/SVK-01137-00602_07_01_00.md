## SVK-01137-00602

### Evolutionary Timeline of the PSA Group Facility in Trnava, Slovakia

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2018**: PSA announces plans to manufacture electric cars, specifically the Peugeot 208 and its electric variant (e-208), at the Trnava facility. The factory is being prepared to host the CMP platform, allowing for the production of both electric and combustion engine models on the same line.
- **March 2019**: Production of the Peugeot e-208 begins, with the first vehicle rolling off the assembly line on March 27, 2019. The e-208 features a 100 kW output and a WLTP range of 340 km, powered by a 50 kWh battery.
- **June 2019**: PSA announces the establishment of battery pack assembly for electric and hybrid cars at the Trnava plant, which will support the production of the e-208 and other models.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2019**: PSA is preparing for the transition to electric vehicles, planning to offer electric or hybrid versions of all 40 models by 2025. The company is investing in battery assembly capabilities at the Trnava facility to support this transition.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2018**: Announcement of electric vehicle production plans at the Trnava facility.
- **March 2019**: Start of production for the Peugeot e-208.
- **June 2019**: Official announcement of battery pack assembly at the Trnava plant.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2019**: The establishment of battery pack assembly is expected to create jobs at the Trnava facility, although specific job creation numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The articles do not specify any joint ventures or partnerships regarding the ownership of the Trnava facility, indicating that PSA Group operates the facility exclusively.
