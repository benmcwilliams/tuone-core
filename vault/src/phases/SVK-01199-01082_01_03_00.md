### Evolutionary Timeline of the Gotion InoBat Batteries Facility in Voderady, Slovakia

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2023**: InoBat launched the first battery cell production line in Slovakia at its research laboratories in Voderady. The pilot line can produce up to 50,000 battery cells annually, with full production expected to commence in early 2024. The production line was custom-built by Wuxi Lead from China and includes 35 machines that cover the entire production process, utilizing high-speed layering technology. 
- **Future Plans**: A large battery cell factory, through a joint venture called Gotion InoBat Batteries (GIB), is planned with an annual capacity of 20 gigawatt-hours (GWh), with potential to double to 40 GWh. Production is scheduled to start in the second quarter of 2026, with series production expected in 2027. Additionally, InoBat plans to establish a 100 MWh pilot production facility and a 10 GWh factory in 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2023**: The establishment of the GIB factory is part of a joint venture between InoBat and Gotion High-Tech, indicating significant investment in the facility's development. Specific investment amounts were not detailed in the article.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2023**: The pilot line was officially launched, marking the beginning of battery cell production in Slovakia. The transfer and installation of the production line from China took several months, highlighting the logistical challenges involved. The GIB factory is set to begin production in Q2 2026, with series production following in 2027.

#### 4. Labour
- **December 2023**: The pilot line will employ 41 operators from InoBat, who will work in shifts to ensure 24/7 operation. The establishment of the GIB factory and additional facilities is expected to create more jobs, although specific job creation numbers were not provided.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2023**: The facility is part of a joint venture called Gotion InoBat Batteries (GIB), indicating that InoBat does not exclusively own the facility but shares ownership with Gotion High-Tech.
