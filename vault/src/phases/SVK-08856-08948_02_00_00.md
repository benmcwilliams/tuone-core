## SVK-08856-08948

### Evolutionary Timeline of the GEOTERM KOSICE Facility in Košice, Slovakia

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Date: 02-09-2023**: The GEOTERM KOSICE facility is focused on utilizing geothermal energy to provide heating solutions. The geothermal resource in Košice is believed to be the largest in Slovakia, capable of generating heat to supply approximately 171,000 customers and offsetting up to 54,000 tons of CO2 emissions annually. The facility will also provide residual heat for agricultural and recreational purposes along a 15-kilometer route of the heat pipe.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Date: 02-09-2023**: The project has been classified as a National Priority Project under the Just Transformation Fund, which has a total allocation of EUR 441 million. The total estimated cost for the geothermal project is EUR 88 million. The project developer, MH Teplarensky Holding, will apply for the allocated fund with the participation of GEOTERM KOSICE.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Date: 12-07-2023**: The Monitoring Committee of the Commission for the Just Transformation Fund approved the proposal for the geothermal project in Košice, marking a significant milestone in its development.
- **Date: 2021**: Initial plans for the Kosice heating plant were first reported, indicating the long-term vision for the geothermal project.

#### 4. Labour
- **Date: 02-09-2023**: While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned, the project is expected to contribute to the local economy and job market through the development and operation of the geothermal facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Date: 02-09-2023**: The facility is a partnership between MH Teplarensky Holding and GEOTERM KOSICE, indicating a joint venture structure rather than exclusive ownership by a single entity.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the GEOTERM KOSICE facility, highlighting its significance in advancing renewable energy in Slovakia.
