## SVK-08856-09421

### Evolutionary Timeline of the MH Teplarensky Holding Facility in Košice, Slovakia

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production**: The facility is focused on geothermal energy production, utilizing the geothermal resource believed to be the largest in Slovakia.
- **Production Capacity**: The geothermal resource can generate heat to supply approximately 171,000 customers and is expected to offset up to 54,000 tons of CO2 emissions annually.
- **Updates**: The project aims to provide residual heat for agriculture, recreational, and other purposes along a 15-kilometer route of the heat pipe.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investments**: The total cost for the geothermal project is estimated at EUR 88 million.
- **Subsidies**: The project has been classified as a National Priority Project and is eligible for funding from the Just Transformation Fund, which has a total allocation of EUR 441 million.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Announcement**: The project was proposed by Minister of Investments, Regional Development and Informatization Peter Balík and was classified as a National Priority Project on July 12, 2023.
- **Construction Start**: Specific construction start dates are not mentioned, but the project has been in development discussions for over two years, with plans first reported in 2021.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: While specific job creation numbers are not provided, the project is expected to contribute to the local economy and energy self-sufficiency, which may imply job opportunities in the construction and operational phases.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The facility is developed by MH Teplarensky Holding in partnership with GEOTERM KOSICE, indicating a joint venture structure for this project.
