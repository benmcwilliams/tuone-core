### Evolutionary Timeline of the Slovenské elektrárne Facility in Jaslovské Bohunice, SVK

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2023**: Slovenské elektrárne proposed five Slovak sites, including Jaslovské Bohunice, for feasibility studies related to the conversion of coal-to-small modular reactors (SMR). The feasibility study aims to assess the suitability of these sites for potential future construction of SMRs, which are intended to complement existing nuclear and renewable energy sources.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2023**: The facility is part of a successful grant application under the US Project Phoenix, which received USD 8 million in support from the US government. This funding is aimed at supporting energy security and climate goals through the development of SMR technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09/2023**: The announcement of the feasibility study for SMR at Jaslovské Bohunice was made during the Three Seas Initiative Summit in Bucharest. This marks a significant milestone in the facility's evolution towards adopting new technologies.

#### 4. Labour
- **09/2023**: The project aims to retain local jobs through workforce retraining as part of the transition to SMR technology, although specific job creation or loss figures were not detailed in the announcement.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **09/2023**: Slovenské elektrárne is involved in a partnership for the Project Phoenix but the ownership structure of the facility itself remains under the control of Slovenské elektrárne, indicating full ownership of the Jaslovské Bohunice facility.
