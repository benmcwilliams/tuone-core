## SVK-09661-09699

### Evolutionary Timeline of the PW Energy Facility in Ziar nad Hronom, Slovakia

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2021**: PW Energy announced the development of a geothermal power plant in Ziar nad Hronom with an initial installed capacity of 6.3 to 6.5 MWe. The facility aims to utilize hot water at approximately 140 degrees Celsius for electricity generation and residual heat for various applications.
- **December 2023**: The project was updated to reflect a total expected generation capacity of 20 MWe. The facility will use deep geothermal water with temperatures between 130 to 140 degrees Celsius for electricity production.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2021**: The estimated investment cost for the first phase of the geothermal project in the Presov basin was reported to be EUR 54 million (approximately $65 million).
- **Mid-2022**: Stredoslovenska energetika Holding (SSE Holding) acquired a stake in PW Geoenergy, a subsidiary of PW Energy, which was established for the implementation of the geothermal power plant in Ziar nad Hronom.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2021**: The project was submitted for environmental impact assessment (EIA) as part of the planning process.
- **Early 2022**: A pre-tender request for information was published by PW Energy for drilling at the Ziar nad Hronom project.
- **December 2023**: A tender was announced for the drilling of two geothermal wells (GTZ-1 and GTZ-2) as part of the project development.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the investments or expansions at the facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Mid-2022**: PW Energy is not exclusively owning the facility as Stredoslovenska energetika Holding (SSE Holding) acquired a stake in PW Geoenergy, indicating a joint venture or partnership structure for the project.
