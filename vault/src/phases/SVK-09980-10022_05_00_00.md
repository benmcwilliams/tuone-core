## SVK-09980-10022

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Slovenské elektrárne Facility in Mochovce, Slovakia

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **1982**: Construction of the first two VVER-440 units at the Mochovce plant began.
- **1986**: Work commenced on units 3 and 4, but was stalled in 1992.
- **1998-1999**: The first two reactors were completed and became operational.
- **2023**: Unit 3 was connected to the grid on January 31, initially at 20% of its nominal power. By March, output increased to 55%, July to 75%, and August to 90%. 
- **October 2023**: Unit 3 reached full power and entered commercial operation.
- **December 2023**: Cold hydro-testing for unit 4 began, marking the start of its commissioning process.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2022**: The Slovak Nuclear Regulatory Authority issued the final authorization for commissioning of unit 3.
- **2023**: Slovenské elektrárne signed an extension to its supply-and-pricing agreement with the Slovak government, ensuring stable pricing for electricity supply through 2027.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2023**: Unit 3 connected to the grid.
- **March 2023**: Output increased to 55%.
- **July 2023**: Output increased to 75%.
- **August 2023**: Output increased to 90%.
- **October 2023**: Unit 3 completed its commissioning process and began commercial operation.
- **December 2023**: Cold hydro-testing for unit 4 commenced.

#### 4. Labour
- **2023**: The commissioning of unit 3 is expected to create jobs related to its operation and maintenance, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Current**: Slovenské elektrárne is equally owned by the Slovak state, Italy's Enel, and the Czech EPH group.
