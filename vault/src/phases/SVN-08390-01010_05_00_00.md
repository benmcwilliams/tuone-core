## SVN-08390-01010

### Evolutionary Timeline of the EDF Facility in Krško, Slovenia

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Production**: The existing facility at Krško is a 696 MWe pressurized water reactor, which generates about one-third of Slovenia's electricity.
- **Future Plans**: Slovenia plans to build a new nuclear power plant, the JEK2 project, with a capacity of up to 2400 MW next to the existing Krško plant. The timeline for new capacity is aimed for the 2030s.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Timeline**: A final investment decision for the JEK2 project is anticipated by 2028, with the aim of new capacity coming online in the 2030s. The government is working to prepare for a referendum that is necessary for the project to proceed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Referendum Plans**: A referendum on the proposed new nuclear capacity at the Krško plant is planned for the second half of 2024. This referendum will determine the future of nuclear energy in Slovenia and the construction of the second block at Krško.
- **Working Group Formation**: A working group of Slovenian government ministers and industry officials was established in September 2023 to expedite the implementation of the JEK2 project.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: While specific job creation figures have not been detailed, the expansion and construction of the JEK2 project are expected to create jobs, particularly in the construction and operational phases.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Co-Ownership**: The existing Krško facility is co-owned by Slovenia and Croatia. The ownership structure for the new JEK2 project has not been explicitly detailed in the articles, but it is implied that similar co-ownership arrangements may be considered.

### Summary
The EDF facility in Krško, Slovenia, currently operates a 696 MWe nuclear reactor and is planning to expand with the JEK2 project, which aims for a capacity of up to 2400 MW. A referendum is set for late 2024 to decide on the future of this expansion, with a final investment decision expected by 2028. The facility is co-owned by Slovenia and Croatia, and while job creation is anticipated, specific numbers have not been provided.
