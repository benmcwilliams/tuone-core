### Evolutionary Timeline of the Korea Hydro & Nuclear Power Facility in Krško, Slovenia

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Facility**: The existing facility at Krško is a 696 MWe pressurized water reactor, which generates about one-third of Slovenia's electricity. 
- **Future Plans**: Slovenia plans to build a new nuclear power plant, the JEK2 project, with a capacity of up to 2400 MW, adjacent to the existing Krško plant. The timeline for the new capacity to come online is projected for the 2030s.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Timeline**: A final investment decision for the JEK2 project is anticipated by 2028. The government is working to prepare for a referendum that is necessary for the project to proceed.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Current Status**: As of January 2024, a working group of Slovenian government ministers and industry officials was established in September 2023 to expedite the JEK2 project.
- **Referendum Plans**: A referendum on the proposed new nuclear capacity at the Krško plant is expected to be held in the second half of 2024, with discussions ongoing regarding the wording of the referendum question.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: While specific job creation figures have not been detailed, the development of the JEK2 project is expected to create jobs, particularly during the construction phase and operational phases once the new capacity is online.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Co-Ownership**: The existing Krško facility is co-owned by Slovenia and Croatia. The ownership structure for the planned JEK2 project has not been explicitly detailed in the articles, but it is implied that Korea Hydro & Nuclear Power is one of the technology providers being considered for the project.

### Summary
The Korea Hydro & Nuclear Power facility in Krško, Slovenia, currently operates a 696 MWe reactor and is involved in the planning of a new nuclear power plant, JEK2, with a capacity of up to 2400 MW. A referendum is planned for 2024 to decide on the future of nuclear energy in Slovenia, with a final investment decision expected by 2028. The facility is co-owned by Slovenia and Croatia, and while job creation is anticipated with the new project, specific figures have not been provided.
