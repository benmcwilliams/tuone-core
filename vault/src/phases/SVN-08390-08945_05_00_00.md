## SVN-08390-08945

### Evolutionary Timeline of the GEN Energija Facility in Krško, Slovenia

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Production**: The Krško facility operates a 696 MWe pressurized water reactor, generating approximately one-third of Slovenia's electricity.
- **Future Plans**: The JEK2 project aims to construct one or two additional nuclear units with a total capacity of up to 2400 MW, enhancing Slovenia's energy independence and reducing CO2 emissions.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Projected Costs**: The estimated cost for the JEK2 project is around EUR 9.3 billion for a reactor of similar size to Korea Hydro & Nuclear Power's 1000 MWe reactor in the Czech Republic, which had a projected cost of EUR 7.9 billion.
- **Funding and Subsidies**: The project is still in the planning stages, with an international review underway to validate economic assumptions and calculations.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2023**: Prime Minister Golob announced the initiation of procedures for the JEK2 project, with a conceptual decision expected on August 1, 2023. A construction decision is anticipated by the end of 2027.
- **August 2024**: The current timeline indicates a final investment decision will be made in 2028, with construction slated to begin in 2032. A referendum on the project is expected to take place later in 2024.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The JEK2 project is expected to create jobs and enhance skills across the Slovenian economy, although specific job numbers have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Ownership**: The Krško facility is co-owned by GEN Energija (Slovenia) and Hrvatska elektroprivreda (HEP Group) from Croatia. The facility is not exclusively owned by GEN Energija.
