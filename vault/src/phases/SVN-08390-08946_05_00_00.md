### Evolutionary Timeline of the GEN Energy Facility in Krško, Slovenia

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Production**: The existing facility at Krško is a 696 MWe pressurized water reactor, generating about one-third of Slovenia's electricity.
- **Future Plans**: There are plans to build a new nuclear power plant, referred to as the JEK2 project, which aims to have a capacity of up to 2400 MW. This new facility is intended to be constructed next to the existing Krško plant.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Timeline**: A final investment decision for the JEK2 project is anticipated by 2028, with the goal of having the new capacity operational in the 2030s. Specific investment amounts have not been disclosed in the articles.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Referendum Plans**: A referendum on the proposed new nuclear capacity at the Krško plant is planned for the second half of 2024. This referendum is crucial for determining the future of nuclear energy in Slovenia and the construction of the second block at Krško.
- **Working Group Formation**: A working group of Slovenian government ministers and industry officials was established in September 2023 to expedite the implementation of the JEK2 project.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The articles do not specify the exact number of jobs that may be created or lost as a result of the JEK2 project or any expansions at the facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Co-Ownership**: The existing Krško nuclear power plant is co-owned by Slovenia and Croatia. The articles do not specify whether GEN Energy will have exclusive ownership of the new JEK2 facility or if it will also be co-owned.

### Summary
The GEN Energy facility in Krško, Slovenia, currently operates a 696 MWe nuclear reactor and is planning to expand with the JEK2 project, which aims for a capacity of up to 2400 MW. A referendum is set for late 2024 to decide on the future of this expansion, with a final investment decision expected by 2028. The facility is co-owned by Slovenia and Croatia, and the ownership structure for the new project remains unclear.
