## SVN-08390-08947

### Evolutionary Timeline of the GEN energija Facility in Krško, Slovenia

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2023**: GEN energija announced plans for the second reactor (Krško 2) at the existing Krško Nuclear Power Plant, projected to have a capacity of 1.1 GW and an annual electricity production of 8.8 TWh. The reactor's lifespan is estimated at 60 years. The construction is contingent on obtaining necessary permits, which are still pending.
- **May 2024**: The estimated construction costs for the new reactor (JEK2) were reported to be between EUR 9.3 billion and EUR 15.4 billion, depending on the capacity (1000 MW to 1650 MW). The optimal size for the new plant was assessed to be up to 1300 MW.
- **September 2024**: A report indicated that the JEK2 project would be built adjacent to the existing Krško NPP, with a capacity of up to 2400 MW. The project is deemed of "exceptional strategic importance" for Slovenia's energy security and decarbonization efforts.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2023**: The estimated cost for the construction of the second unit was projected to be between EUR 5 billion and EUR 6 billion.
- **May 2024**: The internal assessment of construction costs for the JEK2 project was reported at EUR 9300 per kW of installed power, with a total estimated cost of EUR 9.3 billion for a 1000 MW plant and EUR 15.4 billion for a 1650 MW plant.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2023**: GEN energija indicated that the second reactor could be operational by 2035 if all procedures and construction run on schedule. A referendum on the construction is planned for 2027.
- **May 2024**: A referendum on the JEK2 project is suggested to be held later in 2024, with a final investment decision expected in 2028 and construction slated to begin in 2032.
- **September 2024**: The timeline for the JEK2 project remains consistent, with a final investment decision planned for 2028 and construction beginning in 2032.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2024**: The JEK2 project is expected to create approximately 5640 new jobs over the next 10 years, with a projected 37% share of domestic suppliers involved in the project.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2023**: The Krško Nuclear Power Plant is co-owned by GEN energija and Croatia’s government-controlled utility, Hrvatska elektroprivreda (HEP). There is no indication of exclusive ownership by GEN energija for the facility.
