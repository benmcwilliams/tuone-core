## SVN-08390-09150

### Evolutionary Timeline of Holtec International Facility in Krško, Slovenia

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **07/2023**: The facility officially commissioned with the placement of the first HI-STORM FW cask into the used fuel dry storage facility at the Krško nuclear power plant. The facility measures 50m x 70m x 20m and features a reinforced concrete base of 1.75m thick. Each HI-STORM FW cask can hold 37 used fuel elements, with a total of 16 casks planned for the first fuel loading campaign, amounting to 592 used fuel elements by late 2023. The design includes seismic restraints and is capable of withstanding severe environmental conditions.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **02/2017**: Holtec International awarded a contract by Nuklearna Elektrarna Krško (NEK) for the design and construction of the dry storage facility, including the replacement of the fuel handling building crane trolley and supply of necessary equipment and services.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **10/2022**: NEK received the operating license for the dry storage facility from the Slovenian Nuclear Safety Administration (URSJV).
- **03/2020**: NEK completed the process of amending the NPP Regulatory Plan, which included a comprehensive environmental impact assessment and cross-border consultation.
- **02/2021**: NEK obtained an integral building permit for the dry storage facility.
- **07/2023**: The facility was officially commissioned, marking the transition to dry storage for used fuel.
