## SVN-08390-09363

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Krško Nuclear Power Plant (NEK) Facility

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **March 2023:** The Krško Nuclear Power Plant (NEK) is preparing to launch a dry storage facility for used fuel, which is part of a 20-year operating extension. The dry storage facility measures 50m x 70m x 20m and features a reinforced concrete base of 1.75m. It is designed to provide protection against flooding and allows for the monitoring by the International Atomic Energy Agency. The facility will initially transfer 592 used fuel elements into storage, with each container capable of holding 37 used fuel cells.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **March 2023:** The establishment of the dry storage facility is linked to the plant's operational extension for an additional 20 years, which was approved by Slovenia's Ministry of the Environment after an environmental impact assessment.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **March 2023:** Announcement of the dry storage facility's completion later in the year, with the first campaign to move 16 containers with 592 spent fuel cells to dry storage by autumn.

4. **Labour:**

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The Krško Nuclear Power Plant is jointly owned by Slovenia and Croatia, making it unique as it is operated by two countries.
