### Evolutionary Timeline of the Gen-Energija Facility in Vrbina pri Krsko, Slovenia

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **Current Production:** The facility is associated with the Krsko Nuclear Power Plant (NPP), which currently operates a Westinghouse pressurized light water reactor with a thermal power capacity of 2,000 MW, generating approximately 40% of Slovenia's electricity output.
   - **Planned Expansion:** As of September 2023, Gen-Energija is considering expanding the planned capacity of a projected second unit at Krsko NPP from 1,100 MW to 2,400 MW. This expansion could potentially allow for the construction of two nuclear units at the site.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **Legislative Changes:** The increase in planned capacity is facilitated by a change in Slovenian legislation that previously limited the capacity to 1,100 MW. This change, along with the energy crisis, has heightened the need for electricity and increased interest from neighboring countries in co-investing in the project.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **Future Decision Timeline:** A decision regarding the construction of the second unit at Krsko NPP is expected to be made in 2024, contingent upon the results of a feasibility study being prepared by Gen-Energija. The decision may also be subject to a referendum.

4. **Labour:**
   - **Job Creation Potential:** While specific job creation figures have not been mentioned, the expansion of the facility and the construction of a new unit are likely to create jobs, although exact numbers are not provided in the article.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - **Co-Ownership:** The Krsko NPP is co-owned by Gen-Energija and the Croatian power utility Hrvatska Elektroprivreda (HEP). Therefore, Gen-Energija does not have exclusive ownership of the facility.
