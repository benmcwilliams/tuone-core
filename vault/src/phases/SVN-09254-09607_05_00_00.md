### Evolutionary Timeline of Nuklearna elektrarna Krsko Facility in Krsko, Slovenia

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Krsko nuclear power plant (NPP) currently operates a Westinghouse pressurized light water reactor with a thermal power capacity of 2,000 MW. It generates approximately 40% of Slovenia's electricity output.
   - As of September 2023, there are considerations to expand the planned capacity of a projected second unit at Krsko NPP from 1,100 MW to 2,400 MW. This expansion is facilitated by recent changes in Slovenian legislation that removed the previous capacity limit.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The potential capacity increase for the second unit is linked to heightened interest from neighboring countries in co-investing in the project, driven by the ongoing energy crisis. A feasibility study is being prepared by Gen-Energija, which is expected to inform future investment decisions.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - A decision regarding the construction of the second unit at Krsko NPP is anticipated in 2024, with the possibility of a referendum to vote on the project. The feasibility study's conclusions will play a crucial role in this timeline.

4. **Labour:**
   - The articles do not provide specific information regarding job creation or job loss associated with the current operations or the proposed expansions at the Krsko facility.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The Krsko NPP is co-owned by Gen-Energija, a Slovenian state-run energy group, and Hrvatska Elektroprivreda (HEP), a Croatian power utility. This indicates a joint venture ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by a single entity.
