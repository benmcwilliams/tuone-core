## SWE-00114-00582

### Evolutionary Timeline of Northvolt Facility in Borlänge, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2022**: Northvolt announced plans to convert the closed Kvarnsveden Mill in Borlänge into a gigafactory. The facility is expected to produce up to 100 GWh of cathode material and battery cells. The first part of operations is scheduled to begin by the end of 2024.
- **January 2024**: Northvolt announced a review of its expansion plans, including the cathode material plant in Borlänge, which was put on hold.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2022**: Northvolt signed a letter of intent to purchase the Kvarnsveden Mill, indicating a significant investment in the facility's conversion.
- **July 2022**: Northvolt raised $1.1 billion in convertible bonds to expand battery production, which includes the Borlänge facility.
- **January 2024**: Northvolt secured a new $5 billion project financing to support its expansion plans, including the Borlänge facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2022**: Announcement of the conversion of the Kvarnsveden Mill into a gigafactory.
- **End of 2024**: Expected start of the first part of operations at the Borlänge facility.
- **January 2024**: Northvolt announced the suspension of the expansion of the Borlänge facility, indicating a potential cancellation of the project.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2022**: The facility is expected to employ up to 1,000 people once operational.
- **January 2024**: Job creation plans for the Borlänge facility were put on hold due to the suspension of the expansion.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2022**: Northvolt is in the process of acquiring the Kvarnsveden Mill, indicating that it will have full ownership of the facility once the acquisition is complete.

### Summary
The Northvolt facility in Borlänge, Sweden, is in the process of being converted from a closed paper mill into a gigafactory for battery production. Initial plans indicated a significant production capacity and job creation, but recent developments have led to a suspension of expansion plans, raising concerns about the project's future viability. Northvolt is expected to have full ownership of the facility upon completion of the acquisition.
