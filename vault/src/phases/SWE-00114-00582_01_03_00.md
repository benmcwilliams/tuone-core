## SWE-00114-00582

### Evolutionary Timeline of Northvolt Facility in Borlänge, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2022**: Northvolt announced plans to convert the closed Kvarnsveden Mill in Borlänge into a gigafactory. The facility is expected to produce up to 100 GWh of cathode material and battery cells. The first part of operations is scheduled to begin by the end of 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **July 2022**: Northvolt raised $1.1 billion in convertible bonds to expand battery production in Europe, which includes the refurbishment of the Borlänge facility. This funding is part of a larger strategy to meet the demand from major automotive customers.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2022**: Announcement of the acquisition of the Kvarnsveden Mill in Borlänge and plans for its conversion into a gigafactory.
- **End of 2024**: Expected start of the first part of operations at the Borlänge facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2022**: Northvolt's conversion of the Kvarnsveden Mill is expected to create up to 1,000 jobs once the facility is fully operational.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2022**: Northvolt is set to purchase the Kvarnsveden Mill from Stora Enso, indicating that Northvolt will have full ownership of the facility after the acquisition.

### Summary
The Northvolt facility in Borlänge, Sweden, is in the process of being transformed from a closed paper mill into a gigafactory capable of producing 100 GWh of cathode materials and battery cells. The project is backed by significant investment, including a $1.1 billion funding round aimed at expanding production capabilities. The facility is expected to create around 1,000 jobs and is set to begin operations by the end of 2024, with Northvolt acquiring full ownership of the site from Stora Enso.
