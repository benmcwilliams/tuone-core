## SWE-00386-00136

### Evolutionary Timeline of Chalmers University of Technology Facility in Gothenburg, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **08-03-2021**: The facility is part of the Swedish Electric Transport Laboratory (SEEL), which aims to enhance e-mobility in Sweden. The Gothenburg site will focus on heavy industry applications, including trucks, buses, construction equipment, aircraft, and ships. It will feature test benches for various components such as gearboxes, electric motors, batteries, and fuel cells. The facility is set to cover an area of 13,000 square meters.
- **18-10-2023**: Chalmers University develops a new battery recycling process using oxalic acid, which allows for the recovery of 100% of aluminum and 98% of lithium from electric car batteries. This innovative method is being tested on e-car batteries from Volvo and aims to minimize the loss of valuable raw materials.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **08-03-2021**: The investment for the SEEL project increased from 96 million euros to 1.3 billion Swedish kronor (approximately 128 million euros). Funding includes 575 million kronor (over 56 million euros) from the Swedish Energy Agency, with additional support from the Swedish government.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **08-03-2021**: Announcement of the SEEL expansion, which includes the Gothenburg facility as one of three sites. The opening of the Gothenburg site is planned for Q2/2023, which is about six months later than initially scheduled.
- **18-10-2023**: The development of the new battery recycling process is reported, indicating ongoing research and innovation at the facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **08-03-2021**: The expansion of the SEEL facility is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers are not provided in the articles. The focus on testing and development in e-mobility and battery technology suggests potential job growth in these sectors.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **08-03-2021**: The SEEL project is a 50:50 joint venture between Chalmers University of Technology and RISE (Research Institutes of Sweden), with industry partners including Volvo and Scania. This indicates that Chalmers does not own the facility exclusively but shares ownership with RISE and other partners.
