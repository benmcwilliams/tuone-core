### Evolutionary Timeline of the Northvolt Facility in Gothenburg, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2022**: Northvolt and Volvo Cars announced plans to build a joint battery cell factory in Gothenburg, with a potential annual production capacity of up to 50 GWh, sufficient to supply batteries for around 500,000 electric vehicles per year. The factory is located in the Torslanda district, near Volvo's vehicle plant.
- **March 2022**: The facility is confirmed to be part of a larger investment plan, which includes a research and development center.
- **March 2023**: The Swedish Land and Environment Court approved the construction of the factory, allowing the project to move forward.
- **September 2023**: Construction officially began, with the factory expected to be operational by 2026.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2022**: The total investment for the Gothenburg facility and the associated research center is estimated at 30 billion Swedish kronor (approximately 2.9 billion euros).
- **July 2022**: Northvolt raised $1.1 billion in convertible bonds to support its expansion, which includes the Gothenburg facility.
- **August 2023**: Northvolt announced plans to raise an additional $1.2 billion to finance further expansion in Europe and North America, which includes the Gothenburg project.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2022**: Announcement of the joint venture and plans for the factory.
- **March 2022**: Initial plans confirmed, with a focus on battery cell production.
- **March 2023**: Approval from the Swedish Land and Environment Court to proceed with construction.
- **September 2023**: Groundbreaking ceremony held, marking the start of construction.
- **2026**: Expected operational date for the factory.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2022**: The factory is projected to create over 3,000 jobs upon completion.
- **March 2023**: The CEO of Novo Energy (the joint venture) stated that the factory will employ more than 3,000 people.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2022**: The facility is a joint venture between Northvolt and Volvo Cars, operating under the name Novo Energy. This indicates a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by either company.

This timeline captures the key developments and milestones related to the Northvolt facility in Gothenburg, Sweden, focusing on its technology, investment, project status, labor implications, and ownership structure.
