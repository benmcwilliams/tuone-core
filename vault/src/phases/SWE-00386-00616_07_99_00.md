### Evolutionary Timeline of the Polestar Facility in Gothenburg, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **05-10-2018**: Polestar began production of the Polestar 1 electric supercar in Gothenburg, completing 34 handmade prototypes for testing. Serial production is primarily set to occur at a facility in Chengdu, China, which is under construction.
- **24-04-2023**: Polestar announced plans to establish a European development center at the former Saab plant in Trollhättan, Sweden, to develop and test the powertrain for the upcoming Polestar 5, set to launch in 2024. The Gothenburg facility continues to serve as a development center.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12-07-2021**: Volvo Cars increased its stake in Polestar to 49.5% by acquiring shares from PSD Investment, which is owned by Eric Li, chairman of both Volvo Cars and Geely. This acquisition was part of a broader strategy to secure funding for product development and technological innovations, with a capital increase of $550 million from long-term investors earlier in the year.
- **01-02-2024**: Volvo announced it would no longer provide fresh capital for Polestar, indicating a shift in focus towards its own investments in technology and production facilities.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **05-10-2018**: The first Polestar 1 vehicles were produced, marking a significant milestone towards full production.
- **27-09-2021**: Polestar was reported to be close to completing a SPAC merger with Gores Guggenheim, which would facilitate an IPO.
- **28-06-2024**: Polestar launched a new entry-level version of the Polestar 3, with initial deliveries starting at the Gothenburg headquarters.

#### 4. Labour
- **01-02-2024**: Polestar laid off 15% of its workforce as part of a restructuring effort after missing its targets for 2023.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **12-07-2021**: Volvo Cars holds a 49.5% stake in Polestar, while Zhejiang Geely Holding Group is the majority owner. The ownership structure indicates a joint venture between Volvo and Geely, with Polestar operating under this partnership.
