### Evolutionary Timeline of the RISE Facility in Gothenburg, Sweden

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The RISE facility in Gothenburg is part of the Swedish Electric Transport Laboratory (SEEL), which focuses on e-mobility. It will serve as a testing ground for electrical drivelines, energy storage, and entire systems for propulsion and energy management. The facility will include test benches for gearboxes, electric motors, batteries, and fuel cells, with a specific emphasis on heavy industry applications such as trucks, buses, construction equipment, aircraft, and ships.
   - The largest SEEL plant will be located in Säve, Gothenburg, covering an area of 13,000 square meters.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The total investment for the SEEL project increased from 96 million euros to approximately 1.3 billion Swedish kronor (around 128 million euros). 
   - The Swedish Energy Agency is providing funding of 575 million kronor (just over 56 million euros) for the project.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **March 2021:** Announcement of the expansion of the SEEL project to include three sites: Gothenburg, Nykvarn, and Borås. The opening of the Gothenburg facility is planned for Q2/2023, which is about six months later than initially scheduled.

4. **Labour:**
   - The articles do not specify the exact number of jobs created or lost as a result of the investments or expansions related to the RISE facility.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The RISE facility is owned as a 50:50 joint venture between RISE (Research Institutes of Sweden) and Chalmers University of Technology. Industry partners include CEVT, Scania, Volvo Cars, and the Volvo Group.
