## SWE-00386-00706

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Scania Facility in Gothenburg, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Focus**: The facility will be part of the Swedish Electric Transport Laboratory (SEEL), focusing on e-mobility technologies, specifically testing electrical drivelines, energy storage, and entire systems for propulsion and energy management. 
- **Production Capacity**: The largest SEEL plant will be located in Säve, Gothenburg, covering an area of 13,000 square meters, primarily focusing on heavy industry applications such as trucks and buses, construction equipment, aircraft, and ships.
- **Updates**: The facility will include test benches for gearboxes, shaft systems, hubs, electric motors, power electronics, batteries, and fuel cells.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Amount**: The total investment for the SEEL project increased from 96 million euros to approximately 1.3 billion Swedish kronor (around 128 million euros).
- **Funding Sources**: The project receives funding from the Swedish government, with the Swedish Energy Agency contributing 575 million kronor (just over 56 million euros).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Announcement Date**: The expansion of the SEEL project was announced on 08-03-2021.
- **Construction Start**: The opening of the Gothenburg facility is planned for Q2/2023, which is about six months later than initially scheduled.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: Specific job creation numbers have not been detailed in the article, but the expansion of the facility is expected to contribute positively to employment in the region, particularly in the e-mobility sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The SEEL project is a 50:50 joint venture between RISE (Research Institutes of Sweden) and Chalmers University of Technology. Industry partners include Scania, Volvo Cars, and the Volvo Group, indicating that Scania does not own the facility exclusively but is part of a collaborative effort.
