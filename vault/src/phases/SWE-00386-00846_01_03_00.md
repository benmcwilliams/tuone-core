## SWE-00386-00846

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Volvo Group Facility in Gothenburg, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2017**: A fire incident occurred at the Volvo battery laboratory in Gothenburg, where batteries for plug-in hybrids and electric buses are assembled. Production was reported to be unaffected.
- **December 2018**: A project in Gothenburg repurposed old Volvo bus batteries into a stationary energy storage system with a capacity of 200 kWh, aimed at supporting solar energy storage in residential buildings.
- **March 2021**: The Swedish Electric Transport Laboratory (SEEL) announced an expansion to include three sites, with Gothenburg being a key location for testing electrical drivetrains and energy storage systems.
- **February 2022**: Volvo Cars and Northvolt announced plans to build a joint battery cell factory in Gothenburg, with a potential capacity of up to 50 GWh, expected to supply batteries for around 500,000 electric cars annually.
- **September 2023**: Construction of the joint battery cell factory commenced, with an expected operational date in 2026.
- **October 2023**: The factory's construction was officially kicked off, with plans to employ over 3,000 people.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: The SEEL project saw an increase in investment from 96 million euros to approximately 128 million euros, with funding from the Swedish government.
- **February 2022**: Volvo and Northvolt announced a total investment of 30 billion Swedish kronor (about 2.9 billion euros) for the battery cell factory and a joint research facility.
- **October 2023**: The construction of the battery cell factory was confirmed, with the total investment still being part of the previously announced 30 billion kronor.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2017**: Fire incident at the battery lab.
- **December 2018**: Launch of the battery recycling project for old bus batteries.
- **March 2021**: Announcement of the SEEL project expansion.
- **February 2022**: Announcement of the joint battery cell factory with Northvolt.
- **September 2023**: Groundbreaking for the battery cell factory.
- **October 2023**: Official start of construction for the battery cell factory.

#### 4. Labour
- **March 2021**: The SEEL project is expected to create numerous jobs in Gothenburg, although specific numbers were not disclosed.
- **February 2022**: The joint battery cell factory is projected to employ over 3,000 people upon completion.
- **October 2023**: The factory's construction is expected to create several hundred jobs during the building phase.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2022**: The battery cell factory is a joint venture between Volvo Cars and Northvolt, named Novo Energy.
- **October 2023**: Volvo Cars announced intentions to take full ownership of the joint venture due to Northvolt's failure to meet financial obligations, indicating a shift towards exclusive control over the facility's operations.

This timeline encapsulates the significant developments and milestones of the Volvo Group facility in Gothenburg, Sweden, focusing on its technological advancements, investments, project statuses, labor implications, and ownership structure.
