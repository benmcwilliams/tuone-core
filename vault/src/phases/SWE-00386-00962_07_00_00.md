### Evolutionary Timeline of the CEVT Facility in Gothenburg, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Focus**: The CEVT facility is part of the Swedish Electric Transport Laboratory (SEEL), which aims to facilitate testing for e-mobility technologies. This includes electrical drivelines, energy storage systems, and entire systems for propulsion and energy management.
- **Production Capacity**: The largest SEEL plant will be located in Säve, Gothenburg, covering an area of 13,000 square meters, focusing on heavy industry applications such as trucks, buses, construction equipment, aircraft, and ships.
- **Updates**: The facility will include test benches for various components, including gearboxes, electric motors, batteries, and fuel cells.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Amount**: The investment for the SEEL project increased from 96 million euros to approximately 1.3 billion Swedish kronor (around 128 million euros).
- **Funding Sources**: The project receives funding from the Swedish government, with the Swedish Energy Agency contributing 575 million kronor (just over 56 million euros).

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Announcement Date**: The expansion of the SEEL project was announced on 08-03-2021.
- **Construction Timeline**: The opening of the Gothenburg facility is planned for Q2/2023, which is about six months later than initially scheduled.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned in the article, the expansion of the facility and its focus on e-mobility technologies suggest potential job growth in the region, particularly in research, testing, and development roles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The SEEL project is a 50:50 joint venture between RISE (Research Institutes of Sweden) and Chalmers University of Technology. Industry partners include CEVT, Scania, Volvo Cars, and the Volvo Group, indicating that CEVT is part of a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership.
