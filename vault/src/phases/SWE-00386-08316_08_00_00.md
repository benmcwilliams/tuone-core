### Evolutionary Timeline of AstraZeneca Facility in Gothenburg, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09-08-2023**: AstraZeneca enters into a Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) with Statkraft to purchase 200 GWh of energy per year from three new wind farms located in Strömstad and Årjäng. This energy supply will cover approximately 80% of the total electricity needs for AstraZeneca's research facility in Gothenburg and its manufacturing facility in Södertälje. The company has been using 100% certified renewable energy for several years and aims to further enhance its renewable energy access.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09-08-2023**: The agreement with Statkraft is part of AstraZeneca's broader commitment to sustainability and renewable energy. While specific investment amounts are not detailed, the PPA indicates a significant commitment to renewable energy sourcing, which aligns with AstraZeneca's goal to reduce its carbon footprint.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **09-08-2023**: Announcement of the PPA with Statkraft, marking a significant milestone in AstraZeneca's efforts to enhance its renewable energy supply.
- **10-08-2023**: Confirmation of the agreement details, reiterating the commitment to purchase 200 GWh per year for ten years, equivalent to two terawatt-hours.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify any direct job creation or job loss associated with the PPA or the wind farms. However, the commitment to renewable energy may indirectly support job stability or growth in the local renewable energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- AstraZeneca appears to have full ownership of its facilities in Gothenburg and Södertälje, while the wind farms supplying energy are owned by Rabbalshede Kraft, which will operate them. The PPA indicates a partnership for energy supply rather than a joint ownership of the facilities.
