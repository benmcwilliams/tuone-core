## SWE-01027-00582

### Evolutionary Timeline of Northvolt Facility in Skellefteå, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2017**: Northvolt, led by Peter Carlsson, announces plans for a battery cell factory in Sweden, with Skellefteå as a potential location. The projected cost of the facility is €4 billion, with an initial production capacity of 8 GWh, scaling up to 32 GWh by 2023.
- **October 2018**: Northvolt receives environmental permits to build Europe’s largest lithium-ion battery cell factory in Skellefteå.
- **December 2021**: Northvolt produces its first lithium-ion battery cell at the Northvolt Ett factory in Skellefteå.
- **May 2022**: Northvolt begins commercial deliveries of battery cells from the Skellefteå factory.
- **2023**: Northvolt aims to increase production capacity at the Skellefteå facility to 60 GWh to meet customer demand.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2018**: Northvolt secures €15 million from the Swedish Energy Agency for its pilot plant in Västerås, which supports the Skellefteå project.
- **June 2019**: Northvolt raises $1 billion in equity funding for the construction of its Swedish facility.
- **July 2020**: Northvolt secures $1.6 billion in loans to finance its battery cell factories in Sweden and Germany.
- **June 2021**: Northvolt raises $2.75 billion in a financing round to expand its production capacity.
- **January 2024**: Northvolt secures a $5 billion loan for the expansion of the Northvolt Ett gigafactory and its recycling plans.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2017**: Northvolt announces Skellefteå as the chosen location for its Gigafactory, with construction set to begin in the second half of 2018.
- **December 2021**: The first battery cell is produced at Northvolt Ett.
- **May 2022**: Northvolt starts commercial deliveries of battery cells.
- **November 2024**: Northvolt files for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection in the US, aiming to restructure and continue operations.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2024**: Northvolt announces plans to cut around 1,600 jobs across its Swedish locations due to financial pressures and production challenges.
- **November 2024**: Northvolt's restructuring efforts include significant layoffs, with 25% of the workforce in Sweden affected.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2019**: Volkswagen acquires a 20% stake in Northvolt, becoming a significant shareholder.
- **November 2024**: Following financial difficulties, both Volkswagen and Goldman Sachs write down their stakes in Northvolt, indicating a loss of confidence in the company's future.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments in the evolution of the Northvolt facility in Skellefteå, Sweden, highlighting its technological advancements, financial backing, operational milestones, labor impacts, and ownership dynamics.
