### Evolutionary Timeline of the Volkswagen Facility in Skellefteå, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2021**: Volkswagen announced plans for a new battery cell technology roadmap, which includes the establishment of six gigafactories in Europe, with the first being in Skellefteå. The facility is initially designed for a production capacity of 14 GWh, with plans to expand to 32 GWh, producing standardized battery cells primarily for the VW Group's electric vehicles.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: Volkswagen's order for the Skellefteå facility is valued at approximately $14 billion (around €11.75 billion). This investment is part of VW's strategy to secure battery cell production to meet its increased electric vehicle targets.
- **December 2021**: Volkswagen committed around €40 million to support the expansion of renewable energy projects, including the wind farm in Skellefteå, as part of its broader decarbonization strategy.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2021**: Announcement of the Skellefteå gigafactory as part of VW's battery production strategy.
- **April 2023**: The facility is still under development, with production expected to start in 2023. However, there are indications that VW is shifting focus to North America for future battery production, which may impact the timeline and operations at Skellefteå.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2023**: While specific job creation numbers for the Skellefteå facility have not been detailed, the overall investment in battery production is expected to create thousands of jobs in the region, contributing to local economic growth.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2021**: Volkswagen is not operating the Skellefteå facility exclusively; it is a joint venture with Northvolt. VW initially held a 20% stake in Northvolt, with plans to increase this share as part of their collaboration on the battery production.
