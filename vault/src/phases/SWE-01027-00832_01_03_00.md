### Evolutionary Timeline of the Volkswagen Facility in Skellefteå, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **March 2021**: Volkswagen announced plans for a new standardized battery cell to be produced at its gigafactory in Skellefteå, Sweden, with an initial production capacity of 14 GWh, which is expected to be expanded to 32 GWh. The facility will primarily produce battery cells for premium vehicles.
- **June 2022**: Volkswagen confirmed that the Skellefteå facility is part of a broader plan to establish six battery cell factories across Europe, totaling 240 GWh of capacity. The Skellefteå site is one of the confirmed locations alongside Salzgitter and Sagunt.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2021**: Volkswagen announced a significant order worth $14 billion (approximately €11.75 billion) for battery cells from Northvolt, which will be produced at the Skellefteå facility.
- **April 2023**: Volkswagen's plans for the Skellefteå facility were reaffirmed as part of a broader strategy to establish battery production in Europe, although the focus shifted towards North America due to competitive subsidies.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **March 2021**: Announcement of the Skellefteå gigafactory as part of Volkswagen's battery production strategy.
- **December 2021**: Volkswagen's agreement with Northvolt for the Skellefteå project was confirmed, marking it as the largest green electricity project in their portfolio.
- **April 2023**: The facility was still on track to open in 2023, although there were indications of a shift in focus towards North America for future battery production.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2023**: While specific job creation numbers for the Skellefteå facility were not detailed, the overall investment in battery production is expected to create thousands of jobs in the region, contributing to local economic growth.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **March 2021**: Volkswagen is involved in a joint venture with Northvolt for the Skellefteå facility. Volkswagen had previously acquired a 20% stake in Northvolt in 2019 and indicated plans to increase this share, although the exact ownership structure of the Skellefteå facility was not fully disclosed.

This timeline captures the key developments related to the Volkswagen facility in Skellefteå, Sweden, focusing on its technological advancements, investments, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure.
