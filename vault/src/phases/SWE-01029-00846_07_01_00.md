### Evolutionary Timeline of the Volvo Group Facility in Skövde, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2020**: Volvo Cars announced plans to assemble electric motors at its engine plant in Skövde, aiming for complete in-house production by the middle of the decade. The facility will transition from producing internal combustion engines to electric motors, with a focus on energy efficiency and performance optimization.
- **07/2021**: The formation of a joint venture called Aurobay was announced, which will develop combustion engines, transmissions, and hybrid drives. This joint venture will take over the assets of the Powertrain Engineering Sweden (PES) subsidiary, which includes the Skövde facility.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **09/2020**: Volvo announced an investment of 700 million Swedish kroner (approximately 68 million euros) for the establishment of electric motor production at the Skövde plant over the next few years.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2020**: Volvo confirmed the decision to produce electric motors at the Skövde plant, marking a significant shift in the facility's operations.
- **02/2021**: Volvo and Geely announced the cancellation of their proposed merger but confirmed plans for deeper cooperation, including the establishment of the joint venture Aurobay.
- **07/2021**: The joint venture Aurobay was officially formed, transferring all assets of PES, including the Skövde engine manufacturing facility, to focus on developing combustion and hybrid powertrains.

#### 4. Labour
- **09/2020**: The transition to electric motor production is expected to secure jobs at the Skövde facility, as Volvo aims to maintain the location's relevance in the company's future operations. However, specific job creation or loss figures were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **07/2021**: The joint venture Aurobay is initially jointly owned by Volvo Cars and Geely Holding, indicating a shift from exclusive ownership of the Skövde facility to a shared ownership model for the combustion engine and hybrid powertrain development.

This timeline captures the key developments and changes at the Volvo Group facility in Skövde, Sweden, highlighting its transition towards electric motor production and the establishment of a joint venture for combustion engine development.
