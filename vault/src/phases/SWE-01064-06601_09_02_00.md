### Evolutionary Timeline of the Phoenix BioPower Facility in Stockholm, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2022**: Phoenix BioPower is involved in the Bio-FlexGen project, which aims to develop a combined heat and power (CHP) plant system that produces heat, electricity, hydrogen, and biogenic CO2. The facility will utilize forestry residues as its primary fuel source. 
- The project is exploring two sizes for the plant:
  - **Small Facility**: 50 MW fuel input, 25 MWh electricity output, and hydrogen capacity of 34 MW (960 kg/hour).
  - **Large Facility**: 300 MW fuel input, 150 MWh electricity output, and hydrogen capacity of 192 MW (5,760 kg/hour).
- The plant is designed to provide hourly, daily, and seasonal flexibility in energy production, adapting to energy prices and demand.
