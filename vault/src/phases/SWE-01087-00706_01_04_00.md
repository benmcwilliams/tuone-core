## SWE-01087-00706

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Scania Facility in Södertälje, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2020**: Scania announced plans to build a battery assembly plant next to its chassis assembly plant in Södertälje, with an initial production capacity focused on assembling battery modules and packages from cells supplied by Northvolt. The facility will cover 18,000 square meters and is designed to support various applications, including electric buses and trucks.
- **September 2023**: The battery assembly plant officially began operations, enabling the series production of heavy premium electric vehicles. The plant assembles Northvolt's prismatic battery cells, which have a capacity of 157 Ah and are designed to last for 1.5 million kilometers.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2020**: Scania announced an investment of over one billion Swedish kronor (approximately 100 million euros) for the construction of the battery assembly plant.
- **June 2022**: Scania commissioned a new battery laboratory at its R&D facilities in Södertälje, which complements the battery assembly plant and enhances testing capabilities for battery cells, modules, and packs.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Early 2021**: Construction of the battery assembly plant was set to begin.
- **September 2023**: The battery assembly plant commenced operations, marking a significant milestone in Scania's electrification strategy.

#### 4. Labour
- **November 2020**: The battery assembly plant is expected to employ a total of 200 people, primarily sourced from within the company.
- **September 2023**: The operational battery assembly plant is projected to employ 550 people, indicating a significant increase in job creation as the facility becomes fully operational.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- Scania operates the facility as a wholly-owned subsidiary of the Traton Group, with no indication of joint ventures or shared ownership in the articles reviewed.
