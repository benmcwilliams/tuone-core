## SWE-01129-00846

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Volvo Group Facility in Torslanda, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Current Production Capacity**: As of November 2021, the Torslanda plant has a production capacity of 300,000 vehicles per year. This capacity is part of a larger plan to increase the combined capacity of Torslanda and Ghent plants to 1.2 million vehicles by the middle of the decade (2025).
- **Future Plans**: The facility is preparing for the production of the next generation of fully electric cars, with significant updates including the introduction of 'Mega Casting' technology for the electric cars' floor structure, aimed at improving cost, sustainability, and vehicle performance.
- **Battery Assembly Plant**: A battery assembly plant is to be built at Torslanda to integrate battery cells produced at a nearby joint venture battery cell factory with Northvolt, which is expected to be operational by 2026.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment Announcements**: In February 2022, Volvo announced an investment of approximately €1 billion (10 billion SEK) in the Torslanda plant to prepare it for electric vehicle production. This investment is independent of the battery plant investment.
- **Joint Venture Investment**: Volvo and Northvolt plan to invest a total of 30 billion SEK (approximately €2.9 billion) in the battery cell factory and a research and development center in Torslanda.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Expansion Plans**: In November 2021, Volvo announced plans to increase production capacity at Torslanda and Ghent plants to 1.2 million vehicles by 2025.
- **Construction of Battery Plant**: The construction of the joint battery cell factory with Northvolt began in September 2023, with operations expected to start in 2026.
- **Mobility Innovation Destination**: In February 2024, Volvo announced plans to build a new center for mobility innovations in Torslanda, with construction expected to start in the second quarter of 2024 and completion anticipated in 2026.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The new battery cell factory is expected to employ more than 3,000 people once operational in 2026. The Torslanda plant currently employs around 6,500 people, and the investments and expansions are likely to create additional jobs.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: Volvo Cars owns the Torslanda facility exclusively. There are no indications of joint ventures or shared ownership for the vehicle production facility itself, although there is a joint venture with Northvolt for the battery cell production facility.

This timeline captures the significant developments and strategic plans for the Volvo Group facility in Torslanda, Sweden, focusing on its transition towards electric vehicle production and associated infrastructure.
