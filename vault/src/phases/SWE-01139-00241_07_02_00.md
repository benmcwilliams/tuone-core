## SWE-01139-00241

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Evergrande Facility in Trollhättan, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2020**: Evergrande Group announces its intention to fully acquire National Electric Vehicle Sweden (NEVS), which produces electric vehicles, specifically the Saab 9-3 successor and plans for a new E-SUV, the Hengchi 1. The facility in Trollhättan houses production facilities and development laboratories for NEVS.
- **August 2021**: NEVS is expected to manufacture the Sono Motors Sion, with a production capacity of up to 43,000 vehicles per year, starting from 2023. Preparations for pre-series production in 2022 are ongoing.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2020**: Evergrande completes the acquisition of the remaining 17.6% of NEVS for approximately 336 million euros, valuing NEVS at around 1.95 billion euros. This acquisition gives Evergrande full control over the Trollhättan facility.
- **October 2021**: NEVS is seeking new investors due to the financial difficulties of Evergrande, which has implications for the funding and future operations of the facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2020**: Evergrande completes the full takeover of NEVS, gaining complete control over the Trollhättan facility.
- **August 2021**: NEVS lays off 300 of its 650 employees as part of a restructuring due to financial difficulties at Evergrande.
- **October 2021**: NEVS continues to seek new investors while maintaining that it has sufficient financial resources for the time being.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2021**: NEVS lays off 300 employees, reducing its workforce to 350 due to restructuring linked to Evergrande's financial issues. This significant job loss reflects the impact of the parent company's debt crisis on local operations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2020**: Evergrande completes its acquisition of NEVS, achieving full ownership of the facility in Trollhättan. This facility is noted as the only Evergrande plant outside of China. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and changes at the Evergrande facility in Trollhättan, Sweden, highlighting its operational focus, financial challenges, and workforce impacts.
