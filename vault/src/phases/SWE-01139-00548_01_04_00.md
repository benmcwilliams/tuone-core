### Evolutionary Timeline of the NEVS Facility in Trollhättan, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2016**: NEVS plans to produce electric vehicles (EVs) under its own name, moving away from the Saab branding.
- **December 2017**: NEVS begins serial production of the 9-3 EV in Tianjin, China, with plans to start production in Trollhättan in the second half of 2018.
- **March 2018**: NEVS announces plans for a battery factory at the Trollhättan site, aiming for a production capacity of 400,000 to 500,000 battery packs per year.
- **April 2019**: NEVS is set to manufacture the Sion EV for Sono Motors at the Trollhättan facility, with a production run of 260,000 vehicles over eight years starting in mid-2020.
- **June 2020**: NEVS continues to prepare for the production of the Sion EV, with plans for 43,000 vehicles per year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **March 2018**: GSR Capital invests $500 million in NEVS, which includes plans for the battery factory at the Trollhättan site.
- **January 2019**: NEVS acquires a 20% stake in Koenigsegg for $150 million to develop electric cars, with plans for a joint venture.
- **June 2020**: Evergrande Group announces plans to fully acquire NEVS, having already increased its stake to 82.4% by November 2019.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2017**: NEVS kicks off serial production of the 9-3 EV in China.
- **March 2018**: Announcement of the battery factory plans at the Trollhättan site.
- **April 2019**: NEVS to start manufacturing the Sion EV from mid-2020.
- **June 2020**: Evergrande announces intentions to fully acquire NEVS.
- **August 2021**: NEVS lays off 300 of its 650 employees due to financial difficulties at Evergrande.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2021**: NEVS lays off 300 employees as part of a restructuring due to financial issues stemming from Evergrande's debt crisis.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2019**: Evergrande acquires a 51% stake in NEVS, increasing to 82.4% by November 2019.
- **June 2020**: Evergrande announces plans to acquire the remaining 17.6% of NEVS, aiming for full ownership of the Trollhättan facility.
