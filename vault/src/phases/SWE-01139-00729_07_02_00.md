## SWE-01139-00729

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Sono Motors Facility in Trollhättan, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **April 2019**: Sono Motors announces that National Electric Vehicle Sweden (NEVS) will manufacture the Sion solar electric vehicle at the Trollhättan site, with a production capacity of 260,000 vehicles over eight years, starting in the second half of 2020. The expected annual production rate is approximately 43,000 vehicles.
- **March 2020**: Sono Motors confirms that Valoe Corp will supply solar cells for the Sion, enhancing its range by 34 km on sunny days. Production is still slated to begin in 2022.
- **August 2021**: Despite previous announcements, Sono Motors indicates that production preparations for the Sion are on track for pre-series production in 2022 and series production in the first half of 2023.
- **October 2021**: NEVS CEO states that while there is no binding production agreement, preparations for production are ongoing, and the production start for the Sion is still anticipated for 2023.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2019**: The partnership with NEVS is seen as a strategic move for Sono Motors, leveraging NEVS's experience in traditional automotive manufacturing and e-mobility.
- **March 2020**: Valoe Corp's involvement as a technology partner is highlighted, although no specific financial details regarding investments are provided.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2019**: Announcement of NEVS as the production partner for the Sion EV.
- **March 2020**: Confirmation of Valoe as a technology partner for solar cells.
- **August 2021**: NEVS confirms that preparations for production are ongoing, despite financial difficulties.
- **October 2021**: NEVS indicates that while no formal production agreement exists, they are preparing for the anticipated production start in 2023.

#### 4. Labour
- **August 2021**: NEVS lays off 300 of its 650 employees in Trollhättan due to restructuring, which raises concerns about the impact on the production of the Sion. However, Sono Motors states that preparations for production are not affected.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **2019**: Evergrande Group acquires a controlling stake in NEVS, which owns the Trollhättan facility. By June 2020, Evergrande has full ownership of NEVS, thus controlling the facility where the Sion will be produced. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and challenges faced by the Sono Motors facility in Trollhättan, Sweden, as it prepares for the production of the Sion solar electric vehicle.
