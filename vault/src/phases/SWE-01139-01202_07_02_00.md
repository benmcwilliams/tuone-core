## SWE-01139-01202

### Evolutionary Timeline of the National Electric Vehicle Sweden (NEVS) Facility in Trollhättan, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Focus**: NEVS primarily focuses on electric vehicle production, specifically utilizing the Saab 9-3 platform for its vehicles.
- **Production Capacity**: Initially, NEVS planned to produce 260,000 vehicles over eight years, with an annual capacity of approximately 43,000 vehicles in a two-shift operation.
- **Updates**: As of March 2023, NEVS has significantly reduced its operations, laying off 320 of its 340 employees, indicating a drastic reduction in production activities.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Major Investment**: In January 2019, Evergrande Health acquired a 51% stake in NEVS for $930 million, marking a significant investment aimed at expanding into the electric vehicle market.
- **Financial Challenges**: By March 2023, NEVS faced severe financial difficulties, leading to layoffs and a halt in production, as the company sought to cut costs and manage debts.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Ownership Change**: In January 2019, Evergrande Health became the majority owner of NEVS, which was previously entirely Chinese-owned.
- **Production Agreement**: In May 2019, NEVS was announced as the production partner for Sono Motors' Sion solar electric car, with production expected to start in the second half of 2020. However, by October 2021, it was revealed that no binding production agreement had been finalized.
- **Production Plans**: Despite initial plans for production, by March 2023, NEVS had laid off most of its workforce, indicating a halt in production activities and a state of "hibernation" for the facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: Initially, the facility was expected to create jobs related to the production of the Sion and other electric vehicles.
- **Job Loss**: By March 2023, NEVS laid off 320 employees, reducing its workforce to just 20, reflecting significant job losses due to financial struggles and operational cutbacks.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership Status**: As of January 2019, NEVS is primarily owned by Evergrande Health, which holds a 51% controlling stake. Prior to this, NEVS was entirely Chinese-owned but has since transitioned to a structure where a major Chinese real estate company holds the majority share.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and challenges faced by the NEVS facility in Trollhättan, Sweden, highlighting its journey from ambitious electric vehicle production plans to significant operational setbacks.
