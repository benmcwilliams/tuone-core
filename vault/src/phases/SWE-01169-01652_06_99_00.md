### Evolutionary Timeline of the First Solar Facility in Uppsala, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2023**: First Solar acquired Evolar AB, a European perovskite technology developer, marking the establishment of its first Europe-based R&D facility in Uppsala. The facility focuses on developing and commercially scaling thin film photovoltaics, particularly high-efficiency tandem devices. 
- **April 2024**: The Uppsala laboratory was officially designated as First Solar’s European Technology Centre, where it presented a CIGS solar cell with a world record efficiency of 23.64%.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2023**: The acquisition of Evolar AB is part of First Solar's strategy to enhance its R&D capabilities in Europe, although specific financial details regarding the investment in the Uppsala facility were not disclosed.
- **July 2024**: First Solar announced a total investment of nearly $2 billion in R&D across its facilities, including Uppsala, to support the development of next-generation PV technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2023**: First Solar's acquisition of Evolar AB was announced, leading to the establishment of the Uppsala facility as a key R&D site.
- **April 2024**: The Uppsala facility was confirmed as the European Technology Centre, with significant advancements in thin-film solar technology being reported.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2023**: Following the acquisition, 30 of Evolar’s R&D staff were relocated to First Solar’s research technology centre in California and Ohio, indicating a potential reduction in local employment in Uppsala.
- **July 2024**: While specific job creation numbers for the Uppsala facility were not mentioned, First Solar's overall R&D investment is expected to create about 300 new jobs by 2025, primarily at its new Jim Nolan Center in Ohio.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2023**: First Solar acquired Evolar AB, indicating that the Uppsala facility operates under First Solar's full ownership as part of its integration strategy for R&D in Europe.
