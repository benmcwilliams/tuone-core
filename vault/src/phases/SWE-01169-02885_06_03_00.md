### Evolar Facility Timeline - Uppsala, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **November 2020**: Evolar announced its innovative technology for applying perovskite thin-film layers to conventional solar panels, which can increase efficiency by approximately 5%. The facility operates an R&D prototype production line in Uppsala and aims to design and engineer production lines for partners and customers.
- **June 2021**: Evolar is working on a solution for perovskite tandem cell production and plans to offer a turnkey production solution that allows customers to produce perovskite solar cells within 18 months of signing a contract. The company operates a pilot production line using a unique evaporation technology for depositing perovskite layers.
- **August 2022**: Evolar claims that its perovskite cells can remain stable for over 25 years based on accelerated reliability testing. The company is developing production tools for perovskite cell deposition and aims to integrate its "PV Power Booster" technology into existing silicon production lines.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2020**: Norwegian investment firm Magnora ASA acquired a 28.44% interest in Evolar, with options to acquire more shares later. This investment is aimed at supporting Evolar's commercialization efforts.
- **June 2021**: Magnora increased its stake in Evolar to 40.7% as part of a long-term cooperation agreement, exercising the first of three options to increase its share. The financial details of this deal were not disclosed, but it was financed through existing cash holdings.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **November 2020**: Evolar announced its technology and the acquisition of investment from Magnora.
- **June 2021**: Evolar reached milestones from its original business plan, leading to Magnora's increased stake.
- **August 2022**: Evolar completed accelerated reliability testing for its perovskite cells, demonstrating their potential long-term stability.
