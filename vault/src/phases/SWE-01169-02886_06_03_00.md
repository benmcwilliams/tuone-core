### Evolar AB Facility Timeline - Uppsala, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **December 2021**: Evolar AB is developing a bolt-on production equipment that enhances the performance of conventional solar cells by applying a thin film layer of perovskite. This technology aims to upgrade existing cell production lines rather than replace them.
- **May 2023**: Evolar's turnkey production lines are designed to be integrated with customers' silicon PV lines, focusing on high-efficiency tandem PV technology. The company holds the world record for CIGS research solar cells with an efficiency of 23.6%.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2021**: Norwegian investor Magnora ASA increased its stake in Evolar from 40.7% to 50% to fund further development of the perovskite technology.
- **May 2023**: First Solar agreed to acquire Evolar AB for an initial payment of $38 million, with potential additional payments of $42 million based on future technical milestones. This acquisition is aimed at accelerating the development of tandem PV technology.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **December 2021**: Evolar is actively working on the commercialization of its perovskite-boost technology.
- **May 2023**: The acquisition by First Solar marks a significant milestone, as it will integrate Evolar's technology into First Solar's existing R&D efforts.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2023**: Following the acquisition by First Solar, approximately 30 of Evolar's R&D staff will join First Solar, collaborating with a team of around 60 scientists at First Solar's research technology center in Santa Clara, California.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **December 2021**: Evolar AB was partially owned by Magnora ASA, which increased its stake to 50%.
- **May 2023**: First Solar acquired Evolar AB, indicating a shift to full ownership by First Solar, marking it as their first R&D facility in Europe.
