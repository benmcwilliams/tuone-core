### Evolutionary Timeline of the Koenigsegg Facility in Ängelholm, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2019**: Koenigsegg is primarily focused on the production of exclusive hypercars. The facility in Ängelholm serves as a competence center, contributing intellectual property, technology licenses, and product design. 
- **February 2022**: Koenigsegg presents the Quark, a self-developed electric motor generating 250 kW and 600 Nm of torque, weighing only 30 kg. This motor is part of the new electric drive system called 'Raxial Flux', which combines axial and radial flux technologies. The Quark is designed to enhance the performance of the Koenigsegg Gemera, the company's first four-seater hybrid sports car.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2019**: NEVS invests $150 million to acquire 20% of Koenigsegg, establishing a strategic partnership aimed at developing electrified cars and expanding into new market segments. NEVS will invest an additional $150 million in a joint venture where it holds 65% ownership, while Koenigsegg retains 35%.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2019**: The partnership with NEVS is announced, which includes the establishment of a joint venture and the use of NEVS’s production facility in Trollhättan for future projects.
- **February 2022**: Koenigsegg unveils the Quark electric motor and the Terrier EV torque vectoring drive unit, marking a significant milestone in their technological development for hybrid vehicles.

#### 4. Labour
- **January 2019**: Koenigsegg employs slightly more than 115 people at its Ängelholm facility. The partnership with NEVS is expected to create conditions for growth in the hypercar market, potentially leading to job creation in the future.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **January 2019**: Following the investment from NEVS, Koenigsegg operates as a partially owned entity, with NEVS holding 20% of the shares. Koenigsegg retains 35% in the joint venture established with NEVS, indicating a shared ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership.
