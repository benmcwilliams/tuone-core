### Evolutionary Timeline of the SEEL Facility in Borås, Sweden

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The SEEL facility in Borås is focused on safety testing related to charging, short circuits, vibrations, mechanical shock, extreme temperatures, and fire risks. This facility is part of a larger initiative that includes multiple sites across Sweden, with the Borås site specifically dedicated to safety testing for electric mobility technologies.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The overall investment for the SEEL initiative increased from 96 million euros to approximately 1.3 billion Swedish kronor (around 128 million euros). The Swedish Energy Agency is contributing 575 million kronor (just over 56 million euros) to the project. 

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - The announcement regarding the expansion of the SEEL facility, including the Borås site, was made in March 2021. The opening of the Borås facility is planned for Q2 2023, which is about six months later than initially scheduled.

4. **Labour:**
   - The articles do not specify the exact number of jobs created or lost as a result of the SEEL facility in Borås. However, the expansion of the facility is expected to contribute positively to job creation in the region.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The SEEL project is owned as a 50:50 joint venture between RISE (Research Institutes of Sweden) and Chalmers University of Technology. Industry partners include CEVT, Scania, Volvo Cars, and the Volvo Group, indicating a collaborative ownership structure rather than exclusive ownership by a single company.
