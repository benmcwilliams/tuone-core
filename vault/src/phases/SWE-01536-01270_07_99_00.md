### Evolutionary Timeline of the SEEL Facility in Nykvarn, Sweden

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The SEEL facility in Nykvarn is focused on research and testing in battery technology and dynamic testing of components for heavy vehicles. The facility is part of a larger initiative that includes multiple sites across Sweden, with a specific emphasis on e-mobility.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The total investment for the SEEL initiative increased from 96 million euros to approximately 1.3 billion Swedish kronor (around 128 million euros). The Swedish Energy Agency is contributing 575 million kronor (just over 56 million euros) to the project.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **March 2021:** Announcement of the expansion of the SEEL initiative, including the Nykvarn facility. The opening of the Nykvarn facility is planned for Q2/2023, which is about six months later than initially scheduled.

4. **Labour:**
   - The articles do not specify the exact number of jobs created or lost as a result of the SEEL facility in Nykvarn.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The SEEL project is owned as a 50:50 joint venture between Chalmers University of Technology and RISE (Research Institutes of Sweden).
