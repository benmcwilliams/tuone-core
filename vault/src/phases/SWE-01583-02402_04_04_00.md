### Evolutionary Timeline of the Perstorp Facility in Malmö, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2021**: The Swedish Energy Agency allocated €30 million to Project Air, an initiative to produce methanol from various recovered end-of-life streams and hydrogen from electrolysis. This project aims to create a large-scale, commercial, sustainable methanol plant utilizing a carbon capture and utilization (CCU) process. The facility will feature the world's largest hydrogen electrolysis unit for production in the chemical sector.
- **March 2022**: Perstorp, along with Fortum and Uniper, submitted a €97 million application to the EU Innovation Fund for Project Air, which aims to build a sustainable methanol production facility in Stenungssund, Sweden. The project will utilize biogas, hydrogen from electrolysis, and carbon dioxide recovered from Perstorp’s operations.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2021**: The Swedish Energy Agency's allocation of €30 million to Project Air marks a significant investment in the facility's development.
- **March 2022**: The total investment for Project Air is expected to reach €236.3 million, with a €97 million application submitted to the EU Innovation Fund.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2021**: Announcement of Project Air, detailing plans for a sustainable methanol plant in collaboration with Uniper and Fortum.
- **March 2022**: Submission of the application for funding to the EU Innovation Fund, indicating progress in the project development.
