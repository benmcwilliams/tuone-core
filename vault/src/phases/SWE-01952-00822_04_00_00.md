## SWE-01952-00822

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Vattenfall Facility in Luleå, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **08/2021**: Vattenfall, in collaboration with SSAB and LKAB, produced the world's first fossil-free steel using HYBRIT technology, which utilizes 100% fossil-free hydrogen instead of coal and coke.
- **03/2022**: Construction of a 100-cubic-meter rock cavern storage facility for green hydrogen reached the halfway point. The facility is designed to store hydrogen produced during periods of high electricity generation.
- **06/2022**: The hydrogen storage facility was inaugurated, marking a significant milestone in the HYBRIT initiative aimed at fossil-free iron and steel production.
- **07/2022**: The construction of the hydrogen storage facility was completed, and it began a two-year test period to assess its capabilities.
- **09/2022**: The facility was filled with hydrogen gas and reached a maximum operating pressure of 250 bar, marking the start of its operational phase.
- **10/2023**: A month-long commercial test of the hydrogen storage on the electricity market was completed, demonstrating the facility's ability to optimize hydrogen production costs.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04/2021**: The total investment for the hydrogen storage facility was reported to be just over SEK 250 million (€24.4 million), with equal contributions from SSAB, LKAB, and Vattenfall, along with support from the Swedish Energy Agency.
- **03/2022**: The three companies invested a total of around SEK 200 million ($21.1 million) in the construction of the facility, with an additional SEK 50 million from the Swedish Energy Agency.
- **06/2022**: The total investment in the hydrogen storage facility was confirmed to be SEK 259 million, divided equally among the three companies, with SEK 72 million from the Swedish Energy Agency.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04/2021**: Announcement of the start of construction for the hydrogen storage facility.
- **06/2022**: Inauguration of the hydrogen storage facility.
- **07/2022**: Completion of the construction of the hydrogen storage facility.
- **09/2022**: The facility began its operational phase after being filled with hydrogen gas.
- **10/2023**: Completion of a month-long commercial test of the hydrogen storage facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **06/2022**: The HYBRIT initiative was highlighted as a project that would create new jobs in Sweden as part of the transition to fossil-free production methods.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **04/2021**: The hydrogen storage facility is owned equally by Vattenfall, SSAB, and LKAB, indicating a joint venture structure among the three companies.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones of the Vattenfall facility in Luleå, Sweden, focusing on its role in the HYBRIT initiative for fossil-free steel production and hydrogen storage technology.

### Technology and Operations
- **Article 1 (08-04-2021)**: "Vattenfall, SSAB and LKAB have commenced building a rock cavern storage facility for fossil-free hydrogen gas on a pilot scale."
- **Article 3 (21-03-2022)**: "Vattenfall, SSAB and LKAB have reached the halfway point in the construction of a rock cavern storage facility in a coastal city in northern Sweden."
- **Article 5 (14-07-2022)**: "Vattenfall and its partners have finished building a 100-cubic-meter underground facility to store green hydrogen."
- **Article 8 (27-09-2022)**: "The pilot facility is 100 cubic metres in size and could be expanded to 100,000 to 120,000 cubic metres."

### Investment and Development
- **Article 1 (08-04-2021)**: "The investment cost of just over SEK 250m (€24.4m) is divided equally across the holding companies and the Swedish Energy Agency."
- **Article 3 (21-03-2022)**: "The three companies are investing a total of around SEK 200 million ($21.1 million) in three equal parts in the new facility and the Swedish Energy Agency is contributing SEK 50 million."
- **Article 4 (16-06-2022)**: "SSAB, LKAB and Vattenfall invest a total of SEK 259 million in the hydrogen storage itself, divided into three equal parts, and the Swedish Energy Agency contributes with SEK 72 million."

### Project Status and Milestones
- **Article 1 (08-04-2021)**: "The storage facility is expected to be ready and operational from 2022 until 2024."
- **Article 5 (14-07-2022)**: "The inauguration ceremony marks the start of the two-year test period, which will run until 2024."
- **Article 9 (17-10-2023)**: "The hydrogen storage facility, operational since the summer of 2022, will continue testing activities until 2024."

### Labour
- **Article 4 (16-06-2022)**: "Sweden will create new jobs by leading the climate change transition, and HYBRIT is a good example of doing just that with green technology and innovation."

### Ownership Structure
- **Article 5 (14-07-2022)**: "The SEK 200 million ($18.8 million) Hybrit pilot facility is owned in three equal parts by the three companies."
- **Article 8 (27-09-2022)**: "The HYBRIT facility in Luleå, north Sweden, is a collaboration between Vattenfall, steel company SSAB and iron ore producer LKAB."
