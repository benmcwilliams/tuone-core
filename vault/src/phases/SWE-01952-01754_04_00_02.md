## SWE-01952-01754

### Evolutionary Timeline of the LKAB Facility in Luleå, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **08/2021**: The facility, part of the HYBRIT initiative, produced the world's first fossil-free steel using 100% fossil-free hydrogen instead of coal and coke. This marked a significant milestone in the transition to fossil-free iron and steel production.
- **03/2022**: Construction of a 100-cubic-meter rock cavern storage facility for green hydrogen reached the halfway point. The facility is designed to store hydrogen produced during periods of high electricity availability.
- **06/2022**: The hydrogen storage facility was inaugurated, emphasizing its role in stabilizing the production of sponge iron, a key raw material for fossil-free steel.
- **07/2022**: The construction of the hydrogen storage facility was completed, marking the start of a two-year test period for the facility, which is expected to run until 2024.
- **10/2023**: The hydrogen storage facility has been operational since the summer of 2022, with ongoing testing activities aimed at optimizing hydrogen production costs.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04/2021**: The total investment for the hydrogen storage facility was reported at SEK 250 million (€24.4 million), with equal contributions from SSAB, LKAB, and Vattenfall, alongside support from the Swedish Energy Agency.
- **03/2022**: The investment for the construction was confirmed at around SEK 200 million ($21.1 million), with the Swedish Energy Agency contributing SEK 50 million.
- **06/2022**: The total investment for the hydrogen storage facility was updated to SEK 259 million, again divided equally among the three companies, with an additional SEK 72 million from the Swedish Energy Agency.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04/2021**: Announcement of the construction of the hydrogen storage facility as part of the HYBRIT initiative.
- **08/2021**: Delivery of the first fossil-free steel produced using HYBRIT technology.
- **03/2022**: Halfway point reached in the construction of the hydrogen storage facility.
- **06/2022**: Inauguration of the hydrogen storage facility.
- **07/2022**: Completion of the hydrogen storage facility and commencement of a two-year testing phase.
- **10/2023**: Completion of a month-long commercial test of the hydrogen storage on the electricity market.

#### 4. Labour
- **06/2022**: The Minister for Energy and Digital Development highlighted that the HYBRIT initiative would create new jobs as Sweden leads the climate change transition through green technology and innovation.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **07/2022**: The hydrogen storage facility is owned equally by SSAB, LKAB, and Vattenfall, indicating a joint venture structure among the three companies.

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and milestones of the LKAB facility in Luleå, Sweden, focusing on its role in the HYBRIT initiative and the transition to fossil-free steel production.
