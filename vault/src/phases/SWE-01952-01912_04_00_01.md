## SWE-01952-01912

### Evolutionary Timeline of the SSAB Facility in Luleå, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **08/2021**: SSAB, in collaboration with LKAB and Vattenfall, produced the world's first fossil-free steel using HYBRIT technology, which utilizes 100% fossil-free hydrogen instead of coal and coke. This marked a significant milestone in the transition to fossil-free iron and steel production.
- **03/2022**: Construction of a 100-cubic-meter rock cavern storage facility for green hydrogen was reported to be halfway complete. This facility is crucial for stabilizing the production of sponge iron, the raw material for fossil-free steel.
- **06/2022**: The hydrogen storage facility was inaugurated, emphasizing its role in the overall value chain for fossil-free iron and steel production.
- **07/2022**: The construction of the hydrogen storage facility was completed, marking the start of a two-year test period expected to run until 2024.
- **10/2023**: A month-long commercial test of the hydrogen storage facility was completed, demonstrating a significant reduction in the variable cost of hydrogen production by 25% to 40%.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **04/2021**: The investment cost for the hydrogen storage facility was reported to be just over SEK 250 million (€24.4 million), with equal contributions from SSAB, LKAB, and Vattenfall, along with support from the Swedish Energy Agency.
- **03/2022**: The total investment for the hydrogen storage facility was noted to be around SEK 200 million ($21.1 million), with an additional SEK 50 million from the Swedish Energy Agency.
- **06/2022**: The total investment in the hydrogen storage facility was confirmed to be SEK 259 million, with contributions from the three companies and the Swedish Energy Agency.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04/2021**: Announcement of the construction of the hydrogen storage facility as part of the HYBRIT initiative.
- **06/2022**: Inauguration of the hydrogen storage facility.
- **07/2022**: Completion of the hydrogen storage facility and commencement of the two-year test period.
- **10/2023**: Completion of a month-long commercial test of the hydrogen storage facility.

#### 4. Labour
- **06/2022**: The Minister for Energy and Digital Development highlighted that the HYBRIT initiative would create new jobs as Sweden leads the climate change transition through green technology and innovation.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **07/2022**: The hydrogen storage facility is owned equally by SSAB, LKAB, and Vattenfall, indicating a joint venture structure for this facility.
