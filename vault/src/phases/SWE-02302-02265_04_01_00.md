### Evolutionary Timeline of H2 Green Steel Facility in Boden, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **10-06-2022**: H2 Green Steel is developing a facility in Boden, Sweden, that will produce green steel using hydrogen, aiming for carbon neutrality. The facility will utilize hydrogen to react with iron ore, producing only water vapor. The plant is expected to produce five million tonnes of steel by 2030.
- **22-02-2023**: The facility is confirmed to be the first commercial green steel plant in Europe, with production expected to start by 2025. The process will utilize a direct reduction reactor instead of a blast furnace, significantly reducing emissions by 95% compared to traditional steelmaking.
- **30-06-2023**: H2 Green Steel announces plans to operate one of the world's largest electrolyzers in Boden, with a capacity of over 700 MW, to support its hydrogen production needs.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **10-06-2022**: Statkraft, a Norwegian state-owned hydropower company, announces a power purchase agreement to supply renewable electricity to H2 Green Steel's operations from 2026 to 2032.
- **04-05-2023**: H2 Green Steel is exploring a partnership with Gotland Company to establish a production plant that could supply green hydrogen for Gotland's fleet.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **22-02-2023**: H2 Green Steel begins production at the facility, marking it as Europe's first green steel plant. Initial production is set for 2025.
- **30-06-2023**: The facility is set to operate one of the largest electrolyzers globally, enhancing its hydrogen production capabilities.

#### 4. Labour
- **22-02-2023**: The establishment of the facility is expected to create numerous jobs in the region, although specific job creation numbers have not been disclosed.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **22-02-2023**: H2 Green Steel is identified as a spin-off from Northvolt, indicating that it operates as an independent entity but may have shared ownership or investment ties with Northvolt.

#### Summary
The H2 Green Steel facility in Boden, Sweden, is positioned to revolutionize steel production in Europe by utilizing hydrogen to create green steel, significantly reducing carbon emissions. The facility is set to begin operations in 2025, with ambitious production goals and substantial investments in renewable energy sources to support its operations. The project is expected to create jobs and has established partnerships to enhance its hydrogen production capabilities.
