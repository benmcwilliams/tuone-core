## SWE-02347-00006

### Evolutionary Timeline of the ABB Facility in Söderhamn, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2023**: ABB, in collaboration with Lhyfe and Skyborn, announced the SoutH2Port project, which will integrate the production of offshore wind-generated renewable hydrogen. The facility is expected to produce approximately **240 tons of hydrogen per day** with an installed capacity of **600 megawatts (MW)**. This makes it one of the largest suppliers of renewable hydrogen in Europe.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **May 2023**: The project is part of a broader strategy to decarbonize the Swedish energy system and is linked to the development of further processing and use of green hydrogen in the region. Specific investment amounts were not detailed in the articles, but the project aims to explore Power-to-X conversion technologies.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **May 2023**: ABB, Lhyfe, and Skyborn signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) to jointly realize and optimize the SoutH2Port hydrogen project. The facility will be located near Skyborn’s **1 GW offshore wind farm** in Storgrundet, Söderhamn. The project is positioned to support the Swedish government's goal of becoming the world's first fossil-free welfare country by **2045**.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2023**: The SoutH2Port project is expected to create new jobs and business opportunities in the region, contributing to the local economy and enhancing the attractiveness of Söderhamn.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2023**: The facility will be operated by Lhyfe and Skyborn, indicating a **joint venture** structure for the operation of the hydrogen production plant, with ABB providing technological expertise but not owning the facility exclusively.

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic initiatives surrounding the ABB facility in Söderhamn, Sweden, as reported in the articles from May 2023.
