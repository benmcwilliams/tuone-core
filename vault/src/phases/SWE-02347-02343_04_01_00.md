### Evolutionary Timeline of the Lhyfe Facility in Söderhamn, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12-04-2022**: Lhyfe has developed the world’s first industrial site to produce renewable green hydrogen using power from wind turbines, inaugurated in 2021. The facility in Söderhamn is part of a collaboration with wpd Offshore to establish a 600 MW hydrogen production plant, expected to be built from 2025 onwards in several phases.
- **08-05-2023**: The new hydrogen production plant, operated by Lhyfe and Skyborn, will be connected to Skyborn’s 1 GW offshore wind farm in Storgrundet. When fully operational, it is expected to produce about 240 tonnes of hydrogen per day, with an installed capacity of 600 MW, making it one of the largest suppliers of renewable hydrogen in Europe.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12-04-2022**: Mitsui & Co made a €10 million investment in Lhyfe to support its international expansion, following a previous €66 million raised in 2021 from various investors. This investment is aimed at enhancing Lhyfe's capabilities in renewable green hydrogen production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12-04-2022**: Lhyfe and wpd Offshore signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) to establish a facility for green hydrogen production connected to the planned offshore wind farm in Söderhamn.
- **08-05-2023**: ABB joined the SoutH2Port project, collaborating with Lhyfe and Skyborn to optimize the integration of hydrogen and electricity production. The project is positioned to support the decarbonization of the Swedish energy system and is linked to the development of further processing and use of green hydrogen.

#### 4. Labour
- **08-05-2023**: The SoutH2Port project is expected to create new jobs and business opportunities in the region, contributing to the local economy and supporting the decarbonization efforts of the Swedish energy system.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **08-05-2023**: The facility is operated by Lhyfe and Skyborn, indicating a joint venture structure for the hydrogen production plant in Söderhamn.
