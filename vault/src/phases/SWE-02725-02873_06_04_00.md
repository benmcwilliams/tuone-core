### Epishine Facility Evolution Timeline - Linköping, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **09/2021**: Epishine unveiled a semi-transparent organic solar module designed for indoor low-power applications, known as Light Energy Harvesting (LEH). The module has a thickness of 0.2mm and is available in six sizes, with a power conversion efficiency just below 15% under typical indoor illumination. The facility in Linköping is responsible for manufacturing these modules, which can replace or prolong the lifetime of batteries in low-power devices.
- **11/2023**: Epishine completed a new production facility in Linköping, which utilizes roll-to-roll equipment to produce organic PV solar modules. The new facility has an annual production capacity of 100 million units of standard 6-cell 50 mm x 50 mm organic PV modules. The production process involves applying a solar cell stack to foils, followed by lamination, curing, encapsulation, and quality testing.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2019**: Epishine was awarded a grant of approximately SEK 24 million (€2.3 million) from the European Commission under the Horizon 2020 program to support its development efforts.
- **11/2023**: The completion of the new factory indicates significant investment in expanding production capabilities for low-power battery replacement products.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
