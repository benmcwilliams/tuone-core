### Evolutionary Timeline of the Uniper Facility in Oskarshamn, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2022**: The Oskarshamn facility, operated by OKG (a subsidiary of Uniper), has been producing hydrogen from electrolysers powered by its own electricity since 1992. The facility currently produces 12 kg of pink hydrogen per day, primarily for use in reactor coolant. Following the permanent closure of Oskarshamn 1 and 2 reactors in 2016 and 2017, the facility has excess hydrogen production capacity. OKG is modernizing its electrolysis plant to sell excess hydrogen to industrial gases giant Linde.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **November 2023**: Uniper announced that it is investing 8 billion euros in accelerating the green transition, with hopes that a significant portion will be allocated to Sweden, including potential investments related to the Oskarshamn facility.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2022**: The first-ever commercial deal for nuclear-derived hydrogen was signed between OKG and Linde, marking a significant milestone in the facility's operations.
- **November 2023**: Uniper is assessing the possibility of extending the lifespan of the Oskarshamn 3 reactor, indicating ongoing evaluations for future operational milestones.
