### Evolutionary Timeline of the Linde Facility in Oskarshamn, Sweden

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - **January 2022:** The Oskarshamn facility, operated by OKG, produces pink hydrogen through electrolysis powered by nuclear energy. The current production capacity is 12 kg of pink hydrogen per day. The facility has been producing hydrogen since 1992 for use in reactor coolant, but with the closure of Oskarshamn 1 and 2 reactors in 2016 and 2017, it has excess hydrogen production capacity. OKG is modernizing its electrolysis plant to enhance production capabilities and meet growing hydrogen demand.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - **January 2022:** The modernization of the electrolysis plant is supported by investments from its owners, Uniper and Fortum, although the exact size and value of the deal for hydrogen sales to Linde have not been disclosed.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **January 2022:** A commercial deal is signed between OKG and Linde for the sale of pink hydrogen, marking the first-ever commercial deal for nuclear-derived hydrogen. This signifies a milestone in the facility's operations and its role in the hydrogen market.
