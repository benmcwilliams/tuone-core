### Evolutionary Timeline of the OKG Facility in Oskarshamn, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **1992**: OKG began producing hydrogen through electrolysis powered by its own electricity, initially to meet the needs of the nuclear power plant's reactor coolant.
- **2016-2017**: Closure of Oskarshamn units 1 and 2, leading to an overcapacity of hydrogen production as only unit 3 remained operational.
- **2022**: OKG signed its first contract to supply fossil-free hydrogen to industrial gases company Linde, marking its entry into the hydrogen market as a producer and supplier. The facility can produce 12 kg of pink hydrogen per day, with plans for modernization to expand operations.
- **2024**: OKG modernized its hydrogen plant to further expand operations and support the hydrogen economy.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2022**: The exact size and value of the contract with Linde were not disclosed, but it represents a significant step in developing OKG's hydrogen production capabilities. The company received strong support from its owners, Uniper and Fortum, to develop this business opportunity.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2022**: OKG signed its first contract with an external buyer (Linde) for hydrogen production, with the first delivery expected in early 2022.
- **May 2024**: OKG announced plans to expand hydrogen production capabilities and supply hydrogen to Hynion for their filling stations, contributing to the reduction of carbon dioxide emissions.

#### 4. Labour
- **2022**: The expansion of hydrogen production and the signing of contracts with companies like Linde and Hynion are expected to create job opportunities, although specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Current**: OKG is 54.5% owned by Uniper and 45.5% owned by Fortum. This joint ownership structure supports the facility's operations and development in the hydrogen market.
