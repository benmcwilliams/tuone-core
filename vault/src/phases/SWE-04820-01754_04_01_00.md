## SWE-04820-01754

### Evolutionary Timeline of the LKAB Facility in Gällivare, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **06/2022**: The HYBRIT initiative, involving LKAB, SSAB, and Vattenfall, is set to produce hydrogen-based iron and steel. The facility in Gällivare will demonstrate hydrogen direct reduction technology, aiming for an annual production of approximately 1.35 million tonnes of hydrogen-reduced iron (sponge iron), which will contribute to 25% of Sweden's total crude steel production. A new facility will be established for hydrogen-based direct reduction with a capacity of 500 MW for fossil-free electrolysis.
- **09/2022**: The HYBRIT pilot facility is confirmed to be used for large-scale production of fossil-free sponge iron in Gällivare.
- **10/2022**: Plans are revealed to launch industrial production of hydrogen direct reduced sponge iron (H-DRI) by the end of the decade, starting with the pilot facility in Gällivare.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **06/2022**: The HYBRIT initiative receives €143 million ($157 million) from the European Union, with specific allocations for hydrogen production in Gällivare (€108 million) and for the demonstration of fossil-free direct reduced pellets production (€5 million).
  
#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **04/2022**: The EU Innovation Fund grant agreement is signed, marking a significant milestone for the HYBRIT project.
- **06/2022**: The pilot facility for hydrogen storage in Luleå is inaugurated, which is part of the broader HYBRIT initiative.
- **09/2022**: The pilot facility for hydrogen storage is filled with hydrogen gas and begins a two-year testing period, which will continue until 2024.
- **10/2022**: The partners announce their goal to start industrial production of H-DRI by the end of the decade.

#### 4. Labour
- **06/2022**: The establishment of the new facility in Gällivare is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers are not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility in Gällivare is part of the HYBRIT initiative, which is a collaboration between LKAB, SSAB, and Vattenfall. Therefore, it is not exclusively owned by LKAB but is a joint venture among the three companies.
