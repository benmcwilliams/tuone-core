### Evolutionary Timeline of the SSAB Facility in Gällivare, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **September 2022**: The facility is involved in the production of hydrogen direct reduced sponge iron (H-DRI) as part of the HYBRIT project, which aims to utilize hydrogen instead of coking coal for steel production. The technology is expected to be implemented on an industrial scale at the Gällivare facility within the next four years.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **September 2022**: The HYBRIT project, a collaboration between SSAB, Vattenfall, and LKAB, is highlighted as a significant investment in green hydrogen technology. The pilot facility in Luleå, which supports the Gällivare operations, has been commissioned to test hydrogen storage capabilities, indicating a commitment to developing the necessary infrastructure for H-DRI production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2022**: The pilot facility in Luleå is inaugurated, marking the beginning of a two-year testing period for hydrogen storage capabilities.
- **September 2022**: It is announced that industrial production of H-DRI is planned to start by the end of the decade, with the Gällivare facility being a key site for this initiative.

#### 4. Labour
- **September 2022**: While specific job creation figures are not mentioned, the announcement of industrial production plans suggests potential job creation in the Gällivare facility as operations expand to include H-DRI production.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **September 2022**: The facility is part of a joint venture involving SSAB, Vattenfall, and LKAB, indicating that SSAB does not own the facility exclusively but is a key partner in the collaborative effort to develop hydrogen-based steel production.
