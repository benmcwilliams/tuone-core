### Evolutionary Timeline of the Hybrit Development AB Facility in Gällivare, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production Capacity**: The facility will produce approximately 1.35 million tonnes of hydrogen-reduced iron (sponge iron) annually, which will be used to produce crude steel amounting to 25% of Sweden’s total production.
- **Technology**: The facility will utilize hydrogen-based direct reduction technology, replacing traditional coal-based blast furnaces. It will also feature 500MW of fossil-free electrolysis for hydrogen production.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Investment**: On April 1, 2022, the European Union injected €143 million ($157 million) into the HYBRIT initiative, with €108 million ($119 million) specifically allocated for the demonstration of the hydrogen direct reduction process and fossil-free hydrogen production in Gällivare.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Funding Announcement**: The funding was announced on April 6, 2022, as part of the EU Innovation Fund, which supports innovative low-carbon technologies.
- **Facility Establishment**: The project aims to establish a first-of-a-kind facility for producing fossil-free sponge iron on an industrial scale.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: While specific job creation numbers were not mentioned in the article, the establishment of the facility is expected to contribute to job creation in the region as it develops a new industrial process for steel production.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Ownership**: The facility is part of the HYBRIT initiative, which is driven by a collaboration between SSAB, LKAB, and Vattenfall. The article does not specify exclusive ownership, indicating a joint effort among these companies.
