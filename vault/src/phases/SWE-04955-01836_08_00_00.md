## SWE-04955-01836

### Evolutionary Timeline of the OX2 Facility in Offshore South of Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2024**: OX2 and Ingka Investments filed an application to build the offshore energy hub named Neptunus, located approximately 50 kilometers off the southern coast of Sweden. The hub is planned to include up to 207 wind turbines with a total installed capacity of 3.1 GW. It will also feature offshore hydrogen production and a pilot project aimed at oxygenating the Baltic Sea.
- **February 2024**: OX2 and Ingka Investments are expected to apply for a permit for a second project, the Pleione energy park, which will consist of a 1 GW offshore wind farm with 42–70 wind turbines and hydrogen production facilities. This project is part of the partnership established in 2023.
- **September 2023**: OX2 announced the 1.8 GW Ran offshore wind project, which will be developed alongside the Pleione offshore wind farm.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2023**: The partnership between OX2 and Ingka Investments was established, which includes the development of the Pleione energy park as part of the transaction.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2024**: The application for the Neptunus project was filed, with construction expected to start in 2030 if permits are granted.
- **February 2024**: The next milestone for the Pleione-Ran project is to complete the environmental impact assessment (EIA) to enable the filing of the application in spring 2024.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
