## SWE-05043-00822

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Vattenfall Facility in Lulea, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Pilot Plant Operations**: The facility is part of the HYBRIT (Hydrogen Breakthrough Ironmaking Technology) project, which aims to produce steel using hydrogen instead of coking coal, significantly reducing carbon emissions. As of February 2022, the pilot plant had produced a couple of hundred tons of hot briquetted iron ore using this innovative process.
- **Future Plans**: There are plans to construct a larger demonstration plant and begin commercial deliveries by 2026.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Joint Venture**: The HYBRIT project is a joint venture between SSAB, LKAB, and Vattenfall, launched in 2016. This collaboration aims to develop and scale up the production of fossil-free steel.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Pilot Plant Launch**: The pilot plant began operations in 2022, with its first commercial delivery made in the same year.
- **Fossil-Free Steel Agreement**: On December 1, 2023, Vattenfall and SSAB signed a letter of intent for SSAB to supply fossil-free steel produced at the Lulea pilot plant for various Vattenfall projects, including wind developments.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The development of the HYBRIT project and the pilot plant is expected to create jobs, although specific numbers related to job creation or loss have not been detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture Ownership**: The facility is not owned exclusively by Vattenfall; it is part of a joint venture with SSAB and LKAB, indicating shared ownership and collaboration among the three companies.
