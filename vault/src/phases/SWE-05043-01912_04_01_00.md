### Evolutionary Timeline of the SSAB Facility in Lulea, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **08/2022**: The SSAB facility in Lulea is involved in producing steel using the HYBRIT process, which utilizes green hydrogen instead of coking coal, significantly reducing carbon emissions. The pilot plant has produced a couple of hundred tons of hot briquetted iron ore as part of this process. Plans are in place to construct a larger demonstration plant and begin commercial deliveries by 2026.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **08/2022**: The HYBRIT project is a joint venture involving SSAB, LKAB, and Vattenfall, initiated in 2016. The project aims to leverage the decreasing costs of renewable energy to replace fossil fuels in steel production. Specific investment amounts are not detailed in the articles, but the initiative is part of a broader strategy to achieve fossil-free operations by 2045.
