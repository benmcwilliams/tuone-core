### Evolutionary Timeline of Ørsted Facility in Örnsköldsvik, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/2022**: Ørsted announces its investment in the FlagshipONE project, which will produce e-methanol using renewable hydrogen and biogenic CO2. The facility will have an electrolyser capacity of around 70 MW and is expected to produce 50,000 tonnes of e-methanol per year.
- **03/2023**: Ørsted confirms that FlagshipONE will be Europe’s largest commercial production facility for carbon-neutral marine fuels. The facility will utilize four proton exchange membrane (PEM) electrolyzers and is designed to avoid 100,000 tons of CO2 emissions per year in shipping. The production is set to commence in 2025.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12/2022**: Ørsted acquires a 45% stake in Liquid Wind’s FlagshipONE project, marking a significant investment in the development of sustainable e-methanol production. This investment aligns with Ørsted's strategic ambition to lead in green fuels and renewable hydrogen.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2022**: Announcement of Ørsted's entry into the FlagshipONE project, with expectations to commission the facility in 2024, pending final investment decision.
- **03/2023**: Confirmation that the facility is under construction and will serve as a blueprint for future e-methanol plants, with production expected to start in 2025.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
