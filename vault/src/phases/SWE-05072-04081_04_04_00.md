### Evolutionary Timeline of the Liquid Wind Facility in Örnsköldsvik, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **12/2022**: Liquid Wind's FlagshipONE project is introduced as a late-stage development facility aimed at producing e-methanol. It will have an electrolyser capacity of approximately 70MW and is expected to produce 50,000 tonnes of e-methanol annually using renewable hydrogen and biogenic CO2 captured from the Hörneborgsverket combined heat and power plant in Örnsköldsvik.
- **11/2024**: The facility's initial production capacity was planned for 55,000 tonnes per year, but this was later shelved due to low offtake demand and high prices. The focus shifted to developing a second, larger e-methanol plant in Östersund, which is set to produce 100,000 tonnes per year.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **12/2022**: Ørsted acquires a 45% stake in the FlagshipONE project, marking a significant investment in the facility as part of its strategy to lead in green fuels and renewable hydrogen.
- **11/2024**: Liquid Wind secures a €44 million investment from Uniper, Samsung Ventures, and HYCAP to support the development of 10 e-fuel facilities by 2027, indicating strong investor confidence in the company's potential to scale e-fuel production.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **12/2022**: Ørsted and Liquid Wind expect to commission FlagshipONE in 2024, pending a final investment decision (FID).
- **11/2024**: The FID for the initial 55,000-tonne plant in Örnsköldsvik was shelved earlier in the year due to a "challenged business case." However, Liquid Wind continues to develop its second project, NorthStarH2, in Östersund.

#### 4. Labour
- **11/2024**: The investment and development plans indicate potential job creation associated with the scaling of e-fuel production, although specific job numbers related to the Örnsköldsvik facility were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **12/2022**: Liquid Wind retains majority ownership of the FlagshipONE facility, with Ørsted holding a 45% stake, indicating a joint venture structure for this project.
