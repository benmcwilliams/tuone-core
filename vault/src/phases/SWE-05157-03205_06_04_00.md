### Evolutionary Timeline of the Midsummer Facility in Järfälla, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **August 2019**: Midsummer operates a facility producing thin, flexible, and cadmium-free solar cells. The facility utilizes proprietary DUO machines for manufacturing CIGS solar cells, which consist of copper, indium, gallium, and selenium. The solar panels produced are only 2 mm thick, flexible, and durable, designed to integrate with Midsummer's custom metal roofs.
- **November 2019**: The production capacity is enhanced by increasing the radius of the solar cells from 90mm to 100mm, resulting in a 6% increase in power per unit area, achieving an installed power of 100W per square meter for the integrated solar cell roofs. The production is sold out until March 2020, indicating high demand.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2019**: Midsummer issued a green bond amounting to US$20.6 million, which was listed on the Sustainable Bond List of Nasdaq Stockholm. The proceeds are earmarked for expanding production and distribution of solar panels and solar cells, manufacturing equipment, and R&D efforts.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones

- **November 2019**: The facility experiences increased demand for its solar roofs, leading to recruitment of additional staff to meet production needs.
