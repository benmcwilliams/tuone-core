### Evolutionary Timeline of the SSAB Facility in Oxelösund, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **July 2021**: SSAB Oxelösund produced the world's first fossil-free steel using HYBRIT technology, which utilizes 100% fossil-free hydrogen instead of coal and coke. This marked a significant milestone in the transition to fossil-free iron and steel production.
- **April 2022**: SSAB announced plans to replace its traditional blast furnaces with an electric furnace in Oxelösund as part of the HYBRIT initiative. This transition aims to demonstrate the electric melting of hydrogen-based direct reduced iron.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **April 2022**: The HYBRIT initiative received €143 million ($157 million) from the European Union. This funding is allocated for various components of the project, including:
  - €30 million ($33 million) specifically for SSAB's demonstration of electric melting of hydrogen-based direct reduced iron in Oxelösund.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **August 2021**: SSAB delivered the first fossil-free steel to Volvo Group, showcasing the successful application of HYBRIT technology.
- **April 2022**: The EU funding announcement was made, highlighting the ongoing development and future plans for the facility in Oxelösund.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is part of the HYBRIT partnership, which includes SSAB, LKAB, and Vattenfall. While SSAB operates the facility, it is not exclusively owned by SSAB but is part of a collaborative initiative with these partners.
