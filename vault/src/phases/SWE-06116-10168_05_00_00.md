### Evolutionary Timeline of Svensk Kärnbränslehantering AB Facility in Forsmark, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **1988**: The SFR (final repository for low and intermediate-level waste) began operations, initially designed to hold 63,000 cubic metres of waste.
- **April 2023**: SKB submitted an application to extend the SFR repository to almost three times its current size, aiming for a total capacity of approximately 180,000 cubic metres. The extension will include six new rock vaults, each 240-275 metres long, constructed at a depth of 120-140 metres.
- **Expected Completion**: The expansion is anticipated to take six years, with initial earthworks and infrastructure development planned to begin in autumn 2024.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **December 2014**: SKB applied to triple the size of the repository.
- **November 2019**: The application was submitted to the government by the Land and Environment Court and the Radiation Safety Authority (SSM).
- **April 2021**: The municipality of Östhammar approved the extension.
- **December 2021**: The government approved the application, which was then referred back to SSM and the Court.
- **December 2022**: SKB received an environmental permit from the Land and Environment Court for the expansion, which regulates aspects such as noise and transport.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **April 2023**: SKB announced the submission of the application for the extension of the SFR repository.
- **Autumn 2024**: Anticipated start of blasting for the expansion, pending necessary environmental approvals.
- **May 2023**: SKB announced that a poll indicated strong local support for the final disposal facilities in Östhammar and Oskarshamn.

#### 4. Labour
- **April 2023**: The takeover of Forsmark's port by SKB is expected to facilitate job creation related to the upcoming construction projects, although specific job numbers were not detailed in the articles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **April 2023**: SKB took over Forsmark's port from Forsmark's power group (FKA), indicating a shift in operational control. SKB is responsible for port operations, which are crucial for the disposal and transport of radioactive waste. The articles do not specify if SKB has exclusive ownership of the facility, but the takeover suggests a significant level of control over operations related to the facility.
