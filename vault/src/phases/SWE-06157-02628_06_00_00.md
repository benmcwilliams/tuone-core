## SWE-06157-02628

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Alight Facility in Hallstavik, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2023**: Alight announced the development of a **64 MW solar plant** in Hallstavik, Stockholm County. The facility is expected to deliver an annual production of approximately **63 GWh**, which is equivalent to the electricity consumption of roughly **12,600 villas**. The solar park will cover about **71 hectares** (equivalent to 101 football fields) and is set to be the largest solar facility in Sweden upon completion. Construction is scheduled to begin in **June 2023**, with operations expected to start in **Q2 2024**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2023**: Alight secured a **12-year Power Purchase Agreement (PPA)** with Axfood, a Swedish retailer, to sell power from the solar plant at a fixed price lower than the average electricity price in Sweden over the past year. This agreement allows Alight to utilize **non-recourse project financing** for the first time in Sweden, marking a significant shift in financing practices for solar projects in the country. The financing for the Hallstavik project will be provided by **SEB**, a leading northern European financial services group.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2023**: Construction of the solar park in Hallstavik is set to begin immediately following the announcement. The facility is expected to be operational by **Q2 2024**.
- **October 2023**: Alight's CEO, Harald Överholm, highlighted the Hallstavik project as part of the company's mission to add more green megawatts to the grid, emphasizing its importance in accelerating Sweden's transition to sustainable energy.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2023**: While specific job creation numbers were not detailed in the articles, the construction and operation of the solar facility are expected to contribute to local employment opportunities, particularly in the renewable energy sector.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2023**: Alight will **build, own, and operate** the solar farm in Hallstavik. The partnership with Axfood is strictly for the PPA, indicating that Alight retains full ownership of the facility.
