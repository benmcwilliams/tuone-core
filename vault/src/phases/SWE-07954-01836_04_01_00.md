## SWE-07954-01836

### Evolutionary Timeline of the OX2 Facility in Blekinge, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **January 2024**: OX2 and Ingka Investments submitted a permit application for the Neptunus offshore wind hub, which will have a total installed capacity of **3.1 GW** (3,100 MW). The project will consist of **207 wind turbines** with heights up to **420 meters**, located **50 km** offshore. The expected annual electricity production is between **13 and 15 TWh**, sufficient to meet the electricity needs of the Blekinge and Skåne regions. Additionally, the facility aims to produce **370,000 tonnes of offshore hydrogen annually** and includes a pilot project to utilize oxygen, a byproduct of hydrogen production, to oxygenate the Baltic Sea.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **January 2024**: The permit application for the Neptunus project is part of a broader investment strategy by OX2 and Ingka Investments, which includes multiple offshore wind projects in Sweden. The project is in the early stages of development, with **1.9 GW** of the total capacity falling under OX2’s project development portfolio, which totaled **34 GW** by the end of Q3 2023.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **January 2024**: OX2 and Ingka Investments applied for the construction permit for the Neptunus offshore energy hub. If permits are granted, construction is expected to start in **2030**. The project is currently in the early development phase, with previous applications for a Natura 2000 permit also submitted.

#### 4. Labour
- The articles do not specify any job creation or job loss associated with the Neptunus project at this stage, as it is still in the early development phase.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- OX2 and Ingka Investments are joint partners in the Neptunus project, indicating a **joint venture** ownership structure for the facility. OX2 is not the exclusive owner of the facility, as it collaborates with Ingka Investments, the investment arm of the Ingka Group (IKEA).
