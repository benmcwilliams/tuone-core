## SWE-08452-00822

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Vattenfall Facility in Ringhals, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2022**: Vattenfall announced a pilot study to assess conditions for building at least two small modular reactors (SMRs) adjacent to the Ringhals plant, aiming for operation in the early 2030s.
- **November 2023**: Vattenfall applied for planning permission to build new reactors, indicating the need for a detailed plan for the site and the potential sharing of existing infrastructure.
- **June 2024**: Vattenfall shortlisted Rolls-Royce SMR and GE Hitachi Nuclear Energy as potential suppliers for the SMRs, while also evaluating large-scale reactor options.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2022**: The Swedish government adopted a positive stance towards nuclear energy, indicating a shift in policy that supports the construction of new reactors, including SMRs.
- **November 2023**: The government announced a commission to streamline the permitting process for new nuclear power, which is expected to facilitate future investments.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2022**: Initiation of a pilot study for new SMRs.
- **November 2023**: Application for planning permission for new reactors submitted to Varberg municipality.
- **June 2024**: Shortlisting of SMR suppliers and ongoing evaluation of large-scale reactor options.

#### 4. Labour
- **February 2024**: Nuvia was contracted to carry out decommissioning work at Ringhals, which is expected to mobilize up to 400 people from mid-2025 to 2031.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **November 2023**: Vattenfall owns most of the one-square-kilometre area west of the existing plant at Ringhals and is in the process of acquiring additional properties in the area for future developments.

This timeline captures the key developments and changes at the Vattenfall facility in Ringhals, Sweden, focusing on technology, investment, project milestones, labor implications, and ownership structure.
