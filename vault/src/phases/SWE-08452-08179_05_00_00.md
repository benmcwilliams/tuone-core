### Evolutionary Timeline of the Westinghouse Facility in Ringhals, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **1970**: Ringhals 2, a pressurized water reactor designed by Westinghouse, begins operations.
- **2015**: Vattenfall announces the intention to close Ringhals 1 and 2 due to economic factors, limiting investments in the facility.
- **2021**: Vattenfall awards Westinghouse a contract for the segmentation and disposal of reactor pressure vessels, internals, and fuel racks at Ringhals 1 and 2.
- **2022**: Vattenfall initiates a pilot study to assess conditions for building at least two small modular reactors (SMRs) adjacent to the Ringhals plant, aiming for operation in the early 2030s.
- **2024**: Westinghouse teams up with Hyundai E&C to provide design and development of AP1000 technology for new nuclear power projects at Ringhals.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2022**: Vattenfall's pilot study for SMRs indicates potential future investments, although specific financial details are not disclosed.
- **2024**: The teaming agreement between Westinghouse and Hyundai E&C is part of a strategic cooperation to explore new nuclear power opportunities, indicating potential investments in the region.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2015**: Final decision to close Ringhals 1 and 2 is made in October.
- **2021**: Contract awarded to Westinghouse for decommissioning work at Ringhals 1 and 2.
- **2022**: Pilot study for SMRs initiated.
- **2024**: Announcement of the partnership between Westinghouse and Hyundai E&C for new nuclear projects at Ringhals.

#### 4. Labour
- **2024**: Nuvia is set to mobilize up to 400 people for decommissioning work at Ringhals from mid-2025 to 2031, involving the processing of over 30,000 tonnes of materials.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- The facility is owned by Vattenfall, which operates the Ringhals nuclear power plant. Westinghouse has been involved in various capacities, including contracts for decommissioning and potential new builds, but does not own the facility. The partnership with Hyundai E&C for new projects indicates a collaborative approach rather than joint ownership.
