### Evolutionary Timeline of the Enlight Renewable Energy Facility in Bjornberget, Sweden

1. **Technology and Operations:**
   - The Bjornberget facility is a wind farm with a production capacity of 372 MW, featuring 60 Siemens Gamesa 6.2 MW turbines. It is expected to generate approximately 1.1 terawatt-hours of electricity annually. The facility will also include around 64 km of new and upgraded roads and 130 km of cabling.

2. **Investment and Development:**
   - The construction of the wind farm is being managed by RES, which has appointed Peab and SIC as contractors for the balance of plant. Specific investment amounts are not detailed in the article, but the involvement of established contractors indicates significant financial backing for the project.

3. **Project Status and Milestones:**
   - **February 2021:** RES announced the hiring of Peab and SIC as contractors for the Bjornberget wind farm, with construction set to start in 2021. The facility is expected to come online in early 2023.

4. **Labour:**
   - At peak construction, the project is expected to employ approximately 200 people on-site, contributing to local job creation during the construction phase.

5. **Ownership Structure:**
   - The Bjornberget wind farm is jointly owned by Enlight Renewable Energy and Prime Capital’s Prime Green Energy Infrastructure Fund.
