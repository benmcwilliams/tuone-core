## SWE-08712-08840

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Eolus Vind Facility in Västvind, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2023**: Eolus Vind, in collaboration with Volvo Cars, announced the Västvind offshore wind power project. The facility is planned to have a production capacity of **1,000 MW**, with the potential to generate **4-4.5 TWh** of renewable electricity annually. This capacity is intended to meet the electricity needs of the entire city of Gothenburg.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2023**: The partnership with Volvo Cars aims to secure access to locally produced renewable electricity, which is crucial for Volvo's goal of becoming climate-neutral by 2040. The project is positioned as a response to the significant electricity production shortage in Västra Götaland, where demand is expected to nearly double by 2030.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **July 2023**: The permit application and environmental impact assessment for the Västvind project were submitted to the Land and Environmental Court and the Swedish government.
- **October 2023**: Construction is planned to start in **2027**, with completion expected in **2029**.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2023**: The transition to renewable electricity through the Västvind project is anticipated to create many new jobs in the automotive industry in the region, as stated by Volvo Cars' Head of Strategic Collaborations.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2023**: Eolus Vind owns **95%** of the Västvind project, while the **Gothenburg Port Authority** holds the remaining **5%**. 

This timeline encapsulates the key developments and strategic importance of the Eolus Vind facility in Västvind, highlighting its role in supporting renewable energy goals and local economic growth.
