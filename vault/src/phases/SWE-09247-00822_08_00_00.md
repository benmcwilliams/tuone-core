### Evolutionary Timeline of Vattenfall Facility in Lycksele, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2018**: Vattenfall acquired the Vargtrask wind farm, which has a permit for up to 21 turbines and a production capacity of 80MW. The facility is located in the Asele and Lycksele municipalities and is expected to be operational by 2021-2022.
- **May 2022**: Vattenfall inaugurated the Blakliden Fäbodberget onshore wind farm, which has a total capacity of 353MW, equipped with 84 wind turbines. This facility can generate approximately 1TWh of clean energy annually, sufficient to power nearly 220,000 households.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2018**: Vattenfall's acquisition of the Vargtrask wind farm indicates a strategic investment in expanding its renewable energy portfolio, although specific financial details were not disclosed.
- **May 2022**: The Blakliden Fäbodberget project involved significant investment, with construction beginning in 2018 following a procurement process and investment decision.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2018**: Vattenfall announced the acquisition of the Vargtrask wind farm and indicated a final investment decision was expected in 2020.
- **May 2022**: The Blakliden Fäbodberget wind farm was inaugurated on May 23, 2022, marking the completion of construction that began in 2018.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2022**: During the pre-planning, planning, and construction phases of the Blakliden Fäbodberget project, approximately 390 full-time jobs were created, with 300 of those being regional roles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2022**: The Blakliden Fäbodberget wind farm is jointly owned by Vattenfall (30% stake), Vestas, and AIP Management, indicating that Vattenfall does not have exclusive ownership of this facility. The Vargtrask wind farm's ownership structure is not explicitly detailed in the articles, but it is noted that Vattenfall acquired it from Nordisk Vindkraft.
