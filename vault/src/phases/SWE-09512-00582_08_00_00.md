### Evolutionary Timeline of the Northvolt Facility in Hisingen, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **Production**: The facility is involved in the production of batteries, specifically for electric vehicles, in collaboration with Volvo Cars.
- **Capacity**: The facility's production capacity details are not explicitly mentioned in the article, but it is implied that the facility will significantly contribute to the battery supply for Volvo Cars' goal of selling only fully electric cars by 2030.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **Partnership**: Northvolt is collaborating with Volvo Cars on the construction of a new battery factory at Hisingen. The partnership aims to secure renewable electricity for the facility through the Västvind offshore wind power project.
- **Electricity Supply**: The Västvind project, which is expected to supply significant amounts of electricity to the facility, has a planned capacity of 1,000 MW and aims to generate 4-4.5 TWh of renewable electricity annually.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **Collaboration Announcement**: On 18-10-2023, it was announced that Volvo Cars and Eolus Vind signed a letter of intent for the Västvind offshore wind power project, which will support the battery factory at Hisingen.
- **Construction Timeline**: The construction of the Västvind wind farm is planned to start in 2027, with completion expected in 2029.

#### 4. Labour
- **Job Creation**: The transition to renewable electricity and the establishment of the battery factory are expected to create many new jobs in the automotive industry for the region, as stated by Johan Lannering from Volvo Cars.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **Joint Venture**: The battery factory at Hisingen is a collaborative effort between Northvolt and Volvo Cars, indicating a joint venture structure for the facility. Additionally, the Västvind wind power project is primarily owned by Eolus (95%) with Gothenburg Port Authority holding the remaining 5%.
