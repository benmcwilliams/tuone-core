## SWE-09512-00846

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Volvo Group Facility in Hisingen, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **October 2023**: Volvo Cars is collaborating with Eolus Vind on the Västvind offshore wind power project, which aims to supply electricity to Volvo's operations in Hisingen, including a new battery factory being constructed in partnership with Northvolt. The Västvind project is planned to have a capacity of 1,000 MW, generating 4-4.5 TWh of renewable electricity annually, sufficient to meet the electricity needs of the entire city of Gothenburg.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **October 2023**: The partnership with Eolus Vind is part of Volvo's strategy to secure locally produced renewable electricity at a reasonable cost, supporting its goal to sell only fully electric cars by 2030 and to become climate-neutral by 2040. The Västvind project is expected to address the anticipated doubling of electricity demand in the region by 2030.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **October 2023**: A letter of intent was signed between Volvo Cars and Eolus Vind to collaborate on the Västvind offshore wind project. The permit application for the project was submitted in July 2023, with construction planned to start in 2027 and completion expected in 2029.

#### 4. Labour
- **October 2023**: The transition to renewable energy through the Västvind project is expected to create many new jobs in the automotive industry in the region, as stated by Johan Lannering, Head of Strategic Collaborations at Volvo Cars.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **October 2023**: Eolus owns 95% of the Västvind project, while Gothenburg Port Authority holds the remaining 5%. The partnership with Eolus indicates that Volvo does not have exclusive ownership of the wind power project but is involved through a joint collaboration. 

This timeline captures the key developments related to the Volvo Group facility in Hisingen, Sweden, focusing on the collaboration with Eolus Vind for renewable energy supply.
