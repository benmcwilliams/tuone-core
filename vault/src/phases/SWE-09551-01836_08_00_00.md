### Evolutionary Timeline of the OX2 Facility in Harjedalen, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **February 2019**: OX2 is developing the 220MW Harjedalen wind project, which will feature 53 Nordex Delta4000 turbines. The facility is expected to be operational in 2021 and will be one of the largest wind farms in Sweden.
- **May 2022**: OX2 hands over the 286MW Andberg wind farm, which includes 53 Nordex N149 5.4MW wind turbines, to Ardian. OX2 continues to manage the technical and commercial operations of the facility.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **February 2019**: Ardian acquires the Harjedalen wind project from OX2 for €300 million. This acquisition is part of Ardian's strategy to build a Nordic platform for renewable energy investments.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **February 2019**: Announcement of the acquisition of the Harjedalen project by Ardian, with construction to be managed by OX2.
- **May 2022**: Official handover of the Andberg wind farm to Ardian, marking the completion of the project that OX2 developed.

#### 4. Labour
- **May 2022**: OX2 acknowledges the efforts of the project team in delivering the Andberg wind farm during the pandemic, indicating job creation associated with the construction and management of the facility.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **February 2019**: OX2 sells the Harjedalen project to Ardian but retains responsibility for construction and management, indicating a joint operational relationship.
- **May 2022**: OX2 continues to manage the Andberg wind farm after its handover to Ardian, suggesting a continued partnership in operational management despite the change in ownership.
