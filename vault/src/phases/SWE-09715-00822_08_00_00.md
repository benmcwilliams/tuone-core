## SWE-09715-00822

### Evolutionary Timeline of the Vattenfall Facility in Swedish West Coast, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **June 2022**: Vattenfall and Preem announced feasibility studies to assess the development of a value chain connecting offshore wind and hydrogen with the refining industry on the Swedish west coast. The goal is to enable the production of fossil-free hydrogen to decarbonize refineries and increase the production of renewable fuels such as HVO, renewable gasoline, and sustainable aviation fuels. The companies aim to produce 5 million cubic meters of renewable fuels and e-fuels by 2035.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **June 2022**: The feasibility phase initiated by Vattenfall and Preem is aimed at accelerating the development of infrastructure for hydrogen from offshore wind, which is expected to enhance the supply of fossil-free hydrogen and support the transition to a climate-neutral value chain.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **June 2022**: Vattenfall and Preem entered into a feasibility phase to explore large-scale decarbonization using offshore wind and hydrogen, with a target to start the transition to fossil-free fuels by 2030.

#### 4. Labour
- **June 2022**: The partnership between Vattenfall and Preem is expected to create job opportunities in the renewable fuels sector as they work towards increasing the production capacity and transitioning to fossil-free fuels.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **June 2022**: The facility is a collaborative effort between Vattenfall and Preem, indicating a partnership rather than exclusive ownership by either company. 

This timeline captures the key developments and strategic initiatives related to the Vattenfall facility on the Swedish west coast, focusing on the collaboration with Preem to advance clean technology and renewable energy production.

### Technology and Operations
