### Evolutionary Timeline of Vattenfall Facility in Åsele, Sweden

#### 1. Technology and Operations
- **May 2022**: Vattenfall inaugurated the Blakliden Fäbodberget onshore wind farm in Åsele and Lycksele municipalities. The facility has a production capacity of **353 MW** and is equipped with **84 wind turbines**. It is capable of generating **1 TWh** of clean energy annually, sufficient to power nearly **220,000 households**.

#### 2. Investment and Development
- **2018**: Construction works for the Blakliden Fäbodberget wind farm began following a procurement process and investment decision made after the projects were legally approved in **2015** and **2016**.
- **May 2022**: The facility was officially inaugurated, marking the completion of the investment in the wind farm.

#### 3. Project Status and Milestones
- **2015-2016**: Legal approvals for the Blakliden and Fäbodberget wind projects were obtained.
- **2018**: Construction commenced.
- **May 23, 2022**: Inauguration ceremony for the Blakliden Fäbodberget wind farm.

#### 4. Labour
- **2018-2022**: During the project’s pre-planning, planning, and construction phases, approximately **390 full-time jobs** were created, with **300** of those being regional roles.

#### 5. Ownership Structure
- **May 2022**: The Blakliden Fäbodberget wind farm is **jointly owned** by Vattenfall (holding a **30% stake**), Vestas, and AIP Management.
